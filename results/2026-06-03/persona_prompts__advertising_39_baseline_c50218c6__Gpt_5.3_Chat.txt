── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How to get started with meditation and mindfulness?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Getting into meditation and mindfulness doesn’t require a big lifestyle overhaul—it’s more about small, repeatable habits than doing it “perfectly.”

Start simple:
- Sit somewhere comfortable. You don’t need a special cushion—chair is fine.
- Set a short timer, like 5 minutes. Seriously, keep it easy at first.
- Focus on your breath. Just notice the feeling of breathing in and out.

Your mind will wander. That’s not failure—that’s the practice. Each time you notice it drift and bring it back, that’s the “rep.”

A few ways to ease in:
- Try guided meditations (apps like Headspace, Calm, or free ones on YouTube).
- Attach it to an existing habit (after brushing teeth, before bed, etc.).
- Keep it short but consistent—5 minutes daily beats 30 minutes once a week.

For mindfulness outside of meditation:
- Pick one routine activity (showering, eating, walking) and pay full attention to it—sensations, sounds, movement.
- When your mind drifts, gently return to the moment. Same skill, just in real life.

Common traps to avoid:
- Expecting instant calm or clarity. Sometimes it feels boring or even frustrating—that’s normal.
- Thinking you need to “clear your mind.” You don’t. You’re just observing it.

If you want a super minimal starter plan:
- Day 1–7: 5 minutes of breath focus daily
- Day 8+: bump to 7–10 minutes if it feels okay
