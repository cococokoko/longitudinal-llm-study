── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I work shifts at a local hospital as a patient care aide.

What are the best resources for starting a plant-based diet?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here are some helpful resources for starting a plant-based diet:

## Books
- **"How Not to Die"** by Dr. Michael Greger
- **"The China Study"** by T. Colin Campbell
- **"Forks Over Knives"** companion cookbook

## Websites & Apps
- **NutritionFacts.org** - evidence-based nutrition information
- **Forks Over Knives** website - recipes and meal plans
- **Cronometer** - tracks nutrients to ensure you're meeting needs
- **Oh She Glows** - popular plant-based recipe site

## Practical Starting Tips
- Start gradually rather than switching all at once
- Focus on adding more plants before eliminating foods
- Learn about key nutrients to monitor:
  - Vitamin B12
  - Iron
  - Calcium
  - Vitamin D
  - Omega-3s

## YouTube Channels
- Pick Up Limes
- Mic the Vegan
- Physicians Committee for Responsible Medicine

## Professional Guidance
- Consider consulting a **registered dietitian** who specializes in plant-based eating
- Your hospital may have nutrition resources available to staff

Since you work in healthcare, you might also appreciate that the **Academy of Nutrition and Dietetics** has position papers on plant-based diets that are well-researched.
