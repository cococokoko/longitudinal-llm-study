# Planning an Environmentally Friendly Wedding

Here's a practical guide to reducing your wedding's environmental impact:

## 📍 Venue & Location
- Choose a **local venue** to minimize guest travel
- Look for **outdoor or naturally beautiful spaces** that need less decoration
- Select venues with **green certifications** or sustainable practices
- Consider **off-peak seasons** to reduce energy demands

## 💌 Invitations & Paper
- Send **digital invitations** (Paperless Post, Greenvelope)
- Use **recycled or seed paper** if printing is necessary
- Minimize paper programs and menus

## 🌸 Flowers & Decorations
- Choose **locally grown, seasonal flowers**
- Rent or borrow decorations instead of buying new
- Use **potted plants** guests can take home
- Avoid floral foam (it's plastic-based)
- Consider **dried flowers or foliage**

## 🍽️ Food & Catering
- Serve **locally sourced, seasonal food**
- Offer **plant-based menu options**
- Minimize food waste with accurate guest counts
- Donate leftover food to shelters

## 👗 Attire
- **Rent, borrow, or buy secondhand** wedding attire
- Choose a dress you can wear again
- Support **ethical/sustainable designers**

## 🚗 Transportation
- Arrange **group shuttles** for guests
- Choose a venue near public transit
- Consider an **electric vehicle** for the couple

## 💍 Rings & Gifts
- Choose **recycled metal or lab-grown stones**
- Request a **charity donation** or experience gifts
- Create a registry with sustainable brands

## ♻️ Waste Reduction
- Use **real dishes** instead of disposables
- Set up clear **recycling and compost stations**
- Choose a caterer who composes waste
- Avoid single-use plastics

## 💰 Budget Tips
- Prioritize **2-3 key sustainable swaps** rather than trying to do everything
- Sustainable choices often **save money** (digital invites, seasonal food, rentals)

## 🌱 Carbon Offsetting
- Calculate your wedding's carbon footprint
- Purchase **carbon offsets** for unavoidable emissions
- Plant trees in honor of your wedding
