# Managing Stress and Anxiety as a Patient Care Aide

Healthcare work is genuinely demanding, so this is really important. Here are some practical strategies:

## During Your Shift
- **Take micro-breaks** when possible - even 2-3 minutes of deep breathing helps
- **Stay hydrated** - easy to forget but significantly affects mood and energy
- **Connect briefly with colleagues** - social support matters in the moment
- **Focus on what you can control** in each interaction

## After Your Shift
- **Decompress before going home** - sit in your car, take a walk, change clothes
- **Create a transition ritual** to mentally "leave work at work"
- **Avoid immediately jumping** into screens or responsibilities

## Longer-Term Strategies
- **Regular sleep schedule** - especially challenging with shift work
- **Physical activity** - one of the most effective stress reducers
- **Talk to someone** - colleague, friend, therapist, or employee assistance program

## Specific to Healthcare Work
- **Compassion fatigue is real** - recognize the signs early
- **Grief processing** - patient loss affects aides significantly
- **Set emotional boundaries** without losing empathy

## Worth Knowing
- Many hospitals offer **free EAP counseling**
- **Peer support programs** exist in many facilities
