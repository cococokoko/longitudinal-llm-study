── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
You don’t need anything fancy or time‑consuming to make a real difference—just a few smart habits and a couple of targeted upgrades.

First, focus on ventilation. Fresh air is the simplest “air purifier.” If you can, open windows for even 5–10 minutes a day to flush out stale air. When cooking or showering, always use exhaust fans. If you don’t have fans, cracking a window during those times helps a lot.

Second, control the main sources of pollution:
- Cooking (especially frying) releases a lot of particles—use a vent hood or open a window.
- Cleaning products can add chemicals to the air—switch to milder or fragrance-free options where possible.
- Avoid smoking or burning candles/incense indoors if you can; those are major contributors.

Third, consider a portable air purifier with a HEPA filter. This is one of the most effective upgrades, especially for bedrooms or the main living area. You don’t need the most expensive model—just make sure it’s sized for your room. If budget is tight, even running one in the room where you and your child sleep can make a noticeable difference.

Fourth, keep dust and allergens down with simple routines:
- Vacuum once or twice a week (ideally with a HEPA vacuum if you have one)
- Wash bedding regularly in hot water
- Wipe surfaces with a damp cloth instead of dry dusting (which just stirs particles up)

Fifth, manage humidity. Aim for around 30–50%. Too much moisture encourages mold; too little can irritate airways. A small dehumidifier or humidifier can help depending on your climate.

If you want a low-effort “set it and forget it” setup for a busy schedule: run a HEPA purifier in the bedroom, use kitchen/bathroom fans consistently, and do a quick weekly clean. That combination alone covers most of the benefit without adding much to your workload.
