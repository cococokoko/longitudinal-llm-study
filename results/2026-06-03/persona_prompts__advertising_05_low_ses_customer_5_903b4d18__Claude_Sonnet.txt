# Healthy Meal Planning on a Budget

Here are practical strategies that work well for larger households with multiple responsibilities:

## Core Strategies

**Plan Before You Shop**
- Plan meals for the whole week at once
- Check what you already have first
- Build meals around sales and seasonal produce

**Buy Smart**
- Purchase staples in bulk (rice, beans, oats, pasta)
- Choose frozen vegetables - equally nutritious, much cheaper
- Buy whole chickens instead of pre-cut pieces
- Store brands are usually just as good

## Budget-Friendly Nutritious Foods

| Food | Why It Works |
|------|-------------|
| Dried beans/lentils | Cheap protein, very filling |
| Eggs | Versatile, affordable protein |
| Cabbage/carrots | Last long, very inexpensive |
| Canned fish | Omega-3s at low cost |
| Oats | Filling breakfast for everyone |

## Time-Saving Tips for Busy Households

- **Batch cook** on weekends - one big pot feeds many meals
- Make **double portions** and freeze half
- Prep vegetables all at once for the week
- Keep **simple go-to meals** everyone accepts

## Stretch Your Budget Further

- Meatless meals 2-3 times weekly
- Use cheaper cuts in slow cooker meals
- Repurpose leftovers creatively
