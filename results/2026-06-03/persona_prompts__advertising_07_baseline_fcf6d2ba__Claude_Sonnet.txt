# Effective Ways to Reduce Household Energy Use

## Heating & Cooling (typically largest expense)
- **Set thermostats strategically** – 68°F when home, lower when sleeping or away
- **Use programmable/smart thermostats** to automate temperature schedules
- **Seal air leaks** around windows, doors, and outlets
- **Add insulation** to attics, walls, and basements
- **Change HVAC filters regularly** (every 1-3 months)
- **Use ceiling fans** to supplement heating and cooling

## Appliances & Electronics
- **Unplug devices** or use smart power strips to eliminate standby power
- **Run dishwashers and washing machines with full loads**
- **Wash clothes in cold water**
- **Air dry dishes and laundry** when possible
- **Choose ENERGY STAR certified appliances** when replacing

## Lighting
- **Switch to LED bulbs** (use ~75% less energy than incandescent)
- **Use natural light** when available
- **Install motion sensors or timers**

## Water Heating
- **Lower water heater to 120°F**
- **Fix leaky faucets**
- **Take shorter showers**
- **Insulate hot water pipes**

## Bigger Investments
- Solar panels
- Heat pump systems
- Energy audit from a professional

## Quick Wins
- Most utilities offer **free energy audits**
- Many areas have **rebates** for efficiency upgrades
