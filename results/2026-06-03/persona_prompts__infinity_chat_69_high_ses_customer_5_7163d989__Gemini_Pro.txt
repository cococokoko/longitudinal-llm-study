── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
What is the difference between analysis and design? Can one begin to design without analysis? Why? Be concise.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
**The Difference:**
*   **Analysis** defines the *What* and *Why*: It is the process of understanding the business problem, user needs, and system requirements. 
*   **Design** defines the *How*: It is the process of architecting the technical solution, user experience, and system blueprints to solve that problem.

**Can you begin design without analysis?**
Technically yes, but strategically no. 

**Why?**
Skipping analysis means engineering a solution for an unvalidated problem. It inevitably leads to building the wrong product, resulting in wasted capital, poor product-market fit, and expensive technical debt. Analysis is your primary risk-mitigation tool; without it, design is just guesswork.
