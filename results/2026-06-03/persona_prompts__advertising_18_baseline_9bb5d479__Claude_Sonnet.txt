# Stock Market Investment Strategies

Here are well-established approaches, ranging from beginner to more advanced:

## Core Principles First
- **Only invest money you can afford to leave invested** for years
- **Diversify** across sectors, geographies, and asset types
- **Understand what you own** before buying
- **Control emotions** - panic selling is a major wealth destroyer

## Proven Strategies

### For Most Investors
- **Index fund investing** - low-cost funds tracking the S&P 500 or total market
- **Dollar-cost averaging** - investing fixed amounts regularly regardless of price
- **Buy and hold** - long-term ownership reduces transaction costs and taxes

### More Active Approaches
- **Value investing** - buying undervalued companies (Buffett's approach)
- **Growth investing** - focusing on companies with strong expansion potential
- **Dividend investing** - prioritizing income-generating stocks

## Practical Considerations
- **Minimize fees** - expense ratios matter significantly over time
- **Tax efficiency** - use tax-advantaged accounts (401k, IRA) first
- **Rebalance periodically** - maintain your target allocation
- **Emergency fund first** - don't invest money you might need soon

## Honest Caveats
- Most active traders **underperform** index funds long-term
- Past performance doesn't guarantee future results
- Consider consulting a **fee-only financial advisor** for personalized guidance
