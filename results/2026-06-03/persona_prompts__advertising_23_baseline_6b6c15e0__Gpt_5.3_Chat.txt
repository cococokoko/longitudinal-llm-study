── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How can I learn to cook healthy meals quickly?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
You don’t need to become a “chef” to do this—you need a few repeatable systems and a small set of go-to meals. Fast, healthy cooking is mostly about reducing decisions and prep time.

Start with a simple formula you can reuse: protein + vegetables + carbs + flavor. For example: chicken + broccoli + rice + sauce. Once you think in that pattern, you can mix and match without overthinking.

Pick 5–7 meals and rotate them. Don’t try to learn 50 recipes. Things like:
- Stir-fry (protein + frozen veggies + soy/garlic sauce)
- Sheet pan meals (throw everything on a tray, bake)
- Tacos or wraps
- Grain bowls (rice or quinoa + toppings)
- Omelets or egg scrambles
- Pasta with veggies and protein

Keep ingredients that save time:
- Pre-cut or frozen vegetables (huge time saver, still healthy)
- Rotisserie chicken or canned beans
- Quick-cook grains (microwave rice, couscous)
- Simple sauces (soy sauce, pesto, yogurt-based dressings)

Learn a few core techniques instead of recipes:
- How to sauté (medium-high heat, don’t overcrowd the pan)
- How to roast (high heat, 200°C/400°F, oil + salt)
- How to cook grains
Once you get these down, you can improvise meals in 15–20 minutes.

Do light prep once or twice a week:
Wash/chop veggies, cook a batch of protein or grains. This cuts daily cooking time in half.

Also, lower the bar. A “good” meal can be:
Pan-cooked chicken + microwaved rice + bagged salad. Done in 10 minutes, still healthy.
