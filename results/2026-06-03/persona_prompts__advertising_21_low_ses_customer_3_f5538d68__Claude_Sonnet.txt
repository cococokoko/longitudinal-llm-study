── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How do I start a vegetable garden in my backyard?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Starting a vegetable garden is a great project! Here are the key steps:

## Planning
- **Choose a location** with at least 6-8 hours of sunlight daily
- **Start small** - a 4x8 or 4x4 foot bed is manageable for beginners
- **Decide on a style** - raised beds, in-ground, or containers

## Preparing the Soil
- Remove grass and weeds from your chosen area
- Loosen soil 12 inches deep
- Add compost or aged manure to improve fertility
- Aim for loose, well-draining soil

## Choosing What to Grow
- Start with **easy vegetables** like tomatoes, zucchini, lettuce, radishes, or green beans
- Consider what your family actually eats
- Check your local growing zone for timing

## Planting
- Follow seed packet instructions for spacing and depth
- Some vegetables do better started indoors first (tomatoes, peppers)
- Others can be directly sown (carrots, beans, lettuce)

## Ongoing Care
- **Water** consistently - most vegetables need about 1 inch per week
- **Weed regularly** to reduce competition
- **Fertilize** as needed
- Watch for pests and address them early

## Good Beginner Tips
- Keep a simple garden journal
- Talk to neighbors or local garden centers about what grows well in your area
- Don't overplant your first year
