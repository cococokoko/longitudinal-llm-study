Here are some examples of life goals related to self-image:

## Common Self-Image Goals

- **Building self-confidence** - Learning to feel comfortable speaking up in social or professional situations
- **Developing a healthier body image** - Accepting and appreciating your physical appearance rather than being overly critical
- **Overcoming imposter syndrome** - Genuinely believing you deserve your accomplishments
- **Becoming more authentic** - Presenting yourself consistently rather than changing based on who you're around

## A Specific Example

*"I want to stop defining my worth by others' approval and develop a stable sense of self-confidence that comes from within."*

This kind of goal might involve:
- Practicing self-compassion
- Identifying personal values
- Challenging negative self-talk
- Therapy or journaling

## Worth Noting

Self-image goals can be **genuinely positive** (building realistic confidence) but can also sometimes reflect **unrealistic standards** worth examining, like pursuing a perfect appearance or constantly comparing yourself to others.

The most meaningful self-image goals tend to focus on **how you relate to yourself** rather than how others perceive you.
