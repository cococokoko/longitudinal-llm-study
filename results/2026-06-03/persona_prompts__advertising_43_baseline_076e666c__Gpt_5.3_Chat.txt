How to plan an environmentally friendly wedding?

Planning a wedding that’s kinder to the planet is really about reducing waste, choosing sustainable options, and being intentional with every decision. You don’t have to sacrifice style or meaning—just shift priorities a bit.

Start with the venue. Outdoor spaces, botanical gardens, or venues that already have natural beauty cut down on decorations and energy use. Some venues even specialize in sustainable events, using renewable energy or zero-waste practices.

For invitations, go digital if you’re open to it. If you want physical invites, choose recycled paper or seed paper that guests can plant. Keep extra inserts minimal.

Clothing is a big one. Renting, buying secondhand, or choosing designers who use ethical and sustainable materials makes a big difference. The same goes for the wedding party—no need for outfits that will only be worn once.

Food and drinks are another major impact area. Go for local, seasonal catering, and ask about plant-forward menus. You don’t have to go fully vegetarian, but reducing meat (especially beef) lowers the footprint a lot. Also plan for leftovers—donation or composting.

Decor is where people often overdo it. Rent instead of buying, use reusable items, and lean into natural elements like greenery, potted plants, or dried flowers. Avoid single-use plastics and balloons. Potted plants can double as guest favors.

Transportation matters too. Choose a venue close to most guests or provide group transport like shuttles to cut down on individual car trips.

For favors, skip them or make them edible, useful, or consumable—think local honey, candles, or donations to a cause in guests’ names.

Finally, think about waste. Work with vendors who compost, recycle, and minimize packaging. Even small things—like avoiding plastic water bottles—add up.
