── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I am a single parent who often balances childcare with work.

How do I understand what I want?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This is a really thoughtful question, and honestly a brave one to ask - because single parents often spend so much energy on others that their own wants can feel distant or even unfamiliar.

## Some honest starting points

**Why it's hard to know what you want**
- You've likely been in survival mode, which suppresses personal desires
- Your identity may have merged heavily with "parent" and "worker"
- Wanting things for yourself can feel selfish, even when it isn't
- You may not have quiet time to even hear your own thoughts

**Practical ways to start discovering it**

- **Notice small moments of aliveness** - what makes you feel slightly more energized vs. drained?
- **Pay attention to envy** - what do you notice yourself wishing you had that others have? That's information
- **Ask "what would I do with one free hour?"** - your first instinct matters
- **Journal without editing yourself** - even messy, contradictory thoughts reveal patterns
- **Think back to who you were before** major responsibilities - what did you care about?

**Some honest cautions**
- Your wants may feel overwhelming once you start noticing them - that's normal
- Some wants will be realistic, some won't - both are worth knowing
- This takes time, not just one reflection session

**A simple question to sit with:**
*What do you find yourself thinking about when your mind wanders?*

What feels most relevant to your situation right now?
