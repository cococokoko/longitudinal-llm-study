# Mastering Digital Photography

## Core Fundamentals

### The Exposure Triangle
- **Aperture (f-stop)** – Controls depth of field and light intake
- **Shutter Speed** – Freezes or blurs motion
- **ISO** – Sensor sensitivity to light
- *Balancing all three = proper exposure*

---

## Key Concepts to Learn

### 1. **Composition**
- Rule of thirds
- Leading lines
- Framing within a frame
- Negative space
- Symmetry and patterns

### 2. **Light**
- Golden hour vs. harsh midday light
- Direction: front, side, back lighting
- Hard vs. soft light
- Using reflectors and diffusers

### 3. **Focus & Depth of Field**
- Wide aperture (f/1.8) = shallow DOF, blurred background
- Narrow aperture (f/11) = deep DOF, everything sharp
- Autofocus modes (single, continuous, tracking)

---

## Shooting Modes
| Mode | Best For |
|------|----------|
| **Manual (M)** | Full creative control |
| **Aperture Priority (Av)** | Portraits, landscapes |
| **Shutter Priority (Tv)** | Sports, action |
| **Program (P)** | Quick shooting |

---

## Post-Processing Basics
- **Lightroom** – Exposure, color, organization
- **Photoshop** – Advanced editing, compositing
- Key adjustments: exposure, contrast, highlights, shadows, white balance

---

## Practice Tips
✅ Shoot in **RAW** format  
✅ Review your **histogram**  
✅ Shoot **daily** — even with your phone  
✅ Study photographers you admire  
✅ Experiment and **embrace mistakes**
