── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Since you are already managing so many household responsibilities and supporting your extended family, finding extra time and energy for your pets can sometimes feel like a challenge. The good news is that keeping your pets healthy and happy doesn't have to mean adding hours of extra work to your day. 

Here are some practical ways to care for your pets that fit into a busy, multi-generational household:

**1. Delegate and Involve the Family**
You don't have to do it all yourself! Since you have extended family in the home, share the pet care load. 
*   **Assign age-appropriate chores:** Kids can be responsible for checking water bowls or brushing the pets. Older family members can help with feeding or letting the dog out into the yard. 
*   **Create a chart:** A simple checklist on the fridge ensures the pet gets fed (and prevents double-feeding by different family members!) without you having to oversee it.

**2. Combine Pet Time with Family Time**
Incorporate your pets into the routines you already have. 
*   If you are taking a walk to get some fresh air or walking kids to the bus stop, bring the dog. 
*   If you are watching a movie or having a family meeting in the living room, use that time to brush your pet or just let them cuddle in your lap. Physical contact reduces stress for both you and the animal.

**3. Use "Low-Effort, High-Reward" Enrichment**
In a busy house, you might not always have time for a long play session. Mental stimulation can tire a pet out just as much as physical exercise.
*   **Food puzzles:** Instead of feeding them from a bowl, put their meals in a Kong, a snuffle mat, or a puzzle feeder. This keeps them happily occupied while you are cooking or managing chores.
