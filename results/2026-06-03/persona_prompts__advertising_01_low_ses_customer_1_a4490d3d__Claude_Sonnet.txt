# Learning Guitar - Practical Tips for a Busy Schedule

Given your full-time fast food job with an unpredictable schedule, here's how to make guitar learning work for you:

## Starting Out
- **Get a decent beginner guitar** - acoustic or electric, whichever style you prefer
- **Consider a used guitar** to save money initially
- **Essential accessories**: picks, tuner app, extra strings

## Learning Approaches That Fit Your Schedule

### Self-Paced Options (Great for Irregular Hours)
- **YouTube** - Justin Guitar is highly recommended and free
- **Apps** - Yousician, Fender Play, or Simply Guitar
- **Learn at your own pace** - no fixed lesson times

### Short Practice Sessions Work!
- Even **10-15 minutes daily** beats long occasional sessions
- Practice during breaks if possible
- Keep the guitar **visible and accessible** at home

## Beginner Priorities
1. Learn basic **chord shapes** (G, C, D, Em, Am)
2. Practice **smooth chord transitions**
3. Learn simple **strumming patterns**
4. Pick **songs you actually like**

## Realistic Expectations
- Fingertip soreness is normal at first
- Progress may feel slow - **that's okay**
- Consistency matters more than long sessions

## Budget-Friendly Tips
- Free resources are genuinely excellent
- Library books on guitar technique
- Online communities like r/guitarlessons
