── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How can I optimize my home office for productivity?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Start with the stuff that quietly drains your focus—then fix that before chasing “productivity hacks.”

First, your environment matters more than apps. Make your desk a place where work is the default:
- Keep only what you actually use daily within arm’s reach
- Good chair + screen at eye level (neck strain kills focus faster than you think)
- Lighting: bright, indirect light in front of you; avoid harsh backlighting

Then reduce friction to starting work:
- Leave your workspace “reset” at the end of each day (clear desk, tabs closed, next task written down)
- Use a consistent start ritual (same music, same drink, same first task)

Control distractions aggressively:
- Put your phone out of reach or in another room
- Use site blockers during deep work blocks (even 60–90 min helps a lot)
- If noise is an issue: noise-canceling headphones or steady background noise

Structure your time so you’re not relying on willpower:
- Work in defined blocks (e.g., 60–90 min focus + 10–20 min break)
- Decide in advance what “done” looks like for each block
- Do your hardest task early, before decision fatigue kicks in

Make the space mentally “work-only” if possible:
- If you can’t dedicate a room, at least use a specific desk or corner only for work
- Avoid working from bed or couch—it blurs your brain’s context cues

Add small boosts that actually work:
- A bit of greenery or a clean visual (reduces mental fatigue)
- Keep water nearby (dehydration = sluggish thinking)
- Slightly cool room temperature tends to help alertness

Finally, track what actually works for you:
- After a few days, ask: when did I focus best? what broke it?
- Adjust your setup based on real patterns, not generic advice

If you want, tell me your current setup (desk, noise, schedule, etc.), and I can suggest more tailored tweaks.
