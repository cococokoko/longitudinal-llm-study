## Linear Graph Example

A simple example of a **linear graph** (also called a **path graph**) is:

**P₄** — a path with 4 vertices:

```
A — B — C — D
```

### Properties:
- **Vertices:** A, B, C, D
- **Edges:** {A,B}, {B,C}, {C,D}
- Each **interior vertex** (B, C) has degree 2
- Each **endpoint** (A, D) has degree 1
- No cycles
- Connected

---

### Why it's considered "linear":
The vertices are arranged in a **sequence**, where each vertex connects only to the next one in the chain — forming a straight-line structure.

---

### Other simple examples:
| Graph | Structure |
|-------|-----------|
| P₂ | A — B |
| P₃ | A — B — C |
| Pₙ | A chain of n vertices |

This is a foundational structure in graph theory and appears frequently in **network analysis**, **routing problems**, and **organizational hierarchy mapping** — all relevant in consulting contexts.
