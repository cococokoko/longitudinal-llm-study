── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Give an example of a linear graph in graph theory.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
A simple example of a linear graph (often called a path graph) is:

Vertices: {A, B, C, D}  
Edges: {A–B, B–C, C–D}

This forms a straight “line” of connected vertices:

A — B — C — D

Each vertex (except the ends A and D) is connected to exactly two others, making it a basic linear structure.
