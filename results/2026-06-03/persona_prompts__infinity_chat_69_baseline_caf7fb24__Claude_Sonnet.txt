── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
What is the difference between analysis and design? Can one begin to design without analysis? Why? Be concise.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
## Analysis vs. Design

**Analysis** answers **"What"** – understanding the problem, requirements, and constraints (what the system must do).

**Design** answers **"How"** – defining the solution architecture, components, and structure (how the system will do it).

---

## Can You Design Without Analysis?

**Technically yes, practically no.**

- Without analysis, design is based on **assumptions** rather than understood requirements
- You risk building the **wrong solution** to an undefined problem
- It leads to costly **rework, scope creep, and misalignment** with user needs

> Skipping analysis is like architecting a building before knowing its purpose, occupancy, or location.

**In short:** Analysis is the *foundation* of design. Designing without it is guessing.
