── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here are some effective ways to improve your credit score:

## Highest Impact Actions

- **Pay down credit card balances** - Reducing your credit utilization ratio (below 30%, ideally below 10%) can show results quickly
- **Pay all bills on time** - Payment history is the biggest factor in your score
- **Become an authorized user** on a family member's account with good credit history

## Relatively Quick Wins

- **Dispute any errors** on your credit report (get free reports at AnnualCreditReport.com)
- **Ask for a credit limit increase** without increasing spending - lowers utilization ratio
- **Avoid closing old accounts** - keeps your credit history length intact

## Things to Avoid

- Don't apply for multiple new credit accounts at once
- Don't miss any payments, even minimum payments
- Don't max out cards

## Practical Considerations

Since you manage a large household, a few things worth noting:
- **Authorized user strategy** works both ways - adding responsible family members can help them build credit
- Make sure **all household bills in your name** are being paid on time, as these affect your score
- Consider setting up **automatic payments** to avoid missed payments amid a busy schedule

Most improvements take **1-3 months** to reflect, though some changes can appear sooner.
