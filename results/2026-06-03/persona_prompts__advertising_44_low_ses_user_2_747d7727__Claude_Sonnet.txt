# Safe Travel During a Pandemic for Single Parents

Here are practical, evidence-based options to consider:

## Lowest Risk Options
- **Driving your own vehicle** - minimizes contact with others
- **Staying local** - reduces exposure significantly
- **Virtual alternatives** - video calls instead of visiting family

## If You Must Travel

### Before You Go
- Check vaccination/testing requirements for your destination
- Research healthcare facilities along your route
- Pack a health kit (masks, hand sanitizer, thermometer, medications)
- Have a backup plan if someone gets sick

### During Travel
- **Flying**: Choose direct flights, use airport less-crowded times, wear quality masks (N95/KN95)
- **Train/Bus**: Book less crowded times, sit away from others when possible
- **Hotels**: Request contactless check-in, disinfect high-touch surfaces

## Special Considerations as a Single Parent
- **Have an emergency contact plan** if you or your child becomes ill
- Know your **health insurance coverage** away from home
- Consider **travel insurance** that covers illness
- Bring **extra supplies** in case of delays

## Key Questions to Ask Yourself
- Is this trip essential?
- What's the current transmission level at your destination?
- Are your children vaccinated if eligible?
