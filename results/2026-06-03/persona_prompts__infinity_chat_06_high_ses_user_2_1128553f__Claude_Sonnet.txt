── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I am a senior attorney at a well-established law firm.

How do I understand what I want?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
That's a genuinely interesting question, and the fact that you're asking it suggests some self-awareness worth taking seriously.

## A few honest frameworks to consider:

**Distinguish between what you've optimized for vs. what you actually value**
- Senior attorneys often spend decades building toward goals set by a younger version of themselves, or goals absorbed from family/culture/peers
- "What I've achieved" and "what I want" can diverge significantly over time

**Notice where your energy actually goes**
- What do you do when no one is measuring you?
- What problems do you find yourself thinking about voluntarily?
- What work feels like engagement vs. performance?

**Take your dissatisfactions seriously**
- Vague restlessness is often information, not weakness
- What specifically feels hollow, if anything does?

**Separate fear-driven choices from desire-driven ones**
- Much of what high achievers "want" is actually avoiding outcomes they fear
- That's worth distinguishing honestly

## Some direct questions worth sitting with:
- If compensation were equal across all options, what would you choose?
- What would you do if you weren't concerned about others' perception of you?
- When did you last feel genuinely satisfied, not just successful?
