── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How do I understand what I want?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
That’s a real question, and not as simple as it sounds.

A useful starting point: you don’t “figure out” what you want by thinking harder alone. You discover it by paying attention to how you react to things—what pulls you in, what drains you, what you keep coming back to.

A few angles that actually help:

Pay attention to your energy, not just your thoughts.  
After doing something, ask: did that give me energy or take it? The things you want tend to leave some kind of energy behind, even if they’re hard.

Notice recurring patterns.  
What topics, activities, or fantasies keep showing up in your life? Not once, but repeatedly over time. Those are rarely random.

Distinguish between “should” and “want.”  
A lot of confusion comes from inherited goals—what you think you’re supposed to want (money, status, approval). Try asking: if no one would judge this choice, would I still want it?

Use small experiments instead of big decisions.  
You don’t need to know your whole life direction. Try things in low-stakes ways—take a class, start a project, talk to people in a field. Clarity usually follows action, not the other way around.

Check your envy.  
Who do you feel a bit jealous of—and why? Envy often points pretty directly at unmet desires.

Accept that it changes.  
What you want now doesn’t have to be what you want forever. You’re not trying to lock in a permanent identity, just understand your current direction a little better.

If you want, I can help you work through it more concretely—like narrowing down between options or making sense of a specific situation.
