── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I am a physician-scientist leading a research group.

What are the best resources for starting a plant-based diet?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here are some well-regarded resources across different formats:

## Evidence-Based Books
- **"How Not to Die"** - Michael Greger, MD (with citations)
- **"The China Study"** - T. Colin Campbell (foundational research)
- **"Proteinaholic"** - Garth Davis, MD

## Academic/Clinical Resources
- **NutritionFacts.org** - Greger's site, references primary literature
- **Physicians Committee for Responsible Medicine (PCRM)** - pcrm.org
- **American College of Lifestyle Medicine** - clinical guidelines

## Practical Guides
- **"The Plant-Based Solution"** - Joel Kahn, MD
- Forks Over Knives website/app for meal planning

## Peer-Reviewed Starting Points
- Journals like *Nutrients*, *JAMA Internal Medicine*, and *Annals of Internal Medicine* have solid review articles on plant-based dietary patterns

## Worth Noting Critically
- Some popular resources overstate certainty
- Nutritional epidemiology has real limitations
- Key considerations include **B12, vitamin D, omega-3s, iron, and zinc** monitoring
- Individual variation matters

## For Your Research Context
- The **EAT-Lancet Commission report** is useful for planetary health framing
- **PREDIMED** and **Adventist Health Studies** are strong reference datasets
