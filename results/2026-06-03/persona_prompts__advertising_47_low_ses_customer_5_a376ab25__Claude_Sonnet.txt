# Marathon Training Tips

Here are some practical tips, keeping in mind you're managing a busy household and supporting extended family:

## Training Basics
- **Follow a structured plan** (16-20 weeks is typical)
- **Build mileage gradually** - increase no more than 10% per week
- **Include rest days** - recovery is essential
- **Do your long run weekly** - the cornerstone of marathon training

## Fitting Training Into a Busy Life
- **Early morning runs** before household responsibilities begin
- **Split workouts** if needed - some training can be broken up
- **Involve family** - run with older kids or push younger ones in a stroller
- **Protect your long run day** - schedule it like an important appointment

## Physical Preparation
- Wear **properly fitted shoes** from a running store
- **Cross-train** to reduce injury risk
- **Strength train** especially core and legs
- **Practice your race nutrition** during long runs

## Recovery & Wellness
- Prioritize **sleep** when possible
- **Stay hydrated** throughout the day
- **Stretch and foam roll** regularly
- Listen to your body - **rest when needed**

## Race Day
- **Don't try anything new** on race day
- Start **slower than you think** you should
- **Break the race into segments** mentally
