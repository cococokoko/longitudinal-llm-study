## Analysis vs. Design

**Analysis** answers *"What does the system need to do?"* — understanding requirements, problems, and constraints.

**Design** answers *"How will the system do it?"* — defining architecture, components, and solutions.

---

## Can You Design Without Analysis?

**Technically yes, practically no.**

Skipping analysis means you're designing based on **assumptions rather than validated requirements**. This leads to:

- Building the **wrong thing** correctly
- Costly rework when reality contradicts assumptions
- Solutions optimized for the **wrong problem**

---

## The Executive Reality

In fast-moving tech environments, teams often **compress or parallelize** analysis and design — which is acceptable when:

- The domain is well-understood
- Rapid iteration/feedback loops exist (Agile)
- Prototyping is cheap

But even then, *some* analysis is always happening — it's just informal or implicit.

**The risk isn't skipping analysis; it's not knowing you skipped it.**
