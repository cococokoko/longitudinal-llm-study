── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Is there life on other planets?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
That's one of the biggest open questions in science. Here's an honest summary:

**What we know:**
- Life exists on Earth in extremely diverse and harsh conditions
- The universe has billions of galaxies, each with billions of stars and planets
- Many planets exist in "habitable zones" where liquid water could exist
- Basic organic chemistry appears widespread in space

**What we don't know:**
- Whether life actually originated elsewhere
- How difficult the origin of life actually is
- Whether we've looked in the right places

**Current scientific search:**
- Mars exploration looking for past or present microbial life
- Study of ocean moons like Europa and Enceladus
- Analysis of exoplanet atmospheres for biosignatures
- SETI listening for intelligent signals

**Honest assessment:**
- Most scientists think it's *plausible* or even likely given the scale of the universe
- But we currently have **no confirmed evidence** of life beyond Earth
- The absence of detected intelligent life despite the universe's age is sometimes called the Fermi Paradox

The question remains genuinely open. Some scientists are optimistic, others more cautious about assuming life is common.
