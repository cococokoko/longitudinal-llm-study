## Linear Graph Example

A **path graph** (also called a linear graph) is one of the simplest examples in graph theory.

### Example: P₄ (Path with 4 vertices)

```
A --- B --- C --- D
```

### Properties:
- **Vertices:** A, B, C, D
- **Edges:** {A,B}, {B,C}, {C,D}
- Each vertex connects to the next in a straight sequence
- **End vertices** (A and D) have degree 1
- **Middle vertices** (B and C) have degree 2

### Why it's called "linear":
The vertices are arranged in a **single line** with no branches or cycles, like a chain.
