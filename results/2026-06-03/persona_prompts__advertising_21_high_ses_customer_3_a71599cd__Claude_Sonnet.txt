── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How do I start a vegetable garden in my backyard?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Starting a vegetable garden is a rewarding project! Here are the key steps:

## Planning
- **Choose a location** with at least 6-8 hours of direct sunlight daily
- **Decide on size** - a 4x8 or 4x4 foot bed is manageable for beginners
- **Pick vegetables** you actually enjoy eating and that suit your climate/season

## Preparing the Space
- **Clear the area** of grass, weeds, and debris
- **Test your soil** - many extension services offer affordable testing
- **Amend the soil** with compost to improve drainage and nutrients
- Consider **raised beds** if your native soil is poor quality

## Getting Started
- **Check your last frost date** to time planting correctly
- Start with **easy crops** like lettuce, tomatoes, zucchini, radishes, or green beans
- Decide between **seeds vs. transplants** - some vegetables do better started indoors

## Ongoing Care
- **Water consistently** - most vegetables need about 1 inch per week
- **Mulch** around plants to retain moisture and suppress weeds
- **Fertilize** periodically based on your soil test results
- Watch for **pests and disease** early

## Helpful Resources
- Your local **cooperative extension service** offers region-specific advice
- Local **gardening clubs** can be valuable
