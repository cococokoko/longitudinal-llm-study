── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
My girlfriend is buying a house. I plan to marry her in a year or so. I don't know if it's fair for me to pay for the house or not. What's the best solution?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This is a really common situation and worth thinking through carefully. Here are some honest considerations:

## Key Questions to Ask Yourself

- Are you contributing to the down payment, mortgage payments, or both?
- Will you be living there together?
- What happens to your contribution if you don't end up marrying?

## Realistic Options

**Option 1: Don't contribute financially yet**
- She owns it, she pays for it
- Clean and simple until you're actually married
- You could contribute after marriage

**Option 2: Contribute to expenses but not equity**
- Pay rent-equivalent toward bills/mortgage
- Doesn't give you ownership stake
- Fair for shared living costs

**Option 3: Get on the deed**
- If you're contributing significantly, you should have legal ownership stake
- Requires a cohabitation or property agreement
- Protects both of you

**Option 4: Formal written agreement**
- Document whatever you decide
- Specifies what happens if you separate
- Protects both parties

## Honest Advice

**The biggest risk** is contributing significant money to a property you don't legally own. If the relationship ends, you could lose that money.

**A lawyer can draft a simple cohabitation agreement** that protects both of you without being unromantic about it.

What's your specific situation - are you planning to live there together?
