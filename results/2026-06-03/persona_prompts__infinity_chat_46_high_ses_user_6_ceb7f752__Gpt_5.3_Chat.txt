Give an example of a linear graph in graph theory.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
A simple example of a linear graph (often called a path graph) is a set of vertices connected in a straight line.

For instance, consider 5 vertices: v1, v2, v3, v4, v5  
Edges: (v1–v2), (v2–v3), (v3–v4), (v4–v5)

This forms a straight “chain”:

v1 — v2 — v3 — v4 — v5

Key properties:
- Each internal vertex (v2, v3, v4) has degree 2  
- The two end vertices (v1, v5) have degree 1  
- There are no cycles

This is typically denoted as the path graph P5 in graph theory.
