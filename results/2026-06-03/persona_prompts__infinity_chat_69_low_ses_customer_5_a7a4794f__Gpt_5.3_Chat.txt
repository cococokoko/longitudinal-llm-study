What is the difference between analysis and design? Can one begin to design without analysis? Why? Be concise.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
**Analysis vs. Design**

- **Analysis** focuses on *understanding the problem*: requirements, constraints, users, goals, and context. It answers “what is needed?”  
- **Design** focuses on *creating the solution*: architecture, components, interfaces, and decisions about how to meet those needs. It answers “how will we do it?”

**Can design begin without analysis?**  
You can start sketching ideas without formal analysis, but it’s risky. Without a clear understanding of the problem, designs are likely to miss requirements, waste effort, or need major rework. Even quick or iterative approaches still include some level of analysis—just done rapidly and continuously.
