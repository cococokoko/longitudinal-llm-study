── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Totally doable—and you don’t need to become a “chef” to pull it off. What you’re really aiming for is a repeatable system, not fancy recipes.

Start with a simple formula you can reuse: protein + vegetables + a carb + a sauce or seasoning. If you can mix and match those four parts, you can make tons of meals without thinking too hard. For example: grilled chicken, frozen veggies sautéed in olive oil, rice, and a bottled teriyaki sauce. Done in 20 minutes.

Speed comes from reducing decisions and prep time:
- Lean on shortcuts like pre-cut vegetables, rotisserie chicken, canned beans, microwaveable grains, and frozen produce (they’re just as nutritious).
- Cook in batches when you can—make a big pot of something once and eat it for a couple days or freeze portions.
- Keep a few “default meals” you know by heart so you’re not starting from scratch every night.

A few fast meal ideas to rotate:
- Stir-fry: protein + frozen veggie mix + soy sauce/garlic/ginger over rice
- Sheet pan: toss chicken or tofu and vegetables with oil and seasoning, bake ~20–25 min
- Tacos or wraps: beans or ground meat + chopped veggies + store-bought tortillas
- Big salads: greens + protein + nuts/seeds + simple dressing
- Eggs in any form: omelet, scramble, or fried with toast and veggies

Also, don’t underestimate tools: a rice cooker, air fryer, or even just a good nonstick pan can cut your time in half.
