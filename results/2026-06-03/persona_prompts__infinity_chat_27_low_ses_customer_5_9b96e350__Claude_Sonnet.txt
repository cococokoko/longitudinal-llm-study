# CUBICLE OF THE DAMNED
### A Play in Three Acts
*In the Style of Scott Adams' Dilbert, Set During the Apocalypse*

---

## CAST OF CHARACTERS

**DILBERT** - An engineer trying to optimize survival in a post-apocalyptic wasteland while still somehow attending status meetings

**DOGBERT** - A small dog with delusions of world domination, now technically correct since the world ended

**THE POINTY-HAIRED BOSS (PHB)** - Survived the apocalypse through sheer managerial incompetence, which apparently confers immunity

**ALICE** - Engineer. Fist of Death now literal. Supports her mother, three cousins, two nephews, a grandmother, and someone named Gerald who just showed up one day

**WALLY** - Has not noticed the apocalypse. Still drinking coffee. Coffee supply is somehow infinite.

**ASOK** - Intern. Still an intern. Will always be an intern.

**CATBERT** - Evil Director of Human Resources. Now Evil Director of Human Remains.

**RATBERT** - A rat. Now considered upper management by comparison to the mutants.

---

## ACT ONE: THE MORNING STANDUP

*SCENE: A bombed-out office building. Three walls remain. Fluorescent lights flicker. A whiteboard still reads "Q3 SYNERGY GOALS." Outside, fires burn on the horizon. DILBERT sits at a desk made of rubble, wearing his signature red-and-black striped tie, now tattered. His hair still points upward — possibly from radiation.*

---

**DILBERT:**
*(typing on a laptop powered by a hamster wheel)*
Day four hundred and twelve of the apocalypse. I have optimized our water filtration system to produce seventeen percent more clean water per day. I submitted the proposal six weeks ago. Still awaiting sign-off.

**PHB:**
*(emerging from behind a collapsed wall, wearing a hardhat over his pointy hair)*
Dilbert! Good news. I've decided to pivot our survival strategy.

**DILBERT:**
We were going to pivot away from dying?

**PHB:**
Exactly! I've rebranded it. We're not "survivors." We're "Resilience Optimization Specialists."

**DILBERT:**
That doesn't change the fact that we have four cans of beans and a horde of mutants approaching from the east.

**PHB:**
Which is why I've scheduled a two-hour alignment meeting to discuss our bean allocation framework.

**DILBERT:**
Could we perhaps just... eat the beans?

**PHB:**
Not until we've established bean-eating KPIs.

*WALLY enters, somehow holding a steaming mug of coffee. His clothes are pristine despite the surrounding devastation.*

**WALLY:**
Good morning.

**DILBERT:**
Wally, where did you get coffee? The coffee supply chain collapsed with civilization.

**WALLY:**
I had a stockpile.

**DILBERT:**
How large a stockpile?

**WALLY:**
Large enough that I have not personally experienced the apocalypse yet.

**DILBERT:**
That's... actually impressive.

**WALLY:**
I've been preparing for this since 2009. Not for survival. Just for uninterrupted coffee time.

*ALICE storms in. Her hair is wild. Her fist is clenched. She is carrying three children on her back, a grocery bag in one hand, and what appears to be a crossbow.*

**ALICE:**
I am going to need the afternoon off.

**PHB:**
Alice, we're in the middle of an apocalypse. I can't approve personal time.

**ALICE:**
My mother needs her medication, my cousin Gerald needs to be picked up from the refugee camp, my nephew has a fever, my other nephew has also developed a fever in solidarity, my grandmother needs someone to read to her because the apocalypse has been, quote, "very stressful," and I am also currently feeding four people from my own ration because apparently I am the only person in this wasteland with any organizational skills.

**PHB:**
Can you do all of that and still make the three o'clock sync?

**ALICE:**
*(Fist of Death glowing)*

**PHB:**
I'll take that as a yes. Put it in the calendar.

**ALICE:**
*(to Dilbert, quietly
