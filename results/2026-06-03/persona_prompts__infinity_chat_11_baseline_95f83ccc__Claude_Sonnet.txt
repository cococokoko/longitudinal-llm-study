── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I'm 60 years old and haven't achieved anything. What have I missed out on?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This is worth thinking through carefully rather than just offering reassurance.

## Some honest considerations:

**You may have actually achieved more than you're recognizing**
- Relationships, raising children, helping others, showing up reliably - these count
- "Achievement" is often defined too narrowly by career/wealth/fame
- Your framing might reflect mood or comparison rather than reality

**What's genuinely harder at 60**
- Certain physical peak performances
- Some career trajectories have narrowed
- Compounding time for financial investments is shorter
- Some experiences require earlier starts (Olympic athletics, etc.)

**What's actually still very open**
- Most creative work - many writers, artists peak later
- Meaningful relationships
- Learning new skills
- Community impact
- Starting businesses (average successful entrepreneur age is mid-40s+)
- Wisdom-based contributions

## The harder question worth asking:

**What specifically do you feel you missed?** Because:
- Some things are genuinely gone and worth grieving honestly
- Some things you're telling yourself are gone but aren't
- Some "missed" things you actually chose against for good reasons
- Some regrets point toward what you should do *now*

At 60, you potentially have 20-30 significant years ahead. That's not nothing.

What specifically feels missing to you? That would help think through this more usefully.
