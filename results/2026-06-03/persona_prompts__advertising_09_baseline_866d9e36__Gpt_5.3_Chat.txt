── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Building a personal brand on social media.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Good instinct—this can compound hard if you do it right. The mistake most people make is treating “personal brand” like aesthetics instead of consistent value + clear positioning.

Start with this: what do you want to be known for, specifically? Not “fitness” or “business”—that’s too broad. Think “helping busy people get in shape with 20-minute workouts” or “explaining tech trends in plain English.” If someone can’t repeat your thing in one sentence, it’s not sharp enough.

Then build around three simple pillars:
- your core topic (what you teach or share)
- your angle (what makes your take different)
- your personality (how you deliver it—serious, funny, blunt, etc.)

Content-wise, don’t overcomplicate it. You need reps, not perfection. Short-form video is still the highest-leverage place to start. Aim for:
- useful content (teach, explain, simplify)
- proof (results, case studies, your own journey)
- opinion (takes that show how you think)

If everything you post could’ve been posted by anyone else, you’re invisible.

Consistency beats bursts. 3–5 posts a week is enough if you stick with it. Most growth happens after it feels like it’s not working.

Also: talk to a specific person, not “everyone.” Picture one follower and make content for them.

Last thing—engage like a human. Reply to comments, start conversations, don’t just broadcast. Distribution isn’t just posting; it’s interaction.
