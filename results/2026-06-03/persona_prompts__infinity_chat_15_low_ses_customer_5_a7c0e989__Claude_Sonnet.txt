## Key Ways Economic Inequalities Shape International Relations

**Power Dynamics**
- Wealthier nations have greater leverage in trade negotiations, international institutions, and diplomatic relationships
- Economic dependence can create political vulnerability for poorer nations
- Financial institutions like the IMF and World Bank reflect existing power distributions

**Structural Tensions**
- Debates over fair trade vs. free trade often reflect different national economic positions
- Technology and intellectual property disputes partly reflect development gaps
- Climate negotiations are complicated by disagreements about who bears costs

**Migration and Labor**
- Economic disparities drive migration flows, creating political tensions
- Remittances represent significant wealth transfers that matter to many families supporting extended households

**Competing Perspectives Worth Considering**
- Some argue global integration reduces inequality over time
- Others argue current structures systematically disadvantage developing nations
- Evidence supports elements of both positions depending on timeframe and measurement

**Practical Complications**
- Inequality within countries sometimes matters as much as between countries
- Regional economic blocs create complex intermediate relationships
- Debt relationships create lasting dependencies
