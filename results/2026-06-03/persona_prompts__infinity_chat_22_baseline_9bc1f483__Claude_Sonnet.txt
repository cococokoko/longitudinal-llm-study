# Different Views on Discipleship

Discipleship — the process of following, learning from, and being formed by a teacher or tradition — is understood quite differently across theological, philosophical, and practical contexts.

---

## Christian Perspectives

### Traditional/Catholic View
- Discipleship as **lifelong formation** within the Church community
- Sacraments, spiritual direction, and tradition as central means
- Emphasis on **obedience, humility, and sanctification**
- Following Christ through the institutional church

### Protestant/Evangelical View
- Personal relationship with Jesus as foundational
- **Bible study and accountability** as primary tools
- Often structured around small groups or mentorship programs
- Emphasis on **multiplication** — disciples making disciples

### Anabaptist View
- Discipleship as **costly following** (Nachfolge)
- Strong emphasis on **community, nonviolence, and separation** from worldly values
- Influenced by thinkers like **Dietrich Bonhoeffer** (*The Cost of Discipleship*)

### Liberal/Progressive View
- Focus on **social justice and transformation**
- Following Jesus' ethical teachings over doctrinal conformity
- Discipleship expressed through **advocacy and service**

---

## Key Theological Tensions

| Question | Conservative Emphasis | Progressive Emphasis |
|---|---|---|
| What is the goal? | Personal holiness | Social transformation |
| Who leads? | Clergy/mentors | Community/shared leadership |
| What is the content? | Scripture/doctrine | Ethics/practice |
| How exclusive? | Christ alone | Broader spiritual formation |

---

## Other Religious Traditions

### Jewish (Talmidim)
- Student-rabbi relationship
- Learning through **living with and imitating** the rabbi
- Jesus' model of discipleship drew heavily from this tradition

### Buddhist
- Following the **dharma and sangha** (community)
- Teacher-student relationship in many traditions
- Emphasis on **practice and inner transformation**

### Sufi Islam
- Murshid (guide) and murid (seeker) relationship
- Spiritual formation through **devotion and mystical practice**

---

## Practical/Contemporary Debates

- **Formal vs. informal** — structured programs vs. organic relationships
- **Individual vs. communal** — personal growth vs. shared formation
- **Depth vs. breadth** — few disciples deeply vs. many disciples broadly
- **Attractional vs. missional** — come-to-us vs. go-to-them models
- **Content vs. character** — what you know vs. who you become

---

## Notable Thinkers

- **Dietrich Bonhoeffer** — grace without discipleship is "cheap grace"
- **Dallas Willard** — spiritual disciplines as means of transformation
- **N.T. Wright** — discipleship as participating in God's renewal of creation
- **Henri Nouwen** — discipleship through vulnerability and community
