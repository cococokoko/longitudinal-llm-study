── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How can I keep my pets healthy and happy?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Keeping pets healthy and happy really comes down to a few core habits done consistently. Here’s a clear, practical way to think about it:

**1. Good nutrition**
Feed a balanced, species-appropriate diet (not just whatever’s cheap or convenient). Fresh water should always be available. Avoid overfeeding—obesity is one of the most common health problems in pets.

**2. Regular vet care**
Annual (or semi-annual for older pets) checkups matter more than people think. Stay up to date on vaccines, parasite prevention (fleas, ticks, worms), and dental care.

**3. Exercise and mental stimulation**
Pets need both physical activity and mental engagement.  
- Dogs: daily walks, playtime, training  
- Cats: toys, climbing spaces, interactive play  
- Small animals: space to explore and enrichment  
Bored pets often become stressed or destructive.

**4. Safe, clean environment**
Keep their living space clean and comfortable. Remove hazards (toxic plants, small chewable objects, unsafe foods). Make sure they have a quiet place to rest.

**5. Social interaction and attention**
Most pets need companionship—either from you or other animals. Even independent pets benefit from regular interaction.

**6. Grooming and hygiene**
Brush fur, trim nails, clean ears (if needed), and maintain dental hygiene. This prevents discomfort and catches issues early.

**7. Watch for behavior changes**
Subtle changes—eating less, hiding, aggression, lethargy—can signal health problems. Trust your instincts and check with a vet if something seems off.
