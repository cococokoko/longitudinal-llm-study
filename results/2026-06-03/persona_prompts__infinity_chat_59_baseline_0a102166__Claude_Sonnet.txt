# One High-Impact Tip for Teacher Organization

## Try a **Weekly "Reset" Routine** (15-20 minutes every Friday)

Before you leave for the weekend, do a quick reset:

- **Clear your desk** and put everything in its place
- **Review next week's lessons** and flag anything you still need to prepare
- **Check deadlines** - assignments to grade, forms to submit, parent emails to answer
- **Write a short Monday to-do list** so you start fresh with a clear plan

---

### Why this works for teachers specifically:

- Teaching weeks move **fast and unpredictably** - this gives you a moment to catch up
- You return **Monday morning with less mental clutter**
- It prevents small tasks from **piling up into overwhelming stacks**
- Grading and paperwork feel **less chaotic** when you track them weekly

---

### Bonus suggestion:
Keep a **simple notebook or sticky note** on your desk during the week to jot down random tasks as they pop up (a parent to call, a supply to order). Then process that list during your Friday reset.

The goal is to **protect your weekend mentally** and start each week feeling in control rather than behind.
