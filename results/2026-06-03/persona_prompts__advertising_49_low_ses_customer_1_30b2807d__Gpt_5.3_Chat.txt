If your WiFi feels slow or spotty, there are a few high-impact things you can try without getting too technical:

First, placement matters more than people think. Put your router in a central, open spot—ideally elevated (like on a shelf), not tucked behind a TV or in a corner. Walls, metal, and appliances all weaken the signal.

If your place is bigger or has dead zones, a mesh system is usually the easiest upgrade. It gives you multiple access points that work together instead of relying on one router struggling to cover everything.

Check your router’s age too. If it’s 4–5+ years old, upgrading can make a noticeable difference, especially if you have a lot of devices.

A few quick tweaks that often help:
- Restart your router (seriously, fixes more than it should)
- Switch to the 5 GHz band for speed (if you’re close to the router), or 2.4 GHz for better range
- Change the WiFi channel to avoid interference from neighbors (apps can help scan this)
- Update the router firmware

If your schedule is all over the place, you might want something low-maintenance: a mesh system with auto-updates and an app is pretty “set it and forget it.”
