── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Give me a tip to be more organized at work. I'm a high school teacher.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Try a “one home per thing” rule for your classroom and digital materials.

Pick a single, clearly defined place for every recurring item—both physical (handouts, late work, graded papers) and digital (lesson plans, slides, emails). Label those spots and don’t let items live anywhere else, even temporarily.

For example, have one tray labeled “to grade,” one folder in your drive for each class/week, and one consistent naming pattern for files like “Algebra_Week3_Day2.” It sounds simple, but the real payoff is you stop wasting mental energy deciding where things go or hunting for them later.

The key is consistency, not complexity. If a system takes more than a few seconds to use, you won’t stick with it during a busy school day.
