Here’s a simple way to get going without overcomplicating it:

Start small  
Don’t try to use your whole yard right away. A couple of raised beds or even a few containers is plenty for your first season. It keeps things manageable and less time-consuming.

Pick a good spot  
Most vegetables need 6–8 hours of sunlight a day. Watch your yard for a day and pick the sunniest area that’s also easy to access (you’ll be more likely to keep up with it).

Decide between ground, raised beds, or containers

Most gardens fail from inconsistent watering, not lack of effort. Aim for deep watering a few times a week rather than light daily sprinkles. Early morning is best.

Mulch (like straw or wood chips) helps retain moisture and keeps weeds down, which saves you time—important if your schedule’s a bit tight right now.
