Nice, that’s a great rabbit hole to fall into 🙂

If you’re just starting, don’t worry about being “good” yet—focus on getting comfortable. A few things that will make your life way easier early on:

First, learn a handful of basic open chords: G, C, D, Em, and Am. Those alone let you play a ton of songs. Don’t try to learn 15 chords at once—get 2–3 solid, then add more.

Second, practice switching between chords slowly. That’s the real bottleneck at the start, not strumming or speed. Even just going G → C → D over and over is huge progress.

Third, keep your fingers close to the fretboard and press just behind the fret (not on top of it). It’ll help your notes sound clean without needing a death grip.

Your fingers will hurt at first—totally normal. Short daily practice (10–20 minutes) beats long, painful sessions once a week.
