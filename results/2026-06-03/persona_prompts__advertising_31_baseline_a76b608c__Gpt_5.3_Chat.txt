First, pick destinations where your money goes further. Countries in Southeast Asia (like Vietnam or Indonesia), parts of Central America (Guatemala, Nicaragua), and Eastern Europe (Albania, Georgia) tend to be both budget-friendly and rich in nature. These places often rely more on local, low-impact tourism rather than luxury resorts.

Next, avoid peak seasons. Traveling shoulder season (just before or after peak tourist months) cuts costs and reduces strain on the environment. Fewer crowds also means a more authentic experience.

Transportation matters more than people think. If possible, choose closer destinations to avoid long-haul flights, or take trains and buses instead of planes. Once there, walk, bike, or use public transit. It’s cheaper and far more sustainable.

- Locally owned guesthouses or eco-lodges  
- Hostels with sustainability practices  
- Farm stays or homestays

Also, look for destinations that actively protect nature. Countries like Costa Rica or Slovenia invest in conservation and eco-tourism, so your money supports those systems rather than harming them.

- Google Flights or Skyscanner to find cheap routes
