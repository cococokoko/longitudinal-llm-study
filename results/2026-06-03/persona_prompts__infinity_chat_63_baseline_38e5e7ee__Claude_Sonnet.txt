# Partial Derivatives: A Numerical Example

## The Function

Let's use:
$$f(x, y) = 3x^2y + 2xy^3 + 5$$

---

## Finding the Partial Derivatives

### Partial Derivative with Respect to x
*(treat y as a constant)*

$$\frac{\partial f}{\partial x} = 6xy + 2y^3$$

### Partial Derivative with Respect to y
*(treat x as a constant)*

$$\frac{\partial f}{\partial y} = 3x^2 + 6xy^2$$

---

## Numerical Evaluation at Point (2, 3)

**First, find f(2, 3):**
$$f(2,3) = 3(4)(3) + 2(2)(27) + 5 = 36 + 108 + 5 = 149$$

**Partial derivative with respect to x at (2, 3):**
$$\frac{\partial f}{\partial x}\Bigg|_{(2,3)} = 6(2)(3) + 2(3)^3 = 36 + 54 = \boxed{90}$$

> This means: if **x increases by 1 unit** (while y stays fixed at 3), f increases by approximately **90 units**.

**Partial derivative with respect to y at (2, 3):**
$$\frac{\partial f}{\partial y}\Bigg|_{(2,3)} = 3(2)^2 + 6(2)(3)^2 = 12 + 108 = \boxed{120}$$

> This means: if **y increases by 1 unit** (while x stays fixed at 2), f increases by approximately **120 units**.

---

## Verification (Numerical Check)

| Change | New Value | Difference | Matches ∂f/∂? |
|--------|-----------|------------|----------------|
| x: 2 → 2.001, y = 3 | f = 149.090... | Δf/Δx ≈ **90** ✓ | ∂f/∂x = 90 |
| y: 3 → 3.001, x = 2 | f = 149.120... | Δf/Δy ≈ **120** ✓ | ∂f/∂y = 120 |

---

## Key Insight

A partial derivative measures the **rate of change in one direction only**, holding all other variables constant — like measuring how steep a hill is heading **East** vs. heading **North**.
