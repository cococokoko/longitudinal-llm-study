── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This is a genuinely complex situation with several legitimate considerations. Here are the key factors to think through:

## Financial Considerations

**Risks of paying now:**
- If the relationship doesn't work out, recovering your contribution could be legally complicated
- You'd have financial exposure without legal protection
- Property laws vary significantly by jurisdiction

**Practical options to consider:**

1. **She buys it independently** - cleanest separation of finances pre-marriage

3. **Buy it together now** - if you're confident about the future
4. **Wait until marriage** - then buy together with clear legal framework
5. **You provide a documented loan** - protects both parties

## Questions Worth Asking Yourself

- Why is the timing happening *before* marriage rather than after?
- Are you feeling **pressure** or is this genuinely your preference?
- Do you have a prenuptial agreement planned?
- What does "fair" mean to you here - equal contribution or proportional to income?
