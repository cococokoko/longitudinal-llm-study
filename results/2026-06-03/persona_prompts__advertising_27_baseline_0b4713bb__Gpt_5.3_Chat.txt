You don’t need a total lifestyle overhaul to make a real dent—small, consistent habits add up fast. The biggest wins usually come from how you travel, eat, and use energy.

If you drive, smoother acceleration, keeping tires properly inflated, and combining errands all help. Flying less (or choosing trains for shorter trips) also makes a big difference.

Food is another major lever. Eating less red meat and dairy—even a couple of days a week—can significantly lower your footprint. Wasting less food matters just as much: plan meals, store food properly, and use leftovers. If you can, choose seasonal and local foods, but reducing meat and waste tends to matter more than “food miles.”

At home, focus on energy use. Switching to LED bulbs, turning off unused lights and electronics, and using smart power strips are easy wins. Adjusting your thermostat a few degrees (down in winter, up in summer) can cut a lot of energy. Washing clothes in cold water and air-drying when possible also helps. If you have the option, choosing renewable energy from your utility has a big impact.

Then there’s consumption. Buying fewer, longer-lasting items reduces hidden emissions from manufacturing and shipping. Repairing instead of replacing, buying secondhand, and avoiding fast fashion all help. Even small things like using a reusable bottle or bags are good, though they’re less impactful than transport or diet.

Finally, money and influence matter. Supporting companies with lower emissions, voting for climate-friendly policies, and talking about these changes with others can multiply your impact.
