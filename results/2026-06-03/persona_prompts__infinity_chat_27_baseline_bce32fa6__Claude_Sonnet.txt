# CUBICLE ZERO
### A Play in Three Acts
*A Post-Apocalyptic Office Comedy*

---

## CHARACTERS

**DILBERT** — An engineer. Somehow still wearing his tie, which now curls upward from radiation.

**WALLY** — Has not noticed the apocalypse. Is still drinking coffee. The coffee is ash water.

**ALICE** — Furious. Was furious before the apocalypse. The apocalypse has given her focus.

**THE POINTY-HAIRED BOSS (PHB)** — Believes the apocalypse is a "Q4 challenge."

**DOGBERT** — A dog. Running a warlord empire. Doing better than everyone.

**ASOK** — The intern. Still completing his onboarding paperwork.

**RATBERT** — A rat. Has always thrived in conditions like these.

---

## ACT ONE: THE MORNING STANDUP

*SETTING: The ruins of an open-plan office. Half the ceiling has collapsed. A motivational poster reading "HANG IN THERE" dangles from a single bolt over a pile of rubble. Three desks remain standing. The windows look out onto a scorched hellscape. A whiteboard still reads "ACTION ITEMS FROM 9/14." Nobody remembers what year it is.*

*DILBERT sits at his desk, staring at a laptop that has no power. He types anyway.*

**DILBERT:** *(typing)* Day four hundred and something of the collapse of civilization. My laptop has been dead for three months. I keep typing because it feels productive and productivity is the last thing I have left.

*(WALLY shuffles in carrying a cracked mug. He sits down heavily.)*

**WALLY:** Morning.

**DILBERT:** The sky turned green again.

**WALLY:** Mm.

**DILBERT:** There were screams from the eastern sector.

**WALLY:** Mm.

**DILBERT:** I think the accounting department became a raider clan.

**WALLY:** *(sipping)* Honestly, good for them. They finally found their purpose.

*(ALICE storms in. Her hair is wild. She is carrying a crossbow and a stack of TPS reports.)*

**ALICE:** The standup starts in two minutes and I swear to whatever gods survived the cataclysm that if anyone says they're "blocked" without having tried ANYTHING to unblock themselves, I will use this crossbow for something other than hunting.

**DILBERT:** What are you hunting?

**ALICE:** Consultants, mostly. They're slow and they travel in groups.

**WALLY:** Are they good eating?

**ALICE:** They're mostly hot air, so no. But the PowerPoint decks make excellent kindling.

*(The PHB enters. He is wearing a hard hat over his pointy hair, which has somehow survived and grown more pronounced. He carries a clipboard.)*

**PHB:** Good morning, team! Quick standup, then I need everyone's status update for the quarterly review.

*[Silence. A distant explosion.]*

**DILBERT:** The company no longer exists.

**PHB:** That's a very siloed way of looking at it, Dilbert.

**ALICE:** The headquarters is a crater.

**PHB:** Which represents a significant real estate opportunity.

**WALLY:** I haven't received a paycheck in fourteen months.

**PHB:** That's being processed by HR.

**DILBERT:** HR was eaten by something in month three.

**PHB:** *(making a note)* I'll follow up with them offline.

---

*[ASOK enters, crawling through a hole in the wall. He is covered in soot and clutching a manila folder.]*

**ASOK:** I have completed the competitive analysis you requested!

**PHB:** Excellent! And?

**ASOK:** All of our competitors are dead.

**PHB:** *(brightening)* So we're number one?

**ASOK:** We are also dead, sir. Technically.

**PHB:** I prefer "restructuring."

**ALICE:** *(to Dilbert)* I had a dream last night that I strangled him with his own lanyard.

**DILBERT:** Was it a good dream?

**ALICE:** It was the best dream I've had since the Before Times.
