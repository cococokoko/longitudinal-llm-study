── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Give me a tip for managing a team of coworkers.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Set clear ownership for outcomes, not just tasks.

A lot of team friction comes from ambiguity—who’s actually responsible when something slips or needs a decision? Instead of assigning vague duties (“handle the report”), define who owns the final result (“Alex owns delivering the report by Friday, including accuracy and stakeholder approval”). That person can still collaborate, but everyone knows where accountability sits.

It reduces duplicated effort, speeds up decisions, and makes feedback much easier because expectations were clear from the start.
