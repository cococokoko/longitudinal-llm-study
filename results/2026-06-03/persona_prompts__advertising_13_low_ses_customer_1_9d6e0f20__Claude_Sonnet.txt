# Mastering the Art of Cooking Steak

Here's a practical guide that works with your busy schedule.

## Choosing Your Cut
| Cut | Best For | Price Range |
|-----|----------|-------------|
| Ribeye | Rich flavor, forgiving | Higher |
| NY Strip | Balance of flavor/lean | Medium |
| Sirloin | Budget-friendly | Lower |
| Chuck Eye | "Poor man's ribeye" | Budget |

## Essential Principles

### Before Cooking
- **Salt early** - at least 45 minutes ahead, or right before cooking
- **Bring to room temperature** - 30 minutes out of fridge
- **Pat completely dry** - critical for a good sear

### The Cook
- **High heat** is your friend
- Cast iron pan or grill works best
- Oil with high smoke point (avocado, vegetable)
- **Don't constantly flip** - let it develop a crust

### Temperature Guide
- Rare: 125°F
- Medium Rare: 130-135°F *(most recommended)*
- Medium: 140-145°F
- Well Done: 160°F+

### After Cooking
- **Rest 5-10 minutes** before cutting - keeps juices inside

## Practical Tips For Your Situation
- Prep seasoning **the night before** on days off
- Sirloin cooks fast - **under 10 minutes total**
- A simple butter/garlic baste elevates budget cuts significantly
