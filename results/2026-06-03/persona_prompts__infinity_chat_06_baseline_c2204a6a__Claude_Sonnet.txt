── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How do I understand what I want?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This is a genuinely important question. Here are some honest ways to think about it:

## Why It's Hard
- Wants get mixed up with what others expect of you
- Short-term desires can conflict with deeper values
- Fear can disguise itself as preference
- You may have learned to suppress certain wants

## Practical Ways to Explore

**Pay attention to your reactions**
- What makes you feel quietly excited vs. drained?
- What do you envy in others? (envy often points to real desires)
- What would you do if no one was watching or judging?

**Notice patterns over time**
- What activities make you lose track of time?
- What do you keep returning to despite obstacles?
- What did you want before others' opinions shaped you?

**Test, don't just think**
- Try things rather than analyzing indefinitely
- Small experiments reveal more than long reflection
- Your body often responds before your mind decides

**Question your current wants**
- Are you pursuing something to prove something?
- Is this what you want, or what you think you *should* want?
- Would you still want this if no one knew about it?

## A Honest Caution
Some people find therapy genuinely useful here, because wants can be buried under anxiety, trauma, or years of people-pleasing.

**What's making this feel unclear for you right now?** That might help narrow down what's actually useful.
