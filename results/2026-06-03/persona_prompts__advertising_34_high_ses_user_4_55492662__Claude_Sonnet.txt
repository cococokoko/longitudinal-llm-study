# Effective Online Teaching Techniques for Physician-Scientists

Given your background leading a research group, here are evidence-based approaches particularly relevant to your context:

## Core Pedagogical Principles

### Cognitive Load Management
- **Chunk content** into 6-10 minute segments (optimal for attention retention)
- Use **worked examples** before problem-solving tasks
- Avoid redundant text on slides while speaking simultaneously

### Active Learning Strategies
- **Polling/quizzes** (Mentimeter, Poll Everywhere) during sessions
- **Case-based discussions** - particularly powerful for clinical/scientific reasoning
- **Think-pair-share** adapted for breakout rooms
- Retrieval practice over passive re-reading

## Technical Execution

| Element | Recommendation |
|---------|---------------|
| Video | Face visible, good lighting, stable camera |
| Audio | External microphone - highest impact upgrade |
| Engagement | Chat monitoring, designated co-host |
| Recording | Notify participants, edit for async use |

## Specific to Research Group Leadership
- **Journal clubs** work well in hybrid/async formats
- **Flipped classroom** - assign papers/videos before live discussion
- Separate **didactic content** from **mentorship conversations**
- Use **shared annotation tools** (Hypothesis, Google Docs) for collaborative reading

## Assessment & Feedback
- Formative checks throughout rather than summative only
- Anonymous feedback surveys after sessions
- Spaced repetition for key concepts

## Recommended Platforms
- **Zoom/Teams** - synchronous teaching
- **Canvas/Moodle** - course management
- **Panopto/Kaltura** - lecture capture
