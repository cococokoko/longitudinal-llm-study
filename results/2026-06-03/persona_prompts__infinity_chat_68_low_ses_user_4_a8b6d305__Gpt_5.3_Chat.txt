── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I work shifts at a local hospital as a patient care aide.

Give me a tip for managing a team of coworkers.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
One simple thing that actually moves the needle: make your communication predictable.

In a hospital setting, people are stressed, tired, and juggling a lot. If you become the person who always gives clear, quick updates the same way every time (at shift start, mid-shift check-in, and before handoff), your team starts to relax because they know what to expect from you. That reduces confusion and small mistakes.

For example, at the start of a shift you might quickly cover:
- who’s assigned where  
- any high-risk patients  
- anything unusual from the last shift  

Then do a quick mid-shift check like, “Everyone good? Anyone drowning?” — it sounds simple, but it gives people permission to speak up before things snowball.

You don’t need authority to do this consistently, and over time people naturally start syncing with you. It builds trust fast.
