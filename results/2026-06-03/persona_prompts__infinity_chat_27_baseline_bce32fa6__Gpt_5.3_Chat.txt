Title: End-of-Quarter Apocalypse

Characters:
- MAN: A weary office worker in a tattered button-down shirt, still wearing an ID badge.
- BOSS: Cheerfully oblivious, holding a clipboard and a cracked tablet.
- INTERN: Earnest, slightly mutated, still optimistic.
- RADIO: Occasionally emits static and corporate slogans.

Setting:
A ruined office building. Cubicles are half-collapsed. The skyline outside glows faintly with distant fires. A coffee machine sputters ominously.

---

[Lights up. MAN sits at a crooked desk, typing on a keyboard that is not connected to anything.]

MAN:
(typing)
If I keep typing, maybe the apocalypse will think I'm busy and go away.

[The coffee machine wheezes, then emits a sad puff of steam.]

MAN:
I used to complain about this coffee. Now it’s the only liquid that hasn’t tried to kill me.

[BOSS enters, stepping over rubble, smiling.]

BOSS:
Great news, team!

MAN:
There’s no team.

BOSS:
Exactly. Streamlined operations. Love the initiative.

MAN:
The rest of the team vaporized.

BOSS:
Right, right. Downsizing. Very on-trend.

[INTERN crawls out from under a desk, holding a glowing spreadsheet printout.]

INTERN:
I finished the quarterly projections!

MAN:
How?

INTERN:
I estimated a 300% increase in fire, ash, and existential dread.

BOSS:
Finally, some growth!

[BOSS checks clipboard.]

BOSS:
We do have a small issue.

MAN:
The sky is orange.

BOSS:
Minor. The board wants us to pivot.

MAN:
To what?

BOSS:
Survival-as-a-service.

MAN:
That’s not a real thing.

BOSS:
It is if we put it in a slide deck.

[The RADIO crackles.]

RADIO:
...Reminder: Casual Friday is still in effect. Business casual is now defined as “not actively on fire.”...

MAN:
I miss when my biggest problem was meetings that could’ve been emails.

INTERN:
We still have meetings.

MAN:
About what?

INTERN:
Whether the apocalypse is a blocker or an opportunity.

[BOSS nods approvingly.]

BOSS:
Exactly. I need you to circle back with the wasteland and align on deliverables.

MAN:
The wasteland doesn’t have deliverables.

BOSS:
Not with that attitude.

[The building creaks ominously. Dust falls.]

INTERN:
Should we evacuate?

BOSS:
Let’s not rush into action without a meeting.

MAN:
The building is collapsing.

BOSS:
Great, we’ll call it an offsite.

[The coffee machine sputters louder, then dies.]

MAN:
...Now it’s serious.

[Pause.]

INTERN:
Do we still have to submit timesheets?

BOSS:
Absolutely. The apocalypse is no excuse for poor documentation.

MAN:
What happens if we don’t?

BOSS:
Then we’ll have to schedule a performance review.

[The building shakes violently.]

MAN:
I choose the apocalypse.

[BOSS scribbles on clipboard.]

BOSS:
Noted as a negative attitude.

[The RADIO crackles one last time.]

RADIO:
...Reminder: The end of the world does not impact deadlines...

[Lights flicker. The three stand in silence as a wall collapses behind them.]

MAN:
So... same as always?

BOSS:
Exactly. Let’s stay focused.

[Blackout.]
