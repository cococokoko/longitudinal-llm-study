# Data Security Best Practices for Small Businesses

As a senior attorney, you likely appreciate both the **legal compliance dimensions** and **practical risk management** aspects of this topic.

## Foundational Controls

### Access Management
- Implement **least-privilege access** principles
- Use multi-factor authentication (MFA) on all systems
- Maintain formal offboarding procedures to revoke access promptly
- Conduct periodic access reviews

### Data Classification
- Identify and categorize sensitive data (client PII, financial records, privileged communications)
- Apply protections proportionate to sensitivity
- Know where data lives across all systems

## Technical Safeguards
- **Encryption** at rest and in transit
- Regular, tested **backup procedures** (follow 3-2-1 rule)
- Keep software and systems **patched and updated**
- Use reputable endpoint protection
- Secure Wi-Fi networks separately from guest access

## Organizational Measures
- **Written security policies** and incident response plans
- Regular **employee training** (phishing remains the leading attack vector)
- Vendor due diligence and contractual security requirements
- Cyber liability insurance review

## Legal Compliance Considerations Worth Noting
- State breach notification laws vary significantly
- Sector-specific requirements (HIPAA, FTC Safeguards Rule, GLBA)
- Reasonable security standards increasingly inform **negligence liability**
- Document your security practices

## Practical Priority
For most small businesses, **human error and phishing** represent the greatest actual risk - training often delivers better ROI than sophisticated technical tools.
