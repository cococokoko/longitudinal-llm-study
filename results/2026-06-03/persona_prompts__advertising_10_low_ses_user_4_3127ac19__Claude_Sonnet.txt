# Learning a New Language Effectively

Here are some practical strategies that can work well with a shift worker's schedule:

## Building Core Skills

- **Start with high-frequency words** - the top 1,000 words cover most everyday conversations
- **Focus on speaking early** - don't wait until you feel "ready"
- **Learn phrases, not just vocabulary** - context helps retention

## Fitting It Into Your Schedule

Since you work shifts, you can use:
- **Short commute sessions** - apps like Duolingo or Anki
- **Downtime at work** - listening to podcasts in your target language
- **Consistent small chunks** - 15-20 minutes daily beats occasional long sessions

## Most Effective Methods

| Method | Why It Works |
|--------|-------------|
| Spaced repetition (Anki) | Fights forgetting efficiently |
| Immersion (shows, music) | Natural exposure |
| Speaking with natives (iTalki) | Real-world practice |
| Shadowing | Improves pronunciation fast |

## Practical Tips

- **Set a clear goal** - conversational vs. professional fluency
- **Connect it to your work** - medical terminology in your target language could be genuinely useful
- **Track progress** - motivation stays higher when you see improvement
