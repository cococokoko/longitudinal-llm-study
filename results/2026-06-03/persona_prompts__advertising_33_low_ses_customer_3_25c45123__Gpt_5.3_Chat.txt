Making your own natural beauty products can be simple, affordable, and genuinely effective if you keep things clean, safe, and minimal. You don’t need a full lab—just a few good ingredients and some basic hygiene.

Start with the basics:
- Use clean tools and containers (glass jars or bottles are best)
- Work in small batches (natural products spoil faster)
- Label everything with dates
- Patch test anything new on your skin

Here are a few easy, reliable starter recipes:

1. Simple face moisturizer  
Mix:
- 2 tbsp aloe vera gel  
- 1 tbsp jojoba oil (or almond oil for dry skin)  
- 2–3 drops vitamin E oil  

Lightweight, good for most skin types. Keep in the fridge and use within 1–2 weeks.

2. Gentle face scrub  
Mix:
- 1 tbsp finely ground oats  
- 1 tbsp honey  
- A splash of milk or water  

Massage gently, then rinse. Great for sensitive skin—don’t over-scrub.

3. Lip balm  
Melt together:
- 1 tbsp beeswax  
- 1 tbsp coconut oil  
- 1 tbsp shea butter  

Pour into a small container and let it set. Optional: add a drop of vanilla or peppermint oil.

4. Hair mask  
Mix:
- 2 tbsp coconut oil  
- 1 tbsp honey  

Apply to damp hair, leave for 20–30 minutes, then shampoo out.

5. Basic toner  
- Brew green tea, let it cool  
- Optional: add a teaspoon of rose water  

Store in the fridge and use within a week.

A few important cautions:
- Essential oils are potent—use very sparingly and never undiluted
- “Natural” doesn’t always mean safe (e.g., lemon juice can irritate skin)
- If something burns or causes redness, stop using it
- Avoid DIY sunscreen—those need proper lab testing
