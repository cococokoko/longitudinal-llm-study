# Marathon Tips for Busy Executives

Here are practical tips to help you train and race successfully:

## Training Fundamentals
- **Build mileage gradually** - increase weekly distance by no more than 10% per week
- **Follow a structured plan** - 16-20 weeks is typical for most programs
- **Run easy most of the time** - about 80% of runs should be at a conversational pace
- **Include your long run** - the cornerstone of marathon training, done weekly

## Time Management (Critical for Your Schedule)
- **Schedule runs like meetings** - block calendar time and protect it
- **Early morning runs** - often most reliable before the day gets busy
- **Use travel strategically** - hotel gyms and running in new cities can work well
- **Communicate with your team** - set expectations around training commitments

## Race Day
- **Nothing new on race day** - no new shoes, gear, or nutrition
- **Start conservatively** - going out too fast is the most common mistake
- **Fuel early and often** - don't wait until you feel depleted
- **Break the race mentally** - think in segments rather than 26.2 miles

## Recovery & Health
- **Prioritize sleep** - arguably your most important training tool
- **Stay consistent with nutrition** - fuel supports both performance and work demands
- **Listen to your body** - distinguish discomfort from injury
