# Learning a New Language Effectively

As a physician-scientist, you likely have strong analytical skills and a busy schedule — here's a practical, evidence-based approach:

---

## Core Principles

### **Prioritize High-Frequency Vocabulary**
- The top **1,000-2,000 words** cover ~80-90% of everyday speech
- Focus there before pursuing breadth
- Use frequency-based word lists for your target language

### **Spaced Repetition (SRS)**
- Tools like **Anki** are well-validated for retention
- Even 15-20 minutes/day compounds significantly
- Mirrors memory consolidation principles you already know

### **Comprehensible Input**
- Krashen's input hypothesis has reasonable empirical support
- Consume content slightly above your current level
- Podcasts, graded readers, YouTube channels

---

## Practical Structure for a Busy Schedule

| Time Available | Activity |
|---|---|
| 5-10 min | Anki flashcard review |
| 20-30 min | Listening/reading input |
| 1 hour | Conversation practice |
