Best skin routine for dry skin.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Dry skin is mostly about protecting your barrier and keeping water from escaping, not just adding moisture once and hoping it sticks. A simple, consistent routine usually works better than a complicated one.

Morning:
Start with a gentle, non-foaming cleanser (or just rinse with water if you’re not oily overnight). Look for something labeled hydrating or cream-based. Right after, while your skin is still slightly damp, use a hydrating serum with ingredients like hyaluronic acid or glycerin. Then apply a rich moisturizer with ceramides, fatty acids, or squalane to seal that in. Finish with sunscreen (SPF 30 or higher), ideally one with moisturizing properties.

Night:
Cleanse again with a gentle cleanser. If you wear makeup or sunscreen, you might want to use an oil or balm cleanser first, then your regular one. Follow with your hydrating serum again. At night you can go a bit heavier with moisturizer—look for thicker creams or even a sleeping mask. If your skin is very dry, a thin layer of an occlusive like petrolatum (Vaseline) on top can help lock everything in.

A few extra tips that make a big difference:
Avoid hot showers and harsh scrubs—they strip your skin.
Use products with ceramides, glycerin, hyaluronic acid, panthenol, or urea.
Limit strong actives (like high-strength retinoids or exfoliating acids) unless you balance them with good hydration.
A humidifier at night can genuinely help if your environment is dry.

If your skin feels tight, flaky, or stings after products, that’s a sign your barrier needs gentler care, not more aggressive treatment.
