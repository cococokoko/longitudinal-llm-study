## Analysis vs. Design

**Analysis** = Understanding **what** the problem is (requirements, constraints, current state)

**Design** = Determining **how** to solve it (structure, architecture, solution approach)

| Analysis | Design |
|----------|--------|
| Problem space | Solution space |
| "What is needed?" | "How to build it?" |
| Discover & define | Create & plan |

---

## Can You Design Without Analysis?

**Technically yes, practically no.**

- You *can* jump straight to design, but you risk **solving the wrong problem**
- Without analysis, design is based on **assumptions**, which are often wrong
- Skipping analysis typically leads to **rework, wasted resources, and missed requirements**

**Think of it like this:** Designing a house without knowing how many people will live there, their needs, or the budget — you might build something beautiful that completely fails its purpose.

---

## Bottom Line

Analysis and design are **sequential and dependent**. Analysis informs design. Skipping analysis doesn't eliminate the need for it — it just means you'll discover the gaps **later, at greater cost**.
