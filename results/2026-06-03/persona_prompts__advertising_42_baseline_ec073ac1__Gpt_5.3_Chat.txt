What are the best apps for tracking fitness and nutrition?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
It depends a bit on how deep you want to go (casual tracking vs. really dialed-in data), but here are some of the strongest options right now, broken down by style:

If you want simple, all-in-one tracking:
- MyFitnessPal — still the easiest food logger with a huge database. Good for beginners, but many features are now behind a paywall.
- Lose It! — similar to MyFitnessPal, slightly cleaner and often cheaper. Great balance of simplicity and useful insights.

If you care about detailed nutrition (macros, micronutrients):
- Cronometer — probably the best for accuracy. Tracks vitamins, minerals, and even things like amino acids. Ideal if you’re serious about nutrition quality.
- MacroFactor — very smart app that adjusts your calorie targets based on your actual progress. Great if you’re trying to lose or gain weight efficiently.

If you want strong fitness/workout tracking:
- Strong — excellent for logging gym workouts (sets, reps, weights). Simple and fast.
- Hevy — similar to Strong but more modern, with a social/community angle.
- Fitbod — generates workouts for you based on your goals and recovery. Good if you don’t want to plan.

If you want everything connected (health ecosystem):
- Apple Fitness + Apple Health — great if you’re in the Apple ecosystem; integrates steps, workouts, heart rate, etc.
- Google Fit — solid basic tracker for Android users.
- WHOOP — more advanced wearable + app combo focused on recovery, sleep, and strain.
- Garmin Connect — excellent if you have a Garmin device; very detailed performance data.

If your goal is fat loss or body recomposition:
- MacroFactor or Cronometer (nutrition) + Strong or Hevy (workouts) is a really solid combo.
- If you want minimal effort: Lose It! + basic step tracking works surprisingly well.
