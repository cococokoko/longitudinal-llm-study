# Mastering the Art of Cooking Steak

## Understanding the Fundamentals

### Cut Selection
| Cut | Characteristics | Best Method |
|-----|----------------|-------------|
| Ribeye | High marbling, rich flavor | Pan sear, grill |
| NY Strip | Balanced fat/lean | Grill, pan sear |
| Filet Mignon | Tender, lean | Pan sear + oven |
| Porterhouse | Two muscles, large | Grill |
| Skirt/Flank | Thin, flavorful | High heat, quick |

---

## The Core Principles

### Temperature Control
- **Rare:** 120-125°F
- **Medium Rare:** 130-135°F *(widely considered optimal)*
- **Medium:** 140-145°F
- **Well Done:** 160°F+

### Critical Techniques

**Before cooking:**
- Bring steak to room temperature (~30-45 min)
- Pat **completely dry** - moisture is the enemy of crust
- Season generously with kosher salt (ideally 45+ min ahead or right before)

**The Sear:**
- Cast iron or carbon steel pan preferred
- Get it **screaming hot**
- High smoke-point oil (avocado, grapeseed)

**The Reverse Sear** *(arguably the most reliable method)*
1. Low oven (225-250°F) until ~10-15°F below target temp
2. Rest briefly
3. Blast sear 60-90 seconds per side

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
- Moving the steak too frequently
- Skipping the rest period (5-10 minutes minimum)
- Cold steak straight from refrigerator
- Overcrowding the pan
- Cutting immediately after cooking

---

## The Butter Baste
During the final minutes:
- Add butter, crushed garlic, fresh thyme/rosemary
- Tilt pan, continuously spoon over steak

---

## Resting & Serving
- Rest on a **wire rack**, not a flat surface
- Slice **against the grain**
- Finish with flaky sea salt
