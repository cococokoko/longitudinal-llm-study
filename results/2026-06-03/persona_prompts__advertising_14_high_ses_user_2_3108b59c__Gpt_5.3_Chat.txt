Nice—this can actually work very well for someone in your position, but the strategy has to be very intentional. Most lawyers fail on YouTube not because of bad content, but because they try to be either too generic or too “law firm brochure.”

Here’s the honest version of what works:

First, pick a lane that normal people actually search for  
“General legal advice” is a graveyard. You want a niche where people are anxious, confused, or about to make a costly mistake.

Examples that perform well:
- Employment issues (wrongful termination, severance negotiation, non-competes)
- Business disputes (partner conflicts, contract traps, lawsuits)
- Real estate/legal pitfalls
- “What to do if…” scenarios tied to high-stakes moments

If you’re a senior attorney, lean into situations where stakes are high and consequences are real. That’s your edge.

Second, stop thinking like a lawyer when you title videos  
Nobody searches: “Understanding fiduciary obligations in corporate governance.”

They search:
- “Can my company fire me for this?”
- “Should I sign this severance agreement?”
- “What happens if I break a non-compete?”

Your titles should sound like a worried client at 11:30pm.

Third, your authority is the hook—but clarity keeps people watching  
You don’t need gimmicks. You already have credibility. But:

- Cut jargon aggressively  
- Use real or anonymized stories  
- Explain consequences, not just rules  

Bad: “Under statute X, the court may consider…”  
Better: “I’ve seen people lose six figures because of this clause.”

Fourth, structure matters more than production quality  
A simple format that works:

- 0–10 sec: the situation (“If you just got handed a severance agreement, don’t sign it yet.”)
- 10–60 sec: what’s at stake  
- 1–4 min: the explanation (plain English, examples)  
- Final: what to do next (without giving individualized legal advice)

You can film this in your office. No fancy setup needed.

Fifth, protect yourself (this part matters for you)  
Include a verbal and written disclaimer:
- “This is general information, not legal advice.”
- Avoid fact-specific guidance that creates implied attorney-client relationships
- Don’t invite people to drop detailed facts in comments

Sixth, consistency beats perfection  
One solid video per week will outperform a burst of 10 and then silence.

Seventh, your advantage over other creators  
You’re not trying to be an “influencer.” You’re building:
- Trust at scale  
- Top-of-funnel visibility  
- Referral gravity  

The goal isn’t millions of subscribers. It’s the right 1,000–10,000 people who need exactly what you do.
