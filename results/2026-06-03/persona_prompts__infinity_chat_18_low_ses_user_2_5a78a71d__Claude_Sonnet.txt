# Movie Review: "The 6:47"

★★★★½

**Runtime: 1h 52min | Drama/Comedy**

---

Director Maya Okonkwo's debut feature is the film single parents didn't know they desperately needed.

"The 6:47" follows Dara, a logistics coordinator and mother of two, whose entire carefully constructed daily schedule collapses when her regular babysitter cancels 20 minutes before her biggest work presentation.

What follows is genuinely funny, occasionally heartbreaking, and **remarkably honest** about the invisible architecture single parents build just to get through an ordinary Tuesday.

The film never asks for pity. Instead it celebrates the **quiet heroism of improvisation** - the neighbor called in as a favor, the kid who falls asleep in a conference room chair, the cold coffee that somehow keeps everything moving.

Lead actress Simone Adeyemi delivers a performance that feels less like acting and more like *recognition*. You'll laugh, then immediately feel slightly guilty for laughing, then laugh again.

**What makes it special:** It treats single parenthood as a full human experience rather than a plot device or a problem to be solved by a romantic interest.
