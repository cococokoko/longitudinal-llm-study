Got it — you need something effective but low-effort and reliable.

For dry skin, the goal is simple: **cleanse gently, add moisture, lock it in, and don’t strip your skin.** Here’s a routine that won’t eat up your time:

**Morning (2–3 minutes)**
- Gentle cleanser (or just rinse with water if your skin isn’t oily in the morning)
- Moisturizer with hydrating ingredients (look for glycerin, ceramides, or hyaluronic acid)
- Sunscreen (SPF 30 or higher — this matters more than people think, even for dryness)

**Night (3–5 minutes)**
- Gentle cleanser (to remove dirt, sweat, sunscreen)
- Moisturizer (a thicker one than your morning if possible)

**Optional but helpful (a few times a week)**
- Apply moisturizer on slightly damp skin — boosts hydration
- Use a thicker “barrier” cream or a few drops of facial oil at night if your skin feels tight
- Very gentle exfoliation once a week (not daily — overdoing this worsens dryness)

**What to avoid**
- Foaming or “deep clean” cleansers (they strip your skin)
- Hot water on your face
- Overcomplicated routines — consistency beats complexity

**Quick product shorthand (so you don’t have to overthink it)**
- Cleanser: “gentle” or “hydrating” on the label
- Moisturizer: “ceramide,” “cream,” or “repair”
- Sunscreen: anything you’ll actually use daily

If your schedule is chaos, even this stripped-down version works:
- Morning: moisturizer + SPF
- Night: cleanse + moisturizer
