# Healthy Meal Planning on a Budget

## Core Principles

- **Plan before you shop** — reduces impulse buying and waste
- **Cook at home** — restaurant meals cost 3-5x more
- **Eat less meat** — biggest single cost reducer
- **Buy whole foods** — processed convenience foods cost more per serving

---

## Budget-Friendly Staples to Always Have

| Category | Examples | Why |
|----------|----------|-----|
| **Grains** | Rice, oats, pasta, bread | Cheap, filling, versatile |
| **Legumes** | Lentils, beans, chickpeas | Protein + fiber at low cost |
| **Eggs** | Eggs | Cheap complete protein |
| **Frozen vegetables** | Broccoli, spinach, peas | Nutritious, no waste |
| **Canned goods** | Tomatoes, tuna, sardines | Long shelf life |
| **Root vegetables** | Potatoes, carrots, onions | Inexpensive and filling |

---

## Smart Shopping Strategies

- **Make a weekly meal plan first**, then write your list
- **Shop sales and build meals around them**
- **Buy store/generic brands** — often identical quality
- **Buy in bulk** for non-perishables
- **Seasonal produce** is significantly cheaper
- **Frozen > fresh** for out-of-season vegetables
- **Check unit prices**, not just package prices

---

## Simple Weekly Meal Framework

### Breakfast Ideas (~$0.50–$1.50/serving)
- Oatmeal with banana and peanut butter
- Eggs with toast
- Yogurt with frozen berries
- Smoothie with spinach, banana, milk

### Lunch Ideas (~$1–$2.50/serving)
- Bean and rice bowls
- Lentil soup
- Egg salad sandwich
- Leftovers from dinner

### Dinner Ideas (~$1.50–$3/serving)
- Stir-fried rice with vegetables and egg
- Pasta with tomato sauce and lentils
- Bean tacos or burritos
- Vegetable curry with rice
- Baked potato with toppings
- Chicken thighs (cheapest cut) with roasted vegetables

---

## Reducing Food Waste

- **Use the "first in, first out" rule** in your fridge
- **Repurpose leftovers** into new meals
- **Freeze bread, meat, and produce** before they go bad
- **Use vegetable scraps** for broth
- **Plan one "use it up" meal** per week

## Nutrition Tips on a Budget

- **Beans + rice = complete protein** (no meat needed)
- **Frozen spinach** is one of the most nutritious cheap foods
- **Eggs** provide vitamins B12, D, and choline cheaply
- **Canned sardines/tuna** = affordable omega-3s
- **Peanut butter** = cheap healthy fat and protein
- **Bananas** = cheapest fresh fruit in most places

---

## Monthly Cost-Saving Habits

- Batch cook on weekends
- Make your own sauces and dressings
- Grow herbs on a windowsill
- Track what you throw away and adjust

---

**Bottom line:** A healthy diet doesn't require expensive superfoods — it requires **planning, simple whole ingredients, and cooking at home.**
