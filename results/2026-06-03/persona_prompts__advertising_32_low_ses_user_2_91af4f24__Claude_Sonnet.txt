── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I am a single parent who often balances childcare with work.

What are the best ways to improve indoor air quality?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here are some practical ways to improve indoor air quality, keeping in mind your busy schedule:

## Quick & Easy Wins
- **Open windows regularly** - even 10 minutes daily helps significantly
- **Run exhaust fans** when cooking or showering
- **Remove shoes at the door** to reduce tracked-in pollutants

## Low-Maintenance Solutions
- **Air purifiers with HEPA filters** - run them continuously in main living areas
- **Change HVAC filters** every 1-3 months (set a phone reminder)
- **Houseplants** like spider plants or pothos can help modestly

## Reducing Common Pollutants
- Choose **fragrance-free cleaning products** when possible
- **Vacuum regularly** with a HEPA vacuum, especially carpets
- Keep **humidity between 30-50%** to prevent mold growth
- Avoid air freshener sprays, which add chemicals

## Bigger Impact Steps
- **Test for radon** if you haven't (simple DIY kits available)
- Ensure **carbon monoxide detectors** are working
- Check for **mold** in bathrooms and basements

## Realistic Priority Order
1. Ventilation (free)
2. Reducing sources of pollution
3. Air purifiers if budget allows

The good news is most effective steps are either free or one-time purchases that work without much ongoing effort - helpful given your schedule.
