Title: “End of Quarter (and the World)”

Characters:
- MAN (formerly an office worker, now the last surviving “Senior Analyst”)
- ROCK (a rock he occasionally talks to; does not respond)
- DRONE (a malfunctioning corporate drone that still enforces policy)
- VOICE (automated corporate announcement system that somehow still works)

---

Scene 1: A barren wasteland. Rubble. A folding desk sits in the middle. The MAN is adjusting a crooked tie while staring at a cracked laptop with no power.

MAN:
(sighing)
Day 472 since the apocalypse. Still waiting on IT to fix my login issue.

(He taps the dead laptop.)

MAN:
I would escalate this, but the escalation team was eaten by wolves. Which, to be fair, is the most efficient response I’ve seen from management.

(He turns to the ROCK.)

MAN:
I assume you’ve reviewed the quarterly projections?

(Pause.)

MAN:
Right, silence. Strong leadership stance.

---

Scene 2: A buzzing sound. The DRONE descends, wobbling.

DRONE:
Employee detected. You have missed 471 consecutive stand-up meetings.

MAN:
We don’t have legs to stand on anymore. Society collapsed.

DRONE:
Unacceptable excuse. Please provide a more actionable update.

MAN:
Fine. Action item: Survive. Blockers: radiation, famine, existential dread.

DRONE:
Noted. Marking as “at risk.”

---

Scene 3: The VOICE crackles from a distant, half-buried speaker.

VOICE:
Reminder: Employee morale is our top priority.

MAN:
(laughs dryly)
Of course it is.

VOICE:
Please complete your mandatory “Wellness in a Post-Collapse Environment” training module.

MAN:
I tried. The video was just a guy screaming.

VOICE:
Completion rate: 0%. Please try again.

---

Scene 4: The MAN rearranges rubble into a makeshift conference table.

MAN:
Alright, team meeting. Attendance: me, rock, drone, and the ghosts of poor decisions.

DRONE:
Agenda?

MAN:
Same as always. Why did everything collapse?

DRONE:
Preliminary analysis suggests… insufficient documentation.

MAN:
Of course.

---

Scene 5: The MAN stares out at the wasteland.

MAN:
You know, before the apocalypse, I thought things couldn’t get worse than back-to-back meetings.

(He pauses.)

MAN:
I was wrong. Now they’re back-to-back *forever*.

DRONE:
Correction: There is a 15-minute break scheduled.

MAN:
When?

DRONE:
After the heat death of the universe.

---

Scene 6: The MAN loosens his tie and sits on the ground.

MAN:
So… what’s the long-term plan?

DRONE:
We are pivoting.

MAN:
To what?

DRONE:
Survival as a Service.

MAN:
(slow nod)
Do we have a roadmap?

DRONE:
No. But we have a vision statement.

MAN:
That’s worse.

---

Scene 7: The VOICE returns, cheerful as ever.

VOICE:
Congratulations! You are a valued member of the organization.

MAN:
I’m the only member.

VOICE:
Exactly. 100% retention.

---

The MAN looks at the ROCK again.

MAN:
Do you ever feel like we’re just pretending any of this matters?

(Pause.)

MAN:
Yeah. Me too.

(He straightens his tie again.)

MAN:
Anyway, same time tomorrow?

The DRONE hovers. The VOICE hums. The wasteland stretches endlessly.
