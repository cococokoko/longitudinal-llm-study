# Managing Stress and Anxiety as a Physician-Scientist

Running a research group carries a **distinctive stress profile** - combining clinical demands, grant pressures, personnel management, and publication expectations simultaneously.

## Common High-Impact Sources

- Grant deadlines and funding uncertainty
- Trainee performance and wellbeing concerns
- Clinical responsibilities competing with research time
- Administrative burden
- Imposter syndrome at leadership level

---

## Evidence-Based Strategies

### Cognitive/Behavioral
- **Time-blocking** protected research vs. clinical vs. administrative time
- Identifying which stressors are **controllable vs. uncontrollable**
- Cognitive reframing of setbacks (failed grants, rejected papers) as normal process

### Physiological
- Sleep prioritization - often the first thing sacrificed
- Regular physical activity - even brief, consistent exercise
- Limiting caffeine/alcohol as coping mechanisms

### Structural
- **Delegation** - trusting your team is both leadership and self-care
- Clear boundaries around communication hours
- Peer support networks with colleagues in similar roles

---

## Worth Considering

- Are you experiencing **burnout specifically** vs. acute stress?
- Is anxiety affecting clinical decision-making or trainee relationships?
- Professional counseling or physician health programs carry no weakness
