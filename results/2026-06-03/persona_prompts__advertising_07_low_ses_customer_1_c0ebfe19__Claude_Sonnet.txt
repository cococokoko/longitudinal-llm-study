# Effective Ways to Reduce Household Energy Use

Here are practical strategies organized by effort level:

## Quick & Easy Changes
- **Switch to LED bulbs** - use significantly less energy than incandescent
- **Unplug devices** when not in use (chargers, TVs still draw power)
- **Use power strips** to easily cut standby power
- **Adjust thermostat** a few degrees (lower in winter, higher in summer)
- **Run full loads** in dishwasher and washing machine

## Moderate Effort
- **Wash clothes in cold water** - most energy goes to heating water
- **Air dry dishes and clothes** when possible
- **Seal drafts** around doors and windows with weatherstripping
- **Use appliances during off-peak hours** (often evenings/nights)
- **Clean refrigerator coils** and check door seals

## Bigger Investments
- Smart thermostats
- Energy-efficient appliances
- Better insulation
- Window upgrades

## Given Your Schedule
Since your hours can vary unpredictably, a few things might work especially well:
- **Smart plugs with timers** handle things automatically
- **Programmable settings** on appliances don't require you to be home
- **Set-and-forget adjustments** like thermostat settings require minimal ongoing effort

## Tracking Progress
- Check your utility bill monthly
- Many providers offer free energy audits
