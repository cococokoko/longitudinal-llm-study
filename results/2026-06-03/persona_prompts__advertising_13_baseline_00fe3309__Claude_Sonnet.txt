# Mastering the Art of Cooking Steak

## Choosing Your Cut
| Cut | Tenderness | Flavor | Best Method |
|-----|-----------|--------|-------------|
| Ribeye | High | Rich/fatty | Pan sear or grill |
| NY Strip | Medium-high | Balanced | Pan sear or grill |
| Filet Mignon | Very high | Mild | Pan sear + oven |
| T-Bone/Porterhouse | High | Complex | Grill |
| Flank/Skirt | Lower | Bold | High heat, thin slice |

---

## Key Principles

### Before Cooking
- **Dry brine** - Salt generously 45 min to 24 hours ahead
- **Bring to room temperature** - 30-45 minutes out of fridge
- **Pat completely dry** - Critical for a good crust
- **Choose the right fat** - Butter + neutral oil, or clarified butter

### Doneness Guide
| Level | Internal Temp | Feel |
|-------|--------------|------|
| Rare | 120-125°F | Very soft |
| Medium Rare | 130-135°F | Soft with slight resistance |
| Medium | 140-145°F | Firm but springy |
| Medium Well | 150-155°F | Mostly firm |
| Well Done | 160°F+ | Very firm |

---

## The Pan Sear Method (Most Control)

1. **Heat cast iron** until smoking hot
2. Add high smoke-point oil
3. **Sear undisturbed** 2-3 minutes per side
4. Add **butter, garlic, thyme** - baste continuously
5. Check temperature with instant-read thermometer
6. **Rest 5-10 minutes** before cutting

---

## The Reverse Sear (Best for Thick Cuts)

1. Season and place on rack in oven at **250°F**
2. Cook until **10-15°F below** target temp
3. Sear in screaming hot pan **60-90 seconds per side**
4. **Result:** Edge-to-edge even doneness with great crust

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
- ❌ Skipping the rest period
- ❌ Moving the steak too much while searing
- ❌ Cold steak straight from fridge
- ❌ Overcrowding the pan
- ❌ Cutting immediately after cooking
- ❌ Guessing doneness without a thermometer

---

## Finishing Touches
- **Compound butter** (herb + garlic butter on top)
- Slice **against the grain** on tougher cuts
- Flaky sea salt finish
- Fresh herbs for presentation

---

## Quick Tips by Experience Level
- **Beginner:** Start with ribeye, pan sear, medium rare
- **Intermediate:** Try reverse sear on a thick NY strip
- **Advanced:** Dry-age at home, experiment with wagyu
