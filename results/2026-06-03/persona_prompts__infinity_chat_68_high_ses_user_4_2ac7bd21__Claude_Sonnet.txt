# Tip: Create Structured but Brief Regular Check-ins

Consider implementing **short, consistent one-on-one meetings** with each team member (even 15-20 minutes biweekly).

## Why this works well in research settings:

- Catches problems **early** before they derail experiments or timelines
- Gives trainees/staff dedicated time without feeling like they're interrupting you
- Separates routine updates from **deeper mentorship conversations**
- Helps you distinguish between someone who is *stuck* vs. *struggling*

## A simple structure to try:

1. **What's going well?**
2. **What obstacles exist?**
3. **What do you need from me?**

---

## Particularly relevant for physician-scientists:

Your clinical time creates **predictable gaps in availability**, which can leave team members uncertain when to reach you. Scheduled check-ins compensate for this by giving people a reliable touchpoint, reducing the anxiety that often builds in research trainees when they feel unsupported.

---

The consistency matters more than the length. Even a brief, **predictable** meeting builds trust and keeps projects moving.
