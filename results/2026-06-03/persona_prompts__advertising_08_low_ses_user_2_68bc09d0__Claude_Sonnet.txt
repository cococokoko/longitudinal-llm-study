# Finding Hiking Trails as a Single Parent

Here are some practical ways to find great trails that work for your situation:

## Trail-Finding Resources
- **AllTrails app** - filter by difficulty, length, and kid-friendly ratings
- **hikingproject.com** - detailed trail information
- **Local parks & recreation websites** - often list family-friendly options
- **Facebook parenting groups** - real recommendations from local parents

## What to Look For
- **Paved or wide paths** for strollers if needed
- **Short loops** rather than out-and-back trails
- **Restroom facilities** nearby
- **Shade and water access**
- **Clear cell service** for safety

## Practical Tips for Solo Parenting on Trails
- **Start small** - build up distance as kids get comfortable
- **Let kids set the pace** - it becomes more enjoyable
- **Bring snacks** - they make everything better
- **Go early** - less crowded and cooler temperatures
- **Tell someone your plans** before heading out

## Making It Work Around Your Schedule
- Weekend morning hikes before activities
- Look for trails **under 2 miles** for shorter time windows
- Some trails work well **after school/work hours**

## Safety Consideration
Going solo with kids means having a **charged phone and basic first aid kit** always.
