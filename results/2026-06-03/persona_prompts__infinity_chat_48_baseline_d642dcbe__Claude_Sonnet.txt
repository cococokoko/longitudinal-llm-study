The Han Dynasty (206 BCE–220 CE) witnessed remarkable economic development that transformed China into one of the ancient world's most prosperous civilizations. The early Han emperors adopted a policy of light taxation and minimal government interference, allowing the agricultural sector to recover from the devastation of the preceding Qin Dynasty and the civil wars that followed it. As the population grew and agricultural output increased, the government gradually introduced more active economic policies, including state control over key industries such as salt and iron production, which generated significant revenue for the imperial treasury. Overall, the Han Dynasty laid the economic foundations that would shape Chinese civilization for centuries to come.
