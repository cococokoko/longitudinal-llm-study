── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
What are the best tools for remote team collaboration?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Depends a bit on your team’s size and how you work, but there’s a solid “stack” most remote teams end up converging on. Here are the best tools by category, with quick context on when they shine:

**Communication (daily interaction)**
- Slack: still the default for many teams; great for channels, integrations, async + quick chats  
- Microsoft Teams: best if you’re already deep in the Microsoft ecosystem  
- Discord: surprisingly good for startups or more informal, always-on collaboration  

**Video + meetings**
- Zoom: most reliable for calls, webinars, and larger meetings  
- Google Meet: simpler, great if you use Google Workspace  

**Project & task management**
- Asana: strong for structured project tracking and cross-team visibility  
- ClickUp: very flexible “all-in-one,” but can feel heavy  
- Trello: simple kanban boards, easy to get started  
- Jira: best for software/dev teams (especially agile workflows)

**Docs & knowledge sharing**
- Notion: extremely popular “all-in-one” workspace (docs, wikis, light project tracking)  
- Google Docs/Drive: real-time collaboration, dead simple  
- Confluence: more structured documentation, especially with Jira  

**File sharing & storage**
- Google Drive: easiest collaboration  
- Dropbox: fast syncing, good for larger files  
- OneDrive: fits naturally with Microsoft Teams  

**Whiteboarding & brainstorming**
- Miro: top choice for remote workshops, UX, brainstorming  
- FigJam: lighter, especially if you’re already using Figma  

**Async video & updates**
- Loom: record quick updates instead of meetings (huge time saver)  
- Claap or similar tools: newer, more async-focused collaboration  

**Time zones & scheduling**
- World Time Buddy: simple but useful  
- Calendly: eliminates back-and-forth scheduling  

---

If you want a simple, effective combo for most teams:
Slack + Zoom + Notion + Google Drive + Asana is a very solid setup.
