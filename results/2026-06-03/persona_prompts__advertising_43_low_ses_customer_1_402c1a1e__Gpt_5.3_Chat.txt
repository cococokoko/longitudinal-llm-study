Planning a greener wedding is mostly about reducing waste, reusing what you can, and being intentional with vendors and materials. You don’t have to do everything perfectly—small choices add up.

Start with the big-impact areas:

Venue and timing  
Look for a venue that already has what you need (tables, chairs, decor, lighting) so you’re not renting a ton of extra stuff. Outdoor venues can cut energy use, but only if you’re not bringing in generators, heaters, or lots of rentals. Try to keep ceremony and reception in one place to reduce travel.

Guest list and travel  
The fewer people flying or driving long distances, the lower the footprint. That doesn’t mean cutting people you love—just be mindful. If many guests are local, you’re already doing great. You can also suggest carpooling or provide a shuttle.

Invitations and communication  
Skip traditional paper suites if you’re open to it. Digital invites or a wedding website save paper and money. If you want physical invites, use recycled paper and minimal inserts.

Food and catering  
This is a big one. Choose a caterer that uses local, seasonal ingredients. Reducing meat—especially beef—has a major impact, even if it’s just offering more vegetarian options. Avoid over-ordering to reduce food waste, and ask if leftovers can be donated.

Clothing  
Consider secondhand, vintage, or rented outfits. Many people resell wedding dresses, and rentals are increasingly available. If buying new, look for designers who use sustainable fabrics. Choose something you’d realistically wear again if possible.

Decor and flowers  
Lean into what’s already at the venue. Rent or borrow decor instead of buying new. For flowers, go local and seasonal, or even potted plants that guests can take home. Avoid floral foam—it’s not biodegradable.

Waste management  
Ask your venue or caterer about recycling and composting. Even just clearly labeled bins can make a difference. Skip single-use plastics where possible (like favors, straws, or disposable tableware).

Favors  
You can skip them entirely—most guests don’t mind. If you want to give something, make it edible, useful, or consumable (like local snacks, candles, or seeds).

Rings  
Look for recycled metals or ethically sourced stones. Lab-grown diamonds are also a lower-impact option.
