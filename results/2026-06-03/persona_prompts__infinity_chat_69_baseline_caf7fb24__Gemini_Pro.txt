── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
What is the difference between analysis and design? Can one begin to design without analysis? Why? Be concise.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
**The Difference:**
*   **Analysis** focuses on the *problem*. It defines **what** needs to be done and **why** (gathering requirements, understanding constraints, and breaking down the current state).
*   **Design** focuses on the *solution*. It defines **how** the problem will be solved (planning the architecture, interface, and mechanics of the final product).

**Can you begin to design without analysis?**
Technically yes, but it is highly ill-advised. 

**Why?**
Designing without analysis usually leads to solving the wrong problem. If you do not fully understand the requirements, user needs, and constraints first, your design will likely be flawed, fail to meet its objective, and result in wasted time and resources. You cannot effectively build a solution without first defining the problem.
