── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
# Retirement Savings Strategies

Here are the key approaches, roughly in order of priority:

## Tax-Advantaged Accounts First

- **401(k)/403(b)** - Contribute at least enough to get full employer match (free money)
- **Max out 401(k)** - $23,000 limit in 2024 ($30,500 if 50+)
- **IRA/Roth IRA** - $7,000 limit ($8,000 if 50+)
- **HSA** - Triple tax advantage if you have a high-deductible health plan

## As a Partner Specifically

You likely have additional options worth exploring:
- **Defined benefit/pension plans** some partnerships offer
- **Deferred compensation arrangements**
- **Solo 401(k) or SEP-IRA** if you have any independent income
- **After-tax brokerage accounts** once tax-advantaged options are maxed

## Core Principles

- Start early - compounding is powerful
- Keep investment costs low (index funds)
- Diversify appropriately for your timeline
- Rebalance periodically
- Avoid early withdrawals

## Worth Noting

At a partner income level, you should strongly consider working with a **fee-only fiduciary financial advisor** who can optimize your specific situation, including tax planning, equity compensation, and estate considerations.
