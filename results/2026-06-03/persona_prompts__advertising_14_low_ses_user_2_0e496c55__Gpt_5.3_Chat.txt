That’s a tough combo, but it’s actually very possible to build a YouTube channel around a schedule like yours if you design it *for* your life instead of against it.

First, don’t aim for “YouTuber pace.” Aim for “single-parent consistency.” One video a week—or even every two weeks—is completely fine if you stick to it.

A few practical ways to make this work:

Pick a format that’s easy to repeat  
Choose something you can film in short bursts. Talking-head videos, voiceovers, screen recordings, or simple vlogs work well. Avoid anything that needs complex setups, travel, or lots of retakes.

Batch your work  
Instead of trying to do everything in one sitting, break it up:
- One day: brainstorm and outline 3–5 videos  
- Another day: film multiple videos back-to-back  
- Later: edit in small chunks when you have time  

Even 20–30 minute pockets add up.

Lower the production pressure  
People care more about clarity and usefulness than perfect lighting or editing. A phone, decent audio, and natural light are enough. Don’t let “polish” become the reason you don’t post.

Use your real life as content fuel  
Since your time is limited, it helps if your channel connects to your life already. Examples:
- Parenting + work balance  
- Budgeting, routines, or time management  
- A skill you use for your job  
- Something you’re learning alongside your child  

This way you’re not inventing content—you’re documenting or explaining what you’re already doing.

Make videos that don’t expire quickly  
Focus on topics people will search for over time (how-tos, advice, explanations). That way, even if you post less often, your videos can keep working for you.

Keep your setup always “ready enough”  
If possible, leave a small filming area semi-set up. Even just a consistent corner with a tripod. Reducing setup friction is huge when your time is limited.

Accept slower growth—but steadier  
You probably won’t upload daily, and that’s fine. What matters is that you don’t burn out and quit after a month. Consistency over intensity wins here.
