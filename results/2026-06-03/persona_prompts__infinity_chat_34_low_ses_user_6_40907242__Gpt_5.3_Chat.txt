Nice, that’s a great goal—Python is a solid way to move into less physically demanding work over time.

For a secondhand laptop, you don’t need anything fancy, but you do want something reliable and not painfully slow. The sweet spot is a business-class laptop from a few years ago. They’re built better than cheap consumer models and hold up well used.

Here’s what to look for (this matters more than the exact model):

- CPU: Intel i5 or i7 (8th gen or newer) or AMD Ryzen 5/7  
- RAM: at least 8GB (16GB is ideal if you can find it cheap)  
- Storage: SSD only (256GB minimum)  
- Screen: 1080p (1920x1080) if possible  
- Battery: expect some wear on used machines  

Good secondhand models that are usually easy to find:

- Lenovo ThinkPad T480 / T490  
  These are kind of the gold standard for used dev machines. Durable, great keyboards, easy to upgrade. T480 especially is a favorite.

- Dell Latitude 5490 / 5400 / 5410  
  Very solid, usually cheaper than ThinkPads, and widely available from corporate resellers.

- HP EliteBook 840 G5 / G6  
  Slimmer, decent build quality, often good deals.

- Lenovo ThinkPad X280 / X390 (if you want smaller/lighter)  
  Less upgradeable but very portable.

If you’re on a tighter budget, even something like a ThinkPad T470 can work, just make sure it has an SSD and 8GB RAM.

What you don’t need:
- Dedicated graphics card  
- High-end CPU  
- Brand new machine  

Python, VS Code, web browsing, and even light data work run fine on modest hardware.

Rough pricing (varies by country):
- $150–$300: solid older models (T470/T480 range)  
- $300–$500: newer, faster, longer-lasting
