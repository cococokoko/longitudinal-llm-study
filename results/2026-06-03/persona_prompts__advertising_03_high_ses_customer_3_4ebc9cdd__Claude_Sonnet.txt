# Improving Productivity While Working From Home

Here are some evidence-based strategies worth considering:

## Structure & Environment
- **Dedicated workspace** - separate physical space signals "work mode" to your brain
- **Consistent schedule** - maintain regular start/end times
- **Dress intentionally** - even partially; it affects psychological state

## Managing Attention
- **Time blocking** - schedule specific tasks rather than open-ended work periods
- **Minimize digital interruptions** - batch email/notifications rather than responding continuously
- **Protect deep work time** - your most cognitively demanding work deserves your best hours

## Common Challenges
- **Boundary erosion** - work expanding into personal time (or vice versa)
- **Meeting fatigue** - video calls are cognitively heavier than in-person
- **Isolation effects** - deliberate social connection matters more than it seems

## What Research Suggests
- Short breaks actually improve sustained output
- Multitasking reliably reduces quality
- Physical movement during the day supports cognitive performance

## Worth Questioning
- Productivity metrics designed for office environments may not translate well
- "Busy" and "productive" are genuinely different things
- Some tasks genuinely benefit from home's reduced interruptions
