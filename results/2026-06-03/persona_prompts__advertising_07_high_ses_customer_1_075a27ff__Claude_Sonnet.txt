── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here are some effective ways to reduce household energy use:

## Heating & Cooling
- Adjust thermostat settings (programmable/smart thermostats help)
- Seal air leaks around windows, doors, and ducts
- Add insulation to attic and walls
- Service HVAC systems regularly and replace filters

## Lighting
- Switch to LED bulbs (use ~75% less energy than incandescent)
- Use natural light when possible
- Install motion sensors or timers

## Appliances & Electronics
- Unplug devices when not in use (reduces "phantom load")
- Use Energy Star certified appliances
- Run dishwashers and washing machines with full loads
- Wash clothes in cold water

## Water Heating
- Lower water heater temperature to 120°F
- Fix leaky faucets
- Install low-flow showerheads
- Consider a tankless water heater

## Behavioral Changes
- Air-dry dishes and laundry when possible
- Cook efficiently (use lids on pots, right-sized burners)
- Keep refrigerator coils clean

## Bigger Investments
- Solar panels
- Heat pump systems
- Energy audit to identify your biggest opportunities

**Quick wins with the most impact:** smart thermostat, LED lighting, and sealing air leaks typically offer the best return on effort.
