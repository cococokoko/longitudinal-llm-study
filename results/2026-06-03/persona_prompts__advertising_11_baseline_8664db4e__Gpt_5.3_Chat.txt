Totally doable—and honestly, a lot of people stick with it more because it’s simpler.

Start with the basics: your body is already a full gym. Push-ups, squats, lunges, planks, glute bridges, pull-ups (if you’ve got a bar or even a sturdy tree branch). Cardio doesn’t need machines either. Brisk walking, jogging, cycling, jump rope, even fast-paced stair climbing all work. If you’re short on time, short bursts (like 20–30 second sprints with rest) are super effective.

If you want structure, a simple weekly rhythm could look like:
- 3 days strength (full-body bodyweight)
- 2–3 days cardio
- 1 lighter/mobility day (stretching, yoga, or just an easy walk)

Also underrated: daily movement. Walking more, taking stairs, carrying groceries—this adds up a lot more than people think.
