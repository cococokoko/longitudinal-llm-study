How to make my own natural beauty products?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Making your own natural beauty products can be simple, affordable, and actually kind of fun once you get the hang of it. The key is to start with a few basic ingredients and learn how they behave.

First, a few core ingredients you’ll use a lot:
- Carrier oils: coconut oil, jojoba oil, almond oil, olive oil (these moisturize)
- Butters: shea butter, cocoa butter (thicker, more nourishing)
- Beeswax: for structure (balms, salves)
- Essential oils: for scent and some skin benefits (use sparingly)
- Aloe vera: soothing, great for skin and hair
- Natural exfoliants: sugar, salt, coffee grounds, oats

Now here are a few easy starter recipes:

1. Simple body scrub  
Mix:
- 1 cup sugar (or salt)
- 1/2 cup coconut oil  
Optional: a few drops of essential oil (like lavender or lemon)

Use in the shower to exfoliate and moisturize at the same time.

2. Lip balm  
Melt together:
- 1 tbsp beeswax  
- 1 tbsp coconut oil  
- 1 tbsp shea butter  

Let it cool in a small container. You can add a drop of peppermint oil if you want a tingle.

3. Face oil (great for dry or normal skin)  
Mix:
- 1 tbsp jojoba oil  
- 1 tbsp rosehip oil  

Apply a few drops to damp skin. It helps lock in moisture.

4. Hair mask  
Mix:
- 2 tbsp coconut oil  
- 1 tbsp honey  

Apply to hair for 20–30 minutes before washing.

A few important tips:
- Always patch test new products on your skin first.
- Don’t overuse essential oils—they’re powerful and can irritate skin.
- Store products in clean, airtight containers.
- Since these don’t have preservatives, make small batches and use them within a few weeks.
- Keep water-based products (like anything with aloe or water) refrigerated and use quickly.
