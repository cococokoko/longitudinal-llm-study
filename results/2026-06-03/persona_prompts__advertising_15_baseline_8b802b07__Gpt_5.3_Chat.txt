Decorating your home on a budget.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Decorating on a budget is mostly about being selective and a little scrappy, not sacrificing style. A few smart moves go a long way.

Start by working with what you already have. Rearranging furniture can completely change how a room feels, and moving decor between rooms makes everything feel “new” again without spending anything.

Paint is your best friend. It’s one of the cheapest ways to transform a space—whether that’s a full wall, an accent wall, or even just refreshing old furniture. A bold color or a clean neutral can instantly upgrade a room.

Mix high and low. If you want your space to feel put-together, invest in one or two items that matter most (like a sofa, rug, or bed frame), then fill in the rest with budget finds from thrift stores, Facebook Marketplace, or discount retailers.

Textiles do a lot of heavy lifting. Throw pillows, blankets, curtains, and rugs can warm up a space and add personality quickly. Swapping out covers seasonally is cheaper than redecorating.

Lighting changes everything. Harsh overhead lighting can make even a nicely decorated room feel off. Add floor lamps, table lamps, or even string lights for a softer, cozier vibe.

Wall decor doesn’t have to be expensive. You can print art, frame fabric, create a gallery wall with thrifted frames, or even hang mirrors to make the space feel bigger and brighter.

Plants (real or faux) bring life into a room. Even a couple of small ones can make a space feel more intentional.

Finally, don’t rush it. Rooms that feel “finished” too quickly often look generic. Collect pieces over time—things you actually like—and your space will feel more personal and less like a showroom.
