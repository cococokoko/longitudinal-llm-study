What are the best resources for starting a plant-based diet?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Getting into a plant-based diet is way easier now than even a few years ago—there’s a ton of solid, beginner-friendly stuff out there. The “best” resources depend a bit on whether you want recipes, nutrition guidance, or just general inspiration, so here’s a mix that actually covers all that without overwhelming you.

For practical, everyday eating:
- Minimalist Baker – super approachable recipes, usually simple ingredients and quick prep. Great if you don’t want complicated cooking.
- Pick Up Limes (YouTube + website) – really balanced approach, includes nutrition tips from a dietitian, plus calm, realistic meal ideas.
- Rainbow Plant Life – slightly more advanced recipes, but amazing for learning how to make plant-based food taste genuinely rich and satisfying.

For learning the nutrition side (important so you don’t feel drained or miss nutrients):
- NutritionFacts.org (Dr. Greger) – very evidence-focused, though a bit intense; good for understanding the “why.”
- The Vegan Society website – clear basics on nutrients like B12, iron, omega-3s, etc.
- “How Not to Die” or “How Not to Diet” by Dr. Greger – dense but useful if you like science-backed explanations.

For super beginner-friendly structure:
- Forks Over Knives (website + meal planner) – especially good if you want a more whole-food, low-processed approach.
- Plant-Based on a Budget – exactly what it sounds like; very practical and affordable meals.

For motivation / real-life transition help:
- r/plantbaseddiet or r/veganrecipes (Reddit) – surprisingly helpful for seeing what normal people actually eat day to day.
- YouTube channels like Cheap Lazy Vegan – very realistic, low-effort approach that helps with consistency.

A couple of quick, honest tips so you don’t get tripped up:
- Don’t try to be perfect on day one—just start swapping meals (like breakfast or lunch) instead of flipping everything overnight.
- Learn a few “go-to” meals (like a grain bowl, a stir-fry, a pasta) and rotate them.
- Pay attention to protein, B12, iron, and omega-3s early on—it saves you from feeling low-energy later.
