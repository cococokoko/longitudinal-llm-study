── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I work in a warehouse position that requires long hours.

My girlfriend is buying a house. I plan to marry her in a year or so. I don't know if it's fair for me to pay for the house or not. What's the best solution?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This is a really common situation that couples navigate. Here are some practical things to consider:

## Key Questions to Ask Yourself

- Are you contributing to the down payment or mortgage payments?
- Will you be living there together?
- Is the house in her name only right now?

## Practical Options

**Option 1: Rent-style contribution**
- Pay a fair portion of monthly costs without being on the deed
- Protects you if things don't work out before marriage

**Option 2: Be added to the mortgage/deed**
- Makes sense if you're contributing significantly
- Gives you legal ownership stake
- More complicated if relationship ends before marriage

**Option 3: Written agreement**
- Document any financial contributions you make
- A simple cohabitation agreement protects both of you

## Things Worth Considering

- **Your income** from warehouse work - can you realistically contribute?
- **Long hours** mean you're likely contributing time and energy too
- **Marriage timeline** - one year is relatively soon
- **Talk to her openly** about expectations before assuming anything

## Honest Recommendation

**Have a direct conversation with her** about both your expectations. Many couples split costs proportionally based on income rather than 50/50.

Consulting a family law attorney briefly before marriage could also protect both of you.

What specifically feels unfair to you about the situation?
