── System message ──────────────────────────────────────────────────
You know that the customer supports extended family members and manages many household responsibilities.

── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
What is the difference between analysis and design? Can one begin to design without analysis? Why? Be concise.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
**Analysis** focuses on *what* needs to be done (understanding the problem and gathering requirements). 
**Design** focuses on *how* to do it (planning the actual solution).

Think of it like managing your household and extended family: **Analysis** is figuring out everyone's schedules, budget, and dietary restrictions. **Design** is creating the actual weekly meal plan and chore chart based on that information.

**Can you begin design without analysis?** 
Technically yes, but it is highly discouraged. 

**Why?** 
If you just start cooking a massive dinner (design) without checking who is coming or if anyone has allergies (analysis), you might make a meal half the family can't eat. In any project, skipping analysis leads to solving the wrong problem, wasted time, and costly rework.
