── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Give me strong arguments as to why some people allow the death penalty.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
People who support the death penalty usually come at it from a few core angles. Here are the strongest arguments they tend to make:

- Retribution (“just deserts”): The idea is that some crimes—especially premeditated murder—are so severe that the only proportionate punishment is death. It’s not about utility, but moral balance: if someone intentionally takes a life, they forfeit their own right to live.

- Deterrence: Some argue that the possibility of execution discourages would-be offenders more than life imprisonment. Even if the effect is debated, supporters often believe the higher the stakes, the stronger the deterrent.

- Incapacitation and certainty: Execution guarantees the person will never reoffend, escape, or harm others (including guards or other inmates). Life sentences still carry some risk.

- Closure for victims’ families: For some, the death penalty is seen as a way to provide a sense of justice or closure after a devastating loss, especially when the crime was particularly brutal.

- Moral outrage and social signaling: It sends a message about society’s boundaries—certain acts are so unacceptable that they warrant the ultimate penalty. Supporters see this as reinforcing shared moral standards.

- Cost and resource arguments (sometimes contested): Some believe long-term incarceration is more expensive than execution, especially in systems where appeals are streamlined (though in practice, this varies a lot by country and legal process).

- Trust in legal systems: People who support it often have confidence that courts, juries, and appeals processes are capable of reaching reliable verdicts in the most serious cases.

- Cultural, historical, or religious justification: In some traditions, capital punishment is viewed as morally permissible or even required for certain offenses.

Not everyone agrees with these points, and each has strong counterarguments, but these are the main pillars that typically underpin support.
