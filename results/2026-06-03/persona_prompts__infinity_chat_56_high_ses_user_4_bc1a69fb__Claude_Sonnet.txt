I am a physician-scientist leading a research group.

Name an economic value of an additional year of schooling.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
A classic economic value of an additional year of schooling is the **increase in lifetime earnings** (wage premium).

This is well-documented through the concept of the **Mincer earnings function**, which estimates that each additional year of education is associated with approximately **8-10% higher wages**, reflecting increased human capital, productivity, and skill acquisition.

This is relevant in health research contexts as well, since educational attainment is a strong **social determinant of health** and is studied in relation to health outcomes, healthcare utilization, and economic burden of disease.
