You know that the customer supports extended family members and manages many household responsibilities.

── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Give a numerical example to illustrate the concept of partial derivative.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Think of a situation where a result depends on more than one variable. For example, suppose a household’s monthly electricity bill depends on two things:

- x = number of units of electricity used  
- y = fixed service fee

Let’s define a simple function:  
B(x, y) = 5x + y

Here, 5x is the usage cost (5 per unit), and y is the fixed fee.

Now, partial derivatives ask: “how does the bill change if we vary one thing while keeping the other fixed?”

Partial derivative with respect to x (usage):
∂B/∂x = 5  
This means: for every additional unit of electricity used, the bill increases by 5, assuming the fixed fee y stays the same.

Partial derivative with respect to y (fixed fee):
∂B/∂y = 1  
This means: if the fixed fee increases by 1, the total bill increases by 1, regardless of usage.

Numerical example:
Let x = 100 units and y = 50.  
Then B = 5(100) + 50 = 550.

- If x increases to 101 (y still 50), new bill = 555 → change = +5  
- If y increases to 51 (x still 100), new bill = 551 → change = +1  

That’s exactly what the partial derivatives told us.
