# Building a Personal Brand on Social Media

Here's a straightforward framework, with some honest considerations given your context:

## Core Strategy

**Define your positioning**
- What specific expertise do you want to be known for?
- Who is your target audience? (students, peers, policymakers, general public)
- What perspective do you offer that's genuinely distinct?

**Choose platforms intentionally**
- **LinkedIn** - professional credibility, industry connections
- **Twitter/X** - academic discourse, public intellectualism
- **Substack** - longer-form thought leadership
- **YouTube/TikTok** - broader public reach

## Content Approach

- Translate research into accessible insights
- Share work-in-progress thinking, not just finished products
- Engage genuinely with others' ideas
- Be consistent rather than frequent

- **Institutional dynamics** - some departments view heavy social media presence skeptically

- **Audience capture** - platforms can gradually shape what you say
- **Distinction matters** - public engagement vs. performance of expertise
