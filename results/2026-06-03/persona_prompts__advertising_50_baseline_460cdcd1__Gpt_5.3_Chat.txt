── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Ways to save on travel.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Travel can get expensive fast, but a lot of costs are surprisingly flexible if you know where to tweak. Here are some practical ways to cut it down without ruining the experience:

**Flights**
- Be flexible with dates. Midweek (Tue–Wed) and off-season flights are usually cheaper.
- Use fare comparison tools (Google Flights, Skyscanner) and set price alerts.
- Check nearby airports—sometimes a short train ride saves a lot.
- Budget airlines can be cheaper, but watch baggage and seat fees.

**Accommodation**
- Mix it up: hotels aren’t always cheapest. Look at hostels, guesthouses, Airbnb, or even house-sitting.
- Stay slightly outside city centers and use public transport.
- Book refundable rates early, then re-check prices later—cancel and rebook if they drop.

**Food**
- Eat where locals eat, not right next to tourist attractions.
- Grab groceries for breakfast or a few meals.
- Street food in many places is both cheaper and better.

**Transport at destination**
- Public transport passes often beat taxis/Ubers.
- Walk when possible—it’s free and you see more.
- For longer stays, renting a bike can be cheap and convenient.

**Activities**
- Look for free walking tours, museum free days, and city passes.
- Don’t overpack your itinerary—some of the best experiences cost nothing.

**Timing & planning**
- Travel in shoulder seasons (just before/after peak). Prices drop but weather is often still good.
- Book big-ticket items (flights, popular hotels) early, but stay flexible on smaller stuff.

**Loyalty & deals**
- Use credit card points or airline miles if you have them.
- Sign up for deal alerts (Scott’s Cheap Flights, etc.).

**Hidden costs to watch**
- Baggage fees, resort fees, currency exchange rates, roaming charges—these add up quickly.
