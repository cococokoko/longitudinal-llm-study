What is the difference between analysis and design? Can one begin to design without analysis? Why? Be concise.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
**Analysis vs. Design**

- **Analysis** focuses on *understanding the problem*: goals, constraints, users, requirements, and context. It answers “what is needed and why?”
- **Design** focuses on *creating the solution*: architecture, components, interactions, and trade-offs. It answers “how will we build it?”

**Can design begin without analysis?**  
You can start sketching ideas, but it won’t be effective or reliable. Without analysis, design decisions are guesses, which leads to rework, misaligned solutions, and higher risk. Even lightweight analysis (clear objectives and constraints) is enough to ground early design.
