# Best Practices for Organic Gardening

Here's a straightforward guide:

## Soil Health
- **Compost regularly** - adds nutrients and improves soil structure
- **Test soil pH** - most vegetables prefer 6.0-7.0
- **Use cover crops** to prevent erosion and add nitrogen
- **Avoid tilling excessively** to preserve soil microbiome

## Pest & Weed Management
- **Companion planting** - e.g., basil near tomatoes, marigolds as borders
- **Hand-pick pests** when populations are manageable
- **Mulch heavily** to suppress weeds and retain moisture
- **Encourage beneficial insects** like ladybugs and lacewings

## Watering
- Water **deeply and infrequently** rather than shallow/daily
- **Drip irrigation** reduces disease and water waste
- Water in the **morning** to minimize evaporation

## Fertilizing
- Prioritize **compost, worm castings, and fish emulsion**
- Avoid synthetic fertilizers that disrupt soil biology

## General Tips
- **Rotate crops** annually to prevent disease buildup
- **Start with healthy seedlings** or quality seeds
- **Observe regularly** - early detection prevents bigger problems
