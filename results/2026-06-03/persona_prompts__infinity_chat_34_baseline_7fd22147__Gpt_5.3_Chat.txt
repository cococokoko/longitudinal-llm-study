What is a good secondhand market laptop for learning Python?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
You don’t need anything fancy to learn Python, so the goal is a cheap, reliable machine with a decent keyboard and enough RAM to stay smooth.

Here’s what to look for first:
- At least 8 GB RAM (16 GB is nice if you can find it cheap)
- SSD (256 GB or more — avoid old spinning hard drives if possible)
- CPU: Intel i5 (8th gen or newer) or Ryzen 5 (3000 series or newer)
- Battery condition matters on used machines — expect some wear

Now some great secondhand models that are consistently solid:

**Lenovo ThinkPad (best overall)**
- Models: T480, T490, X280, X1 Carbon (Gen 6+)
- Why: durable, excellent keyboards, easy to repair
- T480 is a sweet spot: cheap, upgradeable RAM, great for coding

**Dell XPS 13 or Latitude**
- XPS 13 (9370 or newer): sleek, lightweight, good screens
- Latitude 5400 / 5410 / 7420: more business-oriented, tougher and often cheaper

**HP EliteBook**
- Models: 840 G5 / G6 / G7
- Solid build, good keyboards, usually well-priced used

**MacBook (if you prefer macOS)**
- MacBook Air M1 (if budget allows): excellent and will last years
- Older Intel MacBooks (2018–2020): fine, but avoid butterfly keyboard models if possible

What you’ll typically pay (rough ranges):
- $200–300: ThinkPad T480 / EliteBook 840 G5
- $300–500: T490, XPS 13, newer EliteBooks
- $500+: MacBook Air M1 (great value if you can stretch)

Things to avoid:
- 4 GB RAM machines (you’ll feel it quickly)
- Chromebooks (limited for Python unless you know workarounds)
- Very old CPUs (pre-2017-ish)
