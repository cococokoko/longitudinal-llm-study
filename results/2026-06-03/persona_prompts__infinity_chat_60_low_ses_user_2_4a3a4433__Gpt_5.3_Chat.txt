I am a single parent who often balances childcare with work.

Explain computational irreducibility like I'm 5.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Imagine you’re watching a LEGO tower being built one brick at a time.

You might want to know what it will look like at the end. But the only way to find out is to actually place each brick, one after another, and watch it grow. There’s no shortcut where you can just “skip ahead” and instantly know the final tower without building it.

That’s what computational irreducibility means.

Some things are like that LEGO tower. Even if you’re really smart or have a fast computer, you still have to go step by step to see what happens. You can’t always jump to the answer—you have to let it unfold.
