If you want stuff that actually feels one-of-a-kind, a few good directions:

- Etsy — still the biggest hub for handmade and custom gifts. Best if you search very specifically (e.g., “handmade ceramic mug speckled glaze”) to avoid mass-produced items.
- Local artisan markets / craft fairs — honestly the best option if you can find one nearby. You’ll get truly unique pieces and meet the maker.
- Instagram — search hashtags like #handmadejewelry or #ceramicartist and buy directly from small creators.
- Uncommon Goods — more curated, quirky handmade-style gifts (less raw than Etsy, but easier to browse).
- Not On The High Street (UK-based but ships widely) — great for personalized handmade items.
- Museum gift shops online — surprisingly good for artistic, small-batch pieces.

If you want something really special, commissioning a piece (portrait, jewelry, woodworking, etc.) from an artist is often the most unique route.
