For international students, both the TOEFL (Test of English as a Foreign Language) and IELTS (International English Language Testing System) are widely accepted English proficiency exams, but they differ in format, scoring, and test experience. TOEFL is typically more academic in style and is conducted primarily on a computer, with integrated tasks that combine reading, listening, speaking, and writing skills; it is especially popular with universities in the United States. IELTS, on the other hand, offers both computer-based and paper-based options and includes a face-to-face speaking interview, which some candidates find more natural. Scoring also differs: TOEFL uses a 0–120 scale, while IELTS uses band scores from 1 to 9. Choosing between them often depends on personal test-taking preferences, comfort with technology, and the specific requirements of the institutions or countries to which a student is applying.
