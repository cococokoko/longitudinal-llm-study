For international students, the choice between TOEFL and IELTS largely comes down to format, testing style, and institutional preference rather than difficulty. TOEFL is primarily internet-based and leans toward an academic, multiple-choice structure with integrated tasks that combine reading, listening, and writing or speaking, making it well-suited for those comfortable with standardized testing and American English. IELTS, by contrast, offers both Academic and General versions and features a more varied format, including a face-to-face speaking interview and a mix of question types, which many test-takers find more reflective of real-world communication; it also incorporates a broader range of English accents. While both exams are widely accepted by universities worldwide, some countries or institutions may favor one over the other, so students should verify requirements first and then choose the test that best aligns with their strengths in language use and test-taking style.
