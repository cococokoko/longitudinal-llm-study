How can I make my home more energy efficient?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Great question! Here are some practical ways to make your home more energy efficient:

## Quick & Low-Cost Steps
- **Seal air leaks** around windows, doors, and outlets with weatherstripping or caulk
- **Switch to LED bulbs** throughout the home
- **Use smart power strips** to eliminate phantom energy drain from electronics
- **Adjust your thermostat** - even a few degrees makes a difference

## Appliances & Systems
- **Set water heater to 120°F** rather than higher
- **Clean refrigerator coils** and ensure good airflow around it
- **Run dishwasher and laundry** with full loads on cold/eco settings
- **Replace old appliances** with Energy Star certified models when possible

## Bigger Investments
- **Add insulation** to attic, walls, and basement
- **Upgrade windows** to double or triple pane
- **Install a programmable or smart thermostat**
- **Consider solar panels** if your situation allows

## Habits That Help
- Use natural light during the day
- Cook with lids on pots to retain heat
- Air dry dishes and laundry when possible

## Worth Considering for Your Household
Since you're managing a larger extended family household, **focusing on high-usage areas first** - like water heating, laundry, and cooking - will likely give you the biggest savings given the volume of use.
