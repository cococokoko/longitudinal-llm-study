How can I learn to cook healthy meals quickly?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
You don’t need to become a “great cook” first—you just need a few repeatable systems and 5–10 go-to meals. Speed comes from familiarity, not complexity.

Start with a simple formula you can reuse:
- Protein + vegetables + carb + sauce  
Example: chicken + broccoli + rice + soy/garlic sauce

If you always think in that structure, you can mix and match without overthinking.

Focus on fast cooking methods:
- Stir-fry (10–15 min)
- Sheet pan meals (dump everything on a tray, 20–25 min, minimal effort)
- One-pot meals (soups, lentils, pasta)
- Eggs (scrambles, omelets—super fast and nutritious)

Keep your kitchen “ready to go”:
- Buy pre-cut or frozen veggies (no shame, saves tons of time)
- Use quick proteins: eggs, canned beans, tofu, rotisserie chicken, fish
- Keep flavor boosters: garlic, olive oil, soy sauce, lemon, spices

Learn just a few “base meals” and repeat them:
- Stir-fry (change protein/veg/sauce each time)
- Grain bowl (rice or quinoa + toppings)
- Tacos/wraps
- Omelet or egg scramble
- Sheet pan chicken + veggies

Batch lightly, not intensely:
Cook enough for 2–3 meals, not a whole week. You’ll save time without getting bored.

Use a 15-minute rule:
On busy days, limit yourself to meals you know you can finish in 15 minutes. That constraint forces simplicity.

Example super-quick meals:
- Eggs + spinach + toast (10 min)
