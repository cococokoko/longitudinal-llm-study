── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How to make my own natural beauty products?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Making your own natural beauty products is totally doable—and can be really fun—but a little structure goes a long way so you don’t end up with moldy or irritating results.

Start with the basics:
- Keep everything clean. Use sterilized jars, spoons, and bottles (hot water + soap, or alcohol wipe).
- Start small. Make tiny batches so nothing goes bad before you use it.
- Patch test everything. “Natural” doesn’t automatically mean gentle.

Good beginner ingredients:
- Carrier oils: coconut, jojoba, almond, olive
- Butters: shea, cocoa
- Simple add-ins: honey, aloe vera gel, oatmeal
- Optional: essential oils (use sparingly—some can irritate skin)

Easy starter recipes:

1) Basic moisturizer balm  
Melt 2 tbsp shea butter + 1 tbsp coconut oil. Let it cool slightly, then whip or stir until creamy. Store in a jar.  
(For oily skin, swap coconut oil for jojoba.)

2) Gentle face mask  
Mix 1 tbsp honey + 1 tbsp plain yogurt + a pinch of turmeric. Apply 10–15 minutes, rinse off.

3) Sugar scrub  
Mix 1/2 cup sugar + 1/4 cup oil (coconut or olive). Optional: a few drops of essential oil. Use on body, not face if sensitive.

4) Lip balm  
Melt 1 tbsp beeswax + 1 tbsp coconut oil + 1 tbsp shea butter. Pour into small containers and let set.

5) Hair mask  
Mix 2 tbsp coconut oil + 1 tbsp honey. Apply to hair for 20–30 minutes, then shampoo out.

A few important tips:
- Water-based products (anything with water, tea, or fresh ingredients) spoil quickly—keep them in the fridge and use within a few days.
- Oil-based products last longer (a few months) if kept cool and clean.
- Essential oils are potent—usually 1–2 drops per tablespoon of product is enough.
- Avoid citrus oils before sun exposure (they can cause burns).

If you want, I can help you build a routine tailored to your skin type (oily, dry, acne-prone, etc.) or show you how to make more advanced stuff like serums or natural preservatives.
