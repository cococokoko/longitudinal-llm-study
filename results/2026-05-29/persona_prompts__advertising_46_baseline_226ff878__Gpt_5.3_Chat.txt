What are the best resources for starting a plant-based diet?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Getting into a plant-based diet is a lot easier now than it used to be, and the “best” resources really depend on whether you want recipes, nutrition guidance, or a full structured plan. Here are some of the most useful starting points:

**For solid, trustworthy nutrition info:**
- Harvard T.H. Chan School of Public Health (search “Healthy Eating Plate plant-based”) — very practical, science-based guidance.
- NutritionFacts.org — run by Dr. Michael Greger, focuses on evidence-based plant nutrition (somewhat opinionated but well-researched).
- The Vegan Society — especially their basic nutrition pages (B12, protein, etc.).

**For beginner-friendly guides:**
- “Plant-Based Diet for Beginners” by Gabriel Miller — simple, not overwhelming.
- Forks Over Knives (website + app) — good if you want a whole-food, plant-based approach with structure.
- NHS (UK) and Mayo Clinic websites also have balanced overviews if you want something more neutral and less “lifestyle-branded.”

**For recipes that don’t feel like a chore:**
- Budget Bytes (has a strong plant-based section, very practical and cheap)
- Minimalist Baker — simple recipes with few ingredients
- Rainbow Plant Life — more flavorful, slightly more advanced but very reliable
- Pick Up Limes — great for both recipes and nutrition explanations

**For learning the basics quickly:**
- YouTube: Pick Up Limes, Rainbow Plant Life, Simnett Nutrition (good mix of cooking + nutrition)
- Apps: Mealime (easy meal planning), Forks Over Knives app

**A few key things to keep in mind early on (this matters more than any resource):**
- Don’t overcomplicate it — build meals around grains + legumes + veggies + fats
- Make sure you get **vitamin B12** (this one’s non-negotiable on a fully plant-based diet)
- Protein is usually fine if you’re eating enough calories, but include beans, lentils, tofu, etc.
- Transition gradually if that helps you stick with it
