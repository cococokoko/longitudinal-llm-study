# Learning to Play Guitar

That's a great hobby to pick up! Here are some practical tips that work well with a busy, unpredictable schedule:

## Getting Started
- **Acoustic or electric?** Acoustic requires no extra equipment and is great for beginners
- **Borrow or buy used first** to see if you enjoy it before investing heavily
- **Essential gear:** Guitar, tuner (free apps work great), picks

## Practice Tips for a Busy Schedule
- **Even 10-15 minutes counts** - consistency beats long sessions
- **Keep your guitar visible** (not in a case) so you pick it up spontaneously
- **Practice during breaks** or before/after shifts when you have energy
- **Use your phone** for lessons during downtime

## Free/Low-Cost Learning Resources
- **YouTube:** Justin Guitar (highly recommended for beginners)
- **Apps:** Yousician, GuitarTuna, Fender Play
- **Tabs:** Ultimate Guitar app

## Beginner Priorities
1. Learn basic chords (G, C, D, Em, Am)
2. Practice switching between chords smoothly
3. Learn one simple song you love - keeps motivation high

## Realistic Expectations
- **Fingertip soreness is normal** for the first few weeks
- Progress may feel slow at first, then suddenly click
