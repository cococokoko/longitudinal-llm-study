── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I am a single parent who often balances childcare with work.

what I should look for when I Compare Expat Insurance?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Balancing work and childcare already takes a lot—so when you compare expat insurance, the goal is something that reduces stress, not adds to it. Here are the things that actually matter in real life:

**1. Coverage where you live (and where you travel)**
Make sure the policy fully works in your current country and any nearby places you might visit. Some plans look global but quietly exclude the U.S. or certain regions, or limit care outside your country of residence.

**2. Strong pediatric and family coverage**
Since you have a child, look closely at:
- Routine checkups, vaccinations, and developmental care  
- Emergency care for kids  
- Coverage for common childhood issues (asthma, allergies, infections)  
Some cheaper plans skimp here.

**3. Direct billing vs. pay-and-claim**
Direct billing with hospitals/clinics is a huge time and cash-flow saver. If you have to pay upfront and wait for reimbursement, it can be stressful—especially with frequent child healthcare visits.

**4. Deductibles and out-of-pocket limits**
Low premiums can hide high deductibles. Check:
- Annual deductible  
- Co-pays per visit  
- Maximum out-of-pocket limit (important for worst-case scenarios)

**5. Emergency evacuation and repatriation**
This is critical as a single parent. If something serious happens, you want:
- Medical evacuation to a better facility  
- Coverage for your child to travel with you or be cared for safely

**6. Maternity and future planning (if relevant)**
Even if you’re not planning another child now, check whether maternity is included or can be added later—many plans have waiting periods.

**7. Mental health and support services**
Look for coverage for therapy or counseling. Parenting abroad without a support network can be tough, and this often gets overlooked.

**8. Pre-existing conditions**
If you or your child have any, read this part carefully. Some insurers exclude them, others cover them after a waiting period, and some include them fully (usually at a higher cost).

**9. Customer support quality**
This matters more than you’d think. Look for:
- 24/7 helplines  
- Fast claims processing  
- Good reputation with expats (forums, reviews)

**10. Flexibility and renewability**
You want a plan that:
- Renews reliably (no surprises after a claim)  
- Lets you adjust coverage as your situation changes  

**11. Schools and visa requirements**
Some countries or international schools require specific insurance standards. Make sure your plan meets those to avoid headaches.
