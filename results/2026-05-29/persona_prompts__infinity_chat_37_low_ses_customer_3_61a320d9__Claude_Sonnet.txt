# The Little Cloud Who Couldn't Rain

High up in the sky, where the air was cool and breezy, lived a small fluffy cloud named Pip.

All the other clouds in the sky were very good at their job. Big Bernard could pour down buckets of rain that filled rivers and lakes. Gentle Grace could sprinkle soft showers that made flowers bloom. Even tiny Timothy could manage a decent drizzle on a Tuesday afternoon.

But Pip couldn't rain at all.

Every morning, Pip would puff up as big as possible, scrunch up tight, and try with all his might. But nothing came out. Not a single drop.

"Don't worry," said Big Bernard kindly. "Keep trying. Every cloud finds their rain eventually."

But Pip was worried. Down below, he could see a little farm with a garden full of drooping sunflowers. Their yellow heads hung low, and their leaves were turning brown at the edges. The farmer, an old woman named Rosa, walked among them every day looking sad.

"They need water," Pip said to himself. "And I'm a cloud. I should be able to help."

He tried again. He scrunched. He squeezed. He held his breath and thought about rivers and puddles and the ocean.

Nothing.

Pip drifted sadly over a tall mountain, feeling more useless than ever. As he floated past the cold, snowy peak, something strange happened. The mountain's chill crept right into the middle of Pip, and he began to feel very, very full.

Then, without even trying —

*Drip.*

One tiny raindrop fell.

Then another. And another.

Then suddenly, Pip burst into a wonderful, sparkling shower of rain! It wasn't the biggest rain in the world. It wasn't a thunderstorm or a downpour. It was a gentle, perfect, silvery rain.

And it fell right onto Rosa's garden.

Pip watched with delight as the sunflowers slowly lifted their heads. Their leaves perked up. Their yellow petals seemed to glow. Rosa came running outside, laughing and holding her hands out to catch the drops.

"Rain!" she cried happily. "Finally, beautiful rain!"

Big Bernard floated over and smiled warmly. "You see, Pip? You just needed to find your own way."

"But I didn't even try that time," said Pip, confused.

"Sometimes that's exactly the secret," said Bernard with a chuckle.

From that day on, Pip knew what to do. Whenever he floated near the cool mountain air, he would fill right up and shower the valley below. It wasn't the same as the other clouds, but it was perfectly, wonderfully *his*.

And every time Rosa's sunflowers turned their bright faces up toward the sky, Pip felt like the most important cloud in the whole wide world.

He had found his rain.

And it was just right.
