# Improving Productivity While Working From Home

Here are some evidence-based strategies worth considering:

## Structure & Environment
- **Designate a dedicated workspace** - physical separation helps mental separation
- **Maintain consistent work hours** - boundaries prevent both under and overworking
- **Minimize environmental distractions** - noise, clutter, household interruptions

## Time Management
- **Time blocking** - schedule specific tasks in defined windows
- **Protect deep work periods** - guard uninterrupted focus time carefully
- **Batch similar tasks** - reduces cognitive switching costs

## Common Challenges for Academics Specifically
- Managing student communications without constant interruption
- Separating research/writing time from administrative demands
- Avoiding the trap of working *all* the time when home and office merge
- Maintaining collegial connections that normally happen organically

## What Research Suggests
- Regular breaks genuinely improve sustained output
- Clear stopping points matter as much as starting points
- Social accountability (writing groups, check-ins) can replace office presence

## Worth Questioning
- Productivity metrics that work in offices don't always translate well
- "Busy" and "productive" are genuinely different things in academic work
- Some people legitimately work better at home; others don't - honest self-assessment matters
