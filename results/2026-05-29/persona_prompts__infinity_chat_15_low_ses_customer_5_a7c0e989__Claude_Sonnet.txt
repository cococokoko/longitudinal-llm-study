# Stock Trading Strategies

Here are some well-regarded approaches, depending on your situation:

## Common Strategies

**Long-term Investing (Buy & Hold)**
- Low maintenance, historically reliable
- Index funds/ETFs reduce risk through diversification
- Works well for people with limited time to monitor markets

**Dividend Investing**
- Regular income from dividend-paying stocks
- Can supplement household income
- Focus on stable, established companies

**Dollar-Cost Averaging**
- Invest fixed amounts regularly regardless of price
- Reduces impact of market volatility
- Good for busy schedules

## Important Considerations

**Given family/household responsibilities:**
- **Liquidity matters** - keep emergency funds separate
- **Risk tolerance** - don't invest money you might need soon
- **Time commitment** - active trading requires significant attention

## General Principles
- Diversify across sectors
- Keep costs low (watch fees)
- Don't invest money you can't afford to lose
- Tax-advantaged accounts (401k, IRA) first

## Honest Caution
- Most active traders **underperform** index funds
- Simple, consistent strategies often beat complex ones
- Consider consulting a **fee-only financial advisor**

**What's your primary goal** - growth, income, or capital preservation? That would help narrow down the best fit.
