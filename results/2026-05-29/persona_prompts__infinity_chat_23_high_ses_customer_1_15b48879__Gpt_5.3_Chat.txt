How long doesn it take to get over rejection from a crush

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Short answer: it varies a lot, but for most people the sharp edge fades over a few weeks, and the lingering “ugh” can take a couple of months. If the crush was intense or long-standing, it can stretch longer—sometimes comparable to getting over a short relationship.

What actually drives the timeline isn’t just time, it’s exposure and meaning:
- How often you still see or interact with them
- How much you’d built up a future in your head
- Whether you got clear closure vs. ambiguity
- Your general stress load and support system

A few things that reliably speed recovery:
- Reduce cues: mute their socials, limit contact for a bit. Your brain’s reward circuitry needs fewer reminders.
- Give it a clean story: “they’re not available/interested, and that’s enough.” Avoid endless counterfactuals.
- Replace the habit loop: the time/energy you spent thinking about them needs a new target (friends, projects, dating others).
- Move your body and sleep well—sounds basic, but it directly dampens the rumination cycle.
- Don’t rehearse the rejection. If you catch yourself replaying it, label it and redirect.

If after a couple of months you’re still preoccupied most days, or it’s affecting work/sleep, it’s worth being more deliberate (structured journaling, talking it through with someone, even a few sessions with a therapist).

It’s a pretty normal neurobiological withdrawal—dopamine/novelty plus a hit to social reward—so the discomfort is real, but it does settle.
