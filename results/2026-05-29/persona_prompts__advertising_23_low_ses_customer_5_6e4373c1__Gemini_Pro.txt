Learning to cook healthy meals in your situation shouldn't mean spending hours studying complex recipes; it should be about finding efficient, scalable shortcuts that feed a crowd and minimize cleanup. 

Here is a practical approach to learning and cooking healthy meals quickly, tailored to your busy life:

**1. Master "Formula" Meals Instead of Recipes**
Instead of trying to memorize step-by-step recipes, learn a basic formula: **Grain + Protein + Veggie + Sauce**. 
*   *Example:* Brown rice (grain) + black beans or shredded chicken (protein) + roasted broccoli (veggie) + store-bought salsa or teriyaki (sauce). 
*   Once you know this formula, you can mix and match whatever you have in the fridge without having to read a recipe book.

**2. Embrace Sheet Pan and One-Pot Meals**
When you are cooking for an extended family, the last thing you need is a mountain of dishes. 
*   **Sheet pan meals:** Toss chopped vegetables and a protein (like chicken thighs, sausage, or tofu) in olive oil, salt, pepper, and garlic powder. Roast at 400°F (200°C) for 20-30 minutes. 
*   **One-pot meals:** Soups, stews, and large pasta dishes where the pasta cooks in the sauce save time and are easy to scale up for more people.

**3. Rely on Healthy Shortcuts**
Healthy doesn't have to mean "made from scratch." Take advantage of ingredients that do the prep work for you:
*   **Frozen vegetables:** They are just as nutritious as fresh, already chopped, and won't go bad if your week gets too chaotic.
