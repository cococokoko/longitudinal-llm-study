# Mastering the Art of Cooking Steak

## Understanding the Fundamentals

### Cut Selection
- **Ribeye** - high marbling, forgiving, rich flavor
- **NY Strip** - balanced fat/lean ratio, good texture
- **Filet Mignon** - tender, lean, mild flavor
- **Porterhouse/T-bone** - two cuts in one, technique challenge

---

## Key Principles

### Temperature & Doneness
| Target | Internal Temp | Description |
|--------|--------------|-------------|
| Rare | 120-125°F | Cool red center |
| Medium Rare | 130-135°F | Warm red center |
| Medium | 140-145°F | Pink center |
| Well Done | 160°F+ | Little/no pink |

### The Maillard Reaction
- Requires **surface temp ~300°F+**
- Creates complex flavor compounds
- **Dry surface is critical** - pat steak completely dry

---

## Core Techniques

### The Reverse Sear (Recommended)
1. Season generously, rest uncovered in fridge overnight
2. Low oven (250°F) until ~10-15°F below target
3. **Ripping hot** cast iron sear, 45-60 seconds per side
4. Butter baste with aromatics

### Classic Pan Sear
- Cast iron or carbon steel
- High smoke point oil first
- Continuous flipping actually **improves** crust evenness

---

## Critical Details
- **Rest** 5-10 minutes before cutting
- Season **generously** - steaks are thick
- Room temperature matters less than commonly believed
- Quality butter + thyme + garlic basting = significant upgrade
