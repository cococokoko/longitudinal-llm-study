Here's an overview of common paths people take to reach executive positions:

## Education
- Business degrees (MBA is common)
- Specialized degrees relevant to their industry
- Executive education programs

## Career Progression
- **Working up internally** - starting in entry-level roles and getting promoted over time
- **Demonstrating consistent results** in their area of expertise
- **Taking on increasing responsibility** with each role

## Key Factors
- **Performance** - consistently delivering strong results
- **Leadership skills** - managing teams effectively
- **Networking** - building relationships within and outside their industry
- **Visibility** - getting noticed by senior decision-makers
- **Mentorship** - having sponsors who advocate for them

## Other Routes
- **Founding a company** - becoming an executive by starting the business
- **Specialist expertise** - becoming the go-to person in a critical area
- **Lateral moves** - switching companies to gain broader experience
- **Headhunting** - being recruited based on reputation

## Important Qualities Often Cited
- Strategic thinking
- Communication skills
- Resilience
- Adaptability
- Emotional intelligence

The path varies significantly by industry and company size. Some executives rise quickly, while others spend decades building their way up.
