*   **Simplicity and Speed:** We need techniques that are relatively quick and easy to execute, even when you're tired after a long shift.

Here's a breakdown of how we can approach mastering steak, keeping your situation in mind:

**1. Understanding the Basics:**

*   **Meat Selection:**
    *   **Cuts:** Start with cuts that are relatively easy to cook and forgiving. Good choices for beginners include:
        *   **Ribeye:** Flavorful and cooks well.
        *   **New York Strip:** Good balance of flavor and tenderness.
        *   **Sirloin:** More affordable, but can be a bit tougher.
        *   **Filet Mignon:** Very tender, but can lack flavor compared to other cuts.
    *   **Quality:** Buy the best quality you can afford. Look for good marbling (the white flecks of fat within the meat). This is key for flavor and tenderness.
    *   **Thickness:** Aim for steaks that are at least 1 inch thick. Thicker steaks are easier to cook properly.
*   **Temperature:**
    *   **Internal Temperature is Key:** Invest in a good meat thermometer (digital instant-read is best). This is the only way to guarantee your steak is cooked to your liking.
    *   **Doneness Guide:**
        *   **Rare:** 125-130°F (52-54°C) - Cool red center
        *   **Medium-Rare:** 130-140°F (54-60°C) - Warm red center
        *   **Medium:** 140-150°F (60-66°C) - Warm pink center
        *   **Medium-Well:** 150-160°F (66-71°C) - Slightly pink center
        *   **Well-Done:** 160°F+ (71°C+) - No pink (often dry)
*   **Seasoning:**
    *   **Salt and Pepper:** This is often all you need. Use coarse kosher salt and freshly ground black pepper.
    *   **Timing:** Season generously *at least* 30 minutes before cooking (or even longer, up to overnight in the fridge). This allows the salt to penetrate the meat and season it from the inside out.
*   **Resting:**
    *   **Crucial:** After cooking, let the steak rest for 5-10 minutes (longer for thicker steaks) before slicing. This allows the juices to redistribute, resulting in a more tender and flavorful steak.

**2. Cooking Methods (Prioritizing Speed and Simplicity):**

*   **Sear and Finish (Best for Beginners):**
    *   **Equipment:** Cast iron skillet (highly recommended), or a heavy-bottomed stainless steel pan.
    *   **Process:**
        1.  **Preheat:** Get your pan *very* hot over high heat. You want it smoking.
        2.  **Oil:** Add a high-smoke-point oil (like avocado, canola, or grapeseed) to the pan.
        3.  **Sear:** Place the steak in the hot pan. Sear for 2-3 minutes per side, until a deep brown crust forms. Don't move the steak around!
        4.  **Finish:** Reduce the heat to medium. Add butter, herbs (like rosemary or thyme), and garlic to the pan. Tilt the pan and baste the steak with the melted butter for 1-2 minutes, or until it reaches your desired internal temperature.
        5.  **Rest:** Remove the steak from the pan and let it rest for 5-10 minutes before slicing.
