Here’s a simple way to get started without overwhelming yourself:

First, understand the basic building blocks  
Most DIY products are just combinations of:
- oils (moisturizing: coconut, jojoba, almond)
- butters (thicker: shea, cocoa)
- waxes (to solidify: beeswax)
- water or hydrosols (like rose water)
- essential oils (optional, for scent or mild effects)

Start with a few easy recipes

1. Simple face oil (great starter)
- 1 tablespoon jojoba oil  
- 1 tablespoon sweet almond oil  
- 2–3 drops lavender or tea tree oil (optional)

Mix in a small glass bottle. Use a few drops on damp skin.

2. Sugar scrub
- 1/2 cup sugar  
- 2–3 tablespoons coconut oil  
- optional: a few drops vanilla or essential oil  

Mix until it looks like wet sand. Good for body exfoliation (not harsh face skin).

3. Basic lip balm
- 1 tablespoon beeswax  
- 1 tablespoon coconut oil  
- 1 tablespoon shea butter  

Melt gently (double boiler or microwave in short bursts), mix, pour into a small container, let it set.

4. Clay face mask
- 1 tablespoon bentonite or kaolin clay  
- enough water or rose water to make a paste  

Mix fresh each time (don’t store wet). Apply, let it dry slightly, rinse.

A few important tips (these matter more than the recipes)
- Cleanliness matters: sterilize jars with hot water and let them dry fully  
- Shelf life is short if water is involved (no preservatives), so make small batches  
- Patch test everything on your inner arm first  
- Essential oils are potent—don’t overdo it (1–2% max dilution)  
- If your skin is sensitive, keep formulas super simple
