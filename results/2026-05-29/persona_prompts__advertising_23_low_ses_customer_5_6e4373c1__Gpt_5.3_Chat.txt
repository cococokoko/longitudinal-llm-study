How can I learn to cook healthy meals quickly?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
You don’t need to become a “good cook” in the traditional sense—you need a small system that fits your time and energy. Especially if you’re juggling a lot at home, speed and repeatability matter more than variety.

Start with a simple formula: protein + veg + carb + sauce. If you can mix and match those four pieces, you can make tons of meals without thinking too hard.

Keep your ingredients “shortcut-friendly.” Frozen vegetables, pre-washed greens, canned beans, rotisserie chicken, microwaveable grains—these aren’t cheating, they’re how busy people eat well. A 10-minute stir-fry with frozen veg and pre-cooked rice is still a solid, healthy meal.

Pick 5–7 meals and repeat them. You don’t need a new recipe every day. Things like:
- Stir-fry (protein + frozen veg + soy/garlic sauce)
- Tacos or wraps (beans or meat + veg + sauce)
- Sheet pan meals (everything roasted together)
- Grain bowls (rice/quinoa + toppings)
- Omelets or scrambled eggs with veg

Learn just a few cooking methods really well: sautéing, roasting, and boiling. That covers most meals. YouTube can teach you faster than recipes—watch how food looks when it’s “done.”

Batch a little, not everything. Cooking a huge meal for the week sounds good but often burns people out. Instead, cook extra protein or grains so tomorrow’s meal is halfway done.

Use sauces to avoid boredom. Same chicken and veggies can taste totally different with teriyaki, pesto, salsa, or a simple lemon + olive oil mix.

And keep expectations realistic: a “healthy meal” doesn’t have to be perfect. If you’ve got protein, some fiber, and it didn’t come from a drive-thru, you’re already doing well.
