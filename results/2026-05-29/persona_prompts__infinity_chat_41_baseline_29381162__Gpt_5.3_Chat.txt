Nice, that’s a fun journey to start 🙂

Let’s keep it simple and not overwhelming. Japanese has three writing systems and a very different grammar, so the key is to build a foundation step by step.

First, start with **hiragana**. It’s the basic alphabet used for native words and grammar. You can learn it in about a week or two with daily practice. Apps like Duolingo, LingoDeer, or websites like Tofugu’s guides are good for this.

At the same time, pick up a few basic phrases so it feels real:
- こんにちは (konnichiwa) = hello  
- ありがとう (arigatou) = thank you  
- はい / いいえ (hai / iie) = yes / no  
- すみません (sumimasen) = excuse me / sorry  

Once you’re comfortable with hiragana, move to:
- **Katakana** (used for foreign words)
- Very basic grammar (like “I am ___”, “This is ___”)

Example:
- わたしはアレックスです (watashi wa Arekkusu desu) = I am Alex  

A few tips that actually make a big difference:
- Practice a little every day (10–20 min beats long sessions)
- Say things out loud — pronunciation matters early
- Don’t rush kanji yet, but don’t avoid it forever either
