I work in a warehouse position that requires long hours.

What are the best apps for tracking fitness and nutrition?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Nice—having a physically demanding job actually gives you a solid base to build on. The key is picking apps that are easy to stick with during long shifts, not ones that feel like extra work.

Here are some of the best, depending on what you care about:

For nutrition tracking:
- MyFitnessPal: still the easiest all-around. Huge food database, quick barcode scanning, decent for calorie and macro tracking. Great if you want something simple and fast during breaks.
- MacroFactor: more advanced and very smart. It adjusts your calorie targets based on your actual progress. Less guesswork, but you have to log consistently.
- Cronometer: best if you care about micronutrients (vitamins, minerals). More detailed, slightly less “grab-and-go” friendly.

For fitness tracking:
- Strong: excellent for weightlifting. Clean, quick logging, doesn’t get in your way.
- Hevy: similar to Strong but more modern and social—good if you like seeing others’ workouts for motivation.
- Fitbod: builds workouts for you based on your recovery and available equipment—great if you're tired after work and don’t want to think.

For overall activity / recovery:
- Apple Fitness / Apple Health (if you’re on iPhone): integrates everything and tracks steps, movement, etc.
- Google Fit: simple and works well on Android.
- Whoop or Oura: more advanced wearables that track recovery, sleep, and strain—useful if your job is physically draining and you’re trying not to overdo it.

A realistic setup for someone in your situation:
- MyFitnessPal (quick food logging)
- Strong or Hevy (if you lift)
- Your phone’s built-in health app (for steps/activity)

One honest tip: don’t try to track everything perfectly. With long warehouse shifts, consistency beats precision. Even logging “roughly right” meals and a few workouts per week will get you way better results than going all-in for a week and burning out.
