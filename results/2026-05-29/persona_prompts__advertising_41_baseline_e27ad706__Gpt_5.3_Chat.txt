── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How can I improve my credit score quickly?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
You can’t “hack” your score overnight, but you *can* move it noticeably within a couple of billing cycles if you hit the right levers. Focus on the things that scoring models care about most:

First, get your credit card balances down—this is the fastest win. Your “utilization” (how much of your limit you’re using) has a huge impact. If you can get each card below 30% of its limit (and ideally under 10%), you’ll often see a quick bump once the new balance reports.

Second, never miss a payment. If you’ve got anything coming up, pay at least the minimum before the due date. If you’ve missed one recently, get current ASAP—recent late payments hurt a lot more than older ones.

Third, ask for a credit limit increase (but don’t spend more). If approved, your utilization drops instantly, which can boost your score without paying anything down. Just avoid hard inquiries if your credit is borderline—some issuers do a soft pull, which is better.

Fourth, pay down or clear any small delinquent accounts or collections. Even settling them can help, though newer scoring models treat paid collections more favorably than unpaid ones.

Fifth, avoid opening a bunch of new accounts right now. Each application can ding your score a bit, and too many in a short time looks risky.

If you want a more aggressive short-term boost:
- Make multiple payments per month (so your reported balance stays low)
- Become an authorized user on someone else’s well-managed, low-balance credit card (only if it’s a clean account—no late payments)

And one often overlooked step: check your credit report (free via AnnualCreditReport.com) and dispute any errors. Fixing a mistake can sometimes give a surprisingly fast lift.
