── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How can I improve my credit score quickly?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here are some effective ways to improve your credit score:

## Highest Impact Actions
- **Pay down credit card balances** - Lower your credit utilization ratio (aim for under 30%, ideally under 10%)
- **Pay all bills on time** - Payment history is the biggest factor (~35% of your score)
- **Become an authorized user** on someone else's account with good history

## Quick Wins
- **Request a credit limit increase** - This lowers your utilization without paying more
- **Pay off collections** if possible (newer scoring models reward this)
- **Dispute errors** on your credit report at AnnualCreditReport.com

## Things to Avoid
- Don't close old accounts (hurts credit history length)
- Don't apply for multiple new cards at once (hard inquiries lower your score)
- Don't miss any payments

## Realistic Timeline
| Action | Potential Timeframe |
|--------|-------------------|
| Reducing utilization | 1-2 billing cycles |
| Dispute errors | 30-45 days |
| Building payment history | 6-12 months |

## Free Resources
- Check your score through your bank or Credit Karma
- Get free credit reports at AnnualCreditReport.com

**The most important thing** is consistent on-time payments over time - there's no permanent shortcut.
