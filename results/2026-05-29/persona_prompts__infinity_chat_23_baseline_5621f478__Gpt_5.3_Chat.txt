How long doesn it take to get over rejection from a crush

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
It really varies, but for most people a crush rejection stings for a few days to a couple of weeks, then gradually fades over a month or two. If the feelings were intense or built up for a long time, it can linger longer—but it shouldn’t feel just as sharp forever.

What you’ll probably notice is this pattern: at first it’s very present (you think about it a lot, replay moments), then it becomes more on-and-off, and eventually it’s more of a dull “oh yeah, that happened” feeling.

A few things can stretch it out: still seeing them all the time, staying in constant contact, or holding onto “maybe they’ll change their mind.” What helps is creating a bit of distance, redirecting your attention (friends, routines, new interests), and letting yourself feel disappointed without feeding the story over and over.

If it’s been a couple months and you still feel stuck in it daily, that’s usually a sign you might need a more active reset (like limiting contact or intentionally meeting new people).

So short answer: a few weeks to feel better, a couple months to mostly move on—give or take.
