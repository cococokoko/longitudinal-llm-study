# 13. Time-Saving Tools and Apps

---

## The Core Idea

The right tool disappears into your workflow. You stop thinking about it and start thinking about your work.

The wrong tool does the opposite — it demands attention, creates friction, and quietly drains the hours it promised to save.

Choose less. Choose well.

---

## What Actually Saves Time

Before downloading anything, ask three questions:

- Does this eliminate a task entirely?
- Does this reduce a recurring decision?
- Does this run without me watching it?

If the answer is no to all three, reconsider.

---

## Categories That Matter

---

### 1. Task and Project Management

**The problem:** Things fall through mental cracks.

**The fix:** One trusted system that captures everything.

**Tools worth knowing:**

**Todoist**
Clean, fast, cross-platform. Type "submit report every Friday" and it schedules itself. Works best for individuals managing personal and professional tasks simultaneously.

**Notion**
Flexible workspace combining notes, databases, and task lists. Useful when your work involves connected information — research, writing, planning — not just isolated to-dos.

**Linear**
Built for software teams. Stripped of everything unnecessary. Issues move through stages without meetings about the stages.

**Trello**
Visual board system. Drag cards from "In Progress" to "Done." Effective for teams who think spatially or manage content pipelines.

**Practical example:**
A freelance designer uses Todoist with project labels. Every client gets a label. Every morning, she filters by today's date and sees exactly what needs doing — no scanning through emails, no mental inventory.

---

### 2. Communication and Email

**The problem:** Inbox as a to-do list is a losing strategy.

**The fix:** Process email in batches. Automate the predictable.

**Tools worth knowing:**

**Superhuman**
Email client built around speed. Keyboard shortcuts for everything. Triage an inbox in minutes, not hours. Expensive but fast.

**SaneBox**
Sits on top of any email account. Learns which emails matter and quietly moves the rest into folders you check when you choose. No setup beyond connecting your account.

**Boomerang**
Schedule emails to send later. Set reminders if someone doesn't reply. Removes the mental overhead of following up.

**Calendly**
Eliminates the back-and-forth of scheduling. Share a link. The other person picks a time that works. Meeting appears on both calendars. Done.

**Text Expander / Espanso**
Type a short abbreviation and it expands into a full paragraph. Type `;intro` and get your standard client introduction email. Saves minutes per email, hours per week.

**Practical example:**
A consultant receives 80+ emails daily. She uses SaneBox to filter newsletters and low-priority threads automatically. She checks email twice — 9am and 3pm. Everything else is blocked. Response time improved. Stress dropped.

---

### 3. Automation

**The problem:** Repetitive digital tasks that require no real thinking.

**The fix:** Build it once. Let it run.

**Tools worth knowing:**

**Zapier**
Connects apps and triggers actions automatically. When a form is submitted → create a task in Todoist → send a Slack notification → add a row to a spreadsheet. No code required.

**Make (formerly Integromat)**
More powerful than Zapier for complex workflows. Steeper learning curve. Better for multi-step processes with conditional logic.

**IFTTT**
Simpler, consumer-focused automation. "If I'm tagged in a photo on Instagram, save it to Google Drive." Good for personal life automation.

**Apple Shortcuts / Windows Power Automate**
Device-level automation. Open three specific apps with one tap. Resize and rename a batch of images automatically. Send a pre-written message at a scheduled time.

**Practical example:**
A small business owner uses Zapier to connect her contact form to her CRM. Every new inquiry automatically creates a contact record, sends a welcome email, and adds a follow-up task for three days later. She used to do this manually. It took 8 minutes per lead. Now it takes zero.
