# Healthy Meal Planning on a Budget

Here are practical strategies to eat well without overspending:

## Core Principles

- **Plan before you shop** - decide meals for the week first
- **Build around whole foods** - grains, legumes, eggs, seasonal produce
- **Cook in batches** - saves time and reduces waste

## Budget-Friendly Protein Sources

| Option | Approx. Cost | Notes |
|--------|-------------|-------|
| Eggs | Low | Versatile, nutrient-dense |
| Canned beans/lentils | Very low | High fiber and protein |
| Canned tuna/salmon | Low-moderate | Omega-3 rich |
| Chicken thighs | Moderate | Cheaper than breasts |

## Smart Shopping Habits

- Buy **frozen vegetables** - equally nutritious, less waste
- Shop **store brands** over name brands
- Use **unit pricing** to compare value
- Buy **in bulk** for staples like oats, rice, and beans

## Simple Weekly Framework

- **Breakfast:** Oatmeal, eggs, or yogurt with fruit
- **Lunch:** Grain bowls or repurposed dinner leftovers
- **Dinner:** One protein + one grain + two vegetables

## Reducing Waste

- Store food properly to extend shelf life
- Use vegetable scraps for broth
- Freeze bread and meat before they expire
