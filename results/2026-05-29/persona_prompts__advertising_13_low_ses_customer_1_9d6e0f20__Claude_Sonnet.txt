# Mastering the Art of Cooking Steak

Here's a practical guide that works with your busy, unpredictable schedule.

---

## Choosing Your Cut
| Cut | Best For | Difficulty |
|-----|----------|------------|
| Ribeye | Rich flavor, forgiving | Beginner |
| NY Strip | Balance of flavor/lean | Beginner |
| Filet Mignon | Tender, mild | Intermediate |
| Flank/Skirt | Budget-friendly | Intermediate |

---

## Essential Prep (Can Do Ahead)
- **Salt generously** 45+ minutes before cooking OR right before
- Pat **completely dry** before cooking
- Room temperature helps but isn't critical
- Simple seasoning: salt, pepper, garlic powder

---

## Core Cooking Methods

### Pan Sear (Most Reliable for Home)
1. Cast iron or heavy pan **screaming hot**
2. High smoke point oil (avocado, vegetable)
3. 2-3 minutes per side
4. Butter baste with garlic/herbs at the end

### Reverse Sear (Great for Thick Cuts)
- Low oven first, then quick sear
- **More forgiving** when you're tired after a shift

---

## Temperature Guide
- Medium Rare: **130-135°F** *(most recommended)*
- Medium: **140-145°F**
- **Rest 5 minutes** before cutting

---

## Quick Tips for Busy Days
- Ribeye forgives mistakes most
- A **meat thermometer** removes all guesswork
- Prep seasoning the night before
