── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Starting from scratch (or just trying not to overspend) actually gives you a big advantage—you’re forced to be creative, and that usually ends up looking more personal than expensive décor anyway.

A few ideas that really stretch your budget:

Start with what you already have  
Before buying anything, move things around. A different layout, swapping rooms, or even just decluttering can make a place feel brand new without spending anything.

Focus on high-impact, low-cost changes  
Things like throw pillows, blankets, curtains, or a rug can completely change a room’s vibe. You don’t need many—just a couple of well-chosen pieces.

Use secondhand and discount sources  
Thrift stores, Facebook Marketplace, clearance sections, and outlet stores are goldmines. You can often find solid wood furniture or unique décor for a fraction of retail prices.

DIY where it makes sense  
Simple projects like painting furniture, framing prints, or creating your own wall art can save a lot. Even printing free artwork online and putting it in cheap frames looks surprisingly polished.

Add life with plants (real or fake)  
Plants make a space feel finished. Real ones can be inexpensive if you buy small and let them grow, but even a few good-quality faux plants can do the trick.

Stick to a simple color palette  
When everything loosely matches, even budget items look more intentional and “put together.” Pick 2–3 main colors and repeat them around the space.

Take it slow  
You don’t need to decorate everything at once. It’s actually better (and cheaper) to build your space over time so you don’t end up buying things you’ll regret later—especially while your budget is a bit tight.
