# Mastering the Art of Cooking Steak

## Choosing Your Cut
| Cut | Flavor | Tenderness | Best Method |
|-----|--------|------------|-------------|
| Ribeye | Rich, beefy | Very tender | Pan sear, grill |
| NY Strip | Bold | Moderately tender | Grill, pan sear |
| Filet Mignon | Mild | Most tender | Pan sear, broil |
| Flank/Skirt | Intense | Tougher | High heat, slice thin |
| T-Bone/Porterhouse | Complex | Tender | Grill |

---

## Before You Cook
- **Bring to room temperature** — 30–45 minutes out of the fridge
- **Pat completely dry** — moisture is the enemy of a good sear
- **Season generously** — kosher salt and black pepper at minimum
- **Season early** — ideally 45+ minutes ahead, or right before cooking

---

## Doneness Guide
| Level | Internal Temp | Description |
|-------|--------------|-------------|
| Rare | 120–125°F | Cool red center |
| Medium Rare | 130–135°F | Warm red center *(most recommended)* |
| Medium | 140–145°F | Pink center |
| Medium Well | 150–155°F | Slightly pink |
| Well Done | 160°F+ | No pink |

---

## Core Cooking Methods

### 🔥 Pan Sear (Best for thick cuts indoors)
1. Use **cast iron** — get it screaming hot
2. Add high smoke-point oil (avocado, grapeseed)
3. Sear **2–3 minutes per side** without moving
4. Add **butter, garlic, and thyme** — baste continuously
5. Rest before cutting

### 🥩 Reverse Sear (Best for thick steaks 1.5"+)
1. Season and place on rack in oven at **250°F**
2. Cook until **10–15°F below** target temp
3. Sear in ripping hot pan **60–90 seconds per side**
4. Results in **edge-to-edge** even doneness

### 🔥 Grilling
1. Create **two zones** — high heat and medium heat
2. Sear over high heat, finish over medium if needed
3. Keep lid open for thinner cuts
4. Oil the grate, not just the steak

### 💧 Sous Vide + Sear (Most precise)
1. Vacuum seal seasoned steak
2. Cook in water bath at **exact target temp** (1–4 hours)
3. Pat dry thoroughly
4. Hard sear for **crust only** — 60 seconds per side

---

## Critical Tips

### The Sear
- **Don't move the steak** — let the crust form
- Pan must be **very hot** before the steak goes in
- Press gently for full contact

### The Rest
- Rest **5–10 minutes** after cooking
- Tent loosely with foil
- Allows juices to redistribute

### The Thermometer
- Use an **instant-read thermometer** — don't guess
- Insert into the **thickest part**, away from bone

## Flavor Upgrades
- **Compound butter** — garlic, herb, blue cheese
- **Dry brine** — salt uncovered in fridge overnight
- **Finishing salt** — flaky sea salt right before serving
- **Soy sauce marinade** — adds umami depth
- **Chimichurri or béarnaise** — classic accompaniments

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
- ❌ Cooking cold steak straight from the fridge
- ❌ Wet surface before searing
- ❌ Moving the steak too much
- ❌ Cutting immediately after cooking
- ❌ Using too little heat
- ❌ Overcrowding the pan

---

**The single biggest upgrade:** A good thermometer + proper resting = consistently perfect steak every time.
