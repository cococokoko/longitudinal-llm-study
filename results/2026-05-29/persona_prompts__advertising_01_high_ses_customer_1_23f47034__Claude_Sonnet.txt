That's a great hobby to pick up! Here are some helpful tips to get started:

## Getting Started

- **Choose the right guitar** - acoustic is often recommended for beginners (no amp needed)
- **Learn basic chords first** - G, C, D, Em, Am will get you through hundreds of songs
- **Get a tuner** - a clip-on tuner or app like GuitarTuna works well

## Building Skills

- **Practice consistently** - even 15-20 minutes daily beats occasional long sessions
- **Start slow** - accuracy before speed
- **Learn to read chord charts** and basic tablature (tabs)

## Helpful Resources

- **YouTube channels** - JustinGuitar is widely considered the best free resource
- **Apps** - Yousician, Fender Play
- **JustinGuitar.com** - structured, free lessons

## Practical Tips

- **Fingertip soreness is normal** at first - calluses develop within a few weeks
- **Don't neglect your fretting hand technique** early on
- **Learn songs you actually love** - it keeps motivation high

## As a Neurosurgeon...
You might appreciate that learning guitar is genuinely **excellent for neuroplasticity** - it engages motor cortex, auditory processing, and memory simultaneously.

Your **fine motor precision** from surgery could actually give you an advantage with finger placement!
