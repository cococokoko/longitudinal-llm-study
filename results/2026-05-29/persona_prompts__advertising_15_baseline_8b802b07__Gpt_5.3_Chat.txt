Decorating your home on a budget.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
You don’t need a big budget to make a space feel intentional and stylish—you mostly need a plan and a bit of creativity.

Start by working with what you already have. Rearranging furniture alone can make a room feel brand new. Try pulling pieces away from the walls, swapping items between rooms, or removing a few things entirely to reduce clutter. A cleaner space often feels more “designed” without spending anything.

Paint is one of the cheapest ways to transform a room. Even a single accent wall or painting old furniture can have a big impact. If you can’t paint, peel-and-stick wallpaper or decals can add personality without commitment.

Textiles do a lot of heavy lifting. New pillow covers, a throw blanket, or even a rug can change the whole vibe. You don’t have to buy expensive pieces—mixing textures (like knits, linen, faux fur) makes things feel richer even if the items are affordable.

Lighting is often overlooked. Swapping out harsh bulbs for warm ones, adding a floor lamp, or using string lights can completely shift the mood. Soft lighting almost always makes a space feel more put together.

Wall decor doesn’t need to be pricey. You can frame printable art, personal photos, or even fabric. Thrift stores and flea markets are goldmines for unique frames and decor pieces you can customize.

Plants—real or fake—instantly add life. Even one or two can make a room feel fresher. If you’re on a tight budget, look for cuttings from friends or low-maintenance options.

Finally, be selective. A few well-chosen items usually look better than a lot of random decor. Budget decorating works best when you focus on small, intentional upgrades instead of trying to change everything at once.
