# 13. Time-Saving Tools and Apps

---

## The Core Principle

Technology should reduce friction, not add it. Choose tools that solve real problems in your actual life — not tools that require maintenance themselves.

---

## Household Management

**Grocery & Meal Planning**
- **AnyList** or **OurGroceries** — shared lists that update in real time across family members' phones. Someone adds milk from work, you see it before you leave the store.
- **Mealime** — generates a weekly meal plan and auto-builds the shopping list. Reduces the daily *"what's for dinner"* conversation significantly.

**Home Maintenance**
- **Centriq** — stores appliance manuals, tracks service dates, sends maintenance reminders. One place for everything related to your home's systems.
- A simple shared **Google Calendar** with recurring reminders handles most scheduling needs — filter changes, pest control, school events, medication refills.

---

## Managing Extended Family Logistics

**Communication**
- **GroupMe** or **WhatsApp groups** — separate threads for different family units. Avoids information getting buried.
- **Notion** or **Google Docs** — a shared family document for important information: emergency contacts, medical details, school schedules, caregiver instructions. Everyone accesses the same version.

**Coordinating Care**
- **CareZone** — tracks medications, appointments, and health notes for elderly or dependent family members. Useful when multiple people share caregiving responsibilities.
- **Google Shared Calendars** — create one calendar per person you manage. Overlay them to spot conflicts instantly.

---

## Financial Management

- **YNAB (You Need A Budget)** — particularly useful for households managing irregular expenses across multiple dependents. Assigns every dollar a purpose.
- **Splitwise** — tracks shared expenses with extended family. Eliminates awkward money conversations by keeping a clear, neutral record.
- **Autopay everything possible** — utilities, insurance, subscriptions. Removes dozens of small decisions and deadlines monthly.

---

## Time and Task Management

**For Daily Execution**
- **Todoist** or **TickTick** — assign tasks, set recurring reminders, delegate to family members. A task that recurs automatically never gets forgotten and never needs to be re-entered.
- **Google Keep** — quick capture for notes, lists, and reminders. Low friction, always accessible.

**For Bigger Picture Planning**
- **Notion** — build a simple family dashboard: weekly priorities, ongoing projects, important dates, reference information. One tab replaces scattered notes.
- **Time blocking in Google Calendar** — protect specific hours for focused work, errands, or rest. Visible boundaries help others respect your time.

---

## Communication Efficiency

- **Loom** — record a short video instead of writing a long explanation. Useful for coordinating with family members who need visual instructions.
- **Otter.ai** — transcribes voice memos. Speak your grocery list, to-do items, or notes while driving. Review later.
- **Canned responses in Gmail** — save templates for emails you write repeatedly: school absence notes, appointment requests, caregiver instructions.

---

## Automation Worth Setting Up Once

| Task | Tool | Time Saved |
|---|---|---|
| Bill payments | Bank autopay | 2–4 hrs/month |
| Grocery reorders | Amazon Subscribe & Save | 1–2 hrs/month |
| Appointment reminders | Google Calendar alerts | Prevents missed appointments |
| Password management | **Bitwarden** (free) | Minutes daily |
| Document storage | Google Drive or Dropbox | Eliminates paper searching |

---

## What to Avoid

- **Too many apps doing the same thing.** Pick one task manager. Pick one calendar. Consolidate.
- **Apps that require significant setup or learning time** before delivering value. If it's not useful in the first week, it won't get used.
- **Notification overload.** Turn off all non-essential notifications. Tools should surface information when you need it — not interrupt constantly.

---

## A Practical Starting Point

If you implement nothing else, start here:

1. **One shared family calendar** — Google Calendar, visible to everyone relevant
2. **One shared grocery list** — AnyList or OurGroceries
3. **Autopay for all fixed bills**
4. **One task manager** — Todoist with recurring tasks set up
