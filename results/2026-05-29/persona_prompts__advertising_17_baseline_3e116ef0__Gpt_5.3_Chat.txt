── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How can I make my home more energy efficient?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Great question—there’s a lot you can do, and some changes pay off way faster than others. Here’s a practical mix from quick wins to bigger upgrades:

**Start with the easy, high-impact stuff:**
- Switch to LED bulbs everywhere. They use ~75–80% less energy and last way longer.
- Seal air leaks. Caulk around windows/doors, add weatherstripping, seal gaps around pipes and outlets. This alone can cut heating/cooling waste a lot.
- Use a smart or programmable thermostat. Even a few degrees setback when you’re asleep or away saves energy.
- Wash clothes in cold water and air-dry when possible.

**Heating & cooling (usually the biggest energy user):**
- Replace HVAC filters regularly (every 1–3 months).
- Get an annual tune-up for your system.
- If your system is old, consider a heat pump—it’s very efficient and works for both heating and cooling.
- Add insulation in the attic and walls if it’s lacking—this is one of the highest ROI upgrades.

**Water heating:**
- Lower your water heater temp to about 120°F (49°C).
- Install low-flow showerheads and faucet aerators.
- If replacing, consider a heat pump water heater.

**Appliances & electricity use:**
- Unplug “vampire” electronics or use smart power strips.
- When upgrading appliances, choose high-efficiency models (look for Energy Star or equivalent).
- Run dishwasher and laundry with full loads.

**Windows & sunlight:**
- Use thermal curtains or blinds to keep heat in during winter and out during summer.
- If windows are very old, double/triple-pane replacements help—but sealing drafts is often cheaper and effective first.

**Bigger moves if you’re ready:**
- Solar panels (especially if you have good sun exposure and incentives).
- Home energy audit (often subsidized). It pinpoints exactly where you’re losing energy.

If you want, tell me a bit about your home (apartment vs house, climate, age of building), and I can prioritize the best upgrades for your situation.
