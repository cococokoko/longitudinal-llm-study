# Data Security Best Practices for Small Businesses

As a senior attorney, you likely appreciate both the legal and practical dimensions of this topic. Here's a structured overview:

## Foundational Controls

- **Access management** - Implement least-privilege principles; employees access only what they need
- **Strong authentication** - Multi-factor authentication (MFA) on all critical systems
- **Password policies** - Require complex passwords and use a password manager
- **Regular software updates** - Patch operating systems and applications promptly

## Data Governance

- **Data inventory** - Know what sensitive data you hold and where it lives
- **Data classification** - Distinguish confidential, internal, and public information
- **Retention policies** - Delete data you no longer need
- **Encryption** - Encrypt data at rest and in transit

## Legal & Compliance Considerations
*Particularly relevant to your practice*

- Understand applicable regulations (HIPAA, CCPA, state breach notification laws)
- Maintain written security policies
- Document vendor agreements with data security provisions
- Have an **incident response plan** before you need one

## Employee Training

- Phishing awareness
- Proper handling of client confidential information
- Clear reporting procedures for suspected breaches

## Practical Recommendations

- Regular data backups with tested restoration procedures
- Cyber liability insurance
- Periodic third-party security assessments
