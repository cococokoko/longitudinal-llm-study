I think you might be asking about **synonyms** - words that share the same or similar meanings. Here are some examples:

## Common Synonym Pairs

**Emotions/States**

**Descriptions**
- Big / Large
- Small / Tiny

**Actions**
- Begin / Start
- Help / Assist

## Interesting Cases

Many English synonyms come from **two different origins:**
- **Anglo-Saxon** (older, simpler words) + **Latin/French** (often more formal)
  - Ask / Inquire
  - Buy / Purchase
  - End / Terminate
  - Kingly / Royal / Regal

## Why Does English Have So Many Synonyms?

English borrowed heavily from:
- **Old English/Germanic**
- **French** (after the Norman Conquest)
- **Latin and Greek**

This is why English has an unusually **large vocabulary** compared to many other languages.
