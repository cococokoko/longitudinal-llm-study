Effective ways to reduce household energy use.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here are some effective strategies to reduce household energy use:

## Quick Wins
- Switch to LED lighting
- Unplug devices when not in use (phantom load)
- Use power strips with switches
- Adjust thermostat by a few degrees seasonally

## Heating & Cooling (typically largest expense)
- Seal air leaks around windows and doors
- Add insulation, particularly in attics
- Use programmable or smart thermostats
- Service HVAC systems regularly
- Use ceiling fans to supplement

## Appliances & Behavior
- Run dishwashers and laundry with full loads
- Wash clothes in cold water
- Clean refrigerator coils periodically
- Choose ENERGY STAR appliances when replacing

## Bigger Investments
- Upgrade insulation
- Replace old windows
- Heat pump systems (often significantly more efficient than traditional HVAC)
- Solar panels
- Tankless or heat pump water heaters

## Monitoring
- Home energy audits (often free through utilities)
- Smart meters to identify usage patterns
- Energy monitoring plugs for specific appliances

## Practical Priority
Focus first on **heating/cooling and water heating**, as these typically account for roughly 50-60% of home energy costs, making them the highest-impact areas.
