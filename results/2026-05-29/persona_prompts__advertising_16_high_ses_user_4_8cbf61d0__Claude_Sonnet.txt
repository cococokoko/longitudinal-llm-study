# Managing Stress and Anxiety as a Physician-Scientist

Running a research group carries a **distinctive stress profile** - combining clinical demands, grant pressures, personnel management, and publication expectations simultaneously.

## Common High-Impact Sources

- Grant deadlines and funding uncertainty
- Trainee performance and wellbeing responsibility
- Clinical duties competing with research time
- Manuscript rejections and peer review
- Administrative burden
- Imposter syndrome at leadership level

---

## Evidence-Based Strategies

### Cognitive/Behavioral
- **Compartmentalization** - designated times for different roles
- Reframing rejection as normal scientific process
- Identifying what is/isn't within your control
- Cognitive behavioral techniques (you likely know the literature)

### Structural/Practical
- Protecting non-negotiable recovery time
- Delegation with clear expectations
- Realistic goal-setting with your team
- Saying no strategically

### Physiological
- Sleep prioritization (often the first casualty)
- Exercise as a genuine intervention, not a luxury
- Monitoring caffeine/alcohol patterns

---

## Worth Considering

- **Peer support** with other group leaders - normalization matters
- Formal mentorship or coaching
- Whether your institution has **faculty wellness resources**
- Therapy, particularly if symptoms are persistent
