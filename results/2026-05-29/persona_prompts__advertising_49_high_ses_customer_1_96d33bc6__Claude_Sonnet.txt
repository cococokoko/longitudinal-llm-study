Here are some effective ways to improve your home WiFi:

## Router Placement
- Place it in a **central location** in your home
- Keep it **elevated** and away from walls/floors
- Avoid interference from microwaves, cordless phones, and baby monitors

## Hardware Upgrades
- **Upgrade your router** if it's more than 3-4 years old
- Consider a **mesh network system** (Eero, Google Nest, Orbi) for larger homes
- Add a **WiFi extender/repeater** for dead zones

## Settings Optimization
- Use the **5GHz band** for faster speeds (shorter range)
- Use the **2.4GHz band** for better range/penetration
- **Change your WiFi channel** to avoid neighbor interference (channels 1, 6, or 11 for 2.4GHz)
- Update your **router firmware**

## Reduce Interference
- Keep router away from other electronics
- Use a **wired connection (ethernet)** for devices that need reliability

## Security & Performance
- Use **WPA3 or WPA2** security
- Limit devices on the network
- **Restart your router** periodically

## Check Your Plan
- Verify you're getting the speeds you're **paying for**
- Consider upgrading your **internet plan** if needed
