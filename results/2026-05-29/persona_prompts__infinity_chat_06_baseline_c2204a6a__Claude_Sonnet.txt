── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
That's a reasonable question. Here's a balanced look at it:

## Generally, pacing is fine for most people

- Walking is low-impact exercise
- Your joints are designed to handle regular walking
- It's actually **better for joints** than sitting still for long periods

## Potential concerns worth knowing

**The repetitive pattern aspect:**
- Very tight, repetitive turns could put slightly more stress on ankles and knees than varied walking
- Constant same-direction turning might create minor imbalances over time

**Floor surface matters:**
- Hard floors with no cushioning can be harder on joints than varied terrain
- Wearing supportive shoes helps

**If you already have joint issues:**
- Conditions like arthritis might make any repetitive movement worth discussing with a doctor

## Honest perspective

- For most healthy people, pacing is **not a significant concern**
- The benefits of increasing daily steps likely outweigh minor risks
- It's probably better than the alternative of staying sedentary

## Simple things that help

- Wear supportive footwear
- Vary your direction occasionally
- Mix in outdoor walking when possible for more natural movement patterns
