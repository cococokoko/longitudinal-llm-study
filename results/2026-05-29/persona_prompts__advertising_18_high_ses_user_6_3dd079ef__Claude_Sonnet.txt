# Stock Market Investment Strategies

As a consulting professional, you likely appreciate frameworks and evidence-based approaches. Here are the key strategies worth considering:

## Foundational Principles
- **Define your time horizon** before selecting any strategy
- **Understand your risk tolerance** honestly, not aspirationally
- **Consider tax implications** of each approach

## Core Strategies

### Passive/Index Investing
- Low-cost index funds (S&P 500, total market)
- Consistently outperforms most active managers long-term
- Minimizes fees and behavioral mistakes

### Active Strategies
- Individual stock selection based on fundamental analysis
- Requires significant time and expertise
- Higher risk, inconsistent outperformance

### Factor Investing
- Value, momentum, quality, low-volatility tilts
- Evidence-based middle ground between passive and active

## Key Principles Supported by Evidence
- **Diversification** remains the only "free lunch"
- **Cost minimization** significantly impacts long-term returns
- **Consistent contributions** (dollar-cost averaging) reduce timing risk
- **Rebalancing** maintains intended risk exposure

## Honest Considerations
- Most professional fund managers **underperform** index funds over 15+ years
- Behavioral discipline often matters more than strategy selection
- Your income likely makes **tax-advantaged accounts** especially valuable

## Recommendation
Consider consulting a **fee-only fiduciary financial advisor** for personalized guidance.
