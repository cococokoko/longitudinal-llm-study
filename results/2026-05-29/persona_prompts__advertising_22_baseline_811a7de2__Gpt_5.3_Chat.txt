── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
What are the best tools for remote team collaboration?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
It really depends on what kind of collaboration you mean (chat, project tracking, docs, design, etc.), but here are the most solid, widely used tools by category:

**Team communication**
- Slack: still the go-to for structured chat, channels, integrations
- Microsoft Teams: best if you’re already in the Microsoft ecosystem
- Discord: surprisingly strong for informal or startup-style teams

**Project & task management**
- Asana: great balance of simplicity and structure
- ClickUp: very powerful, lots of customization (can feel heavy)
- Trello: simple kanban boards, easy to start with
- Jira: best for software/dev teams, but overkill for others

**Docs & knowledge sharing**
- Notion: all-in-one workspace (docs, wikis, light project tracking)
- Google Workspace: Docs, Sheets, Drive—still unbeatable for real-time collaboration
- Confluence: more structured documentation, often paired with Jira

**Video meetings**
- Zoom: most reliable for calls and webinars
- Google Meet: simple, works well inside Google ecosystem
- Teams: again, strong if you're using Microsoft

**File sharing & storage**
- Google Drive: easy, collaborative
- Dropbox: fast syncing, clean interface
- OneDrive: best with Microsoft stack

**Whiteboarding & brainstorming**
- Miro: very flexible, great for workshops
- FigJam: simpler, especially good if you use Figma
- MURAL: similar to Miro, slightly more enterprise-focused

**Design & async collaboration**
- Figma: real-time design + comments, great for product teams
- Loom: async video updates, super useful to reduce meetings

If you want a “clean stack” that works for most teams without overcomplication:
- Slack + Notion + Google Drive + Zoom + Asana
