# Stock Trading Strategies

Here's an honest overview of common approaches:

## Long-Term Strategies (Generally Most Reliable)
- **Buy and Hold** - Purchase diversified index funds, hold for years
- **Dollar-Cost Averaging** - Invest fixed amounts regularly regardless of price
- **Dividend Investing** - Focus on stocks with consistent dividend payments

## Medium-Term Strategies
- **Swing Trading** - Hold positions days to weeks based on price momentum
- **Value Investing** - Buy undervalued stocks (Buffett-style)
- **Growth Investing** - Focus on companies with strong earnings growth

## Short-Term/Active Trading
- **Day Trading** - Buy and sell within the same day
- **Momentum Trading** - Follow strong price trends
- **Options Strategies** - Hedging or leveraged positions

---

## Honest Considerations

**What research actually shows:**
- Most active traders **underperform** index funds
- ~70-80% of day traders lose money
- Fees and taxes significantly reduce returns
- Timing the market consistently is extremely difficult

**What tends to work:**
- Low-cost index funds (S&P 500)
- Long time horizons
- Controlling emotions
- Minimizing fees

---

## Key Factors to Consider
- Your **risk tolerance**
- **Time horizon**
- Available **capital**
- Tax implications
