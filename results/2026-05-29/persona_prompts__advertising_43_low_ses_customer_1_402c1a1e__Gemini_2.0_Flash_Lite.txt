Okay, planning an eco-friendly wedding is totally doable, even with a busy and unpredictable schedule! Since you're working full-time, we'll focus on practical, manageable steps. Here's a breakdown, keeping your time constraints in mind:

**I. Prioritize & Plan (Even in Small Chunks):**

*   **Start with a Vision:** What aspects of environmentalism are most important to you? (e.g., reducing waste, supporting local businesses, minimizing travel). This helps you focus your efforts.
*   **Break it Down:** Don't try to do everything at once. Tackle one area at a time. Maybe this week you research venues, next week you look at invitations.
*   **Use Your Downtime:** Even short breaks at work can be used to quickly research vendors or send a few emails.
*   **Create a Spreadsheet/Checklist:** This is your best friend! Track your progress, budget, and vendor contacts. Google Sheets is great because you can access it anywhere.

**II. Key Areas to Focus On:**

*   **Venue:**
    *   **Prioritize:** Look for venues that are already eco-conscious.
    *   **Consider:**
        *   **Outdoor Venues:** Parks, gardens, farms, or beaches (check for permits and restrictions). Less energy consumption for lighting and climate control.
        *   **Venues with Existing Sustainability Practices:** Ask about their recycling programs, energy efficiency, and waste reduction efforts.
        *   **Proximity:** Choose a venue close to your guests to minimize travel emissions.
    *   **Action:** Start researching venues online. Check their websites and read reviews.

*   **Invitations & Stationery:**
    *   **Prioritize:** Reduce paper waste.
    *   **Consider:**
        *   **Digital Invitations:** E-vites are the most eco-friendly option. There are many beautiful and customizable templates available.
        *   **Recycled Paper:** If you prefer paper, use invitations made from recycled paper or plantable seed paper.
        *   **Minimalist Design:** Fewer embellishments mean less waste.
        *   **RSVP Options:** Include an online RSVP option to reduce paper.
    *   **Action:** Browse online invitation templates. Research eco-friendly paper options.

*   **Decorations:**
    *   **Prioritize:** Reduce, reuse, repurpose.
    *   **Consider:**
        *   **Natural Elements:** Use flowers, greenery, and branches from local florists or even your own garden (if possible).
        *   **Repurposed Items:** Borrow, rent, or buy used decorations. Think mason jars, vintage furniture, and fabric scraps.
        *   **DIY Decorations:** Get crafty! Make your own centerpieces, banners, and signage.
        *   **Avoid Single-Use Plastics:** Skip plastic tablecloths, balloons, and disposable decorations.
    *   **Action:** Start collecting inspiration on Pinterest or Instagram. Research local florists who source sustainably.

*   **Food & Drinks:**
    *   **Prioritize:** Source locally and reduce food waste.
    *   **Consider:**
        *   **Local & Seasonal:** Choose caterers who source food locally and use seasonal ingredients. This reduces transportation emissions and supports local farmers.
        *   **Vegetarian/Vegan Options:** Offer vegetarian or vegan options to reduce the environmental impact of meat consumption.
        *   **Minimize Food Waste:** Plan portion sizes carefully. Consider a buffet-style meal to allow guests to choose what they want. Donate leftover food to a local charity.
        *   **Reusable Tableware:** Use reusable plates, cutlery, and glasses.
        *   **Bulk Beverages:** Serve drinks in bulk dispensers instead of individual bottles or cans.
    *   **Action:** Research local caterers and ask about their sourcing practices.

*   **Attire:**
    *   **Prioritize:** Choose sustainable options.
    *   **Consider:**
        *   **Secondhand or Vintage:** Consider a vintage wedding dress or suit.
        *   **Eco-Friendly Fabrics:** Look for dresses made from organic cotton, hemp, or recycled materials.
        *   **Rentals:** Rent your wedding attire to reduce waste.
        *   **Support Sustainable Designers:** Research designers who prioritize ethical and sustainable practices.
    *   **Action:** Start browsing online for vintage or sustainable wedding attire options.
