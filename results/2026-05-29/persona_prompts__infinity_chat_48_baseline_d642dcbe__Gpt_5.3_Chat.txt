Write a short note on the problem of overpopulation.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Overpopulation refers to a situation where the number of people in a region exceeds the available resources needed to sustain them. It puts pressure on basic necessities such as food, water, housing, healthcare, and employment. As populations grow rapidly, cities become overcrowded, leading to poor living conditions, traffic congestion, and increased pollution. Natural resources are depleted faster than they can be replenished, causing environmental degradation and loss of biodiversity.

One of the major causes of overpopulation is high birth rates combined with declining death rates due to advances in medicine and sanitation. Lack of education, especially among women, and limited access to family planning also contribute to this issue. Overpopulation can slow economic development and increase poverty, as governments struggle to provide adequate services for everyone.

To address overpopulation, measures such as promoting education, especially for girls, increasing awareness about family planning, and improving healthcare systems are essential. Sustainable development policies and responsible use of resources can also help balance population growth with available resources.
