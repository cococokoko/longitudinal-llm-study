── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How can I decorate my apartment on a minimalist budget?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here are some practical ways to decorate minimally and affordably:

## Start With What You Have
- Rearrange existing furniture for a fresh feel
- Declutter first - less stuff often looks better
- Repurpose items you already own

## Low-Cost Additions
- **Plants** - even small ones add life and warmth
- **Thrift stores** - frames, vases, art, furniture
- **Dollar stores** - candles, small decorative items
- **Facebook Marketplace/Craigslist** - quality pieces cheap

## DIY Options
- Print free art from sites like Unsplash or museum collections
- Make your own gallery wall with mismatched frames
- Paint an accent wall (one can of paint is inexpensive)
- Use washi tape for temporary wall designs

## Smart Strategies
- Focus on **one or two rooms** rather than spreading thin
- Stick to a **simple color palette** (2-3 colors)
- Good lighting makes a huge difference - swap bulbs or add a lamp
- Textiles like throw pillows or a blanket add coziness cheaply

## Free Resources
- Library book sales for coffee table books
- Nature items like branches, stones, pinecones
- Community "free" groups online

## Key Minimalist Principle
**Fewer, better things** usually looks more intentional than lots of cheap items everywhere.
