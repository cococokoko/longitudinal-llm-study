That's a big question that people have debated for centuries! There's no single, universally agreed-upon answer. Here's a breakdown of the main perspectives:

*   **Moral Objectivism:** This view holds that moral truths exist independently of individual opinions or cultural beliefs. Think of it like math: 2 + 2 = 4, regardless of what anyone thinks. Objectivists believe that certain actions are inherently right or wrong, and we can discover these truths through reason, intuition, or divine revelation.

*   **Moral Subjectivism:** This perspective argues that moral judgments are based on individual feelings, opinions, or preferences. What's right for one person might not be right for another, and there's no objective standard to judge between them.

*   **Moral Relativism:** This is a broader view that says moral truths are relative to a particular culture, society, or historical period. What's considered moral in one culture might be immoral in another.

**Here's how to think about it:**

*   **Objectivism** is like saying there's a "right" answer to a moral question, even if we don't always know what it is.
*   **Subjectivism** is like saying morality is in the eye of the beholder.
*   **Relativism** is like saying morality is determined by the society you live in.

**Important Considerations:**

*   **Complexity:** Moral issues are often complex, and it's hard to find simple answers.
*   **Cultural Differences:** Different cultures have different moral codes, which can make it difficult to find universal truths.
*   **Personal Beliefs:** Your own beliefs and experiences will likely influence your view on this topic.

**In Conclusion:**

There's no easy answer to whether morality is objective or subjective. It's a complex philosophical question with different perspectives.
