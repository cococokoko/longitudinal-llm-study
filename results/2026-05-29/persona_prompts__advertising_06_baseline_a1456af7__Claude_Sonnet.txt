# Mastering Digital Photography

## Core Fundamentals

### The Exposure Triangle
| Element | Controls | Effect |
|---------|----------|--------|
| **Aperture** (f-stop) | Light & depth of field | f/1.8 = blurry background; f/16 = sharp throughout |
| **Shutter Speed** | Motion & light | 1/1000s = freeze action; 1/30s = motion blur |
| **ISO** | Sensor sensitivity | Low ISO = clean; High ISO = grainy/noisy |

---

## Key Concepts to Master

### 📷 Aperture
- **Wide aperture** (f/1.4–f/2.8) → portraits, low light
- **Narrow aperture** (f/8–f/16) → landscapes, architecture
- Controls **bokeh** (background blur)

### ⏱ Shutter Speed
- **Fast** (1/500s+) → sports, wildlife, children
- **Slow** (1/30s and below) → light trails, waterfalls, stars
- Use a **tripod** for anything below 1/60s

### 🔆 ISO
- Start at **ISO 100–400** outdoors
- **ISO 800–3200** for indoor/low light
- Modern cameras handle high ISO well

---

## Composition Rules

- **Rule of Thirds** – place subjects off-center
- **Leading Lines** – draw the eye into the frame
- **Framing** – use natural frames (doorways, trees)
- **Negative Space** – simplify your background
- **Golden Hour** – shoot 1 hour after sunrise/before sunset

---

## Shooting Modes

| Mode | Best For |
|------|----------|
| **Manual (M)** | Full creative control |
| **Aperture Priority (Av/A)** | Portraits, landscapes |
| **Shutter Priority (Tv/S)** | Action, sports |
| **Program (P)** | Quick shooting |

---

## Post-Processing Essentials

### Recommended Software
- **Adobe Lightroom** – best for editing & organizing
- **Photoshop** – advanced retouching
- **Capture One** – professional color grading
- **Darktable** – free alternative

### Basic Editing Workflow
1. **Exposure** – correct brightness first
2. **White Balance** – fix color temperature
3. **Contrast** – add depth
4. **Highlights/Shadows** – recover detail
5. **Clarity & Sharpness** – add definition
6. **Noise Reduction** – clean up high-ISO shots
7. **Crop & Straighten** – refine composition

---

## Gear Essentials

### Camera Types
- **DSLR** – versatile, great battery life
- **Mirrorless** – compact, modern technology
- **Point & Shoot** – travel/casual use

### Must-Have Accessories
- Extra batteries & memory cards
- **Tripod** – essential for long exposures
- **UV/Polarizing filters**
- Camera bag with padding
- Lens cleaning kit

### Lens Priorities
1. **50mm f/1.8** – affordable, sharp, great portraits
2. **Wide angle** (16–35mm) – landscapes, interiors
3. **Telephoto** (70–200mm) – wildlife, sports
4. **Macro** – close-up/detail photography
