── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
What is the biggest controversy regarding morality?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
There are several major controversies in moral philosophy. Here are some of the most significant:

## Foundational Debates

- **Moral realism vs. anti-realism** - whether objective moral facts actually exist
- **Where morality comes from** - reason, emotion, evolution, religion, social agreement
- **Moral relativism** - whether morality is universal or culturally dependent

## Practical Controversies

- Abortion, euthanasia, capital punishment
- Animal rights and moral consideration
- Obligations to distant others (global poverty)
- AI and emerging technology ethics

## Theoretical Conflicts

- **Consequentialism vs. deontology** - do outcomes justify means, or are some acts inherently wrong?
- **Individual rights vs. collective welfare**
- **Free will and moral responsibility** - can people truly be blamed if behavior is determined?

## Perhaps the Deepest Controversy

Many philosophers consider the **is-ought problem** (Hume's guillotine) most fundamental - the question of whether you can derive moral obligations from factual observations at all. This underlies almost every other moral debate.

The **existence of objective morality** itself is arguably the biggest single controversy, since it affects whether moral disagreements even have correct answers.
