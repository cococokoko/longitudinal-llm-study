── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
What's wrong with capitalism? State in-depth reasons why capitalism is inefficient and harmful.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here are substantive critiques of capitalism that economists, philosophers, and social scientists have developed:

## Economic Inefficiencies

**Market Failures**
- Public goods are systematically underprovided since private actors can't capture full returns
- Negative externalities (pollution, health costs) aren't priced into transactions, creating real resource misallocation
- Information asymmetries lead to adverse selection and moral hazard problems
- Natural monopolies tend toward inefficient pricing without regulation

**Business Cycles**
- Inherent boom-bust cycles create periodic mass unemployment and destroyed productive capacity
- Coordination failures mean individual rational decisions produce collectively irrational outcomes
- Financialization can decouple capital allocation from productive investment

## Social Harms

**Inequality**
- Capital returns historically outpace wage growth (Piketty's r>g argument)
- Wealth concentration compounds over generations
- Political power follows economic power, undermining democratic equality

**Labor Issues**
- Structural unemployment serves as a wage-suppression mechanism
- Workers bear disproportionate risk compared to capital holders
- Commodification of labor treats people instrumentally

**Short-termism**
- Quarterly profit pressures discourage long-term investment
- Environmental sustainability conflicts with profit maximization

## Philosophical Critiques

- Reduces human relationships to transactional terms
- Creates artificial scarcity alongside abundance
- Democratic accountability gaps in major economic decisions

## Worth Noting

These critiques vary significantly in strength and many have **contested counterarguments**. Would you like me to also present defenses of capitalism or examine specific critiques more deeply?
