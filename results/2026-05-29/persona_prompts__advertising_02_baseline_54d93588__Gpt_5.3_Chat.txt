Best skin routine for dry skin.

If your skin leans dry, the goal is simple: gently clean, then layer in hydration and seal it in so it doesn’t evaporate.

Here’s a solid, no-fuss routine that works for most people:

Morning:
- Cleanser: Use a gentle, non-foaming or creamy cleanser. Avoid anything that leaves your skin “squeaky.”
- Hydration layer: Apply a hydrating toner or essence (look for glycerin, hyaluronic acid, or aloe).
- Serum (optional but helpful): Hyaluronic acid or a simple hydrating serum.
- Moisturizer: Go for a richer cream with ingredients like ceramides, shea butter, or squalane.
- Sunscreen: SPF 30+ every day. Look for moisturizing formulas (often labeled “hydrating” or “for dry skin”).

Night:
- Cleanser: Same gentle cleanser. If you wear makeup, remove it first with a cleansing balm or oil.
- Hydration layer: Again, something like a hydrating toner or essence.
- Treatment (optional): If you want, use something like niacinamide (for barrier support) or a very gentle retinol 1–2x/week (only if your skin tolerates it).
- Moisturizer: Use a thicker cream at night.
- Seal (optional): If you’re very dry, finish with a thin layer of an occlusive (like petrolatum/Vaseline or a balm) to lock everything in.

Extra tips that actually make a big difference:
- Apply products to slightly damp skin to trap more moisture.
- Avoid hot water on your face—it dries you out more than you think.
- Don’t over-exfoliate. Once a week max, and use gentle exfoliants.
- Humidifier at night can help if your environment is dry.
- Ingredients to look for: ceramides, hyaluronic acid, glycerin, panthenol, squalane.
- Ingredients to be careful with: high alcohol content, strong fragrances, harsh exfoliating acids.
