# Marathon Tips for Busy Executives

Here are practical tips to help you train and race successfully:

## Training
- **Build mileage gradually** - increase weekly distance by no more than 10% per week
- **Follow a structured plan** - 16-20 weeks is typical for most runners
- **Include long runs** - weekend long runs build your aerobic base
- **Don't skip rest days** - recovery is where adaptation happens
- **Cross-train** - cycling or swimming reduces injury risk

## Fitting Training Into a Demanding Schedule
- **Morning runs** often work best before the day's demands take over
- **Treat runs like meetings** - block calendar time and protect it
- **Use travel strategically** - hotel gyms and new cities can be great for runs
- **Communicate with your team** about your training commitments

## Race Day
- **Nothing new on race day** - no new shoes, gear, or food
- **Start conservatively** - going out too fast is the most common mistake
- **Stay hydrated** - drink at aid stations even before you feel thirsty
- **Break the race into segments** mentally

## Recovery
- **Prioritize sleep** - arguably your most important training tool
- **Fuel properly** - don't undereat during heavy training weeks

## Mindset
- **Respect the distance** - the marathon demands consistency over intensity
