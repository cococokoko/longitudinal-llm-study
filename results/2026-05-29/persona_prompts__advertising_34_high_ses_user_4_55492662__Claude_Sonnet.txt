# Effective Online Teaching Techniques for Physician-Scientists

Given your background leading a research group, here are evidence-based approaches particularly relevant to your context:

## Core Pedagogical Principles

### Cognitive Load Management
- **Chunk content** into 6-10 minute segments (optimal for attention retention)
- Use **worked examples** before problem-solving tasks
- Avoid redundant text on slides while speaking simultaneously

### Active Learning Strategies
- **Polling/quizzes** (Mentimeter, Poll Everywhere) during sessions
- **Case-based discussions** - particularly powerful for clinical/scientific reasoning
- **Think-pair-share** adapted for breakout rooms
- Retrieval practice over passive re-reading

## Technical Execution

| Element | Recommendation |
|---------|---------------|
| Video | Face visible, good lighting, stable camera |
| Audio | External microphone significantly improves engagement |
| Pacing | Slower than in-person; build in pauses |
| Recording | Allow asynchronous review |

## Specific to Research Group Teaching
- **Journal clubs** work well in hybrid formats
- **Flipped classroom** - assign papers/videos beforehand, use live time for discussion
- Screen-sharing for data analysis walkthroughs
- Collaborative annotation tools (Hypothesis, Google Docs)

## Common Pitfalls to Avoid
- Lecturing without interaction for >15 minutes
- Neglecting to check comprehension
- Underestimating preparation time (typically 3-4x in-person)

## Recommended Platforms
- **Zoom** with breakout rooms for small groups
- **Canvas/Moodle** for structured courses
- **Slack/Teams** for ongoing group discussion
