Great question! Here's a practical approach given your situation:

## Start Simple and Realistic

**Given your long hours, keep sessions short:**
- Even 20-30 minutes daily beats occasional long sessions

## Recommended Starting Path

**1. Choose one beginner-friendly language first**
- Python is widely recommended for beginners

**2. Free resources to start**
- freeCodeCamp.org

- Codecademy

**3. Build progression**
- Variables and data types
- Functions
- Loops and conditionals
- Then small projects

## Practical Tips

- **Write code, don't just watch tutorials** - this is the biggest mistake beginners make
- Start with tiny projects that interest you
- Use your phone during breaks with apps like Mimo or Grasshopper
- Join communities like r/learnprogramming for support

## Honest Expectations

- Expect **6-12 months** of consistent practice before feeling competent
- It gets frustrating - that's normal
- Small daily progress compounds significantly
