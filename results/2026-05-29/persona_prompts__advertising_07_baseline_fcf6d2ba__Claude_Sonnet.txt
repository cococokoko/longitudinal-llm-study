# Effective Ways to Reduce Household Energy Use

## Heating & Cooling (typically largest expense)
- **Set thermostats strategically** – lower in winter at night, higher in summer when away
- **Seal air leaks** around windows, doors, and outlets
- **Add insulation** to attics, walls, and basements
- **Use ceiling fans** to circulate air more efficiently
- **Replace HVAC filters** regularly (every 1-3 months)

## Appliances & Electronics
- **Unplug devices** when not in use (phantom/standby power adds up)
- **Use power strips** to easily cut power to multiple devices
- **Run dishwashers and washing machines** with full loads only
- **Wash clothes in cold water** when possible
- **Choose ENERGY STAR appliances** when replacing old ones

## Lighting
- **Switch to LED bulbs** – use 75% less energy than incandescent
- **Use natural light** when available
- **Install motion sensors or timers**

## Water Heating
- **Lower water heater temperature** to 120°F
- **Fix leaky faucets** promptly
- **Take shorter showers**
- **Insulate hot water pipes**

## Behavioral Changes
- **Monitor your usage** with a smart meter or energy monitor
- **Air-dry dishes and laundry** when possible
- **Cook efficiently** using lids on pots, right-sized burners
