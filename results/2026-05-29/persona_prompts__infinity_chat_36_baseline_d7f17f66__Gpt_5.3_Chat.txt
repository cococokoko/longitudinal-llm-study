── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here are some familiar examples where users can choose or customize a default landing page (or homepage/dashboard):

- Google (google.com): You can set it so your browser opens directly to Google Search, and within Google services like Gmail or Workspace, users can land on specific views (Inbox, Chat, etc.).

- YouTube: Users can land on the Home feed, Subscriptions tab, or Library depending on preferences or bookmarks.

- Reddit: Lets you choose between Home (personalized feed), Popular, or specific subreddits as your starting point.

- Twitter / X: You can default to “For You” or “Following” timeline.

- LinkedIn: Typically opens to the main feed, but users often navigate directly to Jobs, Messaging, or Notifications as their de facto landing page.

- GitHub: After login, users land on a personalized dashboard, but many go straight to repositories, issues, or pull requests via saved URLs.

- Notion: Lets users set a default page (like a dashboard or workspace page) when opening the app.

- Slack: Opens to the last visited channel, but users can treat a specific channel or DM as their “home.”

- Jira / Confluence (Atlassian): Users can configure dashboards or spaces as their landing page.

- Salesforce: Allows users to customize their default home tab or dashboard view.

- WordPress (admin): You can set the dashboard or specific pages (like posts or stats) as your default admin landing.

- Shopify: Merchants land on their admin dashboard, but can jump directly into orders, products, or analytics as their main entry point.

- Banking apps (like Chase, Revolut): Often let users choose whether they land on account overview, transactions, or cards.

- News sites (like NYTimes): Users can default to sections like World, Business, or a personalized feed.

- “Remember last visited page”
- “Let user pin/set a home”
- “Offer predefined options (Dashboard, Feed, Messages, etc.)”
