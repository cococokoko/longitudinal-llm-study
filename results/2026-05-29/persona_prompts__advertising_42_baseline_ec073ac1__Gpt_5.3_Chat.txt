What are the best apps for tracking fitness and nutrition?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Depends a bit on your style (simple tracking vs deep data nerd), but here are some of the best ones right now, grouped by what they’re strongest at:

**All-in-one fitness + nutrition**
- **MyFitnessPal** – still the most popular. Huge food database, easy calorie tracking, integrates with tons of apps. Best if you want something reliable and simple.
- **Cronometer** – more precise and “science-y.” Tracks micronutrients (vitamins, minerals), not just calories. Great if you care about accuracy or specific diets.
- **Lose It!** – very user-friendly and a bit more goal-focused (weight loss, habits). Less overwhelming than MyFitnessPal.

**Workout tracking (gym / strength)**
- **Strong** – clean interface, great for logging lifts and progressive overload. No fluff.
- **Hevy** – similar to Strong but with a social/community angle. Growing fast.
- **Fitbod** – generates workouts for you based on your history and available equipment.

**Running / cardio**
- **Strava** – the social network of fitness. Great for running/cycling, segments, and motivation.
- **Nike Run Club** – free, polished, with guided runs and coaching.

**Habit + lifestyle tracking**
- **MacroFactor** – excellent for nutrition coaching with adaptive calorie targets. More “smart” than MyFitnessPal.
- **Carbon** – similar idea (created by a well-known coach), focuses on macros and coaching adjustments.

**If you want something simple**
- MyFitnessPal + Strong is a classic combo.

**If you want data + optimization**
- Cronometer + MacroFactor (or Fitbod)

**If motivation/social matters**
- Strava + Hevy
