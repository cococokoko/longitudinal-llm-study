How can I improve my credit score quickly?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here are some effective ways to improve your credit score relatively quickly:

## Highest Impact Actions

- **Pay down credit card balances** - Reducing your credit utilization ratio (below 30%, ideally below 10%) can show results within a billing cycle
- **Pay all bills on time** - Payment history is the largest factor (~35% of your score)
- **Dispute any errors** on your credit reports at Equifax, Experian, and TransUnion

## Moderate Impact Steps

- **Become an authorized user** on someone else's account with good history
- **Ask for a credit limit increase** on existing cards (without spending more)
- **Avoid closing old accounts** - preserves your credit history length
- **Limit new credit applications** - each hard inquiry can temporarily lower your score

## Practical Timeline

| Action | Potential Timeframe |
|--------|-------------------|
| Paying down balances | 1-2 billing cycles |
| Dispute errors | 30-45 days |
| Consistent on-time payments | 3-6 months |

## Quick Checks

- Get free reports at **AnnualCreditReport.com**
- Use free monitoring through your bank or credit card

**The most impactful single action** is usually reducing credit card utilization if that's currently high.
