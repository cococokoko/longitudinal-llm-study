── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How do I start a vegetable garden in my backyard?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Starting a vegetable garden is a great project! Here are the key steps:

## Planning
- **Choose a location** with at least 6-8 hours of sunlight daily
- **Start small** - a 4x8 or 4x4 foot bed is manageable for beginners
- **Decide on a style** - raised beds, in-ground, or containers

## Preparing the Soil
- Remove grass and weeds from your chosen area
- Loosen soil 12 inches deep if planting in-ground
- Add compost to improve drainage and nutrients
- Aim for a slightly acidic pH (6.0-7.0)

## Choosing What to Grow
- **Easy beginner vegetables:** tomatoes, lettuce, zucchini, green beans, radishes
- Consider what your family actually eats
- Check your **growing zone** for what works in your climate
- Start with transplants rather than seeds if you're new to this

## Planting
- Follow spacing guidelines on seed packets or plant tags
- Plant after your last frost date
- Water thoroughly after planting

## Ongoing Care
- Water consistently (about 1 inch per week)
- Weed regularly
- Add mulch to retain moisture and reduce weeds
- Watch for pests

## Helpful Resources
- Your local cooperative extension office often has region-specific advice
- Local garden centers can recommend varieties suited to your area
