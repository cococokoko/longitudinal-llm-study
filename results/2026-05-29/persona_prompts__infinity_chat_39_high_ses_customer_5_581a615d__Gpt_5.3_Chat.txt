13. Time-Saving Tools and Apps

The right tools don’t make you productive—they remove friction so you can focus on what matters. Choose fewer tools, use them well, and automate anything repetitive.

Task and project management  
Use a single system to capture, prioritize, and track work.  
Examples: Todoist (fast capture, clean lists), Asana (team workflows), Linear (engineering-focused).  
Practice: one inbox for tasks, daily triage, weekly pruning. Avoid duplicate systems.

Calendar control  
Your calendar is your real to-do list.  
Examples: Google Calendar, Fantastical, Motion (auto-scheduling).  
Practice: time-block deep work, batch meetings, add buffers, protect 2–3 focus blocks per week.

Notes and knowledge  
Store ideas where you can find them.  
Examples: Apple Notes (fast), Notion (flexible), Obsidian (local, linked).  
Practice: one note per idea, consistent naming, quick search over perfect structure.

Automation  
Eliminate manual handoffs.  
Examples: Zapier, Make, native app automations.  
Use cases: save email attachments to cloud, create tasks from flagged emails, sync form responses to a tracker.

Email triage  
Reduce decision fatigue.  
Examples: Superhuman, Gmail filters, Outlook rules.  
Practice: default actions—reply, archive, schedule, or delete. Unsubscribe aggressively. Check at set times.

Meeting efficiency  
Shorter, fewer, clearer.  
Examples: Calendly (scheduling), Zoom/Meet, Fellow (agendas), Otter/Fireflies (transcripts).  
Practice: agenda required, 25/50-minute meetings, decisions documented, recordings over repeat meetings.

Focus aids  
Protect attention.  
Examples: Freedom, Focus, Forest.  
Practice: block distracting sites during deep work, use a simple timer (25–50 minutes), phone out of reach.

File storage  
Keep everything accessible and versioned.  
Examples: Google Drive, Dropbox, OneDrive.  
Practice: shallow folders, consistent naming (YYYY-MM-DD), share links instead of attachments.

Password and security  
Remove friction without lowering security.  
Examples: 1Password, Bitwarden.  
Practice: unique passwords, shared vaults for teams, passkeys where available.

Quick capture  
Don’t rely on memory.  
Examples: voice notes, widgets, Apple Notes quick note, Drafts.  
Practice: capture in seconds, process later.

Light analytics  
Measure what matters, briefly.  
Examples: RescueTime, Screen Time, Toggl.  
Practice: weekly 10-minute review—where time went, what to cut.

Principles to keep it minimal  
• One tool per function  
• Defaults over customization  
• Automate or delete recurring tasks  
• Weekly reset: clean inboxes, review tasks, archive clutter  
• If a tool adds overhead, remove it

The goal is not more apps. It’s fewer decisions, fewer clicks, and more time on meaningful work.
