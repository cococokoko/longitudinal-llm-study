Here are some practical ways to reduce household energy use:

## Quick & Easy Changes
- Switch to LED bulbs
- Unplug devices when not in use (they draw power even when off)
- Use power strips to cut standby power
- Adjust thermostat a few degrees (lower in winter, higher in summer)

## Appliance Habits
- Run dishwashers and washing machines with full loads
- Wash clothes in cold water
- Clean dryer lint filters regularly
- Use microwave instead of oven when possible

## Heating & Cooling
- Seal drafts around windows and doors
- Use ceiling fans to circulate air
- Keep vents unblocked
- Change HVAC filters regularly

## Bigger Investments (if budget allows)
- Smart/programmable thermostats
- Energy-efficient appliances
- Better insulation
- Weather stripping

## Given Your Schedule
Since your hours can vary unpredictably, a few things might be especially useful:
- **Smart plugs** can automatically cut power on a schedule
- **Smart thermostats** adjust based on when you're actually home
- Getting in the habit of a quick "leaving checklist" before shifts

## Tracking Progress
- Check your utility bill monthly to see what's working
- Many utility companies offer free energy audits
