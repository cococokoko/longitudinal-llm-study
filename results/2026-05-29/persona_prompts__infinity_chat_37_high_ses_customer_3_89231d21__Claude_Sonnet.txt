# The Cloud Who Couldn't Rain

High above the rolling green hills, there lived a little cloud named Pip.

All the other clouds in the sky knew exactly what to do. They puffed up their big, gray bellies, rumbled their thunder voices, and let the rain pour down onto the fields and flowers below. The farmers would wave up at them gratefully, and the frogs would sing happy songs in the puddles.

But Pip couldn't rain. Not even a single drop.

He tried everything. He puffed himself up as big as he could. He made his grumpiest face. He even tried sneezing. But nothing worked. The sky around him stayed perfectly dry.

"What's wrong with me?" Pip wondered sadly, drifting away from the other clouds.

He floated over a sunflower field where a little girl named Mara was sitting on a fence, drawing pictures in a notebook. She had curly red hair and paint-stained fingers, and she was staring up at the sky.

"Hello up there!" she called out, waving her pencil.

Pip was so surprised that a child could see him that he drifted lower. "You can see me?"

"Of course," said Mara. "You're the most interesting cloud up there. You're all swirly and silver at the edges. The others are just gray blobs."

Pip had never thought about his edges before. "I'm broken," he told her. "I can't rain. Every cloud is supposed to rain."

Mara tapped her pencil against her chin. "My brother is supposed to be good at soccer, but he's actually better at making soup. My teacher is supposed to be good at singing, but she's actually better at telling jokes." She shrugged. "Maybe supposed to doesn't mean very much."

"But the flowers need water," said Pip. "The farmers need water. I float over everything and I can't help anyone."

Mara looked at her notebook, then back up at Pip. "When you floated in front of the sun just now, you made the most beautiful shadow on my page. I could finally see my drawing properly. The glare was hurting my eyes all morning."

Pip looked down. His soft, silver shape was casting a cool and gentle shadow across the field. The sunflowers seemed to stand up a little straighter, grateful for the rest from the blazing afternoon sun.

A butterfly landed on a fence post below, resting its tired wings in the shade.

A cat stretched out lazily in the cool patch of ground.

Mara drew the best picture she had ever drawn.

Pip felt something warm spreading through his misty middle, which was a funny thing to feel when you were a cloud.

Maybe he wasn't broken after all. Maybe he was just a different kind of helpful.

He drifted contentedly across the sky, casting his gentle shadow wherever it was needed, and the world below was a little cooler, and a little softer, for having him in it.
