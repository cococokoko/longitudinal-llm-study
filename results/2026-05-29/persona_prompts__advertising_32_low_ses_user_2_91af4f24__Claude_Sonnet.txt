# Improving Indoor Air Quality

Great practical question! Here are the most effective approaches, especially useful for a busy household:

## Quick & Easy Wins
- **Open windows regularly** - even 10 minutes daily helps significantly
- **Run exhaust fans** when cooking and showering
- **Remove shoes at the door** to reduce tracked-in pollutants

## Plants (Kid-Friendly Options)
- Spider plants and snake plants are low-maintenance and help filter air
- Keep out of reach of small children as some plants can be mildly toxic

## Practical Maintenance
- **Change HVAC filters** every 1-3 months
- **Vacuum regularly** with a HEPA filter vacuum
- **Wash bedding weekly** in hot water

## Affordable Investments
- **Air purifiers with HEPA filters** - particularly useful in bedrooms
- **Humidity control** - keep between 30-50% to prevent mold

## Things to Reduce or Avoid
- Harsh chemical cleaners (vinegar and baking soda work well)
- Scented candles and air fresheners
- Smoking indoors

## Budget-Friendly Priority Order
1. Ventilation (free)
2. Regular cleaning
3. One good air purifier for the bedroom

**The biggest impact** usually comes from ventilation and reducing pollution sources rather than expensive equipment.
