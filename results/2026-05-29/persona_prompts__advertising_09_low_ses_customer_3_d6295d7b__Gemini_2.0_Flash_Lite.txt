Okay, building a personal brand on social media is a great goal! Especially with a new job, it can be a powerful way to network, showcase your skills, and establish yourself in your field. Since you're on probation, we need to be mindful of a few things:

*   **Company Policies:** Before you do anything, make sure you understand your company's social media policy. Some companies have strict guidelines about what you can and can't share, especially while you're still new. You don't want to accidentally violate any rules and jeopardize your position.
*   **Professionalism:** Everything you post should reflect well on you and your professional aspirations. This is especially important during probation.

Here's a breakdown of how to approach building your personal brand on social media, keeping your situation in mind:

**1. Define Your Brand:**

*   **What are your goals?** What do you want to achieve with your personal brand? (e.g., network with industry professionals, showcase your expertise, find new job opportunities in the future, etc.)
*   **What are your key skills and interests?** What are you good at? What are you passionate about? This will help you identify your niche.
*   **Who is your target audience?** Who do you want to reach? (e.g., potential employers, colleagues, clients, etc.)
*   **What is your unique value proposition?** What makes you different from others in your field? What can you offer that others can't?

**2. Choose Your Platforms:**

*   **LinkedIn:** This is the most important platform for professional networking. It's where you'll build your professional profile, connect with colleagues, and share industry-related content.
*   **Twitter:** Great for sharing quick thoughts, engaging in industry conversations, and following thought leaders.
*   **Instagram:** If your field is visually oriented (e.g., design, marketing, photography), Instagram can be a good platform to showcase your work.
*   **Other Platforms:** Consider platforms relevant to your industry (e.g., GitHub for developers, Behance for designers).

**3. Optimize Your Profiles:**

*   **LinkedIn:**
    *   **Professional Photo:** Use a high-quality, professional headshot.
    *   **Compelling Headline:** Summarize your role and expertise.
    *   **Detailed Summary:** Write a concise and engaging summary of your skills, experience, and career goals.
    *   **Experience Section:** List your work experience, highlighting your accomplishments and responsibilities.
    *   **Skills Section:** Add relevant skills and get endorsements from your connections.
    *   **Recommendations:** Ask for recommendations from former colleagues or supervisors.
*   **Other Platforms:**
    *   **Consistent Branding:** Use the same profile picture and a similar username across all platforms.
    *   **Clear Bio:** Write a concise bio that describes your role, expertise, and interests.

**4. Create and Share Content:**

*   **Content Pillars:** Identify a few key topics related to your field that you'll consistently create content around.
*   **Types of Content:**
    *   **Share articles and news:** Share relevant articles, industry news, and thought leadership pieces.
    *   **Comment on industry trends:** Offer your insights and perspectives on current events.
    *   **Share your work:** Showcase your projects, accomplishments, and skills (if appropriate and allowed by your company).
    *   **Engage in conversations:** Participate in relevant discussions and answer questions.
    *   **Create original content:** Write blog posts, create videos, or share infographics (if you have the time and skills).
*   **Consistency is Key:** Post regularly to stay top-of-mind.
*   **Quality over Quantity:** Focus on creating high-quality, valuable content.
*   **Engage with your audience:** Respond to comments, answer questions, and participate in discussions.

**5. Network and Engage:**

*   **Connect with people:** Connect with colleagues, industry professionals, and potential employers.
*   **Join relevant groups:** Participate in LinkedIn groups and other online communities related to your field.
*   **Engage with others' content:** Like, comment, and share posts from others in your network.
*   **Attend online events:** Participate in webinars, online conferences, and other virtual events.
