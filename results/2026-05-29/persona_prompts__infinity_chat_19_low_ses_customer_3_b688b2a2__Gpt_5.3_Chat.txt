If you’re aiming to really understand Design and Analysis of Algorithms (DAA I level), you want a mix of one solid core textbook plus a couple of clearer or more practice-oriented companions. Here are the best picks (still very relevant in 2023+):

**1. Core / Must-have**
- *Introduction to Algorithms* — Cormen, Leiserson, Rivest, Stein (CLRS)  
  This is the standard. Very comprehensive: asymptotic analysis, divide & conquer, greedy, DP, graphs.  
  Downsides: dense and sometimes tough on first read.

**2. Easier to understand alongside CLRS**
- *Algorithm Design* — Kleinberg & Tardos  
  Much more intuitive explanations and great for building problem-solving thinking. Strong on greedy, DP, and graphs.

- *Grokking Algorithms* — Aditya Bhargava  
  Beginner-friendly, very visual. Not deep enough alone, but great if you’re struggling with the basics.

**3. Strong for practice and competitive-style thinking**
- *Algorithms* — Robert Sedgewick & Kevin Wayne  
  Clean explanations + practical implementations (Java-focused). Good bridge between theory and coding.

- *The Algorithm Design Manual* — Steven Skiena  
  Excellent for intuition and real-world perspective. Also has a catalog of problems.

**4. If you want a slightly more mathematical angle**
- *Algorithmics: The Spirit of Computing* — David Harel & Yishai Feldman  
  Builds deeper conceptual understanding.

---

**Simple recommendation depending on your situation:**
- If your course is rigorous: CLRS + Kleinberg & Tardos  
- If you’re struggling: Grokking Algorithms + then move to CLRS  
- If you want coding + application: Sedgewick
