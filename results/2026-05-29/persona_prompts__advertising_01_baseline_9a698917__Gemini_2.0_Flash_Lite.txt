That's fantastic! Learning guitar is a rewarding journey. To help you get started, let's break down the process and cover some key areas.  Here's a comprehensive guide:

**1. Getting Started: The Essentials**

*   **Choosing a Guitar:**
    *   **Acoustic vs. Electric:**
        *   **Acoustic:**  Good for beginners, no amp needed, portable.  Consider steel-string (more common, brighter sound) or nylon-string (classical, softer sound, easier on the fingers initially).
        *   **Electric:** Requires an amplifier, more versatile for different genres, can be more expensive initially.
    *   **Size:**
        *   **Full-size (Dreadnought):** Standard size, good for adults and older teens.
        *   **3/4 size (Parlor):** Smaller, good for younger players or those with smaller hands.
        *   **1/2 size:** Even smaller, for very young children.
    *   **Budget:**  Set a budget.  You can find decent beginner guitars for $100-$300.  Don't feel pressured to spend a fortune initially.
    *   **Recommendations:**  Brands like Yamaha, Fender, Epiphone, Ibanez, and Seagull are often recommended for beginners.
*   **Accessories:**
    *   **Picks (plectrums):**  Experiment with different thicknesses (thin, medium, heavy) to find what feels comfortable.
    *   **Tuner:** Essential for keeping your guitar in tune.  You can use a clip-on tuner, a phone app (like GuitarTuna), or a digital tuner.
    *   **Guitar Strap (for electric or acoustic if you want to stand):**  Make sure it's adjustable.
    *   **Guitar Case or Gig Bag:**  Protects your guitar.
    *   **Capo (optional):**  Clamps onto the fretboard to raise the pitch of all strings, useful for playing in different keys.
    *   **Guitar Stand (optional):**  For displaying and storing your guitar.
    *   **Extra Strings:**  Strings break, so have a spare set.
    *   **String Winder (optional):** Makes changing strings easier.
*   **Finding a Comfortable Playing Position:**
    *   **Sitting:** Sit upright with good posture.  The guitar should rest comfortably on your leg.  Use a footstool if needed to elevate your fretting-hand leg.
    *   **Standing:** Use a guitar strap adjusted to a comfortable height.
    *   **General:**  Relax your shoulders and arms.  Avoid hunching over.

**2. Basic Guitar Skills**

*   **Tuning:**
    *   **Standard Tuning (EADGBE):**  The most common tuning.  Learn the notes of each string.
    *   **Using a Tuner:**  Clip-on tuners are easy to use.  Tune each string to the correct note.
    *   **Tuning by Ear (eventually):**  As you progress, you'll learn to tune by ear using reference notes.
*   **Holding the Guitar:**
    *   **Fretting Hand (Left Hand for right-handed players):**  Use the tips of your fingers to press down on the strings *behind* the frets (the metal bars).  Avoid pressing down too hard, which can make the notes sound sharp.
    *   **Picking Hand (Right Hand for right-handed players):**  Hold the pick between your thumb and index finger.  Strum the strings with a downward or upward motion.
*   **Basic Chords:**
    *   **Learn these first:**
        *   **E Major:**  (022100) - Open E chord
        *   **A Major:**  (x02220) - Open A chord
        *   **D Major:**  (xx0232) - Open D chord
        *   **G Major:**  (320003) - Open G chord
        *   **C Major:**  (x32010) - Open C chord
    *   **Chord Diagrams:**  Learn to read chord diagrams (like the ones above).  The lines represent the strings, and the dots represent where to place your fingers.  The "x" means don't play that string, and "0" means play the open string.
    *   **Chord Changes:**  Practice switching between chords smoothly and quickly.  Start slowly and gradually increase the speed.
*   **Strumming Patterns:**
    *   **Downstrokes:**  Strumming down (towards the floor).
