# Learning a New Language Effectively

As a physician-scientist, you likely have strong analytical skills and a demanding schedule — here's a practical, evidence-based approach:

## Core Principles

- **Comprehensible input** is the primary driver of acquisition (Krashen's hypothesis holds up reasonably well)
- **Spaced repetition** for vocabulary retention
- **Consistency beats intensity** — 20-30 min daily outperforms 3-hour weekend sessions
- **Production matters** — speaking/writing accelerates fluency

---

## Practical Framework

### Phase 1: Foundation (Months 1-3)
- Learn the **top 1,000-2,000 most frequent words** via Anki
- Master basic grammar structure
- Tools: Pimsleur, Duolingo (supplementary), a good grammar reference

### Phase 2: Immersion (Months 3-12)
- Podcasts, shows, books in target language
- **Language exchange partners** (iTalki, Tandem)
- Start speaking early — don't wait until you feel "ready"

### Phase 3: Refinement
- Domain-specific vocabulary (medical/scientific if relevant)
- Formal tutoring for precision
