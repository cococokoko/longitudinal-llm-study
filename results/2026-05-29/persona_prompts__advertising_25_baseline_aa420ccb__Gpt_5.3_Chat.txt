── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How can I decorate my apartment on a minimalist budget?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
You don’t need much money—you just need a bit of intention. Minimalist decorating on a budget is really about editing, not adding.

Start by clearing visual clutter. The cheapest way to make a space look better is to remove things that don’t serve a purpose or feel good. Once you’ve simplified, whatever you *do* add will stand out more.

Then focus on a few high-impact areas instead of trying to decorate everything:
- Walls: A couple of large, simple prints (you can print your own or use free art online) look more intentional than lots of small pieces. Even black-and-white prints on plain paper can look clean and modern.
- Textiles: Throw pillows, a blanket, or a rug can change the entire vibe. Stick to 2–3 colors max so it stays cohesive.
- Lighting: Warm lighting makes a huge difference. Swap harsh bulbs for soft warm ones, or add a cheap floor lamp for a cozy feel.

Use what you already have in smarter ways. Stack books neatly, group objects in threes, or rearrange furniture to create more open space. Sometimes just moving things around makes a place feel brand new.

Bring in a bit of nature if you can. A single plant (even a very cheap or propagated one) adds life without clutter. If real plants aren’t your thing, even a branch in a jar can look intentional.

Look for budget sources:
- Thrift stores
- Facebook Marketplace / Buy Nothing groups
- DIY (painting old frames, repurposing jars, etc.)

And one rule that helps keep things minimalist: don’t buy decor just to fill space. If you’re unsure about something, leave the space empty for a while. Empty space is part of the aesthetic.
