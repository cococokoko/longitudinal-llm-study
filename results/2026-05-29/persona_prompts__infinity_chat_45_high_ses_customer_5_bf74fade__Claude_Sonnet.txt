Is moral objective or subjective?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This is a genuinely contested philosophical question. Here's an honest breakdown:

## Arguments for Objectivity
- Some moral claims seem clearly true regardless of opinion (torturing children for fun is wrong)
- Moral progress appears real - we didn't just *change preferences* about slavery, we recognized it was *wrong*
- Mathematical and logical truths are objective without being physical - ethics might be similar
- Evolutionary debunking arguments can cut both ways

## Arguments for Subjectivity
- Significant cross-cultural moral variation exists
- Moral intuitions demonstrably shift with emotion, culture, upbringing
- It's unclear *how* objective moral facts would exist or be known
- Disagreement persists even among careful reasoners

## Important Distinctions
- **Metaethics** (is morality objective?) vs **normative ethics** (what's actually right?)
- You can have **intersubjective** standards - shared frameworks that aren't purely individual but aren't cosmic facts either
- "Subjective" doesn't necessarily mean *arbitrary*

## My honest assessment
The strongest positions acknowledge **genuine uncertainty here**. Many philosophers treat this as unresolved. Confident claims in either direction often oversimplify.

**Practically speaking**, most people reason *as if* some moral claims are more defensible than others - which itself is worth examining.
