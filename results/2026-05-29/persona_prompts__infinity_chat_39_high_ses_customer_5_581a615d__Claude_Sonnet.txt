# Time-Saving Tools and Apps

---

## Core Principle

The right tool eliminates friction. The wrong tool creates it. Choose fewer, better.

---

## Communication

**Superhuman / Spark**
Triage email in half the time. Keyboard shortcuts, AI triage, send-later scheduling. Stop living in your inbox.

**Loom**
Replace a 30-minute meeting with a 3-minute video. Async by default. Your calendar will thank you.

**Calendly / Cal.com**
Eliminate scheduling back-and-forth entirely. One link. Done.

---

## Focus & Deep Work

**Freedom / Cold Turkey**
Block distracting sites during critical work windows. Non-negotiable for deep thinking.

**Focusmate**
Virtual coworking accountability. Surprisingly effective for high-stakes deliverables.

**Notion AI / Obsidian**
Capture, connect, and retrieve thinking fast. Stop recreating context from scratch.

---

## Meetings & Decision-Making

**Otter.ai / Fireflies**
Auto-transcribe every meeting. Search conversations like documents. Stop taking notes manually.

**Reclaim.ai**
Automatically protects focus blocks and reschedules conflicts. Your calendar runs itself.

**Granola**
AI meeting notes built for executives. Clean summaries, action items extracted automatically.

---

## Delegation & Workflow

**Zapier / Make**
Automate repetitive handoffs between tools. If you do it more than twice, automate it.

**Notion / Linear**
Single source of truth for projects. Reduces status update meetings significantly.

**Superhuman for Slack** *(discipline, not a tool)*
Batch Slack to two or three windows daily. Treat it like email, not a chat room.

---

## AI Leverage Points

**ChatGPT / Claude**
First drafts, research synthesis, decision frameworks, communication prep. Use before delegating.

**Perplexity**
Research with citations. Faster than Google for executive-level context gathering.

**Harvey / Ironclad** *(legal)*
Contract review at speed. Reduce legal bottlenecks on routine agreements.

---

## What to Avoid

- Tools that require heavy onboarding
- Platforms your team won't actually use
- Anything solving a problem you don't have

---

## The Executive Standard

> If a tool doesn't save you at least one hour per week within the first two weeks, remove it.

Stack deliberately. Audit quarterly. Less is faster.
