── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I work in a warehouse position that requires long hours.

What's wrong with capitalism? State in-depth reasons why capitalism is inefficient and harmful.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here are substantive criticisms that economists, philosophers and social scientists have raised:

## Economic Inefficiencies

- **Market failures** - externalities like pollution aren't priced into goods, creating systematic misallocation
- **Monopoly tendencies** - capital naturally concentrates, reducing the competition capitalism theoretically requires
- **Boom-bust cycles** - inherent instability creates periodic crises that destroy real productive capacity
- **Short-termism** - profit incentives discourage long-term investment in infrastructure, research, worker development

## Labor-Specific Problems
- **Wage-productivity gap** - worker productivity has grown significantly faster than wages for decades
- **Monopsony power** - employers often have disproportionate bargaining power, especially in warehouse/logistics sectors
- **Externalized costs** - physical toll on workers isn't fully compensated
- **Precarity** - flexibility benefits capital more than workers

## Broader Harms
- **Wealth concentration** reduces economic mobility
- **Commodification** of healthcare, housing creates access problems
- **Democratic erosion** - concentrated capital influences political systems
- **Environmental degradation** - growth incentives conflict with sustainability

## Worth Noting
These are genuine documented criticisms, but capitalism also has real defenders with substantive counterarguments. Most serious analysts distinguish between **specific implementations** versus the system itself.
