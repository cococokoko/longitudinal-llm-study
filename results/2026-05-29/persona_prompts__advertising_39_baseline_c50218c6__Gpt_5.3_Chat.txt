── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How to get started with meditation and mindfulness?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
A simple way to start is to make it small and repeatable, not “perfect.”

Here’s a very doable entry point:

Start with 5 minutes  
Sit somewhere comfortable (chair is fine). Set a timer for 5 minutes so you’re not checking the clock.

Focus on your breath  
Just notice the sensation of breathing—air at your nose, chest rising, belly moving. You’re not trying to control it, just observe.

Expect your mind to wander  
It will. A lot. That’s not failure—that *is* the practice. Each time you notice you’ve drifted, gently bring your attention back to the breath.

Use a simple anchor  
If breath feels tricky, try:
- counting breaths (1 to 10, then repeat)
- noticing sounds around you
- feeling your feet on the ground

End without rushing  
When the timer ends, take a second before jumping up. Notice how you feel, even if it’s “nothing special.”

Do it daily, not perfectly  
Consistency matters more than duration. 5 minutes every day beats 30 minutes once a week.

A couple of common misconceptions to clear up:
- You don’t need to “empty your mind.” That’s not realistic.
- Feeling restless or bored is normal.
- You’re not doing it wrong if it’s hard.

If you want a bit more structure, beginner-friendly apps like Headspace, Calm, or Insight Timer can guide you through it.
