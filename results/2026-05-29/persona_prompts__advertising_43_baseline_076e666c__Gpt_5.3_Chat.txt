── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How to plan an environmentally friendly wedding?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Planning an eco-friendly wedding is mostly about making thoughtful swaps rather than reinventing everything. You’re aiming to reduce waste, emissions, and overconsumption while still making the day feel meaningful and personal.

Start with the big-impact choices. Venue and guest count matter more than small details. Pick a location close to most guests or one that allows ceremony and reception in the same place to cut travel emissions. Outdoor venues or spaces with natural lighting can reduce energy use. Keeping the guest list tighter also lowers the footprint across food, transport, and materials.

For invitations and communication, go digital where you can—wedding websites, email RSVPs, etc. If you want physical invites, choose recycled paper or seed paper and minimal packaging.

Clothing is a great place to be sustainable without sacrificing style. Consider renting, buying secondhand, or choosing designers who use ethical materials. The same goes for suits. You can also rewear outfits or pick something versatile.

Food and drink have a big environmental impact. Opt for local, seasonal catering and try to reduce food waste by working with caterers who donate leftovers. Plant-forward menus (even partially vegetarian) can significantly cut emissions. Skip single-use plastics and go for reusable or compostable tableware.

Decor is where a lot of waste happens. Rent or reuse items instead of buying new. Choose real plants or flowers that are in season and locally grown, or even potted plants guests can take home. Avoid balloon releases, confetti (unless it’s biodegradable), or anything that becomes litter.

For favors, skip them entirely or give something useful or consumable like local food, small plants, or a donation to a cause you care about.

Transportation can be streamlined by organizing carpools or shuttles for guests. If it’s a destination wedding, consider choosing a place where most people already are or combining it with a longer stay to offset travel impact.

Rings and jewelry can be sourced from recycled metals or ethical jewelers. Lab-grown stones are another lower-impact option.

Finally, think about waste at the event itself. Provide clearly labeled recycling and compost bins, and work with vendors who share sustainability values.

You don’t need perfection—just focus on the biggest levers (travel, food, materials), and make choices that align with what actually matters to you.
