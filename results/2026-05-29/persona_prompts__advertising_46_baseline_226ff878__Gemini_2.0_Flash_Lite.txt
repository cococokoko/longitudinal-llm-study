── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
What are the best resources for starting a plant-based diet?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Starting a plant-based diet can be exciting and rewarding! Here are some of the best resources to help you get started, categorized for easier navigation:

**I. General Information & Education:**

*   **Websites & Blogs:**
    *   **Physicians Committee for Responsible Medicine (PCRM):** Offers a wealth of information, recipes, and resources backed by medical professionals. ([https://www.pcrm.org/](https://www.pcrm.org/))
    *   **The Vegan Society:** A UK-based organization with comprehensive information on veganism, including nutrition, recipes, and lifestyle tips. ([https://www.vegansociety.com/](https://www.vegansociety.com/))
    *   **Plant Based News:** A news website covering plant-based food, health, and environmental issues. ([https://plantbasednews.org/](https://plantbasednews.org/))
    *   **Forks Over Knives:** Website and documentary focusing on the health benefits of a whole-food, plant-based diet. ([https://www.forksoverknives.com/](https://www.forksoverknives.com/))
    *   **NutritionFacts.org:** Dr. Michael Greger's website, providing evidence-based information on nutrition and disease prevention. ([https://nutritionfacts.org/](https://nutritionfacts.org/))
    *   **HappyCow:** A directory of vegan and vegetarian restaurants and businesses worldwide. ([https://www.happycow.net/](https://www.happycow.net/))
    *   **Plant Based on a Budget:** Focuses on affordable plant-based eating. ([https://plantbasedonabudget.com/](https://plantbasedonabudget.com/))

*   **Books:**
    *   **"How Not to Die" by Michael Greger, M.D.:** A comprehensive guide to preventing and reversing disease through diet.
    *   **"The China Study" by T. Colin Campbell, Ph.D., and Thomas M. Campbell II, M.D.:** A landmark study on the link between diet and disease.
    *   **"Forks Over Knives – The Cookbook" by Del Sroufe:** Recipes based on the Forks Over Knives philosophy.
    *   **"Becoming Vegan: The Complete Guide to Adopting a Healthy Plant-Based Diet" by Brenda Davis, R.D., and Vesanto Melina, M.S., R.D.:** A comprehensive guide to vegan nutrition.
    *   **"Eat to Live" by Joel Fuhrman, M.D.:** Focuses on nutrient-dense foods for optimal health.
    *   **"The Plant-Based Diet for Beginners" by Gabriel Miller:** A practical guide for starting a plant-based diet.

*   **Documentaries:**
    *   **"Forks Over Knives":** Explores the health benefits of a whole-food, plant-based diet.
    *   **"What the Health":** Examines the impact of diet on health and the environment.
    *   **"Cowspiracy: The Sustainability Secret":** Focuses on the environmental impact of animal agriculture.
    *   **"Game Changers":** Explores the performance benefits of a plant-based diet for athletes.

**II. Nutrition & Meal Planning:**

*   **Websites & Apps:**
    *   **Cronometer:** A free online nutrition tracker that allows you to track your nutrient intake and identify potential deficiencies. ([https://cronometer.com/](https://cronometer.com/))
    *   **MyFitnessPal:** A popular app for tracking calories and macronutrients, with a large database of foods. (While not specifically plant-based, it can be used to track your plant-based meals.)
    *   **Eat This Much:** A meal planning website that generates personalized meal plans based on your dietary preferences and goals. ([https://www.eatthismuch.com/](https://www.eatthismuch.com/))

*   **Key Nutrients to Focus On:**
    *   **Protein:** Beans, lentils, tofu, tempeh, edamame, quinoa, nuts, seeds, and plant-based protein powders.
    *   **Iron:** Lentils, spinach, tofu, fortified cereals, dried fruits, and dark leafy greens.  Pair with Vitamin C to enhance absorption.
    *   **Calcium:** Leafy greens (kale, collard greens), fortified plant milks, tofu (calcium-set), and tahini.
    *   **Vitamin B12:**  Supplementation is essential.  Look for fortified foods (nutritional yeast, plant milks, etc.) or take a B12 supplement
