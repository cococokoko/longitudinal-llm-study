## Core Investment Strategies

**For beginners/long-term:**
- **Index funds** - Low-cost funds tracking the S&P 500 or total market
- **Dollar-cost averaging** - Invest a fixed amount regularly regardless of market conditions
- **Buy and hold** - Avoid frequent trading, which often hurts returns

**Account types to consider:**
- 401(k) - especially if your employer offers matching
- Roth IRA - tax-free growth, good if you expect higher future income
- Regular brokerage account for additional investing

## Practical Principles

- **Diversify** across sectors and asset types
- Keep investment costs low (expense ratios matter)
- Build an emergency fund **before** investing heavily
- Avoid trying to time the market
- Be cautious with individual stocks, especially early on

## Things to Be Realistic About

- Higher returns generally mean higher risk
- Most active traders underperform simple index funds
- Be skeptical of "hot tips" or guaranteed returns
