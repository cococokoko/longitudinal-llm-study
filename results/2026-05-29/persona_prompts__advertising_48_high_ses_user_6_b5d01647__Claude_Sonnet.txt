# Best Practices for Organic Gardening

Here's a straightforward overview:

## Soil Health
- **Compost regularly** - builds nutrients and improves structure
- **Test soil pH** - most vegetables prefer 6.0-7.0
- **Avoid tilling excessively** - disrupts beneficial microorganisms
- **Use cover crops** to prevent erosion and add nitrogen

## Pest & Weed Management
- **Companion planting** - e.g., basil near tomatoes, marigolds as borders
- **Crop rotation** - prevents disease and pest buildup
- **Hand-pull weeds** before they seed
- **Encourage beneficial insects** like ladybugs and lacewings

## Watering
- **Water deeply and infrequently** rather than shallow/frequent
- **Morning watering** reduces fungal disease risk
- **Drip irrigation** maximizes efficiency

## Fertilizing
- Use **compost, worm castings, or fish emulsion**
- Avoid synthetic fertilizers that disrupt soil biology

## General Tips
- **Start with what grows well** in your climate zone
- **Mulch generously** to retain moisture and suppress weeds
- **Keep a garden journal** to track what works
