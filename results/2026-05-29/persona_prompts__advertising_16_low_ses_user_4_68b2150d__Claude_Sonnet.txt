# Managing Stress and Anxiety as a Patient Care Aide

Healthcare work is genuinely demanding, so this is really important. Here are some practical strategies:

## During Your Shift
- **Take micro-breaks** when possible - even 2-3 minutes of deep breathing helps
- **Stay hydrated** - easy to forget but significantly affects mood and energy
- **Connect briefly with colleagues** - social support matters in the moment
- **Focus on what you can control** in each interaction

## After Your Shift
- **Decompress before home** - a short walk, music, or quiet time in your car
- **Create a transition ritual** to mentally "leave work at work"
- **Change clothes** when you get home - symbolic but effective
- **Avoid immediately jumping into home responsibilities**

## Longer-Term Strategies
- **Prioritize sleep** around shift schedules
- **Regular physical activity** - one of the most evidence-based stress reducers
- **Talk to someone** - colleague, friend, therapist, or employee assistance program
- **Set boundaries** around work communication on days off

## Recognizing When It's More Serious
Watch for signs of **compassion fatigue** or **burnout**:
- Emotional numbness toward patients
- Dreading every shift
- Persistent exhaustion
