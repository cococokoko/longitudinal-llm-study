# Learning a New Language Effectively

## Core Principles

**Consistency beats intensity** — 20 minutes daily outperforms 3-hour weekend sessions
**Comprehensible input** — exposure slightly above your current level drives acquisition
**Active use accelerates learning** — producing language, not just consuming it

---

## Proven Strategies

### Getting Started
- Set a **specific, realistic goal** (conversational vs. fluent vs. reading)
- Choose resources matched to your learning style
- Learn the most **frequent 1,000-2,000 words** first (covers ~80% of everyday speech)

### Building Skills
| Skill | Effective Methods |
|-------|------------------|
| Vocabulary | Spaced repetition (Anki, Duolingo) |
| Grammar | Structured courses, pattern recognition |
| Listening | Podcasts, shows, native content |
| Speaking | Language exchange partners, tutors |
| Reading | Graded readers → native texts |

### Staying Motivated
- Track progress visibly
- Connect with native speakers early
- Consume content you **actually enjoy**
- Accept mistakes as data, not failure

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
- Waiting until you're "ready" to speak
- Translating word-for-word in your head
- Studying without real communication practice
- Switching methods too frequently

---

## Useful Tools
- **Anki** – flashcard spaced repetition
- **italki** – tutors and language partners
- **Language Transfer** – free audio courses
- **LingQ** – reading with built-in vocabulary
