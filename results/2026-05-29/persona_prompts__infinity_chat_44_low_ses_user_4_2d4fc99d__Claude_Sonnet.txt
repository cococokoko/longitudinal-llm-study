── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I work shifts at a local hospital as a patient care aide.

Can you write me a series of three questions that can determine if something is morally right or wrong?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here are three questions that can help evaluate if something is morally right or wrong, particularly useful in a healthcare setting:

**1. Does it respect the dignity and autonomy of the person involved?**
This asks whether the action treats the person as a human being worthy of respect, and whether their wishes and rights are being honored.

**2. Would I be comfortable if my supervisor, the patient's family, or the public could see exactly what I am doing and why?**
This is sometimes called a "transparency test" - if you would feel uncomfortable being observed, that discomfort may signal something is ethically wrong.

**3. Does this action cause harm, or does it prevent harm?**
This asks you to honestly weigh the potential negative consequences of an action against its benefits, for the patient and others involved.

---

These three questions cover some core ethical principles relevant to patient care:
- **Respect for persons**
- **Accountability**
- **Do no harm**

Used together, they can give you a fairly well-rounded way to think through difficult situations you might encounter on the floor. Of course, your hospital likely also has an **ethics committee or policy guidelines** that can provide additional guidance in complex situations.
