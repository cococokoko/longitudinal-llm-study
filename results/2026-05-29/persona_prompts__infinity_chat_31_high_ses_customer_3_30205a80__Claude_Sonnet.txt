── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
There are several common pathways people take to reach executive positions:

## Education & Credentials
- MBA or advanced degrees (though not always required)
- Specialized technical expertise in the company's field
- Professional certifications

## Career Progression
- **Internal advancement** - rising through the ranks over many years
- **Functional expertise** - becoming the go-to person in finance, operations, marketing, etc.
- **Cross-functional experience** - deliberately moving across departments to build broad knowledge

## Key Factors
- **Performance track record** - consistently delivering results
- **Visibility** - being known to decision-makers
- **Sponsorship** - having senior advocates who champion your advancement
- **Networking** - building relationships inside and outside the organization
- **Timing and opportunity** - being available when positions open

## Other Routes
- **Entrepreneurship** - founding a company and becoming its executive
- **Board connections** - being recruited through professional networks
- **Private equity** - being placed into portfolio companies
- **Lateral moves** - coming from competitors or adjacent industries

## Research suggests
Factors like social capital, access to informal networks, and frankly **demographic advantages** play significant roles beyond pure merit
