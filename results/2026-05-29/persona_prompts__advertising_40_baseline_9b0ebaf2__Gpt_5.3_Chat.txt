Where can I find unique handmade gifts?

If you’re after truly unique, handmade gifts, there are a few great directions depending on how “one-of-a-kind” you want things to feel:

Online marketplaces  
- Etsy: still the biggest hub for handmade and custom items—jewelry, art, leather goods, ceramics, you name it. Use filters like “handmade” and location for better finds.  
- Uncommon Goods: more curated and design-forward, often with artist backstories.  
- Not On The High Street (UK-focused but ships internationally): lots of personalized handmade gifts.  
- Folksy (UK) or Madeit (Australia): smaller platforms that feel more indie.

Direct from makers  
- Instagram and TikTok are surprisingly good for this—search things like “handmade ceramics” or “custom jewelry” and you’ll find artists selling directly. Often more unique than marketplace listings.  
- Many artists have their own websites (Shopify, Big Cartel), which can give you more custom options.

Local options (often the best for uniqueness)  
- Craft fairs and artisan markets: great for discovering one-off pieces and meeting the maker.  
- Local galleries or gift shops: many stock handmade goods from nearby artists.  
- Pop-up markets and seasonal fairs (especially around holidays).
