There isn’t a single “best” stock trading strategy—what works depends on your time, risk tolerance, and how much attention you can realistically give it (especially if you’re juggling a lot outside of markets).

That said, a few approaches consistently hold up better than most:

**1. Long-term investing (most reliable for most people)**
Buy strong companies or broad index funds and hold for years. This avoids constant decision-making and reduces the chance of costly mistakes.
- Think: S&P 500 ETFs, diversified portfolios
- Works well if you’re busy and can’t watch markets all day

**2. Dollar-cost averaging**
Invest a fixed amount regularly (weekly/monthly), regardless of price.
- Smooths out volatility
- Removes the pressure of “timing the market”

**3. Swing trading (moderate effort)**
Hold stocks for days to weeks, based on trends or patterns.
- Requires some chart reading and discipline
- Less intense than day trading, but still active

**4. Momentum or trend-following**
Buy stocks that are already moving up, sell when momentum fades.
- Simple idea, but emotionally hard to execute consistently

---

**What to avoid (especially if time is limited):**
- Day trading (very time-intensive, high failure rate)
- Chasing hype stocks or social media tips
- Overtrading (fees + bad decisions add up fast)

If you want something practical:
- Put most of your money into low-cost index funds
- Add money regularly (DCA)
- Keep a small portion (like 10–20%) for more active strategies if you enjoy it
