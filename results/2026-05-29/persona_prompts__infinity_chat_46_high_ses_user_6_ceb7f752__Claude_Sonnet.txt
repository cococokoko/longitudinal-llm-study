# Major Controversies in Morality

There are several strong candidates, and reasonable people genuinely disagree about which is *biggest*. Here are the most significant:

## Foundational Controversies
- **Moral realism vs. relativism** - Do objective moral facts exist, or is morality culturally/individually constructed?
- **The is-ought problem** - Can you derive moral obligations from factual observations?

## Practical High-Stakes Controversies
- **Abortion** - Competing claims about personhood, bodily autonomy, and when rights begin
- **Capital punishment** - Justice vs. state-sanctioned killing
- **Animal rights** - The moral circle and who/what deserves consideration
- **End-of-life decisions** - Euthanasia, autonomy vs. sanctity of life

## Deeper Philosophical Tensions
- **Consequentialism vs. deontology** - Do ends justify means?
- **Individual rights vs. collective welfare** - A tension underlying most political debates
- **Moral luck** - Should people be judged for circumstances beyond their control?

## My Honest Assessment
The **foundational question** - whether morality is objective or constructed - arguably underlies *all other* controversies, because your answer shapes every other moral judgment.
