── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
If you’re thinking about where freelance writers actually *get paid reliably* (not just browse gigs), the landscape breaks down into a few tiers:

**1. High-quality marketplaces (better clients, higher rates)**
- **Contently** – Strong for experienced writers. Brands like IBM, Marriott, etc. You apply and wait to be matched; not fast, but good pay when it hits.
- **Skyword** – Similar to Contently, content marketing focus, decent rates.
- **ClearVoice** – You build a portfolio and get invited to projects. Mixed quality, but solid mid-to-high tier clients.
- **nDash** – Writers pitch directly to brands. Good if you’re proactive and niche-focused.

**2. Curated freelance networks (harder to get into, but worthwhile)**
- **Scripted** – Screens writers; once in, you get access to ongoing assignments.
- **Compose.ly** – Known for consistent, structured work and editing support.
- **Verblio** – More volume-based; not the highest pay, but steady.

**3. General freelance platforms (big, competitive, but flexible)**
- **Upwork** – Still one of the largest. Quality varies widely, but strong writers with a niche (like legal content) can command high rates.
- **Contra** – Growing fast, no platform fees, more modern client base.
- **Fiverr Pro** – Only the “Pro” tier is worth it at your level; otherwise too commoditized.

**4. Job boards and direct-to-client channels (often overlooked, very effective)**
- **LinkedIn** – Surprisingly one of the best sources for high-quality clients if you post or network intentionally.
- **ProBlogger Job Board** – Long-standing, decent quality listings.
- **Freelance Writing Jobs (FWJ)** – Aggregated listings, hit-or-miss but useful.
- **Superpath Jobs** – Strong for content marketing roles.

**5. Niche-specific (where you might get the best ROI given your background)**
Since you’re a senior attorney, this is where things get interesting:
- **Legal/content marketing agencies** (e.g., ones specializing in law firm SEO/blogs)
- **Expert networks** like **GLG, AlphaSights** (more consulting than writing, but overlaps)
- **Law firm marketing vendors** often hire freelance legal writers directly
- **Ghostwriting for attorneys/executives on LinkedIn** (very high demand right now)
