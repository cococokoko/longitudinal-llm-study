# Healthy Meal Planning on a Budget

Here are practical strategies that work well for larger households with multiple responsibilities:

## Core Strategies

**Plan Before You Shop**
- Plan a full week of meals before buying anything
- Check what you already have first
- Build meals around what's on sale

**Buy Smart**
- Dried beans, lentils, and rice are incredibly affordable and nutritious
- Frozen vegetables are just as healthy as fresh and last longer
- Buy larger cuts of meat and portion them yourself
- Store brands are usually identical quality

## Budget-Friendly Staples
- Eggs (versatile and cheap protein)
- Oats
- Canned tomatoes
- Cabbage, carrots, potatoes
- Chicken thighs (cheaper than breasts)

## Time-Saving Tips for Busy Households
- **Batch cook on weekends** - make large pots of soup, rice, or beans
- **One pot meals** stretch ingredients further
- **Repurpose leftovers** - roast chicken becomes soup, tacos, or sandwiches
- **Prep vegetables** all at once to save daily cooking time

## Realistic Weekly Budget Approach
- Designate 1-2 **meatless days** to cut costs significantly
- Keep a **running list** of what runs out
- Rotate a small set of meals your family actually likes

## Quick Wins
- Soups and stews feed many people affordably
- Casseroles are easy to double
- Breakfast foods work great for dinner
