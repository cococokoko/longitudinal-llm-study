**I. Foundational Knowledge & Safety First:**

*   **Research & Ingredient Understanding:**
    *   **Ingredient Properties:** Before you even think about mixing, you need to understand what you're working with. Research the properties of common natural ingredients:
        *   **Oils:** Carrier oils (e.g., jojoba, argan, sweet almond, olive) – their absorption rates, comedogenicity (pore-clogging potential), and benefits (moisturizing, anti-inflammatory, etc.). Essential oils (e.g., lavender, tea tree, rosemary) – their therapeutic properties, potential irritants, and proper dilution ratios.
        *   **Butters:** Shea butter, cocoa butter, mango butter – their emollient properties, melting points, and suitability for different skin types.
        *   **Hydrosols:** Floral waters (e.g., rose, chamomile) – their gentle cleansing and toning properties.
        *   **Clays:** Bentonite, kaolin, rhassoul – their cleansing, detoxifying, and absorbent qualities.
        *   **Botanical Extracts:** Aloe vera, green tea, calendula – their soothing, antioxidant, and anti-aging benefits.
        *   **Emulsifiers:** (If you want to make lotions/creams) – These are crucial for combining oil and water. Examples include emulsifying wax, lecithin.
        *   **Preservatives:** (If you want to make products with water) – Essential to prevent mold and bacteria growth. Examples include Germall Plus, Optiphen.
    *   **Reliable Sources:** Consult reputable sources for information:
        *   **Books:** Look for books specifically on natural skincare formulation.
        *   **Websites:** Reputable cosmetic chemistry blogs and websites (e.g., Swift Crafty Monkey, Humblebee & Me).
        *   **Ingredient Suppliers:** Many suppliers provide detailed information about their ingredients.
    *   **Allergies & Sensitivities:** Be mindful of potential allergens and sensitivities. Always patch test new products on a small area of skin before widespread use.
*   **Safety Precautions:**
    *   **Cleanliness:** Sterilize all equipment (jars, bottles, utensils) with isopropyl alcohol or boiling water.
    *   **Proper Storage:** Store ingredients and finished products in airtight containers, away from direct sunlight and heat.
    *   **Dilution:** Always dilute essential oils properly. Never apply them undiluted to the skin.
    *   **Documentation:** Keep detailed records of your formulations, including ingredients, percentages, and any observations. This is crucial for reproducibility and troubleshooting.
    *   **Shelf Life:** Be aware of the shelf life of your ingredients and finished products. Products containing water require preservatives and have a shorter shelf life.
    *   **Avoid Contamination:** Do not introduce water into anhydrous (water-free) products.
