If you’re just starting, the biggest wins early on come from keeping things simple and consistent rather than trying to learn everything at once.

A good way to begin:
- Learn a few basic open chords: G, C, D, Em, and A. Those alone let you play tons of songs.
- Practice switching between two chords at a time (like G → C → D) slowly, even if it feels clunky.
- Get a couple of easy strumming patterns under your fingers (down-down-up-up-down is a classic one).
- Use real songs as practice instead of only drills—it keeps things way more motivating.

Your fingers are probably going to hurt a bit at first. Totally normal. Short daily practice (10–20 minutes) beats long, occasional sessions.
