# Safe Travel During a Pandemic for Single Parents

Here are practical, evidence-based options to consider:

## Lowest Risk Options
- **Driving your own vehicle** - minimal contact with others
- **Staying local** - reduces exposure significantly
- **Virtual alternatives** - video calls instead of visiting family

## If You Must Travel

### Before You Go
- Check current transmission rates at your destination
- Ensure vaccinations are up to date for you and eligible children
- Pack supplies: masks, hand sanitizer, disinfecting wipes

### During Travel
- **Flying**: Choose direct flights, use airport less-crowded times, wear well-fitted masks
- **Public transit**: Travel off-peak hours, maintain distance when possible
- **Hotels**: Request rooms away from high-traffic areas, use contactless check-in

## Specific Considerations for Single Parents
- **Have a backup plan** if you or your child gets sick while traveling
- Know the **nearest medical facilities** at your destination
- Consider **travel insurance** that covers illness
- Keep **emergency contacts** easily accessible

## Practical Tips
- Bring entertainment to minimize touching shared surfaces
- Teach children hand hygiene as a routine
- Carry your own snacks to reduce stops

## Key Question to Ask Yourself
**Is this trip essential?** Weighing necessity against risk helps make the clearest decision.
