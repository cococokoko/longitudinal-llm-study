the best books to learn Design and Analysis of Algorithms I in 2023

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
If you want strong foundations in Design and Analysis of Algorithms (DAA), a mix of theory + problem-solving books works best. Here are some of the most solid picks (still very relevant around 2023 and beyond):

**Core textbooks (must-have level):**
- *Introduction to Algorithms* — Cormen, Leiserson, Rivest, Stein (CLRS)  
  The classic. Very comprehensive (covers almost everything), but can feel dense. Best as a reference + deep study book.

- *Algorithm Design* — Jon Kleinberg & Éva Tardos  
  Much more readable than CLRS. Great for understanding *why* algorithms work, especially greedy, divide-and-conquer, and dynamic programming.

- *Algorithms* — Dasgupta, Papadimitriou, Vazirani  
  Clean, concise, and very student-friendly. Excellent for building intuition without drowning in detail.

**More practical / coding-oriented:**
- *Algorithms* — Robert Sedgewick & Kevin Wayne  
  Good mix of theory and implementation (Java-based). Helpful visuals and explanations.

- *Grokking Algorithms* — Aditya Bhargava  
  Beginner-friendly, very visual. Not deep enough alone, but great if you're new to DAA.

**For problem-solving (very important):**
- *The Algorithm Design Manual* — Steven Skiena  
  Super practical. Teaches how to *approach* problems, not just theory. Includes lots of real-world insights.

- *Competitive Programming* — Halim & Halim  
  Great if you want to apply algorithms under pressure (coding contests, interviews).

**If you want something lighter to start:**
- Start with *Grokking Algorithms* → then move to *Kleinberg & Tardos* → use *CLRS* as a reference.

**If you're aiming for depth (CS degree level):**
- Combine *CLRS* + *Dasgupta* + *Skiena*
