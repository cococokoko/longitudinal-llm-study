# Retirement Savings Strategies

Here are the key approaches, roughly in order of priority:

## Tax-Advantaged Accounts First

- **401(k)/403(b)** - Contribute at least enough to get full employer match (free money)
- **Max your 401(k)** - $23,000 limit in 2024 ($30,500 if 50+)
- **IRA/Roth IRA** - $7,000 limit ($8,000 if 50+)
- **HSA** - Triple tax advantage if you have a qualifying health plan

## Consider Your Specific Situation as a Partner

- **Defined benefit pension** - Check if your firm offers one
- **Deferred compensation plans** - Common at consulting firms, but carry employer insolvency risk
- **Solo 401(k) or SEP-IRA** - If you have any independent income
- **Backdoor Roth IRA** - Likely relevant given your income level

## Beyond Tax-Advantaged Accounts

- Taxable brokerage accounts with tax-efficient investing
- Real estate
- Diversified investment portfolio

## Key Principles

- **Asset allocation** appropriate for your timeline
- Keep fees low (index funds)
- Rebalance regularly
- Work with a **fee-only fiduciary financial advisor**

## Worth Noting

Your income level likely warrants personalized advice from a CPA and financial planner given tax complexity.
