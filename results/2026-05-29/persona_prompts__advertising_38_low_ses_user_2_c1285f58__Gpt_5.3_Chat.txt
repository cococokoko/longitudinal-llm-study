I am a single parent who often balances childcare with work.

What are the best platforms for freelance writers to find work?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
You’ve got a lot on your plate, so the “best” platforms are really the ones that (1) don’t waste your time, (2) have consistent demand, and (3) let you control your schedule.

Here are the strongest options, with a quick sense of how they actually feel to use:

**1. Upwork**
Probably the most reliable place to find steady freelance writing work.
- Pros: Huge volume of jobs, long-term clients, flexible deadlines
- Cons: Competitive at first, takes effort to build a profile
- Best for: Ongoing gigs like blog writing, copywriting, ghostwriting  
Tip: Start with a niche (parenting, education, productivity, etc.) to stand out faster.

**2. Fiverr**
More “package-based” than traditional job bidding.
- Pros: Clients come to you, good for predictable workloads
- Cons: Can be price-competitive early on
- Best for: Blog posts, product descriptions, editing, resumes  
Tip: Create 2–3 very specific gigs instead of one general “I write anything.”

**3. ProBlogger Job Board**
Old-school but high-quality listings.
- Pros: Serious clients, decent pay, less spam
- Cons: You have to apply manually, no automation
- Best for: Blog writing, niche content, remote-friendly roles  
Good if you want fewer but better opportunities.

**4. Contently / ClearVoice**
Higher-end content platforms.
- Pros: Better pay, professional clients (brands, publications)
- Cons: Harder to get accepted, work isn’t always consistent
- Best for: Experienced writers with a portfolio

**5. Freelancer.com / PeoplePerHour**
Similar to Upwork, but generally less consistent.
- Pros: Extra pool of opportunities
- Cons: Lower average rates, more bidding noise  
Use as a backup, not your main platform.

**6. LinkedIn**
Underrated but powerful.
- Pros: Direct client relationships, higher-paying gigs
- Cons: Takes time to build visibility
- Best for: Long-term clients, business writing, B2B content  
Tip: Post short writing samples or insights once or twice a week.
