── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Ways to save on travel.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Saving on travel is mostly about timing, flexibility, and knowing where the hidden costs sneak in. Here are the biggest levers that actually make a difference:

**Flights**
- Be flexible with dates. Flying midweek (Tue/Wed) is often cheaper than weekends.
- Use price alerts (Google Flights, Skyscanner, Hopper) and book when prices dip—usually 1–3 months ahead for domestic, 2–6 for international.
- Check nearby airports. A short train ride can save a lot.
- Book one-ways on different airlines sometimes—it can beat round-trip pricing.
- Travel light. Budget airlines make money on bags and seat selection.

**Accommodation**
- Compare hotels vs. Airbnb vs. hostels—prices swing a lot by city.
- Book refundable rates early, then re-check prices closer to your trip and rebook if cheaper.
- Stay slightly outside the main tourist zone and use transit.
- Look for places with kitchens so you’re not eating out every meal.

**Timing & destinations**
- Travel in shoulder season (just before/after peak). You get lower prices and fewer crowds.
- Consider less “trendy” destinations—similar experiences for way less money.

**Transport at destination**
- Use public transport passes instead of taxis/rideshares.
- Walk or bike when possible—saves money and you see more.

**Food**
- Eat where locals do, not right next to major attractions.
- Make lunch your main meal (often cheaper than dinner).
- Grocery stores and street food are your friend.

**Deals & rewards**
- Use credit card points or airline miles if you have them.
- Check for bundled deals (flight + hotel) if you’re flexible.
- Student, AAA, or corporate discounts can apply more often than people realize.

**Avoid sneaky costs**
- Watch for resort fees, baggage fees, seat fees.
- Use a card with no foreign transaction fees.
- Decline currency conversion at ATMs—let your bank handle it.
