── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I am a physician-scientist leading a research group.

What are the best resources for starting a plant-based diet?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here are some well-regarded resources across different formats:

## Evidence-Based Books
- **"How Not to Die" by Michael Greger, MD** - cites primary literature extensively
- **"The China Study" by T. Colin Campbell** - though note some methodological criticisms exist
- **"Eat to Live" by Joel Fuhrman, MD**

## Academic/Clinical Resources
- **Physicians Committee for Responsible Medicine (PCRM)** - pcrm.org, clinically oriented
- **NutritionFacts.org** - Greger's site, links to primary studies
- **Academy of Nutrition and Dietetics position papers** on plant-based diets

## Practical Guides
- **"The Plant-Based Diet for Beginners" by Gabriel Miller**
- Meal planning apps like **Cronometer** - useful for tracking nutrient adequacy

## Key Nutritional Considerations Worth Flagging
- B12 supplementation is generally necessary
- Monitor vitamin D, omega-3s, iron, zinc, calcium, iodine
- Protein completeness is manageable but worth attention

## Peer-Reviewed Starting Points
- JAMA Internal Medicine and BMJ have published solid systematic reviews on plant-based dietary patterns

## Honest Caveat
The evidence quality varies considerably across this literature - you're well-positioned to critically evaluate primary sources directly.
