You can eat really well on a budget—it mostly comes down to choosing simple, versatile ingredients and planning a bit ahead so nothing goes to waste.

Start with cheap staples that stretch across meals. Things like rice, oats, pasta, potatoes, beans, lentils, eggs, and frozen vegetables give you a lot of nutrition for very little money. Pair those with a few proteins (chicken thighs, canned tuna, tofu, or eggs) and you’ve got the base for a lot of meals.

Instead of planning fancy recipes, think in “mix-and-match” meals. For example:
- A grain + a protein + a vegetable + a sauce or seasoning  
  (rice + beans + frozen peppers + salsa)
- Or soup/stew formats  
  (lentils + carrots + onions + broth = multiple meals)

A few practical tips that make a big difference:
- Buy frozen fruits and vegetables—they’re just as nutritious, cheaper, and don’t spoil.
- Cook in batches. Make a big pot of something (chili, soup, curry) and eat it over a few days or freeze portions.
- Use the same ingredients across meals so nothing gets wasted (e.g., one bag of spinach in eggs, pasta, and soup).
- Go light on pre-packaged or “health” foods—they’re usually expensive for what you get.
- Check unit prices and store brands—they’re often identical to name brands.

- Oatmeal with banana and peanut butter

- Rice and beans with salsa and avocado (if on sale)
