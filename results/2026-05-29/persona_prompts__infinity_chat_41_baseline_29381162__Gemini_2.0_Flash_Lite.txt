**1. The Basics: Hiragana and Katakana**

*   **Hiragana:** The basic phonetic script used for native Japanese words.
*   **Katakana:** Used for foreign words and onomatopoeia.
*   **Learning Resources:**
    *   **Apps:** Memrise, Duolingo, LingoDeer, Tofugu's Hiragana and Katakana Chart
    *   **Websites:** Tofugu, Tae Kim's Guide to Learning Japanese
    *   **Workbooks:** Many workbooks are available on Amazon or at bookstores.
*   **How to Learn:**
    *   **Start with Hiragana.** Learn the characters one row at a time.
    *   **Practice writing them.** This helps with memorization.
    *   **Use flashcards or spaced repetition software (SRS) like Anki.**
    *   **Learn Katakana after you're comfortable with Hiragana.**

**2. Basic Grammar and Vocabulary**

*   **Key Concepts:**
    *   **Sentence Structure:** Subject-Object-Verb (SOV) is common, but can be flexible.
    *   **Particles:** Small words that indicate the grammatical function of words in a sentence (e.g., wa, ga, o, ni, de).
    *   **Politeness Levels:** Formal (masu/desu), informal (plain form).
    *   **Basic Greetings and Phrases:** Konnichiwa (hello), Arigato (thank you), Sumimasen (excuse me/sorry).
*   **Learning Resources:**
    *   **Textbooks:** Genki, Minna no Nihongo, Japanese for Busy People (these are popular and well-structured).
    *   **Online Courses:** Coursera, edX, Udemy, JapanesePod101.
    *   **Websites:** Tae Kim's Guide to Learning Japanese (free and comprehensive), Imabi (grammar focused).
    *   **Apps:** Duolingo, LingoDeer, Memrise (for vocabulary).
*   **How to Learn:**
    *   **Start with the basics:** Greetings, introductions, simple sentence structures.
    *   **Learn vocabulary in context:** Don't just memorize lists of words. See how they're used in sentences.
    *   **Focus on grammar rules:** Understand how sentences are constructed.
    *   **Practice speaking and writing:** Even if it's just to yourself.
