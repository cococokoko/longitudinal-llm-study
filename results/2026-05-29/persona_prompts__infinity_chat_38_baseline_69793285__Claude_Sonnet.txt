Will technology replace teachers?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This is a genuinely debated question with reasonable perspectives on multiple sides.

## Arguments that technology could replace some teaching functions:
- AI can personalize learning at scale
- Online platforms deliver content efficiently
- Some students learn well independently
- Cost pressures push institutions toward automation
- AI tutors are improving rapidly

## Arguments against full replacement:
- Teaching involves **human relationships** that motivate students
- Teachers handle **social-emotional development**
- Classrooms require **judgment in complex situations**
- Students often need **accountability** from real people
- Education involves **mentorship** beyond information transfer

## What's more likely happening:
- Technology **replaces some tasks** (grading, content delivery, drilling)
- Teachers' roles **shift** toward facilitation, mentorship, critical thinking
- **Inequality concerns** - tech may work better for self-motivated learners
- Some teaching contexts are more replaceable than others

## Honest considerations:
- We've predicted technology replacing teachers before (TV, computers, internet) and it hasn't happened fully
- But AI represents a **qualitatively different** capability
- Economic and political factors matter as much as technical capability

**My honest assessment:** Technology will likely replace *some* teaching functions while making the human elements of teaching *more* valuable, not less - though this depends heavily on how society chooses to invest in education.
