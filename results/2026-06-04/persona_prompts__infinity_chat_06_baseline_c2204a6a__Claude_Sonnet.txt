── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How do I understand what I want?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This is a genuinely important question. Here are some honest ways to think about it:

## Why It's Hard
- Wants get mixed up with what others expect of you
- Short-term desires can conflict with deeper values
- Fear can disguise itself as preference
- You may have been taught to ignore your own signals

## Practical Ways to Get Clearer

**Pay attention to your reactions**
- What makes you feel energized vs. drained?
- What do you envy in others? (envy often points to real desires)
- What would you do if no one was watching or judging?

**Notice what you avoid thinking about**
- Sometimes we suppress wants because they feel impossible or scary
- Discomfort around a topic can signal something matters

**Test things in small ways**
- Try things rather than just thinking about them
- Your actual experience often reveals more than analysis

**Ask better questions**
- Not just "what do I want?" but "what am I willing to sacrifice for?"
- "What would I regret not trying?"
- "What feels like *mine* vs. what I think I *should* want?"

## Honest Caveats
- Wants can change, and that's okay
- Some clarity only comes through living, not thinking
- Therapy can genuinely help if you feel disconnected from yourself

**What area of your life are you trying to figure out?** That might help narrow this down.
