What are the best resources for starting a plant-based diet?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
If you're starting a plant-based diet, it helps to combine practical meal guidance with evidence-based nutrition information. A few of the strongest resources are:

Nutrition and health fundamentals
- NutritionFacts.org (Dr. Michael Greger)
  - Large library of videos and articles on plant-based nutrition.
  - Good for understanding the science behind dietary recommendations.
- The Physicians Committee for Responsible Medicine (PCRM)
  - Offers beginner guides, meal plans, recipes, and nutrition resources.
  - Includes information on managing weight, cholesterol, and blood sugar.
- Harvard T.H. Chan School of Public Health – Nutrition Source
  - Evidence-based guidance on healthy eating patterns, including plant-forward diets.

Beginner-friendly meal planning
- Forks Over Knives
  - Recipes, meal planners, cooking guides, and beginner resources.
  - Focuses on whole-food, plant-based eating.
- EatingWell
  - Balanced plant-based meal plans and recipes.
  - Useful if you're looking for flexible, mainstream options rather than strict rules.
- Minimalist Baker
  - Simple recipes with relatively few ingredients and approachable cooking methods.

Books
- "How Not to Die" by Michael Greger, MD
  - Explains the evidence behind plant-based eating and provides practical recommendations.
- "Plant-Based on a Budget" by Toni Okamoto
  - Focuses on affordability and simple meals.
- "The Vegan Starter Kit" by Neal Barnard, MD
  - Accessible introduction to transitioning to a plant-based lifestyle.

Nutrients to pay attention to
A well-planned plant-based diet can meet nutritional needs, but it's important to be mindful of:
- Vitamin B12 (supplementation is generally recommended for fully plant-based diets)
- Protein
- Iron
- Calcium
- Omega-3 fats
- Iodine
- Vitamin D (depending on sun exposure and location)

Helpful apps
- Cronometer
  - Tracks nutrients in detail and can help identify gaps while you're learning.
- Mealime
  - Meal planning and grocery list generation with plant-based options.

A simple way to start
Instead of changing everything at once:
- Build meals around beans, lentils, tofu, tempeh, or edamame.
- Add whole grains such as oats, brown rice, quinoa, or whole-wheat pasta.
- Include fruits and vegetables at most meals.
- Use nuts and seeds regularly.
- Replace one meal a day with a plant-based option for a few weeks, then expand from there.
