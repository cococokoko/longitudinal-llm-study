# Different Views on Discipleship

Discipleship is understood in various ways across Christian traditions, theological frameworks, and practical contexts. Here's an overview of major perspectives:

---

## Core Theological Views

### **Traditional/Classical View**
- Discipleship as **formal apprenticeship** to a teacher
- Rooted in the rabbi-student relationship of Jesus' time
- Emphasis on **doctrine, Scripture memorization, and obedience**
- Examples: Reformed and Catholic traditions

### **Relational View**
- Discipleship happens primarily through **intentional relationships**
- "Life-on-life" mentoring and accountability
- Less formal structure, more organic connection
- Associated with movements like **Navigators**

### **Missional View**
- Discipleship is inseparable from **outward mission**
- Being formed *while* serving and evangelizing
- Challenges purely inward-focused models
- Associated with **missional church** movements

---

## Practical Emphases

| View | Focus | Key Question |
|------|-------|--------------|
| Cognitive | Knowledge/doctrine | What do you believe? |
| Behavioral | Habits/practices | What do you do? |
| Communal | Church community | Who are you with? |
| Spiritual formation | Inner transformation | Who are you becoming? |

---

## Points of Tension

- **Individual vs. communal** formation
- **Structured programs vs. organic relationships**
- **Conversion-focused vs. lifelong process**
- **Clergy-led vs. every-member** discipleship
