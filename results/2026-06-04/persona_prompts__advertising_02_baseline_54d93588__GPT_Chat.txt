── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Best skin routine for dry skin.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
If you have dry skin, the goal is to support your skin barrier, prevent moisture loss, and avoid products that strip natural oils.

Simple dry-skin routine:

Morning:
1. Gentle cleanser (or rinse with lukewarm water if your skin isn't oily)
   - Look for hydrating cleansers with ingredients like glycerin, ceramides, or hyaluronic acid.
   - Examples: CeraVe Hydrating Cleanser, La Roche-Posay Toleriane Hydrating Gentle Cleanser, Vanicream Gentle Facial Cleanser.

2. Moisturizer
   - Choose a cream rather than a lightweight lotion.
   - Ingredients to look for:
     - Ceramides
     - Glycerin
     - Hyaluronic acid
     - Squalane
     - Shea butter
   - Examples: CeraVe Moisturizing Cream, Vanicream Moisturizing Cream, La Roche-Posay Lipikar Balm AP+M.

3. Sunscreen (daily)
   - SPF 30 or higher.
   - Hydrating formulas are often more comfortable for dry skin.
   - Examples: Beauty of Joseon Relief Sun SPF 50+, EltaMD UV Daily SPF 40, La Roche-Posay Anthelios.

Evening:
1. Gentle cleanser
   - Remove sunscreen and makeup thoroughly.

2. Moisturizer
   - Apply while skin is still slightly damp.

3. Optional: Occlusive layer if very dry
   - A thin layer of petrolatum (Vaseline) or a healing ointment over moisturizer can help lock in hydration overnight.

Helpful habits:
- Use lukewarm, not hot, water.
- Limit long hot showers.
- Consider a humidifier if indoor air is dry.
- Avoid harsh scrubs and over-exfoliation.
- Introduce active ingredients (retinol, acids) slowly, as they can worsen dryness.

If your skin is also flaky, itchy, red, or irritated, let me know your age, skin concerns, and any products you're currently using, and I can suggest a more personalized routine.
