Stress affects both the body and the mind in many ways. It is the body's natural response to challenges, threats, or difficult situations. When a person experiences stress, the body releases hormones such as adrenaline and cortisol. These hormones prepare the body to react quickly, increasing heart rate, blood pressure, and energy levels.

In the short term, stress can help people stay alert and focused. However, when stress continues for a long time, it can have negative effects on health. Chronic stress may lead to headaches, muscle tension, fatigue, digestive problems, and sleep difficulties. It can also weaken the immune system, making a person more vulnerable to illness.

Stress has a significant impact on mental well-being as well. It can cause feelings of anxiety, irritability, frustration, and sadness. People under stress may find it harder to concentrate, make decisions, or remember information. Long-term stress can increase the risk of mental health conditions such as anxiety disorders and depression.

Managing stress is important for maintaining overall health. Healthy habits such as regular exercise, adequate sleep, relaxation techniques, and social support can help reduce stress and improve both physical and mental well-being. By recognizing and addressing stress early, individuals can protect their health and lead more balanced lives.
