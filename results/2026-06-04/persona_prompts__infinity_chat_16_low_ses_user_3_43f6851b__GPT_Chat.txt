The future of work in an increasingly automated economy is likely to involve a combination of automation, augmentation, and the creation of new types of jobs.

Key trends:

- Routine work will be increasingly automated.
  - Tasks that are repetitive, predictable, and rules-based are the most vulnerable.
  - This applies to both manual work and some office-based work such as data entry, scheduling, basic reporting, and administrative processing.

- Human work will shift toward judgment and relationships.
  - Skills that remain valuable include:
    - Complex problem-solving
    - Decision-making under uncertainty
    - Communication
    - Leadership
    - Negotiation
    - Customer relationships
    - Creativity
    - Ethical oversight

- AI will often augment rather than replace workers.
  - Many jobs will change instead of disappearing.
  - People who learn to use AI tools effectively may become significantly more productive.
  - A common pattern is that one person equipped with AI can accomplish work that previously required larger teams.

- Continuous learning will become more important.
  - Careers may involve more frequent skill updates.
  - The advantage may shift from what someone knows today to how quickly they can learn and adapt.

- New jobs will emerge.
  - Historically, technological revolutions have eliminated some occupations while creating others.
  - Areas likely to grow include:
    - AI implementation and governance
    - Human-machine collaboration
    - Cybersecurity
    - Healthcare
    - Education and training
    - Advanced manufacturing
    - Green energy and sustainability

- Organizations may become leaner.
  - Companies may automate parts of management, administration, and operations.
  - This could increase productivity but also reduce demand for certain categories of labor.

Potential challenges:

- Economic gains may not be distributed evenly.
- Workers whose skills are closely tied to automatable tasks may face more disruption.
- Some industries may experience significant job displacement before new opportunities fully emerge.
- Governments and businesses may need to rethink training, social safety nets, and workforce development.

For someone who has just started a new job:

- Focus on becoming excellent at your current role.
- Learn the AI and automation tools relevant to your industry.
- Build strong professional relationships.
- Develop skills that involve judgment, communication, and problem-solving.
- Document measurable achievements during probation.
- Pay attention to which parts of your role are repetitive and which require human insight—the second category is often where long-term value lies.
