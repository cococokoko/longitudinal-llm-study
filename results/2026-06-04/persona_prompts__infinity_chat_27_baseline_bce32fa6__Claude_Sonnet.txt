# CUBICLE OF THE DAMNED
### A Play in Three Acts
*In the Style of Scott Adams' Dilbert*
*Set in a Post-Apocalyptic Wasteland, Circa: Sometime After Everything Went Wrong*

---

## CAST OF CHARACTERS

**DILBERT** — An engineer. Somehow still wearing his tie. The tie curls upward at the end, as always. This has not changed despite civilization's collapse.

**THE POINTY-HAIRED BOSS (PHB)** — Survived the apocalypse through sheer managerial incompetence. Leads a tribe of survivors from a folding table he calls "the executive suite."

**WALLY** — Has not done any actual work since before the apocalypse. Thriving.

**ALICE** — Still the most competent person in any given wasteland. Still not promoted.

**DOGBERT** — A small dog with enormous ambitions. Currently self-appointed Warlord of the Eastern Parking Structure.

**ASOK** — The intern. Has been "the intern" for eleven years. The apocalypse did not change his employment status.

**CATBERT** — Former Evil Director of Human Resources. Now Evil Director of Human Remains. Considers this a lateral move.

**THE RAT KING** — A mutant warlord who has come to negotiate. Surprisingly reasonable compared to PHB.

---

## ACT ONE: THE MORNING STANDUP

*SCENE: The ruins of an open-plan office. Several cubicle walls still stand, though the ceiling is mostly gone. A nuclear sky glows orange overhead. DILBERT sits at a desk made from rubble, staring at a laptop that runs on a hand-crank generator. WALLY sits nearby holding a coffee mug. The mug is empty. It has been empty for three years. He holds it anyway.*

---

**DILBERT:** *(typing)* I've finished the structural analysis of the fortified perimeter wall. It should hold against raider attacks for approximately forty years.

**WALLY:** That's impressive.

**DILBERT:** The Boss asked me to do it in PowerPoint.

**WALLY:** How many slides?

**DILBERT:** He wants forty-seven slides. One for each year of projected structural integrity.

**WALLY:** Did you explain that the wall either holds or it doesn't, and that a slide deck has no bearing on the laws of physics?

**DILBERT:** I did. He said I was being resistant to feedback.

**WALLY:** *(nodding slowly)* The apocalypse really hasn't changed anything, has it.

**DILBERT:** I thought it might. I was optimistic for about a week.

**WALLY:** What happened?

**DILBERT:** He scheduled a meeting about the meeting we were going to have about rebuilding society.

**WALLY:** How long was the meeting?

**DILBERT:** Three hours. We established that society was, in fact, gone, and agreed to circle back on next steps.

**WALLY:** Did you circle back?

**DILBERT:** He sent a follow-up email. The server has been down since the bombs fell, so no one received it. He considers this a communication problem on our end.

*PHB enters, wearing his usual suit. One shoulder is on fire. He does not notice.*

**ALICE:** *(through gritted teeth)* That is... actually true.
