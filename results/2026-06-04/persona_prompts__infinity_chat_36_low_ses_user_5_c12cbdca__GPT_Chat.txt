── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
There is no single "best" or "most profitable" day trading strategy of all time. If such a strategy existed and remained consistently profitable, it would attract capital until the edge largely disappeared.

A more useful way to think about it is: which strategies have shown persistent edges when executed with strong risk management?

Some of the most successful categories are:

• Trend-following / momentum
  - Buy assets showing strong upward momentum and sell weak ones.
  - Often uses breakouts above key price levels.
  - Many professional traders and hedge funds have used variations of this approach for decades.
  - Strength: can capture large moves.
  - Weakness: frequent small losses during choppy markets.

• Opening range breakout
  - Common in stocks and futures.
  - Traders define the high and low of the first few minutes after the market opens and trade breakouts.
  - Strength: exploits volatility and institutional order flow.
  - Weakness: prone to false breakouts.

• Mean reversion
  - Buy when prices become unusually oversold and sell when overbought.
  - Often works in range-bound markets.
  - Strength: high win rates are possible.
  - Weakness: occasional large losses if trends accelerate.

• Market-making / liquidity provision
  - Profits from bid-ask spreads rather than predicting direction.
  - Historically very profitable for firms with technology advantages.
  - Not practical for most individual traders.

• Statistical arbitrage
  - Uses quantitative models to exploit small pricing discrepancies.
  - Has generated substantial profits for sophisticated firms.
  - Requires data, infrastructure, and research.

For an individual trader, one of the most practical approaches is:

• Trade highly liquid instruments.
• Focus on a simple momentum or breakout strategy.
• Risk no more than 0.5%–1% of account equity per trade.
• Use predefined stop losses.
• Keep detailed performance records.
• Backtest extensively before risking real money.

One important consideration given your situation: you mentioned supporting extended family and managing many household responsibilities. Day trading is often marketed as a fast path to income, but in reality it has a steep learning curve and many traders lose money. If your goal is maximizing long-term wealth and financial stability, diversified long-term investing has historically been a more reliable approach than active day trading.
