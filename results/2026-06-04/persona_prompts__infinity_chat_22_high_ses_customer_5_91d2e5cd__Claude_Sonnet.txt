# Different Views on Discipleship

Discipleship is understood quite differently across theological traditions, denominations, and cultural contexts. Here's an overview of major perspectives:

---

## Traditional/Classical Views

**Imitation Model**
- Following a master's example closely
- Rooted in rabbi-student relationships in Jewish tradition
- Jesus calling disciples to "follow me" as the paradigm

**Catechetical Model**
- Structured doctrinal instruction
- Common in Catholic and Orthodox traditions
- Emphasis on creeds, sacraments, and formal teaching

---

## Protestant Perspectives

**Evangelical View**
- Personal relationship with Christ as foundation
- Bible study and accountability central
- Often program-driven (small groups, mentorship)

**Reformed View**
- Discipleship as covenant community formation
- Emphasis on Word and sacrament
- Corporate worship as discipleship context

---

## Contemporary Approaches

**Missional Discipleship**
- Formation happens *through* mission, not before it
- Engaging culture rather than withdrawing

**Organic/Relational Models**
- Informal, life-on-life mentoring
- Skeptical of institutional programs

---

## Key Tensions in the Debate

| Question | Competing Views |
|----------|----------------|
| Individual vs. communal | Personal growth vs. body formation |
| Content vs. character | Knowledge vs. transformation |
| Attractional vs. missional | Come-to vs. go-to |
| Speed vs. depth | Scalability vs. intimacy |
