## The short answer: Yes, it's impossible to know everything

Here's why this is pretty well-established:

**Practical limits**
- There's simply too much information - it grows faster than any person could absorb
- Time and cognitive capacity are finite
- Your unpredictable schedule illustrates this - you can't even fully know your own near future

**Deeper logical limits**
- Gödel's incompleteness theorems showed even mathematics has truths that can't be proven within a system
- Some things are genuinely uncertain or random
- Knowing everything would require knowing all future events, which may be fundamentally impossible

**What's worth considering though**
- "Knowing everything" and "knowing enough" are very different standards
- Expertise in specific areas is genuinely achievable
- Wisdom about *how to think* might matter more than accumulating facts

## The honest nuance
Some philosophers debate what "knowing" even means precisely, so the question has layers.

But practically speaking - **no person, and arguably no system, can know everything**. This isn't a personal limitation, it's a feature of reality.
