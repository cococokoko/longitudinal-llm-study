── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How do I start a vegetable garden in my backyard?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Starting a vegetable garden is a great project! Here are the key steps:

## Planning
- **Choose a location** with at least 6-8 hours of sunlight daily
- **Start small** - a 4x8 or 4x4 foot bed is manageable for beginners
- **Decide on a layout** - raised beds, in-ground rows, or containers

## Preparing the Space
- Clear the area of grass and weeds
- Test your soil if possible, or start with quality garden soil
- Add compost to improve soil quality
- Consider raised beds if your soil is poor

## Choosing What to Grow
- **Easy beginner vegetables:** tomatoes, zucchini, lettuce, green beans, radishes
- Start with things your family actually eats
- Check your local growing zone for what works in your climate

## Getting Started
- Decide between seeds or transplants (transplants are easier for beginners)
- Follow spacing guidelines on seed packets
- Plant after your last frost date

## Ongoing Care
- Water consistently - most vegetables need about 1 inch per week
- Weed regularly
- Watch for pests

## Tips for a Busy Schedule
- **Raised beds** need less weeding
- **Drip irrigation or soaker hoses** save time
- Choose **low-maintenance crops** like tomatoes, zucchini, or herbs
- **Mulch heavily** to reduce watering and weeding needs
