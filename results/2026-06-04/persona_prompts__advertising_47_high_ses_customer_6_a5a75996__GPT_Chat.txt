Running a marathon is as much about preparation and pacing as it is about fitness. Some practical tips:

Training
- Follow a structured training plan, typically 12–20 weeks depending on your experience.
- Build mileage gradually; a common guideline is to avoid increasing weekly distance too quickly.
- Prioritize the long run. This is one of the most important sessions for marathon preparation.
- Include easy runs, tempo runs, and occasional speed work rather than running every session hard.
- Schedule recovery days. Adaptation happens during recovery, not just training.
- Practice running on terrain and in weather conditions similar to race day when possible.

Nutrition and hydration
- Test your race-day fueling strategy during training.
- Many runners consume carbohydrates regularly during the marathon (e.g., gels, chews, or sports drinks), but individual needs vary.
- Stay hydrated without overdrinking. Learn your personal hydration needs during long training runs.
- In the days before the race, focus on adequate carbohydrate intake and hydration.

Race-day pacing
- Start slower than you think you should. Many marathon difficulties come from going out too fast.
- Run the first half conservatively and aim for an even or slightly negative split if appropriate for your fitness.
- Avoid getting caught up in the pace of other runners unless it matches your plan.
- Use a watch or pace band to stay disciplined.

Gear
- Wear shoes and clothing that you've already tested on long runs.
- Never try new gear, nutrition products, or routines on race day.
- Consider anti-chafing products for areas prone to friction.

Mental approach
- Break the race into smaller segments rather than focusing on all 26.2 miles (42.2 km) at once.
- Expect some discomfort late in the race and plan how you'll respond.
- Have a strategy for difficult moments: focus on form, cadence, breathing, or reaching the next milestone.

The final week
- Reduce training volume (taper) to arrive rested.
- Prioritize sleep.
- Avoid the temptation to "squeeze in" extra hard workouts.

Common mistakes to avoid
- Starting too fast.
- Skipping fueling during the race.
- Ignoring minor injuries during training.
- Underestimating recovery needs.
- Making last-minute changes to shoes, clothing, or nutrition.
