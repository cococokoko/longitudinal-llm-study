── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Decorating your home on a budget is often about prioritizing high-impact changes and being selective about where you spend money.

Some practical strategies:

- Start with a plan
  - Choose a color palette and overall style before buying anything.
  - Focus on one room at a time to avoid scattered purchases.

- Rearrange what you already own
  - Moving furniture, artwork, or accessories between rooms can make a space feel new at no cost.
  - Remove clutter to create a cleaner, more intentional look.

- Use paint strategically
  - A fresh coat of paint is one of the most cost-effective upgrades.
  - Consider painting an accent wall, furniture, or cabinets instead of replacing them.

- Shop secondhand
  - Check thrift stores, estate sales, flea markets, and online marketplaces.
  - Solid wood furniture can often be refinished for a fraction of the cost of buying new.

- Focus on textiles
  - New throw pillows, blankets, curtains, or rugs can dramatically change a room's appearance.
  - Mixing textures often creates a more expensive look than spending heavily on furniture.

- Upgrade lighting
  - Replace outdated lampshades or fixtures where feasible.
  - Warm lighting can make a room feel more inviting and polished.

- Add plants
  - Houseplants can bring life and color to a space at relatively low cost.
  - Even a few small plants can make a noticeable difference.

- Create inexpensive wall art
  - Frame personal photos, prints, maps, or pages from old books.
  - Large-scale artwork can often be created more cheaply than purchased.

- Use mirrors
  - Mirrors can make rooms feel larger and brighter by reflecting light.

- Prioritize visible areas
  - Spend your limited budget on the rooms and features guests and household members notice most frequently.

A common budget-friendly approach is to allocate funds roughly in this order: paint, lighting, textiles, and then furniture upgrades only where necessary. This often provides the greatest visual improvement per dollar spent.
