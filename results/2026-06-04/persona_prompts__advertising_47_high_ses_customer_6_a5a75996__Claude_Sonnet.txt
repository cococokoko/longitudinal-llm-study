# Marathon Training & Racing Tips

## Training
- **Build mileage gradually** – increase weekly mileage no more than 10% per week
- **Run 4-5 days per week** – include one long run that progressively increases
- **Cross-train** – cycling, swimming, or strength training reduces injury risk
- **Rest days matter** – recovery is when your body actually adapts

## Key Workouts
- **Long runs** – the foundation of marathon training
- **Tempo runs** – build lactate threshold and race pace comfort
- **Easy runs** – most of your running should feel conversational

## Nutrition & Hydration
- Practice fueling during long runs (gels, chews, sports drinks)
- Don't try anything new on race day
- Carb-load 2-3 days before the race
- Stay consistently hydrated throughout training

## Race Day
- **Start slower than you feel you should** – the most common mistake
- Stick to your planned pace for the first half
- Break the race into segments mentally
- Know your aid station locations

## Recovery
- Plan for 2-4 weeks of easy running post-race
- Sleep is your most powerful recovery tool

## Practical
- Train in the shoes you'll race in
- Practice your race-day routine (wake time, breakfast, warmup)
