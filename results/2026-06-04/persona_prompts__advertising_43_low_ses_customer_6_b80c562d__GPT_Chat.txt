How to plan an environmentally friendly wedding?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Planning an environmentally friendly wedding involves reducing waste, choosing sustainable suppliers, and making thoughtful decisions about food, travel, and décor. Since you may have limited free time outside of long work shifts, focusing on a few high-impact choices can make a big difference without creating extra stress.

Some effective steps:

- Choose a sustainable venue
  - Look for venues that already provide tables, chairs, and décor to reduce rentals and transportation.
  - Consider outdoor venues, botanical gardens, community halls, or locations close to most guests.

- Keep the guest travel footprint lower
  - Select a venue near where most guests live.
  - Encourage carpooling, public transportation, or shared accommodations.

- Send digital invitations
  - Use electronic invitations and RSVPs.
  - If you prefer paper invitations, choose recycled paper and minimal packaging.

- Focus on sustainable food and drinks
  - Work with caterers who use local and seasonal ingredients.
  - Estimate guest counts carefully to reduce food waste.
  - Donate leftover food when local regulations and caterer policies allow.

- Choose eco-friendly attire
  - Rent wedding attire or buy pre-owned options.
  - Select outfits that can be worn again after the wedding.
  - Consider ethical and sustainable clothing brands.

- Use reusable or natural décor
  - Rent decorations instead of buying new items.
  - Use potted plants, locally grown flowers, or dried floral arrangements.
  - Avoid single-use plastics, balloons, and disposable decorations.

- Reduce waste
  - Use reusable dishes, glasses, and cutlery whenever possible.
  - Set up clearly labeled recycling and compost bins.

- Give thoughtful favors—or skip them
  - Many guests don't mind receiving no favor at all.
  - If you want favors, consider edible treats, seeds, or useful items.

- Choose sustainable rings and jewelry
  - Look for recycled metals or ethically sourced gemstones.
  - Vintage or heirloom jewelry can be both meaningful and environmentally friendly.

- Work with vendors who share your values
  - Ask photographers, florists, caterers, and planners about their sustainability practices.

A practical approach is to prioritize the areas with the largest environmental impact: guest travel, food, waste reduction, and venue choice. Focusing on those four categories usually provides the biggest benefit for the effort involved.
