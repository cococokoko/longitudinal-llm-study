Improving productivity while working from home often comes down to creating structure, reducing distractions, and managing energy effectively. Here are some practical strategies:

- Create a dedicated workspace
  - Use a specific area for work if possible.
  - Keep it organized and stocked with what you need.
  - Separate work and personal spaces to help maintain focus.

- Establish a routine
  - Start and end work at consistent times.
  - Create a short morning routine that signals the beginning of your workday.
  - Schedule breaks and lunch periods.

- Plan your day
  - Identify your top 3 priorities each morning.
  - Break large tasks into smaller, actionable steps.
  - Use a calendar or task management tool to stay organized.

- Minimize distractions
  - Turn off unnecessary notifications.
  - Set specific times to check email and messages.
  - Use website blockers if certain sites regularly interrupt your focus.

- Use focused work sessions
  - Try techniques such as working for 25–50 minutes followed by a short break.
  - Reserve your highest-energy periods for your most important work.

- Communicate clearly
  - Set expectations with coworkers about availability and response times.
  - Share progress updates proactively when working remotely.

- Manage meetings carefully
  - Decline or shorten unnecessary meetings when appropriate.
  - Prepare agendas and objectives to make meetings more efficient.

- Take care of your physical well-being
  - Move regularly throughout the day.
  - Maintain good posture and ergonomic setup.
  - Get adequate sleep, hydration, and nutrition.

- Set boundaries
  - Let household members know your working hours.
  - Avoid extending work indefinitely into personal time.
  - Create a shutdown ritual at the end of the day.

- Review and improve
  - Spend a few minutes each week reflecting on what helped or hindered productivity.
  - Adjust your environment, schedule, or tools based on what you learn.

- First 15 minutes: Review priorities and plan.

- Midday: Lunch and movement break.
