── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I work full-time in a fast food job with an unpredictable schedule.

Managing stress and anxiety.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
That's a genuinely tough combination - fast food work is physically demanding, the unpredictable scheduling makes it hard to plan your life, and that uncertainty itself creates a lot of anxiety.

## Some practical things that might actually help:

**For the schedule unpredictability specifically:**
- Try to identify what you *can* control (sleep routine, meals, small rituals) even when your schedule shifts
- Give yourself permission to not plan far ahead - working with shorter time horizons can reduce the "what if" anxiety
- If possible, communicate scheduling preferences clearly to your manager, even if they can't always accommodate

**For stress during/after shifts:**
- Decompression time matters - even 10-15 minutes before jumping into other responsibilities
- Physical work means your body holds tension - stretching or a short walk after shifts can help
- Staying hydrated actually affects anxiety levels more than people realize

**For general anxiety management:**
- Consistent sleep timing (even if hours vary) helps regulate your nervous system
- Limiting caffeine, which is tempting in fast food environments but worsens anxiety
- Brief breathing exercises during breaks - genuinely effective, not just advice

## Worth being honest about:
Chronic unpredictable scheduling is a **structural stressor** - some anxiety is a reasonable response to a genuinely difficult situation, not just something to manage away.

What aspect feels most overwhelming right now - the work itself, the scheduling, or something else?
