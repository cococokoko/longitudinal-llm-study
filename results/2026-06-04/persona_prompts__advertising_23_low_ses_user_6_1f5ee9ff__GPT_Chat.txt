── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
If you work long warehouse shifts, the goal is not to become an elaborate cook. It's to build a small set of fast, healthy meals that fit your schedule and energy level.

A practical approach:

- Learn 5–10 simple meals instead of trying to learn everything.
- Focus on meals that take 10–20 minutes or can be prepared in advance.
- Keep ingredients simple and repeat them often.

Good beginner meals:
- Scrambled eggs with vegetables and whole-grain toast
- Chicken, rice, and frozen vegetables
- Stir-fry with protein (chicken, beef, tofu, or shrimp) and mixed vegetables
- Turkey or bean chili
- Oatmeal with fruit and nuts
- Greek yogurt with berries and granola
- Tuna or chicken wraps with vegetables
- Sheet-pan meals (protein and vegetables roasted together)

Skills worth learning first:
- How to cook rice
- How to roast vegetables
- How to cook chicken safely
- How to sauté vegetables
- How to make simple sauces and seasonings

Use shortcuts:
- Frozen vegetables are nutritious and save time.
- Pre-washed salad mixes reduce prep work.
- Rotisserie chicken can provide several meals.
- Microwaveable rice packets are convenient on busy days.
- Canned beans are inexpensive and require almost no preparation.

Meal prep strategy:
- Pick one day each week to prepare food for 3–5 days.
- Cook large batches of protein, rice, potatoes, or pasta.
- Portion meals into containers.
- Keep healthy snacks available:
  - Fruit
  - Nuts
  - String cheese
  - Greek yogurt
  - Hard-boiled eggs

Example warehouse-worker meal plan:
- Breakfast: Oatmeal with fruit and peanut butter
- Lunch: Chicken, rice, and vegetables
- Snack: Apple and nuts
- Dinner: Stir-fry or sheet-pan chicken and vegetables

- Budget Bytes (simple, affordable recipes)
- Meal-prep-focused websites and apps
- Short cooking tutorials that teach one skill at a time
