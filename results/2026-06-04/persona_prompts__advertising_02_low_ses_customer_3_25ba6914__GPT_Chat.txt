For dry skin, the goal is to cleanse gently, maintain the skin barrier, and lock in moisture.

Morning:
- Use a gentle, non-foaming cleanser (or rinse with lukewarm water if your skin isn't oily).
- Apply a hydrating serum with ingredients like hyaluronic acid, glycerin, or panthenol.
- Use a rich moisturizer containing ceramides, squalane, shea butter, or cholesterol.
- Finish with a broad-spectrum sunscreen SPF 30 or higher.

Evening:
- Cleanse with a gentle cream or lotion cleanser.
- Apply a hydrating serum if desired.
- Use a richer moisturizer than you use in the morning.
- If your skin is very dry, seal in moisture with a thin layer of an occlusive product (such as petrolatum-based ointment) on the driest areas.

Helpful ingredients:
- Ceramides
- Glycerin
- Hyaluronic acid
- Squalane
- Urea (lower concentrations, around 5%)
- Colloidal oatmeal

Things to avoid if dryness is a problem:
- Harsh scrubs
- Over-exfoliation
- Cleansers containing strong sulfates
- Very hot showers or face washing with hot water
- Alcohol-heavy skincare products

If you want anti-aging or acne treatments:
- Introduce retinoids gradually (1–2 nights per week at first).
- Pair active ingredients with a good moisturizer to reduce irritation.

A simple starter routine:
- AM: Gentle cleanser → moisturizer → SPF 30+
- PM: Gentle cleanser → rich moisturizer
