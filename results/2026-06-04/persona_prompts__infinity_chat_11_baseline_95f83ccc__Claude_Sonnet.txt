── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I'm 60 years old and haven't achieved anything. What have I missed out on?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This is worth thinking through carefully rather than just offering reassurance.

## Some honest considerations:

**You may have actually achieved more than you're recognizing**
- Relationships maintained, problems solved, work done, people helped
- "Achievement" is often defined too narrowly (career/fame/wealth)
- Survival through difficulties is genuinely hard

**What you might actually be asking:**
- Do I still have time to do meaningful things? (Probably yes - many people do significant work in their 60s-80s)
- Did I waste opportunities? (Possibly some, but this is true for nearly everyone)
- Will I be remembered? (This matters less than it feels like it does)

**What research suggests matters for wellbeing:**
- Quality relationships
- Sense of purpose/contribution
- Health maintenance
- Continued learning and engagement

## The harder question worth asking:

**What specifically feels missing?** Because "haven't achieved anything" could mean:
- Career disappointment
- Relationship regrets
- Creative work never pursued
- Financial insecurity
- Something else entirely

The answer to what you've "missed" depends heavily on what you actually value, not generic achievement metrics.

What's driving this feeling right now? That might be more useful to address directly.
