# Quick Organization Tip for You

## Try the "5-Minute End-of-Day Reset"

Before you leave school each day, spend just 5 minutes doing this:

- **Write down your top 3 priorities** for the next day on a sticky note
- **Clear your desk** of anything you don't need tomorrow
- **Set out materials** you'll need first thing in the morning

### Why This Works Well for Your Situation

As a single parent, your mornings are likely **already hectic** getting kids ready. When you arrive at school, you want to hit the ground running **without having to think too hard** about what comes first.

Coming back to an organized space with a clear plan means:
- Less mental energy wasted on deciding what to do
- Fewer "I forgot to prepare that" moments
- A calmer start to your teaching day

### The Key Benefit

You're essentially doing your **morning planning the night before** at work, so your actual morning brain power can go toward your family and getting out the door.

---

It only costs 5 minutes but can **save you significant stress** throughout the next day.
