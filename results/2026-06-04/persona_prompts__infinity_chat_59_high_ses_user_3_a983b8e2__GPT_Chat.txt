── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
If you're looking for a practical organization tip as a high school teacher:

- Create a single "weekly dashboard" document or page that includes:
  - Your lesson plans for the week
  - Upcoming deadlines (grading, parent communication, meetings)
  - Materials you need to prepare
  - A short list of your top 3 priorities each day

Keeping all of this in one place reduces the time spent searching through emails, notebooks, and folders. Many teachers find that reviewing and updating this dashboard for 10–15 minutes every Friday afternoon or Monday morning helps them stay ahead of grading and lesson preparation.

A useful rule is: if a task takes less than two minutes (replying to a simple email, filing a document, recording a grade), do it immediately rather than adding it to a to-do list. This prevents small tasks from accumulating throughout the week.
