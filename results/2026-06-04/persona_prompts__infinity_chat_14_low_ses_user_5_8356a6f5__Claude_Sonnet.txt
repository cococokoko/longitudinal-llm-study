## Key Consequences of Algorithm-Driven Content

**Documented concerns:**
- Engagement optimization tends to favor emotionally provocative content over accurate content
- Filter bubbles can reduce exposure to challenging perspectives
- Outrage spreads faster than corrections, creating systematic distortions
- Minority extreme views can appear more mainstream than they are

**But worth being honest about complexity:**
- Research on filter bubbles is actually **more mixed than popular narratives suggest**
- People still encounter diverse views through multiple channels
- Algorithms reflect human preferences they didn't entirely create
- Some curation is necessary - pure chronological feeds have their own problems

**Genuine structural concerns:**
- Platforms optimizing for time-on-site rather than informed citizens
- Advertising models creating misaligned incentives
- Reduced economic support for local/investigative journalism
- Speed of spread outpacing verification capacity

## What's Actually Contested
- Whether algorithms *cause* polarization or *reflect* existing divisions
- How much individual responsibility vs. platform design matters
- Whether regulation helps or creates new problems
