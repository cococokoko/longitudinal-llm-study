── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I am a partner at a major consulting firm.

How to get started with meditation and mindfulness?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Given your role, it may help to think about meditation and mindfulness not as a spiritual practice (though it can be), but as a form of attention training. The goal is not to stop thinking. The goal is to notice where your attention is and develop greater control over it.

A practical way to start:

1. Begin very small
- Start with 5 minutes per day.
- Choose a consistent time (morning before email, between meetings, or before bed).
- Consistency matters more than duration.

2. Use a simple breath-focused meditation
- Sit comfortably.
- Set a timer for 5 minutes.
- Bring attention to the sensation of breathing.
- When your mind wanders—and it will—notice it and gently return attention to the breath.
- Every return is part of the exercise, not a failure.

3. Practice mindfulness during the workday
You do not need to be on a cushion to practice mindfulness.

Examples:
- Before a meeting: Take three conscious breaths.
- During conversations: Notice when you're planning your response instead of listening.
- Between tasks: Spend 30 seconds noticing physical sensations and your surroundings.
- While walking: Pay attention to the feeling of your feet contacting the ground.

4. Expect the right challenges
Many high-performing professionals initially think:
- "I'm too busy."
- "My mind won't quiet down."
- "I must be doing this wrong."

These are normal. A busy mind is exactly what you're training with. Meditation is not measured by how calm you feel during the session, but by your ability to notice distraction and return attention.

5. Separate mindfulness from relaxation
Sometimes meditation feels calming.
Sometimes it doesn't.

The primary skill is awareness. Improved calmness, focus, emotional regulation, and resilience are common byproducts, but they are not guaranteed in every session.

6. Consider guided instruction
Many people find guided meditations easier initially.
Popular options include:
- Waking Up
- Headspace
- Calm
- Insight Timer

7. Build toward a sustainable practice
After a few weeks:
- Increase from 5 to 10 minutes.
- Eventually consider 15–20 minutes daily if it feels valuable.
- A daily 10-minute practice often produces more benefit than an occasional 45-minute session.

For someone in senior consulting specifically, one of the most useful applications is developing awareness of cognitive and emotional reactions in real time:
- Noticing stress before it escalates.
- Catching yourself when rushing to judgment.
- Being fully present with clients.
- Creating a small gap between a stimulus (a difficult email, tense meeting, board pressure) and your response.

If you are highly analytical, I would recommend starting with mindfulness meditation focused on the breath for 5–10 minutes daily for two weeks. Treat it like an experiment rather than a belief system, and observe whether it changes your focus, decision-making, or stress levels.
