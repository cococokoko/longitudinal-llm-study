── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How to make my own natural beauty products?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Making your own natural beauty products can be simple, inexpensive, and customizable. The key is to use safe ingredients, maintain good hygiene, and understand that homemade products generally have shorter shelf lives than commercial ones.

Here are some beginner-friendly options:

1. Basic Facial Moisturizer
Ingredients:
- 2 tablespoons jojoba oil, sweet almond oil, or argan oil
- 1 tablespoon aloe vera gel

Instructions:
- Mix until combined.
- Store in a clean container.
- Apply a small amount to damp skin.

Best for:
- Dry to normal skin.

2. Simple Lip Balm
Ingredients:
- 1 tablespoon beeswax
- 1 tablespoon coconut oil
- 1 tablespoon shea butter

Instructions:
- Melt ingredients together over low heat.
- Pour into small containers.
- Allow to cool completely.

3. Sugar Body Scrub
Ingredients:
- 1 cup sugar
- ½ cup coconut oil or olive oil
- Optional: a few drops of essential oil

Instructions:
- Mix ingredients thoroughly.
- Use gently on the body, not on irritated skin.

4. Oatmeal Face Mask
Ingredients:
- 2 tablespoons finely ground oats
- 1 tablespoon honey
- Enough water or yogurt to form a paste

Instructions:
- Apply for 10–15 minutes.
- Rinse with lukewarm water.

Best for:
- Sensitive or dry skin.

5. Hair Conditioning Mask
Ingredients:
- 1 ripe avocado
- 1 tablespoon olive oil
- 1 tablespoon honey

Instructions:
- Blend until smooth.
- Apply to hair for 20–30 minutes.
- Shampoo thoroughly.

Safety Tips:
- Patch-test any new product on a small area of skin for 24–48 hours.
- Avoid using citrus oils before sun exposure.
- Be cautious with essential oils; they can irritate skin if overused.
- Use clean utensils and containers to reduce contamination.
- Products containing water, aloe, fresh fruit, or yogurt should be refrigerated and used within a few days unless a proper preservative is added.

Common Natural Ingredients and Their Uses:
- Shea butter: moisturizing
- Aloe vera: soothing and hydrating
- Jojoba oil: lightweight moisturizer
- Oatmeal: calming for sensitive skin
- Honey: humectant (helps retain moisture)
- Clay (kaolin, bentonite): oil-absorbing masks
- Rose water: refreshing toner ingredient

If you'd like, I can also provide recipes tailored to specific goals such as acne-prone skin, anti-aging skincare, curly hair care, or natural makeup.
