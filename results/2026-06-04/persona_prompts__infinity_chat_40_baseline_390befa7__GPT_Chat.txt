── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
My girlfriend is buying a house. I plan to marry her in a year or so. I don't know if it's fair for me to pay for the house or not. What's the best solution?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
There isn't one universally "fair" answer. The best solution depends on what role you'll have in the house, how serious and imminent the marriage is, and how you both want to handle finances.

A key principle is: your financial contribution should match your ownership rights and the risks you're taking.

Some common options:

- Option 1: She buys the house, and you pay rent.
  - She owns the home and builds equity.
  - You contribute a reasonable amount toward housing costs, similar to what you'd pay elsewhere.
  - This is often the cleanest arrangement when only one partner owns the property.
  - Consider putting expectations in writing, even informally.

- Option 2: You contribute to utilities and shared living expenses, but not the mortgage.
  - She covers the ownership costs.
  - You split groceries, utilities, internet, etc.
  - This avoids ambiguity about whether your payments are creating ownership rights.

- Option 3: You help pay the mortgage and receive an ownership stake.
  - If you're contributing significantly to the purchase or mortgage, it's reasonable to discuss obtaining a legal ownership interest.
  - This should be documented by a lawyer, not based on assumptions or verbal promises.

- Option 4: Wait until marriage to combine housing finances.
  - If marriage is likely within a year, some couples keep ownership separate until then.
  - After marriage, they revisit ownership, refinancing, or adding the spouse to the title.

Questions to discuss together:
- Will your name be on the title or mortgage?
- If you pay toward the mortgage, what do both of you believe that payment represents—rent or equity?
- If you break up before marriage, what happens to the money you've contributed?
- If you marry, do you plan to combine finances or keep some assets separate?
- How much are you contributing compared with local market rent?

A practical middle-ground arrangement for many couples in your situation is:
- She buys the house in her name.
- You pay an agreed-upon amount as rent or toward shared expenses.
- Everyone is clear that the house remains her asset unless and until you both decide to change ownership after marriage.
