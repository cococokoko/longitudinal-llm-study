# Review: *The Last Frequency* (2024)

★★★★½

Director Mara Okonkwo's debut feature is a quiet devastation. Set in a near-future where human memory can be broadcast like radio signals, *The Last Frequency* follows a grieving sound engineer who discovers his late daughter's final thoughts embedded in static between stations.

What could have been cold science fiction becomes something unbearably tender. The film resists spectacle at every turn. There are no villains, no chase sequences — just a father sitting in a cluttered studio, listening.

Lead actor Dominic Farrell gives the performance of his career, communicating entire chapters of loss through the smallest gestures. A coffee cup held too long. A window left open in winter.

The sound design deserves its own paragraph. It *should* win awards. The way ordinary noise — traffic, rainfall, a refrigerator hum — gradually reveals something human underneath is genuinely haunting.

The third act stumbles slightly, leaning on a revelation that the film didn't need. The ambiguity it sacrifices was more powerful than the answer it provides.

Still, *The Last Frequency* is the rare film that makes you want to call someone you love immediately after leaving the theater.

**Bring tissues. Bring patience. It rewards both.**
