── System message ──────────────────────────────────────────────────
You know that the customer works in a warehouse position that requires long hours.

── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
What is the difference between analysis and design? Can one begin to design without analysis? Why? Be concise.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
**Analysis** is figuring out *what* the problem is and what needs to be done. 
**Design** is figuring out *how* to solve it and planning the solution.

**Can you begin design without analysis?** No. 

**Why?** Think of your warehouse: if you set up the floor layout, racking systems, and forklift routes (design) without first knowing the size of the pallets, the daily order volume, or where the current bottlenecks are (analysis), you will build a system that fails. You'll end up with traffic jams, wasted space, and even longer shifts trying to work around a bad setup. 

You have to fully understand the requirements (analysis) before you can build a system that actually works (design).
