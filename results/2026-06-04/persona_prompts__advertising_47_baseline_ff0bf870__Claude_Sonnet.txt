# Tips for Running a Marathon

## Training
- **Follow a structured plan** - Most plans are 16-20 weeks long
- **Build mileage gradually** - Increase weekly mileage by no more than 10% per week
- **Include long runs** - Build up to at least one 20-mile run before race day
- **Rest and recovery** - Schedule rest days to prevent injury

## Nutrition & Hydration
- **Practice fueling during training** - Use gels, chews, or real food every 45-60 minutes
- **Stay hydrated** - Drink water regularly, not just when thirsty
- **Carb load** - Increase carbohydrate intake 2-3 days before the race
- **Nothing new on race day** - Only use foods and gear you've tested in training

## Race Day Strategy
- **Start slow** - Going out too fast is the most common mistake
- **Run your own race** - Ignore the crowd pace early on
- **Break it into segments** - Think in smaller chunks rather than 26.2 miles
- **Know the course** - Identify hills and aid station locations

## Gear
- **Wear broken-in shoes** - Never race in new shoes
- **Use anti-chafe products** - Body Glide or similar products prevent blisters
- **Dress for the weather** - Layer appropriately

## Mental Tips
- **Train your mind** - Expect difficult moments around miles 18-22
- **Have a mantra** - A simple phrase can help push through tough spots
- **Celebrate the journey** - Enjoy the experience!
