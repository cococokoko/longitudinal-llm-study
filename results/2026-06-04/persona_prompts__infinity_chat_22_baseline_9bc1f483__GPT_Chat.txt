── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Different views on discipleship.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Different Christian traditions and thinkers understand discipleship (following Jesus as a disciple) in different ways. While they generally agree that discipleship involves faith in Christ, obedience, and spiritual growth, they emphasize different aspects.

Some major views include:

• Evangelical View
  - Focuses on a personal relationship with Jesus.
  - Emphasizes conversion ("being born again"), Bible study, prayer, evangelism, and moral obedience.
  - Discipleship often involves mentoring relationships, small groups, and intentional spiritual growth.

• Catholic View
  - Sees discipleship as a lifelong journey within the Church.
  - Emphasizes participation in the sacraments, especially the Eucharist.
  - Includes prayer, service, holiness, and formation through the Church's teachings and traditions.

• Eastern Orthodox View
  - Emphasizes union with God (theosis), becoming transformed by divine grace.
  - Discipleship involves worship, prayer, fasting, spiritual disciplines, and participation in the sacramental life of the Church.
  - Focuses strongly on spiritual transformation rather than merely intellectual belief.

• Mainline Protestant View
  - Often emphasizes discipleship through social responsibility, justice, community service, and faithful living.
  - Combines personal faith with engagement in societal issues such as poverty, peace, and equality.

• Anabaptist View
  - Stresses radical obedience to Jesus' teachings, especially the Sermon on the Mount.
  - Often emphasizes nonviolence, simplicity, community, and visible Christian living.
  - Views discipleship as actively imitating Jesus' way of life.

• Wesleyan/Methodist View
  - Focuses on sanctification—the ongoing process of becoming holy.
  - Emphasizes spiritual disciplines, accountability groups, acts of mercy, and growth in love for God and neighbor.

• Reformed View
  - Highlights God's sovereignty and the authority of Scripture.
  - Sees discipleship as growing in obedience to God's Word and living every area of life under Christ's lordship.
  - Often emphasizes doctrinal instruction alongside practical holiness.

• Radical Discipleship Movements
  - Emphasize costly commitment, sacrifice, and following Jesus in a countercultural way.
  - Influenced by figures such as Dietrich Bonhoeffer, who contrasted "costly grace" with "cheap grace."
  - Often stress serving the poor, peacemaking, and willingness to suffer for faith.

Key areas where views differ:
• Whether discipleship is primarily about personal spiritual growth, participation in the Church, social action, or imitation of Jesus' lifestyle.
• The role of sacraments and church authority.
• The relationship between faith, works, and obedience.
• Whether discipleship is viewed mainly as individual or communal.
• The degree to which discipleship requires separation from broader culture.

Despite these differences, most Christian traditions agree that discipleship involves learning from Jesus, following his teachings, and being transformed in character and conduct.
