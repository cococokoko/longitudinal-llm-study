# Effective Online Teaching Techniques

Here are well-established approaches that translate well across professional contexts:

## Engagement & Structure
- **Chunk content** into 10-15 minute segments to maintain attention
- **Use clear learning objectives** at the start of each session
- **Build in regular interaction** rather than long passive lectures
- **Vary delivery formats** - video, text, discussion, case studies

## Active Learning Methods
- Socratic questioning adapted for chat/discussion boards
- Case-based scenarios relevant to your audience
- Polls and quick knowledge checks
- Breakout room discussions for smaller group work

## Technical Best Practices
- **Stable, professional background** and good lighting
- Test audio/video before sessions
- Have a backup communication plan if technology fails
- Record sessions when appropriate for later review

## Feedback & Assessment
- Frequent low-stakes checks rather than infrequent high-stakes tests
- Clear rubrics for any assignments
- Timely, specific feedback

## Relationship Building
- Office hours or async Q&A channels
- Acknowledge participants by name
- Create psychological safety for questions

## Particularly Relevant for Legal Training
- Hypotheticals and problem-based learning work exceptionally well online
- Document annotation tools for reviewing materials together
