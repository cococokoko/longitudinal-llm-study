── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Decorating your home on a budget is all about making thoughtful changes that have a big visual impact without spending a lot of money. Here are some practical ideas:

- Declutter first
  - A cleaner, more organized space instantly looks more polished.
  - Donate, sell, or store items you no longer use.

- Rearrange existing furniture
  - Try different room layouts before buying anything new.
  - Create conversation areas and improve traffic flow.

- Use paint strategically
  - A fresh coat of paint is one of the most affordable ways to transform a room.
  - Consider an accent wall or repainting old furniture.

- Add inexpensive textiles
  - Update throw pillows, blankets, curtains, or rugs.
  - Mixing textures can make a room feel more inviting.

- Shop secondhand
  - Check thrift stores, garage sales, online marketplaces, and estate sales.
  - Look for solid furniture that can be cleaned or refinished.

- Incorporate DIY décor
  - Create your own artwork.
  - Repurpose jars, baskets, or wooden crates for storage and decoration.

- Use lighting creatively
  - Add floor lamps, table lamps, or warm-toned bulbs.
  - Good lighting can dramatically improve a room's atmosphere.

- Bring in plants
  - Houseplants add color, texture, and life.
  - Many affordable varieties are easy to care for.

- Create a gallery wall
  - Frame family photos, postcards, printable art, or your own creations.
  - Mix frame styles for an eclectic look.

- Update hardware and fixtures
  - Replace cabinet handles, drawer pulls, or light switch covers.
  - Small details can modernize a space for relatively little cost.

- Use mirrors
  - Mirrors reflect light and can make rooms feel larger.
  - Look for budget-friendly options at discount stores or secondhand shops.

- Focus on one room at a time
  - Prioritize spaces you use most often.
  - This helps prevent overspending and keeps projects manageable.

Sample budget breakdown for a room refresh:
- Paint: $30–$80
- Throw pillows: $20–$50
- Secondhand décor: $20–$100
- Plants: $10–$40
- New hardware: $15–$50

Even a modest budget can make a noticeable difference when spent on high-impact updates like paint, lighting, and organization.
