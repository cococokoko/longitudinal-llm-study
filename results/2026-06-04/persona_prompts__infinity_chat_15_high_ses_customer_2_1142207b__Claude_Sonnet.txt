## Core Mechanisms Worth Considering

**Structural power dynamics**
- Wealthy nations disproportionately shape international institutions (IMF, WTO, World Bank) and their rules
- This creates genuine tension between formal sovereign equality and actual bargaining power
- Debt relationships create real dependency that constrains policy choices

**Where the evidence is fairly strong**
- Economic leverage demonstrably influences diplomatic alignment
- Trade dependency affects how countries respond to sanctions pressure
- Resource competition drives significant geopolitical maneuvering

**Where analysis gets more contested**
- Whether inequality *causes* conflict or whether both share common causes
- How much agency developing nations actually exercise versus structural determinism
- Whether international institutions primarily entrench inequality or provide genuine pathways out of it

## Honest Complications

The relationship isn't simply "rich countries dominate poor ones" - China's rise demonstrates economic development can substantially shift power relationships. Some smaller economies punch above their weight through strategic positioning.

**Competing frameworks** (dependency theory, liberal institutionalism, realism) each capture real phenomena while missing others.
