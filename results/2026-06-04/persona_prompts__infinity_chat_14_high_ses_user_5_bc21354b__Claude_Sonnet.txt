This is a genuinely important question worth thinking through carefully rather than just validating concerns.

## Real Documented Concerns

**Engagement optimization problems:**
- Outrage and anxiety genuinely drive more clicks than nuanced content
- Recommendation systems can create filter bubbles, though research on magnitude is mixed
- Misinformation can spread faster than corrections in some contexts

**Structural issues:**
- Platforms optimize for metrics that don't align with healthy discourse
- Minority extreme views can appear mainstream through amplification
- Attention economy creates incentives misaligned with informed citizenship

## Where the Narrative Gets Oversimplified

**Worth being honest about:**
- Research on algorithm-driven polarization is actually contested
- People had filter bubbles before social media
- Algorithms sometimes *increase* exposure to diverse views
- Correlation between platform use and polarization doesn't establish causation

## Practical Considerations for Your Position

**Questions worth asking internally:**
- What are you actually optimizing for, and why?
- Do your engagement metrics measure anything genuinely valuable?
- Are you measuring harms systematically or reactively?
- What would you change if regulation required it?

## The Honest Bottom Line

The consequences are real but probably **more complex than either dismissive or catastrophist framings suggest**. The more important question is whether your company is genuinely measuring these effects or primarily managing their perception.
