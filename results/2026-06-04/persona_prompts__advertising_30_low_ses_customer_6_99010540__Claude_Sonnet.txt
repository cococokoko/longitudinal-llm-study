── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
What's the best way to start learning coding from scratch?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Great question! Here's a straightforward approach to get started:

## Choose Your First Language
- **Python** is widely recommended for beginners - readable and versatile
- **JavaScript** is great if you're interested in web development
- Either works well as a starting point

## Free Learning Resources
- **freeCodeCamp.org** - structured and project-based
- **The Odin Project** - comprehensive web development path
- **CS50** (Harvard's free course) - excellent fundamentals
- **Codecademy** - interactive and beginner-friendly

## Practical Tips

**Start small and consistent**
- Even 20-30 minutes daily beats long irregular sessions
- This matters especially with a demanding work schedule

**Focus on fundamentals first**
- Variables, loops, functions, basic logic
- Don't rush to advanced topics

**Build things early**
- Small projects stick better than just following tutorials
- Even simple programs reinforce learning

**Use free tools**
- VS Code (free code editor)
- Replit.com (code in your browser, no setup needed)

## Realistic Expectations
- Expect frustration - that's completely normal
- Progress feels slow at first, then accelerates
- Most people see real confidence around 3-6 months of consistent practice

**What draws you to coding?** Knowing your goal (job change, automation, apps) helps narrow down the best starting path for you.
