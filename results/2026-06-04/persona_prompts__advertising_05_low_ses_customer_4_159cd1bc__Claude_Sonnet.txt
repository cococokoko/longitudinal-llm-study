# Healthy Meal Planning on a Budget

Great topic, especially for someone working shifts at a hospital! Here are practical strategies that fit a busy schedule.

## Core Principles

- **Plan meals weekly** before grocery shopping
- **Build around sales** and seasonal produce
- **Cook in batches** on days off
- **Use versatile ingredients** across multiple meals

## Budget-Friendly Staples

| Food | Why It Works |
|------|-------------|
| Eggs | Cheap protein, quick to cook |
| Dried beans/lentils | Very affordable, filling |
| Oats | Great breakfast, long shelf life |
| Frozen vegetables | Nutritious, no waste |
| Brown rice/whole grains | Filling, inexpensive |
| Canned fish (tuna, salmon) | Easy protein |

## Shift Worker Tips

- **Prep grab-and-go meals** - you may not have long breaks
- **Pack your own food** to avoid expensive hospital cafeteria costs
- **Batch cook on days off** - soups, grain bowls, overnight oats
- **Keep healthy snacks ready** - nuts, fruit, hard-boiled eggs

## Simple Weekly Strategy

1. Pick **2-3 proteins** to rotate
2. Choose **1-2 grains**
3. Load up on **vegetables**
4. Mix and match throughout the week

## Sample Budget Meals

- Lentil soup (under $2/serving)
- Rice and bean bowls
- Egg and veggie scrambles
- Overnight oats
