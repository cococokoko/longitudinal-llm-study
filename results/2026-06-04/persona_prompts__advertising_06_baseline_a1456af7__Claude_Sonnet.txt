# Mastering Digital Photography

## Core Fundamentals

### The Exposure Triangle
| Element | Controls | Effect |
|---------|----------|--------|
| **Aperture** (f-stop) | Light & depth of field | f/1.8 = blurry background; f/16 = sharp throughout |
| **Shutter Speed** | Motion & light | 1/1000s = freeze action; 1/30s = motion blur |
| **ISO** | Sensor sensitivity | Low ISO = clean; High ISO = grainy/noisy |

---

## Essential Concepts

### Aperture
- **Wide aperture** (f/1.4–f/2.8) → portraits, low light
- **Narrow aperture** (f/8–f/16) → landscapes, architecture
- Controls **bokeh** (background blur)

### Shutter Speed
- **Fast** (1/500s+) → sports, wildlife, children
- **Slow** (1/30s and below) → light trails, waterfalls, night shots
- Use a **tripod** for anything below 1/60s

### ISO
- Start at **ISO 100–400** in good light
- Push to **ISO 1600–6400** in low light
- Modern cameras handle high ISO surprisingly well

---

## Composition Rules

- **Rule of Thirds** – place subjects off-center
- **Leading Lines** – draw the eye into the frame
- **Framing** – use natural frames (doorways, trees)
- **Negative Space** – let subjects breathe
- **Golden Hour** – shoot 1 hour after sunrise / before sunset

---

## Camera Modes Explained

| Mode | Best For |
|------|----------|
| **Manual (M)** | Full creative control |
| **Aperture Priority (Av/A)** | Portraits, landscapes |
| **Shutter Priority (Tv/S)** | Action, sports |
| **Program (P)** | Quick shooting, learning |

---

## Focus & Depth of Field

- **Single AF** – still subjects
- **Continuous AF** – moving subjects
- **Manual Focus** – macro, low light, creative control
- Focus on the **eyes** in portraits — always

---

## Lighting Techniques

### Natural Light
- **Golden hour** – warm, soft, flattering
- **Blue hour** – cool, moody
- **Overcast** – natural softbox, great for portraits
- **Harsh midday sun** – use shade or fill flash

### Artificial Light
- **Reflectors** – bounce light, fill shadows
- **External flash** – bounce off ceilings/walls
- **Studio strobes** – full control over lighting

---

## Post-Processing Workflow

### Basic Editing Order
1. **Exposure** – get brightness right
2. **White Balance** – correct color temperature
3. **Highlights/Shadows** – recover detail
4. **Contrast & Clarity** – add punch
5. **Color grading** – set the mood
6. **Sharpening** – final detail
7. **Noise reduction** – clean up high ISO shots

### Popular Software
- **Adobe Lightroom** – best overall workflow
- **Capture One** – professional color grading
- **Photoshop** – advanced retouching
- **Darktable** – free, powerful alternative

---

## Shoot in RAW

> RAW files contain **all sensor data** — giving you maximum editing flexibility. JPEGs discard data permanently.

---

## Gear Priorities

### What Actually Matters
1. **Lenses** > camera body
2. **50mm f/1.8** – best starter lens (cheap & sharp)
3. **Tripod** – essential for landscapes & long exposure
4. **Extra batteries & memory cards** – never miss a shot

### Recommended Starter Kit
- Entry-level mirrorless or DSLR
- 50mm f/1.8 prime lens
- Sturdy tripod
- Extra battery + 64GB card
