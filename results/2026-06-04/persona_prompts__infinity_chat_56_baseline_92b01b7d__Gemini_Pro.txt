── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Name an economic value of an additional year of schooling.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
One of the most direct and significant economic values of an additional year of schooling is **increased individual earnings** (often referred to as the wage premium). 

Labor economists generally estimate that, on average, a single additional year of education increases an individual's future wages by about **8% to 10% per year** over the course of their lifetime. 

Beyond individual wealth, this also creates a macroeconomic value: **higher tax revenues** for the government and a **reduced likelihood of unemployment**, which lowers the economic burden on social safety net programs.
