I recently started a new job and am still on probation.

What is the future of work in an increasingly automated economy?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This is a great question to think about. Here are some key perspectives:

## What's Likely Changing
- **Routine tasks** (both manual and cognitive) are most vulnerable to automation
- **Some jobs will disappear** while new categories emerge
- **Job content is shifting** even where jobs survive - more augmentation than pure replacement in many fields

## Reasonably Confident Predictions
- Continuous skill updating becomes essentially mandatory
- Hybrid human-AI collaboration becomes standard
- Career paths become less linear
- Adaptability matters more than specific credentials

## Genuinely Contested Questions
- **Net job creation vs. destruction** - economists seriously disagree
- **Timeline** - predictions have repeatedly been wrong in both directions
- **Who benefits** - gains may be unevenly distributed
- **Whether wages rise or fall** for most workers

## Practical Considerations for You
Since you're starting a new job:
- **Skills that complement AI** (judgment, creativity, relationship-building, complex problem-solving) tend to be more durable
- Building **domain expertise** still matters
- **Demonstrating value** during probation is actually a good instinct - human performance and reliability still count enormously

## Worth Being Honest About
Nobody has reliably predicted technological employment impacts historically, so **strong certainty from anyone** about specific outcomes deserves skepticism.
