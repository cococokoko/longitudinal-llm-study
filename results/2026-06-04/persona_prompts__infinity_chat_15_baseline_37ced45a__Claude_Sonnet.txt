── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How do global economic inequalities shape international relations?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This is a genuinely complex question worth thinking through carefully rather than just listing factors.

## Core Mechanisms

**Power asymmetries**
- Wealthy states have greater leverage in negotiations, institutions, and coercive capacity
- Economic dependence creates political vulnerability for poorer states
- This can produce relationships resembling patron-client dynamics even between formally equal sovereigns

**Institutional design**
- Bretton Woods institutions (IMF, World Bank) reflect founding power distributions
- Voting structures often favor wealthy contributors
- This creates legitimate grievances about whose interests rules serve

## Competing Interpretations Worth Considering

**Liberal perspective:** Trade interdependence reduces conflict and creates mutual interests despite inequality

**Dependency/structuralist view:** Inequality is reproduced through the system itself, not just reflected by it

**Realist perspective:** Economic inequality is primarily a tool of power competition rather than a cause in itself

## Genuine Tensions and Complications

- Some poor states successfully resist powerful ones (suggesting inequality isn't deterministic)
- Economic growth has reduced some gaps while widening others
- Regional powers complicate simple rich/poor narratives
- Domestic politics within states often matter more than international structure

## Honest Uncertainties

Scholars genuinely disagree about whether current inequalities primarily reflect:
- Historical exploitation
- Institutional lock-in
- Domestic governance differences
- Geographic factors
