── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
What is the difference between analysis and design? Can one begin to design without analysis? Why? Be concise.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
## Analysis vs. Design

| | **Analysis** | **Design** |
|---|---|---|
| **Focus** | *What* the system must do | *How* the system will do it |
| **Question** | Problem understanding | Solution building |
| **Output** | Requirements, models of reality | Blueprints, architecture, specifications |

---

## Key Difference
- **Analysis** = understanding and defining the **problem**
- **Design** = creating a **solution**

---

## Can You Design Without Analysis?

**No** — not effectively.

**Why:**
- Design decisions must be grounded in requirements; without analysis, you don't know *what* you're solving
- Skipping analysis leads to solving the **wrong problem**, wasted effort, and costly rework
- You cannot evaluate whether a design is correct if you have no baseline of requirements to validate against

> **"A solution without a problem definition is just guessing."**

In practice, some iteration occurs (analysis informs design, design reveals gaps in analysis), but design fundamentally **depends on** analysis as its input.
