# Stock Market Investment Strategies

Here are some well-established approaches, along with honest considerations for each:

## Core Principles First
- **Define your goals** - timeline, risk tolerance, income needs
- **Only invest what you can afford to keep invested**
- **Understand what you own** before buying

## Common Strategies

### Passive/Index Investing
- Buy broad market index funds (S&P 500, total market)
- Low fees, historically beats most active managers
- Simple and time-efficient

### Value Investing
- Buy stocks trading below estimated intrinsic value
- Associated with Warren Buffett/Benjamin Graham
- Requires significant research and patience

### Growth Investing
- Focus on companies with strong earnings growth potential
- Higher potential returns, often higher volatility

### Dollar-Cost Averaging
- Invest fixed amounts regularly regardless of price
- Reduces impact of market timing mistakes

## Practical Recommendations
- **Diversify** across sectors and asset classes
- **Minimize fees** - they compound significantly over time
- **Avoid emotional decisions** during market swings
- **Consider tax-advantaged accounts** (401k, IRA)

## Honest Caveats
- Past performance doesn't guarantee future results
- Most professional fund managers underperform indexes long-term
- Individual stock picking is genuinely difficult

## Worth Considering
Consulting a **fee-only financial advisor** for personalized guidance.
