## Linear Graph Example

A **path graph** is a classic example of a linear graph in graph theory.

### Example: P₄ (Path Graph with 4 vertices)

```
A --- B --- C --- D
```

### Properties:
- **Vertices:** {A, B, C, D}
- **Edges:** {AB, BC, CD}
- Each **internal vertex** (B, C) has degree 2
- Each **endpoint** (A, D) has degree 1
- The edges form a single, straight-line sequence with **no cycles**

### Formal Definition:
A path graph **Pₙ** has:
- *n* vertices
- *n - 1* edges
- arranged so each vertex connects to at most two others sequentially

### Why It's "Linear":
The structure is essentially a **sequence of connected nodes**, analogous to a line — no branching, no loops, no cycles.

- A **single edge** (P₂): `A --- B`
- A **longer chain**: P₅, P₆, etc.
