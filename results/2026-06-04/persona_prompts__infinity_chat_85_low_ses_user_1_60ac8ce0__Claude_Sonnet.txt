── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here's a strong rhetorical question for your essay:

**"If we wouldn't hand a teenager a product proven to increase anxiety, depression, and self-harm, why do we casually place one in their pocket every single day?"**

This works well because it:
- Creates a **direct comparison** that's hard to dismiss
- Forces the reader to **confront an inconsistency** in how we treat teen safety
- Implies urgency **without being preachy**

A few alternatives depending on your essay's angle:

- **On comparison culture:** "When did we decide that measuring a child's worth in likes and followers was acceptable?"
- **On addiction:** "Would we call it harmless if teenagers spent six hours a day in a casino?"
- **On responsibility:** "Who is truly responsible when a platform designed to be addictive targets a 13-year-old's developing brain?"

The best choice depends on your **specific argument** - are you focusing more on mental health, corporate responsibility, parental awareness, or regulation?
