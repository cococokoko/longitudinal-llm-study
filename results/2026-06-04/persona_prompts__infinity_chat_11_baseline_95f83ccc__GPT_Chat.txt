I'm 60 years old and haven't achieved anything. What have I missed out on?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
That's a painful question, and many people ask some version of it at 60, 70, or even 90.

The first thing I'd say is that "I haven't achieved anything" is usually a conclusion, not a fact. Achievement can mean public recognition, wealth, career status, creative output, raising a family, helping others, surviving hardship, maintaining relationships, learning, or simply continuing through difficult decades. People often count only a narrow category of accomplishments and ignore the rest.

As for what you may have missed out on, there are two different questions:

- "What opportunities did I not take?"
- "What experiences are no longer available to me?"

For the first, everyone has missed thousands of opportunities. Every life includes paths not taken. Someone who became wealthy may have missed close relationships. Someone who raised a family may have missed adventure. Someone who traveled the world may have missed putting down roots. There is no life that contains all possibilities.

For the second, it is true that some things become harder or impossible with age:
- Being young.
- Certain athletic achievements.
- Having the exact future you could have had at 20 or 30.
- Some career trajectories that depend on starting very early.

That loss is real, and pretending otherwise usually doesn't help.

But many meaningful achievements remain available at 60:
- Learning a new skill or profession.
- Creating art, writing, or building something.
- Deepening relationships or forming new ones.
- Mentoring younger people.
- Contributing to a cause.
- Traveling.
- Improving your health from where it is today.
- Starting a business.
- Becoming knowledgeable in a field.
- Leaving behind work, memories, or service that matter to others.

History is full of people who did significant things later in life, but even if your goal isn't to become notable, a meaningful decade at 60 is still a meaningful decade.

I would also ask: when you say you haven't achieved anything, what specific achievements do you feel you missed? Career success? Money? Marriage? Children? Creative work? Recognition? The answer changes depending on what loss you're grieving.
