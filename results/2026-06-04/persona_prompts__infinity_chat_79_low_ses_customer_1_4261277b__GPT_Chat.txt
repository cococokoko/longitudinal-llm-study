A strong STEM fair project should be:
- Affordable (under about $30–50 if possible)
- Based on a real problem
- Measurable with data
- Practical enough to build and test

One excellent idea related to Vietnam is:

"Low-Cost Water Filtration System for Flood-Prone Communities"

Why it matters:
- Many regions of Vietnam experience seasonal flooding, especially in the Mekong Delta and central provinces.
- Floods can contaminate drinking water with sediment, bacteria, and pollutants.
- Access to affordable clean water is an ongoing challenge in some rural areas.

Project question:
"Which combination of low-cost filtration materials removes the most contaminants from dirty water?"

Materials:
- Plastic bottles
- Sand
- Gravel
- Activated charcoal (often inexpensive at aquarium stores)
- Cotton or coffee filters
- Water testing strips (pH, turbidity, or dissolved solids)
- Measuring cups

Experiment:
1. Build several filter designs using different material combinations.
2. Create samples of dirty water (soil mixed into water).
3. Run equal amounts through each filter.
4. Measure:
   - Clarity (turbidity)
   - pH
   - Total dissolved solids (if you have a cheap TDS meter)
   - Filtration speed
5. Compare results.

STEM concepts:
- Environmental engineering
- Chemistry
- Public health
- Sustainable development

Ways to make it stand out:
- Design a portable filter for emergency flood situations.
- Calculate the cost per liter of filtered water.
- Use locally available materials found in Vietnam.
- Compare your design with commercially available filters.

Other Vietnam-focused STEM ideas:

1. Saltwater Intrusion in the Mekong Delta
- Investigate which low-cost materials help remove salt from water.
- Study how rising sea levels affect freshwater supplies.

2. Solar-Powered Food Dryer
- Vietnam's hot climate makes solar drying practical.
- Build a simple solar dryer and test how effectively it preserves fruit compared with air drying.

3. Air Pollution Monitoring
- Measure particulate matter near busy roads versus parks or residential areas.
- Analyze how traffic affects air quality.

4. Rice Husk as an Eco-Friendly Building Material
- Vietnam produces large amounts of rice husk waste.
- Test whether adding rice husks to clay or cement mixtures changes insulation or strength.

5. Mosquito Control and Standing Water
- Investigate low-cost methods for reducing mosquito breeding.
- Relevant to dengue fever prevention in tropical regions.

If you're aiming for a project that has a good chance of impressing judges while staying affordable, the water filtration project or the rice husk sustainability project are particularly strong because they address major environmental challenges in Vietnam and allow you to collect clear, quantitative data.
