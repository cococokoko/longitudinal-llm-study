── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I recently started a new job and am still on probation.

How do I understand what I want?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Starting a new job, especially while on probation, can make it surprisingly hard to tell what you actually want. You're learning new expectations, trying to make a good impression, and may not yet know what aspects of the role or workplace matter most to you.

A useful approach is to separate three different questions:

• What do I want right now?
• What do I want from this job?
• What do I want from my life and career more broadly?

Sometimes people try to answer the third question when they're really feeling uncertainty about the first.

You might explore your wants by paying attention to your reactions over the next few weeks:

• When during the workday do you feel energized, engaged, or curious?
• What tasks do you find yourself avoiding, even when you're capable of doing them?
• What kinds of conversations leave you feeling good afterward?
• What frustrations keep recurring?
• At the end of a good day, what made it good?

It can also help to write down observations rather than conclusions. For example:

• Observation: "I enjoyed helping a colleague solve a problem."
• Observation: "I felt drained after three hours of administrative work."

After collecting enough observations, patterns often emerge naturally.

Another useful exercise is to imagine that your probation period has ended successfully. Ask yourself:

• What would need to be true for me to feel glad I stayed?
• What would need to improve for me to want to build a future here?
• What would make me decide this role isn't for me, even if I'm performing well?

Notice that these questions focus on your experience rather than on what you think you "should" want.

If you're finding it difficult to identify any preferences at all, that's also common during periods of transition. Sometimes the first step is not figuring out what you want, but noticing what consistently attracts or repels you.

What prompted the question? Are you unsure whether you want this particular job, or are you struggling to know what you want more generally?
