# Mastering the Art of Cooking Steak

Great topic! Here's a solid guide to help you cook steak confidently.

---

## Choosing Your Cut
- **Ribeye** - rich, well-marbled, forgiving
- **New York Strip** - balanced flavor and tenderness
- **Filet Mignon** - very tender, mild flavor
- **Sirloin** - leaner, budget-friendly
- **Flank/Skirt** - great for marinades

---

## Key Principles

### Before Cooking
- **Bring to room temperature** - 30-45 minutes out of the fridge
- **Pat completely dry** - crucial for a good sear
- **Season generously** - kosher salt and black pepper at minimum
- **Consider dry brining** - salt 1-24 hours ahead for deeper flavor

### Cooking Methods
| Method | Best For |
|--------|----------|
| Cast iron pan | Most cuts, great crust |
| Grill | Charcoal adds flavor |
| Reverse sear | Thick cuts (1.5"+) |
| Sous vide | Precise temperature control |

---

## Internal Temperatures
- Rare: **125°F**
- Medium Rare: **130-135°F** *(most recommended)*
- Medium: **140-145°F**
- Well Done: **160°F+**

---

## The Sear
- Get your pan **screaming hot**
- Use high smoke-point oil
- **Don't move it** for 2-3 minutes
- Baste with **butter, garlic, and herbs**

---

## Resting
**Always rest your steak** - 5-10 minutes before cutting

---

## Quick Tips
- A meat thermometer is your best friend
- Thicker cuts = more control
- Simple seasoning often wins
