Provide an example of the name of an optimization technique used in machine learning.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
One of the most common optimization techniques used in machine learning is called **Stochastic Gradient Descent (SGD)**. 

Since you work a fast food job with an unpredictable schedule, the word "stochastic" might actually resonate with you—in math, it basically means "random" or "unpredictable." 

To put it in perspective, you can think of this algorithm a lot like working a chaotic shift:

Imagine you are trying to find the absolute fastest routine to close down the kitchen at night (in machine learning, finding the best or fastest solution is the "optimization" part). 

*   If a machine learning model used **Standard Gradient Descent**, it would be like trying to look at every single dirty dish, every piece of equipment, and every floor tile all at once before making a single move to clean. It takes way too much time and memory to process everything at once.
*   But with **Stochastic Gradient Descent**, the model takes things one random piece at a time. It looks at one task (like wiping the prep table), adjusts its strategy to get a little faster, and then immediately moves on to the next random task. 

Just like you have to constantly adapt on the fly to random lunch rushes, sudden drive-thru spikes, or a schedule that changes week to week, SGD uses randomness to quickly adapt and find the best solution without getting bogged down by looking at the entire dataset at once.
