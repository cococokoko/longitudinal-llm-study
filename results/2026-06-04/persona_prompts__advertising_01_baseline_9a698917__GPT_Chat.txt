Learning guitar is a rewarding skill, and a structured approach can help you make steady progress.

A good beginner path:

- Learn the parts of the guitar and how to hold it comfortably.
- Practice basic open chords:
  - G
  - C
  - D
  - Em
  - Am
  - E
- Work on smooth chord changes between these chords.
- Learn a few basic strumming patterns.
- Practice keeping time with a metronome or drum beat.
- Start learning simple songs that use only a few chords.
- Learn basic guitar tablature (tabs) so you can read simple riffs and melodies.
- Build finger strength and coordination with short daily exercises.

A simple daily practice routine (20–30 minutes):
- 5 minutes: warm-up exercises
- 10 minutes: chord practice and chord changes
- 5 minutes: strumming and rhythm
- 10 minutes: work on a song

Some tips:
- Practice a little every day rather than long sessions once a week.
- Focus on clean notes before speed.
- Keep your guitar in tune.
- Expect sore fingertips during the first few weeks; this is normal.
