── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How to say a formal thank you for your time in the subject line of an email?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here are some formal ways to say **"Thank you for your time"** in an email subject line:

## Direct Options
- **"Thank You for Your Time"**
- **"Thank You for Meeting with Me"**
- **"Thank You for Your Consideration"**

## More Specific Options
- **"Thank You for the Interview – [Your Name]"**
- **"Thank You for the Meeting on [Date]"**
- **"Appreciation for Your Time and Insights"**

## Professional Variations
- **"Grateful for Your Time – [Topic/Position]"**
- **"Following Up – Thank You for Your Time"**
- **"Thank You for the Opportunity to Connect"**

## Tips
- **Be specific** when possible (mention the meeting, interview, or topic)
- **Include your name** if the recipient may not recognize your email
- **Keep it concise** and clear
- Avoid overly casual language like *"Thanks a bunch!"*

The most commonly used and universally appropriate option is simply:
> **"Thank You for Your Time"**
