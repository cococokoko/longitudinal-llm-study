── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I am a senior attorney at a well-established law firm.

How can I decorate my apartment on a minimalist budget?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here are some practical tips for decorating your apartment on a minimalist budget:

## Core Principles
- **Less is more** - focus on a few quality pieces rather than lots of items
- Declutter first before adding anything new

## Budget-Friendly Strategies

**Shop Smart**
- Thrift stores and estate sales for unique pieces
- Facebook Marketplace and Craigslist for furniture
- IKEA for clean, simple basics
- End-of-season sales

**DIY Approaches**
- Repaint existing furniture
- Frame simple prints or personal photos
- Create gallery walls with inexpensive frames

**Free or Nearly Free**
- Rearrange what you already own
- Use plants (pothos and snake plants are cheap and low-maintenance)
- Natural light as a design element - keep windows clean and unobstructed

## Minimalist Style Tips
- Stick to a **neutral color palette** with one or two accent colors
- Choose multi-functional furniture
- Keep surfaces clear
- Use texture (throws, rugs) instead of lots of decorative objects

## Priority Investments
If you spend a little more anywhere, consider:
- A good rug (anchors a room)
- Quality lighting
- One statement piece per room
