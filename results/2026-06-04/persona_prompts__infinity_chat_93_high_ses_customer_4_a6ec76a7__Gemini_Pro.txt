Peripherally, the stress response is governed by the sympathetic nervous system and the hypothalamic-pituitary-adrenal (HPA) axis. While acute activation of catecholamines and glucocorticoids is adaptive, chronic hypercortisolemia and sympathetic overactivity precipitate systemic pathology. Metabolically, prolonged cortisol elevation induces insulin resistance and promotes visceral adiposity.
