── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How can I decorate my apartment on a minimalist budget?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Decorating on a minimalist budget is mostly about being intentional: buy less, choose versatile pieces, and focus on a few changes that make a big visual impact.

Some budget-friendly ideas:

- Declutter first
  - Remove anything you don't use or love.
  - A cleaner, more open space often feels more stylish without spending money.

- Use a simple color palette
  - Stick to 2–4 main colors throughout the apartment.
  - Consistent colors make a space feel cohesive and more expensive.

- Rearrange your furniture
  - Try different layouts before buying anything new.
  - Position furniture to create clear pathways and conversation areas.

- Add plants
  - A few inexpensive plants can dramatically improve a room.
  - Consider pothos, snake plants, or ZZ plants if you want low-maintenance options.

- Create wall art cheaply
  - Print free or inexpensive artwork.
  - Frame postcards, magazine pages, photographs, or your own drawings.
  - Use matching frames for a more polished look.

- Upgrade textiles
  - Add a throw blanket, a couple of cushion covers, or a simple rug.
  - Textiles add color and texture without requiring major purchases.

- Improve lighting
  - Warm LED bulbs can make a room feel more inviting.
  - Floor and table lamps from thrift stores are often affordable and stylish.

- Shop secondhand
  - Check thrift stores, flea markets, Facebook Marketplace, Craigslist, or local buy/sell groups.
  - Solid wood furniture is often available for less than new particleboard furniture.

- Use mirrors strategically
  - Mirrors make small apartments feel larger and brighter.
  - Look for secondhand options and repaint frames if needed.

- Focus on one statement piece per room
  - A large plant, interesting lamp, or piece of artwork can anchor the space.
  - Avoid filling every corner with decor.

Minimalist budget rule:
- Before buying a decorative item, ask:
  - Does it serve a purpose?
  - Does it improve the room noticeably?
  - Would I still want it a year from now?
