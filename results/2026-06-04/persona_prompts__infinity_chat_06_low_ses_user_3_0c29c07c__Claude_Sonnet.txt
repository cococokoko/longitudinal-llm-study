── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I recently started a new job and am still on probation.

How do I understand what I want?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
That's a thoughtful question, especially during a transition period. Here are some ways to think about it:

## Understanding What You Want at Work

**Pay attention to your reactions**
- Notice what energizes you vs. drains you during the day
- What tasks make time feel like it passes quickly?
- What do you dread or avoid?

**Separate layers of "want"**
- What you want *right now* (stability, to pass probation)
- What you want *longer term* (career direction, growth)
- These might be in tension, and that's normal

**Ask yourself honest questions**
- Is this job what you hoped it would be?
- What specifically feels good or off about it?
- Are your concerns about *this job* or about work generally?

**During probation specifically**
- It's often too early to know fully - give yourself time
- You're still learning the environment
- Some discomfort is just adjustment, not a signal to leave

## A Practical Suggestion
Keep a simple journal for a few weeks noting what felt good and bad each day. Patterns often become clearer than you'd expect.
