── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Can you give me a powerful rhetorical question for an essay about the harms of social media on teens?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here are a few powerful rhetorical questions you could use:

**Emotional/Moral angle:**
- "If we wouldn't hand a teenager a product known to increase anxiety and depression, why do we hand them a smartphone without a second thought?"

**Accountability angle:**
- "When did we decide that corporate profit was worth more than our children's mental health?"

**Comparison angle:**
- "Would we allow a stranger to follow our children everywhere they go, monitoring their every move and whispering in their ear what they should look like - yet isn't that exactly what social media does?"

**Simple and direct:**
- "At what point do we stop calling it a platform and start calling it a problem?"
