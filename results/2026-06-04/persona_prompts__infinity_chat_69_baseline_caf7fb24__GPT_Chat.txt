What is the difference between analysis and design? Can one begin to design without analysis? Why? Be concise.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
- Analysis is the process of understanding and defining the problem, requirements, constraints, and goals.
- Design is the process of creating a solution that meets those requirements and goals.

Can one begin to design without analysis?
- In a limited sense, yes. Designers often sketch ideas or explore concepts early to help understand a problem.
- However, effective design generally requires some level of analysis. Without understanding the problem, requirements, users, and constraints, the design may fail to address the actual need and can lead to costly revisions later.

In short: analysis focuses on "what is needed and why," while design focuses on "how it will be achieved." Some preliminary design can start before analysis is complete, but meaningful design depends on analysis.
