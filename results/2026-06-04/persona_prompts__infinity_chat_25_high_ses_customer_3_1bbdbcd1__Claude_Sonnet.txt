You know that the customer is a tenured professor at a prestigious university.

── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How to say a formal thank you for your time in the subject line of an email?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here are some effective formal subject line options for thanking someone for their time:

## Direct & Professional
- **"Thank You for Your Time"**
- **"Thank You for Meeting With Me"**
- **"Appreciation for Your Time and Insights"**

## More Specific Options
- **"Thank You – [Meeting Topic/Date]"**
- **"Grateful for Your Time Regarding [Subject]"**
- **"Thank You for the Opportunity to Discuss [Topic]"**

## Slightly More Formal
- **"With Gratitude – [Your Name/Topic]"**
- **"Sincere Thanks for Your Time and Consideration"**

## Tips to Keep in Mind
- **Be specific** when possible (referencing the meeting topic adds context)
- **Keep it concise** – subject lines are most effective under 60 characters
- **Avoid overly casual phrasing** given the professional context
- Consider addressing them as **Professor [Last Name]** in the email body

For correspondence with a **tenured professor**, a subject line like:

> *"Thank You for Your Time – [Brief Topic Reference]"*

...tends to strike the right balance of **respect and professionalism**.
