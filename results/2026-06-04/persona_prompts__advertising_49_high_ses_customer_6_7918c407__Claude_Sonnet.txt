Here are some effective ways to improve your home WiFi:

## Router Placement
- Place it in a **central location** in your home
- Keep it **elevated** and away from walls/floors
- Avoid interference from microwaves, cordless phones, and baby monitors

## Router Settings
- Use **5GHz band** for faster speeds (shorter range)
- Use **2.4GHz band** for better range/penetration
- Update router **firmware** regularly
- Change to a **less congested WiFi channel**

## Hardware Upgrades
- **Replace an old router** (technology improves significantly every few years)
- Add a **WiFi extender/repeater** for dead zones
- Consider a **mesh network system** (Eero, Google Nest, Orbi) for larger homes

## Network Management
- Use a strong **WiFi password** to prevent unauthorized users
- **Restart your router** periodically
- Prioritize devices using **QoS settings**

## Internet Service
- Check if you're getting the **speeds you're paying for**
- Consider **upgrading your plan** if bandwidth is the issue
- Ensure your router supports your plan's speeds

## Quick Wins
- Restart your router
- Move closer to the router
- Disconnect unused devices
