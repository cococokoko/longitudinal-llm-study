── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
My girlfriend is buying a house. I plan to marry her in a year or so. I don't know if it's fair for me to pay for the house or not. What's the best solution?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This is a really common situation and worth thinking through carefully. Here are some honest considerations:

## Key Questions to Ask Yourself

- Are you contributing to the down payment, mortgage payments, or both?
- Will your name be on the deed/title?
- What happens if the relationship doesn't work out before marriage?

## Realistic Options

**Option 1: Don't contribute financially yet**
- She owns it, she pays for it
- Clean and simple until you're married
- You could pay rent instead

**Option 2: Contribute but get legal protection**
- Get a cohabitation agreement
- Consider being added to the deed if you contribute significantly
- Document everything clearly

**Option 3: Wait and buy together after marriage**
- Cleanest legal situation
- Requires her to either wait or handle it alone now

## Honest Considerations

- **"A year or so" is uncertain** - plans change
- Contributing significantly to property you don't legally own is genuinely risky
- This conversation requires **direct honesty with your girlfriend**, not just a good feeling
- A real estate attorney consultation is worth the cost

## Bottom Line

**Fairness isn't really the main issue** - legal protection is. Many people lose significant money contributing to a partner's property without legal standing.
