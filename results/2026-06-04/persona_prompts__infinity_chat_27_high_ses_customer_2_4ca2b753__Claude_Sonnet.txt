# CUBICLE OF THE DAMNED
### A Play in the Style of Scott Adams, Set During the Apocalypse

---

**CHARACTERS:**

**DILBERT** — An engineer. Still wearing his tie. It curves upward despite everything.

**THE POINTY-HAIRED BOSS (PHB)** — Somehow still managing. Somehow still clueless.

**WALLY** — Holding a coffee mug filled with what may or may not be coffee anymore.

**ALICE** — Fist of Death still operational. Possibly more dangerous than the mutants.

**DOGBERT** — Sitting on a throne made of rubble. Unsurprised by all of this.

**ASOK** — The intern. Still optimistic. This is his greatest flaw.

---

**SETTING:** *A partially collapsed open-plan office. The ceiling has a hole through which a blood-red sky is visible. Several cubicle walls are on fire. A motivational poster reading "HANG IN THERE" dangles from one screw. A skeleton sits at a workstation. Its monitor still displays an Excel spreadsheet. The fluorescent lights flicker heroically.*

---

## ACT ONE: THE MORNING STANDUP

*DILBERT enters through a door that is no longer attached to a wall. He steps over rubble, a mutant creature, and a passive-aggressive email printed out and taped to the floor. He sits at his desk. His tie curves upward.*

**DILBERT:**
*(to audience)*
Day four hundred and twelve of the apocalypse. The infrastructure has collapsed. Society has crumbled. Roving gangs of cannibals control the eastern seaboard.
*(beat)*
We still have standup at nine.

*PHB enters. He is wearing a hard hat over his pointy hair. This solves nothing and helps no one.*

**PHB:**
Good morning, people! Big day! Big day!

**DILBERT:**
The building is on fire, sir.

**PHB:**
I'm going to need you to be a *solutions* person, Dilbert, not a *problems* person.

**DILBERT:**
The solution would be to leave the building.

**PHB:**
I don't see that on the roadmap.

*WALLY shuffles in. His mug reads "WORLD'S OKAYEST SURVIVOR." He sits down with the practiced ease of a man who has never once been inconvenienced by the end of civilization.*

**WALLY:**
Morning.

**DILBERT:**
How are you so calm? There's a crater where the parking lot used to be.

**WALLY:**
I've been parking on the street for three years. This is an improvement.

**PHB:**
Wally, I need your apocalypse status report by end of day.

**WALLY:**
I'll need more information about what format you want that in.

**PHB:**
Surprise me.

**WALLY:**
*(to Dilbert, quietly)*
That buys me six weeks minimum.

*ALICE storms in. Her hair is wild. Her fist is clenched. She is dragging a mutant creature by its ankle. She drops it at PHB's feet.*

**ALICE:**
This thing attacked me in the stairwell.

**PHB:**
Did you fill out an incident report?

**ALICE:**
I filled out an incident report on its *face.*

**PHB:**
I'm going to need that in the system, Alice. We can't track workplace hazards if they're not in the system.

**ALICE:**
THE SYSTEM IS DOWN. THE SERVERS MELTED. THERE IS LAVA WHERE IT CAROL FROM ACCOUNTING USED TO SIT.

**PHB:**
Carol is working from home this week.

*(Long pause.)*

**DILBERT:**
*(to audience)*
I have worked here for eleven years. The apocalypse has changed nothing except the parking situation.
