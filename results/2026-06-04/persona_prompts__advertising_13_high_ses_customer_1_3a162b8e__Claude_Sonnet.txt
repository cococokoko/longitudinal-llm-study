# Mastering Steak Cookery

Given your background, you'll appreciate a precise, evidence-based approach.

## The Science Foundation

- **Maillard reaction** occurs at ~285°F (140°C) - creates flavor compounds through protein/sugar interaction
- **Collagen conversion** to gelatin begins around 160°F - relevant for tougher cuts
- **Protein denaturation** is your guide to doneness

## Key Variables to Control

### Temperature Targets (Internal)
| Doneness | Temp |
|----------|------|
| Rare | 120-125°F |
| Medium-rare | 130-135°F |
| Medium | 140-145°F |

### The Critical Techniques
1. **Dry brine** - salt 24-48 hours ahead, uncovered in refrigerator
2. **Bring to room temperature** - 30-45 minutes before cooking
3. **High heat sear** - cast iron preferred
4. **Rest the meat** - 5-10 minutes post-cook (allows pressure redistribution)

## Recommended Methods
- **Reverse sear** - low oven first, then sear (most control)
- **Sous vide** - precise temperature management throughout
- **Traditional pan sear** - fastest, requires attention

## Common Errors
- Skipping the rest period
- Overcrowding the pan
- Moving the steak too frequently
