── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here are some effective ways to improve indoor air quality:

## Ventilation
- Open windows when outdoor air quality permits
- Use exhaust fans in kitchens and bathrooms
- Ensure HVAC systems are properly maintained

## Filtration
- Use high-quality air filters (MERV 13 or HEPA filters)
- Replace filters regularly per manufacturer recommendations
- Consider standalone air purifiers for specific rooms

## Reducing Pollutant Sources
- Choose low-VOC paints, furniture, and building materials
- Avoid smoking indoors
- Store chemicals and cleaning products properly
- Use natural or fragrance-free cleaning products

## Humidity Control
- Keep humidity between **30-50%** to discourage mold and dust mites
- Use dehumidifiers in damp areas like basements
- Fix leaks and water damage promptly

## Regular Maintenance
- Vacuum with HEPA-filter vacuums regularly
- Clean air ducts periodically
- Test for radon, especially in lower levels of buildings
- Check for carbon monoxide with detectors

## Plants
- Some houseplants may offer modest air-filtering benefits, though research on their effectiveness is mixed

## Professional Assessment
- Consider hiring an indoor air quality specialist for comprehensive testing, particularly if occupants experience health symptoms
