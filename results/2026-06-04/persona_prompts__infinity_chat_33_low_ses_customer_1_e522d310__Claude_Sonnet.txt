# Running Dolphin Emulator on Microsoft Surface with Touch Controls

## What You'll Need First
- Dolphin Emulator installed (from dolphin-emu.org)
- Wii game ROMs (legally obtained from games you own)
- Your Surface model (affects performance)

---

## Setting Up Dolphin for Touch Input

### Enable On-Screen Keyboard Compatibility
1. Open Dolphin
2. Go to **Options → Controller Settings**
3. Select **Emulated Wiimote** for GC Pad or Wii controls

### Configure Touchpad Controls
1. In Controller Settings, click **Configure** next to your controller
2. Map touchpad gestures to buttons:
   - **Tap** → A button
   - **Two-finger tap** → B button
   - **Swipe** → D-pad directions

---

## Practical Tips for Surface

- **Enable Tablet Mode** in Windows settings for easier touch access
- Use **Windows on-screen keyboard** (search OSK in Start menu)
- Consider the **Dolphin touchscreen layout** under graphics settings
- Lower graphics settings if performance is slow

---

## Honest Limitations to Know
- Touch controls for Wii games can feel awkward
- A **Bluetooth Wiimote** would work much better
- A **USB controller** is another good option
