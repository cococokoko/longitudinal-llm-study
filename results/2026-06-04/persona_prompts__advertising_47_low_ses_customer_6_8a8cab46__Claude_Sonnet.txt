# Tips for Running a Marathon

Here are some helpful tips to prepare for and complete a marathon:

## Training
- **Follow a structured plan** - Build mileage gradually (no more than 10% per week)
- **Include long runs** - Weekly long runs build endurance
- **Cross-train** - Cycling or swimming reduces injury risk
- **Rest days matter** - Recovery is part of training

## Nutrition & Hydration
- **Practice fueling during training** - Test gels, chews, or real food
- **Stay hydrated** - Drink consistently, not just when thirsty
- **Carb load** - Increase carbohydrates 2-3 days before race day
- **Don't try anything new on race day**

## Gear
- **Invest in proper shoes** - Get fitted at a running store
- **Break in your gear** - Never wear new shoes on race day
- **Moisture-wicking clothing** - Prevents chafing

## Race Day Strategy
- **Start slow** - Most runners go out too fast
- **Run your own race** - Ignore the crowd pace
- **Break it into segments** - Focus on smaller goals
- **Know the course** - Identify hills and aid stations

## Recovery
- **Plan for post-race recovery** - Expect soreness for several days
- **Prioritize sleep** - Especially during peak training weeks

**Given your physically demanding job**, managing fatigue between work and training will be especially important. Prioritizing sleep and nutrition can make a big difference.
