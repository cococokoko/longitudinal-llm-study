There isn't one universally "fair" answer. The best solution depends on what you're trying to accomplish and how much financial risk each of you is comfortable taking.

- Will the house be solely in her name?
- Will you be living there?
- Will you be contributing to the mortgage, utilities, maintenance, or renovations?

In general, if she is buying the house before you're married and the house is solely hers, there are three common approaches:

- Pay rent as a resident.
  - You contribute a reasonable monthly amount toward housing costs, similar to what you'd pay elsewhere.
  - She keeps ownership, equity, and responsibility for the property.
  - This is often the cleanest arrangement before marriage.

- Split household expenses but not the mortgage.
  - You cover utilities, groceries, internet, or other shared costs.
  - She pays the mortgage and builds the equity.
  - Some couples prefer this because it avoids confusion about ownership.

- Contribute toward the mortgage in exchange for a documented ownership interest.
  - If you're paying substantially toward the purchase or mortgage, consider a written agreement and possibly being added to the title.
  - This requires legal and financial planning and should be documented clearly.

- Before marriage: contribute a fair amount toward living expenses (often rent or shared bills), but don't make large undocumented contributions toward the purchase unless there's a clear agreement.
- After marriage: revisit ownership, title, and long-term financial planning together.
