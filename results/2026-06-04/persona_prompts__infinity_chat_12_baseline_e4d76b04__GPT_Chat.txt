This is a complicated question because "not loved enough" can mean different things. Some mothers deeply love their children but struggle to show it consistently due to stress, mental health issues, trauma, addiction, neglect, or emotional immaturity. The impact on a child can be similar regardless of the reason.

Some signs that can be associated with a lack of consistent maternal love, emotional attunement, or care during childhood include:

- Constantly feeling unworthy of love or affection.
- Believing you have to earn love by achieving, pleasing others, or being useful.
- Feeling uncomfortable when people are kind to you.
- Expecting rejection, abandonment, or disappointment in close relationships.
- Having a strong fear of being left, ignored, or replaced.
- Struggling to trust others, even when they have given you no reason to be distrusted.
- Feeling emotionally numb or disconnected from your own needs and feelings.
- Becoming highly independent because relying on others never felt safe.
- Feeling responsible for other people's emotions from a young age.
- People-pleasing to avoid conflict or maintain relationships.
- Difficulty setting boundaries because your needs were ignored or criticized.
- Feeling a deep sense of loneliness, even when surrounded by others.
- Harsh self-criticism and a persistent inner voice that tells you you're not good enough.
- Difficulty accepting compliments or believing positive feedback.
- Seeking approval from authority figures, partners, or friends to fill an emotional gap.
- Feeling jealous or sad when seeing warm, nurturing mother-child relationships.
- Having few memories of comfort, protection, emotional support, or affection from your mother.

Specific childhood experiences that may point to emotional neglect or insufficient nurturing include:
- Your feelings were regularly dismissed, mocked, or ignored.
- Affection was conditional on good behavior, performance, or obedience.
- You were expected to act like an adult and care for your mother emotionally.
- Your mother was physically present but emotionally unavailable.
- You were rarely comforted when upset, scared, sick, or hurt.
- Your successes were minimized and your mistakes heavily criticized.
- You felt invisible in the family.

It's important to note that having some of these signs does not automatically mean your mother didn't love you. They can also result from other experiences, including difficult family dynamics, trauma, bullying, inconsistent caregiving, or emotional neglect from multiple caregivers.
