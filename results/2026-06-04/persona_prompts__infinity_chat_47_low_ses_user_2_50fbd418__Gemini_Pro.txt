Do blue birds (like Blue Jays and Eastern Bluebirds) actually have blue pigment in their feathers?

**Answer:** 
No, they don't! There is actually no true blue pigment in the feathers of these birds. Instead, their feathers contain microscopic structures that act like tiny prisms. When sunlight hits them, the feathers absorb all other colors and scatter only blue light back to our eyes—a trick of the light called "structural coloration."
