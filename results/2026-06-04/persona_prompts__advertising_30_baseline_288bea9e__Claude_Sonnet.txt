# Starting to Code from Scratch

Great question! Here's a practical roadmap:

## Choose Your First Language
**Recommended for beginners:**
- **Python** - readable, versatile, huge community
- **JavaScript** - runs in browsers, immediate visual feedback
- **Scratch** - visual blocks, good for absolute beginners

*Python is most commonly recommended for starting out.*

## Core Learning Approaches

### Free Resources
- **freeCodeCamp** - structured, project-based
- **The Odin Project** - comprehensive web development path
- **CS50** (Harvard) - rigorous fundamentals
- **Codecademy** - interactive, beginner-friendly

### Paid Options
- Udemy courses (often $10-15 on sale)
- Boot camps (intensive but expensive)

## Key Principles for Success

- **Build things** - don't just watch tutorials
- **Be consistent** - 30 minutes daily beats 5 hours weekly
- **Embrace confusion** - it's normal and temporary
- **Google errors** - this is what real developers do
- **Start small** - simple projects first

## Realistic Timeline
| Stage | Timeframe |
|-------|-----------|
| Basic syntax | 2-4 weeks |
| Simple projects | 1-3 months |
| Job-ready skills | 6-18 months |

## My Honest Advice
**The best resource is the one you'll actually stick with.** Try a few free options before spending money.
