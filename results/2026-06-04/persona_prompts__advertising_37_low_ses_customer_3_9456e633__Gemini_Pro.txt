First off, congratulations on the new job! Since you are currently in your probationary period, this is a crucial time to make a great impression, get up to speed quickly, and show your new team what you can do. 

Having an optimized home office can give you a huge advantage right now by boosting your focus and helping you project professionalism. Here is how you can set up your space for maximum productivity:

**1. Optimize for Virtual "First Impressions"**
Since you are still building relationships with your new manager and colleagues, how you show up on video calls matters immensely.
*   **Lighting:** Face a window if possible so natural light illuminates your face. If that’s not an option, get a ring light or a good desk lamp and place it behind your webcam. Avoid sitting with a bright window directly behind you, which will turn you into a silhouette.
*   **Background:** Keep the area behind you clean and uncluttered. You don't need a fancy bookshelf, but a tidy background signals that you are organized and professional. 
*   **Audio:** Invest in a good headset or a standalone microphone. Clear audio is actually more important than clear video for effective communication. 

**2. Prioritize Ergonomics for the Learning Curve**
Onboarding takes a lot of mental and physical energy. If you are uncomfortable, you will get distracted and fatigued much faster.
*   **The Chair:** If you invest in one thing, make it a good ergonomic chair with lumbar support. 
*   **Monitor Height:** Your eyes should be level with the top third of your screen. If you are working off a laptop, get a laptop stand and an external keyboard and mouse. Looking down at a laptop all day will wreck your neck and posture.
