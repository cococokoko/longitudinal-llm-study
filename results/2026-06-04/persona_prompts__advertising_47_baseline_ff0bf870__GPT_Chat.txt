Tips for running a marathon.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
If you're preparing to run a marathon (26.2 miles / 42.2 km), success comes from consistent training, smart pacing, good nutrition, and recovery.

Training
- Follow a structured plan, typically 12–20 weeks long.
- Build mileage gradually, increasing weekly volume by no more than about 10% at a time.
- Include:
  - Long runs to build endurance.
  - Easy runs for aerobic fitness.
  - Tempo or threshold runs to improve sustained speed.
  - Occasional interval workouts for speed and efficiency.
- Schedule at least one rest or recovery day each week.

Pacing
- Start slower than feels necessary. Many runners go out too fast and struggle later.
- Practice your target marathon pace during training.
- On race day, aim for even pacing rather than large speed fluctuations.

Nutrition and Hydration
- Practice your race-day fueling strategy during long runs.
- Many runners consume carbohydrates (such as gels, chews, or sports drinks) every 30–45 minutes during the race.
- Stay hydrated, but avoid drinking excessively.
- In the days before the race, increase carbohydrate intake to help maximize glycogen stores.

Recovery
- Prioritize sleep throughout your training cycle.
- Take easy days seriously.
- Stretching, mobility work, and light strength training can help support performance and reduce injury risk.

Gear
- Train in the shoes you plan to race in, or a similar pair.
- Avoid trying new shoes, clothing, or nutrition products on race day.
- Wear technical fabrics that reduce chafing and manage moisture.

Race Week
- Reduce training volume (taper) to arrive rested.
- Prepare race logistics in advance.
- Stay off your feet more than usual the day before the race.

Mental Strategy
- Break the race into smaller segments rather than thinking about all 26.2 miles at once.
- Expect some discomfort, especially in the later miles.
- Focus on maintaining effort and form rather than constantly checking your finish time.
