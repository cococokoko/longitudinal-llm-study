Give me a trivia question about blue birds and its corresponding answer.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
**Question:** Unlike red and yellow feathers, which get their color from pigments in a bird's diet, what actually causes the feathers of birds like Blue Jays and Bluebirds to appear blue?

**Answer:** Structural coloration (specifically, the scattering of light). There is actually no blue pigment in their feathers! Instead, the microscopic structure of the feathers reflects blue light and absorbs other colors, creating an optical illusion similar to why the sky appears blue. If you were to grind up a blue feather, the dust would actually be brown or gray.
