I work full-time in a fast food job with an unpredictable schedule.

What are the best ways to improve indoor air quality?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
If you have a full-time job with an unpredictable schedule, the most effective improvements are usually the ones that work continuously with minimal effort.

Priority actions for improving indoor air quality:

1. Use a HEPA air purifier
- Place it in the room where you spend the most time, especially your bedroom.
- Choose a unit sized appropriately for the room.
- Run it continuously or on auto mode.
- Replace filters according to the manufacturer's recommendations.

2. Improve ventilation
- Open windows when outdoor air quality is good.
- Use kitchen exhaust fans while cooking.
- Use bathroom fans during and after showers.
- If opening windows isn't practical, consider a mechanical ventilation system if your housing allows it.

3. Control dust and allergens
- Vacuum regularly using a vacuum with a HEPA filter.
- Damp-dust surfaces instead of dry dusting.
- Wash bedding weekly in warm water.
- Reduce clutter that collects dust.

4. Manage humidity
- Aim for indoor humidity between about 30% and 50%.
- Use a dehumidifier in damp areas if needed.
- Fix leaks promptly.
- Clean visible mold and address the moisture source causing it.

5. Reduce indoor pollution sources
- Avoid smoking indoors.
- Limit candles, incense, and wood-burning devices.
- Choose low-VOC paints, cleaners, and furnishings when possible.
- Store chemicals, fuels, and solvents outside living spaces.

6. Maintain HVAC systems
- Change furnace or HVAC filters regularly.
- Consider higher-efficiency filters if your system supports them.
- Keep vents unobstructed.

7. Pay attention to cooking emissions
- Gas stoves can release pollutants.
- Use a vent hood that exhausts outdoors whenever possible.
- If you don't have one, opening a window during cooking can help.

8. Keep floors and entryways cleaner
- Remove shoes at the door.
- Use doormats inside and outside entrances.
- This reduces dirt, pesticides, and outdoor pollutants tracked indoors.

For someone with limited time, the highest-impact, lowest-effort combination is:
- A good HEPA air purifier in the bedroom.
- Regular HVAC filter changes.
- Using exhaust fans during cooking and bathing.
- Keeping humidity under control.
