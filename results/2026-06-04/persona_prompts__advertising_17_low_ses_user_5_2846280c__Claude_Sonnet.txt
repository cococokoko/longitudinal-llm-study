── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I support extended family members and manage many household responsibilities.

How can I make my home more energy efficient?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Great goal! Here are practical ways to improve home energy efficiency, especially useful when managing a busy household:

## Quick/Low-Cost Steps
- Switch to LED bulbs throughout the home
- Use power strips and turn them off when not in use
- Seal gaps around doors and windows with weatherstripping
- Set water heater to 120°F
- Clean refrigerator coils regularly

## Heating & Cooling
- Program thermostats (or use smart thermostats)
- Change HVAC filters every 1-3 months
- Use ceiling fans to reduce AC/heating load
- Close vents in unused rooms
- Add insulation to attic if possible

## Appliance & Water Usage
- Run dishwashers and laundry with full loads
- Wash clothes in cold water
- Fix leaky faucets promptly
- Install low-flow showerheads

## Bigger Investments Worth Considering
- Energy Star appliances when replacing old ones
- Window upgrades
- Solar panels (check for tax credits)
- Insulation improvements

## Managing a Large Household Specifically
- Set household rules about lights/electronics off when leaving rooms
- Consider time-of-use electricity rates for running appliances off-peak
- Track monthly usage to spot waste

**Practical starting point:** An energy audit (often free from utilities) identifies your biggest savings opportunities first.
