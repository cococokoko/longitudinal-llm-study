Give a numerical example to illustrate the concept of partial derivative.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
A partial derivative measures how a function changes with respect to one variable while keeping the other variables fixed.

Example:

Consider the function

f(x, y) = x²y + 3xy

Suppose we want the partial derivatives at the point (x, y) = (2, 4).

1. Partial derivative with respect to x

Treat y as a constant:

∂f/∂x = 2xy + 3y

Substitute x = 2 and y = 4:

∂f/∂x(2,4) = 2·2·4 + 3·4
            = 16 + 12
            = 28

Interpretation:
Near the point (2,4), if x increases by a small amount while y remains fixed at 4, the function increases at approximately 28 units per unit increase in x.

2. Partial derivative with respect to y

Treat x as a constant:

∂f/∂y = x² + 3x

Substitute x = 2:

∂f/∂y(2,4) = 2² + 3·2
            = 4 + 6
            = 10

Interpretation:
Near the point (2,4), if y increases by a small amount while x remains fixed at 2, the function increases at approximately 10 units per unit increase in y.

Numerical check:

First compute

f(2,4) = 2²·4 + 3·2·4 = 16 + 24 = 40

Increase x slightly to 2.01 while keeping y = 4:

f(2.01,4) = (2.01)²·4 + 3·2.01·4
          = 40.2804

Change in f:

40.2804 − 40 = 0.2804

The partial derivative predicted a change of

28 × 0.01 = 0.28

which is very close to the actual change 0.2804.

This illustrates that a partial derivative gives the rate of change of a multivariable function with respect to one variable while the others are held constant.
