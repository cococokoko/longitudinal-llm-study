# Arguments in Support of the Death Penalty

Here are the strongest arguments made by proponents:

## Deterrence
- May discourage potential offenders from committing capital crimes
- Creates a permanent consequence that lesser punishments cannot match
- Some studies suggest correlations between executions and reduced murder rates

## Justice and Proportionality
- Some crimes are considered so severe that only death constitutes proportional punishment
- Reflects the moral principle that punishment should match the harm caused
- Gives weight to the value of victims' lives

## Public Safety
- Permanently eliminates the possibility of reoffending
- Protects prison staff and other inmates from dangerous individuals
- Prevents escape scenarios

## Closure for Victims' Families
- Some families report a sense of justice being served
- Acknowledges the severity of what was taken from them

## Societal Expression of Values
- Allows society to formally declare certain acts completely unacceptable
- Democratic majorities in many places have chosen to retain it

## Practical Arguments
- Reduces long-term incarceration costs in some analyses
- Frees resources for rehabilitation of other offenders

## Moral Responsibility
- Holds individuals fully accountable for their choices
- Respects human agency by treating offenders as responsible actors
