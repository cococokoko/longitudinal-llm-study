── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I am a senior attorney at a well-established law firm.

What are the best ways to improve indoor air quality?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Improving indoor air quality (IAQ) is usually most effective when approached through four areas: source control, ventilation, filtration, and humidity management.

Key strategies include:

1. Control Pollution at the Source
- Prohibit or limit indoor smoking and vaping.
- Select low-VOC (volatile organic compound) paints, adhesives, furnishings, and cleaning products.
- Store solvents, fuels, and chemicals in detached or well-ventilated areas.
- Address mold, water intrusion, and leaks promptly.
- Use entry mats and encourage removal of outdoor shoes to reduce dirt, pesticides, and other contaminants.
- Regularly clean areas where dust accumulates, including carpets, upholstery, and HVAC components.

2. Improve Ventilation
- Increase outdoor air exchange when weather and outdoor air quality permit.
- Use mechanical ventilation systems that bring in fresh outdoor air.
- Operate kitchen range hoods that vent outdoors while cooking.
- Use bathroom exhaust fans during and after showering.
- Verify that HVAC systems are providing adequate fresh-air intake and that vents are unobstructed.

3. Enhance Air Filtration
- Use high-quality HVAC filters compatible with the system. Many commercial and residential systems perform well with filters rated MERV 13 or higher if the equipment can accommodate them.
- Replace filters on schedule.
- Consider portable HEPA air purifiers in frequently occupied spaces, conference rooms, bedrooms, or areas with known air quality concerns.
- Ensure air cleaners are appropriately sized for the room.

4. Manage Humidity
- Maintain indoor relative humidity generally between 30% and 50%.
- Use dehumidifiers in damp environments.
- Repair leaks and condensation issues quickly.
- Ensure adequate drainage around the building exterior.

5. Address Specific Pollutants
- Radon: Test and mitigate if elevated, particularly in lower levels and certain geographic regions.
- Carbon monoxide: Install and maintain detectors; inspect combustion appliances regularly.
- Allergens: Control pet dander, dust mites, and pollen through cleaning and filtration.
- Particulate matter: Use exhaust ventilation during cooking and consider induction cooking where feasible.

6. Maintain HVAC Systems
- Conduct regular inspections and preventive maintenance.
- Clean coils, drain pans, and other components prone to moisture buildup.
- Balance airflow and ensure proper operation of dampers and ventilation controls.
- Periodically evaluate system performance, especially in older buildings.

For a law office environment specifically:
- Upgrade HVAC filtration where feasible.
- Use portable HEPA units in conference rooms and high-occupancy areas.
- Monitor carbon dioxide levels as an indicator of ventilation adequacy.
- Ensure cleaning contractors use low-emission products.
- Conduct periodic indoor air quality assessments if employees report persistent symptoms or comfort concerns.

The highest-impact measures for most offices are typically: adequate outdoor-air ventilation, MERV 13+ filtration, portable HEPA filtration in occupied spaces, moisture/mold prevention, and source control of pollutants.
