# Improving Productivity While Working From Home

Here are some practical strategies tailored to your situation:

## Creating Structure Around Your Schedule

- **Work during nap times or school hours** when possible
- **Set clear start/end times** so work doesn't bleed into family time
- **Communicate your schedule** to your child so they understand "work time"
- **Build in buffer time** for unexpected interruptions - they will happen

## Managing Interruptions Realistically

- Keep **quiet activities ready** for when you need focused time
- Use **visual cues** (headphones on = busy) that your child can learn
- **Batch your focused work** into shorter, intense sessions rather than expecting long uninterrupted blocks
- **Accept that some interruptions are okay** - fighting them constantly is exhausting

## Practical Tools

- **Time blocking** your calendar around childcare rhythms
- **Task lists organized by effort level** - save easy tasks for when you're interrupted frequently
- **Noise-canceling headphones** for focus
- **Communication tools** to stay connected with colleagues asynchronously

## Being Kind to Yourself

- **Productivity looks different** for single parents - that's completely valid
- **Done is better than perfect** on high-interruption days
- Celebrate what you **did** accomplish rather than focusing on what you didn't

## Quick Win

Identify your **two most important tasks each morning** before anything else gets complicated.
