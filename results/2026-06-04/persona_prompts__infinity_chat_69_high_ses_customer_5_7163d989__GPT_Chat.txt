What is the difference between analysis and design? Can one begin to design without analysis? Why? Be concise.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Analysis and design are related but distinct activities:

- Analysis focuses on understanding the problem: goals, requirements, constraints, stakeholders, and the current situation.
- Design focuses on creating a solution: architecture, structure, components, processes, interfaces, and implementation approaches.

Can design begin without analysis?

- Yes, but only in a preliminary or exploratory sense. Designers often sketch concepts early to help clarify the problem.
- However, effective design requires at least some analysis. Without understanding the problem, requirements, and constraints, the design is likely to be misaligned, inefficient, or incomplete.

In practice, analysis and design are iterative: initial analysis informs design, and design work often reveals the need for further analysis.
