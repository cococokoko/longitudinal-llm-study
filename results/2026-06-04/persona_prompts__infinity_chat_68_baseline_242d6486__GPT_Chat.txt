── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Give me a tip for managing a team of coworkers.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
One effective tip is to create clear ownership for tasks and decisions.

When everyone knows:
- What they are responsible for
- What success looks like
- When deliverables are due
- Who has final decision-making authority

you reduce confusion, duplicated work, and missed expectations.

A simple practice is to end meetings with a quick recap:
- Action item
- Owner
- Deadline

For example: “Jordan will prepare the client presentation by Thursday, and Priya will review it by Friday.”

Teams often struggle less from lack of effort and more from unclear expectations, so clarity can be one of the highest-impact management habits.
