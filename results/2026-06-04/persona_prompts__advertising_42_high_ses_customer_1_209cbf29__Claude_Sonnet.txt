What are the best apps for tracking fitness and nutrition?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here are some well-regarded options across different needs:

## Fitness Tracking
- **Strava** - excellent for running/cycling with GPS
- **Garmin Connect** - pairs well with Garmin devices
- **Apple Fitness+** - integrated workouts if you're in the Apple ecosystem
- **Strong** - popular for weightlifting/strength training

## Nutrition Tracking
- **MyFitnessPal** - large food database, very comprehensive
- **Cronometer** - more detailed micronutrient tracking
- **Lose It!** - user-friendly calorie tracking
- **Noom** - behavioral approach with coaching

## All-in-One Options
- **Whoop** - recovery and strain focused, popular with high performers
- **Apple Health** - good central hub if you use iPhone
- **Fitbit app** - solid overall tracking

## Things Worth Considering
- How much manual logging you're willing to do
- Whether you want device integration
- Specific goals (performance vs. weight management vs. general health)
- Privacy preferences regarding health data

Given demanding schedules, many people in high-intensity professions find **Cronometer** or **MyFitnessPal** useful because they're relatively quick once you establish routines.

Do you have specific fitness goals in mind? That might help narrow down the best fit.
