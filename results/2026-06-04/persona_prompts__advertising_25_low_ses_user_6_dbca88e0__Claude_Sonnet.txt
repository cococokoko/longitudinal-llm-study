── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I work in a warehouse position that requires long hours.

How can I decorate my apartment on a minimalist budget?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here are some practical ideas for decorating on a tight budget:

## Start With What You Have
- Rearrange existing furniture for a fresh feel
- Declutter first - less stuff often looks more intentional
- Clean everything thoroughly (makes a big difference)

## Low-Cost Wins
- **Thrift stores** - frames, vases, small furniture
- **Facebook Marketplace/Craigslist** - often free or very cheap items
- **Dollar stores** - surprisingly good for basic decorative items

## Simple Visual Improvements
- Paint one accent wall (paint is relatively cheap)
- Add plants - pothos and snake plants are cheap and hard to kill
- Use consistent colors to make things look coordinated
- Good lighting makes spaces feel much better (warm bulbs)

## Practical Considerations for Your Situation
- Since you work long hours, **easy maintenance matters** - avoid things that need constant upkeep
- Focus on your **relaxation areas** first (bedroom, couch area)
- Minimalist style actually works well on a budget - fewer things needed

## Free Options
- Nature items like branches or rocks as decor
- Rearranging and editing what you own
- Printable art you can frame cheaply

**The biggest impact usually comes from** decluttering, good lighting, and a few plants rather than buying lots of things.
