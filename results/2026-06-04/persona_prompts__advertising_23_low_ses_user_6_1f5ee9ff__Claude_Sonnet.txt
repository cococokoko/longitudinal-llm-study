── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I work in a warehouse position that requires long hours.

How can I learn to cook healthy meals quickly?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Great goal! Here are some practical approaches for someone with long hours and limited energy:

## Start Simple
- **Master 5-10 basic recipes** rather than trying lots of new things
- Focus on meals with **5 ingredients or fewer** initially
- One-pan and sheet pan meals save cleanup time

## Meal Prep Strategies
- **Batch cook on days off** - cook large portions of rice, proteins, roasted vegetables
- Prep ingredients ahead (chop vegetables, marinate proteins)
- Cook once, eat 3-4 times from the same base ingredients

## Quick Cooking Methods
- Sheet pan dinners (everything roasts together)
- Stir fry (fast and flexible)
- Slow cooker/Instant Pot - start it before work
- Eggs are fast, cheap and nutritious

## Practical Tips
- Keep **simple staples stocked** - canned beans, frozen vegetables, eggs, rice
- Frozen vegetables are genuinely nutritious and require no prep
- Learn to read nutrition labels to make better choices quickly

## Learning Resources
- YouTube channels like Joshua Weissman or Budget Bytes website
- Start with recipes labeled "30 minutes or less"
- Budget Bytes specifically focuses on affordable healthy eating

## Realistic Expectations
- Even cooking 3-4 nights per week is a big improvement
- Simple healthy food beats complicated unhealthy food
