── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
What is a good secondhand market laptop for learning Python?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
For learning Python, you don't need a powerful laptop. A good secondhand business-class laptop is often a better choice than a cheap consumer model because it's more durable, easier to repair, and usually has a better keyboard.

Good secondhand options:

- Lenovo ThinkPad T480
  - Intel 8th-generation Core i5 or i7
  - Upgradeable RAM and SSD
  - Excellent keyboard
  - Supports Windows 11
  - Often considered one of the best-value used laptops

- Lenovo ThinkPad X280
  - Smaller and lighter than the T480
  - Good if portability matters
  - Less upgradeable

- Dell Latitude 5490 or 5500
  - Business-grade build quality
  - Easy maintenance
  - Good Linux compatibility

- HP EliteBook 840 G5 or G6
  - Solid construction
  - Good screen and keyboard
  - Widely available used

- ThinkPad T490
  - Newer than the T480
  - Better battery life and efficiency
  - Usually costs a bit more

Recommended minimum specifications:
- Intel Core i5 8th generation or newer (or AMD Ryzen 5)
- 8 GB RAM minimum (16 GB preferred)
- 256 GB SSD minimum
- 1080p (Full HD) screen if possible

If you plan to:
- Learn basic Python, data structures, web development, automation:
  - 8 GB RAM is fine.
- Use data science tools (Pandas, Jupyter, machine learning):
  - Aim for 16 GB RAM.

Models I'd avoid unless they're extremely cheap:
- Laptops with Intel 6th or 7th generation CPUs
- Machines with only 4 GB RAM
- Mechanical hard drives instead of SSDs
- Very low-resolution (1366×768) displays

For many people, a used Lenovo ThinkPad T480 with an i5-8350U, 16 GB RAM, and a 256–512 GB SSD is the best balance of price, reliability, and performance for learning Python.
