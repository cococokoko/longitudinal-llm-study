# Best Practices for Organic Gardening

Here's a straightforward guide to organic gardening fundamentals:

## Soil Health
- **Compost regularly** - add organic matter to improve structure and nutrients
- **Test soil pH** - most vegetables prefer 6.0-7.0
- **Avoid tilling excessively** - preserves soil microbiome
- **Use cover crops** to prevent erosion and add nitrogen

## Pest & Disease Management
- **Companion planting** - basil near tomatoes, marigolds as borders
- **Encourage beneficial insects** - ladybugs, lacewings, ground beetles
- **Rotate crops** annually to break pest/disease cycles
- **Remove diseased plants promptly**

## Watering
- **Water deeply and infrequently** rather than shallow/frequent
- **Morning watering** reduces fungal disease risk
- **Drip irrigation** maximizes efficiency

## Fertilizing
- Use **compost, worm castings, fish emulsion, or kelp meal**
- Avoid synthetic fertilizers
- **Feed the soil**, not just the plant

## Weed Control
- Mulch heavily (2-3 inches)
- Hand pull when young
- Use cardboard/newspaper as weed barriers

## General Tips
- Start with **easy crops** for your climate
- Keep a **garden journal**
- **Observe regularly** - early detection matters
