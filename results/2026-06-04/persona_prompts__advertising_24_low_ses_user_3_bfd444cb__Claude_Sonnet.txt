── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I recently started a new job and am still on probation.

What are the best ways to save for retirement?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here are some solid retirement saving strategies to consider:

## Start with the Basics

- **Build an emergency fund first** - 3-6 months of expenses so you don't raid retirement savings
- **Understand your employer's benefits** - check if they offer a 401(k) or pension, especially once probation ends

## Key Saving Vehicles

- **401(k)/403(b)** - employer-sponsored plans, often with matching contributions
- **IRA (Traditional or Roth)** - individual accounts you control regardless of employer
- **Roth IRA** - especially good if you're in a lower tax bracket now

## General Priorities

1. Get the full employer match if available (it's essentially free money)
2. Max out an HSA if eligible
3. Max out IRA contributions
4. Return to 401(k) for additional contributions

## Practical Tips

- **Start early** - compound interest rewards time significantly
- **Automate contributions** - pay yourself first
- **Increase contributions gradually** with raises
- **Keep fees low** - look for index funds

## Things to Consider on Probation

- You may not be eligible for employer benefits yet
- Use this time to open a Roth IRA independently
- Even small contributions matter
