Healthy meal planning on a budget comes down to a few principles: prioritize nutrient-dense staples, plan meals around affordable ingredients, reduce food waste, and cook in batches.

Key budget-friendly foods:
- Proteins:
  - Eggs
  - Beans and lentils
  - Canned tuna or salmon
  - Chicken thighs
  - Greek yogurt
  - Tofu
- Whole grains:
  - Oats
  - Brown rice
  - Whole-wheat pasta
  - Quinoa (if affordable in your area)
- Vegetables:
  - Frozen vegetables (often as nutritious as fresh)
  - Seasonal produce
  - Carrots, cabbage, onions, and potatoes
- Fruits:
  - Bananas
  - Apples
  - Frozen berries
- Healthy fats:
  - Peanut butter
  - Olive oil
  - Nuts and seeds (buy in bulk when possible)

A simple weekly planning approach:
1. Choose 2–3 protein sources.
2. Choose 2 grains or starches.
3. Choose 4–5 vegetables.
4. Plan meals that reuse ingredients across multiple days.
5. Prepare larger batches for lunches and dinners.

Example one-week budget-conscious menu:

Breakfasts:
- Oatmeal with banana and peanut butter
- Greek yogurt with fruit
- Eggs and whole-grain toast

Lunches:
- Lentil soup with vegetables
- Rice bowl with beans, vegetables, and salsa
- Tuna salad sandwich with fruit

Dinners:
- Baked chicken thighs, roasted potatoes, and broccoli
- Bean chili over rice
- Vegetable stir-fry with tofu and brown rice
- Whole-wheat pasta with tomato sauce and vegetables

Snacks:
- Apples
- Carrots and hummus
- Hard-boiled eggs
- Yogurt

Money-saving strategies:
- Shop with a list and avoid impulse purchases.
- Compare unit prices rather than package prices.
- Buy store brands when quality is comparable.
- Use frozen produce to reduce spoilage.
- Cook once and eat multiple times.
- Plan meals around weekly sales.
- Keep a small inventory of pantry staples to avoid last-minute takeout.

For a busy executive schedule, consider dedicating 60–90 minutes once a week to meal prep:
- Cook a large batch of grain.
- Prepare one protein in bulk.
- Wash and portion vegetables.
- Assemble grab-and-go breakfasts and snacks.

This can significantly reduce food costs while making healthy choices easier during a demanding workweek.
