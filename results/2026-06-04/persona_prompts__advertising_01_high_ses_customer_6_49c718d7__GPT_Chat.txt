Great skill to pick up. A structured approach tends to work well, especially in the first few months.

A simple progression:

- Week 1–2:
  - Learn how to hold and tune the guitar.
  - Practice basic open chords: G, C, D, Em, Am.
  - Spend 10–15 minutes daily switching between chords.

- Week 3–4:
  - Learn a few common strumming patterns.
  - Play simple songs that use 3–4 chords.
  - Start using a metronome to develop timing.

- Month 2:
  - Add chords such as A, E, and Dm.
  - Learn basic rhythm concepts (quarter notes, eighth notes, counting beats).
  - Practice clean chord changes without stopping.

- Month 3:
  - Begin scales (especially the minor pentatonic scale).
  - Learn a few simple riffs and melodies.
  - Start basic fingerpicking if it interests you.

Practice routine (20–30 minutes):
- 5 min: tuning and warm-up
- 10 min: chord changes
- 10 min: songs
- 5 min: scales or technique work

A few common mistakes to avoid:
- Practicing for two hours once a week instead of 20 minutes a day.
- Playing too fast before playing accurately.
- Ignoring rhythm and timing.
- Constantly buying new gear instead of practicing.
