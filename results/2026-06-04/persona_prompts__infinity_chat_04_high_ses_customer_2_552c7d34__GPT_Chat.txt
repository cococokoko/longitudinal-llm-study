Absolutely. André Gorz sits at the intersection of Marxism, political ecology, labor theory, technology studies, and critiques of capitalism, which leaves a lot of room for original research. One challenge is that many of the standard readings focus on his critique of work and his ecological politics; a stronger thesis often emerges by putting Gorz into conversation with contemporary debates he did not directly address.

Some possible directions:

1. Gorz and Artificial Intelligence: The Crisis of Labor as a Source of Value
- Examine whether contemporary AI validates Gorz's argument that knowledge and immaterial labor undermine classical capitalist value production.
- Compare Gorz's later work (especially Critique of Economic Reason and Reclaiming Work) with contemporary discussions of AI-driven productivity.
- Research question:
  - Does generative AI intensify the contradiction between exchange value and socially produced knowledge that Gorz identified?

2. Gorz versus Marx on the Future of Work
- Gorz gradually moved away from labor-centered conceptions of emancipation.
- Analyze whether his position represents a development of Marx's "realm of freedom" or a departure from Marx's emphasis on labor.
- Research question:
  - Is Gorz best understood as a post-Marxist, neo-Marxist, or heterodox Marxist theorist of liberation beyond work?

3. Gorz and Platform Capitalism
- Apply Gorz's theory of immaterial labor to digital platforms, social media, and data extraction.
- Investigate whether unpaid online activity exemplifies Gorz's claim that capitalism increasingly exploits capacities developed outside formal employment.
- Research question:
  - How does platform capitalism transform Gorz's distinction between autonomous activity and heteronomous work?

4. The Ecological Critique of Productivism
- Compare Gorz's ecological Marxism with contemporary degrowth theory.
- Many degrowth scholars cite Gorz, but there is room to examine where they diverge.
- Research question:
  - Did Gorz anticipate degrowth, or does his political vision differ in important respects from contemporary degrowth movements?

5. Gorz and the Politics of Universal Basic Income
- Gorz was an important advocate of guaranteed income.
- Examine how his arguments differ from liberal or technocratic defenses of UBI.
- Research question:
  - Can Gorz's conception of basic income be understood as an anti-capitalist institution rather than a welfare reform?

6. Gorz and Social Reproduction Theory
- Gorz focused on activities outside wage labor, but feminist theorists often criticized Marxist accounts of reproduction.
- Compare Gorz with Silvia Federici, Nancy Fraser, or Lise Vogel.
- Research question:
  - Does Gorz provide conceptual tools for integrating social reproduction into critiques of political economy?

7. Gorz's Concept of Autonomy and Contemporary Republican Theory
- Place Gorz in dialogue with Philip Pettit or neo-republican theories of freedom.
- Research question:
  - Is Gorz's notion of autonomy closer to socialist self-management or republican non-domination?

8. Gorz and Climate Politics
- Analyze how Gorz's ecological arguments speak to climate-transition policies.
- Research question:
  - Can Gorz's critique of consumerism and growth provide a framework for a democratic climate transition?

9. Knowledge, Commons, and Intellectual Property
- Gorz argued that knowledge resists commodification.
- Examine this claim in relation to software, open-source production, and intellectual-property law.
- Research question:
  - Does contemporary intellectual-property expansion represent capitalism's attempt to overcome the contradictions Gorz identified in the knowledge economy?

10. Existentialism and Marxism in Early Gorz
- Many studies emphasize the later ecological Gorz and neglect his Sartrean roots.
- Trace the continuity between his early existentialism and later political ecology.
- Research question:
  - How did Gorz's Sartrean conception of freedom shape his eventual critique of labor and growth?

A few less-explored combinations that could produce genuinely original work:

- Gorz and AI governance.
- Gorz and social reproduction theory.
- Gorz and intellectual-property law in the knowledge economy.
- Gorz and degrowth viewed through the lens of value theory.
- Gorz's concept of autonomy applied to platform-mediated work.
