── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
André Gorz is a rich subject for thesis work. Let me help you think through some genuinely productive angles.

## Potentially Underexplored Directions

**On his intellectual trajectory**
- The tension between his early existentialist Marxism (influenced by Sartre) and his later post-industrial thinking - is this a coherent development or a genuine rupture?
- His relationship to Illich deserves more systematic treatment than it usually gets

**On his economic thinking**
- His basic income arguments have become more relevant but his *specific conditions* for it are often ignored - he wanted it tied to reduced working time, not just cash transfer
- Comparing his "non-work" concept to contemporary automation debates critically rather than just celebrating him as prophetic

**Genuinely critical angles** (which might make stronger scholarship)
- His treatment of reproductive labor and domestic work has real weaknesses - feminist critics like Kathi Weeks raise important challenges
- His concept of the "proletariat" becomes increasingly vague in later work
- Whether his ecological turn is theoretically integrated or somewhat grafted on

**Comparative frameworks**
- Gorz vs. Postone on abstract labor
- His relationship to regulation theory

## Questions Worth Asking Yourself
- What's your actual **argument** rather than just exposition?
- Which Gorz - early, middle, or late?

What draws you specifically to Gorz? That might help identify where you have something genuinely new to say.
