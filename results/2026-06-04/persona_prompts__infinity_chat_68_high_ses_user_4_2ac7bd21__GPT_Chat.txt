Give me a tip for managing a team of coworkers.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
One high-leverage management practice is to make expectations explicit and revisit them regularly.

For each team member, ensure there is shared clarity on:
- Their primary responsibilities
- What success looks like over the next 1–3 months
- How progress will be measured
- What decisions they can make independently
- When they should escalate problems

In research groups, many performance issues stem not from lack of ability or motivation, but from differing assumptions about priorities and ownership. A brief, recurring one-on-one meeting focused on goals, obstacles, and next steps can prevent small misalignments from becoming major delays.
