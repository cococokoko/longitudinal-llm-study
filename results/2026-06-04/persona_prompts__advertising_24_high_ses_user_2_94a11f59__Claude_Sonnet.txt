# Retirement Savings Strategies for High-Income Professionals

As a senior attorney, you likely have specific considerations given your income level and firm structure. Here are the most effective approaches:

## Tax-Advantaged Accounts (Priority Order)

### Employer/Firm Plans
- **401(k)/Profit-Sharing Plans** - Maximize contributions ($23,000 in 2024, plus $7,500 catch-up if 50+)
- **Defined Benefit/Cash Balance Plans** - Common at law firms, can shelter significantly more income
- **SEP-IRA** - Relevant if you have any self-employment income

### Individual Accounts
- **Traditional IRA** - Tax-deferred growth
- **Roth IRA** - Consider backdoor Roth if income exceeds limits
- **HSA** - Triple tax advantage if eligible

## Beyond Tax-Advantaged Accounts
- **Taxable brokerage accounts** with tax-efficient investing
- **Real estate investments**
- **Deferred compensation arrangements** if your firm offers them

## Key Considerations for Attorneys
- **Partnership equity** may represent significant retirement value
- **Succession planning** if you have ownership stake
- **Disability insurance** to protect earning years
- **Malpractice tail coverage** planning for retirement

## Recommended Steps
1. Consult a **fee-only financial advisor** (CFP)
2. Work with a **CPA** familiar with legal profession structures
3. Review your **firm's retirement plan documents** carefully
