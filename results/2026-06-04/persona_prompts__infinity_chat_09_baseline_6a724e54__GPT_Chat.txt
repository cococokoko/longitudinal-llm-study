What we know:

- Water, organic molecules, and other ingredients associated with life appear to be common in space.

- Space is vast, making direct observations difficult.
- Even if life is common, intelligent civilizations may be rare, short-lived, or far away.
- Detecting biological signatures on distant planets is technically challenging.

- There is no confirmed evidence of extraterrestrial life.
- There is also no evidence that Earth is uniquely capable of supporting life.

So the scientific answer is: life elsewhere in the universe is a credible possibility, but it has not yet been proven.
