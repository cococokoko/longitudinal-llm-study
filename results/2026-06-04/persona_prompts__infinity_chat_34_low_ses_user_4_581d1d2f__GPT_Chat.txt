For learning Python, you don't need a powerful laptop. A reliable business-class laptop from a few years ago is usually the best value on the secondhand market because they're durable, easy to repair, and often much better built than budget consumer laptops.

Good used options:

- Lenovo ThinkPad T480
  - One of the most recommended used laptops.
  - Comfortable keyboard, excellent reliability.
  - Typically available with 8–16 GB RAM.
  - Easy to upgrade RAM and SSD.
  - Often found for around $150–300 USD depending on condition.

- Lenovo ThinkPad T490
  - Newer and lighter than the T480.
  - Good battery life and solid performance.
  - Usually $250–400 USD.

- Dell Latitude 5490 / 5500 / 5400
  - Business-class Dell models.
  - Reliable and commonly available from corporate surplus.
  - Good Linux compatibility if you decide to experiment later.

- HP EliteBook 840 G5 or G6
  - Sturdy aluminum chassis.
  - Good keyboard and display.
  - Often competitively priced used.

- ThinkPad X280 or X390
  - Smaller 12–13-inch laptops.
  - Excellent if portability matters for carrying to work or studying during breaks.
  - Less upgradeable than the T-series.

Minimum specs I would look for in 2025:
- Intel Core i5 8th generation or newer (e.g., i5-8250U, i5-8350U)
- 8 GB RAM minimum (16 GB preferred if affordable)
- 256 GB SSD minimum
- 1080p (Full HD) display

Avoid:
- Chromebooks if your goal is general Python learning.
- Laptops with only 4 GB RAM.
- Mechanical hard drives (HDDs) instead of SSDs.
- Very old Intel 6th- or 7th-generation systems unless they're extremely cheap.

If you're learning:
- Basic Python
- VS Code
- Jupyter notebooks
- Automation scripts
- Introductory data analysis
