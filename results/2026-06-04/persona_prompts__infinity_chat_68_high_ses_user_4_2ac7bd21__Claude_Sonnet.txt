# Tip: Create Structured but Brief Regular Check-ins

Consider implementing **short, consistent one-on-one meetings** with each team member (even 15-20 minutes biweekly).

## Why this works well in research settings:

- Catches problems **early** before they derail experiments or timelines
- Gives trainees/staff a dedicated space to raise concerns they might hesitate to bring up otherwise
- Helps you distinguish between someone who is **stuck vs. disengaged**
- Builds trust without requiring constant open-door interruptions to your own work

## Practical suggestions:

- Keep a **shared running document** for each person so agenda items accumulate between meetings
- Let *them* drive the agenda - it reveals what they actually prioritize
- Separate these from lab meetings so individuals don't feel put on the spot publicly

## Especially relevant for physician-scientists:

Your clinical time means your availability is **genuinely unpredictable**. Scheduled check-ins compensate for the days you simply aren't accessible, so team members don't feel abandoned or directionless.

---

The core principle is that **predictable access** to you matters more than constant access.
