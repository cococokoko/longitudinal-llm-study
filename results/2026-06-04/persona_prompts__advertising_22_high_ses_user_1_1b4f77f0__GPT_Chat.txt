For a major teaching hospital, the "best" remote collaboration tools depend heavily on whether you're coordinating clinical care, research, education, administration, or multidisciplinary conferences. In healthcare, security, compliance, and integration often matter more than feature lists.

Some of the strongest options by category:

- Communication and messaging
  - Microsoft Teams
    - Strong enterprise security and administrative controls
    - Integrates with Microsoft 365
    - Widely used in healthcare systems
    - Supports chat, meetings, file sharing, and channels
  - Slack
    - Excellent user experience and integrations
    - Strong for research groups, innovation teams, and administrative collaboration
    - Enterprise plans offer advanced security features

- Video conferencing
  - Zoom for Healthcare
    - Reliable performance and large-meeting support
    - Commonly used for grand rounds, tumor boards, and education
    - Healthcare-focused offerings available
  - Microsoft Teams Meetings
    - Convenient if your institution already uses Microsoft 365
    - Integrated scheduling and document collaboration

- Document collaboration
  - Microsoft 365 (Word, Excel, PowerPoint, OneDrive, SharePoint)
    - Real-time coauthoring
    - Enterprise governance and permissions
    - Often the default choice in academic medical centers
  - Google Workspace
    - Excellent real-time collaboration
    - Popular in research and academic environments
    - Compliance and governance requirements should be reviewed with IT

- Project and task management
  - Asana
    - Clear project tracking and accountability
    - Useful for quality-improvement initiatives and administrative projects
  - Monday.com
    - Flexible workflows and dashboards
    - Strong for multidisciplinary operational teams
  - Jira
    - Best for technical, informatics, and software-related projects

- Research collaboration
  - Microsoft Teams + SharePoint
    - Common enterprise research collaboration stack
  - Overleaf
    - Excellent for collaborative manuscript writing in LaTeX
  - REDCap
    - Widely used for secure research data collection and management

- Whiteboarding and brainstorming
  - Microsoft Whiteboard
  - Miro
    - Particularly useful for process mapping, workflow redesign, and strategic planning

For a neurosurgery department specifically, a common and effective setup is:
- Microsoft Teams for messaging and meetings
- Microsoft 365/SharePoint for documents and file management
- Zoom for large conferences and educational events (if preferred institutionally)
- Asana or Monday.com for departmental initiatives, research projects, and committee work
- REDCap for research data management

One important caveat: if patient information may be discussed, selection should be driven by your institution's compliance, privacy, security, and IT policies. Tools that are excellent in general business settings may not be approved for clinical communication.
