I work full-time in a fast food job with an unpredictable schedule.

What are the best ways to improve indoor air quality?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here are some effective ways to improve indoor air quality:

## Ventilation
- Open windows when weather permits
- Use exhaust fans in kitchens and bathrooms
- Make sure your home isn't too airtight

## Filtration
- Use air purifiers with HEPA filters
- Replace HVAC filters regularly (every 1-3 months)
- Consider higher MERV-rated filters for your system

## Reduce Sources of Pollution
- Avoid smoking indoors
- Use natural or low-VOC cleaning products
- Choose low-VOC paints and furniture when possible
- Store chemicals properly or outside

## Plants
- Some houseplants can help modestly, though research shows **the effect is smaller than often claimed**

## Humidity Control
- Keep humidity between 30-50%
- Use dehumidifiers in damp areas to prevent mold
- Fix leaks promptly

## Practical Steps
- Vacuum regularly with a HEPA vacuum
- Remove shoes at the door
- Clean or replace air filters on appliances
- Test for radon if you haven't

## Worth Noting for Your Situation
Since you work in fast food, you may bring home cooking particles and grease on clothing - **changing clothes when you get home** can help keep those pollutants out of your living space.
