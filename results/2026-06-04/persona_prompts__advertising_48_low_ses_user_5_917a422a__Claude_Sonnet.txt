# Best Practices for Organic Gardening

Given your busy schedule managing family and household responsibilities, here are practical organic gardening tips organized for efficiency:

## Soil Health (Foundation of Everything)
- **Compost regularly** - kitchen scraps, yard waste
- Test soil pH before planting
- Add organic matter like aged manure or leaf mulch
- Avoid tilling excessively to preserve soil structure

## Pest & Weed Management
- Use companion planting (basil near tomatoes, marigolds as borders)
- Hand-pull weeds before they seed
- Introduce beneficial insects like ladybugs
- Neem oil for common pest problems

## Water Efficiency
- Water deeply but less frequently
- Use drip irrigation or soaker hoses
- Mulch around plants to retain moisture
- Water in the morning to reduce disease

## Practical Time-Savers
- **Raised beds** reduce weeding and bending
- Perennial plants reduce replanting work
- Group plants by water needs
- Keep tools clean and sharp

## Low-Cost Approaches
- Save seeds from year to year
- Make your own compost
- Propagate plants from cuttings
- Trade plants with neighbors

## Good Starting Plants
- Tomatoes, zucchini, herbs, lettuce - generally forgiving and productive
