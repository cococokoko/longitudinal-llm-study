Yes. A strong STEM fair project is one that solves a real local problem, uses measurable data, and can be built inexpensively. Here are several ideas connected to challenges in Vietnam, with one standout option.

1. Low-Cost Flood Warning System (Excellent Choice)

Why it matters:
Vietnam experiences frequent flooding, especially in the Mekong Delta and central provinces. Early warning can save lives and property.

Project:
Build a small flood-monitoring system using inexpensive sensors that detect rising water levels and send an alert (sound, light, or phone notification).

Materials:
- Arduino or ESP32 microcontroller
- Water level sensor or ultrasonic sensor
- Buzzer/LED
- Battery pack
- Plastic container for testing

Estimated cost:
$15–30 USD

STEM components:
- Engineering: Design the warning system
- Technology: Programming the microcontroller
- Mathematics: Analyze water-level data
- Science: Study flooding patterns

Experiment:
- Test how quickly the system detects rising water.
- Compare different sensor placements.
- Measure accuracy under different conditions.

Why judges like it:
It addresses a major environmental issue and demonstrates practical engineering.

2. Water Filtration Using Local Materials

Why it matters:
Some rural areas face challenges with water quality.

Project:
Compare the effectiveness of different filter materials such as sand, charcoal, gravel, coconut fiber, and rice husks.

Cost:
$10–20 USD

Measurements:
- Turbidity (water clarity)
- pH
- Filtration speed

Connection to Vietnam:
Uses materials commonly available in Vietnam.

3. Cooling Homes Naturally in Hot Weather

Why it matters:
Vietnam's temperatures are increasing due to climate change.

Project:
Test different roofing or wall materials to determine which keeps a model house coolest.

Materials:
- Cardboard model houses
- Aluminum foil
- Bamboo sheets
- Plant coverings
- Thermometers

Cost:
$10–25 USD

Data:
Measure temperatures throughout the day.

4. Solar-Powered Rice Drying System

Why it matters:
Rice is one of Vietnam's most important crops, and weather can affect drying.

Project:
Build a miniature solar dryer and compare it with traditional open-air drying.

Cost:
$20–40 USD

Measurements:
- Drying time
- Moisture reduction
- Temperature inside dryer

5. Air Quality Monitor for Urban Areas

Why it matters:
Cities such as Hanoi and Ho Chi Minh City often experience air pollution.

Project:
Build a low-cost air-quality monitor using dust sensors and compare pollution levels at different locations or times.

Cost:
$25–50 USD

Data:
- PM2.5 levels
- Traffic volume
- Time of day

6. Reducing Plastic Waste with Bioplastics

Why it matters:
Plastic pollution affects Vietnam's rivers and coastline.

Project:
Create biodegradable plastic from starch (cassava, corn, or potato) and test its strength and decomposition rate.

Cost:
$10–20 USD

Measurements:
- Tensile strength
- Water resistance
- Decomposition time
