── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I am an executive at a large technology company.

How do I understand what I want?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This is a genuinely important question, and the fact that you're asking it suggests some self-awareness worth building on.

## Why This Is Hard for Executives Specifically

- You've spent years optimizing for **what others need from you**
- Success can come from being responsive rather than intentional
- Your identity and your role can become difficult to separate
- You may have learned to **suppress preferences** to appear decisive or agreeable

## Some Honest Approaches

**Distinguish between what you want and what you've been rewarded for**
- These often diverge significantly over a career
- Rewards shape behavior without necessarily reflecting genuine desire

**Notice what you'd do with no audience**
- Strip away status, approval, and optics
- What problems do you actually find interesting?

**Pay attention to resentment and energy**
- Resentment often signals where your real values are being violated
- Energy (not just adrenaline) points toward genuine engagement

**Take your complaints seriously**
- What you criticize often reveals what you actually value

**Create conditions for honest reflection**
- Unscheduled time is rare but necessary
- Journaling, therapy, or trusted advisors who aren't subordinates

## A Direct Challenge

Sometimes people ask "what do I want" when the harder question is:

**"Am I willing to act on what I already know I want?"**

Which version of the question are you actually facing?
