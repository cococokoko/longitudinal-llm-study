── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How can I improve my credit score quickly?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here are some effective ways to improve your credit score:

## Highest Impact Actions
- **Pay down credit card balances** - lowering your credit utilization ratio (below 30%, ideally below 10%) can show results quickly
- **Pay all bills on time** - payment history is the biggest factor (~35% of your score)
- **Dispute errors on your credit report** - get free reports at AnnualCreditReport.com

## Medium-Term Strategies
- Become an **authorized user** on someone else's account with good history
- Ask for a **credit limit increase** (without spending more)
- Keep old accounts open to maintain credit history length
- Avoid opening too many new accounts at once

## Things to Avoid
- Missing payments
- Maxing out cards
- Closing old credit cards
- Applying for multiple new cards quickly

## Realistic Timeline
- Some changes (like paying down balances) can reflect in **30-60 days**
- Building strong credit typically takes **several months to years**
- Be cautious of services promising instant dramatic improvements

## Free Resources
- Check your score through your bank or Credit Karma
- AnnualCreditReport.com for free official reports
