── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How to plan an environmentally friendly wedding?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Planning an environmentally friendly wedding involves making thoughtful choices that reduce waste, conserve resources, and support ethical businesses. Here are some practical ways to do it:

1. Choose a Sustainable Venue
- Select a venue close to where most guests live to reduce travel emissions.
- Consider outdoor venues, botanical gardens, farms, or community spaces that require less energy for lighting and climate control.
- Ask venues about recycling, composting, renewable energy use, and sustainability policies.

2. Keep the Guest List Intentional
- A smaller guest list generally reduces the environmental impact of food, transportation, and materials.

3. Use Digital Invitations
- Send electronic save-the-dates and invitations.
- If printed invitations are important to you, choose recycled paper, plantable paper, or certified sustainable paper.

4. Select Sustainable Attire
- Wear a vintage, secondhand, rented, or ethically produced wedding outfit.
- Choose attire that can be altered and worn again.
- Consider sustainable fabrics and responsible manufacturers.

5. Choose Eco-Friendly Decorations
- Rent decorations instead of purchasing new items.
- Use reusable candles, potted plants, or locally sourced flowers.
- Avoid single-use plastics, balloons, and glitter.
- Repurpose ceremony decorations for the reception.

6. Opt for Seasonal and Local Food
- Work with caterers who source ingredients locally and seasonally.
- Offer vegetarian or plant-based menu options, which often have a lower environmental footprint.
- Plan portions carefully to minimize food waste.
- Arrange for leftover food to be donated when permitted by local regulations.

7. Select Sustainable Flowers
- Choose locally grown, seasonal flowers.
- Consider potted plants, dried arrangements, or flowers from sustainable farms.
- Donate bouquets and arrangements after the event.

8. Reduce Waste
- Use reusable plates, glasses, linens, and cutlery.
- Set up clearly labeled recycling and composting stations.
- Avoid disposable favors and packaging.

9. Rethink Wedding Favors
- Consider edible favors, charitable donations, seed packets, or no favors at all.
- Choose useful items guests are likely to keep and use.

10. Plan Greener Transportation
- Encourage carpooling, public transportation, or group shuttles.
- Provide transportation information in advance.
- If many guests are traveling, recommend accommodations near the venue.

11. Choose Ethical Vendors
- Ask vendors about their sustainability practices.
- Support local businesses to reduce transportation impacts.
- Prioritize vendors who minimize packaging and waste.

12. Consider Carbon Reduction Efforts
- Reduce emissions where possible rather than relying solely on offsets.
- If desired, purchase verified carbon offsets for unavoidable travel emissions.

13. Donate, Reuse, or Resell Afterward
- Donate flowers, decorations, and unused supplies.
- Resell wedding attire and décor.
- Rent items whenever practical to avoid unnecessary purchases.

Sample eco-friendly wedding priorities:
- Digital invitations
- Local venue
- Seasonal menu with vegetarian options
- Rented décor and tableware
- Locally grown flowers
- Minimal waste and composting plan
- Donation or reuse of items after the event

The most sustainable wedding is usually one that focuses on what matters most to the couple while minimizing unnecessary consumption and waste. Even adopting a few of these practices can significantly reduce the event's environmental impact.
