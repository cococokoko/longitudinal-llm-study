How can I learn to cook healthy meals quickly?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Learning to cook healthy meals quickly is mostly about simplifying your approach and building a small set of reliable skills and recipes.

Here are some practical steps:

• Learn a few core cooking methods
  - Roasting: vegetables, chicken, fish, tofu
  - Stir-frying: vegetables and protein in one pan
  - Sautéing: quick meals with greens, beans, eggs, or shrimp
  - Sheet-pan cooking: protein and vegetables roasted together
  - Grain cooking: rice, quinoa, oats

• Master 5–10 simple meals
  Examples:
  - Vegetable omelet with whole-grain toast
  - Chicken, rice, and roasted vegetables
  - Tofu and vegetable stir-fry
  - Salmon with broccoli and potatoes
  - Bean and vegetable chili
  - Greek yogurt with fruit and nuts
  - Lentil soup
  - Burrito bowls with beans, rice, and vegetables

• Keep healthy staples on hand
  - Frozen vegetables
  - Canned beans
  - Eggs
  - Brown rice or quinoa
  - Oats
  - Greek yogurt
  - Chicken, fish, tofu, or other preferred proteins
  - Olive oil
  - Garlic, onions, and basic spices

• Use time-saving strategies
  - Buy pre-washed greens and pre-cut vegetables when practical.
  - Cook grains in large batches.
  - Prepare extra portions for leftovers.
  - Keep frozen fruits and vegetables available for busy days.
  - Use appliances like rice cookers, air fryers, or pressure cookers if you have them.

• Follow the "healthy plate" guideline
  - Half vegetables
  - One-quarter protein
  - One-quarter whole grains or starchy vegetables
  This keeps meals balanced without requiring calorie counting.

• Learn from beginner-friendly resources
  - YouTube channels focused on simple home cooking
  - Basic healthy-cooking cookbooks
  - Short recipe sites that emphasize 30-minute meals

• Practice one new recipe each week
  - Repeating recipes builds speed and confidence.
  - After making a dish a few times, you'll often cut preparation time significantly.

A simple 15-minute meal example:
- Sauté frozen mixed vegetables in olive oil.
- Add canned beans or cooked chicken.
- Season with garlic powder, paprika, salt, and pepper.
- Serve over microwaveable brown rice.
- Add a squeeze of lemon or a spoonful of salsa.
