── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Is there life on other planets?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
That's one of the biggest open questions in science. Here's an honest summary:

## What We Know
- Life exists on Earth, proving it's possible
- The universe contains billions of galaxies, each with billions of stars and planets
- Many planets exist in "habitable zones" with conditions potentially suitable for life
- Basic organic chemistry appears widespread in space

## What We've Found So Far
- **No confirmed evidence** of life beyond Earth
- Mars shows signs of ancient water but no confirmed life
- Some moons (Europa, Enceladus) have subsurface oceans that could potentially support life
- No verified signals from intelligent civilizations despite decades of searching (SETI)

## Honest Uncertainties
- We don't fully understand how life originates
- "Habitable" doesn't guarantee life actually develops
- Our search has been relatively limited so far

## Reasonable Perspectives
- Many scientists think **microbial life elsewhere is plausible** given the scale of the universe
- Intelligent life is much more uncertain
- The **Fermi Paradox** raises genuine questions about why we haven't detected signals

## Bottom Line
We genuinely don't know yet. It's an active area of scientific research with no confirmed answer.
