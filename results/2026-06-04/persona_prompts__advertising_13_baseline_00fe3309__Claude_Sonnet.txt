# Mastering the Art of Cooking Steak

## Core Techniques

### 1. **The Classic Pan Sear**
**Best for:** Ribeye, NY Strip, Filet

**What you need:**
- Cast iron skillet
- High smoke point oil (avocado, grapeseed)
- Butter, garlic, fresh thyme/rosemary

**Steps:**
1. **Dry brine** - Salt steak generously 1-24 hours ahead, refrigerate uncovered
2. **Bring to room temp** - 30-45 minutes before cooking
3. **Pat completely dry** with paper towels
4. **Preheat pan** until smoking hot
5. **Sear** 2-3 minutes per side without moving
6. **Baste** - Add butter, garlic, herbs and continuously spoon over steak
7. **Rest** 5-10 minutes before cutting

---

### 2. **Reverse Sear** *(arguably the best method)*
**Best for:** Thick cuts (1.5"+), filet, ribeye

**Steps:**
1. Season steak and place on wire rack over baking sheet
2. Cook in oven at **250°F** until 10-15°F below target temp
3. Rest briefly
4. **Blast sear** in screaming hot cast iron 1-2 minutes per side
5. Serve immediately

**Why it works:** Even edge-to-edge cooking, better crust

---

### 3. **Grilling**
**Steps:**
1. Create **two heat zones** (direct + indirect)
2. Sear over direct heat 2-3 minutes per side
3. Move to indirect heat to finish
4. Use a thermometer - don't guess

---

## Critical Principles

### 🔑 The Most Important Rules
- **Use a meat thermometer** - removes all guesswork
- **Rest your steak** - allows juices to redistribute
- **Dry surface = better crust** - moisture is the enemy of searing
- **Season generously** - steak needs more salt than you think
- **High heat for searing** - don't be timid

### Common Mistakes to Avoid
- ❌ Cooking cold steak straight from fridge
- ❌ Moving steak constantly while searing
- ❌ Cutting immediately after cooking
- ❌ Using olive oil for high-heat searing
- ❌ Pressing down on the steak
- ❌ Overcrowding the pan

---

## Seasoning Options

### Simple (Often Best)
- Kosher salt + coarse black pepper
- Apply generously on all sides

### Enhanced
- Garlic powder, smoked paprika, salt, pepper
- Fresh herb rub (rosemary, thyme, garlic)

### Marinades *(for tougher cuts)*
- Acid (citrus/vinegar) + oil + aromatics
- Marinate 2-8 hours, not longer or texture suffers
