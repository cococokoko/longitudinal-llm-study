── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How can I make my home more energy efficient?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Improving your home's energy efficiency can lower utility bills, increase comfort, and reduce environmental impact. Here are some of the most effective measures, roughly in order of cost-effectiveness:

1. Seal air leaks
- Caulk gaps around windows, doors, pipes, and vents.
- Add weatherstripping to exterior doors and windows.
- Seal leaks in attics, basements, and crawl spaces.
- Check for drafts around electrical outlets and recessed lighting.

2. Improve insulation
- Add or upgrade attic insulation, which is often one of the highest-impact improvements.
- Insulate walls, floors over unheated spaces, and crawl spaces if needed.
- Insulate hot water pipes and accessible heating/cooling ducts.

3. Upgrade heating and cooling systems
- Replace older HVAC equipment with high-efficiency models when it reaches the end of its life.
- Maintain systems regularly by changing filters and scheduling inspections.
- Consider a heat pump for efficient heating and cooling.

4. Use a programmable or smart thermostat
- Automatically reduce heating or cooling when you're asleep or away.
- Optimize temperature schedules based on occupancy.

5. Improve window performance
- Install energy-efficient windows if existing windows are old or damaged.
- Use insulating curtains or cellular shades.
- Seal gaps around window frames.

6. Reduce water-heating energy use
- Lower the water heater temperature to a safe, efficient setting (commonly around 120°F / 49°C, if appropriate for your household).
- Install low-flow showerheads and faucet aerators.
- Consider a heat pump water heater when replacing an existing unit.

7. Upgrade lighting
- Replace incandescent and halogen bulbs with LEDs.
- Use occupancy sensors in less frequently used spaces.

8. Choose energy-efficient appliances
- Look for high-efficiency or ENERGY STAR-certified appliances.
- Replace older refrigerators, washers, dryers, and dishwashers when practical.

9. Manage electronics and standby power
- Unplug devices when not in use or use smart power strips.
- Enable power-saving settings on computers and entertainment systems.

10. Consider renewable energy
- Install solar panels if your roof, budget, and local incentives make it practical.
- Explore community solar programs if rooftop solar isn't an option.

11. Get an energy audit
- A professional energy audit can identify the most cost-effective improvements for your specific home.
- Some utility companies offer free or discounted audits.
