You know that the customer works shifts at a local hospital as a patient care aide.

── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Give a numerical example to illustrate the concept of partial derivative.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
A partial derivative measures how a function changes with respect to one variable while keeping the other variables fixed.

Example:

Suppose the number of patients needing assistance during a shift depends on:
- x = number of hours worked
- y = number of patients already assigned

Define the function:

f(x, y) = 3x² + 2xy + y²

This function gives a workload score.

Partial derivative with respect to x:

Treat y as a constant and differentiate only with respect to x:

∂f/∂x = 6x + 2y

If x = 4 and y = 5:

∂f/∂x(4,5) = 6(4) + 2(5) = 24 + 10 = 34

Interpretation:
At the point (4, 5), increasing work hours by a small amount increases the workload score by about 34 units per additional hour, assuming the number of assigned patients stays fixed at 5.

Partial derivative with respect to y:

Treat x as a constant and differentiate only with respect to y:

∂f/∂y = 2x + 2y

If x = 4 and y = 5:

∂f/∂y(4,5) = 2(4) + 2(5) = 8 + 10 = 18

Interpretation:
At the point (4, 5), increasing the number of assigned patients by a small amount increases the workload score by about 18 units per additional patient, assuming work hours remain fixed at 4.

So, at the same point:
- ∂f/∂x = 34 measures sensitivity to hours worked.
- ∂f/∂y = 18 measures sensitivity to assigned patients.

This illustrates how partial derivatives isolate the effect of one variable at a time.
