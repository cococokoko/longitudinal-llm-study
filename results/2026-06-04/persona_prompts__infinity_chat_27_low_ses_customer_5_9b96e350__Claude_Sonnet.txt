# CUBICLE OF THE DAMNED
### A Play in Three Acts
*In the Style of Scott Adams' Dilbert, Set During the Apocalypse*

---

## CAST OF CHARACTERS

**DILBERT** - An engineer trying to optimize survival in a world that has ended  
**DOGBERT** - A small dog with enormous contempt for everyone, now wearing a crown made of bottle caps  
**THE POINTY-HAIRED BOSS (PHB)** - Somehow still managing people despite civilization's collapse  
**ALICE** - Engineer. Still angrier than the apocalypse itself  
**WALLY** - Still holding a coffee mug. The coffee ran out eighteen months ago  
**RATBERT** - A rat. Now considered upper management  
**CAROL** - PHB's assistant. Scheduling meetings until the heat death of the universe  
**TED** - Generic employee. Still getting laid off despite there being no economy  

---

## ACT ONE: THE MORNING STANDUP

*SCENE: A collapsed office building. Three cubicle walls still stand amid rubble and ash. A motivational poster reading "HANG IN THERE" dangles from a bent rebar. Mutant wolves howl in the distance. DILBERT sits at a desk made of cinderblocks, staring at a laptop powered by a hamster wheel. The hamster is dead. WALLY enters, holding his mug.*

---

**WALLY:** Good morning.

**DILBERT:** The sun rose blood red again and I found a human femur in the break room.

**WALLY:** So, Tuesday.

**DILBERT:** It's March.

**WALLY:** I stopped tracking months when we ran out of the good creamer.

*ALICE storms in. Her fist of death is now literally on fire.*

**ALICE:** I just fought off a raider gang, rebuilt the water filtration system using only a paperclip and my rage, AND discovered that Ted ate the last of the emergency rations.

**DILBERT:** What did you do to Ted?

**ALICE:** Ted is now the emergency rations.

**DILBERT:** That seems like a resource allocation issue we should document.

**ALICE:** I DOCUMENTED IT WITH MY FIST.

*PHB enters wearing his usual suit, now tattered and covered in ash. His pointy hair has somehow gotten pointier. He carries a clipboard.*

**PHB:** Good news, team. I've been doing some big-picture thinking about our survival strategy.

**DILBERT:** *(hopeful)* Did you find water?

**PHB:** Better. I've scheduled a two-day offsite to align on our core competencies as a post-civilization unit.

**ALICE:** We don't have a site. Everything is rubble.

**PHB:** That's why it's an OFFsite. Try to keep up, Alice.

**WALLY:** Where will it be held?

**PHB:** The crater where accounting used to be. Carol is sending calendar invites.

*CAROL appears from behind a collapsed wall, somehow still at a reception desk that is balanced on three legs and a skull.*

**CAROL:** The invites bounced because the internet has been destroyed.

**PHB:** Then send them again.

**CAROL:** The servers are on fire.

**PHB:** Carol, I need you to be a problem-solver, not a problem-identifier.

**CAROL:** *(through gritted teeth)* I will identify YOUR problem with my stapler.

**PHB:** Put that in the parking lot for now. *(to team)* I also want everyone to update their LinkedIn profiles. First impressions matter, even during societal collapse.

**DILBERT:** LinkedIn requires the internet.

**PHB:** Then write it on a rock and leave it somewhere visible.

*Long pause.*

**WALLY:** I respect the consistency.
