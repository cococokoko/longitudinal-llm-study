## Analysis vs. Design

**Analysis** answers *"What does the system need to do?"* — understanding requirements, problems, and constraints.

**Design** answers *"How will the system do it?"* — defining architecture, components, and solutions.

---

## Can You Design Without Analysis?

**Technically yes, practically no.**

Skipping analysis means you risk:
- **Building the wrong thing** — solving a misunderstood problem
- **Costly rework** — late-stage corrections are exponentially more expensive
- **Misaligned stakeholders** — no shared understanding of requirements

In fast-moving environments (startups, prototyping), teams sometimes design with *minimal* formal analysis — but they are still implicitly analyzing, just informally and often at greater risk.

---

## The Executive Takeaway

Analysis and design are sequential by necessity, not convention. **You cannot confidently define a solution before you understand the problem.** Organizations that skip or rush analysis typically pay for it in project overruns, failed deployments, or products that miss the market — all of which are far more expensive than the analysis investment itself.
