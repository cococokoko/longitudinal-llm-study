── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
What are the best resources for starting a plant-based diet?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
If you're starting a plant-based diet, a combination of evidence-based nutrition resources, practical meal-planning tools, and simple recipes tends to work best.

Evidence-based nutrition information:
- NutritionFacts.org (Dr. Michael Greger)
  - Free videos and articles focused on plant-based nutrition research.
  - Useful for understanding the health evidence behind dietary choices.

- Physicians Committee for Responsible Medicine (PCRM)
  - Offers free plant-based starter guides, meal plans, recipes, and nutrition information.
  - Especially helpful for beginners.

- Harvard T.H. Chan School of Public Health
  - Provides balanced nutrition guidance, including information on healthy plant-based eating patterns.

Books:
- "How Not to Die" by Michael Greger, MD
  - Explains the research behind plant-based eating and disease prevention.

- "Plant-Based on a Budget" by Toni Okamoto
  - Practical and affordable recipes for everyday cooking.

- "The Vegan Starter Kit" by Neal Barnard, MD
  - Beginner-friendly introduction to plant-based eating and nutrition.

Recipe websites:
- Forks Over Knives
  - Large collection of whole-food, plant-based recipes.

- Minimalist Baker
  - Many simple plant-based recipes with short ingredient lists.

- Nora Cooks
  - Accessible vegan versions of familiar dishes.

- Rainbow Plant Life
  - Flavor-focused recipes and cooking tutorials.

Meal-planning and tracking:
- Forks Meal Planner
  - Structured weekly meal plans and shopping lists.

- Cronometer
  - Useful for tracking nutrients, especially if you're concerned about protein, iron, calcium, or vitamin B12 intake.

Key nutrition topics to learn early:
- Vitamin B12 supplementation (important on a fully vegan diet).
- Sources of protein (beans, lentils, tofu, tempeh, soy milk, edamame, seitan, nuts, seeds).
- Iron-rich foods (legumes, tofu, pumpkin seeds, leafy greens).
- Calcium sources (fortified plant milks, calcium-set tofu, certain greens).
- Omega-3 fats (flaxseed, chia seeds, walnuts, algae-based supplements).

A simple beginner approach:
- Build meals around:
  - Legumes (beans, lentils, chickpeas)
  - Whole grains (oats, brown rice, quinoa)
  - Vegetables
  - Fruit
  - Nuts and seeds
- Replace one meal at a time rather than changing everything overnight.
- Keep easy staples on hand, such as canned beans, frozen vegetables, tofu, oats, and whole-grain pasta.

If your goal is health, weight loss, athletic performance, budget-friendly eating, or transitioning to a fully vegan diet, I can recommend more targeted resources.
