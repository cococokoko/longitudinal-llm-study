# How to Improve Home WiFi

## Quick Fixes
- **Restart your router** regularly (weekly helps)
- **Update router firmware** through the admin settings
- **Change WiFi channel** to avoid interference from neighbors

## Router Placement
- Place it **centrally** in your home
- Keep it **elevated** (shelf or table, not floor)
- Avoid placing near **microwaves, cordless phones, or thick walls**
- Keep it **out in the open**, not in a cabinet

## Settings Adjustments
- Use **5GHz band** for faster speeds (shorter range)
- Use **2.4GHz band** for better range/penetration
- Set a **strong password** to prevent unauthorized users
- Change to a **less congested channel** (use apps like WiFi Analyzer)

## Hardware Upgrades
- **Upgrade your router** if it's older than 4-5 years
- Add a **WiFi extender/repeater** for dead zones
- Consider a **mesh network system** (Eero, Google Nest, etc.)
- Use **ethernet cables** for stationary devices when possible

## Reduce Interference
- Limit devices using bandwidth simultaneously
- Update device network drivers
- Disconnect unused connected devices

## When to Call Your ISP
- Speeds are consistently below what you pay for
- Frequent disconnections
- Problems persist after troubleshooting
