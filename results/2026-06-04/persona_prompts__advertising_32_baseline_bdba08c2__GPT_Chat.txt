── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
What are the best ways to improve indoor air quality?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Improving indoor air quality usually works best when you combine source control, ventilation, filtration, and humidity management.

Here are some of the most effective steps:

• Improve ventilation
  - Open windows and doors when outdoor air quality is good.
  - Use kitchen exhaust fans while cooking.
  - Use bathroom exhaust fans during and after showers.
  - Consider a mechanical ventilation system if your home is tightly sealed.

• Use high-quality air filtration
  - Replace HVAC filters regularly.
  - Use filters with an appropriate MERV rating (often MERV 11–13 if your system supports them).
  - Run the HVAC fan as recommended to circulate and filter air.
  - Consider portable HEPA air purifiers for bedrooms and frequently used rooms.

• Control pollution sources
  - Avoid indoor smoking or vaping.
  - Minimize the use of candles, incense, and wood-burning fireplaces.
  - Choose low-VOC paints, cleaners, and furnishings when possible.
  - Store chemicals, fuels, and solvents outside living areas.

• Reduce cooking-related pollutants
  - Use a vented range hood whenever cooking, especially when frying or using gas stoves.
  - Keep burners clean and properly maintained.

• Manage humidity
  - Aim for indoor humidity between about 30% and 50%.
  - Use dehumidifiers in damp spaces.
  - Fix leaks promptly.
  - Ensure bathrooms, basements, and crawl spaces stay dry.

• Reduce dust and allergens
  - Vacuum regularly with a HEPA-filter vacuum if possible.
  - Dust surfaces with damp cloths rather than dry dusting.
  - Wash bedding regularly in hot water.
  - Consider allergen-resistant mattress and pillow covers.

• Address mold quickly
  - Clean small areas of mold safely.
  - Identify and fix the moisture source causing mold growth.
  - Seek professional remediation for extensive mold problems.

• Test for specific hazards
  - Install carbon monoxide detectors.
  - Test for radon if you live in an area where it is a concern.
  - Consider testing for lead, asbestos, or other contaminants in older buildings before renovations.

• Maintain HVAC systems
  - Have heating and cooling systems inspected and serviced regularly.
  - Keep air ducts, coils, and drain pans clean as needed.

If you're looking for the highest-impact improvements for most homes, start with:
1. Using kitchen and bathroom exhaust fans.
2. Upgrading HVAC filtration or adding a HEPA air purifier.
3. Controlling humidity and fixing moisture issues.
4. Eliminating indoor smoking and reducing combustion sources.
5. Regular cleaning to reduce dust and allergens.
