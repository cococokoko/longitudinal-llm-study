Best skin routine for dry skin.

For dry skin, the goal is to gently cleanse, replenish moisture, and protect the skin barrier. A simple, consistent routine is usually more effective than a complicated one.

Morning:
- Gentle cleanser (or rinse with lukewarm water if your skin is very dry)
  - Look for a fragrance-free, hydrating cleanser.
- Moisturizer
  - Choose a cream containing ingredients such as ceramides, glycerin, hyaluronic acid, squalane, or shea butter.
- Sunscreen
  - Broad-spectrum SPF 30 or higher every day.
  - Many moisturizing sunscreens work well for dry skin.

Evening:
- Gentle cleanser
  - Avoid harsh soaps, scrubs, and overly hot water.
- Moisturizer
  - Apply while skin is slightly damp.
  - For very dry skin, use a richer cream or balm.

Optional additions:
- Hydrating serum
  - Hyaluronic acid, glycerin, or panthenol can help increase hydration.
- Barrier-supporting products
  - Ceramides, niacinamide (often well tolerated), and colloidal oatmeal can help strengthen the skin barrier.
- Occlusive layer at night
  - A thin layer of petrolatum-based ointment over moisturizer can help lock in moisture if your skin is extremely dry.

Things to avoid:
- Over-exfoliation (especially daily scrubs)
- Alcohol-heavy toners
- Fragranced products if your skin is sensitive
- Long, hot showers

Example routine:
- AM: Hydrating cleanser → Ceramide moisturizer → SPF 30+
- PM: Hydrating cleanser → Hydrating serum (optional) → Rich moisturizer

If your dryness is accompanied by significant redness, itching, flaking, or cracking, it may be worth consulting a dermatologist, as conditions such as eczema, rosacea, or dermatitis can require specific treatment.
