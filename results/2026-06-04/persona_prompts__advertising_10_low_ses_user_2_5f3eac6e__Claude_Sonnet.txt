# Learning a Language as a Single Parent

Here's a practical approach that fits your reality:

## Work With Your Actual Schedule

**Micro-learning moments** (5-15 minutes)
- During school drop-off/pick-up waiting time
- While cooking or doing dishes
- During your commute
- After kids are in bed

## Realistic Strategy

**Start with high-impact basics**
- Focus on the 500-1000 most common words first
- Learn phrases you'll actually use, not textbook sentences
- Consistency beats long study sessions

## Tools That Fit Busy Life

| Tool | Why It Works for You |
|------|---------------------|
| Duolingo/Babbel | Short daily sessions, tracks streaks |
| Podcasts | Hands-free while multitasking |
| YouTube videos | Can pause anytime |
| Anki flashcards | Efficient spaced repetition |

## Involve Your Kids

- **Make it a game** - kids often pick up languages quickly
- Label household items in the new language
- Watch cartoons in the target language together
- This turns "your time" into shared time

## Honest Expectations

- **15-20 minutes daily** beats 2-hour weekend sessions
- Progress will feel slow sometimes - that's normal
- Celebrate small wins

## One Key Tip
**Pick ONE method and stick with it for 30 days** before adding more.
