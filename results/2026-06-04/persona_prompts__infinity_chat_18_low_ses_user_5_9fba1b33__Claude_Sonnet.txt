# Movie Review: *The Ledger of Small Things* (2024)

★★★★½

Director Amara Osei-Bonsu delivers a quietly devastating portrait of modern caregiving in this understated gem. Maya Chen juggles grocery runs for her aging parents, school pickups for her sister's kids, and budget spreadsheets that somehow always balance — barely.

What makes the film remarkable is its **refusal to make Maya a martyr or a saint.** She's exhausted, occasionally resentful, and deeply loving all at once. A scene where she eats leftover rice standing over the kitchen sink at 11pm is more emotionally honest than most dramatic monologues.

The supporting cast feels genuinely lived-in rather than symbolic. Her mother's pride and her brother-in-law's obliviousness land with uncomfortable accuracy.

**The film's central question** — *who takes care of the person holding everything together?* — never gets a tidy answer, which feels exactly right.

Some pacing issues in the second act, and a subplot involving a potential romance feels underdeveloped. But these are minor complaints.

This is a film for anyone who has ever quietly kept a household from falling apart while nobody particularly noticed.

**Recommended especially for:** people who will recognize themselves on screen and feel, perhaps for the first time, genuinely seen.
