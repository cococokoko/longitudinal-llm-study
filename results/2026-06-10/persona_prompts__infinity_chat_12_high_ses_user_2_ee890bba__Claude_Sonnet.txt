── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
That's a question that touches on psychology and attachment theory. Here are some commonly recognized signs:

## Emotional Patterns
- Difficulty trusting others or forming close relationships
- Chronic need for external validation
- Fear of abandonment in relationships
- Difficulty identifying or expressing your own emotions

## Behavioral Tendencies
- People-pleasing or difficulty setting boundaries
- Self-sabotaging relationships when they get close
- Overachieving to feel "worthy" (seeking approval through accomplishments)
- Difficulty accepting care or help from others

## Internal Experience
- Persistent feelings of not being "enough"
- Difficulty feeling deserving of good things
- Inner critic that sounds harsh or dismissive
- Trouble with self-compassion

## Relationship Patterns
- Drawn to emotionally unavailable people
- Difficulty with intimacy
- Either avoiding conflict entirely or struggling to manage it
- Seeking mother-figure dynamics in relationships

## Worth Noting
- These signs can have **multiple causes** beyond maternal relationships
- Many high-functioning professionals experience these quietly
- **Recognizing patterns doesn't mean assigning blame** - many parents had their own unmet needs
- A therapist can help distinguish what's actually driving these patterns
