# Different Views on Discipleship

Discipleship is understood quite differently across theological traditions, denominations, and thinkers. Here's a structured overview:

---

## Core Definitions Vary

| View | Emphasis |
|------|----------|
| **Imitation model** | Following Jesus's example in daily life |
| **Apprenticeship model** | Structured learning under a mentor |
| **Community model** | Formation happens primarily in relationship |
| **Missional model** | Discipleship *is* participation in God's mission |

---

## Major Theological Perspectives

**Catholic/Orthodox**
- Sacramental formation is central
- Spiritual direction and tradition carry significant weight
- Discipleship tied to the life of the Church

**Protestant Evangelical**
- Often emphasizes personal Bible study and accountability
- One-on-one mentoring relationships
- Clear conversion as starting point

**Progressive/Mainline**
- Social justice engagement as discipleship
- Emphasis on questioning and intellectual growth
- Community transformation

**Contemplative traditions**
- Interior transformation prioritized
- Silence, prayer, and spiritual disciplines

---

## Key Tensions

- **Individual vs. communal** formation
- **Knowledge vs. character** as the goal
- **Intentional programs vs. organic relationships**
- **Cost of discipleship** (Bonhoeffer's "cheap grace" critique)
