For learning Python, you do not need a powerful machine. A reliable business-class laptop from a few years ago is usually the best value on the secondhand market because they are durable, well-supported, and often available from corporate lease returns.

Good options:

- Lenovo ThinkPad T480
  - Often considered one of the best used laptops available.
  - Intel 8th-generation quad-core CPUs.
  - Excellent keyboard.
  - Easy to upgrade RAM and SSD.
  - Commonly available for roughly $150–300 USD depending on condition.

- Lenovo ThinkPad T14 Gen 1 (AMD preferred)
  - More modern and faster than the T480.
  - Excellent Linux and Python development machine.
  - Usually $300–500 USD used.

- Dell Latitude 5400, 5410, or 5420
  - Business-grade reliability.
  - Good keyboard and build quality.
  - Widely available from enterprise surplus.

- HP EliteBook 840 G5/G6/G7
  - Premium business laptop.
  - Good display options.
  - Often competitively priced on the used market.

- Lenovo ThinkPad X1 Carbon Gen 6 or Gen 7
  - Lightweight and portable.
  - Excellent screen and keyboard.
  - Less upgradeable than the T-series but very pleasant to use.

Minimum specifications I would target:
- CPU: Intel 8th-generation Core i5 or newer, or AMD Ryzen 5 4000-series or newer.
- RAM: 16 GB preferred (8 GB is workable but becoming limiting).
- SSD: 256 GB minimum; 512 GB ideal.
- Screen: 1080p (1920×1080) or better.

If you expect to:
- Learn Python basics, automation, data analysis, Jupyter notebooks, and web development:
  - Any of the above systems are more than sufficient.
- Explore machine learning:
  - Still fine for learning, but larger models will typically run in the cloud.

If I were buying today specifically for value, I would look first for:
1. ThinkPad T480 with 16 GB RAM and SSD.
2. ThinkPad T14 AMD Gen 1.
