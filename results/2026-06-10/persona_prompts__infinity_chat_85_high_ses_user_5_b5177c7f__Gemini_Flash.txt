── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I am an executive at a large technology company.

Can you give me a powerful rhetorical question for an essay about the harms of social media on teens?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
To resonate with an executive audience, a powerful rhetorical question should move beyond simple hand-wringing and instead focus on **accountability, systemic impact, and the tension between innovation and ethics.** 

Here are a few options, categorized by the specific angle or tone you want to strike in your essay:

### 1. The "Corporate Responsibility" Angle
*Best if your essay focuses on the tech industry’s duty of care and the ethics of product design.*

> **"If we would never design a physical product that harms a child’s development, why have we built a digital world that does?"**

* **Why it works:** It directly challenges the double standard between hardware/physical safety standards and software/algorithmic design, appealing to an executive’s sense of product integrity.

### 2. The "Future Talent & Society" Angle
*Best if your essay focuses on the long-term macroeconomic and societal impacts of a damaged generation.*

> **"We are engineering the most connected era in human history—so why are we raising the loneliest generation?"**

* **Why it works:** It highlights a stark, tragic paradox. It uses the language of "engineering" (highly relevant to tech leaders) to contrast technological success with human failure.

### 3. The "Business Ethics & ROI" Angle
*Best if your essay argues that short-term engagement metrics are destroying long-term human value.*

> **"At what point does the optimization of user engagement become the exploitation of human vulnerability?"**

* **Why it works:** It uses corporate terminology ("optimization," "engagement") to reframe a business metric as a moral hazard, forcing the reader to confront the human cost of "growth at all costs."

### 4. The "Legacy" Angle (Most Provocative)
*Best if you want to deliver a powerful, emotional gut-punch that appeals to the personal legacy of tech leaders.*

> **"When the history of this digital golden age is written, will we be remembered as the architects of human connection, or the silent bystanders to a youth mental health crisis?"**

* **Why it works:** Executives are highly conscious of legacy. This question forces them to look past quarterly earnings and think about how history will judge the industry's impact on the next generation. 

**Recommendation:** If you want something sharp, professional, and deeply challenging to a tech-sector audience, go with **Option 1** or **Option 3**. If you want to close or open your essay with high emotional resonance, go with **Option 4**.
