# Tips for Running a Marathon

## Training
- **Follow a structured plan** - Most plans are 16-20 weeks long
- **Build mileage gradually** - Increase weekly mileage by no more than 10% per week
- **Include long runs** - Build up to at least one 20-mile run before race day
- **Rest and recovery** - Schedule rest days to prevent injury

## Nutrition & Hydration
- **Practice fueling during training** - Use gels, chews, or real food on long runs
- **Stay hydrated** - Drink water consistently, not just on run days
- **Carb load** - Increase carbohydrate intake 2-3 days before the race
- **Don't try anything new on race day**

## Race Day Strategy
- **Start slow** - Going out too fast is the most common mistake
- **Run your own pace** - Ignore the crowd excitement early on
- **Break it into segments** - Think in smaller chunks rather than 26.2 miles
- **Walk breaks are okay** - Strategic walking can improve overall time

## Gear
- **Wear tested shoes** - Never race in new shoes
- **Dress for the weather** - Layer if needed
- **Use anti-chafe products** - Bodyglide or vaseline on friction points

## Mental Tips
- **Have a mantra** ready for tough miles
- **Expect hard patches** - They usually pass
- **Celebrate the finish** - Completing a marathon is a major achievement
