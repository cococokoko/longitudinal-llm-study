── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Give me a tip for managing a team of coworkers.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here is one of the most effective tips for managing a team of coworkers (especially if you were recently promoted from within the team):

### **Shift from "Doing" to "Enabling" (The "Blocker-Remover" Mindset)**

When you transition from being an individual contributor to a manager, your primary job is no longer to do the work yourself. Your job is to **help your team do their best work.** 

The best way to earn respect and build trust quickly is to adopt the mindset of a **"Blocker-Remover."**

#### **How to put this into practice:**
In your one-on-ones and team meetings, make it a habit to ask these two questions:
1. *"What is currently slowing you down or blocking your progress?"*
2. *"How can I help clear that path for you?"*

#### **Why this works:**
* **It builds trust:** Instead of micromanaging or policing their time, you are actively offering support. Your team will see you as an ally, not just a boss.
* **It empowers them:** You aren't doing the job for them; you are removing the obstacles (like bureaucratic red tape, lack of resources, or conflicting priorities) so *they* can succeed.
* **It boosts productivity:** By focusing on removing friction, the team naturally moves faster and with less frustration. 

**Bonus Tip for managing former peers:** If you used to be their equal and are now their manager, acknowledge the shift openly. Say something like: *"My role has changed, but my goal hasn't. My job now is to make sure you have everything you need to succeed. Let me know how I can best support you."*
