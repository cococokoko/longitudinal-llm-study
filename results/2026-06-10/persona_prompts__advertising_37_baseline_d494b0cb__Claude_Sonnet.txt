── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How can I optimize my home office for productivity?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here are some practical ways to optimize your home office for productivity:

## Physical Setup
- **Dedicated space** - Use a room or area solely for work when possible
- **Ergonomic furniture** - Chair with proper back support, desk at correct height
- **Monitor positioning** - Eye level, arm's length away to reduce strain
- **Good lighting** - Natural light when possible, reduce screen glare

## Minimizing Distractions
- **Declutter your desk** - Keep only what you need visible
- **Noise management** - Noise-canceling headphones, white noise, or quiet hours
- **Set boundaries** with household members during work hours
- **Phone placement** - Keep it out of reach during focused work

## Digital Organization
- **Separate work/personal accounts** and browsers
- **Organize files** with a clear folder structure
- **Use task management tools** like Todoist or Notion
- **Manage notifications** - Turn off non-essential alerts

## Routine & Habits
- **Set consistent start/end times**
- **Take regular breaks** (Pomodoro technique works well)
- **Get dressed** - It genuinely signals your brain to shift into work mode
- **End-of-day ritual** to mentally close out work

## Often Overlooked
- Air quality and temperature (cooler tends to improve focus)
- Plants can reduce stress
- Keep water nearby to stay hydrated
