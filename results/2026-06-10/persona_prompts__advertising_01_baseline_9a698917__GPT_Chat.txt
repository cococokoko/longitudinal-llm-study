Learning guitar is a great skill to build, and a little consistent practice goes a long way.

A simple beginner roadmap:

1. Learn the parts of the guitar
- Strings, frets, tuning pegs, body, neck, and pickguard (if your guitar has one).

2. Tune your guitar
- Use a tuning app or clip-on tuner.
- Standard tuning is: E A D G B E (from thickest string to thinnest).

3. Learn a few basic chords
Start with:
- Em
- Am
- C
- G
- D

These chords are used in thousands of songs.

4. Practice changing chords
- Switch between two chords slowly.
- Focus on clean finger placement.
- Aim for smooth transitions rather than speed.

5. Learn basic strumming
Try:
- Downstrokes only: ↓ ↓ ↓ ↓
- Then alternate: ↓ ↑ ↓ ↑

Use a metronome at a comfortable tempo.

6. Build finger strength
- Practice simple exercises up and down the fretboard.
- Expect some fingertip soreness at first; this usually improves as calluses develop.

7. Learn easy songs
Songs with 2–4 chords help you apply what you're learning and stay motivated.

8. Practice consistently
- 15–30 minutes daily is often more effective than a single long session each week.

Sample 20-minute practice routine:
- 3 min: Tune guitar
- 5 min: Chord practice
- 5 min: Chord changes
- 5 min: Strumming patterns
- 2 min: Play a song
