Give me a tip for managing a team of coworkers.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
One effective tip is to create clear expectations and follow up consistently.

Make sure each team member understands:
- Their responsibilities
- What success looks like
- Deadlines and priorities
- How their work connects to the team's goals

Then, check in regularly—not to micromanage, but to identify obstacles early, answer questions, and keep everyone aligned. Many team issues stem from unclear expectations rather than lack of effort, so investing time in clarity can prevent misunderstandings and improve performance.
