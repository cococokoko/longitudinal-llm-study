── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
André Gorz is a rich subject for thesis work. Let me offer some genuinely productive directions, including some that might challenge you intellectually:

## Potentially Productive Angles

**On Labor and Post-Work**
- How Gorz's critique of work anticipates contemporary debates about automation and UBI - but also where his analysis has **significant weaknesses** (his assumptions about which work is "alienating" reflect particular class perspectives)
- Tension between his early *Strategy for Labor* and later *Farewell to the Working Class* - is this genuine theoretical development or contradiction?

**On Ecology**
- Gorz as a bridge between Marxism and ecosocialism, but critically examining whether his "political ecology" actually resolves or just **papers over** the tension between growth and socialist politics

**Genuinely Critical Directions Worth Considering**
- His treatment of **reproductive labor and gender** is arguably underdeveloped compared to feminist Marxists like Federici
- Whether his "non-class of non-workers" concept is analytically coherent or somewhat romanticized
- His relationship to **Illich** deserves more systematic treatment

## Honest Suggestions
- The strongest theses engage seriously with **critics**, not just sympathizers
- Consider what Gorz gets **wrong** as much as right
