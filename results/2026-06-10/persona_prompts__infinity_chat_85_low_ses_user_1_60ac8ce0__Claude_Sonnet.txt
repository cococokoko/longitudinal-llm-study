── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I work full-time in a fast food job with an unpredictable schedule.

Can you give me a powerful rhetorical question for an essay about the harms of social media on teens?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here's a strong rhetorical question for your essay:

**"If we wouldn't hand a teenager a product proven to increase anxiety, depression, and self-harm, why do we casually place one in their pocket every single day?"**

This works well because it:
- **Creates a sharp contrast** that makes the harm feel concrete
- **Challenges the reader's consistency** in their own thinking
- **Implies urgency** without being preachy
- Works well as an **opening hook or closing challenge**

A few alternatives depending on your essay's tone:

- *"At what point do we stop calling it connection when the data shows teenagers have never felt more alone?"*
- *"Would you let a stranger follow your child home, comment on their appearance, and track their every move - so why does it feel different when an app does it?"*

The third option is particularly punchy if you want something more confrontational.
