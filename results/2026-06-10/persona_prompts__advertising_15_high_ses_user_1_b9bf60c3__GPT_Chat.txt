── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Decorating your home on a budget is often about making a few high-impact changes rather than buying everything new.

Some practical strategies:

- Start with a plan
  - Choose a color palette and style before purchasing anything.
  - Focus on the rooms you use most often.

- Rearrange what you already own
  - Moving furniture, artwork, and accessories can make a space feel completely different at no cost.

- Use paint strategically
  - A fresh coat of paint is one of the most cost-effective upgrades.
  - Consider painting an accent wall, furniture, or cabinets.

- Shop secondhand
  - Thrift stores, estate sales, flea markets, and online marketplaces often have quality furniture and decor for a fraction of retail prices.

- Add textiles
  - New throw pillows, blankets, curtains, or rugs can dramatically change a room without major expense.

- Incorporate plants
  - Houseplants add color and texture and can make a space feel more inviting.
  - Even inexpensive plants can have a big visual impact.

- Upgrade lighting
  - Replace outdated lampshades, add floor lamps, or switch to warmer bulbs to improve ambiance.

- Create gallery walls
  - Frame personal photos, prints, or inexpensive artwork.
  - Consistent frames can make low-cost art look more polished.

- Focus on one statement piece
  - Rather than buying many small decorative items, invest in a single eye-catching element such as a rug, chair, or artwork.

- DIY when practical
  - Simple projects like refinishing furniture, painting frames, or creating wall art can save money and personalize your space.

A useful rule is to allocate most of your budget to items that affect comfort and daily use (such as seating, mattresses, and lighting) and save on purely decorative accessories. This often produces the best balance between appearance and value.
