# Movie Review: *The Ledger of Small Things* (2024)

**★★★★½**

Director Amara Osei-Bonsu delivers a quietly devastating portrait of a woman holding an entire universe together with grocery lists and phone calls.

Maya, played with extraordinary restraint by Danielle Deadwymon, juggles mortgage payments for her aging parents, school pickups for her sister's kids, and the emotional labor nobody ever puts on a calendar. The film's genius is making *administration* feel cinematic.

The standout scene involves Maya reconciling three different family budgets at a kitchen table at 11pm while simultaneously texting a cousin through a crisis. No dramatic music. Just fluorescent light and competence.

**What works:** The film refuses to make Maya a martyr OR a saint. She's occasionally resentful, sometimes proud, always complicated.

**What doesn't:** A third-act romantic subplot feels borrowed from a different, lesser movie.

The ending — Maya finally asking someone to simply *notice* what she does — will hit differently depending on who you are when you walk into the theater.

Some viewers will cry. Others will finally feel *seen*.
