How to plan an environmentally friendly wedding?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Planning an environmentally friendly wedding involves making intentional choices that reduce waste, conserve resources, and support sustainable businesses while still creating a memorable event.

Key areas to consider:

• Choose a sustainable venue
  - Select a venue close to where most guests live to reduce travel emissions.
  - Consider outdoor venues, botanical gardens, farms, or venues with recognized sustainability practices.
  - Look for venues that use renewable energy, recycling programs, or water-conservation measures.

• Limit guest travel impacts
  - Hold the ceremony and reception at the same location if possible.
  - Arrange group transportation such as shuttles.
  - Provide information about public transit and nearby accommodations.

• Send eco-friendly invitations
  - Use digital invitations and RSVPs.
  - If printing, choose recycled or FSC-certified paper and environmentally responsible inks.
  - Minimize inserts and excess packaging.

• Select sustainable attire
  - Rent wedding attire or purchase pre-owned garments.
  - Choose clothing that can be reworn after the wedding.
  - Consider designers and brands with transparent sustainability practices.

• Choose local and seasonal food
  - Work with caterers who source ingredients locally.
  - Emphasize seasonal menus.
  - Reduce food waste by planning realistic portions and arranging donation of surplus food where permitted.

• Make environmentally conscious décor choices
  - Rent decorations rather than purchasing single-use items.
  - Use potted plants, reusable centerpieces, or locally grown flowers.
  - Avoid balloons, glitter, and other difficult-to-recycle materials.
  - Repurpose ceremony flowers and decorations for the reception.

• Reduce waste
  - Use reusable plates, glassware, and linens instead of disposable products.
  - Provide clearly marked recycling and composting stations.
  - Avoid wedding favors that are likely to be discarded; consider edible, useful, or charitable alternatives.

• Consider sustainable flowers
  - Purchase locally grown, in-season flowers.
  - Use fewer floral installations and more greenery if appropriate.
  - Donate arrangements after the event to hospitals, nursing homes, or community organizations where feasible.

• Select ethical vendors
  - Ask vendors about their sustainability practices.
  - Favor local businesses to reduce transportation impacts.
  - Consider vendors with established environmental commitments.

• Think carefully about gifts and registries
  - Create a registry focused on durable, useful items.
  - Offer a charitable donation option.
  - Consider experiences rather than material gifts.

• Manage energy use
  - Use LED lighting where possible.
  - Minimize energy-intensive features that provide limited value.
  - If hosting an outdoor event, evaluate power requirements in advance.

• Offset unavoidable emissions
  - Calculate major sources of emissions, especially guest travel.
  - Consider purchasing high-quality carbon offsets from reputable programs.

A practical approach is to prioritize the choices with the greatest environmental impact first: guest transportation, venue selection, food sourcing, and waste reduction. These decisions typically yield larger sustainability benefits than smaller details such as stationery or favors.
