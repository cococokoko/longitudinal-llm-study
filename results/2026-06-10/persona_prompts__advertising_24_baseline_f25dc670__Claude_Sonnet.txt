# Best Ways to Save for Retirement

## Tax-Advantaged Accounts (Start Here)

**Employer-Sponsored Plans**
- **401(k) or 403(b)** - contribute enough to get full employer match first (it's free money)
- 2024 contribution limit: $23,000 ($30,500 if 50+)

**Individual Retirement Accounts**
- **Traditional IRA** - tax deduction now, pay taxes in retirement
- **Roth IRA** - pay taxes now, tax-free growth and withdrawals
- 2024 limit: $7,000 ($8,000 if 50+)

## General Priority Order
1. 401(k) up to employer match
2. Pay off high-interest debt
3. Max out HSA (if eligible)
4. Max out Roth/Traditional IRA
5. Return to max out 401(k)
6. Taxable brokerage account

## Key Principles
- **Start early** - compound interest rewards time significantly
- **Invest consistently** regardless of market conditions
- **Keep fees low** - index funds typically outperform actively managed funds
- **Increase contributions** when income rises
- **Diversify** across asset classes

## Common Rules of Thumb
- Save **10-15%** of income for retirement
- Target **25x your annual expenses** as a retirement goal

## Worth Considering
- Consult a **fee-only financial advisor** for personalized guidance
- Your ideal strategy depends on income, age, and goals
