For a neurosurgical team at a major teaching hospital, the best remote collaboration tools depend on whether you're coordinating clinical care, research, education, administration, or a combination of these. Security, compliance, and integration with hospital systems are usually the most important factors.

Commonly recommended options:

- Microsoft Teams
  - Strong enterprise security and administrative controls
  - Integrates with Microsoft 365 (Outlook, Word, Excel, SharePoint)
  - Video meetings, chat, file sharing, channels, and task management
  - Widely used in healthcare organizations

- Zoom Workplace
  - Reliable video conferencing
  - Good for multidisciplinary conferences, tumor boards, and teaching sessions
  - Webinar and large-meeting capabilities
  - Verify your institution's security and compliance configuration

- Slack
  - Excellent for rapid communication and project coordination
  - Channels for research groups, service lines, and committees
  - Extensive integrations with other software
  - More commonly used in research and administrative settings than direct clinical workflows

- Google Workspace
  - Strong real-time document collaboration
  - Google Docs, Sheets, Drive, Meet
  - Often favored by research groups and academic collaborations

Project and task management:

- Asana
  - Structured project tracking
  - Useful for quality-improvement initiatives, accreditation preparation, and research projects

- Monday.com
  - Flexible workflow management
  - Good visual dashboards for departmental operations

- Trello
  - Simple Kanban-style boards
  - Useful for smaller teams and straightforward projects

Knowledge management and documentation:

- Confluence
  - Centralized documentation, protocols, meeting notes, and onboarding materials

- Notion
  - Combines notes, databases, wikis, and project tracking
  - Popular among research and academic teams

Healthcare-specific considerations:

- If discussing patient information, ensure the platform is approved by your institution and configured to meet applicable privacy and security requirements.
- Integration with the EHR (Epic, Cerner/Oracle Health, etc.) may be more valuable than standalone collaboration features.
- Many hospitals standardize on Microsoft Teams because it balances communication, document management, security controls, and enterprise support.

For a typical academic neurosurgery department, a common combination is:
- Microsoft Teams for communication and meetings
- SharePoint or OneDrive for document storage
- Asana or Monday.com for projects
