── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I recently started a new job and am still on probation.

How do I understand what I want?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Starting a new job is a major transition, and being on probation adds a layer of pressure that can make it hard to think clearly. You are likely experiencing a mix of trying to prove yourself, learning new systems, and adjusting to a new culture. 

To figure out what you *actually* want, you need to separate the temporary stress of "being new" from your long-term career and personal desires. 

Here is a structured guide to help you understand what you want during this probationary period.

---

### Step 1: Separate "New Job Anxiety" from "Wrong Job Reality"
Before making any judgments, acknowledge that the first 30 to 90 days of any job are usually uncomfortable. 
* **Ask yourself:** *Am I unhappy because I dislike the work, or am I just stressed because I’m still learning and feel incompetent?*
* **The Rule:** Do not make permanent decisions based on temporary onboarding stress. Give yourself permission to just "exist and learn" for the first month without needing to have it all figured out.

### Step 2: Track Your Daily Energy (The "Energy Audit")
Instead of trying to figure out what you want in the abstract, look at the data of your daily life. For the next two weeks, keep a private journal or spreadsheet. At the end of each day, write down:
* **Energizers:** What tasks, conversations, or moments made you feel engaged, capable, or excited? (e.g., "Solving that Excel issue," "The brainstorming meeting," "Working alone on a report.")
* **Drainers:** What drained your energy or made you feel anxious/bored? (e.g., "Answering client emails," "The lack of structure," "My manager's communication style.")
* **The Clue:** What you want lies in the patterns of your **Energizers**. 

### Step 3: Define Your "Core Four" Career Drivers
People want different things from work at different stages of life. Rank these four drivers in order of importance to you *right now*:
1. **Money & Security:** You want a high salary, good benefits, and stability.
2. **Growth & Learning:** You want mentorship, new skills, and a clear path to promotion.
3. **Alignment & Purpose:** You want to believe in the company’s mission and feel like your work matters.
4. **Lifestyle & Flexibility:** You want a short commute, remote options, and to log off at 5:00 PM to live your life.

*There is no wrong answer.* If you realize you value "Lifestyle" most, but this new job requires 60-hour weeks, you have your answer. If you value "Growth" but the job is highly repetitive, you know what is missing.

### Step 4: Use the "Probation Period" as a Two-Way Street
Remember: **Probation is not just the company testing you; it is you testing the company.** 
Use this time to actively observe:
* **The Culture:** Do people look happy? Do they respect boundaries? Is there a culture of blame or support?
* **The Leadership:** Do you respect your manager? Do they give constructive feedback, or do they micromanage?
* **The Future:** Look at the people who have been there for 2–3 years. Do you want their lives? If the answer is a resounding "no," this is a major clue.

### Step 5: Ask Yourself the "Magic Wand" Questions
If you are struggling to visualize what you want, try these thought experiments:
* **The "No-Obstacles" Question:** If you were guaranteed to succeed and make the exact same amount of money, what would you be doing tomorrow?
* **The "Anti-Goal" Question:** Sometimes it’s easier to know what you *don't* want. Write down your absolute nightmare job. (e.g., "I never want to manage people," "I never want to work weekends," "I never want to sit in meetings all day.") Now, look at the opposite of those statements.

### Step 6: Give Yourself a Deadline
Do not try to solve this today. Give yourself a timeline. 
* **Tell yourself:** *"I am on probation for 3 months. I will not make any rash decisions or overthink my career until Day 75. Until then, I will just observe, learn, and collect data."*
* On Day 75, look back at your Energy Audit (Step 2) and your Core Drivers (Step 3). The answer of whether this job aligns with what you want will be much clearer.

**Summary for right now:** 
Be kind to yourself. You are in a high-stress phase. Focus on doing your job well enough to pass probation (to keep your options open), while quietly observing how the job makes you feel. The clarity you want will come from action and observation, not from overthinking.
