# Learning a Language as a Single Parent

That's a real challenge - here are practical approaches that fit your actual life:

## Work With Your Schedule, Not Against It

**Micro-learning moments**
- 10-15 minutes during nap time or after bedtime
- Commute time with audio lessons or podcasts
- While cooking or doing dishes (audio-based learning)

**Consistency beats intensity**
- 15 minutes daily outperforms 2-hour weekend sessions
- Even 5 minutes counts on hard days

## Practical Tools Worth Considering

| Tool | Why It Works for Busy Parents |
|------|-------------------------------|
| Duolingo | Short sessions, flexible timing |
| Pimsleur | Audio-only, hands-free learning |
| Anki | Quick vocabulary review |
| YouTube channels | Free, varied content |

## Make It Double-Duty

- **Involve your child** - kids often pick up languages quickly and it becomes shared time
- Label household items in the target language
- Watch children's shows in the new language together

## Honest Expectations

- **Progress will be slower** than someone with more free time - that's okay
- Celebrate small wins genuinely
- Skip guilt on days you miss practice

## One Key Question

**What's your goal?** Travel, career, heritage, or connection with someone? That shapes the best approach significantly.
