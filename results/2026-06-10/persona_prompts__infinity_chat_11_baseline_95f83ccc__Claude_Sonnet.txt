── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I'm 60 years old and haven't achieved anything. What have I missed out on?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This is worth thinking through carefully rather than just validating the feeling.

## Some honest considerations:

**"Haven't achieved anything" deserves scrutiny**
- By whose definition of achievement?
- Have you raised children, maintained relationships, helped people, worked honestly? Those count
- We often measure ourselves against highlight reels of others

**What you might genuinely reflect on:**
- Skills or education you wished you'd pursued
- Relationships you didn't invest in
- Health habits that compound over time
- Financial planning that starts earlier being easier

## The realistic part:

**60 is actually not too late for many things**
- Average life expectancy means potentially 20-25+ more years
- Many people start businesses, degrees, relationships, creative work at 60+
- Some things genuinely are harder (certain athletic peaks, compound investment time)

**But some things are worth honestly acknowledging**
- Certain windows do close - having biological children, some career trajectories
- Regret is real and worth processing, not just dismissing

## What might actually help:

- Getting specific about what you feel you missed, rather than general inadequacy
- Talking to someone (therapist, trusted friend) about this
- Distinguishing between genuine loss and comparison thinking

**What specifically feels missing to you?** That would help think through this more usefully than general reassurance.
