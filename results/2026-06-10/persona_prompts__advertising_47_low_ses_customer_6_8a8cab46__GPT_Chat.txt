If you're training for a marathon while also working long warehouse shifts, the biggest challenge is often managing fatigue and recovery in addition to the running itself.

Some key tips:

- Build mileage gradually
  - Increase weekly mileage slowly, typically no more than about 10% per week.
  - Consistency matters more than occasional huge training weeks.

- Prioritize long runs
  - Do one long run each week to build endurance.
  - Most marathon plans peak with long runs in the 18–22 mile (29–35 km) range.

- Keep most runs easy
  - Many runners go too hard on everyday runs.
  - Easy conversational-pace running helps build endurance while reducing injury risk.

- Follow a structured plan
  - A 16–20 week marathon plan is common.
  - Include easy runs, a weekly long run, and one quality workout (tempo run, intervals, or marathon-pace work).

- Fuel properly
  - Practice your race-day nutrition during training.
  - For long runs, many runners take carbohydrates every 30–45 minutes.
  - Stay hydrated and replace electrolytes when needed.

- Take recovery seriously
  - Aim for adequate sleep whenever possible.
  - Because warehouse work can already be physically demanding, don't hesitate to reduce training volume if you're feeling run down.

- Strength train
  - Two short sessions per week can help reduce injury risk.
  - Focus on core, glutes, hamstrings, calves, and single-leg stability exercises.

- Don't ignore pain
  - Mild soreness is normal.
  - Persistent pain that changes your gait or worsens with running should be addressed early.

- Practice race-day pacing
  - Start slower than you think you should.
  - Many marathoners lose time by going out too fast in the first half.

- Taper before the race
  - Reduce mileage during the final 2–3 weeks while maintaining some intensity.
  - Arriving rested is often more important than squeezing in extra training.
