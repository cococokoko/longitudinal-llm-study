# Different Views on Discipleship

Discipleship — the process of following, learning from, and being formed by a teacher or tradition — is understood quite differently across theological, philosophical, and cultural contexts.

---

## Christian Perspectives

### Traditional/Catholic View
- Discipleship as **lifelong formation** in virtue and sacramental life
- Emphasis on **community, tradition, and authority** (church, clergy)
- Following Christ through **imitation** (*imitatio Christi*)

### Protestant/Evangelical View
- Personal relationship with Christ as **central**
- Bible study and **accountability relationships**
- Often structured around **small groups or mentoring**
- Emphasis on **evangelism** as part of discipleship

### Anabaptist/Radical Reformation View
- Discipleship as **costly** — separation from worldly values
- Strong emphasis on **community ethics** and nonviolence
- Following Jesus **literally** in daily life

### Liberation Theology View
- Discipleship involves **solidarity with the poor**
- Following Jesus means **challenging unjust structures**
- Praxis (action + reflection) is central

---

## Jewish Perspectives
- Rabbi-disciple (*talmid*) relationship
- Learning through **proximity and observation**
- Torah study as the primary vehicle of formation
- Discipleship tied to **halakhic practice**

---

## Islamic Perspectives
- Following the **Prophet's example** (Sunnah/Hadith)
- Sufi tradition emphasizes **spiritual master (sheikh)** guiding the student (murid)
- Formation through **prayer, fasting, and community**

---

## Buddhist Perspectives
- Student-teacher relationship central, especially in **Tibetan Buddhism**
- Emphasis on **personal transformation** through practice
- Sangha (community) as one of the Three Jewels
- Discipleship as **gradual path** toward enlightenment

---

## Key Theological Debates

| Question | Different Answers |
|----------|------------------|
| **Who leads?** | Clergy, mentors, community, or the Spirit directly? |
| **What is the goal?** | Salvation, virtue, enlightenment, justice, knowledge? |
| **How long does it take?** | Lifelong vs. defined programs |
| **Is it individual or communal?** | Personal faith vs. corporate formation |
| **What does it cost?** | Grace-based vs. demanding sacrifice |

---

## Contemporary Debates
- **Programmatic vs. relational** discipleship
- Whether **church attendance** equals discipleship
- Role of **suffering and hardship** in formation
- Discipleship in **digital/online** contexts
- **Decolonizing** discipleship models
