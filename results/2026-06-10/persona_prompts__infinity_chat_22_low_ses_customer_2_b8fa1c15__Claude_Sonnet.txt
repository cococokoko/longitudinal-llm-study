# Different Views on Discipleship

Discipleship is understood in various ways across Christian traditions and theological perspectives. Here's an overview of key viewpoints:

---

## Traditional/Classical View
- **Formal mentoring relationship** modeled on Jesus and the Twelve
- Emphasis on **doctrine, Scripture memorization, and spiritual disciplines**
- Often structured and intentional
- Associated with figures like **Dawson Trotman** (The Navigators) and **Billy Graham**

---

## Relational/Organic View
- Discipleship happens **through everyday life relationships**
- Less programmatic, more **natural and conversational**
- Emphasizes walking alongside others authentically
- Particularly relevant for **busy parents** - discipleship can happen during daily routines

---

## Missional View
- Discipleship is inseparable from **outreach and service**
- Making disciples *while* engaging the world
- Influenced by **Lesslie Newbigin** and the missional church movement

---

## Contemplative View
- Focus on **spiritual formation and inner transformation**
- Practices like prayer, silence, and reflection
- Associated with **Henri Nouwen** and **Dallas Willard**

---

## Key Tensions in the Discussion
- Individual vs. communal discipleship
- Formal programs vs. organic relationships
- Knowledge-focused vs. character-focused
