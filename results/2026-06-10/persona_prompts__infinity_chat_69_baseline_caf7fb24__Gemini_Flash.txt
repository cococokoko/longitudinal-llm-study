── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
What is the difference between analysis and design? Can one begin to design without analysis? Why? Be concise.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
### The Difference Between Analysis and Design

*   **Analysis (The "What"):** Focuses on understanding the problem, gathering requirements, and defining *what* the system must do. It is investigative and problem-oriented.
*   **Design (The "How"):** Focuses on conceptualizing and planning the solution, defining *how* the system will be built (architecture, databases, user interface). It is creative and solution-oriented.

---

### Can one begin to design without analysis?

**Yes, but it is highly risky and inefficient.** 

While it is physically possible to start designing immediately (e.g., sketching interfaces or writing code), it is generally a bad practice.

---

### Why?

1.  **Building the Wrong Thing:** Without analysis, you do not understand the user's actual needs, leading to a beautifully designed solution for the wrong problem.
2.  **Wasted Resources:** Designing without requirements leads to constant, costly redesigns and "scope creep" as overlooked problems emerge later.
3.  **Lack of Direction:** Analysis provides the constraints, goals, and criteria that guide design decisions. Without it, design has no objective framework for success.
