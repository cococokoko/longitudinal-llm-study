### **Day 1: Castles, Playgrounds, and Easy Dining**
*Focus: Outdoor space to burn off energy, easy walking, and a relaxed pace.*

*   **Morning: Osaka Castle Park (Osaka-jo)**
    *   **Why it works:** The castle itself is beautiful, but the real gem for parents is the massive park surrounding it. 
    *   **For the Kid:** There is a fantastic adventure playground called **Playville** (located in the park) with indoor and outdoor play areas. 
    *   **For You:** The park is highly stroller-friendly. Near the playground, there are cafes (like Starbucks) with outdoor seating where you can sit with a coffee and easily send a few emails while keeping an eye on your child.
*   **Lunch: Jo-Terrace Osaka**
    *   Located right in the park, this is a modern, open-air collection of restaurants. It’s incredibly family-friendly, spacious, and has plenty of high chairs and kid-friendly food options (like ramen, udon, and Italian).
*   **Afternoon: Kids Plaza Osaka**
    *   **Why it works:** If you need a spot where your child can run wild in a safe, indoor environment, this is it. It’s a massive, interactive hands-on museum designed entirely for kids. 
    *   **For You:** There are plenty of benches throughout the play areas. The environment is loud enough that if you need to take a quick work call, no one will mind, and the facility is completely secure.
*   **Dinner: Tenjinbashi-suji Shopping Street**
    *   Located right outside Kids Plaza, this covered arcade is great for a casual stroll. Grab some *takoyaki* (octopus balls) or sit down at a casual diner for an early dinner before heading back to the hotel.

---

### **Day 2: Marine Life & Giant Ferris Wheels**
*Focus: The stunning bay area, which is spacious, modern, and very easy to navigate with a stroller.*

*   **Morning: Osaka Aquarium Kaiyukan**
    *   **Why it works:** This is one of the world’s best aquariums. The layout is a gentle, sloping ramp that winds downward around a massive central tank. It is completely stroller-accessible and mesmerizing for kids of all ages.
    *   **Tip:** Go right at opening (usually 10:00 AM) to beat the crowds.
*   **Lunch: Tempozan Marketplace**
    *   Located right next to the aquarium. It features a food court (great for quick, picky-eater-friendly meals) and "Naniwa Kuishinbo Yokocho," a cute, retro-themed street food alley.
*   **Afternoon: Tempozan Ferris Wheel & Legoland Discovery Center**
    *   **For the Kid:** Right inside the marketplace is the **Legoland Discovery Center** (an indoor playground) or you can take a ride on the giant Ferris Wheel next door for great views of the bay.
    *   **For You:** The marketplace has excellent free Wi-Fi and several quiet seating areas. If your child falls asleep in the stroller after the aquarium, this is a perfect 1-2 hour window to sit in a cafe and get some focused work done.
*   **Evening: Dotonbori (The Neon Lights)**
    *   Head to the famous canal area just before sunset. It’s bright, bustling, and exciting. 
    *   **Parent Tip:** Dotonbori can get very crowded. For a stress-free dinner, grab some takeout *Okonomiyaki* (savory pancakes) and eat it by the river steps, or head into one of the department store dining halls (like Daimaru in Shinsaibashi) which are spacious and very welcoming to parents with kids.

### **Day 3: Retro Osaka & Stress-Free Departure**
*Focus: A gentle morning, packing up, and a smooth journey to the airport for your 7:00 PM flight.*

*   **9:00 AM - 11:00 AM: Tennoji Park (Tenshiba) & Keitakuen Garden**
    *   **Why it works:** "Tenshiba" is a beautiful, wide-open lawn area right in front of Tennoji Station. It’s a very safe, car-free zone where your child can run around on the grass. 
    *   **For You:** There are lovely lawn-side cafes where you can sit, enjoy breakfast, and do a final check on your work tasks while your child plays nearby.
*   **11:30 AM: Check out of hotel & Head to Namba Station**
    *   **Logistics Tip:** Go to **Namba Station** (or Tennoji, depending on which airport you are flying out of). Store your luggage in a station locker or use the hotel's luggage holding service so you are hands-free.
*   **12:00 PM - 2:30 PM: Shinsekai & Tsutenkaku Tower**
    *   Just a short walk from Tennoji is Shinsekai, a retro neighborhood that feels like a time capsule. 
    *   **For the Kid:** The **Tsutenkaku Tower** has a fun observation deck, and there is a giant slide (Tower Slider) that older kids can ride down.
    *   **Lunch:** Try *Kushikatsu* (skewered fried foods)—it’s a local specialty, served fast, and kids love the bite-sized portions.
*   **3:00 PM: Retrieve luggage and head to the Airport**
    *   *If flying from Kansai International (KIX):* Take the **Nankai Rap:t Express** train from Namba Station. It takes exactly 38 minutes, is incredibly spacious, has luggage racks, and requires reserved seating (meaning you are guaranteed a quiet, comfortable seat to relax or work).
    *   *If flying from Itami (ITM):* Take the easy airport limousine bus from Namba or Tennoji (approx. 30 minutes).
*   **4:30 PM: Arrive at the Airport**
    *   Arriving 2.5 hours early gives you a massive buffer. Both KIX and Itami airports have excellent kids' play areas post-security, nursing rooms, and free Wi-Fi, allowing you to relax, let your child burn off their last bit of energy, and board your 7:00 PM flight completely stress-free.

### **Parent Pro-Tips for Osaka:**
1.  **Elevator Map App:** Japanese train stations are highly accessible, but finding the elevator can sometimes require a detour. Download the **Google Maps** app and set your route options to "wheelchair accessible"—this will automatically show you routes with elevators for your stroller.
2.  **Convenience Stores (Conbini):** Lawson, 7-Eleven, and FamilyMart are your best friends. They have high-quality, fresh snacks (like fruit cups, milk, and rice balls) and ATMs, and you can even buy emergency diapers or wipes there.
