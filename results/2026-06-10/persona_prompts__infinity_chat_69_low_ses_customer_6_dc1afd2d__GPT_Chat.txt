What is the difference between analysis and design? Can one begin to design without analysis? Why? Be concise.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Analysis and design are related but distinct phases of problem-solving:

- Analysis focuses on understanding the problem, requirements, constraints, and user needs. It answers: “What needs to be solved?”
- Design focuses on creating a solution that meets those requirements. It answers: “How will it be solved?”

Can design begin without analysis?
- In a limited sense, yes—you can start sketching ideas or concepts.
- However, effective design requires analysis. Without understanding the problem and requirements, the design may fail to meet actual needs, leading to rework, inefficiency, or poor results.

In short, analysis provides the foundation for design, making it essential for creating effective solutions.
