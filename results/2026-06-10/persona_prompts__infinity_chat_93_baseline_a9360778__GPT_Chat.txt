Stress affects both the body and the mind in many ways. When a person experiences stress, the body releases hormones such as adrenaline and cortisol. These hormones prepare the body to respond to challenges by increasing heart rate, blood pressure, and energy levels. In the short term, this response can be helpful because it improves alertness and reaction time. However, prolonged stress can have negative effects on health.

Chronic stress may weaken the immune system, making a person more vulnerable to illnesses. It can also contribute to headaches, muscle tension, digestive problems, sleep disturbances, and an increased risk of heart disease. People under constant stress may feel physically exhausted even after resting.

Stress also affects mental and emotional well-being. It can cause anxiety, irritability, difficulty concentrating, and memory problems. Some individuals may feel overwhelmed or lose motivation to complete daily tasks. Over time, ongoing stress can increase the risk of mental health conditions such as depression and anxiety disorders.

Managing stress is important for maintaining overall health. Regular exercise, adequate sleep, healthy eating habits, relaxation techniques, and social support can help reduce stress levels. By recognizing the signs of stress and taking steps to address them, individuals can protect both their physical and mental well-being.
