── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
What are the best ways to improve indoor air quality?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Improving indoor air quality usually works best when you address the major sources of pollution, improve ventilation, and filter the air effectively.

Key steps:

- Increase ventilation
  - Open windows and doors when outdoor air quality is good.
  - Use exhaust fans in kitchens and bathrooms.
  - Consider a mechanical ventilation system (such as an energy recovery ventilator or heat recovery ventilator) if your home is tightly sealed.

- Use high-quality air filtration
  - Replace HVAC filters regularly.
  - Use filters with an appropriate MERV rating (often MERV 13 or higher if your system can support it).
  - Run the HVAC fan according to manufacturer recommendations to circulate filtered air.

- Add portable air purifiers
  - Choose units with a true HEPA filter.
  - Size the purifier appropriately for the room.
  - Focus on bedrooms and living areas where people spend the most time.

- Control moisture and humidity
  - Keep indoor humidity around 30–50%.
  - Fix leaks promptly.
  - Use dehumidifiers in damp areas.
  - Clean and address mold growth quickly.

- Reduce pollution at the source
  - Avoid indoor smoking or vaping.
  - Limit burning candles, incense, and wood indoors.
  - Choose low-VOC paints, furnishings, and cleaning products when possible.
  - Store solvents, fuels, and chemicals outside living spaces.

- Improve kitchen air quality
  - Use a vented range hood whenever cooking, especially when frying or using gas appliances.
  - Ensure the hood vents outdoors rather than recirculating air when possible.

- Keep the home clean
  - Vacuum regularly using a vacuum with a HEPA filter.
  - Dust with damp cloths to avoid redistributing particles.
  - Wash bedding regularly.
  - Remove shoes at the door to reduce dirt, pesticides, and other contaminants.

- Manage pets and allergens
  - Groom pets regularly.
  - Wash pet bedding.
  - Consider HEPA filtration if pet dander or allergies are a concern.

- Monitor air quality
  - Use indoor air quality monitors for particulate matter (PM2.5), carbon dioxide (CO₂), humidity, and sometimes volatile organic compounds (VOCs).
  - Install and maintain carbon monoxide detectors.

- Maintain combustion appliances
  - Have furnaces, water heaters, fireplaces, and gas appliances inspected regularly.
  - Ensure proper venting and operation.

If you're looking for the biggest impact per dollar, the most effective combination is often:
1. Eliminate indoor smoking.
2. Use a vented kitchen exhaust fan.
3. Upgrade HVAC filtration (if compatible).
4. Add HEPA air purifiers in occupied rooms.
5. Control moisture and prevent mold.
