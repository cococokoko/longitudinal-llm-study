── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
What are the best apps for tracking fitness and nutrition?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The "best" fitness and nutrition app depends heavily on your specific goals—whether you want to lose weight, build muscle, track macronutrients, or find guided workouts. 

Here is a breakdown of the best apps on the market for 2024, categorized by their strengths.

---

### 1. Best Overall for Nutrition & Calorie Tracking: **MyFitnessPal**
MyFitnessPal remains the industry giant because of its unmatched food database.
*   **Best for:** General calorie counting, macro tracking, and weight loss.
*   **Key Features:**
    *   A database of over 14 million foods (including restaurant items and international foods).
    *   Barcode scanner for quick logging (Note: This is now a premium feature in some regions).
    *   Connects with almost every major fitness tracker (Apple Watch, Fitbit, Garmin).
*   **Pros:** Massive community, seamless integration with other apps.
*   **Cons:** The free version has a lot of ads; the premium version is relatively expensive ($19.99/month or $79.99/year).

### 2. Best for Clean UI & Detailed Macro Tracking: **Cronometer**
If you are a data nerd who cares about vitamins, minerals, and precise macronutrients, Cronometer is the gold standard.
*   **Best for:** Keto dieters, vegans, or anyone tracking micronutrients (vitamins/minerals).
*   **Key Features:**
    *   Every food submission is verified by staff for accuracy (unlike MyFitnessPal, which has user-submitted duplicates).
    *   Tracks up to 82 micronutrients.
    *   Free barcode scanner.
*   **Pros:** Highly accurate data, excellent free version.
*   **Cons:** The database is smaller than MyFitnessPal's (though still very large).

### 3. Best for Weight Loss & Behavior Change: **Noom**
Noom focuses on the psychology behind eating rather than just counting calories.
*   **Best for:** People who struggle with emotional eating or need psychological support to build healthy habits.
*   **Key Features:**
    *   Daily 5-10 minute psychology lessons.
    *   Color-coded food system (Green, Yellow, Orange) based on caloric density.
    *   Access to a personal coach and peer group.
*   **Pros:** Focuses on long-term lifestyle changes rather than quick fixes.
*   **Cons:** Very expensive (subscription-based, usually around $40-$70/month); food logging interface is less detailed than competitors.

### 4. Best for Smart/Adaptive Nutrition: **MacroFactor**
Co-created by fitness science experts (including Stronger by Science), MacroFactor uses a smart algorithm to adjust your targets based on your actual metabolism.
*   **Best for:** Bodybuilders, athletes, and those who want a science-backed, "adherence-neutral" approach.
*   **Key Features:**
    *   Calculates your dynamic "Expenditure" (how many calories you actually burn daily).
    *   Adjusts your weekly calorie/macro targets based on your weight trends and food logs.
    *   "Adherence-neutral": It doesn't shame you or use red text if you go over your calories.
*   **Pros:** Incredibly fast food logger; highly scientific and effective.
*   **Cons:** No free version (paid only, $11.99/month or $71.99/year).

---

### 5. Best for Gym Workouts & Weightlifting: **Strong** or **Hevy**
These two apps are very similar and are the gold standard for tracking strength training.
*   **Best for:** Weightlifters, powerlifters, and gym-goers who want to track their lifts.
*   **Key Features:**
    *   Clean, minimal interface to log sets, reps, and weight.
    *   Rest timers built into the app.
    *   Graphs showing your volume and 1-Rep Max progress over time.
    *   *Hevy* has a social media aspect (like Strava for lifting), while *Strong* is more private.
*   **Pros:** No clutter, very easy to use while resting between sets.
*   **Cons:** Limited free versions (you can only save a few custom routines before needing to upgrade).

### 6. Best for Guided Workouts (At Home or Gym): **Nike Training Club (NTC)**
*   **Best for:** Beginners and those who want high-quality video workouts without a subscription.
*   **Key Features:**
    *   Hundreds of workouts ranging from yoga and HIIT to strength training.
    *   Classes led by world-class Nike trainers.
    *   Programs designed for different fitness levels and equipment availability.
*   **Pros:** **100% Free.** No hidden paywalls or premium tiers.
*   **Cons:** Does not track nutrition; limited custom workout creation.

### 7. Best for Running, Cycling, & Outdoor Cardio: **Strava**
Strava is the ultimate social network for athletes.
*   **Best for:** Runners, cyclists, hikers, and swimmers.
*   **Key Features:**
    *   GPS tracking for outdoor activities.
    *   "Segments" that let you compare your times on specific hills/routes against others.
    *   Social feed to share workouts, photos, and leave comments ("Kudos") for friends.
*   **Pros:** Unmatched community; excellent data analysis for cardio.
*   **Cons:** Not designed for weightlifting or nutrition tracking.

---

### Summary Recommendation: How to Combine Them
Most people find that using **one nutrition app** and **one fitness app** is the perfect combination. Here are the best pairings:

*   **The "I want to lose weight and get healthy" pair:** *MyFitnessPal* (for food) + *Nike Training Club* (for workouts).
*   **The "I want to build muscle/strength" pair:** *MacroFactor* (for precise macros) + *Hevy* (for tracking lifts).
*   **The "I want to run/cycle and eat clean" pair:** *Cronometer* (for micronutrients) + *Strava* (for cardio).
