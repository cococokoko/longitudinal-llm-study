# Mastering the Art of Cooking Steak

Great topic! Here's a practical guide to help you cook steak confidently.

## Choosing Your Cut
- **Ribeye** - rich, well-marbled, forgiving for beginners
- **New York Strip** - leaner, great flavor
- **Filet Mignon** - tender, mild flavor
- **Sirloin** - budget-friendly, solid choice

## Key Principles

### Before Cooking
- **Bring to room temperature** - 30-45 minutes out of the fridge
- **Pat completely dry** - moisture is the enemy of a good sear
- **Season generously** - kosher salt and black pepper at minimum
- **Preheat your pan** - cast iron works best, get it very hot

### Doneness Guide
| Level | Internal Temp |
|-------|--------------|
| Rare | 125°F |
| Medium Rare | 130-135°F |
| Medium | 140-145°F |
| Well Done | 160°F+ |

### The Cook
- Use high smoke-point oil (avocado, canola)
- **Don't move it** - let it sear properly
- Baste with **butter, garlic, and thyme**
- **Rest 5-10 minutes** before cutting

## Pro Tips
- A meat thermometer removes all guesswork
- Thicker cuts benefit from **reverse searing**
- Cast iron holds heat better than most pans
