## Linear Graph Example

A **path graph** is a classic example of a linear graph in graph theory.

### Example: P₄ (Path Graph with 4 vertices)

```
A --- B --- C --- D
```

### Properties:
- **Vertices:** {A, B, C, D}
- **Edges:** {AB, BC, CD}
- Each vertex connects to at most **2 neighbors**
- Forms a simple, straight-line structure
- No cycles

### Key Characteristics of Linear Graphs:
| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| Vertices | n |
| Edges | n - 1 |
| Max degree | 2 |
| Min degree | 1 (endpoints) |

### Notes:
- The **endpoints** (A and D) have degree **1**
- The **interior vertices** (B and C) have degree **2**
- A linear graph is essentially equivalent to a **path graph (Pₙ)**

This is one of the simplest graph structures and serves as a foundational example in graph theory, relevant to topics such as **Hamiltonian paths**, **tree structures**, and **network topology**.
