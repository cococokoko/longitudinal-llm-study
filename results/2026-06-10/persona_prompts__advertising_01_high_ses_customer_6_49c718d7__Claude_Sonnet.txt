# Learning Guitar as a Busy Professional

Given your background, you likely appreciate **structured, efficient approaches** to skill acquisition. Here's a practical framework:

## Getting Started Efficiently

**First decisions:**
- **Acoustic vs. Electric** - Acoustic builds finger strength faster; electric is easier on fingers initially
- **Lessons vs. Self-teaching** - A good instructor front-loads the right habits and saves significant time
- **Time commitment** - Even **15-20 minutes daily** beats 2-hour weekend sessions

## Structured Learning Path

| Stage | Focus | Timeline |
|-------|-------|----------|
| Foundation | Basic chords (G, C, D, Em, Am) | Weeks 1-4 |
| Building | Chord transitions, strumming patterns | Months 2-3 |
| Expanding | Barre chords, simple songs | Months 4-6 |
| Developing | Your preferred style/genre | 6+ months |

## High-ROI Resources
- **Apps:** Fender Play, Yousician, Justin Guitar (free)
- **YouTube:** JustinGuitar channel is exceptionally well-structured
- **Instructor:** Worth the investment for accountability

## Realistic Expectations
- **Finger soreness** is normal for 2-3 weeks
- Most people can play recognizable songs within **4-6 weeks**
- Consistency matters far more than session length

What style of music are you hoping to play? That shapes the best approach significantly.
