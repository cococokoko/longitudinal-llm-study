---

### Step 1: Choose the Right Business Model (Low-Maintenance is Key)
When you have limited time and energy at the end of the day, you want a business model that doesn't require you to pack boxes, run to the post office, or manage heavy physical inventory. 

*   **Digital Products (E-books, Guides, Templates):** You create them once, and they sell automatically 24/7. Zero shipping, zero inventory.
*   **Print on Demand (POD):** You design t-shirts, mugs, or phone cases. When a customer buys, a third-party company prints and ships it for you. 
*   **Affiliate Marketing:** You promote other companies' products and earn a commission on sales. No customer service or shipping required.
*   **Dropshipping (with caution):** Selling products where the supplier ships directly to the customer. (Requires good automation so you aren't manually processing orders after a long shift).

### Step 2: Define Your Niche (Solve a Problem You Know)
The easiest business to start is one where you already understand the customer. 
*   *Example:* If you know what it’s like to work a physically demanding job, could you sell digital fitness/stretching guides for manual laborers? Ergonomic product recommendations? High-quality lunch prep guides for people who eat on the go? 
*   **Action:** Write down 3 things you are knowledgeable about or problems you know how to solve.

### Step 3: Build Your "Low-Effort" Tech Stack
You want tools that do the heavy lifting for you while you are busy or resting.
*   **Website/Storefront:** Use **Shopify** (for physical/dropshipped goods) or **Stan Store / Gumroad** (incredibly simple for digital products). They take minutes to set up.
*   **Payment Processing:** Stripe or PayPal (built into the platforms above) so you get paid automatically.
*   **Email Marketing:** **Klaviyo** or **ConvertKit** to send automated "Welcome" and "Thank you" emails to your customers.

### Step 4: The "Micro-Time" Strategy (How to build it on a busy schedule)
When you work long hours, you cannot rely on having 4-hour blocks of free time. You have to use **micro-pockets of time**:
*   **The 15-Minute Rule:** Do one small thing before work or right when you get home. (e.g., Day 1: Buy the domain name. Day 2: Write the product description. Day 3: Upload one image). 
*   **Use Your Commute/Breaks:** Use your lunch break or commute to listen to business podcasts, brainstorm ideas on your phone's notepad, or dictating ideas into a voice recorder.
*   **Automate Social Media:** Use tools like **Buffer** or **Later** to schedule a week’s worth of social media posts in one hour on your day off.

### Step 5: Keep It Legal & Official
Before you launch, make sure you cover the basics:
1.  **Choose a Name:** Keep it simple and check if the `.com` and social media handles are available.
2.  **Register the Business:** Depending on your country/state, look into getting a simple LLC or Sole Proprietorship.
3.  **Separate Your Finances:** Open a separate bank account (even a free online one) just for your business expenses and income. This makes tax season a breeze.
