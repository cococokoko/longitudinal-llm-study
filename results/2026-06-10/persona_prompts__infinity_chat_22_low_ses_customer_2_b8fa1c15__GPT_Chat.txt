In Christianity, "discipleship" generally means following Jesus and growing as his disciple, but different traditions emphasize different aspects of what that looks like.

Some common views include:

- Evangelical Protestant view
  - Focuses on a personal relationship with Jesus.
  - Emphasizes Bible study, prayer, evangelism, and spiritual growth.
  - Discipleship is often understood as a lifelong process of becoming more like Christ.

- Catholic view
  - Sees discipleship as following Christ through faith, participation in the sacraments, prayer, and service.
  - The Church and its traditions play an important role in forming disciples.
  - Emphasizes holiness and spiritual formation within the Christian community.

- Eastern Orthodox view
  - Emphasizes union with God (theosis), spiritual transformation, and participation in the life of the Church.
  - Discipleship involves prayer, worship, fasting, and spiritual guidance.

- Mainline Protestant view
  - Often highlights both personal faith and social responsibility.
  - Discipleship includes worship, spiritual growth, justice, compassion, and service to others.

- Anabaptist view
  - Strongly emphasizes living according to Jesus' teachings, especially the Sermon on the Mount.
  - Focuses on community, peace, simplicity, and practical obedience.

- Pentecostal and Charismatic view
  - Emphasizes the work of the Holy Spirit in the believer's life.
  - Discipleship includes spiritual gifts, prayer, worship, and active ministry.

There are also different approaches to discipleship across traditions:

- Knowledge-centered: learning biblical teachings and doctrine.
- Relationship-centered: mentoring and learning from mature believers.
- Service-centered: growing through acts of ministry and compassion.
- Mission-centered: making disciples and sharing the gospel.
- Community-centered: formation through participation in a faith community.

Despite their differences, most Christian traditions agree that discipleship involves following Jesus, growing in faith, and living out that faith in everyday life.
