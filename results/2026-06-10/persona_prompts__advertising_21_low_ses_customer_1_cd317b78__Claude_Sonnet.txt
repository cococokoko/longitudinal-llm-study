── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How do I start a vegetable garden in my backyard?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Starting a vegetable garden is a great project! Here are the key steps:

## Planning
- **Choose a location** with at least 6-8 hours of sunlight daily
- **Start small** - a 4x8 or 4x4 foot bed is manageable for beginners
- **Pick vegetables you actually eat** and enjoy

## Preparing the Space
- Clear the area of grass and weeds
- Test your soil if possible, or start with raised beds
- Add compost to improve soil quality
- Consider raised beds for better drainage and easier maintenance

## Getting Started
- **Decide between seeds or transplants** - transplants are easier for beginners
- Check your local frost dates to know when to plant
- Group plants with similar water and sun needs together

## Good Beginner Vegetables
- Tomatoes, zucchini, lettuce, radishes, green beans
- Herbs like basil and parsley are also easy

## Maintenance Tips
- Water consistently, usually 1 inch per week
- Mulch around plants to retain moisture and reduce weeds
- Check plants regularly for pests

## Practical Considerations for a Busy Schedule
- **Low-maintenance options** like zucchini or cherry tomatoes are forgiving
- Drip irrigation or soaker hoses can reduce daily watering needs
- Raised beds tend to need less weeding overall
