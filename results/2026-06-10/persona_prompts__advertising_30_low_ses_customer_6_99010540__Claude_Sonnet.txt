── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Great question! Here's a straightforward approach to get started:

## Choose Your First Language
- **Python** is widely recommended for beginners - readable and versatile
- **JavaScript** is good if you're interested in web development
- Pick one and stick with it initially

## Start with Free Resources
- **freeCodeCamp.org** - structured, project-based
- **The Odin Project** - comprehensive web dev path
- **CS50** (Harvard's free course) - solid fundamentals
- **Codecademy** - interactive and beginner-friendly

## Build Good Habits Early
- Code **a little every day** rather than long occasional sessions
- Actually type the code, don't just read it
- Try to break and fix things on purpose
- Keep notes on what you learn

## Practical Tips
- Start with small, simple projects
- Google errors freely - everyone does this
- Join communities like Reddit's r/learnprogramming
- Don't try to learn everything at once

## Realistic Expectations
- Frustration is normal and part of the process
- Progress feels slow at first, then accelerates
- Most people can build basic things within a few months of consistent practice

**Given your work schedule**, even 20-30 minutes daily on a phone app like **Mimo** or **Grasshopper** can build real momentum without requiring long sessions.
