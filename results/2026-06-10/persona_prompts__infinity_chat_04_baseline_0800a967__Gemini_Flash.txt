### 1. Gorz, Generative AI, and the "Cognitive Rent"
*The Core Idea:* Gorz was one of the earliest theorists of "immaterial labor" and "cognitive capitalism" (especially in *Reclaiming Work* and *Immaterial*). He argued that when knowledge becomes the primary force of production, the labor theory of value undergoes a crisis because knowledge cannot be measured in labor-time. 

*The New Angle:* Apply Gorz’s critique of cognitive capitalism to the current explosion of **Generative AI (LLMs, image generators)**. 
*   **The Argument:** AI companies are currently "enclosing" the cognitive commons (scraping human culture and language) to turn it into proprietary capital. Gorz’s work on "knowledge-work" can be used to argue that AI does not represent the "end of work" via automation, but rather the ultimate stage of capital expropriating the "general intellect."
*   **Key Gorzian Concept:** *The crisis of the measure of value* and *the democratization of knowledge*.
*   **Research Question:** *How does Gorz’s theory of cognitive capitalism anticipate the enclosure of the digital commons by generative AI, and how can his concept of "non-work" guide a Marxist politics of algorithmic liberation?*

### 2. The Red-Green Synthesis: Gorz, Degrowth, and the "Imperial Mode of Living"
*The Core Idea:* Gorz is a founding father of political ecology. He argued that you cannot solve the ecological crisis within a capitalist framework because capitalism requires infinite growth. Today, the "Degrowth" movement (led by figures like Jason Hickel and Kohei Saito) is gaining massive traction in Marxist circles.

*The New Angle:* Use Gorz to bridge the gap between **Western Marxist Degrowth** and the **Global South’s critique of the "Imperial Mode of Living."**
*   **The Argument:** Some Marxist critics argue that degrowth is a middle-class, Eurocentric fantasy. However, Gorz’s ecology was deeply rooted in *autonomy* and the reduction of the "sphere of necessity." You can argue that Gorzian degrowth is not about "scarcity" or "belt-tightening" (which capitalism imposes through austerity), but about "ecological sufficiency" as a tool for anti-imperialist solidarity.
*   **Key Gorzian Concept:** *The distinction between "autonomous" and "heteronomous" spheres*; *ecological sufficiency*.
*   **Research Question:** *How does Gorz’s ecological existentialism provide a defense of Degrowth against charges of neo-Malthusianism and Eurocentrism within contemporary Marxist geography?*

### 3. Gorz and the Queer/Feminist Critique of "Social Reproduction"
*The Core Idea:* Gorz’s *Farewell to the Working Class* argued that the traditional industrial proletariat was no longer the revolutionary subject. Instead, he pointed to a "non-class of non-workers." 

*The New Angle:* Put Gorz in direct conversation with **Marxist-Feminist Social Reproduction Theory (SRT)** (e.g., Lise Vogel, Silvia Federici, Tithi Bhattacharya).
*   **The Argument:** Historically, Marxist-feminists criticized Gorz for ignoring domestic and reproductive labor when he defined the "sphere of autonomy." However, a modern reading can synthesize them. Gorz’s advocacy for a Universal Basic Income (UBI) decoupled from work aligns closely with feminist demands for "Wages for Housework" and the liberation of life from the wage-relation. 
*   **Key Gorzian Concept:** *The decoupling of income from work*; *the expansion of the sphere of autonomy*.
*   **Research Question:** *To what extent can Gorz’s post-work politics be reconciled with Social Reproduction Theory to form a unified Marxist-feminist strategy for the abolition of the wage-relation?*

### 4. "Non-Reformist Reforms" in the Age of Capitalist Realism
*The Core Idea:* Gorz’s most famous strategic contribution to Marxist theory is the concept of **"non-reformist reforms"** (or structural reforms) from his 1964 book *Strategy for Labor*. These are reforms that do not seek to patch up capitalism, but rather to undermine its internal logic and build counter-power (e.g., UBI, radical work-time reduction).

*The New Angle:* Re-evaluate this strategy in the context of **"Capitalist Realism"** (Mark Fisher) and the failure of recent democratic socialist projects (Syriza, Corbynism, Sanders).
*   **The Argument:** In a world where it is "easier to imagine the end of the world than the end of capitalism," how do we actually implement non-reformist reforms? You can analyze modern policy proposals—like the **Green New Deal**, **Universal Basic Services (UBS)**, or the **4-day workweek**—through a strict Gorzian lens to determine if they are truly "non-reformist" or merely social-democratic stabilization mechanisms.
*   **Key Gorzian Concept:** *Non-reformist reforms*; *worker self-management (auto-gestion)*.
*   **Research Question:** *In the wake of the defeat of 2010s democratic socialism, how does Gorz’s dialectic of "non-reformist reforms" provide a viable revolutionary strategy for 21st-century eco-socialism?*
