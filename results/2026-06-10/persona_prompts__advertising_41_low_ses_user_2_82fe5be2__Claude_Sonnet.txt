# Improving Your Credit Score as a Busy Single Parent

Here are practical steps organized by impact and effort:

## Highest Impact Actions

**Pay bills on time**
- Set up automatic payments for minimum amounts
- Payment history is ~35% of your score

**Reduce credit card balances**
- Try to keep balances below 30% of your credit limit
- This "utilization ratio" is ~30% of your score

## Quick Wins (Can Help Within 30-60 Days)

- **Request a credit limit increase** on existing cards without spending more
- **Pay down balances** before your statement closing date
- **Dispute any errors** on your credit report at AnnualCreditReport.com (free)

## Practical Tips for Your Situation

- **Automate everything possible** to reduce mental load
- **Set calendar reminders** for bill due dates
- Check your free credit score through your bank app regularly

## Things to Avoid

- Opening multiple new accounts quickly
- Closing old credit cards
- Missing payments even once

## Free Resources

- **AnnualCreditReport.com** - free weekly reports
- **Credit Karma** - free score monitoring
- **NFCC.org** - nonprofit credit counseling

## Realistic Timeline
Most people see meaningful improvement in **3-6 months** with consistent effort.
