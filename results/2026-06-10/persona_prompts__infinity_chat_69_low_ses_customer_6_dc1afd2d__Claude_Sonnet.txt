## Analysis vs. Design

**Analysis** = Understanding **what** the problem is (requirements, constraints, current state)

**Design** = Determining **how** to solve it (structure, architecture, solution approach)

---

## Can You Design Without Analysis?

**Technically yes, practically no.**

- Without analysis, design is essentially **guessing** — you may build an elegant solution to the **wrong problem**
- You risk wasted effort, rework, and solutions that don't meet actual needs
- Some overlap exists (they're iterative), but skipping analysis leads to **costly mistakes**

**Bottom line:** Analysis defines the target; design hits it. Skipping analysis means you don't know what you're aiming at.
