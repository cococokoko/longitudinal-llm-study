── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
My girlfriend is buying a house. I plan to marry her in a year or so. I don't know if it's fair for me to pay for the house or not. What's the best solution?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This is a really common situation and worth thinking through carefully. Here are some practical considerations:

## Key Questions to Ask Yourself

- Are you contributing to the down payment, mortgage payments, or both?
- Will your name be on the deed/title?
- What happens if the relationship doesn't work out before marriage?

## Realistic Options

**Option 1: Stay separate for now**
- She buys it in her name only
- You pay rent to her or split expenses fairly
- Cleaner legally if things don't work out

**Option 2: Both go on the mortgage/deed**
- Makes sense if you're both financially committed
- Requires a cohabitation agreement to protect both of you
- More complicated if relationship ends

**Option 3: Contribute but document it**
- If you help with down payment, get it in writing
- Could be structured as a loan or documented contribution
- Protects you legally

## Honest Considerations

- **"A year or so"** is still uncertain - that matters legally
- A cohabitation or property agreement **protects both of you**, not just one person
- Talking to a **real estate attorney** is genuinely worth the cost here

## The Fairness Question
Contributing to a home you live in is reasonable, but **without legal protection**, you could lose that contribution if circumstances change.
