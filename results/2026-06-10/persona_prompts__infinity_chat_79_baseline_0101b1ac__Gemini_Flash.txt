To create an "incredible" STEM fair project, it needs to solve a real-world problem, use the scientific method or engineering design process, and have a "wow" factor—all while keeping costs low. 

Vietnam faces unique environmental and agricultural challenges. Here is an incredible, highly relevant, and affordable STEM fair project idea:

---

### Project Title: **The "Green Filter" – Upcycling Agricultural Waste into Low-Cost Water Purifiers**

#### **The Vietnam Connection (The "Why"):**
Vietnam is one of the world’s largest producers of coffee and rice. However, processing these crops generates millions of tons of waste: **coffee husks/grounds** and **rice husks**. At the same time, many rural areas in Vietnam (especially in the Mekong Delta and mountainous regions) struggle with water pollution, including heavy metals from industrial runoff and organic pollutants from agriculture.

#### **The STEM Concept:**
This project uses **activated carbon** made from Vietnamese agricultural waste (rice husks or spent coffee grounds) to filter pollutants out of water. 

*   **Science:** Chemistry (adsorption, carbonization, pH levels).
*   **Technology/Engineering:** Designing and testing a gravity-fed filtration column.
*   **Math:** Calculating filtration rates, efficiency percentages, and cost-benefit analysis.

---

### **How to Do It (Step-by-Step & Affordable):**

#### **1. Materials Needed (Under $15 USD / 350,000 VND):**
*   **Waste materials:** Spent coffee grounds (free from a local café) or rice husks (free/cheap from a market or farm).
*   **Activating Agent:** Lemon juice (citric acid) or vinegar (acetic acid)—very cheap.
*   **Heat source:** A household oven, a backyard fire/charcoal grill, or a portable gas stove.
*   **An empty metal can** (with a lid) to act as a crucible.
*   **Testing materials:** 
    *   Dirty water (you can make this using water mixed with food coloring, soy sauce, or soil).
    *   Plastic bottles (for the filter casing).
    *   Sand and gravel (free from outdoors).
    *   A cheap TDS (Total Dissolved Solids) meter or pH strips (approx. $3–$5 on Shopee/Lazada).

#### **2. The Experiment (The Science):**
1.  **Create the Bio-char:** Dry your coffee grounds or rice husks. Place them in the metal can, seal it (leave a tiny hole for gas to escape), and heat it over a fire or stove for 30–45 minutes. This turns the waste into charcoal (carbonization).
2.  **Activate the Carbon:** Soak the charcoal in lemon juice or vinegar for 24 hours. This "activates" it by creating millions of microscopic pores, vastly increasing its surface area. Bake it one more time to dry it.
3.  **Build the Filter:** Cut a plastic bottle in half. Layer it from bottom to top: cotton ball/coffee filter, your activated carbon, sand, and gravel.
4.  **The Test:** Run "polluted" water (water colored with food dye or soy sauce) through the filter. 

#### **3. What to Measure (The Data):**
*   **Independent Variable:** The type of waste used (Coffee grounds vs. Rice husks) or the activation method (Lemon juice vs. plain water).
*   **Dependent Variables:** 
    *   **Clarity:** Use a smartphone camera and a free light-meter app to measure how much light passes through the filtered water (turbidity).
    *   **TDS/pH:** Measure the water before and after filtration.
    *   **Flow Rate:** How many milliliters of water are filtered per minute?

---

### **Why this Project is "Incredible" for a STEM Fair:**

1.  **Circular Economy:** It takes a waste product that causes pollution (rotting coffee/rice husks release methane) and uses it to solve another environmental issue (water pollution).
2.  **High Visual Impact:** During the fair, you can have a live demonstration. Pouring dark, muddy, or dyed water into your filter and watching it drip out crystal clear is highly satisfying and grabs judges' attention.
3.  **Scalability:** You can present this as a viable, ultra-low-cost solution for rural Vietnamese households. You can calculate: *"For just 10,000 VND, a family can build a filter that lasts for 3 months."*
4.  **Local Relevance:** It shows the judges that you care about Vietnam's specific agricultural economy and environmental future.

### **How to present it for maximum points:**
*   **The Hook:** Start your presentation with a statistic about water quality in the Mekong Delta or the amount of coffee waste Vietnam produces daily.
*   **The Display:** Show samples of the raw rice husks, the black activated carbon, and the "before and after" water samples in clear jars.
