## Analysis vs. Design

**Analysis** answers *"What does the system need to do?"* — understanding requirements, problems, and constraints.

**Design** answers *"How will the system do it?"* — defining architecture, components, and implementation approach.

---

## Can You Design Without Analysis?

**Technically yes, practically no.**

Skipping analysis means you're designing a solution to a problem you haven't clearly defined. This leads to:

- **Rework** — building the wrong thing correctly
- **Scope creep** — undefined boundaries invite endless changes
- **Misaligned stakeholders** — different people assume different outcomes

In fast-moving environments, analysis and design often **overlap iteratively** (as in Agile), but even then, *some* analysis always precedes each design decision — the sequence is compressed, not eliminated.

---

**Bottom line:** Analysis and design are distinct disciplines. Bypassing analysis is a common source of failed technology investments — you optimize execution of the wrong solution.
