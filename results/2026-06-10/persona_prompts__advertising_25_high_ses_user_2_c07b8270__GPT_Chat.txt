── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I am a senior attorney at a well-established law firm.

How can I decorate my apartment on a minimalist budget?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
If you're a senior attorney, the goal is often less about filling space and more about creating an environment that feels intentional, calm, and well-curated. A minimalist budget doesn't require a minimalist aesthetic—it means spending selectively on items that have a disproportionate impact.

Some high-impact, low-cost approaches:

- Edit before you buy.
  - Remove clutter and unnecessary furniture.
  - A sparse, organized apartment often looks more expensive than a crowded one.

- Focus on lighting.
  - Good lighting can transform a space more than new furniture.
  - Add a floor lamp or table lamp with warm bulbs (2700K–3000K).
  - Avoid relying solely on overhead lighting.

- Use large art instead of many small pieces.
  - One oversized framed print can create a sophisticated focal point.
  - Consider downloadable artwork from online marketplaces and inexpensive frames.

- Add plants.
  - A few larger plants usually look more refined than many small ones.
  - Snake plants, ZZ plants, and pothos are relatively affordable and low-maintenance.

- Upgrade textiles.
  - Neutral curtains hung higher than the window frame make ceilings appear taller.
  - A quality-looking throw blanket and a few well-chosen pillows can elevate a living room.
  - If possible, invest in a larger rug rather than a small one.

- Create a cohesive color palette.
  - Limit yourself to two or three primary colors plus neutrals.
  - Consistency tends to make spaces look more polished and expensive.

- Display books selectively.
  - Curate shelves rather than filling them.
  - As an attorney, a small collection of attractive books can add personality without appearing staged.

- Use mirrors strategically.
  - A large mirror increases light and makes rooms feel larger.
  - Secondhand mirrors are often excellent values.

- Shop secondhand.
  - Estate sales, Facebook Marketplace, Craigslist, and local consignment stores often have solid wood furniture at a fraction of retail prices.
  - Prioritize timeless pieces over trendy decor.

- Upgrade hardware.
  - Replacing cabinet pulls, drawer handles, or outdated light switch plates is inexpensive and can make a noticeable difference.
