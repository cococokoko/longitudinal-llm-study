# Tips for Running a Marathon

## Training
- **Follow a structured plan** - Most plans are 16-20 weeks long
- **Build mileage gradually** - Increase weekly mileage by no more than 10% per week
- **Include long runs** - Build up to at least one 20-mile training run
- **Rest days matter** - Allow your body to recover between runs

## Nutrition & Hydration
- **Practice fueling during training** - Don't try new foods on race day
- **Carbohydrate loading** - Increase carbs 2-3 days before the race
- **Stay hydrated** - Drink water consistently, not just during runs
- **Use energy gels or chews** - Practice taking them during long runs

## Race Day
- **Start slow** - Many runners go out too fast and struggle later
- **Stick to your pace** - Resist the crowd energy early on
- **Break it into segments** - Focus on smaller milestones
- **Know the course** - Identify hills and aid stations in advance

## Recovery
- **Cool down properly** after long runs
- **Stretch and foam roll** regularly
- **Prioritize sleep** for muscle repair
- **Listen to your body** - Address pain early to avoid injury

## Gear
- **Wear broken-in shoes** - Never race in new shoes
- **Moisture-wicking clothing** to prevent chafing
- **Body Glide** for high-friction areas
