── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Can you give me a powerful rhetorical question for an essay about the harms of social media on teens?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
To choose the most powerful rhetorical question, you should match it to the specific angle or tone of your essay. 

Here are a few options categorized by the specific harm they focus on:

### 1. Focus on Mental Health & Self-Esteem
*Best for essays about depression, anxiety, body image, and the "highlight reel" effect.*
> **"How can we expect a generation to build a stable sense of self when their self-worth is constantly being weighed, measured, and judged by the cold calculus of a 'like' button?"**

### 2. Focus on Addiction & Tech Monopolies
*Best for essays about screen time, dopamine loops, and how tech companies exploit teens.*
> **"When did we decide that the mental well-being of our youth was an acceptable price to pay for a tech company’s advertising revenue?"**

### 3. Focus on Isolation & Loneliness
*Best for essays about how social media actually disconnects teens from real-world relationships.*
> **"Are we raising the most connected generation in human history, or the most painfully isolated?"**

### 4. Focus on the Loss of Childhood
*Best for essays about the pressure to grow up too fast and the constant surveillance teens face.*
> **"What happens to a childhood when every awkward phase, every mistake, and every private moment is curated for a public audience and preserved forever in a digital archive?"**

### 5. Short, Punchy, and Direct (Great for an Intro or Conclusion)
*Best for grabbing immediate attention or leaving a lasting impression.*
> **"We have given teenagers a window to the entire world, but have we trapped them inside it?"**

### How to use it in your essay:
* **In the Introduction (The Hook):** Start with the question to immediately challenge the reader's assumptions. 
  * *Example:* "Are we raising the most connected generation in human history, or the most painfully isolated? While social media promised a global village, it has instead delivered a digital epidemic of teenage loneliness..."
* **In the Conclusion (The Call to Action):** Use it at the very end to leave the reader thinking.
  * *Example:* "...As we look to the future, we must ask ourselves: when did we decide that the mental well-being of our youth was an acceptable price to pay for a tech company’s advertising revenue?"
