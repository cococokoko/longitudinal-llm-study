── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How to plan an environmentally friendly wedding?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Planning an environmentally friendly wedding involves making choices that reduce waste, conserve resources, and support ethical businesses while still creating a meaningful celebration. Here are some practical ways to do that:

1. Choose a Sustainable Venue
- Select a venue close to where most guests live to reduce travel emissions.
- Consider outdoor venues, botanical gardens, farms, or community spaces that require less decoration.
- Look for venues with sustainability policies, renewable energy use, recycling programs, or water-saving practices.

2. Keep the Guest List Thoughtful
- A smaller wedding generally has a lower environmental impact.
- Focus on inviting people who are most important to you.

3. Use Digital Invitations
- Send invitations electronically when possible.
- If you prefer paper invitations, choose recycled paper, plantable paper, or sustainably sourced materials.

4. Choose Sustainable Attire
- Wear a pre-owned, vintage, rented, or ethically produced wedding outfit.
- Encourage wedding party members to wear outfits they already own or can wear again.
- Donate, resell, or repurpose attire after the wedding.

5. Source Local and Seasonal Food
- Work with caterers who prioritize local ingredients.
- Choose seasonal menus to reduce transportation impacts.
- Offer vegetarian or plant-based options, which often have a lower carbon footprint.
- Plan portions carefully to minimize food waste.

6. Reduce Single-Use Items
- Use reusable dishes, glassware, and linens instead of disposable alternatives.
- Avoid plastic straws, bottles, and utensils.
- Rent decorations rather than buying items that may only be used once.

7. Select Eco-Friendly Decorations
- Use potted plants, rented décor, candles, or reusable items.
- Choose locally grown flowers or dried floral arrangements.
- Avoid balloons, glitter, and decorations that are difficult to recycle.

8. Plan Responsible Transportation
- Arrange group transportation such as shuttles.
- Encourage carpooling.
- Provide information about public transit options.

9. Give Sustainable Favors—or Skip Them
- Consider edible favors, local products, seeds, or charitable donations.
- Many guests are happy without traditional favors.

10. Minimize Waste
- Set up clearly labeled recycling and composting stations.
- Donate leftover flowers to hospitals, care facilities, or community organizations where permitted.
- Donate unused food when local regulations and caterers allow.

11. Choose Ethical Vendors
- Ask vendors about their sustainability practices.
- Support local businesses and artisans.
- Prioritize vendors who reduce packaging and waste.

12. Consider Carbon Offsetting
- If significant travel is unavoidable, consider contributing to reputable carbon-offset projects.
- Focus first on reducing emissions before offsetting.

Sample eco-friendly priorities:
- Digital invitations
- Local venue
- Seasonal, mostly plant-based menu
- Reusable décor and tableware
- Locally sourced flowers
- Group transportation
- Composting and recycling plan

The most sustainable wedding is usually one that aligns with your values and avoids unnecessary consumption, rather than trying to achieve perfection in every category.
