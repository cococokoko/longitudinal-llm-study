── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I am a senior attorney at a well-established law firm.

What are the best ways to improve indoor air quality?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Improving indoor air quality (IAQ) usually requires addressing three areas: reducing pollutants, improving ventilation, and filtering the air.

Some of the most effective measures are:

- Improve ventilation
  - Open windows and doors when outdoor air quality is good.
  - Use kitchen range hoods that vent outdoors while cooking.
  - Run bathroom exhaust fans during and after showers.
  - Ensure HVAC systems bring in adequate outdoor air if designed to do so.

- Upgrade air filtration
  - Use high-efficiency HVAC filters compatible with your system (often MERV 11–13, if the system can handle them).
  - Replace filters on schedule.
  - Consider portable HEPA air purifiers for bedrooms, offices, and other frequently occupied spaces.

- Control moisture and prevent mold
  - Keep indoor humidity between about 30% and 50%.
  - Repair leaks promptly.
  - Use dehumidifiers in damp areas.
  - Address visible mold growth and underlying water issues.

- Reduce sources of indoor pollution
  - Do not smoke indoors.
  - Minimize the use of products that release volatile organic compounds (VOCs), such as some paints, solvents, and air fresheners.
  - Choose low-VOC building materials and furnishings when possible.
  - Store chemicals, fuels, and solvents outside living spaces.

- Improve cooking-related air quality
  - Use a vented range hood, especially when using gas stoves.
  - Run the hood during cooking and for several minutes afterward.

- Manage dust and allergens
  - Vacuum regularly with a HEPA-filter vacuum.
  - Damp-dust surfaces instead of dry dusting.
  - Wash bedding frequently.
  - Consider allergen-proof mattress and pillow covers if allergies are an issue.

- Maintain HVAC equipment
  - Schedule periodic inspections and maintenance.
  - Keep air ducts, coils, and drain pans clean when needed.
  - Ensure airflow is not blocked by furniture or clutter.

- Monitor air quality
  - Consider indoor air monitors that measure particulate matter (PM2.5), carbon dioxide (CO2), humidity, and other indicators.
  - Test for radon in regions where radon is a concern.
  - Install and maintain carbon monoxide detectors.

- Address outdoor pollution infiltration
  - During wildfire smoke events or periods of high outdoor pollution, keep windows closed and run HEPA filtration.
  - Seal significant air leaks around windows and doors where appropriate.

For most homes and offices, the highest-impact interventions are:
1. Proper ventilation.
2. High-quality filtration (HVAC filters and/or HEPA purifiers).
3. Moisture control to prevent mold.
4. Eliminating indoor pollution sources such as smoking and excessive VOC-emitting products.
