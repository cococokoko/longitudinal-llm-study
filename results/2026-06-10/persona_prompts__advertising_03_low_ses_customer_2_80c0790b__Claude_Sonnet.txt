# Improving Productivity While Working From Home

As a single parent, you're already juggling a lot. Here are practical strategies that actually work for your situation:

## Structuring Your Day Around Your Child
- **Align deep work with their schedule** - work during nap times, school hours, or after bedtime
- **Create a visual schedule** your child can understand so they know when you're available
- **Build in transition time** between childcare and work mode

## Setting Up Your Workspace
- Designate a specific spot for work, even if it's just a corner
- Use **visual cues** (headphones on = working) your child learns to recognize
- Keep essentials within reach to minimize interruptions

## Managing Interruptions Realistically
- **Batch your focused tasks** during your most reliable quiet windows
- Accept that interruptions happen and **plan buffer time** in your schedule
- Use the **"two-minute rule"** - handle quick child needs immediately rather than letting them escalate

## Practical Time Strategies
- Prepare the night before (lunches, clothes, work priorities)
- Use **time blocking** rather than endless to-do lists
- Communicate your schedule clearly with your employer

## Protecting Your Energy
- **Don't skip breaks** - they actually improve output
- Set a firm end time to prevent work from bleeding into family time
- Ask for help when possible - neighbors, family, childcare swaps

## Key Mindset Shift
**Progress over perfection.** Some days childcare wins, and that's okay.
