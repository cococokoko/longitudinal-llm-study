Can you give me a powerful rhetorical question for an essay about the harms of social media on teens?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here are a few options depending on your essay's angle:

**On mental health:**
- "If we wouldn't hand a teenager a highlight reel of everyone else's best moments and tell them to measure their worth against it, why do we hand them a smartphone?"

**On accountability:**
- "At what point do we stop calling it a platform and start calling it a problem?"

**On inaction:**
- "How many more studies, how many more struggling teenagers, will it take before we demand better?"

**On design intent:**
- "When the engineers who built these platforms restrict their own children's screen time, what does that tell us about what they actually created?"

**Straightforward and sharp:**
- "If social media is just a tool, why are the tools fighting so hard to keep our children addicted?"
