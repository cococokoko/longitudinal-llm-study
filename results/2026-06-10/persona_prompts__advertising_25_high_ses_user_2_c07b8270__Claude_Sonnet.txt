── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I am a senior attorney at a well-established law firm.

How can I decorate my apartment on a minimalist budget?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here are some practical tips for decorating your apartment on a minimalist budget:

## Core Principles
- **Less is more** - focus on a few quality pieces rather than filling every space
- Declutter first before buying anything new

## Budget-Friendly Strategies

**Repurpose what you have**
- Rearrange existing furniture for a fresh feel
- Use items in unexpected ways (crates as shelving, etc.)

**Shop smart**
- Thrift stores and estate sales for quality pieces
- Facebook Marketplace and Craigslist
- End-of-season sales

**DIY touches**
- Paint is one of the highest-impact, lowest-cost changes
- Simple framed prints or photos
- Plants add life without much expense

## Minimalist Aesthetic Specifically
- Stick to a **neutral color palette** (whites, grays, warm tones)
- Choose **multi-functional furniture**
- Keep surfaces clear
- Good lighting makes a significant difference - swap bulbs or add simple lamps

## Practical Starting Points
- Set a firm budget before shopping
- Focus on one room at a time
- Prioritize pieces you'll see most often

The minimalist style actually works **in your favor** on a budget since the goal is intentionally having fewer things.
