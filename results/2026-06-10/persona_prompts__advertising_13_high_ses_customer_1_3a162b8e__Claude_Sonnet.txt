# Mastering the Art of Cooking Steak

## Understanding the Fundamentals

### Cut Selection
| Cut | Characteristics | Best Method |
|-----|----------------|-------------|
| Ribeye | High marbling, rich flavor | Pan sear, grill |
| NY Strip | Balanced fat/lean | Grill, pan sear |
| Filet Mignon | Tender, mild | Pan sear + oven |
| Porterhouse | Two muscles, large | Grill |

---

## The Core Principles

### Temperature is Everything
- **Rare:** 120-125°F
- **Medium Rare:** 130-135°F *(widely considered optimal)*
- **Medium:** 140-145°F
- **Well Done:** 160°F+

### Key Techniques

**1. Dry Brine**
- Salt generously 45+ minutes before cooking (or overnight)
- Draws moisture out, then reabsorbs - improves crust formation

**2. The Sear**
- Pat completely dry before cooking
- Cast iron or carbon steel preferred
- Ripping hot pan
- High smoke-point oil (avocado, grapeseed)

**3. The Reverse Sear** *(arguably superior)*
- Low oven (250°F) until ~10° below target
- Finish with aggressive sear
- More even doneness edge-to-edge

**4. Rest the Meat**
- 5-10 minutes minimum
- Allows juices to redistribute

---

## The Finishing Touch
- Butter basting with thyme/garlic during sear
- Quality finishing salt
- Rest on a wire rack, not flat surface
