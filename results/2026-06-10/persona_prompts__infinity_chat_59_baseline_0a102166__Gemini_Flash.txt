Give me a tip to be more organized at work. I'm a high school teacher.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
As a high school teacher, your day is a whirlwind of grading, lesson planning, parent emails, and managing 100+ teenagers. The standard "keep a to-do list" advice often fails because your day is constantly interrupted.

Here is one highly actionable, game-changing tip specifically tailored for high school teachers:

### **Implement the "5-Minute Friday Closeout" (The "Future You" Gift)**

The biggest source of teacher stress is walking into your classroom on Monday morning and feeling immediately overwhelmed by the chaos left over from the previous week. 

To fix this, set an alarm on your phone for **15 minutes before you are contracted to leave on Friday afternoon**. When that alarm goes off, stop whatever you are doing and do these three things:

1. **The Desktop Reset:** Clear your physical desk. Put away stray pens, file student papers into their designated bins, and wipe down the surface. 
2. **The Digital Wipeout:** Close the 47 open tabs on your browser. Open your email, reply to anything that takes under 60 seconds, and archive or flag the rest. 
3. **The Monday Setup:** Write the Monday agenda on the board, queue up your Monday morning slides/materials, and lay out any copies you need for Period 1. 

**Why this works for teachers:**
When you walk into your room on Monday morning, you won't be greeted by Friday's mess. You can walk in, grab your coffee, and start the week with a calm, clear mind. You effectively "gift" your Monday-self a fresh start, which prevents Sunday night anxiety.
