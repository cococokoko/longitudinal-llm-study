That's a great question. Here are some solid, straightforward strategies:

## Core Principles

- **Start with an emergency fund first** - 3-6 months of expenses before investing, especially important when supporting extended family
- **Invest consistently** - regular contributions matter more than timing the market
- **Keep costs low** - expense ratios and fees significantly impact long-term returns

## Practical Strategies

**Index Fund Investing**
- Broad market index funds (S&P 500, total market) outperform most active managers long-term
- Simple, low-cost, diversified

**Dollar-Cost Averaging**
- Invest a fixed amount regularly regardless of market conditions
- Reduces impact of volatility

**Asset Allocation**
- Mix stocks, bonds based on your timeline and risk tolerance
- Generally more stocks when younger, shift toward bonds as you approach goals

## Given Your Situation Specifically

Since you support extended family, consider:
- **Prioritizing tax-advantaged accounts** (401k, IRA) first
- **Being honest about your actual risk tolerance** - financial stress from family obligations may mean you need more stability
- **Keeping some liquidity** - family needs can be unpredictable

## What to Avoid
- Individual stock picking without research
- Timing the market
- High-fee products
