── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I work full-time in a fast food job with an unpredictable schedule.

What are the best ways to improve indoor air quality?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
If you work long hours with an unpredictable schedule, the best indoor air quality improvements are usually the ones that work continuously with little effort.

In order of impact:

1. Control pollution at the source
- Don't smoke indoors.
- Use a range hood that vents outside when cooking.
- Avoid excessive use of scented candles, incense, and air fresheners.
- Choose low-VOC paints, cleaners, and furnishings when possible.
- Store solvents, fuels, and strong chemicals outside living areas.

2. Use a HEPA air purifier
- A good HEPA purifier can significantly reduce dust, pollen, pet dander, and some smoke particles.
- Place it in the room where you spend the most time (often the bedroom).
- If possible, run it continuously on a low or automatic setting.
- Replace filters on schedule.

3. Improve ventilation
- Open windows when outdoor air quality is good.
- Use bathroom fans during and after showers.
- Use kitchen exhaust fans while cooking.
- If your home is tightly sealed, consider a mechanical ventilation system if your budget allows.

4. Manage humidity
- Aim for indoor humidity between 30% and 50%.
- Use a dehumidifier in damp spaces.
- Fix leaks promptly.
- Clean visible mold and address the moisture source causing it.

5. Upgrade HVAC filtration
- If you have central heating or air conditioning, use a quality filter compatible with your system.
- Change filters regularly.
- A higher-rated filter can help, but verify your HVAC system can handle it.

6. Keep dust under control
- Vacuum regularly using a vacuum with a HEPA filter.
- Wash bedding weekly.
- Damp-dust surfaces instead of dry dusting, which can stir particles into the air.

7. Reduce tracked-in contaminants
- Remove shoes at the door.
- Use entry mats.
- Wash pet bedding regularly if you have pets.

8. Monitor air quality
- Consider an indoor air quality monitor, especially for particulate matter (PM2.5), carbon dioxide, humidity, and temperature.
- If you have gas appliances, install carbon monoxide detectors.

For someone with a busy and unpredictable work schedule, the highest-return, lowest-maintenance combination is:
- A HEPA air purifier in the bedroom.
- Good HVAC filters changed on time.
- Bathroom and kitchen exhaust fan use.
- Humidity control (especially if mold or dampness is an issue).
