# CUBICLE OF THE DAMNED
### A Play in Three Acts
*In the Style of Scott Adams' Dilbert, Set During the Apocalypse*

---

## CAST OF CHARACTERS

**DILBERT** - An engineer trying to optimize survival in a post-apocalyptic wasteland while still somehow attending status meetings

**DOGBERT** - A small dog with delusions of world domination, now technically correct that the world needs dominating

**THE POINTY-HAIRED BOSS (PHB)** - Somehow survived. Nobody is surprised. Cockroaches and middle management endure.

**ALICE** - Engineer. Fist of Death now legally classified as a "productivity tool"

**WALLY** - Has been preparing for this his entire career by doing absolutely nothing

**ASOK** - The intern. Still an intern. The apocalypse did not come with a promotion budget.

**RATBERT** - A rat. Now considered upper management relative to the remaining population.

---

## ACT ONE: THE MORNING STANDUP

*SCENE: A collapsed office building. Three cubicle walls still stand inexplicably. A whiteboard reads "Q4 SURVIVAL GOALS." Fires burn in the distance. DILBERT sits at a desk made of rubble, wearing his signature tie, which is now also used as a tourniquet on alternating days.*

---

**DILBERT:**
*(narrating, staring blankly at the audience)*
Day four hundred and twelve of the apocalypse. The electromagnetic pulse destroyed all computers. The PHB has called this "an exciting opportunity to return to our core competencies." I have seventeen cousins living in my bunker. My mother's sister's family arrived last Tuesday with a goat. The goat has better project management skills than anyone I work with. I named her Carol. She's already been voluntold to lead the water procurement initiative.

*(PHB enters, wearing a hardhat with a corporate logo on it, carrying a clipboard)*

**PHB:**
Good news, people! I've been on a call with corporate — well, I've been *imagining* a call with corporate since the satellites are down — and they want us to pivot our survival strategy to better align with our pre-apocalypse brand values.

**ALICE:**
*(sharpening a spear, not looking up)*
Our brand values were "innovation" and "synergy."

**PHB:**
Exactly! So I need each of you to synergize your foraging with cross-functional team alignment.

**ALICE:**
I will synergize my spear with your kneecap if you say that word again.

**PHB:**
I'm going to put that in the "parking lot."

*(He gestures to an actual parking lot full of abandoned, burned cars)*

**WALLY:**
*(reclining in a lawn chair, holding a cup of something)*
I've already completed my survival tasks for the quarter.

**DILBERT:**
Wally, the quarter started this morning.

**WALLY:**
I know. I'm very efficient. I've decided that the optimal survival strategy is to conserve all energy. I am, in effect, the most prepared person here. I have been training for a low-output existence my entire professional life. The apocalypse is simply my performance review finally catching up with reality.

**ASOK:**
*(raising hand eagerly, covered in soot)*
I have prepared a forty-seven slide deck on water purification strategies, cross-referenced with our remaining supply chain assets, projected forward eighteen months with three scenario models!

*Everyone stares at him.*

**ASOK:**
I also found a granola bar.

**DILBERT:**
Give us the granola bar.

**ASOK:**
But the deck—

**ALICE:**
GRANOLA BAR, ASOK.

*(ASOK hands it over. ALICE breaks it into pieces with her bare hands, which is somehow more violent than necessary)*
