### 1. Best for Ultra-Durable Workwear & Everyday Basics
If you need clothing that is sustainable but also tough enough to handle a lot of physical activity, bending, and long shifts, these brands are excellent:

*   **Patagonia:** A pioneer in sustainability. They use organic cotton, recycled polyester, and Fair Trade Certified sewing. Their clothing is famous for its durability, and they have a "Worn Wear" program where they will repair your clothes for free or cheap to keep them out of landfills. Their hemp-based workwear line is incredibly tough.
*   **Pact:** If you need comfortable, breathable basics (like t-shirts, socks, hoodies, and underwear) to wear under work gear or for daily life, Pact is the go-to. Everything is made with GOTS-certified organic cotton in Fair Trade factories. It’s soft, breathable, and holds up well to frequent washing.
*   **Toad&Co:** They make durable, utilitarian clothing using organic cotton, hemp, and recycled fibers. Their clothes are designed for movement and active lifestyles, making them great for long days where you need to stretch and bend.

### 2. Best for High-Quality, Rugged Denim & Casual Wear
*   **Nudie Jeans:** If you wear jeans for work or daily life and need them to last, Nudie Jeans is incredible. They use 100% organic cotton and offer **free repairs for life** at their repair shops (or they will send you a free repair kit). 
*   **Outerknown:** Founded by pro-surfer Kelly Slater, this brand focuses heavily on coastal style, but their "Blanket Shirts" and utility pants are incredibly rugged, made from organic cotton, and built to last for years.

### 3. Best for Activewear & High-Movement Clothing
If you need moisture-wicking fabrics, stretch, and comfort for long, active days:

*   **Girlfriend Collective:** They make incredible activewear (leggings, shorts, tees) out of recycled materials like water bottles and fishing nets. Their gear is highly durable, supportive, and designed for heavy movement.
*   **Prana:** Known for outdoor and yoga gear, Prana uses organic cotton, hemp, and recycled polyester. Their stretch Zion fabric is legendary for being virtually indestructible while allowing for a full range of motion.

### What to Look For (How to Spot the Real Deal)
When shopping online or in stores, look for these certifications to ensure the brand isn't just "greenwashing" (pretending to be sustainable):
*   **GOTS (Global Organic Textile Standard):** The gold standard for organic fibers.
*   **Fair Trade Certified:** Ensures the workers who made the clothes were paid fair wages and worked in safe conditions.
*   **OEKO-TEX Standard 100:** Guarantees the finished fabric is free from harmful levels of toxic chemicals.
