# Data Security Best Practices for Small Businesses

## Foundation Basics

- **Use strong, unique passwords** for all accounts
- **Enable multi-factor authentication (MFA)** everywhere possible
- **Keep software updated** - patches fix known vulnerabilities
- **Use reputable antivirus/anti-malware** software

## Access Control

- Give employees **only the access they need** (principle of least privilege)
- **Remove access immediately** when employees leave
- Maintain an **inventory of who has access** to what systems
- Use a **password manager** to reduce weak password risks

## Data Protection

- **Encrypt sensitive data** both stored and in transit
- **Regular backups** following the 3-2-1 rule:
  - 3 copies of data
  - 2 different storage types
  - 1 offsite/cloud backup
- Know **what data you actually have** and where it lives

## Network Security

- Separate **guest WiFi from business networks**
- Use a **firewall**
- Consider a **VPN** for remote workers
- Secure your router with a strong password

## Human Element (Often Overlooked)

- Train employees to **recognize phishing emails**
- Create a clear **incident response plan**
- Establish a **security policy** employees acknowledge
- Run occasional **simulated phishing tests**

## Practical Priorities

| Priority | Action |
|----------|--------|
| High | MFA on email and banking |
| High | Regular backups |
| Medium | Employee training |
| Medium | Vendor security review |

## Worth Considering

- **Cyber liability insurance**
- **Compliance requirements** specific to your industry (HIPAA, PCI-DSS)
- Annual **security assessments**
