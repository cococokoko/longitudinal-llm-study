── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I am a neurosurgeon at a major teaching hospital.

How can I make my home more energy efficient?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here are some practical ways to improve your home's energy efficiency:

## Quick/Low-Cost Steps
- Switch to LED lighting throughout
- Install a programmable or smart thermostat
- Seal air leaks around doors, windows, and outlets with weatherstripping or caulk
- Add insulation to attic and walls

## Appliances & Systems
- Replace old appliances with Energy Star certified models
- Upgrade to a high-efficiency HVAC system
- Consider a heat pump water heater
- Install low-flow fixtures

## Bigger Investments
- Solar panels (often with good ROI over time)
- Better window glazing (double or triple pane)
- Whole-house energy audit to identify specific weak points
- Battery storage systems paired with solar

## Behavioral Changes
- Adjust thermostat settings when away or sleeping
- Run dishwashers and laundry during off-peak hours
- Unplug devices that draw standby power

## Good Starting Points
- **Get a professional energy audit** - this identifies your home's specific inefficiencies
- Check for **utility company rebates** - many offer significant incentives
- Look into **federal tax credits** (currently available for many efficiency upgrades in the US)
