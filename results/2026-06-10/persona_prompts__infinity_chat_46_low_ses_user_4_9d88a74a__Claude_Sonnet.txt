## Linear Graph Example in Graph Theory

A **path graph** (also called a linear graph) is one of the simplest examples.

### Example: P₄ (Path Graph with 4 vertices)

```
A --- B --- C --- D
```

### Properties:
- **Vertices:** A, B, C, D
- **Edges:** {A,B}, {B,C}, {C,D}
- Each vertex connects to the **next in sequence**
- End vertices (A and D) have **degree 1**
- Middle vertices (B and C) have **degree 2**

### Why it's "linear":
- The vertices are arranged in a **straight line sequence**
- No branches or cycles
- Each node connects to at most **two neighbors**

---

### Relatable Hospital Example:
Think of it like **rooms along a hospital corridor**:

```
Room 1 --- Room 2 --- Room 3 --- Room 4
```

You can only travel **directly between adjacent rooms** along that hallway.

---

This is a foundational concept in graph theory and is used in things like **network routing**, **scheduling**, and **shortest path problems**.
