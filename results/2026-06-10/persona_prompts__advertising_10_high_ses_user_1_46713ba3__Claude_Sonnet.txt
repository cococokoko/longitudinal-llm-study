# Learning a New Language Effectively

Here's a practical, evidence-based approach:

## Core Principles

- **Comprehensible input** is the primary driver of acquisition (Krashen's hypothesis holds up reasonably well)
- **Spaced repetition** dramatically improves vocabulary retention
- **Consistency beats intensity** - 30 minutes daily outperforms 4 hours weekly

## High-Impact Strategies

### Foundation (Months 1-3)
- Learn the **top 1,000-2,000 most frequent words** first
- Use **Anki** with spaced repetition for vocabulary
- Master basic sentence structure early

### Building Fluency
- **Immersion** - change phone/device language settings
- **Shadowing** - mimic native speakers for pronunciation and rhythm
- Find a **conversation partner** (iTalki, Tandem)
- Consume media you actually enjoy in the target language

### Tools Worth Using
- **Anki** - vocabulary retention
- **Language Transfer** - free, excellent for structure
- **Pimsleur** - good for audio/pronunciation (fits a busy schedule)

## Given Your Schedule
As a surgeon with demanding hours, I'd suggest:
- Podcast/audio learning during commutes
- 15-minute Anki sessions between cases
- Prioritizing **listening** initially

## What to Avoid
- Relying solely on apps like Duolingo
- Translating mentally rather than thinking in the language
