── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I am a senior attorney at a well-established law firm.

What are the best ways to save for retirement?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
For a senior attorney, the "best" retirement strategy usually depends less on basic retirement principles and more on tax efficiency, income level, partnership status, and estate-planning goals. In general, the most effective approach is to maximize available tax-advantaged vehicles before investing heavily in taxable accounts.

Common priorities include:

- Maximize employer-sponsored retirement plans
  - Contribute the maximum allowed to a 401(k), 403(b), or similar plan.
  - If your firm offers matching contributions, ensure you receive the full match.
  - If you are age 50 or older, take advantage of any available catch-up contributions.

- Evaluate a cash balance or defined benefit plan
  - High-income professionals often benefit significantly from cash balance plans.
  - These plans can permit substantially larger tax-deferred contributions than a 401(k) alone, depending on age and income.
  - Many law firms use these plans to accelerate retirement savings and reduce current taxable income.

- Consider Roth strategies
  - If your income exceeds direct Roth IRA limits, investigate whether a backdoor Roth IRA is appropriate.
  - If your firm's plan permits it, a "mega backdoor Roth" strategy may allow additional Roth savings beyond standard contribution limits.

- Build a taxable investment portfolio
  - After maximizing tax-advantaged accounts, invest excess savings in a diversified taxable brokerage account.
  - Tax-efficient index funds and ETFs are commonly used because they can reduce ongoing tax drag.
  - A taxable portfolio also provides flexibility before retirement age and can support tax diversification.

- Use Health Savings Accounts (HSAs), if eligible
  - If covered by a qualifying high-deductible health plan, an HSA offers valuable tax advantages:
    - Tax-deductible contributions
    - Tax-deferred growth
    - Tax-free withdrawals for qualified medical expenses
  - Many planners view HSAs as an additional retirement savings vehicle.

- Review asset location
  - Different assets may be more tax-efficient in different account types.
  - For example, tax-inefficient fixed-income investments are often placed in tax-deferred accounts, while tax-efficient equity funds may be held in taxable accounts.

- Consider deferred compensation plans
  - Some firms offer nonqualified deferred compensation arrangements.
  - These can provide additional tax deferral but involve employer-credit risk and require careful review.

- Coordinate with estate planning goals
  - For high-net-worth attorneys, retirement planning is often intertwined with:
    - Trust planning
    - Charitable giving strategies
    - Succession planning
    - Lifetime gifting strategies

- Maintain appropriate insurance and risk management
  - Disability insurance is particularly important for professionals whose earnings depend on continued practice.
  - Liability protection and asset-protection planning may also be relevant depending on your jurisdiction and practice area.

If you are a partner in a law firm, an equity partner, or a solo practitioner, the optimal retirement structure can differ significantly.
