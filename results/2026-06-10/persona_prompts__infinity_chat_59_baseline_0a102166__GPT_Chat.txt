── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Give me a tip to be more organized at work. I'm a high school teacher.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
A practical tip for high school teachers: create a single "daily dashboard" that you check at the beginning and end of each day.

Keep it to one page (digital or paper) with:
- Today's classes
- Top 3 priorities for the day
- Assignments to collect/grade
- Students who need follow-up
- Meetings, parent contacts, or deadlines

At the end of the day, spend 5 minutes updating it for tomorrow. This reduces the mental effort of remembering dozens of small tasks and gives you one trusted place to see what needs attention.

Many teachers find that organization problems come less from having too much to do and more from having information scattered across emails, sticky notes, lesson plans, and gradebooks. A single dashboard helps bring those pieces together.
