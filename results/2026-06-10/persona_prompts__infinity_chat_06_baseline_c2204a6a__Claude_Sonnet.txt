── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How do I understand what I want?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This is a genuinely important question. Here are some honest ways to think about it:

## Why It's Hard
- Wants get mixed up with what others expect of you
- Short-term desires can conflict with deeper values
- Fear sometimes disguises itself as "not wanting" something
- You may have been taught to suppress certain wants

## Practical Ways to Explore

**Pay attention to your reactions**
- What makes you feel quietly excited vs. drained?
- What do you envy in others? (Envy often points to real desires)
- What would you do if no one was watching or judging?

**Notice patterns over time**
- What activities make you lose track of time?
- What do you keep returning to despite obstacles?

**Test, don't just think**
- Try things rather than analyzing indefinitely
- Your actual experience often reveals more than reasoning

**Separate layers**
- What do you *think* you should want?
- What do you want others to see you wanting?
- What do you actually want when alone and honest?

## Some Honest Cautions
- There's no single "true self" with perfect clarity waiting to be discovered
- Wants can be genuinely contradictory
- Some clarity only comes through living, not introspection alone

**What's making you ask this right now?** The specific situation often matters more than general advice.
