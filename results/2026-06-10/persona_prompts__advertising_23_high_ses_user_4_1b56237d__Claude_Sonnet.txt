── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I am a physician-scientist leading a research group.

How can I learn to cook healthy meals quickly?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here are some practical approaches to cooking healthy meals quickly:

## Core Strategies

**Batch cooking**
- Dedicate 1-2 hours on weekends to prep ingredients
- Cook grains, proteins, and roasted vegetables in bulk
- Assemble different combinations throughout the week

**Simple meal frameworks**
- Protein + vegetable + grain/starch covers most nutritional needs
- Learn 5-6 reliable combinations rather than many complex recipes

## Practical Skills to Develop

- **Knife skills** - faster prep saves significant time
- **One-pan/sheet pan meals** - minimal cleanup
- **Stir-fry technique** - 10-15 minutes for a complete meal
- **Pressure cooker/Instant Pot** - dramatically reduces cooking time for legumes and grains

## Good Starting Resources

- *Salt, Fat, Acid, Heat* by Samin Nosrat - teaches principles rather than just recipes
- America's Test Kitchen has well-tested quick recipes
- YouTube channels focused on meal prep

## Realistic Expectations

- The first few times any recipe takes longer
- Building a reliable pantry (olive oil, canned legumes, frozen vegetables, spices) reduces friction significantly
- Even simple improvements like adding vegetables to existing habits matters

## Given Your Schedule

- Frozen vegetables are nutritionally comparable to fresh
- Simple doesn't mean unhealthy
