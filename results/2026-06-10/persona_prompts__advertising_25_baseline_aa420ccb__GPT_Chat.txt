── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How can I decorate my apartment on a minimalist budget?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Decorating on a minimalist budget works best when you focus on a few high-impact changes instead of buying lots of items. The goal is to make the space feel intentional, not full.

Here are some cost-effective ideas:

- Start with decluttering
  - Remove anything you don't use or love.
  - Organize visible surfaces so the room feels cleaner and larger.
  - A tidy space often looks more expensive without spending money.

- Use textiles for instant impact
  - Add a throw blanket to a sofa or bed.
  - Replace or add a few cushion covers in complementary colors.
  - Consider an affordable area rug if the floor feels bare.

- Incorporate plants
  - Even one or two plants can make a room feel more alive.
  - Look for low-maintenance options like pothos, snake plants, or ZZ plants.
  - If real plants aren't practical, high-quality faux plants can work.

- Rearrange what you already own
  - Move furniture to improve flow and create focal points.
  - Group decorative objects together rather than scattering them around.
  - Repurpose items from one room in another.

- Add affordable wall art
  - Print public-domain artwork or your own photos.
  - Use inexpensive frames from discount stores or thrift shops.
  - Create a gallery wall with a consistent frame color.

- Focus on lighting
  - Warm-toned LED bulbs make spaces feel cozier.
  - A floor lamp or table lamp often creates a better atmosphere than overhead lighting alone.
  - Look for secondhand lighting fixtures.

- Shop secondhand
  - Thrift stores, flea markets, online marketplaces, and estate sales often have unique pieces.
  - A single vintage side table or mirror can add character without much cost.

- Use mirrors strategically
  - Mirrors reflect light and can make small apartments feel larger.
  - A large mirror often has more impact than several small decorative items.

- Stick to a simple color palette
  - Choose 2–3 main colors and repeat them throughout the apartment.
  - Consistency helps a space feel cohesive and intentional.

- Upgrade small details
  - Swap out cabinet knobs, drawer pulls, or lampshades.
  - These changes are often inexpensive but noticeable.

If you're aiming for a minimalist look specifically:
- Keep decorations sparse.
- Choose a few larger pieces rather than many small ones.
- Prioritize functionality.
- Leave some empty space on shelves and surfaces.

If you tell me your budget (for example, $50, $100, or $300) and whether you're decorating a studio, one-bedroom, or larger apartment, I can suggest a more specific plan.
