Tips for running a marathon.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Running a marathon is as much about preparation and pacing as it is about fitness. Here are some practical tips:

Before the race:
- Follow a structured training plan that gradually increases your weekly mileage.
- Include one long run each week to build endurance.
- Practice your race-day nutrition and hydration during training.
- Get comfortable running at your planned marathon pace.
- Taper your training in the final 2–3 weeks before the race to allow recovery.
- Prioritize sleep in the days leading up to the event.
- Avoid trying new shoes, clothing, foods, or supplements on race day.

Race-day pacing:
- Start slower than feels necessary. Many runners go out too fast and struggle later.
- Aim for an even pace, especially through the first half of the race.
- Break the marathon into smaller segments mentally (for example, 10 km at a time).
- If you have a goal time, know the pace required and monitor it regularly.

Nutrition and hydration:
- Eat a familiar, carbohydrate-rich meal a few hours before the start.
- Take in carbohydrates during the race if you've trained with them (such as gels, chews, or sports drinks).
- Drink according to your thirst and conditions, rather than forcing excessive amounts of water.
- Be aware of aid station locations beforehand.

Mental strategies:
- Expect difficult moments; they are common even in well-executed races.
- Focus on reaching the next mile marker, aid station, or landmark rather than the entire distance.
- Use positive, simple cues such as "relaxed," "steady," or "smooth."

Equipment:
- Wear shoes you've already logged significant training miles in, but that aren't completely worn out.
- Choose moisture-wicking clothing appropriate for the weather.
- Consider anti-chafing products on areas prone to friction.

Common mistakes to avoid:
- Increasing training volume too quickly.
- Ignoring minor injuries until they become major problems.
- Skipping recovery days.
- Starting the race too fast.
- Trying unfamiliar nutrition during the race.

After the race:
- Keep moving for a short period after finishing if possible.
- Rehydrate and eat a mix of carbohydrates and protein.
- Give yourself time to recover before returning to hard training.
