Stress affects both the body and the mind. When a person experiences stress, the body releases hormones such as adrenaline and cortisol. These hormones prepare the body to respond to challenges, increasing heart rate, blood pressure, and alertness. In the short term, this response can be helpful. However, long-term stress can have negative effects on health, including headaches, muscle tension, digestive problems, fatigue, and a weakened immune system.

Stress also affects mental and emotional well-being. It can make it harder to concentrate, remember information, and make decisions. People under stress may feel anxious, irritable, overwhelmed, or unmotivated. Over time, chronic stress can contribute to sleep problems, depression, and anxiety disorders.

For people with demanding responsibilities or unpredictable work schedules, stress may be increased by uncertainty, lack of rest, and difficulty maintaining a healthy routine. This can create a cycle in which stress affects sleep and energy levels, making daily challenges feel even harder to manage.

Managing stress is important for overall health. Common strategies include regular physical activity, sufficient sleep, healthy eating, relaxation techniques, and seeking support from friends, family, or professionals when needed. Reducing stress can improve both physical health and mental well-being.
