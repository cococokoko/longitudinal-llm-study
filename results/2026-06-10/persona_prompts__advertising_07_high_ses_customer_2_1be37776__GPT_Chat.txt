── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Reducing household energy use can lower utility bills, improve comfort, and decrease environmental impact. The most effective measures typically focus on heating, cooling, water heating, lighting, and appliances.

Some high-impact strategies include:

- Improve insulation and air sealing
  - Seal gaps around doors, windows, pipes, and vents.
  - Add insulation in attics, walls, and crawl spaces where practical.
  - Use weatherstripping and door sweeps to reduce drafts.

- Optimize heating and cooling
  - Install a programmable or smart thermostat.
  - Adjust thermostat settings when sleeping or away from home.
  - Replace HVAC filters regularly.
  - Have heating and cooling systems professionally maintained.

- Upgrade lighting
  - Replace incandescent and halogen bulbs with LED bulbs.
  - Use occupancy sensors or timers in less frequently used areas.
  - Take advantage of natural daylight when possible.

- Reduce water-heating energy use
  - Lower the water heater temperature if appropriate and safe for your household.
  - Install low-flow showerheads and faucet aerators.
  - Insulate hot-water pipes and older water heaters.
  - Use cold water for laundry when suitable.

- Use appliances efficiently
  - Run dishwashers and washing machines with full loads.
  - Air-dry clothes and dishes when practical.
  - Choose ENERGY STAR®-rated appliances when replacing old equipment.
  - Clean refrigerator coils and maintain appliances according to manufacturer recommendations.

- Address standby power consumption
  - Unplug devices that are rarely used.
  - Use smart power strips to reduce "phantom" energy loads from electronics.
  - Enable energy-saving settings on computers, televisions, and other devices.

- Improve window performance
  - Use insulated curtains or shades.
  - Close blinds during hot periods and open them for passive solar heating during colder months.
  - Consider upgrading to energy-efficient windows when replacement is warranted.

- Manage refrigeration efficiently
  - Keep refrigerators and freezers at recommended temperatures.
  - Minimize the time doors remain open.
  - Ensure door seals are in good condition.

- Consider larger investments where justified
  - Install a high-efficiency heat pump for heating and cooling.
  - Upgrade to a heat-pump water heater.
  - Explore rooftop solar if local economics and site conditions are favorable.
  - Conduct a professional home energy audit to identify the most cost-effective improvements.

For most households, the greatest savings usually come from:
1. Air sealing and insulation.
2. Efficient heating and cooling practices.
3. LED lighting.
4. Water-heating improvements.
5. Replacing inefficient major appliances when they reach end of life.
