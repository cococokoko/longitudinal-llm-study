── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
When advising international students on their academic journeys, choosing between the TOEFL and the IELTS is a pivotal decision that depends heavily on a student’s personal communication style, testing preferences, and ultimate destination. The **TOEFL (Test of English as a Foreign Language)** is primarily geared toward American English and is highly favored by U.S. institutions; it is a fully computer-based exam where students interact solely with a screen, even during the speaking section, making it ideal for those who excel at typing and prefer a standardized, tech-driven environment. Conversely, the **IELTS (International English Language Testing System)** leans toward British English and is widely preferred in the UK, Canada, Australia, and New Zealand. It offers both paper-based and computer-based formats, and its defining feature is a live, face-to-face speaking interview with a human examiner, which often suits students who find natural conversation more comfortable than speaking into a microphone. Ultimately, while both exams are globally respected and measure the same core language skills, students should select the test that aligns with their target country’s preferences and their own comfort level with digital versus interpersonal testing formats.
