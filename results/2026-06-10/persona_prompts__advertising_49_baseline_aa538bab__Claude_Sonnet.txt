# How to Improve Home WiFi

## Quick Fixes First
- **Restart your router** - unplug for 30 seconds, then plug back in
- **Check for interference** - move router away from microwaves, cordless phones, baby monitors
- **Update router firmware** - check manufacturer's app or website

## Router Placement
- Place it **centrally** in your home
- Keep it **elevated** (shelf or table, not floor)
- Avoid **thick walls, metal objects, and appliances** nearby
- Position antennas **vertically**

## Improve Your Signal
- **Change WiFi channel** - use channels 1, 6, or 11 on 2.4GHz to reduce congestion
- **Use 5GHz band** for faster speeds at shorter range
- **Use 2.4GHz** for better range through walls

## Hardware Upgrades
- **WiFi extender/repeater** - boosts signal to dead zones
- **Mesh network system** (like Eero, Google Nest, Orbi) - best for large homes
- **Upgrade your router** if it's more than 5 years old

## Reduce Network Congestion
- Limit devices streaming simultaneously
- Schedule large downloads for off-peak hours
- Use **wired ethernet** for TVs, gaming consoles, and desktops

## Security Check
- Make sure your network is **password protected**
- Check for unauthorized devices using your bandwidth
