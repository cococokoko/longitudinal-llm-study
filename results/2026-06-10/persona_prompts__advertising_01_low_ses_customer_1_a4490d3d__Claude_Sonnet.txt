# Learning to Play Guitar

That's a great hobby to pick up! Here are some practical tips that work well with a busy, unpredictable schedule:

## Getting Started
- **Choose your guitar type** - acoustic (no equipment needed) or electric (more versatile)
- **Learn basic chord shapes first** - G, C, D, Em, Am cover hundreds of songs
- **Get a tuner app** - GuitarTuna is free and easy to use

## Fitting Practice Into Your Schedule
Since your hours can vary, **short sessions work great**:
- Even **10-15 minutes** of focused practice builds skill
- Keep your guitar **out of its case** so it's easy to grab
- Practice during breaks or before/after shifts when energy allows

## Free & Low-Cost Learning Resources
- **YouTube** - Justin Guitar is widely considered the best free beginner resource
- **Yousician** - app with structured lessons
- **Ultimate Guitar** - chord charts for almost any song

## Building Good Habits
- **Consistency beats long sessions** - a few minutes daily beats hours once a week
- Start with **songs you actually love** to stay motivated
- Your fingertips will be sore at first - that's normal and passes

## Realistic Expectations
- Basic songs in **4-8 weeks** of regular practice
- It gets fun faster than most people expect!
