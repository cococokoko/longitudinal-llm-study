What are the best resources for starting a plant-based diet?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
If you're starting a plant-based diet, it helps to combine a few reliable resources: nutrition guidance, practical recipes, and meal-planning tools.

Some of the best places to start:

- Nutrition information
  - Physicians Committee for Responsible Medicine (PCRM) — evidence-based guides, meal plans, and nutrition articles.
  - NutritionFacts.org — research summaries on plant-based nutrition.
  - The Vegan Society — beginner guides covering nutrients, shopping, and meal planning.

- Beginner-friendly recipe sites
  - Forks Over Knives — whole-food plant-based recipes and meal ideas.
  - Minimalist Baker — many simple recipes with relatively few ingredients.
  - Rainbow Plant Life — flavorful recipes with detailed cooking tips.
  - Nora Cooks — accessible vegan versions of familiar foods.

- Books
  - "How Not to Die" by Michael Greger
  - "Plant-Based on a Budget" by Toni Okamoto
  - "The Forks Over Knives Plan" by Alona Pulde and Matthew Lederman

- Meal-planning and tracking
  - Cronometer — useful for checking whether you're getting enough protein, iron, calcium, vitamin B12, and other nutrients.
  - Mealime — helps generate simple weekly meal plans and shopping lists.

- Key nutrients to learn about early
  - Protein
  - Vitamin B12 (typically requires fortified foods or a supplement)
  - Iron
  - Calcium
  - Omega-3 fats
  - Vitamin D (depending on sun exposure and location)

A simple beginner approach is:
- Build meals around beans, lentils, tofu, tempeh, or edamame.
- Add whole grains such as oats, rice, quinoa, or whole-wheat pasta.
- Include fruits and vegetables daily.
- Use nuts and seeds for healthy fats.
- Make sure you have a reliable source of vitamin B12.

If you're balancing a busy schedule while settling into a new job, focusing on a handful of easy meals—such as oatmeal, bean chili, lentil curry, tofu stir-fry, and grain bowls—can make the transition much easier than trying to overhaul everything at once.
