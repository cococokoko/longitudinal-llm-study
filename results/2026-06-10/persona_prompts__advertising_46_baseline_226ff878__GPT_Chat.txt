── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
What are the best resources for starting a plant-based diet?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
If you're starting a plant-based diet, the most helpful resources are those that combine practical meal ideas, nutrition guidance, and evidence-based information. Here are some of the best options:

Books
- "How Not to Die" by Dr. Michael Greger and Gene Stone
  - Focuses on the health benefits of whole-food, plant-based eating.
- "The Plant-Based Diet for Beginners" by Gabriel Miller
  - Simple introduction with meal plans and recipes.
- "Fiber Fueled" by Dr. Will Bulsiewicz
  - Explains gut health and how to gradually increase plant foods.
- "Plant-Based on a Budget" by Toni Okamoto
  - Practical and affordable approach to plant-based eating.

Websites
- NutritionFacts.org
  - Free, evidence-based nutrition information from Dr. Michael Greger.
- Forks Over Knives (forksoverknives.com)
  - Recipes, meal plans, cooking tips, and beginner guides.
- Physicians Committee for Responsible Medicine (pcrm.org)
  - Nutrition resources, recipes, and educational materials.
- EatingWell and BBC Good Food
  - Large collections of vegetarian and vegan recipes.

Registered Dietitian Resources
- The Vegan Society (vegansociety.com)
  - Excellent guidance on nutrients such as vitamin B12, iron, calcium, iodine, and omega-3s.
- Academy of Nutrition and Dietetics (eatright.org)
  - Articles written by credentialed nutrition professionals.

Apps
- Cronometer
  - Helps track nutrients and identify potential gaps.
- Mealime
  - Meal planning and grocery list generation.
- Forks Meal Planner
  - Plant-based meal planning service.

YouTube Channels
- Pick Up Limes
  - Registered dietitian with beginner-friendly recipes and nutrition advice.
- Rainbow Plant Life
  - Flavorful, practical plant-based cooking.
- Yeung Man Cooking
  - Easy plant-based meals with clear instructions.

Key Nutrition Topics to Learn Early
- Vitamin B12 supplementation (essential for vegans)
- Protein sources (beans, lentils, tofu, tempeh, edamame, soy milk, seitan)
- Iron-rich foods and pairing them with vitamin C
- Calcium sources (fortified plant milks, tofu, leafy greens)
- Omega-3 fats (flax, chia, hemp, walnuts, algae-based supplements)

Simple Starter Strategy
- Replace one meal a day with a plant-based option.
- Build meals around:
  - Beans or lentils
  - Whole grains (rice, oats, quinoa, whole-wheat pasta)
  - Vegetables
  - Fruit
  - Nuts and seeds
- Keep a few easy staples on hand:
  - Canned beans
  - Frozen vegetables
  - Oats
  - Tofu
  - Plant milk
  - Nut butter
