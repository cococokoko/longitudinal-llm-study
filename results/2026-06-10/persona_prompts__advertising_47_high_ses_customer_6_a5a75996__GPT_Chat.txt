Tips for running a marathon.

If you're preparing to run a marathon, success comes from a combination of training, fueling, pacing, recovery, and race-day execution. Here are some practical tips:

Training
- Build mileage gradually. A common guideline is to avoid increasing weekly mileage by more than about 10% at a time.
- Prioritize consistency over occasional heroic workouts. Regular training is more valuable than a few extremely hard sessions.
- Include a weekly long run to build endurance. Many marathon plans peak with long runs of 18–22 miles (29–35 km), depending on experience.
- Incorporate easy runs. Most training should be at a comfortable conversational pace.
- Add some speed or tempo work if appropriate for your experience level.
- Schedule recovery weeks every few weeks to reduce fatigue and lower injury risk.

Pacing
- Practice your target marathon pace during training.
- Start conservatively on race day. Many runners lose time by going out too fast.
- Use a watch, pace group, or planned splits to stay disciplined.
- Expect the race to feel relatively easy early on; that's often a sign you're pacing appropriately.

Nutrition and Hydration
- Practice race-day fueling during long runs. Never try a new nutrition strategy on race day.
- Consume carbohydrates during long runs and the marathon itself, often around 30–60 grams per hour, though individual needs vary.
- Stay hydrated, but avoid excessive fluid intake.
- In the days before the race, focus on adequate carbohydrate intake and hydration.

Recovery and Injury Prevention
- Get sufficient sleep. Recovery is where much of the adaptation occurs.
- Include strength training, especially for the core, glutes, and legs.
- Address minor aches early before they become significant injuries.
- Take rest days seriously.

Gear
- Train in the shoes you plan to race in.
- Avoid wearing brand-new shoes, clothing, or accessories on race day.
- Test socks, anti-chafing products, and nutrition well before the event.

Race-Week Preparation
- Taper appropriately by reducing training volume while maintaining some activity.
- Review logistics: transportation, start time, weather, gear check, and fueling plan.
- Lay out everything you'll need the night before.

Mental Strategy
- Break the race into segments rather than focusing on the full distance.
- Prepare for difficult moments and decide in advance how you'll respond.
- Stay focused on your pacing and execution rather than comparing yourself to other runners.
