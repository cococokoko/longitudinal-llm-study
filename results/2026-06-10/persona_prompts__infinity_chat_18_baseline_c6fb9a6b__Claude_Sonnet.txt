# Review: *The Last Frequency* (2024)

★★★★½

Director Mara Okonkwo delivers what may be the most quietly devastating science fiction film in years. *The Last Frequency* follows a radio astronomer who discovers that a signal she's been tracking for a decade is actually a recording — of her own future voice, saying goodbye.

What could have been a gimmicky thriller instead becomes a meditation on grief, choice, and whether knowing the end of your story changes how you live it. Lead actress (in a career-defining performance) carries nearly every scene with a restraint that makes the film's final twenty minutes absolutely unbearable in the best possible way.

The cinematography leans heavily on vast, empty landscapes contrasted with cramped control rooms, and the sound design deserves its own award conversation entirely.

The film stumbles slightly in the second act, where a subplot involving a government agency feels borrowed from better conspiracy thrillers and briefly loses the intimate tone that makes everything else work so well.

But *The Last Frequency* recovers beautifully, sticking a landing that earns every tear it demands.

A rare film that trusts its audience to sit with an unanswered question and find it more satisfying than any explanation could be.

*Opens in limited release Friday.*
