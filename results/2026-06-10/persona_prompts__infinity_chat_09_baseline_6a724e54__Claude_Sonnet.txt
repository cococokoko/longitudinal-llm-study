── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Is there life on other planets?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
That's one of the biggest open questions in science. Here's an honest summary:

## What We Know
- Life exists on Earth, proving it's possible
- The universe has billions of galaxies, each with billions of stars and planets
- Many planets exist in "habitable zones" with conditions potentially suitable for life
- Basic organic chemistry appears widespread in space

## What We've Found So Far
- **No confirmed evidence** of life beyond Earth
- Mars shows signs of ancient water but no confirmed life
- Some moons (Europa, Enceladus) have subsurface oceans that could potentially support microbial life

## Honest Assessment
- **Microbial life elsewhere** - many scientists consider it plausible or even likely given the scale of the universe
- **Intelligent life** - much more uncertain
- The **Fermi Paradox** raises the question: if life is common, why haven't we detected signals?

## What Scientists Are Doing
- SETI searches for radio signals
- James Webb Telescope analyzing planetary atmospheres
- Mars missions searching for biosignatures

## Bottom Line
We genuinely don't know yet. The question is scientifically serious and actively researched. Many scientists think the probability is reasonable, but **we currently have no confirmed evidence**.
