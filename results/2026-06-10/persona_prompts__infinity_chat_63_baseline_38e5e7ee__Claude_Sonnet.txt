# Partial Derivatives: A Numerical Example

## The Function

Consider the function:

$$f(x, y) = 3x^2y + 2xy^3 + 5$$

---

## Finding the Partial Derivatives

### Partial Derivative with Respect to x
*(treat y as a constant)*

$$\frac{\partial f}{\partial x} = 6xy + 2y^3$$

### Partial Derivative with Respect to y
*(treat x as a constant)*

$$\frac{\partial f}{\partial y} = 3x^2 + 6xy^2$$

---

## Numerical Evaluation at Point (2, 3)

**First, find the function value:**
$$f(2, 3) = 3(4)(3) + 2(2)(27) + 5 = 36 + 108 + 5 = 149$$

**Evaluate ∂f/∂x at (2, 3):**
$$\frac{\partial f}{\partial x}\Bigg|_{(2,3)} = 6(2)(3) + 2(3)^3 = 36 + 54 = \boxed{90}$$

> This means: if x increases by 1 unit (while **y is held fixed at 3**), f increases by approximately **90 units**.

**Evaluate ∂f/∂y at (2, 3):**
$$\frac{\partial f}{\partial y}\Bigg|_{(2,3)} = 3(2)^2 + 6(2)(3)^2 = 12 + 108 = \boxed{120}$$

> This means: if y increases by 1 unit (while **x is held fixed at 2**), f increases by approximately **120 units**.

---

## Numerical Verification

To verify ∂f/∂x ≈ 90, increase x by a small amount (Δx = 0.001):

| Point | Value |
|-------|-------|
| f(2.001, 3) | 3(2.001)²(3) + 2(2.001)(27) + 5 = **149.090...** |
| f(2, 3) | 149 |
| **Difference/0.001** | ≈ **90** ✓ |

---

## Key Insight

| Derivative | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|
| ∂f/∂x = 90 | Rate of change in the **x-direction** only |
| ∂f/∂y = 120 | Rate of change in the **y-direction** only |

The partial derivative isolates the effect of **one variable at a time** by freezing all others.
