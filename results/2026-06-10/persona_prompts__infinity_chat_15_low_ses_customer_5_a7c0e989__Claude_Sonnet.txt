## Key Ways Economic Inequalities Shape International Relations

**Power Dynamics**
- Wealthier nations have greater leverage in trade negotiations, international institutions, and diplomatic relationships
- Economic dependence can create political vulnerability for poorer nations
- Financial institutions like the IMF and World Bank reflect existing power distributions

**Structural Tensions**
- Debates over fair trade vs. free trade often reflect different national economic positions
- Technology and intellectual property disputes partly reflect development gaps
- Climate negotiations are complicated by disagreements about who bears costs

**Migration and Labor**
- Economic disparities drive migration patterns that create political friction
- Remittances represent significant wealth transfers affecting both sending and receiving countries

**Areas of Genuine Debate**
- Whether foreign aid helps or creates dependency
- Whether globalization reduces or increases inequality between nations
- How much sovereignty poorer nations actually exercise in international agreements

**Competing Perspectives**
- Liberal institutionalists emphasize cooperation potential despite inequality
- Dependency theorists argue the system structurally disadvantages developing nations
- Realists focus on how economic power translates to geopolitical leverage

## Worth Noting
The evidence is genuinely mixed on several questions here - reasonable analysts disagree about whether current international economic arrangements primarily help or harm developing nations.
