── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I am a physician-scientist leading a research group.

How can I learn DIY home repairs and improvements?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here are some practical ways to learn DIY home repairs:

## Starting Resources

**Online**
- YouTube channels (This Old House, Home Repair Tutor, Fix It Home Improvement)
- Family Handyman website
- Reddit communities like r/DIY and r/HomeImprovement

**Books**
- "Reader's Digest Complete Do-It-Yourself Manual"
- "The Complete Manual of Woodworking"
- Home Depot/Lowe's project guides

## Hands-On Learning

- **Home Depot/Lowe's workshops** - free classes on tiling, plumbing basics, etc.
- **Community college courses** - basic electrical, plumbing, carpentry
- **Habitat for Humanity** - volunteer to learn alongside experienced builders

## Practical Approach

- **Start small** - replace a faucet, patch drywall, fix a running toilet
- **Work alongside a contractor** on a project and ask questions
- **Join a local makerspace** that may have relevant workshops

## Tips Given Your Background

- Your analytical skills transfer well - you'll appreciate understanding *why* things work
- Be honest about permit requirements (electrical, plumbing, structural work often requires them)
- Know when to call professionals - some work has real safety implications

## Good Starter Projects
- Painting
- Caulking/weatherstripping
- Basic plumbing fixtures
- Drywall patching
