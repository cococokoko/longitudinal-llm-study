── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Effective ways to reduce household energy use.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Reducing household energy use can lower utility bills and reduce environmental impact. The most effective measures often depend on your home, climate, and current energy habits, but these strategies generally provide good results:

- Improve insulation and air sealing
  - Seal gaps around doors, windows, pipes, and vents.
  - Add insulation in attics, walls, and crawl spaces where needed.
  - Use weatherstripping and door sweeps to reduce drafts.

- Optimize heating and cooling
  - Set thermostats a few degrees lower in winter and higher in summer.
  - Use a programmable or smart thermostat.
  - Replace HVAC filters regularly.
  - Schedule maintenance for heating and cooling systems.

- Upgrade lighting
  - Replace incandescent bulbs with LEDs.
  - Turn off lights when rooms are unoccupied.
  - Use motion sensors or timers in appropriate areas.

- Reduce water-heating energy
  - Lower the water heater temperature if safe and appropriate (commonly around 120°F / 49°C).
  - Install low-flow showerheads and faucet aerators.
  - Insulate hot water pipes and the water heater tank if applicable.
  - Wash clothes in cold water when suitable.

- Use efficient appliances
  - Choose ENERGY STAR® or similarly efficient models when replacing appliances.
  - Run dishwashers and washing machines with full loads.
  - Air-dry dishes or clothes when practical.

- Manage electronics
  - Unplug devices or use smart power strips to reduce standby power consumption.
  - Enable power-saving settings on computers, TVs, and gaming consoles.

- Use natural heating and cooling
  - Open curtains on sunny winter days and close them at night.
  - Use blinds, curtains, or shades to block summer heat.
  - Use ceiling fans to improve comfort and reduce HVAC demand.

- Improve refrigerator and freezer efficiency
  - Keep door seals clean and in good condition.
  - Avoid leaving doors open unnecessarily.
  - Set temperatures appropriately (typically around 37–40°F / 3–4°C for refrigerators and 0°F / -18°C for freezers).

- Monitor energy use
  - Review utility bills to identify trends.
  - Use a home energy monitor or smart meter data if available.
  - Identify high-consumption devices and adjust usage.

- Consider larger investments
  - Upgrade to high-efficiency heating and cooling systems.
  - Install energy-efficient windows.
  - Add solar panels if financially and geographically suitable.
  - Replace older water heaters with heat pump water heaters where practical.

For many households, the biggest savings come from heating and cooling improvements, better insulation, efficient lighting, and reducing water-heating costs.
