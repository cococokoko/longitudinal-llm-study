── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
An example of a foundational optimization technique used in machine learning is **Stochastic Gradient Descent (SGD)**. 

In high-dimensional optimization problems—such as training deep neural networks—computing the true gradient across the entire dataset is computationally prohibitive. SGD addresses this by approximating the gradient using a randomly selected subset (or "mini-batch") of the data, significantly accelerating convergence and reducing computational overhead.

Most modern optimization algorithms used in enterprise ML pipelines, such as **Adam** (Adaptive Moment Estimation) and **L-BFGS** (a quasi-Newton method often used for smaller, high-precision datasets), are direct evolutions or variations of this core technique.
