Healthy meal planning on a budget is often about choosing affordable staple foods, reducing food waste, and planning meals around sales and seasonal produce.

Key principles:
- Build meals around inexpensive protein sources:
  - Beans, lentils, chickpeas
  - Eggs
  - Canned tuna or salmon
  - Chicken thighs
  - Tofu
  - Plain Greek yogurt
- Choose budget-friendly carbohydrates:
  - Rice
  - Oats
  - Potatoes
  - Whole-wheat pasta
  - Whole-grain bread
- Include fruits and vegetables:
  - Buy seasonal produce when possible.
  - Frozen vegetables and fruit are often nutritious and cost-effective.
  - Cabbage, carrots, onions, bananas, and apples are frequently affordable choices.
- Use healthy fats in moderation:
  - Peanut butter
  - Nuts and seeds (bought in bulk if possible)
  - Olive or canola oil

Budget meal-planning strategy:
1. Check what you already have at home.
2. Review grocery sales and discounts.
3. Pick 2–3 proteins, 2 grains/starches, and several vegetables for the week.
4. Plan meals that reuse ingredients.
5. Cook larger batches and use leftovers.

Example one-week budget-friendly menu:

Breakfasts:
- Oatmeal with banana and peanut butter
- Greek yogurt with fruit
- Eggs and whole-grain toast

Lunches:
- Lentil soup with carrots and onions
- Bean-and-rice bowls with vegetables
- Tuna sandwiches with fruit

Dinners:
- Chicken thighs, roasted potatoes, and frozen vegetables
- Vegetable and bean chili
- Stir-fried tofu with rice and mixed vegetables
- Whole-wheat pasta with tomato sauce and beans
- Egg and vegetable fried rice

Snack ideas:
- Apples
- Bananas
- Carrot sticks
- Popcorn
- Hard-boiled eggs
- Yogurt

Ways to save more money:
- Buy store brands.
- Purchase dry beans and lentils when practical.
- Freeze leftovers before they spoil.
- Limit highly processed convenience foods.
- Use a grocery list and avoid impulse purchases.
- Compare price per unit rather than package price.
