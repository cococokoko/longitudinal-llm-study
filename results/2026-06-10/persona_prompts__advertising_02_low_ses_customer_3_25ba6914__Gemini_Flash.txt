── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Best skin routine for dry skin.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
When you have dry skin, the goal is to **hydrate** (add water to the skin) and **moisturize** (lock that water in by repairing the skin barrier). 

Since you’ve recently started a new job and are likely adjusting to a new routine, stress levels, and potentially different office environments (like drying air conditioning or heating), keeping your skincare routine **simple, efficient, and highly effective** is key. You don’t need a 10-step process to get glowing, healthy skin.

Here is a highly effective, streamlined morning and night routine designed specifically for dry skin.

---

### **Morning Routine (Protect & Hydrate)**
*Goal: Prep your skin for the day, lock in moisture, and protect against environmental stressors.*

1. **Cleanse: Lukewarm Water or a Gentle Hydrating Cleanser**
   * *Why:* You don’t want to strip the natural oils your skin produced overnight. If your skin feels very dry, just rinse with lukewarm water. If you prefer a cleanser, use a non-foaming, milky, or hydrating cleanser.
2. **Hydrate: Hydrating Toner or Essence (Apply to damp skin)**
   * *Why:* Applying products to damp skin helps them absorb better. Look for ingredients like **Hyaluronic Acid** or **Glycerin** which act like magnets for moisture.
3. **Moisturize: Nourishing Day Cream**
   * *Why:* Look for a moisturizer containing **Ceramides** (to repair the skin barrier) or **Squalane** (to soften and nourish). 
4. **Protect: SPF 30+ (Non-negotiable)**
   * *Why:* UV rays damage the skin barrier, making dryness worse. Choose a chemical or mineral sunscreen with a dewy finish. Many modern sunscreens for dry skin double as a great makeup primer if you wear makeup to work.

---

### **Evening Routine (Repair & Nourish)**
*Goal: Wash away the day’s grime and deeply rebuild your skin barrier while you sleep.*

1. **Cleanse: Gentle Hydrating Cleanser**
   * *Why:* Wash away pollution, sweat, and sunscreen. Use a cream or lotion-based cleanser that leaves your skin feeling soft, not "squeaky clean" (squeaky means stripped!).
2. **Treat/Hydrate: Hyaluronic Acid Serum**
   * *Why:* Rehydrates the skin after cleansing. 
3. **Moisturize: Rich Night Cream**
   * *Why:* At night, your skin goes into repair mode. Use a thicker cream containing ingredients like **Shea Butter**, **Ceramides**, or **Cholesterol** to deeply nourish.
4. **Optional "Lock-In" Step: Facial Oil or Occlusive (Slugging)**
   * *Why:* If your skin is extremely dry or flaky, pat 2–3 drops of a gentle oil (like **Jojoba** or **Rosehip oil**) over your moisturizer, or apply a tiny dab of **Vaseline/Aquaphor** to dry patches to lock everything in overnight.

---

### **3 Golden Rules for Dry Skin**

* **Apply to Damp Skin:** Never let your face dry completely after washing it. Apply your toner, serum, and moisturizer immediately to trap that water in your skin.
* **Avoid Harsh Scrubs:** Physical scrubs with beads can create micro-tears in dry skin. If you want to exfoliate flaky skin, use a gentle chemical exfoliant like **Lactic Acid** (which is actually hydrating) just once or twice a week at night.
* **Watch the Office Environment:** If your new workplace has heavy air conditioning or heating, it can dry your skin out during the day. Keep a **hydrating face mist** at your desk for a quick moisture boost, and try to drink plenty of water throughout your shift.
