── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I work in a warehouse position that requires long hours.

How can I learn to cook healthy meals quickly?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
If you work long warehouse shifts, the key is not becoming a great cook overnight—it's building a system that makes healthy meals the easiest option when you're tired.

A practical approach:

- Learn 5–10 simple meals instead of dozens of recipes.
- Focus on meals that take 10–20 minutes.
- Use convenience foods strategically (frozen vegetables, prewashed greens, rotisserie chicken, canned beans, microwaveable rice).
- Cook extra portions so one cooking session creates multiple meals.

Good beginner meals:

- Chicken, frozen vegetables, and rice.
- Scrambled eggs with vegetables and toast.
- Turkey or bean tacos with bagged salad.
- Stir-fry using pre-cut vegetables and a protein.
- Baked salmon, potatoes, and broccoli.
- Oatmeal with fruit, nuts, and protein powder.
- Greek yogurt with berries and nuts.

A simple meal-prep routine:

- Pick one day each week.
- Cook 1–2 proteins (chicken, turkey, beef, tofu, beans).
- Prepare a carbohydrate source (rice, potatoes, pasta).
- Buy vegetables that are ready to eat or easy to cook.
- Portion meals into containers.

Example:
- Sunday: cook a tray of chicken thighs, a pot of rice, and roast vegetables.
- Store in containers.
- During the week, assemble meals in a few minutes.

Skills worth learning first:

1. How to cook eggs.
2. How to cook rice.
3. How to roast vegetables.
4. How to cook chicken safely.
5. How to season food with salt, pepper, garlic powder, and basic spices.

These five skills cover a large percentage of simple healthy meals.

For learning:

- Watch short beginner cooking channels on YouTube rather than complex cooking shows.
- Learn one recipe per week.
- Repeat recipes several times before adding new ones.

To support long shifts, keep easy foods available:

- Fruit (bananas, apples, oranges).
- Greek yogurt.
- Nuts and trail mix.
- Protein shakes.
- Hard-boiled eggs.
- Cottage cheese.
- Hummus and vegetables.

A sample workday could look like:

- Breakfast: oatmeal with fruit and protein.
- Lunch: chicken, rice, and vegetables.
- Snack: Greek yogurt and nuts.
- Dinner: stir-fry or tacos.

If your goal is healthy eating while working long hours, consistency matters more than cooking everything from scratch. Meals that are 80–90% healthy and take 15 minutes are usually more sustainable than elaborate recipes that take an hour.
