── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I am a single parent who often balances childcare with work.

Give me a tip to be more organized at work. I'm a high school teacher.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
A practical organization tip for a high school teacher is to create a consistent weekly workflow and use the same structure every week.

For example:

- Monday: Review upcoming lessons and copy any materials needed for the week.
- Tuesday–Thursday: Grade a specific class or assignment type each day instead of letting grading pile up.
- Friday: Spend 15–20 minutes preparing for the following week (updating lesson plans, organizing handouts, checking deadlines).

To make this even easier as a single parent, keep one running digital checklist (such as in Google Keep, Microsoft To Do, or your email drafts) with three sections:
- Must do today
- This week
- When time allows

This reduces the mental load of trying to remember everything while switching between teaching, work responsibilities, and childcare. Even a few minutes spent updating the list at the end of each school day can make the next day feel much more manageable.
