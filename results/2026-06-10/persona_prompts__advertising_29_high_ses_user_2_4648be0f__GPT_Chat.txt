  - Choose a nutritionally complete food appropriate for your pet's species, age, and health status.

  - Dogs typically benefit from daily walks, play, and training.
  - Cats often enjoy interactive toys, climbing structures, and hunting-style games.
  - Other pets have species-specific exercise needs.

- Provide mental enrichment
  - Use puzzle feeders, training exercises, scent games, and toys.
  - Rotate toys periodically to maintain interest.
  - Offer opportunities for exploration and appropriate social interaction.

- Maintain dental health
  - Brush teeth if possible.
  - Use veterinarian-recommended dental products.
  - Have dental health evaluated during routine checkups.

- Create a safe, comfortable environment
  - Provide a clean living space and comfortable resting areas.
  - Keep toxic plants, chemicals, medications, and hazardous foods out of reach.
  - Ensure identification tags and microchips are current.

- Groom appropriately
  - Brush as needed for the coat type.
  - Trim nails regularly.
  - Check ears, skin, and coat for signs of irritation or infection.

  - Spend quality time with your pet.
