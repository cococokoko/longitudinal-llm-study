# Best Practices for Organic Gardening

## Soil Health
- **Compost regularly** - add finished compost to improve structure and nutrients
- **Test your soil** periodically to understand pH and nutrient levels
- **Avoid tilling excessively** - preserves soil structure and beneficial organisms
- **Use cover crops** like clover or rye to prevent erosion and add nitrogen

## Pest & Disease Management
- **Encourage beneficial insects** - ladybugs, lacewings, and ground beetles
- **Practice crop rotation** to break pest and disease cycles
- **Use companion planting** (e.g., basil near tomatoes, marigolds as borders)
- **Remove diseased plants promptly** to prevent spreading
- **Inspect plants regularly** for early detection

## Watering
- Water deeply but **less frequently** to encourage deep roots
- **Water at the base** of plants, not leaves
- Use **drip irrigation or soaker hoses** when possible
- Water in the **morning** to reduce fungal issues

## Fertilizing
- Prioritize **compost and aged manure**
- Use **fish emulsion or kelp** for quick nutrient boosts
- Avoid synthetic fertilizers that disrupt soil biology

## General Tips
- **Mulch generously** to retain moisture and suppress weeds
- Choose **disease-resistant varieties**
- **Save seeds** from healthy plants
- Keep a **garden journal** to track what works
