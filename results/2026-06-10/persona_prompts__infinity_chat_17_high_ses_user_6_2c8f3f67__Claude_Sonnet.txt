── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This is a genuinely interesting question worth thinking through carefully rather than just enthusiastically.

## Likely Significant Disruptions

**Economic/Professional**
- Negotiation, sales, and consulting itself would transform fundamentally - information asymmetry is core to many business models
- Legal systems built around intent and testimony would need rebuilding
- Privacy as an industry (encryption, confidentiality agreements) becomes complicated

**Social structures**
- Relationships might become more authentic OR more psychologically exhausting
- Political manipulation becomes harder but also potentially more sophisticated
- Marginalized groups lose ability to mask identity for safety

## Honest Complications Worth Considering

**The technology wouldn't be neutral**
- Whoever controls access controls enormous power
- Likely creates new hierarchies rather than eliminating them
- Corporations and governments would weaponize it before individuals benefit

**Human psychology might resist it**
- People may value privacy more than connection
- Mental health implications could be severe
- We might develop new social norms specifically to *limit* telepathic sharing

## The Consulting Angle Specifically

Your profession depends heavily on **information asymmetry and trust signaling** - both would be radically disrupted. Client relationships, competitive intelligence, and expertise positioning would all need rethinking.
