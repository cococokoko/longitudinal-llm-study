Decorating your home on a budget.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Decorating your home on a budget is all about making thoughtful changes that have a big visual impact without spending a lot of money. Here are some practical ideas:

- Rearrange your furniture
  - Moving furniture into a new layout can make a room feel completely different at no cost.
  - Create conversation areas and improve the flow of the space.

- Use paint strategically
  - A fresh coat of paint is one of the most affordable ways to transform a room.
  - Consider an accent wall for a dramatic effect while using less paint.

- Add textiles
  - New throw pillows, blankets, curtains, or rugs can refresh a room quickly.
  - Look for sales, discount stores, or secondhand options.

- Incorporate plants
  - Houseplants add color, texture, and life to a space.
  - Choose easy-care varieties if you're new to plant care.

- Shop secondhand
  - Thrift stores, flea markets, estate sales, and online marketplaces often have unique furniture and decor at low prices.
  - A little cleaning or paint can make older pieces look new.

- Create DIY wall art
  - Frame printable artwork, family photos, postcards, or fabric samples.
  - Gallery walls can make a strong design statement without a large investment.

- Update hardware and fixtures
  - Replacing cabinet handles, drawer pulls, or light fixtures can modernize a room inexpensively.

- Declutter and organize
  - Removing excess items can instantly make your home feel larger and more polished.
  - Use baskets, bins, and shelves for attractive storage.

- Use mirrors
  - Mirrors reflect light and can make small rooms feel bigger.
  - Check secondhand stores for affordable options.

- Focus on one room at a time
  - Prioritize spaces you use most often.
  - Setting a budget for each room helps prevent overspending.

Sample budget-friendly upgrades:
- Under $50: throw pillows, candles, plants, picture frames, paint for a small project.
- $50–$200: area rugs, curtains, accent chairs from secondhand sources, lighting updates.
- $200+: furniture makeovers, larger rugs, statement lighting, or multiple coordinated updates.

The key is to mix inexpensive improvements with a few carefully chosen focal pieces so your home feels intentional and personalized rather than expensive.
