── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Best skin routine for dry skin.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The key to managing dry skin is **hydration (adding water)** and **moisturization (locking that water in)** while avoiding anything that strips your skin’s natural lipid barrier. 

Here is a highly effective, dermatologist-approved daily skincare routine for dry skin, along with product recommendations and crucial tips.

---

### ☀️ Morning Routine (Focus: Hydrate & Protect)
The goal in the morning is to prep your skin for the day and protect it from environmental stressors without stripping away the oils your skin produced overnight.

1. **Cleanse (or just rinse):** 
   * **What to do:** Wash with lukewarm water only, OR use a very gentle, non-foaming hydrating cleanser. 
   * *Why:* Dry skin rarely needs a full cleanse in the morning.
2. **Hydrating Toner or Essence (Optional but highly recommended):**
   * **What to do:** Pat a hydrating toner onto damp skin. Look for ingredients like **hyaluronic acid, glycerin, or beta-glucan**.
3. **Vitamin C Serum (Antioxidant):**
   * **What to do:** Apply a few drops. 
   * *Why:* Protects against pollution and brightens. Look for a Vitamin C formulated with Vitamin E or Ferulic Acid, which are nourishing for dry skin.
4. **Moisturizer:**
   * **What to do:** Apply a cream-based moisturizer while your skin is still slightly damp.
   * *Look for:* **Ceramides** (to repair the skin barrier), **hyaluronic acid**, and **shea butter** or **squalane**.
5. **Sunscreen (SPF 30+):**
   * **What to do:** Apply a generous amount.
   * *Why:* UV rays damage the skin barrier, making dryness worse. Choose a chemical or mineral sunscreen with a dewy, moisturizing finish.

---

### 🌙 Night Routine (Focus: Repair & Nourish)
Nighttime is when your skin repairs itself. This is the time to use richer products and active ingredients.

1. **Double Cleanse (If wearing makeup/SPF):**
   * **Step 1:** Use a **cleansing balm or oil** to break down makeup and sunscreen without drying out the skin.
   * **Step 2:** Follow with a **hydrating, non-foaming cream cleanser**.
2. **Hydrating Toner/Essence:**
   * **What to do:** Apply to damp skin to prep for serums.
3. **Treatment/Active (Choose ONE per night):**
   * **For Hydration:** A Hyaluronic Acid or Polyglutamic Acid serum.
   * **For Anti-aging/Texture:** **Retinol** (use 2–3 times a week). *Tip: Use the "sandwich method" (moisturizer, then retinol, then moisturizer) to prevent dryness.*
   * **For Exfoliation (1–2 times a week max):** Use a gentle **Lactic Acid** or **PHA** (Polyhydroxy Acid). Avoid harsh scrubs or strong Salicylic Acid.
4. **Rich Night Cream:**
   * **What to do:** Apply a thick, occlusive moisturizer. 
   * *Look for:* A heavier cream than your morning one, containing lipids, fatty acids, and ceramides.
5. **Face Oil or "Slugging" (Optional - Great for very dry skin):**
   * **What to do:** Pat 2–3 drops of a pure oil (like **Squalane, Jojoba, or Rosehip oil**) over your moisturizer. 
   * *Alternatively:* Apply a thin layer of **Vaseline or Aquaphor** (called "slugging") as the final step to lock in 100% of moisture overnight.

---

### 🔑 Key Ingredients to Look For
* **Ceramides:** Essential lipids that rebuild and restore the skin barrier.
* **Hyaluronic Acid & Glycerin:** Humectants that pull water into the skin.
* **Squalane:** A lightweight oil that mimics skin’s natural sebum.
* **Shea Butter & Cocoa Butter:** Rich emollients that smooth and soften.
* **Lactic Acid:** An AHA that exfoliates *and* hydrates.

### 🚫 Ingredients to Avoid
* **Alcohol (SD Alcohol, Alcohol Denat):** Extremely drying.
* **Salicylic Acid & Benzoyl Peroxide:** Great for acne, but very drying (use only as spot treatments).
* **Harsh Foaming Agents (Sodium Lauryl Sulfate/SLS):** Strips the skin barrier.
* **Heavy Fragrances:** Can irritate dry, compromised skin.

### 💡 Golden Rules for Dry Skin
1. **Apply products to DAMP skin:** Never let your face dry completely after washing. Apply your toner and moisturizer immediately to trap that water.
2. **Turn down the water temperature:** Hot water strips the natural oils from your skin. Use lukewarm or cool water.
3. **Get a humidifier:** Run a cool-mist humidifier in your bedroom at night, especially during winter or if you use air conditioning/heating.
4. **Pat, don't rub:** When drying your face with a towel, gently pat the skin. Rubbing causes micro-tears and irritation.
