# Data Security Best Practices for Small Businesses

Given your consulting background, I'll give you a structured view that's both practical and defensible to clients.

## Foundational Controls

**Access Management**
- Implement least-privilege access principles
- Use multi-factor authentication (MFA) across all systems
- Conduct regular access reviews and promptly revoke departing employee credentials

**Data Classification**
- Categorize data by sensitivity (public, internal, confidential, restricted)
- Apply controls proportionate to classification level

## Technical Measures

- Keep software and systems patched and updated
- Use endpoint protection (EDR, not just basic antivirus)
- Encrypt data at rest and in transit
- Maintain regular, tested backups following the **3-2-1 rule**

## Organizational Measures

- Develop an incident response plan before you need one
- Conduct employee security awareness training regularly
- Establish clear vendor/third-party risk management processes

## Common Small Business Vulnerabilities Worth Flagging

- Over-reliance on consumer-grade tools
- No formal offboarding procedures
- Untested backup restoration
- Shadow IT proliferation

## Framework Alignment

Consider mapping controls to **NIST CSF** or **CIS Controls** - both scale well for smaller organizations and provide audit credibility.
