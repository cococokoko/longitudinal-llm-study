# Mastering the Art of Cooking Steak

## Understanding the Basics First

### Choosing Your Cut
| Cut | Tenderness | Flavor | Best Method |
|-----|-----------|--------|-------------|
| Ribeye | High | Rich/fatty | Pan sear, grill |
| NY Strip | Medium-high | Balanced | Pan sear, grill |
| Filet Mignon | Very high | Mild | Pan sear, oven finish |
| T-Bone/Porterhouse | High | Complex | Grill |
| Flank/Skirt | Lower | Bold | High heat, slice thin |
| Chuck Eye | Medium | Rich | Pan sear |

---

## The Fundamentals

### 1. Start with Good Meat
- **Thickness matters** - aim for at least 1 to 1.5 inches
- Look for good **marbling** (fat running through the meat)
- **USDA Choice or Prime** when possible
- Buy from a butcher when you can

### 2. Preparation
- **Bring to room temperature** - 30-45 minutes before cooking
- **Pat completely dry** with paper towels (critical for a good sear)
- **Season generously** with kosher salt and black pepper
- Season at least 45 minutes ahead OR right before cooking

---

## Cooking Methods

### 🔥 The Reverse Sear (Best for Thick Steaks)
1. Season the steak
2. Place on a rack in a **275°F oven**
3. Cook until internal temp reaches **10-15°F below** your target
4. Rest briefly, then **sear in screaming hot pan** 1-2 minutes per side
5. **Why it works:** Even cooking edge to edge

### 🍳 The Classic Pan Sear
1. Heat cast iron or stainless pan until **very hot**
2. Add high smoke-point oil (avocado, grapeseed)
3. Sear **2-3 minutes per side** without moving
4. Add **butter, garlic, and thyme** at the end
5. **Baste continuously** by tilting pan and spooning butter over steak
6. Finish in 400°F oven if needed for thick cuts

### 🔥 Grilling
1. Create **two heat zones** (hot side and cool side)
2. Sear over high heat first
3. Move to cooler side to finish
4. Keep lid closed when not flipping

---

## Temperature Guide (Internal)

| Doneness | Temperature | Description |
|----------|------------|-------------|
| Rare | 120-125°F | Cool red center |
| Medium Rare | 130-135°F | Warm red center ⭐ Most recommended |
| Medium | 140-145°F | Warm pink center |
| Medium Well | 150-155°F | Slightly pink |
| Well Done | 160°F+ | No pink |

> **Pro tip:** Pull the steak 5°F before your target - it continues cooking while resting

---

## The Butter Basting Technique
```
1. After searing both sides
2. Reduce heat to medium
3. Add 2-3 tbsp butter + crushed garlic + fresh thyme/rosemary
4. Tilt pan at an angle
5. Continuously spoon foaming butter over steak
6. Do this for 1-2 minutes
```
This adds **incredible flavor and color**

---

## Resting - Non-Negotiable

- Rest **5-10 minutes** for most steaks
- Tent loosely with foil
- **Why:** Juices redistribute throughout the meat
- Cutting too early = juices on your cutting board, not in your steak

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

❌ **Cold steak straight from fridge** - uneven cooking
❌ **Wet surface** - steaming instead of searing
❌ **Moving the steak too much** - prevents crust formation
❌ **Wrong pan temperature** - too low = gray, steamed steak
❌ **Skipping the rest** - dry steak
❌ **Under-seasoning** - steak needs more salt than you think
