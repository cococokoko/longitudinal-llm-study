── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
When stress strikes, it triggers a "fight-or-flight" response designed for survival, but chronic stress takes a heavy toll on both body and mind. 

Physically, stress floods the system with hormones like cortisol and adrenaline. This keeps your heart rate and blood pressure elevated, which over time increases the risk of heart disease. It also disrupts your digestive system, leading to stomach aches or appetite changes, and weakens the immune system, making you more susceptible to getting sick. When you are constantly on the go, chronic muscle tension can set in, causing persistent headaches, shoulder tightness, and physical exhaustion.

Mentally, stress impairs the brain’s ability to function optimally. It clouds your focus, making decision-making and concentration incredibly difficult. It also disrupts sleep patterns, leaving you trapped in a cycle of fatigue and irritability. Over time, the mental strain of constant pressure can lead to feelings of being overwhelmed, anxiety, and burnout, making it hard to find joy in daily life. 

Recognizing these signs is the first step in protecting your well-being, reminding us that finding even small moments to rest and recharge is vital for both physical and mental health.
