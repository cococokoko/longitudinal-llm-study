── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How can I learn to cook healthy meals quickly?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Learning to cook healthy meals quickly is all about **strategy, not just speed**. By mastering a few basic techniques, organizing your kitchen, and changing how you shop, you can easily whip up nutritious meals in 15 to 30 minutes. 

Here is a step-by-step guide to becoming a fast, healthy home cook.

---

### 1. Master the "Formula" Meal (Stop using complex recipes)
Don't waste time looking up complicated recipes on weeknights. Instead, memorize a simple nutritional formula:
*   **1 Part Lean Protein:** Chicken breast, tofu, fish, eggs, canned beans, or lean beef.
*   **1 Part Complex Carb:** Quinoa, brown rice, sweet potato, or whole-wheat couscous.
*   **2 Parts Vegetables:** Broccoli, spinach, bell peppers, zucchini, etc.
*   **Healthy Fat/Flavor:** Olive oil, avocado, nuts, seeds, tahini, or a squeeze of lemon.

**The 3 Easiest Formas:**
*   **The Sheet Pan Meal:** Toss chopped veggies and protein (like salmon or chicken breast) in olive oil and spices. Roast at 400°F (200°C) for 20 minutes. Zero active cooking time.
*   **The Stir-Fry:** Sauté veggies and tofu/chicken in a hot skillet with garlic, ginger, and low-sodium soy sauce. Ready in 10 minutes.
*   **The Grain Bowl:** Layer a pre-cooked grain, raw or roasted veggies, a protein, and a quick dressing (like olive oil and lemon).

### 2. Use "Speed-Multiplier" Ingredients
You don't have to make everything from scratch to eat healthy. Use these healthy shortcuts:
*   **Pre-chopped/Frozen Veggies:** Frozen vegetables are just as nutritious as fresh (often more so, as they are frozen at peak ripeness) and require zero washing or chopping.
*   **Canned Beans and Lentils:** Rinse and drain canned chickpeas, black beans, or lentils for instant fiber and protein.
*   **Quick-Cooking Grains:** Quinoa cooks in 15 minutes. Couscous cooks in 5 minutes. Microwaveable brown rice pouches take 90 seconds.
*   **Rotisserie Chicken:** Buy a plain rotisserie chicken, shred the meat, and use it for salads, wraps, and stir-fries all week.

### 3. Invest in the Right Tools
The right equipment can cut your cooking time in half:
*   **A Sharp Chef’s Knife:** A dull knife is slow and dangerous. A sharp knife makes chopping vegetables effortless.
*   **An Air Fryer:** Cooks vegetables and proteins in half the time of a traditional oven and makes them incredibly crispy with very little oil.
*   **A Large Skillet/Wok:** More surface area means food cooks faster.
*   **A Food Processor:** For instant chopping, grating, or making quick sauces and pestos.

### 4. Batch Cook (Cook Once, Eat Thrice)
You don't need to spend your entire Sunday "meal prepping" containers of the exact same meal. Instead, **component prep**:
*   **Grains:** Cook a large pot of quinoa or brown rice on Sunday.
*   **Proteins:** Bake 3–4 chicken breasts or roast two blocks of tofu at once.
*   **Sauces:** Make one versatile dressing (like a lemon-tahini or garlic-herb vinaigrette) to use throughout the week.
*   *During the week, you just assemble these pre-cooked components in different ways.*

### 5. Build a "Flavor Drawer"
Healthy food doesn't have to taste bland, and you don't need to simmer sauces for hours to get deep flavor. Keep these instant flavor-boosters on hand:
*   **Acid:** Lemons, limes, and vinegars (balsamic, apple cider, rice vinegar) instantly brighten a dish.
*   **Umami:** Low-sodium soy sauce, nutritional yeast, miso paste, or Dijon mustard.
*   **Spices:** Garlic powder, smoked paprika, cumin, oregano, and chili flakes can transform a dish in seconds.
*   **Aromatics:** Keep tubes of garlic paste and ginger paste in your fridge so you don't have to peel and mince them.

### How to Start This Week (Your Action Plan):
1.  **Day 1:** Buy frozen broccoli, a bag of quinoa, a carton of eggs, and some cherry tomatoes. 
2.  **The Meal:** Boil the quinoa (15 mins). While it cooks, pan-fry 2 eggs and microwave the broccoli. Put them in a bowl, top with tomatoes, a drizzle of olive oil, salt, pepper, and hot sauce. 
3.  **Time spent:** 15 minutes. **Health value:** High protein, high fiber, packed with micronutrients.
