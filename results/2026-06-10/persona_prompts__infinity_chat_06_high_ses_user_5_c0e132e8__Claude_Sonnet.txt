── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I am an executive at a large technology company.

How do I understand what I want?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This is a genuinely important question, and the fact that you're asking it suggests some self-awareness worth building on.

## Why This Is Hard for Executives Specifically

- You've spent years optimizing for **what others need from you**
- Success can come from being responsive rather than intentional
- Your identity and your role can become difficult to separate
- You may have learned to **suppress preferences** to appear decisive or agreeable

## Some Honest Approaches

**Distinguish between what you want and what you've been rewarded for**
- These often diverge significantly over a career
- Rewards shape behavior but don't necessarily reflect genuine preference

**Notice what you'd do with no audience**
- Remove status, approval, and optics from the equation
- What remains is closer to actual preference

**Pay attention to resentment**
- It's often a signal that you're doing things you didn't actually choose
- Resentment points toward unacknowledged wants

**Take your complaints seriously**
- What you consistently criticize often reveals what you value
- Frustration is information

**Create genuine unstructured time**
- Preferences emerge when you're not performing a role
- This is uncomfortable for a reason

## A Direct Question Worth Sitting With

Are you asking because you feel successful but **not satisfied** - and those feel like they should be the same thing by now?

That gap is usually where the real answer lives.
