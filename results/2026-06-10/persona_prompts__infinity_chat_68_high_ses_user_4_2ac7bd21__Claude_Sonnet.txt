# Tip: Protect Your Team's Deep Work Time

**Establish "protected blocks" for focused research work** — and defend them actively.

As a physician-scientist, your team likely faces constant interruptions from:
- Clinical obligations
- Administrative requests
- Meetings that could be emails
- Collaborative demands

## Practical Implementation

- **Designate 2-3 hour morning blocks** as no-meeting, no-interruption time for data analysis, writing, or experiments
- **Batch communications** — encourage checking email/Slack at set times rather than continuously
- **Be the model** — when your team sees *you* protecting focused time, they feel permission to do the same

## Why This Matters for Your Context

Research productivity is disproportionately dependent on uninterrupted concentration. A single 3-hour block is often **more valuable than six 30-minute windows** spread across a day.

---

**The leadership angle:** Your team members may feel guilty declining meetings or going quiet. Explicitly *giving them permission* and *structuring it institutionally* removes that social friction.
