── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How do I start a vegetable garden in my backyard?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Starting a backyard vegetable garden can be simple if you begin with a manageable space and a few reliable crops.

1. Choose a location
- Pick a spot that gets at least 6–8 hours of direct sunlight daily.
- Make sure water is easily accessible.
- Avoid areas where water pools after rain.

2. Decide on a garden type
- In-ground garden: Less expensive if you have good soil.
- Raised beds: Easier to manage, better drainage, and fewer soil issues.
- Containers: Good for small spaces or patios.

3. Test and improve the soil
- Most vegetables prefer loose, well-draining soil rich in organic matter.
- Mix in compost before planting.
- Consider a soil test through a local extension service to check pH and nutrient levels.

4. Start with easy vegetables
Good beginner choices include:
- Lettuce
- Radishes
- Green beans
- Zucchini
- Tomatoes
- Peppers
- Cucumbers
- Herbs such as basil, parsley, and chives

5. Plan your layout
- Place taller plants on the north side (in the Northern Hemisphere) so they don't shade shorter crops.
- Leave enough space between plants according to seed packet or plant tag instructions.
- Start small—something like a 4 ft × 8 ft (1.2 m × 2.4 m) bed is often plenty for beginners.

6. Plant at the right time
- Check your local last frost date.
- Cool-season crops (lettuce, peas, spinach) can often be planted earlier.
- Warm-season crops (tomatoes, peppers, cucumbers) should be planted after frost danger has passed.

7. Water consistently
- Most gardens need about 1 inch (2.5 cm) of water per week, including rainfall.
- Water deeply rather than lightly and frequently.
- Morning watering helps reduce disease problems.

8. Mulch the soil
- Apply straw, shredded leaves, or other organic mulch around plants.
- Mulch helps retain moisture, suppress weeds, and moderate soil temperature.

9. Monitor for pests and diseases
- Check plants regularly.
- Remove weeds promptly.
- Hand-pick larger pests when possible.
- Encourage beneficial insects by growing flowers nearby.

10. Harvest regularly
- Many vegetables produce more when harvested frequently.
- Pick crops when they're young and tender for the best flavor.
