Mastering the art of cooking steak.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Mastering steak cooking comes down to understanding a few key variables: the cut, temperature, seasoning, and technique.

1. Choose the right cut
- Ribeye: Rich, well-marbled, forgiving for beginners.
- New York strip: Beefy flavor with a firmer texture.
- Filet mignon: Very tender but leaner.
- Sirloin: More affordable, great when cooked properly.
- Flank or skirt steak: Best cooked quickly over high heat and sliced against the grain.

2. Start with a dry steak
- Pat the steak dry with paper towels.
- Moisture on the surface prevents a good crust from forming.

3. Season generously
- Kosher salt and freshly ground black pepper are often all you need.
- Salt can be applied just before cooking or 1–24 hours ahead and left uncovered in the refrigerator for deeper seasoning.

4. Bring the steak closer to room temperature (optional)
- A short rest outside the refrigerator while you prepare your cooking area can help with more even cooking, though it's less important than many people think.

5. Use high heat
- Cast-iron pans are excellent for developing a crust.
- Preheat the pan thoroughly before adding oil with a high smoke point (such as avocado or canola oil).

6. Learn temperature targets
Use an instant-read thermometer for consistency:
- Rare: 120–125°F (49–52°C)
- Medium-rare: 130–135°F (54–57°C)
- Medium: 140–145°F (60–63°C)
- Medium-well: 150–155°F (66–68°C)
- Well done: 160°F+ (71°C+)

Remove the steak a few degrees before the final target because it will continue cooking while resting.

7. Baste for extra flavor
- Near the end of cooking, add butter, garlic, and herbs such as thyme or rosemary.
- Tilt the pan and spoon the melted butter over the steak repeatedly.

8. Rest before slicing
- Rest for about 5–10 minutes depending on thickness.
- This helps juices redistribute throughout the meat.

9. Slice correctly
- For cuts with pronounced muscle fibers (flank, skirt, hanger), slice against the grain.
- This makes the steak noticeably more tender.

10. Practice one method repeatedly
A simple pan-seared steak method:
- Preheat a cast-iron skillet.
- Season steak with salt and pepper.
- Add oil.
- Sear 2–4 minutes per side depending on thickness.
- Add butter, garlic, and herbs.
- Baste for 1–2 minutes.
- Check temperature.
- Rest and serve.

Since you work shifts as a patient care aide, a practical approach is to pick one cut (such as ribeye or strip steak) and cook it the same way several times. Keeping notes on thickness, cooking time, and final temperature will improve your results faster than constantly changing techniques.
