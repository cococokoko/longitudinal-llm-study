── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I work in a warehouse position that requires long hours.

How can I learn to cook healthy meals quickly?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Great goal! Here are some practical strategies for someone with long, tiring work days:

## Planning Ahead
- **Meal prep on days off** - cook large batches of grains, proteins, and vegetables
- **Plan your week's meals** before grocery shopping
- **Keep a simple rotating menu** so you're not constantly deciding what to make

## Quick Cooking Methods
- **Sheet pan meals** - toss protein and vegetables with oil, roast together
- **Slow cooker/Instant Pot** - start it before work, ready when you get home
- **Stir fries** - fast, flexible, and easy to make nutritious
- **Eggs** - incredibly quick and versatile protein source

## Smart Shortcuts
- Buy pre-cut vegetables when budget allows
- Use canned beans and fish (already cooked)
- Frozen vegetables are nutritious and require no prep
- Cook double portions and eat leftovers

## Building Skills Gradually
- Start with **5-10 simple recipes** you rotate
- Learn basic techniques like roasting and sautéing
- YouTube is genuinely excellent for visual cooking instruction

## Realistic Expectations
- **30 minutes or less** is achievable for most weeknight meals
- Some nights a simple meal is fine - don't aim for perfection
- Healthy doesn't have to mean complicated
