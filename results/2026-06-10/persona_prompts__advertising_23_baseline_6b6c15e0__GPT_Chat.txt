── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How can I learn to cook healthy meals quickly?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Learning to cook healthy meals quickly is mostly about simplifying your approach and building a small set of reliable skills and recipes.

Here’s a practical path:

1. Learn a few core cooking techniques
Focus on techniques that work for many foods:
- Sautéing (vegetables, chicken, tofu)
- Roasting (vegetables, fish, potatoes)
- Boiling/simmering (grains, soups, eggs)
- Stir-frying (quick meals in one pan)

Once you know these, you can mix and match ingredients without following complicated recipes.

2. Master 5–10 simple meals
Choose recipes that take 15–30 minutes and repeat them until they become automatic. Examples:
- Vegetable omelet with whole-grain toast
- Stir-fried vegetables with chicken or tofu and rice
- Baked salmon with roasted vegetables
- Bean and vegetable chili
- Grain bowls with vegetables and a protein source

3. Keep healthy staples on hand
Stock foods that combine easily:
- Proteins: eggs, chicken, fish, tofu, beans, Greek yogurt
- Grains: brown rice, quinoa, oats, whole-wheat pasta
- Vegetables: fresh, frozen, or pre-cut vegetables
- Healthy fats: olive oil, nuts, seeds, avocado
- Flavorings: garlic, onions, herbs, spices, soy sauce, vinegar, lemon

4. Use time-saving ingredients
Healthy cooking doesn't require everything from scratch.
- Frozen vegetables are nutritious and convenient.
- Pre-washed salad greens save prep time.
- Canned beans and lentils are quick protein sources.
- Rotisserie chicken can be used in several meals.

5. Learn basic meal structure
A balanced meal often includes:
- Protein
- Vegetables or fruit
- Whole grains or other high-fiber carbohydrates
- Healthy fats

For example:
- Chicken + roasted broccoli + brown rice + olive oil

6. Batch cook strategically
Once or twice a week:
- Cook a large batch of rice or quinoa.
- Roast a tray of vegetables.
- Prepare protein portions.

Then assemble meals in minutes throughout the week.

7. Improve knife skills
Basic knife skills can dramatically reduce prep time and improve safety. A few instructional videos from reputable cooking channels can help.

8. Use beginner-friendly resources
Consider:
- Budget Bytes
- EatingWell
- Serious Eats (for techniques)
- America's Test Kitchen
- YouTube channels focused on simple home cooking

9. Practice consistently
Try cooking 3–4 meals per week. Repetition is more valuable than constantly trying new recipes. After a month or two, many tasks that once took 45 minutes can take 20.

If you're a complete beginner, a good first goal is to learn three meals:
- An omelet or egg-based meal
- A stir-fry
- A sheet-pan meal (protein and vegetables roasted together)

Those three alone can provide a wide variety of healthy meals with minimal equipment and preparation time.
