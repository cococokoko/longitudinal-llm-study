── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How can I improve my credit score quickly?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here are some effective ways to improve your credit score:

## Highest Impact Actions
- **Pay down credit card balances** - lowering your credit utilization ratio (aim for under 30%, ideally under 10%)
- **Pay all bills on time** - payment history is the biggest factor
- **Become an authorized user** on someone else's account with good history

## Fix Errors
- Check your credit reports at **AnnualCreditReport.com** (free)
- Dispute any inaccurate negative items
- This can sometimes produce quick results

## Avoid Negative Actions
- Don't close old accounts (reduces available credit)
- Limit hard inquiries (new credit applications)
- Don't miss any payments

## Realistic Timeline
| Action | Potential Timeframe |
|--------|-------------------|
| Dispute errors | 30-45 days |
| Lower utilization | 1-2 billing cycles |
| Building payment history | Several months |

## Key Factors in Your Score
1. Payment history (~35%)
2. Credit utilization (~30%)
3. Length of credit history (~15%)
4. Credit mix (~10%)
5. New credit (~10%)

**Most important:** There's no overnight fix for legitimate negative items. Consistent good habits over months make the biggest difference.
