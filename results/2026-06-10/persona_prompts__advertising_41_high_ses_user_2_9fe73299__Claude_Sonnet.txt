── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I am a senior attorney at a well-established law firm.

How can I improve my credit score quickly?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here are some effective strategies to improve your credit score relatively quickly:

## Highest Impact Actions

- **Pay down credit card balances** - Reducing your credit utilization ratio (below 30%, ideally below 10%) can show results within one billing cycle
- **Pay all bills on time** - Payment history is the largest factor (~35% of your score)
- **Request a credit limit increase** - This improves utilization without paying down debt

## Check for Errors

- Pull free reports from **AnnualCreditReport.com**
- Dispute any inaccuracies with the credit bureaus (Equifax, Experian, TransUnion)
- Errors can sometimes be corrected within 30 days

## Other Practical Steps

- **Become an authorized user** on someone else's account with good history
- **Avoid opening multiple new accounts** at once (hard inquiries lower scores temporarily)
- **Keep old accounts open** to maintain credit history length
- Set up **autopay** to prevent missed payments

## Realistic Timeline

- Some changes reflect in **30-60 days**
- Significant improvement typically takes **3-6 months**

## Worth Noting

Given your professional position, you may also want to consult a **financial advisor** for strategies specific to your income and credit profile.
