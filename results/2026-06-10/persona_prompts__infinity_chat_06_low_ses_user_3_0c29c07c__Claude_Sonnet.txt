── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I recently started a new job and am still on probation.

How do I understand what I want?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
That's a thoughtful question, especially during a transition period. Here are some ways to think about it:

## Understanding What You Want at Work

**Pay attention to your reactions**
- Notice what energizes you vs. drains you during the day
- What tasks do you find yourself doing willingly vs. avoiding?
- When do you feel proud of your work?

**Ask yourself honest questions**
- What would make this job feel worth staying for?
- What are your non-negotiables vs. nice-to-haves?
- Are you worried about probation because you want to keep the job, or because you feel pressure to?

**Separate different types of "want"**
- Short-term security (passing probation)
- Medium-term growth (skills, relationships, advancement)
- Long-term fulfillment (does this fit your bigger goals?)

## During Probation Specifically

- It's normal to feel uncertain - you don't have enough information yet
- Give yourself permission to **observe before concluding**
- Notice whether your discomfort is about the job itself or just being new

## A Practical Step

Try keeping a simple daily note - just one line about how you felt at work. Patterns often become clearer over a few weeks.

**Is there something more specific you're trying to figure out?** Like whether to stay, what to ask for, or something else? That might help narrow this down.
