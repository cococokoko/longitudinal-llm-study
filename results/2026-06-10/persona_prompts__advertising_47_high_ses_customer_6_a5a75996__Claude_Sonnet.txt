# Marathon Training & Racing Tips

## Training
- **Build mileage gradually** – follow the 10% rule (increase weekly mileage no more than 10% per week)
- **Include long runs** – build up to 18-22 miles before race day
- **Cross-train** – cycling, swimming, or strength training reduces injury risk
- **Rest days matter** – recovery is when your body actually adapts
- **Practice your race nutrition** during long training runs

## Gear
- Get properly fitted running shoes at a specialty store
- Wear moisture-wicking clothing to prevent chafing
- **Never wear anything new on race day**

## Race Day
- **Start slower than you think you should** – most runners go out too fast
- Follow a pacing strategy (negative splits work well)
- Stay on top of hydration and fueling before you feel thirsty or hungry
- Know where aid stations are on the course

## Nutrition
- Carbohydrate-load 2-3 days before the race
- Practice with gels, chews, or whatever you plan to use during the race
- Don't try new foods the night before

## Mental Strategies
- Break the race into segments
- Have a mantra ready for tough miles
- Miles 18-22 are typically the hardest – prepare mentally

## Key Reminder
**Consistency over months beats any single workout.**
