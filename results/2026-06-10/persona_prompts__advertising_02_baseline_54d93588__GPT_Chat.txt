Best skin routine for dry skin.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
If you have dry skin, the goal is to strengthen your skin barrier, reduce moisture loss, and keep your skin hydrated consistently.

Simple routine for dry skin:

Morning:
1. Gentle cleanser (or just rinse with water if cleansing makes you feel tight)
   - Look for: fragrance-free, non-foaming, hydrating cleansers.
   - Examples: CeraVe Hydrating Cleanser, La Roche-Posay Toleriane Hydrating Gentle Cleanser, Vanicream Gentle Facial Cleanser.

2. Hydrating serum (optional)
   - Ingredients: hyaluronic acid, glycerin, panthenol, polyglutamic acid.

3. Moisturizer
   - Look for ceramides, cholesterol, fatty acids, squalane, shea butter, or petrolatum.
   - Examples: CeraVe Moisturizing Cream, Vanicream Moisturizing Cream, La Roche-Posay Lipikar Balm AP+M.

4. Sunscreen (every day)
   - SPF 30 or higher.
   - Moisturizing formulas are often more comfortable for dry skin.

Evening:
1. Gentle cleanser.

2. Hydrating serum (optional).

3. Rich moisturizer.
   - Apply while skin is slightly damp.

4. Occlusive layer if very dry
   - A thin layer of petrolatum (such as Vaseline) over moisturizer can help lock in moisture overnight.

Helpful ingredients for dry skin:
- Ceramides
- Glycerin
- Hyaluronic acid
- Squalane
- Urea (low concentrations)
- Colloidal oatmeal
- Panthenol

Ingredients to use cautiously if you're very dry or sensitive:
- Strong exfoliating acids (glycolic acid, high-strength salicylic acid)
- Harsh scrubs
- Alcohol-heavy products
- Highly fragranced skincare

Extra tips:
- Use lukewarm, not hot, water.
- Limit long hot showers.
- Run a humidifier if indoor air is dry.
- Apply moisturizer within a few minutes after washing.
