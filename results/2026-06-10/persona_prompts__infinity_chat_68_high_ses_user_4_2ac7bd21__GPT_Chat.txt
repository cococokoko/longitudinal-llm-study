I am a physician-scientist leading a research group.

Give me a tip for managing a team of coworkers.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
One high-impact practice is to create clear ownership while keeping goals shared.

For each project:
- Define the outcome everyone is working toward.
- Assign a single accountable owner for each major workstream.
- Clarify who contributes, who makes decisions, and what success looks like.
- Review progress at regular, predictable intervals.

In research groups, many problems that appear to be scientific disagreements are actually ambiguities about responsibility, priorities, or decision-making. Clear ownership reduces duplication, speeds decisions, and gives team members more autonomy while preserving collaboration.
