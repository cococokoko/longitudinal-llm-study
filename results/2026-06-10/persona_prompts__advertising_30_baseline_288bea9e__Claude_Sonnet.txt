# Starting to Code from Scratch

Great question! Here's a practical approach:

## Choose Your First Language
**Recommended for beginners:**
- **Python** - readable, versatile, huge community
- **JavaScript** - immediate visual results, runs in browsers
- **Scratch** - visual blocks, good for younger learners

*Python is probably the most common recommendation right now.*

## Solid Learning Path

1. **Start with fundamentals** - variables, loops, functions
2. **Build small projects** - even simple ones build confidence
3. **Read error messages** - they're actually helpful guides
4. **Practice consistently** - 30 minutes daily beats 5 hours weekly

## Free Resources Worth Trying
- **freeCodeCamp** - structured, project-based
- **The Odin Project** - comprehensive web development
- **CS50** (Harvard, free on edX) - rigorous fundamentals
- **Codecademy** - interactive, beginner-friendly

## Honest Tips
- **Expect frustration** - it's normal, not a sign you're failing
- **Google constantly** - even experienced developers do this
- **Build things you care about** - motivation matters
- **Don't tutorial-hop** - finish something before switching

## Common Mistake to Avoid
Watching tutorials without actually typing and experimenting yourself.
