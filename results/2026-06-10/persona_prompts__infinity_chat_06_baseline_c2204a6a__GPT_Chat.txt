── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How do I understand what I want?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Figuring out what you want is often less about discovering a hidden answer and more about paying attention to patterns in your experiences over time.

A few approaches can help:

- Notice what energizes you.
  - After activities, conversations, projects, or days, ask:
    - What gave me energy?
    - What drained me?
    - What would I willingly do again?
  - Desire often shows up as sustained interest rather than intense certainty.

- Pay attention to envy.
  - When you're jealous of someone, ask:
    - What exactly do I wish I had?
    - Their freedom? Their creativity? Their relationships? Their status?
  - Envy can reveal unacknowledged wants.

- Look at your choices, not just your thoughts.
  - What do you actually spend time, money, and attention on?
  - Sometimes our behavior reveals priorities that our self-image misses.

- Separate your wants from expectations.
  - Ask:
    - If nobody judged me, what would I choose?
    - If success were guaranteed, what would I try?
    - If nobody would ever know, would I still want this?
  - These questions help distinguish personal desire from social pressure.

- Experiment instead of waiting for certainty.
  - You don't have to know what you want before acting.
  - Try small versions:
    - Take a class.
    - Volunteer.
    - Go on a date.
    - Start a project.
    - Travel somewhere new.
  - Information comes from experience.

- Notice recurring themes.
  - What have you been drawn to repeatedly over months or years?
  - Interests that keep returning often matter, even if you've ignored them.

- Consider what kind of life you want, not just what goal.
  - Ask:
    - How do I want my days to feel?
    - What kind of people do I want around me?
    - How much stability, novelty, challenge, or freedom do I want?
  - Sometimes the lifestyle is clearer than the specific objective.

- Accept that wants can be uncertain and change.
  - Many people assume they should have a single, clear answer. Often what you want becomes clearer through living, not through pure introspection.

If you'd like, tell me a little about the area where you're feeling stuck—career, relationships, where to live, life direction, or something else—and I can help you explore it more specifically.
