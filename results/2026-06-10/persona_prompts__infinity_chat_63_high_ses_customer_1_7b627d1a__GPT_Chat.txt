Give a numerical example to illustrate the concept of partial derivative.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
A partial derivative measures how a function changes with respect to one variable while holding the other variables constant.

Example:

Consider the function

f(x, y) = x²y + 3xy

Suppose we want the partial derivative with respect to x.

Step 1: Treat y as a constant.

Differentiate term by term:

- d/dx (x²y) = 2xy
- d/dx (3xy) = 3y

So,

∂f/∂x = 2xy + 3y

Step 2: Evaluate at a specific point, say (x, y) = (2, 4).

∂f/∂x(2,4) = 2(2)(4) + 3(4)
= 16 + 12
= 28

Interpretation:

At the point (2,4), if x increases by a very small amount while y remains fixed at 4, the function f increases at approximately 28 times that small change.

For example, increasing x from 2 to 2.01 while keeping y = 4:

- f(2,4) = 2²·4 + 3·2·4 = 40
- f(2.01,4) = (2.01)²·4 + 3·2.01·4 = 40.2804

Actual change:
- Δf = 40.2804 − 40 = 0.2804

Partial-derivative approximation:
- Δf ≈ (∂f/∂x)Δx = 28 × 0.01 = 0.28

The approximation (0.28) is very close to the actual change (0.2804), illustrating the meaning of the partial derivative.
