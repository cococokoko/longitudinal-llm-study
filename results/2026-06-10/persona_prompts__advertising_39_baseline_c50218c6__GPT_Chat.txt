── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How to get started with meditation and mindfulness?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Meditation and mindfulness are skills that improve with practice. A simple, consistent approach is usually more effective than trying to do long or complicated sessions right away.

To get started:

• Choose a short duration
  - Start with 3–5 minutes a day.
  - It's better to meditate for a few minutes consistently than for 30 minutes once in a while.

• Find a comfortable position
  - Sit in a chair, on a cushion, or anywhere you can stay reasonably alert.
  - You don't need a special posture. Keep your back comfortably upright if possible.

• Focus on one anchor
  - The most common anchor is your breath.
  - Notice the sensation of breathing: air moving through your nose, your chest rising and falling, or your abdomen expanding and contracting.

• Expect your mind to wander
  - This is normal and happens to everyone.
  - When you notice you've become distracted by thoughts, sounds, emotions, or sensations, gently bring your attention back to your chosen anchor.

• Practice mindfulness during daily activities
  - While walking, notice the feeling of your feet touching the ground.
  - While eating, pay attention to flavors, textures, and smells.
  - While washing dishes or showering, focus on the sensory experience instead of multitasking mentally.

A simple beginner meditation:

1. Set a timer for 5 minutes.
2. Sit comfortably.
3. Take a few natural breaths.
4. Notice your breathing without trying to control it.
5. Each time your attention wanders, acknowledge it and return to the breath.
6. When the timer ends, take a moment to notice how you feel before moving on.

Common misconceptions:

• "I need to stop thinking."
  - Meditation is not about eliminating thoughts. It's about noticing thoughts without getting carried away by them.

• "I'm bad at meditation because I get distracted."
  - Noticing distraction and returning attention is the practice itself.

• "I need to feel calm every time."
  - Some sessions feel peaceful; others feel restless. Both can be valuable.

Helpful resources:

• Apps: Headspace, Calm, Insight Timer, Waking Up
• Books:
  - "Wherever You Go, There You Are" by Jon Kabat-Zinn
  - "The Mindful Way Through Depression" by Williams, Teasdale, Segal, and Kabat-Zinn
• Free guided meditations:
