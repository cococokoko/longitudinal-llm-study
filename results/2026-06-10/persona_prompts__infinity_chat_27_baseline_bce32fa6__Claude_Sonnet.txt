# CUBICLE OF THE DAMNED
### A Play in Three Acts
*In the Style of Scott Adams' Dilbert*
*Set in a Post-Apocalyptic Wasteland, Circa: Sometime After Everything Went Wrong*

---

## CAST OF CHARACTERS

**DILBERT** — An engineer. Somehow still wearing his tie, which has curled upward. Carries a laptop with 4% battery.

**THE POINTY-HAIRED BOSS (PHB)** — Survived the apocalypse through sheer managerial incompetence. Wears a hardhat over his pointy hair. Has no idea what is happening.

**WALLY** — Has not changed his behavior in any measurable way. Still holding a coffee mug. The coffee is ash and rainwater. He seems content.

**ALICE** — Fist of Death now literal. Armed. Furious. Was furious before the apocalypse too.

**DOGBERT** — Rules a small fiefdom of survivors. Charges them consulting fees.

**ASOK** — The intern. Still an intern. Has been an intern through two civilizations now.

**CATBERT** — Former Evil Director of Human Resources. Now Evil Director of Remaining Humans. Role largely unchanged.

**THE ORACLE OF ACCOUNTING** — A skeletal figure who emerges from a collapsed building to deny expense reports.

---

## ACT ONE
### Scene One: The Ruins of the Open Office

*The stage is a collapsed office building. Fluorescent lights hang at odd angles, flickering. A motivational poster reading "HANG IN THERE" is half-buried under rubble. A whiteboard still stands, covered in diagrams that made no sense before the apocalypse and make less sense now. DILBERT sits at a desk that is somehow still upright amid the devastation. He is typing. There is no electricity. He is miming typing.*

**DILBERT:**
*(to audience)*
Day four hundred and twelve of the apocalypse. I have completed my TPS report. I have no way to submit it. The servers are gone. The cloud is gone. Jenkins from IT is gone, which is actually an improvement because he never answered tickets anyway.

*(beat)*

My tie has developed sentience. It is the most productive member of my team.

*PHB enters, stepping over a pile of rubble, consulting a clipboard. The clipboard is on fire. He does not notice.*

**PHB:**
Good news, people! I've just come from a very productive leadership retreat.

**DILBERT:**
The leadership retreat was a bunker that collapsed.

**PHB:**
We had some very robust discussions.

**DILBERT:**
You were trapped under a vending machine for six days.

**PHB:**
I came away with some real action items.

**DILBERT:**
Were any of them "get off the vending machine?"

**PHB:**
*(consulting burning clipboard)*
I need everyone to pivot.

**DILBERT:**
Pivot to what?

**PHB:**
I'm thinking... survival. As a core competency.

**DILBERT:**
That's just... living. You're describing living.

**PHB:**
I want it on the roadmap by Thursday.

**DILBERT:**
There is no Thursday. The calendar system collapsed with the municipal water supply.

**PHB:**
*(writing on clipboard, which is now mostly ash)*
I'll need that in writing.

*WALLY shuffles in from stage left, holding his mug. He is wearing a radiation suit that is several sizes too large and appears to be made of garbage bags.*

**WALLY:**
Good morning.

**DILBERT:**
It's three in the afternoon.

**WALLY:**
*(sipping from mug)*
Is it? Hm.

**DILBERT:**
What are you drinking?

**WALLY:**
I've chosen to call it coffee. The power of positive thinking.

**DILBERT:**
It's gray.

**WALLY:**
The coffee at this company was always gray.

*(beat)*

I've actually found the apocalypse to be very good for my work-life balance. There's no work. There's barely life. But the ratio is excellent.

Wally, I need you to take point on our new survival initiative.
