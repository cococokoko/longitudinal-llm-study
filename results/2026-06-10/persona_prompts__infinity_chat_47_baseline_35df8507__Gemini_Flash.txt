Here is a trivia question about bluebirds:

**Question:** 
Unlike many other birds whose feathers contain actual blue pigments, bluebirds aren't technically blue. What physical phenomenon causes their feathers to appear blue to the human eye?

**Answer:** 
**Structural coloration (or light scattering).** 

Bluebird feathers contain no blue pigment (they actually contain melanin, which is brown/black). Instead, the microscopic structure of their feathers reflects and scatters light in a way that only allows blue wavelengths to escape and reach our eyes—a phenomenon similar to why the sky appears blue (known as Rayleigh scattering).
