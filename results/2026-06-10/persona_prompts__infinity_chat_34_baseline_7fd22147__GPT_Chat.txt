What is a good secondhand market laptop for learning Python?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
For learning Python, you do not need a powerful laptop. A reliable used business laptop is usually a much better value than a used consumer model because they are built to last, have better keyboards, and often have upgradeable RAM and storage.

Good secondhand options:

- Lenovo ThinkPad T480
  - One of the best values on the used market.
  - 8th-generation Intel CPUs (important because they support Windows 11).
  - Excellent keyboard.
  - Easy to upgrade RAM and SSD.
  - Commonly available for $150–300 USD depending on condition.

- Lenovo ThinkPad T490
  - Newer and lighter than the T480.
  - Good performance for programming.
  - Typically $250–400 used.

- Dell Latitude 5490 or 5400
  - Business-class laptops with solid build quality.
  - Easy to find refurbished.
  - Usually $150–350 used.

- HP EliteBook 840 G5 or G6
  - Premium business laptops.
  - Good screens and keyboards.
  - Often available for $200–400 used.

- Lenovo ThinkPad X280 or X390
  - More portable 12–13 inch options.
  - Great if you travel frequently.
  - Slightly less upgradeable than the T480.

Minimum specifications I would look for:
- CPU: Intel Core i5 8th generation or newer (e.g., i5-8250U, i5-8350U)
- RAM: 8 GB minimum, 16 GB preferred
- Storage: 256 GB SSD minimum
- Screen: 1080p (1920×1080) if possible

If you plan to:
- Learn Python basics, web development, automation, data analysis, or machine learning fundamentals:
  - Any of the above laptops with 16 GB RAM will work well.
- Train large AI models locally:
  - A different class of machine with a dedicated GPU would be needed, but that's not necessary for learning Python.
