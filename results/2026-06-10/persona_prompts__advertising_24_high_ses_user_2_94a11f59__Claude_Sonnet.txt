# Retirement Savings Strategies for High-Income Professionals

As a senior attorney, you likely have specific considerations given your income level and professional structure. Here are the most effective approaches:

## Employer-Sponsored Plans
- **401(k)** - Maximize contributions ($23,000 in 2024, plus $7,500 catch-up if 50+)
- **Profit-sharing plans** - Common at law firms, can significantly boost contributions

## If You're a Partner/Self-Employed
- **Solo 401(k)** - Can contribute up to ~$69,000 annually (2024)
- **SEP-IRA** - Up to 25% of compensation, simpler administration
- **Defined Benefit Plan** - Can shelter very large amounts annually

## Additional Vehicles
- **Backdoor Roth IRA** - Useful given likely income limits
- **Health Savings Account (HSA)** - Triple tax advantage
- **Deferred Compensation Plans** - If your firm offers them
- **Taxable brokerage accounts** - For flexibility beyond tax-advantaged limits

## Key Considerations
- **Tax diversification** across traditional, Roth, and taxable accounts
- **Asset allocation** appropriate to your timeline
- **Malpractice/liability protection** for assets
- Work with a **fee-only financial advisor** and **CPA** familiar with legal professionals

## Worth Noting
Your firm's structure (partnership vs. employee) significantly affects your best options.
