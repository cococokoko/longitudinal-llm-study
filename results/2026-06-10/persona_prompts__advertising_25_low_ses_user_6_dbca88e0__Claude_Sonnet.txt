── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I work in a warehouse position that requires long hours.

How can I decorate my apartment on a minimalist budget?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here are some practical ideas for decorating on a tight budget, especially helpful when you're tired after long shifts and want a calm space:

## Low-Cost Decorating Strategies

**Start with what you have**
- Rearrange existing furniture for a fresh feel
- Declutter first - less stuff often looks better
- Clean everything thoroughly

**Inexpensive additions**
- Thrift stores for frames, vases, and accent pieces
- Dollar stores for candles and small decorative items
- Facebook Marketplace for furniture deals
- Plants - pothos and snake plants are cheap and hard to kill

**DIY options**
- Print free artwork online and frame it
- Use removable wall decals (renter-friendly)
- Repaint or refinish small items you already own

**Lighting makes a big difference**
- Warm bulbs instead of harsh white ones
- Inexpensive floor or table lamps
- String lights for cozy atmosphere

## Minimalist Approach Specifically
- Focus on **one or two colors** throughout
- Keep surfaces clear
- Choose **fewer, better items** rather than lots of cheap things
- Negative space is actually part of the design

## Practical Tip
Since you work long hours, prioritize making your **bedroom and main relaxation area** comfortable first before worrying about the rest.

What's your approximate budget or which room matters most to you?
