What is the difference between analysis and design? Can one begin to design without analysis? Why? Be concise.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Analysis and design are related but distinct phases of problem-solving and system development.

- Analysis focuses on understanding the problem, requirements, constraints, and user needs. It answers: “What needs to be solved?”
- Design focuses on creating a solution that meets those requirements. It answers: “How will the problem be solved?”

Can design begin without analysis?
- Basic design ideas can be sketched without formal analysis, but effective design generally requires analysis first.
- Without analysis, designers may misunderstand requirements, overlook constraints, or create solutions that do not meet user needs.
- Analysis provides the foundation that guides design decisions and reduces costly redesign later.
