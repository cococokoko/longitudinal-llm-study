── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How can I learn to cook healthy meals quickly?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Learning to cook healthy meals quickly is mostly about simplifying your approach and building a small set of reliable skills.

Here are some practical steps:

• Master 5–10 basic meals
  - Instead of learning hundreds of recipes, learn a handful that you enjoy.
  - Examples:
    - Stir-fry with vegetables and protein
    - Sheet-pan chicken and vegetables
    - Omelet with vegetables
    - Grain bowl (rice, beans, vegetables, sauce)
    - Pasta with vegetables and lean protein
    - Soup or chili

• Learn a few core cooking techniques
  - Sautéing
  - Roasting
  - Boiling grains and pasta
  - Making simple sauces and dressings
  - Once you understand techniques, you can improvise with whatever ingredients you have.

• Keep healthy staples on hand
  - Proteins: eggs, chicken, fish, tofu, beans, Greek yogurt
  - Vegetables: frozen vegetables are convenient and nutritious
  - Carbohydrates: rice, oats, whole-grain pasta, potatoes
  - Flavorings: olive oil, garlic, onions, herbs, spices, soy sauce, vinegar

• Use the "protein + vegetable + carbohydrate" formula
  - This creates balanced meals without needing a recipe.
  - Example:
    - Grilled chicken + broccoli + rice
    - Tofu + mixed vegetables + noodles
    - Salmon + roasted vegetables + potatoes

• Practice knife skills
  - Faster chopping significantly reduces cooking time.
  - Learn how to safely dice onions, slice vegetables, and mince garlic.

• Meal prep strategically
  - Cook ingredients, not necessarily full meals.
  - Prepare a batch of rice, roasted vegetables, and protein that can be mixed into different meals throughout the week.

• Choose recipes with short ingredient lists
  - Start with recipes that use 5–10 ingredients and take 30 minutes or less.
  - Complexity does not always improve nutrition or taste.

• Use time-saving tools
  - Sheet pans
  - Air fryer
  - Rice cooker
  - Slow cooker or pressure cooker
  - Frozen pre-cut vegetables

• Follow reliable learning sources
  - YouTube channels that teach techniques, not just recipes
  - Beginner-friendly cookbooks
  - Cooking apps with step-by-step instructions

• Cook regularly
  - Even cooking three or four times a week for a month will build confidence much faster than reading recipes without practicing.

A simple beginner goal is to learn one healthy breakfast, two lunches, and three dinners that you can cook in 20–30 minutes. Once those become automatic, expanding your skills becomes much easier.
