── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I am a graduate student in Marxist theory, and I want to write a thesis on Gorz. Can you help me think of some new ideas?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Absolutely. André Gorz is an unusually fertile figure for thesis work because he sits at the intersection of Marxism, existentialism, ecology, labor theory, technology studies, and political strategy. Many discussions still treat these themes separately, which leaves room for original research.

Here are several directions that may generate novel thesis ideas.

1. Gorz as a theorist of post-work before the digital economy

Common interpretations present Gorz as a critic of work and advocate of reduced labor time. But much contemporary post-work literature (Srnicek, Williams, Bastani, etc.) rarely engages deeply with Gorz's theory of autonomy.

Possible research question:
- How does Gorz's distinction between autonomous and heteronomous activity differ from contemporary post-work theories centered on automation?

Potential argument:
- Gorz's goal was not simply less work, but the expansion of spheres of life beyond economic rationality. This may provide a critique of contemporary techno-utopian post-work politics.

Relevant texts:
- Critique of Economic Reason
- Paths to Paradise
- Reclaiming Work

2. Gorz and ecological Marxism: beyond productivism

Gorz developed ecological concerns earlier than many Marxists. Yet he is often overshadowed by thinkers such as John Bellamy Foster, James O'Connor, or Kohei Saito.

Possible research question:
- Does Gorz offer an alternative ecological Marxism that is less focused on production and more focused on autonomy and needs?

Potential argument:
- Gorz shifts ecological critique from the contradiction between capital and nature to a critique of socially manufactured needs and consumerism.

This could contribute to debates on:
- Degrowth
- Sustainable socialism
- Ecological planning

3. Gorz and AI: the crisis of labor-value

Gorz's later writings anticipated a transition toward knowledge-based production and the declining centrality of labor-time.

Possible research question:
- Can Gorz's analysis of immaterial labor help explain the political economy of generative AI?

Potential argument:
- Gorz foresaw a contradiction in which knowledge becomes a productive force that resists commodification, creating tensions for capitalism that AI intensifies.

You could place him in dialogue with:
- Marx's "general intellect" passages
- Antonio Negri
- Yann Moulier-Boutang
- Contemporary AI political economy

4. Gorz's critique of identity-based politics

This topic is less explored.

Gorz consistently emphasized autonomy and self-production rather than fixed social identities.

Possible research question:
- What resources does Gorz provide for thinking beyond both class reductionism and identity essentialism?

Potential argument:
- Gorz develops a concept of political subjectivity that is neither purely class-based nor identity-based, but grounded in practical autonomy.

This could connect to:
- Recognition theory
- Contemporary social movements
- Post-Marxism

5. Existential Marxism in Gorz

Many scholars discuss Sartre's influence, but fewer examine how Gorz transformed existentialism into social theory.

Possible research question:
- How does Gorz reconcile existential freedom with structural constraints under capitalism?

Potential argument:
- Gorz's concept of autonomy serves as a mediation between existential freedom and Marxist social determination.

This would engage:
- Sartre
- Marx
- Lukács
- Critical Theory

6. Gorz as a critic of economic reason

One of the most ambitious projects would be to reconstruct Gorz's critique of economic rationality as a whole.

Possible research question:
- Is Gorz's Critique of Economic Reason an alternative to both neoliberalism and traditional socialist planning?

Potential argument:
- Gorz challenges the assumption that social life should be organized through economic calculation, whether capitalist or state-socialist.

This has relevance for:
- Market socialism
- Democratic planning
- Commons-based production

7. Gorz and the politics of universal basic income

Gorz's views on income guarantees evolved over time.

Possible research question:
- How does Gorz's defense of a social income differ from contemporary liberal defenses of UBI?

Potential argument:
- For Gorz, income guarantees were meant to decommodify labor and expand autonomy, not merely alleviate poverty.

This could be compared with:
- Philippe Van Parijs
- Basic income debates
- Degrowth economics

8. The concept of needs in Gorz

This is surprisingly understudied.

Possible research question:
- How does Gorz distinguish autonomous needs from capitalist-produced needs?

Potential argument:
- The theory of needs is the hidden foundation linking his ecological, political, and economic writings.

This topic could bridge:
- Marx
- Marcuse
- Illich
- Contemporary consumption studies

9. Gorz and the commons

Gorz's later work on knowledge and immaterial production can be reread through contemporary commons theory.

Possible research question:
- Does Gorz anticipate current theories of digital commons and peer production?

Potential argument:
- Gorz identifies forms of wealth that cannot easily be measured by labor-time and therefore challenge capitalist property relations.

- Elinor Ostrom
