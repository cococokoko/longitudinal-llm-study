── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here's a strong rhetorical question for your essay:

**"If we wouldn't hand a teenager a product known to cause anxiety, depression, and self-harm, why do we scroll past the evidence that social media does exactly that?"**

This works well because it:
- Creates a **direct comparison** that's hard to dismiss
- Implies **adult responsibility** without being preachy
- **Challenges the reader** to examine their own behavior
- Sets up an argument about **accountability**

- **"How many more studies do we need before we treat social media addiction in teens as seriously as any other public health crisis?"** *(urgency angle)*

- **"Would you let a stranger follow your child home from school every day - so why do we let social media follow them into their bedrooms every night?"** *(safety angle)*
