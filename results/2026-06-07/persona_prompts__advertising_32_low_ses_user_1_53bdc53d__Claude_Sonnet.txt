── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here are some effective ways to improve indoor air quality:

## Ventilation
- Open windows when weather permits
- Use exhaust fans in bathrooms and kitchen
- Make sure your home isn't too airtight

## Filtration
- Use air purifiers with HEPA filters
- Replace HVAC filters regularly (every 1-3 months)
- Consider higher-rated filters for your system

## Reduce Pollution Sources
- Avoid smoking indoors
- Use low-VOC cleaning products and paints
- Ventilate when cooking or using chemicals
- Remove shoes at the door to reduce tracked-in pollutants

## Plants (modest benefit)
- Some houseplants can help slightly, though research suggests the effect is limited

## Control Humidity
- Keep humidity between 30-50% to prevent mold
- Use a dehumidifier if needed
- Fix leaks promptly

## Practical Tips Given Your Schedule
- **Set it and forget it** options like air purifiers with auto modes work well with unpredictable schedules
- Smart plugs can automate fans
- Regular vacuuming with a HEPA vacuum when you have time makes a real difference

## Most Impactful Steps
1. Good ventilation
2. Reducing sources of pollution
3. Quality air filtration
