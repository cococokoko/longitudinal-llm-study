Here are some practical recommendations for starting to learn coding:

## Choose a First Language
- **Python** is widely recommended for beginners - readable syntax, versatile, strong job market
- **JavaScript** is good if you're interested in web development specifically

## Structured Starting Points
- **Free options:** freeCodeCamp, The Odin Project, CS50 (Harvard's free course)
- **Paid platforms:** Codecademy, Coursera, Udemy (often on sale)
- **Books:** "Automate the Boring Stuff with Python" is genuinely excellent

## Key Principles
- **Build things** rather than just following tutorials - projects stick better
- **Expect frustration** - debugging is a normal part of the process, not a sign you're failing
- **Consistency matters more than intensity** - 30 minutes daily beats occasional marathons

## Practical Tips
- Start with problems you actually care about solving
- Learn to read error messages rather than just copying solutions
- Find a community (Reddit's r/learnprogramming, local meetups)

## Honest Expectations
- Basic competency takes months of regular practice
- The learning curve is real but manageable

Given your academic background, you might find CS50 particularly engaging - it's rigorous and well-structured. What draws you to coding? That might help narrow down the best approach.
