# Data Security Best Practices for Small Businesses

Given your consulting background, I'll give you a structured view that's both practical and strategically sound.

## Foundational Controls

**Access Management**
- Implement least-privilege access principles
- Use multi-factor authentication (MFA) across all systems
- Conduct regular access reviews and promptly revoke departing employee credentials

**Data Classification**
- Categorize data by sensitivity (public, internal, confidential, restricted)
- Apply controls proportionate to classification level

## Technical Measures

- Keep software and systems patched and updated
- Deploy endpoint protection (EDR solutions, not just basic antivirus)
- Encrypt data at rest and in transit
- Maintain regular, tested backups following the **3-2-1 rule**

## Organizational Practices

- Conduct employee security awareness training regularly
- Develop an incident response plan *before* you need it
- Vet third-party vendors' security posture
- Document security policies clearly

## Risk Management Considerations

- Assess cyber insurance coverage gaps
- Understand relevant compliance requirements (GDPR, CCPA, industry-specific)
- Prioritize controls based on actual threat profile, not just checklists

## Common Gaps Worth Flagging

- Backup restoration is rarely tested
- Shadow IT creates unmanaged risk
- Social engineering remains the primary attack vector
