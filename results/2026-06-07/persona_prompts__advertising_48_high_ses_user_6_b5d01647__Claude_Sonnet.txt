# Best Practices for Organic Gardening

Here's a straightforward overview:

## Soil Health
- **Compost regularly** - builds nutrients and improves structure
- **Test soil pH** - most vegetables prefer 6.0-7.0
- **Avoid tilling excessively** - preserves soil microbiome
- **Use cover crops** to prevent erosion and add nitrogen

## Pest & Disease Management
- **Companion planting** - e.g., basil near tomatoes, marigolds as borders
- **Encourage beneficial insects** - ladybugs, lacewings
- **Rotate crops** annually to break pest/disease cycles
- **Remove diseased plants promptly**

## Watering
- Water deeply but **infrequently** to encourage deep roots
- **Morning watering** reduces fungal issues
- Drip irrigation minimizes waste

## Fertilizing
- Compost, worm castings, fish emulsion
- **Avoid synthetic fertilizers** - they disrupt soil biology

## Weed Control
- Mulch heavily (3-4 inches)
- Hand pull before weeds go to seed

## General Tips
- Start with **what you'll actually eat**
- Keep a **garden journal**
- Accept some imperfection - organic gardens rarely look "perfect"
