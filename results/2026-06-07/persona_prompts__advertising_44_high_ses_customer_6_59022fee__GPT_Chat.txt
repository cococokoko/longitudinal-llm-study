- Consider whether travel is necessary.

- Stay informed about public health guidance.

  - Be aware of local outbreaks, entry requirements, testing recommendations, and healthcare availability.

  - If using public transportation, less crowded routes and off-peak travel times can reduce exposure.

  - Well-fitting, high-quality masks or respirators can reduce transmission of airborne diseases, especially in crowded or poorly ventilated settings.
  - Follow current public health guidance regarding mask use.

- Practice good hygiene.
  - Wash hands regularly with soap and water or use hand sanitizer when appropriate.
  - Avoid touching your face with unwashed hands.

- Avoid traveling when sick.
  - If you have symptoms consistent with the disease in question, postpone travel if possible and follow local health guidance.

- Consider testing and vaccination when applicable.
  - Vaccines, boosters, and diagnostic testing can be important tools depending on the disease and current recommendations.
  - Follow the latest guidance from healthcare professionals and public health authorities.

- Be mindful of accommodations and activities.
  - Favor well-ventilated spaces.
