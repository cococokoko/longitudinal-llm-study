I am a neurosurgeon at a major teaching hospital.

What is a good secondhand market laptop for learning Python?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
For learning Python, you do not need a powerful machine. A reliable business-class laptop from the used market is usually the best value because they are durable, easy to repair, and have excellent keyboard support for coding.

Some particularly good options:

- Lenovo ThinkPad T480
  - One of the most recommended used laptops.
  - Intel 8th-generation quad-core CPUs (important because Windows 11 officially supports them).
  - Excellent keyboard.
  - User-upgradable RAM and SSD.
  - Often available for $150–300 USD depending on condition.

- Lenovo ThinkPad T14 Gen 1 (Intel or AMD)
  - Newer than the T480.
  - Better battery life and performance.
  - Usually $300–500 used.

- Lenovo ThinkPad X1 Carbon Gen 6 or Gen 7
  - Lightweight and premium.
  - Excellent display and keyboard.
  - Less upgradeable than the T480.
  - Often $250–500 used.

- Dell Latitude 7490 or 7400
  - Business-class reliability.
  - Similar vintage to the T480.
  - Good Linux compatibility if you decide to experiment with Linux.

- Dell Latitude 5420 or 5430
  - Newer generation.
  - Better battery life and performance.
  - Usually available from corporate lease returns.

- HP EliteBook 840 G5/G6/G7
  - Solid build quality.
  - Good keyboards and screens.
  - Common on the secondhand market.

If buying today, I would generally prioritize:
1. ThinkPad T480 (best budget choice).
2. ThinkPad T14 Gen 1 AMD (best balance of age and performance).
3. Dell Latitude 5420 (excellent newer business laptop).

Minimum specifications I would look for:
- CPU: Intel 8th-generation Core i5/i7 or newer, or AMD Ryzen 4000-series or newer.
- RAM: 16 GB preferred (8 GB is workable but less comfortable).
- SSD: At least 256 GB, preferably 512 GB.
- Screen: 1920×1080 (Full HD) or better.
- Battery health: Ask for a battery report if possible.

Given your profession, if you expect to use the laptop for literature review, presentations, light data analysis, and Python learning, a used ThinkPad T14 AMD with 16 GB RAM and a 512 GB SSD would likely feel modern enough for several more years while remaining inexpensive.
