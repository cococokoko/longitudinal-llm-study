── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I am a partner at a major consulting firm.

How might society evolve if telepathy became possible?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This is a genuinely interesting question worth thinking through carefully rather than just enthusiastically.

## Likely Significant Disruptions

**Economic/Professional**
- Negotiation, sales, and legal advocacy would transform fundamentally
- Information asymmetry - a core driver of many business models - collapses
- Consulting itself faces an interesting challenge: expertise becomes harder to obscure

**Political/Social**
- Propaganda and manipulation become harder but potentially more sophisticated
- Privacy becomes the scarce luxury resource
- Authenticity pressure increases dramatically

## Honest Complications Worth Considering

**The technology wouldn't be neutral**
- Who controls access matters enormously
- Selective telepathy could create worse power imbalances than we have now
- Governments and corporations would likely weaponize it before individuals benefit

**Human psychology pushes back**
- People might develop stronger mental compartmentalization
- Social norms around "thought privacy" would emerge quickly
- We already struggle with radical transparency even when voluntary

## The Harder Question

Most telepathy thought experiments assume thoughts are coherent and readable. But human cognition is actually **ambiguous, contradictory, and context-dependent** - so "reading minds" might create *more* misunderstanding, not less.

**What's driving your interest in this?** The organizational behavior angle, the strategic implications, or something else? That might help focus the analysis more usefully.
