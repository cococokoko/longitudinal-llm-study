# Movie Review: *The Ledger of Small Things* (2024)

**★★★★½**

Director Amara Osei-Bonsu has crafted something quietly devastating in this drama about a woman holding together four generations under one roof while her own ambitions slowly collect dust in a drawer.

The film never announces itself as important, which is precisely what makes it so. There are no dramatic breakdowns, no triumphant speeches — just the accumulation of grocery runs, insurance calls, school pickups, and the particular exhaustion of being the person everyone assumes will simply *handle it*.

Lead actress Priya Chandrasekaran delivers a performance built entirely from small gestures. A pause before answering the phone. The way she eats standing up.

The screenplay resists making anyone a villain, which feels honest and occasionally frustrating in the best way. The people she supports love her genuinely. That doesn't make the weight lighter.

The final scene — involving a birthday cake and a calendar — will mean different things depending on who you are when you watch it.

**Recommended especially for** anyone who has ever quietly rearranged their own life to make room for someone else's needs, and wondered if anyone noticed.

*They noticed. They just didn't know how to say it.*
