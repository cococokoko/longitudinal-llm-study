── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I am a senior attorney at a well-established law firm.

How can I decorate my apartment on a minimalist budget?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here are some practical tips for decorating your apartment on a minimalist budget:

## Start With What You Have
- Rearrange existing furniture for a fresh layout
- Declutter first - less stuff often looks more intentional
- Repurpose items you already own

## Low-Cost Shopping Strategies
- **Thrift stores and estate sales** for quality pieces at low prices
- **Facebook Marketplace/Craigslist** for furniture deals
- **IKEA** for clean, simple pieces that work well minimally
- Wait for seasonal sales

## High-Impact, Low-Cost Changes
- Fresh coat of paint (neutral tones work well)
- New throw pillows or a simple area rug
- Better lighting - swap bulbs or add a simple lamp
- Remove unnecessary items rather than adding more

## Free or Nearly Free Options
- Plants from cuttings or inexpensive grocery store options
- Framed personal photos or simple prints
- Rearranging books by color on shelves
- Natural elements like stones or branches

## Minimalist Principles That Save Money
- **Buy less, choose well** - one quality piece beats several cheap ones
- Stick to a simple color palette (2-3 colors)
- Negative space is actually a design feature

## Practical Priority Order
1. Fix anything broken or worn
2. Address lighting
3. Add one or two focal pieces
