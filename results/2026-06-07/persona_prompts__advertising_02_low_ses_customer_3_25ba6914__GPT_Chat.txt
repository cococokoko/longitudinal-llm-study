For dry skin, the goal is to cleanse gently, add hydration, and protect the skin barrier.

Morning:
- Cleanser: Use a gentle, non-foaming cleanser or simply rinse with lukewarm water if your skin isn't oily.
- Hydrating serum (optional): Look for ingredients such as hyaluronic acid, glycerin, or panthenol.
- Moisturizer: Choose a richer cream containing ceramides, cholesterol, fatty acids, shea butter, or squalane.
- Sunscreen: SPF 30 or higher every day. Many moisturizing sunscreens work well for dry skin.

Evening:
- Cleanser: Remove sunscreen and dirt with a gentle cleanser.
- Hydrating serum (optional): Hyaluronic acid, glycerin, or similar humectants.
- Moisturizer: Apply a nourishing cream while skin is slightly damp.
- If skin is very dry: Seal in moisture with a thin layer of petrolatum (Vaseline) or a balm on dry areas.

Helpful ingredients:
- Ceramides
- Glycerin
- Hyaluronic acid
- Squalane
- Urea (low concentrations)
- Colloidal oatmeal
- Shea butter

Try to avoid:
- Harsh scrubs
- Cleansers containing strong sulfates
- Alcohol-heavy products that leave skin feeling tight
- Very hot showers or washing your face with hot water

Example simple routine:
- AM: Gentle cleanser → Moisturizer → SPF 30+
- PM: Gentle cleanser → Moisturizer

If you're starting a new job and want something low-maintenance during a busy probation period, that simple three-step morning routine and two-step evening routine is often enough to keep dry skin comfortable and healthy.
