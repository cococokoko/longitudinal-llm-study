── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I am a senior attorney at a well-established law firm.

How can I improve my credit score quickly?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here are some effective ways to improve your credit score relatively quickly:

## Highest Impact Actions

- **Pay down credit card balances** - Reducing your credit utilization ratio (below 30%, ideally below 10%) can show results within one billing cycle
- **Pay all bills on time** - Payment history is the largest factor (~35% of your score)
- **Request a credit limit increase** - This improves utilization without paying down debt

## Moderate Impact

- **Dispute inaccurate items** on your credit reports (check all three bureaus - Equifax, Experian, TransUnion)
- **Become an authorized user** on a responsible person's account
- **Pay down installment loans** to reduce overall debt

## Things to Avoid

- Opening multiple new accounts quickly (hard inquiries lower scores temporarily)
- Closing old accounts (reduces available credit and history length)
- Missing any payments

## Practical Timeline

- **1-2 months:** Utilization improvements show up
- **3-6 months:** Consistent payment history builds momentum
- **12+ months:** More significant rebuilding if starting from a lower score

## Free Resources

- AnnualCreditReport.com for free reports
- Many credit cards offer free score monitoring
