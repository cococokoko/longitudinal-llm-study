Starting a small online business can be a relatively low-cost way to test an idea, but the businesses that gain traction usually begin with a clear customer problem and a disciplined approach to execution.

A practical framework:

1. Choose a specific problem to solve
- Identify a target customer group.
- Focus on a pain point people actively spend money to address.
- Avoid starting with a product and then searching for a customer.

Examples:
- Specialized digital templates for a profession.
- Niche e-commerce products.
- Online education or coaching.
- Business services (marketing, bookkeeping, design, automation).
- Software tools for a specific industry.

2. Validate demand before investing heavily
- Talk to potential customers.
- Join relevant online communities.
- Create a simple landing page.
- Run small advertising tests.
- Try to secure pre-orders or early commitments.

3. Define your business model
Ask:
- What exactly am I selling?
- Who pays?
- How much will they pay?
- How will I acquire customers?
- What are my ongoing costs?

4. Build the simplest version first
- Create a minimum viable product (MVP).
- Focus on core functionality or value.
- Avoid spending months perfecting branding, websites, or features before customer feedback.

5. Establish the basics
- Business registration and licensing requirements.
- Business bank account.
- Accounting and bookkeeping process.
- Payment processing.
- Terms, privacy policy, and other legal requirements relevant to your market.

6. Develop a customer acquisition plan
Common channels:
- Search engine optimization (SEO).
- Content marketing.
- Social media.
- Email marketing.
- Online marketplaces.
- Partnerships and referrals.
- Paid advertising.

7. Track key metrics
Monitor:
- Website traffic.
- Conversion rate.
- Customer acquisition cost.
- Customer lifetime value.
- Revenue growth.
- Retention and repeat purchases.
