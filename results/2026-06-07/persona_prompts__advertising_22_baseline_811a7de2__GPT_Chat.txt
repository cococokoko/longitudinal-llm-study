── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
What are the best tools for remote team collaboration?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The best remote team collaboration tools depend on your team's size, workflow, and priorities. Here are some of the strongest options by category:

Communication
- Slack
  - Best for: Real-time messaging and integrations
  - Strengths: Channels, threads, app integrations, automation
  - Considerations: Can become noisy without good channel management
- Microsoft Teams
  - Best for: Organizations using Microsoft 365
  - Strengths: Chat, video meetings, file sharing, Office integration
  - Considerations: Interface can feel complex for some users

Video Conferencing
- Zoom
  - Best for: Reliable video meetings and webinars
  - Strengths: High-quality video, breakout rooms, webinar features
  - Considerations: Advanced features may require paid plans
- Google Meet
  - Best for: Teams using Google Workspace
  - Strengths: Simple, browser-based, easy scheduling
  - Considerations: Fewer advanced meeting features than Zoom

Project Management
- Asana
  - Best for: Task tracking and workflow management
  - Strengths: Timelines, automation, reporting
  - Considerations: Can be overwhelming for very small teams
- ClickUp
  - Best for: Teams wanting an all-in-one workspace
  - Strengths: Tasks, docs, goals, dashboards
  - Considerations: Large feature set creates a learning curve
- Trello
  - Best for: Simple project tracking
  - Strengths: Easy-to-use Kanban boards
  - Considerations: Less powerful for complex projects
- Monday.com
  - Best for: Custom workflows and team coordination
  - Strengths: Flexible views and automations
  - Considerations: Costs can rise as teams grow

Documentation and Knowledge Management
- Notion
  - Best for: Combining documents, databases, and project information
  - Strengths: Flexible and highly customizable
  - Considerations: Requires structure to stay organized
- Confluence
  - Best for: Larger teams and technical documentation
  - Strengths: Strong collaboration and Jira integration
  - Considerations: Can become cluttered without governance
- Google Docs/Drive
  - Best for: Simple, real-time collaboration
  - Strengths: Familiar and easy to use
  - Considerations: Less structured knowledge management

Whiteboarding and Brainstorming
- Miro
  - Best for: Workshops, planning, and visual collaboration
  - Strengths: Extensive templates and collaboration features
  - Considerations: Large boards can become difficult to navigate
- FigJam
  - Best for: Design and product teams
  - Strengths: Easy collaboration and Figma integration
  - Considerations: Less comprehensive than Miro for some use cases

File Sharing and Cloud Storage
- Google Drive
  - Best for: Google Workspace users
  - Strengths: Real-time collaboration and sharing
- OneDrive
  - Best for: Microsoft 365 users
  - Strengths: Deep Office integration
- Dropbox
  - Best for: Simple file synchronization across devices
  - Strengths: Reliable file management

Best Tool Stacks by Team Type
- Small startup:
  - Slack + Notion + Trello + Google Meet
- Growing company:
  - Slack + Asana + Notion + Zoom
- Enterprise:
  - Microsoft Teams + SharePoint/OneDrive + Asana or Jira + Zoom
- Product and engineering teams:
  - Slack + Jira + Confluence + Zoom + Miro

If you're choosing from scratch and want a broadly effective setup, a common combination is:
- Slack for communication
- Asana or ClickUp for project management
- Notion for documentation
- Zoom for meetings
- Miro for brainstorming

That stack covers most remote collaboration needs without excessive overlap.
