── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
That's a genuinely interesting philosophical question, and your work situation actually connects to it in a real way.

## The short answer: Yes, it's impossible to know everything

Here's why this is pretty well established:

**Practical limits**
- There's simply too much information - it grows faster than any person can absorb
- Time and attention are finite
- Your unpredictable schedule is actually a good real-world example of how circumstances limit what you can even *plan* to learn

**Deeper logical limits**
- Gödel's incompleteness theorems showed even mathematics has truths that can't be proven within a system
- Some things are genuinely uncertain or unknowable in principle
- Knowing everything would require knowing future events that haven't happened yet

**What's worth considering though:**
- "Knowing everything" and "knowing enough" are very different goals
- Expertise in specific areas is genuinely achievable
- Your work experience likely gives you real knowledge others lack - about managing stress, unpredictability, customer behavior, physical demands

## The honest bottom line
Anyone claiming to know everything is demonstrably wrong. But that's actually somewhat freeing - the goal isn't total knowledge, it's **useful, honest understanding**.

Is there a specific area you're thinking about learning or wondering about? That might be a more productive angle.
