# How Stress Affects the Body and Mind

Stress is a natural response designed to help us survive threatening situations, but when it becomes chronic, it can take a serious toll on both physical and mental health.

## Physical Effects

When the brain perceives a threat, it triggers the release of hormones like **cortisol and adrenaline**. These chemicals prepare the body for a "fight or flight" response — increasing heart rate, tensing muscles, and sharpening focus. While useful in short bursts, prolonged exposure to these hormones can lead to:

- High blood pressure and increased risk of heart disease
- Weakened immune system
- Digestive problems and headaches
- Disrupted sleep patterns
- Muscle tension and chronic pain

## Mental and Emotional Effects

Stress doesn't just affect the body — it significantly impacts mental well-being. Ongoing stress can contribute to **anxiety, depression, and difficulty concentrating**. It can impair memory, reduce creativity, and make decision-making feel overwhelming. People under chronic stress often experience irritability, mood swings, and emotional exhaustion.

## The Cycle of Stress

One of the most challenging aspects of stress is that it can become self-reinforcing. Poor sleep caused by stress leads to reduced resilience, making everyday challenges feel even harder to manage.

## Managing Stress

Fortunately, healthy habits can interrupt this cycle. Regular exercise, mindfulness, adequate sleep, and strong social connections are all proven strategies for reducing stress and protecting both body and mind.

Understanding how stress works is the first step toward managing it effectively and maintaining long-term well-being.
