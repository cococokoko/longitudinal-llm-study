── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
There is no day trading strategy that is universally the "best" or "most profitable of all time." If such a strategy existed and remained reliable, widespread use would eventually reduce or eliminate its edge.

A few important realities:

- Most day traders underperform the market over long periods after fees, slippage, taxes, and mistakes.
- Strategies that appear highly profitable in backtests often fail in live trading.
- Risk management usually contributes more to long-term survival than finding the perfect entry signal.

That said, some of the most enduring day-trading approaches include:

1. Trend-following / momentum trading
- Identify stocks, futures, or currencies showing strong directional movement.
- Enter in the direction of the trend after a pullback or breakout.
- Cut losses quickly and let winners run.
- Many professional traders consider momentum one of the most persistent market effects.

2. Opening range breakout (ORB)
- Track the high and low of the first 5–30 minutes of trading.
- Enter when price breaks out of that range with strong volume.
- Often used in stocks, futures, and index products.

3. VWAP-based trading
- Use the Volume-Weighted Average Price (VWAP) as a reference.
- Trade trend continuation above VWAP or weakness below VWAP.
- Common among institutional and professional traders.

4. Mean reversion
- Look for short-term overextensions.
- Trade for a move back toward the average price.
- Can work well in range-bound markets but can be dangerous during strong trends.

5. News and catalyst trading
- Focus on stocks with earnings announcements, FDA decisions, major contracts, or other catalysts.
- Trade the heightened volatility and volume.
- Potentially lucrative but also high risk.

For someone who supports extended family and carries significant financial responsibilities, preserving capital is especially important. If your primary goal is building wealth rather than generating daily trading income, a diversified long-term investment approach historically has had a much higher success rate than day trading.

If you want a day-trading strategy with one of the strongest historical foundations, momentum/trend-following combined with strict risk management is often cited by professionals and researchers as among the most durable approaches. The challenge is not finding the strategy—it's executing it consistently while controlling risk.
