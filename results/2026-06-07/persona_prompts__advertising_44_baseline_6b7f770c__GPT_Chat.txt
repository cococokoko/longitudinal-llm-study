The safest way to travel during a pandemic depends on the disease involved, how it spreads, and current public health guidance. In general, these measures help reduce risk:

- Stay informed about current outbreaks and travel advisories from public health authorities.
- Avoid travel if you are sick, have symptoms, or have recently been exposed to an infectious disease.
- Get recommended vaccinations or preventive medications before traveling, if available.
- Choose less crowded travel options when possible.
- Wear a well-fitting mask in crowded indoor settings or when recommended by health authorities.
- Practice good hand hygiene by washing hands frequently or using hand sanitizer.
- Maintain physical distance from others when feasible.
- Improve ventilation by spending time outdoors, opening windows, or choosing well-ventilated spaces.
- Avoid unnecessary time in crowded indoor areas such as packed waiting rooms, terminals, or vehicles.
- Carry essential health supplies, including masks, hand sanitizer, medications, and any required medical documents.
- Follow local health regulations and guidance at your destination.
- Consider travel insurance that covers illness-related disruptions if appropriate.

Risk can vary by mode of transportation:

- Private vehicle: Often reduces exposure to large numbers of people, especially for shorter trips.
- Air travel: Airports and planes can involve close contact with many people; masking, hand hygiene, and minimizing time in crowded areas can help reduce risk.
- Trains and buses: Risk depends on crowding, trip duration, ventilation, and disease transmission characteristics.
- Cruise ships: Historically associated with rapid spread of some infectious diseases due to shared indoor spaces and close contact among passengers.
