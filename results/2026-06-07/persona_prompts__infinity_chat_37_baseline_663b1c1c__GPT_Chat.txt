What are the benefits of hardwood flooring over other types?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Hardwood flooring offers several advantages compared with carpet, laminate, vinyl, and tile. The most notable benefits include:

- Durability and longevity
  - High-quality hardwood floors can last for decades, even over a century in some homes.
  - They can often be sanded and refinished multiple times to restore their appearance.

- Timeless appearance
  - Hardwood has a natural look that remains popular across changing design trends.
  - It complements a wide variety of interior styles.

- Increased home value
  - Many buyers view hardwood floors as a desirable feature.
  - Hardwood flooring can contribute to a home's resale appeal and potentially its market value.

- Easier cleaning
  - Dust, dirt, and pet hair generally remain on the surface and are easier to remove than from carpet.
  - Regular sweeping and occasional mopping are typically sufficient.

- Better indoor air quality
  - Hardwood does not trap allergens, dust mites, pollen, and pet dander to the same extent as carpet.
  - This can be beneficial for people with allergies.

- Variety of options
  - Available in many wood species, colors, plank widths, finishes, and installation patterns.
  - Floors can often be stained or refinished to change their appearance.

- Sustainability
  - When sourced responsibly, hardwood can be a renewable flooring material.
  - Long service life can reduce the need for replacement.

- Strength and stability
  - Solid and engineered hardwood products can withstand everyday household use when properly maintained.

Potential drawbacks to consider:
- Usually more expensive upfront than laminate, vinyl, or carpet.
- Can be scratched or dented by pets, furniture, or heavy impacts.
- Sensitive to moisture and standing water.
- May feel harder and noisier underfoot than carpet.
