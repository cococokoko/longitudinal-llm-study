── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How do I start a vegetable garden in my backyard?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Starting a vegetable garden is a rewarding project! Here are the key steps:

## Planning
- **Choose a location** with at least 6-8 hours of direct sunlight daily
- **Decide on size** - starting small (4x8 feet) is often wise for beginners
- **Pick your vegetables** - start with easier crops like tomatoes, lettuce, zucchini, beans, or radishes

## Preparing the Bed
- **Clear the area** of grass and weeds
- **Test your soil** - many extension services offer inexpensive testing
- **Amend the soil** with compost to improve drainage and nutrients
- **Consider raised beds** if your native soil is poor quality

## Getting Started
- **Check your growing zone** (USDA hardiness zone) to know timing
- **Start seeds indoors** for crops like tomatoes 6-8 weeks before last frost
- **Direct sow** crops like beans, carrots, and radishes where they'll grow

## Ongoing Care
- Water consistently, typically 1 inch per week
- Mulch to retain moisture and suppress weeds
- Watch for pests and address them early
- Fertilize as needed based on soil test results

## Helpful Resources
- Your local cooperative extension service is excellent for region-specific advice
- Local gardening clubs can offer practical knowledge
