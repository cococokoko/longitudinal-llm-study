── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How to plan an environmentally friendly wedding?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Planning an environmentally friendly wedding involves reducing waste, minimizing emissions, and making thoughtful choices about materials, food, and vendors. Here are some practical ways to do it:

1. Choose a sustainable venue
- Select a venue close to where most guests live to reduce travel emissions.
- Consider outdoor venues, botanical gardens, community halls, or eco-certified venues.
- Hold both the ceremony and reception in the same location to minimize transportation.

2. Keep the guest list intentional
- A smaller guest list generally reduces the environmental impact from food, travel, and resource use.

3. Use digital invitations
- Send invitations, RSVPs, and updates electronically.
- If printed invitations are important to you, use recycled or FSC-certified paper and eco-friendly inks.

4. Select sustainable attire
- Wear a pre-owned, vintage, rented, or ethically made wedding outfit.
- Choose outfits that can be worn again after the wedding.
- Encourage attendants to wear clothing they already own or can reuse.

5. Prioritize local and seasonal food
- Work with caterers who source ingredients locally.
- Serve seasonal produce.
- Consider offering more plant-based menu options, which typically have a lower environmental footprint.
- Plan portions carefully to reduce food waste.

6. Reduce single-use items
- Use reusable plates, glasses, cutlery, linens, and decorations.
- Avoid disposable favors, plastic water bottles, and excessive packaging.

7. Choose eco-friendly flowers and décor
- Use locally grown, seasonal flowers.
- Rent decorations when possible.
- Incorporate potted plants, dried flowers, or reusable décor.
- Avoid floral foam, which can be environmentally harmful.

8. Rethink wedding favors
- Skip favors altogether or choose practical, consumable items such as local honey, seeds, baked goods, or charitable donations made in guests' honor.

9. Plan transportation thoughtfully
- Arrange group transportation such as buses or shuttles.
- Encourage carpooling.
- Provide information about public transit options.

10. Manage waste responsibly
- Set up clearly labeled recycling and composting stations.
- Donate leftover flowers, décor, and unopened food where local regulations allow.
- Work with vendors who have waste-reduction practices.

11. Select environmentally conscious vendors
- Ask vendors about their sustainability efforts.
- Prioritize local businesses to reduce transportation impacts.

12. Consider wedding rings and gifts
- Choose rings made from recycled metals or ethically sourced materials.
- Create a registry focused on experiences, charitable contributions, or items you genuinely need.

13. Offset unavoidable emissions
- If guests will travel long distances, consider supporting credible carbon-reduction or carbon-removal projects as a complement to reducing emissions where possible.

A sustainable wedding doesn't have to be perfect. Focusing on a few high-impact areas—guest travel, food, venue, and waste reduction—can significantly lower the event's environmental footprint while still creating a memorable celebration.
