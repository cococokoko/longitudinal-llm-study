── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I recently started a new job and am still on probation.

What are the best ways to save for retirement?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here are some solid retirement saving strategies to consider:

## Start with the Basics

- **Build an emergency fund first** - 3-6 months of expenses so you don't raid retirement savings
- **Understand your employer's benefits** - check if they offer a 401(k) match once probation ends

## Common Retirement Accounts

- **401(k)/403(b)** - employer-sponsored, often with tax advantages
- **IRA (Traditional)** - tax-deductible contributions, taxes paid at withdrawal
- **Roth IRA** - contributions after tax, but **tax-free growth and withdrawals**
- **HSA** - if you have a high-deductible health plan, this has triple tax benefits

## General Principles

- **Start early** - compound interest rewards time significantly
- **Contribute consistently** - even small amounts matter
- **Get the full employer match** - it's essentially free money
- **Increase contributions gradually** - even 1% more per year helps
- **Keep fees low** - look for index funds with low expense ratios

## Things to Consider in Your Situation

- You may have limited benefits during probation
- Focus on what you **can** control now, like a Roth IRA
- Learn your employer's vesting schedule for matching contributions
