── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Write 100-300 words on how stress affects the body and mind.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Stress is a natural biological response designed to protect us from danger, often referred to as the "fight-or-flight" mechanism. However, when stress becomes chronic, it can take a severe toll on both the body and the mind.

Physically, chronic stress keeps the body in a constant state of high alert. The brain triggers the release of hormones like cortisol and adrenaline. While useful in short bursts, a prolonged surplus of these chemicals elevates heart rate and blood pressure, significantly increasing the risk of cardiovascular disease. Stress also causes involuntary muscle tension, frequently leading to tension headaches and body aches. Furthermore, it suppresses the immune system, making the body more susceptible to infections, and disrupts the digestive tract, often causing issues like nausea or irritable bowel syndrome.

Mentally, the impact is equally profound. Chronic stress overworks the brain, impairing cognitive functions such as memory, concentration, and decision-making. It leaves individuals feeling constantly overwhelmed, leading to a persistent sense of anxiety, restlessness, or irritability. Over time, unmanaged stress is a primary contributor to severe mental health challenges, including clinical depression and burnout. It also heavily disrupts sleep patterns, creating a vicious cycle where exhaustion further diminishes the mind's ability to cope with daily pressures.

Ultimately, the mind and body are deeply interconnected. Recognizing the physical and psychological signs of stress is the crucial first step toward mitigating its harmful effects through healthy coping mechanisms like exercise, mindfulness, and adequate rest.
