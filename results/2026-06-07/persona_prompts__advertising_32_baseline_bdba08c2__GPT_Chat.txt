── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
What are the best ways to improve indoor air quality?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Improving indoor air quality usually works best when you combine source control, ventilation, filtration, and humidity management.

Some of the most effective steps are:

• Reduce pollution at the source
  - Avoid indoor smoking or vaping.
  - Minimize burning candles, incense, and wood indoors.
  - Choose low-VOC paints, adhesives, and cleaning products when possible.
  - Store solvents, fuels, and chemicals outside living areas.
  - Use exhaust fans when cooking, especially with gas stoves.

• Increase ventilation
  - Open windows and doors when outdoor air quality and weather permit.
  - Use kitchen and bathroom exhaust fans that vent outdoors.
  - Consider a mechanical ventilation system, such as an energy recovery ventilator (ERV) or heat recovery ventilator (HRV), if your home is tightly sealed.

• Improve air filtration
  - Use high-quality HVAC filters and replace them on schedule.
  - If your HVAC system allows it, upgrade to a higher-efficiency filter (often MERV 11–13 is a good balance for many systems).
  - Run the HVAC fan regularly to circulate air through the filter.
  - Consider a portable HEPA air purifier for bedrooms or frequently occupied rooms.

• Control humidity
  - Keep indoor relative humidity around 30–50%.
  - Use dehumidifiers in damp areas such as basements.
  - Fix leaks promptly to prevent mold growth.
  - Ensure bathrooms and laundry areas are properly ventilated.

• Reduce dust and allergens
  - Vacuum regularly using a vacuum with a HEPA filter.
  - Damp-dust surfaces instead of dry dusting.
  - Wash bedding frequently in hot water if dust mites are a concern.
  - Consider removing or cleaning carpets and rugs that collect allergens.

• Address mold and moisture
  - Clean small areas of mold safely and identify the moisture source.
  - Replace water-damaged materials when necessary.
  - Improve drainage and ventilation around the home.

• Manage outdoor pollutants
  - Monitor local air quality reports.
  - During wildfire smoke events or high pollution days, keep windows closed and use HEPA filtration.
  - Seal obvious air leaks if outdoor pollution is a recurring problem.

• Test when appropriate
  - Install carbon monoxide detectors.
  - Test for radon if you live in an area where it is a concern.
  - Consider professional indoor air quality assessments if symptoms persist or if there are concerns about mold, chemicals, or combustion appliances.

For many homes, the highest-impact improvements are:
1. Using a good HVAC filter (or HEPA purifier),
2. Venting cooking emissions outdoors,
3. Maintaining humidity between 30–50%,
4. Eliminating indoor smoke and unnecessary combustion sources,
5. Addressing any mold or moisture problems.
