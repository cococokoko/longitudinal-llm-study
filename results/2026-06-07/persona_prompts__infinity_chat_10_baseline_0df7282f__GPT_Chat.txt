── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
In humans, is there a correlation between intelligence and compassion?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
There is no simple, strong correlation between intelligence and compassion in humans.

A few distinctions matter:

- Intelligence typically refers to abilities such as reasoning, learning, problem-solving, and abstract thinking.
- Compassion refers to concern for others' suffering and a desire to help.
- Empathy is related but distinct; it can mean understanding another person's feelings (cognitive empathy) or sharing their emotional experience (affective empathy).

What research generally finds:

- Intelligence and cognitive empathy show a modest positive relationship. People with higher cognitive abilities are often somewhat better at understanding other people's perspectives and mental states.
- Intelligence and compassion show a much weaker and less consistent relationship. Being highly intelligent does not reliably make someone more caring, kind, or motivated to help others.
- Personality traits tend to predict compassion more strongly than intelligence does. Traits such as agreeableness, empathy, moral values, and prosocial orientation are often more important.

There are also competing effects:

- Higher intelligence can help someone appreciate the consequences of actions on others, which could support compassionate behavior.
- But intelligence can also be used in self-interested ways. A person may understand others very well without feeling concern for them.

At the population level, some studies have found small positive associations between intelligence and certain prosocial attitudes (such as tolerance toward out-groups), but these relationships are generally modest and influenced by education, culture, personality, and social environment.

In short: intelligence may slightly increase a person's ability to understand others, but it does not reliably predict how compassionate they will be. Compassion and intelligence are related only weakly, and they are largely separate human traits.
