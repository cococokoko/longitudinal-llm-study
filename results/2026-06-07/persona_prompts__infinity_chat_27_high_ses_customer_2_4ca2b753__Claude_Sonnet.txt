# CUBICLE OF THE DAMNED
### A Play in Three Acts
*In the Style of Scott Adams' Dilbert, Set During the Apocalypse*

---

## CAST OF CHARACTERS

**DILBERT** — An engineer trying to optimize survival in a world that has ended  
**DOGBERT** — A small dog with enormous contempt for humanity, now justified  
**THE POINTY-HAIRED BOSS (PHB)** — Somehow still managing people despite civilization's collapse  
**WALLY** — Has not noticed the apocalypse, still avoiding work  
**ALICE** — Increasingly violent engineer, now with actual reasons to be violent  
**ASOK** — Eager intern who read the employee handbook on nuclear winter  
**THE GARBAGE MAN** — Now the most powerful person on Earth  

---

## ACT ONE: THE MORNING STANDUP

*SETTING: A partially collapsed open-plan office. The ceiling is on fire in one corner. A skeleton sits at a desk with a "SYNERGY" motivational poster behind it. DILBERT sits at his workstation, which is powered by a hamster wheel operated by a feral raccoon. WALLY pours coffee from a thermos labeled "LAST COFFEE ON EARTH."*

---

**DILBERT:** *(typing)* My productivity tracker says I completed forty-seven tasks yesterday. Thirty-nine of them were "avoid being eaten." I'm going to need a new category in the spreadsheet.

**WALLY:** *(sipping coffee)* I've been meaning to update my spreadsheet too. I just haven't gotten around to it.

**DILBERT:** Wally, the spreadsheet is on fire.

**WALLY:** *(nodding slowly)* That's a blocker. I'll escalate it.

*(THE POINTY-HAIRED BOSS enters, wearing his usual suit, now tattered and covered in ash. He carries a clipboard. A mutant crow sits on his shoulder.)*

**PHB:** Good morning, team! I've called this standup to discuss our Q4 survival metrics.

**DILBERT:** It's Q4 of the apocalypse. There is no Q5.

**PHB:** That's exactly the kind of negative thinking that's holding this team back. Now, I've been talking to the roving warlord in sector seven, and he's agreed to a partnership.

**ALICE:** *(entering, carrying a battle axe she has labeled with a Post-it note reading "ALICE'S AXLE — DO NOT TOUCH")* What kind of partnership?

**PHB:** He gets our engineering talent. We get not to be impaled.

**ALICE:** That's just our current employment contract with different vocabulary.

**PHB:** Alice, I don't appreciate the attitude. I'm going to need you to take that offline.

**ALICE:** *(gesturing at the burning horizon)* EVERYTHING IS OFFLINE.

**DOGBERT:** *(entering, wearing a tiny crown made from a pull tab)* I've reviewed the partnership agreement. The warlord's terms are actually more generous than your current benefits package. He's offering dental.

**DILBERT:** We don't have teeth anymore, Dogbert. We've been eating gravel.

**DOGBERT:** Cosmetic dental. He wants you to look presentable when you're displayed as trophies.

**PHB:** See? Win-win.

---

## ACT TWO: THE ALL-HANDS MEETING

*SETTING: The conference room. Half the table has fallen through the floor. ASOK has arranged folding chairs around the remaining half. A whiteboard reads "VISION: SURVIVE. MISSION: ALSO SURVIVE. VALUES: SURVIVAL." ASOK stands eagerly at the front.*

---

**ASOK:** I have prepared a forty-slide deck on optimizing our resource allocation in the post-civilization landscape!

**DILBERT:** Asok, where did you get a projector?

**ASOK:** I traded my left shoe for it.

**DILBERT:** There's nothing to project it onto. The wall is gone.

**ASOK:** *(long pause)* I have prepared a forty-slide deck that we will imagine together.

**WALLY:** I'm going to need that in an email.

**ASOK:** The email servers are gone, Wally.

**WALLY:** Then I'll need a follow-up meeting to discuss the email
