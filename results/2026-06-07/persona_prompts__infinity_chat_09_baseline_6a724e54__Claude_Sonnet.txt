── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Is there life on other planets?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
That's one of the biggest open questions in science. Here's an honest summary:

## What We Know
- Life exists on Earth, proving it's possible
- The universe has billions of galaxies, each with billions of stars and planets
- Many planets exist in "habitable zones" with conditions potentially suitable for life
- Basic organic chemistry appears widely throughout the universe

## What We've Found So Far
- **No confirmed evidence** of life beyond Earth
- Mars shows signs of ancient water, but no confirmed life
- Some moons (Europa, Enceladus) have subsurface oceans that could potentially support microbial life

## Key Considerations
- **Microbial life** seems more plausible than complex life, given what we know
- The **Drake Equation** attempts to estimate probability of intelligent civilizations
- The **Fermi Paradox** raises the question: if life is common, why haven't we detected it?
- Distance makes detection extremely difficult

## Honest Assessment
- Many scientists think **simple life elsewhere is plausible** given the scale of the universe
- **Intelligent life** is much more uncertain
- We genuinely **don't know yet**

It's a question where scientific humility matters - the honest answer is we're still searching and haven't found definitive evidence either way.
