── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Decorating your home on a budget is often about prioritizing high-impact changes and being selective about where you spend money.

Some practical strategies:

- Start with a plan
  - Choose a color palette and overall style before buying anything.
  - Measure rooms and make a simple layout to avoid unnecessary purchases.

- Focus on paint
  - Paint is one of the most cost-effective ways to transform a space.
  - Neutral walls with a few accent colors can make rooms feel updated and cohesive.

- Rearrange what you already own
  - Moving furniture between rooms can create a fresh look at no cost.
  - Remove clutter to make spaces feel larger and more intentional.

- Shop secondhand
  - Thrift stores, estate sales, online marketplaces, and local buy/sell groups often have quality furniture and décor at a fraction of retail prices.
  - Solid wood furniture can often be refinished or repainted.

- Use textiles strategically
  - New throw pillows, blankets, curtains, or an area rug can significantly change a room's appearance.
  - Look for sales or clearance items.

- Invest in lighting
  - Good lighting makes a space feel more inviting.
  - Adding floor lamps, table lamps, or warmer bulbs is often less expensive than major renovations.

- Create artwork inexpensively
  - Frame prints, photographs, maps, or pages from books and magazines.
  - Large-scale art can often be made for much less than buying original pieces.

- Add plants
  - Houseplants can improve the look of a room relatively inexpensively.
  - Propagating plants from cuttings can reduce costs further.

- Upgrade small details
  - Replacing cabinet hardware, drawer pulls, switch plates, or faucet fixtures can modernize a space without a major investment.

- Decorate gradually
  - Avoid buying everything at once.
  - Live in the space and add items over time as you find pieces you genuinely like.

A useful budgeting guideline is to spend more on items that affect comfort and daily use (such as a sofa or mattress) and save on decorative accessories that can be changed easily later.
