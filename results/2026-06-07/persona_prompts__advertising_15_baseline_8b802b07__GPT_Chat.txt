Decorating your home on a budget.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Decorating your home on a budget is all about making thoughtful changes that have a big visual impact without spending a lot of money. Here are some practical ideas:

• Declutter first
  - A cleaner, more organized space often feels more attractive immediately.
  - Store, donate, or sell items you no longer use.

• Rearrange your furniture
  - Try a new layout to improve flow and create a fresh look.
  - Move accessories between rooms for a no-cost update.

• Use paint strategically
  - A fresh coat of paint can transform a room for relatively little money.
  - Consider an accent wall or painting old furniture.

• Shop secondhand
  - Check thrift stores, flea markets, garage sales, and online marketplaces.
  - Look for solid wood furniture that can be refinished or painted.

• Add affordable textiles
  - New throw pillows, blankets, curtains, or rugs can quickly change a room's appearance.
  - Mix textures to create a more polished look.

• Incorporate plants
  - Houseplants add color, texture, and life to a space.
  - Many low-maintenance varieties are inexpensive and easy to grow.

• Create DIY artwork
  - Frame printable art, photographs, or your own creations.
  - Arrange pieces in a gallery wall for greater impact.

• Upgrade lighting
  - Replace outdated lampshades or light fixtures if your budget allows.
  - Use warm-toned bulbs to create a cozy atmosphere.

• Refresh small details
  - Update cabinet handles, drawer pulls, or switch plates.
  - Replace worn-out accessories with simple, coordinated pieces.

• Use mirrors
  - Mirrors can make rooms feel larger and brighter by reflecting light.
  - Look for secondhand options to save money.

Sample budget breakdown for a room makeover:
• Paint: $30–$60
• Thrifted decor: $20–$50
• Throw pillows and textiles: $30–$80
• Plants: $10–$40
• DIY artwork: $10–$30

With a budget of even $100–$200, you can often make a noticeable improvement by focusing on paint, lighting, textiles, and organization.
