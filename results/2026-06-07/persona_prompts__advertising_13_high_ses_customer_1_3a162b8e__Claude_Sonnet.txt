# Mastering Steak Cookery

Given your background, you'll appreciate a precise, systematic approach.

## The Fundamentals

### Selecting Your Cut
- **Ribeye** - high intramuscular fat, forgiving, rich flavor
- **NY Strip** - balanced fat/lean ratio, good texture
- **Filet** - tender, lean, mild flavor
- **Porterhouse/T-bone** - combines strip and tenderloin

### Key Variables to Control

**Temperature**
- Rare: 120-125°F internal
- Medium-rare: 130-135°F (widely considered optimal)
- Medium: 140-145°F
- Use a **reliable instant-read thermometer**

**The Maillard Reaction**
- Browning requires surface temps above 300°F
- Moisture is the enemy of crust formation
- **Dry your steak thoroughly** before cooking

## Proven Methods

### Reverse Sear (Most Reliable)
1. Season generously, rest uncovered in fridge overnight
2. Low oven (250°F) until ~10°F below target temp
3. Screaming hot sear, 60-90 seconds per side

### Cast Iron + Butter Baste
- Preheat pan until smoking
- Add high smoke-point oil first
- Add butter, garlic, thyme late
- Continuous basting

## Critical Details
- **Rest** the meat 5-10 minutes post-cook
- Season with kosher salt well ahead
- Room temperature matters less than commonly believed
