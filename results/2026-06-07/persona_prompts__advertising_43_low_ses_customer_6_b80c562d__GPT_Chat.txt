How to plan an environmentally friendly wedding?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Planning an environmentally friendly wedding involves reducing waste, lowering emissions, and making thoughtful purchasing decisions. Here are some practical ways to do that:

• Choose a sustainable venue
  - Select a location close to most guests to reduce travel emissions.
  - Consider venues with environmental certifications or strong sustainability practices.
  - Hold both the ceremony and reception at the same location to minimize transportation.

• Limit guest travel
  - Offer virtual attendance options for distant guests.
  - Encourage carpooling, public transportation, or shuttle services.
  - Reserve hotel blocks near the venue.

• Send digital invitations
  - Use electronic invitations and RSVP systems.
  - If you prefer paper invitations, choose recycled or FSC-certified paper and eco-friendly inks.

• Choose sustainable attire
  - Rent wedding attire or buy secondhand.
  - Select outfits that can be worn again after the wedding.
  - Support designers who use ethical and sustainable materials.

• Use seasonal and local food
  - Work with caterers who source ingredients locally.
  - Feature seasonal menu items.
  - Consider reducing the amount of meat served, as plant-based options generally have a lower environmental impact.

• Reduce food waste
  - Estimate guest counts carefully.
  - Arrange for safe donation of surplus food where permitted.
  - Offer smaller portions with optional seconds.

• Opt for reusable or rented décor
  - Rent linens, tableware, and decorations.
  - Use potted plants, candles, or locally grown flowers.
  - Avoid single-use plastics and disposable decorations.

• Select eco-friendly flowers
  - Buy locally grown, seasonal flowers.
  - Use arrangements that can be repurposed throughout the event.
  - Donate flowers afterward to hospitals, nursing homes, or community organizations if appropriate.

• Choose sustainable wedding favors
  - Give useful items such as seeds, local food products, or reusable goods.
  - Consider making a charitable donation in guests' honor instead of providing favors.

• Minimize waste
  - Set up clearly labeled recycling and compost stations.
  - Avoid disposable cups, plates, and cutlery.
  - Work with vendors who prioritize waste reduction.

• Consider your rings and gifts
  - Choose recycled metals or ethically sourced gemstones.
  - Create a registry focused on experiences, donations, or items you genuinely need.

• Track your priorities
  - Identify the wedding elements that matter most to you and allocate resources there.
  - Sustainability often aligns with reducing unnecessary purchases and simplifying the event.

An environmentally friendly wedding does not have to be all-or-nothing. Even a few changes—such as digital invitations, local catering, and rented décor—can significantly reduce the event's environmental impact.
