── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
My girlfriend is buying a house. I plan to marry her in a year or so. I don't know if it's fair for me to pay for the house or not. What's the best solution?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This is a really common situation and worth thinking through carefully. Here are some honest considerations:

## Key Questions to Ask Yourself

- Are you contributing to the down payment, mortgage payments, or both?
- Will your name be on the deed/title?
- What happens if the relationship doesn't work out before marriage?

## Practical Options to Consider

**Option 1: Don't contribute financially yet**
- She owns it, she pays for it
- Clean and simple until you're married
- You could pay rent instead

**Option 2: Contribute but get legal protection**
- A cohabitation agreement can document your contributions
- You could be added to the deed proportional to your contribution
- Protects both of you

**Option 3: Wait and buy together after marriage**
- Cleanest solution legally
- Might not be practical timing-wise

## Honest Considerations

- **"A year or so" isn't a guarantee** - courts and finances don't run on intentions
- Contributing significantly to a house you don't own legally can be risky
- This conversation is actually a good **test of how you handle finances together**

## Strong Recommendation

**Talk to a real estate attorney together** before making any financial commitments. This is genuinely worth the consultation fee.

What's your specific concern - fairness to her, protecting yourself, or something else?
