── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Given that you work long hours in a warehouse, your approach to learning to code needs to be tailored to your schedule. After a physically demanding shift, you likely won't have the energy for three-hour study marathons—and that is completely okay! 

The "best" way for you to learn is going to be about **low-friction setup, consistency, and micro-learning**. Here is a realistic roadmap to get started from scratch:

### 1. Focus on "Micro-Learning" (15–30 Minutes a Day)
Don't try to cram on your days off. Instead, aim for just 15 to 30 minutes a day. Consistency builds the "muscle memory" of coding much better than binge-studying. If you only have 15 minutes after a long shift before you need to sleep, that is enough to make progress.

### 2. Pick One Beginner-Friendly Language
Don't get bogged down in the "which language is best" debate. Pick one of these two based on your interests and stick with it for at least 6 months:
*   **Python:** Best for general programming, data, and automation. It reads a lot like plain English, making it very beginner-friendly.
*   **HTML/CSS, then JavaScript:** Best if you are a visual person and want to build websites or web apps.
