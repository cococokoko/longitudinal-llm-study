# Improving Productivity While Working From Home

Here are practical strategies tailored to your situation as a single parent:

## Setting Up Your Environment
- **Designate a workspace** - even a corner with a consistent setup signals "work mode"
- Keep essentials within reach to minimize interruptions
- Use noise-canceling headphones during focused work

## Managing Time Around Childcare
- **Work in focused blocks** during nap times, school hours, or quiet activities
- Identify your **peak energy hours** and protect them for demanding tasks
- Prepare a simple schedule the night before so mornings run smoother

## Handling Interruptions Realistically
- Create a **visual signal** (like a colored card) so older children know when you need focus time
- Build in **buffer time** - things will come up, and that's okay
- Batch similar tasks together to get back on track faster

## Tools That Help
- Task managers like **Todoist or Trello** to capture priorities quickly
- **Timers** for short focused sprints (even 20-25 minutes counts)
- Meal prep on weekends to reclaim weekday time

## Being Kind to Yourself
- **Flexible productivity** beats rigid schedules when kids are involved
- Celebrate what you *did* accomplish, not just what's unfinished
- Some days childcare *is* the priority - that's not failure
