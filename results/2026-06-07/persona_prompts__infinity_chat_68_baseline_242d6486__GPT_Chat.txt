Give me a tip for managing a team of coworkers.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
One effective tip is to create clear expectations and follow up consistently.

Make sure everyone understands:
- Their responsibilities
- Team goals and priorities
- How success will be measured
- Deadlines and key milestones

Then, check in regularly—not to micromanage, but to identify obstacles, answer questions, and keep work aligned with goals. Many team problems come not from lack of effort, but from unclear expectations or assumptions that everyone is on the same page.

A simple weekly team check-in where people share progress, challenges, and next steps can significantly improve coordination and accountability.
