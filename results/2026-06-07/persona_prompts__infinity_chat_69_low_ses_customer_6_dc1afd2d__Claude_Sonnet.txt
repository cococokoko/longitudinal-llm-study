## Analysis vs. Design

**Analysis** = Understanding **what** the problem is (requirements, constraints, current state)

**Design** = Determining **how** to solve it (structure, architecture, solution approach)

---

## Can You Design Without Analysis?

**Technically yes, practically no.**

Designing without analysis means:
- Solving the **wrong problem**
- Making assumptions that may be **incorrect**
- Producing solutions that **don't meet actual needs**
- Wasting time and resources on **rework**

---

## Key Distinction

| Analysis | Design |
|----------|--------|
| What is needed? | How to build it? |
| Problem space | Solution space |
| Discovery | Creation |
| Requirements | Blueprint |

**Bottom line:** Analysis defines the *target*; design defines the *path*. Designing without analysis is like building a warehouse layout without knowing what inventory it needs to hold — you may build something functional but completely wrong for the actual purpose.
