── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I am a physician-scientist leading a research group.

How can I learn to cook healthy meals quickly?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
That's a straightforward practical question! Here are some efficient approaches:

## Core Strategies for Busy Professionals

**Batch cooking**
- Dedicate 1-2 hours on weekends to prep ingredients
- Cook grains, proteins, and roasted vegetables in bulk
- Assemble meals quickly during the week

**Simple meal frameworks**
- Protein + vegetable + grain/starch covers most nutritional bases
- Learn 5-10 reliable recipes rather than constantly trying new ones

**Equipment that saves time**
- Instant Pot/pressure cooker
- Sheet pan for roasting multiple things simultaneously
- Sharp knives (genuinely speeds up prep)

## Learning Resources

- **Books**: *Salt Fat Acid Heat* (Samin Nosrat), *How to Cook Everything* (Bittman)
- **YouTube**: Ethan Chlebowski, Joshua Weissman focus on efficient techniques
- **Apps**: Mealime or Paprika for planning

## Practical Quick Wins

- Keep frozen vegetables on hand (nutritionally comparable to fresh)
- Eggs are fast, nutritious, and versatile
- Canned legumes require no cooking
- Pre-marinated proteins reduce decision fatigue

## Realistic Expectation

Most people find that **20-30 minutes** is achievable for healthy meals once you have basic skills and a stocked pantry.
