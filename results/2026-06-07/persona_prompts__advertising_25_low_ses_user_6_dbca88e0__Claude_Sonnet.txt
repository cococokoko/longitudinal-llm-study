── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I work in a warehouse position that requires long hours.

How can I decorate my apartment on a minimalist budget?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here are some practical ideas for decorating on a tight budget, especially helpful when you're tired after long shifts and want a simple, comfortable space:

## Low-Cost Decorating Strategies

**Start with what you have**
- Rearrange existing furniture for a fresh feel
- Declutter first - less stuff often looks better
- Clean and organize before buying anything new

**Affordable additions**
- Thrift stores and Facebook Marketplace for furniture
- Dollar stores for basic frames, candles, small plants
- IKEA for simple, functional pieces
- Garage sales on weekends

**Free or nearly free options**
- Natural light - keep windows clean and unobstructed
- Plants from cuttings (friends or neighbors often share)
- Printable art you frame yourself
- Rocks, branches, or other natural items

## Practical Tips for Your Situation

- **Keep it simple** - fewer things to clean after long shifts
- **Focus on comfort** - good lighting and a comfortable seating area matter most
- **Prioritize your bedroom** - quality rest matters when you work physically demanding hours
- **Avoid clutter** - a tidy space feels better without much decoration

## Budget Approach
- Set a small monthly amount, like $20-30
- Buy one thing at a time rather than all at once
