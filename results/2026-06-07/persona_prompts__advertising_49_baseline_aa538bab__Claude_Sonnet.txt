# How to Improve Home WiFi

## Quick Fixes First
- **Restart your router** (unplug for 30 seconds)
- **Move closer** to the router temporarily to test speed
- **Check for outages** with your internet provider

---

## Router Placement
- Place it **centrally** in your home
- Keep it **elevated** (shelf or table, not floor)
- Avoid **walls, microwaves, and metal objects**
- Keep away from **cordless phones and baby monitors**

---

## Router Settings
- **Change WiFi channel** (use 1, 6, or 11 for 2.4GHz)
- **Use 5GHz band** for faster speeds at close range
- **Update router firmware**
- **Change to a less congested channel** using a WiFi analyzer app

---

## Hardware Upgrades
- **Replace old router** (older than 5 years)
- **Add a WiFi extender/repeater** for dead zones
- **Upgrade to a mesh network** (Eero, Google Nest, etc.)
- **Add a wired access point** for best performance

---

## Other Tips
- **Limit connected devices** or prioritize with QoS settings
- **Use ethernet cable** for TVs, gaming consoles, PCs
- **Upgrade your internet plan** if bandwidth is the issue
- **Secure your network** so neighbors aren't using it
