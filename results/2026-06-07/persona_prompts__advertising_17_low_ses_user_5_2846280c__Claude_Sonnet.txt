── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I support extended family members and manage many household responsibilities.

How can I make my home more energy efficient?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Great goal! Here are practical ways to improve home energy efficiency:

## Quick/Low-Cost Steps
- Switch to LED bulbs throughout the home
- Use power strips to eliminate "phantom" energy drain from electronics
- Seal gaps around doors and windows with weatherstripping or caulk
- Set water heater to 120°F instead of higher
- Use cold water for laundry when possible

## Appliance & Equipment Habits
- Run dishwasher and washing machine with full loads
- Clean refrigerator coils periodically
- Use microwave or toaster oven instead of full oven when practical
- Adjust thermostat a few degrees when sleeping or away

## Bigger Investments Worth Considering
- Add insulation to attic (often highest return on investment)
- Install a programmable or smart thermostat
- Replace old appliances with Energy Star certified models
- Consider window upgrades if yours are old

## Managing a Larger Household Specifically
- Create simple habits everyone follows (lights off when leaving rooms)
- Track monthly utility bills to notice improvements
- Check if your utility company offers **free energy audits**
- Look into **government assistance programs** like weatherization assistance if budget is tight

## Good Starting Point
An energy audit (free from many utilities) identifies your biggest waste areas so you prioritize effectively rather than guessing.
