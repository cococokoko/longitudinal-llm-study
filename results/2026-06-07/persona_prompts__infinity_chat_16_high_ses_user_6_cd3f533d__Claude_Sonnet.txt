# The Future of Work in an Automated Economy

This is genuinely one of the most important strategic questions right now. Let me give you a honest, structured perspective rather than just reassuring talking points.

## What the Evidence Actually Suggests

**Automation displaces tasks, not just jobs - but the distinction matters less than often claimed**
- Most jobs contain automatable *components*, reshaping roles rather than eliminating them entirely
- However, displacement can still cause serious economic disruption even if "jobs" technically persist
- Historical analogies to previous automation waves may be **misleading** given the pace and breadth of AI

## Key Dynamics Worth Tracking

### What's genuinely at risk
- Routine cognitive work (significant consulting exposure here, frankly)
- Middle-skill knowledge work
- Entry-level positions that previously built expertise pipelines

### What remains durable
- Complex judgment under genuine uncertainty
- Relationship-intensive work
- Novel problem framing (vs. solving)
- Cross-domain synthesis

## The Harder Questions Firms Should Be Asking

- **Who captures productivity gains?** Labor vs. capital distribution is a political choice, not an economic inevitability
- **What happens to the expertise pipeline?** If junior roles disappear, where does senior judgment come from?
- **Retraining at what scale?** Most corporate programs are dramatically undersized relative to the challenge

## My Honest Assessment

The optimistic "new jobs will emerge" narrative is **probably partially true but dangerously incomplete**. The transition costs are real and unevenly distributed.
