Here are some effective approaches for learning a musical instrument online:

## Structured Learning Platforms
- **Yousician** - interactive feedback on your playing
- **Simply Piano** or **Flowkey** - good for piano specifically
- **Fender Play** - designed for guitar beginners
- **Lessonface** - connects you with live instructors

## Video Resources
- YouTube channels dedicated to your specific instrument
- Look for teachers who explain *why*, not just *what* to play
- Structured playlists that build progressively

## Key Practices That Actually Work
- **Consistent short sessions** (20-30 minutes daily beats 2 hours weekly)
- **Slow, deliberate practice** rather than rushing through pieces
- **Record yourself** to identify issues you can't hear in the moment
- **Use a metronome** from the beginning

## Getting Feedback
- Consider occasional live online lessons even if self-teaching primarily
- Online communities (Reddit's r/guitar, r/piano, etc.) can review recordings
- Apps with real-time feedback help fill the gap

## Realistic Expectations
- Progress is often nonlinear - plateaus are normal
- Fundamentals (scales, technique) pay off more than jumping to songs
- Most people underestimate how much consistent practice matters
