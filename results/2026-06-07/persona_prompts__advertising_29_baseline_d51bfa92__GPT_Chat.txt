Keeping pets healthy and happy involves meeting their physical, mental, and emotional needs consistently. The specifics vary by species, breed, age, and individual personality, but these general principles apply to most pets:

- Provide proper nutrition
  - Feed a balanced diet appropriate for your pet's species, age, size, and health status.
  - Avoid overfeeding and monitor treats.
  - Ensure fresh, clean water is always available.

- Schedule regular veterinary care
  - Keep up with wellness exams, vaccinations, and preventive care.
  - Discuss parasite prevention (fleas, ticks, heartworms, etc.) with your veterinarian.
  - Address changes in behavior, appetite, weight, or energy promptly.

- Encourage exercise and activity
  - Dogs often benefit from daily walks, play, and training.
  - Cats may enjoy climbing structures, toys, and interactive play sessions.
  - Small animals, birds, and reptiles also need species-appropriate opportunities for movement and enrichment.

- Provide mental stimulation
  - Use puzzle feeders, training exercises, toys, and exploration opportunities.
  - Rotate toys periodically to maintain interest.
  - Teach new behaviors or skills using positive reinforcement.

- Create a safe, comfortable environment
  - Provide a clean living space with appropriate shelter, bedding, and temperature control.
  - Remove access to toxic plants, dangerous foods, medications, and household hazards.
  - Ensure pets have a quiet place to rest.

- Support emotional well-being
  - Spend quality time with your pet through play, training, grooming, or companionship.
  - Respect their need for rest and personal space.
  - Maintain predictable routines when possible.

- Maintain grooming and hygiene
  - Brush as needed for the coat type.
  - Trim nails when appropriate.
  - Follow veterinary recommendations for dental care.

- Monitor weight and overall health
  - Keep track of body condition, mobility, and behavior.
  - Sudden changes can be early signs of illness.
