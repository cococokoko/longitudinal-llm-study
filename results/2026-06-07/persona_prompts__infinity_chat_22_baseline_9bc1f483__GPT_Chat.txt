── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Different views on discipleship.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Different Christian traditions understand discipleship in somewhat different ways, though most agree that discipleship means following Jesus, growing in faith, and living according to his teachings. Here are some major views:

• Evangelical Protestant View
  - Emphasizes a personal relationship with Jesus Christ.
  - Focuses on Bible study, prayer, evangelism, and spiritual growth.
  - Often sees discipleship as a lifelong process of becoming more like Christ.
  - Small groups, mentoring relationships, and accountability are commonly used.

• Reformed View
  - Stresses God's sovereignty and the authority of Scripture.
  - Discipleship involves growing in knowledge of God, holiness, and obedience.
  - Often emphasizes participation in the local church and the means of grace (preaching, sacraments, prayer).

• Lutheran View
  - Centers on justification by faith alone.
  - Discipleship is a response to God's grace rather than a means of earning favor.
  - Focuses on hearing God's Word, receiving the sacraments, and serving one's neighbor.

• Methodist/Wesleyan View
  - Emphasizes sanctification and growth in holiness.
  - Encourages spiritual disciplines, accountability groups, and acts of mercy.
  - Often highlights the possibility of significant spiritual transformation in this life.

• Roman Catholic View
  - Sees discipleship as participation in the life of Christ through faith, sacraments, and the Church.
  - Emphasizes prayer, moral formation, service, and the imitation of Christ.
  - Includes devotion to saints as examples of faithful discipleship.

• Eastern Orthodox View
  - Focuses on theosis (becoming united with God through grace).
  - Discipleship involves prayer, worship, fasting, repentance, and participation in the sacramental life of the Church.
  - Strong emphasis on spiritual formation and transformation.

• Anabaptist View
  - Emphasizes practical obedience to Jesus' teachings.
  - Focuses on community, nonviolence, simplicity, and ethical living.
  - Often stresses that genuine faith should be visibly expressed in daily life.

• Pentecostal/Charismatic View
  - Highlights the work of the Holy Spirit in the believer's life.
  - Encourages spiritual gifts, prayer, worship, and evangelism.
  - Discipleship includes growing in spiritual power and sensitivity to the Spirit.

• Radical Discipleship Movements
  - Stress total commitment to Jesus, often including sacrificial living.
  - May emphasize social justice, mission, simplicity, or intentional community.
  - Often draw inspiration from Jesus' call to "take up your cross" and follow him.

Some key debates between these views include:
- Whether discipleship primarily involves doctrinal learning, spiritual formation, moral obedience, or all three.
- The role of the Church and sacraments in spiritual growth.
- The relationship between faith, works, and holiness.
- Whether discipleship focuses mainly on personal transformation or social transformation.
- The extent to which discipleship should require radical lifestyle changes.

Despite these differences, most Christian traditions agree that discipleship involves learning from Jesus, trusting him, and allowing that faith to shape one's life.
