Making your own natural beauty products can be simple, but it's important to approach it with the same care you'd use for any product applied to skin. "Natural" ingredients can still cause irritation, allergic reactions, contamination, or instability.

A few beginner-friendly options:

1. Simple body oil
Ingredients:
- 2–4 oz (60–120 mL) carrier oil (jojoba, sweet almond, grapeseed, or argan oil)
- Optional: 1–3 drops essential oil per ounce (30 mL)

Method:
- Mix in a clean glass bottle.
- Shake gently.
- Apply to damp skin after bathing.

2. Sugar body scrub
Ingredients:
- 1 cup sugar
- 1/2 cup coconut oil or sweet almond oil

Method:
- Mix until uniform.
- Use on the body (not the face) 1–2 times per week.
- Avoid introducing water into the container to reduce microbial growth.

3. Oatmeal face mask
Ingredients:
- 2 tablespoons finely ground oats
- Water or plain yogurt to form a paste

Method:
- Apply for 10–15 minutes.
- Rinse gently with lukewarm water.

4. Lip balm
Ingredients:
- 1 tablespoon beeswax
- 1 tablespoon shea butter
- 1 tablespoon carrier oil

Method:
- Melt together using gentle heat.
- Pour into small containers.
- Allow to cool and solidify.

Safety considerations:
- Perform a patch test on a small area of skin before broader use.
- Be cautious with essential oils; they are highly concentrated and can irritate skin.
- Avoid homemade products around the eyes unless specifically formulated for that purpose.
- Products containing water generally require preservatives to prevent bacterial and fungal growth.
- Label containers with ingredients and preparation dates.
- Discard products if they change odor, color, or texture.
