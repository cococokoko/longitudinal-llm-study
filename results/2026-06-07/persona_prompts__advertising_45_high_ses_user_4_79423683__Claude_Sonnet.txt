I am a physician-scientist leading a research group.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here are some practical ways to learn DIY home repairs:

## Starting Resources

**Online**
- YouTube channels (This Old House, Home Repair Tutor, April Wilkerson)
- Family Handyman website
- Reddit communities like r/DIY and r/HomeImprovement

**Books/Publications**
- "The Complete Manual of Home Repair" type references
- Family Handyman magazine
- Local library has many practical guides

## Hands-On Learning

- **Home Depot/Lowe's workshops** - free classes on specific skills
- **Community college courses** - basic electrical, plumbing, carpentry
- **Habitat for Humanity volunteering** - learn while helping others

## Practical Approach

- **Start small** - replace outlets, fix leaky faucets, patch drywall
- **Work alongside a contractor** occasionally and ask questions
- **Join a local makerspace** that may have relevant workshops

## Useful Habits

- Document what you do with photos
- Keep a home maintenance log
- Build a basic tool kit gradually
- Know when to call a professional (permits, structural, major electrical)

## Given Your Background

Your analytical and research skills actually transfer well - you'll likely appreciate understanding *why* techniques work, not just the steps.
