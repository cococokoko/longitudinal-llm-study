Healthy meal planning on a budget.

Healthy meal planning on a budget is largely about choosing nutrient-dense staples, minimizing food waste, and building meals around versatile ingredients.

A practical framework

- Base meals on affordable staples:
  - Oats
  - Brown rice
  - Potatoes
  - Whole-wheat pasta
  - Beans, lentils, and chickpeas
  - Frozen vegetables
  - Eggs

- Choose cost-effective protein sources:
  - Beans and lentils
  - Eggs
  - Canned tuna or salmon
  - Chicken thighs
  - Plain Greek yogurt
  - Tofu

- Buy produce strategically:
  - Prioritize seasonal fruits and vegetables.
  - Use frozen produce when fresh options are expensive.
  - Purchase only what you can realistically consume before it spoils.

- Plan around overlapping ingredients:
  - Use the same ingredients across multiple meals to reduce waste.
  - Example: spinach can be used in omelets, salads, grain bowls, and pasta dishes.

Sample budget-friendly week

Breakfasts
- Oatmeal with fruit and nuts
- Greek yogurt with berries
- Vegetable omelet with whole-grain toast

Lunches
- Lentil soup with side salad
- Rice bowl with beans, vegetables, and salsa
- Tuna sandwich with fruit

Dinners
- Chicken, roasted vegetables, and brown rice
- Bean chili with baked potatoes
- Stir-fried tofu and vegetables over rice
- Whole-wheat pasta with tomato sauce and vegetables

Snacks
- Apples
- Bananas
- Carrot sticks
- Popcorn
- Yogurt

Ways to reduce grocery costs

- Create a weekly meal plan before shopping.
- Shop with a list and avoid impulse purchases.
- Compare unit prices rather than package prices.
- Buy staples in bulk when they are items you use regularly.
- Cook larger batches and freeze portions.
- Incorporate one or two meatless days per week.

Example budget allocation for one person (varies by region)
- 30–35%: proteins
- 25–30%: fruits and vegetables
- 20–25%: grains and starches
- 10–15%: dairy or alternatives
- 5–10%: healthy fats and seasonings

For a busy executive schedule, batch-cooking on weekends and preparing two or three core meals that can be mixed and matched throughout the week often provides the best balance of nutrition, cost control, and convenience.
