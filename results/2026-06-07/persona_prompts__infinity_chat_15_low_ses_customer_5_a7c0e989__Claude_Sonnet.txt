## Key Ways Economic Inequalities Shape International Relations

**Power Dynamics**
- Wealthier nations have greater leverage in trade negotiations, international institutions, and diplomatic relationships
- Economic dependence can create political vulnerability for poorer nations
- Countries with resource wealth sometimes gain disproportionate influence despite other weaknesses

**Institutional Influence**
- Voting structures in IMF and World Bank historically favor wealthy contributors
- This shapes which economic models get promoted globally
- Developing nations have pushed back through coalitions like G77

**Areas of Genuine Tension**
- Debt relationships between creditor and debtor nations create complex dependencies
- Trade agreements often reflect negotiating power asymmetries
- Climate negotiations involve real conflicts over who bears costs of transition

**Where Analysis Gets Complicated**
- Economic interdependence can both reduce conflict AND create new friction points
- Foreign aid relationships involve genuine humanitarian goals alongside strategic interests
- Rising powers like China challenge existing hierarchies in ways that create both opportunities and tensions for developing nations

## Honest Acknowledgments
- Scholars genuinely disagree about whether inequality primarily causes conflict or cooperation
- Historical patterns show both exploitation AND genuine development partnerships
- Simple narratives (purely exploitative OR purely beneficial) tend to miss important nuances
