The best app depends on what you want to track and how much effort you're willing to put into logging. Since you work full-time with an unpredictable schedule, apps that are quick to use and sync automatically with devices may be especially helpful.

For nutrition:
- MacroFactor
  - Strong food database and barcode scanning.
  - Uses your weight and food logs to adjust calorie targets automatically.
  - Popular with people focused on body composition.
  - Paid subscription.

- Cronometer
  - Excellent for tracking both macros and micronutrients (vitamins, minerals, fiber, etc.).
  - More detailed than most nutrition apps.
  - Free version is quite capable.

- MyFitnessPal
  - One of the largest food databases.
  - Easy to find restaurant and fast-food items.
  - Good if convenience matters more than detailed nutrition analysis.

For fitness/workout tracking:
- Strong
  - Simple and effective for strength training.
  - Tracks sets, reps, and progress over time.
  - Easy to use during short gym sessions.

- Hevy
  - Similar to Strong, with a more social component.
  - Clean interface and solid exercise library.

- Fitbod
  - Generates workouts based on available equipment and recovery.
  - Helpful if your schedule changes frequently and you need flexible workout planning.

For overall health and activity:
- Apple Health (iPhone)
  - Central hub that combines data from many apps and devices.

- Google Fit (Android)
  - Simple activity tracking and integration with many fitness services.

- Garmin Connect
  - Excellent if you use a Garmin watch.
  - Tracks workouts, sleep, heart rate, and recovery metrics.

For habits and consistency:
- Streaks (iPhone)
  - Good for building simple daily habits.
  - Useful if your work schedule makes routine difficult.

- HabitNow (Android)
  - Flexible habit and goal tracking with reminders.

If you're looking for the most effective combination for fitness and nutrition:
- Nutrition: MacroFactor or Cronometer
- Workouts: Strong or Hevy
- Activity and health data: Apple Health, Google Fit, or Garmin Connect

That setup gives you detailed nutrition tracking, easy workout logging, and automatic activity tracking without requiring a lot of time each day.
