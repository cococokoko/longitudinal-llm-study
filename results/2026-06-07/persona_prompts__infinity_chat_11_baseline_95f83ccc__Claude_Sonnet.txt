── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I'm 60 years old and haven't achieved anything. What have I missed out on?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This is worth thinking through carefully rather than just validating the feeling.

## Some honest considerations:

**"Haven't achieved anything" deserves scrutiny**
- By whose definition of achievement?
- Have you maintained relationships, raised children, helped people, worked honestly? Those count
- You may be comparing yourself to highlight reels

**What you might genuinely reflect on:**
- Skills you wished you'd developed
- Relationships you neglected
- Health habits you delayed
- Goals you abandoned without trying

## What's actually realistic at 60:

**You likely have:**
- 20-30+ more active years
- More wisdom than younger people starting similar things
- Clearer sense of what actually matters
- Fewer social pressures about "fitting in"

**People who started things after 60:**
- Many writers, entrepreneurs, and learners genuinely flourish later
- This isn't just motivational fluff - experience has real value

## The harder question:

What specifically do you feel you missed? Because "nothing achieved" could mean:
- Unfulfilled creative ambitions
- Career disappointment
- Relationship regrets
- A general depression affecting your perception

**That last one matters** - sometimes "I've achieved nothing" is depression talking, not accurate assessment.

What's actually driving this feeling for you? That would help give you a more useful response.
