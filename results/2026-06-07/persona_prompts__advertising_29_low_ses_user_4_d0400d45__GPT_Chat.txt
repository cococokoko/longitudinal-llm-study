── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Working shifts at a hospital can make pet care more challenging because your schedule may change and you may be away for long periods. The key is providing consistency, enrichment, and preventive care.

Some general tips:

- Maintain regular feeding times as much as possible. Automatic feeders can help if your shifts vary.
- Ensure fresh water is always available. Consider a water fountain for cats and dogs that prefer running water.
- Schedule regular veterinary checkups and keep vaccinations, parasite prevention, and dental care up to date.
- Provide daily exercise appropriate for the species, breed, age, and health of your pet.
- Monitor weight, appetite, energy level, and bathroom habits for signs of illness.
- Pet-proof your home and keep toxic foods, medications, cleaning products, and small objects out of reach.
- Make sure pets have identification tags and, if appropriate, a microchip.

For dogs:
- Arrange walks, playtime, or a dog walker if you're gone for long shifts.
- Use puzzle toys, chew toys, and training games to provide mental stimulation.
- Maintain a predictable routine when possible.
- Give them opportunities for social interaction if they enjoy it.

For cats:
- Provide scratching posts, climbing spaces, and hiding spots.
- Engage them with interactive toys and short play sessions.
- Keep litter boxes clean; a common guideline is one box per cat plus one extra.
- Offer window perches or other safe ways to observe their environment.

For small pets, birds, reptiles, or fish:
- Research the specific environmental, dietary, and enrichment needs of the species.
- Maintain proper habitat conditions such as temperature, humidity, lighting, and water quality.
- Clean enclosures regularly and monitor for signs of stress or illness.

Given your hospital work:
- Have a backup caregiver who can help if you're unexpectedly held over or called in.
- Consider pet cameras to check on animals while you're away.
- Prepare an emergency plan that includes feeding instructions, medications, and veterinary contact information.
