# Effective Ways to Reduce Household Energy Use

Here are practical strategies organized by category:

## Quick & Low-Cost Changes
- **Adjust thermostat** - 7-10°F when sleeping or away saves ~10% on heating/cooling
- **Switch to LED bulbs** - use 75% less energy than incandescent
- **Unplug devices** when not in use (phantom/standby power adds up)
- **Use cold water** for laundry when possible
- **Run full loads** in dishwasher and washing machine

## Appliance & Equipment Habits
- **Air dry dishes** instead of using heated drying
- **Clean refrigerator coils** regularly for efficiency
- **Use microwave or toaster oven** instead of full oven for small meals
- **Run large appliances** during off-peak hours if your utility offers lower rates

## Bigger Impact Investments
- Programmable or smart thermostats
- Energy Star appliances when replacing old ones
- Improved insulation and weatherstripping
- Low-flow fixtures to reduce water heating costs

## Practical for Larger Households
- **Establish simple rules** everyone follows (lights off when leaving rooms)
- **Zone heating/cooling** to focus on occupied areas
- **Batch cooking** reduces oven use throughout the week
- **Power strips** make it easy to cut power to multiple devices at once

## Monitoring Progress
- Check your utility bills monthly to track improvements
- Many utilities offer **free energy audits**
