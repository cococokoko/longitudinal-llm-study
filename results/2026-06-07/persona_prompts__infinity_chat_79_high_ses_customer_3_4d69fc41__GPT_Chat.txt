Research Question:
How effective is rice husk biochar at removing contaminants from water compared with sand or commercial activated carbon?

Materials (typically under $30–50):
- Rice husks (often free or very cheap)
- Metal can or container for making biochar
- Sand and gravel
- Plastic bottles or PVC pipe
- Water-testing strips (pH, nitrate, etc.)
- Measuring cups and scales

Experimental Design:
1. Create biochar from rice husks by heating them with limited oxygen.
2. Build several filtration columns:

4. Measure:

   - pH
   - Nitrate concentration
   - Filtration rate
5. Compare results statistically.
