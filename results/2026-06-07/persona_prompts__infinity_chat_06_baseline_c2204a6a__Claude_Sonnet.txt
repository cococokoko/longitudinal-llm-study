── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How do I understand what I want?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This is a genuinely important question. Here are some honest ways to think about it:

## Why It's Hard
- Wants get mixed up with what others expect of you
- Short-term desires can conflict with deeper values
- Fear can disguise itself as preference
- You may have been taught to ignore your own signals

## Practical Ways to Get Clearer

**Pay attention to your reactions**
- What makes you feel energized vs. drained?
- What do you envy in others? (envy often points to real desires)
- What would you do if no one was watching or judging?

**Test, don't just think**
- Try things rather than analyzing indefinitely
- Notice how you feel *after* experiences, not just before
- Small experiments reveal more than long reflection

**Remove noise**
- What would you want if money wasn't a constraint?
- What would you want if your parents/friends approved of everything?
- These questions isolate your actual preferences

**Notice resistance**
- Sometimes what you avoid most is what matters most
- Discomfort isn't always a sign something is wrong

## Honest Complications
- Some people genuinely have underdeveloped preferences from suppressing them long-term
- Wanting can take time to develop - it's okay not to know
- Therapy can help if you feel consistently disconnected from your own desires

**What specifically are you trying to figure out?** The answer might be more targeted with context.
