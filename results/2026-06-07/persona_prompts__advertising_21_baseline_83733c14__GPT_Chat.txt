── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How do I start a vegetable garden in my backyard?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Starting a backyard vegetable garden is easier if you begin small and build experience over time.

1. Choose a good location
- Pick a spot that gets at least 6–8 hours of direct sunlight daily.
- Make sure it's close to a water source.
- Avoid areas where water pools after rain.

2. Decide on a garden type
- In-ground garden: Uses existing soil and is often the least expensive option.
- Raised beds: Easier to manage, improve drainage, and can provide better soil quality.
- Containers: Good for small spaces, patios, or poor soil conditions.

3. Prepare the soil
- Remove weeds and grass.
- Mix in compost or well-rotted organic matter to improve fertility and soil structure.
- If possible, get a soil test through a local extension service to check pH and nutrient levels.

4. Start with easy vegetables
Good beginner crops include:
- Lettuce
- Radishes
- Green beans
- Zucchini
- Tomatoes
- Peppers
- Cucumbers
- Herbs such as basil, parsley, and chives

5. Plan your layout
- Place taller plants where they won't shade shorter ones.
- Follow spacing recommendations on seed packets or plant labels.
- Leave room to walk between rows or beds.

6. Plant at the right time
- Check your local growing season and last frost date.
- Cool-season crops (lettuce, peas, spinach) are planted earlier.
- Warm-season crops (tomatoes, peppers, cucumbers) are planted after frost danger has passed.

7. Water consistently
- Most vegetables need about 1 inch (2.5 cm) of water per week, more during hot weather.
- Water deeply rather than lightly and frequently.
- Morning watering helps reduce disease.

8. Mulch the garden
- Apply a layer of straw, shredded leaves, or other organic mulch.
- Mulch helps retain moisture, suppress weeds, and regulate soil temperature.

9. Monitor for pests and diseases
- Inspect plants regularly.
- Remove damaged leaves and hand-pick larger pests when practical.
- Encourage beneficial insects by planting flowers nearby.

10. Harvest regularly
- Many vegetables produce more when picked frequently.
- Harvest at peak size and ripeness for the best flavor.

Example beginner garden (about 4 ft × 8 ft / 1.2 m × 2.4 m):
- 2 tomato plants
- 1 zucchini plant
- 1 cucumber plant
- 1 row of bush beans
- 1 row of lettuce
- A few basil plants
