── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
My girlfriend is buying a house. I plan to marry her in a year or so. I don't know if it's fair for me to pay for the house or not. What's the best solution?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This is as much a relationship question as a financial and legal one. The best solution depends on what role you'll have in the house before and after marriage.

A few principles can help:

- If the house is solely in her name and she's making the down payment and taking the mortgage, it's generally reasonable for her to build the ownership equity.
- If you live there before marriage, it's usually fair for you to contribute something toward housing costs, because you'd be benefiting from living there.
- The key question is whether your payments are treated as rent/shared expenses or as contributions toward ownership.

Common approaches:

1. Pay rent or a fixed monthly amount
- She owns the house.
- You pay an agreed amount comparable to what you'd pay for housing elsewhere.
- She remains responsible for ownership-related gains and losses.
- This is often the cleanest option before marriage.

2. Split household expenses but not the mortgage principal
- You contribute to utilities, groceries, internet, maintenance, etc.
- She pays the mortgage and property taxes.
- This avoids ambiguity about ownership.

3. Buy into ownership formally
- If you're contributing to the down payment or mortgage with the expectation of owning part of the house, get that documented legally.
- This could mean being added to the title or having a written agreement specifying your ownership percentage.
- Do not rely on verbal understandings for this.

4. Wait until marriage and revisit
- Since you expect to marry within about a year, you could keep ownership separate for now and decide after marriage whether to combine finances, refinance, add you to the title, or purchase a future home together.

A few questions matter:
- Are you planning to move into the house immediately?
- Is she asking you to help with the down payment?
- Will your name be on the title or mortgage?
- Are your incomes similar, or would a 50/50 split be burdensome for one of you?

One thing I'd be cautious about: paying substantial amounts toward a down payment or mortgage principal without any written agreement about ownership. That can create confusion and resentment later, even in healthy relationships.

A practical middle-ground solution for many couples in your situation is:
- She buys the house in her name.
- You contribute a mutually agreed monthly amount as rent or toward shared living costs.
- You do not contribute to the down payment unless you're receiving a documented ownership interest.
- After marriage, revisit the arrangement together.
