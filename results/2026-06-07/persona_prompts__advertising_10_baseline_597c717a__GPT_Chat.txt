── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Learning a new language effectively.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Learning a new language effectively is less about talent and more about consistent exposure, active use, and efficient study methods. Here are some evidence-based strategies:

1. Focus on high-frequency content
- Learn the most common words and phrases first.
- The top 1,000–2,000 words often cover a large percentage of everyday conversations.
- Prioritize practical language you'll actually use.

2. Study regularly
- Short daily sessions (20–60 minutes) are usually more effective than occasional long sessions.
- Consistency helps memory retention and skill development.

3. Use spaced repetition
- Review vocabulary and phrases at increasing intervals.
- Apps like Anki and other flashcard systems use this principle to improve long-term memory.

4. Learn in context
- Memorizing isolated words is less effective than learning them in sentences.
- Study example conversations, stories, and real-life situations.

5. Practice listening early
- Listen to podcasts, videos, music, or conversations in the target language.
- Don't wait until you understand everything; gradual exposure trains your ear.

6. Speak as soon as possible
- Use the language actively, even with limited vocabulary.
- Language exchange partners, tutors, and conversation groups can help.
- Mistakes are a normal part of learning.

7. Read at an appropriate level
- Start with graded readers, children's content, or simple articles.
- Choose material that is challenging but not overwhelming.

8. Write regularly
- Keep a journal, write short messages, or summarize things you've learned.
- Writing reinforces vocabulary and grammar.

9. Learn grammar strategically
- Understand core grammar patterns, but avoid getting stuck studying rules without using them.
- Apply grammar immediately in speaking and writing.

10. Immerse yourself when possible
- Change your phone settings, follow social media accounts, watch videos, and consume content in the language.
- Create frequent contact with the language throughout your day.

11. Set clear goals
- Examples:
  - Hold a 10-minute conversation within three months.
  - Read a short book within six months.
  - Understand a podcast episode without subtitles within a year.
- Specific goals help maintain motivation.

12. Track progress
- Record yourself speaking.
- Keep a log of study time and milestones.
- Review older material to see how much you've improved.

Example daily routine (45 minutes):
- 10 minutes: Spaced-repetition vocabulary review.
- 10 minutes: Grammar or lesson study.
- 15 minutes: Listening or reading practice.
- 10 minutes: Speaking or writing practice.
