# Different Views on Discipleship

Discipleship is understood in various ways across Christian traditions and theological perspectives. Here's an overview of key viewpoints:

---

## Traditional/Classical View
- **Formal mentorship** modeled on Jesus and the Twelve
- Emphasis on **doctrine, Scripture memorization, and spiritual disciplines**
- Often structured and intentional
- Associated with figures like **Dallas Willard** and **Dietrich Bonhoeffer**

---

## Relational/Organic View
- Discipleship happens **naturally through authentic relationships**
- Less programmatic, more life-on-life
- Emphasizes **walking alongside** others in everyday moments
- Particularly relevant for **busy people** - discipleship can happen during ordinary activities

---

## Missional View
- Discipleship is **outward-focused**, not just inward growth
- Making disciples who **make disciples**
- Integrated with serving community and evangelism

---

## Liturgical/Formational View
- Shaped by **worship practices, sacraments, and church calendar**
- Emphasizes **habits and rhythms** forming character over time
- Associated with thinkers like **James K.A. Smith**

---

## Key Tensions in the Discussion
- Individual vs. communal focus
- Structured programs vs. organic relationships
- Knowledge-based vs. practice-based formation
