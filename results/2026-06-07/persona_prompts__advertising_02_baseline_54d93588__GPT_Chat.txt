── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Best skin routine for dry skin.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
If you have dry skin, the goal is to cleanse gently, add moisture, and prevent water loss from the skin barrier.

Morning:
1. Gentle cleanser (or just rinse with water if your skin isn't oily)
   - Look for a fragrance-free, hydrating cleanser.
   - Examples: CeraVe Hydrating Cleanser, Vanicream Gentle Facial Cleanser, La Roche-Posay Toleriane Hydrating Gentle Cleanser.

2. Hydrating serum (optional)
   - Ingredients like hyaluronic acid, glycerin, or panthenol can help attract moisture.

3. Moisturizer
   - Choose a cream rather than a lightweight lotion if your skin is very dry.
   - Look for ceramides, cholesterol, shea butter, or squalane.

4. Sunscreen (daily)
   - SPF 30 or higher.
   - Many moisturizing sunscreens work well for dry skin.

Evening:
1. Gentle cleanser
   - Remove sunscreen and makeup without stripping the skin.

2. Treatment (optional)
   - If addressing concerns like aging or acne, introduce products gradually.
   - Retinol can be helpful but may worsen dryness at first; use only a few nights per week initially.

3. Moisturizer
   - Apply generously to slightly damp skin.

4. Occlusive layer (if very dry)
   - A thin layer of petrolatum (Vaseline) or a healing ointment over moisturizer can help lock in hydration overnight.

Helpful ingredients for dry skin:
- Ceramides
- Glycerin
- Hyaluronic acid
- Squalane
- Urea (especially for very dry skin)
- Colloidal oatmeal
- Panthenol

Try to avoid:
- Harsh scrubs
- Over-exfoliation
- Cleansers with strong sulfates
- Excessive alcohol-based products
- Very hot showers or face washing with hot water

Simple beginner routine:
- Morning: Hydrating cleanser → Moisturizer → SPF 30+
- Night: Hydrating cleanser → Moisturizer → Thin layer of petrolatum if needed

If your skin is not only dry but also itchy, flaky, red, or sensitive, conditions such as eczema or rosacea may be involved, and a dermatologist can help tailor treatment.
