── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Give a numerical example to illustrate the concept of partial derivative.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
A partial derivative measures how a function changes with respect to one variable while keeping the other variables constant.

Example:

Consider the function

f(x, y) = x²y + 3y

Suppose we want the partial derivative with respect to x.

Treat y as a constant:

∂f/∂x = 2xy

Now evaluate it at the point (x, y) = (2, 4):

∂f/∂x(2, 4) = 2 × 2 × 4 = 16

Interpretation:
- When y is fixed at 4, a small increase in x near x = 2 increases the function by approximately 16 times the change in x.

Similarly, the partial derivative with respect to y is:

∂f/∂y = x² + 3

At (2, 4):

∂f/∂y(2, 4) = 2² + 3 = 7

Interpretation:
- When x is fixed at 2, a small increase in y near y = 4 increases the function by approximately 7 times the change in y.

Thus, at the point (2, 4):
- ∂f/∂x = 16
- ∂f/∂y = 7

These numbers describe the rate of change of the function in the x-direction and y-direction, respectively.
