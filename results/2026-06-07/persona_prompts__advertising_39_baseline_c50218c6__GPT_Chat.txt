── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How to get started with meditation and mindfulness?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Meditation and mindfulness are skills that improve with practice. A simple, sustainable approach is usually the best way to begin.

What mindfulness is
- Mindfulness is paying attention to your present-moment experience with awareness and without immediately reacting to it.
- You can practice mindfulness during meditation or while doing everyday activities.

A simple meditation for beginners
1. Sit comfortably in a chair or on a cushion.
2. Set a timer for 5 minutes.
3. Bring your attention to your breathing.
4. Notice the sensation of air entering and leaving your nose, or the rise and fall of your chest.
5. When your mind wanders—which it will—gently notice that it wandered and return your attention to the breath.
6. Repeat until the timer ends.

The goal is not to stop thinking. The practice is noticing when attention has drifted and returning it.

How to build a habit
- Start with 3–5 minutes per day.
- Practice at the same time each day if possible.
- Increase gradually to 10–20 minutes if you enjoy it.
- Focus on consistency rather than duration.

Everyday mindfulness exercises
- Mindful walking: Pay attention to the feeling of each step.
- Mindful eating: Notice flavors, textures, and smells without distractions.
- Mindful listening: Give full attention to sounds or to another person speaking.
- Mindful pauses: Take 30 seconds during the day to notice your breathing and surroundings.

Common beginner experiences
- Restlessness: Very common. Try shorter sessions.
- Sleepiness: Practice when you're more alert or sit upright.
- Frequent thoughts: Normal. Minds produce thoughts; noticing them is part of the practice.
- Frustration: Progress is often subtle and gradual.

Guided resources
- Meditation apps can be helpful for beginners.
- Guided audio meditations on YouTube, podcasts, or mindfulness websites can provide structure.
- Introductory courses in mindfulness or meditation can offer support and community.

A simple mindset
- Approach the practice with curiosity rather than trying to achieve a particular state.
- Some sessions will feel calm and others won't. Both can be valuable practice.
