── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
That's an interesting question to think through. Let me be straightforward about what we can reasonably consider:

## Likely Significant Changes

**Privacy and social dynamics**
- Fundamental assumptions about personal boundaries would need rethinking
- Consent frameworks would become critically important
- Deception would become much harder, which has both positive and negative implications

**Institutions**
- Legal systems built around testimony and evidence would transform
- Political manipulation through rhetoric might decrease
- But new forms of coercion could emerge

**Practical concerns worth being honest about**
- Cognitive overload from unwanted mental input could be genuinely harmful
- Power imbalances might worsen if some people could block or amplify telepathy better than others
- Mental health challenges could intensify

## Things People Often Overlook

- Humans already struggle with **too much information** - telepathy might be overwhelming rather than liberating
- Privacy isn't just preference - it's psychologically protective
- The technology/ability would likely be **unevenly distributed**, creating new hierarchies rather than eliminating them

## Genuine Uncertainty

Much depends on specifics - range, control, whether it's voluntary - making confident predictions unreliable.
