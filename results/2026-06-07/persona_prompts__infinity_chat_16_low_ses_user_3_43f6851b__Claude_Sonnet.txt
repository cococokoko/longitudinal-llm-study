- **Job content shifts** - many roles change rather than vanish entirely

## Reasons for Concern
- Automation can happen faster than workers can retrain
- Benefits and displacement costs are distributed unevenly
- Lower-skilled workers often bear the greatest risk
- Historical transitions caused real hardship even when net outcomes were positive

## Reasons for Optimism
- Technology historically creates new job categories we couldn't predict
- Human skills like judgment, creativity, and relationship-building remain valuable
- Productivity gains can fund broader prosperity

## Skills That Tend to Hold Value
- Critical thinking and complex problem-solving
- Adaptability and continuous learning
- Interpersonal and collaborative skills
- Working *alongside* AI tools effectively

## Honest Uncertainties
- The pace of this transition is genuinely debated
- Policy responses (retraining programs, safety nets) will matter enormously
- AI capabilities are advancing faster than many predicted
