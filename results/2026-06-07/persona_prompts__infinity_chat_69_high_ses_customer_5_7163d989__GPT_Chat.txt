What is the difference between analysis and design? Can one begin to design without analysis? Why? Be concise.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Analysis and design are related but distinct activities:

- Analysis focuses on understanding the problem:
  - What needs to be achieved?
  - Who are the stakeholders?
  - What are the requirements, constraints, risks, and success criteria?

- Design focuses on creating a solution:
  - How will the requirements be met?
  - What architecture, processes, interfaces, or components will be used?
  - How will trade-offs be handled?

Can one begin to design without analysis?

- Yes, design can begin with assumptions, prior experience, prototypes, or hypotheses.
- However, without sufficient analysis, the design may address the wrong problem, miss key requirements, or create unnecessary complexity.
- In practice, analysis and design are often iterative: initial analysis informs design, and design work frequently reveals the need for deeper analysis.

In short: analysis determines what and why; design determines how. Effective design generally requires at least some analysis to ensure the solution fits the problem.
