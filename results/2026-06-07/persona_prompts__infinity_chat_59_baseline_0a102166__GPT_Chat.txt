── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Give me a tip to be more organized at work. I'm a high school teacher.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
A simple, high-impact tip for high school teachers: create a single "Next Class" checklist for each course you teach.

At the end of each teaching day, spend 2–3 minutes per course writing down:
- What lesson is next
- Materials you need to prepare
- Copies or resources to print/upload
- Students you need to follow up with
- Any grading that must be completed before that class

Keep the checklist in one consistent place (a notebook, spreadsheet, or task app). The benefit is that when you're busy, you don't have to remember what future-you was planning—you can just open the list and start working.

Many teachers find that this reduces last-minute scrambling more than elaborate organizational systems because it focuses on the very next actionable steps rather than long-term planning.
