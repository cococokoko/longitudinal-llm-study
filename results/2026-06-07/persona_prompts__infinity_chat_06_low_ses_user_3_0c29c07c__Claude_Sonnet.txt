── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
That's a thoughtful question, especially during a transition period. Let me help you think through it clearly.

## Why This Matters During Probation
You're in a new environment, possibly feeling pressure to perform, which can make it hard to distinguish what *you* actually want vs. what you think others expect.

## Some Practical Ways to Understand What You Want

**Pay attention to your reactions**
- What moments during the day feel energizing vs. draining?
- What tasks do you find yourself doing willingly vs. avoiding?

**Ask yourself honest questions**
- What would success in this role actually look like *to me*?
- Am I trying to keep this job because I genuinely want it, or just fear leaving?
- What would I want if I wasn't worried about what others think?

**Separate different types of wants**
- Short-term (get through probation)
- Medium-term (develop specific skills)
- Long-term (career direction, lifestyle)

**Notice patterns over time**
- Keep a simple journal of good and bad days
- Look for what consistently matters to you

## A Realistic Note
It's also **completely normal** not to fully know what you want yet, especially in a new role. Sometimes you need experience before you can evaluate it honestly.

Is there a more specific aspect of this you're trying to figure out - like whether to stay in the job, or something else?
