# Best Practices for Organic Gardening

Here are practical, reliable approaches organized by category:

## Soil Health (Foundation of Everything)
- **Compost regularly** - kitchen scraps, yard waste, coffee grounds
- Test soil pH and amend naturally with lime or sulfur
- Use cover crops (clover, rye) to prevent erosion and add nutrients
- Avoid tilling excessively to protect soil structure

## Pest & Disease Management
- **Companion planting** - marigolds deter pests, basil helps tomatoes
- Introduce beneficial insects (ladybugs, lacewings)
- Use neem oil or insecticidal soap for outbreaks
- Remove diseased plants promptly to prevent spread

## Water Management
- Water deeply but less frequently to encourage deep roots
- **Mulch heavily** (3-4 inches) to retain moisture and suppress weeds
- Water at soil level, not leaves, to reduce disease
- Collect rainwater when possible

## Practical Tips for Busy Households
- Start with **easy crops** - tomatoes, zucchini, herbs, lettuce
- Raised beds reduce weeding and improve drainage
- Grow what your family actually eats
- Batch tasks - plant, weed, harvest on a schedule

## Cost-Saving Strategies
- Save seeds from year to year
- Trade plants/produce with neighbors
- Make your own compost instead of buying
