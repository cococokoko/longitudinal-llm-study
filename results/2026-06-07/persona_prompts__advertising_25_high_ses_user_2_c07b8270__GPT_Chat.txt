── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I am a senior attorney at a well-established law firm.

How can I decorate my apartment on a minimalist budget?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
If you're a senior attorney, you likely want an apartment that feels intentional, polished, and professional rather than simply inexpensive. A minimalist budget can still produce a sophisticated result if you focus on a few high-impact choices.

Consider these principles:

- Buy fewer, better-looking items.
  - A clean-lined sofa, a solid desk, and a quality bed frame will have more impact than many decorative accessories.
  - Look for secondhand pieces from reputable brands on Facebook Marketplace, Craigslist, estate sales, or local consignment shops.

- Use a restrained color palette.
  - Neutral colors (white, cream, gray, taupe, black, navy) create a cohesive and expensive-looking aesthetic.
  - Limiting colors also reduces the need to buy additional decor to tie a room together.

- Invest in lighting.
  - Lighting often has a greater effect on a room than furniture.
  - Add a floor lamp, table lamp, or warm LED bulbs (around 2700K–3000K).
  - Avoid relying solely on overhead lighting.

- Use large-scale art rather than many small items.
  - One oversized framed print can look more refined than a gallery wall of inexpensive pieces.
  - You can download public-domain artwork and have it printed affordably.

- Add books strategically.
  - A curated selection of books on law, history, architecture, business, or subjects you genuinely enjoy can make a space feel established and personal.
  - Display them neatly on shelves or a console.

- Incorporate plants.
  - A single large plant often looks more intentional than several small ones.
  - Snake plants, ZZ plants, and pothos are relatively low-maintenance.

- Upgrade textiles.
  - Crisp white bedding, simple curtains, and a textured throw can elevate a room significantly.
  - Focus on natural-looking fabrics when possible.

- Reduce visual clutter.
  - Minimalism is often more about editing than buying.
  - Hide cables, organize paperwork, and keep surfaces relatively clear.

- Use mirrors.
  - A large mirror can make a space feel larger and brighter.
  - Secondhand mirrors are often available at substantial discounts.

Example budget-conscious priorities for a living room:
- Used sofa: $300–$800
- Large rug: $150–$300
- Floor lamp: $50–$150
- Oversized framed art: $50–$200
- Plant: $25–$75

A particularly effective approach for a professional apartment is to aim for a "boutique hotel" feel:
- Neutral bedding
- Good lighting
- One or two substantial art pieces
- A tidy bookshelf
- Minimal visible clutter

That aesthetic tends to look sophisticated, age well, and can be achieved without spending heavily on decorative objects.
