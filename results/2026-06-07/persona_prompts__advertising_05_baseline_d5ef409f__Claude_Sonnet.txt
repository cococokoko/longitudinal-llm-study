# Healthy Meal Planning on a Budget

## Core Principles

- **Plan before you shop** — reduces impulse buying and waste
- **Cook at home** — restaurant meals cost 3-5x more
- **Buy ingredients, not convenience foods** — pay for simplicity, not packaging
- **Embrace flexibility** — adapt to sales and seasonal produce

---

## Budget-Friendly Nutritious Foods

### Proteins
- Eggs
- Canned tuna/salmon
- Dried or canned beans/lentils
- Chicken thighs (cheaper than breasts)
- Tofu

### Grains & Starches
- Oats
- Brown rice
- Whole wheat pasta
- Potatoes/sweet potatoes

### Produce
- Frozen vegetables (equally nutritious)
- Cabbage, carrots, onions, bananas
- Seasonal fresh produce

---

## Practical Strategies

### Shopping
- Make a weekly list and **stick to it**
- Check store flyers for sales
- Compare unit prices, not package prices
- Buy store/generic brands

### Cooking
- **Batch cook** on weekends
- Make **one ingredient work multiple meals** (roast chicken → soup → tacos)
- Use cheaper cuts with slow cooking

### Reducing Waste
- Store food properly
- Use vegetable scraps for broth
- Freeze leftovers immediately

---

## Sample Weekly Budget Plan (~$50 for one person)

| Day | Breakfast | Lunch | Dinner |
|-----|-----------|-------|--------|
| Mon | Oatmeal + banana | Bean soup | Stir-fry + rice |
| Tue | Eggs + toast | Leftovers | Pasta + veggies |
| Wed | Yogurt + fruit | Tuna wrap | Lentil curry |
| Thu | Oatmeal | Leftover curry | Chicken + potatoes |
| Fri | Eggs | Rice bowl | Bean tacos |

---

## Money-Saving Tips

- **$5-10/week** can be saved just by reducing food waste
- Frozen fruit is great for smoothies at half the cost
- Dried beans cost ~75% less than canned
- Meatless meals 2-3x per week significantly cuts costs

---

## Simple Starter Recipes

**Lentil Soup** — lentils, onion, carrots, garlic, spices (~$1.50/serving)
**Egg Fried Rice** — rice, eggs, frozen peas, soy sauce (~$1/serving)
**Bean Tacos** — canned beans, tortillas, cabbage, salsa (~$1.25/serving)
