What are the best resources for starting a plant-based diet?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
If you're starting a plant-based diet, the most useful resources tend to fall into three categories: evidence-based nutrition guidance, practical meal planning, and recipes.

Evidence-based nutrition

- NutritionFacts.org (Dr. Michael Greger)
  - Extensive library of videos and articles focused on plant-based nutrition and health research.
  - Good for understanding the science behind dietary choices.

- Physicians Committee for Responsible Medicine (PCRM)
  - Offers free plant-based starter guides, meal plans, and educational resources.
  - Strong emphasis on clinical research and practical implementation.

- Harvard T.H. Chan School of Public Health
  - Resources on healthy eating patterns, plant proteins, whole grains, and dietary quality.
  - Useful for balanced, mainstream nutrition guidance.

- Academy of Nutrition and Dietetics
  - Position papers and articles on vegetarian and vegan diets.
  - Helpful for understanding nutrient requirements.

Books

- How Not to Die by Michael Greger
  - Research-focused overview of plant-based eating and chronic disease prevention.

- The Plant-Based Diet for Beginners by Gabriel Miller
  - Accessible introduction with meal plans and recipes.

- Fiber Fueled by Dr. Will Bulsiewicz
  - Focuses on gut health and increasing plant diversity in the diet.

Meal planning and tracking

- Forks Over Knives
  - Recipes, meal planners, cooking guides, and educational content.
  - Particularly useful if you're interested in whole-food, plant-based eating.

- Cronometer
  - Nutrition tracking app that can help ensure you're meeting needs for protein, iron, calcium, zinc, omega-3s, and vitamin B12.

Recipe resources

- Minimalist Baker
  - Simple recipes with limited ingredients.

- Rainbow Plant Life
  - Flavor-focused vegan cooking with excellent tutorials.

- Love & Lemons
  - Vegetable-centered recipes that appeal to both plant-based and omnivorous eaters.

- Pick Up Limes
  - Recipes, meal plans, and nutrition education from a registered dietitian.

Key nutrients to pay attention to

- Vitamin B12 (supplementation is generally recommended for fully plant-based diets)
- Protein
- Iron
- Calcium
- Omega-3 fatty acids
- Iodine
- Zinc
- Vitamin D (depending on sun exposure and location)

A practical way to begin is:
- Start with one plant-based meal per day for 1–2 weeks.
- Build meals around legumes, whole grains, vegetables, fruits, nuts, and seeds.
- Replace familiar foods rather than overhaul everything at once (e.g., bean chili instead of beef chili).
- Learn 5–10 reliable recipes before expanding further.

If your goal is health optimization, athletic performance, weight management, sustainability, or reducing animal products without going fully vegan, I can recommend a more tailored set of resources.
