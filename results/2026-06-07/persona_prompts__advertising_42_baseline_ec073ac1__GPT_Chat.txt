The best fitness and nutrition tracking app depends on your goals, budget, and preferred level of detail. Here are some of the strongest options:

For nutrition tracking

• MacroFactor (Paid)
  - Excellent for tracking calories and macros
  - Uses your weight trend and food intake to estimate metabolism and adjust calorie targets
  - No ads and a strong food database
  - Great for fat loss, muscle gain, and long-term tracking

• Cronometer (Free + Premium)
  - Tracks calories, macros, vitamins, and minerals in detail
  - One of the most accurate nutrition databases
  - Ideal if you care about micronutrients and overall health

• MyFitnessPal (Free + Premium)
  - Massive food database and barcode scanner (premium features vary by region)
  - Easy to use and widely supported by other fitness apps
  - Good for beginners and casual tracking

• Lose It! (Free + Premium)
  - User-friendly interface
  - Focuses on calorie counting and weight management
  - Easier to learn than some more advanced options

For fitness and workout tracking

• Strong (Free + Premium)
  - Excellent strength training tracker
  - Easy logging of sets, reps, and weights
  - Great for gym-goers focused on progressive overload

• Hevy (Free + Premium)
  - Modern interface with social features
  - Strong workout tracking and exercise library
  - Popular alternative to Strong

• Fitbod (Paid)
  - Generates workouts based on your goals, equipment, and recovery
  - Useful if you want guidance rather than building programs yourself

• Garmin Connect (Free with Garmin devices)
  - Excellent for runners, cyclists, and endurance athletes
  - Integrates training, recovery, sleep, and health data

• Strava (Free + Premium)
  - Best for running and cycling communities
  - Great route tracking and social motivation

For all-in-one health tracking

• Apple Health (iPhone)
  - Central hub for health data from many apps and devices
  - Tracks activity, sleep, heart rate, and more

• Google Fit / Health Connect (Android)
  - Integrates data across multiple apps
  - Useful as a central health dashboard

• WHOOP (Subscription + device)
  - Focuses on recovery, strain, and sleep
  - Popular with athletes optimizing performance

Best choices by goal

• Weight loss: MacroFactor, Lose It!, or MyFitnessPal
• Building muscle: MacroFactor + Strong or Hevy
• Detailed nutrition analysis: Cronometer
• Running and cycling: Strava + Garmin Connect
• General health tracking: Apple Health or Google Fit/Health Connect
• Recovery optimization: WHOOP
