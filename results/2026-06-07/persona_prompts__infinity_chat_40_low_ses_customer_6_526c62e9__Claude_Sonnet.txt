My girlfriend is buying a house. I plan to marry her in a year or so. I don't know if it's fair for me to pay for the house or not. What's the best solution?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This is a really common situation and there are a few reasonable approaches to think through:

## Key Considerations

**Your current situation**
- You're not married yet, so you have no legal claim to the property
- If you contribute significantly and things don't work out, you could lose that money
- Her name on the deed means she has full ownership rights

## Practical Options

**Option 1: Don't contribute to the mortgage**
- Pay rent to her instead
- Keeps finances clean and protects you both
- Simple if the relationship doesn't work out

**Option 2: Formal contribution agreement**
- If you contribute, get a **legal cohabitation agreement** documenting your investment
- A lawyer can help protect your financial interest

**Option 3: Wait until marriage**
- Add your name to the deed after marriage
- Refinance together
- Cleaner legal standing

**Option 4: Split expenses differently**
- You cover other household costs like utilities, groceries, repairs
- She covers the mortgage
- Balances financial contribution without the legal complexity

## My Honest Recommendation

**Talk to a real estate attorney before committing money.** Given your warehouse work schedule, protecting your hard-earned income matters. A cohabitation agreement is relatively inexpensive and protects you both.

**Most importantly - have an open financial conversation with your girlfriend** about expectations before the purchase closes.
