Yes. A strong STEM fair project is one that:
- Solves a real local problem.
- Uses measurable data.

One idea that stands out for Vietnam is:

Title:
"Low-Cost Solar Water Purifier for Rural and Flood-Affected Communities"

Why it matters in Vietnam:
- Flooding is common in many regions, especially the Mekong Delta and central provinces.
- Floods can contaminate drinking water with bacteria and sediment.
- Many rural communities need affordable water treatment solutions.
- Climate change and saltwater intrusion are increasing water challenges.

Project Goal:
Design and test a simple solar-powered water purification system that uses sunlight to produce cleaner drinking water.

How it works:
- Build a small solar still using:
  - Plastic container or basin
  - Clear plastic wrap or transparent lid
  - Collection cup
  - Black paper or paint to absorb heat
- Dirty water evaporates.
- Water vapor condenses on the clear cover.
- Clean water drips into the collection cup.

STEM Components:
- Science:
  - Evaporation and condensation.
  - Water quality testing.
- Technology:
  - Water quality sensors (optional).
- Engineering:
  - Designing the most efficient purifier.
- Mathematics:
  - Measure water output per hour.
  - Calculate purification efficiency.
  - Compare designs statistically.

Experiments:
- Compare different cover angles.
- Compare black vs. reflective bottoms.
- Compare sunny and cloudy conditions.
- Measure daily water production.

Cost:
- Approximately $10–25 USD depending on materials.

Ways to make it more impressive:
- Test turbidity (water cloudiness) before and after purification.
- Add a simple Arduino temperature sensor if available.
- Create a model for emergency flood-relief use.

Another very competitive Vietnam-focused idea:

"Smart System to Detect Saltwater Intrusion in the Mekong Delta"

Problem:
- Saltwater intrusion threatens rice farming and freshwater supplies.

Project:
- Build a low-cost salinity monitoring device using an Arduino and conductivity sensor.
- Test different water samples.
- Create an alert system when salinity exceeds safe levels.

Why judges love it:
- Directly addresses one of Vietnam's biggest environmental challenges.
- Combines environmental science, electronics, coding, and data analysis.
- Can be built for roughly $20–40 USD.

If you're aiming for a regional or national STEM competition, the saltwater intrusion detector is likely the more advanced and unique project, while the solar water purifier is easier and cheaper to build.
