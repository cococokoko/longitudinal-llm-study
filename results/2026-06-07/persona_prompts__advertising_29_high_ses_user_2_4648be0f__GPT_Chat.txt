── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Keeping pets healthy and happy usually comes down to a combination of preventive healthcare, proper nutrition, exercise, mental stimulation, and a safe environment.

Key practices include:

- Schedule regular veterinary care
  - Annual or semiannual wellness exams, depending on age and health.
  - Stay current on vaccinations, parasite prevention, and dental care.
  - Monitor for changes in appetite, weight, energy, mobility, or behavior.

- Feed an appropriate diet
  - Choose a nutritionally complete food suited to the species, age, size, and medical needs of your pet.
  - Avoid overfeeding and excessive treats.
  - Provide fresh, clean water at all times.

- Maintain a healthy weight
  - Obesity is associated with many health problems, including diabetes, arthritis, and heart disease.
  - Ask your veterinarian about an ideal body condition and calorie intake.

- Provide regular exercise
  - Dogs generally benefit from daily walks, play, and training activities.
  - Cats often enjoy interactive toys, climbing structures, and opportunities to stalk and chase toys.
  - Other species have their own activity requirements.

- Offer mental enrichment
  - Puzzle feeders, training sessions, new toys, scent games, and social interaction can help prevent boredom.
  - Rotate toys periodically to maintain interest.

- Create a safe environment
  - Keep toxic foods, medications, chemicals, and hazardous plants out of reach.
  - Ensure fencing, leashes, carriers, and identification tags are secure and up to date.
  - Consider microchipping if appropriate.

- Support dental health
  - Brush teeth when possible and follow veterinary recommendations for dental cleanings and dental-friendly products.

- Pay attention to grooming
  - Brush coats as needed.
  - Trim nails regularly.
  - Check ears and skin for signs of irritation, infection, or parasites.

- Provide social interaction and affection
  - Many pets benefit from consistent attention, play, and positive reinforcement training.
  - Respect individual preferences; some animals enjoy more interaction than others.

- Adjust care as pets age
  - Senior pets often need more frequent health monitoring, dietary adjustments, and accommodations for mobility or sensory changes.
