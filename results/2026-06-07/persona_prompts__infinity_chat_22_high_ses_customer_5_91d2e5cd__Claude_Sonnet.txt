# Different Views on Discipleship

Discipleship is understood quite differently across theological traditions, denominations, and thinkers. Here's a structured overview:

---

## Core Theological Perspectives

### **Traditional/Classical View**
- Discipleship as **imitation of Christ** (following his example and teachings)
- Emphasis on spiritual disciplines, prayer, Scripture study
- Formation within the **community of the church**
- Associated with thinkers like Thomas à Kempis (*The Imitation of Christ*)

### **Reformation View**
- Discipleship rooted in **grace, not merit**
- Luther emphasized faith as the foundation; works flow from it
- Calvin stressed **sanctification** as a lifelong process of conformity to Christ

### **Bonhoeffer's View**
- Distinguished **costly grace vs. cheap grace**
- Discipleship demands real sacrifice and obedience
- Famous work: *The Cost of Discipleship*

---

## Contemporary Perspectives

### **Evangelical View**
- Strong emphasis on **personal conversion** followed by growth
- Bible study, accountability, mentoring relationships
- Often structured programs (e.g., Navigator methods)

### **Catholic View**
- Discipleship through **sacraments, tradition, and community**
- Formation is lifelong and ecclesial
- Mary and saints as models

### **Liberation Theology View**
- Discipleship expressed through **solidarity with the poor**
- Following Jesus means confronting systemic injustice
- Associated with Gustavo Gutiérrez

### **Missional View**
- Discipleship is inherently **outward-focused**
- Every believer as a missionary in their context
- Associated with Alan Hirsch, Michael Frost

---

## Key Tensions in the Debate

| Question | Competing Views |
|----------|----------------|
| Individual vs. communal? | Personal growth vs. collective formation |
| Knowledge vs. practice? | Doctrinal understanding vs. lived obedience |
| Structured vs. organic? | Programs vs. relational apprenticeship |
| Inward vs. outward? | Spiritual formation vs. social engagement |
| Clergy-led vs. lay-driven? | Institutional vs. decentralized models |
