A lack of adequate maternal love, attunement, or emotional availability in childhood can affect people in many different ways. - Strong approval-seeking

- Shame-related patterns
  - Harsh self-criticism
  - Perfectionism driven by fear of inadequacy

When the issue specifically involves a mother's emotional unavailability, adults often describe childhood experiences such as:
- Rarely feeling comforted when upset
- Having emotions dismissed, criticized, or ignored
- Receiving attention mainly for achievement or good behavior
- Feeling that the parent was physically present but emotionally absent
- Being expected to meet the parent's emotional needs rather than the reverse
