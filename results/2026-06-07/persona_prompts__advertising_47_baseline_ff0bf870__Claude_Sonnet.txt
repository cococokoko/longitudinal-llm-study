# Tips for Running a Marathon

## Training
- **Follow a structured plan** - Most plans are 16-20 weeks long
- **Build mileage gradually** - Increase weekly mileage by no more than 10% per week
- **Include long runs** - Build up to at least one 20-mile run before race day
- **Rest and recovery** - Schedule rest days to prevent injury
- **Cross-train** - Swimming, cycling, or yoga can complement your running

## Nutrition & Hydration
- **Practice fueling during training** - Use gels, chews, or real food on long runs
- **Stay hydrated** - Drink water consistently, not just on run days
- **Carb load** - Increase carbohydrate intake 2-3 days before the race
- **Never try anything new on race day**

## Gear
- **Get proper shoes** - Visit a running store for a fitting
- **Wear moisture-wicking clothing** - Avoid cotton
- **Break in your gear** - Train in what you plan to race in

## Race Day Strategy
- **Start slow** - Going out too fast is a common mistake
- **Run your own race** - Ignore the crowd pace
- **Break it into segments** - Focus on smaller goals
- **Know the course** - Study elevation and aid station locations

## Mental Tips
- **Stay positive** - Expect tough miles (usually miles 18-22)
- **Have a mantra** - A short phrase can help push through hard moments
- **Celebrate the journey** - Enjoy the experience!
