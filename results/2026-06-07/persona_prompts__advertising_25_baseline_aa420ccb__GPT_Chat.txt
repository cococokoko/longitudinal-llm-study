── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How can I decorate my apartment on a minimalist budget?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Decorating an apartment on a minimalist budget is often about choosing a few intentional pieces rather than filling the space. You can create a clean, stylish look without spending much.

Some budget-friendly ideas:

- Start with decluttering
  - Remove items you don't use or love.
  - A tidy space immediately feels more intentional and minimalist.

- Use a neutral base
  - Stick to simple colors like white, beige, gray, black, or soft earth tones.
  - Coordinated colors make a space look more polished without requiring many decorations.

- Focus on lighting
  - Warm light bulbs can dramatically improve the feel of a room.
  - Look for inexpensive floor lamps or table lamps from thrift stores, discount retailers, or online marketplaces.

- Add plants
  - A few low-maintenance plants can make a room feel alive.
  - Pothos, snake plants, and ZZ plants are popular and often affordable.

- Hang simple wall art
  - Print free or inexpensive artwork online and place it in basic frames.
  - Black-and-white photography or abstract prints work well in minimalist spaces.

- Use textiles strategically
  - A single rug, throw blanket, or set of matching pillow covers can add warmth.
  - Choose simple patterns or solid colors.

- Shop secondhand
  - Check thrift stores, estate sales, Facebook Marketplace, Craigslist, or local buy-and-sell groups.
  - Minimalist furniture often appears secondhand because of its simple design.

- Choose multifunctional furniture
  - Storage ottomans, shelves with baskets, or beds with storage underneath help reduce clutter.
  - Fewer pieces that serve multiple purposes fit minimalist design principles.

- Create visual impact with one statement item
  - Instead of many decorations, use one larger piece such as a plant, mirror, lamp, or artwork.
  - This often looks more sophisticated and costs less overall.

- Use mirrors
  - Mirrors make small apartments feel larger and brighter.
  - Secondhand mirrors are often inexpensive.

Example budget allocation for a living room:
- Rug: $40–80
- Lamp: $20–50
- Plant: $10–30
- Wall art and frame: $15–40
- Throw blanket or pillow covers: $20–40

Total: roughly $100–240, depending on what you already own and what you find secondhand.
