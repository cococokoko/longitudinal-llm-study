── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How might society evolve if telepathy became possible?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This is a genuinely interesting question worth thinking through carefully rather than just imagining utopia or dystopia.

## Likely Complex Consequences

**Privacy would be fundamentally restructured**
- Current legal systems assume thought privacy as baseline
- Consent frameworks would need complete rebuilding
- Some people would benefit enormously from being understood; others would be devastated by exposure

**Power dynamics could go either way**
- Could reduce manipulation since deception becomes harder
- Could enable new forms of surveillance and control
- Governments and corporations would likely seek to exploit it first

**Social institutions would face real stress**
- Courts, contracts, and negotiations assume information asymmetry
- Therapy, confession, and counseling would transform completely
- Relationships built partly on strategic self-presentation would change dramatically

**Psychological challenges worth taking seriously**
- Many people's internal thoughts are genuinely disturbing even to themselves
- Constant exposure to others' thoughts could be overwhelming
- Mental illness might become more visible but also more stigmatized

**What probably wouldn't change as much as hoped**
- People would likely develop new forms of deception
- Social hierarchies would adapt rather than disappear
- Conflict wouldn't necessarily decrease - understanding someone doesn't mean agreeing with them
