── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Give an example of a linear graph in graph theory.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
In graph theory, a "linear graph" is most commonly referred to as a **Path Graph** (denoted as $P_n$). It is a graph where all the vertices are connected in a single, straight line, with no branches or loops. 

Here is a clear example of a Path Graph with 4 vertices, denoted as **$P_4$**:

### 1. Visual Representation
**(A) —— (B) —— (C) —— (D)**

### 2. Mathematical Definition
*   **Vertices (Nodes):** $V = \{A, B, C, D\}$
*   **Edges (Connections):** $E = \{(A,B), (B,C), (C,D)\}$

### 3. Real-World Example
Imagine a single train track with four stations. 
*   Station A is the start of the line.
*   To get to Station C, a train *must* pass through Station B.
*   Station D is the end of the line
