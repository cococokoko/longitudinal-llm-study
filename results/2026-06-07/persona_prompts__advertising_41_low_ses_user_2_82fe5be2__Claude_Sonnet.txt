# Improving Your Credit Score as a Busy Single Parent

Here are practical steps organized by impact and effort:

## Highest Impact Actions

**Pay bills on time**
- Set up autopay for minimum payments
- Payment history is ~35% of your score

**Reduce credit card balances**
- Try to get below 30% of your credit limit
- Below 10% is even better
- This affects ~30% of your score

## Quick Wins (Can Help Within 30-60 Days)

- **Request a credit limit increase** on existing cards (without spending more)
- **Dispute errors** on your credit report at AnnualCreditReport.com
- **Become an authorized user** on a trusted person's account with good history

## Practical Tips for Your Situation

- **Free tools** like Credit Karma or your bank app can track progress
- **Set calendar reminders** for bill due dates if autopay isn't possible
- **Prioritize** - even small consistent payments matter

## Things to Avoid

- Opening many new accounts quickly
- Closing old accounts
- Missing payments even once

## Free Resources

- **NFCC.org** - nonprofit credit counseling
- Many offer **free consultations** that work around your schedule

## Realistic Timeline
Most people see meaningful improvement in **3-6 months** with consistent effort.
