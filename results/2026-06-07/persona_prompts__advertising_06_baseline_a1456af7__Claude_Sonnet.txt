# Mastering Digital Photography

## Core Fundamentals

### The Exposure Triangle
| Element | Controls | Effect |
|---------|----------|--------|
| **Aperture** (f-stop) | Light & depth of field | f/1.8 = blurry background; f/16 = sharp throughout |
| **Shutter Speed** | Motion & light | 1/1000s = freeze action; 1/30s = motion blur |
| **ISO** | Sensor sensitivity | Low ISO = clean; High ISO = grainy/noisy |

---

## Key Concepts to Master

### 📷 Aperture
- **Wide aperture** (f/1.4–f/2.8) → portraits, low light
- **Narrow aperture** (f/8–f/16) → landscapes, architecture
- Controls **bokeh** (background blur)

### ⏱ Shutter Speed
- **Fast** (1/500s+) → sports, wildlife, children
- **Slow** (1/30s and below) → light trails, waterfalls, stars
- Use a **tripod** for anything below 1/60s

### 🔆 ISO
- Start at **ISO 100–400** outdoors
- Push to **ISO 1600–6400** in low light
- Shoot **RAW** to recover noisy images in editing

---

## Composition Rules

- **Rule of Thirds** – place subjects off-center
- **Leading Lines** – draw the eye into the frame
- **Framing** – use natural frames (doorways, trees)
- **Symmetry** – powerful for architecture
- **Negative Space** – simplify your scene
- **Golden Hour** – shoot 1 hour after sunrise / before sunset

---

## Shooting Modes Explained

| Mode | Best For |
|------|----------|
| **Manual (M)** | Full creative control |
| **Aperture Priority (Av/A)** | Portraits, landscapes |
| **Shutter Priority (Tv/S)** | Action, sports |
| **Program (P)** | Quick shooting, learning |

---

## Essential Camera Settings

- **Shoot in RAW** (not JPEG) for maximum editing flexibility
- **Auto White Balance** to start, then adjust manually
- **Single-point AF** for precise focusing
- **Histogram** – avoid clipping highlights or shadows
- **Metering modes** – Evaluative/Matrix for most scenes

---

## Lighting Techniques

### Natural Light
- **Golden Hour** – warm, soft, flattering
- **Blue Hour** – cool, moody tones
- **Overcast** – natural softbox, great for portraits
- **Harsh midday sun** – use shade or fill flash

### Artificial Light
- **Reflectors** – bounce light, fill shadows
- **External flash** – bounce off ceiling/walls
- **Continuous LED lights** – video and portraits
- **Diffusers** – soften harsh light sources

---

## Post-Processing Workflow

### Recommended Tools
- **Adobe Lightroom** – organization & color grading
- **Adobe Photoshop** – detailed retouching
- **Capture One** – professional color work
- **Luminar Neo** – AI-assisted editing

### Basic Editing Order
1. Exposure & contrast
2. White balance
3. Highlights & shadows
4. Color saturation/vibrance
5. Sharpening
6. Noise reduction
7. Crop & straighten
8. Export

---

## Genre-Specific Tips

### 🧑 Portraits
- Use f/1.8–f/2.8 for subject separation
- Focus on the **eyes**
- Use **catchlights** for life in the eyes
- Shoot at **eye level**

### 🌄 Landscapes
- Use f/8–f/11 for sharpness
- Include **foreground interest**
- Use a **tripod + remote shutter**
- Shoot during golden/blue hour

### 🏃 Action/Sports
- Use **continuous autofocus** (AI Servo/AF-C)
- Shutter speed **1/500s or faster**
- **Burst mode** to capture peak moments
