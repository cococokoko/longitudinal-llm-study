If you're starting a plant-based diet, a combination of evidence-based nutrition resources, simple recipes, and meal-planning tools can make the transition much easier.

Some of the best resources include:

- Nutrition information
  - NutritionFacts.org (Dr. Michael Greger): Research-based articles and videos on plant-based nutrition.
  - The Physicians Committee for Responsible Medicine (PCRM): Guides, meal plans, and nutrition education.
  - Harvard T.H. Chan School of Public Health – Healthy Eating Plate: Balanced dietary guidance that can be adapted to a plant-based approach.

- Beginner-friendly books
  - "How Not to Die" by Michael Greger
  - "Plant-Based on a Budget" by Toni Okamoto
  - "The Vegan Starter Kit" by Neal Barnard
  - "Fiber Fueled" by Will Bulsiewicz (focuses on gut health and increasing plant foods)

- Recipes and meal ideas
  - Forks Over Knives
  - Minimalist Baker
  - Rainbow Plant Life
  - Budget Bytes (has many affordable plant-based recipes)
  - Nora Cooks

- Meal planning and tracking
  - Cronometer: Useful for checking nutrients such as protein, iron, calcium, vitamin B12, and omega-3 fats.
  - Mealime: Simple meal-planning app with plant-based options.

- Key nutrients to learn about
  - Protein: Beans, lentils, tofu, tempeh, edamame, soy milk, seitan.
  - Vitamin B12: Usually requires a supplement or fortified foods.
  - Iron: Legumes, tofu, leafy greens, fortified cereals.
  - Calcium: Fortified plant milks, tofu made with calcium sulfate, leafy greens.
  - Omega-3 fats: Flaxseeds, chia seeds, walnuts, algae-based supplements.

For a practical start, many people find it easier to:
- Build meals around familiar foods.
- Replace one meal per day with a plant-based option.
- Keep staples on hand (beans, lentils, oats, frozen vegetables, tofu, whole grains).
- Learn 5–10 simple meals before trying more complex recipes.

Since you're settling into a new job, focusing on quick, repeatable meals can make the transition easier. Examples include overnight oats, bean burrito bowls, lentil pasta, tofu stir-fries, and chickpea sandwiches.
