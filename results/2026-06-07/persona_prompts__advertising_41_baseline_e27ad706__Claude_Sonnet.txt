── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How can I improve my credit score quickly?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here are some effective ways to improve your credit score:

## Highest Impact Actions
- **Pay down credit card balances** - reducing your credit utilization ratio (below 30%, ideally below 10%) can show results quickly
- **Pay all bills on time** - payment history is the biggest factor (~35% of your score)
- **Become an authorized user** on someone else's account with good history

## Fix Errors
- Check your credit reports at **AnnualCreditReport.com** (free)
- Dispute any inaccurate negative items
- This can sometimes raise scores within 30-60 days

## Other Helpful Steps
- **Don't close old accounts** - length of credit history matters
- **Avoid applying for new credit** frequently (hard inquiries lower scores temporarily)
- **Ask for a credit limit increase** on existing cards (lowers utilization without new debt)

## Realistic Timeline
- Some changes reflect in **30-60 days**
- Significant improvement typically takes **3-6+ months**
- Serious negative marks (bankruptcy, collections) take longer to overcome

## Things to Avoid
- Credit repair companies that charge fees for things you can do yourself
- Closing multiple accounts at once
