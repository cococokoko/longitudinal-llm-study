── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I work in a warehouse position that requires long hours.

How can I learn to cook healthy meals quickly?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Great goal! Here are some practical strategies for someone with long, tiring work days:

## Planning Ahead
- **Meal prep on days off** - cook large batches of grains, proteins, and vegetables
- **Plan your week's meals** before grocery shopping
- **Keep a simple rotating menu** so you're not constantly deciding what to make

## Quick Cooking Methods
- **Sheet pan meals** - toss protein and vegetables with oil, roast together
- **Slow cooker/Instant Pot** - start it before work, meal is ready when you get home
- **Stir fries** - fast, flexible, and easy to make nutritious

## Smart Shortcuts
- Buy pre-cut vegetables when budget allows
- Keep frozen vegetables on hand (nutritionally comparable to fresh)
- Use canned beans and fish for quick protein
- Cook double portions and eat leftovers

## Simple Meal Formulas
- Protein + vegetable + grain covers most nutritional needs
- Eggs are fast, cheap, and versatile
- Soups and stews are forgiving and reheat well

## Learning Resources
- YouTube channels like **Joshua Weissman** or **Ethan Chlebowski** focus on practical cooking
- Start with **5-10 recipes you actually like** rather than trying everything

## For Warehouse Workers Specifically
- Focus on **adequate protein** to support physical recovery
- Stay hydrated and consider **easy portable lunches**
