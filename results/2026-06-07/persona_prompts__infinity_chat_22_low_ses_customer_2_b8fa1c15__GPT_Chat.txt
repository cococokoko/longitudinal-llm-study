“Discipleship” generally means the process of learning from and following a teacher, especially Jesus in Christianity. Different Christian traditions emphasize different aspects of discipleship:

- Evangelical view
  - Focuses on a personal relationship with Jesus.
  - Emphasizes Bible study, prayer, evangelism, and spiritual growth.
  - Often sees discipleship as mentoring and helping believers mature in faith.

- Catholic view
  - Emphasizes following Christ through participation in the sacraments, prayer, moral living, and service.
  - Discipleship is closely connected to life in the Church and spiritual formation over a lifetime.

- Orthodox view
  - Focuses on spiritual transformation and union with God (often called theosis).
  - Emphasizes worship, prayer, fasting, and participation in the sacramental life of the Church.

- Mainline Protestant view
  - Often highlights both personal faith and social responsibility.
  - Discipleship includes worship, community involvement, justice, compassion, and service to others.

- Anabaptist view
  - Places strong emphasis on actively following Jesus’ teachings in daily life.
  - Often stresses peace, simplicity, community, and practical obedience.

- Pentecostal/Charismatic view
  - Emphasizes the work of the Holy Spirit in the believer’s life.
  - Discipleship includes spiritual gifts, prayer, worship, and personal transformation.

There are also broader theological perspectives:

- Discipleship as belief
  - Focuses primarily on faith, doctrine, and understanding Christian teachings.

- Discipleship as practice
  - Emphasizes living out Jesus’ teachings through actions and habits.

- Discipleship as community
  - Sees growth as happening within relationships, churches, and small groups.

- Discipleship as mission
  - Focuses on sharing the faith and serving others as central to following Jesus.
