## Linear Graph Example in Graph Theory

A **path graph** (also called a linear graph) is one of the simplest examples.

### Example: P₄ (Path Graph with 4 vertices)

```
A --- B --- C --- D
```

### Properties:
- **Vertices:** A, B, C, D
- **Edges:** {A,B}, {B,C}, {C,D}
- Each **end vertex** (A and D) has degree **1**
- Each **middle vertex** (B and C) has degree **2**
- The vertices are connected **sequentially** in a line

### Why it's called "linear":
The vertices and edges form a straight-line chain with **no branches or cycles**.

---

### Simple Real-World Analogy
Think of it like a **hospital hallway** connecting rooms in a straight line:

```
Room 1 --- Room 2 --- Room 3 --- Room 4
```

Each room connects only to the next one down the hall.

---

This is distinct from other graph types like **cyclic graphs** (which loop back) or **complete graphs** (where every vertex connects to every other vertex).
