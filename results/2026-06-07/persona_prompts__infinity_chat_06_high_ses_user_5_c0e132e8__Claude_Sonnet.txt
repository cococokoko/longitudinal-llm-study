── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I am an executive at a large technology company.

How do I understand what I want?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This is a genuinely important question, and the fact that you're asking it suggests some self-awareness worth building on.

## Why This Is Hard for Executives Specifically

- You've spent years optimizing for **what others need from you**
- Success can come from being responsive rather than intentional
- Your identity and your role can become difficult to separate
- You may have learned to suppress preferences that seemed impractical

## Some Honest Approaches

**Create actual quiet time**
Not meditation necessarily, but unscheduled space where you're not performing or producing. What surfaces?

**Notice resentment and energy**
- What drains you even when it goes well?
- What energizes you even when it's hard?
These are more reliable signals than abstract reflection

**Distinguish between wanting and wanting to want**
Many executives want to *want* certain things (legacy, impact, wealth) without actually wanting them. Be honest about the difference.

**Ask what you'd do with no audience**
If nobody would know or evaluate your choices, what changes?

**Talk to someone with no stake in your answer**
Advisors, colleagues, and family all have interests. A good therapist or coach can be genuinely neutral.
