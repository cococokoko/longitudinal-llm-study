── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I am a single parent who often balances childcare with work.

Give me a tip to be more organized at work. I'm a high school teacher.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
One practical tip: create a "daily launch sheet" for each teaching day.

At the end of the school day (or before you leave), spend 5 minutes writing down:
- The top 3 tasks you must complete tomorrow.
- Any materials you'll need for each class.
- Students who need follow-up, grading, or communication.
- One personal reminder related to childcare or family logistics.

Keep it to a single page or digital note. When your day starts, you won't have to spend mental energy figuring out what to do first, which can be especially helpful when you're balancing classroom responsibilities and parenting demands. Many teachers find that this small planning habit reduces forgotten tasks and makes interruptions easier to recover from.
