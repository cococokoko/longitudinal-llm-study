# CUBICLE ZERO
### A Play in Three Acts
*A Post-Apocalyptic Office Comedy*

---

## CHARACTERS

**DILBERT** - An engineer who survived the apocalypse and is deeply disappointed that bureaucracy also survived

**WALLY** - Who has not changed in any measurable way and considers this a personal victory

**ALICE** - Whose fist of death has become significantly more literal

**POINTY-HAIRED BOSS (PHB)** - Who somehow still has a corner office, despite the building having no corners left

**DOGBERT** - Who predicted all of this and is furious nobody listened

**ASOK** - The intern, who is now also the IT department, the HR department, and a load-bearing wall

**CATBERT** - Former Evil HR Director, now just Evil, which is actually a promotion

---

## ACT ONE

### Scene One

*The remains of what was once a mid-sized tech company. The ceiling has partially collapsed. Radiation glows faintly through broken windows. A motivational poster reading "HANG IN THERE" dangles from one screw. Three desks remain, arranged in the same soul-crushing configuration as before the apocalypse. DILBERT sits at his desk, wearing his signature white shirt, now tattered. His tie points upward, as always. He is filling out a form.*

---

**DILBERT:** *(to audience)* The bombs fell on a Tuesday. Which was actually an improvement over our all-hands meeting, which was scheduled for Wednesday. I survived by hiding in the server room. The servers did not survive. Ironically, they are now being used as a server. *(beat)* For soup.

*(WALLY enters, carrying a coffee mug. The mug is glowing slightly.)*

**WALLY:** Good morning.

**DILBERT:** The sky is orange, Wally.

**WALLY:** I know. I asked for it to be changed back but apparently that requires a ticket submitted to Facilities, and Facilities is now a crater.

**DILBERT:** Did you submit the ticket anyway?

**WALLY:** I did. I feel I've done my part.

*(WALLY sits. He sips his coffee. He stares into the middle distance with the contentment of a man who has achieved perfect detachment from all human suffering.)*

**DILBERT:** How is the coffee?

**WALLY:** Radioactive.

**DILBERT:** Is that bad?

**WALLY:** It's warm. I've lowered my standards accordingly.

*(ALICE storms in. She is wearing shoulder armor made from filing cabinet drawers. She is carrying an axe AND a performance review.)*

**ALICE:** I just walked seventeen miles through a wasteland of burning rubble and feral middle managers to get to this office, and do you know what I found on my desk?

**DILBERT:** A reason to keep going?

**ALICE:** A request for a status update on the Henderson project.

**DILBERT:** The Henderson project was destroyed in the initial blast.

**ALICE:** So was Henderson.

**WALLY:** Did you update the project management software?

**ALICE:** The project management software is running on a server that is now part of a warlord's throne.

**WALLY:** So that's a no?

**ALICE:** *(gripping axe)* I am going to need you to stop talking, Wally, or I will use my fist of death, which, I want to be clear, is no longer a metaphor.

**WALLY:** *(sipping coffee)* Noted. I'll note it here. *(writes nothing)*

---
