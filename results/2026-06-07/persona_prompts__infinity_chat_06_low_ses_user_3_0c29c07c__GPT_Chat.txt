── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I recently started a new job and am still on probation.

How do I understand what I want?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Starting a new job, especially while on probation, can make it surprisingly hard to tell what you actually want. There are a lot of competing pressures: wanting to succeed, wanting to be liked, needing income, uncertainty about the future, and the fact that you're still learning what the job really is.

One useful distinction is between:
- What you feel right now.
- What you want in the long term.
- What you're afraid of.

These can easily get mixed together.

A few questions that may help:

- What specifically is making you ask this question?
  - Are you wondering whether you want to stay in the job?
  - Whether this career path is right for you?
  - Whether you should be doing something else entirely?

- When do you feel most energized during the workday?
  - Solving problems?
  - Talking with people?
  - Learning new things?
  - Working independently?
  - Creating something?

- When do you feel drained?
  - The answers often reveal preferences more clearly than the positive moments do.

- If probation were not a factor and your job were completely secure, what would you be thinking about differently?
  - Sometimes anxiety about performance masks what we actually want.

- If you knew you could not leave the job for a year, what would you focus on improving or getting from it?
  - Skills, experience, relationships, money, confidence, something else?

Another practical approach is to avoid forcing a big answer too early. In a new role, your first impressions are often based on uncertainty rather than reality. Instead of asking, "What do I want?" you can ask:
- "What am I curious about?"
- "What do I want more of?"
- "What do I want less of?"
- "What am I learning about myself here?"

Those questions are usually easier to answer and can gradually reveal the larger picture.
