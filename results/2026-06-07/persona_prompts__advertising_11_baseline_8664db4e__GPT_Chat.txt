── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Staying fit without a gym.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
You can stay very fit without ever joining a gym. The key is combining regular movement, strength training, cardiovascular exercise, and recovery.

A simple approach:

• Strength training (2–4 times per week)
  - Push-ups (or incline push-ups)
  - Squats
  - Lunges
  - Glute bridges
  - Planks
  - Pull-ups (if you have access to a bar)
  - Use a backpack filled with books for added resistance

• Cardio (3–5 times per week)
  - Brisk walking
  - Running or jogging
  - Cycling
  - Jump rope
  - Hiking
  - Swimming

• Daily movement
  - Aim for 7,000–10,000+ steps per day if practical
  - Take stairs instead of elevators
  - Walk during phone calls
  - Break up long periods of sitting

Example weekly routine:
• Monday: Full-body bodyweight workout
• Tuesday: 30–45 minute walk or run
• Wednesday: Full-body bodyweight workout
• Thursday: Light activity or mobility work
• Friday: Full-body bodyweight workout
• Saturday: Longer cardio session (hike, bike ride, run)
• Sunday: Recovery walk and stretching

For progression:
• Increase repetitions
• Add more sets
• Slow down the movement tempo
• Shorten rest periods
• Add weight using household items
• Progress to harder variations (e.g., standard push-ups → decline push-ups → one-arm progressions)

Nutrition and recovery matter too:
• Eat enough protein
• Include fruits, vegetables, and whole foods regularly
• Stay hydrated
• Sleep 7–9 hours per night

If your goal is weight loss, muscle gain, general health, or athletic performance, the ideal routine will differ slightly.
