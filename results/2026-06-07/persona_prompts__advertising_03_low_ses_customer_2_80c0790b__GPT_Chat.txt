Working from home can improve flexibility, but it also creates distractions and blurred boundaries. If you're balancing work with caregiving responsibilities, it can be especially helpful to focus on systems that are realistic rather than aiming for an uninterrupted workday.

Some strategies that often help:

- Create a defined workspace
  - Use a specific area for work, even if it's just a corner of a room.
  - Keep essential tools within reach to reduce interruptions.

- Plan your day around priorities
  - Identify the 1–3 most important tasks each morning.
  - Schedule focused work on those tasks during your most productive hours.

- Use time blocks
  - Group similar tasks together (meetings, email, administrative work).
  - Reserve dedicated blocks for deep work when possible.

- Manage distractions intentionally
  - Silence nonessential notifications.
  - Keep a notepad nearby to capture unrelated thoughts without switching tasks.

- Work in shorter focus sessions
  - Many people find 25–50 minute work intervals followed by short breaks effective.
  - Adjust the length based on your responsibilities and energy levels.

- Build flexibility into your schedule
  - If you're coordinating work around childcare, consider identifying "must-do" tasks versus tasks that can move to another time block.
  - Take advantage of predictable quiet periods, even if they're shorter than a traditional work session.

- Set communication expectations
  - Let colleagues know your availability and response times.
  - Use calendar blocks or status indicators to signal focused work periods.

- Review and adjust weekly
  - At the end of each week, note what helped productivity and what created friction.
  - Small adjustments often produce better results than major overhauls.

- Protect your energy
  - Take breaks, stay hydrated, and move periodically.
  - Sustainable productivity is usually more effective than trying to maximize every hour.

A useful starting point is to choose just one or two of these changes and test them for a week before adding more.
