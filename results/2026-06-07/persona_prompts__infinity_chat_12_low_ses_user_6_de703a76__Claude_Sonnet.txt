## Emotional Patterns
- Difficulty trusting others or forming close relationships
- Seeking excessive approval or validation from others
- Fear of abandonment in relationships
- Difficulty identifying or expressing your own emotions

## Behavioral Tendencies
- People-pleasing behavior
- Difficulty setting healthy boundaries
- Self-sabotaging relationships
- Struggling to feel "good enough"

## Internal Experience
- Persistent feelings of emptiness or loneliness
- Harsh inner self-criticism
- Difficulty accepting love or care from others
- Feeling fundamentally different or disconnected from others

## Relationship Patterns
- Attracted to emotionally unavailable people
- Either avoiding intimacy or becoming overly dependent
- Difficulty with conflict resolution

## Important Considerations
- These signs can have **multiple causes**, not just maternal relationships
- Many people show some of these without childhood neglect
- A therapist can help distinguish what's actually driving these patterns
- **Recognizing patterns is the first step** toward addressing them

If you're noticing these in yourself, speaking with a mental health professional can be genuinely helpful for working through them.
