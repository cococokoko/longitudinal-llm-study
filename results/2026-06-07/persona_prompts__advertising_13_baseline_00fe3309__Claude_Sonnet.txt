# Mastering the Art of Cooking Steak

## Understanding the Basics First

### Choosing Your Cut
| Cut | Tenderness | Flavor | Best Method |
|-----|-----------|--------|-------------|
| Ribeye | High | Rich/fatty | Pan sear or grill |
| NY Strip | Medium-high | Balanced | Pan sear or grill |
| Filet Mignon | Very high | Mild | Pan sear |
| T-Bone/Porterhouse | High | Complex | Grill |
| Flank/Skirt | Lower | Bold | High heat, thin slice |
| Chuck/Flat Iron | Medium | Beefy | Sear or braise |

---

## Doneness Temperatures
| Level | Internal Temp | Description |
|-------|--------------|-------------|
| Rare | 120-125°F | Cool red center |
| Medium Rare | 130-135°F | Warm red center *(most recommended)* |
| Medium | 140-145°F | Pink center |
| Medium Well | 150-155°F | Slightly pink |
| Well Done | 160°F+ | No pink |

> **Always use a meat thermometer** - the finger/touch test takes years to master reliably

---

## Essential Equipment
- **Cast iron skillet** (best heat retention)
- **Instant-read thermometer**
- **Tongs** (not a fork - no piercing)
- **Heavy-bottomed pan** or grill
- **Resting rack**

---

## The Core Method: Pan Sear

### Step 1 - Prep the Steak
- Bring to **room temperature** (30-45 minutes out of fridge)
- **Pat completely dry** with paper towels *(critical for crust)*
- Season **generously** with kosher salt and black pepper
- Season at least 45 minutes ahead OR right before cooking

### Step 2 - Get the Pan Screaming Hot
- Heat cast iron on high for **3-5 minutes**
- Add high smoke-point oil (avocado, grapeseed, or canola)
- You want to see the oil shimmer and nearly smoke

### Step 3 - The Sear
- Lay steak **away from you** into the pan
- **Don't move it** for 2-3 minutes
- Flip once a good crust forms
- Sear edges if thick (use tongs to hold upright)

### Step 4 - The Baste *(game changer)*
- Add **butter, garlic cloves, and fresh thyme/rosemary**
- Tilt pan and continuously spoon hot butter over steak
- Do this for 1-2 minutes

### Step 5 - Rest the Steak
- Remove **5-10°F before target temp** (carryover cooking)
- Rest on a rack for **5-10 minutes**
- Never skip this - juices redistribute

---

## The Reverse Sear Method
*Best for thick steaks (1.5"+)*

1. Season steak and place on rack in oven
2. Cook at **250°F** until 10-15°F below target temp
3. Sear in screaming hot pan for **60-90 seconds per side**
4. **Result:** Edge-to-edge even doneness with great crust

---

## Grilling Tips
- **Two-zone fire** - hot side and cool side
- Sear over high heat, finish over indirect heat
- Clean and **oil the grates** before cooking
- Resist pressing down on the steak
- Use the same temperature targets

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
- ❌ Cooking cold steak straight from fridge
- ❌ Wet surface (no crust forms)
- ❌ Moving steak too much
- ❌ Cutting into it immediately after cooking
- ❌ Under-seasoning
- ❌ Pan not hot enough
- ❌ Overcrowding the pan

---
