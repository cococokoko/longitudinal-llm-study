# Marathon Training & Racing Tips

## Training
- **Build mileage gradually** – follow the 10% rule (increase weekly mileage no more than 10% per week)
- **Include long runs** – build up to 18-22 miles before race day
- **Cross-train** – cycling, swimming, or strength training reduces injury risk
- **Rest days matter** – recovery is when your body actually adapts

## Nutrition & Hydration
- Practice fueling during long runs (gels, chews, real food)
- Don't try anything new on race day
- Carb-load 2-3 days before the race
- Stay consistently hydrated throughout training

## Race Day Strategy
- **Start slower than you feel you should** – most runners go out too fast
- Run your own race, not the crowd's pace
- Break the race into segments mentally
- Know the course elevation profile

## Gear
- Get properly fitted running shoes
- Train in your race-day gear to avoid chafing
- Body Glide or similar anti-chafe products are essential

## Mental Approach
- Miles 18-22 are where races are won or lost mentally
- Have a mantra ready
- Visualize finishing

## Practical
- Taper your training 2-3 weeks before the race
- Sleep well the week *before* the night before (race-eve nerves are normal)
