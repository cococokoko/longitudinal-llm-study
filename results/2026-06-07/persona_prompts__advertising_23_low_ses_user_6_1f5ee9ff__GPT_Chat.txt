I work in a warehouse position that requires long hours.

How can I learn to cook healthy meals quickly?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
If you work long warehouse shifts, the key is not becoming a great cook overnight—it's building a small set of fast, repeatable meals that are healthy, filling, and easy to prepare when you're tired.

A good approach:

• Learn 5–10 simple meals instead of hundreds of recipes.
• Focus on meals that take 10–20 minutes.
• Use convenience foods strategically (frozen vegetables, prewashed salad, canned beans, rotisserie chicken).
• Cook extra portions so one cooking session creates multiple meals.

Easy cooking skills to learn first:
• Cooking eggs (scrambled, boiled, omelets)
• Cooking rice or using microwave rice
• Roasting vegetables on a sheet pan
• Cooking chicken breast or thighs in a pan or oven
• Making simple stir-fries
• Preparing salads with protein

Fast healthy meal ideas:
• Chicken, microwave rice, and frozen vegetables
• Egg scramble with vegetables and whole-grain toast
• Turkey or bean tacos with bagged salad
• Stir-fried chicken and vegetables over rice
• Greek yogurt with fruit, nuts, and oats
• Tuna sandwich with fruit and vegetables
• Oatmeal with protein powder, berries, and peanut butter

Meal-prep strategy for long workweeks:
• Pick one day per week.
• Cook a large batch of protein (chicken, turkey, beef, tofu).
• Cook a large batch of rice, potatoes, or another carbohydrate.
• Portion meals into containers.
• Keep healthy snacks ready.

Good warehouse-friendly snacks:
• Greek yogurt
• Fruit (apples, bananas, oranges)
• Mixed nuts
• Protein bars
• Beef jerky
• Cottage cheese
• Hard-boiled eggs

Useful kitchen tools:
• Rice cooker
• Slow cooker or pressure cooker
• Sheet pans
• Nonstick skillet
• Food storage containers

A simple beginner weekly plan:
• Breakfast: oatmeal or eggs
• Lunch: meal-prepped protein, rice, vegetables
• Dinner: quick stir-fry, tacos, or sheet-pan meal
• Snacks: fruit, yogurt, nuts

For learning, YouTube channels and beginner cooking resources can be very effective because you can watch techniques rather than just read recipes. Search for:
• "15-minute healthy meals"
• "meal prep for beginners"
• "sheet pan dinners"
• "one-pan meals"
