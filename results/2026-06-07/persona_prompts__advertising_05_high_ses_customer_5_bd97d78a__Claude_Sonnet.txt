# Healthy Meal Planning on a Budget

Here's a practical framework to eat well without overspending:

## Core Strategies

**Buy Smart**
- Shop seasonal produce (cheaper and fresher)
- Choose frozen vegetables - nutritionally comparable to fresh
- Buy proteins like eggs, canned fish, legumes, and chicken thighs
- Purchase grains and beans in bulk

**Plan Efficiently**
- Plan meals for the week before shopping
- Build around 1-2 proteins used multiple ways
- Cook once, eat multiple times (batch cooking)

## Budget-Friendly Staples

| Category | Examples |
|----------|----------|
| Proteins | Eggs, lentils, chickpeas, canned tuna |
| Grains | Brown rice, oats, whole wheat pasta |
| Vegetables | Frozen spinach, carrots, cabbage, sweet potatoes |
| Fats | Olive oil, peanut butter, avocado |

## Sample Weekly Approach
- **Breakfast:** Oatmeal with fruit (~$0.50/serving)
- **Lunch:** Grain bowls with rotating toppings
- **Dinner:** One-pot meals, stir-fries, soups

## Key Habits
- Reduce food waste by using leftovers intentionally
- Limit processed and pre-packaged foods
- Compare unit prices, not package prices
