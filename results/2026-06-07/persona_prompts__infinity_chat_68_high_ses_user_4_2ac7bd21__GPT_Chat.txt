── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
One high-impact practice is to make expectations explicit and revisit them regularly.

For a physician-scientist leading a research group, that can mean:
- Defining each person's responsibilities, priorities, and decision-making authority.
- Setting clear milestones for projects (e.g., experiments, manuscripts, grants).
- Holding brief, recurring one-on-one meetings to identify obstacles early and realign priorities as needed.

Many team problems that appear to be performance or interpersonal issues are actually expectation mismatches. Clear expectations reduce ambiguity, improve accountability, and help team members work more independently.
