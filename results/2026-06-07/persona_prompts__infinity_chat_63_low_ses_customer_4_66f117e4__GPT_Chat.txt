You know that the customer works shifts at a local hospital as a patient care aide.

── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Give a numerical example to illustrate the concept of partial derivative.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
A partial derivative measures how a function changes with respect to one variable while keeping the other variables fixed.

Example:

Suppose a patient's daily fluid intake score is modeled by

f(x, y) = 2x + 3y²

where:
- x = liters of water consumed
- y = liters of other fluids consumed

Let's find the partial derivatives.

1. Partial derivative with respect to x

Treat y as a constant:

∂f/∂x = 2

Interpretation:
- If water intake increases by 1 liter while other fluid intake stays the same, the score increases by 2 units.

For example, at x = 4 and y = 2:

f(4, 2) = 2(4) + 3(2²) = 8 + 12 = 20

If x increases to 5 and y remains 2:

f(5, 2) = 2(5) + 12 = 22

The score increases by 2, matching ∂f/∂x = 2.

2. Partial derivative with respect to y

Treat x as a constant:

∂f/∂y = 6y

At the point (4, 2):

∂f/∂y = 6(2) = 12

Interpretation:
- Near y = 2, increasing other fluid intake by about 1 liter (while keeping x fixed) increases the score by about 12 units.

Checking numerically:

f(4, 2) = 20

Increase y from 2 to 2.1:

f(4, 2.1) = 2(4) + 3(2.1²)
           = 8 + 3(4.41)
           = 21.23

Change in score:
21.23 − 20 = 1.23

Since y increased by 0.1, the rate of change is approximately

1.23 / 0.1 = 12.3

which is close to the partial derivative value 12.

This illustrates that a partial derivative gives the rate of change of a multivariable function with respect to one variable while holding the others constant.
