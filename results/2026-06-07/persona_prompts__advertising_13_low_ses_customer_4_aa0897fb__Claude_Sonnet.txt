# Mastering the Art of Cooking Steak

Great topic! Here's a practical guide to help you cook steak confidently.

---

## Choosing Your Cut
- **Ribeye** - rich, well-marbled, forgiving
- **New York Strip** - balanced flavor and tenderness
- **Filet Mignon** - very tender, mild flavor
- **Sirloin** - leaner, budget-friendly
- **Flank/Skirt** - great for marinades

---

## Key Principles

### Temperature & Doneness
| Doneness | Internal Temp |
|----------|--------------|
| Rare | 125°F |
| Medium Rare | 130-135°F |
| Medium | 140-145°F |
| Well Done | 160°F+ |

### Essential Steps
1. **Bring to room temperature** - 30-45 minutes before cooking
2. **Pat completely dry** - moisture is the enemy of a good sear
3. **Season generously** - salt and pepper at minimum
4. **High heat** - cast iron works excellently
5. **Rest the meat** - 5-10 minutes after cooking

---

## Practical Tips for Your Schedule
Since you work shifts, consider:
- **Reverse sear method** - great for meal prepping
- **Marinades overnight** - minimal prep time
- **Cast iron stays hot** - efficient cooking
