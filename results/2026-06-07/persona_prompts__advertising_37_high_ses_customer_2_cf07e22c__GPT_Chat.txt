For a senior attorney, the most productive home office is usually one that reduces friction for deep legal work, client communications, and document management while maintaining confidentiality and professionalism.

Consider focusing on these areas:

• Ergonomics
  - Invest in a high-quality chair with proper lumbar support.
  - Position your monitor(s) so the top of the screen is at or slightly below eye level.
  - Use an adjustable desk if you spend long periods reviewing documents or drafting.
  - Ensure keyboard and mouse placement keeps wrists in a neutral position.

• Workspace Layout
  - Separate your primary work area from personal living space if possible.
  - Keep frequently used resources within easy reach.
  - Create a clean desktop with minimal visual clutter.
  - Maintain dedicated zones for drafting, reading, and video calls if space allows.

• Technology
  - Use dual or triple monitors if your workflow involves reviewing pleadings, exhibits, contracts, or research simultaneously.
  - Ensure reliable high-speed internet with a backup option (e.g., hotspot).
  - Use a quality webcam, microphone, and lighting setup for client meetings and court appearances.
  - Consider a document scanner for handling signed materials and correspondence efficiently.

• Confidentiality and Security
  - Use a lockable office door and secure storage for sensitive documents.
  - Enable full-disk encryption on all work devices.
  - Use multi-factor authentication for firm systems and email.
  - Position screens to prevent viewing by visitors, household members, or windows facing public areas.
  - Shred confidential paper documents rather than discarding them intact.

• Lighting
  - Maximize natural light while avoiding monitor glare.
  - Add task lighting for reading and document review.
  - Use front-facing lighting for video conferences.

• Acoustics
  - Reduce echo with rugs, bookshelves, curtains, or acoustic panels.
  - Use noise-canceling headphones for concentration and confidential calls.
  - Consider a white-noise machine if privacy is a concern.

• Document Management
  - Develop a consistent filing system for both physical and digital records.
  - Keep active matters readily accessible and archive closed matters systematically.
  - Use searchable digital storage whenever permitted by firm policy and client requirements.

• Productivity Systems
  - Time-block substantial periods for drafting, analysis, and strategy work.
  - Reserve specific windows for email and administrative tasks.
  - Use task management tools integrated with your firm's workflow.
  - Minimize interruptions during high-concentration work by silencing nonessential notifications.

• Video Conference Readiness
  - Maintain a professional, neutral background.
  - Test camera angle, audio quality, and lighting before important meetings.
  - Keep key materials accessible but off-camera.

• Environmental Factors
  - Maintain a comfortable temperature.
  - Keep water and basic office supplies nearby to reduce unnecessary interruptions.
  - Consider a few plants or understated décor if they help create a focused environment.
