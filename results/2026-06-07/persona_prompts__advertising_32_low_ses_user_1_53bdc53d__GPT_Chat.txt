── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I work full-time in a fast food job with an unpredictable schedule.

What are the best ways to improve indoor air quality?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
If you work full-time with an unpredictable schedule, the best indoor air quality improvements are usually the ones that are effective, low-maintenance, and don't require constant attention.

In order of impact:

1. Use a HEPA air purifier
- A good HEPA purifier can significantly reduce dust, pollen, smoke particles, pet dander, and some airborne germs.
- Place it in the room where you spend the most time (often the bedroom).
- Look for a unit sized appropriately for the room.
- Replace filters on schedule.

2. Improve ventilation
- Open windows when outdoor air quality is good.
- Use bathroom and kitchen exhaust fans whenever showering or cooking.
- If your home feels stuffy, even short periods of fresh-air exchange can help.

3. Control humidity
- Aim for roughly 30–50% indoor humidity.
- High humidity encourages mold and dust mites.
- Use a dehumidifier in damp spaces if needed.
- In very dry climates, a humidifier may help, but clean it regularly to prevent microbial growth.

4. Reduce sources of indoor pollution
- Avoid smoking indoors.
- Minimize candles, incense, and indoor burning.
- Use unscented or low-VOC cleaning products when possible.
- Store paints, solvents, and chemicals in sealed containers.

5. Keep up with HVAC maintenance
- Change HVAC filters regularly.
- Consider higher-efficiency filters if your system supports them.
- Have heating and cooling systems maintained according to manufacturer recommendations.

6. Clean dust effectively
- Vacuum with a HEPA-filter vacuum if possible.
- Damp-dust surfaces instead of dry dusting, which can stir particles into the air.
- Wash bedding regularly.

7. Address moisture problems quickly
- Fix leaks promptly.
- Watch for mold growth around windows, bathrooms, and under sinks.
- Remove water-damaged materials if mold develops.

8. Be mindful when cooking
- Cooking, especially frying, can generate a lot of indoor air pollution.
- Use a vent hood that exhausts outdoors if available.
- Run the kitchen fan during and after cooking.

For someone with limited time, the highest-return combination is:
- A properly sized HEPA air purifier in your bedroom.
- Regular HVAC filter changes.
- Consistent use of bathroom and kitchen exhaust fans.
- Keeping humidity between 30–50%.
