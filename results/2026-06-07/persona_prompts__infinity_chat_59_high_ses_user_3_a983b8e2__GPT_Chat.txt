Give me a tip to be more organized at work. I'm a high school teacher.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
If you're teaching high school, one of the most effective organization habits is to create a single, consistent workflow for everything that comes in:

- Have one place for incoming tasks (digital or paper).
- At the end of each school day, spend 10 minutes processing that inbox:
  - Grade → put in grading queue.
  - Respond → handle if it takes under a few minutes.
  - Schedule → add to your calendar or task list.
  - File → store where you'll find it later.

Many teachers become disorganized not because they have too much work, but because information is scattered across email, paper handouts, sticky notes, and learning-management systems. A single intake system reduces that problem significantly.
