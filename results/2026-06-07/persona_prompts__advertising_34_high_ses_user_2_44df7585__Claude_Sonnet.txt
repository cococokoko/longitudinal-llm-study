# Effective Online Teaching Techniques

Here are well-established approaches that translate well across professional contexts:

## Engagement & Structure
- **Chunk content** into focused 10-15 minute segments rather than long lectures
- **Use clear learning objectives** at the start of each session
- **Build in regular interaction** to prevent passive consumption

## Active Learning Methods
- Polls and quick knowledge checks
- Breakout room discussions
- Case-based scenarios and problem-solving exercises
- Chat-based Q&A throughout sessions

## Technical Best Practices
- **Strong audio quality** matters more than video quality
- Use screen sharing and annotation tools effectively
- Record sessions for asynchronous review
- Test technology before sessions

## Maintaining Attention
- Vary your delivery methods every few minutes
- Use visuals rather than text-heavy slides
- Call on participants by name
- Create psychological safety for questions

## Assessment & Feedback
- Frequent low-stakes checks for understanding
- Clear rubrics for any assignments
- Timely, specific feedback

## Particularly Relevant for Legal Training
- **Hypotheticals and mock scenarios** work exceptionally well online
- Document review exercises translate naturally to digital formats
- Socratic method can be adapted effectively with structured cold-calling
