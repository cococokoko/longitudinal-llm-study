── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
What are the best resources for starting a plant-based diet?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
If you're starting a plant-based diet, the most helpful resources tend to fall into a few categories: nutrition education, meal planning, recipes, and practical guidance.

Nutrition basics
- Physicians Committee for Responsible Medicine (PCRM) — evidence-based guides on plant-based nutrition, meal plans, and recipes.
  - https://www.pcrm.org
- NutritionFacts.org — reviews nutrition research in an accessible format.
  - https://nutritionfacts.org
- Harvard T.H. Chan School of Public Health Nutrition Source — balanced information on healthy eating patterns, including plant-focused diets.
  - https://nutritionsource.hsph.harvard.edu

Beginner-friendly meal plans and guides
- Forks Over Knives — meal planners, recipes, and articles focused on whole-food, plant-based eating.
  - https://www.forksoverknives.com
- Vegan Society — practical guides for transitioning to a vegan diet.
  - https://www.vegansociety.com
- NHS (UK) vegan diet guide — straightforward nutritional advice.
  - https://www.nhs.uk

Recipe websites
- Minimalist Baker — many simple plant-based recipes with short ingredient lists.
  - https://minimalistbaker.com
- Rainbow Plant Life — beginner-friendly recipes and cooking tutorials.
  - https://rainbowplantlife.com
- Love & Lemons — vegetable-focused meals and meal-prep ideas.
  - https://www.loveandlemons.com
- Nora Cooks — approachable vegan comfort foods.
  - https://www.noracooks.com

Books
- "Plant-Based on a Budget" by Toni Okamoto — practical and affordable.
- "How Not to Die" by Michael Greger, MD — nutrition-focused overview.
- "The Plant-Based Diet for Beginners" by Gabriel Miller — simple introduction and meal plans.
- "Fiber Fueled" by Will Bulsiewicz, MD — focuses on gut health and plant diversity.

Apps
- Cronometer — useful for tracking nutrients, especially during the transition.
- Mealime — meal planning and grocery lists with plant-based options.
- Forks Meal Planner — structured plant-based meal planning.

Key nutrients to pay attention to
- Protein: beans, lentils, tofu, tempeh, edamame, soy milk, seitan.
- Vitamin B12: supplementation is generally recommended for fully plant-based diets.
- Iron: legumes, tofu, fortified cereals, leafy greens; pair with vitamin C sources.
- Calcium: fortified plant milks, calcium-set tofu, leafy greens.
- Omega-3 fats: flaxseeds, chia seeds, walnuts; some people choose algae-based supplements.
- Vitamin D: food, sunlight, and/or supplements depending on location and lifestyle.

A simple way to start
- Build meals around:
  - 1 protein source (beans, tofu, lentils)
  - 1 whole grain (brown rice, oats, quinoa)
  - Several vegetables
  - A healthy fat (nuts, seeds, avocado)
- Replace one meal a day with a plant-based option for a few weeks before expanding.
