For international students, the TOEFL (Test of English as a Foreign Language) and IELTS (International English Language Testing System) are the two most widely accepted English-language proficiency exams used for university admissions, immigration, and professional registration. Both assess the four core language skills—reading, listening, speaking, and writing—but they differ in format and testing experience. TOEFL is primarily computer-based and is often preferred by students who are comfortable with academic English and integrated tasks that combine multiple skills. IELTS is available in both computer-delivered and paper-based formats and includes a face-to-face speaking interview with an examiner, which some candidates find more natural. Universities and institutions around the world generally accept both tests, so the best choice depends on a student's strengths, testing preferences, target institutions, and preparation strategy rather than on any significant difference in acceptance or academic value.
