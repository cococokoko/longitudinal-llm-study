# Tips for Running a Marathon

Here are some helpful tips to prepare for and complete a marathon:

## Training
- **Follow a structured plan** - Most plans are 16-20 weeks long
- **Build mileage gradually** - Increase weekly mileage by no more than 10% per week
- **Include long runs** - Build up to at least one 20-mile training run
- **Rest days matter** - Allow your body to recover between runs

## Nutrition & Hydration
- **Practice fueling during training** - Use gels, chews, or real food on long runs
- **Stay hydrated** - Drink water consistently, not just during runs
- **Carb load** - Increase carbohydrate intake 2-3 days before race day

## Gear
- **Invest in good shoes** - Get fitted at a running store
- **Wear moisture-wicking clothing** - Avoid cotton
- **Break in your gear** - Never wear anything new on race day

## Race Day
- **Start slow** - Go out slower than you think you need to
- **Stick to your pace** - Avoid getting caught up in the crowd excitement
- **Know the course** - Identify water stations and elevation changes

## Recovery
- **Plan for post-race recovery** - Expect soreness for several days
- **Prioritize sleep** - Especially during heavy training weeks

**Given your demanding work schedule**, prioritizing rest and recovery will be especially important for your training success.
