## Analysis vs. Design

**Analysis** answers *"What does the system need to do?"* — understanding requirements, problems, and constraints.

**Design** answers *"How will the system do it?"* — defining architecture, components, and solutions.

---

## Can You Design Without Analysis?

**Technically yes, practically no.**

Skipping analysis means you're designing based on **assumptions rather than validated requirements**. This leads to:

- Building the **wrong thing** correctly
- Costly rework when reality doesn't match assumptions
- Solutions optimized for the **wrong problem**

---

## The Executive Reality

In fast-moving tech environments, teams often **compress or parallelize** analysis and design — which is acceptable. What's dangerous is **eliminating** analysis entirely.

> The cost of fixing a design flaw discovered in production is **10-100x** greater than catching it during analysis.

Even experienced teams that "skip" formal analysis are typically drawing on **implicit prior analysis** — they're not truly starting from zero.

**Bottom line:** Analysis and design are sequential by nature. You can overlap them pragmatically, but designing without *any* analysis is essentially guessing — and at enterprise scale, that's an expensive gamble.
