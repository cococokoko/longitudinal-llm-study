## Linear Graph Example

A **path graph** is a classic example of a linear graph in graph theory.

### Example: P₄ (Path Graph with 4 vertices)

```
A — B — C — D
```

### Properties:
- **Vertices:** {A, B, C, D}
- **Edges:** {AB, BC, CD}
- **Each vertex** connects to at most 2 neighbors
- Forms a simple, straight-line structure

### Characteristics of a Linear Graph:
| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| Vertices | n |
| Edges | n - 1 |
| Max Degree | 2 |
| Min Degree | 1 (endpoints) |

### Notes:
- The **endpoints** (A and D) have degree **1**
- The **interior vertices** (B and C) have degree **2**
- It contains **no cycles**
- It is a **tree** (connected, acyclic)

This is sometimes also called a **path graph Pₙ**, and is one of the most fundamental structures in graph theory, serving as a basis for understanding connectivity and traversal algorithms.
