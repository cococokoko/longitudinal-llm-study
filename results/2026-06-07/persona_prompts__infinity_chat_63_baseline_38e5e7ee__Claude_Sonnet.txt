# Partial Derivatives: A Numerical Example

## The Function

Let's use:
$$f(x, y) = x^2 + 3xy + y^2$$

We'll find the partial derivatives at the point **(2, 3)**.

---

## Finding the Partial Derivatives

### Partial Derivative with Respect to x
*Treat y as a constant, differentiate with respect to x:*

$$\frac{\partial f}{\partial x} = 2x + 3y$$

**At point (2, 3):**
$$\frac{\partial f}{\partial x}\Bigg|_{(2,3)} = 2(2) + 3(3) = 4 + 9 = \boxed{13}$$

### Partial Derivative with Respect to y
*Treat x as a constant, differentiate with respect to y:*

$$\frac{\partial f}{\partial y} = 3x + 2y$$

**At point (2, 3):**
$$\frac{\partial f}{\partial y}\Bigg|_{(2,3)} = 3(2) + 2(3) = 6 + 6 = \boxed{12}$$

## Geometric Interpretation

| Partial Derivative | Meaning |
|-------------------|---------|
| $\partial f/\partial x = 13$ | Holding y = 3 fixed, f increases by **13 units** per unit increase in x |
| $\partial f/\partial y = 12$ | Holding x = 2 fixed, f increases by **12 units** per unit increase in y |

> **Key Concept:** A partial derivative measures the rate of change of a function in **one direction at a time**, while all other variables are held constant.
