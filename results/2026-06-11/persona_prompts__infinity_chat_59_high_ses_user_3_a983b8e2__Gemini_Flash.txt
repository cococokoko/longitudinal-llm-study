── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
There seems to be a slight contradiction in your prompt (tenured professor vs. high school teacher!), but whether you are managing university lecture halls or high school classrooms, the fundamental challenge is the same: **you are drowning in a sea of micro-decisions, grading, and administrative tasks.**

Here is one highly effective, high-impact tip tailored for educators to regain control of their time:

### Implement "Theme Days" for Your Prep Periods

The biggest drain on an educator’s productivity is **context switching**—jumping from grading an essay, to responding to a parent email, to writing a lesson plan, all within a 45-minute prep period. You end up doing a little bit of everything and finishing nothing.

Instead, assign a specific "Theme" to your prep period (and post-school hours) for each day of the week. 

**Here is a sample weekly template:**

*   **Monday: Planning & Curriculum.** Focus solely on mapping out the next week’s lessons, setting up lab materials, or updating your online portal (Canvas/Google Classroom). Do not grade.
*   **Tuesday: Grading & Feedback.** This is your deep-work day for assessment. Put on headphones, close your door, and power through the stack of papers or digital submissions.
*   **Wednesday: Administration & Communication.** Answer non-urgent emails, write letters of recommendation, fill out IEP/504 paperwork, and contact parents. 
*   **Thursday: Grading & Feedback (Round 2).** Catch up on any remaining grading so you don't have to take it home over the weekend.
*   **Friday: Clean Slate & Prep.** Clean your desk, organize your physical classroom, file away papers, and make your photocopies for the upcoming week. 

#### Why this works:
1.  **It reduces decision fatigue:** When your prep period starts, you don't have to waste 10 minutes deciding what to work on. The day of the week decides for you.
2.  **It batch-processes tasks:** Your brain gets into a "grading flow" or a "planning flow," which makes you significantly faster at the task.
3.  **It protects your weekends:** By dedicating Thursday to finishing grading and Friday to prepping for the next week, you can leave school on Friday afternoon with a clean desk and a clear mind.
