## Analysis vs. Design

**Analysis** answers *"What does the system need to do?"* — understanding requirements, constraints, and the problem domain.

**Design** answers *"How will the system do it?"* — defining architecture, components, and implementation approach.

---

## Can You Design Without Analysis?

**Technically yes, practically no.**

Skipping analysis means you're designing a solution to a problem you haven't clearly defined. This leads to:

- **Rework** — building the wrong thing correctly
- **Scope creep** — undefined boundaries invite endless changes
- **Technical debt** — structural decisions made on false assumptions

---

## The Executive Reality

In fast-moving tech environments, teams often compress or parallelize analysis and design — which is acceptable **if you acknowledge the risk**. What's *not* acceptable is confusing speed with skipping rigor entirely.

> **The cost of poor analysis compounds. A flawed design is expensive to fix; a design solving the wrong problem is catastrophic.**

The most common failure mode in large technology initiatives isn't poor design — it's excellent design of the wrong solution.
