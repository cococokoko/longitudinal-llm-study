# CUBICLE OF THE DAMNED
### A Play in the Style of Scott Adams, Set During the Apocalypse

---

**CHARACTERS:**

**DILBERT** - An engineer trying to apply logical processes to the end of civilization

**THE POINTY-HAIRED BOSS (PHB)** - Somehow still managing, despite the collapse of society

**WALLY** - Has not changed his behavior whatsoever

**ALICE** - Increasingly violent, which is now considered a professional asset

**DOGBERT** - Running a consulting firm that advises warlords

**ASOK** - The intern, still hoping for a performance review

---

*SETTING: A partially collapsed open-plan office. The ceiling has a hole through which a blood-red sky is visible. Several cubicle walls are on fire. A motivational poster reading "HANG IN THERE" features a skeleton cat. DILBERT sits at his desk, which is surrounded by rubble, typing on a laptop powered by a hamster wheel operated by a feral child.*

---

**ACT ONE: THE MORNING STANDUP**

---

*(DILBERT types. A distant explosion rattles the building. He does not look up.)*

**DILBERT:**
*(to himself)*
The structural integrity of this building has degraded by approximately sixty-three percent. I have documented this in a report that no one will read, which is consistent with my pre-apocalypse experience.

*(PHB enters, wearing his usual suit, which is now partially on fire. He does not notice.)*

**PHB:**
Good morning, people! I have some exciting news about our Q4 roadmap!

**DILBERT:**
The concept of Q4 implies the existence of Q1 through Q3, which in turn implies a calendar year, which implies a functioning civilization.

**PHB:**
Right, so we're pivoting to an agile framework.

**DILBERT:**
We are being hunted by cannibals.

**PHB:**
Which is why I want daily standups instead of weekly ones.

*(WALLY shuffles in carrying a coffee mug. The coffee mug contains something that is definitely not coffee. He looks exactly the same as always.)*

**WALLY:**
I've been thinking about the apocalypse, and I've decided it doesn't really change my core strategy.

**DILBERT:**
Which is?

**WALLY:**
Doing as little as possible while appearing to be indispensable.

**DILBERT:**
How are you indispensable during the collapse of human civilization?

**WALLY:**
I told the warlord tribe to the east that I'm the only one who knows the password to the water filtration system.

**DILBERT:**
Do you know the password?

**WALLY:**
*(sipping from mug)*
I know that I don't know it, which is more than they know.

*(ALICE enters. She is carrying an axe, which she sets on her desk next to a nameplate that reads "ALICE - SENIOR ENGINEER." She sits down and begins typing with one hand while sharpening the axe with the other.)*

**ALICE:**
I have been passed over for promotion for the fourteenth consecutive year. I have decided this is no longer acceptable.

**PHB:**
Alice, I hear your concerns, and I want you to know that your feedback is very important to—

**ALICE:**
*(not looking up)*
I have an axe, Gerald.

**PHB:**
...I'll put you down for a mid-level rating.

**ALICE:**
*(still not looking up)*
I have a LARGE axe, Gerald.

**PHB:**
...Exceeds expectations.

**ALICE:**
*(finally looks up, smiles)*
Thank you for this growth opportunity.

I've called this all-hands to discuss our strategic vision for surviving the apocalypse. I've created a slide deck.

*(He gestures to a wall where the slides have been painted in what appears to be mud.)*

**DILBERT:**
Your first slide says "SYNERGY."

**PHB:**
Yes.
