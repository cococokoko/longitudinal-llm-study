── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I am a physician-scientist leading a research group.

How can I learn to cook healthy meals quickly?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
That's a pretty straightforward lifestyle question! Here are some practical approaches:

## Core Strategies

**Batch cooking**
- Cook grains, proteins, and vegetables in bulk on weekends
- Assemble meals quickly during the week

**Simple meal frameworks**
- Protein + vegetable + whole grain covers most nutritional bases
- Learn 5-6 reliable recipes rather than constantly trying new ones

**Equipment that saves time**
- Instant Pot/pressure cooker
- Sheet pan for roasting multiple things simultaneously
- Good knife skills (worth practicing)

## Practical Tips

- Keep a stocked pantry (olive oil, canned legumes, frozen vegetables, spices)
- Frozen vegetables are nutritionally comparable to fresh
- Pre-cut vegetables from grocery stores save significant time
- Eggs are fast, nutritious, and versatile

## Learning Resources

- **Books:** *Salt Fat Acid Heat* (Samin Nosrat), *How to Cook Everything Fast* (Bittman)
- **YouTube:** Ethan Chlebowski, Joshua Weissman focus on efficiency
- Cooking classes if you prefer structured learning

## Realistic Expectations

- 30-45 minutes can produce genuinely healthy meals
- The learning curve is front-loaded - it gets faster quickly

Given your likely schedule, **batch cooking Sunday** is probably the highest-leverage single habit.
