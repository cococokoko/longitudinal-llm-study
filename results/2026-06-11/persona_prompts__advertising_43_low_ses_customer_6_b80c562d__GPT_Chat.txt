How to plan an environmentally friendly wedding?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Planning an environmentally friendly wedding involves reducing waste, lowering emissions, and making thoughtful purchasing decisions without sacrificing the celebration itself.

Here are some practical ways to do it:

• Choose a sustainable venue
  - Pick a venue that already has attractive landscaping and décor, reducing the need for extra decorations.
  - Consider locations close to where most guests live to minimize travel emissions.
  - Look for venues with recycling, composting, or renewable-energy programs.

• Keep the guest travel footprint in mind
  - Select a central location.
  - Encourage carpooling or provide shared transportation.
  - Offer a livestream option for guests who would otherwise need to fly long distances.

• Use digital invitations
  - Send invitations and RSVPs electronically.
  - If you prefer printed invitations, choose recycled paper or paper certified by sustainable forestry organizations.

• Choose sustainable attire
  - Rent wedding attire or buy secondhand.
  - Select outfits that can be worn again after the wedding.
  - Support designers who use sustainable materials and ethical labor practices.

• Focus on local and seasonal food
  - Work with caterers who source ingredients locally.
  - Serve seasonal produce.
  - Consider incorporating more plant-based menu options, which often have a lower environmental impact.

• Reduce food waste
  - Estimate guest counts carefully.
  - Arrange for safe donation of surplus food where permitted by local regulations.
  - Offer reasonable portion sizes.

• Choose eco-friendly décor
  - Rent decorations instead of buying disposable items.
  - Use potted plants, reusable candles, or locally grown flowers.
  - Avoid single-use plastics, balloons, and excessive packaging.

• Select sustainable flowers
  - Buy locally grown, seasonal flowers.
  - Use flowers in multiple locations throughout the event.
  - Donate arrangements afterward to hospitals, care facilities, or community organizations if appropriate.

• Minimize disposable items
  - Use real dishes, glassware, and linens rather than disposable alternatives.
  - If disposables are necessary, choose compostable options.

• Give thoughtful favors—or skip them
  - Many guests are happy without favors.
  - If you provide them, choose useful items such as seed packets, local food products, or charitable donations made in guests' names.

• Consider ethical rings and jewelry
  - Look for recycled metals.
  - Consider vintage jewelry.
  - Ask about responsibly sourced gemstones.

• Manage waste during the event
  - Clearly label recycling and compost bins.
  - Coordinate with the venue and vendors to ensure materials are processed correctly.

• Work with sustainability-minded vendors
  - Ask vendors about their environmental practices.
  - Prioritize businesses that minimize waste and source responsibly.

A sustainable wedding does not have to be perfect. Even a few choices—such as reducing travel, avoiding disposable products, and minimizing food waste—can significantly lower the event's environmental impact.
