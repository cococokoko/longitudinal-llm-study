── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How do I start a vegetable garden in my backyard?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Starting a vegetable garden is a rewarding project! Here's a straightforward guide:

## Planning
- **Choose a location** with at least 6-8 hours of sunlight daily
- **Start small** - a 4x8 or 10x10 foot bed is manageable for beginners
- **Decide on a style** - raised beds, in-ground, or containers

## Preparing the Soil
- Remove grass and weeds from your chosen area
- Loosen soil 12 inches deep if planting in-ground
- Add compost to improve drainage and nutrients
- Aim for a slightly acidic pH (6.0-7.0)

## Choosing What to Grow
- Start with **easy vegetables** like tomatoes, zucchini, lettuce, beans, or radishes
- Consider what your family actually eats
- Check your **growing zone** (USDA zone map) for what works in your climate

## Planting
- Follow seed packet instructions for spacing and depth
- Some plants do better started indoors first (tomatoes, peppers)
- Others can be direct sown (beans, carrots, lettuce)

## Ongoing Care
- Water consistently - most vegetables need about 1 inch per week
- Mulch to retain moisture and reduce weeds
- Watch for pests and disease
- Fertilize as needed

## Good First Steps
1. Pick your spot
2. Decide your garden size
3. Build or prepare your bed
4. Start with 3-5 easy vegetables
