── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I am a single parent who often balances childcare with work.

Give me a tip to be more organized at work. I'm a high school teacher.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
As a single parent and a high school teacher, you are essentially running two high-stakes, high-energy organizations simultaneously. Your time is incredibly fragmented, so the best organization tips are those that require zero brainpower at the end of a long day.

Here is one highly effective, teacher-tested tip tailored for your specific situation:

### **Implement the "Friday 15" (The Monday-Ready Desk)**

As a single parent, your weekends are packed with parenting, chores, and hopefully a little rest. The absolute worst feeling is walking into your classroom on Monday morning feeling already behind. 

To prevent this, dedicate the **last 15 minutes of your work week on Friday** to a strict "Monday-Ready" routine. Do not grade, do not answer emails, and do not lesson plan during this time. 

Before you leave school on Friday, your only goal is to make Monday morning as frictionless as possible. Use those 15 minutes to do these three things:

1. **The Physical Reset:** Clear your desk completely. Put away stray pens, file loose papers, and wipe down the surface. 
2. **The Visual Queue:** Lay out *only* the materials you need for your very first period on Monday (the lesson plan, the handouts, the slides queued up on your computer). 
3. **The "Top 3" Sticky Note:** Write down the absolute top 3 things you must accomplish on Monday. Stick it right in the middle of your clean desk. 

### Why this works for a single parent/teacher:
* **It shuts your brain down for the weekend:** When you leave a messy desk, your brain carries that open mental loop home. By closing the loop on Friday, you can fully present with your child over the weekend without "teacher guilt."
* **It protects you from morning chaos:** If your child wakes up late, loses a shoe, or has a meltdown on Monday morning, you will likely arrive at school flustered and running on adrenaline. Walking into a calm, clean room where your first lesson is already laid out lowers your cortisol levels instantly. You don't have to think; you just have to execute.
