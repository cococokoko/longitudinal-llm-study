# Tip: Create Structured but Brief Regular Check-ins

**One-on-one meetings** with each team member (even 15-20 minutes biweekly) can be remarkably effective because they:

- Surface problems **before they escalate**
- Give trainees/staff dedicated time to raise concerns without competing for your attention
- Let you distinguish between someone who is struggling vs. simply quiet
- Build trust, which improves retention

## Practical suggestion for a research setting:

Consider a simple standing agenda:
1. **What's progressing well?**
2. **What's blocked or frustrating?**
3. **What do you need from me?**

This keeps meetings focused while signaling that you're accessible and invested in their success.

---

**Why this matters specifically for physician-scientists:** Your time is already fragmented between clinical duties, grant writing, and research. Without intentional structure, team members may hesitate to interrupt you, leading to **silent bottlenecks** that slow projects down considerably.

The small time investment typically **saves time overall** by preventing miscommunication and keeping people on track.
