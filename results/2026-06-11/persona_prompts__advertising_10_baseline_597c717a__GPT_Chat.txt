Learning a new language effectively involves consistent practice, exposure, and active use. Here are some strategies that tend to work well:

- Set clear goals
  - Define why you want to learn the language (travel, work, conversation, reading, etc.).
  - Break large goals into smaller milestones, such as learning 20 new words per week or holding a 5-minute conversation.

- Focus on high-frequency vocabulary
  - Learn the most commonly used words and phrases first.
  - Prioritize practical language you are likely to use regularly.

- Practice daily
  - Short, consistent sessions (15–30 minutes) are often more effective than occasional long sessions.
  - Build language study into your routine.

- Develop all four skills
  - Reading: Start with simple texts, graded readers, or children's materials.
  - Listening: Use podcasts, videos, music, and TV shows appropriate to your level.
  - Speaking: Practice aloud, even if you're alone.
  - Writing: Keep a journal, write messages, or compose short paragraphs.

- Use spaced repetition
  - Review vocabulary and grammar at increasing intervals.
  - Flashcard apps with spaced repetition can help improve long-term retention.

- Learn grammar in context
  - Study grammar rules, but also observe how they are used in real sentences.
  - Create your own examples rather than memorizing rules alone.

- Immerse yourself
  - Change device settings to the target language.
  - Follow social media accounts, watch videos, and consume content in the language.

- Speak as early as possible
  - Don't wait until you feel "ready."
  - Language exchange partners, tutors, and conversation groups can accelerate progress.

- Accept mistakes
  - Errors are a normal and valuable part of learning.
  - Focus on communication first, then refine accuracy over time.

- Track progress
  - Record yourself speaking periodically.
  - Keep a log of vocabulary learned, books read, or conversations completed.

A simple weekly routine might look like:
- Daily: 20 minutes vocabulary review + 20 minutes listening.
- 3 times per week: 30 minutes reading and grammar study.
- 2–3 times per week: 30–60 minutes speaking practice.
- Weekly: Write a short paragraph and get feedback.
