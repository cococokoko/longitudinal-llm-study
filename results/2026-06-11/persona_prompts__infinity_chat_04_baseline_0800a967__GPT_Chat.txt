1. Gorz and the Digital Economy: The Fate of the "Knowledge Economy"

Research question:
How accurately did Gorz anticipate contemporary platform capitalism and AI-driven production?

Background:
In works such as Critique of Economic Reason and Reclaiming Work, Gorz argued that knowledge increasingly becomes a productive force that cannot be fully measured by labor time. This creates tensions for capitalism, which depends on commodification and quantification.

Possible angle:
- Compare Gorz's theory of knowledge production with contemporary AI systems, open-source software, and digital platforms.
- Examine whether AI strengthens or weakens Gorz's prediction of a crisis of value.
- Contrast Gorz with contemporary theorists such as Nick Srnicek, Shoshana Zuboff, or Evgeny Morozov.

Original contribution:
Rather than treating Gorz as a theorist of post-Fordism, reinterpret him as an early theorist of AI capitalism.

2. Gorz and Marx's Fragment on Machines

Research question:
Did Gorz develop a more coherent political theory from Marx's Fragment on Machines than contemporary autonomist thinkers?

Background:
Both Gorz and Italian autonomists drew on Marx's discussion of the "general intellect."

Possible angle:
- Compare Gorz with Negri, Virno, Lazzarato, and Hardt.
- Analyze their different understandings of labor, technology, and political agency.
- Investigate why Gorz remained more skeptical about the emancipatory potential of immaterial labor.

Original contribution:
Show that Gorz offers an alternative path from Marx's Fragment on Machines that avoids some of the optimism found in autonomist Marxism.
