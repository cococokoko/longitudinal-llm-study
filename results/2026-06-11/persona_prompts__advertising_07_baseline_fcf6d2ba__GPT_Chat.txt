── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Effective ways to reduce household energy use.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Reducing household energy use can lower utility bills and decrease environmental impact. Some of the most effective strategies include:

• Improve insulation and air sealing
  - Seal gaps around doors, windows, vents, and pipes.
  - Add insulation to attics, walls, and crawl spaces where needed.
  - Use weatherstripping and door sweeps to reduce drafts.

• Upgrade lighting
  - Replace incandescent bulbs with LED bulbs.
  - Turn off lights when rooms are unoccupied.
  - Use natural daylight whenever possible.

• Use heating and cooling efficiently
  - Install a programmable or smart thermostat.
  - Adjust thermostat settings a few degrees to reduce energy consumption.
  - Regularly replace HVAC filters and maintain equipment.
  - Use ceiling fans to improve comfort and reduce reliance on air conditioning.

• Choose energy-efficient appliances
  - Look for high-efficiency or ENERGY STAR-certified models.
  - Replace older refrigerators, washers, and other major appliances when practical.
  - Run appliances according to manufacturer recommendations for efficiency.

• Reduce water-heating energy
  - Lower the water heater temperature to a safe, efficient setting.
  - Install low-flow showerheads and faucets.
  - Wash clothes in cold water when appropriate.
  - Insulate hot water pipes and the water heater if suitable.

• Manage electronics and standby power
  - Unplug devices that are not in use.
  - Use advanced power strips to reduce "phantom" energy consumption.
  - Enable energy-saving settings on computers, TVs, and other electronics.

• Use appliances more efficiently
  - Run dishwashers and washing machines with full loads.
  - Air-dry clothes and dishes when possible.
  - Keep refrigerator and freezer doors closed as much as possible.

• Improve window performance
  - Use insulated curtains or blinds.
  - Close window coverings during hot summer days and cold winter nights.
  - Consider upgrading to energy-efficient windows if replacement is needed.

• Monitor energy use
  - Review utility bills to identify trends.
  - Use smart meters, energy monitors, or utility-provided tools to track consumption.
  - Focus on the largest energy users first, such as heating, cooling, and water heating.

• Consider renewable energy
  - Install solar panels if financially and geographically practical.
  - Explore community solar programs where available.

For most homes, the biggest savings usually come from improving heating and cooling efficiency, reducing water-heating costs, and sealing air leaks.
