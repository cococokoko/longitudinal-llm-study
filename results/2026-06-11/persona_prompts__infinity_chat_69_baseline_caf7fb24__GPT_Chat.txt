What is the difference between analysis and design? Can one begin to design without analysis? Why? Be concise.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
- Analysis is the process of understanding a problem, its requirements, constraints, and goals. It focuses on "what" needs to be achieved.
- Design is the process of creating a solution that meets those requirements. It focuses on "how" the goals will be achieved.

Can one begin to design without analysis?
- Generally, no. Effective design depends on understanding the problem first. Without analysis, the design may fail to meet user needs, overlook constraints, or solve the wrong problem.
- In practice, some preliminary design ideas may emerge early, but they should be informed and refined through analysis.
