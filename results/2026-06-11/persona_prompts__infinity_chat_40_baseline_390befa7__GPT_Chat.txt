── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
My girlfriend is buying a house. I plan to marry her in a year or so. I don't know if it's fair for me to pay for the house or not. What's the best solution?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
There isn't one universally "fair" answer. The best solution depends on balancing three things:

- The financial reality (who owns the asset and who is building equity)
- The relationship reality (you're planning a future together)
- The legal reality (you are not married yet)

A useful starting point is this: if your girlfriend is buying the house in her name before you're married, then she is taking the ownership risk and receiving the ownership benefits. In that situation, many people view it as fair for the non-owner partner to contribute toward living expenses, but not necessarily to pay the mortgage in the same way an owner would.

Some common approaches:

- Pay a rent-like amount.
  - She owns the house.
  - You contribute a mutually agreed monthly amount for living there.
  - Usually this amount is lower than half the mortgage if she is also building equity.
  - This is often the cleanest arrangement before marriage.

- Split household expenses but not principal.
  - You help with utilities, groceries, internet, maintenance, etc.
  - She pays the mortgage and property taxes.
  - This clearly separates ownership from occupancy.

- Contribute toward the mortgage and create a written agreement.
  - If you are paying substantially toward the house, consider a legal agreement defining whether you are acquiring an ownership interest or whether the payments are simply rent.
  - Without documentation, misunderstandings can develop later.

- Wait until marriage and then revisit ownership.
  - After marriage, you can decide whether to add you to the title, refinance jointly, or make other arrangements.
  - The rules vary significantly depending on where you live.

Questions worth discussing together:

- Will the house be solely in her name?
- Is she making the down payment herself?
- If you break up before marriage, what would each of you consider fair?
- If you marry, do you expect the house to become a shared asset?
- What monthly contribution would allow both of you to feel respected and secure?

If I were looking for the lowest-conflict option before marriage, it would usually be:
- She buys and owns the house.
- You pay an agreed rent-like contribution and share living expenses.
- You do not assume ownership rights unless there is a written legal agreement.

What country or state/province are you in? The legal side of this can change quite a bit depending on location.
