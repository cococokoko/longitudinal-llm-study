How do I start a vegetable garden in my backyard?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Starting a backyard vegetable garden is easier if you begin small and choose crops that fit the time you have available.

1. Pick a location
- Choose a spot that gets at least 6–8 hours of sunlight per day.
- Make sure it's close to a water source.
- Avoid areas where water pools after rain.

2. Start small
- A bed around 4 ft × 8 ft (1.2 m × 2.4 m) is plenty for a first garden.
- It's easier to maintain and less overwhelming than a large plot.

3. Prepare the soil
- Remove grass and weeds.
- Mix in compost or other organic matter to improve soil quality.
- If possible, get a soil test through a local extension service to check nutrient levels and pH.

4. Choose beginner-friendly vegetables
Good options include:
- Lettuce
- Green beans
- Radishes
- Zucchini
- Tomatoes
- Peppers
- Cucumbers

5. Decide between seeds and transplants
- Seeds are cheaper and work well for beans, radishes, carrots, and lettuce.
- Transplants are often easier for tomatoes, peppers, and cucumbers.

6. Water consistently
- Most vegetables need about 1 inch (2.5 cm) of water per week.
- Water deeply rather than lightly.
- Mulch around plants to reduce weeds and retain moisture.

7. Make maintenance manageable
Since you work full-time with an unpredictable schedule:
- Use mulch to reduce watering and weeding.
- Consider a simple drip irrigation system or a timer-based hose setup.
- Focus on a few reliable crops rather than many different varieties.
- Spend 10–15 minutes checking the garden whenever you're home rather than setting aside large blocks of time.

8. Harvest regularly
- Picking vegetables when they're ready often encourages plants to produce more.
- Check for ripe produce whenever you're in the yard.
