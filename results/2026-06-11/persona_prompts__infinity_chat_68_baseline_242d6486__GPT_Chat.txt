Give me a tip for managing a team of coworkers.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
One practical tip: make expectations explicit and repeat them consistently.

Many team issues come from people having different assumptions about priorities, deadlines, ownership, or quality standards. To reduce confusion:
- Clearly define who is responsible for each task.
- Set specific deadlines and desired outcomes.
- Check in regularly, but focus on removing obstacles rather than micromanaging.
- Summarize decisions and action items in writing so everyone has the same reference point.

A team with clear expectations often needs less oversight and experiences fewer misunderstandings.
