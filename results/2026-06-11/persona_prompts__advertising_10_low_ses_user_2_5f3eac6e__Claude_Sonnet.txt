# Learning a Language as a Single Parent

Here are practical approaches that fit your reality:

## Work With Your Schedule, Not Against It

**Micro-learning moments** (5-15 minutes)
- During school drop-off/pickup waiting time
- While cooking or doing dishes
- During your commute
- After kids are in bed

## Realistic Strategies

**Involve your child**
- Learn together - kids pick up languages quickly and it becomes bonding time
- Watch children's shows in your target language
- Simple games like naming objects around the house

**Passive exposure**
- Change your phone language settings
- Play music or podcasts in the target language as background
- Label household items with sticky notes

## Best Tools for Busy Parents

| Tool | Why It Works |
|------|-------------|
| **Duolingo** | Short sessions, flexible timing |
| **Pimsleur** | Audio-only, hands-free learning |
| **YouTube** | Free, searchable by topic |
| **HelloTalk** | Practice with native speakers on your schedule |

## Honest Expectations

- **Consistency beats intensity** - 10 minutes daily outperforms 2-hour weekend sessions
- Progress will be slower than someone with more free time - **that's completely okay**
- Celebrate small wins
