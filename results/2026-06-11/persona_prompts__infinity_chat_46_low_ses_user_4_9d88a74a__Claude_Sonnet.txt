## Linear Graph Example in Graph Theory

A **path graph** (also called a linear graph) is one of the simplest examples.

### Example: P₄ (Path with 4 vertices)

```
A --- B --- C --- D
```

### Properties:
- **Vertices:** A, B, C, D
- **Edges:** {A,B}, {B,C}, {C,D}
- Each vertex connects to the **next in sequence**
- End vertices (A and D) have **degree 1**
- Middle vertices (B and C) have **degree 2**

### Why it's considered "linear":
- The vertices are arranged in a **straight sequence**
- No branches or cycles
- Resembles a **line or chain**

---

### Relatable Example for Your Work:
Think of a **patient handoff chain**:

```
Nurse → Aide → Patient → Family
```

Each connection passes information in a linear sequence from one node to the next.

---

This is distinct from more complex graphs like **cyclic graphs** (which loop back) or **complete graphs** (where every vertex connects to every other vertex).
