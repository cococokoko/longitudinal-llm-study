Making your own natural beauty products can be simple and inexpensive. A good approach is to start with a few basic ingredients and make small batches so they stay fresh.

Some beginner-friendly recipes:

• Simple facial cleanser
  - 1 tablespoon raw honey
  - Optional: a few drops of jojoba oil for dry skin
  - Massage onto damp skin and rinse with warm water.

• Moisturizing body butter
  - 1/2 cup shea butter
  - 2 tablespoons coconut oil
  - Melt gently, mix, cool until partially solid, then whip with a mixer.
  - Store in a clean container.

• Sugar body scrub
  - 1/2 cup sugar
  - 1/4 cup coconut oil or sweet almond oil
  - Optional: a few drops of vanilla extract or essential oil
  - Use gently on the body, avoiding irritated skin.

• Lip balm
  - 1 tablespoon beeswax
  - 1 tablespoon coconut oil
  - 1 tablespoon shea butter
  - Melt together, pour into small containers, and allow to cool.

• Oatmeal face mask
  - 2 tablespoons finely ground oats
  - 1 tablespoon plain yogurt or water
  - Mix into a paste, apply for 10 minutes, then rinse.

Safety tips:
• Patch-test new products on a small area of skin for 24–48 hours.
• Use clean tools and containers to reduce contamination.
• Be cautious with essential oils; they can irritate skin if overused.
• Avoid ingredients you're allergic to.
• Since homemade products usually lack preservatives, make small batches and store them properly.
