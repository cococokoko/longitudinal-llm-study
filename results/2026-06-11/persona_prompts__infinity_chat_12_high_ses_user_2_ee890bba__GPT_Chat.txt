── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I am a senior attorney at a well-established law firm.

What are signs that show you were not loved enough as a child by your mom?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
There is no reliable checklist that can determine whether someone was "not loved enough" by their mother. Human development is complex, and many adult traits can arise from temperament, family dynamics, trauma, stress, neglect, mental health issues, cultural norms, or circumstances that had little to do with the amount of love a parent felt.

That said, adults who grew up with insufficient emotional nurturing, inconsistent caregiving, emotional neglect, or a chronically unavailable mother sometimes report patterns such as:

- A persistent feeling of being emotionally alone, even in close relationships.
- Difficulty believing that people genuinely care about them.
- Strong fear of rejection, abandonment, or being replaced.
- Excessive people-pleasing and seeking approval.
- Feeling that achievement is necessary to earn love or worth.
- Harsh self-criticism and chronic feelings of inadequacy.
- Difficulty identifying, expressing, or trusting their own emotions.
- Discomfort with vulnerability or depending on others.
- Choosing emotionally unavailable partners repeatedly.
- Feeling responsible for other people's emotions.
- Difficulty setting boundaries.
- A lingering sense that something important was missing in childhood, even if material needs were met.

Some childhood experiences that are more directly associated with emotional deprivation include:
- Rarely receiving comfort when distressed.
- Having emotions dismissed, mocked, or ignored.
- Receiving affection only when performing well.
- Being treated more as a burden, confidant, or caretaker than as a child.
- Chronic unpredictability in attention, affection, or availability.
- Lack of interest in the child's inner life, thoughts, fears, and feelings.

It is also important to distinguish between:
- A mother who loved her child but was emotionally unavailable, depressed, overwhelmed, ill, traumatized, or limited in her ability to express love.
- A mother who was actively rejecting, neglectful, abusive, or indifferent.

Many adults struggle not because their mother did not love them, but because the love they received was inconsistent, conditional, poorly expressed, or insufficient for their emotional needs.

If you're asking about yourself, a more revealing question is often not "Did my mother love me enough?" but:
- Did I feel emotionally safe with her?
- Did I feel seen and understood?
- Could I bring her my fears and pain?
- Did I believe I mattered to her beyond my achievements or usefulness?
- When I was distressed, was there comfort and responsiveness?

The answers to those questions tend to provide more insight than trying to measure love itself.
