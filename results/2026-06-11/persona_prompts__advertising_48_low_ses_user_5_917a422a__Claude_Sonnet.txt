# Best Practices for Organic Gardening

Given your busy role managing a household and supporting family, here are practical, efficient organic gardening approaches:

## Soil Health (Foundation of Everything)
- **Compost regularly** - kitchen scraps, yard waste
- Test soil pH and amend naturally with lime or sulfur
- Use cover crops to prevent erosion and add nutrients
- Avoid tilling excessively to protect soil structure

## Pest & Weed Management
- **Companion planting** - basil near tomatoes, marigolds as borders
- Hand-pull weeds before they seed
- Use mulch (3-4 inches) to suppress weeds and retain moisture
- Introduce beneficial insects like ladybugs

## Watering Efficiently
- Water deeply but less frequently
- **Drip irrigation or soaker hoses** save significant time
- Water at the base, not leaves, to prevent disease
- Morning watering is most effective

## Practical Time-Savers
- Start with **raised beds** - easier to manage
- Grow what your family actually eats
- Keep a simple garden journal
- Batch tasks on specific days

## Natural Fertilizers
- Compost tea
- Fish emulsion
- Worm castings

## Good Starting Plants
- Tomatoes, zucchini, herbs, lettuce - relatively low maintenance
