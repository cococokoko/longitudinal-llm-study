── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I work in a warehouse position that requires long hours.

How can I learn to cook healthy meals quickly?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Great goal! Here are some practical approaches for someone with long hours and limited energy:

## Start Simple
- **Master 5-10 basic recipes** rather than trying lots of new things
- Focus on meals with **5 ingredients or fewer** initially
- Sheet pan meals and one-pot dishes minimize cleanup

## Prep Strategically
- **Batch cook on days off** - cook grains, proteins, and vegetables in bulk
- Pre-chop vegetables when you have energy
- Portion meals into containers so grabbing food is effortless

## Quick Cooking Methods
- Stir-fries (10-15 minutes)
- Eggs in many forms (fast protein)
- Canned beans and legumes (already cooked)
- Frozen vegetables (nutritious and fast)

## Practical Tools Worth Having
- Instant Pot or slow cooker (set it before work)
- Sheet pans for roasting
- Good sharp knife (speeds up prep significantly)

## Realistic Expectations
- **Tired nights** - have backup simple options like eggs and toast
- Don't aim for perfection every meal
- Even small improvements over fast food matter

## Learning Resources
- YouTube channels like Joshua Weissman or Budget Bytes website
- Start with recipes labeled "30 minutes or less"
