── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Can you give me a powerful rhetorical question for an essay about the harms of social media on teens?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
To choose the most powerful rhetorical question, you should match it to the specific angle or tone of your essay. 

Here are a few options categorized by the specific harm they focus on:

### 1. Focus on Mental Health & Self-Esteem
*Best for essays about depression, anxiety, body image, and the "highlight reel" of social media.*
> **"How can we expect a generation to build a stable sense of self when their self-worth is constantly being weighed, measured, and quantified by the clicks of strangers?"**

### 2. Focus on Addiction & Tech Monopolies
*Best for essays about screen time, attention spans, and how tech companies design apps to be addictive.*
> **"When did we decide that the undivided attention of our youth was a fair price to pay for the profit margins of Silicon Valley?"**

### 3. Focus on Isolation & Loneliness
*Best for essays about how online "connection" actually isolates teenagers in the real world.*
> **"Are we raising the most connected generation in human history, or simply the loneliest?"**

### 4. Focus on the Loss of Childhood
*Best for essays about the pressure on teens to grow up too fast, perform for an audience, and lose their privacy.*
> **"What happens to a childhood when every mistake is permanently archived, and every private moment is staged for public approval?"**

### 5. Short & Punchy (Great for a Hook or Conclusion)
*Best for a dramatic opening sentence or a final, lingering thought.*
> **"We gave them a window to the world, but did we accidentally lock them inside?"**

### Tip for using these in your essay:
* **If using it as a Hook (Introduction):** Follow it immediately with a shocking statistic. *(e.g., "Are we raising the most connected generation in human history, or simply the loneliest? According to recent studies, teen loneliness has doubled since...")*
* **If using it as a Clincher (Conclusion):** Leave it as your very last sentence so the reader is forced to sit with the question after they finish reading.
