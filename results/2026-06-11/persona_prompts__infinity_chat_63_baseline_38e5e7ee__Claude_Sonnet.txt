# Partial Derivatives: A Numerical Example

## The Function

Let's use:
$$f(x, y) = x^2 + 3xy + y^2$$

We'll find the partial derivatives at the point **(2, 3)**.

---

## Finding the Partial Derivatives

### Partial Derivative with Respect to x
**(treat y as a constant)**

$$\frac{\partial f}{\partial x} = 2x + 3y$$

**At point (2, 3):**
$$\frac{\partial f}{\partial x}\Bigg|_{(2,3)} = 2(2) + 3(3) = 4 + 9 = \boxed{13}$$

### Partial Derivative with Respect to y
**(treat x as a constant)**

$$\frac{\partial f}{\partial y} = 3x + 2y$$

**At point (2, 3):**
$$\frac{\partial f}{\partial y}\Bigg|_{(2,3)} = 3(2) + 2(3) = 6 + 6 = \boxed{12}$$

## Interpretation

| Partial Derivative | Value | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| $\partial f/\partial x = 13$ | 13 | f increases by **~13 units** per unit increase in x (y held fixed at 3) |
| $\partial f/\partial y = 12$ | 12 | f increases by **~12 units** per unit increase in y (x held fixed at 2) |

> **Key idea:** A partial derivative measures the rate of change of a function in **one direction at a time**, holding all other variables constant.
