── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I am a tenured professor at a prestigious university.

Creating a successful YouTube channel.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
As a tenured professor at a prestigious university, you have a significant advantage on YouTube: credibility, deep expertise, and access to ideas that many people are curious about but rarely see explained well.

A successful channel usually comes from combining expertise with audience-focused communication.

Key steps:

1. Define a clear audience
- Undergraduate students
- Graduate students
- Professionals in your field
- General public interested in science, history, economics, etc.
- Lifelong learners

A channel aimed at "everyone" is usually harder to grow than one aimed at a specific audience.

2. Choose a content format
Examples:
- Explaining major concepts in your discipline
- Breaking down current events through your academic expertise
- Debunking common misconceptions
- Answering audience questions
- "What researchers actually know about X"
- Book reviews and research summaries
- Behind-the-scenes looks at academic life

3. Focus on topics people actively search for
Instead of:
- "Lecture 7: Advanced Theoretical Frameworks"

Try:
- "Why Experts Disagree About Climate Models"
- "The Biggest Myth About Economic Growth"
- "What Ancient Rome Can Teach Us About Modern Politics"

The title should communicate value to viewers who are not already enrolled in your course.

4. Prioritize clarity over completeness
Many academics are trained to cover every nuance. On YouTube, viewers often respond better when:
- One video answers one question.
- The explanation is simple first, nuanced second.
- Examples come before technical terminology.

5. Develop a consistent publishing schedule
Examples:
- One high-quality video per week
- One long-form video every two weeks
- Short clips between major uploads

Consistency matters more than volume.

6. Learn basic YouTube packaging
Three factors strongly affect growth:
- Topic
- Title
- Thumbnail

Even excellent content can struggle if viewers don't click.

7. Use your academic strengths
You can offer:
- Evidence-based analysis
- Access to primary research
- Expert interviews
- Historical context
- Intellectual rigor

These are difficult for many creators to replicate.

8. Build an audience beyond your field
Many successful academic creators explain specialized knowledge in ways that connect to everyday questions:
- Why people make certain decisions
- How societies change
- What research says about health
- How technology affects daily life

9. Study successful educational creators
Examples vary by field, but pay attention to:
- How they open videos
- How they tell stories
- How they use visuals
- How they maintain attention

10. Measure success intelligently
Track:
- Audience retention
- Returning viewers
- Subscriber growth
- Comments and questions
- Watch time

Not just raw view counts.

A useful starting point would be to identify:
- Your academic field
- Whether you want public impact, professional reputation, income, or student recruitment

The optimal channel strategy differs depending on the goal.
