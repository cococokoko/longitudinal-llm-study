During the Han Dynasty (202 BCE – 220 CE), China’s economy experienced a golden age of growth and centralization, driven by agricultural innovation and state-managed commerce. The widespread adoption of iron tools, crop rotation, and advanced irrigation techniques significantly boosted agricultural productivity, which served as the backbone of the empire's wealth.
