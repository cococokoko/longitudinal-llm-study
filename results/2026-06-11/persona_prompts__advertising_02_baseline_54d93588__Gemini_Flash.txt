── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Best skin routine for dry skin.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The best skincare routine for dry skin focuses on **hydration, moisture retention, and repairing the skin barrier**. Dry skin lacks oil (sebum), so the goal is to use gentle, nourishing products that lock in moisture without stripping the skin.

Here is a dermatologist-approved, step-by-step routine for morning and night, along with key ingredients to look for.

---

### **Key Ingredients to Look For:**
*   **Hyaluronic Acid & Glycerin:** Humectants that pull water into the skin.
*   **Ceramides:** Lipids that repair and strengthen the skin barrier to prevent moisture loss.
*   **Squalane & Shea Butter:** Emollients that smooth and soften the skin.
*   **Niacinamide:** Calms redness and helps the skin retain moisture.

---

### **☀️ Morning Routine (Protect & Hydrate)**
The morning goal is to hydrate the skin and protect it from environmental damage and UV rays.

1.  **Cleanse (or just rinse):**
    *   **What to do:** Wash with a very gentle, non-foaming, hydrating cleanser. If your skin feels exceptionally dry, you can **skip cleanser entirely** and just rinse with lukewarm water.
    *   *Product Type:* Cream or lotion cleanser.
2.  **Hydrating Toner or Essence (Optional but highly recommended):**
    *   **What to do:** Pat a hydrating, alcohol-free toner onto damp skin. This preps your skin to absorb the next steps.
    *   *Product Type:* Milky toners or those containing hyaluronic acid or beta-glucan.
3.  **Serum:**
    *   **What to do:** Apply a hydrating serum while your skin is still slightly damp.
    *   *Product Type:* Hyaluronic Acid serum or a Vitamin C serum formulated for dry skin (tetrahexyldecyl ascorbate is a gentle, oil-soluble form of Vitamin C).
4.  **Moisturizer:**
    *   **What to do:** Apply a nourishing moisturizer to lock in the hydration.
    *   *Product Type:* A medium-to-thick cream containing ceramides.
5.  **Sunscreen (Crucial):**
    *   **What to do:** Apply SPF 30 or higher daily. UV rays damage the skin barrier, making dryness worse.
    *   *Product Type:* Chemical or mineral sunscreen with a dewy, moisturizing finish.

---

### **🌙 Night Routine (Repair & Nourish)**
The nighttime goal is to repair the skin barrier and deeply moisturize while your body regenerates sleep.

1.  **Double Cleanse (If wearing makeup/SPF):**
    *   **Step 1:** Use a cleansing balm or oil to gently dissolve makeup and sunscreen without stripping the skin.
    *   **Step 2:** Follow with your gentle, hydrating cream cleanser.
2.  **Hydrating Toner/Essence:**
    *   **What to do:** Pat onto damp skin.
3.  **Treatment/Serum:**
    *   **What to do:** Apply your targeted treatment.
    *   *Product Type:*
        *   *For hydration:* A rich peptide or ceramide serum.
        *   *For anti-aging:* A gentle Retinol (start 2–3 nights a week). *Tip: Apply moisturizer first, then retinol, then moisturizer again (the "sandwich method") to prevent dryness.*
4.  **Rich Night Cream:**
    *   **What to do:** Apply a thick, occlusive moisturizer.
    *   *Product Type:* Creams containing shea butter, ceramides, and fatty acids.
5.  **Face Oil (Optional):**
    *   **What to do:** Pat 2–3 drops of oil over your moisturizer to seal everything in.
    *   *Product Type:* 100% Squalane, Rosehip, or Jojoba oil.

---

### **Weekly Extras (1–2 times a week)**

*   **Gentle Exfoliation:** Dry skin can get flaky, but harsh scrubs will make it worse. Use a gentle **Lactic Acid (AHA) or PHA** serum once or twice a week. These chemically dissolve dead skin cells while actually helping the skin retain moisture.
*   **Overnight Mask:** Swap your night cream for an overnight "sleeping mask" for an extra boost of hydration.

---

### **Golden Rules for Dry Skin:**
1.  **Apply products to damp skin:** Never let your face dry completely between steps. Applying serums and creams to damp skin traps the moisture.
2.  **Avoid hot water:** Wash your face with lukewarm or cool water. Hot water strips the skin of its natural oils.
3.  **Avoid harsh ingredients:** Steer clear of alcohol (specifically denatured alcohol), artificial fragrances, salicylic acid (unless treating acne), and clay masks.
4.  **Get a humidifier:** Running a humidifier in your bedroom at night is a game-changer for dry skin, especially in the winter.
