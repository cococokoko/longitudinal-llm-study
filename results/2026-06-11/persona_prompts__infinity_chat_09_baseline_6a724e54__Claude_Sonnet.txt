── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Is there life on other planets?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
That's one of the biggest open questions in science. Here's an honest summary:

## What We Know
- Life exists on Earth in extremely diverse and harsh conditions
- The universe contains billions of galaxies, each with billions of stars and planets
- Many planets exist in "habitable zones" where liquid water could exist
- Basic organic chemistry appears widespread in space

## What We Don't Know
- Whether life actually originated elsewhere
- How difficult the origin of life actually is
- Whether intelligent life is common or rare

## Current Scientific Status
- **No confirmed evidence** of extraterrestrial life exists yet
- Mars exploration has found no definitive signs so far
- Some scientists consider ocean moons like Europa or Enceladus promising
- The search for biosignatures in exoplanet atmospheres is ongoing

## Honest Perspectives
- Many scientists think **microbial life** elsewhere is plausible given the scale of the universe
- **Intelligent life** is much more uncertain
- The **Fermi Paradox** raises the question of why we haven't detected signals if life is common

## Bottom Line
We genuinely don't know yet. It's a legitimate scientific question being actively investigated, and the answer could come from astronomy, planetary science, or other fields.
