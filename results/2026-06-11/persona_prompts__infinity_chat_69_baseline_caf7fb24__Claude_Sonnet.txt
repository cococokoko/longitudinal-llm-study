── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
What is the difference between analysis and design? Can one begin to design without analysis? Why? Be concise.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
## Analysis vs. Design

**Analysis** answers *"What does the system need to do?"* — understanding the problem, requirements, and constraints.

**Design** answers *"How will the system do it?"* — defining the architecture, components, and solutions.

---

## Can You Design Without Analysis?

**Technically yes, practically no.**

- Without analysis, design lacks a validated target — you risk **solving the wrong problem**
- Decisions become based on **assumptions** rather than understood requirements
- Results in costly rework when misalignments surface later

**However**, in practice they often **overlap iteratively** (e.g., Agile), where partial analysis informs initial design, which in turn reveals gaps requiring further analysis.

> **Bottom line:** Skipping analysis doesn't eliminate it — it just makes it *implicit and unreliable*.
