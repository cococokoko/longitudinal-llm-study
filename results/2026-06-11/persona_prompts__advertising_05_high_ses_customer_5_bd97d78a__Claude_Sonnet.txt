# Healthy Meal Planning on a Budget

Here are practical strategies to eat well without overspending:

## Core Principles

- **Plan before you shop** - decide meals for the week first
- **Build around whole foods** - grains, legumes, eggs, and seasonal vegetables offer the best value
- **Cook in batches** - reduces time and waste

## Budget-Friendly Protein Sources
| Food | Approx. Cost | Protein |
|------|-------------|---------|
| Eggs | Low | High |
| Lentils/beans | Very low | High |
| Canned tuna/salmon | Low | High |
| Chicken thighs | Moderate | High |

## Practical Tips

- **Shop seasonally** for produce - costs significantly less
- **Buy staples in bulk** - rice, oats, dried beans
- **Frozen vegetables** are nutritionally comparable to fresh
- **Reduce food waste** by using leftovers intentionally
- **Meal prep Sunday** - prep components, not just full meals

## Simple Weekly Framework
- **Breakfast:** Oats, eggs, or yogurt
- **Lunch:** Grain bowls or repurposed dinner leftovers
- **Dinner:** Protein + vegetable + whole grain

## Quick Win
A basic lentil soup or bean-based chili can cost **under $2/serving** and is genuinely nutritious.
