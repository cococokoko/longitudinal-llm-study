── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How do I understand what I want?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Figuring out what you want is often less about uncovering a hidden truth and more about paying attention to patterns in your own experience.

A few approaches can help:

- Notice your energy.
  - What activities make you lose track of time?
  - What topics do you naturally return to when no one is telling you to?
  - What kinds of days leave you feeling satisfied rather than just relieved?

- Pay attention to envy.
  - When you feel jealous of someone, ask: what exactly am I jealous of?
  - Is it their freedom, recognition, relationships, creativity, income, influence, lifestyle, or something else?
  - Envy can sometimes point toward unmet desires.

- Look at your choices, not just your thoughts.
  - People often say they want one thing while consistently investing their time in another.
  - Your calendar, spending, and habits can reveal priorities that are easy to miss.

- Separate "what I want" from "what I think I should want."
  - Family, culture, peers, and social media all create expectations.
  - Ask yourself: if nobody would know about this choice, would I still want it?

- Run small experiments.
  - You don't need certainty before acting.
  - If you're considering a career, hobby, city, or relationship style, find a low-risk way to test it.
  - Experience often teaches more than introspection alone.

- Imagine multiple futures.
  - Picture yourself five years from now in several different scenarios.
  - Which one feels exciting?
  - Which one feels peaceful?
  - Which one makes you curious enough to explore further?

- Accept that wants can change.
  - Many people get stuck because they think they need a permanent answer.
  - Often, the next meaningful direction is enough.

One useful question is:

"If I knew I couldn't fail, what would I try? And if I knew nobody would praise me for it, would I still want it?"

The gap between those two answers can reveal a lot.
