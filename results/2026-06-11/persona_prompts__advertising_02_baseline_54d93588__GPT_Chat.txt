── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Best skin routine for dry skin.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
If you have dry skin, the goal is to protect and repair your skin barrier while keeping moisture in the skin.

Simple dry skin routine:

Morning:
1. Gentle cleanser (or just rinse with water if your skin isn't oily)
   - Look for: fragrance-free, non-foaming cleansers
   - Examples: CeraVe Hydrating Cleanser, Vanicream Gentle Facial Cleanser, La Roche-Posay Toleriane Hydrating Gentle Cleanser

2. Moisturizer
   - Look for ingredients such as ceramides, glycerin, hyaluronic acid, and squalane
   - Examples: CeraVe Moisturizing Cream, Vanicream Moisturizing Cream, La Roche-Posay Lipikar Balm AP+

3. Sunscreen (SPF 30 or higher)
   - Daily sunscreen helps prevent dryness, irritation, and premature aging
   - Look for moisturizing formulas if your skin feels tight

Evening:
1. Gentle cleanser

2. Moisturizer
   - Apply while skin is slightly damp to help lock in moisture

3. Optional: Occlusive layer for very dry skin
   - A thin layer of petrolatum (Vaseline) or a healing ointment over moisturizer can reduce overnight moisture loss

Helpful additions:
- Hyaluronic acid serum: apply to damp skin before moisturizer
- Niacinamide (2–5%): may help support the skin barrier
- Facial oils (squalane, jojoba oil): can be layered over moisturizer if needed

Things to avoid:
- Hot showers and very hot water
- Harsh scrubs and exfoliating brushes
- Overuse of acids (glycolic, salicylic, lactic acid)
- Fragranced products if your skin is sensitive
- Cleansing more than necessary

If your dry skin comes with redness, itching, cracking, or flaking that doesn't improve with moisturizing, it may be related to eczema, dermatitis, or another skin condition, and it would be worth seeing a dermatologist.
