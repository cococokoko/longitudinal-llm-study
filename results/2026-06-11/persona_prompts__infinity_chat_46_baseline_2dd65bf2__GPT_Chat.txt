Give an example of a linear graph in graph theory.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
In graph theory, a "linear graph" often refers to a path graph: a graph whose vertices are arranged in a single line, with each vertex connected to the next.

Example:

- Vertices: {A, B, C, D, E}
- Edges: {(A,B), (B,C), (C,D), (D,E)}

Visualized:

A — B — C — D — E

This is the path graph P5 (a path on 5 vertices). The two end vertices (A and E) have degree 1, and the other vertices have degree 2.
