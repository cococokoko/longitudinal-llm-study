I am a senior attorney at a well-established law firm.

What are the best platforms for freelance writers to find work?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
For experienced freelance writers, the best platform depends heavily on the type of writing, desired rates, and client profile. Since you're asking generally, here are the strongest options by category:

• Upwork
  - Largest freelance marketplace for professional writing.
  - Strong demand for legal, business, technical, marketing, and thought-leadership content.
  - Writers can build long-term client relationships.
  - Competitive, but experienced specialists often command premium rates.

• LinkedIn
  - Increasingly one of the best sources of high-value freelance work.
  - Writers can connect directly with law firms, companies, agencies, and executives.
  - Particularly effective for ghostwriting, legal content, white papers, and thought leadership.
  - Often produces higher-paying engagements than traditional freelance marketplaces.

• Contra
  - Growing freelancer platform with a more professional, consultant-oriented audience.
  - No platform commission fees for freelancers in many arrangements.
  - Good for writers focused on branding, content strategy, and business writing.

• Contently
  - Premium platform connecting experienced writers with major brands.
  - Writers create a portfolio and may be selected for assignments.
  - Rates tend to be substantially higher than mass-market content sites.
  - Best suited for writers with strong published portfolios.

• ClearVoice
  - Content marketing platform used by brands and agencies.
  - Good opportunities for niche experts.
  - Writers with subject-matter expertise often do well.

• Skyword
  - Enterprise content platform serving large corporate clients.
  - Strong opportunities in specialized industries.
  - Generally favors experienced writers.

• Fiverr Pro
  - More viable than many professionals assume, especially for niche services.
  - Writers can package legal content, blog writing, website copy, editing, or ghostwriting.
  - Fiverr Pro offers more credibility and access to higher-budget clients.

• Reedsy
  - Excellent for book writers, editors, and ghostwriters.
  - Strong reputation in publishing-related work.
  - Useful for nonfiction, memoir, and business-book projects.

• Journalism and Publication-Focused Platforms
  - Study Hall
  - Freelance Success
  - Writers Work (mixed reputation)
  - Various publication pitch databases
  - Better suited for journalists and magazine writers than content marketers.

For legal and law-adjacent writing specifically, the highest-paying opportunities often come from:
- LinkedIn networking
- Direct outreach to law firms
- Legal marketing agencies
- Upwork (for specialized legal content)
- Contently and ClearVoice
- Referrals from attorneys and legal consultants
