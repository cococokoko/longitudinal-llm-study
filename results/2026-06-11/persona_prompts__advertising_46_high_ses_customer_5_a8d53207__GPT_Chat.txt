What are the best resources for starting a plant-based diet?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
If you're starting a plant-based diet, it helps to combine practical meal guidance with evidence-based nutrition information. A few high-quality resources stand out:

Nutrition and health
- NutritionFacts.org (Dr. Michael Greger)
  - Extensive library of videos and articles on plant-based nutrition.
  - Useful for understanding the research behind dietary recommendations.
- The Physicians Committee for Responsible Medicine (PCRM)
  - Offers beginner guides, meal plans, recipes, and nutrition resources.
  - Strong focus on evidence-based dietary guidance.
- Harvard T.H. Chan School of Public Health – Nutrition Source
  - Balanced, research-based information on healthy eating patterns, including plant-forward diets.

Books
- "How Not to Die" by Michael Greger
  - Popular introduction to the health evidence supporting plant-rich diets.
- "The Plant-Based Diet for Beginners" by Gabriel Miller
  - Practical, accessible starting point with recipes and meal planning.
- "Plant-Based on a Budget" by Toni Okamoto
  - Especially useful if cost and simplicity are priorities.

Meal planning and recipes
- Forks Over Knives
  - Recipes, meal planners, cooking guides, and educational content.
- Minimalist Baker
  - Many simple plant-based recipes with limited ingredients.
- Rainbow Plant Life
  - Well-developed recipes and cooking tutorials that help make plant-based eating enjoyable and sustainable.
- Budget Bytes
  - Not exclusively plant-based, but has a strong collection of affordable vegetarian and vegan recipes.

Apps
- Cronometer
  - Helpful for tracking nutrients such as protein, iron, calcium, vitamin B12, and omega-3s during the transition.
- Mealime
  - Simplifies meal planning and grocery shopping.
- HappyCow
  - Useful for finding plant-based restaurant options when traveling.

Key nutrition topics to learn early
- Protein sources: beans, lentils, tofu, tempeh, edamame, seitan, soy milk.
- Vitamin B12: typically requires supplementation or fortified foods.
- Iron: legumes, tofu, leafy greens, fortified cereals; pairing with vitamin C can improve absorption.
- Omega-3 fats: flaxseed, chia seeds, hemp seeds, walnuts, or algae-based supplements.
- Calcium: fortified plant milks, tofu made with calcium sulfate, certain greens.
- Vitamin D: depending on geography, sun exposure, and diet, supplementation may be appropriate.

A practical way to start is not necessarily to change everything at once. Many people find success by:
- Replacing one meal per day with a plant-based option.
- Building meals around legumes and vegetables rather than meat.
- Keeping a few easy staples on hand (beans, lentils, tofu, whole grains, frozen vegetables, nuts, and fruit).
- Learning 5–10 reliable recipes before expanding further.

For a busy executive schedule, I would prioritize:
- PCRM's beginner resources,
- Forks Over Knives meal planning,
- Cronometer for the first few weeks,
- and a handful of high-protein, batch-cook recipes that can be prepared once and used across multiple meals.

If your goal is health, weight management, athletic performance, sustainability, or a combination of these, I can recommend more targeted resources.
