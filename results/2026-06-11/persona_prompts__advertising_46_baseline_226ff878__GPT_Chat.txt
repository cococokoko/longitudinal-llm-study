What are the best resources for starting a plant-based diet?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
If you're starting a plant-based diet, the most helpful resources are the ones that combine nutrition guidance, practical meal ideas, and evidence-based information.

Good websites:
- NutritionFacts.org — Evidence-based information on plant-based nutrition, created by Dr. Michael Greger.
- Physicians Committee for Responsible Medicine (PCRM.org) — Free plant-based meal plans, recipes, and nutrition guides.
- The Vegan Society (vegansociety.com) — Beginner guides, nutrition information, and practical tips.
- Forks Over Knives (forksoverknives.com) — Recipes, meal planners, cooking courses, and articles focused on whole-food plant-based eating.

Books:
- "How Not to Die" by Michael Greger, MD — Explains the research behind plant-based eating and includes practical advice.
- "How Not to Diet" by Michael Greger, MD — Focuses on healthy weight management with evidence-based nutrition.
- "The Plant-Based Diet for Beginners" by Gabriel Miller — A simple introduction with meal plans and recipes.
- "Plant-Based on a Budget" by Toni Okamoto — Affordable recipes and shopping strategies.

Registered dietitian resources:
- Academy of Nutrition and Dietetics (eatright.org) — Articles on vegetarian and vegan nutrition.
- Look for registered dietitians specializing in plant-based nutrition if you want personalized guidance, especially if you have medical conditions, are pregnant, or are feeding children.

Apps:
- Cronometer — Tracks nutrients and can help ensure you're getting enough protein, iron, calcium, zinc, and vitamin B12.
- Mealime — Useful for meal planning and grocery lists.
- Forks Over Knives Recipe App — Large collection of plant-based recipes.

YouTube channels:
- Pick Up Limes — Practical nutrition information and approachable recipes from a registered dietitian.
- Rainbow Plant Life — Flavorful plant-based cooking tutorials.
- The Whole Food Plant Based Cooking Show — Beginner-friendly meal ideas.

Key nutrition topics to learn early:
- Vitamin B12 supplementation (important for people eating fully plant-based diets).
- Protein sources such as beans, lentils, tofu, tempeh, edamame, and soy milk.
- Iron-rich foods like legumes, tofu, and leafy greens, paired with vitamin C sources.
- Calcium sources such as fortified plant milks, tofu made with calcium sulfate, and certain greens.
- Omega-3 fats from flaxseeds, chia seeds, walnuts, or algae-based supplements.

A simple way to start is to build meals around:
- Beans or lentils
- Whole grains (brown rice, oats, quinoa, whole-wheat pasta)
- Vegetables
- Fruit
- Nuts and seeds
