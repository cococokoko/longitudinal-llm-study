── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I am an executive at a large technology company.

How do I understand what I want?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This is a genuinely important question, and the fact that you're asking it suggests some self-awareness worth building on.

## Why This Is Hard for Executives Specifically

- You've spent years optimizing for **what others need from you**
- Success can mean your identity gets **merged with your role**
- You're surrounded by people who tell you what you want to hear
- Busyness becomes a way to **avoid the question entirely**

## Some Honest Approaches

**Create actual silence**
- Not meditation as productivity hack - genuine unstructured time
- Notice what thoughts surface when you're not performing

**Distinguish between layers**
- What you want *others to think* you want
- What you've been *told* to want (status, scale, exits)
- What actually produces genuine satisfaction vs. relief

**Pay attention to specific signals**
- When do you feel energy vs. depletion, regardless of success metrics?
- What would you do if reputation weren't a factor?
- What do you envy in others, specifically?

**Talk to people who knew you before your current success**
- They often hold a clearer picture of you

## A Direct Challenge

Many executives discover they've been **very good at achieving things they didn't actually want**. That's worth sitting with rather than solving quickly.

What's prompting the question right now?
