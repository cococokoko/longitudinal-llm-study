── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How can I learn to cook healthy meals quickly?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Learning to cook healthy meals quickly is mostly about building a small set of reliable skills and recipes rather than trying to master everything at once.

Here’s a practical approach:

• Learn 5–10 simple meals first
  - Stir-fry with vegetables and protein
  - Sheet-pan chicken and vegetables
  - Omelet or frittata with vegetables
  - Grain bowls (rice, quinoa, or farro + protein + vegetables)
  - Bean chili
  - Pasta with vegetables and lean protein
  - Salmon or tofu with roasted vegetables

• Master a few cooking techniques
  - Roasting: vegetables, chicken, fish
  - Sautéing: quick vegetable and protein dishes
  - Boiling: eggs, pasta, grains
  - Baking: simple proteins and casseroles
  - Slow cooker or pressure cooker basics (optional)

• Keep healthy staples on hand
  - Proteins: eggs, chicken, fish, tofu, beans, lentils, Greek yogurt
  - Vegetables: fresh or frozen vegetables
  - Grains: rice, oats, quinoa, whole-wheat pasta
  - Healthy fats: olive oil, nuts, seeds, avocado
  - Flavor boosters: garlic, onions, lemon, soy sauce, herbs, spices

• Use a simple meal formula
  - 1 protein
  - 1–2 vegetables
  - 1 whole-grain or starchy carbohydrate
  - Healthy fat or sauce

  Example:
  - Grilled chicken + broccoli + brown rice + olive oil and lemon

• Practice meal prep
  - Cook proteins in batches once or twice a week.
  - Wash and cut vegetables ahead of time.
  - Prepare grains in larger quantities and refrigerate or freeze portions.

• Use time-saving ingredients
  - Frozen vegetables
  - Prewashed greens
  - Canned beans
  - Rotisserie chicken
  - Microwaveable brown rice

• Learn from beginner-friendly resources
  - YouTube channels focused on simple home cooking
  - Healthy meal-prep creators
  - Cookbooks with recipes requiring 30 minutes or less

• Aim for repetition
  - Cook the same recipe 3–5 times before moving on.
  - Repetition builds speed, confidence, and technique much faster than constantly trying new recipes.

A good beginner goal is to learn three breakfasts, three lunches, and three dinners that you can prepare in 20–30 minutes. Once those become automatic, expanding your cooking skills becomes much easier.
