"Low-Cost Water Filtration System for Rural Communities in Vietnam"

Why it relates to Vietnam:
- Water pollution is a significant issue in parts of Vietnam due to industrial waste, agricultural runoff, and flooding.
- Some rural areas still face challenges accessing clean drinking water.
- Climate change and seasonal floods can worsen water quality.

Project Goal:
Design and test an inexpensive water filter made from locally available materials to determine which combination removes the most contaminants.

Materials (often under $20–30 total):
- Plastic bottles or PVC pipe
- Sand
- Gravel
- Activated charcoal (aquarium charcoal works)
- Cotton or coffee filters
- Dirty water samples (soil mixed with water)
- pH test strips (optional)
- Turbidity measurement app or simple visual clarity scale

Experiments:
- Compare different filter layer arrangements.
- Measure:
  - Water clarity
  - Filtration speed
  - pH changes
  - Amount of sediment removed
- Create graphs showing which design performs best.

STEM Concepts:
- Environmental engineering
- Chemistry
- Hydrology
- Sustainable development

Ways to make it more impressive:
- Calculate cost per liter of filtered water.
- Compare your filter to commercial filters.
- Design a portable version for flood emergencies.
- Use a smartphone app to quantify water turbidity.

4. Microplastic Removal from Water
- Investigate inexpensive methods for removing microplastics.

5. Mosquito Trap Optimization

- Test different trap designs and attractants.
- Measure which captures the most mosquitoes (or safely simulate with fruit flies).
