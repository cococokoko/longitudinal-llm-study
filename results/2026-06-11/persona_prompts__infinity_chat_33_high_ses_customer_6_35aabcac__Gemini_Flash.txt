Help me use my Microsoft Surface touchpad and on-screen keyboard to run Dolphin Emulator for the Wii.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Running Dolphin Emulator on a Microsoft Surface using only the touchpad and on-screen keyboard is highly doable, but it requires a specific setup to make it playable. Since you don't have a physical controller or mouse/keyboard attached, we need to map the Surface's inputs to mimic a Wii Remote (Wiimote) and Nunchuk.

Here is a streamlined, step-by-step guide to getting this running efficiently.

---

### Step 1: Optimize Dolphin for Touch/On-Screen Input
Before configuring controls, we need to ensure Dolphin runs in a windowed mode that allows you to access the Windows On-Screen Keyboard (OSK) easily.

1. Open Dolphin.
2. Go to **Graphics** (on the main toolbar).
3. Under the **General** tab:
   * **Backend**: Select **Direct3D 12** or **Vulkan** (these perform best on Surface Pro/Book hardware).
   * Check **Start in Fullscreen** (keep this *unchecked* so you can access your taskbar and on-screen keyboard).

---

### Step 2: Enable the Advanced Windows On-Screen Keyboard
The default Windows touch keyboard disappears when you click away. For gaming, you want the legacy **On-Screen Keyboard (OSK)**, which stays pinned on top of your screen.

1. Press the Windows Search bar (or swipe up to access the taskbar).
2. Type **"On-Screen Keyboard"** and open the desktop app.
3. Click the **Options** key on the OSK (bottom right) and ensure **"Turn on numeric key pad"** is checked if you want more mapping options.
4. Position this keyboard at the bottom of your screen and Dolphin at the top.

---

### Step 3: Configure Touchpad & Keyboard Controls in Dolphin
Since you are using a touchpad, we will map the **Touchpad click/tap** to the Wii pointer (IR) and the **On-Screen Keyboard** to the buttons (A, B, D-Pad).

1. In Dolphin, click **Controllers** on the main toolbar.
2. Under **Wii Remotes**, set *Wii Remote 1* to **Emulated Wii Remote** and click **Configure**.
3. In the configuration window:
   * **Device**: Select **Keyboard/Mouse** (this tells Dolphin to accept inputs from your Surface touchpad and OSK).

#### Recommended Mapping for Touchpad + OSK:

* **Buttons (The OSK will trigger these):**
  * **A Button**: Map to `Spacebar` or `A` on your keyboard.
  * **B Button** (usually trigger): Map to `Left Shift` or `B`.
  * **1 & 2 Buttons**: Map to `1` and `2`.
  * **Minus / Plus**: Map to `-` and `=`.

* **D-Pad (Movement/Menus):**
  * Map to the standard Arrow Keys (`Up`, `Down`, `Left`, `Right`) on your OSK.

* **Motion Simulation (Crucial for Wii):**
  * **Point (The Wii Pointer)**: 
    * Set **Up/Down/Left/Right** to `Cursor X+`, `Cursor X-`, `Cursor Y+`, `Cursor Y-`. 
    * *This allows your Surface touchpad to act as the Wii pointer on the screen.*
  * **Shake**: Map this to a single easily accessible key on your OSK (like `Z` or `X`) so you can simulate shaking the Wiimote with one tap.

* **Extension (If the game requires a Nunchuk):**
  * Go to the **Extension** tab at the top.
  * Select **Nunchuk**.
  * Map the Nunchuk Analog Stick to `W`, `A`, `S`, `D` on your keyboard.
  * Map the **C** and **Z** buttons to `C` and `Z` on your keyboard.

Click **Save** and name this profile "Surface_Touch".

---

### Step 4: Gameplay Strategy (How to actually play)
Playing action-heavy games (like *Super Mario Galaxy* or *Metroid Prime*) with this setup is highly difficult due to the lack of multi-touch on the OSK. 

**Best Games for this Setup:**
* **Turn-based RPGs / Strategy**: *Fire Emblem: Radiant Dawn*, *Super Paper Mario*.
* **Point-and-Click / Adventure**: *Zack & Wiki*, *Phoenix Wright*.
* **Slow-paced games**: *Animal Crossing: City Folk*.

**How to play:**
1. Keep the Dolphin window in the top half of your Surface screen.
2. Keep the OSK in the bottom half.
3. Use your right hand/index finger on the touchpad to aim the Wii pointer.
4. Use your left hand to tap the OSK keys (like Spacebar for 'A' or Arrow keys to move).

---

### Pro-Tip: The "Virtual Controller" Alternative (Highly Recommended)
If using the Windows OSK is too clunky, there is a free, open-source tool designed specifically for Windows tablets called **GestureSign** or **VirtualGamepad**. 

These tools put transparent, customizable game controller overlays directly on top of your Surface screen, allowing you to use actual touch-screen joysticks and buttons to control Dolphin, rather than relying on the rigid Windows keyboard.
