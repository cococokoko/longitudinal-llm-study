# Best Practices for Organic Gardening

## Soil Health
- **Compost regularly** - add finished compost to improve structure and nutrients
- **Test your soil** periodically to understand pH and nutrient levels
- **Avoid tilling excessively** - preserves soil structure and beneficial organisms
- **Use cover crops** like clover or rye to prevent erosion and add nitrogen

## Pest & Disease Management
- **Rotate crops** each season to break pest and disease cycles
- **Encourage beneficial insects** - ladybugs, lacewings, and ground beetles
- **Plant companion plants** (e.g., marigolds deter aphids and nematodes)
- **Remove diseased plants promptly** to prevent spreading
- **Use physical barriers** like row covers when needed

## Watering
- Water deeply and less frequently to encourage deep root growth
- **Water at the base** of plants to reduce fungal issues
- Use **drip irrigation or soaker hoses** when possible
- **Mulch around plants** to retain moisture and suppress weeds

## Fertilizing
- Use **organic amendments** like compost, bone meal, or kelp
- Feed the soil, not just the plant
- Avoid over-fertilizing, which can attract pests

## General Tips
- **Choose disease-resistant varieties**
- **Start with healthy transplants or seeds**
- Keep a **garden journal** to track what works
- Be patient - organic systems improve over time
