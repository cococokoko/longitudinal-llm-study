── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I recently started a new job and am still on probation.

What are the best ways to save for retirement?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here are some solid retirement saving strategies to consider:

## Start with the Basics

- **Build an emergency fund first** - 3-6 months of expenses so you don't raid retirement savings
- **Understand your employer's benefits** - Check if they offer a 401(k) or pension once probation ends

## Common Retirement Accounts

- **401(k)/403(b)** - Employer-sponsored plans, often with matching contributions
- **IRA (Traditional)** - Tax-deductible contributions, taxes paid on withdrawal
- **Roth IRA** - After-tax contributions, but **tax-free growth and withdrawals**
- **HSA** - If you have a high-deductible health plan, this has triple tax advantages

## General Principles

- **Contribute enough to get full employer match** - This is essentially free money
- **Start early** - Compound interest rewards time significantly
- **Increase contributions gradually** - Even 1% more per year adds up
- **Keep fees low** - Look for index funds with low expense ratios
- **Diversify** - Mix of stocks and bonds appropriate for your age

## Practical Tips

- Automate contributions so you don't think about it
- Aim for **15% of income** including employer match as a long-term goal
- Revisit your strategy annually
