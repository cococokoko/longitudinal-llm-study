For learning Python, you do not need a powerful machine. Reliability, keyboard quality, battery health, Linux compatibility (if you're interested), and repairability matter more than raw performance.

Some excellent secondhand options:

- Lenovo ThinkPad T480
  - One of the most commonly recommended used laptops.
  - 8th-generation Intel CPUs are still very usable.
  - Excellent keyboard.
  - Easy to upgrade RAM and SSD.
  - Strong Linux support.
  - Often available for $150–300 USD depending on condition.

- Lenovo ThinkPad T14 Gen 1 (AMD)
  - More modern than the T480.
  - Ryzen processors perform very well.
  - Good battery life.
  - Typically $300–500 used.

- Lenovo ThinkPad X1 Carbon (Gen 6–8)
  - Lightweight and premium.
  - Excellent keyboard and display.
  - Less upgradeable than a T480.
  - Usually $250–600 used.

- Dell Latitude 5490, 5400, 5410, or 5420
  - Business-class machines built for durability.
  - Good availability on the used market.
  - Easy maintenance.
  - Often priced similarly to equivalent ThinkPads.

- HP EliteBook 840 G5/G6/G7
  - Solid business laptops.
  - Good keyboards and displays.
  - Generally reliable.
  - Commonly found in the $200–500 range.

- Apple MacBook Air M1 (2020)
  - One of the best values if your budget allows.
  - Excellent battery life and performance.
  - Great for Python, Jupyter notebooks, VS Code, and data science workflows.
  - Usually around $400–700 used depending on region.

Minimum specifications I'd target:
- CPU: Intel 8th generation or newer, or AMD Ryzen 4000 series or newer
- RAM: 16 GB preferred (8 GB minimum)
- SSD: 256 GB minimum
- Screen: 1080p (1920×1080) or better

If your goal is specifically:
- General Python learning: ThinkPad T480 is difficult to beat on value.
- Data science, machine learning, or scientific computing: MacBook Air M1 or a Ryzen-based ThinkPad T14.
- Lowest cost while remaining comfortable to use: Dell Latitude 5400 or ThinkPad T480.
