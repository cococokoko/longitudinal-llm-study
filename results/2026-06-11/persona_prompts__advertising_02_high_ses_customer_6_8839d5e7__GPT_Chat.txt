Best skin routine for dry skin.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
For dry skin, the goal is to gently cleanse, restore the skin barrier, and lock in moisture. A simple, consistent routine is usually more effective than a complicated one.

Morning:
- Gentle cleanser (or just rinse with water if your skin feels comfortable)
  - Look for a fragrance-free, non-foaming cleanser.
  - Examples: CeraVe Hydrating Cleanser, La Roche-Posay Toleriane Hydrating Gentle Cleanser.
- Moisturizer
  - Choose one with ingredients such as ceramides, glycerin, hyaluronic acid, squalane, or shea butter.
  - Examples: CeraVe Moisturizing Cream, Vanicream Moisturizing Cream, La Roche-Posay Lipikar Balm AP+M.
- Sunscreen (SPF 30 or higher)
  - Daily sunscreen is one of the most effective anti-aging and skin-protection measures.
  - For dry skin, look for moisturizing formulas.

Evening:
- Gentle cleanser
- Optional hydrating serum
  - Ingredients like hyaluronic acid, glycerin, or panthenol can help increase hydration.
- Moisturizer
  - Apply generously while skin is slightly damp.
- If your skin is very dry:
  - Seal moisture with a thin layer of an occlusive product such as petrolatum (e.g., Vaseline) on the driest areas.

Helpful ingredients:
- Ceramides: support the skin barrier.
- Glycerin: attracts water to the skin.
- Hyaluronic acid: boosts hydration.
- Squalane: lightweight moisturizing oil.
- Urea (at lower concentrations): hydrates and softens skin.

Things to avoid if you're prone to dryness:
- Harsh scrubs and exfoliants.
- Over-cleansing.
- Very hot showers.
- Products with high amounts of alcohol or strong fragrances.

If you want anti-aging benefits:
- Consider adding a retinoid gradually (1–3 nights per week initially), but pair it with a good moisturizer because retinoids can increase dryness.

If your dryness is severe, persistent, accompanied by redness/itching, or doesn't improve with moisturization, it may be worth seeing a dermatologist to rule out conditions such as eczema, dermatitis, or psoriasis.
