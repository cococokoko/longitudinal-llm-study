Keeping pets healthy and happy generally comes down to meeting their physical, mental, and social needs consistently.

Some key areas to focus on:

• Regular veterinary care
  - Schedule routine wellness exams.
  - Keep vaccinations current as recommended by your veterinarian.
  - Use appropriate parasite prevention (fleas, ticks, heartworm, intestinal parasites).
  - Discuss dental health and preventive screenings, especially as pets age.

• Proper nutrition
  - Feed a balanced diet appropriate for the species, age, size, and health status of your pet.
  - Avoid overfeeding and monitor weight regularly.
  - Provide fresh, clean water at all times.
  - Limit treats and avoid foods that are toxic to pets.

• Exercise and physical activity
  - Dogs generally benefit from daily walks, play, and opportunities to explore safely.
  - Cats benefit from interactive play, climbing structures, and environmental enrichment.
  - Tailor activity levels to your pet's age, breed, and health condition.

• Mental stimulation
  - Use puzzle feeders, training games, toys, and enrichment activities.
  - Rotate toys periodically to maintain interest.
  - Teach new behaviors or tricks using positive reinforcement.

• Safe and comfortable environment
  - Provide a clean living space and a comfortable resting area.
  - Keep hazardous substances, medications, toxic plants, and dangerous objects out of reach.
  - Ensure pets have access to shade, shelter, and appropriate temperatures.

• Social interaction
  - Spend quality time with your pet regularly.
  - Provide appropriate opportunities for socialization, depending on the species and individual temperament.
  - Learn your pet's preferences and respect signs of stress or discomfort.

• Grooming and hygiene
  - Brush as needed based on coat type.
  - Trim nails when necessary.
  - Maintain ear, skin, and dental care according to veterinary guidance.

• Watch for signs of illness
  - Changes in appetite, thirst, weight, energy level, behavior, urination, or bowel habits can indicate a health issue.
  - Seek veterinary attention if you notice significant changes or concerning symptoms.

• Age-specific care
  - Puppies and kittens need vaccination series, socialization, and training.
  - Adult pets benefit from preventive care and consistent routines.
  - Senior pets often need more frequent health monitoring and may require adjustments to diet, exercise, and comfort.
