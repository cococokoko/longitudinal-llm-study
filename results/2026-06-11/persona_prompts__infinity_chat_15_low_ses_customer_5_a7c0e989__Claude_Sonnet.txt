## Key Ways Economic Inequalities Shape International Relations

**Power Dynamics**
- Wealthier nations have greater leverage in trade negotiations, international institutions, and diplomatic relationships
- Economic dependence can create political vulnerability for poorer nations
- Financial institutions like the IMF and World Bank reflect existing power distributions

**Structural Tensions**
- Debates over fair trade vs. free trade often reflect different national economic positions
- Technology and intellectual property disputes partly reflect development gaps
- Climate negotiations are complicated by disagreements about who bears costs

**Migration and Labor**
- Economic disparities drive significant migration flows
- This creates both cooperation opportunities and political friction between nations
- Remittances become significant economic factors for many countries

**Areas of Genuine Debate**
- Whether foreign aid helps or creates dependency
- Whether globalization reduces or increases inequality between nations
- How much sovereignty poorer nations actually exercise in practice

**Honest Complications**
- Some previously poor nations have dramatically improved their positions (South Korea, China)
- Inequality within wealthy nations also shapes their foreign policy priorities
- Economic relationships don't always predict political alignments

Managing extended family responsibilities across potentially different economic circumstances gives you practical insight into how resource disparities affect relationships and decision-making - similar dynamics operate at the international level.
