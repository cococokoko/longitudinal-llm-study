I support extended family members and manage many household responsibilities.

How can I make my home more energy efficient?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Great goal! Here are practical ways to improve home energy efficiency, especially useful when managing a busy household:

## Quick/Low-Cost Steps
- **Switch to LED bulbs** throughout the home
- **Use power strips** to eliminate phantom/standby power drain
- **Adjust thermostat settings** - even 2-3 degrees makes a difference
- **Seal drafts** around doors and windows with weatherstripping
- **Clean HVAC filters** regularly (monthly if heavily used)

## Moderate Investments
- **Smart/programmable thermostat** - can save significantly on heating/cooling
- **Low-flow showerheads and faucet aerators**
- **Insulate water heater** and hot water pipes
- **Energy-efficient appliances** when replacing old ones (look for Energy Star)

## Bigger Projects Worth Considering
- **Attic/wall insulation** - often the highest return
- **Window upgrades** if current ones are old
- **Solar panels** if you own your home

## Managing a Large Household Specifically
- Run dishwashers and laundry **during off-peak hours**
- **Full loads only** for washing machines
- Set **water heater to 120°F** (safe and efficient)
- Educate family members on simple habits

## Getting Help
- Many utility companies offer **free energy audits**
- Check for **federal/state rebates** on efficiency upgrades
