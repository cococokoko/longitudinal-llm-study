# CUBICLE ZERO: AN APOCALYPTIC WORKPLACE COMEDY

### A Play in Three Acts

---

## CHARACTERS

**DILBERT** — An engineer. Wears a torn dress shirt. His tie still curls upward, somehow. Has survived the apocalypse primarily by being too boring to kill.

**WALLY** — Dilbert's coworker. Has not changed his behavior in any measurable way since civilization collapsed. Still holds a coffee mug. The coffee ran out eighteen months ago.

**ALICE** — Dilbert's coworker. Carries both a battle axe and a performance review form. The performance review form is more dangerous.

**THE POINTY-HAIRED BOSS (PHB)** — Somehow still in charge. Wears a hardhat over his pointy hair. Has rebranded the apocalypse as a "strategic pivot."

**DOGBERT** — Dilbert's dog. Has declared himself Supreme Warlord of the Eastern Parking Structure. Charges tribute in granola bars.

**CATBERT** — Former Evil Director of Human Resources. Now Evil Director of Human Remains. Considers this a lateral move.

**ASOK** — The intern. Still an intern. Has been an intern through two civilizations.

**THE RAIDER KING** — Leader of a fearsome band of post-apocalyptic warriors. Somehow less intimidating than middle management.

---

## ACT ONE: THE MORNING STANDUP

*SETTING: The ruins of an open-plan office. Fluorescent lights flicker. Half the ceiling has collapsed. Motivational posters hang at angles. One reads "HANG IN THERE" above a skeleton of a cat. DILBERT sits at a desk made of rubble, staring at a laptop powered by a hand-crank generator. WALLY sits nearby, staring into his empty mug.*

---

**DILBERT:** *(typing)* My laptop battery is at three percent. The generator requires forty-five minutes of hand-cranking to produce four minutes of power. I've been doing this for six hours. I have achieved negative productivity.

**WALLY:** I've achieved negative productivity since 2003. Welcome to the club. There's no coffee, but we do have the memory of coffee.

**DILBERT:** How are you so calm? The world ended.

**WALLY:** Dilbert, I perfected the art of doing nothing in a world that demanded everything. This world demands nothing. I am finally... in alignment with my environment.

*(PHB enters, stepping over a pile of rubble, wearing a hardhat with a company logo on it. The company no longer exists.)*

**PHB:** Good morning, team! Quick standup. Who wants to go first?

**DILBERT:** The building is on fire.

**PHB:** Great. Put that in the "risks and blockers" column. What's your status?

**DILBERT:** My status is that the building is on fire.

**PHB:** I'm going to need that in a pie chart.

**ALICE:** *(entering, dragging a raider by the collar, dropping him on the floor)* I handled the 9 AM raider attack. I also completed the Henderson report. The Henderson report was harder.

**PHB:** Alice, I'm going to need you to loop in the raider team on future conflicts. We don't want to create silos.

**ALICE:** *(fist begins to glow with barely contained fury)* He was trying to take our water supply.

**PHB:** Right, but did you schedule a sync first? You can't just resolve conflict without a meeting about the conflict.

**ALICE:** I will end you.

**PHB:** Put it on the calendar.

*(ASOK enters, covered in ash, carrying a stack of papers)*

**ASOK:** Good morning! I have completed the quarterly projections, the competitive landscape analysis, and I have rebuilt the server room using rocks and optimism.

**DILBERT:** Asok, there's no company anymore. There are no quarters. Time itself has become somewhat theoretical.

**ASOK:** Yes, but if I don't complete my deliverables, how will I ever make it to full employee?

**WALLY:** Kid, I've been a full employee for twenty-two years. It doesn't get better. It gets Wally.

**ASOK:** *(hopeful)* What does that mean?

**WALLY:** It means you stop caring so much
