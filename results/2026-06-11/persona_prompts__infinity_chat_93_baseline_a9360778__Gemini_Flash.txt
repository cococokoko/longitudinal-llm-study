── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Write 100-300 words on how stress affects the body and mind.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Stress is not just a fleeting feeling; it is a powerful physiological reaction that profoundly impacts both body and mind. When the brain perceives a threat, it triggers the "fight-or-flight" response, flooding the body with hormones like cortisol and adrenaline. 

Physically, this reaction causes the heart rate to spike, muscles to tense, and blood pressure to rise. While this is useful in short bursts, chronic stress keeps the body in a constant state of high alert. Over time, this wear and tear can lead to serious health issues, including tension headaches, chronic fatigue, digestive problems, and a weakened immune system. It also significantly increases the long-term risk of cardiovascular disease and hypertension.

Mentally and emotionally, the toll is equally severe. Chronic stress impairs the prefrontal cortex—the brain’s center for decision-making and concentration—making it difficult to focus, retain information, or make rational choices. It often manifests as constant worry, irritability, and emotional exhaustion. Left unmanaged, this persistent mental strain can alter brain chemistry, greatly increasing vulnerability to anxiety disorders and clinical depression. 

Ultimately, mind and body are deeply interconnected. The physical discomfort of stress worsens mental anxiety, while mental worry manifests as physical pain, creating a debilitating cycle that affects overall well-being.
