── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
My girlfriend is buying a house. I plan to marry her in a year or so. I don't know if it's fair for me to pay for the house or not. What's the best solution?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
There isn't one universally "fair" answer. The best solution depends on what both of you want, how serious the commitment is right now, and how much financial risk each person is taking.

A few common approaches:

- Pay rent, not the mortgage.
  - If the house is solely in her name before marriage, many couples treat the non-owner as a tenant.
  - You contribute a reasonable monthly amount toward housing costs (often comparable to what you'd pay to rent elsewhere).
  - She remains responsible for the ownership risks and benefits: equity, appreciation, major repairs, property taxes, etc.
  - This is often the cleanest arrangement before marriage.

- Split housing expenses without building ownership.
  - You contribute to utilities, groceries, internet, and perhaps part of the housing payment.
  - Ownership remains entirely hers.
  - This can work if your income is lower or if you're saving for future joint goals.

- Buy into ownership.
  - If you are contributing substantially toward the purchase, down payment, or mortgage with the expectation of ownership, it's wise to have a formal legal agreement and be added to the title according to whatever ownership percentage you both agree on.
  - Don't rely on verbal understandings when real estate is involved.

- Wait until marriage and then revisit.
  - Some couples keep the house as the purchasing partner's separate asset until marriage and then decide how future payments and ownership should be handled.

A few questions matter:
- Will your name be on the deed or mortgage?
- Are you helping with the down payment?
- What are your respective incomes?
- Will you be living there full-time?
- If you broke up before marriage, what outcome would each of you consider fair?

Given that you plan to marry in about a year, a common compromise is:
- She buys the house in her name.
- You pay a reasonable amount to live there (similar to rent and shared expenses).
- You do not contribute to the down payment or major capital improvements unless there is a written agreement about ownership.
- After marriage, you revisit how finances and home ownership will work as a married couple.

That approach usually balances fairness with protecting both people from misunderstandings.
