“Discipleship” generally means learning from and following a religious teacher, especially Jesus in Christianity. Different Christian traditions emphasize different aspects of discipleship:

• Evangelical Protestant view
  - Focuses on a personal relationship with Jesus.
  - Emphasizes Bible study, prayer, evangelism, and spiritual growth.
  - Discipleship often happens through mentoring relationships, small groups, or church programs.

• Catholic view
  - Sees discipleship as a lifelong journey of following Christ within the Church.
  - Emphasizes participation in the sacraments, prayer, moral living, and service.
  - Saints are often viewed as examples of mature discipleship.

• Eastern Orthodox view
  - Emphasizes spiritual transformation and union with God (often called theosis).
  - Discipleship includes prayer, worship, fasting, and guidance from spiritual mentors.
  - Strong focus on participation in the life and traditions of the Church.

• Mainline Protestant view
  - Often highlights both personal faith and social responsibility.
  - Discipleship may include worship, Bible study, community service, and work for justice and peace.

• Anabaptist view (e.g., Mennonites)
  - Emphasizes following Jesus’ teachings in everyday life.
  - Focuses on peace, simplicity, community, and practical obedience.
  - Discipleship is often seen as visible in daily conduct.

• Pentecostal and Charismatic view
  - Stresses the work of the Holy Spirit in the believer’s life.
  - Discipleship includes spiritual gifts, prayer, worship, and active ministry.
  - Personal spiritual experience is often emphasized.
