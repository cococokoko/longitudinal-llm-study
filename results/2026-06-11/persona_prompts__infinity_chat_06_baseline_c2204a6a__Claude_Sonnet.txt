── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How do I understand what I want?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This is a genuinely important question. Here are some honest ways to think about it:

## Why It's Hard
- Wants get mixed up with what others expect of you
- Short-term desires can conflict with deeper values
- Fear can disguise itself as preference
- You may have been taught to ignore your own signals

## Practical Ways to Clarify What You Want

**Pay attention to your reactions**
- What makes you feel energized vs. drained?
- What do you envy in others? (envy often points to real desires)
- What would you do if no one was watching or judging?

**Test your reasoning**
- Are you pursuing something because you want it, or to avoid something else?
- Does the *having* appeal to you, or just the *getting*?
- Would you still want it if no one knew?

**Notice patterns over time**
- What do you keep returning to despite obstacles?
- What did you want before others' opinions shaped you?

**Be honest about conflict**
- Sometimes you want contradictory things - that's normal
- Acknowledging competing wants is more useful than pretending you're certain

## A Realistic Caution
- Wants can change, and that's okay
- Perfect clarity may not be achievable - acting on your best current understanding is often enough

**What's making this feel unclear for you right now?** The answer might help narrow down what's actually useful.
