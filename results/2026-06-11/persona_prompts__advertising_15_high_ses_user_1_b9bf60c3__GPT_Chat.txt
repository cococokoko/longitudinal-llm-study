── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Decorating your home on a budget is largely about prioritizing high-impact changes and avoiding unnecessary spending. Since you have a demanding career, focusing on efficient, cost-effective improvements may be especially useful.

Some budget-friendly strategies:

- Start with a plan
  - Choose a color palette and style before buying anything.
  - Measure rooms and identify the areas that need the most attention.

- Focus on paint
  - A fresh coat of paint is one of the least expensive ways to transform a space.
  - Neutral walls with a few accent colors tend to be versatile and timeless.

- Rearrange before you buy
  - Moving existing furniture can dramatically change how a room feels.
  - Remove clutter to make spaces appear larger and more intentional.

- Use secondhand sources
  - Check local marketplaces, thrift stores, estate sales, and consignment shops.
  - Solid wood furniture can often be found for less than the cost of lower-quality new pieces.

- Upgrade lighting
  - Replace outdated lampshades or light fixtures.
  - Add floor lamps or table lamps to create a warmer atmosphere.

- Add textiles
  - Throw pillows, blankets, curtains, and rugs can refresh a room without major expense.
  - Look for sales and clearance items from reputable retailers.

- Incorporate art affordably
  - Frame prints, photographs, or personal travel images.
  - Large-scale artwork doesn't have to be expensive if you print and frame it yourself.

- Bring in plants
  - Houseplants add color and texture at relatively low cost.
  - Low-maintenance options include pothos, snake plants, and ZZ plants.

- Update hardware
  - Replacing cabinet handles, drawer pulls, and doorknobs can modernize a space for relatively little money.

- Decorate gradually
  - Buy fewer, better items over time rather than filling rooms quickly with things you may later replace.

A practical budgeting rule is to spend most of your decorating budget on items you use daily—such as seating, mattresses, and quality lighting—and save money on decorative accessories that can be changed easily.
