How to say a formal thank you for your time in the subject line of an email?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here are a few professional and formal ways to say "thank you for your time" in an email subject line, depending on the context of your email:

### For a Job Interview or Professional Meeting
If you are following up after an interview or a formal meeting, keep it concise and polite:
*   **Thank You for Your Time – [Your Name]**
*   **Thank You – [Your Name] for [Job Title] Interview**
*   **Appreciation for Your Time today / yesterday**
*   **With Sincere Thanks – [Your Name]**

### For a General Inquiry or Requesting Help
If you are thanking someone who took the time to answer questions, give advice, or help you with something:
*   **Thank You for Your Time and Assistance**
*   **Sincere Thanks for Your Time and Guidance**
*   **Thank You for Your Time and Consideration**
*   **In Appreciation of Your Time – [Your Name]**

### For a Business Follow-Up
If you are following up on a previous discussion or proposal:
*   **Thank You for Your Time: Follow-Up on [Topic]**
*   **Appreciated Our Conversation Today – [Your Name]**
*   **Thank You for Your Time regarding [Project/Topic]**

### Quick Tips for Subject Lines:
*   **Keep it short:** Subject lines can get cut off on mobile screens, so try to keep it under 60 characters.
*   **Include your name:** Adding your name (e.g., *Thank You for Your Time - Jane Doe*) helps the recipient immediately identify who the email is from, especially if they have a busy inbox.
