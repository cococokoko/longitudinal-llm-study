── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Give me a tip to be more organized at work. I'm a high school teacher.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
As a high school teacher, your day is a whirlwind of grading, lesson planning, parent emails, and managing 100+ teenagers. The standard advice of "keep a clean desk" doesn't work for you because your desk is covered in papers within five minutes of first period.

Here is one highly actionable, game-changing tip specifically tailored for high school teachers:

### **Implement the "Monday-to-Friday" Drawer/Bin System**

Stop trying to organize your materials by *subject* or *class period* on your desk. Instead, organize them by **the day of the week you will actually use them.**

#### **How to set it up:**
1. **Get the gear:** Buy a 5-drawer plastic rolling cart (like the Sterilite ones) or 5 stackable plastic letter trays. 
2. **Label them:** Label the drawers **Monday, Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday, and Friday**.
3. **The Rule:** *Nothing* stays on your desk. If a paper, worksheet, quiz, or lesson plan is meant for a specific day, it goes directly into that day's drawer.

#### **How this saves your sanity:**
* **No more "Where is that worksheet?":** When 3rd period starts on Wednesday, you don't search your desk or your computer. You simply open the "Wednesday" drawer. Everything you need for that day—copies, answer keys, prep notes, and exit tickets—is right there.
* **Batch-copying made easy:** When you go to the copier on Friday afternoon for the upcoming week, you don't leave the copies in a giant, chaotic stack. You walk back to your room and distribute them immediately into the Monday through Friday drawers. 
* **Sub-ready in seconds:** If you wake up sick on Tuesday morning, you can text a colleague or your department chair and say, *"My sub plans and all copies are in the Tuesday drawer on my cart."* You don't have to worry about explaining where your files are.

**Bonus Tip for High School:** If you teach multiple preps (e.g., Algebra 1 and AP Calculus), use **color-coded folders** inside each daily drawer. (e.g., Blue folders for Algebra, Red folders for Calculus). When you open the "Thursday" drawer, you grab the blue folder for your morning classes and the red folder for your afternoon classes.
