── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How do I start a vegetable garden in my backyard?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Starting a backyard vegetable garden can be simple if you begin small and focus on a few easy crops.

1. Choose a good location
- Pick a spot that gets at least 6–8 hours of direct sunlight daily.
- Make sure it's close to a water source.
- Avoid areas where water pools after rain.

2. Decide on garden type
- In-ground garden: Uses existing soil and is usually the least expensive.
- Raised bed: Easier to manage, improves drainage, and can provide better soil conditions.
- Containers: Great if space is limited or soil quality is poor.

3. Prepare the soil
- Remove weeds and grass.
- Mix in compost or well-rotted organic matter to improve fertility and drainage.
- If possible, get a soil test to check pH and nutrient levels.

4. Start with easy vegetables
Good beginner choices include:
- Lettuce
- Radishes
- Green beans
- Tomatoes
- Zucchini
- Cucumbers
- Peppers
- Herbs such as basil, parsley, and chives

5. Plan your layout
- Follow spacing recommendations on seed packets or plant labels.
- Put taller plants on the north side (in the Northern Hemisphere) so they don't shade shorter plants.
- Leave room to walk between rows or beds.

6. Plant at the right time
- Check your local last frost date.
- Cool-season crops (lettuce, peas, spinach) can often be planted earlier.
- Warm-season crops (tomatoes, peppers, cucumbers) should be planted after frost danger has passed.

7. Water consistently
- Most vegetables need about 1 inch (2.5 cm) of water per week, more during hot weather.
- Water deeply rather than lightly and frequently.
- Water near the soil level to reduce disease risk.

8. Mulch and maintain
- Apply mulch (straw, shredded leaves, or wood chips around plants) to retain moisture and suppress weeds.
- Check regularly for pests and diseases.
- Pull weeds while they are small.

9. Harvest regularly
- Many vegetables produce more when picked frequently.
- Harvest at peak ripeness for the best flavor.

For a first garden, a raised bed about 4 ft × 8 ft (1.2 m × 2.4 m) planted with a mix of tomatoes, peppers, lettuce, beans, and herbs is a manageable and productive size.
