Since you are communicating in a professional, executive-level context, you want to strike the right balance between warmth, confidence, and professionalism. 

Here are a few ways to say "fingers crossed," categorized by the specific tone you want to strike:

### 1. Polished & Executive (Best for clients, board members, or external partners)
These options sound sophisticated and forward-looking, replacing "luck" with "anticipation of success."
* **"I look forward to a successful outcome."** (Strong, confident, and professional)
* **"Wishing the team a smooth and successful execution."** (Great for a project launch or major milestone)
* **"I’m confident we’ll see a great result."** (Projects executive presence and reassurance)
* **"Wishing you all the best for a seamless rollout."**

### 2. Warm & Collaborative (Best for your direct reports or close peers)
These maintain a supportive, encouraging tone without sounding overly casual.
* **"Hoping for a smooth run today."**
* **"Wishing us a seamless execution on this."**
* **"I have my sights set on a great outcome."**
* **"Sending positive thoughts for a successful launch."**

### 3. Short & Action-Oriented (Best for Slack, Teams, or quick emails)
If you want to keep it brief but professional:
* **"Wishing you a smooth delivery."**
* **"Hoping for the best possible outcome."**
* **"All the best for a successful run."**
