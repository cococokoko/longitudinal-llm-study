**TITLE: THE END OF THE WORLD (AND OTHER BUDGET VARIANCES)**

**CHARACTERS:**

*   **DILBERT:** A systems engineer, now a "Survival Systems Engineer." He wears a tattered dress shirt, a tie that permanently curves upward, and a tactical chest rig filled with pencils.
*   **WALLY:** A senior engineer. He has modified his coffee mug to hold recycled gray-water. He has not done any work since the asteroid hit.
*   **POINTY-HAIRED BOSS (PHB):** The Sector Overlord. He wears a crown made of rusted gears and a suit made of Kevlar.
*   **ALICE:** A fierce engineer who has modified her fist with a pneumatic piston. 

---

**[SCENE START]**

**INT. THE CUBICLE WASTELAND - DAY**

The office is a ruin. Half the ceiling is gone, revealing a toxic purple sky. A sign on the wall reads: "IT HAS BEEN [ 0 ] DAYS SINCE THE LAST MUTANT ATTACK." 

DILBERT sits at his desk, typing on a laptop powered by a hand-crank generator. He is wearing a gas mask around his neck. 

WALLY sits in the adjacent cubicle, sipping from his mug.

**DILBERT**
I’ve analyzed our water purification system. If we optimize the charcoal filters, we can increase our drinkable yield by twelve percent. This will allow us to support my cousin’s family, my wife’s parents, and the three stray goats we’ve adopted for milk.

**WALLY**
If you optimize the filters, the Boss will realize we have a surplus. He’ll allocate the extra water to the marketing department to wash their dune buggies.

**DILBERT**
But the marketing department was eaten by radioactive sand worms last Tuesday.

**WALLY**
Exactly. And yet, their budget remains untouched. I’m hoping to transfer my soul into their payroll database before the next fiscal quarter.

ALICE stomps into the aisle. Her pneumatic fist is smoking. She drops a heavy, glowing metal cylinder onto Dilbert’s desk.

**ALICE**
I just fought off a horde of scavengers to retrieve this plutonium core. It can power our life-support systems for a decade. 

**DILBERT**
Great. Did you fill out the hazardous materials acquisition form?

**ALICE**
The printer was destroyed by a mortar shell.

**DILBERT**
Then the system will flag this as an unauthorized donation. We’ll be taxed on the radiation.

**ALICE**
(Clenching her pneumatic fist)
If the tax auditor comes near me, I will introduce him to my new upgrade. It’s called "The Out-of-Network Provider."

The POINTY-HAIRED BOSS (PHB) walks down the aisle. He is holding a tablet that is literally on fire.

**PHB**
Good morning, team. As you know, the sky is raining acid and the currency has been replaced by bottle caps and human teeth. 

**DILBERT**
Yes, we’ve been managing. I’ve successfully budgeted our tooth-reserves to cover my mother-in-law’s insulin and the kids' radiation-scrubbing soap.

**PHB**
Excellent. Because of the economic downturn, we’re doing some restructuring. We need to increase synergy.

**DILBERT**
Synergy? The air is forty percent sulfur.

**PHB**
I’ve decided to merge our department with the mutant cannibal tribe in Sector 4. 

**ALICE**
They literally tried to eat my leg yesterday!

**PHB**
Yes, but they have excellent interpersonal skills and a very flat organizational structure. I’ve scheduled a team-building exercise. We’re going to do a trust fall into their spike pit.

**WALLY**
Will there be donuts?

**PHB**
Only if you forage them from the ruins of the old grocery store. Watch out for the feral laser-dogs.

**DILBERT**
Boss, this is highly inefficient. If we focus on securing our perimeter and managing our household resources, we can survive the winter. If we merge with the cannibals, we will be digested.

**PHB**
Dilbert, you’re not being a team player. The cannibals have agreed to adopt our Key Performance Indicators. 

**DILBERT**
How does a cannibal track a KPI?

**PHB**
Mostly through skull-counting. It’s very visual. I’ve created a PowerPoint presentation about it.

The PHB points his tablet at the wall. It projects a slide of a pie chart. One slice is labeled "Us," the other slice is labeled "Food."

**PHB**
As you can see, our growth potential is enormous.

**WALLY**
(Taking a sip from his mug)
I’m on board. The cannibals don’t require weekly status reports. They just sniff your neck to see if you’re still alive. It’s much less invasive than our current system.

**DILBERT**
Wally, they will eat you!

**WALLY**
Yes, but the transition period will involve a lot of paid administrative leave.

**ALICE**
(To PHB)
I’m going to go to the server room and scream into the intake fan.

**PHB**
Make sure to log those hours as "Professional Development."

Alice stomps off, her fist whirring angrily.

**DILBERT**
(Sighs, looking at his laptop)
I have to go. I need to barter three rusted gears and a copy of my resume for some baby formula. 

**PHB**
Before you go, Dilbert, I need you to update your self-evaluation. Under "Areas of Improvement," write down "More resistant to radiation."

**DILBERT**
I’ll put it right under "Ability to tolerate management."

**PHB**
Great. Let’s circle back after the apocalypse.

The PHB walks away. 

Dilbert looks at Wally. Wally is asleep, his head resting on his desk, his mug still held perfectly upright.

Dilbert sighs, pulls his gas mask over his face, and begins cranking his laptop generator again.
