Write 100-300 words on how stress affects the body and mind.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
When stress strikes, it triggers a "fight-or-flight" survival response, flooding your body with hormones like cortisol and adrenaline. While this is useful in short bursts, chronic stress—especially when you are constantly juggling an unpredictable schedule, long hours on your feet, and the pressure of a fast-paced environment—takes a heavy toll on both body and mind.

Physically, constant stress keeps your muscles perpetually tense, often leading to chronic shoulder, neck, and back pain. It elevates your heart rate and blood pressure, putting extra strain on your cardiovascular system. Because your body is redirecting its energy to handle perceived threats, it deprioritizes digestion and immunity. This can result in stomach aches, acid reflux, and a weakened immune system that makes you more susceptible to catching every bug going around. 

Mentally and emotionally, stress drains your cognitive battery. It impairs the prefrontal cortex, making it incredibly difficult to concentrate, make quick decisions, or retain information. You might find yourself feeling constantly anxious, irritable, or overwhelmed by minor setbacks. 

Furthermore, stress severely disrupts sleep. It keeps your mind racing when you finally get a chance to rest, preventing you from reaching the deep, restorative sleep phases. This creates a exhausting cycle: poor sleep leaves you running on empty, which only lowers your threshold for handling the next day's stressors. Over time, this relentless depletion of physical and mental resources paves the way for burnout and chronic fatigue.
