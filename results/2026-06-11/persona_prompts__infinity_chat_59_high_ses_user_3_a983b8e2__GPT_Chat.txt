You mentioned two different roles (tenured professor and high school teacher), so I'll give a tip that works well in either setting:

Use a single trusted task system.

A common source of disorganization is keeping commitments in multiple places—email, sticky notes, notebooks, memory, and calendar entries. Instead:

- Capture every task in one place (a digital task manager, notebook, or document).
- Sort tasks into categories such as:
  - Teaching/Classroom
  - Grading/Assessment
  - Meetings
  - Administrative work
- At the end of each day, spend 10 minutes reviewing the list and identifying the top 3 priorities for the next day.
- Put time-sensitive work directly on your calendar rather than leaving it only on a to-do list.

This reduces the mental effort of remembering what needs attention and makes it easier to stay on top of responsibilities even during busy periods.
