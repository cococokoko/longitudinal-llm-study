# Effective Ways to Reduce Household Energy Use

## Heating & Cooling (typically largest expense)
- **Set thermostats strategically** – lower in winter at night/when away, higher in summer
- **Seal air leaks** around windows, doors, and outlets
- **Add insulation** to attic, walls, and basement
- **Use ceiling fans** to circulate air efficiently
- **Replace HVAC filters** regularly (every 1-3 months)
- **Get annual HVAC maintenance**

## Appliances & Electronics
- **Unplug devices** or use smart power strips to eliminate standby power
- **Run dishwashers and washing machines** with full loads
- **Wash clothes in cold water**
- **Choose ENERGY STAR appliances** when replacing old ones
- **Use microwave or air fryer** instead of conventional oven when possible

## Lighting
- **Switch to LED bulbs** (use ~75% less energy than incandescent)
- **Use natural light** when possible
- **Install motion sensors or timers**

## Water Heating
- **Lower water heater to 120°F**
- **Fix leaky faucets**
- **Take shorter showers**
- **Insulate hot water pipes**

## Smart Habits
- **Use a programmable/smart thermostat**
- **Monitor usage** with a home energy monitor
- **Air-dry dishes and laundry** when possible

## Quick Wins vs. Long-Term Investments
| Quick & Free | Worth Investing In |
|---|---|
| Adjust thermostat | Smart thermostat |
| Unplug devices | New insulation |
| Cold water washing | LED lighting |
| Full loads only | ENERGY STAR appliances |
