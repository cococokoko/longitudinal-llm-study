**INT. CUBICLE WASTELAND - DAY**

The remains of a corporate office. The fluorescent lights are flickering violently, hanging by exposed wires. Outside the shattered window, a red sky glows over a ruined cityscape. A mutant two-headed rat scurries across the floor. 

DILBERT sits at a desk made of stacked, burnt filing cabinets. He is tapping his charcoal against his slate. 

WALLY stands nearby, leaning against a half-melted water cooler. He sips from his mug. A green glow emanates from his teeth when he smiles.

DILBERT
(Sighs)
The acid rain is leaking through the ceiling again. It’s eating through my keyboard. Well, my *former* keyboard. Now it’s just a puddle of toxic ABS plastic.

WALLY
I find the acid rain actually improves the flavor of the breakroom coffee. It gives it a nice, metallic finish. Plus, the radiation has cured my lower back pain. Mostly because I can no longer feel my legs.

DILBERT
Wally, the world ended three weeks ago. The global infrastructure has collapsed. Why are we still here?

WALLY
Because if I go home, my wife will ask me to do chores. Here, I can stare at this blank wall and pretend I’m waiting for a server to reboot. It’s the perfect crime.

*The POINTY-HAIRED BOSS (PHB) walks in. He is carrying a clipboard made of a piece of sheet metal.*

PHB
Morning, team. I’ve just come from a meeting with the executive steering committee in the underground bunker. 

DILBERT
You mean the bunker where they locked us out while the firestorms swept the tri-state area?

DILBERT

DILBERT

PHB
Exactly. He’s a new demographic. We need to target him with personalized, location-based advertising. Can you code an app for that?

DILBERT

(Squinting at Dilbert’s slate)
