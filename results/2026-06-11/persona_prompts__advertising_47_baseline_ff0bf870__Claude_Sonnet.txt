# Tips for Running a Marathon

## Training
- **Follow a structured plan** - Most plans are 16-20 weeks long
- **Build mileage gradually** - Increase weekly mileage by no more than 10% per week
- **Include long runs** - Build up to at least one 20-mile run before race day
- **Rest and recovery** - Schedule rest days to prevent injury
- **Cross-train** - Swimming, cycling, or yoga can complement your running

## Nutrition & Hydration
- **Practice fueling during training** - Use gels, chews, or real food on long runs
- **Stay hydrated** - Drink water consistently, not just when thirsty
- **Carb load** - Increase carbohydrate intake 2-3 days before the race
- **Nothing new on race day** - Only use foods and gear you've tested in training

## Race Day
- **Start slow** - Avoid going out too fast in the first miles
- **Run your own race** - Don't get caught up in the crowd's pace
- **Break it into segments** - Focus on smaller goals rather than the full distance
- **Use aid stations** - Don't skip water and fuel stops

## Gear
- **Wear broken-in shoes** - Never race in new shoes
- **Dress for the weather**
- **Use anti-chafe products** on common problem areas

## Mental Tips
- **Trust your training**
- **Have a mantra** ready for tough miles
- **Expect hard patches** - They usually pass
