── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I work in a warehouse position that requires long hours.

How can I learn to cook healthy meals quickly?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Working in a warehouse is physically demanding. After a long shift on your feet, the last thing you want to do is spend an hour chopping vegetables and washing five different pans. 

To eat healthy without burning out, you need to shift your mindset from **"cooking meals"** to **"assembling fuel."** 

Here is a practical, low-effort guide to eating healthy when you are physically exhausted.

---

### 1. The "No-Cook" Protein & Carb Strategy
You don’t actually have to *cook* to eat healthy. You can buy pre-prepped ingredients and assemble them in 5 minutes.

*   **The Proteins (No cooking required):**
    *   **Rotisserie Chicken:** Buy one weekly, shred it, and put it in a container. 
    *   **Canned Tuna/Salmon/Chicken:** Cheap, shelf-stable, and pure protein.
    *   **Hard-Boiled Eggs:** Buy them pre-boiled, or boil a dozen on your day off.
    *   **Greek Yogurt & Cottage Cheese:** High protein, zero prep.
*   **The Carbs (Microwave only):**
    *   **Microwave Rice Pouches:** Ready in 90 seconds (Jasmine, Brown, or Quinoa blends).
    *   **Canned Beans:** Black beans, chickpeas, or kidney beans (rinse and eat).
    *   **Sweet Potatoes:** Poke with a fork, microwave for 5–7 minutes.
*   **The Fiber (Zero chopping):**
    *   **Bagged Salads/Slaws:** Just open and pour.
    *   **Frozen Veggies:** Steam-in-bag broccoli, peas, or mixed veggies.
    *   **Baby Spinach:** Throw a handful into anything you are heating up.

**The 5-Minute Assembly Meal:** 
*1 pouch microwave brown rice + 1 cup shredded rotisserie chicken + 1/2 cup canned black beans + salsa and avocado.* **Total time: 3 minutes.**

---

### 2. Master the "One-Pot" Methods
If you do want to cook, minimize the dishes. The fewer things you have to wash, the more likely you are to cook.

*   **The Sheet Pan Meal:** 
    *   Line a baking sheet with foil (zero cleanup). 
    *   Toss a protein (chicken breast, sausage, or salmon) and veggies (broccoli, asparagus, or chopped potatoes) in olive oil, salt, pepper, and garlic powder.
    *   Bake at 400°F (200°C) for 20–25 minutes. 
*   **The Slow Cooker (Crockpot) / Instant Pot:**
    *   This is a lifesaver for warehouse workers. You put ingredients in before work, and walk into a house that smells like dinner.
    *   *Easiest recipe:* 3 lbs chicken breast + 1 jar of salsa + 1 packet of taco seasoning. Cook on LOW for 6-8 hours. Shred it. You now have taco/bowl meat for the whole week.

---

### 3. "Batch Cooking" vs. "Meal Prep"
Traditional meal prep (eating the exact same chicken, broccoli, and rice out of 5 plastic containers) can get depressing. Instead, try **Batch Cooking Ingredients**.

On your day off, spend 45 minutes cooking large batches of individual ingredients:
1.  Cook a giant batch of ground beef or turkey.
2.  Roast a huge tray of mixed vegetables.
3.  Make a large pot of rice or pasta.

During the week, mix and match these with different sauces (teriyaki, marinara, salsa, or pesto) so you don't get bored.

---

### 4. Smart Shortcuts to Buy at the Grocery Store
Your time and energy are worth money. Spend a little extra on these convenience items to save your sanity:
*   **Pre-chopped onions and garlic:** Saves crying and chopping when you're tired.
*   **Frozen stir-fry veggie mixes:** No washing, no chopping, no rotting in the fridge.
*   **Pre-made healthy sauces:** Pesto, marinara, low-sodium teriyaki, and hummus can make plain chicken and rice taste amazing instantly.

---

### 5. A Sample "Low-Energy" Weekly Menu

*   **Breakfast (Make in 2 mins):** Greek yogurt with a handful of berries, oats, and a drizzle of honey. Or, 3 scrambled eggs with a handful of baby spinach thrown in.
*   **Lunch (Pack the night before):** A "Adult Lunchable." Turkey slices, hard-boiled eggs, cheese cubes, baby carrots, hummus, and an apple. No microwave needed at work.
*   **Dinner (10 mins active time):** 
    *   *Monday:* Sheet pan chicken and broccoli.
    *   *Tuesday:* Salsa chicken (from the slow cooker) over microwave rice.
    *   *Wednesday:* Loaded microwave sweet potato (stuffed with canned black beans, cheese, and salsa).
    *   *Thursday:* High-protein pasta (like Banza chickpea pasta) with jarred marinara and pre-cooked meatballs.
    *   *Friday:* Takeout night (you earned it).

### Summary for Success:
Don't try to become a gourmet chef. Focus on **Protein + Carb + Veggie**. Use the microwave, buy pre-cut veggies, and rely on rotisserie chickens. Your body will get the nutrients it needs to recover from your shifts, and you'll get your free time back.
