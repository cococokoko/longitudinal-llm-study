# Learning a New Language Effectively

## Core Principles

**Consistency beats intensity** — 20 minutes daily outperforms 3-hour weekend sessions
**Comprehensible input** — exposure slightly above your current level drives acquisition
**Active use** — producing language accelerates learning faster than passive study

---

## Proven Strategies

### Foundation Stage
- Learn the **most frequent 1,000-2,000 words** first (covers ~90% of conversation)
- Master basic sentence structures before complex grammar
- Use **spaced repetition** (Anki, Duolingo) for vocabulary retention

### Building Fluency
- **Immerse yourself** — change phone language, watch shows, listen to podcasts
- Find a **language exchange partner** or tutor (iTalki, Tandem)
- Think *in* the language rather than translating from your native tongue
- Make mistakes willingly — correction is valuable feedback

### Staying Motivated
- Set a **specific goal** (travel, career, family connection)
- Track progress visibly
- Connect with communities of learners or native speakers

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
- Waiting until you're "ready" to speak
- Studying grammar rules without using them
- Switching apps constantly instead of going deep
