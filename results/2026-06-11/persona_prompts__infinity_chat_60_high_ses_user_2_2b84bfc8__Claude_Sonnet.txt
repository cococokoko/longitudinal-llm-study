# Computational Irreducibility (For a 5-Year-Old)

Imagine you want to know **where a bouncy ball will be after 1,000 bounces**.

You have two choices:

- **Watch all 1,000 bounces** happen one by one
- **Find a shortcut** - a magic formula that just *tells* you the answer instantly

---

## The Key Idea

For **some things**, the shortcut exists.

> Like adding: instead of counting 100 + 100 one penny at a time, you just *know* it's 200.

But for **other things** - no shortcut exists. **Ever.**

The only way to know what happens at step 1,000 is to **actually go through all 1,000 steps**. You cannot skip ahead. The universe itself has to "do the work."

That's **computational irreducibility** - some processes cannot be shortcut. You have to watch them play out.

---

## Why This Matters Legally (Since You Asked)

This is actually profound for your work:

- It suggests some complex systems - **markets, human behavior, social consequences of laws** - may be genuinely **unpredictable in principle**, not just in practice
- It provides a theoretical foundation for why **unintended consequences** aren't always negligence
- No amount of expert analysis can always forecast outcomes

**The universe doesn't offer a summary judgment on everything.**
