── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
That's an interesting question. Here's an honest breakdown:

## What Research Suggests

**Some positive correlations found:**
- Higher cognitive ability is associated with greater empathy in some studies
- Intelligence may help people *understand* others' perspectives more effectively
- More educated people sometimes show broader moral concern

**But it's complicated:**
- The relationship is **weak and inconsistent** across studies
- High intelligence can actually be used to **rationalize selfish behavior** more sophisticatedly
- Emotional intelligence and cognitive intelligence are largely separate systems
- Some research shows highly intelligent people can be *more* capable of motivated reasoning that justifies unkind conclusions

## Honest Caveats
- Compassion is genuinely **multi-dimensional** (feeling, motivation, action)
- Intelligence measures are themselves debated
- Many brilliant people have been notably cruel
- Many people with average cognitive scores show remarkable compassion

## Practical Reality
Most researchers would say **character, upbringing, culture, and deliberate values** predict compassionate behavior more reliably than IQ does.
