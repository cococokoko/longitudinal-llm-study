── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Where can I find organic and sustainable clothing?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Finding organic and sustainable clothing has become much easier as the "slow fashion" movement has grown. To ensure you are actually buying sustainable clothing (and avoiding "greenwashing"), it helps to know **what certifications to look for** and **which brands are leading the industry**.

Here is a comprehensive guide on where to find organic and sustainable clothing, categorized by brand type, marketplaces, and what to look for.

---

### 1. Top Sustainable & Organic Clothing Brands
These brands are highly rated for their transparency, ethical labor practices, and use of organic materials.

#### **Everyday Basics & Casual Wear**
*   **Organic Basics:** (Denmark/Global) Specializes in high-quality organic cotton underwear, activewear, and tees. They use GOTS-certified organic cotton and recycled materials.
*   **Pact:** (USA) One of the most affordable and accessible organic brands. Everything they make is GOTS-certified organic cotton and Fair Trade Certified. Great for basics, bedding, and loungewear.
*   **Colorful Standard:** (Europe/Global) Known for colorful, high-quality basics made from 100% organic cotton and extra-fine merino wool. They use environmentally friendly dyes and zero-waste manufacturing.
*   **Kowtow:** (New Zealand/Global) Offers beautiful, structured, and modern designs made entirely from Fairtrade organic cotton.

#### **Outdoor & Activewear**
*   **Patagonia:** (Global) The pioneer of sustainable outdoor gear. They use organic cotton, recycled polyester, and offer a lifetime repair guarantee. They are also a certified B-Corp.
*   **Girlfriend Collective:** (USA/Global) Known for size-inclusive activewear made from recycled materials (like plastic water bottles and fishing nets) and eco-friendly dyes.

#### **Denim & Casual Chic**
*   **Nudie Jeans:** (Sweden/Global) A leader in sustainable denim. They use 100% organic cotton and offer **free repairs for life** at their repair shops or via free repair kits.
*   **MATE the Label:** (USA) Uses only organic cotton, linen, and non-toxic dyes to create cozy sweatsets, tees, and activewear.
*   **Amour Vert:** (USA) Focuses on classic, feminine styles using sustainable fabrics like organic cotton, washable silk, and TENCEL. They also plant a tree for every tee purchased.

### 2. Multi-Brand Sustainable Marketplaces
If you want to browse multiple brands in one place, these online marketplaces vet brands for their ethical and environmental standards:

*   **Our Commonplace:** A certified sustainable marketplace featuring clothing, beauty, and home goods. You can filter by values (e.g., toxic-free, woman-owned, cruelty-free).
*   **Made Trade:** A curated marketplace for exceptionally designed ethical and sustainable goods. They verify every brand for fair wages and sustainable materials.
*   **Earthhero:** Often called the "sustainable Amazon." They have a great selection of casual clothing, activewear, and accessories.
*   **Sustainably Chic:** While primarily a blog, this site has an extensive directory of vetted sustainable brands categorized by clothing type.

---

### 3. Secondhand & Circular Fashion (The Most Sustainable Option)
The most sustainable garment is the one that already exists. You can find high-quality organic and sustainable brands secondhand at a fraction of the price:

*   **Noihsaf Bazaar:** An independent resale platform specifically for independent, ethical, and slow-fashion brands.
*   **Poshmark & Depop:** Search for specific sustainable brands (like "Pact organic cotton" or "Patagonia") to find great deals.
*   **The RealReal:** Excellent for high-end, luxury sustainable designers (like Stella McCartney or Eileen Fisher).
*   **Worn Wear (Patagonia) / Re-Spun (Marine Layer):** Many sustainable brands now have their own trade-in and resale programs on their websites.

---

### 4. What to Look For (How to Avoid Greenwashing)
When shopping, look for these third-party certifications on clothing tags or website "About" pages to ensure the brand is truly sustainable:

*   **GOTS (Global Organic Textile Standard):** The gold standard for organic fibers. It ensures the cotton was grown without toxic pesticides and that the manufacturing process was socially responsible.
*   **OEKO-TEX Standard 100:** Guarantees that the finished fabric is free from harmful levels of toxic chemicals and heavy metals.
*   **Fair Trade Certified:** Ensures that the farmers and factory workers who made the clothing were paid fair wages and worked in safe conditions.
*   **Certified B-Corporation (B-Corp):** A certification for businesses that meet the highest standards of verified social and environmental performance, public transparency, and legal accountability.
*   **Bluesign:** Indicates that the textiles were manufactured with the lowest possible impact on people and the environment, particularly regarding chemical safety.

### A Quick Tool to Help You Shop:
Download the **"Good On You"** app or use their website. It is a directory that rates thousands of fashion brands on their treatment of people, the planet, and animals, giving them a score from "We Avoid" to "Great."
