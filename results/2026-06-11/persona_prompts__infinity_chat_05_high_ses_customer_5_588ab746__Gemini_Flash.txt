### The Feature: **Guardian Circle**
Instead of a reactive device that only triggers *after* an emergency (like a standard SOS button), **Guardian Circle** is a proactive, AI-driven ecosystem that monitors micro-behaviors to predict risks, detect anomalies, and seamlessly loop in a designated care circle without stigmatizing the user.

#### **How It Works (The Tech Stack & UX):**

1. **Predictive Gait & Balance Analysis (The Prevention):**
   * **The Tech:** Utilizing the watch’s high-frequency accelerometer and gyroscope, the device continuously analyzes the wearer’s walking patterns (gait, stride length, and tempo). 
   * **The Value:** If the algorithm detects a gradual decline in stability over a 7-day period (indicating a high risk of an impending fall), it sends a gentle, private prompt to the senior suggesting simple balance exercises, while sending a low-priority notification to their family app: *"Dad’s stability index is down 12% this week. Consider checking if his footwear is worn out or scheduling a routine physical."*

2. **Ambient Fall Detection with "Zero-Friction" Verification:**
   * **The Tech:** Standard fall detection often triggers false alarms, causing anxiety. Guardian Circle uses barometric pressure sensors (to detect rapid altitude changes) combined with heart rate spikes to verify a true fall.
   * **The UX:** If a fall is detected, the watch doesn't just beep loudly. It uses a haptic "heartbeat" vibration and asks in a calm, clear voice: *"I felt a bump. Are you okay?"* 
   * **The Escalation:** If there is no response within 15 seconds, the watch automatically activates its LTE speakerphone, dials the primary contact, and broadcasts the senior's GPS location and real-time heart rate.

3. **"Passive Check-In" (Replacing the "Are you alive?" text):**
   * **The Tech:** One of the biggest pain points for seniors is the constant, hovering check-ins from worried adult children. Guardian Circle solves this passively.
   * **The UX:** The family smartphone app features a simple, ambient dashboard. It doesn't track the senior's exact GPS location 24/7 (preserving dignity and privacy). Instead, it shows a simple status indicator: **"Active & Safe."** 
   * It compiles passive data: *Device is on wrist, battery is at 60%, steps are normal for a Tuesday morning, and morning medication routine was logged.* No intrusive phone calls required.
