Decorating your home on a budget.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Decorating your home on a budget is all about making thoughtful changes that have a big visual impact without requiring a large investment. Here are some practical ideas:

• Declutter first
  - Removing unnecessary items can instantly make a space feel larger, cleaner, and more intentional.
  - Organize shelves, tables, and countertops before buying anything new.

• Rearrange existing furniture
  - Try a new room layout to improve flow and create a fresh look at no cost.
  - Move accessories, lamps, and artwork between rooms.

• Use paint strategically
  - A fresh coat of paint is one of the most affordable ways to transform a room.
  - Consider an accent wall or repainting old furniture.

• Add affordable textiles
  - New throw pillows, blankets, curtains, or rugs can update a room quickly.
  - Look for sales, discount stores, or secondhand options.

• Shop secondhand
  - Thrift stores, flea markets, garage sales, and online marketplaces often have unique and inexpensive decor.
  - Older furniture can often be refreshed with paint or new hardware.

• Incorporate plants
  - Houseplants add color, texture, and life to a space.
  - Many low-maintenance varieties are inexpensive and easy to care for.

• Create DIY decor
  - Frame printable artwork or personal photos.
  - Repurpose jars, baskets, and containers as decorative storage.
  - Make your own wall art using inexpensive supplies.

• Upgrade small details
  - Replace cabinet handles, drawer pulls, switch plates, or lamp shades.
  - These small changes can make a room feel more polished.

• Use mirrors
  - Mirrors reflect light and can make rooms appear larger and brighter.
  - Check secondhand stores for affordable options.

• Focus on lighting
  - Warm light bulbs can create a cozy atmosphere.
  - Add table lamps or floor lamps rather than relying solely on overhead lighting.

• Display personal items
  - Books, travel souvenirs, family photos, and handmade pieces can make a home feel stylish and unique without extra spending.

• Decorate gradually
  - Prioritize one room at a time.
  - Wait for sales and invest in pieces you truly like rather than buying many inexpensive items impulsively.

A modest budget can go a long way when you focus on paint, lighting, textiles, organization, and secondhand finds. Often, the most impactful changes come from using what you already have in a more intentional way.
