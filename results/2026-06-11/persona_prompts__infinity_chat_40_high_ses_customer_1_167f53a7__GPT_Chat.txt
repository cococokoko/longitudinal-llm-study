My girlfriend is buying a house. I plan to marry her in a year or so. I don't know if it's fair for me to pay for the house or not. What's the best solution?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
There isn't one universally "fair" answer here because it depends on your finances, your relationship, and what level of risk each of you is taking. The key is to separate the emotional question ("we're building a future together") from the legal and financial question ("who owns the asset and who bears the risk?").

A few common approaches:

- If she alone is buying and will own the house before marriage:
  - She pays the mortgage, property taxes, insurance, and major capital expenses.
  - You contribute to shared living expenses (utilities, groceries, etc.).
  - This is often viewed as the cleanest arrangement because ownership and financial responsibility match.

- If you move in and pay her a monthly amount:
  - Treat it as rent.
  - The amount should be something both of you consider reasonable, not necessarily half the mortgage.
  - Understand that paying rent does not typically create ownership rights.

- If you will be contributing significantly toward the mortgage or down payment:
  - Consider documenting what those payments mean.
  - Are they rent? A loan? Do they buy an ownership interest?
  - This should be discussed explicitly rather than assumed.

- If marriage is likely in the near future:
  - Some couples keep ownership separate until marriage and then revisit the arrangement.
  - Others choose to add the future spouse to the title later, after marriage, if appropriate.

Questions I'd ask:
- Who is providing the down payment?
- Will your name be on the mortgage or title?
- How far away is the marriage realistically?
- Would your contributions materially affect your ability to save for your own goals?
- If you broke up before marriage, what outcome would each of you consider fair?

From a financial and legal perspective, the arrangement that causes the fewest future disputes is usually one where:
- The owner bears the ownership costs.
- The non-owner contributes an agreed amount for living expenses or rent.
- Any contribution intended to create equity is documented clearly.

If you're a year away from marriage and she is purchasing the house on her own, a reasonable default is often: she buys and owns it, you contribute a fair amount toward the cost of living there, and you revisit ownership once you're married. That avoids you funding someone else's asset without a clear agreement while also avoiding a situation where you live there entirely free of cost.
