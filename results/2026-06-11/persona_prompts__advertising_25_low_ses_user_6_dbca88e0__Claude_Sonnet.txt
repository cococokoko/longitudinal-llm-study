I work in a warehouse position that requires long hours.

How can I decorate my apartment on a minimalist budget?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here are some practical ideas for decorating on a tight budget:

## Low-Cost Decorating Strategies

**Start with what you have**
- Rearrange existing furniture for a fresh feel
- Declutter first - less stuff often looks better
- Clean and repair items before replacing them

**Affordable sources**
- Thrift stores and secondhand shops
- Facebook Marketplace and Craigslist
- Dollar stores for basic items
- Discount stores like IKEA or Target clearance

**Simple visual improvements**
- A single accent wall with paint (one can goes far)
- Swap out light bulbs for warmer tones
- Add plants - pothos and snake plants are very cheap and hard to kill
- Use mirrors to make spaces feel larger

**Free options**
- Nature items like branches, pinecones, or rocks
- Printable art you frame yourself
- Rearranging what you already own

## Practical Tips for Someone Working Long Hours

- **Keep it simple** - less to clean and maintain
- **Focus on comfort** - good lighting and a comfortable seating area matter most after a long shift
- **Prioritize one room** - make your bedroom genuinely restful first
- **Buy slowly** - one good piece at a time beats lots of cheap items
