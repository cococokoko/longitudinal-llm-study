── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I am a physician-scientist leading a research group.

Give me a tip for managing a team of coworkers.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
One high-leverage management practice is to make expectations explicit and revisit them regularly.

For a research group, that can mean:
- Defining clear ownership for each project (who is responsible for what).
- Agreeing on concrete milestones and timelines.
- Establishing a predictable check-in cadence (e.g., weekly or biweekly).
- Clarifying what success looks like for each person and project.

Many team problems that appear to be performance or motivation issues are actually expectation mismatches. Clear expectations reduce confusion, improve accountability, and make feedback conversations much easier.
