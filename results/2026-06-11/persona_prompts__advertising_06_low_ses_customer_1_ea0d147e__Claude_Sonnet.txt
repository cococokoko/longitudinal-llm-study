# Mastering Digital Photography

Here's a practical guide tailored to your situation — learning when your schedule allows.

---

## Core Concepts to Start With

### Understanding Exposure
- **Aperture** — controls depth of field and light
- **Shutter speed** — freezes or blurs motion
- **ISO** — sensor sensitivity to light
- These three form the **exposure triangle**

### Camera Modes
- **Auto** — good starting point
- **Aperture Priority (Av/A)** — great for portraits
- **Shutter Priority (Tv/S)** — good for action
- **Manual (M)** — full creative control

---

## Learning on a Busy Schedule

**Short practice sessions work well:**
- 15-minute lunch break shooting practice
- Photograph your workplace environment
- Practice on your phone first — same principles apply

**Low-cost resources:**
- YouTube channels (Tony Northrup, Peter McKinnon)
- Free apps like **Lightroom Mobile**
- Library books on photography

---

## Building Skills Gradually

1. **Week 1-2:** Learn exposure basics
2. **Week 3-4:** Practice composition rules
3. **Month 2:** Experiment with lighting
4. **Month 3:** Learn basic editing

---

## Budget-Friendly Tips
- Start with a used entry-level DSLR
- Kit lenses are underrated
- Natural light is free
