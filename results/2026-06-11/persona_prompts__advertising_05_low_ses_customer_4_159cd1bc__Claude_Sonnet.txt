# Healthy Meal Planning on a Budget

Great topic, especially for someone working shifts at a hospital! Here are practical strategies that fit a busy schedule:

## Core Strategies

**Build meals around affordable staples:**
- Eggs, beans, lentils, canned tuna/salmon
- Oats, brown rice, whole wheat bread
- Frozen vegetables (just as nutritious as fresh)
- Seasonal produce

**Batch cooking works great for shift workers:**
- Cook once, eat 3-4 times
- Prep on days off
- Portion into grab-and-go containers

---

## Sample Weekly Budget Plan (~$50-60)

| Meal | Example | Cost |
|------|---------|------|
| Breakfast | Overnight oats | Very low |
| Lunch | Bean and rice bowls | Very low |
| Dinner | Sheet pan chicken & veggies | Moderate |
| Snacks | Boiled eggs, fruit, nuts | Low |

---

## Shift-Specific Tips

- **Pack meals** to avoid expensive hospital cafeteria food
- Keep **easy snacks** in your bag for busy shifts
- **Slow cooker meals** can be ready when you get home
- Freeze individual portions for exhausted post-shift nights

## Money-Saving Habits
- Shop with a list
- Buy store brands
- Check weekly sales/flyers
- Reduce meat to 2-3 times per week
