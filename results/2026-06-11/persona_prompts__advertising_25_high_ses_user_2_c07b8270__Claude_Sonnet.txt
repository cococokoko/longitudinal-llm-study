── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I am a senior attorney at a well-established law firm.

How can I decorate my apartment on a minimalist budget?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here are some practical tips for decorating your apartment on a minimalist budget:

## Core Principles
- **Less is more** - focus on a few quality pieces rather than filling every space
- Declutter first before adding anything new

## Budget-Friendly Strategies

**Shop Smart**
- Thrift stores and estate sales for unique finds
- Facebook Marketplace and Craigslist for furniture
- IKEA for clean, simple basics
- End-of-season sales at stores like Target or West Elm

**DIY Touches**
- Paint is one of the highest-impact, lowest-cost changes
- Reframe existing artwork or photos
- Simple floating shelves add storage and visual interest

**Free or Nearly Free**
- Rearrange what you already own
- Plants from cuttings or affordable nurseries
- Natural elements like stones, branches, or pinecones

## Minimalist Aesthetic Tips
- Stick to a **neutral color palette** with one or two accent colors
- Choose **multi-functional furniture**
- Use negative space intentionally
- Good lighting makes a significant difference - swap bulbs or add simple lamps

## Practical Starting Points
- Set a firm budget before shopping
- Focus on one room at a time
- Pinterest boards can help clarify your actual style preferences
