Can you give me a powerful rhetorical question for an essay about the harms of social media on teens?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here's a strong rhetorical question for your essay:

**"If we wouldn't hand a teenager a product we knew caused anxiety, depression, and self-harm, why do we treat social media any differently?"**

This works well because it:
- **Forces comparison** to things society already regulates for teens
- **Implies hypocrisy** without directly accusing the reader
- **Assumes shared values** most readers already hold
- Makes the reader feel the **logical inconsistency**

A few alternatives depending on your essay's angle:

- *"At what point did we decide that a teenager's mental health was less important than a corporation's engagement metrics?"*
- *"Would you let a stranger follow your child everywhere, cataloging their insecurities and showing them curated images designed to make them feel inadequate?"*

**My recommendation** is the second alternative if you want something more emotionally vivid and concrete.
