# Marathon Training & Racing Tips

## Training
- **Build mileage gradually** – increase weekly mileage no more than 10% per week
- **Run 4-5 days per week** with one long run on weekends
- **Include rest days** – recovery is as important as training
- **Cross-train** with cycling or swimming to reduce injury risk
- **Follow a structured plan** (16-20 weeks is typical for beginners)

## Nutrition & Hydration
- Practice fueling during long runs (gels, chews, or real food)
- Hydrate consistently throughout training, not just race day
- **Don't try anything new on race day**
- Carb-load 2-3 days before the race

## Race Day
- **Start slower than you feel you should** – most runners go out too fast
- Break the race into segments mentally
- Use aid stations consistently
- **Stick to your pace plan** for the first 18 miles

## Recovery
- Expect 2-4 weeks of easy running post-race
- Prioritize sleep and protein intake
- Address any injuries promptly

## Mindset
- Respect the distance – the last 6 miles are a different race
- Have a **backup goal** in case conditions are tough
- Enjoy the experience
