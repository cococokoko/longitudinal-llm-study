── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I recently started a new job and am still on probation.

How do I understand what I want?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
That's a really thoughtful question, especially during a transition period. Here are some ways to think about it:

## Understanding What You Want at Work

**Pay attention to your reactions**
- Notice what energizes you vs. drains you during the day
- What tasks make time pass quickly?
- What do you dread or avoid?

**Separate layers of "want"**
- What you want *right now* (stability, to pass probation)
- What you want *longer term* (career direction, growth)
- What you want *fundamentally* (purpose, recognition, autonomy)

**Ask yourself honest questions**
- What would you do differently if you weren't being evaluated?
- What would make you feel proud of this job in a year?
- What are you hoping this role leads to?

## During Probation Specifically

- It's normal to feel uncertain - you're still learning the environment
- You might not know what you want until you see more options
- Give yourself permission to **observe before deciding**

## Practical Steps

- Keep a simple journal of daily highs and lows
- Talk to people in roles you might want someday
- Notice what you envy in colleagues (that often signals values)

**One honest note:** Probation can create pressure that makes it hard to think clearly about wants. It might be worth distinguishing between *surviving probation* and *figuring out your direction* as two separate tasks.

What specifically are you trying to figure out - the job itself, or something broader?
