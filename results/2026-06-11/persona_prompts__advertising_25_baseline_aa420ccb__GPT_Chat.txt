How can I decorate my apartment on a minimalist budget?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Decorating an apartment on a minimalist budget is mostly about choosing a few high-impact pieces and avoiding clutter. The goal is to make the space feel intentional rather than empty.

Here are some affordable approaches:

- Start with a neutral base
  - Use simple bedding, curtains, and rugs in neutral colors.
  - Consistent colors make inexpensive items look more cohesive.

- Focus on lighting
  - Warm LED bulbs can dramatically improve a room.
  - Add a floor lamp or table lamp from a thrift store or discount retailer.
  - Avoid relying only on harsh overhead lighting.

- Use textiles for warmth
  - Add one or two throw pillows.
  - Use a simple blanket on the sofa or bed.
  - A modest rug can define a space and make it feel finished.

- Incorporate plants
  - Low-maintenance plants like pothos, snake plants, or ZZ plants are often inexpensive.
  - Even one plant can make a room feel more alive.

- Create simple wall art
  - Print free or inexpensive artwork and frame it.
  - Use black, white, or natural wood frames for a clean look.
  - Consider a single larger piece instead of many small items.

- Shop secondhand
  - Check thrift stores, Facebook Marketplace, garage sales, and local buy/sell groups.
  - Look for solid wood furniture, lamps, mirrors, and frames.

- Add mirrors
  - Mirrors make small apartments feel brighter and larger.
  - A large secondhand mirror can be one of the best value purchases.

- Prioritize functional decor
  - Attractive storage baskets.
  - Floating shelves.
  - A stylish tray for organizing everyday items.
  - Items that serve both a practical and decorative purpose fit well with minimalism.

- Leave some empty space
  - Minimalist spaces benefit from breathing room.
  - Avoid filling every wall, shelf, or corner.

Sample budget breakdown (around $150–$250):
- Rug: $40–$80
- Lamp: $20–$50
- Plant: $10–$30
- Artwork and frames: $20–$40
- Throw blanket and pillows: $30–$60
