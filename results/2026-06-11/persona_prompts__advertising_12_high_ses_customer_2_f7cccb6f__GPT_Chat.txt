Eco-friendly travel options vary by distance, destination, and budget, but some choices consistently have a lower environmental impact than others:

• Walking and cycling
  - Among the most sustainable forms of transportation.
  - Produce little to no direct emissions.
  - Well suited for local travel and sightseeing.

• Public transportation
  - Trains, subways, trams, and buses generally emit less carbon per passenger than private vehicles.
  - Rail travel is often one of the lowest-emission options for medium- and long-distance trips.

• High-speed and intercity rail
  - Frequently a lower-carbon alternative to flying, particularly for trips of a few hundred miles/kilometers.
  - Often provides city-center to city-center convenience.

• Shared transportation
  - Carpooling and ridesharing reduce emissions per traveler compared with single-occupancy vehicle use.
  - Useful where public transit is limited.

• Electric vehicles (EVs)
  - Typically produce lower lifecycle emissions than conventional gasoline vehicles, especially when charged using cleaner energy sources.
  - Rental EVs are increasingly available in many destinations.

• Direct flights when air travel is necessary
  - Takeoffs and landings are energy-intensive, so nonstop flights can reduce emissions compared with itineraries involving multiple connections.
  - Economy-class seating generally has a lower carbon footprint per passenger than premium cabins because space is used more efficiently.

• Sustainable accommodations
  - Consider hotels or lodging certified by recognized environmental programs.
  - Look for properties with energy-efficiency measures, renewable energy use, water conservation practices, and waste-reduction programs.

• Responsible tourism practices
  - Support local businesses and guides.
  - Minimize single-use plastics.
  - Respect conservation rules in natural areas.
  - Choose operators with credible sustainability commitments.

• Carbon reduction and offsetting
  - Prioritize reducing emissions through travel choices first.
  - If desired, consider high-quality carbon offset programs that use independently verified standards.

For most journeys, the general order from lowest to highest environmental impact is often:
1. Walking/cycling
2. Rail and other public transit
3. Shared ground transportation
4. Private electric vehicles
5. Conventional private vehicles
6. Air travel
