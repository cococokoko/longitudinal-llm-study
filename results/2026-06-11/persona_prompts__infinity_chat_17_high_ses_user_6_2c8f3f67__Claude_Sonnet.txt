I am a partner at a major consulting firm.

How might society evolve if telepathy became possible?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This is a genuinely interesting question worth thinking through carefully rather than just enthusiastically.

## Likely Significant Disruptions

**Economic/Professional**
- Negotiation, sales, and consulting itself would transform fundamentally - information asymmetry is core to many business models
- Legal systems built around intent and testimony would need rebuilding
- Privacy as an industry (encryption, NDAs, confidentiality agreements) becomes complicated

**Social structures**
- Relationships might become more authentic OR more psychologically exhausting
- Social hierarchies could flatten OR new hierarchies based on "mental discipline" could emerge
- Political manipulation becomes harder but also potentially more sophisticated

## Honest Complications Worth Considering

- **Consent frameworks** would be enormously contested - who controls access?
- **Mental privacy** might become the defining civil rights issue
- People have genuinely disturbing thoughts regularly - forced transparency could be socially destabilizing rather than liberating
- Power asymmetries might actually *increase* if telepathy were unevenly distributed

## What History Suggests

Previous "transparency technologies" (printing press, internet) promised democratization but also created new control mechanisms. Telepathy would likely follow similar patterns - **contested, uneven, and shaped by existing power structures** rather than automatically transformative.
