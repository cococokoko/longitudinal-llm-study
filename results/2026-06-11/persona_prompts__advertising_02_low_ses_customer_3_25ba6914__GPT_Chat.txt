Best skin routine for dry skin.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
For dry skin, the goal is to cleanse gently, add moisture, and protect the skin barrier.

Morning:
- Cleanse with a gentle, non-foaming cleanser (or just rinse with lukewarm water if your skin isn't oily).
- Apply a hydrating serum if desired (look for hyaluronic acid, glycerin, or panthenol).
- Use a rich moisturizer containing ingredients such as ceramides, squalane, shea butter, or fatty acids.
- Finish with a broad-spectrum SPF 30 or higher every day.

Evening:
- Cleanse gently to remove sunscreen, makeup, and dirt.
- Apply any treatment products if needed (introduce actives slowly, as dry skin can be sensitive).
- Use a richer moisturizer than in the morning.
- If your skin is very dry, apply a thin layer of an occlusive product (such as petrolatum-based ointment) over dry areas to lock in moisture.

Helpful ingredients for dry skin:
- Ceramides
- Glycerin
- Hyaluronic acid
- Squalane
- Urea (in lower concentrations)
- Colloidal oatmeal

Try to avoid:
- Harsh scrubs
- Over-cleansing
- Very hot showers
- Alcohol-heavy skincare products that can be drying

A simple beginner routine could be:
- Gentle cleanser
- Ceramide moisturizer
- SPF 30+ (morning only)

If your dry skin is accompanied by significant redness, itching, cracking, or eczema, it may be worth seeing a dermatologist for tailored advice.
