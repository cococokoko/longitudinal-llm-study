# Starting to Code from Scratch

Great question! Here's a practical roadmap:

## Choose Your First Language
**Recommended for beginners:**
- **Python** - readable, versatile, huge community
- **JavaScript** - runs in browsers, immediate visual feedback
- **Scratch** - visual blocks, good for absolute beginners

*Python is most commonly recommended for starting out.*

## Core Learning Approaches

**Free Resources:**
- freeCodeCamp.org
- The Odin Project
- CS50 (Harvard's free course on edX)
- Codecademy (free tier)
- YouTube tutorials

**Paid Options:**
- Udemy courses (often $10-15 on sale)
- Coursera
- Boot camps (intensive, expensive)

## Practical Tips That Actually Help

- **Code daily** - even 20-30 minutes beats long occasional sessions
- **Build projects** - don't just follow tutorials endlessly
- **Accept confusion** - it's normal and temporary
- **Google constantly** - experienced developers do this too
- **Join communities** - Reddit r/learnprogramming, Discord servers

## Realistic Expectations
- Basic competency: **3-6 months** of consistent practice
- Job-ready skills: typically **1-2 years**
- You'll never stop learning

## A Simple Starting Point
1. Pick Python
2. Start CS50 or freeCodeCamp
3. Build one small project after basics

**What's your goal?** (web development, data science, games, etc.) - that might change the recommendation.
