# Review: *The Last Frequency* (2024)

★★★★½

Director Mara Okonkwo's debut feature is a quiet devastation. Set in a dying radio station in rural Montana, the film follows two estranged siblings who discover their late father left behind recordings that seem to predict events that haven't happened yet.

What could easily become a thriller gimmick instead becomes something far more intimate — a meditation on grief, regret, and whether knowing the future would actually change anything about how we live.

Lead performances from the two central actors carry enormous weight in long, wordless scenes that somehow never drag. The cinematography leans into the vast emptiness of the landscape without making it feel like a postcard.

The film's final twenty minutes are genuinely surprising without feeling manipulative, which is rarer than it should be.

**The verdict:** *The Last Frequency* trusts its audience in ways mainstream cinema rarely does. It's slow, it's sad, and it lingers with you for days afterward. Exactly the kind of film that gets quietly overlooked and then quietly beloved.
