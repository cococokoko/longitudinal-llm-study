── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Help me use my Microsoft Surface touchpad and on-screen keyboard to run Dolphin Emulator for the Wii.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Running the Dolphin Emulator (for Wii and GameCube games) on a Microsoft Surface using only the touchpad and the on-screen keyboard is entirely possible, but it requires some specific configuration. 

Because Wii games originally used motion controls (the Wii Remote) and physical buttons, you will need to map these controls to your virtual keyboard and touchpad.

Here is a step-by-step guide to setting this up for the best possible experience.

---

### Step 1: Optimize your Surface On-Screen Keyboard
The default Windows touch keyboard is too small and doesn't have all the keys you need (like Shift, Ctrl, or Arrow keys). You need to enable the **Full Keyboard Layout**.

1. Open Windows **Settings** (Gear icon).
2. Go to **Time & Language** > **Typing** > **Touch keyboard**.
3. Look for **Keyboard layout** and set it to **Traditional** or **Full** (this adds the Fn keys, Alt, Ctrl, and Arrow keys).
4. *Tip:* You can also make the keyboard transparent or dock it to the bottom of the screen so you can see the game.

---

### Step 2: Configure Dolphin's Controller Settings
Since you don't have a physical Wii Remote, you must tell Dolphin to **emulate** a Wii Remote using your keyboard and touchpad.

1. Open Dolphin.
2. Click on the **Controllers** button on the main toolbar.
3. Under **Wii Remotes**, change "Wii Remote 1" to **Emulated Wii Remote**.
4. Click **Configure** next to it.

---

### Step 3: Map the Controls (The Key Step)
In the configuration window, you will map Wii Remote inputs to your keyboard and touchpad. Here is the best setup for a touch/touchpad user:

#### 1. General Buttons
Map these to keys that are easy to reach on your on-screen keyboard.
*   **A Button:** Spacebar (easy to tap)
*   **B Button:** `Z` key
*   **1 Button:** `1` key
*   **2 Button:** `2` key
*   **Minus (-):** `Q` key
*   **Plus (+):** `E` key
*   **Home:** `H` key

#### 2. D-Pad (Used for menus or movement)
*   Map these to your keyboard's **Arrow Keys** (Up, Down, Left, Right).

#### 3. Motion Simulation (Crucial for Wii Games)
Wii games require you to point at the screen. You will use your **Surface Touchpad** to simulate this.
*   Go to the **Motion Simulation** tab in the controller config.
*   Under **Point**, right-click the box next to **Up**, **Down**, **Left**, and **Right**.
*   Set these to your **Mouse/Touchpad movement**. 
    *   *Alternatively:* You can map "Point" to the **Cursor** so that wherever you slide your finger on the touchpad, the Wii pointer follows.

#### 4. Shake (For games like *Super Mario Bros. Wii* or *Donkey Kong*)
Many Wii games require you to shake the controller.
*   Under **Motion Simulation** > **Shake**, map **X**, **Y**, and **Z** to a single, easy-to-reach key, like the **Shift** key or **`X`** key. 
*   Now, whenever you press that key, Dolphin will register a "shake."

---

### Step 4: Adjust Dolphin for Better Touch Performance
Surface devices are portable, but running Wii games can be demanding. To ensure smooth gameplay:

1. In Dolphin, go to **Graphics**.
2. Under the **General** tab, set the **Backend** to **Direct3D 12** or **Vulkan** (these work best with Intel/AMD integrated graphics on Surface devices).
3. Under the **Enhancements** tab, set the **Internal Resolution** to **1x Native (640x528)** or **Auto (Window Size)**. Keeping it low ensures your Surface won't lag.
4. Check the box for **Start in Fullscreen** if you want a seamless touch experience, but keeping it in **Windowed Mode** makes it easier to access your on-screen keyboard.

---

### Pro-Tips for Playing

*   **Use "Toggle" Keys:** If a game requires you to hold down a button while moving the touchpad (which is hard to do with touch controls), you can right-click any button in the Dolphin config and set it as a "Toggle." This means tapping the key once keeps it "held down" until you tap it again.
*   **Consider a Virtual Joystick App:** If the Windows on-screen keyboard is too clunky, you can download a free third-party tool like **VirtualGamepad** or **GestureSign**. These tools let you draw custom, transparent game buttons directly on your Surface screen that map to keyboard keys.
*   **Turn on "Horizontal Wii Remote":** For games like *New Super Mario Bros. Wii* or *Mario Kart*, go to the **General** tab of the Controller config and check **Sideways Wii Remote**. This makes the controls much simpler (D-pad to move, 1 and 2 to run and jump).
