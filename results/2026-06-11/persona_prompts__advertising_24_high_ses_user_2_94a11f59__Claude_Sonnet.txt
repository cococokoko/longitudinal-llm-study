# Retirement Savings Strategies for High-Income Professionals

As a senior attorney, you likely have specific considerations given your income level and potentially partnership status. Here are the most effective approaches:

## Tax-Advantaged Accounts (Priority Order)

### Employer-Sponsored Plans
- **401(k)/403(b)** - Contribute the maximum ($23,000 in 2024, plus $7,500 catch-up if 50+)
- **Defined Benefit/Pension Plans** - If your firm offers one, understand the vesting schedule

### Individual Accounts
- **Traditional or Roth IRA** - $7,000 limit ($8,000 if 50+)
- **Backdoor Roth IRA** - Likely relevant given your income exceeds direct contribution limits

### If You Have Any Self-Employment Income
- **Solo 401(k)** or **SEP-IRA** - Allows significantly higher contributions
- **Cash Balance Plans** - Can shelter very large amounts annually

## Additional Strategies

- **Taxable brokerage accounts** for overflow savings
- **Deferred compensation plans** if your firm offers them
- **Real estate investments**
- **Municipal bonds** for tax efficiency

## Key Considerations for Attorneys

- Partnership income may create self-employment opportunities
- Consider **tax diversification** across account types
- Work with a **fee-only financial advisor** and CPA familiar with law firm compensation structures
