── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I recently started a new job and am still on probation.

How do I understand what I want?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Starting a new job is a vulnerable and overwhelming time. When you are on probation, your brain is naturally in "survival and adaptation" mode—trying to learn names, systems, culture, and expectations. 

In this state, it is very difficult to know what you *actually* want because you are too busy trying to *fit in*. 

To understand what you want during this transition, you need to shift from being a passive observer to an active researcher of your own experience. Here is a step-by-step framework to help you figure it out.

---

### 1. Separate "New Job Anxiety" from "This Isn't Right"
In the first 30 to 90 days, it is normal to feel incompetent, tired, and doubtful. This is called the **transition dip**. Before deciding you don't want this job, ask yourself:
* *Am I unhappy because the work is bad, or am I just exhausted from learning?*
* *If I knew exactly how to do this job perfectly, would I enjoy it?*
* If the answer to the second question is "yes," what you want is **mastery and time**, not a different job.

### 2. Run a Daily "Energy Audit"
Instead of trying to figure out your "passion," track your energy. For the next two weeks, keep a small notepad or a digital document. At the end of each day, write down:
* **Red (Draining):** What tasks, meetings, or interactions made you feel bored, anxious, or depleted? (e.g., "Spent 3 hours on spreadsheets," "Micromanaged by X.")
* **Green (Energizing):** What moments made you feel engaged, curious, or proud? (e.g., "Explaining a concept to a teammate," "Solving that coding bug," "The quiet hour of writing.")
* **Blue (Neutral):** Tasks that were fine, but didn't spark much emotion.

*Why this works:* Over time, patterns will emerge. You will see exactly what kind of work you want *more* of and what you want to *minimize*.

### 3. Look Around (The "Internal Menu")
Since you are new, you have a golden ticket to ask questions. Use your probation period to look at other people’s jobs. 
* **Who has the job you want in 3 years?** Look at your manager, your peers, and people in other departments. Whose daily life looks appealing to you?
* **Conduct "Micro-Interviews":** Ask colleagues, "What is your favorite part of your week?" or "How did you get into this specific role?" 
* *Why this works:* Sometimes we don't know what we want because we don't know what options exist. Treat your company as a menu of potential career paths.

### 4. Define Your "Three Pillars"
To understand what you want, you must define your currency. We all work for different combinations of three things. Rank these in order of importance to you *right now*:
1. **The Work (Content):** Do you want intellectually stimulating tasks, creativity, or predictable routines?
2. **The Environment (Culture & Lifestyle):** Do you want flexibility, high-paying stress, a collaborative team, or to be left alone to do your work?
3. **The Growth (Future):** Do you want a prestigious resume builder, a stepping stone to management, or a stable place to stay long-term?

*Note: Your priorities can change. What you wanted at 22 might not be what you want at 30. Be honest about what you need today.*

### 5. Use the "Process of Elimination"
If you don't know what you *do* want, start with what you *don't* want. It is much easier to identify dealbreakers.
* "I know I do *not* want to work in client-facing roles."
* "I know I do *not* want to work weekends."
* "I know I do *not* want a boss who doesn't give feedback."
By carving away what you dislike, the shape of what you actually want will naturally appear.

### 6. Give Yourself a "Decision Date"
Do not try to decide your entire future today. It adds too much pressure to an already stressful probation period. 
* Set a date on your calendar (e.g., **Day 80 of your 90-day probation**).
* Tell yourself: *"Until that date, I am not allowed to worry about whether this is my forever job. I am just here to learn, observe, and collect data."*
* When that date arrives, look at your Energy Audit and your Three Pillars, and make an informed decision about whether to stay, pivot internally, or start looking elsewhere.

### Summary Questions to Ask Yourself Tonight:
1. *What was the best 30 minutes of my work week so far?*
2. *If I could delete one responsibility from my plate with no consequences, what would it be?*
3. *Am I running toward a goal, or am I just running away from discomfort?*
