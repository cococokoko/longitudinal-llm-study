Effective ways to reduce household energy use.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Effective ways to reduce household energy use generally fall into a few categories: heating and cooling, appliances, lighting, water heating, and home efficiency.

• Improve insulation and air sealing
  - Seal gaps around doors, windows, vents, and utility penetrations.
  - Add or upgrade attic, wall, and floor insulation where cost-effective.
  - Use weatherstripping and door sweeps to reduce drafts.

• Optimize heating and cooling
  - Install a programmable or smart thermostat.
  - Adjust temperature settings when the home is unoccupied or at night.
  - Replace HVAC filters regularly.
  - Maintain heating and cooling equipment to ensure efficient operation.
  - Consider upgrading to a high-efficiency heat pump or HVAC system when replacement is due.

• Reduce water-heating energy
  - Lower the water heater temperature if appropriate and safe for household needs.
  - Insulate hot-water pipes and older water heaters.
  - Install low-flow showerheads and faucet aerators.
  - Use cold-water settings for laundry when suitable.

• Upgrade lighting
  - Replace incandescent and halogen bulbs with LEDs.
  - Use occupancy sensors or timers in less frequently used areas.
  - Take advantage of natural daylight where practical.

• Use appliances more efficiently
  - Choose ENERGY STAR–rated appliances when replacing old equipment.
  - Run dishwashers and washing machines with full loads.
  - Clean refrigerator coils periodically.
  - Air-dry dishes or clothing when practical.

• Manage electronics and standby power
  - Unplug devices that draw power when not in use.
  - Use smart power strips to reduce "phantom" energy consumption.
  - Enable energy-saving settings on computers, televisions, and other electronics.

• Improve window performance
  - Use blinds, shades, or curtains to reduce unwanted heat gain in summer and heat loss in winter.
  - Consider double- or triple-pane windows if undertaking a larger renovation.

• Reduce cooking-related energy use
  - Use microwave ovens, pressure cookers, or air fryers for suitable tasks, as they often consume less energy than conventional ovens.
  - Match pot sizes to burner sizes and keep lids on pots when possible.

• Monitor consumption
  - Review utility bills and usage reports to identify trends.
  - Consider a home energy monitor to identify high-consumption devices.
  - Many utilities offer energy audits that can help prioritize improvements.

For most households, the highest-return measures are often air sealing, insulation improvements, thermostat optimization, LED lighting, and efficient heating and cooling systems. The most cost-effective strategy depends on the home's age, climate, utility rates, and existing equipment.
