# Mastering the Art of Cooking Steak

## Understanding the Basics First

### Choosing Your Cut
| Cut | Tenderness | Flavor | Best Method |
|-----|-----------|--------|-------------|
| Ribeye | High | Rich/fatty | Pan sear, grill |
| NY Strip | Medium-high | Balanced | Pan sear, grill |
| Filet Mignon | Very high | Mild | Pan sear, oven finish |
| T-Bone/Porterhouse | High | Complex | Grill |
| Flank/Skirt | Lower | Bold | High heat, slice thin |
| Chuck Eye | Medium | Rich | Pan sear |

---

## The Fundamentals

### 1. Start with Good Meat
- **Thickness matters** - aim for at least 1–1.5 inches
- Look for **good marbling** (fat running through the meat)
- **USDA Choice or Prime** when possible
- Buy from a butcher when you can

### 2. Preparation
- **Dry brine** - salt generously 45 minutes to 24 hours ahead
- Pat completely **dry** before cooking
- Bring closer to room temperature (30 min out of fridge)
- Season simply - salt, pepper, maybe garlic powder

---

## Cooking Methods

### 🔥 The Pan Sear (Most Control)
**What you need:** Cast iron skillet, high smoke-point oil

**Steps:**
1. Heat pan until **smoking hot**
2. Add oil (avocado, grapeseed, or refined coconut)
3. Place steak - **don't move it**
4. Sear 2–3 minutes per side
5. Add butter, garlic, thyme in last minute
6. **Baste continuously** by tilting pan and spooning butter over steak
7. Rest before cutting

### 🥩 Reverse Sear (Best for Thick Cuts)
1. Season and place on rack in oven at **250°F (120°C)**
2. Cook until internal temp is **10–15°F below** target
3. Remove, let rest briefly
4. Sear in screaming hot pan **1–2 minutes per side**
5. **Result:** Edge-to-edge perfect doneness

### 🔥 Grilling
- Clean, oiled grates
- Two-zone fire (hot side + cool side)
- Sear on hot side, finish on cool if needed
- Watch for flare-ups

### 🌡️ Sous Vide (Most Precise)
- Vacuum seal seasoned steak
- Cook in water bath at exact target temp
- Finish with hard sear for crust

## Doneness Guide

| Level | Internal Temp | Description |
|-------|--------------|-------------|
| Rare | 120–125°F | Cool red center |
| Medium Rare | 130–135°F | Warm red center ⭐ |
| Medium | 140–145°F | Pink center |
| Medium Well | 150–155°F | Slightly pink |
| Well Done | 160°F+ | No pink |

> ⭐ **Medium rare is widely considered the sweet spot** for most cuts

## Critical Techniques

### The Crust
- Pan must be **very hot** before steak goes in
- Steak must be **dry** (moisture = steam = no crust)
- Don't crowd the pan
- **Don't press down** on the steak

### The Butter Baste
```
Butter + crushed garlic cloves + fresh thyme/rosemary
Tilt pan → spoon hot butter over steak repeatedly
```
This adds flavor and helps even cooking

### Resting
- **Rest 5–10 minutes** after cooking
- Allows juices to redistribute
- Tent loosely with foil
- **Never skip this step**

### Slicing
- Always cut **against the grain**
- Especially important for tougher cuts
- Use a sharp knife
