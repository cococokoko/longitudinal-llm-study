── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I am an executive at a large technology company.

Where can I find unique handmade gifts?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
As an executive at a technology company, you likely value a seamless purchasing experience, high-quality craftsmanship, and a compelling story behind the products you buy—whether for client appreciation, board member gifts, or personal use. 

Here is a curated guide to finding unique, high-end handmade gifts, categorized by the type of experience and product you are looking for.

---

### 1. High-End Curated Marketplaces (For Convenience & Quality)
If you want the charm of handmade but need a vetted, premium shopping experience with reliable shipping and customer service.

*   **Artsy & 1stDibs:** While known for fine art and antique furniture, both platforms have extensive sections for contemporary, handmade studio ceramics, hand-blown glass, and artisan-made home decor. This is where you find museum-quality, investment-grade handmade pieces.
*   **The New Craftsmen:** Based in London (but shipping globally), they represent a curated selection of Britain’s finest makers. They offer exquisite, limited-edition handmade items spanning textiles, silverware, ceramics, and woodwork. 
*   **The Citizenry:** Excellent for home goods. They partner directly with master artisans worldwide (from master weavers in Peru to woodworkers in Ireland) to create exclusive, modern, handmade collections. They handle the supply chain, ensuring high-end packaging and reliable delivery.
*   **Etsy (The "Etsy Journal" & "Etsy Registry" filters):** While Etsy has a lot of noise, you can filter for "Etsy Wed" or look at their curated design awards to find world-class independent woodworkers, leather crafters, and jewelers. 

### 2. Direct-from-Artisan Platforms (For Authenticity & Story)
If you want to support individual master craftspeople directly and value the narrative of *who* made the gift.

*   **Incollect:** A highly curated platform for design professionals where you can buy directly from studio-craft artists who specialize in custom, handmade furniture, lighting, and objets d'art.
*   **Novica (in association with National Geographic):** Excellent for corporate gifting with a social impact angle. They connect you directly with master artisans in developing nations (specializing in alpaca wool, hand-carved wood, and traditional jewelry). Every item comes with a detailed biography of the artisan.
*   **Savoir Faire / Local Guilds:** Look up the craft guilds in regions known for specific heritages. For example:
    *   *The Center for Art in Wood* (Philadelphia) for high-end wood art.
    *   *The glassblowing studios of Murano, Italy* (many now sell directly online via certified collectives).
    *   *Kyoto Crafts Exhibition* (Japan) for traditional lacquerware and ceramics.

### 3. Bespoke & Commissioned Gifts (For VIP Clients & Milestones)
If you need something truly one-of-a-kind (e.g., a custom leather portfolio, a hand-bound book, or a custom-engraved tech accessory).

*   **CustomMade:** A platform that connects you with independent artisans to commission custom jewelry, leather goods, and woodwork. You describe your vision, and makers bid on the project.
*   **Local Art Academies & Museums:** Reach out to the development offices of top-tier art schools (e.g., RISD, Cranbrook, or the Royal College of Art). They can often connect you with alumni or faculty for high-end, custom commissions.
*   **Luxury Bookbinders (e.g., Aspinal of London or Smythson):** For bespoke, hand-bound leather journals, planners, or custom-printed company histories.

### 4. Physical Destinations (For Hands-On Curation)
If you prefer to touch and feel the items before purchasing.

*   **Museum Gift Shops (SFMOMA, MoMA, Cooper Hewitt, V&A):** These shops are meticulously curated by design experts. They feature limited-run, handmade items from global designers that appeal specifically to design- and tech-forward aesthetics.
*   **The Renegade Craft Fair / American Craft Council (ACC) Shows:** The ACC hosts premier craft shows in major tech hubs (San Francisco, Baltimore, St. Paul). These events feature the top tier of American craft artists making jewelry, clothing, furniture, and home decor.

### Executive Tip: How to Gift "Handmade" in Tech
When gifting to other tech executives, the contrast of **high-touch, analog gifts** often makes the biggest impact. 
*   **The "Analog Tech" Gift:** A hand-turned wooden docking station, a hand-stitched leather cord organizer, or a hand-blown glass blue-light glasses stand.
*   **The Story Card:** Always request or print a card detailing the artisan’s background, the materials used (e.g., "reclaimed 100-year-old walnut"), and the hours of labor involved. In a digital world, the *provenance* is the real luxury.
