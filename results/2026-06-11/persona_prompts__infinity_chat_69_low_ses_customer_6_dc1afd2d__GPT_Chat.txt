What is the difference between analysis and design? Can one begin to design without analysis? Why? Be concise.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Analysis and design are related but distinct phases of problem-solving and system development:

- Analysis focuses on understanding the problem, requirements, constraints, and user needs. It answers: “What needs to be done?”
- Design focuses on creating a solution that meets those requirements. It answers: “How will it be done?”

Can design begin without analysis?
- Technically, yes, but it is usually risky and inefficient.
- Without analysis, designers may misunderstand requirements, overlook constraints, or create solutions that do not solve the actual problem.
- Some preliminary or conceptual design can start early, but effective design generally depends on at least some analysis to guide decisions.

In short, analysis defines the problem; design defines the solution. Design can start before analysis is complete, but it should not proceed without sufficient understanding of the problem.
