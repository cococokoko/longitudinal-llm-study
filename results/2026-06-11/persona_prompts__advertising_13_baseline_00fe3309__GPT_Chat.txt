── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Mastering the art of cooking steak.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Mastering steak cooking comes down to understanding a few key variables: the cut, temperature, seasoning, cooking method, and resting time.

1. Start with a good cut
- Ribeye: Rich, flavorful, well-marbled.
- New York strip: Firm texture, beefy flavor.
- Filet mignon: Extremely tender, milder flavor.
- T-bone/Porterhouse: Combines strip and tenderloin.
- Sirloin: Leaner and often more affordable.

2. Bring the steak close to room temperature
- Remove it from the refrigerator 30–60 minutes before cooking.
- Pat it dry with paper towels to encourage a better crust.

3. Season properly
- Generously season with kosher salt.
- Add freshly ground black pepper before or after cooking depending on preference.
- Simple seasoning often produces the best results.

4. Use high heat
- Cast-iron pans are excellent for creating a crust.
- Preheat the pan until very hot before adding the steak.
- Add a high-smoke-point oil such as avocado, canola, or grapeseed oil.

5. Learn internal temperatures
Use an instant-read thermometer for accuracy:
- Rare: 120–125°F (49–52°C)
- Medium-rare: 130–135°F (54–57°C)
- Medium: 140–145°F (60–63°C)
- Medium-well: 150–155°F (66–68°C)
- Well done: 160°F+ (71°C+)

Remove the steak about 5°F (3°C) below your target temperature because it will continue cooking while resting.

6. Build a crust
For a 1-inch (2.5 cm) steak:
- Sear 2–4 minutes per side over high heat.
- Avoid moving it constantly.
- Flip when a deep brown crust develops.

7. Try butter basting
During the final minutes:
- Add butter, garlic, and herbs such as thyme or rosemary.
- Tilt the pan and spoon the melted butter over the steak repeatedly.

8. Rest before slicing
- Rest 5–10 minutes for smaller steaks.
- Rest 10–15 minutes for thicker cuts.
- This helps retain juices.

9. Slice correctly
- Identify the direction of the muscle fibers.
- Slice across the grain for a more tender bite.

Common mistakes to avoid
- Cooking cold steak straight from the refrigerator.
- Using a pan that isn't hot enough.
- Overcrowding the pan.
- Cutting into the steak immediately after cooking.
- Relying solely on cooking time instead of temperature.

A simple restaurant-style method:
1. Dry and salt a 1½-inch ribeye.
2. Heat a cast-iron skillet until very hot.
3. Add a small amount of high-smoke-point oil.
4. Sear 3–4 minutes on the first side.
5. Flip and cook 2–3 minutes.
6. Add butter, garlic, and thyme; baste for 1–2 minutes.
7. Remove at 130°F (54°C) for medium-rare.
8. Rest 10 minutes.
9. Slice and serve.

The single biggest upgrade for most home cooks is using an instant-read thermometer. It removes the guesswork and dramatically improves consistency.
