**Question:** 
Unlike many other birds, Eastern Bluebirds don't just rely on trees for nesting. What man-made object do they famously love to nest in, which has helped their population bounce back from near-extinction?

**Answer:** 
**Bluebird boxes (or nest boxes/birdhouses).** 

Because they are "cavity nesters" but cannot drill their own holes like woodpeckers, they rely heavily on these specially designed wooden birdhouses. The creation of "bluebird trails" (lines of these boxes set up by volunteers and families) successfully saved the species from a severe population decline in the mid-20th century!
