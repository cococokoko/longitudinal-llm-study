Mastering steak cooking comes down to understanding a few key variables: the cut, seasoning, heat, and doneness.

1. Choose the right cut
- Ribeye: Rich, well-marbled, forgiving for beginners.
- New York strip: Good balance of flavor and texture.
- Filet mignon: Very tender but less beefy flavor.
- Sirloin: Leaner and often more affordable.

2. Prepare the steak
- Pat it dry with paper towels.
- Season generously with salt 30–60 minutes before cooking, or immediately before it goes into the pan.
- Add black pepper just before or after cooking to reduce the chance of burning.

3. Bring it close to room temperature
- A short rest outside the refrigerator (20–30 minutes) can help promote more even cooking, though it's not essential.

4. Use high heat
- Cast-iron pans are excellent for developing a crust.
- Preheat the pan until it's very hot.
- Add a high-smoke-point oil such as avocado, canola, or grapeseed oil.

5. Sear properly
- Place the steak in the pan and avoid moving it for the first couple of minutes.
- Flip once a good crust develops.
- For thicker steaks, finish in a 400°F (205°C) oven if needed.

6. Use a thermometer
Target internal temperatures:
- Rare: 120–125°F (49–52°C)
- Medium-rare: 130–135°F (54–57°C)
- Medium: 140–145°F (60–63°C)
- Medium-well: 150–155°F (66–68°C)
- Well done: 160°F+ (71°C+)

Remove the steak about 5°F (3°C) below your target temperature because it will continue cooking while resting.

7. Baste for extra flavor
- Near the end of cooking, add butter, garlic, and herbs such as thyme or rosemary.
- Tilt the pan and spoon the melted butter over the steak repeatedly.

8. Rest before slicing
- Let the steak rest for 5–10 minutes.
- Slice against the grain for maximum tenderness.

For someone working long shifts at a hospital, a practical approach is to buy thicker steaks (1–1.5 inches), season them the night before, and cook them in a cast-iron pan when you get home. A thermometer takes much of the guesswork out and speeds up the learning process.

A simple beginner method:
- 1-inch ribeye or strip steak
- Salt generously
- Sear 3–4 minutes per side in a very hot cast-iron pan
- Add butter for the final minute
- Rest 5 minutes
- Check temperature and adjust future cooks based on the result

After a few attempts using a thermometer, you'll quickly learn how your pan, stove, and preferred steak thickness behave.
