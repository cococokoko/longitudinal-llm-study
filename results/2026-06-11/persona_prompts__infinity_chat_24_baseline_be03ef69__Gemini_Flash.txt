── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Write an email to organize a catch-up with a referral partner.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here are a few templates you can use, depending on how well you know the referral partner and how formal your relationship is. 

### Option 1: Warm and casual (Best for established partners)
**Subject:** Great to connect again? / Catch-up over coffee?

Hi [Partner's Name],

I hope you’re having a great week and that business is treating you well! 

It’s been a little while since we last connected, and I’d love to jump on a quick call (or grab a coffee if you're free) to catch up. 

I’d love to hear how things are going on your end, get an update on your current focus areas, and see how I can keep sending the right kinds of referrals your way. I can also share a quick update on what’s new over here. 

How does your schedule look next week? I’m relatively flexible on [Day of week] or [Day of week], but let me know what works best for you.

Looking forward to catching up!

Best,

[Your Name]  
[Your Title]  
[Your Phone Number]

***

### Option 2: Professional and value-focused (Best for newer or more formal partners)
**Subject:** Catch-up & collaboration update – [Your Company] + [Their Company]

Hi [Partner's Name],

I hope this email finds you well. 

As we head into [current quarter/season], I wanted to reach out and schedule some time for us to reconnect. Our partnership is highly valuable to us, and I want to ensure we are staying aligned on how we can best support each other’s clients.

I’d love to get a brief update on your current target market and any new services you’ve rolled out, as well as share a few updates from our side that might benefit your network. 

Would you have 15–20 minutes for a phone or Zoom call next week? Here are a few times that work well for me:
*   [Option 1: e.g., Tuesday, Oct 24th at 10:00 AM]
*   [Option 2: e.g., Thursday, Oct 26th at 2:00 PM]

If none of those work, feel free to send over a few times that do, or you can book directly onto my calendar here: [Link to Calendly/Booking tool].

Hope to speak soon!

Warm regards,

[Your Name]  
[Your Title]  
[Your Phone Number]

***

### Option 3: Short and sweet (Best for a quick text-style email)
**Subject:** Quick catch-up?

Hi [Partner's Name],

Hope you're doing well! 

It’s been a minute since we last chatted. I’d love to do a quick 15-minute check-in next week to hear what you're working on and see how I can support you with referrals this quarter. 

Let me know if you have any availability next [Day of week] or [Day of week] for a quick call. 

Best,

[Your Name]  
[Your Title]  

***

### Tips before you send:
*   **Include a booking link:** If you use a tool like Calendly or HubSpot Meetings, including it (especially in Option 2) reduces the back-and-forth of scheduling.
*   **Focus on *them* first:** Notice how these templates frame the meeting around how *you* can help *them* (e.g., "how I can keep sending referrals your way"). This makes them much more likely to accept the invitation.
