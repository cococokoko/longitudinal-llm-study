── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How to plan an environmentally friendly wedding?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Planning an environmentally friendly (or "eco-chic") wedding doesn’t mean you have to sacrifice style, elegance, or fun. In fact, sustainable weddings often feel more intimate, unique, and intentional. 

The key to an eco-friendly wedding is focusing on the **"Five Rs": Refuse (unnecessary items), Reduce, Reuse, Repurpose, and Recycle.**

Here is a comprehensive guide to planning a beautiful, low-impact wedding.

---

### 1. The Venue & Timing
Your venue is the biggest decision you’ll make and has the largest impact on your wedding's carbon footprint.

*   **Choose One Location:** Host your ceremony and reception at the same venue. This eliminates the need for guests to drive between locations, drastically reducing transportation emissions.
*   **Embrace Nature:** Outdoor venues like botanical gardens, state parks, beaches, or vineyards require very little decor because the natural backdrop does the work for you.
*   **Look for Green Certifications:** If choosing an indoor venue (like a hotel or historic estate), ask if they have LEED certification, use energy-efficient lighting, or have a robust recycling/composting program.
*   **Utilize Daylight:** Host a morning or afternoon wedding. Utilizing natural sunlight reduces the need for heavy electricity and lighting.

### 2. Invitations & Paper Goods
The wedding industry goes through an enormous amount of paper. You can easily cut this down.

*   **Go Digital:** Send digital Save-the-Dates and invitations via platforms like Greenvelope or Paperless Post. You can also direct guests to a wedding website for RSVPs, registry details, and maps.
*   **Use Seed Paper:** If you prefer physical invitations, use biodegradable seed paper. Guests can plant the invitation in their garden afterward, and wildflowers will grow.
*   **Recycled Paper & Soy Inks:** If using traditional printing, opt for 100% post-consumer recycled paper printed with eco-friendly soy-based inks.
*   **Ditch the Programs and Menus:** Instead of printing individual ceremony programs and menus, use a chalkboard, a wooden sign, or a mirror to display the schedule and menu for everyone to see.

### 3. Attire & Rings
Fashion is one of the most polluting industries, but wedding attire is ripe for sustainable alternatives.

*   **Vintage or Pre-Loved:** Look for vintage wedding dresses or suits. Websites like Stillwhite, Nearly Newlywed, and Wear Your Love offer gorgeous, gently-used designer gowns.
*   **Rent the Look:** Renting tuxedos/suits is common, but bridesmaids can also rent dresses from services like Rent the Runway.
*   **Wear It Again:** Choose bridesmaid dresses and groomsmen attire that your wedding party can actually wear again to other events.
*   **Ethical Jewelry:** For wedding bands, choose recycled metals or lab-grown diamonds. Look for jewelers certified by the Responsible Jewellery Council (RJC).

### 4. Flowers & Decor
Traditional floral arrangements often involve imported flowers, pesticides, and non-biodegradable floral foam.

*   **Local and Seasonal:** Work with a local florist who sources in-season flowers from local flower farms. This reduces the carbon footprint of shipping flowers globally.
*   **Potted Plants and Herbs:** Instead of cut flowers, use potted plants, succulents, or herbs (like rosemary and lavender) as centerpieces. Guests can take them home to plant afterward.
*   **Ban Floral Foam:** Ask your florist to use sustainable alternatives to green floral foam (which is a toxic plastic that doesn't biodegrade), such as chicken wire or reusable metal frogs.
*   **Rent, Don't Buy:** Rent all your decor—vases, arches, candles, and table runners. If you do buy items, source them from thrift stores and resell them after the wedding.

### 5. Food, Drink, and Waste
Food waste and single-use plastics are major culprits at weddings.

*   **Farm-to-Table Catering:** Hire a caterer that specializes in organic, locally sourced, and seasonal ingredients. 
*   **Go Plant-Forward:** Meat has a high carbon footprint. Consider an all-vegetarian or vegan menu, or at least offer a 50% plant-based menu.
*   **Say No to Single-Use Plastics:** Ensure your caterer uses real glassware, flatware, and china. If you are having an informal wedding, use certified compostable bamboo or sugarcane plates.
*   **Donate Leftovers:** Partner with a local food rescue organization (like food banks or shelters) to donate leftover food. Ask your caterer to package leftovers for donation.
*   **Compost and Recycle:** Set up clearly labeled recycling and compost bins alongside trash cans at the venue.

### 6. Favors & Send-offs
Many wedding favors end up in the trash. Focus on consumables or experiences.

*   **Edible Favors:** Give guests something they can eat or drink, like local honey, homemade jam, fair-trade coffee beans, or locally baked cookies.
*   **Charitable Donations:** In lieu of physical favors, make a donation to an environmental charity on behalf of your guests.
*   **Eco-Friendly Send-offs:** Instead of throwing plastic confetti, rice (which can harm birds), or using sparklers, use dried flower petals, lavender buds, eucalyptus leaves, or blow bubbles.

### 7. Registry & Honeymoon
*   **The "Experience" Registry:** Instead of registering for physical household items you may not need, register for experiences (honeymoon excursions, cooking classes, or museum memberships) or a down payment fund.
*   **Eco-Tourism:** For your honeymoon, choose an eco-resort that practices sustainability, or plan a "staycation" or road trip to reduce flight emissions. If you do fly, consider purchasing carbon offsets.

### Pro-Tip: Hire a Green Coordinator
If your budget allows, hire a wedding planner who specializes in sustainable events. They will have a network of eco-friendly vendors, caterers, and florists already vetted, saving you hours of research.
