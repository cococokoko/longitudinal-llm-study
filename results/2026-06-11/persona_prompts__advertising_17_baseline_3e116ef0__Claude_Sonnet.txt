── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How can I make my home more energy efficient?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here are some effective ways to make your home more energy efficient:

## Quick & Low-Cost Steps
- **Seal air leaks** around windows, doors, and outlets with weatherstripping or caulk
- **Switch to LED bulbs** throughout your home
- **Use a programmable or smart thermostat**
- **Unplug electronics** when not in use (they draw "phantom" power)
- **Change HVAC filters** regularly

## Moderate Investments
- **Add insulation** to attic, walls, or basement
- **Install low-flow showerheads and faucets**
- **Upgrade to Energy Star appliances** when replacing old ones
- **Use ceiling fans** to reduce heating/cooling needs
- **Install window coverings** to reduce heat gain/loss

## Larger Projects
- **Replace old windows** with double or triple-pane versions
- **Upgrade your HVAC system** to a high-efficiency model
- **Install a heat pump** for heating and cooling
- **Add solar panels**
- **Upgrade water heater** to tankless or heat pump style

## Behavioral Changes
- Wash clothes in cold water
- Run dishwasher only when full
- Lower water heater temperature to 120°F
- Adjust thermostat when sleeping or away

## Getting Started
Consider getting a **home energy audit** - many utility companies offer them free or at low cost - to identify your biggest opportunities for savings.
