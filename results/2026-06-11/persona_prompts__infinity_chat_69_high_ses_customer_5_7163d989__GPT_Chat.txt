What is the difference between analysis and design? Can one begin to design without analysis? Why? Be concise.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Analysis and design are related but distinct activities:

- Analysis focuses on understanding the problem: goals, requirements, constraints, stakeholders, and the current situation.
- Design focuses on creating a solution: architecture, processes, interfaces, structures, and implementation approaches that satisfy the analyzed requirements.

Can one begin to design without analysis?
- Yes, design can begin before analysis is complete, especially in iterative or agile environments. Early design concepts can help clarify requirements and expose unknowns.
- However, designing without sufficient analysis increases the risk of solving the wrong problem, missing constraints, or creating solutions that do not meet stakeholder needs.

In practice, analysis and design often overlap and inform each other, but some level of analysis is usually necessary to produce an effective design.
