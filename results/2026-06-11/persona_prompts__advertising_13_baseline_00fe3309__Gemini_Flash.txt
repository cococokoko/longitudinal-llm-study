── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Mastering the art of cooking steak.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Cooking the perfect steak is one of the most satisfying culinary skills you can master. While it may seem intimidating, it actually relies on a few simple, scientific principles. 

Here is your ultimate guide to mastering the art of cooking steak, from selecting the cut to the final rest.

---

### Phase 1: The Selection (Choosing Your Cut)
Great steak starts at the butcher counter. Look for **marbling**—the white flecks of intramuscular fat distributed throughout the meat (not the thick strip of fat on the edge). Marbling melts during cooking, giving the steak its flavor and tenderness.

*   **Ribeye:** The king of flavor. Highly marbled, juicy, and rich. Best cooked to medium-rare or medium to render the fat.
*   **Filet Mignon (Tenderloin):** The most tender cut, but has the least fat and flavor. Best cooked rare to medium-rare.
*   **New York Strip:** A crowd-pleaser. Great beefy flavor with a tight texture. 
*   **Thickness is Key:** Always buy steaks that are at least **1.5 to 2 inches thick**. Thin steaks overcook in the middle before you can get a good crust on the outside.

---

### Phase 2: The Prep (The Secret to the Crust)
The enemy of a good sear is moisture. 

1.  **Dry Brining (The Pro Move):** 12 to 24 hours before cooking, heavily salt your steak with kosher salt on all sides. Place it on a wire rack in the fridge uncovered. The salt draws out moisture, dissolves, and is reabsorbed into the meat, seasoning it throughout. The refrigerator air dries out the surface, ensuring an incredible crust.
2.  **Bring to Temp (Optional but helpful):** Take the steak out of the fridge 30–45 minutes before cooking to take the chill off. 
3.  **The Final Seasoning:** Just before cooking, pat the steak completely dry with paper towels. Season with freshly cracked black pepper (and more salt if you didn't dry brine). 

---

### Phase 3: The Cooking Method
For thick-cut steaks, there are two champion methods.

#### Method A: The Classic Pan-Sear & Butter Baste (Best for cuts under 1.5 inches)
*   **The Gear:** A heavy cast-iron skillet or stainless steel pan. (Avoid non-stick; it can't handle the heat).
*   **The Oil:** Use a high-smoke-point oil (avocado, canola, or grapeseed oil). Do not use butter or olive oil yet—they will burn.

1.  **Get it Hot:** Heat your skillet over high heat until the oil is just starting to smoke.
2.  **The Sear:** Lay the steak in the pan away from you (so oil doesn't splash). Sear for about 2 minutes without moving it, then flip.
3.  **The Butter Baste (Arrosé):** Once flipped, turn the heat down to medium. Toss in 2–3 tablespoons of unsalted butter, a few smashed cloves of garlic, and a sprig of rosemary or thyme. 
4.  **Tilt and Spoon:** Tilt the pan slightly and use a large spoon to continuously scoop the melted, foaming, herb-infused butter over the top of the steak. This cooks the steak gently and evenly while adding rich flavor.

#### Method B: The Reverse Sear (Best for cuts 1.5 to 2+ inches thick)
This is the foolproof method used by modern steakhouses to get a perfect edge-to-edge pink interior.

1.  **The Bake:** Preheat your oven to 225°F (105°C). Place the seasoned steak on a wire rack on a baking sheet. Bake until the internal temperature reaches about 10–15°F *below* your target temperature (e.g., pull at 115°F for medium-rare).
2.  **The Rest:** Take it out and let it rest for 10 minutes. (Yes, rest *before* the sear for this method).
3.  **The Sear:** Get your cast-iron skillet screaming hot with a tablespoon of oil. Sear the steak for just 1 minute per side to develop a deep, golden-brown crust. 

---

### Phase 4: Temperature is King
Never guess when a steak is done. Use an **instant-read digital meat thermometer** inserted into the thickest part of the meat. 

*Pull the steak 5°F before your target temperature, as it will continue to cook while resting.*

| Doneness | Pull Temp (Fahrenheit) | Final Temp (After Rest) | Center Color |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Rare** | 120°F | 125°F | Cool, bright red |
| **Medium-Rare** | 130°F | 135°F | Warm, rich pink (Highly Recommended) |
| **Medium** | 140°F | 145°F | Hot, light pink |
| **Medium-Well** | 150°F | 155°F | Mostly gray-brown |
| **Well Done** | 160°F+ | 165°F+ | Gray-brown throughout |

---

### Phase 5: The Rest and the Slice
*   **The Rest:** Transfer the steak to a warm plate or cutting board and let it rest for **5 to 10 minutes**. During cooking, the muscle fibers tighten, pushing juices to the center. Resting allows the fibers to relax and the juices to redistribute. If you cut it immediately, all the juice will run out onto the board, leaving you with a dry steak.
*   **The Slice:** Always slice **against the grain** (perpendicular to the direction the muscle fibers run). This shortens the fibers, making every bite incredibly tender.
*   **The Finish:** Sprinkle a pinch of flaky sea salt (like Maldon) over the fresh slices for a professional touch.
