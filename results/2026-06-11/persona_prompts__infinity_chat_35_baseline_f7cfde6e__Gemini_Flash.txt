── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Give me a 3-day plan for Osaka with a flight leaving on the 3rd day at 7pm.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here is a highly optimized, 3-day itinerary for Osaka. Since your flight leaves at 7:00 PM on Day 3, you will need to head to the airport around 4:00 PM (for international flights) or 5:00 PM (for domestic). 

This itinerary is structured geographically to minimize travel time and maximize your experience of Osaka’s food, culture, and modern sights.

---

### **Day 1: Castle, History & Neon Lights (Central & South Osaka)**
*Focus: Osaka’s iconic landmarks and street food.*

*   **Morning: Osaka Castle (9:00 AM - 12:00 PM)**
    *   Start your trip at **Osaka Castle**. Walk through the massive stone walls and the scenic Nishinomaru Garden. 
    *   *Tip:* Go inside the castle museum if you love history, or just enjoy the view from the outside to save time.
*   **Lunch: Kuromon Ichiba Market (12:30 PM - 2:00 PM)**
    *   Take the subway to Nipponbashi Station. Walk through this bustling wet market known as "Osaka's Kitchen."
    *   *What to eat:* Grilled wagyu skewers, fresh sushi, giant scallops, and takoyaki (octopus balls).
*   **Afternoon: Shinsaibashi & America-mura (2:30 PM - 5:30 PM)**
    *   Walk off lunch by exploring **Shinsaibashi-suji**, a massive covered shopping arcade.
    *   Cross over to **America-mura (Amemura)**, the epicenter of Osaka’s youth culture, vintage clothing shops, and quirky street art.
*   **Evening: Dotonbori (6:00 PM - Late)**
    *   As the sun sets, head to **Dotonbori**. This is the iconic Osaka you see in photos, packed with neon lights and giant mechanical food signs (like the moving crab).
    *   Take a photo with the **Glico Running Man** on the Ebisubashi Bridge.
    *   *Dinner:* Grab okonomiyaki (savory cabbage pancakes) at *Mizuno* or *Ajinoya*, and try Kushikatsu (deep-fried skewers).

---

### **Day 2: Retro Vibes & Futuristic Views (South & North Osaka)**
*Focus: Nostalgic Osaka, shopping, and city views.*

*   **Morning: Shinsekai & Tsutenkaku Tower (9:30 AM - 12:00 PM)**
    *   Head to **Shinsekai**, a neighborhood designed in 1912 to look like Paris and New York. It has a wonderfully gritty, retro Showa-era vibe.
    *   Take photos of **Tsutenkaku Tower**.
    *   *Lunch:* Shinsekai is the birthplace of **Kushikatsu**. Sit at a counter at *Yaekatsu* or *Kushikatsu Daruma* and dip your skewers in the delicious black sauce (remember: no double-dipping!).
*   **Afternoon: Umeda (Kita District) (1:00 PM - 4:30 PM)**
    *   Take the Midosuji subway line north to **Umeda** (Osaka Station area). This is the modern, sleek side of Osaka.
    *   Explore **Grand Front Osaka** or **Hep Five** (which has a giant red Ferris wheel on the roof).
*   **Late Afternoon/Sunset: Umeda Sky Building (5:00 PM - 6:30 PM)**
    *   Walk to the **Umeda Sky Building** and take the futuristic glass escalator up to the *Floating Garden Observatory*. It offers a stunning 360-degree open-air view of the city as the sun sets.
*   **Evening: Dinner in Nakazaki-cho or Omoide Yokocho (7:00 PM - Late)**
    *   For a trendy, hipster vibe, head to nearby **Nakazaki-cho**, an area of old wooden houses converted into cafes and boutiques.
    *   Alternatively, explore the tiny alleyways of **Umeda Lantern Street (Umeda Chika-gai)** for yakitori and beer.

---

### **Day 3: Culture, Last-Minute Shopping & Departure**
*Focus: Shrines, kitchenware, and a smooth journey to the airport.*

*   **Morning: Shitenno-ji Temple or Sumiyoshi Taisha (9:00 AM - 10:30 AM)**
    *   Check out of your hotel and ask them to hold your bags (or use coin lockers at Namba Station).
    *   Visit **Sumiyoshi Taisha**, one of Japan’s oldest Shinto shrines, famous for its beautiful arched red bridge. It is much quieter and more spiritual than the castle.
*   **Late Morning: Sennichimae Doguyasuji Shopping Street (11:00 AM - 12:30 PM)**
    *   Head back to the Namba area. Walk through this unique street dedicated entirely to kitchenware. 
    *   It’s the perfect place to buy high-quality Japanese chef knives, beautiful ceramics, or incredibly realistic plastic food samples as souvenirs.
*   **Lunch: Namba Parks (12:30 PM - 2:00 PM)**
    *   Have lunch at **Namba Parks**, a stunning shopping mall with a massive terraced rooftop garden. There are dozens of excellent restaurants on the upper floors.
*   **Afternoon: Retrieve Bags & Head to Airport (3:00 PM - 4:00 PM)**
    *   Collect your luggage.
    *   **If flying from Kansai International Airport (KIX):** 
        *   Go to **Namba Station** and board the **Nankai Rap:t Express train**. It takes exactly 38 minutes to get directly to KIX. 
        *   *Aim to board the train around 3:00 PM or 3:30 PM to arrive at the airport by 4:00 PM - 4:15 PM.*
    *   **If flying from Itami Airport (ITM - Domestic):**
        *   Take the airport limousine bus from Namba or Umeda (approx. 30 minutes).

---

### **Quick Tips for this Trip:**
1.  **Where to Stay:** Stay in **Namba** or **Shinsaibashi**. This keeps you close to the nightlife on Days 1 & 2, and makes it incredibly easy to catch the direct train to Kansai Airport on Day 3.
2.  **Luggage:** On Day 3, if you don't want to return to your hotel, use the large coin lockers at **Namba Station** to store your bags.
3.  **Transit Pass:** Consider buying an **Osaka Amazing Pass** for Day 1 or 2. It covers your subway rides and gives you free entry to the Castle, Umeda Sky Building, and the Hep Five Ferris Wheel.
