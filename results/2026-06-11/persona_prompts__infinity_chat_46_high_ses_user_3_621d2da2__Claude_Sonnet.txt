## Linear Graph Example

A **path graph** (also called a linear graph) is one of the simplest examples.

### Example: P₄ (Path graph with 4 vertices)

```
1 — 2 — 3 — 4
```

### Formal Definition

- **Vertices:** V = {1, 2, 3, 4}
- **Edges:** E = {(1,2), (2,3), (3,4)}

### Key Properties

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| Number of vertices | 4 |
| Number of edges | 3 |
| Degree of endpoints (1 and 4) | 1 |
| Degree of interior vertices (2 and 3) | 2 |

### Characteristics

- Each vertex connects to **at most two neighbors**
- Forms a single **non-branching chain**
- No cycles
- Exactly **two vertices of degree 1** (the endpoints)

### General Pattern

For a path graph **Pₙ**, there are:
- **n** vertices
- **n − 1** edges

This is a foundational structure that appears frequently in algorithms, network topology, and combinatorics.
