# Mastering Digital Photography

## Core Fundamentals

### The Exposure Triangle
| Element | Controls | Effect |
|---------|----------|--------|
| **Aperture** (f-stop) | Light & depth of field | f/1.8 = blurry background; f/16 = sharp throughout |
| **Shutter Speed** | Motion & light | 1/1000s = freeze action; 1/30s = motion blur |
| **ISO** | Sensor sensitivity | Low ISO = clean; High ISO = grainy/noisy |

---

## Camera Modes

- **Auto** – Camera decides everything
- **Aperture Priority (Av/A)** – You set aperture, camera sets shutter
- **Shutter Priority (Tv/S)** – You set shutter, camera sets aperture
- **Manual (M)** – Full control *(recommended for mastery)*
- **RAW vs JPEG** – Shoot RAW for maximum editing flexibility

---

## Composition Techniques

- **Rule of Thirds** – Place subjects off-center
- **Leading Lines** – Draw the eye into the frame
- **Framing** – Use natural frames (doorways, trees)
- **Negative Space** – Empty space adds impact
- **Golden Hour** – Shoot 1 hour after sunrise/before sunset

---

## Key Skills to Develop

### Focus
- Single-point AF for precise control
- Continuous AF for moving subjects
- Manual focus for macro/low light

### Lighting
- **Natural light** – Soft, diffused is usually best
- **Golden/Blue hour** – Most flattering outdoor light
- **Flash** – Learn bounce flash to avoid harsh shadows
- **Reflectors** – Cheap, effective fill light

---

## Post-Processing Workflow

1. **Import & organize** (Lightroom, Capture One)
2. **Cull** – Select your best shots
3. **Basic adjustments** – Exposure, contrast, white balance
4. **Color grading** – HSL panel, tone curves
5. **Sharpening & noise reduction**
6. **Export** – Correct format for intended use

---

## Practice Exercises

| Exercise | Skill Built |
|----------|-------------|
| Shoot only in Manual for 30 days | Exposure mastery |
| One location, 50 different shots | Composition creativity |
| Shoot the same subject in different light | Lighting awareness |
| Recreate a photo you admire | Technical analysis |

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- ❌ Always shooting in Auto
- ❌ Ignoring the background
- ❌ Centering every subject
- ❌ Over-editing photos
- ❌ Not reviewing shots critically
- ❌ Buying gear instead of practicing

---

## Recommended Learning Path

1. **Week 1-2** – Master exposure triangle
2. **Week 3-4** – Shoot in Aperture Priority
3. **Month 2** – Switch to full Manual
4. **Month 3** – Focus on composition
5. **Month 4+** – Learn post-processing

---
