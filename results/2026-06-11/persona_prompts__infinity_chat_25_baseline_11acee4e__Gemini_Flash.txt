── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How to say a formal thank you for your time in the subject line of an email?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
To write a formal "thank you for your time" in an email subject line, you want to be **concise, professional, and clear** about the context of the email. 

Here are several options categorized by the situation:

### 1. After an Interview (Most Common)
These are polished, respectful, and include your name so the hiring manager immediately knows who you are.
*   **Thank You: [Your Name] – [Job Title] Interview** *(Highly recommended: clean and professional)*
*   **Thank you for your time today – [Your Name]**
*   **Appreciation for your time: [Your Name] for [Job Title]**
*   **Thank you for the opportunity – [Your Name]**

### 2. After a Business Meeting or Consultation
Use these after meeting with a client, partner, or vendor.
*   **Thank you for your time today / [Project Name or Topic]**
*   **Appreciation for your time and insights**
*   **Follow-up: Thank you for your time today**
*   **Thank you for a productive meeting**

### 3. After an Informational Interview or Networking Call
Use these when someone has done you a favor by giving you career advice or industry insights.
*   **Thank you for your time and guidance**
*   **With appreciation for your time and advice – [Your Name]**
*   **Thank you for sharing your expertise today**
*   **Great connecting with you today / Thank you**

### 4. Short & Traditional (Safe for any formal context)
If you want to keep it strictly traditional and brief:
*   **Thank You – [Your Name]**
*   **In Appreciation of Your Time**
*   **Thank You for Your Time**

### 💡 Pro-Tips for Formal Subject Lines:
*   **Keep it under 60 characters:** This ensures the entire subject line is visible on mobile devices.
*   **Send it within 24 hours:** For interviews and business meetings, promptness is part of the formality.
*   **Capitalize properly:** Use Title Case (e.g., *Thank You for Your Time*) to make it look polished.
