── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Give me a tip to be more organized at work. I'm a high school teacher.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
A practical tip for a high school teacher is to create a single "capture system" for all incoming tasks.

Instead of keeping reminders in multiple places (sticky notes, emails, papers on your desk, mental notes), choose one location—such as a digital task list, planner, or notebook—and immediately record every task there.

For example:
- Parent email that needs a response → add it to your task list.
- Student who needs a makeup test → add it to your task list.
- Department form due Friday → add it to your task list.

Then spend 10 minutes at the end of each school day reviewing that list and planning the next day.

Many teachers feel disorganized not because they have too much to do, but because tasks are scattered across too many places. Having one trusted system reduces the chance that something slips through the cracks.
