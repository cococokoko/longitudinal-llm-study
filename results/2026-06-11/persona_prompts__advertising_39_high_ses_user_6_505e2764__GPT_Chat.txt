── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I am a partner at a major consulting firm.

How to get started with meditation and mindfulness?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Given your role, it may help to think about meditation and mindfulness not as a wellness activity, but as attention training. The goal is not to empty your mind or feel calm all the time. The goal is to become more aware of where your attention is and to develop the ability to direct it intentionally.

A practical way to start:

1. Begin very small
- Start with 5 minutes per day.
- Consistency matters more than duration.
- Attach it to an existing habit (after coffee, before checking email, before bed).

2. Use a simple breath-focused meditation
- Sit comfortably.
- Set a timer for 5 minutes.
- Pay attention to the sensation of breathing.
- When your mind wanders—and it will—notice it and gently return attention to the breath.
- Each return is part of the training, not a mistake.

3. Practice mindfulness during your workday
Formal meditation is useful, but informal mindfulness often fits better into demanding schedules.

Examples:
- Before entering a client meeting, take one conscious breath and notice your mental state.
- During conversations, focus fully on listening instead of planning your response.
- Between meetings, spend 30 seconds noticing physical sensations, sounds, or breathing.
- When reading email, notice the impulse to switch tasks and decide deliberately whether to do so.

4. Lower expectations
Common misconceptions:
- "I should stop thinking." No. Thoughts continue.
- "I should feel relaxed." Sometimes you will; sometimes you won't.
- "I'm bad at meditation because my mind is busy." A busy mind simply gives you more opportunities to practice returning attention.

5. Consider guided resources
Many professionals find guided sessions easier initially:
- Waking Up (Sam Harris)
- Headspace
- Calm
- Ten Percent Happier

6. Track outcomes that matter
Rather than asking, "Was the meditation good?"
Ask:
- Was I more present in meetings?
- Did I react less impulsively under stress?
- Was I able to focus on one thing for longer?
- Did I notice distractions sooner?

A simple first-month plan:
- Week 1: 5 minutes daily, focus on breath.
- Week 2: 7–8 minutes daily.
- Week 3: Add one mindful pause before a major meeting.
- Week 4: 10 minutes daily plus mindful listening in conversations.

For someone in senior consulting leadership, one of the biggest benefits is often not relaxation but improved attention, emotional regulation under pressure, and greater awareness of automatic reactions during high-stakes interactions. Those skills tend to compound over time, much like physical fitness or executive presence.
