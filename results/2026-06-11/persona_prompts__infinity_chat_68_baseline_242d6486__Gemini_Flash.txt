Give me a tip for managing a team of coworkers.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here is one of the most effective tips for managing a team of coworkers (especially if you were recently promoted from within the team):

### **Shift from "Doer" to "Enabler" (The "How Can I Help?" Mindset)**

When you transition into managing coworkers, the biggest trap is trying to remain the "super-worker" who solves every problem and does the heavy lifting. This leads to micromanagement, burnout, and resentment from your team.

Instead, adopt the mindset that **your success is now measured by *their* success, not your individual output.**

#### **How to put this into practice:**
*   **Ask, don't tell:** Instead of giving step-by-step instructions, present the goal and ask: *"How do you think we should approach this?"* This builds trust and ownership.
*   **Clear the obstacles:** In your 1-on-1 meetings, make your signature question: *"What is blocking your progress right now, and how can I help remove it?"* (This could be a bottleneck in another department, a lack of tools, or conflicting priorities).
*   **Protect their time:** Buffer your team from unnecessary administrative noise, endless meetings, and shifting priorities from upper management so they can focus on their actual work.

**Why this works:** 
When your former peers see that your primary goal is to make *their* jobs easier and help *them* look good, any awkwardness about your shift in authority quickly melts away. They will view you as an ally, not just a boss.
