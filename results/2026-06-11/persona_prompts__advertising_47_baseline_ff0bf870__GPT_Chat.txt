Tips for running a marathon.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Running a marathon is as much about preparation and pacing as it is about fitness. Here are some key tips:

Training
- Follow a structured training plan that gradually increases your weekly mileage.
- Build your long run progressively, typically peaking at 18–22 miles (29–35 km) depending on your plan.
- Increase total weekly mileage gradually to reduce injury risk.
- Include easy runs, long runs, and occasional speed or tempo workouts.
- Schedule rest days and recovery weeks.

Before Race Day
- Practice your race-day nutrition and hydration during training. Don't try anything new on race day.
- Get adequate sleep in the days leading up to the race.
- Taper your training in the final 2–3 weeks to allow your body to recover and strengthen.
- Prepare your gear in advance, including shoes, clothing, and race bib.

Nutrition and Hydration
- Eat a carbohydrate-rich meal before the race if it works well for you.
- Stay hydrated, but avoid overdrinking.
- Take in carbohydrates during the race (such as gels, chews, or sports drinks) according to your needs, often around 30–60 grams per hour for many runners.
- Practice fueling during long training runs.

Pacing
- Start slower than feels necessary. Many runners go out too fast and struggle later.
- Aim for an even pace or a slightly slower first half than second half.
- Avoid getting caught up in the excitement of the start.

During the Race
- Break the race into smaller segments mentally.
- Focus on maintaining good form: relaxed shoulders, efficient arm swing, and steady breathing.
- Walk briefly through aid stations if it helps you hydrate and fuel more effectively.
- Expect some discomfort, especially in the later miles, and focus on staying consistent.

Mental Strategies
- Have a plan for difficult moments.
- Use positive and realistic self-talk.
- Focus on reaching the next mile marker, aid station, or landmark rather than thinking about the entire distance.

After the Finish
- Keep moving for a few minutes if possible.
- Rehydrate and eat a mix of carbohydrates and protein.
- Allow yourself time to recover before returning to intense training.
