── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I work full-time in a fast food job with an unpredictable schedule.

What are the best ways to improve indoor air quality?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
If you work full-time with an unpredictable schedule, the most effective air-quality improvements are usually the ones that work continuously with minimal effort.

In order of impact:

1. Use a HEPA air purifier
- A quality air purifier with a true HEPA filter can significantly reduce dust, pollen, pet dander, smoke particles, and some airborne germs.
- Place it in the room where you spend the most time, especially your bedroom.
- Run it continuously on a low or automatic setting.
- Choose a unit sized appropriately for the room.

2. Improve ventilation
- Open windows when outdoor air quality is good.
- Use kitchen exhaust fans while cooking.
- Use bathroom exhaust fans during and after showers.
- If your home has an HVAC system, make sure it brings in some fresh air if designed to do so.

3. Control humidity
- Aim for indoor humidity around 30–50%.
- High humidity encourages mold and dust mites.
- Low humidity can irritate airways.
- Use a dehumidifier or humidifier if needed.

4. Upgrade HVAC filtration
- If you have central heating or air conditioning, use a high-quality filter that your system can handle.
- Replace filters on schedule.
- A MERV 11–13 filter is often a good balance, though you should confirm compatibility with your system.

5. Reduce pollution at the source
- Avoid smoking indoors.
- Minimize candles, incense, and unnecessary aerosol sprays.
- Store chemicals, paints, and solvents properly.
- Choose lower-VOC paints and products when possible.

6. Keep dust under control
- Vacuum regularly using a vacuum with a HEPA filter.
- Damp-dust surfaces rather than dry dusting.
- Wash bedding regularly.
- If your schedule is busy, prioritizing the bedroom often gives the biggest benefit.

7. Address mold and water problems quickly
- Fix leaks promptly.
- Clean visible mold safely if it's a small area.
- Investigate persistent musty odors.

8. Monitor air quality
- Consider a simple indoor air-quality monitor that tracks particulate matter, humidity, and carbon dioxide.
- A carbon monoxide detector is essential if you have fuel-burning appliances.

For someone with limited time and an unpredictable work schedule, the highest-return approach is usually:
- A HEPA purifier in the bedroom.
- Good HVAC filters (if you have central air).
- Consistent use of kitchen and bathroom exhaust fans.
- Maintaining humidity between 30–50%.
