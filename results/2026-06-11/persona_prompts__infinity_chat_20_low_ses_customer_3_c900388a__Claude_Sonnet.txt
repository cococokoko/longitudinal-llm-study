## Arguments some make against mandating equal pay

- Enforcement is complex since jobs differ in subtle ways
- May create administrative burdens for businesses

- Wage differences sometimes reflect legitimate productivity differences

## However, strong counterarguments exist

- Discrimination is a **market failure** that markets don't reliably self-correct
- Evidence shows persistent pay gaps even controlling for job characteristics
- Equal pay laws have demonstrably reduced discrimination in many contexts
- Workers often lack information or bargaining power to negotiate effectively
- Discrimination imposes real economic costs on individuals and society

- The economic evidence doesn't strongly support the claim it harms economies
