Give a numerical example to illustrate the concept of partial derivative.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
A partial derivative measures how a function changes with respect to one variable while keeping the other variables fixed.

Example:

Suppose the temperature in a room is modeled by

T(x, y) = 2x² + 3xy + y²

where:
- x is the east-west position,
- y is the north-south position,
- T is the temperature.

Partial derivative with respect to x:

Treat y as a constant and differentiate only with respect to x:

∂T/∂x = 4x + 3y

At the point (x, y) = (1, 2):

∂T/∂x(1,2) = 4(1) + 3(2) = 10

Interpretation:
- If you move slightly in the x-direction while keeping y = 2 fixed, the temperature increases at approximately 10 units per unit increase in x.

Partial derivative with respect to y:

Treat x as a constant and differentiate only with respect to y:

∂T/∂y = 3x + 2y

At (1, 2):

∂T/∂y(1,2) = 3(1) + 2(2) = 7

Interpretation:
- If you move slightly in the y-direction while keeping x = 1 fixed, the temperature increases at approximately 7 units per unit increase in y.

Summary:
- Function: T(x, y) = 2x² + 3xy + y²
- ∂T/∂x = 4x + 3y, and at (1,2) it equals 10
- ∂T/∂y = 3x + 2y, and at (1,2) it equals 7

This shows that the rate of change can be different depending on which variable you vary while holding the others constant.
