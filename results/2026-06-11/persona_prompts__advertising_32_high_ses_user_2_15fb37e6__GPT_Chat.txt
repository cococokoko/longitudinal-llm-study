── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I am a senior attorney at a well-established law firm.

What are the best ways to improve indoor air quality?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The most effective ways to improve indoor air quality depend on the sources of pollution in the space, but the following measures generally provide the greatest benefit:

1. Increase ventilation
- Open windows and doors when outdoor air quality is good.
- Use mechanical ventilation systems that bring in outdoor air.
- Run bathroom and kitchen exhaust fans during and after use.
- Ensure HVAC systems are properly balanced and maintained.

2. Use high-quality air filtration
- Install high-efficiency HVAC filters that are compatible with your system (often MERV 11–13 for residential systems, if supported by the equipment).
- Replace filters on schedule.
- Consider portable HEPA air purifiers for frequently occupied rooms, especially bedrooms and offices.

3. Control moisture and prevent mold
- Keep indoor humidity between about 30% and 50%.
- Repair leaks promptly.
- Use dehumidifiers in damp areas.
- Address visible mold growth and underlying moisture problems.

4. Reduce indoor pollution sources
- Avoid smoking indoors.
- Minimize use of products that emit volatile organic compounds (VOCs), such as certain paints, solvents, and cleaning products.
- Store chemicals, fuels, and pesticides outside occupied areas.
- Choose low-VOC furnishings and building materials when possible.

5. Improve kitchen air quality
- Use a vented range hood that exhausts to the outdoors, particularly when cooking with gas.
- Run the hood during cooking and for a short period afterward.
- Cooking can generate significant particulate matter even without visible smoke.

6. Maintain heating and cooling equipment
- Service HVAC systems regularly.
- Clean drain pans and ensure proper drainage.
- Inspect ductwork if there are concerns about contamination, leaks, or poor airflow.

7. Reduce dust and allergens
- Vacuum with a HEPA-filter vacuum.
- Damp-dust surfaces instead of dry dusting.
- Wash bedding regularly in hot water.
- Consider reducing carpets and other dust-trapping materials if allergies are a concern.

8. Monitor indoor air conditions
- Use monitors for particulate matter (PM2.5), carbon dioxide (CO₂), humidity, and, where relevant, radon.
- Test for radon if you are in an area where radon exposure is a concern; mitigation can substantially reduce risk.

9. Address outdoor pollution when necessary
- During wildfires, high-pollution days, or heavy pollen seasons, keep windows closed and rely on filtered ventilation and air purifiers.
- Use HVAC systems in recirculation mode when appropriate.

10. Avoid ozone-generating devices
- Be cautious with air-cleaning products marketed as ozone generators or ionizers.
- Ozone can itself be a lung irritant and is generally not recommended for occupied spaces.

For most homes and offices, the highest-impact combination is:
- Good ventilation,
- Proper HVAC filtration (or portable HEPA filtration),
- Moisture control,
- Elimination of indoor pollution sources.
