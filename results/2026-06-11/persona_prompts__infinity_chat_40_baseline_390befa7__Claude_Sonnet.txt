── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
My girlfriend is buying a house. I plan to marry her in a year or so. I don't know if it's fair for me to pay for the house or not. What's the best solution?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This is a really common situation and there are several reasonable approaches. Here are some things to consider:

## Key Questions to Think Through

- **Are you moving in together?** If so, you'll likely benefit from the house
- **What are your relative financial situations?** Equal contribution isn't always the fairest approach
- **What happens if you don't get married?** This is important to plan for honestly

## Practical Options

**Option 1: Contribute to shared expenses, not the mortgage**
- Pay rent/utilities while she owns the house
- Keeps finances cleaner if things don't work out

**Option 2: Formal co-ownership agreement**
- Both contribute to mortgage
- Legal documentation of each person's equity stake
- Protects both of you

**Option 3: Wait until marriage**
- Refinance together after marrying
- Simpler legally but she carries the burden alone initially

**Option 4: Gift/loan with documentation**
- If you contribute a lump sum, document it clearly

## Strong Recommendations

- **Talk to a real estate attorney** before any money changes hands
- **Have an honest conversation with your girlfriend** about expectations
- **Don't assume marriage will happen** - protect both of you legally regardless

The biggest risk is contributing money without legal protection and having the relationship not work out.
