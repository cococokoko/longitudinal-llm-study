# Best Practices for Organic Gardening

Here's a straightforward overview:

## Soil Health
- **Compost regularly** - builds nutrients and beneficial microorganisms
- **Test soil pH** - most vegetables prefer 6.0-7.0
- **Avoid tilling excessively** - disrupts soil structure and earthworms
- **Use cover crops** - clover, rye to prevent erosion and add nitrogen

## Pest & Weed Management
- **Companion planting** - basil near tomatoes, marigolds as pest deterrents
- **Hand-pick pests** when populations are manageable
- **Mulch heavily** - suppresses weeds and retains moisture
- **Encourage beneficial insects** - ladybugs, lacewings

## Watering
- **Water deeply and infrequently** rather than shallow/frequent
- **Morning watering** reduces fungal disease risk
- **Drip irrigation** maximizes efficiency

## Fertilizing
- **Compost tea, fish emulsion, kelp** as natural nutrient sources
- **Rotate crops** to prevent nutrient depletion

## General Tips
- Start with **healthy, disease-resistant varieties**
- Keep a **garden journal** to track what works
- **Patience** - organic systems improve significantly over 2-3 seasons
