# Different Views on Discipleship

Discipleship is understood quite differently across theological traditions, philosophical frameworks, and cultural contexts. Here's an overview of major perspectives:

---

## Christian Theological Traditions

### Traditional/Orthodox View
- Formal relationship between teacher and student
- Emphasis on **obedience, submission, and imitation**
- Rooted in Jesus's call to "follow me"
- Lifelong commitment to spiritual formation

### Evangelical View
- Focus on **intentional one-on-one mentoring**
- Bible study and accountability central
- Often structured programs (e.g., Navigators model)
- Goal: reproducing disciples who make disciples

### Catholic View
- Discipleship embedded in **sacramental community life**
- Formation through liturgy, tradition, and spiritual direction
- Less individualistic than evangelical models

### Liberation Theology View
- Discipleship expressed through **solidarity with the poor**
- Following Jesus means confronting systemic injustice
- Praxis-oriented (action + reflection)

---

## Philosophical/Secular Parallels

- **Socratic tradition** - questioning, dialogue, intellectual formation
- **Mentorship models** in professional settings
- **Apprenticeship** - skill transmission through relationship

---

## Key Tensions in the Debate

| Question | Competing Views |
|----------|----------------|
| Individual vs. communal? | Personal growth vs. collective formation |
| Structured vs. organic? | Programs vs. natural relationships |
| Cognitive vs. behavioral? | Belief change vs. lifestyle change |
| Exclusive vs. inclusive? | Narrow commitment vs. broad engagement |
