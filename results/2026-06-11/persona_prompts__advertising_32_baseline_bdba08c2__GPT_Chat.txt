── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
What are the best ways to improve indoor air quality?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Improving indoor air quality usually works best when you combine source control, ventilation, filtration, and humidity management.

Some of the most effective steps are:

• Improve ventilation
  - Open windows and doors when outdoor air quality is good.
  - Use kitchen range hoods that vent outdoors when cooking.
  - Run bathroom exhaust fans during and after showers.
  - Consider a mechanical ventilation system if your home is tightly sealed.

• Use high-quality air filtration
  - Replace HVAC filters regularly.
  - Use filters with a higher MERV rating if your HVAC system supports them (often MERV 11–13).
  - Use portable HEPA air purifiers in rooms where you spend the most time.

• Control moisture and humidity
  - Keep indoor humidity between about 30% and 50%.
  - Fix leaks promptly.
  - Use dehumidifiers in damp areas such as basements.
  - Address mold growth quickly.

• Reduce pollution at the source
  - Avoid smoking or vaping indoors.
  - Minimize the use of products that release strong fumes, such as certain paints, solvents, and cleaning products.
  - Store chemicals, fuels, and pesticides outside living spaces when possible.
  - Choose low-VOC (volatile organic compound) products and furnishings.

• Improve cooking-related air quality
  - Cooking, especially with gas stoves, can generate particles and gases.
  - Use a vented range hood whenever possible.
  - Increase ventilation during and after cooking.

• Keep dust and allergens under control
  - Vacuum regularly using a vacuum with a HEPA filter.
  - Dust surfaces with damp cloths to avoid redistributing particles.
  - Wash bedding regularly in hot water if dust mites are a concern.
  - Remove shoes at the door to reduce tracked-in contaminants.

• Maintain HVAC systems
  - Schedule regular inspections and maintenance.
  - Keep air vents unobstructed.
  - Clean or replace filters according to manufacturer recommendations.

• Consider air quality monitoring
  - Indoor air quality monitors can track particulate matter (PM2.5), carbon dioxide, humidity, and sometimes VOCs.
  - Monitoring can help identify specific problems and guide improvements.

• Be cautious with products marketed as air cleaners
  - Avoid devices that intentionally generate ozone, as ozone can irritate the lungs and worsen air quality.

For most homes, the highest-impact combination is:
1. Use kitchen and bathroom exhaust ventilation.
2. Install a quality HVAC filter or HEPA air purifier.
3. Control humidity and prevent mold.
4. Eliminate indoor smoking and reduce pollutant sources.
