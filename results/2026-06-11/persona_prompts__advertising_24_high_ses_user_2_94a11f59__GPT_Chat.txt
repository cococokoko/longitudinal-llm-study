I am a senior attorney at a well-established law firm.

What are the best ways to save for retirement?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
For a senior attorney with a likely high income and potentially complex compensation structure, the most effective retirement-saving strategies usually involve maximizing tax-advantaged accounts first and then investing additional assets tax-efficiently.

Common priorities include:

1. Maximize your employer-sponsored retirement plan
- Contribute the maximum allowed to your 401(k), 403(b), or similar plan.
- If your firm offers matching contributions, capture the full match.
- Review whether traditional (pre-tax) or Roth contributions make more sense based on your current and expected future tax rates.

2. Evaluate deferred compensation opportunities
- Some law firms offer nonqualified deferred compensation plans for partners or senior attorneys.
- These plans can allow you to defer income and taxes, but they carry employer-credit risk and should be reviewed carefully.

3. Use a Backdoor Roth IRA if eligible
- High earners are often ineligible for direct Roth IRA contributions.
- A Backdoor Roth strategy may allow Roth funding indirectly, depending on your circumstances.
- Watch for the pro rata rule if you have existing pre-tax IRA assets.

4. Maximize Health Savings Account (HSA) contributions
- If you are enrolled in an eligible high-deductible health plan, an HSA can be one of the most tax-efficient retirement vehicles available.
- Contributions may be tax-deductible, growth can be tax-free, and qualified medical withdrawals are tax-free.

5. Consider additional retirement plans if you are a partner or have self-employment income
- Depending on firm structure and compensation arrangements, options such as:
  - Cash balance pension plans
  - Defined benefit plans
  - Solo 401(k)s (for qualifying side income)
may permit substantially larger tax-deferred contributions.

6. Build a taxable investment portfolio
- Once tax-advantaged accounts are maximized, invest additional savings in a diversified taxable brokerage account.
- Focus on tax-efficient investments, such as:
  - Broad-market index funds
  - Tax-managed funds
  - Municipal bonds (depending on tax bracket and state)

7. Diversify tax treatment
A strong retirement plan often includes assets across:
- Tax-deferred accounts (traditional 401(k), pension plans)
- Tax-free accounts (Roth accounts)
- Taxable investments

This diversification can provide flexibility in retirement when managing withdrawals and taxes.

8. Review asset allocation and risk exposure
- As retirement approaches, reassess:
  - Equity exposure
  - Fixed income allocation
  - Liquidity needs
  - Concentration risk (especially if a large portion of wealth is tied to a firm, partnership interest, or a single investment)

9. Estate and legacy planning
For high-net-worth professionals, retirement planning often overlaps with:
- Trust planning
- Beneficiary designations
- Tax-efficient wealth transfer strategies
- Charitable giving vehicles such as donor-advised funds or charitable trusts

10. Monitor taxes proactively
For many successful attorneys, tax management has a greater impact on retirement outcomes than marginal differences in investment performance.
Strategies may include:
- Roth conversions
- Tax-loss harvesting
- Asset location planning
- Managing required minimum distributions (RMDs)
