Here are 3 short, actionable tips for self-development:

1. **Embrace the "Two-Minute Rule"**
   If a task takes less than two minutes to do (like washing your coffee cup, replying to an email, or putting away your shoes), do it immediately. This builds momentum, prevents mental clutter, and trains your brain to take action rather than procrastinate.

2. **Practice "Habit Stacking"**
   To build a new positive habit, anchor it to an existing one. Use the formula: *"After I [Current Habit], I will [New Habit]."* For example: "After I pour my morning coffee, I will write down my top 3 priorities for the day." This uses established neural pathways to make new behaviors stick.

3. **Do a Weekly "Brain Dump"**
   Every Sunday, spend 5 minutes writing down everything cluttering your mind—tasks, worries, ideas, and random thoughts. Once it is on paper, categorize it into "actionable" or "let it go." Clearing your mental RAM reduces anxiety and gives you absolute clarity for the week ahead.
