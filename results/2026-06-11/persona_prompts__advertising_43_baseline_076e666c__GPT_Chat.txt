── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How to plan an environmentally friendly wedding?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Planning an environmentally friendly wedding involves reducing waste, conserving resources, and making thoughtful choices throughout the event. Here are some practical ways to do it:

1. Choose a Sustainable Venue
- Select a venue close to most guests to reduce travel emissions.
- Consider outdoor venues, botanical gardens, community centers, or eco-certified locations.
- Look for venues that use renewable energy, recycling programs, or water-saving practices.

2. Limit Guest Travel Impact
- Hold the ceremony and reception at the same location.
- Encourage carpooling, public transportation, or shuttle services.
- If many guests are traveling, provide information on eco-friendly lodging options.

3. Send Digital Invitations
- Use electronic invitations and RSVP systems.
- If you prefer printed invitations, choose recycled paper and environmentally friendly inks.

4. Select Sustainable Attire
- Rent wedding attire or buy secondhand.
- Choose outfits that can be worn again after the wedding.
- Support designers who use sustainable fabrics and ethical production practices.

5. Source Local and Seasonal Food
- Work with caterers who prioritize local ingredients.
- Serve seasonal dishes to reduce transportation impacts.
- Consider reducing the amount of meat served, as plant-based options generally have a lower environmental footprint.

6. Reduce Food Waste
- Estimate guest counts carefully.
- Arrange for safe donation of surplus food where local regulations permit.
- Compost food scraps if possible.

7. Use Eco-Friendly Decorations
- Rent décor instead of buying disposable items.
- Use potted plants, reusable decorations, or locally grown flowers.
- Avoid single-use plastics, balloons, glitter, and other difficult-to-recycle materials.

8. Choose Sustainable Flowers
- Buy locally grown, seasonal flowers.
- Consider dried flowers or potted plants.
- Donate arrangements after the event to hospitals, care homes, or community organizations if appropriate.

9. Minimize Single-Use Items
- Use reusable plates, glassware, and linens.
- Avoid disposable favors and packaging.
- Set up clearly labeled recycling and compost bins.

10. Give Thoughtful Wedding Favors
- Consider edible favors, seed packets, small plants, or charitable donations made in guests' honor.
- Skip favors entirely if they are unlikely to be used.

11. Choose Responsible Vendors
- Ask vendors about their sustainability practices.
- Work with local businesses when possible.
- Prioritize vendors who minimize waste and use environmentally responsible materials.

12. Consider Rings and Jewelry
- Choose recycled metals or ethically sourced gemstones.
- Consider vintage or heirloom jewelry.

13. Reduce Energy Use
- Use natural daylight when possible.
- Choose energy-efficient lighting.
- Limit unnecessary power consumption during the event.

14. Manage Waste After the Wedding
- Donate leftover decorations, flowers, and supplies.
- Recycle materials appropriately.
- Compost organic waste when facilities are available.

Sample priorities if you're working with a limited budget:
- Digital invitations.
- Local venue.
- Seasonal catering.
- Rented décor and attire.
- Reusable tableware.
- Reduced guest travel.

An environmentally friendly wedding does not require perfection. Focusing on a few high-impact areas—travel, food, waste reduction, and reusable materials—can significantly lower the event's environmental footprint while still creating a memorable celebration.
