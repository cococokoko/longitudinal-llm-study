Learning guitar is a great skill to build, and steady practice matters more than long practice sessions.

A simple beginner roadmap:

1. Learn the parts of the guitar
- Strings (E, A, D, G, B, E)
- Frets
- Pickups (if electric)
- Tuning pegs

2. Get comfortable tuning
- Use a tuner app or clip-on tuner.
- Practice tuning every time you play.

3. Learn a few basic chords
Start with:
- Em
- E
- Am
- C
- G
- D

These chords appear in thousands of songs.

4. Practice chord changes
- Switch slowly between two chords.
- Focus on accuracy before speed.
- Spend 5–10 minutes daily on transitions.

5. Learn basic strumming
Try a simple pattern:
- Down, down, down, down
Then:
- Down, down-up, up-down-up

Use a metronome at a slow tempo.

6. Build finger strength
- Practice simple exercises up and down the strings.
- Expect some fingertip soreness at first.

7. Learn easy songs
Look for songs that use 3–4 chords. Playing real music keeps practice motivating.

Example 20-minute daily routine:
- 3 minutes: tuning
- 5 minutes: finger exercise
- 7 minutes: chords and chord changes
- 5 minutes: play a song
