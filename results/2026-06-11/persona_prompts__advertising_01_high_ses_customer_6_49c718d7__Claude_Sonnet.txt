# Learning Guitar as a Busy Professional

Given your background, you likely appreciate **structured, efficient approaches** to skill acquisition. Here's a practical framework:

## Getting Started

**Choose your instrument**
- Acoustic - more forgiving for beginners, no equipment needed
- Electric - easier on fingers, better for certain genres
- Classical - excellent technique foundation

**Essential first investments**
- Decent beginner guitar ($150-400 range is the sweet spot)
- Tuner app (GuitarTuna is free and reliable)
- Pick variety pack

## Efficient Learning Path

**Weeks 1-4: Foundations**
- Basic chord shapes (G, C, D, Em, Am)
- Proper posture and hand position
- Simple strumming patterns

**Months 2-3: Building repertoire**
- Chord transitions
- 3-4 songs you actually enjoy
- Basic music theory

## Learning Resources
- **Structured:** JustinGuitar.com (free, highly regarded)
- **Flexible:** Fender Play or Yousician apps
- **Accelerated:** Weekly lessons with a local teacher

## Realistic Expectations
- **15-20 minutes daily** beats 2-hour weekend sessions
- Fingertip soreness is normal for 2-3 weeks
- Most people play recognizable songs within **4-6 weeks**

## The Key Insight
Consistency matters far more than volume. Even **10 minutes daily** compounds significantly.
