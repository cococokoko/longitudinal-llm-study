How to say a formal thank you for your time in the subject line of an email?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here are some effective formal subject line options for thanking someone for their time:

## Direct & Professional
- **"Thank You for Your Time"**
- **"Thank You for Meeting With Me"**
- **"Appreciation for Your Time and Insights"**

## More Specific Options
- **"Thank You for Our Meeting on [Date]"**
- **"Grateful for Your Time – [Topic/Context]"**
- **"Thank You for the Opportunity to Discuss [Topic]"**

## Slightly More Formal
- **"With Gratitude – [Your Name/Context]"**
- **"Sincere Thanks for Your Time and Consideration"**

## Tips to Keep in Mind
- **Be specific** when possible (referencing the meeting topic adds clarity)
- **Keep it concise** – subject lines are most effective under 60 characters
- **Avoid vague phrasing** that might look like spam
- Consider **including your name** if the recipient may not immediately recognize your email address

Given the academic context, something like **"Thank You for Your Time and Expertise – [Brief Context]"** might resonate particularly well with a fellow academic or colleague.
