Improving productivity while working from home often comes down to creating structure, reducing distractions, and managing energy effectively. Here are some practical strategies:

• Establish a dedicated workspace
  - Use a specific area for work if possible.
  - Keep your workspace organized and comfortable.
  - Separate work and personal activities to create mental boundaries.

• Set a consistent schedule
  - Start and finish work at regular times.
  - Plan your day in advance.
  - Include breaks and lunch in your schedule.

• Prioritize tasks
  - Identify the most important tasks each day.
  - Break large projects into smaller steps.
  - Use a task management system, whether digital or paper-based.

• Minimize distractions
  - Silence unnecessary notifications.
  - Set specific times to check email and messages.
  - Communicate your work hours to household members when appropriate.

• Use time-management techniques
  - Try methods such as the Pomodoro Technique (e.g., 25 minutes of focused work followed by a short break).
  - Time-block your calendar for deep work, meetings, and administrative tasks.

• Maintain communication
  - Keep colleagues informed about progress and availability.
  - Participate in team check-ins and meetings.
  - Clarify expectations and deadlines.

• Take care of your physical and mental well-being
  - Stand up and move regularly.
  - Stay hydrated and eat balanced meals.
  - Get adequate sleep and maintain healthy routines.

• Review and improve
  - At the end of each week, evaluate what worked and what did not.
  - Adjust your schedule and habits based on results.

• First 15 minutes: Review priorities and plan the day.
• Morning: Focus on the most important work.
• Midday: Take a proper break.
• Afternoon: Meetings, collaboration, and secondary tasks.
• Last 15 minutes: Review progress and prepare for tomorrow.
