# Mastering the Art of Cooking Steak

Here's a practical guide that works around a busy schedule too!

---

## Choosing Your Cut
| Cut | Best For | Difficulty |
|-----|----------|------------|
| Ribeye | Rich flavor, forgiving | Easy |
| NY Strip | Balance of lean/fat | Easy |
| Filet Mignon | Tender, mild | Medium |
| Flank/Skirt | Budget-friendly | Medium |

---

## The Fundamentals

### Before Cooking
- **Bring to room temperature** - 30 minutes out of fridge
- **Pat completely dry** with paper towels
- **Season generously** - salt and pepper at minimum
- **Salt early** if possible (even the night before)

### Cooking Methods
**Pan Sear (Quickest - 10 minutes)**
- Cast iron skillet works best
- High heat with butter or oil
- 2-3 minutes per side

**Reverse Sear (Most Foolproof)**
- Low oven first, then quick sear
- Great for thicker cuts

---

## Doneness Guide
- **Rare** - 125°F
- **Medium Rare** - 135°F *(most recommended)*
- **Medium** - 145°F
- **Well Done** - 160°F+

---

## Critical Tips
- **Always rest** 5 minutes before cutting
- Use a **meat thermometer** to remove guesswork
- **Don't overcrowd** the pan
