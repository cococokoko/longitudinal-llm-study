What are the best resources for starting a plant-based diet?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
If you're starting a plant-based diet, it helps to use resources that focus on practical meals, nutrition basics, and meal prep. Since you work long hours in a warehouse, resources that emphasize easy, filling, portable foods may be especially useful.

Good beginner resources:

- Physicians Committee for Responsible Medicine (PCRM)
  - Website: pcrm.org
  - Offers free plant-based starter guides, meal plans, recipes, and nutrition information.

- NutritionFacts.org
  - Evidence-based nutrition information created by Dr. Michael Greger.
  - Useful for understanding the health effects of different foods.

- Forks Over Knives
  - Website, recipes, meal planner, and educational content.
  - Focuses on whole-food, plant-based eating.

- The Vegan Society
  - Provides beginner guides, shopping tips, and nutrition information.
  - Helpful even if your goal is simply eating more plant-based foods rather than becoming fully vegan.

Books:
- "How Not to Die" by Michael Greger, MD
  - Explains the science behind plant-based eating and includes practical guidance.

- "Plant-Based on a Budget" by Toni Okamoto
  - Focuses on affordable, simple meals.

- "The Fiber Fueled Cookbook" by Will Bulsiewicz, MD
  - Offers recipes and guidance for increasing plant foods gradually.

YouTube channels:
- Pick Up Limes
  - Beginner-friendly recipes and nutrition information.

- Rainbow Plant Life
  - Flavorful meals with clear cooking instructions.

- Cheap Lazy Vegan
  - Simple, budget-conscious meal ideas.

For someone with a physically demanding job:
- Prioritize protein-rich foods such as beans, lentils, tofu, tempeh, edamame, soy milk, and pea-protein products.
- Include calorie-dense foods like nuts, seeds, nut butters, oats, potatoes, rice, and whole-grain breads to maintain energy.
- Consider meal-prepping items that travel well:
  - Bean-and-rice bowls
  - Lentil chili
  - Overnight oats
  - Peanut butter sandwiches
  - Hummus wraps
  - Trail mix and fruit

Nutrients to pay attention to:
- Vitamin B12 (often requires supplementation)
- Iron
- Calcium
- Vitamin D
- Omega-3 fats (flaxseed, chia seeds, walnuts, or algae-based supplements)

A simple way to start is to replace one meal per day with a plant-based option for a few weeks before making larger changes. This tends to be easier to sustain than a complete overnight switch.
