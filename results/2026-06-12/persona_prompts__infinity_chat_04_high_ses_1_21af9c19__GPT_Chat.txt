Yes. André Gorz sits at the intersection of Marxism, ecology, technology, labor theory, and critiques of political economy, which makes him especially fertile for original thesis work. One challenge is that many discussions focus on a few well-known themes (the end of work, basic income, political ecology), so originality often comes from connecting Gorz to contemporary debates.

Some possible directions:

• Gorz and the politics of artificial intelligence
  - Gorz argued that automation undermines the centrality of wage labor.
  - Examine whether contemporary AI confirms or complicates his account of the "abolition of work."
  - Compare Gorz with recent theorists of platform capitalism, digital labor, and AI-driven productivity.

Research question:
- Does generative AI strengthen Gorz's argument that capitalism increasingly depends on activities that cannot be adequately measured by labor-time?

• Gorz as a theorist of immaterial labor before the Italian autonomists
  - Scholars often emphasize Negri, Lazzarato, and Virno.
  - Investigate whether Gorz anticipated key claims about knowledge, communication, and social cooperation as productive forces.
  - Analyze similarities and differences regarding value theory.

Research question:
- Was Gorz an overlooked precursor to theories of immaterial labor, or does his framework ultimately diverge from autonomist Marxism?

• Gorz and Marx's "general intellect"
  - Connect Gorz's later work to the Fragment on Machines in the Grundrisse.
  - Explore how collective knowledge functions as a productive force.
  - Compare his reading with contemporary interpretations from post-operaismo and critical political economy.

Research question:
- How does Gorz reinterpret the general intellect, and what political implications follow from his interpretation?

• The ecological critique of value
  - Gorz is often read as an ecological socialist, but less attention is paid to how ecology challenges labor-based value production.
  - Explore tensions between ecological limits and endless accumulation.
  - Compare Gorz with Kohei Saito, John Bellamy Foster, or Jason Moore.

Research question:
- Can Gorz's ecological thought be understood as a revision of Marxian value theory?

• Gorz versus productivist Marxism
  - Many Marxist traditions assume liberation through expanded production.
  - Gorz was skeptical of this orientation.
  - Study his critique of both capitalism and traditional socialist developmentalism.

Research question:
- Does Gorz offer a post-productivist Marxism, and what are its implications for socialist strategy?

• The concept of autonomy in Gorz
  - Autonomy is central across his career.
  - Trace its development from existentialism and Sartre through Marxism and ecology.
  - Examine whether autonomy is compatible with collective socialist planning.

Research question:

• Gorz and contemporary degrowth theory

  - Explore whether degrowth inherits Gorz's political project or selectively appropriates it.

Research question:

Research question:
