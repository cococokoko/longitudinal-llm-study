- Create a dedicated work zone

  - Use a desk, small table, room divider, or shelving unit to create a visual boundary between work and home life.

  - Position your monitor at eye level.
  - Use a chair that supports your back.

- Improve lighting
  - Natural light can help reduce eye fatigue and improve alertness.
  - If natural light is limited, add a desk lamp with adjustable brightness.

- Reduce distractions
  - Keep only frequently used items on your desk.
  - Use headphones or noise-canceling earbuds if household noise is common.
  - Silence nonessential notifications during focused work periods.

- Build around your childcare reality
  - Keep a small basket of quiet activities, books, or toys nearby for times when a child needs to be close while you work.
  - Consider a flexible task list with:
    - Deep-focus tasks for quieter periods
    - Administrative or routine tasks for times when interruptions are more likely

- Organize cables and supplies
  - Use cable clips, trays, or ties to reduce clutter.
  - Store supplies within arm's reach so you don't lose time searching for them.

- Use productivity tools
  - Calendar blocking can help you protect focused work periods.
  - Task managers or simple to-do lists can help you quickly resume work after interruptions.

- Establish visual signals
  - If your children are old enough, a simple signal (such as a colored card, lamp, or sign) can indicate when you're in a meeting versus available for questions.
