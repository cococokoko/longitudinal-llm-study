What is the difference between analysis and design? Can one begin to design without analysis? Why? Be concise.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
## Analysis vs. Design

| | Analysis | Design |
|---|---|---|
| **Focus** | **What** the system must do | **How** the system will do it |
| **Goal** | Understand the problem | Build the solution |
| **Output** | Requirements, models of reality | Blueprints, architecture, specifications |

---

## Can You Design Without Analysis?

**Technically yes, practically no.**

- Without analysis, you don't fully understand the **problem**, so you risk designing the **wrong solution**
- You may miss requirements, misunderstand constraints, or solve a symptom rather than the root cause
- The result is often **rework**, wasted resources, and system failure

> *"There is nothing so useless as doing efficiently that which should not be done at all."* — Peter Drucker

**In short:** Analysis defines *what problem to solve*; design defines *how to solve it*. Skipping analysis means you're guessing at the problem — making good design nearly impossible.
