── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I work full-time in a fast food job with an unpredictable schedule.

Effective ways to reduce household energy use.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
With a full-time job and an unpredictable schedule, the most effective energy-saving strategies are usually the ones that work automatically and don't require daily attention.

Here are some high-impact options:

• Use a programmable or smart thermostat
  - Automatically reduce heating or cooling when you're away.
  - Even modest temperature adjustments can noticeably lower energy use.
  - Smart thermostats are especially useful if your schedule changes often because many can be controlled from your phone.

• Switch to LED bulbs
  - LEDs use much less electricity than incandescent bulbs and last far longer.
  - Prioritize lights you use most often, such as kitchen, bathroom, and living room fixtures.

• Eliminate standby power use
  - TVs, game consoles, chargers, and other electronics can draw power even when not actively used.
  - Plug multiple devices into a power strip and switch it off when leaving home.

• Wash clothes with cold water when possible
  - Water heating is a significant energy expense.
  - Modern detergents generally work well in cold water.

• Run full loads
  - Use the dishwasher and washing machine only when reasonably full.
  - This reduces the energy and water used per item cleaned.

• Adjust water heater settings
  - Lowering the temperature slightly can reduce energy consumption while still providing comfortable hot water.
  - Check the manufacturer's recommendations before changing settings.

• Seal drafts
  - Weatherstripping around doors and caulking around windows can reduce heating and cooling losses.
  - This is often a relatively inexpensive improvement.

• Use curtains and blinds strategically
  - Close them during hot sunny periods to reduce cooling needs.
  - Open them during cooler seasons to take advantage of natural warmth and light.

• Replace HVAC filters regularly
  - Clean filters help heating and cooling systems operate more efficiently.
  - Set a phone reminder so you don't have to remember during busy work periods.

• Reduce dryer use
  - Air-dry some clothing items when practical.
  - Clean the dryer's lint filter after each load to improve efficiency.

If you're renting and can't make major upgrades, the biggest low-effort wins are usually:
1. Smart thermostat (if allowed).
2. LED bulbs.
3. Power strips for electronics.
4. Cold-water laundry.
5. Sealing drafts with removable weatherstripping.
