# Review: *The Last Frequency* (2024)

★★★★½

Director Mara Okonkwo delivers a quietly devastating science fiction drama that deserves to be mentioned alongside the great films of its genre.

Set in a near-future Norway, *The Last Frequency* follows a radio operator at a remote Arctic station who begins receiving transmissions she believes are coming from a parallel version of Earth — one where a catastrophic climate event was somehow avoided. What unfolds is less about the science and more about grief, specifically the grief of watching a world slowly fail while glimpsing what could have been.

Lead actress Sigrid Haugen gives a performance of remarkable restraint. She carries nearly every scene alone, and you feel every minute of her character's isolation without the film ever becoming tedious. The sparse, almost architectural score by composer Dayo Mensah haunts you long after the credits roll.

Where the film stumbles slightly is in its third act, which reaches for an emotional resolution that feels slightly too tidy given how carefully ambiguous everything before it was.

Still, *The Last Frequency* is the rare film that trusts its audience completely. It asks big questions about responsibility and regret and has the wisdom not to answer all of them.

**Not to be missed.**
