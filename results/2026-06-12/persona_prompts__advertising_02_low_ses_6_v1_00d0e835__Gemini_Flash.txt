── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I work in a warehouse position that requires long hours.

Best skin routine for dry skin.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Working in a warehouse is incredibly tough on the skin. Between the dry, climate-controlled (or non-climate-controlled) air, cardboard dust (which literally sucks moisture out of your hands and face), physical exertion, and long hours, your skin barrier takes a beating. 

To survive 10–12 hour shifts, your routine needs to focus on **barrier repair, deep hydration, and protection**. 

Here is a highly effective, low-maintenance routine designed specifically for dry skin in a warehouse environment.

---

### The Golden Rule for Warehouse Work: "Slugging" & Layering
Dry skin lacks oil, but it also suffers from **Transepidermal Water Loss (TEWL)**—meaning the dry warehouse air is actively pulling moisture out of your skin. You must layer your products: **Hydrate first (water), lock it in second (oil/cream).**

---

### Morning Routine (Before Your Shift)
*Goal: Create a protective shield against dust and dry air.*

1. **Cleanse (Gentle):** Do not use a foaming cleanser in the morning; it strips your natural oils. Wash your face with **lukewarm water only**, or use a very gentle, non-foaming hydrating cleanser.
   * *Product Recommendation:* **CeraVe Hydrating Facial Cleanser** or **La Roche-Posay Toleriane Hydrating Gentle Cleanser**.
2. **Hydrate (The "Damp Skin" Trick):** While your face is still damp, apply a hydrating serum. This binds water to your skin.
   * *Product Recommendation:* **The Ordinary Hyaluronic Acid 2% + B5** or **Cosrx Snail Mucin 96 Power Essence** (excellent for healing a damaged skin barrier).
3. **Moisturize (The Shield):** Apply a thick, ceramide-rich moisturizer. Ceramides act like mortar between your skin-cell "bricks," keeping moisture in and dust out.
   * *Product Recommendation:* **Vanicream Moisturizing Cream** (highly recommended for sensitive/dry skin) or **Illiyoon Cerato Ato Concentrate Cream**.
4. **Protect (Sunscreen):** Even if you are indoors, warehouse lighting and loading docks expose you to UV rays. Use a moisturizing sunscreen.
   * *Product Recommendation:* **Beauty of Joseon Relief Sun (SPF 50)** or **La Roche-Posay Double Repair Face Moisturizer UV**.

---

### Mid-Shift Maintenance (Quick & Easy)
You can’t do a full routine at work, but you can prevent the "tight, itchy" feeling.
* **The Hand/Face Savior:** Keep a tube of **Aquaphor Healing Ointment** or **CeraVe Healing Ointment** in your pocket. 
  * *How to use:* If your knuckles, lips, or the skin around your nose/eyes start cracking or feeling raw from cardboard dust, dab a tiny bit of this ointment on top of those areas. It acts as a physical barrier.
* **Avoid Face Mists:** Spraying water on your face in a dry warehouse can actually make your skin *drier* because the dry air evaporates the mist and pulls your skin's natural moisture out with it.

---

### Night Routine (After Your Shift)
*Goal: Wash away warehouse grime and deeply repair your skin while you sleep.*

1. **Double Cleanse (Crucial):** Warehouse dust, sweat, and sunscreen build up. You need to get this off without stripping your skin.
   * **Step 1:** Use an oil cleanser or cleansing balm to melt away the grime and sunscreen. (e.g., **Kose Softymo Speedy Cleansing Oil** or **Banila Co Clean It Zero**).
   * **Step 2:** Follow with your gentle morning cleanser to wash it all away.
2. **Hydrate:** Apply your Hyaluronic Acid or Snail Mucin to damp skin.
3. **Deep Moisturize:** Apply a generous layer of your heavy moisturizer (Vanicream or CeraVe).
4. **"Slug" (The Game Changer):** 2–3 nights a week, lock everything in by applying a very thin layer of **Vaseline** or **CeraVe Healing Ointment** over your moisturizer. This creates an occlusive seal, forcing your skin to absorb the moisture overnight. You will wake up with soft, plump skin.

---

### Don't Forget Your Hands and Body
Cardboard boxes are notorious for drying out hands and causing painful micro-cuts.
* **For Hands:** After washing your hands, immediately apply a heavy hand cream. **O'Keeffe's Working Hands** or **Neutrogena Norwegian Formula Hand Cream** are industry standards for a reason. Apply a thick layer before bed and wear cotton gloves to sleep if your hands are cracking.
* **For Body:** Shower in warm (not hot) water, and apply lotion (like **Eucerin Advanced Repair**) within 3 minutes of stepping out of the shower to lock in the moisture.
