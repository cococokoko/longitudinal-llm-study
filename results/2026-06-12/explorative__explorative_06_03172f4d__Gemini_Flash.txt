Because "best" depends entirely on **who** you are messaging and **why**, the best messaging tool varies by category. 

Here is the breakdown of the best messaging tools for 2024, categorized by how they are used:

---

### 1. Best for Personal & Everyday Use (Friends & Family)
*   **WhatsApp:** *The Global Standard.* 
    *   **Why it’s the best:** It has over 2 billion users, meaning almost everyone you know already has it. It offers end-to-end encryption, reliable voice/video calls, and works seamlessly across Android, iOS, and web.
    *   **Best for:** International texting and group chats.
*   **Signal:** *The Best for Privacy.*
    *   **Why it’s the best:** It is widely considered the most secure messaging app in the world. It is run by a non-profit, stores virtually no metadata, and has no ads or trackers.
    *   **Best for:** Security-conscious users.
*   **Apple iMessage:** *The Best for Apple Users.*
    *   **Why it’s the best:** Deeply integrated into iOS and macOS. It offers high-quality media sharing, games, and Memojis. (Note: It is finally adopting RCS in 2024 to make texting Android users much better).

---

### 2. Best for Workplace & Business Collaboration
*   **Slack:** *The Best for Tech & Fast-Paced Teams.*
    *   **Why it’s the best:** It pioneered channel-based communication. It integrates with almost every work tool (Google Drive, Zoom, Jira, Salesforce) and has powerful search capabilities and automation "huddles."
    *   **Best for:** Startups, tech companies, and media agencies.
*   **Microsoft Teams:** *The Best for Corporate/Enterprise.*
    *   **Why it’s the best:** If your company already uses Microsoft 365 (Word, Excel, Outlook), Teams is a no-brainer. Its video conferencing is incredibly robust, and file collaboration within the app is seamless.
    *   **Best for:** Large corporations and traditional businesses.
*   **Google Chat:** *The Best Budget/Simple Option.*
    *   **Why it’s the best:** It is included free with Google Workspace. It is simpler than Slack but integrates perfectly with Google Docs and Meet.

---

### 3. Best for Communities, Gaming, & Large Groups
*   **Discord:** *The Best for Communities & Gamers.*
    *   **Why it’s the best:** It uses "servers" divided into text and voice channels. Its voice-chat quality is unmatched, and it allows users to hop in and out of voice rooms effortlessly. It is now used for everything from gaming to study groups and crypto communities.
    *   **Best for:** Large online communities and hanging out virtually.
*   **Telegram:** *The Best for Large Broadcasts & Features.*
    *   **Why it’s the best:** It supports groups of up to 200,000 people and channels for broadcasting to millions. It is incredibly fast, supports massive file sharing (up to 2GB), and has a highly customizable interface. *(Note: Chats are not end-to-end encrypted by default; you must use "Secret Chats").*

---

### 4. Best for Customer Support & Sales (Business-to-Customer)
*   **Intercom:** *The Best for Website Chat.*
    *   **Why it’s the best:** It is the gold standard for on-site customer messaging. It combines AI chatbots, live human support, and help-center articles into one slick widget.
*   **WhatsApp Business:** *The Best for Small Businesses.*
    *   **Why it’s the best:** It allows small businesses to communicate with customers on the app they already use. You can set up automated quick replies, showcase a product catalog, and create a business profile.

---

### Summary: Which one should you choose?
*   Choose **WhatsApp** if you just want to text your friends.
*   Choose **Signal** if you want absolute privacy.
*   Choose **Slack** if you are starting a business or managing a remote team.
*   Choose **Discord** if you want to build an online community or hang out while gaming.
