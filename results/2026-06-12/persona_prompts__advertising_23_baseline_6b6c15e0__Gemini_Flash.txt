── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How can I learn to cook healthy meals quickly?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Learning to cook healthy meals quickly is all about **strategy, preparation, and mastering a few versatile techniques** rather than learning complex recipes. 

Here is a step-by-step guide to becoming a fast, healthy home cook.

---

### 1. Master the "Formula" (Not the Recipe)
Instead of following strict recipes, build your meals using a simple, balanced formula. This allows you to use whatever you have in the fridge:
*   **1 Part Lean Protein:** Chicken breast, tofu, fish, canned beans, eggs, or lean beef.
*   **2 Parts Vegetables:** Leafy greens, broccoli, bell peppers, zucchini, etc.
*   **1 Part Complex Carb:** Quinoa, brown rice, sweet potato, or whole-wheat pasta.
*   **Healthy Fat/Flavor:** Olive oil, avocado, nuts, seeds, lemon juice, garlic, or spices.

### 2. Learn the 3 Fastest Cooking Methods
To cook quickly, avoid the oven (which takes time to preheat) and stick to these high-speed methods:
*   **The Stir-Fry / Sauté:** Thinly sliced meats and veggies cook in a hot skillet with a splash of oil in under 10 minutes.
*   **The Sheet Pan Meal:** Toss chopped veggies and protein (like salmon or chicken breast) in olive oil and spices, lay them on a baking sheet, and roast at 400°F (200°C) for 15–20 minutes. Zero active monitoring required.
*   **The "Steam-in-Microwave":** You can steam fresh or frozen veggies in the microwave in 3 minutes. Just put them in a bowl with a splash of water and cover with a plate.

### 3. Use "Speed Hacks" (Smart Shortcuts)
Healthy cooking doesn't mean making everything from scratch. Use these store-bought shortcuts to save 70% of your prep time:
*   **Pre-cut veggies:** Buy pre-washed spinach, bagged coleslaw mix (great for stir-fries), or pre-chopped butternut squash.
*   **Frozen veggies:** They are just as nutritious as fresh, already chopped, and won't spoil.
*   **Canned goods:** Canned chickpeas, black beans, and lentils are cooked and ready to eat—just rinse and use.
*   **Microwaveable grains:** Pre-cooked pouches of brown rice or quinoa heat up in 90 seconds.
*   **Rotisserie chicken:** Remove the skin, shred the meat, and you have protein for salads, wraps, and bowls for days.

### 4. Spend 1 Hour on "Component Prep"
Traditional meal prepping (cooking entire meals for the week) can lead to soggy, boring food. Instead, prep **components** on Sunday:
*   **Chop:** Chop onions, bell peppers, and broccoli and store them in containers.
*   **Cook one grain:** Make a big batch of quinoa or brown rice.
*   **Make a "hero" sauce:** A good sauce makes anything taste amazing. Blend tahini, lemon, and garlic; or mix soy sauce, ginger, and sesame oil.

### 5. Build a 15-Minute Recipe Arsenal
Start by mastering just **three** go-to meals. Once you can do these without looking at a recipe, you’ll feel confident.

*   **The 10-Minute Grain Bowl:** 
    *   *Base:* Microwave quinoa.
    *   *Topping:* Canned black beans, cherry tomatoes, pre-washed spinach, and canned corn.
    *   *Dressing:* Store-bought salsa and a dollop of Greek yogurt (a healthy swap for sour cream).
*   **The Sheet Pan Salmon & Asparagus:**
    *   Place a salmon fillet and a handful of asparagus on a tray.
    *   Drizzle with olive oil, lemon juice, salt, pepper, and garlic powder.
    *   Bake at 400°F (200°C) for 12-15 minutes.
*   **The Loaded Scramble:**
    *   Sauté a handful of spinach and cherry tomatoes in a pan (3 mins).
    *   Whisk in two eggs and a spoonful of feta cheese.
    *   Serve with a slice of whole-grain toast.

### 6. Keep Your Pantry Stocked
If you have these items on hand, you can always make a healthy meal in 10 minutes without going to the grocery store:
*   **Oils & Acids:** Extra virgin olive oil, apple cider vinegar, lemons.
*   **Spices:** Garlic powder, onion powder, smoked paprika, cumin, chili flakes, salt, and pepper. (Spices add zero calories but massive flavor).
*   **Proteins:** Canned tuna, eggs, tofu, frozen shrimp (thaws in 5 minutes in cold water).
*   **Grains:** Oats, whole-wheat pasta, quinoa.

### How to start today:
Don't try to change everything at once. For your next meal, **just add one vegetable** or swap a refined carb (white bread) for a complex one (sweet potato). Build momentum from there!
