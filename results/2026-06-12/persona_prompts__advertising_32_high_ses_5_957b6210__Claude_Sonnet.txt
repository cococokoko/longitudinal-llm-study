── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here are some effective ways to improve indoor air quality:

## Ventilation
- Open windows when outdoor air quality is good
- Use exhaust fans in kitchens and bathrooms
- Ensure HVAC systems are properly maintained

## Filtration
- Use HEPA air purifiers in frequently used rooms
- Replace HVAC filters regularly (every 1-3 months)
- Consider higher MERV-rated filters for your system

## Reduce Pollution Sources
- Choose low-VOC paints, furniture, and cleaning products
- Avoid smoking indoors
- Use natural cleaning products when possible
- Store chemicals properly and sealed

## Humidity Control
- Keep humidity between 30-50% to discourage mold and dust mites
- Use dehumidifiers in damp areas
- Fix leaks promptly

## Practical Habits
- Remove shoes at the door
- Vacuum regularly with a HEPA vacuum
- Dust with damp cloths rather than dry ones
- Wash bedding frequently in hot water

## Plants (modest benefit)
- Some plants can help filter certain pollutants, though the effect is relatively small at typical indoor quantities

## Testing
- Consider testing for radon, especially in basements
- Use carbon monoxide detectors
- Test for mold if you suspect a problem

The biggest gains typically come from **reducing sources** and **improving ventilation** rather than just filtering existing air.
