Best skin routine for dry skin.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
If you have dry skin, the goal is to gently cleanse, add hydration, and lock in moisture while avoiding products that strip your skin.

Morning:
1. Gentle cleanser
   - Use a hydrating, fragrance-free cleanser.
   - Examples: CeraVe Hydrating Cleanser, Vanicream Gentle Facial Cleanser, La Roche-Posay Toleriane Hydrating Gentle Cleanser.

2. Moisturizer
   - Choose a cream containing ingredients like ceramides, glycerin, hyaluronic acid, or squalane.
   - Apply while skin is still slightly damp.

3. Sunscreen
   - Use SPF 30 or higher every day.
   - For dry skin, moisturizing sunscreens often feel more comfortable.

Evening:
1. Gentle cleanser
   - Wash away dirt, sweat, and sunscreen without over-cleansing.

2. Hydrating serum (optional)
   - Look for hyaluronic acid, glycerin, or panthenol.

3. Rich moisturizer
   - Use a thicker cream at night.
   - If your skin is very dry, apply a thin layer of petrolatum (Vaseline) over moisturizer on the driest areas.

Helpful ingredients:
- Ceramides
- Glycerin
- Hyaluronic acid
- Squalane
- Shea butter
- Colloidal oatmeal
- Panthenol

Try to avoid:
- Harsh scrubs
- Alcohol-heavy products
- Strong foaming cleansers
- Excessive exfoliation
- Very hot showers or face washing

Since you work long hours in a warehouse, your skin may be exposed to dry air, dust, temperature changes, or frequent handwashing. Consider:
- Applying hand cream throughout the day, especially after washing.
- Using a thicker moisturizer before and after work.
- Keeping a small fragrance-free moisturizer at work for touch-ups.
- Wearing gloves when appropriate if your hands are exposed to irritants.

If your skin is persistently flaky, itchy, cracked, or develops a rash, it may be worth seeing a dermatologist, as conditions like eczema can mimic simple dryness.
