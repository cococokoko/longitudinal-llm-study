Starting a small online business can be a great way to build income, test an idea, or create more flexibility in your work.

A simple approach is:

1. Choose a problem to solve
- Think about skills you have, industries you know, or problems people frequently complain about.
- Examples:
  - Selling handmade products
  - Freelance services (design, writing, coding, bookkeeping)
  - Digital products (templates, ebooks, courses)
  - Print-on-demand products
  - Niche ecommerce stores

2. Validate demand
- Search for competitors.
- Check online communities, social media, and search trends.
- Talk to potential customers.
- Try to confirm that people are already spending money on similar solutions.

3. Define your offer
- What exactly are you selling?
- Who is it for?
- Why should someone choose you?

4. Set up the basics
- Business name
- Website or storefront (Shopify, Etsy, Squarespace, etc.)
- Payment processing
- Business registration and tax requirements based on your location

5. Create a simple marketing plan
- Focus on one or two channels first:
  - Instagram
  - TikTok
  - LinkedIn
  - Email marketing
  - SEO/content
  - Online communities

6. Launch quickly
- Avoid spending months perfecting everything.
- Get feedback from real customers and improve over time.

7. Track key numbers
- Revenue
- Expenses
- Profit
- Customer acquisition cost
- Repeat customer rate

- What country you're in

- Whether you want to sell products or services
- How many hours per week you can commit
