Figuring out what you want is often less about having a sudden insight and more about gathering evidence from your own experiences.

A few approaches that can help:

- Pay attention to energy, not just thoughts.
  - Notice when you feel engaged, curious, relieved, proud, or energized.
  - Also notice what consistently drains you or creates resistance.
  - Your reactions often reveal preferences before your mind can explain them.

- Separate "what I want" from "what I think I should want."
  - Ask yourself:
    - If nobody judged me, what would I choose?
    - If nobody praised me, what would I still care about?
    - Which goals belong to me, and which came from family, friends, or social expectations?

- Look for patterns in your past.
  - Think about times you felt most satisfied.
  - What were you doing?
  - Who were you with?
  - What values were being fulfilled (learning, security, creativity, achievement, freedom, connection, etc.)?

- Run small experiments.
  - You don't need to decide your whole future.
  - Try things on a small scale and observe your response.
  - Action often creates clarity faster than endless reflection.

- Distinguish between immediate wants and deeper wants.
  - For example, wanting to quit a job might actually reflect a desire for autonomy, better management, more challenge, or less stress.
  - Keep asking, "What would that give me?" until you reach the underlying need.

- Write without editing yourself.
  - Set a timer for 10 minutes and answer:
    - What do I want more of?
    - What do I want less of?
    - What am I curious about?
    - What am I avoiding?

Since you've recently started a new job and are still on probation, it may also help to avoid assuming that every feeling about the job reflects a final preference. Early stages of a role can involve uncertainty, learning curves, and pressure. Instead of asking, "Do I want this job forever?", try asking:
- What parts of this job do I enjoy?
- What parts do I dislike?
- What skills or environments seem to suit me?
- What do I want to learn from this experience, regardless of whether I stay long-term?
