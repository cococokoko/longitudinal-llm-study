What is the difference between analysis and design? Can one begin to design without analysis? Why? Be concise.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here is a concise breakdown of the differences, relationship, and dependency between analysis and design:

### 1. The Difference
*   **Analysis (The "What"):** Focuses on understanding the problem, gathering requirements, and defining *what* the system needs to do. It is about investigation and discovery.
*   **Design (The "How"):** Focuses on the solution, architecture, and defining *how* the system will be built to meet those requirements. It is about planning and creation.

### 2. Can you begin to design without analysis?
**Technically yes, but practically no.** 

While you *can* physically start coding or drawing blueprints immediately, you cannot create a *successful* design without some level of analysis. 

### 3. Why?
*   **Lack of Direction:** Without analysis, you do not know what problem you are trying to solve. You risk designing the wrong product perfectly.
*   **Wasted Resources:** Designing without analysis leads to constant backtracking, scope creep, and costly redesigns when overlooked requirements inevitably surface later. 
*   **Foundation:** Analysis provides the constraints, rules, and goals that dictate design choices. You cannot plan *how* to build something until you know *what* you are building.
