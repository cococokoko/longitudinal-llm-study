What is a good secondhand market laptop for learning Python?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
For learning Python, you do not need a powerful laptop. A good secondhand business-class laptop is often a better value than a cheap new consumer model because it will usually have better build quality, keyboard, and repairability.

Good used options:

- Lenovo ThinkPad T480
  - One of the most commonly recommended used laptops.
  - 8th-generation Intel CPUs support modern Windows versions well.
  - Excellent keyboard.
  - Easy to upgrade RAM and SSD.
  - Aim for 16 GB RAM and a 256 GB+ SSD if possible.

- Lenovo ThinkPad T490
  - Thinner and newer than the T480.
  - Good battery life and reliability.
  - Usually costs a bit more.

- Dell Latitude 5490, 5400, or 5410
  - Business-grade laptops with solid keyboards and durability.
  - Widely available on the secondhand market.

- HP EliteBook 840 G5 or G6
  - Premium business laptops.
  - Good screen quality and build quality.
  - Usually available at reasonable prices used.

- Lenovo ThinkPad X280 or X390
  - More portable 12–13 inch models.
  - Great if you travel often.
  - Less upgradeable than T-series models.

Minimum specifications to look for:
- CPU: Intel Core i5 8th generation or newer (i5-8250U, i5-8350U, etc.)
- RAM: 8 GB minimum, 16 GB preferred
- Storage: SSD, 256 GB or larger
- Screen: 1920×1080 (Full HD) preferred
- Battery: Ask about battery health if buying used

Avoid:
- Mechanical hard drives (HDDs) as the main storage.
- Intel 6th- or 7th-generation processors if the price difference to 8th-gen models is small.
- Chromebooks if you want the simplest path for Python development.
- Very low-end Celeron or Pentium systems.

Typical budget ranges (depending on country and condition):
- $150–250: ThinkPad T480, Latitude 5490, EliteBook 840 G5
- $250–400: ThinkPad T490, Latitude 5400/5410, EliteBook G6
- $400+: Newer business laptops with 16 GB RAM and better batteries
