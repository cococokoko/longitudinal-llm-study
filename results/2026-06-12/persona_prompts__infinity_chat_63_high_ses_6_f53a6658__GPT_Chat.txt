Give a numerical example to illustrate the concept of partial derivative.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
A partial derivative measures how a function changes with respect to one variable while keeping the other variables fixed.

Example:

Consider the function

f(x, y) = 3x²y + 2y³

Suppose we want the partial derivatives with respect to x and y.

1. Partial derivative with respect to x

Treat y as a constant:

∂f/∂x = ∂/∂x (3x²y + 2y³)
       = 6xy + 0
       = 6xy

Now evaluate it at the point (x, y) = (2, 1):

∂f/∂x(2,1) = 6 × 2 × 1 = 12

Interpretation:
When y is held fixed at 1, a small increase in x near x = 2 increases the function by about 12 times the change in x.

2. Partial derivative with respect to y

Treat x as a constant:

∂f/∂y = ∂/∂y (3x²y + 2y³)
       = 3x² + 6y²

At the point (2,1):

∂f/∂y(2,1) = 3(2²) + 6(1²)
           = 12 + 6
           = 18

Interpretation:
When x is held fixed at 2, a small increase in y near y = 1 increases the function by about 18 times the change in y.

Numerical check:

First compute the function value at (2,1):

f(2,1) = 3(2²)(1) + 2(1³)
       = 12 + 2
       = 14

Increase x slightly from 2 to 2.01 while keeping y = 1:

f(2.01,1) = 3(2.01²)(1) + 2
          ≈ 14.1203

Change in f ≈ 14.1203 − 14 = 0.1203

Since ∂f/∂x(2,1) = 12, the partial derivative predicts:

Δf ≈ 12 × 0.01 = 0.12

which is very close to the actual change 0.1203.

This illustrates that a partial derivative gives the rate of change of a multivariable function with respect to one variable while the others are held constant.
