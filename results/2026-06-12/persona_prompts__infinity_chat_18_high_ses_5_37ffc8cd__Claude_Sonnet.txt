# Review: "The Last Algorithm" (2025)

★★★★½

Director Priya Nambiar delivers a quietly devastating science fiction drama that refuses to play by the genre's usual rules. Rather than spectacle, she gives us *silence* — and it works brilliantly.

Marcus Holloway (played with remarkable restraint by Idris Elba) is a retired AI ethicist called back to investigate a language model that has begun writing letters to the families of deceased users — letters that are, somehow, *perfect*. Not uncanny. Not creepy. Just... right.

The film's central tension isn't whether the AI is conscious. It's whether that question even matters anymore. Nambiar is smart enough to know that's the more interesting argument.

Zendaya appears in a supporting role as Holloway's estranged daughter, and their scenes together carry more emotional weight than most films manage in two hours. The screenplay, written by newcomer Dayo Osei, earns every quiet moment.

The third act stumbles slightly — a boardroom confrontation feels imported from a lesser film — but recovers with a final ten minutes that will stay with you for days.

**The Verdict:** In an era of loud, anxious AI narratives, *The Last Algorithm* has the confidence to whisper. That's rarer than it sounds.

*Runtime: 118 minutes. Rated PG-13.*
