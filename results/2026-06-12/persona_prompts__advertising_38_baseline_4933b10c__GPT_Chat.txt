── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
What are the best platforms for freelance writers to find work?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The best platform depends on your experience level, niche, and whether you're looking for one-off projects or long-term clients. Here are some of the strongest options:

- Upwork
  - One of the largest freelance marketplaces.
  - Good for content writing, copywriting, technical writing, ghostwriting, and SEO writing.
  - Competitive, especially for new freelancers, but there are many opportunities.
  - Best for: Writers willing to build a profile and gather reviews over time.

- Fiverr
  - Writers create service packages ("gigs") and clients come to them.
  - Can work well if you clearly define a niche (e.g., blog posts, email copy, LinkedIn content).
  - Best for: Writers who are good at packaging and marketing their services.

- Contra
  - Growing freelance platform with no commission fees for freelancers in many cases.
  - More focused on professional portfolios and direct client relationships.
  - Best for: Experienced freelancers and specialists.

- LinkedIn
  - Not a freelance marketplace, but a powerful source of clients.
  - Many companies post contract writing opportunities.
  - Publishing content and networking can attract inbound leads.
  - Best for: Building long-term professional relationships.

- ProBlogger Job Board
  - Dedicated writing job board.
  - Features blogging, content marketing, and copywriting opportunities.
  - Best for: Content writers and bloggers.

- Freelance Writing Jobs (FWJ)
  - Curates writing opportunities from multiple sources.
  - Good for finding new leads without searching dozens of sites.
  - Best for: Writers looking for consistent job listings.

- Contently
  - Premium platform connecting experienced writers with major brands.
  - Requires a strong portfolio.
  - Best for: Established writers seeking higher-paying assignments.

- ClearVoice
  - Content marketing platform connecting freelancers with brands.
  - Portfolio-focused.
  - Best for: Writers with expertise in specific industries.

- WriterAccess
  - Content marketplace with opportunities across many industries.
  - Writers are rated and matched with clients.
  - Best for: Freelancers seeking ongoing content work.

- JournalismJobs
  - Focused on journalism, reporting, editing, and editorial work.
  - Best for: Journalists and news writers.

- Wellfound (formerly AngelList)
  - Startup-focused job board.
  - Often includes contract and freelance content positions.
  - Best for: Writers interested in startups and technology.

For many freelance writers, the most effective combination is:
- Upwork for immediate opportunities.
- LinkedIn for networking and long-term clients.
- A niche-specific platform (Contently, ClearVoice, or ProBlogger) depending on your specialty.

If you're just starting out, I can also suggest the best platforms based on your writing niche (blogging, copywriting, technical writing, ghostwriting, journalism, SEO content, etc.).
