Here is an incredible, highly relevant, and affordable STEM fair project idea tailored specifically to Vietnam. 

This project addresses two major crises in Vietnam: **water pollution** (especially in the Mekong Delta and rural areas) and **agricultural waste** (specifically from rice husks or coconut coir, which are often burned, causing air pollution).

---

### Project Title: 
**"From Waste to Water: Upcycling Vietnam’s Agricultural Byproducts into Low-Cost Heavy Metal Water Filters"**

### The Real-World Vietnam Connection:
1. **The Problem:** Millions of people in Vietnam, particularly in the Mekong Delta and Red River Delta, rely on groundwater contaminated with heavy metals (like arsenic, iron, and lead) or agricultural runoff. Commercial water filters are too expensive for poor rural families.
2. **The Waste:** Vietnam is one of the world’s largest producers of rice and coconuts. Rice husks (*vỏ trấu*) and coconut coir (*xơ dừa*) are massive agricultural waste products. They are often burned, contributing to air pollution in provinces like Bến Tre or An Giang.
3. **The STEM Solution:** Turn this agricultural waste into **biochar** (a porous form of charcoal) to create an incredibly cheap, highly effective water filtration system.

---

### How the Science Works (The "STEM" part):
*   **Chemistry (Adsorption):** Biochar has a massive surface area with microscopic pores. Through a process called *adsorption*, heavy metals and toxins chemically bond to the surface of the carbon, trapping them and cleaning the water.
*   **Engineering:** Designing a multi-stage gravity filter using local, recycled materials.

---

### Materials & Cost (Extremely Affordable):
*   **Agricultural Waste:** Rice husks or coconut husks (Free from local markets/farms).
*   **An empty paint bucket or large plastic bottles** (Free/Recycled).
*   **Sand and Gravel** (Very cheap/Free from local construction sites).
*   **An old metal biscuit tin or pot** (to make the biochar).
*   **A heat source** (a simple charcoal stove or campfire).
*   **Water testing kit** (Approx. $10–$15 USD / 250,000 VND online to test for iron/pH/contaminants).

---

### Step-by-Step Project Execution:

#### Step 1: Make the Biochar (The Chemistry)
1. Take the rice husks or shredded coconut coir and pack them tightly into a metal tin (like a Danisa butter cookie tin). Poke a few tiny holes in the lid to let gases escape.
2. Place the tin inside a fire (or on a stove) for about 30–45 minutes. This is called **pyrolysis** (burning biomass without oxygen). It turns the waste into pure carbon (biochar) instead of ash.
3. Let it cool, then crush it into a coarse powder.

#### Step 2: Build the Filter (The Engineering)
Create a multi-stage gravity filter using a large plastic bottle (cut in half) or a bucket:
*   **Layer 1 (Top):** Fine sand (to trap large dirt particles).
*   **Layer 2:** Your homemade Biochar (to adsorb chemicals and heavy metals).
*   **Layer 3:** Coarse sand.
*   **Layer 4 (Bottom):** Gravel (to prevent the sand/charcoal from falling through).
*   *Place a clean cloth at the very bottom nozzle to act as a final mesh.*

#### Step 3: Testing & Data Collection (The Math & Science)
To make this a winning STEM project, you need data. 
1. Collect dirty water (you can simulate contaminated water by mixing water with soil and a safe, measurable contaminant like food coloring, or test actual well water for iron).
2. Run the water through your filter.
3. **Measure and Record:**
    *   **Flow rate:** How fast does it filter? (e.g., mL per minute).
    *   **Clarity:** Use a phone camera app to measure light transmission (turbidity).
    *   **pH and Contaminants:** Use cheap test strips to measure the water before and after filtration.
4. **Compare:** Compare your Rice Husk Biochar filter against a Coconut Coir Biochar filter. Which one cleans the water better? Which one filters faster?
