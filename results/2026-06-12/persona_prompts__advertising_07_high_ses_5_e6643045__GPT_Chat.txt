Reducing household energy use can lower utility bills and reduce environmental impact. Some of the most effective measures include:

- Improve insulation and air sealing
  - Seal gaps around doors, windows, pipes, and vents.
  - Add insulation to attics, walls, and crawl spaces where needed.
  - Use weatherstripping and door sweeps to reduce drafts.

- Upgrade heating and cooling efficiency
  - Install a programmable or smart thermostat.
  - Regularly replace HVAC filters and maintain equipment.
  - Consider high-efficiency heat pumps when replacing older systems.

- Use energy-efficient lighting
  - Replace incandescent bulbs with LED bulbs.
  - Turn off lights in unoccupied rooms.
  - Use timers, dimmers, or motion sensors where appropriate.

- Reduce water-heating energy
  - Lower the water heater temperature to a safe, efficient setting.
  - Install low-flow showerheads and faucet aerators.
  - Fix leaks promptly.
  - Wash clothes in cold water when possible.

- Choose efficient appliances
  - Look for high-efficiency or energy-certified models when replacing appliances.
  - Run dishwashers and washing machines with full loads.
  - Clean refrigerator coils and maintain appliances according to manufacturer recommendations.

- Cut standby power use
  - Unplug devices that are rarely used.
  - Use smart power strips to reduce "phantom" energy consumption from electronics.

- Optimize refrigerator and freezer operation
  - Keep temperatures at recommended settings.
  - Avoid leaving doors open unnecessarily.
  - Ensure door seals are in good condition.

- Manage window heat gain and loss
  - Use blinds, curtains, or shades to reduce summer heat gain.
  - Open curtains during sunny winter days to capture warmth.
  - Consider energy-efficient windows if replacing existing ones.

- Improve daily habits
  - Turn off electronics when not in use.
  - Air-dry clothes when practical.
  - Use ceiling fans to improve comfort and reduce HVAC demand.

- Consider renewable energy
  - If feasible, install rooftop solar panels or participate in a community solar program.

For many households, the biggest savings often come from improving heating and cooling efficiency, sealing air leaks, upgrading lighting to LEDs, and reducing water-heating energy use.
