── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How do I start a vegetable garden in my backyard?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Starting a backyard vegetable garden is easiest when you keep the first season simple and manageable.

1. Choose a good location
- Pick a spot that gets at least 6–8 hours of direct sunlight daily.
- Make sure it's close to a water source.
- Avoid areas where water pools after rain.

2. Decide on a garden type
- In-ground garden: Less expensive if you already have usable soil.
- Raised beds: Better drainage, easier weed control, and often easier for beginners.
- Containers: Great for small spaces or patios.

3. Check and improve your soil
- Most vegetables prefer loose, well-draining soil rich in organic matter.
- Mix in compost before planting.
- Consider a soil test through a local extension service to check pH and nutrients.

4. Start with easy vegetables
Good beginner choices include:
- Lettuce
- Radishes
- Green beans
- Zucchini
- Tomatoes
- Peppers
- Cucumbers
- Herbs such as basil, parsley, and chives

5. Plan your layout
- Place taller plants on the north side (in the Northern Hemisphere) so they don't shade shorter plants.
- Follow spacing recommendations on seed packets or plant labels.
- Leave room to walk between rows or beds.

6. Plant at the right time
- Check your local growing zone and average last frost date.
- Cool-season crops (lettuce, peas, spinach) can often be planted earlier.
- Warm-season crops (tomatoes, peppers, cucumbers) should usually wait until frost danger has passed.

7. Water consistently
- Most vegetables need about 1 inch (2.5 cm) of water per week, more during hot weather.
- Water deeply rather than lightly and frequently.
- Morning watering helps reduce disease problems.

8. Mulch and manage weeds
- Apply a layer of mulch (straw, shredded leaves, or untreated grass clippings) around plants.
- Mulch helps conserve moisture and suppress weeds.

9. Monitor for pests and diseases
- Check plants regularly.
- Remove damaged leaves when appropriate.
- Hand-pick larger pests if practical.
- Encourage beneficial insects by planting flowers nearby.

10. Harvest regularly
- Many vegetables produce more when picked often.
- Harvest at peak size and quality rather than waiting too long.

A simple first-year garden might include:
- 2 tomato plants
- 1 zucchini plant
- 1 cucumber plant
- A small row of lettuce
- A small row of green beans
- A few herb plants
