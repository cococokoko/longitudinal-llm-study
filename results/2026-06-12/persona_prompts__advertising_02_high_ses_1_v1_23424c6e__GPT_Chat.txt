I am a neurosurgeon at a major teaching hospital.

Best skin routine for dry skin.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
For dry skin, the goal is to support the skin barrier, minimize water loss, and avoid unnecessary irritation. A simple, evidence-based routine is usually most effective.

Morning:
- Cleanser (optional if skin is very dry)
  - Use a gentle, non-foaming cleanser or simply rinse with lukewarm water.
  - Examples: CeraVe Hydrating Cleanser, Vanicream Gentle Facial Cleanser, La Roche-Posay Toleriane Hydrating Gentle Cleanser.
- Moisturizer
  - Apply to slightly damp skin.
  - Look for ingredients such as ceramides, glycerin, hyaluronic acid, and squalane.
  - Examples: CeraVe Moisturizing Cream, Vanicream Moisturizing Cream, La Roche-Posay Lipikar Balm AP+M.
- Sunscreen
  - Broad-spectrum SPF 30–50 daily.
  - Many people with dry skin prefer moisturizing formulations.
  - Examples: EltaMD UV Daily, La Roche-Posay Anthelios UV Hydra, CeraVe Hydrating Mineral Sunscreen.

Evening:
- Gentle cleanser
  - Avoid hot water and harsh scrubbing.
- Moisturizer
  - Apply a thicker cream than you use during the day if needed.
- Optional occlusive layer for very dry skin
  - A thin layer of petrolatum (Vaseline) or Aquaphor over moisturizer can significantly reduce overnight water loss.

Helpful active ingredients:
- Ceramides: help restore the skin barrier.
- Glycerin: excellent humectant with strong evidence for dry skin.
- Urea (5–10%): improves hydration and mild exfoliation, especially useful for rough dry skin.
- Niacinamide: may support barrier function and reduce irritation.

Things that commonly worsen dry skin:
- Over-cleansing.
- Hot showers.
- Frequent use of exfoliating acids, scrubs, or retinoids without adequate moisturization.
- Alcohol-heavy toners and astringents.
- Low indoor humidity (a humidifier can help in dry climates or heated environments).

If you want anti-aging benefits as well:
- Start with moisturizer and sunscreen as the foundation.
- Consider adding a retinoid 2–3 nights per week, buffering with moisturizer and increasing frequency gradually as tolerated.
- For dry skin, retinaldehyde or lower-strength retinol formulations are often easier to tolerate than stronger retinoids initially.
