Great question! Here's a practical approach:

## Start with the Right Mindset
- Expect a learning curve - it's normal to feel confused initially
- Focus on problem-solving, not just memorizing syntax
- Consistency matters more than long sessions

## Choose a First Language
**Python** is widely recommended because:
- Clean, readable syntax
- Huge community and resources
- Versatile (web, data science, automation)
- Beginner-friendly

## Practical Steps

1. **Pick one resource and stick with it**
   - freeCodeCamp, Codecademy, or CS50 (Harvard's free course)
2. **Learn fundamentals first**
   - Variables, loops, functions, conditionals
3. **Write code daily** - even 20-30 minutes helps
4. **Build small projects** as soon as possible
5. **Learn to debug** - reading error messages is a real skill

## Common Pitfalls to Avoid
- Tutorial hopping without practicing
- Trying to learn multiple languages simultaneously
- Waiting until you "know enough" to build things

## Good Free Resources
- **CS50** (cs50.harvard.edu)
- **freeCodeCamp**
- **Python.org** tutorials
- **The Odin Project**

## Realistic Timeline
Expect 3-6 months of consistent practice to feel genuinely comfortable with basics.
