── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Learning to cook healthy meals quickly is very achievable! Here are some practical strategies:

## Start with Simple Techniques
- **Stir-frying** - fast, uses lots of vegetables
- **Sheet pan meals** - toss ingredients together, roast while you do other things
- **One-pot meals** - less cleanup, easy to manage

## Build a Basic Skill Set
- Learn to cook a few proteins (eggs, chicken, beans)
- Practice chopping vegetables efficiently
- Understand basic seasoning

## Work Around an Unpredictable Schedule
- **Batch cook when you have time** - make a big pot of rice, beans, or roasted vegetables
- **Keep it simple** - meals with 3-5 ingredients are totally valid
- **Use frozen vegetables** - already washed and cut, nutritious, and quick
- **Stock your pantry** - canned beans, canned tomatoes, whole grains last a long time

## Realistic Quick Meals
- Eggs with frozen vegetables (10 minutes)
- Rice bowls with whatever you have
- Bean and vegetable wraps
- Pasta with olive oil and vegetables

## Learning Resources
- YouTube channels like Joshua Weissman or Ethan Chlebowski have beginner-friendly videos
- Start with just **2-3 recipes** you rotate until comfortable
