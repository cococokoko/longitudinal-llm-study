── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
What are the best ways to improve indoor air quality?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Improving indoor air quality usually works best when you combine source control, ventilation, filtration, and humidity management.

Key strategies:

• Reduce pollution at the source
  - Avoid smoking or vaping indoors.
  - Use low-VOC (volatile organic compound) paints, cleaners, and furnishings when possible.
  - Store chemicals, fuels, and solvents in sealed containers away from living spaces.
  - Use exhaust fans when cooking, especially with gas stoves.
  - Regularly clean to reduce dust, pet dander, and allergens.

• Improve ventilation
  - Open windows and doors when outdoor air quality and weather permit.
  - Use kitchen and bathroom exhaust fans that vent outdoors.
  - Consider a mechanical ventilation system, such as an energy recovery ventilator (ERV) or heat recovery ventilator (HRV), in tightly sealed homes.

• Use effective air filtration
  - Replace HVAC filters on schedule and consider higher-efficiency filters if your system supports them.
  - Portable air purifiers with HEPA filters can help reduce particles such as dust, pollen, smoke, and pet dander.
  - Place purifiers in rooms where people spend the most time, such as bedrooms and living rooms.

• Control humidity
  - Aim for indoor relative humidity between about 30% and 50%.
  - Use dehumidifiers in damp areas to discourage mold growth.
  - Fix leaks promptly and address water damage quickly.

• Reduce allergens and dust
  - Vacuum with a HEPA-filter vacuum.
  - Wash bedding regularly in hot water if dust mites are a concern.
  - Consider allergen-resistant mattress and pillow covers.
  - Remove shoes at the door to reduce dirt and pollutants tracked indoors.

• Address combustion pollutants
  - Ensure furnaces, water heaters, fireplaces, and gas appliances are properly maintained.
  - Install carbon monoxide detectors on every level of the home.
  - Never run vehicles, generators, or fuel-burning equipment in garages or enclosed spaces.

• Monitor air quality
  - Indoor air quality monitors can track particulate matter (PM2.5), carbon dioxide (CO₂), humidity, and sometimes VOCs.
  - Monitoring can help identify ventilation or pollution problems that are not obvious.

Common misconceptions:
• Houseplants can be pleasant, but they generally do not significantly improve indoor air quality in real-world homes.
• Air fresheners may mask odors but can add chemicals to the air rather than improve air quality.
