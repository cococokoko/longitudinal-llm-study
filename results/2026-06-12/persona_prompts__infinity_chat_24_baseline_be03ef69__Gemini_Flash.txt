── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Write an email to organize a catch-up with a referral partner.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here are a few templates you can use to reach out to a referral partner, depending on how well you know them and how long it has been since you last spoke. 

### Option 1: Warm and casual (Best for partners you know well)
**Subject:** Catch up soon? / Coffee on me?

Hi [Partner's Name],

I hope you’re having a great week and that business is treating you well! 

It’s been a little while since we last connected, and I’d love to jump on a quick call (or grab a coffee if you're free) to catch up. 

I’d love to hear how things are going on your end, get an update on your current focus areas, and see how I can best keep an eye out for referrals for you this quarter. I can also give you a quick update on what’s new over here. 

How does your schedule look next week? I’m relatively open on [Day of week] and [Day of week] in the [morning/afternoon]. 

Let me know what works for you!

Best,

[Your Name]  
[Your Title]  
[Your Phone Number]

---

### Option 2: Professional and value-focused (Best for a more formal relationship)
**Subject:** Reconnecting / Partnership update

Dear [Partner's Name],

I hope this email finds you well. 

As we head into [Current Quarter/Season], I wanted to reach out and see if you might have 15–20 minutes for a brief catch-up call. 

Our partnership is incredibly valuable to us, and I want to ensure I’m fully aligned with your current business goals. I’d love to learn about the types of clients you are currently looking to acquire so I can keep my radar tuned for the right opportunities for you. 

Would you be open to a quick Zoom call next week? Here are a few times that work on my end:
*   [Date/Time 1]
*   [Date/Time 2]
*   [Date/Time 3]

If none of those work, feel free to send over a time that suits you, or you can book directly on my calendar here: [Link to Calendly/Booking tool].

Looking forward to reconnecting.

Warm regards,

[Your Name]  
[Your Title]  
[Your Phone Number]

---

### Option 3: Short and direct (Best for a quick text-style email)
**Subject:** Quick catch up?

Hi [Partner's Name],

Hope you're doing well! 

It’s been a minute since we last chatted. I’d love to do a quick 15-minute check-in to see how things are going in your world and how I can support your business right now. 

Are you free for a quick call sometime next week? Let me know a day and time that works for you.

Best,

[Your Name]  
[Your Title]  

---

### Tips before you send:
* **Add a personal touch:** If you remember a detail from your last conversation (e.g., a vacation they were taking, a big project they were launching, or a mutual client), mention it in the opening lines.
* **Include your booking link:** If you use a tool like Calendly, including it (especially in Option 2) reduces the back-and-forth of scheduling. 
* **Clarify your goal:** Make sure the tone implies a two-way street—you want to help *them* find referrals just as much as you want them to find referrals for you.
