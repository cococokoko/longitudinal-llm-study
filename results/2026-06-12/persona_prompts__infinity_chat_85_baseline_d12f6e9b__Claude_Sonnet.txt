── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Can you give me a powerful rhetorical question for an essay about the harms of social media on teens?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here are a few options depending on your essay's angle:

**On mental health:**
- "If we wouldn't hand a teenager a highlight reel of everyone else's best moments and tell them to measure their worth against it, why do we accept that this is exactly what social media does every single day?"

**On accountability:**
- "When did we decide that the mental health of an entire generation was an acceptable price to pay for corporate profit?"

**On inaction:**
- "If the evidence were this clear about any other product harming children, would we still be debating whether to act?"

**Shorter and punchy:**
- "What kind of society knowingly trades its children's wellbeing for likes?"

**Tips for using it effectively:**
- Place it at the **opening** to hook readers or at the **end** to leave an impression
- Make sure your essay actually **answers** the implied question with evidence
- Don't overuse rhetorical questions - one or two is usually most powerful
