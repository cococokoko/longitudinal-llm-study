Help me use my Microsoft Surface touchpad and on-screen keyboard to run Dolphin Emulator for the Wii.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Running a Wii emulator like Dolphin using only a Microsoft Surface touchpad and an on-screen keyboard is challenging, but absolutely possible. Since you work long hours in a warehouse, you likely want a setup that is easy to launch, highly responsive, and doesn't require you to carry extra gear like a controller or mouse.

Here is a step-by-step guide to setting up Dolphin for the best possible experience using only your Surface's built-in screen and touchpad.

---

### Step 1: Optimize the Windows On-Screen Keyboard
The default Windows touch keyboard can sometimes minimize when you click inside a game. We need to use the **legacy On-Screen Keyboard (OSK)**, which stays on top of games.

1. Press the **Windows Key**, type **On-Screen Keyboard**, and press Enter.
2. A traditional keyboard will appear on your screen.
3. Click the **Options** key on this keyboard (bottom right) and ensure **"Fade"** is turned off so it remains visible.
4. You can drag this keyboard to the bottom or top of your screen.

*Tip: If you are using Windows 11, you can also go to Settings > Time & Language > Typing > Touch Keyboard and set it to "Show when no keyboard attached" and choose the "Traditional" layout which includes function keys.*

---

### Step 2: Configure Dolphin's Controller Settings
Since you don't have a physical Wii Remote, we need to map the Wii controls to your keyboard and touchpad.

1. Open **Dolphin Emulator**.
2. Click on **Controllers** in the top toolbar.
3. Under **Wii Remote 1**, select **Emulated Wii Remote** from the dropdown, then click **Configure**.

#### Recommended Key Mapping for Touchpad & Keyboard:
Since you are using a touchpad, you want to avoid games that require rapid pointer movement (like *Red Steel* or *Super Mario Galaxy* pointer-heavy sections) and focus on games that can be played with a classic controller layout (like *New Super Mario Bros. Wii*, *Mario Kart Wii*, or *Super Smash Bros. Brawl*).

**For a standard "Sideways Wii Remote" layout (e.g., Mario Bros):**
*   **D-Pad (Movement):** Up = `W`, Left = `A`, Down = `S`, Right = `D`
*   **Buttons:**
    *   `2` (Jump/Confirm) = `Spacebar` or `J`
    *   `1` (Run/Action) = `Left Shift` or `K`
    *   `A` = `Enter`
    *   `B` = `BackSpace`
*   **Plus (+):** `Escape`
*   **Minus (-):** `Tab`
*   **Shake (Motion Control):** Map "Shake X" to the `Q` key. (This is crucial for games like *New Super Mario Bros* where you have to shake the controller to spin).

**For Pointer Emulation (if a game requires pointing at the screen):**
*   Under the **Motion Simulation** tab, look for **Point**.
*   Set the **Up/Down/Left/Right** to your touchpad's mouse movement, or map them to the `I`, `K`, `J`, `L` keys on your keyboard for easier access.

---

### Step 3: Optimize Graphics for Microsoft Surface
Surface devices are great, but they can run hot and throttle performance during long gaming sessions. To keep the frame rate smooth:

1. In Dolphin, click **Graphics**.
2. **Backend:** Change this to **Direct3D 12** or **Vulkan** (Vulkan usually performs best on Surface Intel/AMD graphics).
3. **Resolution:** Keep **Internal Resolution** at **1x Native (640x528)** or **Auto (Window Size)**. Pushing it to 1080p will cause lag and drain your battery quickly.
4. **Aspect Ratio:** Set to **Auto** or **Force 16:9** depending on your preference.
5. Go to the **Advanced** tab and check **"Start in Fullscreen"** if you want an immersive experience, though keeping it in **Windowed Mode** makes it easier to access your on-screen keyboard.

---

### Step 4: Best Games for this Setup
Because you are limited to a touchpad and on-screen keyboard, you should choose games that require minimal inputs and no precise aiming:
*   **New Super Mario Bros. Wii:** Very easy to play with just a D-pad, two buttons, and one key mapped to "Shake."
*   **Fire Emblem: Radiant Dawn:** A turn-based strategy game that can be played entirely at your own pace using just the touchpad to click on menus.
*   **Animal Crossing: City Folk:** Very relaxed, can be played almost entirely with the touchpad acting as the Wii pointer.
*   **Turn-based RPGs (like Xenoblade Chronicles):** If you map the Classic Controller layout to your keyboard.

---

### Pro-Tip for Warehouse Breaks: "Tablet Mode" Mapping
If you want to play without the keyboard cluttering the screen, you can download a free third-party tool called **VirtualGamepad** or **GestureSign** from the Microsoft Store. These apps allow you to draw custom, transparent game buttons directly onto your Surface screen that map to keyboard keys, turning your Surface into a giant Nintendo Switch.
