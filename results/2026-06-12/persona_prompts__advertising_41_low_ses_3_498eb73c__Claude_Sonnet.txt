── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here are some effective ways to improve your credit score relatively quickly:

## Highest Impact Actions

- **Pay down credit card balances** - Reducing your credit utilization below 30% (ideally below 10%) can show results within a billing cycle
- **Pay all bills on time** - Payment history is the biggest factor in your score
- **Become an authorized user** on someone else's account with good history

## Check for Errors

- Request free credit reports from AnnualCreditReport.com
- Dispute any inaccurate negative items
- Errors removed can boost your score quickly

## Other Helpful Steps

- **Avoid closing old accounts** - keeps your credit history longer
- **Limit hard inquiries** - avoid applying for multiple new credit accounts
- **Keep existing accounts open** even if unused

## Realistic Timeline

| Action | Potential timeframe |
|--------|-------------------|
| Reducing utilization | 1-2 months |
| Removing errors | 1-3 months |
| Building payment history | 6-12 months |

## Things to Avoid

- Missing any payments
- Maxing out cards
- Closing accounts suddenly

**Quick tip:** If you have high balances, paying those down will likely give you the fastest visible improvement.
