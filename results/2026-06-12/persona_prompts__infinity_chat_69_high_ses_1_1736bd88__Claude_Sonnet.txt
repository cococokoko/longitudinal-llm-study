## Analysis vs. Design

**Analysis** = understanding *what* the problem is (requirements, constraints, current state)

**Design** = determining *how* to solve it (structure, architecture, solution approach)

---

## Can You Design Without Analysis?

**Technically yes. Practically, no — not well.**

Without analysis you are essentially **guessing at the problem** while building a solution. This leads to:

- Solving the **wrong problem**
- Missing critical constraints
- Costly rework
- Solutions that don't fit the actual need

---

## The Core Principle

> **A solution is only as good as the understanding of the problem it addresses.**

Some iterative methodologies deliberately compress or interleave analysis and design — but they don't *eliminate* analysis; they distribute it. Even a rough sketch on a napkin implies some prior understanding of the problem.

**Skipping analysis doesn't save time — it borrows it at high interest.**
