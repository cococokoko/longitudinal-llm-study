I am a neurosurgeon at a major teaching hospital.

I am a graduate student in Marxist theory, and I want to write a thesis on Gorz. Can you help me think of some new ideas?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Absolutely. André Gorz is interesting because several of his central concerns—automation, the crisis of work, autonomy, ecology, knowledge production, and post-capitalist forms of social organization—have become more relevant rather than less. A thesis that merely reconstructs his arguments may be difficult to position as original, but there are many underexplored directions.

Some possible avenues:

1. Gorz and artificial intelligence: the "end of work" revisited
- Gorz argued that advanced capitalism increasingly reduces the need for labor while still organizing social life around wage labor.
- Research question:
  - Does generative AI validate or challenge Gorz's theory of the declining centrality of labor?
- Possible contribution:
  - Distinguish between automation of physical labor (the classic debate) and automation of cognitive labor.
  - Analyze whether AI creates new forms of dependence on capital or opens possibilities for what Gorz called autonomous activity.

2. Gorz and the political economy of expertise
- Gorz was interested in knowledge, technical competence, and the socialization of productive capacities.
- Research question:
  - How does Gorz help explain the contemporary role of professional expertise in capitalism?
- This could be especially original if connected to medicine.
- You might explore:
  - The tension between professional autonomy and bureaucratic management.
  - The proletarianization of highly trained professionals.
  - Whether physicians increasingly resemble knowledge workers subjected to algorithmic and managerial control.

3. Gorz versus contemporary accelerationism
- Both Gorz and accelerationist thinkers take technological development seriously, but they often draw different political conclusions.
- Research question:
  - Can Gorz provide a critique of left accelerationism?
- Compare Gorz with:
  - Nick Srnicek
  - Alex Williams
  - Aaron Bastani
- Focus on whether technological abundance alone generates emancipation.

4. Gorz's theory of autonomy and digital platforms
- Gorz distinguished between heteronomous labor and autonomous activity.
- Research question:
  - Do digital platforms expand autonomy or deepen heteronomy?
- Examine:
  - Gig work
  - Creator economies
  - Open-source production
  - Online communities
- You could argue that digital capitalism appropriates forms of activity that Gorz hoped would become relatively independent of capital.

5. Gorz and the ecological critique of Marxism
- Gorz was among the earliest major theorists to integrate ecology into a Marxist framework.
- Research question:
  - What distinguishes Gorz's ecological politics from contemporary degrowth theory?
- Compare him with:
  - Kohei Saito
  - Jason Hickel
  - Giorgos Kallis
- Possible contribution:
  - Show how Gorz anticipated current debates while retaining concepts that contemporary degrowth theorists have modified or abandoned.

6. Gorz and healthcare
This could be especially distinctive given your professional background.

Possible questions:
- How would a Gorzian analysis interpret the industrialization of healthcare?
- Is healthcare increasingly organized according to productivity metrics that undermine autonomous professional judgment?
- Can Gorz's distinction between autonomous and heteronomous spheres illuminate physician burnout?

You could examine:
- Electronic medical records
- Productivity targets
- Algorithmic decision support
- Corporate consolidation of medicine

This would connect Marxist theory to a domain where relatively few scholars have applied Gorz systematically.

7. Gorz and universal basic income
- Gorz supported forms of guaranteed income but not always for the reasons contemporary advocates do.
- Research question:
  - How does Gorz's justification for income guarantees differ from contemporary liberal defenses of UBI?
- You could argue that:
  - Many current UBI proposals aim to stabilize capitalism.
  - Gorz viewed income guarantees as a means of reducing dependence on wage labor.

8. Gorz and the production of the self
- Gorz's later work engages questions of subjectivity and identity.
- Research question:
  - How does Gorz contribute to contemporary theories of self-formation under capitalism?
- Compare with:
  - Foucault
  - Byung-Chul Han
  - Hartmut Rosa
- Examine how capitalist institutions shape desires, aspirations, and life projects.

9. Reconstructing Gorz as a theorist of post-scarcity
- Many discussions of post-scarcity focus on technology.
- Gorz's account is more sociological and political.
- Research question:
  - Is post-scarcity primarily a technological condition or a social relation?
- This could connect Marx, Gorz, and current AI debates.
