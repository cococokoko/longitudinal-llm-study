# Effective Ways to Reduce Household Energy Use

## Quick Wins (Low/No Cost)
- **Adjust thermostat settings** – 68°F in winter, 78°F in summer saves significantly
- **Unplug devices** when not in use to eliminate standby power draw
- **Use cold water** for laundry (works well for most loads)
- **Run full loads** in dishwashers and washing machines
- **Switch to LED bulbs** if you haven't already

## Behavioral Changes
- Turn off lights when leaving rooms
- Air-dry dishes and clothes when possible
- Cook with lids on pots to retain heat
- Use microwaves/toaster ovens instead of full ovens for small meals
- Shorten showers (water heating is a major energy cost)

## Moderate Investments
- **Smart thermostats** – can reduce heating/cooling costs 10-15%
- **Weatherstripping and caulking** around doors and windows
- **Insulation improvements** in attic and walls
- **Low-flow fixtures** to reduce hot water demand

## Larger Upgrades
- Energy Star appliances when replacing old ones
- Heat pump water heaters
- Solar panels (ROI typically 6-10 years)
- Heat pump HVAC systems

## Monitoring
- Use a home energy monitor to identify high-consumption devices
- Review utility bills for usage trends
