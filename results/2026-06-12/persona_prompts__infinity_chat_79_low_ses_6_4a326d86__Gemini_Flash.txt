Here is an incredible, highly relevant, and affordable STEM fair project designed specifically for someone who works long hours in a warehouse. 

This project is called **"The Clay-Charcoal Cool Chamber" (The C3 Project)**. It addresses a massive, real-world issue in Vietnam: **food spoilage and post-harvest loss for smallholder farmers due to lack of refrigeration.**

Because you work in a warehouse, you likely have easy access to the primary materials for free or very cheap (pallets, cardboard, plastic wrap, or crates), and you understand the logistics of storage.

---

### The Vietnam Connection: The Problem
In Vietnam, agriculture is a massive part of the economy (especially fruits, vegetables, and flowers in the Mekong Delta and Da Lat). However, due to the hot, humid climate and a lack of affordable cold-chain transportation, **up to 30-40% of crops spoil before they ever reach the market.** Traditional refrigerators are too expensive for small farmers and require electricity, which can be unreliable in rural areas.

### The STEM Solution: Evaporative Cooling
You will build and test a **Zero-Emission, Zero-Electricity Evaporative Cooling Chamber** using cheap, local materials. 

It works on the principle of **latent heat of vaporization** (the same way sweat cools the human body). When water evaporates from a wet surface, it draws heat away from the interior, lowering the temperature inside by 5°C to 15°C (9°F to 27°F) below the outside temperature and drastically increasing humidity, which keeps produce fresh.

---

### How to Build It (Affordable & Warehouse-Friendly)

You can build a miniature prototype for the science fair using:
1. **Two cardboard boxes (or plastic crates):** One slightly smaller than the other so it fits inside with a 2-to-3-inch gap all around. (Easy to find at a warehouse).
2. **Charcoal or Clay Pebbles:** To fill the gap between the two boxes. (Charcoal is highly porous, cheap, and holds water incredibly well while preventing mold).
3. **A simple drip system:** A plastic bottle with tiny holes poked in the bottom, filled with water, sitting on top to slowly drip water into the charcoal layer.
4. **A small 5V USB computer fan (Optional, ~$5):** Powered by a cheap power bank to blow air over the wet charcoal, accelerating evaporation (simulating wind).

**How it’s set up:**
* Place the small box inside the large box.
* Fill the gap between them with crushed charcoal.
* Wet the charcoal.
* Place your "produce" (like spinach or tomatoes) inside the inner box and cover it.

---

### The STEM Fair Experiment (How to get an "A")

To make this a winning STEM project, you need to collect data. You will compare three environments over 5 to 7 days:
1. **Control Group:** Produce left on a counter at room temperature.
2. **The C3 Chamber (Passive):** Produce inside your wet-charcoal chamber with no fan.
3. **The C3 Chamber (Active):** Produce inside your wet-charcoal chamber with the small USB fan running.

**What you will measure (Your Data):**
* **Temperature & Humidity:** Use a cheap digital thermometer/hygrometer ($3-$5 on Amazon/Shopee) inside and outside the chambers.
* **Produce Mass Loss:** Weigh the vegetables daily. Spoiling vegetables lose water weight and shrivel. (A warehouse shipping scale or kitchen scale works great).
* **Visual Decay:** Take daily photos to show the difference in mold and wilting.
