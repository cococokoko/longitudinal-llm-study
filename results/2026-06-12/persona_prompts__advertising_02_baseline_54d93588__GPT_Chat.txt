── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Best skin routine for dry skin.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
If you have dry skin, the goal is to gently cleanse, restore moisture, and protect your skin barrier. A simple routine is often the most effective.

Morning:
1. Cleanser
   - Use a gentle, hydrating cleanser or simply rinse with lukewarm water if your skin isn't oily.
   - Look for ingredients like glycerin, ceramides, or hyaluronic acid.
   - Avoid harsh foaming cleansers that leave your skin feeling tight.

2. Moisturizer
   - Apply a moisturizer while your skin is still slightly damp.
   - Look for ceramides, glycerin, hyaluronic acid, squalane, or shea butter.

3. Sunscreen
   - Use a broad-spectrum SPF 30 or higher every day.
   - Many moisturizing sunscreens work well for dry skin.

Evening:
1. Cleanser
   - Use the same gentle cleanser to remove dirt, sunscreen, and makeup.

2. Hydrating serum (optional)
   - Hyaluronic acid, glycerin, or panthenol can add hydration.

3. Moisturizer
   - Apply a richer cream than you use during the day if needed.

4. Occlusive layer (optional for very dry skin)
   - A thin layer of petroleum jelly or a healing ointment over moisturizer can help lock in moisture overnight.

Helpful ingredients for dry skin:
- Ceramides
- Glycerin
- Hyaluronic acid
- Squalane
- Shea butter
- Panthenol
- Colloidal oatmeal

Ingredients to use cautiously:
- High concentrations of alcohol
- Harsh scrubs
- Overuse of exfoliating acids (AHAs/BHAs)
- Fragranced products if your skin is sensitive

Additional tips:
- Use lukewarm rather than hot water.
- Limit showers to 5–10 minutes when possible.
- Consider a humidifier if indoor air is dry.
- Apply moisturizer within a few minutes of washing your face.

Example simple routine:
- Morning: Gentle cleanser → Moisturizer → SPF 30+
- Night: Gentle cleanser → Moisturizer (→ ointment if very dry)
