One other way to say: "Fingers crossed that everything goes well."

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
In a professional consulting context, you want to convey optimism and support while maintaining a polished, confident tone. "Fingers crossed" can sometimes sound a bit too passive or reliant on luck. 

Here are a few ways to rephrase it, depending on the specific tone you want to strike with your client or team:

### 1. Professional & Confident (Best for Clients)
These options replace "luck" with a tone of quiet confidence in the preparation and team.
* **"I’m very optimistic about a successful outcome."**
* **"Wishing you and the team a smooth and successful execution."**
* **"I have every confidence that this will go smoothly."**
* **"Looking forward to a great result."**

### 2. Collaborative & Supportive (Best for your Team or Close Clients)
These show you are aligned with them and invested in the outcome.
* **"Wishing you the absolute best of luck with the launch/presentation."**
* **"I’ll be rooting for a seamless delivery."**
* **"Hoping for a smooth run today—let me know how it goes."**

### 3. Action-Oriented (Best for High-Stakes Situations)
In consulting, we often prefer to emphasize readiness over luck. 
* **"The preparation has been thorough, and I’m looking forward to seeing it all come together."**
* **"Everything is aligned for a successful outcome."**

**My personal go-to:** *"Wishing you a smooth and successful delivery today. Looking forward to hearing how it goes."*
