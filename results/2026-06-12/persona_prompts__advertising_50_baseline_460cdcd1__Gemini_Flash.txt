── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Ways to save on travel.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Saving money on travel doesn’t mean you have to sacrifice having a great experience. It just requires strategic planning, flexibility, and knowing where to look for deals. 

Here is a comprehensive guide on how to save money before, during, and after your trip, categorized by stage.

---

### 1. Planning & Booking (Before You Go)

#### Flights
*   **Be flexible with dates and times:** Flying mid-week (Tuesdays and Wednesdays) is usually cheaper than weekends. Early morning or late-night (red-eye) flights are also significantly less expensive.
*   **Use incognito mode and flight aggregators:** Search for flights using Google Flights, Skyscanner, or Momondo. Use your browser's "Incognito" or private mode to prevent airlines from raising prices based on your search history.
*   **Set fare alerts:** Use Google Flights or Hopper to track prices for your destination and get notified when they drop.
*   **Embrace the "Everywhere" search:** If you just want to go *somewhere*, use Skyscanner’s "Explore Everywhere" feature to find the cheapest destinations from your home airport.
*   **Fly budget airlines (with caution):** Airlines like Ryanair, Spirit, or EasyJet offer cheap base fares, but charge for *everything* else. Travel with a personal item only to avoid baggage fees.

#### Accommodation
*   **Look beyond hotels:** Consider hostels (many offer private rooms), guesthouses, homestays, or vacation rentals (Airbnb, Vrbo). 
*   **Try house sitting or home exchanges:** Websites like TrustedHousesitters allow you to stay in someone’s home for free in exchange for watching their pets.
*   **Book directly:** Once you find a hotel on a booking site (like Booking.com), call or email the hotel directly. They will often beat the online price or throw in free breakfast to avoid paying the booking site's commission.
*   **Stay slightly outside the tourist center:** Accommodations just a 10–15 minute walk or train ride outside the main tourist zone are often 30–50% cheaper.

---

### 2. Transportation & Getting Around

*   **Embrace public transit:** Avoid taxis and rideshares (like Uber). Use subways, buses, and trains. Many cities offer multi-day transit passes that save you money.
*   **Walk:** It’s free, healthy, and the best way to see a city.
*   **Night trains and buses:** If traveling between cities, book a night train or bus (like FlixBus). You save the cost of a night’s accommodation and don't lose a day of sightseeing.
*   **Rent cars strategically:** Avoid renting at the airport, as they charge high airport concession fees. Rent from a location in the city center instead.

---

### 3. Food & Drink

*   **Eat your main meal at lunch:** Many high-end restaurants offer "lunch specials" or *menus del día* that feature the same food as dinner but at half the price.
*   **Shop at local supermarkets:** Buy pastries, fruit, cheese, and bread at local grocery stores or outdoor markets for cheap breakfasts and picnic lunches.
*   **Avoid eating near major tourist attractions:** Restaurants facing the Colosseum, Eiffel Tower, or Times Square are overpriced and often low quality. Walk 3 to 4 blocks away into residential neighborhoods for authentic, cheaper food.
*   **Bring a reusable water bottle:** In countries with safe tap water, this saves you $5–$10 a day. If the water isn't safe, buy a bottle with a built-in filter (like LifeStraw).

---

### 4. Activities & Sightseeing

*   **Take free walking tours:** Almost every major city has them. They are a great way to get oriented. (Just remember to tip the guide a few dollars/euros at the end).
*   **Look for free museum days:** Many world-class museums have free entry on certain days of the week or month (e.g., the Louvre is free on the first Friday of the month from 6 PM, except in July and August).
*   **Use city tourism passes:** If you plan on seeing a lot of paid attractions, passes like the "Go City" pass or local city cards can save you up to 50% on entry fees and include public transit.
*   **Enjoy nature:** Hiking, visiting public parks, beaches, and exploring local neighborhoods cost absolutely nothing.

---

### 5. Financial & Packing Hacks

*   **Get a travel credit card:** Earn points and miles on your everyday spending to redeem for free flights and hotel stays. Ensure your card has **no foreign transaction fees**.
*   **Avoid airport currency exchanges:** They offer the worst exchange rates. Instead, withdraw local currency from a bank ATM when you arrive. (Use a debit card like Charles Schwab, which refunds all ATM fees worldwide).
*   **Pack light (Carry-on only):** You will save money on checked bag fees, avoid the risk of lost luggage, and be much more mobile on public transit.
*   **Get travel insurance:** It seems like an extra expense, but a medical emergency or trip cancellation can cost you thousands of dollars. 

### Summary: The Golden Rule of Budget Travel
You can save money by trading **convenience for time**, or **luxury for authenticity**. Decide what matters most to you, plan ahead, and remain flexible!
