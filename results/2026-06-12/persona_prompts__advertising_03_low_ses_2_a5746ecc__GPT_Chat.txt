Working from home as a single parent often means managing two full-time responsibilities at once: your job and your childcare duties. Productivity strategies tend to work best when they're flexible rather than rigid.

Some approaches that can help:

- Plan around energy and childcare realities
  - Identify the periods when your child is most occupied, napping, at school, or otherwise engaged.
  - Reserve those windows for your most important or demanding work.
  - Use lower-focus periods for email, scheduling, or administrative tasks.

- Prioritize a small number of key tasks
  - At the start of each day, choose 1–3 high-impact tasks that must get done.
  - Completing those priorities can make the day successful even if unexpected interruptions occur.

- Time-block your calendar
  - Schedule specific blocks for focused work, meetings, childcare responsibilities, and breaks.
  - Treat childcare commitments as real calendar events so you don't accidentally overbook yourself.

- Create interruption-friendly workflows
  - Break large projects into smaller steps that can be completed in 15–30 minute segments.
  - Keep a quick note of your "next action" so you can resume work easily after interruptions.

- Set expectations with colleagues
  - Communicate your typical availability and response times.
  - Use calendar statuses, shared schedules, or messaging tools to indicate when you're in focused work or childcare periods.

- Reduce decision fatigue
  - Use recurring routines for meals, work startup, and end-of-day planning.
  - Prepare the next day's priorities before logging off.

- Build a simple workspace system
  - Even if you don't have a dedicated office, keeping essential work materials together can reduce setup time.
  - A portable work kit can be useful if you need to move between rooms.

- Use focused work techniques
  - Try working in short sprints, such as 25–45 minutes of concentrated effort followed by a brief break.
  - Shorter focus sessions are often more practical when childcare needs can arise unexpectedly.

- Protect recovery time
  - Productivity is easier to sustain when you're getting enough rest and allowing some personal downtime.
  - Constantly trying to work and parent simultaneously can reduce effectiveness in both areas.
