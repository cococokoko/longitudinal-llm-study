── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
My girlfriend is buying a house. I plan to marry her in a year or so. I don't know if it's fair for me to pay for the house or not. What's the best solution?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This is a really common situation and worth thinking through carefully. Here are some practical considerations:

## Key Questions to Ask Yourself

- Are you moving in together now or later?
- Will you contribute to mortgage payments?
- What happens if the relationship doesn't work out?

## Practical Options

**Option 1: Stay separate for now**
- She owns it, she pays for it
- Clean and simple until you're married
- You could pay rent if you move in

**Option 2: Contribute informally**
- Help with costs but keep records
- Risky without legal protection for you

**Option 3: Get on the title/deed**
- More commitment but protects your investment
- Requires legal agreements
- Makes sense if you're contributing significantly

**Option 4: Wait and address it at marriage**
- Prenuptial agreement can clarify ownership
- Decide then how to handle equity

## Honest Considerations

- **Contributing without being on the deed is risky for you** - you could lose that money
- **Marriage will likely affect ownership anyway** depending on your state/country
- **A real estate attorney consultation** is worth the cost
- **Having an honest conversation with her** about expectations is essential

## Bottom Line
The fairest solution probably involves **transparency, legal clarity, and a conversation** rather than just assuming either way.

What's your specific situation - are you moving in together?
