# Finding the Perfect Hiking Trails

Here are some great ways to discover trails that match your needs:

## Online Resources
- **AllTrails** - Most popular app/website with reviews, photos, and difficulty ratings
- **Hiking Project** - Detailed trail maps and community reviews
- **TrailLink** - Great for rail-trails and multi-use paths
- **Recreation.gov** - For national parks and federal lands

## Key Factors to Consider

### Difficulty
- **Easy** - Flat, short, well-maintained
- **Moderate** - Some elevation gain, longer distance
- **Hard** - Steep, technical, longer mileage

### Personal Needs
- Distance and elevation gain
- Trail surface (paved, dirt, rocky)
- Dog-friendly or not
- Accessibility requirements
- Crowd levels

## Tips for Choosing Well
- **Read recent reviews** - Conditions change seasonally
- **Check elevation profiles** - Distance alone can be misleading
- **Know your fitness level** honestly
- **Consider the season** - Some trails close in winter
- **Look at photos** from other hikers

## Local Options
- Visit a **local REI** or outdoor shop for recommendations
- Contact **ranger stations** for insider knowledge
- Join **local hiking groups** on Facebook or Meetup
