Here's a practical tip from my experience working in a professional environment:

**Communicate expectations clearly and consistently.**

When managing a team, make sure everyone understands:
- Their specific roles and responsibilities
- Project deadlines and priorities
- How their work contributes to the bigger picture

Ambiguity is one of the biggest sources of conflict and inefficiency on a team. When people aren't sure what's expected of them, mistakes happen, deadlines get missed, and frustration builds.

I'd also add that **regular check-ins** - even brief ones - go a long way toward keeping everyone aligned and giving team members a chance to raise concerns before they become bigger problems.

Clear communication builds trust, and trust is really the foundation of any effective team.
