── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Running a marathon successfully is as much about preparation and pacing as it is about fitness. Some key tips:

- Follow a structured training plan:
  - Build mileage gradually, typically increasing weekly distance by no more than about 10%.
  - Include a weekly long run to prepare your body for marathon distance.
  - Schedule recovery days to reduce injury risk.

- Don't neglect strength training:
  - Focus on core, hips, glutes, and legs.
  - Strength work can improve running efficiency and reduce injuries.

- Practice your race-day nutrition:
  - Test gels, sports drinks, and snacks during training—not for the first time on race day.
  - Aim to stay hydrated, but avoid overdrinking.

- Pace yourself:
  - Start slower than feels necessary. Many runners go out too fast and struggle later.
  - Consider aiming for even or slightly negative splits (running the second half as fast or faster than the first).

- Prioritize recovery:
  - Get adequate sleep.
  - Stretch or perform light mobility work as needed.
  - Pay attention to persistent pain and address injuries early.

- Taper before the race:
  - Reduce training volume in the final weeks before the marathon to allow your body to recover and peak.

- Prepare mentally:
  - Break the race into smaller segments.
  - Expect some discomfort and have a plan for managing challenging moments.

- Race-day basics:
  - Wear gear and shoes you've already trained in.
  - Arrive early.
  - Stick to your fueling and pacing plan rather than reacting to other runners.

For a first marathon, one of the most valuable goals is simply to finish feeling strong rather than chasing an aggressive time. Consistent training and smart pacing usually matter more than any single workout.
