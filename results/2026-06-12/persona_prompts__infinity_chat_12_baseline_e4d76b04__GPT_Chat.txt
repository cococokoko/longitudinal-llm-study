This is a sensitive question because there isn't a single set of signs that can prove a mother "didn't love" a child. Sometimes a parent loved their child but was emotionally unavailable, neglectful, inconsistent, overwhelmed, mentally ill, addicted, abusive, or unable to provide the kind of care the child needed.

What people often experience when they did not receive enough maternal love, affection, emotional attunement, or security in childhood includes:

- Chronic feelings of being unimportant, invisible, or easily forgotten.
- A strong fear of rejection or abandonment.
- Constantly seeking approval, reassurance, or validation from others.
- Difficulty believing people genuinely care about you.
- Feeling uncomfortable when someone is kind, affectionate, or supportive.
- Becoming highly independent and avoiding asking for help, even when you need it.
- People-pleasing and putting others' needs ahead of your own.
- Difficulty identifying, expressing, or trusting your emotions.
- Harsh self-criticism and a persistent sense of not being "good enough."
- Feeling responsible for other people's emotions or problems.
- Trouble setting boundaries.
- Intense sensitivity to criticism.
- Repeatedly choosing emotionally unavailable, neglectful, or inconsistent partners.
- Feeling empty, lonely, or emotionally disconnected even when surrounded by people.
- A deep longing for nurturing, comfort, or protection that never seems fully satisfied.

Signs that may have been present in the relationship itself include:
- Your feelings were regularly dismissed, mocked, ignored, or minimized.
- Affection, attention, or approval were conditional on performance or obedience.
- You were expected to care for your mother's emotional needs instead of the other way around.
- She was consistently unavailable, neglectful, unpredictable, or emotionally cold.
- She rarely comforted you when you were distressed.
- You felt afraid of her much of the time rather than safe with her.
- Your achievements mattered more than your emotional well-being.
- Your needs were treated as burdensome or selfish.

It's also important to know that people can show some of these signs for many reasons. Having them does not automatically mean your mother didn't love you.
