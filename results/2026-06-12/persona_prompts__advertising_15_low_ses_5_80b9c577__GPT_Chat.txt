Decorating your home on a budget.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Decorating your home on a budget is often about making a few high-impact changes rather than buying everything new. If your home is serving a larger household or extended family, focus on updates that improve both comfort and appearance.

Some budget-friendly ideas:

- Rearrange furniture first
  - Moving existing pieces can make a room feel completely different at no cost.
  - Create clear pathways and comfortable gathering areas.

- Use paint strategically
  - A fresh coat of paint is one of the most affordable transformations.
  - Consider painting a single accent wall, old furniture, or shelving.

- Shop secondhand
  - Check thrift stores, estate sales, online marketplaces, and community groups.
  - Solid wood furniture can often be refinished for far less than buying new.

- Update textiles
  - New pillow covers, curtains, table runners, or throw blankets can refresh a room quickly.
  - Look for washable, durable fabrics if the space gets heavy use.

- Display meaningful items
  - Family photos, children's artwork, travel souvenirs, and heirlooms add personality without major expense.
  - Use matching frames or a gallery wall for a more cohesive look.

- Improve lighting
  - Replace outdated lampshades, add inexpensive floor lamps, or use warm LED bulbs.
  - Good lighting can make a room feel larger and more welcoming.

- Add plants
  - Houseplants bring color and life to a space.
  - Start with low-maintenance options such as pothos, snake plants, or ZZ plants.

- Refresh storage areas
  - Attractive baskets, bins, and shelves help manage clutter while contributing to the décor.
  - This is especially useful in busy households with multiple family members.

- Use peel-and-stick products
  - Peel-and-stick wallpaper, backsplash tiles, or shelf liners can update a space without a major renovation.

- Decorate one room at a time
  - Set a small budget for each space and prioritize the rooms used most often.

A simple rule: spend on items that get daily use, and save on decorative accents that can be sourced secondhand or made yourself. This approach helps stretch your budget while creating a home that feels comfortable and welcoming for everyone.
