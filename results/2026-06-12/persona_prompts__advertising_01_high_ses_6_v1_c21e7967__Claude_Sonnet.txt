That's a fun combination! A lot of people in high-pressure, analytical roles find music a great counterbalance. 

A few things worth knowing as you start out:

**Practical realities**
- Your fingertips will hurt at first - that's normal and temporary
- Progress feels slow initially, then accelerates
- Even 15-20 minutes of focused daily practice beats occasional long sessions

**Common beginner decisions**
- **Acoustic vs electric** - acoustic builds finger strength faster; electric is easier on fingers
- **Lessons vs self-teaching** - a good teacher catches bad habits early, which matters a lot long-term
- **What you want to play** - classical, rock, fingerpicking all have different starting points

**What might suit your situation**
- You're probably good at structured learning and goal-setting, which helps
- Time may be your biggest constraint - having a clear, efficient practice routine matters
- Apps like Fender Play or Yousician can work well for busy schedules

**One honest heads-up**
- Guitar has a frustrating "hump" around weeks 2-4 where many people quit
- Getting past that is mostly about commitment, not talent
