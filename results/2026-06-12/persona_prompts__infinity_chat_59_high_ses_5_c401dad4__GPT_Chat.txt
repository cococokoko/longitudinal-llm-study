── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
One practical tip: create a single “daily dashboard” for your teaching day.

At the start of each day, keep one page (digital or paper) that includes:
- Today's classes
- Any materials you need for each class
- Students who need follow-up
- Grading tasks
- Time-sensitive deadlines or meetings

As new tasks arise, add them to that same page instead of scattering notes across emails, sticky notes, and notebooks.

For high school teachers, a lot of stress comes from managing many small responsibilities across multiple classes. Having one central place to capture and review everything can reduce missed tasks and make transitions between classes much smoother.
