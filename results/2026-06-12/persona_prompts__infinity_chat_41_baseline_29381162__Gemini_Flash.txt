Here are 3 short, actionable tips for self-development:

1. **Embrace the "Two-Minute Rule"**
If a task takes less than two minutes to do (like washing a dish, replying to an email, or putting away your shoes), do it immediately. This builds momentum, prevents procrastination, and keeps your environment and mind clutter-free.

2. **Read 10 Pages of a Book Daily**
Don't pressure yourself to read hours a day. Just 10 pages of a non-fiction or self-improvement book daily adds up to about 3,650 pages a year—roughly 12 to 15 books. Small, consistent inputs lead to massive knowledge over time.

3. **Practice "Active Reflection" Weekly**
Every Sunday, spend 10 minutes asking yourself two questions: *What went well this week?* and *Where did I struggle?* Adjust your habits based on the answers. Self-awareness is the foundation of all personal growth.
