── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
What are the benefits of hardwood flooring over other types?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Hardwood flooring offers several advantages compared with carpet, vinyl, laminate, and some other flooring materials:

- Durability and longevity
  - Solid hardwood can last for decades, often 50 years or more with proper care.
  - Many hardwood floors can be sanded and refinished multiple times to remove scratches and wear.

- Appearance and value
  - Hardwood has a natural, timeless look that many homeowners find attractive.
  - It can complement a wide range of interior design styles.
  - Homes with hardwood flooring are often perceived as more desirable and may have stronger resale appeal.

- Easy maintenance
  - Dust, dirt, and pet hair can be swept, vacuumed, or mopped up relatively easily.
  - Hardwood does not trap allergens the way carpet can.

- Variety
  - Available in many wood species, colors, grain patterns, plank widths, and finishes.
  - Can be stained or refinished to change its appearance over time.

- Indoor air quality
  - Hardwood does not hold dust, pollen, pet dander, and other allergens as readily as carpet.
  - This can make cleaning easier and may contribute to a cleaner indoor environment.

- Strength
  - Quality hardwood is resistant to everyday wear when properly finished and maintained.
  - Harder wood species can withstand significant foot traffic.

- Sustainability
  - Wood is a renewable resource when sourced from responsibly managed forests.
  - Because hardwood floors last a long time, they may require replacement less frequently than some alternatives.

However, hardwood also has some drawbacks compared with other flooring types:
- Generally more expensive than laminate, vinyl, or carpet.
- Can be scratched or dented by pets, furniture, or heavy impacts.
- Sensitive to moisture and humidity changes.
- May feel harder and noisier underfoot than carpet.

Hardwood is often a strong choice when long-term durability, appearance, and resale value are priorities, while other flooring types may be preferable for high-moisture areas, tighter budgets, or maximum comfort underfoot.
