Explain computational irreducibility like I'm 5.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Imagine you want to know what happens at the very end of a movie. 

Usually, you can just ask a friend who has seen it, or read a quick summary online. That’s a **shortcut**.

But imagine a special, magical movie where **shortcuts are impossible**. The only way to find out how it ends is to sit down and watch every single second of it, from the very beginning to the very end. You can't fast-forward, and you can't guess.

**Computational irreducibility** is just a big, fancy science word for that kind of movie. 

It means that for some things in nature—like the weather, or how a pile of leaves swirls in the wind—there is **no cheat code or math trick** to predict what will happen next. 

The universe is running a simulation, and the only way to see what happens tomorrow is to wait and let tomorrow happen!
