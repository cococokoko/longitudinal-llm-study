- Durability and longevity
  - High-quality hardwood can last for decades, often 50 years or more with proper care.

- Appearance
  - Hardwood has a natural, timeless look that many people find attractive.

  - Hardwood floors are often viewed as a premium feature by homebuyers.
  - They can help improve a home's resale appeal.

- Easier cleaning
  - Hardwood does not trap dust, pet dander, and allergens the way carpet can.
  - Regular sweeping and occasional mopping are usually enough for maintenance.

- Versatility
  - Hardwood works well with many decorating styles, from traditional to modern.
  - It can be refinished in different stain colors if design preferences change.

- Better indoor air quality
  - Unlike carpet, hardwood does not hold as many allergens, dust mites, or odors.

Compared with other flooring types:
- Vs. carpet: Easier to clean, longer-lasting, and less likely to trap allergens.
- Vs. laminate: Can typically be refinished rather than replaced when worn.
- Vs. vinyl: Often considered more premium and may add more resale value.
- Vs. tile: Warmer and more comfortable underfoot in many climates.

Potential drawbacks to consider:
- Usually more expensive upfront than laminate, vinyl, or carpet.
- Can be scratched by pets, furniture, or heavy foot traffic.
- May be damaged by excessive moisture or standing water.
- Can feel harder underfoot than carpet, which may matter if you spend long periods standing.
