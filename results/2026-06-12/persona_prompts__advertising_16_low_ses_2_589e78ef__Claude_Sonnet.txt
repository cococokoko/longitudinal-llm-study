# Managing Stress and Anxiety as a Single Parent

You're already doing something remarkable by juggling childcare and work solo. Here are practical strategies that fit real life:

## Quick Relief (When You Have Minutes)
- **Deep breathing** - even 3-4 slow breaths helps reset your nervous system
- **Step outside briefly** - fresh air and a change of scene
- **Grounding technique** - name 5 things you can see right now
- **Shake it out** - literally shake your hands/body to release tension

## Daily Habits That Build Resilience
- **Sleep protection** - prioritize this even when it's hard
- **Movement** - even a 10-minute walk with your child counts
- **Limit news/social media** scrolling, especially at night
- **One small thing for yourself** daily, however small

## Mindset Shifts
- **"Good enough" parenting** is genuinely good parenting
- Let go of comparing yourself to two-parent households
- **Ask for help** - it models healthy behavior for your child

## Building Your Support System
- Connect with other single parents (online communities count)
- Be specific when asking for help ("Can you watch them Tuesday?")
- Look into local family support resources

## When to Seek More Support
If anxiety is affecting sleep, work, or daily functioning, talking to a doctor or therapist is a real strength, not a weakness.
