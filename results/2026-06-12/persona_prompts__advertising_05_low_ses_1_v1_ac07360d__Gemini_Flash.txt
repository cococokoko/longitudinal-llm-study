── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I work full-time in a fast food job with an unpredictable schedule.

Healthy meal planning on a budget.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Working full-time in fast food with an unpredictable schedule is a unique challenge. You are on your feet all day, surrounded by free or discounted unhealthy food, and your sleep/wake cycles are constantly shifting. 

To make healthy eating work, your system needs to be **low-effort, highly flexible, and cheap.** 

Here is a realistic, budget-friendly guide tailored specifically to your lifestyle.

---

### Phase 1: The "No-Cook" Grocery List (Under $40/week)
When you get off a closing shift at 2 AM, you will not want to cook. You need foods that require zero prep but are highly nutritious.

*   **Proteins:**
    *   **Canned Tuna/Salmon:** $1.00/can (Mix with Greek yogurt or mustard instead of heavy mayo).
    *   **Eggs:** $2.50/dozen (The ultimate cheap, fast protein).
    *   **Greek Yogurt (Plain, large tub):** $3.50 (High protein, great for breakfast or snacks).
    *   **Rotisserie Chicken:** $6.00–$8.00 (Buy one, shred it immediately, and use it for 3–4 meals).
*   **Carbs & Grains:**
    *   **Oats (Rolled or Instant):** $2.50/large tub (Extremely cheap, filling, and fast).
    *   **Canned Black/Pinto Beans:** $0.80/can (Rinse and eat).
    *   **Brown Rice Microwave Pouches:** $1.25/pouch (Saves time when you can't wait 40 minutes for rice to boil).
*   **Produce (Cheap & Long-Lasting):**
    *   **Bananas:** $1.50/bunch (The cheapest pre-workout/pre-shift snack).
    *   **Frozen Broccoli/Spinach:** $1.25/bag (Won't rot in your fridge if your schedule changes).
    *   **Apples or Oranges:** $3.00/bag (Lasts for weeks).

---

### Phase 2: The "Unpredictable Schedule" Meal Prep Strategy
Traditional "Sunday Prep" doesn't work when you work weekends or split shifts. Instead, use **Component Prep**. 

Instead of making 5 identical meals, prep 3 versatile components that take under 20 minutes total:

1.  **The Grain:** Boil a large pot of brown rice or quinoa (15 mins).
2.  **The Protein:** Hard-boil 6 eggs (10 mins) and shred a store-bought rotisserie chicken (5 mins).
3.  **The Veggie:** Roast a sheet pan of frozen broccoli with olive oil, salt, and garlic powder (20 mins).

**How to assemble in 2 minutes before a shift:**
*   **Bowl 1:** Rice + Shredded Chicken + Frozen Broccoli + Soy Sauce (Microwave at work).
*   **Bowl 2:** 2 Hard-boiled eggs + Black beans + Salsa + Greek yogurt (Cold lunch, no microwave needed).
*   **Bowl 3:** Oats + Scoop of Greek yogurt + Banana + Cinnamon (Perfect before an early morning opening shift).

---

### Phase 3: Surviving the Shift (Without eating the menu)
The temptation to eat free/cheap fast food is your biggest budget and health obstacle. 

*   **The "One-Item" Rule:** If you get free or discounted meals, don't reject them—just hack them. 
    *   *Instead of:* Burger, fries, and a soda.
    *   *Try:* Just the grilled chicken breast or burger patty (no bun/sauce), and bring your own apple and water from home.
*   **The Locker Stash:** Keep a "survival kit" in your locker or car. If you get stuck working a double, you won't resort to the fryer.
    *   Peanut butter jar + a spoon.
    *   Bananas.
    *   Almonds or peanuts (buy in bulk).
    *   Protein shaker bottle with a scoop of protein powder (just add water).

---

### Phase 4: 3 Ultra-Cheap, 5-Minute Recipes

#### 1. The "Post-Close" Savory Oats (Cost: ~$1.00)
*   **Ingredients:** 1/2 cup oats, 1 cup water, 1 egg, handful of frozen spinach, soy sauce.
*   **How-to:** Microwave oats and spinach in water for 90 seconds. Crack a raw egg directly into the hot oatmeal, stir vigorously (the heat cooks the egg, making it creamy), and top with soy sauce and hot sauce. 

#### 2. The 3-Minute Mediterranean Tuna Salad (Cost: ~$1.50)
*   **Ingredients:** 1 can drained tuna, 1/2 cup rinsed canned chickpeas, 1 spoonful of Greek yogurt, lemon juice, salt, pepper.
*   **How-to:** Mix everything in a bowl. Eat with a fork or scoop with cheap tortilla chips. No cooking required.

#### 3. The "Before-Opening-Shift" Smoothie (Cost: ~$1.20)
*   **Ingredients:** 1 banana, 1/2 cup Greek yogurt, 1 tbsp peanut butter, 1/2 cup water or milk, ice.
*   **How-to:** Blend and drink on your commute. It has protein, healthy fats, and complex carbs to keep you full through the morning rush.

### Summary Checklist for Success:
*   **Never go to a shift hungry.** Eat a high-protein snack (like Greek yogurt or eggs) *before* you clock in.
*   **Buy frozen veggies.** They don't go bad if your schedule gets crazy and you can't cook.
*   **Invest in a good water bottle.** Fast food environments are hot and dehydrating; drinking water stops you from mistaking thirst for sugar cravings.
