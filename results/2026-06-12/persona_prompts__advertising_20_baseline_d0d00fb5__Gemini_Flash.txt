── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Where can I find organic and sustainable clothing?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Finding organic and sustainable clothing has become much easier as the "slow fashion" movement has grown. When shopping, the key is to look for third-party certifications to ensure the brands are actually sustainable (and not just "greenwashing"). 

Here is a comprehensive guide on where to find sustainable clothing, categorized by online marketplaces, specific brands, budget-friendly options, and what certifications to look for.

---

### 1. Multi-Brand Online Marketplaces (Best for Variety)
These platforms curate hundreds of sustainable brands in one place, making it easy to compare styles and prices.

*   **Our Common Industries (formerly Earthhero):** A great one-stop shop for eco-friendly clothing, home goods, and travel gear.
*   **Made Trade:** A curated marketplace featuring exceptionally designed, ethically made, and vegan clothing and home goods.
*   **DoneGood:** Often called the "Amazon of social good," this site lets you search thousands of ethical brands with easy filters for organic, vegan, or USA-made.
*   **TenTree:** Known for planting 10 trees for every item purchased, they offer highly sustainable, certified organic cotton and recycled polyester activewear and basics.

### 2. Top Sustainable Brands (By Category)

#### **For Everyday Basics & Loungewear**
*   **Organic Basics:** (Denmark/Global) Focuses on high-quality, certified organic cotton and Tencel underwear, tees, and activewear.
*   **PACT:** (USA) One of the most accessible organic brands. Everything they make is GOTS-certified organic cotton and Fair Trade Certified. Great for bedding, underwear, and casual wear.
*   **Kowtow:** (New Zealand) Offers beautiful, structured, minimalist designs made entirely from 100% Fairtrade organic cotton.

#### **For Denim & Casual Wear**
*   **Nudie Jeans:** (Sweden) A pioneer in sustainable denim. They use 100% organic cotton and offer **free repairs for life** on their jeans.
*   **Outerknown:** (USA) Founded by pro-surfer Kelly Slater, they focus on coastal-style clothing made from organic cotton, hemp, and recycled fishing nets (Econyl).
*   **MATE the Label:** (USA) Uses only organic cotton, linen, and non-toxic dyes to create incredibly comfortable sweatsets and basics.

#### **For Workwear & Elevated Style**
*   **Eileen Fisher:** A pioneer in luxury sustainable fashion. They use organic linen, cotton, and ethical wool, and have a robust circular take-back program ("Renew").
*   **Amour Vert:** (USA) Uses organic cotton, silk, and modal. They plant a tree for every tee purchased and make most of their clothes in limited quantities in California.
*   **People Tree:** (UK/Europe) A pioneer in Fair Trade fashion, offering beautiful organic cotton dresses, knitwear, and work-appropriate attire.

---

### 3. Budget-Friendly & Circular Options
Sustainable fashion can be expensive. Here are the best ways to shop sustainably on a budget:

*   **Secondhand & Vintage (The most sustainable option):**
    *   **Online Consignment:** **ThredUP** and **The RealReal** (for luxury) allow you to filter by brand and material.
    *   **Peer-to-Peer Apps:** **Depop**, **Poshmark**, and **Vinted** are great for finding specific sustainable brands (like PACT or Patagonia) secondhand for a fraction of the price.
*   **Brand Take-Back Sites:** Many sustainable brands have their own resale platforms where they sell gently used items. Examples include **Worn Wear (Patagonia)**, **Eileen Fisher Renew**, and **Taylor Stitch Restitch**.

---

### 4. What to Look For (Certifications)
To ensure a brand is actually organic and sustainable, look for these logos on their website or clothing tags:

*   **GOTS (Global Organic Textile Standard):** The gold standard for organic fibers. It ensures the cotton was grown without toxic pesticides and that the manufacturing process was socially responsible.
*   **OEKO-TEX Standard 100:** Guarantees the finished fabric is free from harmful levels of toxic chemicals and heavy metals.
*   **Fair Trade Certified:** Ensures the factory workers who made the clothes were paid fair wages and worked in safe conditions.
*   **B Corp:** Certification awarded to companies that meet the highest standards of social and environmental performance, transparency, and accountability.
*   **Bluesign:** Indicates that the textiles were manufactured with the lowest possible impact on people and the environment.

### A Quick Tip for Shopping:
If you find a brand you like but aren't sure if they are truly ethical, look them up on **"Good On You"** (goodonyou.eco). It is a free directory and app that rates thousands of fashion brands on their treatment of people, the planet, and animals.
