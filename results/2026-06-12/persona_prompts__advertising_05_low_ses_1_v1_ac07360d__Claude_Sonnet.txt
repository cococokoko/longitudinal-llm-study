# Healthy Meal Planning for Fast Food Workers on a Budget

## Your Specific Challenges
- Irregular shift times make cooking schedules hard
- Tired after shifts = low motivation to cook
- Budget constraints are real
- You may eat at work sometimes

---

## Core Strategy: Cook Once, Eat Multiple Times

### Batch cooking on your day off
Pick **1-2 days** to prep food for the whole week
- Takes 1-2 hours total
- Covers most of your meals

---

## Budget-Friendly Staple Foods

| Food | Why It Works | Approx. Cost |
|------|-------------|--------------|
| Dried beans/lentils | Protein, filling, very cheap | ~$1-2/lb |
| Rice or oats | Energy, versatile | ~$1-2/lb |
| Frozen vegetables | Nutritious, no waste | ~$1-2/bag |
| Eggs | Fast protein, cheap | ~$3-4/dozen |
| Bananas | Quick energy, portable | ~$0.20 each |

---

## Practical Meal Ideas

**Quick after-shift meals (under 10 minutes)**
- Rice + canned beans + frozen veggies
- Scrambled eggs + toast
- Oatmeal with peanut butter and banana

**Grab-and-go options**
- Hard boiled eggs
- Peanut butter sandwiches
- Portioned trail mix

---

## Realistic Weekly Budget

**~$40-50/week** can cover most meals if you focus on staples

---

## One Honest Tip
**Don't aim for perfect** - eating reasonably healthy 70% of the time is a real win given your schedule.
