# Different Views on Discipleship

Discipleship is understood quite differently across theological traditions, denominations, and thinkers. Here's an overview of major perspectives:

---

## Traditional/Classical Views

- **Imitation model** – Following Christ means literally patterning one's life after Jesus (Thomas à Kempis, *Imitation of Christ*)
- **Catechetical model** – Discipleship as formal instruction in doctrine and practice
- **Monastic model** – Intensive community-based formation under spiritual direction

---

## Protestant Perspectives

- **Reformation emphasis** – Discipleship grounded in Scripture alone; personal Bible study central
- **Pietist tradition** – Heart transformation, small groups, experiential faith
- **Reformed view** – Discipleship as covenant community life and sanctification
- **Evangelical model** – Personal conversion followed by intentional mentoring relationships

---

## Catholic/Orthodox Views

- **Sacramental formation** – Discipleship mediated through liturgy and sacraments
- **Spiritual direction** – One-on-one guidance by a trained director
- **Community of practice** – Formation happens within the Body of the Church

---

## Contemporary Debates

| Question | Competing Views |
|----------|----------------|
| Is discipleship individual or communal? | Both emphases exist |
| Role of doctrine vs. practice | Varies widely |
| Intentional programs vs. organic relationships | Ongoing tension |
| Cultural engagement vs. separation | Significant disagreement |

---

## Notable Thinkers

- **Dietrich Bonhoeffer** – *Cost of Discipleship*; costly grace vs. cheap grace
- **Dallas Willard** – Spiritual disciplines; transformation of character
- **N.T. Wright** – Kingdom-focused discipleship
- **Henri Nouwen** – Contemplative, servant-oriented formation
