What is the difference between analysis and design? Can one begin to design without analysis? Why? Be concise.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Analysis and design are related but distinct phases of problem-solving and system development.

- Analysis focuses on understanding the problem, requirements, constraints, and user needs. It answers: "What needs to be done?"
- Design focuses on creating a solution that meets those requirements. It answers: "How will it be done?"

Can design begin without analysis?
- In a limited or preliminary sense, yes. Designers may sketch ideas or explore concepts early to help clarify requirements.
- However, effective design generally requires analysis first. Without understanding the problem and requirements, the design may fail to meet user needs, violate constraints, or require costly rework.

In short, analysis defines the problem; design defines the solution. Some initial design exploration can occur before analysis is complete, but sound design depends on adequate analysis.
