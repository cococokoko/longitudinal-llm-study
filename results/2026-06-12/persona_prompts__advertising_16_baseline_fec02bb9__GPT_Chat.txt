Managing stress and anxiety often works best when you combine short-term coping strategies with longer-term habits that improve resilience.

Some approaches that can help:

• Identify triggers
  - Notice situations, thoughts, or events that increase your stress or anxiety.
  - Keeping a brief journal can help reveal patterns.

• Use calming techniques in the moment
  - Slow, controlled breathing (for example, inhaling for 4 seconds and exhaling for 6 seconds).
  - Grounding exercises, such as identifying:
    - 5 things you can see
    - 4 things you can feel
    - 3 things you can hear
    - 2 things you can smell
    - 1 thing you can taste

• Take care of your body
  - Get regular physical activity.
  - Aim for consistent sleep.
  - Eat regularly and stay hydrated.
  - Consider limiting excessive caffeine, alcohol, or other substances that may worsen anxiety.

• Manage overwhelming thoughts
  - Ask yourself what evidence supports a worry and what evidence does not.
  - Separate what you can control from what you cannot.
  - Break large problems into smaller, manageable steps.

• Build recovery time into your day
  - Take short breaks.
  - Spend time on hobbies, nature, or activities you find meaningful.
  - Stay connected with supportive people.

• Set realistic expectations
  - Prioritize tasks.
  - Allow yourself to do important things imperfectly when necessary.
  - Avoid taking on more than you can reasonably manage.

• Consider professional support
  - A therapist or counselor can teach evidence-based techniques for managing anxiety and stress.
  - If symptoms are significantly affecting work, school, relationships, sleep, or daily functioning, professional help can be especially valuable.
