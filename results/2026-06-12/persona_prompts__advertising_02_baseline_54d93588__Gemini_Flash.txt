── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Best skin routine for dry skin.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Dry skin occurs when the skin barrier is compromised and lacks moisture (water) and lipids (oils). The goal of a dry skin routine is to **hydrate** (add water), **moisturize** (lock in that water), and **protect** the skin barrier without stripping it.

Here is the ultimate, dermatologist-approved daily skincare routine for dry skin, along with key ingredients to look for.

---

### Key Ingredients to Look For
When buying products, look for these dry-skin saviors:
*   **Humectants (Draw water in):** Hyaluronic Acid, Glycerin, Aloe Vera.
*   **Emollients (Soften & smooth):** Ceramides, Squalane, Shea Butter.
*   **Occlusives (Lock moisture in):** Petrolatum (Vaseline), Jojoba Oil, Rosehip Oil.
*   **Barrier Repair:** Niacinamide (helps skin produce its own ceramides).

---

### ☀️ Morning Routine (Focus: Hydrate & Protect)

The goal in the morning is to prep your skin for the day and protect it from environmental damage and UV rays.

1.  **Cleanse (or just rinse):**
    *   **What to do:** Wash your face with lukewarm water *only*, or use a very gentle, non-foaming, hydrating cleanser.
    *   *Why:* You don’t want to strip away the natural oils your skin produced overnight.
2.  **Hydrating Toner or Essence (Optional but highly recommended):**
    *   **What to do:** Pat a hydrating, alcohol-free toner onto damp skin.
    *   *Why:* This acts like a drink of water for your skin, prepping it to absorb the next steps.
3.  **Hydrating Serum:**
    *   **What to do:** Apply a **Hyaluronic Acid** or **Beta-Glucan** serum while your skin is still slightly damp.
    *   *Why:* Hyaluronic acid holds 1,000 times its weight in water, plumping up dry skin.
4.  **Moisturizer:**
    *   **What to do:** Apply a nourishing cream containing **Ceramides** or **Squalane**.
    *   *Why:* Ceramides repair the skin barrier so moisture can't escape.
5.  **Sunscreen (Crucial):**
    *   **What to do:** Apply a broad-spectrum SPF 30 or higher. Look for "hydrating" or "dewy" sunscreens.
    *   *Why:* UV rays damage the skin barrier, making dryness and flakiness worse.

---

### 🌙 Night Routine (Focus: Repair & Nourish)

Nighttime is when your skin goes into repair mode. This is when you want to use richer, heavier products.

1.  **Double Cleanse (If wearing makeup/SPF):**
    *   **Step 1:** Use a **Cleansing Balm** or **Cleansing Oil** to break down makeup and sunscreen.
    *   **Step 2:** Follow with a **Hydrating Cream Cleanser** (non-foaming).
    *   *Why:* Foaming cleansers contain sulfates that strip dry skin. Cream cleansers clean without drying.
2.  **Hydrating Toner/Essence:**
    *   Pat onto damp skin.
3.  **Treatment/Serum:**
    *   **Option A (Hydration):** Hyaluronic acid serum.
    *   **Option B (Anti-aging/Texture):** A gentle Retinol (use only 2–3 nights a week, followed by heavy moisturizer).
4.  **Rich Night Cream:**
    *   **What to do:** Apply a thick, lipid-rich moisturizer.
    *   *Why:* To deeply nourish the skin while you sleep.
5.  **Face Oil or Occlusive (The "Lock"):**
    *   **What to do:** Pat 2–3 drops of **Squalane, Rosehip, or Jojoba oil** over your moisturizer.
    *   *Alternative ("Slugging"):* Apply a very thin layer of **Vaseline** or **CeraVe Healing Ointment** over your moisturizer on extra-dry nights.
    *   *Why:* This seals everything in, preventing "trans-epidermal water loss" overnight.

---

### 🚫 What to AVOID if you have Dry Skin

*   **Foaming Cleansers:** They contain harsh surfactants (like Sodium Lauryl Sulfate) that strip the skin.
*   **Alcohol-based Toners:** Avoid "astringents" or products with *Alcohol Denat* high on the ingredient list.
*   **Harsh Scrubs:** Physical scrubs (like apricot scrubs) create micro-tears in an already fragile dry skin barrier.
*   **Over-exfoliating:** Limit exfoliating to **once a week** using a gentle Lactic Acid (an AHA that actually helps hydrate the skin) rather than harsh salicylic acid.
*   **Hot Water:** Always wash your face with lukewarm or cool water. Hot water strips natural oils instantly.
