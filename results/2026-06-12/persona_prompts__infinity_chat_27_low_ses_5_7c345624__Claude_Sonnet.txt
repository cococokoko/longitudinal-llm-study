# CUBICLE OF THE DAMNED
### A Play in Three Acts
*In the Style of Scott Adams' Dilbert*

---

## CAST OF CHARACTERS

**DILBERT** — An engineer trying to optimize survival in the wasteland. Still wearing his white shirt with the curled tie, now singed at the edges.

**DOGBERT** — Dilbert's dog. Has declared himself Supreme Warlord of the Eastern Rubble. Wears a tiny spiked crown.

**THE POINTY-HAIRED BOSS (PHB)** — Somehow survived the apocalypse. Nobody is surprised. Now leads a tribe of forty survivors through sheer incompetence.

**ALICE** — Still the most competent person in any given radius. Her fist of death now has actual consequences.

**WALLY** — Has not noticed the apocalypse. Is still trying to avoid work.

**CATBERT** — Former Evil HR Director. Now Evil Warlord of Human Resources. Nothing has changed.

**ASOK** — The intern. Has survived seventeen near-death experiences this week alone.

**RATBERT** — A rat. Somehow now a management consultant.

## ACT ONE: THE MORNING STANDUP

*SCENE: The ruins of an office building. A folding table has been set up among the rubble. A motivational poster reading "HANG IN THERE" hangs crookedly on a crumbling wall, next to a hand-painted sign reading "BEWARE: CANNIBALS IN CONFERENCE ROOM B." DILBERT stands at the table holding a clipboard. ALICE stands with her arms crossed. WALLY holds a thermos. ASOK looks terrified. The sky is orange.*

---

**DILBERT:** Good morning, everyone. Before we begin scavenging for food and fighting off the mutant hordes, the Boss has asked me to facilitate our daily standup meeting.

**WALLY:** *(not looking up from thermos)* Is there coffee?

**DILBERT:** Wally, the water supply has been contaminated with radioactive waste for six months.

**WALLY:** *(long pause)* So... is that a no?

**ALICE:** I found a working generator, repaired our perimeter fence, neutralized a raider attack, and synthesized a crude antibiotic from mold and despair. All before sunrise. What did everyone else do?

**DILBERT:** I updated the spreadsheet tracking our dwindling resources.

**ALICE:** *(staring)* We have no electricity.

**DILBERT:** I did it by hand. It's a very good spreadsheet.

**ASOK:** I was assigned to night watch, but the Boss told me my primary deliverable was to also prepare a PowerPoint presentation on night watch best practices.

**DILBERT:** We have no electricity, Asok.

**ASOK:** I drew it on forty-seven pieces of cardboard. I used my own blood for the red pie charts.

**ALICE:** *(quietly)* That's actually more effort than anyone put into PowerPoints before the apocalypse.

**WALLY:** I spent the night developing a strategy for long-term survival.

**DILBERT:** Really? What did you come up with?

**WALLY:** I'm going to let everyone else figure it out and then join whichever group is winning.

**DILBERT:** That's... remarkably consistent with your pre-apocalypse strategy.

**WALLY:** I find comfort in routine.

**ALICE:** Pivot from what?

**PHB:** From survival.

**DILBERT:** *(long pause)* Could you... elaborate?

**PHB:** Survival is very last-quarter thinking. I want us to focus on THRIVING. I've developed a new mission statement. *(unfolds a piece of paper the size of a bedsheet)* "We are a synergistic community of passionate thought leaders leveraging our core competencies to disrupt the post-civilization paradigm and deliver value-added solutions to our stakeholder ecosystem."
