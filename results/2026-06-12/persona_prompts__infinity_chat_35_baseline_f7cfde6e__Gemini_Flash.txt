── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Give me a 3-day plan for Osaka with a flight leaving on the 3rd day at 7pm.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here is a highly optimized, 3-day itinerary for Osaka. Since your flight leaves at 7:00 PM on Day 3, you will need to head to the airport around 4:00 PM (for international flights) or 5:00 PM (for domestic). 

This itinerary is structured to maximize your time, minimize unnecessary travel, and ensure you are near a major transit hub on your final afternoon for an easy departure.

---

### **Day 1: Historic Osaka & Neon Nights (Central & South)**
*Focus: Culture, street food, and the iconic Osaka nightlife.*

*   **Morning: Osaka Castle (9:00 AM – 12:00 PM)**
    *   Start your trip at **Osaka Castle**. Walk through the massive stone walls and the scenic Nishinomaru Garden. 
    *   *Tip:* If the line to go inside the castle museum is too long, skip it. The view from the outside is the real highlight, and you have a busy day ahead.
*   **Lunch: Kuromon Ichiba Market (12:30 PM – 2:00 PM)**
    *   Take the subway to Nipponbashi Station. Walk through this bustling covered market known as "Osaka's Kitchen."
    *   *What to eat:* Freshly grilled wagyu beef skewers, giant scallops, uni (sea urchin), and takoyaki (octopus balls).
*   **Afternoon: Shinsaibashi & America-mura (2:00 PM – 5:00 PM)**
    *   Walk off lunch by exploring **Shinsaibashi-suji**, a massive shopping arcade.
    *   Head west into **America-mura (Amemura)**, Osaka’s hub for youth culture, vintage clothing shops, and quirky street art.
*   **Evening: Dotonbori & Namba (5:30 PM – Late)**
    *   As the sun sets, head to **Dotonbori**. This is the iconic Osaka you see in photos, lit up by massive neon signs.
    *   Take a photo with the **Glico Running Man** on the Ebisubashi Bridge.
    *   *Dinner:* Grab a table at *Chibo* or *Ajinoya* for **Okonomiyaki** (savory cabbage pancakes), Osaka's signature dish.

---

### **Day 2: Modern Heights & Retro Vibes (North & South-Central)**
*Focus: Futuristic views, retro Osaka, and deep-fried skewers.*

*   **Morning: Umeda Sky Building (9:30 AM – 11:30 AM)**
    *   Head to the Umeda district (North Osaka). Visit the **Umeda Sky Building** and ride the futuristic escalator to the **Floating Garden Observatory** for a 360-degree open-air view of the city.
*   **Lunch: Osaka Station City / Lucua Osaka (11:45 AM – 1:15 PM)**
    *   The area around Osaka/Umeda station is a foodie paradise. Head to the basement floors of the **LUCUA** department store (B2F Lucua Food Hall) for incredible ramen, sushi, or bento boxes.
*   **Afternoon: Shinsekai & Tsutenkaku Tower (2:00 PM – 5:00 PM)**
    *   Take the Midosuji Subway Line straight down to Dobutsuen-mae Station. 
    *   Step back in time to **Shinsekai**, a neighborhood developed in 1912 to look like Paris and New York. It has a gritty, nostalgic, retro-futuristic charm.
    *   Snap photos of the **Tsutenkaku Tower** and the giant blowfish lanterns.
*   **Dinner: Kushikatsu in Shinsekai (5:00 PM – 7:00 PM)**
    *   Shinsekai is the birthplace of **Kushikatsu** (deep-fried meat and vegetables on skewers). 
    *   Sit at the counter at *Yaekatsu* or *Kushikatsu Daruma*. (Remember the golden rule: No double-dipping in the communal sauce!).
*   **Nightcap: Hozenji Yokocho (7:30 PM onwards)**
    *   Head back toward Namba and slip into **Hozenji Yokocho**, a quiet, stone-paved alleyway that feels like Kyoto. Visit the moss-covered Hozenji Temple and grab a quiet drink at a tiny bar.

---

### **Day 3: The Bay Area & Easy Airport Departure**
*Focus: Stunning bay views, giant aquarium, and a seamless transit to the airport.*

* **Logistics Tip for Day 3:** 
    * Check out of your hotel in the morning. 
    * **If flying from Kansai International Airport (KIX):** Take your bags and store them in a locker at **Namba Station**. The *Nankai Rap:t Express* train goes directly from Namba to KIX in just 34 minutes.
    * **If flying from Itami Airport (ITM - Domestic):** Store your bags at **Umeda/Osaka Station**, where you can catch the direct airport limousine bus (30 mins).

*   **Morning: Osaka Aquarium Kaiyukan (9:30 AM – 12:30 PM)**
    *   Take the Chuo Subway Line to Osakako Station. 
    *   Visit **Kaiyukan**, one of the world’s largest and most spectacular aquariums. The centerpiece is a massive 9-meter-deep tank housing a whale shark. 
    *   *Note:* Book your tickets online in advance to skip the morning entry line.
*   **Lunch: Tempozan Marketplace (12:30 PM – 2:00 PM)**
    *   Right next to the aquarium is the Tempozan Marketplace. 
    *   Go to the **Naniwa Kuishinbo Yokocho**, a food theme park inside the mall designed to look like 1960s Osaka. It’s a great place to grab a final lunch of takoyaki, omurice (omelet rice), or udon.
*   **Early Afternoon: Tempozan Ferris Wheel (2:00 PM – 3:00 PM)**
    *   Before you leave, ride the giant Ferris wheel next to the mall for a beautiful view of Osaka Bay, the harbor, and the city skyline.
*   **Departure Prep (3:00 PM – 4:00 PM)**
    *   Take the subway back to your designated station (Namba or Umeda) to retrieve your luggage.
*   **Head to the Airport (4:00 PM)**
    *   *For KIX (International):* Board the **Nankai Rap:t** train from Namba at ~4:00 PM. You will arrive at the airport by 4:40 PM, leaving you plenty of time for your 7:00 PM flight.
    *   *For ITM (Domestic):* Board the airport bus from Umeda at ~5:00 PM. You will arrive by 5:30 PM, which is plenty of time for a domestic flight.
