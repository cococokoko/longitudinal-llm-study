Give a numerical example to illustrate the concept of partial derivative.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
A partial derivative measures how a function changes with respect to one variable while keeping the other variables fixed.

Example:

Consider the function

f(x, y) = x²y + 3xy

Suppose we want the partial derivative with respect to x.

1. Treat y as a constant.
2. Differentiate with respect to x:

∂f/∂x = 2xy + 3y

Now evaluate this at the point (x, y) = (2, 4):

∂f/∂x(2,4) = 2(2)(4) + 3(4)
            = 16 + 12
            = 28

Interpretation:
- At the point (2,4), if x increases by a very small amount while y remains fixed at 4, the function f increases at a rate of approximately 28 units per unit increase in x.

Similarly, find the partial derivative with respect to y:

∂f/∂y = x² + 3x

At (2,4):

∂f/∂y(2,4) = 2² + 3(2)
            = 4 + 6
            = 10

Interpretation:
- At the point (2,4), if y increases slightly while x remains fixed at 2, the function increases at a rate of approximately 10 units per unit increase in y.

This shows that the function can change at different rates in different directions, which is exactly what partial derivatives measure.
