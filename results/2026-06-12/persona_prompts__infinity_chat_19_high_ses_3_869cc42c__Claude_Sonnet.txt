## Likely Developments Worth Considering

**Communication changes**
- AI mediators may filter, translate, and reframe conversations in real-time
- This could reduce misunderstanding but also potentially sanitize authentic conflict
- People may increasingly interact *through* AI rather than directly

**Relationship dynamics**
- AI companions may genuinely compete with human relationships for emotional investment
- This raises serious questions about whether people will develop tolerance for the friction that makes human relationships meaningful

**Social skill concerns**
- Genuine worry that reduced practice with difficult conversations could atrophy social capabilities
- Historical parallel: literacy changed memory; AI may change interpersonal reasoning

## Where I'd Push Back on Common Predictions

- "AI will bring people together" assumes technology determines outcomes rather than human choices
- Previous communication technologies (social media) produced unexpected fragmentation
- We should be honest that we have limited predictive track record here

## Genuinely Hard Questions

- Will people *want* unmediated human interaction if AI interaction is easier?
- Who controls the AI mediating your relationships controls significant social power
- Does convenience in communication actually deepen or shallow relationships?

**My honest assessment:** The trajectory depends heavily on deliberate choices made in the next 10-15 years, not technological inevitability.
