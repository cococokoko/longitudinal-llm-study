── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Give me a tip for managing a team of coworkers.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
If you are managing a team of people who used to be your peers (or are technically your coworkers), the single most effective tip is to **shift your focus from "managing" them to "clearing the path" for them.**

This is known as **Servant Leadership**, and it is highly effective because it reduces the natural friction or resentment that can happen when a coworker is promoted over others.

Here is how to put this into practice:

### Ask the "Magic Question"
In your one-on-ones or team meetings, stop telling people what to do. Instead, ask this question:
> **"What is blocking you from doing your best work right now, and how can I help remove that block?"**

### Why this works:
* **It builds trust:** It shows your team that you are there to support them, not just police them.
* **It establishes authority naturally:** You aren't demanding respect; you are earning it by solving their problems (e.g., getting them better tools, dealing with bureaucracy, or clarifying confusing goals).
* **It keeps them accountable:** When you remove their obstacles, you give them the ownership to deliver results. 

**The Golden Rule:** Your job is no longer to be the smartest person in the room or the one with all the answers. Your job is to make sure *they* have everything they need to succeed.
