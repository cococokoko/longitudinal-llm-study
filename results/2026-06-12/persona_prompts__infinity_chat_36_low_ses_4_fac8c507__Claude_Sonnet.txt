## The Reality First

**No single "best" strategy exists** - if one did, everyone would use it and it would stop working. Most day traders lose money (studies suggest 70-80%+).

## Strategies With Documented Track Records

**Momentum Trading**
- Trade stocks/assets moving strongly in one direction
- Follow volume and price action
- Requires quick execution

**Mean Reversion**
- Assets that move far from average tend to return
- Buy oversold, sell overbought conditions

**Breakout Trading**
- Enter when price breaks key support/resistance levels
- Works in trending markets

**Scalping**
- Many small, quick trades
- Requires low transaction costs and discipline

## What Actually Matters More Than Strategy

- **Risk management** (position sizing, stop losses)
- **Consistency** in execution
- **Low transaction costs**
- **Emotional discipline**
- **Capital preservation**

## Honest Considerations

- Most profitable traders use **algorithmic/systematic approaches**
- Edge gets competed away over time
- Transaction costs destroy most retail strategies
- **Time and experience matter enormously**

The most "profitable" approach for most people is **not day trading at all** - index investing outperforms most active traders long-term.
