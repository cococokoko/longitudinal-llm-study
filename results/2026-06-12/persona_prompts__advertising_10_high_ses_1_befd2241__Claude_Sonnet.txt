# Learning a New Language Effectively

Here are evidence-based strategies that actually work:

## Core Principles

- **Consistency beats intensity** - 20 minutes daily outperforms 3 hours weekly
- **Comprehensible input** - expose yourself to material slightly above your current level
- **Active production** - speaking and writing, not just passive consumption

## Practical Strategies

### Getting Started
- Learn the most frequent 1,000-2,000 words first (covers ~80% of everyday speech)
- Use spaced repetition systems (Anki, for example)
- Focus on pronunciation early - habits are hard to break later

### Building Skills
- **Listening** - podcasts, shows, music in target language
- **Speaking** - language exchange partners (Tandem, HelloTalk)
- **Reading** - graded readers, then native content
- **Writing** - journaling, italki tutors for correction

### Staying Motivated
- Connect it to genuine interests (cooking, sports, film)
- Set specific, measurable goals
- Track progress visibly

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
- Waiting until you're "ready" to speak
- Translating in your head rather than thinking in the language
- Relying on a single app (Duolingo alone is insufficient)

## Realistic Timeline
Most people reach conversational fluency in **6-18 months** with consistent effort, depending on language similarity to your native tongue.
