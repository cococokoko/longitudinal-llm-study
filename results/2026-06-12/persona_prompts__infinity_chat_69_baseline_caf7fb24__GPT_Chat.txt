── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
What is the difference between analysis and design? Can one begin to design without analysis? Why? Be concise.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
- Analysis focuses on understanding the problem: identifying requirements, constraints, stakeholders, and goals. It answers “What needs to be done?”
- Design focuses on creating a solution: defining architecture, components, interfaces, and implementation approaches. It answers “How will it be done?”

Can one begin to design without analysis?
- Yes, preliminary or conceptual design can start with limited analysis, especially when requirements are incomplete.
- However, effective design generally depends on sufficient analysis. Without understanding the problem, the design may fail to meet needs, overlook constraints, or require significant rework later.
