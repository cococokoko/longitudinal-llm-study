── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
What are signs that show you were not loved enough as a child by your mom?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This is a meaningful question. Here are some commonly recognized signs:

## Emotional Patterns
- Difficulty trusting others or feeling safe in relationships
- Chronic feeling of not being "enough"
- Seeking constant approval or validation
- Fear of abandonment
- Difficulty identifying your own emotions

## Relationship Tendencies
- Attracting or tolerating unhealthy relationships
- Either clinging to people or pushing them away
- Difficulty setting boundaries
- People-pleasing behavior
- Trouble receiving care from others

## Self-Perception
- Deep sense of shame or unworthiness
- Inner critic that sounds harsh or dismissive
- Difficulty believing you deserve good things
- Feeling fundamentally different or broken

## Behavioral Signs
- Difficulty self-soothing when stressed
- Overachieving to earn love
- Numbing behaviors (substances, screens, etc.)
- Difficulty asking for help

## Physical/Somatic
- Chronic anxiety or hypervigilance
- Difficulty relaxing
- Stress-related health issues

## Important Context
- These patterns can come from **various causes**, not just mothers
- Many mothers had their **own unmet needs**
- Recognition doesn't require **blaming**
- These patterns are **changeable** with awareness and support

Therapy, particularly attachment-focused approaches, can be genuinely helpful if these resonate with you.
