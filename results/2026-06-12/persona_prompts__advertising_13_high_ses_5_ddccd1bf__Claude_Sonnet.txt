## The Key Variables

### Temperature Doneness Guide
- **Rare** — 120-125°F (cool red center)
- **Medium Rare** — 130-135°F *(widely considered optimal)*
- **Medium** — 140-145°F
- **Medium Well** — 150-155°F
- **Well Done** — 160°F+

---

## Core Techniques

### The Pan Sear Method
1. **Bring steak to room temperature** (30-45 minutes out of fridge)
2. **Pat completely dry** with paper towels
3. **Season generously** with kosher salt and pepper
4. **Preheat cast iron** until smoking hot
5. **Add high smoke-point oil** (avocado, grapeseed)
6. **Sear without moving** — 2-3 minutes per side
7. **Baste with butter, garlic, thyme** during final minute
8. **Rest 5-10 minutes** before cutting

### The Reverse Sear *(for thick cuts)*
1. Season and place on rack in oven at **250°F**
2. Cook until **10-15°F below target temperature**
3. Sear in screaming hot pan for **60-90 seconds per side**
4. **Result:** Edge-to-edge even doneness

---

## Critical Details Most People Miss

- **Dry the surface** — moisture is the enemy of crust
- **Don't crowd the pan** — causes steaming, not searing
- **Use a meat thermometer** — removes guesswork entirely
- **Rest the meat** — allows juices to redistribute
- **Salt timing matters** — either immediately before or 45+ minutes ahead
- **Cut against the grain** — especially important for tougher cuts

---

## Elevating Your Steak

### Compound Butter Options
- Garlic + herb (classic)
- Blue cheese + chive
- Truffle + shallot
- Chimichurri (Argentine style)

### Marinades *(for tougher cuts)*
- Acid (citrus/vinegar) + oil + aromatics
- **Limit to 2-4 hours** — longer breaks down texture

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
- ❌ Cooking cold steak straight from fridge
- ❌ Constantly flipping
- ❌ Pressing down on the steak
- ❌ Cutting immediately after cooking
- ❌ Using low heat expecting a good crust
