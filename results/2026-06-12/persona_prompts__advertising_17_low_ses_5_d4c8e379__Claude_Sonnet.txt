Here are some effective ways to make your home more energy efficient:

## Quick & Low-Cost Steps
- Switch to LED light bulbs
- Use smart power strips to eliminate phantom energy drain
- Adjust thermostat settings (lower in winter, higher in summer)
- Seal gaps around doors and windows with weatherstripping or caulk

## Appliances & Equipment
- Replace old appliances with Energy Star certified models
- Use a programmable or smart thermostat
- Run dishwashers and washing machines with full loads
- Wash clothes in cold water

## Insulation & Air Sealing
- Add insulation to attic, walls, and basement
- Seal air leaks around outlets, pipes, and ducts
- Insulate your water heater and hot water pipes

## Bigger Investments
- Upgrade to energy-efficient windows
- Install solar panels
- Replace old HVAC systems
- Consider a heat pump water heater

## Behavioral Changes
- Unplug devices when not in use
- Use natural light when possible
- Lower water heater temperature to 120°F
- Use ceiling fans to reduce heating/cooling needs

## Getting Started
- **Get a home energy audit** - many utility companies offer these free or at low cost
- Check for **local rebates and tax credits** for energy improvements
