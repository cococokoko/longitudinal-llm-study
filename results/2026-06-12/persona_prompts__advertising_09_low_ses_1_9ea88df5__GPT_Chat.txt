Building a personal brand on social media is much easier if you keep it simple and sustainable, especially with a full-time job and an unpredictable schedule.

A practical approach:

- Pick one main topic:
  - Your work experiences and lessons from fast food/customer service
  - Career growth and personal development
  - Fitness, gaming, cooking, or another genuine interest
  - A combination, such as "life while working full-time"

- Choose one primary platform:
  - TikTok: short videos, fastest organic reach
  - Instagram: reels plus personal storytelling
  - LinkedIn: professional growth and career content
  - YouTube Shorts: good for long-term discoverability

- Create content pillars (3–4 recurring themes):
  - Daily lessons learned
  - Behind-the-scenes moments
  - Skills you're developing
  - Personal goals and progress updates

- Use a low-maintenance content system:
  - Keep notes on your phone throughout the week
  - Record multiple short videos when you have free time
  - Schedule posts in batches if possible
  - Aim for consistency rather than daily posting

- Focus on stories, not perfection:
  - What happened?
  - What did you learn?
  - How can others apply it?

Examples:
- "3 customer-service skills that help in any career."
- "What working an unpredictable schedule taught me about time management."
- "How I balance personal goals with a full-time job."

- Engage with others:
  - Reply to comments
  - Comment thoughtfully on creators in your niche
  - Build relationships instead of only posting

For someone with a demanding schedule, posting 3–4 times per week consistently is usually more effective than trying to post every day and burning out.
