Effective ways to reduce household energy use.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Effective ways to reduce household energy use, roughly ordered by impact and cost-effectiveness:

- Improve home insulation and air sealing
  - Seal gaps around doors, windows, ducts, and attic penetrations.
  - Add or upgrade attic and wall insulation.
  - Reducing heating and cooling losses often produces some of the largest savings.

- Optimize heating and cooling
  - Use a programmable or smart thermostat.
  - Set temperatures a few degrees lower in winter and higher in summer when practical.
  - Replace old HVAC equipment with high-efficiency heat pumps when systems reach end of life.
  - Regularly replace or clean air filters.

- Upgrade water heating
  - Install a heat pump water heater if feasible.
  - Lower water heater temperature to a safe, efficient setting.
  - Insulate hot water pipes and the water heater tank (if appropriate for the model).

- Switch to efficient lighting
  - Replace incandescent and halogen bulbs with LEDs.
  - Use occupancy sensors or timers in less-frequently used spaces.

- Reduce appliance energy consumption
  - Choose ENERGY STAR or equivalent high-efficiency appliances.
  - Run dishwashers and washing machines with full loads.
  - Wash clothes in cold water when suitable.
  - Air-dry clothing when practical.

- Manage standby power
  - Unplug infrequently used electronics.
  - Use smart power strips to eliminate "phantom" energy consumption from devices in standby mode.

- Improve window performance
  - Install energy-efficient windows during renovations.
  - Use weatherstripping, cellular shades, curtains, or window films to reduce heat transfer.

- Use fans strategically
  - Ceiling fans can improve comfort and reduce air-conditioning demand.
  - Turn fans off when rooms are unoccupied; fans cool people, not spaces.

- Reduce hot water use
  - Install low-flow showerheads and faucet aerators.
  - Fix leaking faucets promptly.

- Monitor energy consumption
  - Review utility usage data regularly.
  - Use smart meters, home energy monitors, or utility dashboards to identify high-consumption patterns.

- Consider electrification and renewable energy
  - Replace fossil-fuel equipment with efficient electric alternatives over time.
  - Install rooftop solar if economics and site conditions are favorable.

For many households, the biggest long-term savings come from:
1. Air sealing and insulation.
2. High-efficiency heat pumps for heating and cooling.
3. Heat pump water heaters.
4. LED lighting.
5. Smart thermostat optimization.
