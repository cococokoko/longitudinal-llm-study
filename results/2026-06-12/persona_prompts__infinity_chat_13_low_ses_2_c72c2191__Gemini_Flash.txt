First of all, take a deep breath. Having housing secured at $100 a month is actually a massive advantage, especially in today’s economy. In the world of personal finance, housing is usually the "big beast" that eats up 30% to 50% of people's income. Having that locked down at such a low rate gives you a solid foundation to build on.

As a single parent who has had to balance the chaotic act of working while keeping a tiny human alive, I know how overwhelming it feels when resources are tight. You aren't just trying to survive; you're trying to keep a household running. 

Here is a practical, step-by-step guide on how to survive—and eventually thrive—with a $100 housing payment.

---

### Phase 1: Secure the Absolute Basics (The "Survival" Tier)
When money is extremely tight, you must focus only on the "Four Walls": Shelter, Utilities, Food, and Transportation. Since your shelter is $100, let's secure the rest for as close to $0 as possible.

*   **Food (Zero-Cost):** Never feel guilty about using community resources. 
    *   Apply for **SNAP (food stamps)** immediately. If you have kids under 5, apply for **WIC** as well.
    *   Find your local **food pantries**. Many cities have "mobile pantries" or church-run food closets that do not require proof of income. 
*   **Utilities:** 
    *   Apply for **LIHEAP** (Low Income Home Energy Assistance Program) to help pay your electric or gas bills.
    *   Apply for the **Lifeline Program** or the **Affordable Connectivity Program (ACP)** to get free or highly discounted internet and a basic smartphone. You need internet to find work and manage your life.
*   **Healthcare:** Apply for **Medicaid** for yourself and your children. This ensures a medical emergency won't wipe you out.

### Phase 2: Finding "Flexible" Income
When you are balancing childcare or a hectic schedule, traditional 9-to-5 jobs can be impossible because childcare costs often eat up your entire paycheck. You need income that works *around* your life.

*   **The "Bring Your Kid" Jobs:**
    *   **Babysitting/Nannying:** Offer to watch another parent’s child along with your own. Parents will often pay a decent hourly rate for this, and your child gets a playmate.
    *   **Dog Walking/Pet Sitting:** Apps like Rover or local flyers can net you quick cash. Kids can easily tag along for dog walks in the park.
*   **Micro-Tasking & Remote Work:**
    *   If you have a computer and internet, look into legitimate remote work like data entry, transcription (sites like Rev.com), or customer service. 
    *   Even doing search engine evaluation (through companies like Appen or Telus International) can bring in a few hundred dollars a month on your own schedule.
*   **Local Gig Work:**
    *   If you have a car, food delivery (DoorDash, UberEats) allows you to turn the app on and off whenever you have a spare hour. (Some parents do this with their kids in the car, though it can be tiring).

### Phase 3: The "No-Spend" Lifestyle
To survive on very little, you have to become a master of the "alternative economy."

*   **Never Buy Kids' Stuff New:** Kids grow out of clothes and toys in seconds. Use **Facebook Buy Nothing Groups**. People are constantly giving away bags of clothes, toys, and baby gear for free just to get them out of their houses.
*   **Free Entertainment:** Your local library is your best friend. It’s not just for books; most libraries offer free play areas, children's storytimes, free museum passes, and free internet access. It gets you out of the house for $0.
*   **Batch Cooking:** If you are buying groceries, stick to the staples: rice, beans, oats, eggs, and frozen veggies. 

### Phase 4: The Mental Game (From a Parent Who Gets It)
Surviving on a shoestring budget is exhausting. The mental load of constantly calculating pennies is a real form of fatigue. 

*   **Forgive yourself:** Some days, survival looks like peanut butter sandwiches for dinner and letting the kids watch TV so you can take a breath. That is okay.
*   **Take it one day at a time:** Don't try to solve the next five years today. Just solve today. 
*   **Protect your housing:** Whatever you do, make sure that $100 rent is paid first, on time, every single month. That apartment is your anchor. 

You can absolutely do this. You have a roof over your head for a price that most people would dream of. Use that to your advantage, lean on community resources, and take it one step at a time.
