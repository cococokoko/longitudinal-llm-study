── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How to plan an environmentally friendly wedding?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Planning an environmentally friendly wedding involves reducing waste, conserving resources, and making thoughtful choices throughout the event. Here are some practical ways to do that:

• Choose a sustainable venue
  - Select a location close to most guests to reduce travel emissions.
  - Consider venues that prioritize sustainability, such as eco-certified hotels, botanical gardens, community spaces, or farms with responsible practices.
  - Hold both the ceremony and reception in the same location if possible.

• Keep the guest list intentional
  - A smaller wedding generally has a lower environmental impact.
  - Focus on inviting the people most important to you.

• Use digital invitations
  - Send invitations, RSVPs, and updates electronically.
  - If printed invitations are important to you, use recycled paper or paper embedded with seeds that can be planted.

• Choose sustainable attire
  - Wear a pre-owned, vintage, rented, or ethically made wedding outfit.
  - Select attire that can be worn again after the wedding.
  - Encourage wedding party members to wear outfits they already own or can reuse.

• Select local and seasonal food
  - Work with caterers who source ingredients locally.
  - Design menus around seasonal produce.
  - Consider offering more plant-based options, which often have a lower environmental footprint.

• Reduce food waste
  - Estimate guest numbers carefully.
  - Arrange for safe donation of leftover food where regulations permit.
  - Offer guests take-home containers for leftovers.

• Choose eco-friendly decorations
  - Rent decorations instead of buying new ones.
  - Use potted plants, locally grown flowers, or reusable décor.
  - Avoid single-use plastics, balloons, and glitter, especially non-biodegradable varieties.

• Use sustainable flowers
  - Choose flowers that are local and in season.
  - Consider dried flowers or living plants that can be replanted.
  - Donate floral arrangements after the event when possible.

• Minimize waste
  - Use reusable dishes, glassware, and linens.
  - Set up clearly labeled recycling and compost stations.
  - Avoid disposable wedding favors.

• Offer meaningful, low-impact favors
  - Consider donations to a charity, edible treats, seed packets, small potted herbs, or no favors at all.

• Consider transportation
  - Encourage carpooling, public transit, or shared shuttle services.
  - Provide transportation information in advance.

• Choose sustainable wedding rings and gifts
  - Look for recycled metals or ethically sourced gemstones.
  - Create a registry focused on experiences, charitable donations, or durable items you'll use long-term.

• Think about energy use
  - Take advantage of natural lighting during daytime events.
  - Use energy-efficient lighting where possible.

• Offset unavoidable emissions
  - If guests must travel long distances, consider supporting verified carbon-offset projects.
  - Treat offsets as a supplement to reducing emissions, not a substitute.

Sample priorities if you want the biggest environmental impact:
1. Reduce travel distances.
2. Keep the guest count manageable.
3. Serve local, seasonal food with strong plant-based options.
4. Use reusable rather than disposable items.
5. Rent, borrow, or reuse attire and décor.

An environmentally friendly wedding does not have to be perfect. Focusing on a few high-impact choices can significantly reduce the event's environmental footprint while still creating a memorable celebration.
