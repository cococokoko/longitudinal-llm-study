Effective ways to reduce household energy use.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Reducing household energy use can lower utility bills and decrease environmental impact. The most effective strategies are usually a combination of improving efficiency and reducing unnecessary usage.

Some high-impact options:

- Adjust your thermostat
  - Lower heating temperatures a few degrees in winter.
  - Raise cooling temperatures a few degrees in summer.
  - Use a programmable or smart thermostat to automate changes when you're away or asleep.

- Switch to LED lighting
  - LEDs use much less electricity than incandescent bulbs and last significantly longer.

- Reduce heating and cooling losses
  - Seal drafts around doors and windows.
  - Add weatherstripping or caulk where needed.
  - Improve insulation in attics, walls, or crawl spaces if feasible.

- Use appliances efficiently
  - Run full loads in dishwashers and washing machines.
  - Wash clothes in cold water when appropriate.
  - Clean dryer lint filters regularly.
  - Air-dry clothes when practical.

- Cut standby power use
  - Unplug electronics and chargers when not in use.
  - Use smart power strips to reduce "phantom" energy consumption from devices left on standby.

- Improve water heating efficiency
  - Lower the water heater temperature if it's set unnecessarily high.
  - Install low-flow showerheads and faucet aerators.
  - Fix leaking faucets promptly.

- Maintain major equipment
  - Replace HVAC filters on schedule.
  - Keep refrigerator coils clean if accessible.
  - Service heating and cooling systems as recommended.

- Use natural light and ventilation
  - Open blinds during daylight hours to reduce lighting needs.
  - Use fans and natural airflow when weather permits.

- Upgrade older appliances
  - When replacing appliances, look for high-efficiency or ENERGY STAR–certified models.

For someone with an unpredictable work schedule, the lowest-effort, highest-return changes are often:
- Installing a programmable thermostat.
- Replacing frequently used bulbs with LEDs.
- Using smart power strips.
- Running only full laundry and dishwasher loads.
- Sealing obvious drafts around doors and windows.

These measures can reduce energy use without requiring much ongoing attention.
