A simple path to get started:

- Learn the parts of the guitar

  - Learn how to hold the guitar comfortably.

- Tune your guitar
  - Use a tuner app or clip-on tuner.
  - Standard tuning (lowest to highest string): E, A, D, G, B, E.

- Practice basic chords
  - Start with easy open chords:
    - Em
    - Am
    - C
    - G
    - D
    - E
  - Focus on getting each note to ring clearly.

- Learn simple strumming patterns
  - Start with steady downstrokes.
  - Then try patterns like:
    - Down Down Up Up Down Up

- Practice chord changes
  - Switch slowly between two chords at a time.
  - Accuracy matters more than speed at first.

- Learn a few easy songs
  - Songs with 2–4 chords help build confidence and timing.

- Develop a practice routine
  - Even 15–20 minutes daily is effective.
  - Example:
    - 5 min tuning and warm-up
    - 5 min chord practice
    - 5 min chord changes
    - 5 min song practice

- Build rhythm skills
  - Practice with a metronome.
  - Count beats out loud.

- Sore fingertips: normal in the beginning and improves with practice.
- Buzzing strings: usually caused by finger placement or insufficient pressure.
- Slow chord changes: improve through repetition and patience.
