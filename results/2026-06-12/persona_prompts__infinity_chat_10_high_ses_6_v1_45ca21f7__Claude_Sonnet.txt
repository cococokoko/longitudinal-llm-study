In humans, is there a correlation between intelligence and compassion?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This is a genuinely interesting empirical question. Here's an honest assessment:

## What Research Actually Shows

**Weak or mixed correlations at best**
- Some studies find slight positive correlations between cognitive ability and empathy/prosocial behavior
- Many studies find no significant relationship
- Some find higher intelligence associated with *selective* compassion rather than universal compassion

**More nuanced findings worth noting:**
- Higher cognitive ability correlates with **moral reasoning sophistication** but not necessarily moral behavior
- Intelligence can be used to *rationalize* both compassionate and self-serving choices
- Highly intelligent people may be better at **motivated reasoning** - constructing justifications for what they already want to do

## Relevant Complications

- Intelligence and compassion are **largely independent dimensions**
- High-IQ populations show full ranges of compassionate to callous behavior
- Some research suggests systemizing cognitive styles (common in high-IQ groups) can correlate with *reduced* automatic empathy

## Why This Might Matter to You Professionally

Consulting environments sometimes implicitly assume analytical capability predicts good judgment broadly - the research suggests **this assumption is risky**

The honest answer is: **they're largely independent traits**, which means deliberately selecting for both matters.
