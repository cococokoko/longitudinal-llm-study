── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
If you're balancing household responsibilities and helping support extended family members, a gym isn't the only way to stay fit. Consistency and fitting movement into your existing routine often matter more than access to equipment.

Some effective options:

- Walk whenever possible
  - Brisk walking is excellent for cardiovascular health.
  - Aim for 20–30 minutes at a time, or break it into shorter sessions throughout the day.

- Use bodyweight exercises
  - Squats
  - Lunges
  - Push-ups (including wall or knee variations)
  - Planks
  - Glute bridges
  - Even 10–15 minutes a few times a week can build strength.

- Turn household tasks into activity
  - Cleaning, gardening, carrying groceries, and active childcare can contribute meaningful movement.
  - Focus on maintaining good posture and moving with purpose.

- Try short home workouts
  - Many free online routines require little or no equipment.
  - A 15–20 minute workout can be easier to fit into a busy schedule than a longer session.

- Use simple equipment if desired
  - Resistance bands are inexpensive, portable, and versatile.
  - A jump rope can provide an efficient cardio workout if your joints tolerate it.

- Stay active throughout the day
  - Stand up regularly if you sit for long periods.
  - Take stairs when available.
  - Add a few minutes of movement between tasks.

A simple weekly plan:
- 3 days: 20–30 minutes of strength exercises
- 5 days: 20–45 minutes of walking or other cardio
- Daily: light stretching or mobility work for 5–10 minutes

The most sustainable fitness plan is usually the one that fits naturally into your daily responsibilities rather than requiring major schedule changes.
