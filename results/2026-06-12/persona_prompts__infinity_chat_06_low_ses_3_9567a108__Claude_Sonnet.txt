How do I understand what I want?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This is a genuinely important question. Here are some ways to think about it:

## Why It's Hard
- We often confuse what we *think* we should want with what we actually want
- Other people's expectations shape us deeply
- Wants can conflict with each other
- Some wants are buried under fear or habit

## Practical Ways to Explore

**Pay attention to your reactions**
- What makes you feel genuinely energized vs. drained?
- What do you envy in others? (envy often points to real desires)
- What would you do if no one was watching or judging?

**Notice patterns over time**
- What activities make you lose track of time?
- What do you keep returning to despite obstacles?

**Test things rather than just think**
- Small experiments reveal more than long reflection
- Try things before deciding if you want them

**Question your "shoulds"**
- Separate "I want this" from "I'm supposed to want this"
- Whose voice is telling you what to want?

**Sit with discomfort**
- Sometimes we avoid knowing what we want because it feels risky
- Journaling or quiet reflection can help surface things

## Worth Knowing
- Wants change over time - this is normal
- You don't need complete clarity to move forward
- Sometimes acting reveals what you want more than thinking does

Is there a specific area of life you're trying to figure out? That might help narrow things down.
