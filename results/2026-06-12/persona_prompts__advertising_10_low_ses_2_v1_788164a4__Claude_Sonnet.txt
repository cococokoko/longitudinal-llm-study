# Learning a New Language as a Single Parent

Here's a practical approach that fits your reality:

## Work With Your Actual Schedule
- **Micro-sessions work** - 10-15 minutes consistently beats rare 2-hour blocks
- Early morning, lunch break, or after bedtime are often the most realistic windows
- **Don't wait for "perfect time"** - it won't come

## Involve Your Child
- Learn *together* - kids pick up languages quickly and it becomes bonding time
- Label household objects in the target language
- Watch children's shows in the new language (they're designed for simple vocabulary)
- Make it a game during meals or car rides

## Low-Effort High-Return Methods
- **Podcasts/audio** while commuting, cooking, or doing chores
- Apps like Duolingo during waiting room time or school pickup lines
- Change your phone language settings for passive exposure

## Realistic Expectations
- **Progress over perfection** - consistency matters more than intensity
- Missing days is normal, not failure
- Even 5 minutes daily adds up significantly over months

## Free/Low-Cost Resources
- YouTube channels in target language
- Library language learning programs
- Language exchange apps (free conversation practice)

## Honest Consideration
What language are you learning and **why**? Knowing your motivation helps identify the most efficient approach for your specific situation.
