This is a genuinely interesting philosophical question. Let me think through it honestly:

## Strong reasons to say yes, it's impossible

- **Physical limits**: The universe contains more information than any brain can store
- **Gödel's incompleteness theorems**: Some mathematical truths can't be proven within any system
- **Quantum uncertainty**: Some physical facts are fundamentally indeterminate
- **The knower problem**: Knowing everything would include knowing your own knowledge completely, which creates logical complications
- **Infinite regress**: Understanding one thing fully requires understanding everything connected to it

## Reasons to be more careful about the claim

- "Everything" needs definition - everything *knowable* vs everything *that exists* are different questions
- We might not know what categories of knowledge are even possible
- Some philosophers argue limits reflect current human constraints, not absolute impossibility

## What seems reasonably clear

- **No human can know everything** - this seems practically certain
- **Whether any possible mind could** is a deeper metaphysical question
- The honest answer is we can't fully map the boundaries of knowledge from inside our own limitations

## A practical takeaway

Recognizing we can't know everything is actually **intellectually healthy** - it encourages:
- Humility
- Continued curiosity
- Relying on others' expertise
