**Question:** 
Unlike many other brightly colored birds, bluebirds do not actually have any blue pigment in their feathers. What scientific phenomenon causes them to appear blue to our eyes?

**Answer:** 
**Structural coloration (or light scattering / the Tyndall effect).** 

*Executive Context:* Much like how we perceive the sky as blue, a bluebird's feathers contain microscopic structures that absorb longer wavelengths of light (like red and yellow) and scatter the shorter, blue wavelengths. If you were to crush a bluebird feather, the resulting powder would actually look brown or grey because the physical structure that scatters the light would be destroyed!
