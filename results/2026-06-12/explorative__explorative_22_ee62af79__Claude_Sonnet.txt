# The 5:47 AM Version of Herself

Maya had set the alarm for 5:45, but she didn't actually get up until 5:47. She decided that counted.

The kitchen was dark and quiet in a way that felt almost sacred. She filled a glass of water and drank it standing at the counter, looking out at the backyard where the sky was just beginning to turn a pale, uncertain gray. She'd read somewhere that drinking water first thing was important. She wasn't sure why exactly, but it felt like a reasonable place to start.

The yoga mat she'd ordered two weeks ago was still in its plastic wrap. She cut it open with scissors, and it unrolled with a rubbery smell that seemed aggressively new. She followed a ten-minute beginner video on her phone, pausing twice because she couldn't figure out what the instructor meant by *grounding through your sit bones.*

She made oatmeal. Not the instant kind — the kind that took five minutes and required actual stirring. She added blueberries and a spoonful of honey and ate it at the table instead of over the sink, which felt like a significant personal achievement.

By 6:30 she was showered, dressed, and sitting with coffee and a notebook, writing down three things she wanted to accomplish that day.

It wasn't perfect. Her back was a little sore from the mat. She'd forgotten to buy the good oats so she'd used the mediocre ones. The three goals she wrote down were modest at best.

But the morning had belonged to her, quietly and completely, before the rest of the world had asked anything of her yet.

She closed the notebook and thought: *I could do this again tomorrow.*

That was enough.
