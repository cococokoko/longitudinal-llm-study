# Mastering Digital Photography

## Core Foundations

### Understanding Exposure
The **exposure triangle** controls how light hits your sensor:

- **Aperture (f-stop)** – Controls depth of field and light intake
- **Shutter Speed** – Freezes or blurs motion
- **ISO** – Sensor sensitivity to light

> Learning to balance these three elements is the single most important skill in photography.

---

## Key Technical Skills

### Aperture
| f-stop | Effect |
|--------|--------|
| f/1.8 - f/2.8 | Shallow depth of field, blurred background |
| f/8 - f/11 | Sharp throughout, ideal for landscapes |
| f/16+ | Maximum depth, some diffraction |

### Shutter Speed
- **Fast (1/500s+)** – Freeze sports, wildlife
- **Slow (1/30s-)** – Light trails, silky water
- **Bulb mode** – Long exposures, star trails

### ISO
- Keep as **low as possible** to minimize noise
- Modern cameras handle ISO 3200+ well
- Shoot RAW to recover detail in post-processing

---

## Composition Principles

- **Rule of thirds** – Place subjects off-center
- **Leading lines** – Draw the eye through the frame
- **Framing** – Use natural frames within scenes
- **Negative space** – Let subjects breathe
- **Golden hour** – Shoot 1 hour after sunrise/before sunset

---

## Essential Gear

### Camera Bodies
- **Entry level** – Canon Rebel, Nikon D3500
- **Mid-range** – Sony A7 III, Fujifilm X-T4
- **Professional** – Canon R5, Nikon Z9, Sony A1

### Must-Have Lenses
1. **50mm f/1.8** – Versatile, affordable, sharp
2. **24-70mm f/2.8** – Professional workhorse
3. **70-200mm f/2.8** – Portraits, sports, wildlife
4. **16-35mm** – Landscapes, architecture

### Accessories
- Sturdy tripod
- Extra batteries
- ND filters
- Camera bag with weather protection

---

## Post-Processing Workflow

### Software Options
- **Adobe Lightroom** – Industry standard for organization/editing
- **Capture One** – Superior color handling
- **Darktable** – Free, powerful alternative

### Basic Editing Order
1. Correct exposure
2. Adjust white balance
3. Recover highlights/shadows
4. Adjust contrast and clarity
5. Color grade
6. Sharpen and reduce noise
7. Export at appropriate resolution

---

## Shooting in Different Conditions

### Low Light
- Use fast lenses (f/1.8 or wider)
- Raise ISO strategically
- Use image stabilization
- Shoot RAW

### Outdoor/Natural Light
- Avoid harsh midday sun
- Use reflectors or fill flash
- Shoot in shade for portraits
- Embrace overcast days for even light

### Action/Sports
- Use continuous autofocus (AI Servo/AF-C)
- Shutter priority mode
- Burst mode shooting
- Pre-focus on anticipated locations

---

## Building Your Skills

### Practice Habits
- **Shoot daily** – Even with your phone
- **Study great photographers** – Analyze what works
- **Join photo walks** – Learn from community
- **Review your failures** – Understand what went wrong
- **Recreate shots you admire** – Build technical understanding

### Recommended Learning Path
1. Master manual mode
2. Learn one genre deeply (portrait, landscape, street)
3. Develop a consistent editing style
4. Build a portfolio of 10-20 strong images
5. Seek honest critique

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- ❌ Shooting only in auto mode
- ❌ Neglecting composition for technical perfection
- ❌ Over-editing images
- ❌ Buying gear instead of developing skills
- ❌ Never printing or sharing your work
