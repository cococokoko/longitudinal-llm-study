── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Give an example of a linear graph in graph theory.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
A common example of a linear graph is a path graph.

For instance, the graph:

V = {A, B, C, D}
E = {(A,B), (B,C), (C,D)}

looks like:

A — B — C — D

Each vertex (except the two ends) is connected to exactly two neighboring vertices, forming a single straight chain. In graph theory, this is called the path graph P4 because it has 4 vertices connected in a line.
