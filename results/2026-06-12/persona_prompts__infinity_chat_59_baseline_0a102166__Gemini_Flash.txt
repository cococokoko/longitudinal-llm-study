── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Give me a tip to be more organized at work. I'm a high school teacher.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
As a high school teacher, your day is a whirlwind of grading, lesson planning, parent emails, and managing 100+ teenagers. The standard advice of "keep a to-do list" doesn't cut it. 

Here is one highly actionable, game-changing tip specifically for high school teachers:

### **Implement the "Batch & Box" Grading System**

Grading is the #1 source of clutter (both physical and mental) for teachers. If you don't control it, it controls you. To fix this, use a physical and digital "Batch & Box" system.

#### **1. The Physical "Box" (The 3-Drawer Tower)**
Buy a cheap plastic 3-drawer organizer for your desk. Label the drawers:
*   **Drawer 1: TO GRADE** (Every paper handed in goes here immediately. Never leave papers in a stack on your desk).
*   **Drawer 2: TO ENTER** (Papers that are graded but need to be put into the online gradebook).
*   **Drawer 3: TO RETURN** (Papers ready to go back to students).

**Why this works:** It clears your desk clutter instantly. You always know exactly where a student's missing paper is, and you never have to ask yourself, *"Did I grade this class's quizzes yet?"*

#### **2. The "Batch" (The 20-Minute Rule)**
Never grade one paper at a time as they come in, and never take grading home over the weekend if you can avoid it. Instead, **batch grade**.
*   Set a timer for 20 minutes during your prep period or right after school.
*   Grade *only* one specific assignment for one specific class during that time. 
*   If you don't finish, put them back in Drawer 1. 

**Why this works:** High school teachers often have 5 or 6 different preps/classes. Switching your brain between grading Algebra 1 and AP Calculus (or 9th-grade English and 12th-grade English) causes "cognitive switching cost," which slows you down. Grading in batches of the same assignment keeps your brain in the zone.

### **Bonus Quick Tip: The "Friday Close-Out"**
Spend the last 15 minutes of your Friday doing two things:
1.  **Clean your whiteboard** and write the agenda for Monday.
2.  **Empty your physical inbox.** 

Walking into a clean, prepped classroom on Monday morning is the best gift you can give your future self.
