Here are some solid resources to get started:

**Books**
- *How Not to Die* by Dr. Michael Greger
- *The China Study* by T. Colin Campbell
- *Forks Over Knives* (also a documentary)
- *Plant-Based on a Budget* by Toni Okamoto

**Websites & Apps**
- NutritionFacts.org - evidence-based nutrition information
- Forks Over Knives website - recipes and meal plans
- Cronometer - tracking nutrients to ensure you're meeting needs
- Oh She Glows - practical recipes

**Practical Starting Points**
- Focus on whole foods: legumes, grains, vegetables, fruits, nuts
- Learn about key nutrients to monitor (B12, iron, omega-3s, vitamin D)
- Start with familiar meals and swap ingredients gradually
- Batch cooking saves significant time

**Communities**
- Reddit communities like r/PlantBasedDiet or r/veganrecipes
- Local meetup groups for support and recipe sharing

**Things Worth Knowing**
- Consulting a registered dietitian can help personalize your approach
- Transition pace matters - gradual changes often stick better
- Processed plant-based foods aren't necessarily healthier
- Budget-friendly options exist (beans and lentils are very affordable)
