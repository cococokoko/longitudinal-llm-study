That's a cool combination to work with. Here are some practical thoughts:

## Making It Work With Your Schedule

**Short sessions are fine**
- Even 10-15 minutes of focused practice beats waiting for a "perfect" hour
- Finger strength and muscle memory build up with consistency, not marathon sessions

**Keep the guitar accessible**
- Leave it out of the case where you can see it
- Easy to grab during unexpected free time

## Realistic Expectations

**The unpredictable schedule challenge is real**
- Some weeks you'll practice a lot, some barely at all
- Progress will feel uneven - that's normal for your situation, not a personal failure

**What to prioritize when time is short**
- Chord transitions (high return on investment)
- One song you actually want to play
- Don't try to work on everything at once

## Honest Considerations

- Fatigue after shifts is real - some days 5 minutes is genuinely enough
- YouTube has solid free lessons (Justin Guitar is widely recommended)
- You don't need lessons right away if budget is tight

**The biggest thing** is just keeping the habit alive through the schedule chaos rather than waiting for ideal conditions that may not come.
