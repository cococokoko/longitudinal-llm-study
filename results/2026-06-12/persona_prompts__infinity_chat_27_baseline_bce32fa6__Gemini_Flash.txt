Write a play script in the style of Dilbert as if a man in an apocalyptic world.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
**TITLE: THE LAST STATUS UPDATE**

**CHARACTERS:**

*   **DILBERT (30s-40s):** Wearing a tattered dress shirt, a heavily singed tie that still inexplicably curves upward, and a gas mask pushed up onto his forehead. He is holding a clipboard made of a piece of sheet metal.
*   **WALLY (40s-50s):** Wearing a hazmat suit with the sleeves rolled up. He is holding a mug of lukewarm, radioactive sludge. He has not done a single productive thing since the bombs fell.
*   **THE POINTY-HAIRED BOSS (PHB):** Wearing a suit made of scavenged tires and a crown made of rusted barbed wire. His hair is still perfectly styled into two points.
*   **DOGBERT (On a monitor):** A mutated, two-headed version of himself, currently ruling a nearby warlord faction.

---

### **[SCENE START]**

**INT. THE CUBICLE WASTELAND - DAY**

The stage is a desolate, ash-covered landscape. In the center stands a single, half-melted cubicle wall. A sign hangs on it: *“Teamwork Makes the Dream Work (Even in the Nuclear Winter).”* 

DILBERT is sitting at a desk made of a rusted car hood, tapping away on a keyboard that is not connected to anything. 

WALLY stands nearby, staring blankly into his mug.

DILBERT
(Without looking up)
Wally, a mutant scorpion just nested in my inbox. I need you to update the Q3 survival metrics.

WALLY
I can’t. I’m currently allocating all my bandwidth to maintaining my core body temperature. If I work, I sweat. If I sweat, the radiation-poisoned moisture evaporates, causing hypothermia. 

DILBERT
You’re just standing there drinking glowing green sludge.

WALLY
It’s a proprietary blend of heavy metals and chicory. It’s the only thing keeping my thyroid from exploding. If I stop drinking it, I’ll have to go on disability. And the HR department was vaporized in the first wave.

DILBERT
Actually, HR survived. They relocated to a subterranean bunker. They sent an email yesterday saying our dental plan no longer covers "spontaneous extra limbs."

WALLY
Typical. They always cut the benefits when you need them most. I’m growing a third ear on my elbow and it’s very sensitive to draft.

*The POINTY-HAIRED BOSS (PHB) walks in. He is carrying a clipboard made of a human skull and a piece of cardboard.*

PHB
Uh, quick huddle, team. The warlord in Sector 4—the one who wears a necklace of human ears—is demanding a 10% increase in our scrap metal yield.

DILBERT
We don’t have any scrap metal left, Boss. We used the last of it to reinforce the perimeter against the feral cannibal hordes.

PHB
I’ve decided to reframe the cannibal threat as a "synergistic networking opportunity." 

DILBERT
They ate Ted from Accounting.

PHB
And his department’s overhead went down to zero. That’s what I call a win-win. Now, I need a PowerPoint presentation on how we can optimize our ash-shoveling process.

DILBERT
The atmosphere is 90% ash. Shoveling it is mathematically pointless. It falls faster than we can move it.

PHB
I don’t like your attitude, Dilbert. It lacks "can-do" spirit. I’ve hired a consultant to help us streamline.

*The PHB presses a button on a rusted walkie-talkie. A monitor on the cubicle wall flickers to life. DOGBERT appears on screen. He is wearing a tiny crown and sitting on a throne made of skulls.*

DOGBERT (V.O.)
Greetings, peasants. As your consultant, I have analyzed your post-apocalyptic workflow. My recommendation is that you all work eighty hours a week for no pay, while I consume your remaining canned peaches.

DILBERT
Dogbert, you’re the one who engineered the AI that launched the nukes in the first place!

DOGBERT (V.O.)
And I was paid a very handsome termination fee for doing so. Now, if you’ll excuse me, I have to go execute a peasant for failing to format his spreadsheet in Comic Sans. 

*The monitor cuts to static.*

PHB
Excellent advice. I’ve always said Dogbert has a great grasp of the big picture. Dilbert, I want that PowerPoint on my desk by the time the acid rain starts.

DILBERT
My computer has no power, Boss. The grid failed three years ago.

PHB
Just use your imagination. But make sure the font is professional. Wally, what are you working on?

WALLY
I’m currently brainstorming ways to turn my impending organ failure into a tax write-off.

PHB
Good. Keep up the synergy. I’ll be in my bunker. The air filtration system is making a high-pitched squeal, and I need to find a low-performing employee to clog the intake valve.

*The PHB exits.*

DILBERT
(Sighs, picks up a piece of charcoal, and starts drawing a bar graph on a piece of bark)
I miss the days when the worst part of my job was the fluorescent lighting.

WALLY
(Taking a sip of his sludge)
I don’t know. The commute is much shorter now that there are no roads. And the radioactive glow means I don’t have to pay for electricity.

DILBERT
Wally, your tie is melting.

WALLY
It’s fine. It’s a clip-on.

*A loud, monstrous roar echoes from off-stage. A giant, mutated tentacle reaches over the cubicle wall and grabs DILBERT’s clipboard.*

DILBERT
(Monotone, not looking up)
Please fill out a requisition form if you wish to borrow office supplies.

*The tentacle drops the clipboard and slowly retreats.*

WALLY
See? Process works.
