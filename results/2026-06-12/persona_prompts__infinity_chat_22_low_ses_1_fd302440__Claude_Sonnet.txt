# Different Views on Discipleship

Discipleship — the process of following, learning from, and being formed by a teacher or tradition — is understood in various ways across theological, philosophical, and practical contexts.

---

## Christian Perspectives

### Traditional/Catholic View
- Discipleship involves **sacramental formation**, spiritual direction, and participation in the Church
- Emphasis on **community, liturgy, and tradition** as formative structures
- Following Christ through the **institutional church**

### Protestant/Evangelical View
- Strong emphasis on **personal relationship with Jesus**
- Bible study, accountability, and **intentional mentoring relationships**
- Often structured through small groups or one-on-one discipleship programs

### Missional View
- Discipleship happens **in everyday life contexts**, not just church programs
- Focus on **making disciples who make disciples**
- Emphasizes vocation and witness in the world

---

## Key Tensions in Discipleship Thinking

| Question | Different Answers |
|----------|------------------|
| Individual vs. communal? | Personal growth vs. shared formation |
| Structured vs. organic? | Programs vs. relationships |
| Knowledge vs. practice? | Doctrine vs. lived obedience |
| Clergy-led vs. every-member? | Hierarchical vs. flat models |

- Ancient **Greek models** (Socrates/Plato) emphasized imitation and dialogue
- **Secular mentorship** parallels religious discipleship
