# Data Security Best Practices for Small Businesses

Great question. Small businesses are actually frequent targets precisely because security is often weaker. Here are the most impactful practices:

## Foundation Essentials
- **Strong password policies** - minimum 12 characters, unique passwords per account
- **Multi-factor authentication (MFA)** on all critical systems
- **Regular software updates** and patch management
- **Encrypted storage** for sensitive data

## Access Control
- Apply **least privilege principles** - employees access only what they need
- Remove access immediately when employees leave
- Separate admin accounts from daily-use accounts

## Data Management
- **Regular backups** following the 3-2-1 rule (3 copies, 2 media types, 1 offsite)
- Test backup restoration periodically
- Classify data by sensitivity level
- Have a clear data retention and deletion policy

## Network Security
- Separate guest WiFi from business networks
- Use a business-grade firewall
- Consider VPN for remote workers

## Human Element (Often Overlooked)
- **Employee training** on phishing recognition
- Clear incident reporting procedures
- Written security policies

## Practical Starting Points
- Conduct a simple risk assessment
- Consider cyber liability insurance
- Use reputable cloud providers rather than managing everything yourself

## Worth Noting
Security doesn't require huge budgets - consistency and basics done well matter more than expensive tools.
