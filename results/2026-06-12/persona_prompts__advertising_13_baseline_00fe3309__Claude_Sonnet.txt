# Mastering the Art of Cooking Steak

## Understanding the Basics First

### Key Variables
- **Cut selection** - affects cooking method
- **Thickness** - determines timing
- **Temperature management** - the most critical skill
- **Resting** - often overlooked but essential

---

## Choosing Your Cut

| Cut | Best For | Flavor | Tenderness |
|-----|----------|--------|------------|
| Ribeye | Pan/grill | Rich, fatty | High |
| NY Strip | Pan/grill | Balanced | Medium-high |
| Filet Mignon | Pan/oven | Mild | Very high |
| Sirloin | Grill | Beefy | Medium |
| Flank/Skirt | Quick grill | Intense | Lower |
| T-Bone/Porterhouse | Grill | Complex | High |

## Doneness Temperatures

| Level | Internal Temp | Description |
|-------|--------------|-------------|
| Rare | 120-125°F | Cool red center |
| Medium Rare | 130-135°F | Warm red center *(most recommended)* |
| Medium | 140-145°F | Pink center |
| Medium Well | 150-155°F | Slightly pink |
| Well Done | 160°F+ | No pink |

## Core Techniques

### 1. The Classic Pan Sear
**Best for:** Ribeye, NY Strip, Filet (1-1.5" thick)

**Steps:**
1. Bring steak to **room temperature** (30-45 min out of fridge)
2. Pat **completely dry** with paper towels
3. Season generously with **kosher salt and pepper**
4. Heat cast iron skillet until **smoking hot**
5. Add high smoke-point oil (avocado, grapeseed)
6. Sear **2-3 minutes per side** without moving
7. Add **butter, garlic, thyme** - baste continuously
8. Check internal temperature
9. **Rest 5-10 minutes** before cutting

### 2. Reverse Sear
**Best for:** Thick cuts (1.5"+), more control

**Steps:**
1. Season steak, place on rack in oven
2. Cook at **250°F** until ~10-15°F below target temp
3. Remove, let rest briefly
4. Sear in screaming hot pan **60-90 seconds per side**
5. Results in **edge-to-edge** even doneness

### 3. Grilling
**Best for:** Most cuts, especially bone-in

**Tips:**
- Create **two heat zones** (direct and indirect)
- Start on high heat for sear marks
- Move to indirect heat to finish
- Keep lid closed when not flipping

## Critical Techniques Explained

### Getting the Perfect Crust
- Moisture is the **enemy of crust** - always pat dry
- Salt **45+ minutes ahead** or right before cooking
- Pan must be **very hot** before steak goes in
- Don't crowd the pan
- **Don't press down** on the steak

### Basting
- Add butter in the **last 1-2 minutes**
- Tilt pan and spoon butter over steak repeatedly
- Add aromatics: garlic cloves, rosemary, thyme

### Resting
- Allows juices to **redistribute**
- Rest **half the cooking time** as a general rule
- Tent loosely with foil
- **Don't skip this step**

---

## Seasoning Approaches

### Simple (Often Best)
- Kosher salt + coarse black pepper
- Apply generously - more than feels comfortable

### Enhanced
- Garlic powder, smoked paprika, onion powder
- Fresh herb rubs
- Dry brine overnight in refrigerator

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

❌ **Cooking cold steak** - uneven cooking  
❌ **Not drying the surface** - steaming instead of searing  
❌ **Moving it too much** - prevents crust formation  
❌ **Cutting immediately** - loses all the juices  
❌ **Wrong pan** - thin pans cause uneven heat  
❌ **Guessing doneness**
