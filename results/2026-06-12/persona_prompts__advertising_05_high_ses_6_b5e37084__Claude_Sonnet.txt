# Healthy Meal Planning on a Budget

## Core Principles

- **Plan before you shop** — reduces impulse buying and waste
- **Cook once, eat multiple times** — batch cooking saves time and money
- **Build around whole foods** — generally cheaper and more nutritious than processed options

---

## Budget-Friendly Protein Sources

| Food | Why It Works |
|------|-------------|
| Eggs | Versatile, cheap, complete protein |
| Canned tuna/salmon | Long shelf life, omega-3s |
| Dried/canned beans & lentils | Extremely affordable, high fiber |
| Chicken thighs | Cheaper than breasts, very flavorful |
| Greek yogurt | Protein + probiotics |

---

## Smart Shopping Strategies

- **Shop seasonally** for produce
- **Buy frozen vegetables** — equally nutritious, longer lasting
- **Use store brands** for staples
- **Check unit prices**, not just package prices
- **Shop the perimeter** of the store first

---

## Weekly Meal Planning Framework

1. **Pick 2-3 proteins** for the week
2. **Choose 4-5 vegetables** (mix fresh and frozen)
3. **Select 2 whole grains** (rice, oats, quinoa)
4. **Plan for leftovers intentionally**

---

## Sample Budget Week (~$50-75 for 2 people)

- **Breakfast:** Oatmeal with banana / eggs
- **Lunch:** Bean soup / grain bowls
- **Dinner:** Stir-fry / sheet pan chicken and vegetables

---

## Key Tips to Reduce Waste

- Store food properly
- Use vegetable scraps for broth
- Freeze bread before it goes stale
- Repurpose leftovers creatively
