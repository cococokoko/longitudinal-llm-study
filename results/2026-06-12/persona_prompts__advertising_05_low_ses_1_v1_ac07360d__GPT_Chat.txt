── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
With a full-time fast food job and an unpredictable schedule, the most useful approach is usually not a strict meal plan but a flexible system you can adapt each week.

Budget-friendly healthy meal strategy

1. Build meals from inexpensive staples
Keep a few items on hand from each category:

- Protein:
  - Eggs
  - Canned tuna or salmon
  - Beans and lentils
  - Greek yogurt
  - Chicken thighs
  - Peanut butter
  - Tofu (if you like it)

- Carbohydrates:
  - Rice
  - Oats
  - Potatoes
  - Whole-wheat bread
  - Pasta

- Vegetables and fruit:
  - Frozen vegetables (often cheaper and don't spoil)
  - Bananas
  - Apples
  - Carrots
  - Cabbage
  - Onions

- Healthy fats:
  - Peanut butter
  - Nuts (buy in bulk if possible)
  - Olive or canola oil

2. Prepare ingredients, not full meals
Because your schedule changes, it can help to spend 30–60 minutes once or twice a week preparing basics:

- Cook a batch of rice.
- Hard-boil eggs.
- Cook a package of chicken thighs or a pot of beans.
- Wash and cut vegetables.
- Portion snacks into grab-and-go containers.

Then you can quickly assemble meals depending on when you're working.

3. Keep simple backup meals
Have a few meals that take less than 10 minutes:

- Oatmeal with peanut butter and banana
- Rice, frozen vegetables, and eggs
- Tuna sandwich with fruit
- Bean and cheese burritos
- Greek yogurt with oats and fruit

4. Pack food for work
Fast food workplaces make it easy to spend money and eat less healthy options.

Easy portable options:
- Peanut butter sandwiches
- Hard-boiled eggs
- Apples or bananas
- Yogurt cups
- Homemade burritos
- Trail mix
- Leftovers in a microwave-safe container

5. Use a "3 meals + snacks" framework
Instead of assigning meals to specific times, prepare:
- 3–4 breakfast options
- 3–4 lunch/dinner options
- Several snacks

Choose based on your shift that day.

Example budget week

Breakfasts:
- Oatmeal + banana + peanut butter
- Eggs + toast

Lunches/dinners:
- Rice + chicken + frozen vegetables
- Bean and vegetable burritos
- Pasta with tuna and vegetables
- Potato, eggs, and sautéed vegetables

Snacks:
- Apples
- Carrots
- Yogurt
- Hard-boiled eggs

6. Save money on groceries
- Buy store brands.
- Choose frozen vegetables when cheaper.
- Compare price per unit, not package price.
- Buy proteins on sale and freeze portions.
- Use beans and lentils several times per week.
- Plan meals around weekly grocery sales.

Sample weekly budget grocery list (often around $40–60 in many U.S. areas, depending on local prices)
- Oats
- Rice
- Eggs
- Chicken thighs
- Dry beans or canned beans
- Frozen mixed vegetables
- Bananas
- Apples
- Potatoes
- Bread
- Peanut butter
- Greek yogurt
