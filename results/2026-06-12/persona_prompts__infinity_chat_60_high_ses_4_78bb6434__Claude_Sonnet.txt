# Computational Irreducibility (Like You're 5)

Imagine you want to know **where a bouncy ball will be after 1000 bounces**.

You have two choices:

**Option 1: Take a shortcut** 🧮
Do some quick math and jump straight to the answer. Like knowing 2+2=4 without counting on your fingers.

**Option 2: Just watch it bounce** 👀
Actually wait and watch all 1000 bounces happen, one by one.

---

**Computational irreducibility means:**

For some things, **there is no shortcut.**

The only way to know what happens is to **actually let it play out**, step by step. You can't skip ahead. You can't do clever math to jump to the end.

The universe itself has to "do the work."

---

## A simple example

A **simple rule** like "double the number each time" → you can shortcut that with math.

But some rules, even **really simple ones**, produce results so complicated and unpredictable that **nothing is faster than just running them.**

---

## Why it matters for you as a scientist

It means some biological or physical systems **cannot be predicted** without essentially *simulating every step* — no equation will save you.

It's a fundamental **limit on what science can shortcut.**

Stephen Wolfram developed this idea. It's humbling — simple rules, unknowable futures.
