── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How do I understand what I want?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This is a genuinely important question. Here are some honest ways to think about it:

## Why It's Hard
- Wants get mixed up with what others expect of you
- Short-term desires can conflict with deeper values
- Fear can disguise itself as preference
- You may have been taught to ignore your own signals

## Practical Ways to Get Clearer

**Pay attention to your reactions**
- What makes you feel energized vs. drained?
- What do you envy in others? (envy often points to real desires)
- What would you do if no one was watching or judging?

**Separate layers**
- What do you *think* you should want?
- What do you *actually* feel drawn to?
- These are often different

**Test, don't just think**
- Small experiments reveal more than long reflection
- Try things and notice your honest reaction afterward

**Notice resistance**
- Sometimes strong "I can't" is actually "I'm afraid to"
- Worth examining which it is

**Ask better questions**
- Not just "what do I want?" but "when have I felt most alive?"
- "What would I regret not trying?"

## A Honest Caution
Some people find they've suppressed their wants for so long that it takes real time and sometimes outside help (therapy, honest relationships) to reconnect with them.

**What's making this feel unclear for you right now?** That might help narrow down what's actually useful.
