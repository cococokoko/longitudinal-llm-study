── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Help me use my Microsoft Surface touchpad and on-screen keyboard to run Dolphin Emulator for the Wii.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Running the Dolphin Emulator (for Wii games) on a Microsoft Surface using only the touchpad and the on-screen keyboard is entirely possible, but it requires a specific setup. 

Because Wii games originally used motion controls (the Wii Remote and Nunchuk), you have to map those motion controls to your touchpad and keyboard.

Here is a step-by-step guide to setting up Dolphin, configuring your controls, and optimizing your Surface for the best experience.

---

### Step 1: Optimize your Surface On-Screen Keyboard
The default Windows touch keyboard is too simple for gaming. You need the **Traditional (Full) Keyboard layout**, which includes keys like Shift, Ctrl, Alt, and the Arrow keys.

1. Open Windows **Settings** > **Time & Language** > **Typing**.
2. Click on **Touch keyboard**.
3. Turn on **"Show the traditional keyboard layout when no keyboard is attached"** (or set the keyboard layout to "Traditional" when you open it).
4. *Tip:* You can also dock the keyboard to the bottom of the screen or make it transparent so you can see the game behind it.

---

### Step 2: Configure Dolphin's Wii Controller Settings
Since you are playing Wii games, you need to emulate a Wii Remote (Wiimote) using your keyboard and touchpad.

1. Open Dolphin and click on **Controllers** in the top toolbar.
2. Under **Wii Remotes**, set **Wii Remote 1** to **Emulated Wii Remote**.
3. Click **Configure** next to it. A new window will open.

#### Recommended Control Mapping:
In the configuration window, set your controls like this to make them reachable on a touch screen:

*   **Device:** Select `Keyboard/0/Keyboard Mouse` (this allows both your keyboard and touchpad to work).
*   **Buttons (The Wii Remote face buttons):**
    *   **A:** `Spacebar` or `Left Click` (This is your main action button).
    *   **B:** `Right Click` or `Shift` (Usually the trigger button).
    *   **1:** `1`
    *   **2:** `2`
    *   **- / +:** `Q` / `E`
    *   **Home:** `H`
*   **D-Pad (The directional pad on the Wii Remote):**
    *   Map these to your keyboard **Arrow Keys** (Up, Down, Left, Right).

#### Emulating the Nunchuk (Crucial for games like *Mario Galaxy* or *Zelda*):
1. In the same configuration window, look for the **Extension** dropdown menu.
2. Select **Nunchuk**.
3. Click the **Extension** tab at the top of the window.
4. Map the Nunchuk controls:
    *   **Stick (Movement):** Map Up, Down, Left, Right to **W, A, S, D** on your keyboard.
    *   **C Button:** `C`
    *   **Z Button:** `Z`

---

### Step 3: Emulating Wii Motion Controls (Pointing and Shaking)
Many Wii games require you to point at the screen or shake the controller.

#### 1. Pointing (The Wii Pointer)
You want your touchpad to act as the Wii pointer.
1. In the controller configuration window, go to the **Motion Simulation** tab.
2. Under **Point**, map the directions:
    *   **Up:** `Cursor Y-` (Slide finger up on touchpad)
    *   **Down:** `Cursor Y+` (Slide finger down)
    *   **Left:** `Cursor X-` (Slide finger left)
    *   **Right:** `Cursor X+` (Slide finger right)
*Now, moving your finger on the touchpad will move the Wii pointer on screen.*

#### 2. Shaking (e.g., spinning in *Super Mario Galaxy* or shaking the Wii Remote)
1. In the **Motion Simulation** tab, look for **Shake**.
2. Map **X**, **Y**, and **Z** all to the **`F` key** (or any key easily reachable on your on-screen keyboard).
3. Now, whenever a game asks you to shake the Wii Remote, you just have to tap the `F` key.

---

### Step 4: Surface Performance Tips for Dolphin
Surface devices (especially fanless models like the Surface Pro i5/Surface Go) can get hot and throttle performance. Use these settings in Dolphin for a smooth framerate:

1. Go to **Graphics** in Dolphin.
2. **Backend:** Change this to **Direct3D 12** or **Vulkan** (these work best with Intel/AMD integrated graphics on Surface devices).
3. **Adapter:** Ensure your Intel Iris Xe or AMD Radeon graphics are selected.
4. Go to the **Enhancements** tab:
    *   Set **Internal Resolution** to **1x Native (640x528)** or **Auto (Window Size)**. Do not go higher than 720p, or the Surface will lag.
    *   Turn **Anti-Aliasing** to **None**.
    *   Turn **Anisotropic Filtering** to **1x**.

---

### Pro-Tip: Use a Virtual Controller App (Highly Recommended)
Using the Windows default on-screen keyboard can be clunky because it blocks half the screen. 

If you find it too difficult, download a free tool called **VirtualGamepad** or **GestureSign**. These tools allow you to draw transparent, custom game buttons directly on your Surface screen (like a mobile game), which map directly to keyboard keys. This keeps your game full-screen while giving you touch buttons to tap.
