# Best Ways to Save for Retirement

Here are the most effective strategies, roughly in order of priority:

## Tax-Advantaged Accounts First

- **401(k)/403(b)** - Contribute at least enough to get your full employer match (free money)
- **IRA (Traditional or Roth)** - $7,000/year limit (2024); Roth grows tax-free
- **HSA** - If eligible, triple tax advantage; excellent for retirement healthcare costs

## General Principles

- **Start early** - Compound growth is powerful over decades
- **Maximize contributions** when possible
- **Automate contributions** so you don't skip them
- **Increase contributions** with each raise

## Investment Strategy

- **Low-cost index funds** - Minimize fees (expense ratios matter significantly)
- **Diversification** - Mix of stocks, bonds based on your timeline
- **Target-date funds** - Simple, automatic rebalancing option
- **Avoid frequent trading** - Stay the course during market downturns

## As a Physician-Scientist Specifically

- **Backdoor Roth IRA** - Useful if income exceeds standard limits
- **Solo 401(k) or SEP-IRA** - If you have independent/consulting income
- **Defined benefit plans** - Worth exploring if self-employed

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Waiting to start
- Cashing out accounts when changing jobs
- Paying high fund fees
- Being too conservative too early
