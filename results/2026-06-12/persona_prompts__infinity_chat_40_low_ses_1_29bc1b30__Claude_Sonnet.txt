This is a really common situation and worth thinking through carefully. Here are some practical considerations:

## Key Questions to Ask Yourself

- **What can you realistically contribute** given your unpredictable income?
- **Are you both on the same page** about the relationship timeline?
- **What happens if the relationship doesn't work out?**

## Practical Options to Consider

**Option 1: Contribute what you can afford**
- Pay a fair share of living expenses without being on the mortgage
- This protects you both if circumstances change

**Option 2: Be added to the mortgage/deed**
- Makes sense if you're genuinely committed and financially stable
- Requires lender approval and affects both credit profiles

**Option 3: Formal cohabitation agreement**
- Spells out financial contributions and ownership rights
- Protects both parties

## Honest Considerations

- **Your unpredictable schedule** means your income may fluctuate, so **don't overcommit financially**
- A mortgage is a long-term legal obligation - marriage plans aren't guaranteed
- **She takes on significant risk** buying alone, but also has full ownership rights

## Recommended Steps

- **Have an honest conversation** with your girlfriend about expectations
- **Consult a family law attorney** before making financial commitments
- Consider **waiting until after marriage** to combine finances this significantly

The fairness question really depends on what you both agree to openly. What matters most is **clear communication** before commitments are made.
