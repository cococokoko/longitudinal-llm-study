While current smartwatches have basic fall detection, they are reactive—they trigger *after* the impact has occurred, and they suffer from high false-positive rates (e.g., clapping, dropping the watch) and false-negative rates (e.g., "slow slides" down a wall).

Here is a proposal for a clinical-grade smartwatch feature designed specifically for senior citizens, leveraging principles of neurology and neuro-otology:

---

### Feature Name: **Vestibulo-Ocular & Gait Stability Index (VOGSI) with "Pre-Fall" Autonomic Triggering**

Instead of waiting for a high-G impact, this feature uses the smartwatch as a continuous, non-invasive neurological screening tool to **predict and prevent falls**, while providing ultra-precise triage data to neurotrauma teams if a fall does occur.

#### How It Works (The Technology)

1. **Continuous Gait & Micro-Oscillation Analysis:**
   Using high-frequency inertial measurement units (IMUs) and gyroscopes, the watch continuously analyzes the wearer’s gait. It measures micro-hesitations, step-to-step variability, and lateral sway. 
   * *The Clinical Connection:* It detects early signs of parkinsonian gait, normal pressure hydrocephalus (NPH) magnetic gait, or cerebellar ataxia long before the patient or their family notices.

2. **Photoplethysmography (PPG) Autonomic Coupling:**
   The watch couples movement data with real-time autonomic nervous system monitoring (heart rate variability [HRV] and micro-sweat/galvanic skin response). 
   * *The Clinical Connection:* A sudden drop in blood pressure (orthostatic hypotension) is often preceded by a spike in heart rate and a drop in HRV. If the watch detects this autonomic signature *simultaneously* with a sudden postural instability, it knows a syncopal episode is imminent.

3. **Active "Pre-Fall" Haptic Intervention:**
   If the VOGSI algorithm detects a critical loss of balance or a syncopal onset *before* the impact (a 500ms to 2-second window):
   * It delivers a high-intensity, distinct **vibrotactile cue** to the wrist. This acts as an external sensory prompt to stimulate the vestibulospinal reflex, prompting the senior to widen their stance or grab a support.
   * It emits an immediate auditory alert: *"Sit down immediately."*

---

#### The "Neurosurgical Hotline" Integration (If a Fall Occurs)

If the fall cannot be prevented and an impact is registered, the watch doesn't just call 911; it transmits a **"Neuro-Trauma Packet"** to the emergency responders and the receiving hospital’s trauma line. This packet includes:

* **The G-Force and Vector of Impact:** Was it a direct occipital strike (high risk for subdural hematoma/coup-contrecoup injury) or a lateral hip impact?
* **Pre-Fall Physiology:** Was the fall preceded by a cardiac arrhythmia (e.g., AFib causing syncope) or was it purely mechanical?
* **Anticoagulant Registry:** A prominent, lock-screen display of the patient's medication list, specifically highlighting if they are on **DOACs (Apixaban, Rivaroxaban) or Warfarin**.
