What are the best ways to improve indoor air quality?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Improving indoor air quality is usually most effective when you address the main sources of pollutants, increase ventilation, and filter the air.

Some of the most impactful steps are:

- Improve ventilation
  - Open windows and doors when outdoor air quality is good.
  - Use exhaust fans in bathrooms and kitchens.
  - Make sure HVAC systems bring in adequate outdoor air if they are designed to do so.

- Use high-quality air filtration
  - Install a high-efficiency HVAC filter that your system can handle (often MERV 13 or higher, if compatible).
  - Replace filters on the recommended schedule.
  - Consider portable air purifiers with HEPA filters for bedrooms and frequently used rooms.

- Control moisture and humidity
  - Keep indoor humidity between about 30% and 50%.
  - Fix leaks promptly.
  - Use dehumidifiers in damp areas.
  - Prevent mold growth by drying wet materials quickly.

- Reduce indoor pollution sources
  - Avoid smoking or vaping indoors.
  - Limit the use of candles, incense, and wood-burning fireplaces.
  - Choose low-VOC paints, adhesives, and furnishings when possible.
  - Store solvents, fuels, and chemicals outside living spaces.

- Improve kitchen air quality
  - Use a vented range hood when cooking, especially with gas stoves.
  - Run the fan during and after cooking.
  - Cooking can be a major source of indoor particulate pollution.

- Clean effectively
  - Vacuum regularly using a vacuum with a HEPA filter.
  - Dust with damp cloths to avoid redistributing particles.
  - Wash bedding regularly to reduce dust mites and allergens.

- Manage allergens
  - Keep pets groomed and clean.
  - Use allergen-proof mattress and pillow covers if allergies are a concern.
  - Reduce clutter that collects dust.

- Monitor air quality
  - Consider an indoor air quality monitor that measures particles (PM2.5), carbon dioxide (CO₂), humidity, and sometimes volatile organic compounds (VOCs).
  - Test for radon if you live in an area where it is a concern; radon is a significant indoor air hazard in some regions.

For many homes, the highest-return combination is:
1. A properly maintained HVAC system with a good filter.
2. A HEPA air purifier in the rooms where people spend the most time.
3. Good kitchen and bathroom ventilation.
4. Humidity control and moisture prevention.
