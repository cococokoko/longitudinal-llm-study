# Effective Online Teaching Techniques

## Core Principles First
- **Know your audience** - understand students' tech access, backgrounds, and goals
- **Design intentionally** - online teaching requires more structure than in-person
- **Prioritize connection** - relationships matter even through screens

## Engagement Strategies

### Active Learning
- Use polls, quizzes, and quick checks during sessions
- Breakout rooms for small group discussion
- Think-pair-share adapted for chat or collaborative docs
- Problem-solving activities rather than passive listening

### Communication
- Clear, consistent instructions (assume nothing)
- Multiple channels (video, text, discussion boards)
- Regular announcements to maintain momentum
- Prompt feedback on assignments

## Technical Best Practices
- Keep recordings and videos **short** (under 10-15 minutes)
- Use captions for accessibility
- Test technology before sessions
- Have backup plans when tech fails

## Building Community
- Icebreakers and informal check-ins
- Discussion forums with genuine prompts
- Peer collaboration opportunities
- Be visible and responsive

## Assessment
- Varied formats (not just tests)
- Frequent low-stakes checks over rare high-stakes exams
- Clear rubrics
- Timely, specific feedback

## Common Pitfalls to Avoid
- Lecturing for long periods without interaction
- Overloading students with content
- Assuming everyone has equal tech access
- Neglecting student isolation

## What matters most?
**Consistency + responsiveness + genuine engagement** tend to outweigh any specific tool or technique.
