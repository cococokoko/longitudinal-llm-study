Here are some effective ways to make your home more energy efficient:

## Quick/Low-Cost Steps
- Replace incandescent bulbs with LED lighting
- Use smart power strips to reduce standby power
- Seal gaps around doors and windows with weatherstripping or caulk
- Set your thermostat a few degrees lower in winter, higher in summer

## Appliances & Equipment
- Upgrade to Energy Star certified appliances
- Install a programmable or smart thermostat
- Use energy-efficient showerheads and faucets
- Unplug devices when not in use

## Insulation & Building
- Add or improve attic and wall insulation
- Install double or triple-pane windows
- Insulate hot water pipes and your water heater

## Heating & Cooling
- Schedule regular HVAC maintenance
- Change air filters regularly
- Use ceiling fans to reduce AC/heating load
- Consider a heat pump system

## Bigger Investments
- Solar panels
- Tankless water heater
- Energy-efficient windows and doors

## Good Habits
- Run dishwashers and laundry during off-peak hours
- Use natural light when possible
- Keep refrigerator coils clean
