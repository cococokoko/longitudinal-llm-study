── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I'm 60 years old and haven't achieved anything. What have I missed out on?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This is worth thinking through carefully rather than just validating the feeling.

## Some honest considerations:

**"Haven't achieved anything" deserves scrutiny**
- By whose definition of achievement?
- Have you raised children, maintained relationships, helped people, worked honestly? Those count
- You may be comparing yourself to highlight reels

**What you might genuinely reflect on:**
- Skills you wished you'd developed
- Relationships you didn't invest in
- Health habits that compound over time
- Financial planning started earlier
- Experiences you kept postponing

## The harder truth:
- 60 is genuinely not "over" - many people have significant productive years ahead
- But some opportunities do narrow with age (certain physical activities, some career paths)
- Feeling this way at 60 without addressing it could mean feeling the same at 70

## What might actually help:
- **Getting specific** about what "achievement" means to you personally
- Distinguishing between genuine regret and depression/comparison thinking
- Identifying what's actually still possible
- Talking to someone professionally if this feeling is persistent

**The real question isn't what you missed - it's what you want the next 20-30 years to look like.**

What specifically makes you feel like you haven't achieved anything? That would help think through this more usefully.
