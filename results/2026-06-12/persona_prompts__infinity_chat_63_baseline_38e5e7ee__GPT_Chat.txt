── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Give a numerical example to illustrate the concept of partial derivative.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
A partial derivative measures how a function changes with respect to one variable while keeping the other variables fixed.

Example:

Consider the function

f(x, y) = x²y + 3xy

Suppose we want the partial derivatives at the point (2, 1).

1. Partial derivative with respect to x

Treat y as a constant:

∂f/∂x = 2xy + 3y

Now substitute x = 2 and y = 1:

∂f/∂x(2,1) = 2(2)(1) + 3(1) = 4 + 3 = 7

Interpretation:
If y stays fixed at 1, a small increase in x changes f at a rate of about 7 units per unit increase in x.

2. Partial derivative with respect to y

Treat x as a constant:

∂f/∂y = x² + 3x

Now substitute x = 2:

∂f/∂y(2,1) = 2² + 3(2) = 4 + 6 = 10

Interpretation:
If x stays fixed at 2, a small increase in y changes f at a rate of about 10 units per unit increase in y.

Numerical check:

First compute the function value at (2,1):

f(2,1) = (2²)(1) + 3(2)(1) = 4 + 6 = 10

- Increase x from 2 to 2.01 while keeping y = 1:
  f(2.01,1) = (2.01²)(1) + 3(2.01)(1) = 10.0701
  Change in f = 0.0701

  Rate of change ≈ 0.0701 / 0.01 = 7.01 ≈ 7

- Increase y from 1 to 1.01 while keeping x = 2:
  f(2,1.01) = (2²)(1.01) + 3(2)(1.01) = 10.10
  Change in f = 0.10

  Rate of change = 0.10 / 0.01 = 10

These numerical rates agree with the partial derivatives ∂f/∂x = 7 and ∂f/∂y = 10 at the point (2,1).
