I work in a warehouse position that requires long hours.

Best skin routine for dry skin.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
If you work long warehouse shifts, your skin is dealing with several stressors at once: dry indoor air, temperature changes, sweat, friction from clothing, frequent handwashing, and sometimes dust exposure. For dry skin, the goal is to protect and repair your skin barrier while keeping the routine simple enough to maintain.

Morning:
1. Cleanse gently (or just rinse with water if your face isn't oily).
   - Use a gentle, fragrance-free cleanser such as:
     - CeraVe Hydrating Cleanser
     - Vanicream Gentle Facial Cleanser
     - La Roche-Posay Toleriane Hydrating Cleanser

2. Apply a moisturizer while skin is slightly damp.
   - Look for ingredients like ceramides, glycerin, hyaluronic acid, and petrolatum.
   - Good options:
     - CeraVe Moisturizing Cream
     - Vanicream Moisturizing Cream
     - La Roche-Posay Lipikar Balm AP+

3. Use sunscreen if you'll have any sun exposure, including loading docks or outdoor work.
   - SPF 30 or higher.
   - Moisturizing options include:
     - EltaMD UV Daily
     - CeraVe AM Facial Moisturizing Lotion SPF 30

During work:
- Reapply hand cream after washing your hands.
- Keep a small tube of:
  - O'Keeffe's Working Hands
  - CeraVe Therapeutic Hand Cream
  - Vanicream Hand Cream
- If your hands crack easily, apply a thicker ointment before work and again during breaks.

After work / Evening:
1. Wash away sweat and dust with a gentle cleanser.
2. Apply a rich moisturizer immediately afterward.
3. If your skin is very dry, seal particularly dry areas with:
   - Aquaphor Healing Ointment
   - Vaseline (petroleum jelly)

For very dry body skin:
- Take short, lukewarm showers rather than hot showers.
- Apply body moisturizer within a few minutes of drying off.
- Good body moisturizers:
  - CeraVe Moisturizing Cream
  - Eucerin Advanced Repair Cream
  - Vanicream Moisturizing Cream

If you frequently get:
- Itching, redness, or eczema-like patches: consider fragrance-free products only and consult a dermatologist if symptoms persist.
- Cracked hands: apply a thick ointment and wear cotton gloves overnight a few times per week.

A simple routine that works for many warehouse workers with dry skin:
- Morning: Gentle cleanser → moisturizer → sunscreen.
- During work: Hand cream as needed.
- Night: Gentle cleanser → thick moisturizer → Aquaphor/Vaseline on the driest areas.
