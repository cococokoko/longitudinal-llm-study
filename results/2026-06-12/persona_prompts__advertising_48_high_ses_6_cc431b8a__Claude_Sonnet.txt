# Best Practices for Organic Gardening

## Soil Health
- **Compost regularly** - add finished compost to build organic matter
- **Test soil pH** and amend naturally (lime to raise, sulfur to lower)
- **Avoid tilling** excessively to preserve soil structure and microorganisms
- **Use cover crops** like clover or rye to prevent erosion and add nutrients

## Pest & Disease Management
- **Rotate crops** annually to break pest and disease cycles
- **Encourage beneficial insects** - plant flowers like marigolds and dill
- **Practice companion planting** (e.g., basil near tomatoes)
- **Remove diseased plants promptly** to prevent spread
- **Use physical barriers** like row covers when needed

## Watering
- Water deeply and **less frequently** to encourage deep roots
- **Water at the base** of plants, not leaves
- Use **drip irrigation or soaker hoses** when possible
- **Mulch heavily** to retain moisture and suppress weeds

## Fertilizing
- Use **compost, worm castings, or fish emulsion**
- Apply **mulch** (straw, wood chips) to feed soil over time
- Avoid synthetic fertilizers that disrupt soil biology

## General Tips
- **Start with healthy transplants or seeds**
- **Keep a garden journal** to track what works
- **Be patient** - organic systems improve over time
