# Effective Online Teaching Techniques

Here are key strategies organized by category:

## Engagement & Interaction
- **Break content into shorter segments** (10-15 minutes max for video lessons)
- Use polls, quizzes, and discussion boards regularly
- Ask questions throughout rather than just at the end
- Create opportunities for student-to-student interaction

## Clear Communication
- Set explicit expectations for assignments and deadlines
- Provide detailed rubrics upfront
- Respond to student questions within a reasonable, stated timeframe
- Use multiple formats (video, text, audio) for important information

## Flexible Design
- **Record live sessions** for students who can't attend
- Offer asynchronous options alongside synchronous ones
- Allow multiple ways to demonstrate learning
- Build in buffer time around deadlines

## Building Community
- Use introductory activities to help students connect
- Create informal discussion spaces
- Give personalized feedback, not just grades

## Technical Considerations
- Keep technology simple and accessible
- Test everything before students encounter it
- Have backup plans for technical failures
- Ensure content works on mobile devices

## Assessment
- Use varied assessment types
- Consider open-book or project-based assessments
- Check for understanding frequently rather than just at major tests

## Particularly Relevant for Your Students
Given that many may have unpredictable schedules, **asynchronous flexibility** and **recorded content** are especially important.
