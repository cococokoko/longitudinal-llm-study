## Real Concerns Worth Taking Seriously

**Documented effects:**

**Structural problems:**
- Platforms have financial incentives misaligned with informational quality
- Opacity makes accountability difficult
- Scale amplifies effects that might be manageable in smaller contexts

## Where I'd Push Back on Common Claims

**The evidence is more complicated than often presented:**
- Filter bubbles may be less severe than initially claimed - research is genuinely mixed
- People actively seek confirming information regardless of algorithms
- Traditional media also optimized for engagement (tabloids predate social media)
- Causation vs. correlation with polarization is genuinely contested

## Legitimate Legal/Policy Considerations

- Section 230 liability questions remain unresolved
- Transparency requirements seem more defensible than content mandates
- First Amendment constraints limit regulatory options significantly
- Algorithmic accountability frameworks are still developing
