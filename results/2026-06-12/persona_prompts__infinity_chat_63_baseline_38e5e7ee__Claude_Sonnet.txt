# Partial Derivatives: A Numerical Example

## The Function

Let's use:
$$f(x, y) = x^2 + 3xy + y^2$$

We'll find the partial derivatives at the point **(2, 3)**.

---

## Finding the Partial Derivatives

### ∂f/∂x (treat y as a constant)

$$\frac{\partial f}{\partial x} = 2x + 3y$$

**At (2, 3):**
$$\frac{\partial f}{\partial x}\Bigg|_{(2,3)} = 2(2) + 3(3) = 4 + 9 = \boxed{13}$$

### ∂f/∂y (treat x as a constant)

$$\frac{\partial f}{\partial y} = 3x + 2y$$

**At (2, 3):**
$$\frac{\partial f}{\partial y}\Bigg|_{(2,3)} = 3(2) + 2(3) = 6 + 6 = \boxed{12}$$

---

## Numerical Verification

Use the definition: $\dfrac{\partial f}{\partial x} \approx \dfrac{f(x+h,\ y) - f(x,\ y)}{h}$ with **h = 0.001**

| Calculation | Value |
|---|---|
| f(2, 3) = 4 + 18 + 9 | **31** |
| f(2.001, 3) = (2.001)² + 3(2.001)(3) + 9 | **31.013003** |
| [f(2.001,3) − f(2,3)] / 0.001 | **≈ 13.003 ≈ 13** ✓ |

---

## Interpretation

| Partial Derivative | Meaning |
|---|---|
| ∂f/∂x = 13 | Holding y = 3 fixed, f increases by **~13 units** per unit increase in x |
| ∂f/∂y = 12 | Holding x = 2 fixed, f increases by **~12 units** per unit increase in y |

> **Key idea:** A partial derivative measures the rate of change of a function in **one direction at a time**, while all other variables remain frozen.
