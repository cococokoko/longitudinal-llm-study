What are the best apps for tracking fitness and nutrition?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
When you’re balancing work and childcare, you don’t have time for overly complicated apps that require hours of manual entry. The "best" apps for a busy single parent are those that are **fast, intuitive, and offer high value with minimal effort**. 

Here are the best fitness and nutrition apps categorized by how they fit into a packed schedule:

---

### **1. Best for Quick Nutrition Tracking: MyFitnessPal**
* **Why it’s great for busy parents:** It has the largest food database in the world. The **barcode scanner** is a lifesaver—you can scan the mac-and-cheese box or your meal-prep container in two seconds flat. 
* **Key Features:** 
  * Barcode scanner for instant logging.
  * "Recipe importer" that lets you paste a recipe URL from the web to instantly calculate the nutrition.
  * Saves your "frequent meals" so you can log your daily coffee or go-to breakfast with one tap.
* **Cost:** Free basic version (which is usually enough); Premium is $19.99/month.

### **2. Best for Quick, No-Equipment Workouts: Nike Training Club (NTC)**
* **Why it’s great for busy parents:** When you can't get to a gym, you need workouts you can do in your living room while your child is napping or playing nearby. NTC offers high-quality, trainer-led videos that require zero equipment.
* **Key Features:**
  * Filter workouts by time (from 5-minute stretches to 45-minute sweat sessions).
  * "Yoga for bedtime" or "quick core" classes that fit into tiny pockets of your day.
  * **100% Free** (Nike made all premium content free permanently).
* **Cost:** Free.

### **3. Best for "Mindless" Tracking: Lifesum**
* **Why it’s great for busy parents:** If counting exact calories feels too stressful, Lifesum is much more visual and lifestyle-focused. It uses a simple rating system (smiling faces and color codes) to tell you if you're on track.
* **Key Features:**
  * Beautiful, clean interface that doesn't feel clinical.
  * Quick-tracking for water intake (just tap a glass icon).
  * Pre-made meal plans and quick, healthy recipes if you are stuck on what to cook for the family.
* **Cost:** Free basic version; Premium starts at $4.17/month.

### **4. Best for Short, High-Intensity Workouts: Seven (7 Minute Workout)**
* **Why it’s great for busy parents:** If you literally only have 10 minutes between putting the kids to bed and logging back onto your work laptop, this app is for you. It is based on scientific studies to give you the maximum benefit in the shortest time.
* **Key Features:**
  * Workouts are exactly 7 minutes long.
  * Requires nothing but a wall, a chair, and your own body weight.
  * Gamified system (you start with three "lives"—miss a day, and you lose a life) to keep you motivated.
* **Cost:** Free basic version; Club membership is $9.99/month.

### **5. Best All-in-One (If you have an Apple Watch): Apple Fitness+**
* **Why it’s great for busy parents:** If you are already in the Apple ecosystem, this integrates seamlessly. You can cast a workout to your iPad or TV while your watch tracks your heart rate.
* **Key Features:**
  * "Time to Walk" audio episodes (great for pushing a stroller or walking during a phone meeting).
  * Ultra-short 5 and 10-minute workouts.
  * Family Sharing: One subscription can be shared with up to five family members.
* **Cost:** $9.99/month (often comes free for 3 months with a new Apple Watch).

---

### **A Quick Tip for Busy Parents:**
If you want to track both fitness and nutrition without opening five different apps, **sync everything to Apple Health (iOS) or Google Fit (Android)**. 

For example, you can track your food in *MyFitnessPal* and your workouts in *Seven*, and both apps will send their data to Apple Health. You’ll get a complete picture of your health in one central dashboard without any extra data entry.
