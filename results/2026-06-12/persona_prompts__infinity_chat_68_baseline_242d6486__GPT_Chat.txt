── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Give me a tip for managing a team of coworkers.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
One effective tip: set clear expectations and follow up consistently.

Make sure everyone understands:
- What their responsibilities are
- What success looks like
- When work is due
- How progress will be communicated

Then, check in regularly—not to micromanage, but to remove obstacles, answer questions, and keep priorities aligned. Many team problems stem from unclear expectations rather than lack of effort.

A simple practice is to end meetings with:
- Who is doing what
- By when
- What support they need

This creates accountability while helping team members feel supported.
