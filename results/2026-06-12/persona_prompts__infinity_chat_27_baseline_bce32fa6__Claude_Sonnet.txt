# CUBICLE ZERO
### A Play in Three Acts
*A Dilbert-Style Comedy Set at the End of the World*

---

## CHARACTERS

**DILBERT** — An engineer. Somehow still wearing his tie, which has curled upward at the tip despite the collapse of civilization.

**WALLY** — Dilbert's coworker. Has not noticed the apocalypse. Is still holding a coffee mug, though coffee has been extinct for six months.

**ALICE** — Dilbert's coworker. Fist of Death remains fully operational. Possibly the most dangerous person in the wasteland, which is saying something.

**THE POINTY-HAIRED BOSS (PHB)** — Has survived through mechanisms science cannot explain. Still manages people. This is the true horror.

**DOGBERT** — Dilbert's dog. Now rules a small fiefdom of survivors. Charges consulting fees.

**ASOK** — The intern. Still an intern. Has been an intern through two civilizations now.

**RATBERT** — A rat. Was always prepared for this.

---

## SETTING

*The ruins of a mid-sized corporation's open-plan office. The roof is partially collapsed. A mutant warlord's army can be heard in the distance. A motivational poster reading "HANG IN THERE" still clings to one crumbling wall, though the cat in the photo has long since been eaten. Fluorescent lights flicker. One of them has been flickering since 2019 and no one has fixed it.*

---

# ACT ONE
## "THE STRATEGIC INITIATIVE"

*Lights rise on DILBERT sitting at his desk, which is now surrounded by rubble. He is typing on a laptop powered by a hand-crank generator. WALLY sits across from him, staring into his empty mug with the contentment of a man who has made peace with everything.*

---

**DILBERT:**
*(typing)*
I've completed the structural analysis of our defensive perimeter. If we reinforce the north wall with the server racks from the old data center, we can withstand a siege of approximately—

**WALLY:**
*(not looking up)*
I've decided not to participate in the apocalypse.

**DILBERT:**
You can't opt out of the apocalypse.

**WALLY:**
I opted out of the Henderson project in 2021 and nobody noticed for eight months. I have a system.

**DILBERT:**
The Henderson project wasn't actively trying to kill us.

**WALLY:**
Debatable.

*A distant explosion. Neither of them reacts.*

**DILBERT:**
That's the third explosion this morning. The warlord's forces are getting closer.

**WALLY:**
I find that if you wait long enough, most problems resolve themselves or get assigned to someone else.

**DILBERT:**
What if the problem is a heavily armed militia?

**WALLY:**
*(considering)*
Then I wait longer.

*ALICE enters, dragging a mutant raider by the collar. The raider is unconscious. She drops him on the floor.*

**ALICE:**
I captured one of their scouts.

**DILBERT:**
How?

**ALICE:**
He made the mistake of telling me my defensive fortification plan was "a little aggressive for a first draft."

**DILBERT:**
*(looking at the unconscious raider)*
Is he... okay?

**ALICE:**
He's fine. Mostly. He'll walk again. Probably.
*(beat)*
The point is, I got us intelligence. Their warlord is planning a full assault at dawn. We need a strategy.

**DILBERT:**
I have a strategy. I've been working on it for—

*PHB enters. He is wearing his usual suit, now tattered, with a spiked shoulder pad that someone has duct-taped to him, presumably against his will. He carries a clipboard.*

**PHB:**
Good news, people. I've just come from a very productive meeting with myself, and I've decided we need to pivot.

**DILBERT:**
Pivot from what?

**PHB:**
From whatever we're currently doing.

**ALICE:**
We're currently trying to survive.

**PHB:**
Right, and I think we've been approaching survival all wrong. I want us to think outside the box.

**DILBERT:**
The box is on fire.
