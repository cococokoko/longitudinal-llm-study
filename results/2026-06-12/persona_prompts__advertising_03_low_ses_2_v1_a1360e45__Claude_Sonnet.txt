This is genuinely challenging, so let's focus on **practical strategies** that work with your real situation.

---

## Structuring Your Day

- **Work during natural quiet windows** - nap times, early morning, after bedtime
- **Time-block ruthlessly** - assign specific tasks to specific hours
- **Identify your 2-3 most critical tasks** each day rather than long to-do lists
- **Batch similar tasks** together to reduce mental switching

---

## Managing Childcare Overlap

- **Designate "independent activity" time** for kids during your focused work periods
- **Rotate engaging activities** so children stay occupied longer
- **Be honest with employers/clients** about your schedule when possible
- **Use school hours as prime work time** if applicable

---

## Reducing Daily Friction

- Prep the night before (clothes, meals, work priorities)
- Create a simple morning routine that minimizes decisions
- Keep a dedicated workspace, even if small
- Use noise-canceling headphones as a focus signal

---

## Being Realistic

- **Some days will be interrupted** - build buffer time into deadlines
- **Done is better than perfect** on difficult days
- **Ask for help** - neighbors, family, co-op childcare arrangements
