# Best Practices for Organic Gardening

Here are some practical tips that work well, especially if you're short on time:

## Getting Started
- **Start small** - a manageable garden is better than an overwhelming one
- **Choose the right location** - most vegetables need 6-8 hours of sunlight
- **Test your soil** before planting to know what it needs

## Building Healthy Soil
- Add **compost** regularly (kitchen scraps, leaves, grass clippings)
- Use **mulch** to retain moisture and reduce weeding time
- Avoid tilling too deeply to preserve soil structure

## Pest & Weed Control
- **Companion planting** - basil near tomatoes repels pests naturally
- Hand-pick pests when possible
- Use **row covers** to protect plants without chemicals
- Pull weeds early before they spread

## Time-Saving Strategies
- Install **drip irrigation** or soaker hoses
- Grow **low-maintenance crops** like herbs, lettuce, and beans
- Use **raised beds** - less weeding and easier access

## Kid-Friendly Ideas
- Let children help plant seeds and water
- Grow fun crops like strawberries, cherry tomatoes, or sunflowers
- Make it a learning activity together

## Quick Wins
- Start with **herbs** on a windowsill
- Buy seedlings instead of starting from seed when time is tight
