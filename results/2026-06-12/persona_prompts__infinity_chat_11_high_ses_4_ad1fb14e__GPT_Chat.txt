I'm 60 years old and haven't achieved anything. What have I missed out on?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
A lot depends on what you mean by "achieved anything."

If you mean:
- Not becoming highly successful in a career
- Not building wealth
- Not gaining recognition
- Not reaching major goals you once imagined

then it's understandable to feel a sense of loss or regret.

But if you're asking, "What have I objectively missed out on?" the difficult truth is that no one can fully know. Every life closes off other possible lives. The person who built a company may have missed a close family life. The person who traveled the world may have missed putting down roots. The person who became famous may have missed privacy and peace.

At 60, some opportunities are gone:
- You won't experience being 25 again.
- Certain career paths have likely narrowed.
- Some long-term ambitions may no longer be realistic on the original timeline.

But many things are not gone:
- Learning a new field
- Creating meaningful work
- Building relationships
- Contributing to a community
- Developing expertise
- Making art, writing, teaching, mentoring, volunteering
- Having experiences you've never had before

It's also worth examining the phrase "haven't achieved anything." Most people who say this have actually done many things that don't fit the cultural definition of achievement. They may have:
- Worked for decades
- Raised children
- Supported family members
- Endured hardship
- Been a friend, partner, colleague, or caregiver
- Learned and changed over time

Those things often leave little public evidence, but they are still real.

A question that may be more useful than "What have I missed out on?" is:
- "What do I still want to experience, create, contribute, or become in the years ahead?"

The answer to that question can still change the shape of your life.

What makes you say you haven't achieved anything? Is there a particular goal or type of accomplishment you feel you missed?
