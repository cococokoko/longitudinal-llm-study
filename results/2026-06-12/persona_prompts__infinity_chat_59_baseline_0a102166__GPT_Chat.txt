── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Give me a tip to be more organized at work. I'm a high school teacher.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
A practical tip for high school teachers: create a single "capture system" for everything that needs your attention.

Pick one place—such as a digital task manager, a notebook, or a notes app—and make it the only place where you record:
- Tasks (e.g., "grade essays")
- Follow-ups (e.g., "email parent about missing work")
- Ideas (e.g., "use this article in next week's lesson")
- Deadlines and reminders

The key is to stop relying on memory or scattered sticky notes, emails, and scraps of paper. Whenever something comes up during class, a meeting, or planning time, immediately put it into your capture system.

For teachers, a useful routine is a 10-minute review at the end of each school day:
- Check upcoming lessons for the next two days.
- Review your task list.
- Prepare any materials you'll need tomorrow.
- Identify the top 1–3 priorities for the next day.

This small daily habit often prevents last-minute scrambling and makes the school week feel much more manageable.
