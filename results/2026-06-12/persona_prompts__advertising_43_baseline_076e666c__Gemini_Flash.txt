── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How to plan an environmentally friendly wedding?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Planning an environmentally friendly (or "eco-chic") wedding doesn’t mean sacrificing style, elegance, or fun. In fact, sustainable weddings often feel more intimate, unique, and intentional. 

The average wedding produces about **400 pounds of garbage and 63 tons of CO2**. By making conscious choices, you can drastically reduce that footprint. Here is a comprehensive guide to planning a beautiful, low-impact wedding.

---

### 1. The Venue & Travel (The Biggest Impact)
Transportation is the largest contributor to a wedding’s carbon footprint. 

*   **Keep it Local:** Choose a venue close to where the majority of your guests live to minimize flights.
*   **All-in-One Location:** Host the ceremony and reception at the same venue to eliminate the need for guests to drive between locations.
*   **Choose a "Naturally Beautiful" Venue:** Botanical gardens, state parks, beaches, or greenhouses require very little additional decor. 
*   **Eco-Certified Venues:** Look for venues with LEED certification, or those that run on renewable energy and have robust recycling/composting programs.
*   **Group Transportation:** Provide a shuttle bus or encourage carpooling/public transit for guests staying at nearby hotels.

### 2. Invitations & Paper Goods
*   **Go Digital:** Save-the-dates, RSVPs, and even invitations can be sent digitally via sites like Greenvelope or WithJoy. This also makes tracking RSVPs much easier.
*   **Seed Paper:** If you want physical invitations, use biodegradable seed paper. Guests can plant the invitation in their garden to grow wildflowers or herbs.
*   **Recycled Paper:** Use 100% post-consumer recycled paper printed with soy-based inks.
*   **Ditch the Programs and Menus:** Instead of printing individual programs and menus, use a single, beautiful chalkboard, wooden sign, or mirror to display the schedule and menu.

### 3. Attire with a Past (and Future)
The fashion industry is highly polluting. Opt for circular fashion.

*   **Vintage or Pre-Loved:** Look for vintage gowns or buy a gently used designer dress from sites like Stillwhite, Nearly Newlywed, or Wear Your Love.
*   **Rent the Look:** Rent tuxedos/suits (from Black Tux or Generation Tux) and encourage bridesmaids to rent dresses (from Rent the Runway).
*   **Wear It Again:** If buying new, choose bridesmaid dresses and groom/groomsmen suits that can actually be worn again to future events.
*   **Ethical Designers:** If buying a new wedding dress, support designers who use organic silk, hemp, or recycled fabrics (e.g., Reformation, Leila Hafzi).

### 4. Flowers & Decor
Traditional floral arrangements often use toxic floral foam (which is microplastic) and fly flowers in from thousands of miles away.

*   **Local and Seasonal:** Work with a florist who sources flowers from local, organic flower farms. 
*   **Potted Plants and Herbs:** Use potted orchids, ferns, succulents, or herbs as centerpieces. Guests can take them home afterward, or you can plant them in your garden.
*   **No Floral Foam:** Ask your florist to use reusable metal frogs, chicken wire, or water vials instead of green floral foam.
*   **Rent, Don't Buy:** Rent all glassware, plates, linens, and arches. If you do buy decor, source it secondhand from thrift stores or Facebook Marketplace, and resell it after the wedding.
*   **Eco-Friendly "Exit" Toss:** Instead of plastic confetti or rice, have guests toss dried lavender, flower petals, eucalyptus leaves, or birdseed.

### 5. Food, Drink, and Waste Management
Food waste and single-use plastics are major wedding culprits.

*   **Farm-to-Table Catering:** Choose a caterer that uses locally sourced, organic, and seasonal ingredients. This reduces "food miles" and supports local agriculture.
*   **Go Vegetarian/Vegan (or Plant-Forward):** Meat (especially beef) has a massive carbon footprint. Consider an all-vegetarian menu, or at least offer highly appealing plant-based options.
*   **Zero Single-Use Plastics:** Ensure the caterer and bar use real glassware, plates, and silverware. If disposables are necessary, use certified compostable bamboo or sugarcane plates.
*   **Bulk Alcohol:** Serve beer from kegs and wine from local vineyards (often available in bulk) rather than individual bottles and cans.
*   **Plan for Leftovers:** Ask your caterer if leftover food can be donated to a local shelter, or arrange for a composting service to collect food scraps.

### 6. Rings and Jewelry
*   **Lab-Grown or Recycled Diamonds:** Lab-grown diamonds are chemically identical to mined diamonds but have a fraction of the environmental and ethical toll.
*   **Recycled Metals:** Choose jewelers who use 100% recycled gold, platinum, or silver.
*   **Heirlooms:** Reset a family heirloom stone into a new band.

### 7. Registry and Favors
*   **Charity Registry:** In lieu of physical gifts, ask guests to donate to your favorite environmental charities (e.g., One Tree Planted, Ocean Conservancy).
*   **Experience Registry:** Ask for contributions toward your honeymoon, cooking classes, or concert tickets rather than physical "stuff."
*   **Consumable Favors:** If you want to give favors, make them consumable so they don't end up in a landfill. Think local honey, organic coffee beans, homemade jam, or custom seed packets.

### 8. Offset Your Carbon Footprint
Despite your best efforts, your wedding will still have a carbon footprint. 
*   Calculate your wedding's footprint using an online calculator (like *Terrapass*).
*   Purchase carbon offsets to fund renewable energy, forestry, or methane capture projects to bring your wedding's net impact down to zero.

### Pro-Tip: Communicate Your Vision
Let your vendors know early on that sustainability is a priority for you. Most vendors are happy to accommodate eco-friendly requests (like no plastic straws at the bar or composting kitchen scraps) if they are notified in advance!
