Learning to cook healthy meals quickly is mostly about simplifying your approach, building a small set of reliable techniques, and reducing decision-making.

Here are some practical ways to get started:

- Learn 5–10 basic meals instead of dozens of recipes.
  Examples:
  - Stir-fry with vegetables and protein
  - Sheet-pan chicken and vegetables
  - Omelets or frittatas
  - Grain bowls (rice, quinoa, or farro with vegetables and protein)
  - Bean or lentil soups
  - Tacos with lean protein and vegetables

- Master a few cooking methods.
  Focus on:
  - Roasting
  - Sautéing
  - Steaming
  - Grilling or broiling
  - Pressure cooking or slow cooking
  Once you understand these methods, you can cook many different ingredients without following complex recipes.

- Keep healthy staples on hand.
  Examples:
  - Frozen vegetables
  - Canned beans
  - Eggs
  - Greek yogurt
  - Whole grains
  - Lean meats, fish, or tofu
  - Olive oil
  - Garlic, onions, and basic spices

- Use a simple meal formula.
  Build meals from:
  - Protein: chicken, fish, beans, tofu, eggs
  - Vegetables: fresh or frozen
  - Healthy carbohydrate: brown rice, potatoes, whole-grain pasta
  - Flavor: herbs, spices, citrus, sauces

- Meal prep strategically.
  Instead of preparing entire meals, batch-cook components:
  - Cook a large batch of rice or grains.
  - Roast a tray of vegetables.
  - Prepare several servings of protein.
  Mix and match throughout the week.

- Choose recipes with short ingredient lists.
  Look for meals with:
  - 5–10 ingredients
  - 30 minutes or less cooking time
  - One-pan or one-pot preparation

- Use technology.
  Many websites and apps allow filtering recipes by:
  - Preparation time
  - Health goals
  - Dietary preferences
  - Available ingredients

- Practice knife skills safely.
  Basic chopping and slicing techniques can significantly reduce preparation time.

A simple 15-minute healthy dinner might be:
- Sauté diced chicken or tofu.
- Add frozen mixed vegetables.
- Season with garlic, ginger, and soy sauce.
- Serve over microwaveable brown rice.

Repeating a few healthy meals regularly is often more effective than constantly trying new recipes. As your confidence grows, you can gradually expand your repertoire.
