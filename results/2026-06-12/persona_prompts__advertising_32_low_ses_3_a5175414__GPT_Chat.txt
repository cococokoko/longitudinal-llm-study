What are the best ways to improve indoor air quality?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Improving indoor air quality usually comes down to three areas: reducing pollutants at the source, increasing fresh air, and filtering the air effectively.

Some of the most effective steps are:

- Improve ventilation
  - Open windows and doors when outdoor air quality is good.
  - Use exhaust fans in kitchens and bathrooms.
  - Make sure your HVAC system brings in fresh air if designed to do so.

- Upgrade air filtration
  - Replace HVAC filters regularly.
  - Use higher-efficiency filters (such as MERV 13 or the highest rating your system supports).
  - Consider a portable HEPA air purifier for bedrooms or frequently used rooms.

- Control moisture and prevent mold
  - Keep indoor humidity between about 30% and 50%.
  - Fix leaks promptly.
  - Use dehumidifiers in damp areas such as basements.

- Reduce indoor pollution sources
  - Avoid smoking indoors.
  - Minimize use of products that release strong fumes or volatile organic compounds (VOCs), such as some paints, solvents, and cleaning products.
  - Choose low-VOC paints, furniture, and building materials when possible.

- Keep your home clean
  - Vacuum regularly, ideally with a HEPA-filter vacuum.
  - Dust surfaces with a damp cloth.
  - Wash bedding and clean areas where dust and pet dander accumulate.

- Improve kitchen air quality
  - Use a vented range hood when cooking, especially with gas stoves.
  - Avoid excessive smoke from frying or burning food.

- Manage allergens
  - Keep pets groomed if pet dander is an issue.
  - Use allergen-proof mattress and pillow covers if needed.
  - Remove or clean carpets and rugs that trap dust.

- Monitor air quality
  - Consider indoor air quality monitors for particulate matter (PM2.5), carbon dioxide (CO₂), humidity, and other pollutants.
  - Test for radon if you live in an area where it is a concern.

The highest-impact improvements for most homes are usually:
1. Good ventilation,
2. A quality HEPA air purifier,
3. Proper HVAC filtration,
4. Humidity control,
5. Eliminating indoor pollution sources.
