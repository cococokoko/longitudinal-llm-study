── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How do I understand what I want?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Understanding what you want is often less about finding a hidden answer and more about noticing patterns in your experiences over time. Many people expect their desires to feel clear and certain, but they are often revealed through exploration rather than introspection alone.

A few approaches can help:

- Pay attention to energy.
  - What activities make you lose track of time?
  - What topics do you keep returning to, even when nobody asks you to?
  - What kinds of days leave you feeling satisfied rather than merely relieved?

- Notice envy and admiration.
  - When you envy someone, ask what exactly you envy.
  - Is it their freedom, recognition, creativity, relationships, financial security, influence, or something else?
  - Envy can sometimes point toward unmet desires.

- Separate your wants from expectations.
  - Ask yourself: "If nobody would judge me, what would I choose?"
  - Also ask: "If success were guaranteed, what would I pursue?"
  - These questions can reveal desires that get buried under fear or obligation.

- Look at your actions, not just your thoughts.
  - People often say they want one thing while repeatedly investing their time in another.
  - Your behavior can reveal priorities that your self-image overlooks.

- Experiment instead of deciding from a distance.
  - If you're unsure whether you want a new career, relationship, hobby, or lifestyle, try a small version of it.
  - Real experience tends to clarify desire much faster than endless analysis.

- Distinguish between wanting a feeling and wanting a thing.
  - Sometimes what we want is not "a promotion" but competence, status, security, or challenge.
  - Understanding the underlying feeling gives you more options for pursuing it.

- Imagine two futures.
  - One where you pursue the path you're considering.
  - One where you don't.
  - Which future brings more regret? Which brings more curiosity?

If you're feeling especially lost, it can help to answer three questions:
- What do I enjoy?
- What do I value?
- What am I willing to struggle for?

The overlap between those answers often contains important clues about what you truly want.
