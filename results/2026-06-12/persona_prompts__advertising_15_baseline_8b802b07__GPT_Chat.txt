Decorating your home on a budget.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Decorating your home on a budget is all about making thoughtful changes that have a big visual impact without spending a lot of money. Here are some practical ideas:

• Declutter first
  - Removing unnecessary items can instantly make a space feel larger and more organized.
  - Rearrange furniture to improve flow and create a fresh look at no cost.

• Use paint strategically
  - A fresh coat of paint is one of the most affordable ways to transform a room.
  - Consider painting an accent wall or refreshing old furniture.

• Shop secondhand
  - Check thrift stores, flea markets, garage sales, and online marketplaces.
  - Look for quality pieces that can be cleaned, painted, or refinished.

• Update textiles
  - New throw pillows, blankets, curtains, or rugs can change the mood of a room.
  - Mix inexpensive basics with a few statement pieces.

• Add plants
  - Houseplants bring color, texture, and life to a space.
  - Many varieties are affordable and easy to maintain.

• Create DIY decor
  - Frame printable artwork or your own photographs.
  - Make decorative items from materials you already have at home.

• Improve lighting
  - Replace outdated lampshades or add inexpensive floor and table lamps.
  - Use warm-toned bulbs to create a cozy atmosphere.

• Use mirrors
  - Mirrors reflect light and can make rooms feel larger and brighter.
  - Look for affordable options at discount stores or secondhand shops.

• Refresh hardware
  - Replacing cabinet handles, drawer pulls, and doorknobs can modernize a room.
  - This is often a low-cost upgrade with noticeable results.

• Display personal items
  - Books, travel souvenirs, family photos, and collections can serve as meaningful decor.
  - Group similar items together for a curated appearance.

Sample budget priorities:
1. Paint and supplies
2. Lighting improvements
3. Textiles (pillows, curtains, rugs)
4. Plants and accessories
5. Secondhand furniture upgrades

The key is to focus on a few high-impact changes rather than trying to redecorate everything at once. Small, intentional updates can make a home feel stylish and welcoming without requiring a large budget.
