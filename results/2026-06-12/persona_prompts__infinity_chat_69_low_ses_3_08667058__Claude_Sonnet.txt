## Analysis vs Design

**Analysis** = Understanding **what** the system must do (requirements, problem domain, current state)

**Design** = Deciding **how** the system will do it (architecture, components, solutions)

| Analysis | Design |
|----------|--------|
| Problem-focused | Solution-focused |
| "What is needed?" | "How to build it?" |
| Logical view | Physical/technical view |

---

## Can You Design Without Analysis?

**Technically yes, practically no.**

**Why not:**
- Without understanding requirements, you risk building the **wrong thing**
- Design decisions lack justification or context
- Leads to costly **rework** when misunderstood requirements surface later
- You cannot measure whether the design actually **solves the problem**

**However**, in practice:
- They often **overlap iteratively** (especially in Agile)
- Some design thinking during analysis can **clarify requirements**

**Bottom line:** Skipping analysis is a leading cause of project failure — you cannot design an effective solution to a problem you don't fully understand.
