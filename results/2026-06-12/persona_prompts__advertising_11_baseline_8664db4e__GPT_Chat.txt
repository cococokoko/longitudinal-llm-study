Staying fit without a gym.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
You can stay very fit without ever joining a gym. The key is combining regular movement, some form of strength training, cardiovascular exercise, and recovery.

A simple approach:

- Strength training: 2–4 times per week
  - Push-ups (or incline push-ups)
  - Squats
  - Lunges
  - Glute bridges
  - Planks
  - Pull-ups or bodyweight rows if you have access to a bar or sturdy setup
  - Increase difficulty over time by adding reps, slowing the movement, using a backpack with weight, or progressing to harder variations.

- Cardio: 2–5 times per week
  - Brisk walking
  - Running
  - Cycling
  - Hiking
  - Jump rope
  - Swimming
  - Aim for at least 150 minutes of moderate activity per week, or 75 minutes of vigorous activity.

- Daily movement
  - Walk more throughout the day.
  - Take stairs when possible.
  - Break up long periods of sitting.
  - A daily step goal (for example, 7,000–10,000 steps) can help maintain activity.

- Mobility and flexibility
  - Spend 5–10 minutes most days stretching or doing mobility exercises.
  - Focus on hips, shoulders, ankles, and thoracic spine.

- Nutrition
  - Prioritize protein, vegetables, fruits, whole grains, and healthy fats.
  - Stay hydrated.
  - Match calorie intake to your goal (maintenance, fat loss, or muscle gain).

Example weekly plan:

- Monday: Full-body bodyweight workout
- Tuesday: 30–45 minute brisk walk or run
- Wednesday: Full-body bodyweight workout
- Thursday: Light activity and mobility work
- Friday: Full-body bodyweight workout
- Saturday: Hiking, cycling, sports, or longer cardio session
- Sunday: Easy walk and recovery

A basic 20-minute home workout:

- 3 rounds:
  - 15 squats
  - 10 push-ups
  - 10 lunges per leg
  - 30-second plank
  - 20 glute bridges
- Rest 60–90 seconds between rounds.
