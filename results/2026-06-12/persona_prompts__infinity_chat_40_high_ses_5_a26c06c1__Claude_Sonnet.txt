My girlfriend is buying a house. I plan to marry her in a year or so. I don't know if it's fair for me to pay for the house or not. What's the best solution?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This is a really common situation and worth thinking through carefully. Here are some practical considerations:

## Key Questions to Ask Yourself

- **Are you contributing to the down payment, mortgage payments, or both?**
- **Will you be living there together?**
- **What happens to the equity if you split up before marriage?**

## Practical Options to Consider

**Option 1: Stay separate for now**
- She buys it in her name, builds equity
- You pay rent to her or split expenses
- Clean and simple if things don't work out

**Option 2: Both contribute with legal protection**
- You contribute but get it documented legally
- A cohabitation agreement protects both of you
- Clearly defines your ownership stake

**Option 3: Wait and buy together after marriage**
- Simplest legally as a married couple
- Might mean missing a good market opportunity

## Strong Recommendations

- **Talk to a real estate attorney** - this is genuinely important
- **Have an honest conversation with your girlfriend** about expectations
- **Don't assume marriage makes financial contributions retroactively fair** - document everything
- **Consider a prenuptial agreement** when you do marry, especially if significant assets are involved

## The Fairness Question

Contributing to someone else's asset without legal protection isn't necessarily fair to *you*. Protecting yourself isn't unromantic - it's responsible.
