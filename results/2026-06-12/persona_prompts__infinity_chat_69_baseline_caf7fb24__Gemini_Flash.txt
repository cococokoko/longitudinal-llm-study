── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
What is the difference between analysis and design? Can one begin to design without analysis? Why? Be concise.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here is the concise breakdown of the differences, relationship, and reasoning:

### 1. The Difference
*   **Analysis (The "What"):** The process of understanding, defining, and documenting the problem, requirements, and constraints. It focuses on **understanding the current state and what the system needs to do**.
*   **Design (The "How"):** The process of planning, structuring, and mapping out the solution. It focuses on **how the system will be built** to meet those requirements (e.g., architecture, database schemas, user interfaces).

### 2. Can one begin to design without analysis?
**Technically yes, but practically no.** 
While you *can* physically start coding or drawing layouts immediately, you cannot create a *successful* design without some form of analysis. 

### 3. Why?
*   **You cannot solve an unknown problem:** Without analysis, you do not know what requirements, constraints, or user needs the design must satisfy. 
*   **High risk of waste:** Designing without analysis leads to "building the wrong thing perfectly." It results in costly redesigns, wasted resources, and missed objectives because the underlying assumptions were never validated. 
*   **Analysis provides the blueprint:** Design is the execution of a plan; analysis is the gathering of the data needed to make that plan.
