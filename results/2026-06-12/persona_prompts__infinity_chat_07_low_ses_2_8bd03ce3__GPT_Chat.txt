── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Yes. As far as we understand, it is impossible for any person—and likely any finite system—to know everything.

There are several reasons:

- The universe may contain an enormous, possibly infinite, amount of information. If so, no finite mind could hold all of it.
- Knowledge keeps growing. New discoveries, events, and facts are constantly being generated.
- Some things may be fundamentally unknowable. For example, in physics there are limits on what can be measured simultaneously, and in mathematics there are true statements that cannot be proven within certain formal systems.
- Any knower has limits: time, memory, attention, and processing capacity.

There's also a philosophical distinction:
- "Knowing everything" could mean knowing every true fact.
- It could also mean having complete understanding of why every fact is true.

Both goals appear beyond reach.

That said, humans don't need complete knowledge to understand a great deal. Science, education, and experience allow us to build increasingly accurate models of the world, even if total knowledge remains unattainable.
