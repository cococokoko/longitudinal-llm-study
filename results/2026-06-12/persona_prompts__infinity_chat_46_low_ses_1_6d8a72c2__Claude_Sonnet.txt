## Example of a Linear Graph (Path Graph)

A simple example of a **linear graph** (also called a **path graph**) is:

```
P₄: A — B — C — D
```

### Properties of this graph:
| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Vertices** | A, B, C, D (4 vertices) |
| **Edges** | AB, BC, CD (3 edges) |
| **Degree sequence** | 1, 2, 2, 1 |

### Key Characteristics:
- **Endpoints** (A and D) have degree **1**
- **Interior vertices** (B and C) have degree **2**
- Each vertex connects to the **next in sequence**
- No cycles or branches

### General Form:
A path graph **Pₙ** has:
- **n** vertices
- **n − 1** edges
- Exactly **two vertices of degree 1** (the endpoints)

This is one of the most fundamental structures in graph theory, representing a simple chain of connected nodes.
