**TITLE: THE END OF THE WORLD (AND OTHER BUDGET CUTS)**

**CHARACTERS:**

*   **DILBERT (40s):** An engineer wearing a tattered, radiation-singed dress shirt and a tie that permanently curves upward. He is exhausted, cynical, and trying to manage a household of ten extended family members in a fallout shelter.
*   **THE POINTY-HAIRED BOSS (PHB):** Now the "Warlord" of the sector. He wears a skull mask, but his hair tufts still stick out. He has no idea how the apocalypse works.
*   **WALLY (40s):** Dilbert’s coworker, currently hoarding the shelter’s only working water purifier to avoid doing chores.

**[SCENE START]**

**INT. FALLOUT SHELTER - DAY**

The shelter is a cramped concrete bunker. Pipes leak glowing green sludge. In the corner, a massive pile of laundry sits next to a makeshift hydroponic garden. 

DILBERT sits at a desk made of rusted oil drums, typing on a laptop powered by a hand-crank generator. 

WALLY sits nearby in a lawn chair, holding a mug of lukewarm, recycled gray-water. He is not working.

DILBERT
(Without looking up)
Wally, did you fix the air scrubber? My mother, three uncles, and my second cousin twice-removed are currently sharing one oxygen mask in the pantry.

WALLY
I’m pacing myself, Dilbert. If I fix the scrubber today, the family will expect me to fix the waste-reclamation unit tomorrow. By maintaining a baseline of total uselessness, I protect my mental health.

DILBERT
The waste-reclamation unit exploded two hours ago. We are currently breathing 40% carbon dioxide and 10% of my Uncle Ted’s body odor.

WALLY
(Sips his water)
I find that if I don't breathe too deeply, it’s actually quite relaxing.

*Enter THE POINTY-HAIRED BOSS (PHB). He wears a leather vest with spikes, but still has his corporate ID badge clipped to it. He carries a clipboard.*

PHB
Good morning, team. As you know, the nuclear winter has entered its third year, and our quarterly survival metrics are down. 

DILBERT
That’s because the sky is raining acid and we’ve eaten all the leather shoes.

PHB
I don’t want excuses, Dilbert. I want synergy. I’ve decided to restructure the shelter. To save on heating costs, we are merging our bunker with the mutant cannibal clan next door.

DILBERT
(Stares)
You’re merging us with the people who wear human skulls as hats.

PHB
They prefer to be called "External Stakeholders." They’ve agreed to a hostile takeover of our pantry. In exchange, they’ve promised not to eat us until Q4.

DILBERT
How is that a benefit to us?

PHB
It frees up our storage space. Plus, it aligns with our new mission statement: "To be eaten by a more agile competitor."

WALLY
Will the cannibals require me to do any work?

PHB
No, they actually prefer their meat sedentary. It keeps the muscle fibers tender.

WALLY
I support this merger.

DILBERT
(Rubbing his temples)
I can’t deal with this. I have to manage the chore wheel. My sister-in-law refuses to scavenge for canned peaches because she says the radiation ruins her complexion, and my grandfather keeps trying to use the Geiger counter as a TV remote.

*DOGBERT enters. He is wearing a golden crown and a cape made of tinfoil. Two giant, glowing mutant rats follow him, carrying a sedan chair.*

DOGBERT
Greetings, peasants. 

DILBERT
Dogbert, please tell your cult members to stop leaving charred offerings on the welcome mat. It’s attracting the giant scorpions, and I’m the one who has to sweep them up.

DOGBERT
The Great Dogbert does not concern himself with the maintenance of the temple. The mutants have decreed that tomorrow is "Sacrifice an Engineer" day. I suggest you wear your good tie.

DILBERT
Can we reschedule? I have to explain to my aunt why we can’t stream her soap operas using a potato battery.

DOGBERT
No. The calendar is booked. But on the bright side, your sacrifice will be tax-deductible for the survivors.

PHB
(Nodding)
Excellent. Dilbert, I’m going to need you to write a PowerPoint presentation on the benefits of your upcoming sacrifice before you get eaten. Keep it to three slides.

DILBERT
Who would I even present it to?

PHB
The cannibals. They love bullet points. It helps them digest.

WALLY
(To Dilbert)
Can I have your desk when you’re dead? It’s closer to the air vent.

DILBERT
The air vent that’s currently spewing radioactive ash?

WALLY
It’s a dry heat.

*Dilbert sighs, turns back to his laptop, and begins typing.*

DILBERT
I’ll put the PowerPoint in the shared drive. Assuming the server doesn’t melt from the acid rain.

PHB
That’s the spirit! I love a team player. I’ll be in my office, trying to figure out how to monetize the apocalypse.

*PHB exits. Dogbert snaps his paws, and his mutant rats carry him out.*

WALLY
You know, Dilbert, if you play your cards right, you can use your sacrifice to get out of doing the dishes tonight.

DILBERT
(Staring blankly ahead)
That is literally the first good news I’ve heard all year.
