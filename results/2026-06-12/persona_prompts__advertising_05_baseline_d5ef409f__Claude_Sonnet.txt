# Healthy Meal Planning on a Budget

## Core Principles

- **Plan before you shop** — reduces impulse buying and waste
- **Cook at home** — restaurant meals cost 3-5x more
- **Buy ingredients, not convenience foods** — pay for simplicity, not packaging
- **Embrace repetition** — rotating a few meals saves money and time

---

## Budget-Friendly Nutritious Foods

### Proteins
- Eggs
- Canned tuna/salmon
- Dried or canned beans/lentils
- Chicken thighs (cheaper than breasts)
- Tofu

### Grains & Starches
- Oats
- Brown rice
- Whole wheat pasta
- Potatoes/sweet potatoes

### Produce
- Frozen vegetables (equally nutritious, cheaper)
- Seasonal fresh produce
- Bananas, apples, cabbage, carrots

---

## Practical Strategies

### Shopping
- Make a weekly list and **stick to it**
- Compare unit prices, not package prices
- Buy store/generic brands
- Shop sales and plan meals around them

### Cooking
- **Batch cook** on weekends
- Use cheaper cuts with slow cooking
- Repurpose leftovers creatively
- Learn a few **versatile base recipes**

---

---

## Money-Saving Tips

- **Freeze bread, meat, and bananas** before they go bad
- **Grow herbs** at home (basil, parsley save money)
- Eat **less meat** — even 2-3 meatless days saves significantly
- Buy **whole foods** and prep yourself (block cheese vs. shredded, whole chicken vs. pieces)
- Use **every part** — vegetable scraps for broth, chicken bones for stock

---

## Quick Budget Meal Ideas

- **Rice and beans** — complete protein, very cheap
- **Vegetable frittata** — uses leftover veggies
- **Lentil soup** — filling, nutritious, ~$1/serving
- **Oatmeal** — customize with fruit, nuts, honey
- **Stir-fry** — flexible, fast, uses any vegetables

---

## Realistic Weekly Routine

1. **Sunday** — plan meals, make shopping list
2. **Sunday/Monday** — shop once
3. **Sunday** — batch cook grains, roast vegetables
4. **Throughout week** — assemble meals quickly
