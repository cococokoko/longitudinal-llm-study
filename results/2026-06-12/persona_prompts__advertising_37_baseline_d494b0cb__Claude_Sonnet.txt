── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How can I optimize my home office for productivity?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here are some practical ways to optimize your home office for productivity:

## Physical Setup
- **Ergonomic chair and desk** at proper height to reduce fatigue
- **Monitor at eye level** to prevent neck strain
- **Good lighting** - natural light when possible, plus task lighting
- **Minimize clutter** - keep only what you regularly use on your desk

## Environment
- **Control noise** - use headphones, white noise, or soundproofing if needed
- **Maintain comfortable temperature** (most people focus best around 70-77°F)
- **Separate space** from living areas when possible to create mental boundaries

## Digital Organization
- **Dedicated browser profiles** for work vs. personal
- **Organize files consistently** so you're not searching constantly
- **Use a task manager** to track priorities

## Routine & Habits
- **Set consistent work hours** and stick to them
- **Take scheduled breaks** (Pomodoro technique works well)
- **Have a startup/shutdown ritual** to signal work mode

## Common Pitfalls to Avoid
- Working from bed or couch long-term
- Leaving notifications constantly on
- Skipping breaks thinking it saves time

## Worth Considering
The biggest gains usually come from **reducing interruptions** and **clear priorities** rather than perfect equipment.
