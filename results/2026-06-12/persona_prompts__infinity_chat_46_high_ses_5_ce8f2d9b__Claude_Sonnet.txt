## Example of a Linear Graph (Path Graph)

A simple example of a **linear graph** (also called a **path graph**) is:

**P₄** — a path with 4 vertices:

```
A — B — C — D
```

### Properties of this graph:
| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| Vertices | A, B, C, D |
| Edges | {A,B}, {B,C}, {C,D} |
| Number of vertices | 4 |
| Number of edges | 3 |

### Key Characteristics:
- **Each vertex** connects to at most **2 neighbors**
- **End vertices** (A and D) have **degree 1**
- **Interior vertices** (B and C) have **degree 2**
- The graph forms a single straight chain with **no cycles**

### Why it's called "linear":
The vertices can be arranged in a **sequence**, where each vertex is adjacent only to the next one in the line — resembling a linear structure.

---

This type of graph commonly models things like **network chains**, **pipeline systems**, or **sequential workflows** — which are relevant in many technology infrastructure contexts.
