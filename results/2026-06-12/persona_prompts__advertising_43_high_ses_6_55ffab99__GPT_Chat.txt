How to plan an environmentally friendly wedding?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Planning an environmentally friendly wedding involves making thoughtful choices that reduce waste, conserve resources, and support ethical suppliers. Here are some practical ways to do it:

• Choose a sustainable venue
  - Select a venue close to where most guests live to reduce travel emissions.
  - Consider outdoor venues, botanical gardens, community spaces, or venues with sustainability certifications.
  - Look for locations that use renewable energy, recycling programs, or water-saving practices.

• Keep the guest list intentional
  - A smaller wedding generally has a lower environmental footprint.
  - Focus on inviting the people who matter most to you.

• Use digital invitations
  - Send invitations and RSVPs electronically.
  - If printed invitations are important to you, choose recycled paper, plantable paper, or paper certified by sustainable forestry programs.

• Choose sustainable attire
  - Buy secondhand, vintage, rented, or ethically produced wedding attire.
  - Select outfits that can be worn again after the wedding.
  - Encourage the wedding party to wear clothing they already own or can reuse.

• Source local and seasonal food
  - Work with caterers that prioritize local, seasonal ingredients.
  - Consider plant-forward or vegetarian menu options, which often have a lower environmental impact.
  - Plan portions carefully to reduce food waste.
  - Arrange for leftover food donation where permitted by local regulations.

• Reduce single-use items
  - Use reusable dishes, glassware, linens, and decorations.
  - Avoid disposable plastics, including straws, cups, and cutlery.

• Choose eco-friendly flowers and decor
  - Buy locally grown, seasonal flowers.
  - Rent decorations instead of purchasing new items.
  - Use potted plants that can be replanted afterward.
  - Repurpose ceremony flowers for the reception.

• Think about transportation
  - Provide group transportation such as shuttle buses.
  - Encourage carpooling.
  - Offer information about public transportation options.

• Select sustainable wedding favors
  - Consider edible favors, charitable donations, seeds, small plants, or locally made products.
  - Skip favors altogether if they are unlikely to be used.

• Choose environmentally conscious vendors
  - Ask vendors about their sustainability practices.
  - Prioritize businesses that minimize waste, source responsibly, and reduce emissions.

• Manage waste thoughtfully
  - Set up clearly labeled recycling and composting stations.
  - Work with the venue to ensure materials are properly processed.
  - Donate flowers, decor, and unused supplies after the event.

• Consider your rings and jewelry
  - Choose recycled metals, vintage jewelry, or ethically sourced gemstones.
  - Ask jewelers about sourcing and certification standards.

• Offset unavoidable emissions
  - If guests must travel long distances, consider contributing to reputable carbon-offset projects.
  - Focus first on reducing emissions before offsetting them.

Sample priorities if you're trying to maximize impact:
1. Venue location and guest travel.
2. Food choices and food waste reduction.
3. Reusable decor and tableware.
4. Sustainable attire and rings.
5. Waste management and recycling.

A sustainable wedding does not have to be expensive. Many environmentally friendly choices—such as renting, buying secondhand, using digital invitations, and minimizing waste—can also reduce costs.
