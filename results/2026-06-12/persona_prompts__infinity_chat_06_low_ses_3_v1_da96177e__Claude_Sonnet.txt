── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I recently started a new job and am still on probation.

How do I understand what I want?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
That's a really thoughtful question, especially during a transition period. Here are some ways to think about it:

## Understanding What You Want at Work

**Pay attention to your reactions**
- Notice what energizes you vs. drains you during the day
- What tasks make time pass quickly?
- What do you dread or avoid?

**Ask yourself honest questions**
- What would you do differently if you had more control?
- What bothers you that others seem fine with?
- What are you proud of at the end of the day?

**Separate layers of "want"**
- Short-term comfort vs. long-term growth
- What you *think* you should want vs. what actually matters to you
- Security needs vs. fulfillment needs

## During Probation Specifically

- You're still gathering information - it's okay not to know yet
- Notice whether your discomfort is **adjustment** (normal) or **misalignment** (meaningful signal)
- Give yourself permission to observe before concluding

## Practical Steps

- Keep a simple journal of daily highs and lows
- Talk to trusted people outside the job
- Notice patterns over weeks, not just single days

**One key thing:** Probation can create pressure to just *perform* rather than reflect. Both matter.
