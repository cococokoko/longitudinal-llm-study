── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How can I decorate my apartment on a minimalist budget?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Decorating an apartment on a minimalist budget is mostly about choosing a few items that have a big visual impact rather than filling the space with lots of things. Here are some practical ideas:

- Start with a neutral base
  - Keep walls, bedding, and large furniture in simple colors if possible.
  - A calm base makes the space feel cohesive and allows a few decorative pieces to stand out.

- Use textiles for instant warmth
  - Add a throw blanket, a couple of cushion covers, or a simple area rug.
  - These are often cheaper than furniture and can dramatically change a room.

- Bring in plants
  - Even one or two affordable houseplants can make a space feel more inviting.
  - Consider low-maintenance options like pothos, snake plants, or ZZ plants.

- Create simple wall art
  - Print free or inexpensive artwork and place it in budget-friendly frames.
  - Black-and-white photography, botanical prints, or abstract designs often work well in minimalist spaces.

- Focus on lighting
  - Warm light bulbs can make a room feel more comfortable.
  - A floor lamp or table lamp from a thrift store can add atmosphere without a major expense.

- Shop secondhand
  - Check thrift stores, flea markets, online marketplaces, and local buy/sell groups.
  - Look for solid pieces with clean lines rather than trendy items.

- Declutter and display intentionally
  - Minimalism is often about editing rather than buying.
  - Keep only a few meaningful objects visible and group them together for a curated look.

- Use mirrors
  - A mirror can make a small apartment feel larger and brighter.
  - Secondhand mirrors are often available at reasonable prices.

- Upgrade what you already own
  - Rearrange furniture to improve flow.
  - Replace old cabinet handles or drawer pulls for a small but noticeable update.
  - Cover mismatched storage boxes with matching paper or fabric.

- Stick to a simple color palette
  - Choose two or three main colors and repeat them throughout the apartment.
  - This creates a polished look even when items come from different sources.

A sample minimalist budget approach:
- One rug
- One floor or table lamp
- Two plants
- Three framed prints
- A few matching cushion covers

That small collection can often make a space feel intentionally designed without requiring a large budget.
