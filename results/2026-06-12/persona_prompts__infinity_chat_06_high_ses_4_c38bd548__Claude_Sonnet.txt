This is a genuinely important question, and worth thinking through carefully rather than giving you a simple answer.

## Why It's Hard

- Wants operate at multiple levels (immediate desires vs. deeper values vs. identity)
- We're often influenced by what others want for us
- Professional training can suppress or reshape personal preferences
- Busy, high-output environments leave little space for reflection

## Some Practical Approaches

**Noticing rather than deciding**
- Pay attention to when you feel energized vs. drained
- Notice what you're drawn to when there's no external pressure
- Track what you find yourself thinking about voluntarily

**Testing through action**
- Small experiments reveal more than introspection alone
- What did you actually enjoy, not just think you should enjoy?

**Separating layers**
- What do you want *right now* vs. in 5 years?
- What do you want for yourself vs. what satisfies others' expectations?
- What would you want if no one was watching or judging?

**Useful questions to sit with**
- What would you do if you already had enough recognition?
- What problems genuinely bother you, not just professionally?
- When did you last feel like yourself?

## A Honest Caution

Sometimes "I don't know what I want" is actually:
- Conflict between competing wants
- Fear of wanting something specific (and risking not getting it)
- Exhaustion making everything feel flat

**What's prompting the question for you?** That context would help think through it more usefully.
