For a warehouse position that requires long hours on your feet, the decision comes down to one critical factor: **physical support and durability.** 

Based on your job requirements, the clear winner is the **used Adidas sneaker** (assuming they are in decent, wearable condition). 

Here is a detailed breakdown of why this is the right decision for your feet, your wallet, and your workday:

### 1. Cushioning and Support (The Most Important Factor)
*   **Adidas:** Adidas is an athletic brand that invests millions in footwear technology. Even a used pair of Adidas—especially if they feature **Boost, Bounce, or Cloudfoam** midsoles—will offer significantly better arch support, shock absorption, and heel cushioning than a fashion shoe. This is vital for preventing plantar fasciitis, lower back pain, and knee fatigue after an 8-to-12 hour shift on hard concrete.
*   **Zara:** Zara is a fast-fashion brand. Their sneakers are designed for aesthetics, not ergonomics. They typically have flat, hard, and thin insoles with very little shock absorption. Wearing Zara sneakers for a long warehouse shift will likely lead to sore feet and aching joints very quickly.

### 2. Durability and Build Quality
*   **Adidas:** Athletic shoes are built to withstand high impact, lateral movement, and heavy wear. The outsoles are made of durable rubber designed to grip surfaces (which also helps prevent slips on dusty warehouse floors).
*   **Zara:** Fast-fashion shoes are made cheaply to be worn occasionally. The synthetic leathers and glues used in Zara shoes are highly likely to crack, peel, or split at the sole under the daily stress of warehouse work (bending, lifting, pushing). 

### 3. Breathability
*   **Adidas:** Most Adidas sneakers utilize breathable mesh, Primeknit, or perforated leather, which helps keep your feet cool and dry, reducing the risk of blisters and odor.
*   **Zara:** Zara often uses cheap synthetic materials (polyurethane/faux leather) that do not breathe, leading to sweaty, uncomfortable feet during a long shift.

---

### What to check before buying the used Adidas:
Since they are used, do a quick quality check before buying:
1.  **Check the tread:** Make sure the rubber grip on the bottom isn't completely worn flat, as you need traction on warehouse floors.
2.  **Check the inner heel:** Ensure the fabric inside the heel isn't worn down to the plastic, which can cause blisters.
3.  **Sanitize them:** Spray the inside with a disinfectant spray (like Lysol) and let them air out before wearing.

### Pro-Tip for Warehouse Workers:
Whichever shoe you choose, consider buying a pair of **high-quality aftermarket insoles** (like Superfeet, Dr. Scholl’s Work Insoles, or FP Insoles). Putting a fresh, supportive insole into a used pair of Adidas will make them feel brand new and give you the ultimate setup for a pain-free shift.
