Tips for running a marathon.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Running a marathon is as much about preparation and pacing as it is about fitness. Here are some key tips:

Training
- Follow a structured training plan, ideally 12–20 weeks long, based on your experience level.
- Build mileage gradually. A common guideline is to avoid increasing weekly mileage by more than about 10% at a time.
- Include a weekly long run to build endurance.
- Schedule recovery days and easier weeks to reduce injury risk.
- Practice running at your intended marathon pace during some training runs.

Nutrition and Hydration
- Practice your race-day fueling strategy during training.
- Consume carbohydrates during long runs (often starting around 60 minutes into the run).
- Stay hydrated, but avoid overdrinking. Learn what works for your body.
- In the days before the race, focus on balanced meals with adequate carbohydrates.

Race-Day Pacing
- Start slower than you think you should. Many runners go out too fast and struggle later.
- Aim for an even pace or a slight negative split (running the second half slightly faster).
- Avoid making major pace adjustments based on adrenaline early in the race.

Gear
- Wear shoes and clothing you've already tested during training.
- Don't try new gear, foods, or supplements on race day.
- Consider anti-chafing products for areas prone to irritation.

Mental Strategy
- Break the race into smaller segments rather than focusing on all 26.2 miles (42.2 km) at once.
- Expect difficult moments and have a plan for handling them.
- Focus on maintaining effort and form rather than constantly checking the finish line distance.

Recovery
- After the race, walk for a few minutes if possible.
- Rehydrate and eat a meal or snack containing carbohydrates and protein.
- Give yourself time to recover before returning to hard training.

Common Mistakes to Avoid
- Starting too fast.
- Skipping recovery days.
- Ignoring minor injuries during training.
- Underfueling during long runs and the race.
- Changing your routine dramatically in the final week.

If you're training for a specific marathon, sharing your experience level and current weekly mileage can help tailor the advice.
