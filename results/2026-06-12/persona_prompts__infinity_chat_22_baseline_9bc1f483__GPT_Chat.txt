Different views on discipleship.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
“Discipleship” generally means the process of following, learning from, and becoming like a teacher or spiritual leader. Different Christian traditions and theological perspectives emphasize different aspects of discipleship.

Some major views include:

• Evangelical view
  - Discipleship is a personal relationship with Jesus Christ.
  - Emphasizes Bible study, prayer, obedience, evangelism, and spiritual growth.
  - Often focuses on mentoring relationships and small-group accountability.
  - Key idea: becoming more like Christ through faith and obedience.

• Roman Catholic view
  - Discipleship is lived within the Church and nourished through the sacraments.
  - Emphasizes participation in the Eucharist, prayer, service, and the teachings of the Church.
  - Sees discipleship as a lifelong journey toward holiness.
  - Key idea: following Christ through faith, sacramental life, and good works empowered by grace.

• Eastern Orthodox view
  - Emphasizes union with God (theosis), becoming transformed by divine grace.
  - Focuses on worship, prayer, fasting, spiritual disciplines, and participation in the sacraments.
  - Spiritual guidance from experienced mentors is often important.
  - Key idea: discipleship as spiritual transformation into Christlikeness.

• Mainline Protestant view
  - Often emphasizes both personal faith and social responsibility.
  - Encourages discipleship through worship, service, justice, and community life.
  - May place strong emphasis on ethical living and care for society.
  - Key idea: following Jesus in both personal devotion and public action.

• Anabaptist view
  - Stresses obedience to Jesus' teachings, especially the Sermon on the Mount.
  - Emphasizes community, simplicity, peacemaking, and nonviolence.
  - Discipleship is often understood as visible, practical obedience.
  - Key idea: faith demonstrated through a transformed way of life.

• Reformed view
  - Emphasizes God's sovereignty and the authority of Scripture.
  - Discipleship involves growing in holiness through God's grace.
  - Often highlights teaching, doctrine, and covenant community.
  - Key idea: lifelong sanctification under God's Word.

• Wesleyan/Methodist view
  - Focuses on spiritual growth and sanctification.
  - Encourages personal and social holiness.
  - Historically emphasizes accountability groups and disciplined Christian living.
  - Key idea: growing in love for God and neighbor.

Contemporary perspectives on discipleship:

• Relational discipleship
  - Growth occurs primarily through close relationships and mentoring.

• Missional discipleship
  - Following Jesus is inseparable from participating in God's mission in the world.

• Spiritual formation approach
  - Emphasizes practices such as prayer, silence, fasting, and reflection to shape character.

• Social justice approach
  - Focuses on following Jesus through advocacy for the poor, marginalized, and oppressed.

Despite these differences, most Christian traditions agree that discipleship involves learning from Jesus, growing in faith, and living in a way that reflects his teachings.
