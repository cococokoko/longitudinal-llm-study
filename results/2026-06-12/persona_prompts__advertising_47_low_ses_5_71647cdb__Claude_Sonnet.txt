# Tips for Running a Marathon

## Training
- **Follow a structured plan** - typically 16-20 weeks of preparation
- **Build mileage gradually** - increase weekly distance by no more than 10%
- **Include long runs** - build up to 20-22 miles before race day
- **Rest and recovery days** are essential, not optional

## Nutrition & Hydration
- Practice fueling during long training runs
- Use **energy gels or chews** every 45-60 minutes during the race
- **Hydrate consistently** - don't wait until you're thirsty
- Carb-load 2-3 days before the race

## Gear
- Invest in **properly fitted running shoes**
- Wear moisture-wicking clothing
- **Break in your gear** before race day - never wear anything new
- Consider anti-chafe products for long distances

## Race Day Strategy
- **Start slower than you think you should** - avoid going out too fast
- Run your own pace, not others'
- Break the race into smaller segments mentally
- Know the course and aid station locations

## Mental Preparation
- Practice positive self-talk
- Have a **mantra** ready for tough miles
- Expect difficult patches around miles 18-22
- Celebrate small milestones during the race

## Recovery
- Plan for rest after the race
- Expect soreness for several days
- Gradually return to running
