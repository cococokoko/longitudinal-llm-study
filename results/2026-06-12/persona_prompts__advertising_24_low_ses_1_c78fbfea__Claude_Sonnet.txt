── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here are some practical retirement saving strategies, including options that work for people with variable income:

## Start with the Basics
- **Emergency fund first** - having 1-3 months of expenses saved prevents raiding retirement savings
- **Save consistently**, even small amounts add up over time
- **Take advantage of compound interest** - starting early matters a lot

## Account Options
- **401(k)** - if your employer offers one, especially with matching contributions
- **IRA (Individual Retirement Account)** - Roth or Traditional, available to anyone with earned income
- **Roth IRA** - often good for lower income earners since contributions grow tax-free

## Practical Tips for Variable Income
- **Save a percentage rather than a fixed amount** - easier to manage with unpredictable paychecks
- **Automate savings** when possible, even small amounts
- **Save more during good weeks**, less during slow ones
- **Set up automatic transfers** on payday

## Simple Starting Points
- Even **$25-50 per month** makes a difference over time
- Many brokerages have **no minimum** to open an IRA
- Apps like **Acorns** can round up purchases to invest spare change

## Key Principle
**Something is always better than nothing** - don't let perfect be the enemy of good.
