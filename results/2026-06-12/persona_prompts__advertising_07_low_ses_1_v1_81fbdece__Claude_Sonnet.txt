# Reducing Household Energy Use with a Busy, Unpredictable Schedule

Here are practical strategies that work well when you're not home on a consistent schedule:

## Biggest Impact Changes

**Heating & Cooling**
- Get a smart or programmable thermostat - it adjusts automatically even when your schedule changes
- Set temperatures lower/higher when away (68°F heating, 78°F cooling when home is a common starting point)
- Use fans instead of AC when possible

**Lighting**
- Switch to LED bulbs - they use ~75% less energy and you don't have to think about them
- Use power strips to easily cut power to multiple devices at once

## Easy Daily Habits

- Unplug chargers and electronics when not in use (they draw power even off)
- Run dishwasher and laundry on **cold water** and **full loads only**
- Air dry dishes and clothes when possible

## Low/No Cost Fixes

- Seal drafts around doors and windows with weatherstripping
- Keep refrigerator coils clean
- Use microwave instead of oven when possible

## Realistic Priorities

Given your schedule, focus on **set-it-and-forget-it solutions** first rather than habits requiring daily attention.

**Biggest savings typically come from:** heating/cooling → water heating → appliances
