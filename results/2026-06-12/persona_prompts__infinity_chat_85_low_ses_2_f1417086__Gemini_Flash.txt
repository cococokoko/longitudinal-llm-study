Here are a few options, depending on the specific angle and tone of your essay. 

As a parent, I often think about how we try so hard to protect our kids in the physical world, only to hand them a device that bypasses all our defenses. 

Here are some powerful rhetorical questions you can use, categorized by the specific harm you want to focus on:

### 1. Focus on Mental Health & Self-Esteem
*   "Are we raising a generation that measures its self-worth in likes, views, and algorithms, rather than character, passion, and real-world connection?"
*   "How can we expect our teenagers to build a stable sense of self when they are constantly viewing their lives through the distorted, curated lens of someone else’s highlight reel?"

### 2. Focus on the Tech Companies (Accountability)
*   "When did we decide that the profit margins of Silicon Valley tech giants were worth more than the mental health and well-being of our children?"
*   "If any other product on the market caused this level of anxiety, depression, and sleep deprivation in our youth, wouldn't we have recalled it by now?"

### 3. Focus on Isolation & Lost Childhood
*   "In our rush to connect the world, have we accidentally isolated our youth behind glowing screens, leaving them more connected yet lonelier than any generation before them?"
*   "Are we willing to stand by and watch as the vibrant, messy, and essential experiences of a real childhood are replaced by a sterile, digital simulation?"

### 4. Short & Punchy (Great for an Introduction or Conclusion)
*   "We shield our children from the dangers of the physical world, but who is protecting them from the dangers in their pockets?"
*   "At what point does 'staying connected' turn into being trapped?"

**Tip for your essay:** Place this question either at the very end of your introduction (as a hook leading into your thesis) or at the beginning of your conclusion to leave a lasting impression on the reader.
