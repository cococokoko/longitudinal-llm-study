Since you've recently started a new job and are still on probation, it makes sense to focus on low-cost changes that make a big visual impact without committing to expensive purchases.

Some budget-friendly ways to decorate a minimalist apartment:

- Start with decluttering
  - Minimalist spaces look best when surfaces are clear and only a few items are displayed.
  - Store or donate things you don't use regularly.

- Use a limited color palette
  - Pick 2–3 main colors (for example: white, beige, black, or gray with one accent color).
  - Consistent colors make a space feel intentional and cohesive.

- Add inexpensive textiles
  - A simple area rug, throw blanket, or cushion covers can transform a room.
  - Check discount stores, thrift shops, or online marketplaces.

- Bring in plants
  - A few low-maintenance plants can add warmth and life.
  - Snake plants, pothos, and ZZ plants are often affordable and easy to care for.

- Improve lighting
  - Warm LED bulbs can make a space feel more inviting.
  - A secondhand floor lamp or table lamp can create a cozy atmosphere without much expense.

- Create simple wall art
  - Print free or inexpensive artwork and place it in basic frames.
  - Black-and-white photography and abstract prints work well with minimalist styles.

- Rearrange furniture before buying anything
  - Sometimes a better layout makes a room feel larger and more polished at no cost.

- Use mirrors strategically
  - A mirror can make a small apartment feel brighter and more spacious.
  - Look for secondhand options.

- Focus on one statement piece per room
  - Instead of many decorative items, choose one attractive feature such as a plant, lamp, artwork, or chair.

- Shop secondhand
  - Facebook Marketplace, thrift stores, estate sales, and local buy/sell groups often have quality furniture and decor for a fraction of retail prices.

If you're trying to keep spending especially low during your probation period, consider setting a decorating budget of a fixed amount per month and upgrading the apartment gradually rather than all at once. This helps you create a comfortable space while maintaining financial flexibility.
