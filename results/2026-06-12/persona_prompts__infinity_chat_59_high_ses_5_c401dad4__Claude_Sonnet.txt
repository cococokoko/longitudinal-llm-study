Here's a practical tip for teachers:

## Use a Weekly "Brain Dump" on Sunday Evening

Take 10-15 minutes before the week starts to:

- **Write down everything** on your mind - assignments to grade, emails to send, copies to make, parent contacts needed
- **Prioritize 3 must-do tasks** for the week
- **Block specific times** for grading so it doesn't pile up

**Why this works for teachers specifically:**
- Your days are unpredictable once students arrive
- Having a clear plan *before* chaos hits keeps you in control
- It reduces that mental load of trying to remember everything during class

**Bonus:** Keep a small notepad on your desk during the day to capture things that come up, then add them to next week's list instead of trying to handle everything immediately.

This simple habit can help you feel less reactive and more in control of your workload.
