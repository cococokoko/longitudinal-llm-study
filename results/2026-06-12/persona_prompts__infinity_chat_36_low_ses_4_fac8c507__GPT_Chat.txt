What is the best and most profitable day trading strategy of all time?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
There is no single "best" or "most profitable" day trading strategy of all time.

If such a strategy existed and remained consistently profitable, widespread use would tend to reduce or eliminate its edge over time. Markets change, and strategies that work well in one environment can fail in another.

That said, some of the most successful and enduring day-trading approaches share common characteristics:

- Trend-following
  - Buy assets showing strong upward momentum and sell those in strong downtrends.
  - Often uses moving averages, breakouts, or price action.
  - Can produce large winners but often has a lower win rate.

- Momentum trading
  - Focuses on stocks or assets experiencing unusually high volume and volatility.
  - Traders attempt to capture short-term continuation moves.
  - Common among active retail day traders.

- Opening range breakout
  - Uses the high and low established during the first few minutes of trading.
  - Trades breakouts above or below that range.
  - Popular because early-session volatility creates opportunities.

- Mean reversion
  - Assumes price moves that are too far from average levels will revert.
  - Often uses indicators such as VWAP, Bollinger Bands, or statistical measures.
  - Can work well in range-bound markets but may suffer during strong trends.

- Market making and statistical arbitrage
  - Historically among the most profitable approaches for professional firms.
  - Relies on speed, technology, and quantitative models.
  - Generally inaccessible to most retail traders.

For individual traders, the biggest determinants of long-term profitability are often:
- Risk management
- Position sizing
- Transaction costs
- Discipline and consistency
- Ability to adapt to changing market conditions

If the goal is maximum historical profitability, high-frequency trading and quantitative market-making firms have likely generated some of the largest trading profits in aggregate. However, these strategies require substantial capital, infrastructure, and expertise.
