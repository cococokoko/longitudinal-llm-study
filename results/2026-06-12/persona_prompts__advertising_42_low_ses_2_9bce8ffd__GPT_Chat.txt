The best app depends on whether you want workout tracking, nutrition tracking, or an all-in-one solution. If you're balancing work and childcare, apps with quick logging and good automation can save time.

Fitness + Nutrition (all-in-one)
- MyFitnessPal

  - Calorie and macro tracking
  - Integrates with many fitness devices and apps
  - Good if nutrition is your main focus

- Cronometer
  - Tracks calories, macros, and micronutrients in detail
  - Accurate food database
  - Useful if you care about nutrient quality, not just calories

- Fitbit
  - Activity, sleep, heart rate, and food logging
  - Works best with a Fitbit device but can be used without one
  - Strong health overview in a single app

Workout Tracking
- Strong
  - Excellent for strength training
  - Easy workout logging and progress tracking
  - Clean interface with minimal setup

- Hevy
  - Popular strength-training tracker
  - Visual progress charts
  - Includes a social component if you enjoy sharing workouts

- Nike Training Club
  - Free guided workouts
  - Wide range of workout lengths, including short sessions
  - Helpful if you need flexible workouts around a busy schedule

Running and Cardio
- Strava
  - Tracks running, cycling, and other activities
  - Strong community features
  - Excellent performance tracking

- Runna
  - Personalized running plans
  - Good for training toward specific race goals

Health and Habit Tracking
- Apple Health (iPhone)
  - Central hub for health and fitness data
  - Integrates with many apps

- Google Fit (Android)
  - Simple activity tracking
  - Works with numerous fitness apps and wearables
