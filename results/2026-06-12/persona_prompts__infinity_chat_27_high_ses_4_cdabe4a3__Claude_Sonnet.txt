### A Play in Three Acts
*In the Style of Scott Adams' Dilbert, Set During the Apocalypse*

---

## ACT ONE: THE ALL-HANDS MEETING AT THE END OF THE WORLD

*SETTING: A collapsed office building in a scorched wasteland. Fluorescent lights flicker overhead, powered by unknown means. A whiteboard still reads "Q3 SYNERGY GOALS." Skeletal remains of motivational posters cling to crumbling walls. DILBERT sits at a desk made of rubble, wearing his signature tie, which now doubles as a bandage on his left arm. WALLY sits across from him, holding a coffee mug that somehow still steams.*

---

**DILBERT:** *(staring at a cracked laptop)* According to my calculations, civilization collapsed seventeen days ago.

**WALLY:** Hm. That explains the free parking.

**DILBERT:** Wally, the meteor strike destroyed forty percent of the northern hemisphere.

**WALLY:** And yet, somehow, the coffee machine survived.

**DILBERT:** That's because I built a protective bunker around it in 2019. I had a feeling.

**WALLY:** I always said you were the most prepared person in this office.

**DILBERT:** You said I was a "sad little man who needed to get out more."

**WALLY:** Both things can be true.

*(PHB enters, wearing a hard hat over his pointy hair, carrying a clipboard. His suit is on fire, slightly. He does not acknowledge this.)*

**PHB:** Good news, people. Leadership has decided to pivot.

**DILBERT:** Your jacket is on fire.

**PHB:** We're calling it "agile combustion." It's very cutting edge. Now. I've called this all-hands meeting because we need to address our post-apocalyptic roadmap.

**DILBERT:** There are four of us. Three, if you count Wally as a person, which I've stopped doing.

**WALLY:** *(nodding)* Reasonable.

**PHB:** I've been on a call with corporate—

**DILBERT:** Corporate was vaporized.

**PHB:** —and they want us to maintain our productivity metrics through the extinction event.

**DILBERT:** The extinction event is ongoing.

**PHB:** Which is why I'm asking everyone to work weekends.

*(Long pause. A distant explosion rumbles. Dust falls from the ceiling.)*

**DILBERT:** I want to be clear that I am experiencing a full range of human emotions right now.
