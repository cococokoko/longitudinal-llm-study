# Different Views on Discipleship

Discipleship — the process of following, learning from, and being formed by a teacher or tradition — is understood quite differently across theological, philosophical, and cultural contexts.

---

## Christian Perspectives

### Traditional/Catholic View
- Discipleship as **lifelong formation** in virtue and doctrine
- Mediated through the Church, sacraments, and spiritual direction
- Emphasis on **obedience** to teaching authority

### Protestant/Evangelical View
- Personal relationship with Christ as central
- **Bible study and accountability** as primary tools
- Often structured around intentional mentoring relationships
- "Multiplication" model — disciples making disciples

### Contemplative/Monastic View
- Formation through **silence, prayer, and community rule**
- Slow, deep transformation over years
- Associated with figures like Benedict, Thomas à Kempis

### Liberation Theology View
- Discipleship expressed through **solidarity with the poor**
- Following Jesus means **social and political engagement**
- Orthopraxis (right action) alongside orthodoxy

## Key Theological Debates

- **Cost vs. grace** — Bonhoeffer's "costly grace" vs. "cheap grace"
- **Individual vs. communal** formation
- **Imitation vs. transformation** — copying behavior vs. inner change
- **Formal vs. organic** — structured programs vs. relational apprenticeship
- **Evangelism vs. social justice** as discipleship expression

---

## Contemporary Discussions

- **Consumerism critique** — discipleship reduced to church attendance
- **Deconstruction movement** — questioning inherited models
- **Missional discipleship** — formation happening in everyday life contexts
- **Digital discipleship** — online community and formation
