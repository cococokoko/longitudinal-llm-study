Best skin routine for dry skin.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
For dry skin, the goal is to gently cleanse, replenish moisture, and protect the skin barrier.

Morning:
- Cleanser: Use a gentle, fragrance-free hydrating cleanser (or rinse with water if cleansing makes your skin feel tight).
- Moisturizer: Apply a rich moisturizer containing ingredients such as ceramides, glycerin, hyaluronic acid, squalane, or petrolatum.
- Sunscreen: Use a broad-spectrum SPF 30 or higher every day. Many moisturizing sunscreens work well for dry skin.

Evening:
- Cleanser: Use the same gentle cleanser to remove dirt, sunscreen, and makeup.
- Moisturizer: Apply a thicker moisturizer or cream while the skin is still slightly damp.
- Optional: If your skin is very dry, apply a thin layer of an occlusive product (such as petrolatum-based ointment) over particularly dry areas.

Helpful ingredients:
- Ceramides: Help repair the skin barrier.
- Glycerin: Draws water into the skin.
- Hyaluronic acid: Helps retain moisture.
- Urea (lower concentrations): Can improve hydration.
- Squalane: Lightweight moisturizing oil.
- Petrolatum: Excellent for preventing water loss.

Things to avoid:
- Harsh soaps and foaming cleansers that leave skin feeling tight.
- Excessive exfoliation (scrubs, frequent acids, or retinoids without proper moisturizing).
- Very hot showers or washing with hot water.
- Fragranced products if your skin is sensitive.

Example simple routine:
- Morning: Hydrating cleanser → moisturizer → SPF 30+
- Night: Hydrating cleanser → rich moisturizer

If your dry skin is accompanied by significant redness, itching, cracking, or a rash, conditions such as eczema, dermatitis, or psoriasis may be contributing, and evaluation by a dermatologist could be helpful.
