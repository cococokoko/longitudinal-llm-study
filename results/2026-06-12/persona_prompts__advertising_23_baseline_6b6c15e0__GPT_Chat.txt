── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How can I learn to cook healthy meals quickly?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Learning to cook healthy meals quickly is mostly about building a small set of skills, recipes, and habits rather than mastering complicated techniques.

Here’s a practical approach:

1. Learn 5–10 simple "core meals"
Start with recipes that take 15–30 minutes and use common ingredients. Examples:
- Stir-fry with vegetables and chicken, tofu, or shrimp
- Sheet-pan chicken and vegetables
- Omelets or frittatas with vegetables
- Grain bowls (rice, quinoa, or farro + protein + vegetables)
- Bean or lentil soups
- Tacos with fish, chicken, or beans
- Pasta with vegetables and lean protein

Once you can make a few meals without looking at a recipe, cooking becomes much faster.

2. Master a basic meal formula
Most healthy meals can be built from:
- Protein: chicken, fish, eggs, tofu, beans, lentils, Greek yogurt
- Vegetables: fresh or frozen
- Carbohydrates: rice, potatoes, whole-grain pasta, oats
- Healthy fats: olive oil, nuts, avocado

For example:
- Grilled chicken + roasted vegetables + rice
- Tofu + stir-fried vegetables + noodles
- Beans + sweet potato + salad

3. Use time-saving ingredients
Healthy cooking doesn't require everything to be made from scratch.
- Frozen vegetables
- Pre-washed greens
- Canned beans
- Rotisserie chicken
- Microwaveable brown rice or quinoa
- Pre-cut vegetables when needed

These can reduce preparation time significantly.

4. Learn a few key techniques
Focus on:
- Roasting vegetables
- Sautéing
- Stir-frying
- Cooking grains
- Baking fish or chicken

A handful of techniques can be used for hundreds of meals.

5. Meal prep strategically
You don't need to spend hours preparing food.
Try:
- Cooking extra protein for future meals
- Making a large batch of rice or grains
- Washing and chopping vegetables ahead of time
- Preparing ingredients for 2–3 days at a time

6. Follow beginner-friendly resources
Look for sources that emphasize quick, healthy meals:
- YouTube cooking channels for beginners
- Budget-focused healthy cooking websites
- Apps with 20–30 minute recipes
- Cookbooks centered on weeknight meals

Watch videos to learn techniques visually.

7. Build a simple pantry
Keep staples available:
- Olive oil
- Garlic and onions
- Salt and pepper
- Canned beans
- Whole grains
- Dried herbs and spices
- Canned tomatoes

A stocked pantry makes it easier to cook without extra shopping trips.

8. Practice consistently
Aim to cook:
- 2–3 meals per week at first
- Repeat recipes several times
- Add one new recipe each week

Repetition builds speed and confidence much faster than constantly trying new dishes.

If your goal is maximum efficiency, a good target is learning 7–10 healthy meals that each take under 30 minutes. That gives you enough variety for most weeks while keeping cooking simple and sustainable.
