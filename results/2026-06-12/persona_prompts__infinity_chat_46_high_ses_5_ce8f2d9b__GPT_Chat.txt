A common example of a linear graph in graph theory is a path graph.

For instance, the graph P4 consists of four vertices connected in a single line:

A — B — C — D

- Vertices: {A, B, C, D}
- Edges: {(A,B), (B,C), (C,D)}

Each internal vertex (B and C) has degree 2, and the two end vertices (A and D) have degree 1. This graph forms a simple chain, which is why it is often called a linear graph or path graph.
