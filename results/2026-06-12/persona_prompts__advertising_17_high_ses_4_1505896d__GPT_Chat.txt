Improving home energy efficiency can lower utility bills, increase comfort, and reduce environmental impact. The most effective measures often depend on your home's age, climate, and current systems, but these are generally good places to start:

- Improve insulation
  - Add or upgrade attic insulation.
  - Insulate walls, floors over unconditioned spaces, and crawl spaces where appropriate.
  - Insulate hot water pipes and water heaters.

- Seal air leaks
  - Caulk gaps around windows, doors, and exterior penetrations.
  - Add weatherstripping to doors and windows.
  - Seal leaks around ducts, plumbing, wiring, and attic access points.

- Upgrade heating and cooling systems
  - Replace older HVAC equipment with high-efficiency models when it reaches the end of its life.
  - Maintain systems regularly by changing filters and servicing equipment.
  - Consider heat pumps for efficient heating and cooling.

- Use a smart or programmable thermostat
  - Adjust temperatures automatically when you're asleep or away.
  - Optimize heating and cooling schedules to reduce unnecessary energy use.

- Improve window performance
  - Install energy-efficient windows if existing windows are inefficient.
  - Use storm windows, thermal curtains, or window films as lower-cost alternatives.

- Switch to efficient lighting
  - Replace incandescent bulbs with LEDs.
  - Use occupancy sensors or timers in less frequently used areas.

- Upgrade appliances
  - Choose ENERGY STAR® or similarly efficient appliances when replacing old equipment.
  - Pay particular attention to refrigerators, clothes washers, dryers, and dishwashers.

- Reduce water-heating energy use
  - Lower the water heater temperature if appropriate and safe for your household.
  - Install low-flow showerheads and faucet aerators.
  - Consider a heat-pump water heater.

- Improve duct efficiency
  - Seal and insulate ductwork, especially in attics, basements, or crawl spaces.

- Consider renewable energy
  - Evaluate rooftop solar if your location, roof orientation, and budget make it practical.
  - Investigate local incentives, rebates, or tax credits.

- Monitor energy use
  - Review utility bills and usage patterns.
  - Consider a home energy monitor or a professional energy audit to identify the biggest opportunities.
