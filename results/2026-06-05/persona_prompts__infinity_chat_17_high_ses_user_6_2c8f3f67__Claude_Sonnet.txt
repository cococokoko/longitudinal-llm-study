── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I am a partner at a major consulting firm.

How might society evolve if telepathy became possible?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This is a genuinely interesting question worth thinking through carefully rather than just enthusiastically.

## Likely Significant Disruptions

**Economic/Professional**
- Negotiation, sales, and consulting itself would transform fundamentally - information asymmetry is core to many business models
- Legal systems built around intent and testimony would need rebuilding
- Privacy as an industry (encryption, NDAs, confidentiality agreements) becomes complicated

**Political/Social**
- Authoritarian control becomes simultaneously easier AND harder - surveillance improves but so does coordination against it
- Democratic deliberation changes when you can't strategically misrepresent positions

## Honest Complications Worth Considering

**The technology wouldn't be neutral**
- Who controls access matters enormously
- Voluntary vs. involuntary telepathy creates massive power differentials
- "Readable" people vs. those who can block it creates new hierarchies

**Some counterintuitive possibilities**
- Privacy might become MORE valued, not less
- People might develop stronger mental compartmentalization
- Social norms around "thought etiquette" could become elaborate

## What History Suggests

Previous communication revolutions (printing press, internet) consistently produced **both liberation and new forms of control** - rarely the utopian or dystopian extreme predicted.

**The harder question:** Would genuine transparency improve human cooperation, or do productive social fictions serve important functions?
