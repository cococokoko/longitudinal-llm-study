How to make my own natural beauty products?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Making your own natural beauty products can be simple, affordable, and customizable. The key is to use safe ingredients, maintain good hygiene, and understand that "natural" does not automatically mean risk-free.

Basic guidelines:
- Use clean bowls, utensils, and containers.
- Make small batches, especially if products contain water.
- Label products with ingredients and the date made.
- Perform a patch test on a small area of skin before wider use.
- Avoid using ingredients that irritate your skin.
- Be cautious with essential oils; they can cause allergic reactions and should be used in low concentrations.

Easy DIY beauty products:

1. Simple Lip Balm
Ingredients:
- 1 tablespoon beeswax pellets
- 1 tablespoon coconut oil
- 1 tablespoon shea butter

Instructions:
- Melt ingredients together using a double boiler.
- Stir well.
- Pour into small containers and allow to cool.

2. Sugar Body Scrub
Ingredients:
- 1 cup sugar
- 1/2 cup coconut oil or sweet almond oil
- Optional: a few drops of vanilla extract or a skin-safe essential oil

Instructions:
- Mix until combined.
- Store in a sealed container.
- Use gently; avoid over-exfoliating.

3. Oatmeal Face Mask
Ingredients:
- 2 tablespoons finely ground oats
- 1 tablespoon plain yogurt or water
- 1 teaspoon honey

Instructions:
- Mix into a paste.
- Apply for 10–15 minutes.
- Rinse with lukewarm water.

4. Rosewater Facial Mist
Ingredients:
- Pure rosewater

Instructions:
- Pour into a clean spray bottle.
- Refrigerate if desired.
- Mist lightly onto the face.

5. Nourishing Body Oil
Ingredients:
- 2 tablespoons jojoba oil
- 2 tablespoons sweet almond oil
- Optional: 1–2 drops lavender essential oil

Instructions:
- Mix and store in a dark bottle.
- Apply to damp skin after bathing.

Storage and safety:
- Oil-only products can often last several months if stored properly.
- Products containing water, fresh fruit, yogurt, or herbal infusions spoil quickly and should be refrigerated and used within a few days unless preserved appropriately.
- If a product changes smell, color, or texture, discard it.
- Citrus essential oils can increase sun sensitivity.
