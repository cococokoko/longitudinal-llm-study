### 1. The "Free and Immediate" Fixes
*   **Relocate your router:** WiFi signals struggle to pass through thick walls, metal, and water. Move your router to a central location in your home, ideally elevated on a shelf. Keep it away from microwaves, cordless phones, and large metal appliances.
*   **Switch to the 5GHz band:** Most modern routers are "dual-band" (2.4GHz and 5GHz). The 2.4GHz band reaches further but is slower and easily congested. The 5GHz band is much faster. Connect your work computer to the 5GHz network, and move smart home devices (like smart plugs or TVs) to the 2.4GHz network to free up bandwidth.
*   **Change your WiFi channel:** If you live in an apartment or a dense neighborhood, your router might be on the same "channel" as your neighbors, causing interference. You can use a free app like WiFi Analyzer (Android) or Airport Utility (iOS) to find the least crowded channel, then log into your router's settings to switch to it.
