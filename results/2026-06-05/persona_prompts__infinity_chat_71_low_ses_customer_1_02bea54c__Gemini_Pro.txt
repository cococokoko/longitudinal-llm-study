── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
A very common optimization technique used in machine learning is called **Stochastic Gradient Descent (SGD)**. 

The word "stochastic" actually means random or unpredictable. In a way, it's a lot like managing a full-time job in fast food with a schedule that's always changing—you can't always predict what your shifts or the daily lunch rush will look like, so you just have to adapt on the fly! 

In machine learning, instead of trying to process a massive, overwhelming amount of data all at once, SGD looks at random, bite-sized pieces of data one at a time. It makes quick, continuous adjustments based on those unpredictable samples until it eventually finds the best, most optimal solution.
