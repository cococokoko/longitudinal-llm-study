── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I am a tenured professor at a prestigious university.

Creating a successful YouTube channel.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
As a tenured professor, you have several advantages for building a successful YouTube channel: subject-matter expertise, credibility, access to research, and experience explaining complex ideas. The challenge is translating academic knowledge into content that a broad audience wants to watch.

Key principles:

- Focus on a specific audience.
  - Undergraduate students?
  - Graduate students?
  - Professionals?
  - Curious general audiences?
  - Policymakers?

- Solve a clear problem.
  - People rarely subscribe because someone is knowledgeable.
  - They subscribe because the content consistently helps them learn, understand, or achieve something.

- Make content accessible.
  - Avoid excessive jargon.
  - Explain concepts through stories, examples, demonstrations, and visuals.
  - Assume viewers are intelligent but unfamiliar with the field.

Content formats that often work well:

- Explainer videos
  - "What everyone gets wrong about..."
  - "The science behind..."
  - "Why this research matters"

- Myth-busting
  - Address common misconceptions in your discipline.

- Research translation
  - Summarize important new papers in plain language.

- Lecture-style content
  - Shorter and more focused than a university lecture.
  - 8–20 minutes is often more effective than posting full class sessions.

- Commentary on current events
  - Connect your expertise to topics already receiving attention.

- Behind-the-scenes academia
  - Research process
  - Grant writing
  - Publishing
  - Academic careers

A simple production setup:

- Good microphone (often more important than camera quality).
- Decent lighting.
- Clean background.
- Clear slides, diagrams, or visuals.
- Consistent editing style.

Publishing strategy:

- Post consistently.
  - One high-quality video per week is often enough to start.

- Create compelling titles.
  - Focus on curiosity and value.
  - Example:
    - Less effective: "Lecture 12: Behavioral Economics"
    - More effective: "Why Smart People Make Irrational Decisions"

- Design strong thumbnails.
  - Simple, readable, visually clear.

- Study analytics.
  - Watch time and audience retention are especially important.

Common mistake professors make:

- Treating YouTube like a classroom.
  - Viewers can leave instantly.
  - The first 15–30 seconds should clearly explain why the video is worth watching.

Potential advantage for you:

- Many academic fields are underrepresented by authoritative voices on YouTube.
- A professor who can communicate clearly can build a substantial audience while also increasing public impact, attracting students, and extending the reach of their research.
