I am a senior attorney at a well-established law firm.

How can I decorate my apartment on a minimalist budget?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here are some practical tips for decorating your apartment on a minimalist budget:

## Core Principles First
- **Declutter before decorating** - removing items often improves a space more than adding things
- Focus on a few quality pieces rather than many cheap ones

## Budget-Friendly Strategies

**Shop Smart**
- Thrift stores and estate sales for unique pieces
- Facebook Marketplace and Craigslist for furniture
- IKEA for clean, functional basics
- End-of-season sales at home stores

**DIY Approaches**
- Repaint existing furniture
- Frame simple prints or personal photos
- Use plants as affordable, living decor

**Free or Nearly Free**
- Rearrange what you already own
- Borrow or swap items with friends
- Use books as decorative objects

## Minimalist-Specific Tips
- **Stick to a neutral color palette** - whites, grays, natural tones
- **Invest in good lighting** - makes a huge difference
- **Quality textiles** - one good rug or throw can anchor a room
- **Negative space is intentional** - empty space is part of the design

## Practical Priority Order
1. Seating and sleeping (function first)
2. Lighting
3. One or two statement pieces
4. Accessories last
