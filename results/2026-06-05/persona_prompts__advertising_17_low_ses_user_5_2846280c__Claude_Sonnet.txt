── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I support extended family members and manage many household responsibilities.

How can I make my home more energy efficient?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here are some practical ways to make your home more energy efficient, especially useful when managing a busy household:

## Quick, Low-Cost Changes
- Switch to LED bulbs throughout the home
- Use power strips to eliminate "phantom" energy drain from electronics
- Adjust thermostat settings (lower at night, when away)
- Seal gaps around doors and windows with weatherstripping

## Appliance & Equipment Improvements
- Run dishwashers and washing machines with full loads
- Wash clothes in cold water when possible
- Clean refrigerator coils regularly
- Replace old appliances with Energy Star certified models when they need replacing

## Bigger Investments Worth Considering
- Add insulation to attic and walls
- Install a programmable or smart thermostat
- Upgrade to energy-efficient windows
- Consider a tankless water heater

## Managing a Larger Household Specifically
- Set household routines around energy use (everyone showers during off-peak hours)
- Assign simple energy habits to family members
- Track your monthly bills to spot unusual increases
- Check if your utility company offers **free energy audits**

## Financial Help Available
- Federal tax credits for efficiency upgrades
- Many utilities offer rebates on appliances
- Some states have low-income weatherization programs

**Starting point I'd recommend:** Contact your utility company about a free home energy audit - it identifies your biggest savings opportunities specific to your home.
