What is a good secondhand market laptop for learning Python?

For learning Python, you do not need a powerful laptop. A good secondhand business-class laptop is often a better choice than a cheap consumer model because it will usually have better build quality, keyboard, and repairability.

Some excellent used options:

- Lenovo ThinkPad T480
  - Intel 8th-generation Core i5 or i7
  - Upgradeable RAM and storage
  - Excellent keyboard
  - Often available for $150–300 USD
  - One of the most commonly recommended used laptops for programming

- Lenovo ThinkPad X280
  - More compact and portable than the T480
  - Similar performance
  - Usually $150–300 USD

- Dell Latitude 5490 or 5491
  - Business-grade build quality
  - Easy to service
  - Usually $150–300 USD

- Dell Latitude 5400
  - 8th-generation Intel CPU
  - Good battery life
  - Often found around $200–350 USD

- HP EliteBook 840 G5
  - Solid keyboard and display
  - Professional build quality
  - Usually $200–350 USD

Minimum specifications I would look for:
- CPU: Intel Core i5 8th generation or newer, or AMD Ryzen 5
- RAM: 8 GB minimum, 16 GB preferred
- Storage: 256 GB SSD minimum
- Screen: 1920×1080 (Full HD) preferred
- Battery: Verify health if possible

Avoid:
- Intel Celeron or Pentium processors
- 4 GB RAM systems
- Mechanical hard drives (HDDs) as the main drive
- Very old Intel 6th-generation CPUs or older unless the price is extremely low

If your budget is under $200:
- ThinkPad T480 is usually the best value if you can find one.
- Latitude 5490 is another strong choice.

If you plan to use Linux, the ThinkPad T480 is particularly popular because Linux support is excellent and many programming tutorials assume a Linux-like environment.
