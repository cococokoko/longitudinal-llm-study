# Starting to Code from Scratch

Great question! Here's a practical approach:

## Choose Your First Language Wisely
- **Python** - Most recommended for beginners (readable, versatile)
- **JavaScript** - Good if you're interested in web development
- **Scratch** - Visual option if you want something very beginner-friendly

## Solid Learning Resources
- **Free:** freeCodeCamp, The Odin Project, CS50 (Harvard), Codecademy
- **Paid:** Udemy courses (often $10-15 on sale), Scrimba
- **Books:** "Automate the Boring Stuff with Python" (free online)

## Practical Tips That Actually Help

**Start small and consistent**
- 30 minutes daily beats 5-hour weekend sessions
- Build projects as soon as possible, even simple ones

**When you get stuck**
- Read error messages carefully
- Use Google and Stack Overflow freely
- This is normal, not a sign of failure

**Build things you care about**
- A simple game, a tool that solves your problem
- Personal interest keeps motivation up

## Honest Expectations
- The first few weeks feel confusing for almost everyone
- Progress becomes more noticeable around month 2-3
- Consistency matters more than raw talent
