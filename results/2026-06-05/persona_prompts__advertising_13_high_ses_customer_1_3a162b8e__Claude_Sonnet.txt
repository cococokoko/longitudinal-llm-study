# Mastering Steak Cookery

Given your background, you'll appreciate a systematic, evidence-based approach.

## The Fundamentals

### Selection
- **Grade matters**: USDA Prime > Choice > Select
- **Marbling** is the key predictor of flavor and tenderness
- **Thickness**: 1-1.5 inches minimum for proper crust-to-interior control

### The Science of Temperature
| Doneness | Internal Temp | Texture |
|----------|--------------|---------|
| Rare | 120-125°F | Cool red center |
| Medium Rare | 130-135°F | Warm red center |
| Medium | 140-145°F | Pink center |
| Well Done | 160°F+ | Not recommended |

## Key Techniques

**Dry Brining**
- Salt 24-48 hours ahead
- Draws moisture out, then reabsorbs - improves crust formation

**The Sear**
- Cast iron or carbon steel pan
- Screaming hot before the steak touches it
- Maillard reaction begins around 285°F

**Resting**
- 5-10 minutes post-cook
- Allows protein fiber relaxation and juice redistribution

## Common Methods
- **Reverse sear** - oven first, then sear (most control)
- **Traditional sear** - high heat throughout
- **Sous vide** - precise temperature control
