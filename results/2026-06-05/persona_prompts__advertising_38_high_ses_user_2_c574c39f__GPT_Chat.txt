── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I am a senior attorney at a well-established law firm.

What are the best platforms for freelance writers to find work?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
For experienced freelance writers, the best platform depends on the type of writing, target clients, and desired rates. Broadly, the strongest options today are:

• Upwork
  - Largest general freelance marketplace.
  - Strong demand for legal, business, technical, marketing, and content writing.
  - Writers can build long-term client relationships.
  - Competitive, especially for newer profiles, but experienced professionals often do well.
  - Generally considered the strongest mainstream platform for professional freelance writing.

• LinkedIn
  - Not a traditional freelance marketplace, but increasingly one of the best sources of high-value clients.
  - Companies often post contract writing opportunities directly.
  - Useful for thought leadership, networking, and inbound leads.
  - Particularly effective for writers with expertise in law, finance, healthcare, technology, or other specialized fields.

• Contra
  - Growing platform focused on independent professionals.
  - No commission fees charged to freelancers.
  - Strong for marketing, content, and business writing.
  - More curated and less crowded than some larger marketplaces.

• Contently
  - Premium platform connecting experienced writers with major brands.
  - Best for journalists, content marketers, and subject-matter experts.
  - Rates are often significantly higher than on mass-market platforms.
  - Acceptance is selective.

• ClearVoice
  - Content marketing platform used by businesses and agencies.
  - Writers create portfolios and can be matched with assignments.
  - Particularly useful for industry experts.

• Skyword
  - Enterprise content platform serving large corporate clients.
  - Strong opportunities for experienced writers with professional credentials.
  - Often focuses on specialized industries.

• Fiverr Pro
  - Standard Fiverr can be highly price-competitive, but Fiverr Pro targets vetted professionals.
  - Works best when writers can package services clearly (e.g., legal blog posts, white papers, website copy).

• WriterAccess
  - Content marketplace with tiered writer ratings.
  - Consistent workflow for content marketing writers.
  - Better suited to writers seeking recurring assignments.

For legal and professional subject-matter experts specifically, there are additional avenues that often outperform freelance marketplaces:

• Legal publishers (LexisNexis, Thomson Reuters, Wolters Kluwer, Bloomberg Law)
• Legal marketing agencies
• Law firm content agencies
• Bar association publications
• Trade journals and industry publications
• Direct outreach to law firms and legal technology companies

If your interest is specifically in freelance legal writing—such as articles, white papers, CLE materials, legal marketing content, or ghostwriting for attorneys—the most effective channels are often LinkedIn, direct networking, legal-industry publishers, and specialized agencies rather than general freelance platforms.
