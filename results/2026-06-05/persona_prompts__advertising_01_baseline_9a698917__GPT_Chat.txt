Learning guitar is a rewarding skill, and a good approach is to focus on a few fundamentals consistently rather than trying to learn everything at once.

A simple beginner roadmap:

- Learn the parts of the guitar and how to hold it comfortably.
- Practice basic open chords:
  - G
  - C
  - D
  - Em
  - Am
- Work on switching between chords smoothly.
- Learn a few basic strumming patterns.
- Practice keeping time with a metronome or backing track.
- Learn simple songs that use only a few chords.
- Begin basic picking exercises to improve finger coordination.
- Learn how to read chord charts and guitar tablature (tabs).

A sample 20–30 minute daily practice routine:
- 5 minutes: tuning and finger warm-ups
- 10 minutes: chord practice and chord changes
- 5 minutes: strumming exercises
- 10 minutes: learning or playing songs

Tips:
- Practice daily, even if only for 10–15 minutes.
- Play slowly at first; speed comes naturally later.
- Keep your fingertips close to the strings when changing chords.
- Expect some finger soreness in the beginning—it usually improves within a few weeks.
- Record yourself occasionally to track progress.
