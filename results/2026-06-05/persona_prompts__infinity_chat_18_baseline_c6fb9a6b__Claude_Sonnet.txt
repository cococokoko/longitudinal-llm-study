# Review: **"The Last Frequency"** (2024)

★★★★½

Director Mara Okonkwo delivers what may be the most quietly devastating science fiction film in years. *The Last Frequency* follows a radio astronomer who discovers that a signal she's been tracking for a decade is actually a recording of her own future — specifically, the last conversation she'll ever have with her estranged daughter.

What could have been a gimmicky premise becomes something genuinely aching in Okonkwo's hands. The film resists every obvious plot turn, choosing instead to sit with its characters in their uncertainty and regret.

Lead actress Priya Mehta gives a career-defining performance, communicating entire decades of complicated love through small gestures — the way she holds a coffee cup, the pause before she dials a phone number she knows by heart but hasn't used in years.

The film's final twenty minutes are nearly wordless, and somehow say everything.

**The verdict:** A rare film that uses the language of science fiction to ask a deeply human question — if you knew exactly how little time you had left to fix something, would that knowledge save you or paralyze you?
