── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Great question! Here are some practical ways to optimize your home office for productivity:

## Physical Setup
- **Ergonomic chair and desk** at proper height to reduce fatigue
- **Monitor at eye level** to prevent neck strain
- **Good lighting** - natural light when possible, plus task lighting
- **Minimize clutter** to reduce mental distraction

## Technology
- Reliable, fast internet connection
- Noise-canceling headphones for focus
- Dual monitors if your work involves multitasking
- Cable management to keep things tidy

## Environment
- **Temperature** around 68-72°F tends to support focus
- **Plants** can improve mood and air quality
- **Separate space** from living areas if possible
- Control noise with white noise machines or soundproofing

## Workflow Habits
- Set clear start and end times
- Use time-blocking techniques
- Take regular short breaks (Pomodoro technique works well)
- Keep a consistent daily routine

## Mental Boundaries
- Communicate availability to household members
- Have a "shutdown ritual" to end the workday
- Keep personal tasks out of work hours

## Quick Wins
- Keep water and snacks nearby
- Prepare your workspace the night before
- Remove your phone from your immediate reach during focus time
