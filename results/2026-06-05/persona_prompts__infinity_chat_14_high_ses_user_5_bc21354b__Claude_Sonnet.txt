## Real Documented Effects

**Concerning patterns with evidence:**
- Engagement optimization rewards outrage and emotional content
- Filter bubbles can narrow information exposure
- Misinformation can spread faster than corrections
- Attention fragmentation makes sustained reasoning harder

**But worth being honest about complexity:**
- People sought like-minded information before algorithms existed
- Some research shows algorithms expose people to *more* diverse content than social networks
- The relationship between online content and real-world polarization is genuinely contested

## What's Actually Driving Problems

The core tension is that **engagement ≠ quality discourse**. Your platforms likely optimize for time-on-site or interaction, which systematically selects for:
- Emotional provocation over accuracy
- Simplicity over nuance
- Conflict over resolution

## Executive-Level Honest Considerations

- Your company likely has internal research showing these effects more clearly than public research does
- "We didn't intend this" doesn't reduce responsibility for known outcomes
- Regulatory pressure is increasing regardless of your position
- There are genuine tradeoffs between business models and discourse quality
