── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How can I improve my credit score quickly?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here are some effective ways to improve your credit score:

## Highest Impact Actions
- **Pay down credit card balances** - lowering your credit utilization ratio (below 30%, ideally below 10%) can show results quickly
- **Pay all bills on time** - payment history is the biggest factor (~35% of your score)
- **Dispute errors on your credit report** - check all three bureaus (Equifax, Experian, TransUnion) at AnnualCreditReport.com

## Medium-Term Strategies
- Keep old accounts open (length of credit history matters)
- Avoid applying for multiple new credit accounts at once
- Become an authorized user on someone else's account with good history

## Things to Avoid
- Missing payments
- Maxing out credit cards
- Closing old accounts unnecessarily
- Applying for lots of new credit

## Realistic Timeline
- Some changes (like paying down balances) can reflect in **30-60 days**
- Building a strong history takes **several months to years**
- There are no legitimate overnight fixes

## Free Resources
- Check your free credit reports at **AnnualCreditReport.com**
- Many banks offer free credit score monitoring

**The most honest advice:** consistent on-time payments and low balances are the most reliable path.
