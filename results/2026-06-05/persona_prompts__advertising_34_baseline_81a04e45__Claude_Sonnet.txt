# Effective Online Teaching Techniques

## Engagement & Interaction
- **Break content into shorter segments** (10-15 minutes max for video lectures)
- Use polls, quizzes, and chat features regularly
- Ask questions throughout rather than just at the end
- Create discussion forums with clear prompts
- Use breakout rooms for small group work

## Clear Communication
- Set explicit expectations for participation and deadlines
- Provide detailed rubrics and examples
- Respond to students within a defined timeframe
- Use multiple formats (video, text, audio) for key concepts

## Active Learning Strategies
- Flipped classroom approaches
- Case studies and problem-solving activities
- Peer review assignments
- Project-based learning
- Real-world application tasks

## Technical Best Practices
- Keep recordings accessible and captioned
- Test technology before sessions
- Have backup plans for technical failures
- Use simple, reliable tools rather than complex ones

## Building Community
- Create informal spaces for student connection
- Use icebreakers and introductions
- Acknowledge student contributions publicly
- Hold regular virtual office hours

## Assessment
- Vary assessment types to reduce academic dishonesty
- Use frequent low-stakes checks rather than few high-stakes tests
- Provide timely, specific feedback

## What Research Suggests Works Best
- **Presence matters** - students perform better when instructors are visibly active
- **Consistency** in structure reduces cognitive load
- **Flexibility** with deadlines when possible improves completion rates
