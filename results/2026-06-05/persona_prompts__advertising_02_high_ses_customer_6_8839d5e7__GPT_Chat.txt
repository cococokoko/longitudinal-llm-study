Best skin routine for dry skin.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
For dry skin, the goal is to gently cleanse, replenish moisture, and protect the skin barrier. A simple, consistent routine is usually more effective than a complicated one.

Morning:
- Gentle cleanser (or just rinse with lukewarm water if your skin is very dry)
  - Look for fragrance-free, hydrating cleansers with ingredients like glycerin or ceramides.
- Moisturizer
  - Choose a cream rather than a lightweight lotion.
  - Helpful ingredients: ceramides, hyaluronic acid, glycerin, squalane, and shea butter.
- Sunscreen (SPF 30 or higher)
  - Daily sun protection helps prevent dryness, irritation, and premature aging.
  - Many moisturizing sunscreens work well for dry skin.

Evening:
- Gentle cleanser
  - Avoid harsh scrubs and strong foaming cleansers.
- Moisturizer
  - Apply while skin is still slightly damp.
- Optional: occlusive layer if very dry
  - A thin layer of petrolatum (e.g., petroleum jelly) or a rich balm can help lock in moisture overnight.

Useful additions:
- Hyaluronic acid serum
  - Apply to slightly damp skin before moisturizer.
- Ceramide-rich products
  - Particularly helpful if your skin barrier feels compromised.
- Gentle exfoliation
  - Once weekly at most, using a mild lactic acid or polyhydroxy acid (PHA) product if needed for flaking.
  - Avoid over-exfoliating.

Habits that help:
- Use lukewarm rather than hot water.
- Limit long, hot showers.
- Run a humidifier in dry environments.
- Avoid heavily fragranced products if your skin is sensitive.

If you also experience significant redness, itching, eczema, or persistent flaking despite moisturizing, it may be worth seeing a dermatologist, as treatment may differ from a standard dry-skin routine.
