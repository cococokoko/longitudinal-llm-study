# Best Practices for Organic Gardening

Here's a straightforward overview:

## Soil Health
- **Compost regularly** - builds nutrients and beneficial microorganisms
- **Test soil pH** - most vegetables prefer 6.0-7.0
- **Avoid tilling excessively** - preserves soil structure and earthworms
- **Use cover crops** - prevents erosion and adds nitrogen

## Pest & Weed Management
- **Companion planting** - e.g., basil near tomatoes, marigolds as borders
- **Hand-pick pests** when populations are small
- **Mulch heavily** - suppresses weeds and retains moisture
- **Encourage beneficial insects** - ladybugs, lacewings

## Watering
- Water deeply but **infrequently** to encourage deep roots
- **Morning watering** reduces fungal disease risk
- Drip irrigation is highly efficient

## Plant Selection
- Choose **disease-resistant varieties**
- Prioritize **native or regionally adapted** plants
- Rotate crops annually to prevent soil depletion

## Fertilizing
- Prioritize **compost and worm castings**
- Use fish emulsion or kelp for quick nutrient boosts
