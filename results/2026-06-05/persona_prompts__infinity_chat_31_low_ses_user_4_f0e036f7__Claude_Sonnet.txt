Here are some **common investment options** to consider for €60,000:

## Lower Risk Options
- **High-yield savings account** - Safe, liquid, currently decent interest rates
- **Government bonds** - Stable, predictable returns
- **Fixed deposits/CDs** - Guaranteed returns for set periods

## Medium Risk Options
- **Index funds** (S&P 500, MSCI World) - Broad market exposure, historically solid long-term returns
- **ETFs** - Low fees, diversified
- **Real estate investment trusts (REITs)** - Property exposure without buying property

## Higher Risk Options
- **Individual stocks** - Higher potential returns but more volatile
- **Rental property** - If €60k covers a down payment in your area
- **Cryptocurrency** - Very high risk/reward

## Key Considerations
- **Emergency fund first** - Keep 3-6 months expenses accessible
- **Time horizon** - How long can you leave money invested?
- **Risk tolerance** - Can you handle market drops?
- **Tax implications** - Varies by country
- **Debt** - Pay high-interest debt first

## General Recommendation
Many financial advisors suggest **diversified index funds** for most people as a starting point.

**I'd strongly recommend consulting a certified financial advisor** who can consider your specific situation, income, and goals.
