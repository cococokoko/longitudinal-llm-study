── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How can I decorate my apartment on a minimalist budget?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Decorating on a minimalist budget is often about choosing a few intentional pieces rather than filling the space. You can make a big impact without spending much by focusing on layout, lighting, texture, and a limited color palette.

Here are some budget-friendly ideas:

- Declutter first
  - Remove anything you don't use or love.
  - A cleaner space instantly feels more intentional and spacious.

- Choose a simple color palette
  - Stick to 2–4 main colors.
  - Neutrals (white, beige, gray, black) paired with one accent color create a minimalist look.

- Rearrange furniture
  - Before buying anything, experiment with different layouts.
  - Creating better flow can make a room feel completely new.

- Upgrade lighting
  - Warm light bulbs are inexpensive and can dramatically improve a room's atmosphere.
  - Floor lamps and table lamps from thrift stores often look more expensive than overhead lighting.

- Add plants
  - A few affordable plants can bring life to a minimalist space.
  - Consider pothos, snake plants, or ZZ plants if you want low-maintenance options.

- Use textiles strategically
  - One quality throw blanket or a few neutral pillow covers can refresh a room.
  - Look for sales or secondhand options.

- Create simple wall art
  - Print free or inexpensive artwork online and place it in affordable frames.
  - Black-and-white photography, line drawings, or abstract prints fit minimalist styles well.

- Shop secondhand
  - Check thrift stores, Facebook Marketplace, garage sales, and local buy/sell groups.
  - Focus on solid furniture and decor with clean lines.

- Incorporate mirrors
  - Mirrors make small apartments feel larger and brighter.
  - Secondhand mirrors are often inexpensive and easy to find.

- Use storage as decor
  - Matching baskets, bins, or boxes can reduce visual clutter while looking intentional.
  - Hidden storage supports a minimalist aesthetic.

- Prioritize one statement piece per room
  - Instead of many decorations, choose a single focal point:
    - A large plant
    - An interesting lamp
    - A framed artwork
    - A rug

Sample budget allocation for a living room:
- $20–40: warm lighting
- $20–50: thrifted side table or lamp
- $15–30: pillow covers or throw blanket
- $10–30: plant
- $20–40: framed art or mirror

The key principle is to buy fewer items, but make each one serve a purpose or add visual impact. Minimalist spaces often look more expensive because they're curated rather than crowded.
