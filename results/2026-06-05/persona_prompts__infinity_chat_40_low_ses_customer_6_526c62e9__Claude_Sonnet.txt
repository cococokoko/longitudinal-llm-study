── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
My girlfriend is buying a house. I plan to marry her in a year or so. I don't know if it's fair for me to pay for the house or not. What's the best solution?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This is a really common situation that couples navigate. Here are some practical options to consider:

## Financial Arrangements

**Option 1: Contribute to expenses without ownership**
- Pay rent to her or split household costs
- Clear but you build no equity

**Option 2: Be added to the mortgage/title**
- Requires lender approval
- Gives you legal ownership stake
- More complex if things don't work out

**Option 3: Cohabitation agreement**
- Legal document outlining financial contributions
- Protects both of you

## Key Questions to Think Through

- **How much would you contribute** - mortgage payments, utilities, renovations?
- **What happens if you don't get married** - do you get money back?
- **Are you both on the same page** about the timeline and intentions?

## Practical Suggestions

- **Talk openly with her** about expectations before she closes
- **Consult a real estate attorney** - relatively inexpensive and very helpful
- **Consider a prenuptial agreement** when you do marry, especially since she's bringing property into the marriage

## My Honest Take

Contributing to someone else's asset without legal protection carries real risk. **Getting something in writing** before moving in together is smart for both of you, not just you.
