What is the difference between analysis and design? Can one begin to design without analysis? Why? Be concise.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
## Analysis vs. Design

| | **Analysis** | **Design** |
|---|---|---|
| **Focus** | *What* the system must do | *How* the system will do it |
| **Question** | Problem understanding | Solution construction |
| **Output** | Requirements, models of reality | Blueprints, architecture, specifications |

---

## Can You Design Without Analysis?

**Technically yes, practically no.**

- **Without analysis**, you don't know *what problem you're solving*, leading to:
  - Building the **wrong system**
  - Costly rework
  - Missed requirements

- Some overlap exists (they are iterative), but **analysis must logically precede design** because:
  > *You cannot define a solution before you understand the problem.*

Skipping analysis is a common cause of **project failure** — you risk designing an elegant solution to the wrong problem.
