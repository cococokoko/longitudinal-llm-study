# Effective Ways to Reduce Household Energy Use

Here are practical strategies organized by category:

## Quick & Low-Cost Changes
- Switch to LED bulbs throughout the home
- Unplug electronics and chargers when not in use
- Use power strips to eliminate "phantom" energy drain
- Wash clothes in cold water
- Run dishwashers and washing machines with full loads

## Heating & Cooling (Often Biggest Savings)
- Set thermostats a few degrees lower in winter, higher in summer
- Use programmable or smart thermostats
- Seal drafts around doors and windows with weatherstripping
- Change HVAC filters regularly
- Use ceiling fans to reduce AC reliance

## Kitchen & Appliances
- Match pot size to burner size
- Use microwave or air fryer instead of full oven when possible
- Keep refrigerator coils clean
- Air dry dishes instead of using heated drying

## Bigger Investments Worth Considering
- Improve attic/wall insulation
- Upgrade to Energy Star appliances
- Install solar panels if feasible

## Managing a Larger Household Specifically
- Create simple habits everyone follows (lights off when leaving rooms)
- Assign energy-awareness responsibilities to older family members
- Track monthly usage to spot patterns and progress

## Helpful Tools
- Your utility company often offers **free energy audits**
- Many utilities offer **rebates** on efficient appliances
