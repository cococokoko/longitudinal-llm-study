One excellent idea related to Vietnam is:

"Low-Cost Flood Warning System for Rural Communities"

Why it matters:
- Flooding is a major issue in Vietnam, especially in the Mekong Delta and central provinces.
- Climate change and rising sea levels increase flood risks.
- Many rural areas cannot afford expensive monitoring systems.

Project concept:
Build a small prototype that detects rising water levels and sends an alert.

Materials (often under $30–$50 total):
- Arduino Uno or ESP32 microcontroller
- Water level sensor or ultrasonic distance sensor
- Buzzer and LED
- Small breadboard and wires
- Plastic container to simulate a river or canal

How it works:
- The sensor measures water level.
- When the water reaches a dangerous height, the system activates a buzzer and flashing LED.
- If using an ESP32, you can also send alerts to a smartphone via Wi-Fi.

Experiments:
- Test accuracy at different water levels.
- Compare different sensor types.
- Measure how quickly the system responds.
- Analyze how early warnings could reduce flood damage.

Data to collect:
- Water height (cm)
- Sensor error (%)
- Response time (seconds)
- Number of successful alerts

STEM fields involved:
- Science: Hydrology and environmental science
- Technology: Sensors and electronics
- Engineering: Designing the warning system
- Mathematics: Data analysis and accuracy calculations

This project is:

- Strongly connected to environmental issues in Vietnam
- Rich in scientific testing and data collection
