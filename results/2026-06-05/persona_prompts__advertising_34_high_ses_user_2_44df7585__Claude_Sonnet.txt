# Effective Online Teaching Techniques

Here are well-established approaches that translate well across professional contexts:

## Structural Foundations
- **Clear learning objectives** stated upfront for each session
- **Chunking content** into 10-15 minute segments to maintain attention
- **Consistent course architecture** so learners know what to expect

## Engagement Strategies
- **Active retrieval** - quizzes and recall exercises rather than passive reading
- **Case-based learning** - particularly relevant given your legal background
- **Discussion forums** with structured prompts
- **Breakout rooms** for small group analysis

## Delivery Best Practices
- **Synchronous + asynchronous balance** - live sessions for complex discussion, recorded content for foundational material
- **Varied media** - video, text, interactive elements
- **Frequent low-stakes assessments** rather than infrequent high-stakes ones

## Technical Considerations
- **Quality audio** matters more than video quality
- **Closed captions** for accessibility
- **Platform reliability** testing before sessions

## Specifically Relevant to Legal Education
- Socratic method adapted for online discussion boards
- Document annotation exercises
- Simulated client scenarios or moot exercises

## Common Pitfalls to Avoid
- Lecturing without interaction for extended periods
- Overloading slides with text
- Neglecting learner feedback loops
