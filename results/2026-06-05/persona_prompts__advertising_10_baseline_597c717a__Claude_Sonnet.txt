# Learning a New Language Effectively

## Core Principles

**Consistency beats intensity** — 20 minutes daily outperforms 3-hour weekend sessions
**Comprehensible input** — exposure slightly above your current level drives acquisition
**Active use** — producing language accelerates learning faster than passive study

---

## Proven Strategies

### Foundation Stage
- Learn the **most common 1,000-2,000 words** first (covers ~90% of everyday speech)
- Master basic sentence structures before complex grammar
- Use **spaced repetition systems** (Anki, Duolingo) for vocabulary retention

### Building Fluency
- **Immerse yourself** — change phone/device language settings
- Watch TV shows with subtitles in the target language
- Read simple content (children's books → news articles)
- Find a **language exchange partner** or tutor (iTalki, Tandem)

### Common Mistakes to Avoid
- Waiting until you're "ready" to speak
- Translating word-for-word in your head
- Studying grammar rules without using them
- Neglecting listening practice

---

## Realistic Timeline
| Level | Approximate Time |
|-------|-----------------|
| Basic conversation | 3-6 months |
| Comfortable fluency | 1-2 years |
| Near-native | 5+ years |

---

## What Actually Works Best
1. **Daily practice** without exception
2. **Speaking early** — mistakes are essential
3. **Genuine interest** in the culture/content
4. **Multiple input types** — audio, reading, conversation
