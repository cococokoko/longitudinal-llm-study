If you're starting a plant-based diet, a mix of evidence-based nutrition resources, simple recipes, and meal-planning tools can make the transition much easier—especially if you're balancing the demands of a new job and limited time.

Some of the most useful resources:

• Nutrition information
  - NutritionFacts.org (Dr. Michael Greger): Research summaries on plant-based nutrition.
  - NHS Eatwell Guide (UK) or MyPlate (US): Practical guidance for balanced eating patterns.
  - Academy of Nutrition and Dietetics: Articles on vegetarian and vegan nutrition.

• Books
  - "How Not to Die" by Michael Greger, MD
  - "Plant-Based on a Budget" by Toni Okamoto
  - "The Plant-Based Diet for Beginners" by Gabriel Miller
  - "Vegan for Everybody" by America's Test Kitchen

• Recipe websites
  - Forks Over Knives
  - Minimalist Baker (many simple plant-based recipes)
  - Budget Bytes (plant-based and budget-friendly options)
  - Rainbow Plant Life
  - Cookie and Kate (vegetarian recipes, many vegan)

• Meal-planning and tracking apps
  - Cronometer: Helpful for tracking nutrients such as protein, iron, calcium, and vitamin B12.
  - Mealime: Easy meal planning and grocery lists.
  - Forks Meal Planner (subscription-based).

• YouTube channels
  - Pick Up Limes (nutrition-focused, practical recipes)
  - Rainbow Plant Life
  - Yeung Man Cooking
  - Simnett Nutrition

Key nutrients to pay attention to:
• Protein: Beans, lentils, tofu, tempeh, edamame, soy milk.
• Vitamin B12: Usually requires a supplement or fortified foods.
• Iron: Legumes, tofu, nuts, seeds, leafy greens.
• Calcium: Fortified plant milks, calcium-set tofu, certain greens.
• Omega-3 fats: Flaxseeds, chia seeds, walnuts, algae-based supplements.

A simple beginner approach:
• Build meals around beans, lentils, tofu, or tempeh.
• Add whole grains such as oats, rice, quinoa, or whole-wheat bread.
• Include fruits and vegetables daily.
• Use fortified plant milk.
• Consider a vitamin B12 supplement.
