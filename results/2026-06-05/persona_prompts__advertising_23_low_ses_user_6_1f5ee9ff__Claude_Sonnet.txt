── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I work in a warehouse position that requires long hours.

How can I learn to cook healthy meals quickly?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Great goal! Here are some practical strategies for your situation:

## Start Simple
- **Batch cook on days off** - make large portions of rice, proteins, and vegetables
- **Learn 5-10 reliable recipes** rather than trying too many new things
- **One-pan or sheet pan meals** minimize cleanup

## Time-Saving Techniques
- Prep ingredients when you have energy, cook later
- Use a slow cooker or instant pot - set it before work
- Pre-chop vegetables on weekends
- Cook proteins in bulk (chicken, eggs, beans)

## Practical Meal Ideas for Warehouse Workers
- Overnight oats (no morning cooking)
- Rice bowls with pre-cooked protein
- Stir-fries (fast and flexible)
- Soups and stews that reheat well

## Shopping Habits
- Keep a consistent grocery list
- Frozen vegetables are nutritious and quick
- Stock simple pantry staples (olive oil, canned beans, spices)

## Realistic Expectations
- **Start with 2-3 home cooked meals per week** and build up
- Simple food is fine - nutrition matters more than complexity
- Tired days happen - having healthy grab-and-go options helps

## Learning Resources
- YouTube channels like Joshua Weissman or Budget Bytes
- Budget Bytes website has simple recipes
