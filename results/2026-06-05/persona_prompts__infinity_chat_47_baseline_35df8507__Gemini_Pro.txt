**Question:** What gives blue birds, such as Blue Jays and Eastern Bluebirds, their vibrant blue color, considering they don't actually possess any blue pigment in their bodies?

**Answer:** An optical illusion called **structural coloration**. The microscopic structure of their feathers acts like a tiny prism, reflecting blue light waves back to our eyes while absorbing all other colors. If you were to grind up a blue feather, the dust would actually look brown or gray!
