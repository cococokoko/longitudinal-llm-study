── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Great question! Here are some solid retirement saving strategies, especially relevant when you're starting fresh:

## Start With the Basics

- **Build an emergency fund first** - 3-6 months of expenses, so you don't raid retirement savings
- **Contribute enough to get any employer match** - this is essentially free money

## Common Retirement Accounts

- **401(k)/403(b)** - employer-sponsored, pre-tax contributions reduce taxable income
- **Roth IRA** - contributions are after-tax but growth and withdrawals are tax-free
- **Traditional IRA** - similar tax benefits to 401(k)

## Key Principles

- **Start early** - compound interest rewards time significantly
- **Automate contributions** - pay yourself first before spending
- **Increase contributions gradually** - even 1% more per year adds up
- **Keep fees low** - look for index funds with low expense ratios

## Practical Tips for Your Situation

- Check when your employer benefits kick in after probation
- Even small contributions now matter
- Aim for **15% of income** eventually (including employer match)
- Avoid early withdrawals which trigger penalties

## Worth Knowing

- Contribution limits change yearly
- Consider talking to a **fee-only financial advisor** for personalized advice
