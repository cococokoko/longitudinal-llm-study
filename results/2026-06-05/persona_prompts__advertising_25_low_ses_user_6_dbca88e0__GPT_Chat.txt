── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I work in a warehouse position that requires long hours.

How can I decorate my apartment on a minimalist budget?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
If you work long hours and want your apartment to feel comfortable without spending much money, focus on a few high-impact changes rather than buying lots of decor.

Some budget-friendly ideas:

- Improve lighting
  - Warm LED bulbs can make a space feel much more inviting.
  - Use floor or table lamps from thrift stores instead of relying only on overhead lighting.

- Add textiles
  - Throw blankets, pillow covers, and curtains can dramatically change a room.
  - Check discount stores, thrift shops, or online marketplaces for inexpensive options.

- Use plants (real or artificial)
  - Low-maintenance plants like pothos, snake plants, or ZZ plants are good if you're away from home a lot.
  - Even one or two plants can make a room feel more alive.

- Decorate with things you already own
  - Books, records, travel souvenirs, sports equipment, or hobby items can double as decor.
  - Group similar items together for a more intentional look.

- Create simple wall art
  - Print free or inexpensive artwork and place it in budget frames.
  - You can often find frames cheaply at thrift stores and repaint them if needed.

- Focus on one accent color
  - Pick a color you like and repeat it through pillows, blankets, artwork, or small accessories.
  - This creates a coordinated look without requiring many purchases.

- Use mirrors
  - A mirror can make a small apartment feel larger and brighter.
  - Secondhand mirrors are often available at low cost.

- Declutter strategically
  - Minimalist decorating often costs less because fewer items are needed.
  - Clear surfaces and organized storage can make a space feel more intentional and relaxing.

- Shop secondhand first
  - Thrift stores, estate sales, Facebook Marketplace, Craigslist, and local buy-nothing groups often have furniture and decor at a fraction of retail prices.

- Prioritize comfort in the spaces you actually use
  - If you spend most of your time in the living room after work, put your budget there first.
  - A comfortable chair, soft lighting, and a cozy blanket may have more impact than decorating every room.

A simple minimalist-budget formula is:
- 1 plant
- 1 lamp with warm light
- 1 piece of wall art
- 2–3 textiles (pillows, blanket, curtains)
- A decluttered, organized space

Those five elements can make an apartment feel significantly more finished without requiring a large budget.
