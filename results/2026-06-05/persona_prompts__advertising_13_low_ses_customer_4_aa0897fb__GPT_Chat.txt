Mastering the art of cooking steak.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Mastering steak cooking comes down to understanding a few core principles rather than memorizing a single recipe.

1. Start with a good steak
- Popular cuts: ribeye, New York strip, filet mignon, sirloin.
- Look for marbling (thin streaks of fat throughout the meat), which adds flavor and tenderness.
- Thicker steaks (1–1.5 inches / 2.5–4 cm) are generally easier to cook accurately.

2. Season properly
- Pat the steak dry with paper towels.
- Season generously with salt before cooking. Black pepper can be added before or after cooking depending on preference.
- For thicker steaks, salting 1–24 hours in advance can improve flavor and texture.

3. Bring the surface dry, not necessarily the steak warm
- A dry surface helps create a better crust.
- You don't need to leave the steak out for a long time; simply drying it well is often more important.

4. Use high heat for a good sear
- Cast-iron skillets are excellent for steak.
- Heat the pan until very hot before adding a small amount of high-smoke-point oil (such as avocado or canola oil).
- Place the steak in the pan and avoid moving it constantly.

5. Learn internal temperatures
- Rare: 120–125°F (49–52°C)
- Medium-rare: 130–135°F (54–57°C)
- Medium: 140–145°F (60–63°C)
- Medium-well: 150–155°F (66–68°C)
- Well done: 160°F+ (71°C+)

A meat thermometer is one of the best tools for consistency.

6. Try the butter-basting technique
- Near the end of cooking, add butter, garlic, and herbs such as thyme or rosemary.
- Tilt the pan and spoon the melted butter over the steak repeatedly.

7. Rest before slicing
- Let the steak rest for 5–10 minutes after cooking.
- This helps retain juices and promotes even temperature throughout the meat.

8. Reverse sear for thick steaks
- Cook the steak gently in an oven at a low temperature until it's about 10–15°F (5–8°C) below your target temperature.
- Finish with a very hot sear in a pan or on a grill.
- This method provides excellent control and an even interior.

Common mistakes
- Cooking in a pan that isn't hot enough.
- Skipping the thermometer and guessing.
- Cutting into the steak immediately after cooking.
- Overcrowding the pan.
- Using too little salt.

A simple practice routine:
- Buy the same cut several times.
- Cook it using the same method.
- Track thickness, cooking time, and final temperature.
- Adjust one variable at a time.
