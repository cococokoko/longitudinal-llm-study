Give me a tip to be more organized at work. I'm a high school teacher.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
A simple organization habit that works well for teachers at many levels is to use a single "master task list" instead of keeping tasks in multiple places.

For example:

- Keep one list for everything that requires action: grading, lesson planning, parent communication, meetings, administrative tasks, etc.
- At the end of each workday, spend 5 minutes reviewing the list and identifying the 3 most important tasks for the next day.
- Block specific times on your calendar for those tasks rather than relying on memory.
- Keep reference materials (lesson resources, policies, student information) separate from your task list so actionable items don't get buried.

Whether you're teaching high school students or working in higher education, reducing the number of places where tasks can hide is often one of the fastest ways to become more organized.
