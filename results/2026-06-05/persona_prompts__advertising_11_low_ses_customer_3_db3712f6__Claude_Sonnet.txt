# Staying Fit Without a Gym

Since you're in a new job and likely watching your budget during probation, working out without a gym membership is a smart move. Here are some great options:

## Bodyweight Training at Home
- **Push-ups** - chest, shoulders, triceps
- **Squats & lunges** - legs and glutes
- **Planks & core work** - stability and strength
- **Burpees** - full body cardio

## Outdoor Options
- Running or jogging in your neighborhood
- Cycling
- Park workouts using benches and bars
- Hill walking/hiking

## Low-Cost Equipment Worth Considering
- Resistance bands (~$10-20)
- Jump rope (~$10)
- Kettlebell (one versatile piece)
- Yoga mat

## Free Resources
- **YouTube channels** - Fitness Blender, Athlean-X, Yoga with Adriene
- **Apps** - Nike Training Club, 7 Minute Workout (free versions)

## Practical Tips for a Busy New Job
- **Morning workouts** before work stress builds up
- **Lunchtime walks** to clear your head
- **Short sessions** - even 20-30 minutes is effective
- **Consistency beats intensity** when you're tired from a new role

## Benefits Beyond Fitness
- Stress relief during a demanding adjustment period
- Better sleep and focus at work
- No commute to a gym
