Yes. André Gorz is an especially fertile figure for new research because he sits at the intersection of Marxism, ecology, technology, labor theory, existentialism, and post-work politics. Here are several thesis directions that could produce original contributions.

1. Gorz as a theorist of artificial intelligence and automation before AI

Core question:
- To what extent do Gorz's analyses of automation anticipate contemporary debates about AI-generated knowledge and cognitive labor?

Argument:
- Most readings treat Gorz as a theorist of industrial automation.
- You could argue that his later work anticipated the automation of intellectual labor itself.
- Compare Gorz's concept of knowledge becoming a productive force with contemporary AI systems.

Relevant texts:
- Critique of Economic Reason
- Reclaiming Work
- The Immaterial

Potential contribution:
- Reconstruct Gorz as a major precursor to contemporary theories of AI capitalism.

2. Gorz versus accelerationism

Core question:
- Does Gorz offer a stronger Marxist response to technological development than contemporary accelerationist theory?

Argument:
- Accelerationists often argue that technological development should be intensified to transcend capitalism.
- Gorz was skeptical of technological progress detached from democratic control.
- Compare Gorz with Nick Srnicek, Alex Williams, or earlier accelerationist tendencies.

Potential contribution:
- Position Gorz as a neglected alternative to technological utopianism.

3. The ecological turn in Marxism through Gorz

Core question:
- How did Gorz contribute to ecological Marxism before the current climate crisis?

Argument:
- Gorz's ecological concerns emerged before ecological Marxism became mainstream.
- Examine similarities and differences between Gorz, John Bellamy Foster, Kohei Saito, and James O'Connor.

Potential contribution:
- Clarify whether Gorz should be understood as an ecological Marxist, an eco-socialist, or something distinct.

4. Gorz and the crisis of value theory

Core question:
- What happens to Marx's labor theory of value in Gorz's account of immaterial labor?

Argument:
- Gorz argued that knowledge cannot be measured through labor-time in the traditional way.
- Investigate whether this implies a revision, extension, or abandonment of Marxian value theory.

Comparison:
- Antonio Negri
- Carlo Vercellone
- Moishe Postone

Potential contribution:
- Connect Gorz to contemporary debates on value in digital capitalism.

5. Gorz's concept of autonomy and its existential roots

Core question:

Argument:
- Gorz was deeply influenced by Sartre.
- Explore whether autonomy functions as the hidden foundation of his critique of capitalism.

Potential contribution:
- Challenge readings that present Gorz as simply a Marxist theorist of labor.

Potential contribution:
