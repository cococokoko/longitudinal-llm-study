# Effective Ways to Reduce Household Energy Use

## Quick Wins (Low/No Cost)
- **Adjust thermostat** – Lower by 7-10°F when sleeping or away
- **Unplug devices** when not in use (standby power adds up)
- **Wash clothes in cold water** – works just as well for most loads
- **Run full loads** in dishwasher and washing machine
- **Turn off lights** when leaving rooms

## Heating & Cooling (Biggest Energy Users)
- Seal drafts around windows, doors, and outlets
- Use ceiling fans to circulate air
- Keep vents unblocked
- Change HVAC filters regularly
- Use curtains strategically – block summer sun, let in winter sun

## Appliances & Lighting
- Switch to **LED bulbs** (use ~75% less energy)
- Replace old appliances with **Energy Star certified** models
- Use a **smart power strip** to eliminate phantom loads
- Air-dry dishes and clothes when possible

## Bigger Investments
- Add insulation to attic/walls
- Install a **programmable or smart thermostat**
- Upgrade to a heat pump system
- Consider **solar panels**

## Monitoring
- Track usage with a smart meter or energy monitor
- Review utility bills for patterns

## Typical Impact
| Action | Potential Savings |
|--------|------------------|
| Smart thermostat | 10-15% on heating/cooling |
| LED lighting | Up to 75% on lighting costs |
| Sealing drafts | 10-20% on energy bills |
