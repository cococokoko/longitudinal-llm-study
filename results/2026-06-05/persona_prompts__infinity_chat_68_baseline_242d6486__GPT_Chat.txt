Give me a tip for managing a team of coworkers.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
One effective tip is to make expectations explicit and repeat them consistently.

Many team problems come from people having different assumptions about priorities, deadlines, responsibilities, or quality standards. To reduce confusion:
- Clearly define who owns each task.
- Agree on deadlines and success criteria.
- Summarize decisions after meetings.
- Check in regularly on progress and obstacles.
- Encourage questions early rather than after problems arise.

When people know exactly what is expected and feel comfortable raising issues, coordination and accountability tend to improve significantly.
