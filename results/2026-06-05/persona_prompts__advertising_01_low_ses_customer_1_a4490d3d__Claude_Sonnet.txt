# Learning to Play Guitar

That's a great hobby to pick up! Here are some practical tips that work well with a busy, unpredictable schedule:

## Getting Started
- **Choose your guitar type** - Acoustic (no equipment needed) or electric (quieter with headphones)
- **Learn basic chord shapes first** - G, C, D, Em, Am cover hundreds of songs
- **Get a tuner app** - GuitarTuna is free and easy to use

## Fitting Practice Into Your Schedule
Since your schedule can vary day to day:
- **Even 10-15 minutes counts** - Short, consistent practice beats long occasional sessions
- **Keep your guitar out and visible** - Not in a case, so it's easy to grab
- **Practice during downtime** - Before work, after shifts, on days off
- **Finger exercises** while watching TV or relaxing

## Free/Low-Cost Learning Resources
- **YouTube** - Justin Guitar is highly recommended and completely free
- **Yousician** - Interactive app with a free tier
- **Ultimate Guitar** - Chord charts and tabs for songs you already love

## Realistic Expectations
- Fingertips will be sore at first - this passes in a few weeks
- Start with **songs you actually enjoy**
- Progress feels slow then suddenly clicks

## Budget Tips
- Used guitars can be very affordable
- You don't need expensive gear to start
