# Mastering Digital Photography

## Core Fundamentals

### The Exposure Triangle
| Element | Controls | Effect |
|---------|----------|--------|
| **Aperture** (f-stop) | Light & depth of field | f/1.8 = blurry background; f/16 = sharp throughout |
| **Shutter Speed** | Motion & light | 1/1000s = freeze action; 1/30s = motion blur |
| **ISO** | Sensor sensitivity | Low ISO = clean; High ISO = grainy/noisy |

---

## Essential Concepts

### Aperture
- **Wide aperture** (f/1.4–f/2.8) → portraits, low light
- **Narrow aperture** (f/8–f/16) → landscapes, architecture
- Controls **bokeh** (background blur)

### Shutter Speed
- **Fast** (1/500s+) → sports, wildlife, children
- **Slow** (1/30s and below) → light trails, waterfalls, night shots
- Use a **tripod** for anything below 1/60s

### ISO
- Start at **ISO 100–400** in good light
- Push to **ISO 1600–6400** in low light
- Modern cameras handle high ISO surprisingly well

---

## Composition Rules

- **Rule of Thirds** — place subjects off-center
- **Leading Lines** — draw the eye into the frame
- **Framing** — use natural frames (doorways, trees)
- **Negative Space** — let subjects breathe
- **Golden Hour** — shoot 1 hour after sunrise / before sunset

---

## Camera Modes Explained

| Mode | Best For | You Control |
|------|----------|-------------|
| **Auto** | Beginners | Nothing |
| **Av/A** (Aperture Priority) | Portraits, landscapes | Aperture |
| **Tv/S** (Shutter Priority) | Action, sports | Shutter speed |
| **M** (Manual) | Full creative control | Everything |
| **RAW** | Post-processing | File format |

---

## Shooting Tips

### Focus
- Use **single-point AF** for precise control
- **Continuous AF (AI Servo)** for moving subjects
- **Back-button focus** separates focus from shutter

### Light
- **Golden hour** → warm, soft, flattering
- **Blue hour** → moody, cool tones
- **Overcast days** → natural diffused light (great for portraits)
- **Harsh midday sun** → use shade or fill flash

### Histogram
- Avoid **clipping** (blown highlights or crushed blacks)
- Expose to the **right** (brighter) without overexposing

---

## Post-Processing Workflow

### Basic Editing Order
1. **Crop & straighten**
2. **Exposure & contrast**
3. **White balance**
4. **Highlights & shadows**
5. **Color grading**
6. **Sharpening & noise reduction**
7. **Export**

### Recommended Software
- **Adobe Lightroom** — industry standard
- **Capture One** — professional color grading
- **Darktable** — free, powerful alternative
- **Luminar Neo** — AI-assisted editing

## Gear Progression

### Beginner
- Entry-level DSLR or mirrorless
- Kit lens (18-55mm)
- 50mm f/1.8 ("nifty fifty")

### Intermediate
- Better body with weather sealing
- 24-70mm f/2.8 zoom
- Prime lenses for specific uses

### Advanced
- Full-frame sensor
- Specialized lenses (macro, telephoto, tilt-shift)
- External flash/strobes
