── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I work full-time in a fast food job with an unpredictable schedule.

Can you give me a powerful rhetorical question for an essay about the harms of social media on teens?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here's a strong rhetorical question for your essay:

**"If we wouldn't hand a teenager a product proven to increase anxiety, depression, and self-harm, why do we casually place one in their pocket every single day?"**

This works well because it:
- Creates a **logical contrast** that's hard to dismiss
- Makes the reader feel the **weight of the contradiction**
- Doesn't require the reader to already agree with you

A few alternatives depending on your tone:

- **More emotional:** "How many more teenagers need to suffer before we take seriously what the research has been telling us for years?"
- **More challenging:** "At what point does our convenience become their crisis?"
- **More direct:** "Would you knowingly give your child something designed to be addictive?"

The best choice depends on **where in your essay** you place it - opening, closing, or a transition point.

Would you like help developing the argument around whichever question fits your essay best?
