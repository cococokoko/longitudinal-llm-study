── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I work full-time in a fast food job with an unpredictable schedule.

What are the best ways to improve indoor air quality?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
If you work full-time with an unpredictable schedule, the best air-quality improvements are usually the ones that are effective, low-maintenance, and don't require constant attention.

In order of impact:

1. Use a HEPA air purifier
- A good HEPA purifier can significantly reduce dust, pollen, smoke particles, pet dander, and some airborne contaminants.
- Place it in the room where you spend the most time, usually the bedroom.
- If possible, run it continuously on a low or automatic setting.
- Check and replace filters according to the manufacturer's schedule.

2. Improve ventilation
- Open windows when outdoor air quality is good.
- Use bathroom exhaust fans during and after showers.
- Use a kitchen range hood that vents outdoors when cooking.
- Even a few minutes of fresh-air exchange can help reduce indoor pollutant buildup.

3. Control humidity
- Aim for indoor humidity between 30% and 50%.
- High humidity encourages mold and dust mites.
- Use a dehumidifier in damp areas if needed.
- Fix leaks promptly.

4. Reduce sources of pollution
- Avoid smoking indoors.
- Minimize candles, incense, and aerosol sprays.
- Store paints, solvents, and strong chemicals in sealed containers away from living spaces.
- Choose lower-VOC products when possible.

5. Keep your home clean
- Vacuum regularly using a vacuum with a HEPA filter if possible.
- Dust with damp cloths rather than dry dusting, which can spread particles into the air.
- Wash bedding regularly.
- If you have pets, groom them and clean pet areas frequently.

6. Replace HVAC filters regularly
- If you have central heating or air conditioning, replace filters on schedule.
- Higher-efficiency filters can capture more particles, provided your system supports them.
- Set reminders on your phone if your schedule makes it easy to forget.

7. Monitor air quality
- Consider a simple indoor air-quality monitor that measures particulate matter (PM2.5), humidity, and sometimes carbon dioxide.
- This can help identify issues that aren't obvious.

8. Pay attention to cooking emissions
- Cooking, especially frying and high-heat cooking, can generate significant indoor pollution.
- Use the range hood and, if practical, open a nearby window while cooking.

For someone with limited time, the highest-value combination is usually:
- A quality HEPA purifier in the bedroom.
- Regular HVAC filter replacement.
- Consistent use of bathroom and kitchen exhaust fans.
- Humidity control if your home tends to be damp.
