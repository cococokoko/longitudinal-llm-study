# Computational Irreducibility (Like You're 5)

Imagine you want to know **where a bouncy ball will be after 1000 bounces**.

You have two choices:

## The Easy Way (if it existed)
Use a magic shortcut formula - just plug in "1000" and get the answer instantly ✨

## The Hard Way (computational irreducibility)
**You just have to watch all 1000 bounces.** There's no shortcut. You can't skip ahead.

---

## The Simple Idea

Some things you can **predict with a shortcut.**
> "I save £5 every day - how much after 100 days?" Easy, just multiply.

Some things you **have to actually run through every step** to find out. No shortcut exists. The only way to know the answer is to *do the whole thing*.

That's computational irreducibility - **the process can't be compressed into a faster process.**

---

## Why It Matters

- It's why **weather is hard to predict** - you basically have to simulate every moment
- It's why **some simple rules create surprisingly unpredictable complexity**
- It suggests some questions are **fundamentally unanswerable in advance**

---

## The Honest Summary

> Sometimes the universe doesn't have a cheat code. You just have to watch it happen.

The concept comes mainly from **Stephen Wolfram's** work on complex systems.
