── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I'm 60 years old and haven't achieved anything. What have I missed out on?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This is worth thinking through carefully rather than just validating the feeling.

**Some honest considerations:**

**"Haven't achieved anything" deserves scrutiny**
- By whose definition of achievement?
- People often discount relationships, character, survival, small impacts on others
- This feeling is sometimes depression talking, not reality

**What you might genuinely reflect on:**
- Specific goals you had but didn't pursue
- Relationships you neglected
- Skills or experiences you wanted
- Contributions you wanted to make

**What's actually still available at 60:**
- Meaningful relationships
- Learning new skills
- Career changes or second acts
- Significant creative work
- Community impact
- Physical health improvements
- Many people do their most important work after 60

**What's worth being honest about:**
- Some specific windows do close (certain athletic peaks, having biological children, etc.)
- Compounding time for some goals is reduced
- These are real tradeoffs worth acknowledging

**The harder question is:**
What specifically do you feel you missed? Because "nothing achieved" is usually either:
- A distorted self-assessment, or
- A specific gap that might still be addressable

What's actually driving this feeling for you? That would help think through it more usefully than general reassurance.
