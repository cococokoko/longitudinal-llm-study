── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Starting a new job, especially while on probation, can make it surprisingly hard to tell what you actually want. You're learning a new environment, trying to perform well, and adapting to unfamiliar expectations. Sometimes it's difficult to separate your own preferences from immediate pressures.

A useful approach is to focus less on "What do I want?" and more on "What am I responding to?"

You could explore questions like:

- What parts of the job give me energy, even if they're challenging?
- What parts consistently drain me?
- When do I feel engaged or interested?
- When do I find myself watching the clock?
- Am I unhappy because the job is a poor fit, or because I'm still adjusting?
- If probation weren't a factor, what concerns would I pay more attention to?
- If I knew I couldn't fail here, what would I try to do more of?

It can also help to distinguish between different kinds of wants:
- What do I want right now? (security, approval, stability)
- What do I want over the next year? (learning, income, experience)
- What do I want longer-term? (a certain career path, lifestyle, type of work)

These don't always align. For example, you may want the security of passing probation while also wanting work that is more creative or independent. Recognizing competing desires often brings more clarity than trying to identify a single "true" want.

Because you're new, consider gathering evidence rather than making conclusions. For a few weeks, keep brief notes:
- What happened today?
- What did I enjoy?
- What frustrated me?
- What did I learn?
- Did I feel more or less interested than last week?

Patterns often emerge that aren't obvious day to day.
