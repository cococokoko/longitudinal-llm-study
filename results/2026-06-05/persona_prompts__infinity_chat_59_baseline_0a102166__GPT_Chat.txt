── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Give me a tip to be more organized at work. I'm a high school teacher.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
A practical tip for a high school teacher is to create a single "capture system" for everything that needs your attention.

Instead of keeping reminders in emails, sticky notes, your gradebook, and your memory, choose one place (a notebook, task app, or document) where every task goes immediately.

For example:
- During class: jot down "email parent of Alex" or "copy quiz for Friday."
- During meetings: add action items to the same list.
- When reading email: either do it immediately (if it takes under 2 minutes) or add it to the list.

Then spend 10 minutes at the end of each school day:
- Review the list.
- Mark completed items.
- Pick the 3 most important tasks for tomorrow.
- Prepare any materials you'll need for first period.

Many teachers find that the biggest source of disorganization isn't having too much to do—it's having tasks scattered across too many places. A single trusted list reduces that mental load.
