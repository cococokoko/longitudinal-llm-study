── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I work in a warehouse position that requires long hours.

How can I decorate my apartment on a minimalist budget?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here are some practical ideas for decorating on a tight budget:

## Start With What You Have
- Rearrange existing furniture for a fresh feel
- Declutter first - less stuff often looks more intentional
- Clean everything thoroughly (makes a big difference)

## Low-Cost Wins
- **Thrift stores** - frames, vases, lamps often cost very little
- **Facebook Marketplace/Craigslist** - free or cheap furniture
- **Dollar stores** - surprisingly good for basic decorative items

## Simple Visual Improvements
- Paint one accent wall (a little paint goes a long way)
- Add plants - pothos and snake plants are cheap and hard to kill
- Swap out light bulbs for warmer tones
- Use mirrors to make spaces feel larger

## Free Options
- Natural elements like branches, rocks, pinecones
- Rearrange what you own seasonally
- Print free artwork online and use cheap frames

## Practical Considerations for Your Situation
- Since you work long hours, **prioritize comfort** over aesthetics
- Focus on your bedroom and main relaxation area first
- Keep it simple so cleaning is easy on your days off
- Good lighting matters a lot for mood after tiring shifts

## Budget Priority Order
1. Lighting
2. Comfortable seating
3. Then decorative touches
