Give a numerical example to illustrate the concept of partial derivative.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
A partial derivative measures how a function changes with respect to one variable while keeping the other variables fixed.

Example:

Consider the function

f(x, y) = x²y + 3y

Suppose we want the partial derivative with respect to x.

Treat y as a constant:

∂f/∂x = 2xy

Now evaluate it at the point (x, y) = (2, 4):

∂f/∂x(2, 4) = 2 × 2 × 4 = 16

Interpretation:
- Near the point (2, 4), if x increases by a small amount while y stays fixed at 4, the function increases by about 16 times that small amount.

For example:

- f(2, 4) = 2²·4 + 3·4 = 16 + 12 = 28
- Increase x by 0.01 to 2.01:
  - f(2.01, 4) = (2.01)²·4 + 12 = 28.1604
- Actual change in f:
  - 28.1604 − 28 = 0.1604
- Partial derivative estimate:
  - 16 × 0.01 = 0.16

The estimate (0.16) is very close to the actual change (0.1604), illustrating what the partial derivative represents.
