── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Effective ways to reduce household energy use.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Reducing household energy use can lower utility bills and decrease environmental impact. Some of the most effective strategies include:

• Improve heating and cooling efficiency
  - Set thermostats a few degrees lower in winter and higher in summer.
  - Use programmable or smart thermostats.
  - Replace HVAC filters regularly.
  - Schedule maintenance for heating and cooling systems.
  - Seal air leaks around doors, windows, and ducts.

• Increase insulation
  - Add insulation to attics, walls, and crawl spaces where needed.
  - Weather-strip doors and windows.
  - Use insulated curtains to reduce heat loss and gain.

• Upgrade lighting
  - Replace incandescent bulbs with LED bulbs.
  - Turn off lights when rooms are unoccupied.
  - Use motion sensors or timers in appropriate areas.

• Reduce appliance energy consumption
  - Choose ENERGY STAR-certified appliances when replacing old equipment.
  - Run dishwashers and washing machines with full loads.
  - Wash clothes in cold water when possible.
  - Air-dry clothes and dishes when practical.

• Eliminate standby power use
  - Unplug electronics that are not in use.
  - Use smart power strips to cut power to idle devices.
  - Enable energy-saving settings on computers, televisions, and game consoles.

• Improve water heating efficiency
  - Lower the water heater temperature to a safe, efficient setting (commonly around 120°F/49°C, if appropriate for your household).
  - Insulate hot water pipes and the water heater tank if applicable.
  - Install low-flow showerheads and faucet aerators.
  - Fix leaking faucets promptly.

• Use energy more efficiently in the kitchen
  - Keep refrigerator and freezer doors closed as much as possible.
  - Clean refrigerator coils periodically.
  - Use microwaves, pressure cookers, or air fryers when suitable, as they often use less energy than conventional ovens.

• Manage cooling naturally
  - Use ceiling fans to improve comfort.
  - Close blinds or curtains during hot sunny periods.
  - Plant shade trees or use exterior shading where feasible.

• Monitor energy use
  - Review utility bills for trends.
  - Use home energy monitors or smart meters if available.
  - Identify and address unusually high energy consumption.

• Consider renewable energy and major upgrades
  - Install solar panels if financially and geographically suitable.
  - Replace old HVAC systems with high-efficiency models.
  - Upgrade to high-performance windows during renovations.

For many households, the biggest savings typically come from improving heating and cooling efficiency, sealing air leaks, upgrading insulation, switching to LED lighting, and reducing hot water use.
