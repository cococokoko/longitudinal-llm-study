For learning Python, you do not need a powerful machine. A good secondhand business-class laptop is usually a better value than a consumer laptop because of durability, keyboard quality, repairability, and Linux compatibility.

Some excellent options on the used market:

- Lenovo ThinkPad T480
  - Often considered one of the best used laptops available.
  - 8th-generation Intel CPUs support modern Windows versions well.
  - Upgradeable RAM and storage.
  - Excellent keyboard.
  - Typical used price: roughly $150–350 USD depending on condition and specs.

- Lenovo ThinkPad T14 Gen 1 (Intel or AMD)
  - More modern than the T480.
  - Better battery life and performance.
  - Typical used price: roughly $300–600 USD.

- Dell Latitude 5490 / 5500 / 5510
  - Business-class reliability.
  - Easy to find from corporate fleet sales.
  - Good Linux support.
  - Typical used price: roughly $200–500 USD.

- HP EliteBook 840 G5 or G6
  - Premium build quality.
  - Good display options.
  - Comfortable keyboard.
  - Typical used price: roughly $200–500 USD.

- Lenovo ThinkPad X1 Carbon (Gen 6 or Gen 7)
  - Lightweight and portable.
  - Excellent screen and keyboard.
  - Less upgradeable than a T-series ThinkPad.
  - Typical used price: roughly $300–700 USD.

- Apple MacBook Air M1
  - If you prefer macOS.
  - Outstanding battery life.
  - Excellent Unix-based environment for Python development.
  - Typical used price: roughly $450–800 USD.

For Python specifically, I would aim for:
- At least 16 GB RAM (8 GB is workable but less comfortable).
- 256 GB SSD minimum; 512 GB is preferable.
- 8th-generation Intel Core i5/i7 or newer, or AMD Ryzen 4000-series or newer.
- A 1080p display if possible.

If your goal is:
- Pure Python learning, scripting, automation, data analysis: ThinkPad T480 is probably the best value.
- Machine learning coursework with local models: ThinkPad T14 AMD or a used MacBook Air M1.
- Maximum portability: ThinkPad X1 Carbon or MacBook Air M1.
