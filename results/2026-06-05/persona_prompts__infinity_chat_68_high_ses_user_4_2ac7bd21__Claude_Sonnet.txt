# Tip: Create Structured but Brief Regular Check-ins

Consider implementing **short, consistent one-on-one meetings** with each team member (even 15-20 minutes biweekly).

## Why this works well in a research setting:

- Catches problems **before they derail experiments or deadlines**
- Gives trainees a dedicated space to raise concerns they might hesitate to bring up publicly
- Helps you distinguish between someone who is **stuck vs. disengaged vs. overwhelmed**
- Builds trust, which improves honest reporting of negative results

## Practical suggestions:

- Keep a shared running document so both of you can add agenda items between meetings
- Ask open-ended questions like *"What's your biggest obstacle right now?"*
- Separate these from formal performance reviews so they feel **low-stakes**

## Especially relevant for physician-scientists:

Your clinical time can make you feel **less visible** to your lab team than a full-time PI. Regular check-ins signal consistent investment in their progress even when you're pulled in multiple directions.

---

The goal is **early signal detection** - the same principle that makes good clinical and scientific thinking effective.
