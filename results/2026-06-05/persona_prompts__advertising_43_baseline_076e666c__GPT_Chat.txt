── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How to plan an environmentally friendly wedding?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Planning an environmentally friendly wedding involves making choices that reduce waste, conserve resources, and support ethical businesses while still creating a meaningful celebration.

Here are some practical ways to do it:

• Choose a sustainable venue
  - Select a venue close to most guests to reduce travel emissions.
  - Consider outdoor venues, botanical gardens, community centers, or locations with sustainability certifications.
  - Use a venue that already has attractive décor to minimize additional materials.

• Keep the guest list intentional
  - Smaller weddings generally have a lower environmental impact.
  - Focus on inviting the people most important to you.

• Send digital invitations
  - Use electronic invitations and RSVPs.
  - If you prefer paper, choose recycled paper, plantable seed paper, or sustainably sourced materials.

• Select eco-friendly attire
  - Wear vintage, secondhand, rented, or ethically made wedding attire.
  - Choose outfits that can be worn again after the wedding.
  - Consider sustainable fabrics and local designers.

• Source flowers responsibly
  - Use locally grown, seasonal flowers.
  - Incorporate potted plants, dried flowers, or reusable arrangements.
  - Donate flowers after the event to hospitals, nursing homes, or community organizations where appropriate.

• Focus on sustainable food and drinks
  - Work with caterers that use local, seasonal ingredients.
  - Offer more plant-based menu options, which often have a lower environmental footprint.
  - Avoid excessive food waste by planning portions carefully.
  - Donate surplus food when local regulations and organizations allow.

• Reduce single-use items
  - Use real dishes, glasses, and cutlery instead of disposable versions.
  - Avoid single-use decorations, favors, and plastic products.
  - Rent items rather than purchasing new ones when possible.

• Choose thoughtful decorations
  - Reuse, rent, borrow, or buy secondhand décor.
  - Use candles, fabric, greenery, and natural materials.
  - Avoid glitter, balloons, and items that are difficult to recycle.

• Offer sustainable wedding favors
  - Consider edible favors, charitable donations, seeds, small plants, or locally made products.
  - You can also skip favors entirely.

• Manage transportation
  - Encourage carpooling, public transportation, or shared shuttles.
  - Provide information about sustainable travel options for guests.

• Reduce waste and recycle
  - Set up clearly marked recycling and compost bins.
  - Work with vendors who have waste-reduction practices.
  - Plan ahead for donating or reusing decorations and leftover materials.

• Choose ethical vendors
  - Ask vendors about their sustainability policies.
  - Support local businesses to reduce transportation impacts.
  - Prioritize vendors who minimize packaging and waste.

• Consider carbon offsets
  - If guests must travel long distances, consider purchasing carbon offsets through reputable organizations.
  - Offsets are most effective when paired with efforts to reduce emissions first.

Sample eco-friendly wedding priorities:
1. Local venue.
2. Digital invitations.
3. Seasonal, locally sourced food.
4. Reusable décor and tableware.
5. Sustainable attire.
6. Waste reduction and composting.
7. Low-impact transportation options.

The most environmentally friendly wedding is usually one that prioritizes reducing consumption and waste while focusing resources on the parts of the celebration that matter most to you and your guests.
