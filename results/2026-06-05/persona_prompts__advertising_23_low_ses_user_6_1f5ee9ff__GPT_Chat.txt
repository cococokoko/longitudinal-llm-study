── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I work in a warehouse position that requires long hours.

How can I learn to cook healthy meals quickly?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
If you work long warehouse shifts, the key is not becoming a great cook overnight—it's building a small set of fast, repeatable meals that are healthy, filling, and easy to prepare when you're tired.

A practical approach:

- Learn 5–10 simple meals instead of hundreds of recipes.
- Focus on meals that take 10–20 minutes.
- Use convenience foods strategically (frozen vegetables, pre-washed greens, canned beans, rotisserie chicken, microwaveable rice).
- Cook extra portions so one cooking session creates multiple meals.

Some beginner-friendly healthy meals:

- Chicken, frozen vegetables, and rice
  - Cook chicken in a pan.
  - Microwave vegetables and rice.
  - Add seasoning or a simple sauce.

- Egg scramble
  - Eggs, vegetables, and cheese if desired.
  - Ready in about 10 minutes.

- Turkey or bean tacos
  - Cook ground turkey or heat canned beans.
  - Serve in tortillas with lettuce and salsa.

- Sheet-pan meal
  - Put chicken sausage, vegetables, and potatoes on a baking sheet.
  - Bake 20–30 minutes with minimal effort.

- Stir-fry
  - Protein + frozen stir-fry vegetables + bottled stir-fry sauce.
  - Serve over rice.

Skills worth learning first:

1. Cooking eggs
2. Cooking rice
3. Baking chicken
4. Sautéing vegetables
5. Using seasonings (salt, pepper, garlic powder, paprika)

Once you know those five skills, you can make dozens of healthy meals.

For meal prep:

- Pick one day per week.
- Cook:
  - A protein (chicken, turkey, tofu, beans)
  - A carbohydrate (rice, potatoes, pasta)
  - Vegetables
- Store in containers for grab-and-go meals.

Good YouTube channels for quick learning:

- Budget Bytes (also has a website)
- Brian Lagerstrom
- Ethan Chlebowski
- Pro Home Cooks

For long shifts, also prioritize meals that:
- Contain protein (helps fullness and recovery)
- Include fruits or vegetables
- Are easy to pack and reheat
- Don't require much decision-making after work

A simple example day:

- Breakfast: Greek yogurt, fruit, and oats
- Lunch: Chicken, rice, and vegetables
- Snack: Apple and nuts
- Dinner: Egg scramble with vegetables and toast

This provides balanced nutrition without requiring advanced cooking skills or a lot of time.
