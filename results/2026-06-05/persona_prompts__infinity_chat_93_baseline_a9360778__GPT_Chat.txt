Stress affects both the body and the mind in many ways. When a person experiences stress, the body releases hormones such as adrenaline and cortisol. These hormones prepare the body to respond to challenges by increasing heart rate, blood pressure, and energy levels. In the short term, this response can be helpful because it improves focus and reaction time. However, long-term stress can have negative effects on health.

Physically, chronic stress may cause headaches, muscle tension, fatigue, digestive problems, and difficulty sleeping. It can also weaken the immune system, making a person more vulnerable to illnesses. Over time, ongoing stress may increase the risk of serious health conditions, including heart disease and high blood pressure.

Stress also affects mental and emotional well-being. People under stress may feel anxious, irritable, overwhelmed, or unable to concentrate. Memory and decision-making can become more difficult, and prolonged stress may contribute to mental health conditions such as anxiety disorders and depression.

Managing stress is important for maintaining overall health. Healthy habits such as regular exercise, adequate sleep, relaxation techniques, and social support can help reduce stress and improve both physical and mental well-being. By recognizing and addressing stress early, individuals can protect their health and improve their quality of life.
