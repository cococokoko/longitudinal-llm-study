── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
# Improving Indoor Air Quality

Here are the most effective approaches:

## Ventilation
- Open windows regularly when outdoor air quality permits
- Use exhaust fans in kitchens and bathrooms
- Ensure HVAC systems are properly maintained

## Filtration
- Use **HEPA air purifiers** in frequently occupied rooms
- Replace HVAC filters regularly (typically every 1-3 months)
- Consider higher MERV-rated filters for your system

## Source Control
- Choose low-VOC paints, furniture, and building materials
- Avoid smoking indoors
- Store chemicals and cleaning products properly
- Use natural or fragrance-free cleaning products

## Humidity Management
- Keep humidity between **30-50%** to discourage mold and dust mites
- Use dehumidifiers in damp areas
- Fix leaks promptly

## Reducing Specific Pollutants
- Test for **radon** (especially in lower levels)
- Install carbon monoxide detectors
- Have gas appliances inspected regularly
- Remove or encapsulate asbestos-containing materials professionally

## Practical Habits
- Remove shoes at the door
- Vacuum with HEPA-filter vacuums regularly
- Groom pets frequently
- Avoid synthetic air fresheners

## Plants
- Some houseplants may offer modest air quality benefits, though research on their effectiveness is **limited**
