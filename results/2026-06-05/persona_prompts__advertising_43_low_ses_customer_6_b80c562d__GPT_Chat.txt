How to plan an environmentally friendly wedding?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Planning an environmentally friendly wedding is often about reducing waste, choosing reusable or local options, and being intentional about purchases. If you work long hours, focusing on a few high-impact decisions can make the process more manageable.

Some of the most effective steps include:

- Choose a venue that already fits your vision
  - Outdoor spaces, botanical gardens, community halls, or venues with built-in décor can reduce the need for additional materials.
  - Consider venues close to where most guests live to reduce travel emissions.

- Keep the guest list intentional
  - Smaller weddings generally have a lower environmental impact and can reduce costs.

- Use digital invitations
  - Send invitations and RSVPs online.
  - If you prefer printed invitations, choose recycled paper and eco-friendly inks.

- Select sustainable attire
  - Rent wedding attire or buy secondhand.
  - Choose outfits that can be worn again after the wedding.
  - Consider vintage or locally made options.

- Focus on local and seasonal food
  - Work with caterers who source ingredients locally.
  - Seasonal menus often require fewer resources.
  - Offer vegetarian or plant-based options, which typically have a lower environmental footprint.

- Reduce single-use items
  - Use reusable plates, glasses, and cutlery.
  - Avoid disposable decorations, balloons, and plastic favors.

- Decorate with reusable or natural materials
  - Rent décor instead of buying.
  - Use potted plants, candles, greenery, or locally grown flowers.
  - Donate flowers after the event if possible.

- Choose sustainable wedding favors
  - Consider edible favors, seed packets, small plants, or charitable donations made in guests' honor.
  - Many couples skip favors altogether.

- Minimize transportation impacts
  - Arrange carpools, shuttle services, or group transportation.
  - Provide information about public transit options.

- Manage waste thoughtfully
  - Set up recycling and composting stations if the venue allows.
  - Coordinate with local organizations to donate leftover food.

- Support responsible vendors
  - Ask vendors about their sustainability practices.
  - Prioritize local businesses to reduce transportation and support the community.

If you're short on planning time due to a demanding work schedule, prioritize these three areas first:
1. Venue choice.
2. Food and catering.
3. Reusable or rented décor.

Those decisions typically have the biggest environmental impact and can simplify many other choices.
