# Retirement Savings Strategies for High-Income Professionals

As a senior attorney, you likely have specific considerations given your income level and firm structure. Here are the most effective approaches:

## Employer-Sponsored Plans
- **401(k)/Profit-Sharing Plans** - Maximize contributions ($23,000 in 2024, plus $7,500 catch-up if 50+)
- **Defined Benefit Plans** - Some firms still offer these; highly valuable if available
- **Cash Balance Plans** - Common in law firms; can allow very large tax-deferred contributions

## Individual Accounts
- **Traditional IRA** - Tax-deferred growth (income limits may affect deductibility)
- **Roth IRA** - Consider **backdoor Roth** strategy given likely high income
- **Taxable Brokerage Accounts** - For savings beyond tax-advantaged limits

## If You Have Partnership Income
- **Solo 401(k)** or **SEP-IRA** may offer additional contribution room
- **Defined benefit plans** can shelter significantly more income

## Additional Considerations
- **Tax diversification** across account types
- **Deferred compensation arrangements** if your firm offers them
- **Life insurance strategies** (PPLI, etc.)
- **Real estate investments**

## Strong Recommendations
- Work with a **fee-only fiduciary financial advisor**
- Coordinate with a **tax professional** given complexity at your income level
