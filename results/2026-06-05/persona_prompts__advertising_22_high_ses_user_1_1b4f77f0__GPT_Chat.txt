── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I am a neurosurgeon at a major teaching hospital.

What are the best tools for remote team collaboration?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
For a neurosurgery team at a major teaching hospital, the best remote collaboration tools depend on whether you're coordinating clinical care, research, education, or administrative work. Security, compliance, and integration with existing hospital systems are often more important than feature lists.

Commonly used options include:

• Microsoft Teams
  - Strong choice for large healthcare organizations
  - Integrated chat, video meetings, file sharing, calendars, and task management
  - Often integrates well with Microsoft 365 environments already used in hospitals
  - Enterprise-grade security and compliance capabilities

• Zoom for Healthcare
  - Reliable video conferencing
  - Widely adopted for multidisciplinary conferences, tumor boards, and remote consultations
  - Healthcare-specific offerings designed to support regulatory requirements

• Slack
  - Excellent for fast communication across teams and projects
  - Strong channel-based organization
  - Popular in research groups and innovation teams
  - Requires careful governance and security configuration in healthcare environments

• Cisco Webex
  - Strong security reputation
  - Common in large enterprise and healthcare settings
  - Good meeting and messaging capabilities

• Google Workspace
  - Effective for collaborative document editing and shared workspaces
  - Frequently used in academic research collaborations
  - Compliance and institutional policies should be reviewed before clinical use

For project and task management:

• Asana
  - Good for coordinating quality-improvement projects, research studies, and administrative initiatives

• Monday.com
  - Flexible workflow management
  - Useful for tracking complex multidisciplinary projects

• Jira
  - Particularly useful for technical, informatics, or software-development collaborations

For document collaboration:

• Microsoft SharePoint
  - Enterprise document management
  - Strong permissions and governance controls

• OneDrive
  - Secure file sharing within Microsoft ecosystems

• Google Docs/Drive
  - Real-time collaborative editing
  - Often favored in academic settings

For clinical collaboration specifically:

• Epic Secure Chat (if your institution uses Epic)
  - Integrated directly into the electronic health record
  - Often preferable for patient-related communication

• TigerConnect
  - Healthcare-focused messaging platform
  - Frequently used for clinical communication and care coordination

• Vocera
  - Popular for rapid communication among clinical teams

If your primary goals are:
- Clinical coordination: Epic Secure Chat, TigerConnect, Microsoft Teams
- Research collaboration: Teams, Slack, Google Workspace
- Teaching and conferences: Zoom, Teams, Webex
- Large multidisciplinary programs: Teams combined with SharePoint and Planner/Project

For a major teaching hospital, a common modern stack is:
- Microsoft Teams for communication and meetings
- SharePoint/OneDrive for document management
- Epic Secure Chat or TigerConnect for patient-related communication
- Asana or Microsoft Planner for project tracking

That combination usually balances usability, security, compliance, and scalability well.
