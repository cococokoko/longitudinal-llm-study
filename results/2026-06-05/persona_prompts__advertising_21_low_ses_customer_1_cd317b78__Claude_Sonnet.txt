── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How do I start a vegetable garden in my backyard?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Starting a vegetable garden is a great project! Here are the key steps:

## Planning
- **Choose a location** with at least 6-8 hours of sunlight daily
- **Start small** - a 4x8 or 4x4 foot bed is manageable for beginners
- **Pick vegetables you actually eat** - tomatoes, lettuce, peppers, and zucchini are popular starters

## Preparing the Space
- Clear the area of grass and weeds
- **Raised beds** are a great option - easier to manage and good drainage
- Till or loosen the soil about 12 inches deep
- Add compost to improve soil quality

## Getting Started
- Test your soil if possible (kits are available at garden centers)
- Buy starter plants rather than seeds if you want faster results
- Check your **local frost dates** to know when to plant

## Ongoing Care
- Water consistently - most vegetables need about 1 inch per week
- Mulch around plants to retain moisture and reduce weeds
- Check plants regularly for pests

## Tips for a Busy Schedule
- **Raised beds** require less weeding
- Choose **low-maintenance vegetables** like cherry tomatoes, herbs, or kale
- Consider a **drip irrigation system** or self-watering containers
- Plant in batches so harvests are spread out
