Acutely, the stress response is a highly conserved survival mechanism driven by the sympathetic-adrenal-medullary (SAM) system and the hypothalamic-pituitary-adrenal (HPA) axis. However, chronic activation leads to a high allostatic load, fundamentally altering both physiological and cognitive homeostasis.

Physiologically, sustained elevation of glucocorticoids and catecholamines drives systemic inflammation, endothelial dysfunction, and metabolic dysregulation. Chronic cortisol exposure induces maladaptive neuroplasticity—most notably dendritic atrophy in the hippocampus and prefrontal cortex, coupled with hypertrophy in the amygdala. Clinically, this structural remodeling manifests as impaired executive function, memory deficits, and a heightened vulnerability to neuropsychiatric conditions such as major depressive disorder and anxiety.
