# Learning a Language as a Single Parent

Here's a practical approach that fits your real life:

## Work With Your Schedule, Not Against It

**Micro-learning moments** (5-15 minutes each)
- Morning routine while getting kids ready
- Commute time with audio lessons
- Lunch breaks
- After kids are in bed

## Best Methods for Limited Time

**High-impact, low-effort options:**
- **Duolingo/Babbel** - short daily streaks, no prep needed
- **Podcasts** - learn while driving or doing chores
- **YouTube channels** - watch during downtime
- **Pimsleur** - audio-only, great for commutes

## Involve Your Kids

- **Make it a game together** - kids often pick up languages quickly
- Label household items in the new language
- Watch kids' shows in your target language
- Turn it into bonding time instead of solo study

## Realistic Expectations

- **15-20 minutes daily beats 2 hours on weekends**
- Consistency matters more than intensity
- Celebrate small wins

## Free/Low-Cost Resources

- Library apps (Mango Languages often free)

- Language exchange apps like Tandem
