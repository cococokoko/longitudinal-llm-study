# How Stress Affects the Body and Mind

Stress is a natural response designed to help us survive threatening situations, but when it becomes chronic, it can take a serious toll on both physical and mental health.

## Physical Effects

When the brain perceives a threat, it triggers the release of hormones like **cortisol and adrenaline**. These chemicals prepare the body for a "fight or flight" response — increasing heart rate, tensing muscles, and sharpening focus. While useful in short bursts, prolonged exposure to these hormones can cause:

- High blood pressure and increased risk of heart disease
- Weakened immune system
- Digestive problems and headaches
- Disrupted sleep patterns
- Muscle tension and chronic pain

## Mental and Emotional Effects

Stress doesn't just affect the body — it significantly impacts the mind. Ongoing stress can lead to:

- **Anxiety and irritability**
- Difficulty concentrating and making decisions
- Memory problems
- Depression and emotional exhaustion
- Feelings of being overwhelmed or out of control

## The Cycle of Stress

One of the most challenging aspects of stress is that it can become self-reinforcing. Poor sleep caused by stress leads to reduced resilience, making everyday challenges feel even harder to manage.

## Managing Stress

Fortunately, healthy habits can interrupt this cycle. Regular exercise, adequate sleep, mindfulness practices, and strong social connections all help regulate the body's stress response.

Understanding how stress works is the first step toward managing it effectively. Recognizing early warning signs — both physical and emotional — allows people to take action before stress becomes overwhelming and harmful to long-term health.
