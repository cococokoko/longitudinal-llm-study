── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Decorating your home on a budget is often about maximizing impact while minimizing cost. A few strategies can make a space feel significantly more polished without requiring a major investment:

- Start with a plan
  - Choose a color palette and style before buying anything.
  - Focus on the rooms you use most frequently.

- Rearrange what you already own
  - Moving furniture, artwork, and accessories can dramatically change a room at no cost.
  - Remove clutter to make spaces feel larger and more intentional.

- Use paint strategically
  - A fresh coat of paint is one of the most cost-effective upgrades.
  - Consider painting an accent wall, old furniture, or cabinets.

- Shop secondhand
  - Thrift stores, estate sales, online marketplaces, and charity shops often have quality furniture and décor for a fraction of retail prices.
  - Look for solid construction rather than trendy finishes, which can often be updated.

- Focus on lighting
  - Replace harsh bulbs with warm, consistent lighting.
  - Floor lamps and table lamps can make a room feel more inviting without expensive renovations.

- Add textiles
  - Throw pillows, blankets, curtains, and area rugs can introduce color and texture.
  - Mixing inexpensive textiles can create a more finished look.

- Create a gallery wall
  - Frame personal photos, prints, or downloadable artwork.
  - Consistent frames can make inexpensive art look more sophisticated.

- Incorporate plants
  - Houseplants add color, texture, and life to a room.
  - Many attractive varieties are inexpensive and easy to maintain.

- DIY where practical
  - Simple projects such as painting furniture, building shelves, or updating hardware can provide a high return for relatively little money.

- Upgrade small details
  - New cabinet handles, switch plates, curtain rods, and decorative accessories can refresh a room without major expense.

A useful budgeting approach is to allocate most of your funds to a few high-impact items—such as a rug, lighting fixture, or statement chair—and save money on accessories and decorative accents. This often creates a more cohesive and expensive-looking result than spreading a small budget across many purchases.
