# Mastering Digital Photography

Here's a structured overview to help you build strong skills:

---

## Core Technical Foundations

### Exposure Triangle
- **Aperture** (f-stop) — controls depth of field and light
- **Shutter Speed** — freezes or blurs motion
- **ISO** — sensor sensitivity; manage noise carefully

### Shooting Modes
- **Manual (M)** — full control
- **Aperture Priority (Av)** — great for portraits/landscapes
- **Shutter Priority (Tv)** — useful for action shots

---

## Composition Principles
- Rule of thirds
- Leading lines
- Framing within the frame
- Negative space
- Light direction and quality

---

## Key Skills to Develop
- **RAW shooting** vs. JPEG — RAW gives far more editing latitude
- **Histogram reading** — avoid blown highlights
- **White balance** — critical for accurate color
- **Focusing modes** — single vs. continuous AF

---

## Post-Processing
- **Lightroom** — industry standard for organization/editing
- **Photoshop** — advanced compositing
- **Capture One** — preferred by many professionals

---

## Practical Suggestions
- Shoot daily, even with a phone
- Study light at different times of day
- Analyze photos you admire technically
