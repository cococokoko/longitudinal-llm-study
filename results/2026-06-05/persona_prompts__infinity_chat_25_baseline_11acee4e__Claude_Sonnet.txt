── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How to say a formal thank you for your time in the subject line of an email?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here are some formal ways to say **"Thank you for your time"** in an email subject line:

## Direct Options
- **"Thank You for Your Time"**
- **"Thank You for Meeting With Me"**
- **"Thank You for Your Consideration"**

## More Specific Options
- **"Thank You for Your Time – [Meeting/Interview Date]"**
- **"Thank You for the Opportunity to Meet"**
- **"Appreciation for Your Time and Insights"**

## Professional Variations
- **"Grateful for Your Time – [Your Name]"**
- **"Thank You – [Topic of Meeting]"**
- **"Following Up With Gratitude – [Context]"**

## Tips
- **Be specific** when possible (e.g., mention the interview or meeting)
- **Keep it concise** and clear
- **Add context** so the recipient knows what it refers to
- Avoid being overly casual in formal settings

The most widely used and safe option is simply:
> **"Thank You for Your Time"**
