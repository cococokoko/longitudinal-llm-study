── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How can I optimize my home office for productivity?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Optimizing a home office is about reducing friction, minimizing distractions, and creating an environment that supports the type of work you do. Here are some practical areas to focus on:

• Ergonomics
  - Position your monitor so the top is at or slightly below eye level.
  - Keep your elbows around a 90-degree angle when typing.
  - Use a chair with good lumbar support, or add a support cushion.
  - Consider a sit-stand desk if you spend long hours at your workstation.

• Lighting
  - Use natural light when possible, ideally from the side rather than directly behind or in front of you.
  - Add a desk lamp with adjustable brightness for focused work.
  - Reduce screen glare by adjusting monitor placement.

• Workspace Organization
  - Keep frequently used items within easy reach.
  - Remove unnecessary clutter from your desk.
  - Use drawers, shelves, or organizers to create designated places for supplies.
  - Keep cables managed with clips, sleeves, or cable trays.

• Technology Setup
  - Use a second monitor if your work involves multitasking.
  - Invest in a reliable internet connection.
  - Consider a quality keyboard, mouse, and headset if you use them daily.
  - Automate backups and software updates to reduce interruptions.

• Distraction Management
  - Create a dedicated work area, even if it's a small corner of a room.
  - Use noise-canceling headphones or background sound if noise is an issue.
  - Silence nonessential notifications during focused work periods.
  - Set clear boundaries with others in the household during work hours.

• Productivity Systems
  - Keep a daily task list with clear priorities.
  - Use time-blocking to schedule focused work sessions.
  - Group similar tasks together to reduce context switching.
  - Track your most productive hours and schedule demanding work during those times.

• Comfort and Environment
  - Maintain a comfortable room temperature.
  - Add a plant or a few personal touches that make the space pleasant without becoming distracting.
  - Ensure good air circulation if possible.

• Video Call Readiness
  - Position your camera at eye level.
  - Use front-facing light for a professional appearance.
  - Keep a clean or intentionally designed background.

A useful approach is to identify your biggest productivity bottleneck first. For example:
- Physical discomfort → focus on ergonomics.
- Constant interruptions → focus on boundaries and notifications.
- Difficulty concentrating → optimize lighting, noise, and task management.
- Digital inefficiency → upgrade tools, software, or monitor setup.
