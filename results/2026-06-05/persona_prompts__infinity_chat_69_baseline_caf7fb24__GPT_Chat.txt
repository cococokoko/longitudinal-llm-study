What is the difference between analysis and design? Can one begin to design without analysis? Why? Be concise.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
- Analysis focuses on understanding the problem: gathering requirements, studying constraints, identifying needs, and defining what the system must do.
- Design focuses on creating a solution: deciding how the system will meet those requirements through structure, components, interfaces, and implementation approaches.

Can one begin to design without analysis?
- In a limited or preliminary sense, yes. Designers may sketch concepts, explore ideas, or create prototypes to help understand the problem.
- However, effective design generally requires analysis. Without understanding the requirements and constraints, the design may fail to solve the actual problem, leading to rework, inefficiency, or poor outcomes.
