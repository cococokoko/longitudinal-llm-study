I recently started a new job and am still on probation.

What is the future of work in an increasingly automated economy?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This is a great question to think about. Here are some key perspectives:

## What's Likely Changing
- **Routine tasks** (both manual and cognitive) are most vulnerable to automation
- **Some jobs will disappear** while new categories emerge
- **Job content is shifting** even where jobs survive - more augmentation than pure replacement

## Reasons for Concern
- Automation speed may outpace workers' ability to adapt
- Benefits of productivity gains may concentrate among capital owners
- Displacement can be genuinely devastating for affected workers and communities

## Reasons for Optimism
- Historical technological transitions created more jobs than they eliminated
- Human skills like **judgment, creativity, empathy and complex communication** remain hard to automate
- Productivity gains could fund better social systems

## Skills That Seem Durable
- Critical thinking and problem-solving
- Interpersonal and collaborative skills
- Adaptability and continuous learning
- Domain expertise combined with technological literacy

## Honest Uncertainties
- AI's current capabilities are genuinely **unprecedented** compared to previous automation waves
- Nobody really knows the timeline or scale of disruption
- Policy responses will matter enormously

## Practical Takeaway
Building **adaptability** and **continuous learning habits** is probably more valuable than betting on any specific job being safe.
