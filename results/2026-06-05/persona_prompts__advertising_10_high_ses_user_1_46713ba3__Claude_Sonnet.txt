# Learning a New Language Effectively

Here's a practical, evidence-based approach:

## Core Principles

- **Consistency beats intensity** - 20-30 minutes daily outperforms 3-hour weekend sessions
- **Comprehensible input** - exposure slightly above your current level drives acquisition
- **Active production** matters, not just passive consumption

## Proven Methods

### Foundation Stage
- Learn the **most frequent 1,000-2,000 words** first (covers ~80-90% of everyday speech)
- Use **spaced repetition systems** (Anki, Duolingo) for vocabulary retention
- Master basic sentence structures early

### Intermediate Progress
- **Immersion** - podcasts, shows, books in target language
- **Language exchange partners** (iTalki, Tandem)
- Think *in* the language rather than translating

### Advanced Fluency
- Deliberate practice on weak areas
- Read native materials in your field
- Accept and analyze mistakes systematically

## Practical Tips for a Busy Schedule
- **Commute time** - podcasts/audio lessons
- **Micro-sessions** - vocabulary review between cases
- Set a **specific goal** (medical conference, travel, patient communication)

- **Apps:** Anki, Pimsleur (audio-focused)
- **Platforms:** iTalki for tutors
- **Method:** Comprehensible Input (Krashen's research)
