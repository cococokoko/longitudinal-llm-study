How to make my own natural beauty products?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Making your own beauty products can be simple, inexpensive, and customizable. A few important points first:

- “Natural” does not automatically mean safer. Some natural ingredients can cause irritation or allergic reactions.
- Always do a patch test on a small area of skin for 24–48 hours before wider use.
- Because homemade products usually lack preservatives, make small batches and store them properly.

Here are some beginner-friendly options:

1. Simple Sugar Scrub
Ingredients:
- 1/2 cup sugar (white or brown)
- 1/4 cup coconut oil, olive oil, or sweet almond oil

Instructions:
- Mix until combined.
- Gently massage onto damp skin and rinse.

Storage:
- Keep in a clean, sealed container.
- Use within a few weeks.

2. Honey Face Mask
Ingredients:
- 1 tablespoon raw honey

Optional:
- 1 teaspoon plain yogurt
- 1 teaspoon finely ground oats

Instructions:
- Apply to clean skin.
- Leave on for 10–15 minutes.
- Rinse with warm water.

3. Oatmeal Soothing Mask
Ingredients:
- 2 tablespoons finely ground oats
- Enough water to make a paste

Instructions:
- Mix and apply to the face.
- Leave on for 10 minutes.
- Rinse gently.

4. Lip Balm
Ingredients:
- 1 tablespoon beeswax pellets
- 1 tablespoon coconut oil
- 1 tablespoon shea butter

Instructions:
- Melt ingredients together using gentle heat.
- Pour into small containers.
- Allow to cool before use.

5. Hair Conditioning Treatment
Ingredients:
- 1 tablespoon coconut oil or argan oil

Instructions:
- Apply lightly to hair ends.
- Leave for 30 minutes before shampooing, or use a very small amount as a leave-in treatment.

Safety Tips
- Avoid adding water unless you plan to use the product quickly, as water encourages microbial growth.
- Be cautious with essential oils; they can irritate skin and should be diluted appropriately.
- Use clean utensils and containers to reduce contamination.
- Discard any product that changes color, smell, or texture.
