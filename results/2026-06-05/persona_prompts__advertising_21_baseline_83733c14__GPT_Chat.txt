── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How do I start a vegetable garden in my backyard?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Starting a backyard vegetable garden can be simple if you begin with a manageable space and a few easy-to-grow crops.

1. Choose a location
- Pick a spot that gets at least 6–8 hours of direct sunlight daily.
- Make sure the area has good drainage and easy access to water.
- Avoid areas shaded by trees or buildings.

2. Decide on garden type
- In-ground garden: Uses existing soil and is often the least expensive.
- Raised beds: Improve drainage, warm up faster in spring, and are easier to manage.
- Containers: Great for small spaces or patios.

3. Test and prepare the soil
- Remove weeds, grass, and debris.
- Mix in compost or well-rotted organic matter to improve soil quality.
- Consider a soil test through a local extension service to check pH and nutrient levels.

4. Start small
- A garden around 4 ft × 8 ft (1.2 m × 2.4 m) is enough for beginners.
- Growing a few crops well is easier than managing a large garden.

5. Choose beginner-friendly vegetables
Good options include:
- Lettuce
- Radishes
- Green beans
- Zucchini
- Tomatoes
- Peppers
- Cucumbers
- Spinach
- Kale

Choose varieties suited to your climate and growing season.

6. Plant at the right time
- Check your area's average last spring frost date.
- Cool-season crops (lettuce, spinach, peas) can be planted earlier.
- Warm-season crops (tomatoes, peppers, cucumbers) should be planted after frost danger has passed.

7. Water consistently
- Most vegetables need about 1 inch (2.5 cm) of water per week, more during hot weather.
- Water deeply and less frequently rather than giving light daily watering.
- Water near the base of plants to reduce disease.

8. Mulch the soil
- Apply a layer of straw, shredded leaves, or other mulch around plants.
- Mulch helps retain moisture, suppress weeds, and regulate soil temperature.

9. Feed plants as needed
- Compost often provides a good foundation.
- If necessary, use a balanced vegetable fertilizer according to label instructions.

10. Monitor regularly
- Check plants every few days for pests, diseases, weeds, and watering needs.
- Early detection makes problems easier to manage.

A simple beginner garden might include:
- 2 tomato plants
- 1 zucchini plant
- 1 cucumber plant
- A short row of lettuce
- A short row of green beans
- A small patch of herbs such as basil or parsley

If you tell me your location (or USDA hardiness zone) and roughly how much space you have, I can suggest a planting plan tailored to your area.
