How do I start a vegetable garden in my backyard?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Starting a backyard vegetable garden can be as simple or as ambitious as you want. A good approach is to begin with a manageable space and expand as you gain experience.

1. Choose a suitable location
- Pick a spot that receives at least 6–8 hours of direct sunlight daily.
- Ensure the area has good drainage and is reasonably close to a water source.
- Avoid locations where large trees will compete for light and nutrients.

2. Decide on the garden type
- In-ground garden: Often the most economical option if you have decent soil.
- Raised beds: Improve drainage, reduce soil compaction, and make maintenance easier.
- Containers: Useful for small spaces or patios.

3. Assess and improve the soil
- Test your soil if possible; many extension services offer inexpensive testing.
- Add compost or well-rotted organic matter to improve soil structure and fertility.
- Remove weeds and debris before planting.

4. Select crops appropriate for your climate
For beginners, reliable vegetables often include:
- Lettuce
- Bush beans
- Radishes
- Zucchini
- Tomatoes
- Peppers
- Kale

Check local planting calendars to determine the best planting dates for your region.

5. Plan the layout
- Place taller crops where they won't shade shorter plants.
- Follow spacing recommendations on seed packets or plant labels.
- Leave pathways so you can access all parts of the garden without stepping on the soil.

6. Plant
- Some vegetables are usually started from seed (e.g., beans, radishes, carrots).
- Others are often easier to grow from transplants (e.g., tomatoes, peppers).
- Water thoroughly after planting.

7. Water consistently
- Most vegetable gardens need about 1 inch (2.5 cm) of water per week, though needs vary with weather and soil.
- Water deeply rather than lightly and frequently.
- Morning watering is generally preferable.

8. Mulch and maintain
- Apply mulch to help conserve moisture and suppress weeds.
- Check regularly for pests and diseases.
- Remove weeds before they become established.

9. Harvest regularly
- Many vegetables produce more when harvested frequently.
- Harvest at the recommended stage for the best flavor and texture.

A simple first garden might consist of one 4 × 8 ft (about 1.2 × 2.4 m) raised bed containing:
- 2 tomato plants
- 2 pepper plants
- A row of lettuce
- A row of bush beans
- A few herb plants such as basil or parsley
