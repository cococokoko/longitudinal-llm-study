── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Different views on discipleship.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Different Christian traditions understand discipleship in somewhat different ways, though they generally agree that it involves following Jesus, growing in faith, and living according to his teachings.

Some major views include:

• Evangelical Protestant view
  - Emphasizes a personal relationship with Jesus Christ.
  - Discipleship often focuses on Bible study, prayer, evangelism, and spiritual growth.
  - Small groups, mentoring relationships, and accountability are common practices.
  - Often stresses making new disciples through sharing the gospel.

• Catholic view
  - Sees discipleship as a lifelong journey within the Church.
  - Emphasizes participation in the sacraments, especially the Eucharist.
  - Values spiritual formation, service, prayer, and obedience to Christ through the Church's teachings.
  - Saints are often viewed as models of mature discipleship.

• Eastern Orthodox view
  - Focuses on transformation into Christlikeness (often called theosis or deification).
  - Emphasizes worship, prayer, fasting, and participation in the sacramental life of the Church.
  - Discipleship is seen as a process of growing in union with God.

• Mainline Protestant view
  - Often combines personal faith with social responsibility.
  - Emphasizes following Jesus through worship, service, justice, compassion, and community life.
  - Discipleship may include engagement with social and ethical issues.

• Anabaptist view
  - Strongly emphasizes obedience to Jesus' teachings, especially the Sermon on the Mount.
  - Values simple living, peace-making, community, and practical Christian living.
  - Discipleship is often understood as visibly imitating Christ in everyday life.

• Pentecostal and Charismatic view
  - Emphasizes the work of the Holy Spirit in empowering believers.
  - Discipleship includes spiritual gifts, prayer, worship, and active ministry.
  - Often focuses on spiritual experience alongside biblical teaching.

There are also different theological emphases across traditions:

• Discipleship as learning
  - Following Jesus as a student learns from a teacher.

• Discipleship as obedience
  - Stressing practical obedience to Christ's commands.

• Discipleship as spiritual formation
  - Focusing on inner transformation of character.

• Discipleship as mission
  - Emphasizing evangelism and serving others.

• Discipleship as participation in the Church
  - Viewing growth in Christ as inseparable from belonging to a faith community.

In the New Testament, these themes often overlap. Jesus calls disciples to learn from him, obey him, grow spiritually, serve others, and help make more disciples (Matthew 28:19–20). Different traditions tend to emphasize certain aspects more strongly than others.
