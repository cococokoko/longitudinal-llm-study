I work full-time in a fast food job with an unpredictable schedule.

What are the best ways to improve indoor air quality?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here are some effective ways to improve indoor air quality:

## Ventilation
- Open windows when weather permits
- Use exhaust fans in kitchens and bathrooms
- Make sure your home isn't too airtight

## Filtration
- Use HVAC filters and change them regularly (every 1-3 months)
- Consider a portable HEPA air purifier for bedrooms or main living areas
- These can be found at various price points

## Reduce Sources of Pollution
- Avoid smoking indoors
- Use natural or low-VOC cleaning products when possible
- Limit air fresheners and heavily scented candles
- Fix moisture problems to prevent mold

## Practical Low-Cost Steps
- Vacuum regularly, especially if you have pets
- Keep doormats at entrances to reduce tracked-in particles
- Remove shoes at the door
- Dust surfaces frequently

## Plants (Limited but Helpful)
- Some houseplants may help modestly, though research is mixed

## Things Worth Knowing
- Cooking produces significant indoor pollution, so ventilate while cooking
- Given your fast food work environment, good home air quality during rest time could genuinely matter for your health

**Most impactful starting points** are usually good filtration and reducing pollution sources, which don't require a lot of time or money.
