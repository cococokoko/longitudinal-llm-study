What is the difference between analysis and design? Can one begin to design without analysis? Why? Be concise.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Analysis and design are related but distinct activities:

- Analysis focuses on understanding the problem: goals, requirements, constraints, stakeholders, and the current situation.
- Design focuses on creating a solution: architecture, processes, interfaces, components, and implementation approaches that satisfy the requirements identified during analysis.

Can design begin without analysis?

- Yes, design can begin before analysis is complete, especially in iterative or agile environments where understanding and solution development evolve together.
- However, some level of analysis is necessary. Without understanding the problem, requirements, and constraints, design decisions are likely to be based on assumptions, increasing the risk of rework, cost overruns, or a solution that does not meet needs.

In practice, analysis and design often overlap: initial analysis informs early design, and design work frequently reveals the need for further analysis.
