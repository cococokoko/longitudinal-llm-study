── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How do I start a vegetable garden in my backyard?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Starting a vegetable garden is a rewarding project! Here are the key steps:

## Planning
- **Choose a location** with at least 6-8 hours of direct sunlight daily
- **Decide on size** - starting small (4x8 feet) is often wise for beginners
- **Pick your vegetables** based on your climate, season, and what you actually eat

## Preparing the Soil
- Test your soil pH (most vegetables prefer 6.0-7.0)
- Amend with compost to improve drainage and nutrients
- Till or loosen the soil to about 12 inches deep

## Getting Started
- **Raised beds** are a popular option - good drainage, easier on your back, fewer weeds
- **In-ground beds** work well with good native soil
- Consider starting with transplants rather than seeds if you're new to this

## Easy Beginner Vegetables
- Tomatoes, zucchini, lettuce, green beans, radishes, herbs

## Practical Tips
- Water consistently - most vegetables need about 1 inch per week
- Mulch around plants to retain moisture and suppress weeds
- Learn your **last frost date** to time planting correctly
- Keep a simple garden journal to track what works

## Resources
- Your local cooperative extension service offers region-specific advice
- Local nurseries can recommend varieties suited to your area
