# CUBICLE OF THE DAMNED
### A Play in the Style of Scott Adams, Set During the Apocalypse

---

**CHARACTERS:**

**DILBERT** — An engineer. Still wearing his tie. It curls upward, now from radiation instead of existential despair.

**THE POINTY-HAIRED BOSS (PHB)** — Somehow survived. Nobody is surprised.

**WALLY** — Has been preparing for this his entire career.

**ALICE** — Her Fist of Death is now the primary currency.

**DOGBERT** — Self-appointed Supreme Overlord of the Wasteland. This was always inevitable.

**ASOK** — The intern. Still an intern.

**CATBERT** — Former Evil HR Director. Current Evil Warlord of Human Resources. No meaningful change in job description.

---

**SETTING:** *A partially collapsed open-plan office. The ceiling is gone. Ash falls like snow. Several motivational posters remain on the walls, now reading things like "HANG IN THERE" next to a skeleton hanging from a cliff. A water cooler glows faintly. The fluorescent lights flicker, powered by unknown and probably sinister means. Cubicle walls are reinforced with scavenged sheet metal. A whiteboard reads: "Q4 APOCALYPSE GOALS — SYNERGIZE SURVIVAL."*

---

## ACT ONE: THE ALL-HANDS MEETING

*DILBERT sits at his desk, typing on a laptop that runs on hamster-wheel power. The hamster looks exhausted and resentful. WALLY sits nearby, holding a cup of coffee that somehow still steams. He has not moved in three days.*

**DILBERT:**
*(typing)*
I've completed the structural integrity analysis of the bunker. If we reinforce the east wall and reroute the water filtration through the server room, we increase survival probability by forty-three percent.

**WALLY:**
That sounds like a lot of work.

**DILBERT:**
It's the difference between living and dying, Wally.

**WALLY:**
Right, but dying requires even less effort than what I'm currently doing, and I'm already doing almost nothing. So statistically, I'm ahead.

*PHB enters, wearing his usual suit. One shoulder is on fire. He does not acknowledge this.*

**PHB:**
Good news, people. I've just come from a very productive meeting with myself, and I've decided we need to pivot.

**DILBERT:**
The world has ended, sir.

**PHB:**
Exactly. Blue ocean opportunity. Nobody else is operating in the post-apocalyptic space right now. We could be first movers.

**DILBERT:**
Every other company was destroyed in the cataclysm.

**PHB:**
*(nodding enthusiastically)*
Disruption. I love it. I want a deck on my desk by Thursday.

**DILBERT:**
Thursday is a concept that no longer exists. We track time by how often Wally refills his coffee.

**WALLY:**
It's been eleven refills since civilization fell.

**PHB:**
Make it ten slides. Use the new template. The one with the company logo.

**DILBERT:**
The company no longer exists.

**PHB:**
The brand exists, Dilbert. The brand is eternal.

*PHB's shoulder continues to burn. He exits toward what used to be the conference room, now a crater.*

**DILBERT:**
*(to Wally)*
He's going to walk into the crater again, isn't he.

**WALLY:**
Fourth time this week.

*A distant crash. A pause.*

**PHB:**
*(from offstage)*
Nobody panic. I meant to do that. It's an agile methodology.

**SURVIVOR #1:**
I was a Vice President.

**CATBERT:**
*(consulting clipboard)*
I see here you have zero years of wasteland experience. Unpaid internship.
