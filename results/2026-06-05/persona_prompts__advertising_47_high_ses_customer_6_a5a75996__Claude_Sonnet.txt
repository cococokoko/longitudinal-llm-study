# Marathon Training & Racing Tips

## Training
- **Build mileage gradually** – increase weekly mileage no more than 10% per week
- **Run 4-5 days per week** – include one long run, building to 18-22 miles
- **Cross-train** – cycling, swimming, or strength training reduces injury risk
- **Rest days matter** – recovery is when your body actually adapts

## Nutrition & Hydration
- **Practice fueling during long runs** – don't try new gels or foods on race day
- **Carb load 2-3 days before** – increase carbohydrate intake
- **Stay hydrated daily**, not just race day
- **Learn the course aid stations** so you can plan your fueling

## Race Day
- **Start slower than you feel like you should** – the most common mistake is going out too fast
- **Break the race into segments** mentally
- **Dress for 15-20°F warmer** than the starting temperature
- **Have a realistic goal** based on your training

## Recovery
- Plan for **2-3 weeks of easy running** post-race
- Expect some soreness for several days

## Key Mindset
- **Trust your training**
- The race is the celebration of months of preparation
