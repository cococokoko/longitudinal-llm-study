── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
My girlfriend is buying a house. I plan to marry her in a year or so. I don't know if it's fair for me to pay for the house or not. What's the best solution?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This is a really common situation and worth thinking through carefully. Here are some practical considerations:

## Key Questions to Ask Yourself

- Are you moving in together now or after marriage?
- Will you contribute to mortgage payments?
- What happens if the relationship doesn't work out?

## Realistic Options

**Option 1: She buys it solely in her name**
- You pay rent or contribute to expenses
- Clean and simple if things don't work out
- She builds equity you don't share

**Option 2: You contribute but get legal protection**
- A cohabitation agreement can document your contributions
- Could establish your equity stake formally
- Protects both of you

**Option 3: Wait and buy together after marriage**
- Cleaner legal situation as spouses
- Might delay her purchase unnecessarily

**Option 4: You contribute to down payment as a documented loan**
- Creates a paper trail
- Protects your money if relationship ends

## Honest Considerations

- **A year is not guaranteed** - relationships change
- Contributing significantly to someone else's asset without legal protection is genuinely risky
- Marriage changes property rights significantly depending on your state/country

## Strong Recommendation

**Talk to a family law attorney** before making financial decisions - the rules vary significantly by location and the stakes are high.
