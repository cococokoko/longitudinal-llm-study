── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I am a neurosurgeon at a major teaching hospital.

How can I make my home more energy efficient?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here are some practical ways to improve your home's energy efficiency:

## Quick/Low-Cost Steps
- Switch to LED lighting throughout
- Install a programmable or smart thermostat
- Seal air leaks around windows, doors, and outlets
- Add weatherstripping to doors
- Use power strips to eliminate phantom/standby power draw

## Moderate Investments
- Add insulation to attic, walls, or crawl spaces
- Upgrade to energy-efficient appliances (look for Energy Star ratings)
- Install low-flow fixtures to reduce water heating costs
- Replace old windows with double or triple-pane versions

## Larger Investments
- Solar panels (often with good ROI over time)
- Heat pump systems for heating/cooling
- Tankless or heat pump water heaters
- EV charger if you drive electric

## Behavioral Changes
- Wash clothes in cold water
- Run dishwashers and laundry during off-peak hours
- Adjust thermostat settings when away or sleeping
- Use ceiling fans strategically

## Getting Started
- **Home energy audit** - many utilities offer these free or subsidized
- Check for **federal tax credits** (Inflation Reduction Act offers significant incentives)
- Contact your **utility company** for rebate programs
