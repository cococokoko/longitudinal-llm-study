# Improving Your Credit Score as a Busy Single Parent

Here are practical steps organized by impact and effort:

## Highest Impact Actions

**Pay bills on time**
- Set up autopay for minimum payments
- Payment history is ~35% of your score

**Reduce credit card balances**
- Try to keep balances below 30% of your credit limit
- This affects ~30% of your score

## Relatively Quick Wins

- **Check your credit report for errors** at AnnualCreditReport.com (free)
- Dispute any mistakes you find - errors are common
- **Become an authorized user** on a trusted family member's good account

## Things to Avoid

- Don't close old credit card accounts
- Avoid applying for multiple new credit cards at once
- Don't miss payments even during tight months

## Realistic Timeline

| Timeframe | Expected Progress |
|-----------|------------------|
| 1-3 months | Small improvements from reduced balances |
| 6 months | Noticeable improvement with consistent payments |
| 12+ months | Significant rebuilding possible |

## As a Single Parent Specifically

- Look into **credit counseling nonprofits** (often free)
- Some utilities report on-time payments now, which can help
