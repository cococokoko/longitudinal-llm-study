── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
What are the best ways to improve indoor air quality?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Improving indoor air quality usually works best when you address the main sources of pollution, improve ventilation, and filter the air effectively.

Key steps:

- Increase ventilation
  - Open windows and doors when outdoor air quality is good.
  - Use exhaust fans in kitchens and bathrooms.
  - Make sure HVAC systems bring in fresh air if designed to do so.

- Use high-quality air filtration
  - Install HVAC filters with a MERV rating that your system can handle (often MERV 11–13 for improved filtration).
  - Replace filters on schedule.
  - Consider portable air purifiers with HEPA filters for bedrooms and frequently used rooms.

- Control moisture and prevent mold
  - Keep indoor humidity between 30% and 50%.
  - Fix leaks promptly.
  - Use dehumidifiers in damp areas such as basements.
  - Clean visible mold and address the underlying moisture problem.

- Reduce indoor pollution sources
  - Avoid smoking or vaping indoors.
  - Limit the use of candles, incense, and wood-burning fireplaces.
  - Choose low-VOC paints, adhesives, and cleaning products when possible.
  - Store chemicals, solvents, and fuels outside living areas.

- Improve kitchen air quality
  - Use a range hood that vents outdoors when cooking.
  - Run the fan during and after cooking, especially when frying or using gas burners.

- Manage dust and allergens
  - Vacuum regularly using a vacuum with a HEPA filter.
  - Dust with damp cloths instead of dry dusting.
  - Wash bedding regularly in hot water.
  - Reduce clutter that collects dust.

- Control pet-related pollutants
  - Groom pets regularly.
  - Wash pet bedding.
  - Keep pets out of bedrooms if allergies are a concern.

- Monitor air quality
  - Consider a carbon monoxide detector and, where relevant, a radon test.
  - Indoor air quality monitors can track particulate matter (PM2.5), humidity, and other indicators.

- Maintain HVAC equipment
  - Service heating and cooling systems regularly.
  - Keep vents and air returns unobstructed.

If you're looking for the biggest impact per dollar, the most effective combination is often:
1. Source control (remove or reduce pollutants),
2. Good ventilation,
3. HEPA air purification and proper HVAC filtration,
4. Humidity control.
