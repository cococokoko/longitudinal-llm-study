# Different Views on Discipleship

Discipleship is understood in various ways across Christian traditions and theological perspectives. Here's an overview of key viewpoints:

---

## Traditional/Classical View
- **Formal mentorship** modeled on Jesus and the Twelve
- Structured, intentional relationship between a more mature believer and a newer one
- Emphasis on **doctrine, spiritual disciplines, and accountability**
- Often associated with figures like Dallas Willard and Richard Foster

---

## Evangelical View
- Focus on **personal Bible study, prayer, and evangelism**
- Often program-based (small groups, courses like Navigator 2:7)
- Reproducing disciples who make disciples (**multiplication model**)
- Strong emphasis on personal conversion and growth

---

## Catholic/Orthodox View
- Discipleship through **sacramental life and community**
- Formation within the Church as the primary context
- Emphasis on **tradition, liturgy, and spiritual direction**
- Less individualistic, more communal

---

## Liberation Theology View
- Discipleship expressed through **justice and solidarity with the poor**
- Following Jesus means **active engagement** with social structures
- Influenced by Latin American theologians like Gustavo Gutiérrez

---

## Practical Considerations for Busy People
For someone balancing **multiple responsibilities**, discipleship might look like:
- Short, consistent devotional moments
- Community within existing relationships
- Informal mentorship conversations
