That's a genuinely interesting philosophical question, and your work situation actually connects to it in a meaningful way.

## The short answer: Yes, it's impossible to know everything

Here's why this is pretty well-established:

**Practical limits**
- There's simply too much information - it grows faster than any person could absorb
- Time and attention are finite
- Your unpredictable schedule is actually a good real-world example of how circumstances limit what you can even *plan* to learn

**Deeper logical limits**
- Gödel's incompleteness theorems showed there are mathematical truths that can't be proven within any system
- Some things are genuinely uncertain or unknowable in principle
- You can't fully know your own cognitive blind spots

**What's worth considering though**
- "Knowing everything" and "knowing enough" are very different goals
- Expertise in specific areas is genuinely achievable
- Understanding *how to find* information may matter more than storing it

## The honest nuance
Some people debate *what counts* as knowledge, and whether certain things are unknowable vs. just currently unknown.

But the straightforward answer is that **no individual, institution, or system has ever come close to knowing everything**, and there are good reasons to think it's not just difficult but actually impossible.
