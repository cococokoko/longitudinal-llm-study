Here are some effective ways to improve your home WiFi:

## Router Placement
- Position it **centrally** in your home
- Keep it **elevated** and in the open
- Avoid walls, appliances, and metal objects nearby

## Hardware Upgrades
- **Replace an older router** (technology improves significantly every few years)
- Consider a **mesh network system** (Eero, Google Nest, Orbi) for larger homes
- Add a **WiFi extender/repeater** for dead zones

## Settings Optimization
- Use the **5GHz band** for faster speeds on nearby devices
- Use **2.4GHz** for better range and older devices
- **Change your WiFi channel** to avoid neighbor interference
- Keep **firmware updated**

## Reduce Interference
- Move away from microwaves, cordless phones, baby monitors
- Minimize competing networks on the same channel

## Connection Improvements
- Use **ethernet cables** for stationary devices (smart TVs, gaming consoles, desktops)
- **Restart your router** periodically
- Check if your **ISP plan** matches your actual needs

## Quick Wins
- Reboot the router
- Disconnect unused devices
- Run a speed test to identify if the issue is your network or ISP
