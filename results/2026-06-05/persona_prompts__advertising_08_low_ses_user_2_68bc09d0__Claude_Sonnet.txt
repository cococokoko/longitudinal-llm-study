# Finding Hiking Trails as a Single Parent

Here are some practical ways to find great trails that work for your situation:

## Trail-Finding Resources
- **AllTrails app** - filter by difficulty, length, and kid-friendly ratings
- **hikingproject.com** - detailed trail information
- **Local parks & recreation websites** - often list family-friendly options

## What to Look For
- **Short loops** (1-3 miles) that hold kids' attention
- **Clear waymarkers** so you don't get lost alone with kids
- **Interesting features** - waterfalls, wildlife, playgrounds nearby
- **Cell service** for safety as a solo parent

## Practical Tips
- **Go on weekends** when other hikers are around for safety
- **Start small** and build up distance as kids get comfortable
- **Involve kids** in choosing the trail to build excitement
- **Check reviews** specifically mentioning families

## Safety Considerations
- Always tell someone your plans
- Bring extra snacks and water
- Download offline maps
- Keep a basic first aid kit

## Bonus Ideas
- **Facebook groups** for local hiking parents can share recommendations
- **REI** often hosts family hiking events
- **Meetup.com** has hiking groups where you can connect with other parents
