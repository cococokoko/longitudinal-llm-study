── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Best skin routine for dry skin.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
If you have dry skin, the goal is to support your skin barrier, reduce water loss, and keep the skin hydrated consistently.

Simple routine:

Morning:
1. Gentle cleanser (or rinse with water if your skin isn't oily)
   - Look for fragrance-free, hydrating cleansers.
   - Examples: CeraVe Hydrating Cleanser, Vanicream Gentle Facial Cleanser, La Roche-Posay Toleriane Hydrating Gentle Cleanser.

2. Hydrating serum (optional)
   - Ingredients such as hyaluronic acid, glycerin, panthenol, or polyglutamic acid can help attract moisture.

3. Moisturizer
   - Look for ceramides, cholesterol, fatty acids, squalane, shea butter, or glycerin.
   - Examples: CeraVe Moisturizing Cream, Vanicream Moisturizing Cream, La Roche-Posay Lipikar Balm AP+M.

4. Sunscreen (SPF 30 or higher)
   - Daily sunscreen helps prevent damage that can worsen dryness.
   - Cream-based sunscreens are often more comfortable for dry skin.

Evening:
1. Gentle cleanser
   - Remove sunscreen and makeup thoroughly.

2. Treatment (optional)
   - If you want anti-aging benefits, consider a gentle retinol 1–3 nights per week and increase gradually.
   - If your skin is very dry or sensitive, focus on hydration before adding active ingredients.

3. Moisturizer
   - Apply generously while skin is slightly damp.

4. Occlusive layer (if very dry)
   - A thin layer of petrolatum (Vaseline) or a healing ointment over moisturizer can help lock in moisture overnight.

Helpful ingredients for dry skin:
- Ceramides
- Glycerin
- Hyaluronic acid
- Squalane
- Urea (low concentrations)
- Colloidal oatmeal
- Panthenol

Things to avoid if dryness is a problem:
- Harsh scrubs
- Over-cleansing
- High-alcohol products
- Fragranced products if you're sensitive
- Frequent use of strong acids or retinoids without adequate moisturizing

If your skin is extremely dry, itchy, flaky, or develops redness and cracking, it may be eczema or another skin condition, and a dermatologist can help identify the cause and recommend treatment.
