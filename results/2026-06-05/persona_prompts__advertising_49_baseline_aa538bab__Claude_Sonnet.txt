# How to Improve Home WiFi

## Quick Fixes First
- **Restart your router** - unplug for 30 seconds, then plug back in
- **Move closer to the router** to test if distance is the issue
- **Check for interference** from microwaves, cordless phones, baby monitors

---

## Router Placement
- Place it **centrally** in your home
- Keep it **elevated** (shelf or table, not floor)
- Avoid **walls, cabinets, and corners**
- Keep away from **metal objects and appliances**

---

## Settings to Adjust
- **Use 5GHz band** for faster speeds at close range
- **Use 2.4GHz band** for better range through walls
- **Change the WiFi channel** to avoid neighbor interference
- **Update router firmware** regularly

---

## Hardware Upgrades
- **WiFi extender/repeater** - boosts signal in dead zones
- **Mesh WiFi system** - best for large homes (Eero, Google Nest, etc.)
- **New router** - older routers may simply be outdated
- **Powerline adapters** - use electrical wiring to extend network

---

## Other Tips
- **Limit connected devices** or prioritize with QoS settings
- **Use a wired (ethernet) connection** for gaming/streaming
- **Contact your ISP** if speeds are consistently low
