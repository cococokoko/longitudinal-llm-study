── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How do I understand what I want?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This is a genuinely important question. Here are some honest ways to think about it:

## Why It's Hard
- Wants get mixed up with what others expect of you
- Short-term desires can conflict with deeper ones
- Fear can disguise itself as "not wanting" something
- We sometimes want things we think we *should* want

## Practical Ways to Get Clearer

**Pay attention to your reactions**
- What makes you feel genuinely energized vs. drained?
- What do you envy in others? (Envy often points to real desires)
- What would you do if no one was watching or judging?

**Notice patterns over time**
- What do you keep returning to despite obstacles?
- What did you love before you cared what others thought?

**Test, don't just think**
- Small experiments reveal more than long reflection
- Try things and notice your actual response, not your predicted one

**Ask harder questions**
- "Do I want this, or do I want to *have wanted* it?"
- "Am I avoiding this because I don't want it, or because I'm scared?"
- "Whose voice is this - mine or someone else's?"

## Something Worth Considering
Sometimes people don't know what they want because **knowing would require making a difficult choice**. Clarity can feel threatening.

What's making you ask this right now? That context might help think through it more specifically.
