# Best Practices for Organic Gardening

Here are practical, straightforward tips organized by category:

## Soil Health
- **Compost regularly** - kitchen scraps, yard waste, coffee grounds
- Test soil pH (most vegetables prefer 6.0-7.0)
- Add organic matter annually to improve structure
- Avoid tilling excessively to protect soil microbes

## Pest & Weed Management
- **Companion planting** - basil near tomatoes, marigolds as borders
- Hand-pick larger pests when possible
- Use mulch (3-4 inches) to suppress weeds and retain moisture
- Encourage beneficial insects like ladybugs and lacewings

## Watering
- Water deeply but less frequently to encourage deep roots
- Morning watering reduces disease risk
- Drip irrigation saves water and keeps foliage dry

## Practical Tips for Busy Households
- **Start small** - a manageable garden beats an overwhelming one
- Grow high-yield crops like tomatoes, zucchini, and beans
- Perennial herbs (mint, chives, oregano) require minimal effort
- Involve family members to share the workload

## Cost-Saving Strategies
- Save seeds from year to year
- Start plants from seed indoors
- Trade plants/seeds with neighbors

## Quick Wins
- Grow what your family actually eats
- Keep a simple garden journal to track what works
