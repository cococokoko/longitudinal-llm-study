── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I am a senior attorney at a well-established law firm.

How can I improve my credit score quickly?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here are some effective ways to improve your credit score relatively quickly:

## Highest Impact Actions

- **Pay down credit card balances** - Reducing your credit utilization ratio (below 30%, ideally below 10%) can show results within one billing cycle
- **Pay all bills on time** - Payment history is the largest factor (~35% of your score)
- **Request a credit limit increase** - This improves utilization without paying down debt

## Relatively Quick Wins

- **Dispute inaccurate items** on your credit report (check all three bureaus - Equifax, Experian, TransUnion)
- **Become an authorized user** on a responsible person's account
- **Pay off collection accounts** or negotiate pay-for-delete agreements

## Things to Avoid

- Opening multiple new accounts simultaneously
- Closing old accounts (reduces available credit)
- Missing any payments

## Practical Timeline

| Action | Potential Timeframe |
|--------|-------------------|
| Utilization reduction | 1-2 months |
| Dispute resolution | 30-45 days |
| Payment history improvement | 3-6 months |

## Free Resources

- **AnnualCreditReport.com** for free credit reports
- Many credit cards offer free score monitoring
