What is the difference between analysis and design? Can one begin to design without analysis? Why? Be concise.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Analysis and design are related but distinct phases of problem-solving:

- Analysis focuses on understanding the problem, requirements, constraints, and goals. It answers: "What needs to be solved?"
- Design focuses on creating a solution that meets those requirements. It answers: "How will it be solved?"

Can design begin without analysis?

- In a limited sense, yes—you can start sketching ideas or concepts.
- However, effective design requires at least some analysis. Without understanding the problem and requirements, the design may fail to meet user needs, constraints, or objectives.

In short, analysis provides the foundation for design, making it more likely that the final solution will be appropriate and successful.
