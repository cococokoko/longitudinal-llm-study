## Key Ways Economic Inequalities Shape International Relations

**Power Dynamics**
- Wealthier nations have greater leverage in trade negotiations, international institutions, and diplomatic relationships
- Economic dependence can create political vulnerability for poorer nations
- Countries with resource wealth sometimes gain disproportionate influence despite other weaknesses

**Institutional Influence**
- Voting structures in IMF and World Bank historically favor wealthy donor nations
- This shapes which development models get promoted globally
- Developing nations have pushed back through coalitions like G77

**Areas of Genuine Tension**
- Debt relationships between nations create complex dependencies
- Trade agreements often reflect negotiating power imbalances
- Climate negotiations involve real disputes about who bears costs

**Where Analysts Disagree**

- How much inequality drives migration pressures versus other factors
- Whether current institutions are reformable or fundamentally biased

**Honest Complications**
- Some poorer nations have gained significant influence through strategic positioning
- Economic inequality doesn't automatically translate to political powerlessness
- Regional economic blocs have shifted some traditional power relationships
