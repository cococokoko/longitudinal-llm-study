── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I am a senior attorney at a well-established law firm.

What are the best ways to improve indoor air quality?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Improving indoor air quality (IAQ) is usually most effective when approached through source control, ventilation, filtration, and humidity management. For a law office, professional workplace, or residence, the following measures generally provide the greatest benefit:

1. Control Pollution at the Source
- Prohibit indoor smoking and vaping.
- Choose low-VOC (volatile organic compound) paints, adhesives, furnishings, and cleaning products.
- Store solvents, fuels, and chemicals in appropriate areas away from occupied spaces.
- Address water leaks promptly to prevent mold growth.
- Use walk-off mats at entrances to reduce dirt, dust, and outdoor pollutants tracked indoors.

2. Improve Ventilation
- Increase the intake of outdoor air where climate and outdoor air conditions permit.
- Ensure HVAC systems are operating according to design specifications.
- Verify that outdoor air dampers are functioning properly and are not closed or obstructed.
- In conference rooms and densely occupied spaces, consider enhanced ventilation rates.
- When outdoor air quality is poor (e.g., wildfire smoke, high pollution days), rely more heavily on filtration rather than simply opening windows.

3. Upgrade Air Filtration
- Use HVAC filters with an appropriate Minimum Efficiency Reporting Value (MERV) rating, often MERV 13 or higher if the system can accommodate it.
- Replace filters on schedule.
- Consider portable HEPA air cleaners for offices, conference rooms, reception areas, or other high-occupancy spaces.
- Ensure portable units are sized appropriately for the room's square footage and air-cleaning needs.

4. Manage Humidity
- Maintain indoor relative humidity generally between 30% and 50%, and preferably below 60%.
- Use dehumidifiers in damp areas.
- Prevent condensation on windows, pipes, and HVAC components.
- Monitor humidity levels with inexpensive sensors.

5. Prevent and Remediate Mold
- Inspect for leaks, water intrusion, and damp materials.
- Dry water-damaged materials promptly, ideally within 24–48 hours.
- Remove and replace mold-contaminated porous materials when necessary.
- Investigate persistent musty odors.

6. Maintain HVAC Systems
- Conduct regular inspections and preventive maintenance.
- Clean drain pans and condensate lines.
- Ensure air distribution systems are balanced and functioning correctly.
- Verify that filters are installed properly without bypass gaps.

7. Reduce Particulate Matter
- Vacuum with HEPA-filter-equipped vacuums.
- Dust surfaces using methods that capture rather than redistribute particles.
- Minimize combustion sources indoors, including candles and certain space heaters.
- For buildings near busy roads or construction sites, enhanced filtration can be particularly valuable.

8. Monitor Air Quality
- Consider indoor air quality monitors that track:
  - Carbon dioxide (CO₂)
  - Fine particulate matter (PM2.5)
  - Temperature
  - Relative humidity
  - In some cases, VOCs
- Elevated CO₂ can indicate inadequate ventilation in occupied spaces.

9. Be Cautious with Air-Cleaning Technologies
- HEPA filtration has strong evidence supporting its effectiveness for particulate removal.
- Some technologies marketed as air purifiers (such as certain ozone-generating devices) can create health concerns and are generally not recommended for occupied spaces.
- Evaluate claims regarding ionizers and other emerging technologies carefully.

For a modern law office, the highest-impact interventions are often:
- Upgrading HVAC filtration to MERV 13 or better where feasible.
- Increasing and verifying proper ventilation.
- Using appropriately sized portable HEPA units in conference rooms and enclosed offices.
- Maintaining humidity in a healthy range.
- Addressing any water intrusion or mold issues immediately.

These measures generally provide the best combination of effectiveness, practicality, and cost-efficiency.
