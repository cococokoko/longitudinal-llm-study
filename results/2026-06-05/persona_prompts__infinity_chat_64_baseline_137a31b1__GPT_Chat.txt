TOEFL and IELTS are the two most widely accepted English-language proficiency exams for international students applying to universities abroad. While both assess reading, listening, speaking, and writing skills, they differ in format and testing experience. The TOEFL iBT is primarily delivered online and emphasizes academic English commonly used in university settings, with integrated tasks that combine multiple language skills. IELTS is available in both computer-based and paper-based formats and offers either Academic or General Training versions; most university applicants take the Academic test. IELTS speaking is conducted as a live interview with an examiner, whereas TOEFL speaking responses are typically recorded and evaluated later. Because acceptance policies vary by institution, students should verify specific score requirements and preferences of their target universities.
