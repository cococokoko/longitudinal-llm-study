# Healthy Meal Planning on a Budget

Great topic, especially for someone working shifts at a hospital! Here are practical strategies:

## Planning Basics
- **Plan meals weekly** before grocery shopping
- **Build around sales** and seasonal produce
- **Cook once, eat multiple times** - batch cooking fits shift work perfectly

## Budget-Friendly Protein Sources
- Eggs (~$0.25 each)
- Canned tuna/salmon
- Dried beans and lentils
- Chicken thighs (cheaper than breasts)
- Greek yogurt

## Smart Shopping Tips
- Buy **frozen vegetables** - just as nutritious, much cheaper
- Purchase **store brands**
- Shop at discount grocers when possible
- Avoid pre-cut or pre-packaged convenience items

## Shift Work Considerations
- **Meal prep on days off** so healthy food is ready when you're tired
- Pack meals to avoid expensive hospital cafeteria food
- Keep **grab-and-go snacks** ready (boiled eggs, cut veggies, nuts)
- Invest in a good **insulated lunch bag**

## Sample Weekly Budget Meals
| Meal | Approximate Cost |
|------|-----------------|
| Lentil soup | ~$1.50/serving |
| Rice and beans | ~$0.75/serving |
| Egg stir-fry with frozen veggies | ~$1.25/serving |

## Quick Tip
Spending **2-3 hours prepping on your day off** can save significant money and support your energy through long shifts.
