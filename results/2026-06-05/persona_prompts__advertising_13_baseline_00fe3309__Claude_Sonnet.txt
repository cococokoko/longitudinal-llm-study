# Mastering the Art of Cooking Steak

## Understanding the Basics First

### Choosing Your Cut
| Cut | Tenderness | Flavor | Best Method |
|-----|-----------|--------|-------------|
| Ribeye | High | Rich/fatty | Pan sear or grill |
| NY Strip | Medium-high | Balanced | Pan sear or grill |
| Filet Mignon | Very high | Mild | Pan sear |
| T-Bone/Porterhouse | High | Complex | Grill |
| Flank/Skirt | Lower | Bold | High heat, thin slice |
| Chuck Eye | Medium | Rich | Pan sear |

---

## The Fundamentals

### 1. Start with Good Meat
- **Thickness matters** - aim for at least 1–1.5 inches
- Look for **good marbling** (fat running through the meat)
- **Dry-aged** beef has deeper flavor
- Buy from a butcher when possible

### 2. Preparation
- **Bring to room temperature** - 30–45 minutes before cooking
- **Pat completely dry** with paper towels (critical for a good sear)
- Season **generously** with kosher salt and black pepper
- Season at least 45 minutes ahead OR right before cooking

---

## Cooking Methods

### 🔥 Pan Searing (Most Control)
**What you need:** Cast iron skillet, high smoke-point oil, butter, aromatics

**Step-by-step:**
1. Heat pan until **smoking hot**
2. Add oil (avocado, grapeseed, or canola)
3. Place steak away from you
4. Sear **2–3 minutes per side** without moving
5. Add **butter, garlic, and thyme** in final minutes
6. **Baste continuously** by tilting pan and spooning butter over steak
7. Rest before serving

### 🥩 Reverse Sear (Best for Thick Cuts)
1. Season steak and place on rack in oven
2. Cook at **250°F (120°C)** until 10–15°F below target temp
3. Remove and let rest briefly
4. Sear in screaming hot pan **60–90 seconds per side**
5. **Result:** Edge-to-edge even doneness with great crust

### 🔥 Grilling
1. Create **two heat zones** (direct and indirect)
2. Sear over direct heat 2–3 minutes per side
3. Move to indirect heat to finish
4. Keep lid closed when not flipping
5. Only flip **once or twice**

### 💧 Sous Vide + Sear (Most Precise)
1. Season and vacuum seal steak
2. Cook in water bath at **exact target temperature**
3. Pat dry thoroughly after removing
4. Sear in very hot pan for crust only (60–90 seconds per side)

## Doneness Guide

| Doneness | Internal Temp | Feel | Description |
|----------|--------------|------|-------------|
| Rare | 120–125°F | Very soft | Cool red center |
| Medium Rare | 130–135°F | Soft with slight resistance | Warm red center ✅ Most recommended |
| Medium | 140–145°F | Firm but springy | Pink center |
| Medium Well | 150–155°F | Mostly firm | Slightly pink |
| Well Done | 160°F+ | Very firm | No pink |

**Use a meat thermometer** - don't guess

---

## The Crust (The Most Important Part)

### Getting a Perfect Sear
- Pan must be **extremely hot** before steak goes in
- Steak must be **bone dry**
- Don't overcrowd the pan
- **Don't move it** - let the crust form
- The Maillard reaction creates flavor - you want deep brown, not grey

---

## Resting — Non-Negotiable

- Rest **5–10 minutes** for most steaks
- Tent loosely with foil
- Juices redistribute throughout the meat
- **Cutting too early = dry steak**
