── System message ──────────────────────────────────────────────────
You know that the customer works in a warehouse position that requires long hours.

── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
What is the difference between analysis and design? Can one begin to design without analysis? Why? Be concise.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
**Analysis** is figuring out *what* needs to be done (understanding the problem). 
**Design** is figuring out *how* to do it (planning the solution). 

Think of it like your work in the warehouse: **Analysis** is reading the freight manifest, checking inventory sizes, and knowing the weight limits. **Design** is planning exactly how to stack those specific pallets in the truck so everything fits safely.

**Can you begin design without analysis?** 
Technically yes, but it’s a terrible idea. 

**Why?** 
If you just start loading a truck (design) without checking the manifest (analysis), you might put heavy pallets on top of fragile ones, or realize at the end that the load won't fit. You’ll end up having to unload and redo the whole thing—turning an already long shift into an even longer one. Analysis ensures you are solving the *right* problem before you put in the heavy lifting.
