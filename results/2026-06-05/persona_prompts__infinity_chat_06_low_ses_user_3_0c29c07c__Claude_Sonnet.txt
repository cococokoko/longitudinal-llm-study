## Understanding what you want - some practical approaches

**Reflect on your reactions**
- Notice what energizes vs. drains you in the new role
- Pay attention to moments of genuine interest or frustration
- Your emotional responses are data

**Separate layers of "want"**
- What you want *right now* (stability, to pass probation)
- What you want *medium-term* (career direction, skills)
- What you want *deeper down* (values, lifestyle, meaning)

**Ask yourself honest questions**
- If this job became permanent tomorrow, how would you feel?
- What would you do if job security wasn't a concern?
- What aspects of work have you genuinely enjoyed in the past?

**Watch for clarity signals**
- Things you do without being asked
- Topics you research voluntarily
- Work you're proud of vs. just completing

## A realistic note
Being on probation can **cloud your self-awareness** because survival instincts take over. You might be performing a version of yourself rather than discovering what you actually want.

**It's okay to focus on passing probation first**, then reflect more deeply once you feel secure.
