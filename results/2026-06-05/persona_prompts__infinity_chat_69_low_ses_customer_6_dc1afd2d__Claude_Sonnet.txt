## Analysis vs. Design

**Analysis** = Understanding **what** the problem is (requirements, constraints, current state)

**Design** = Determining **how** to solve it (structure, architecture, solution approach)

---

## Can You Design Without Analysis?

**Technically yes, practically no.**

Designing without analysis means:
- Solving the **wrong problem**
- Making assumptions that may be **incorrect**
- Producing a solution that **doesn't fit real needs**
- Wasting time and resources on **rework**

---

## Key Distinction

| | Analysis | Design |
|---|---|---|
| **Question** | What & Why? | How? |
| **Focus** | Problem space | Solution space |
| **Output** | Requirements | Blueprint/Plan |

---

**Bottom line:** Analysis defines the *target*; design plots the *path*. Skipping analysis is like designing a route before knowing the destination — you may build something, but likely not something useful.
