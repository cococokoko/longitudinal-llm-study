── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I am a physician-scientist leading a research group.

Give me a tip for managing a team of coworkers.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
One high-leverage management practice is to make expectations explicit and revisit them regularly.

For a research group, that can mean:
- Defining clear ownership for each project (who is responsible for driving it forward).
- Agreeing on concrete milestones and timelines.
- Discussing what success looks like before work begins (e.g., pilot data, manuscript draft, grant submission, assay validation).
- Reviewing progress in brief, recurring one-on-one meetings rather than waiting for problems to surface.

Many team issues that appear to be performance or communication problems are actually expectation mismatches. Clear expectations reduce ambiguity, improve accountability, and make feedback much easier to give and receive.
