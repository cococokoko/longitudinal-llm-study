For dry skin, the goal is to cleanse gently, add hydration, and prevent moisture loss. A simple routine is often the most effective.

Morning:
- Gentle cleanser (or just rinse with lukewarm water if your skin feels comfortable)
  - Look for fragrance-free, non-foaming cleansers.
- Hydrating serum (optional)
  - Ingredients like hyaluronic acid, glycerin, or panthenol can help attract moisture.
- Moisturizer
  - Choose a cream rather than a lightweight lotion if your skin is very dry.
  - Ingredients such as ceramides, squalane, shea butter, and glycerin are helpful.
- Sunscreen (SPF 30 or higher)
  - Daily sunscreen helps protect the skin barrier and prevent premature aging.

Evening:
- Gentle cleanser
- Hydrating serum (optional)
- Rich moisturizer
  - Apply while skin is slightly damp to help lock in moisture.
- If your skin is extremely dry:
  - Consider a thin layer of an occlusive product (such as petrolatum-based ointment) on the driest areas to reduce water loss overnight.

Additional tips:
- Avoid hot showers and harsh scrubs.
- Use lukewarm water when washing your face.
- Limit exfoliation to once weekly, if at all, and choose gentle chemical exfoliants rather than abrasive scrubs.
- Use a humidifier if indoor air is very dry.
- Patch-test new products before applying them to your whole face.

Example routine:
- Morning: Gentle cleanser → Ceramide moisturizer → SPF 30+
- Evening: Gentle cleanser → Hydrating serum → Rich ceramide cream
