# Healthy Meal Planning on a Budget

## Core Principles

- **Plan before you shop** — reduces impulse buying and food waste
- **Cook at home** — restaurant meals cost 3-5x more
- **Eat less meat** — biggest single budget saver
- **Buy whole foods** — processed convenience foods cost more per serving

---

## Budget-Friendly Staples to Keep on Hand

| Protein | Carbs | Produce | Other |
|--------|-------|---------|-------|
| Eggs | Oats | Bananas | Olive oil |
| Canned beans | Brown rice | Cabbage | Canned tomatoes |
| Lentils | Pasta | Carrots | Frozen vegetables |
| Canned tuna | Potatoes | Seasonal produce | Spices |
| Peanut butter | Bread | Apples | Greek yogurt |

---

## Smart Shopping Strategies

- **Shop seasonally** — produce is cheaper and fresher
- **Buy frozen vegetables** — equally nutritious, less waste
- **Compare unit prices** — not package prices
- **Store brands** work just as well for most staples
- **Shop sales and plan meals around them**
- **Reduce food waste** by using leftovers intentionally

---

## Simple Weekly Meal Framework

### Breakfast Ideas (~$0.50–$1.50/serving)
- Oatmeal with banana and peanut butter
- Eggs with toast
- Greek yogurt with fruit

### Lunch Ideas (~$1–$3/serving)
- Bean and rice bowls
- Lentil soup
- Leftovers from dinner

### Dinner Ideas (~$2–$4/serving)
- Stir-fry with frozen vegetables and eggs over rice
- Black bean tacos
- Pasta with canned tomatoes and vegetables
- Lentil curry
- Baked potato with toppings

---

## Batch Cooking Tips

- Cook a **big pot of grains** (rice, oats) at the start of the week
- Make **large batches of beans or lentils** — freeze extras
- Prep **vegetables** all at once
- Double dinner recipes for easy lunches

---

## Nutrition on a Budget

- **Protein:** eggs, beans, lentils, canned fish, peanut butter
- **Fiber:** oats, beans, vegetables, fruit
- **Vitamins:** frozen spinach, carrots, sweet potatoes, bananas
- **Healthy fats:** peanut butter, eggs, olive oil, canned sardines

## Quick Tips to Save More

- ✅ Eat before grocery shopping
- ✅ Make a list and stick to it
- ✅ Use apps like **Flipp** for sales or **Ibotta** for rebates
- ✅ Grow herbs at home (saves $3–5/bunch)
- ✅ Drink water instead of beverages
- ✅ Repurpose scraps (vegetable broth from peels)
