Eating healthy on a budget is mostly about choosing simple, flexible foods and planning just enough to avoid waste. You don’t need anything fancy—just a few smart habits.

Start with cheap staples that can stretch across multiple meals:
- Grains: rice, oats, pasta, potatoes

- Produce: frozen vegetables, carrots, cabbage, onions, bananas, seasonal fruit

Frozen and canned (low-sodium if possible) are totally fine and often cheaper than fresh.

Build meals around a simple formula:
- Grain + protein + vegetables + flavor

Make a big pot of something 2–3 times a week (chili, soup, stir-fry, curry). Eat it for a couple meals and freeze leftovers.

- Breakfast: oatmeal with banana and peanut butter  
- Lunch: rice bowl with beans, frozen veggies, and a fried egg  
- Dinner: roasted potatoes, carrots, and chicken thighs (or chickpeas if vegetarian)

Keep snacks simple:
Fruit, yogurt, hard-boiled eggs, popcorn

Money-saving tips that actually matter:
- Plan 3–4 meals per week and repeat them instead of trying to cook something new every day  
- Shop with a list and avoid impulse buys  
- Buy store brands—they’re usually identical  
- Use what you already have before buying more  
- Don’t overbuy fresh produce (that’s where waste happens)
