Nice—local businesses can actually compete really well if they play it smart. Since you’re working long hours, I’ll focus on things that give you solid return without eating all your time.

First, the big truth: locally, visibility + trust beats everything. People buy from who they recognize.

Here are the most effective approaches:

• Google Business Profile (this is non-negotiable)  
If you do nothing else, do this. Set up or optimize your Google Business profile so you show up in local searches and Maps. Add photos, services, hours, and post updates occasionally. Ask every happy customer for a review—this alone can drive steady business.

• Word of mouth, but engineered  
Don’t just “hope” people refer you. Give them a reason.  
Example: “Refer a friend, both of you get 10% off” or a small freebie.  
Even a simple “If you know anyone who needs this, I’d really appreciate you passing my name along” works more than people think.

• Facebook local groups + community pages  
These are gold for local exposure. Don’t spam. Just:  
– Answer questions  
– Offer helpful advice  
– Occasionally mention your service when relevant  
People notice consistency and helpfulness more than ads.

• Simple social presence (don’t overcomplicate it)  
Pick ONE platform your customers actually use (usually Facebook or Instagram).  
Post 2–3 times a week:  
– Before/after  
– Behind-the-scenes  
– Quick tips  
Consistency > perfection.

• Flyers + physical presence (still works locally)  
Old-school, but effective depending on your business:  
– Coffee shops  
– Community boards  
– Local stores  
Make it clean, simple, and with a clear offer.

• Partnerships with other local businesses  
This is underrated and powerful.  
Example: if you clean homes → partner with realtors  
If you do fitness → partner with supplement shops  
If you do repairs → partner with property managers  
You both send customers to each other.

• First-time customer offer  
Give people a low-risk reason to try you:  
– Discount  
– Bonus item/service  
– Free consultation  
Once they trust you, retention is easier.

• Text or simple follow-up system  
Most small businesses lose money here.  
Keep track of customers and follow up:  
“Hey, just checking if you need anything else”  
or reminders if your service repeats. This builds repeat business without new marketing effort.
