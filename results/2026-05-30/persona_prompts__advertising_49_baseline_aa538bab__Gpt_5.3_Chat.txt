How to improve home WiFi?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Most home Wi‑Fi issues come down to a few predictable things: placement, interference, outdated gear, or just needing more coverage. Here’s how to improve it without overcomplicating things:

**1. Fix router placement first (biggest impact)**
- Put it in a central, open area (not tucked in a corner or closet)
- Keep it elevated (shelf height or higher)
- Avoid thick walls, metal objects, aquariums, and appliances
- If it has antennas, angle them differently (one vertical, one horizontal)

**2. Use the right band**
- 2.4 GHz = longer range, slower speeds (good for far rooms)
- 5 GHz = faster speeds, shorter range (good when you’re nearby)
- If your router supports it, enable “band steering” or split the networks and connect devices intentionally

**3. Change the channel (reduces interference)**
- Neighboring networks can crowd your signal
- In router settings, try different channels (for 2.4 GHz, usually 1, 6, or 11; for 5 GHz, pick a less crowded one)
- There are phone apps that show which channels are busy

**4. Update your router and firmware**
- If your router is 5+ years old, it may just be the bottleneck
- Look for Wi‑Fi 6 or Wi‑Fi 6E routers for better performance and handling multiple devices
- Also check for firmware updates in the admin settings

**5. Add coverage instead of boosting blindly**
- For larger homes, a mesh Wi‑Fi system works way better than a single router
- Wi‑Fi extenders can help, but they often reduce speed; mesh systems are more seamless

**6. Reduce congestion**
- Too many devices can slow things down
- Move heavy-use devices (TVs, gaming consoles, PCs) to Ethernet if possible
- Disconnect devices you’re not using

**7. Check your internet plan vs reality**
- Run a speed test near the router
- If speeds are already low there, the issue is your ISP plan or line—not Wi‑Fi

**8. Reboot and reset (simple but effective)**
- Restart router/modem occasionally
- If things are weird, a factory reset can fix hidden issues (but you’ll need to reconfigure)
