── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I work in a warehouse position that requires long hours.

What's wrong with capitalism? State in-depth reasons why capitalism is inefficient and harmful.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here are substantive critiques that economists, philosophers and social scientists have raised:

## Economic Inefficiencies

- **Boom-bust cycles** - markets systematically overproduce then crash, causing unemployment waves
- **Externalities** - costs like pollution get pushed onto society rather than priced into products
- **Monopoly tendencies** - successful companies naturally accumulate market power, undermining the competition capitalism requires to function
- **Short-term thinking** - quarterly profit pressure discourages long-term investment

## Labor-Specific Problems (Relevant to Your Situation)
- **Bargaining power imbalance** - workers need jobs more urgently than employers need any individual worker
- **Productivity-wage gap** - warehouse productivity has increased dramatically while wages haven't kept proportional pace
- **Physical toll externalization** - companies profit from labor intensity while workers bear long-term health costs

## Broader Critiques
- **Wealth concentration** - capital returns historically outpace wage growth
- **Commodification** - market logic applied to healthcare, housing creates access problems
- **Democratic tension** - concentrated economic power translates to political influence

## Worth Noting
These are **genuine documented critiques** with real evidence behind them, but capitalism also has serious defenders with counterarguments worth understanding.
