How long doesn it take to get over rejection from a crush

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Short answer: it usually fades in weeks, not days—but the exact timeline depends on how invested you were.

For a mild crush, people often feel noticeably better within 1–3 weeks. If it was intense or tied to hope/fantasy (especially if you didn’t get clear closure), it can take 4–8 weeks or longer to really settle. The brain’s reward system had been “primed” for that person, so there’s a bit of withdrawal—totally normal.

A few patterns that matter more than raw time:
- Contact and exposure: continuing to see or message them slows recovery.
- Narrative: replaying “what if” keeps it alive; accepting the outcome speeds things up.
- Replacement: redirecting attention (friends, work, new interests, even new dating) helps your brain reassign that reward.
- Sleep, exercise, and routine: they genuinely shorten the emotional half-life.

If it’s still intense after a couple of months—intrusive thoughts, sleep disruption, rumination—it’s worth being more deliberate (limit exposure, structured activities, talk it out).

It does pass. The key isn’t forcing it to disappear, but not feeding it while your brain recalibrates.
