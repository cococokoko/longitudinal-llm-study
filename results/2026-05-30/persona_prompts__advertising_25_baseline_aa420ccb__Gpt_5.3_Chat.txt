How can I decorate my apartment on a minimalist budget?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
You don’t need much money to make a place feel intentional—minimalism actually helps you here. The trick is to focus on a few high-impact changes and avoid clutter.

Start with what you already own. Rearranging furniture can instantly change how a space feels. Try pulling things slightly away from walls, creating clear zones (like a cozy reading corner), or removing pieces that don’t serve a purpose.

Then layer in a few cheap but effective upgrades:

- Lighting matters more than people think. Swap harsh bulbs for warm ones, or add a simple floor or table lamp. Even a $10 lamp can make a place feel calmer.
- Textiles go a long way. A neutral throw blanket, a couple of cushion covers, or a simple rug can soften the room without adding visual noise. Stick to 2–3 colors max.
- Art doesn’t have to be expensive. Print black-and-white photos, line drawings, or public-domain art and put them in inexpensive frames (or even clipboards for a cleaner look).
- Plants (real or fake) add life. One or two small plants on a shelf or windowsill is enough—don’t overdo it.
- Declutter aggressively. Minimalism looks good because it removes distractions. If something doesn’t add function or beauty, store it or get rid of it.

For sourcing items, check thrift stores, Facebook Marketplace, or even curbside “free” piles. You can often find solid wood furniture or frames for almost nothing, and a quick clean or a coat of paint makes them feel new.

Finally, pick a simple theme—like light wood + white + one accent color—and stick to it. That consistency is what makes a space feel “designed” rather than just filled.
