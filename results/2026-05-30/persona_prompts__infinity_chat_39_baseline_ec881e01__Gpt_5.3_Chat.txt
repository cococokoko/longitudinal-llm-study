13. Time-Saving Tools and Apps

Focus on tools that reduce friction, not add complexity. Fewer tools, used well, beat many tools used poorly.

Core categories:

• Task capture and organization  
Use one place to store everything. Avoid scattered notes.  
Examples:  
– Todoist: quick capture, recurring tasks, simple filters  
– Microsoft To Do: clean, minimal, integrates with Outlook  
– Apple Reminders: lightweight, location-based alerts  

• Calendar control  
Your calendar is your real to-do list. Time-block key work.  
Examples:  
– Google Calendar: easy scheduling, shared calendars  
– Fantastical: natural language input (“meeting tomorrow 2pm”)  

• Automation  
Automate repetitive steps. Small savings add up.  
Examples:  
– Zapier: connect apps (save email attachments to Drive automatically)  
– IFTTT: simple triggers (mute phone during work hours)  

• Note-taking and knowledge  
Store ideas in a searchable system. Avoid over-structuring.  
Examples:  
– Notion: flexible, all-in-one workspace  
– Apple Notes: fast, no setup  
– Obsidian: local files, strong linking between ideas  

• Focus and distraction control  
Protect attention, not just time.  
Examples:  
– Forest: stay off your phone by “growing” a tree  
– Freedom: block distracting sites across devices  
– Pomofocus: simple Pomodoro timer in browser  

• Email management  
Reduce inbox time, don’t aim for perfection.  
Examples:  
– Gmail filters: auto-label and sort incoming mail  
– Superhuman or Spark: faster triage, keyboard shortcuts  

• File storage and access  
Keep files accessible anywhere, avoid duplicates.  
Examples:  
– Google Drive: collaboration and sharing  
– Dropbox: reliable syncing  

Simple use cases:

• Capture → Plan → Do  
Capture tasks in Todoist → schedule in Google Calendar → block distractions with Freedom

• Reduce repetition  
New client email arrives → Zapier creates task + saves attachment → you only review, not process

• Faster daily reset  
End of day: clear inbox to zero or near-zero, review tomorrow’s calendar, adjust tasks in under 10 minutes

Guiding principles:

• One tool per purpose whenever possible  
• Default to the simplest option  
• If you stop using it, remove it  
• Tools should save time within a week, or they’re not worth keeping
