# Effective Online Teaching Techniques for Physician-Scientists

Given your background leading a research group, here are evidence-based approaches particularly relevant to your context:

## Core Pedagogical Principles

### Cognitive Load Management
- **Chunk content** into 6-10 minute segments (optimal for attention retention)
- Use **worked examples** before asking learners to solve problems independently
- Avoid redundant information across audio and text simultaneously

### Active Learning Strategies
- **Retrieval practice** - frequent low-stakes quizzes outperform passive review
- **Spaced repetition** for complex concepts (immunology, pharmacology, etc.)
- **Case-based learning** translates well to online formats

## Practical Techniques

| Technique | Best For | Tools |
|-----------|----------|-------|
| Flipped classroom | Complex concepts | Pre-recorded lectures + live discussion |
| Think-pair-share | Critical reasoning | Breakout rooms |
| Live polling | Formative assessment | Poll Everywhere, Mentimeter |
| Annotation | Data interpretation | Hypothesis, Perusall |

## Specific to Research Training
- **Journal club formats** work well asynchronously
- **Screen-share data analysis** sessions are highly effective
- Record **lab meeting discussions** for asynchronous review

## Common Pitfalls to Avoid
- Replicating in-person lectures verbatim online
- Neglecting **social presence** and community building
- Underestimating technical barriers for learners
