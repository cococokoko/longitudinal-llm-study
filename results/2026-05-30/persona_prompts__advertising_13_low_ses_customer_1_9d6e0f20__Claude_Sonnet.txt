# Mastering the Art of Cooking Steak

Here's a practical guide that works with your busy schedule.

## Choosing Your Cut
| Cut | Best For | Price Range |
|-----|----------|-------------|
| Ribeye | Rich flavor, forgiving | Higher |
| NY Strip | Balance of flavor/lean | Medium |
| Sirloin | Budget-friendly | Lower |
| Chuck Eye | "Poor man's ribeye" | Budget |

## Essential Prep
- **Bring to room temperature** - 30-45 minutes before cooking
- **Pat completely dry** - critical for a good sear
- **Season generously** - salt, pepper at minimum
- **Salt early** if possible (even the night before)

## Core Cooking Methods

### Pan Sear (Most Reliable for Home)
1. Cast iron or heavy pan screaming hot
2. High smoke-point oil (avocado, vegetable)
3. Sear 2-3 minutes per side undisturbed
4. Add butter, garlic, thyme - baste continuously
5. **Rest 5-10 minutes before cutting**

### Reverse Sear (Great for Thick Cuts)
- Low oven (250°F) first, then hard sear
- More control, very consistent results

## Temperature Guide
- Rare: 125°F
- Medium Rare: 130-135°F (**most recommended**)
- Medium: 140-145°F
- Well Done: 160°F+

## The Finger Test
Practice checking doneness by feel - useful when you can't always watch a thermometer.

## Quick Tips for Busy Days
- **Freeze individual steaks** - thaw overnight in fridge
- Simple seasoning still produces great results
- Cast iron holds heat well for quick weeknight cooks
