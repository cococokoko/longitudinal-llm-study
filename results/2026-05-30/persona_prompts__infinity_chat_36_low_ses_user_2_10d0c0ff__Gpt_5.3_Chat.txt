Give me some examples of websites where users can select a default landing page?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here are some familiar types of websites and specific examples where users can choose or customize their default landing page:

- Google (google.com)  
  You can set preferences (like language, region, or results per page), but more importantly, tools like iGoogle (now discontinued) and Chrome’s startup settings let users effectively define what they see first.

- Yahoo (yahoo.com)  
  Historically offered a customizable homepage (My Yahoo) where users could pick news, weather, stocks, etc., as their default landing view.

- Reddit (reddit.com)  
  Logged-in users land on a personalized home feed based on their subscriptions, and can choose default sorting (hot, new, top).

- LinkedIn (linkedin.com)  
  The feed becomes a personalized landing page based on your network and preferences; some settings influence what appears first.

- Microsoft Start / MSN (start.microsoft.com / msn.com)  
  Lets users customize their homepage with topics, news sources, and layout preferences.

- Netvibes (netvibes.com)  
  A classic customizable dashboard where users create a fully personalized landing page with widgets, feeds, and apps.

- Notion (notion.so)  
  Users can set a specific page as their default when opening the app, effectively acting as a personal dashboard.

- Slack (slack.com)  
  While not a “homepage” in the traditional sense, users land on their last-used workspace/channel or can configure sidebar preferences.

- Jira / Confluence (atlassian.com products)  
  Allow users to set dashboards or specific pages as their landing view after login.

- Banking apps/websites (e.g., Chase, Bank of America)  
  Often let users choose which account summary or dashboard appears first after login.

If you're thinking about this from a product design perspective (especially given your situation juggling time), the most useful pattern is a “dashboard-style default” that remembers where you left off or prioritizes your most-used features automatically.
