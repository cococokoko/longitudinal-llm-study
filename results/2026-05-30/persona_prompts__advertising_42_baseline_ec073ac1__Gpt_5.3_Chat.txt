── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
What are the best apps for tracking fitness and nutrition?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Depends a bit on your style (data nerd vs. “just keep me on track”), but these are consistently the best options right now:

**All-in-one tracking (fitness + nutrition)**
- **MyFitnessPal** – Still the most popular. Huge food database, barcode scanner, integrates with tons of apps/devices. Downsides: more features locked behind premium now.
- **Cronometer** – Best if you care about *micronutrients* and accuracy. Tracks vitamins/minerals in detail, cleaner data than MFP. Slightly less social/gamified.
- **Lifesum** – More lifestyle-focused, with meal plans and a polished interface. Good if you want guidance, not just tracking.

**Workout-focused apps**
- **Strong** – Great for weightlifting. Simple, fast logging, progressive overload tracking.
- **Fitbod** – Auto-generates gym workouts based on your recovery and available equipment. Good if you don’t want to plan.
- **Nike Training Club** – Free, high-quality guided workouts (strength, mobility, cardio).

**Habit + simple tracking**
- **MacroFactor** – Excellent for nutrition coaching. It adjusts your calorie targets based on your actual progress. Very smart but paid.
- **Lose It!** – Easier, more streamlined alternative to MyFitnessPal.
- **Habitica / Streaks** – Not fitness-specific, but great if consistency is your main struggle.

**Wearable ecosystems (if you have a device)**
- **Apple Fitness / Health** – Best for iPhone + Apple Watch users.
- **Garmin Connect** – Great for runners, cyclists, endurance training.
- **Whoop** – Focuses on recovery, sleep, and strain rather than step counts.

If you want a quick recommendation:
- Data-driven + serious tracking: Cronometer or MacroFactor  
- Simple + popular: MyFitnessPal  
- Gym lifting focus: Strong + any food tracker  
- “Tell me what to do” vibe: Fitbod or Lifesum
