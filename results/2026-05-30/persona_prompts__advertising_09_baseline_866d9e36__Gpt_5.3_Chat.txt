── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Good move—but “build a personal brand” is vague enough to waste a year if you don’t narrow it.

Start here: pick a sharp positioning. Not “I talk about business,” but something like “I help early-career designers land their first remote job” or “I break down AI tools for non-technical founders.” If someone can’t immediately say “oh, you’re the ___ person,” it’s too broad.

Then commit to 2–3 content pillars. These are the repeatable themes you’ll post about. For example:
- Practical how-tos (tips, frameworks, walkthroughs)
- Proof (case studies, results, behind-the-scenes)
- Opinions (takes on trends, myths, mistakes)

Platform matters less than consistency, but don’t try to be everywhere. Pick one primary channel where your audience already spends time. If you’re unsure:
- LinkedIn → professional growth, B2B, career
- Instagram/TikTok → lifestyle, creators, visual storytelling
- X (Twitter) → ideas, commentary, fast iteration
- YouTube → deeper teaching, long-term discoverability

Content that actually grows:
- Specific > broad (“3 mistakes junior devs make in interviews” beats “how to succeed”)
- Personal > generic (your experiences, even small ones, are your edge)
- Clear > clever (don’t try to sound smart—be understood)

Cadence: aim for 3–5 posts per week. Not forever—just for the first 60–90 days to find your voice and what resonates.

Engagement is half the game. Reply to comments, start conversations, and comment thoughtfully on other people’s posts in your niche. That’s how you get seen early.

Track simple signals:
- Saves and shares (strong content)
- Comments (engagement)
- Follower growth (direction, not ego)

Monetization comes later, but build with it in mind:
- What problem are you becoming known for solving?
- Could that turn into consulting, digital products, a course, or a service?

Big mistake to avoid: copying big creators too closely. Their content works because of their context, not just their format. Study them, don’t clone them.
