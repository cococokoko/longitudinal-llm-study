# Retirement Savings for Warehouse Workers

Great question! Here are practical strategies that work well for physically demanding jobs:

## Start with Employer Benefits
- **401(k) match** - contribute at least enough to get the full employer match (free money)
- Check if your employer offers a **pension plan**
- Look into **union benefits** if you're in a union

## Account Types to Consider
- **Traditional 401(k)** - reduces taxable income now
- **Roth IRA** - tax-free withdrawals in retirement
- **HSA (Health Savings Account)** - triple tax advantage, good for future medical costs

## Practical Tips
- **Start early** - even small amounts compound significantly over time
- **Automate contributions** so you don't have to think about it
- Aim for **15% of income** including employer match when possible
- Increase contributions with every raise

## Things Worth Thinking About
- **Physical wear on your body** may mean earlier retirement than planned
- Consider **disability insurance** as a safety net
- Build an **emergency fund** first (3-6 months expenses)

## Getting Help
- Many employers offer **free financial advisors**
- Look into **nonprofit credit counseling**
- The **CFPB website** has free retirement planning tools
