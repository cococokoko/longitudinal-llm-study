Starting a new job—especially while you are still navigating your probation period—can be an exciting but high-pressure time. You are learning new systems, meeting new people, and naturally wanting to prove yourself. It is actually the perfect time to introduce meditation and mindfulness into your routine to help manage "new job jitters," improve your focus, and keep your stress levels in check.

Here is a beginner-friendly guide to getting started, tailored to fit into a busy, transitional time in your life:

### 1. Understand the Difference
*   **Mindfulness** is the act of being fully present in the moment, without judgment. You can be mindful while drinking coffee, walking to work, or listening to a colleague.
*   **Meditation** is the intentional practice or "workout" where you set aside time to train your brain to be mindful. 

### 2. Start Small (The 3-Minute Rule)
When you are exhausted from absorbing new information at work all day, a 30-minute meditation will feel like a chore. Start with just **3 to 5 minutes a day**.
