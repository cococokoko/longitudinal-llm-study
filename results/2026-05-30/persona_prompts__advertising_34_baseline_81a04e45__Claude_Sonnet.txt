# Effective Online Teaching Techniques

## Engagement & Interaction
- **Break content into shorter segments** (10-15 minutes max for video lectures)
- Use **polls, quizzes, and chat** to maintain active participation
- Incorporate **breakout rooms** for small group discussions
- Ask questions regularly rather than lecturing continuously

## Clear Communication
- Set explicit expectations for assignments, deadlines, and participation
- Create a **course roadmap** so students understand the structure
- Respond to messages within a defined timeframe (and tell students what it is)
- Use multiple formats (video, text, audio) for key concepts

## Building Community
- Start with **icebreakers and introductions**
- Use discussion boards for peer interaction
- Acknowledge student contributions publicly
- Create informal spaces for connection

## Assessment & Feedback
- Provide **timely, specific feedback** rather than just grades
- Use varied assessment types (projects, discussions, quizzes)
- Consider **low-stakes frequent checks** over high-stakes rare tests
- Allow revision opportunities when possible

## Technical & Practical
- Keep the platform interface **simple and consistent**
- Record live sessions for students in different time zones
- Test technology before sessions
- Have a backup plan for technical failures

## What Research Suggests Works Best
- **Active learning** consistently outperforms passive consumption
- **Presence** (being visible and responsive) strongly affects outcomes
- Regular **structured interaction** reduces dropout rates
