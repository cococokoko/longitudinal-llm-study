# Staying Fit Without a Gym

Here are practical, time-efficient approaches that work well for a busy schedule:

## Bodyweight Training
- **Push-up variations** (standard, wide, diamond, pike)
- **Squats and lunges** for lower body strength
- **Planks and core work** — 10 minutes is genuinely effective
- **Pull-ups** with a doorframe bar (~$30)

## High-Intensity Options
- **HIIT circuits** — 20-30 minutes, high return on time invested
- **Tabata intervals** — 4 minutes of serious work
- **Jump rope** — excellent cardio, portable, inexpensive

## Daily Movement (Often Underrated)
- Walking meetings or calls
- Stairs over elevators
- Standing desk portions of the day
- 10-minute walks after meals

## Equipment Worth Owning
| Item | Cost | Value |
|------|------|-------|
| Resistance bands | ~$20 | High |
| Kettlebell | ~$50 | Very high |
| Pull-up bar | ~$30 | High |

## Realistic Framework
- **3-4 sessions/week**, 20-30 minutes each
- Consistency beats intensity
- Sleep and nutrition matter more than most people expect

## Apps Worth Considering
- Nike Training Club (free)
- Seven (7-minute workouts)
- Peloton (if you travel and use hotels)
