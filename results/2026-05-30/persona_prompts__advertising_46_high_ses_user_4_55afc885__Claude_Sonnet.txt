── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I am a physician-scientist leading a research group.

What are the best resources for starting a plant-based diet?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here are some well-regarded resources across different formats:

## Evidence-Based Books
- **"How Not to Die"** - Michael Greger, MD (reviews mortality research)
- **"The China Study"** - T. Colin Campbell (though note some methodological critiques exist)
- **"Eat to Live"** - Joel Fuhrman, MD

## Peer-Reviewed/Academic
- **NutritionFacts.org** - Greger's team reviews primary literature
- **PCRM.org** (Physicians Committee for Responsible Medicine)
- Journal *Nutrients* has substantial plant-based diet research

## Practical/Clinical
- **Forks Over Knives** - meal planning tools
- **Academy of Nutrition and Dietetics** position papers on vegetarian/vegan diets
- **UpToDate** has clinical summaries on plant-based diets

## Key Nutritional Considerations to Monitor
- B12 supplementation
- Vitamin D
- Omega-3 fatty acids (EPA/DHA)
- Iron and zinc bioavailability
- Calcium
- Iodine

## Worth Noting
As a physician-scientist, you'll likely want to critically evaluate some popular sources, as the evidence quality varies considerably across this literature.

Would you like recommendations focused on a specific aspect - clinical implementation, the research literature, or practical meal planning?
