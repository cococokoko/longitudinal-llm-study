── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Eco-friendly travel options.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
If you want to travel with a lighter environmental footprint, the big idea is simple: fewer emissions per mile, and fewer miles when possible. Here are some solid options, from most to least eco-friendly in general:

**1. Walking & cycling**  
Best zero-emission choices. Great for exploring cities or short distances, and often more immersive than any other way.

**2. Public transportation**  
Trains, subways, trams, and buses spread emissions across many passengers. Electric rail systems are especially efficient. In many regions, trains beat planes by a wide margin for trips under ~800–1,000 km.

**3. Car-sharing or ride-sharing**  
If you need a car, sharing it reduces per-person emissions. Apps, carpooling, or splitting rides with other travelers all help.

**4. Electric vehicles (EVs)**  
Lower emissions than gas cars, especially if powered by renewable electricity. Ideal for road trips where charging infrastructure is available.

**5. Coaches (long-distance buses)**  
Often overlooked, but they’re among the most efficient motorized options per passenger.

**6. Flying smarter (when you must fly)**  
- Choose nonstop flights (takeoff/landing burn the most fuel).  
- Fly economy (more passengers = lower emissions per person).  
- Pack light.  
- Consider airlines with newer, more efficient fleets.  
- Use carbon offset programs cautiously—they’re not perfect, but better than nothing if credible.

**7. Stay local or slow travel**  
Fewer long-haul trips, longer stays. You reduce transport emissions and usually have a richer experience.

**8. Eco-conscious lodging**  
Look for certified green hotels, renewable energy use, water-saving systems, and local sourcing.

**9. Pack & behave sustainably**  
Reusable bottle, minimal plastic, reef-safe sunscreen, respect wildlife and local ecosystems.

If you tell me where you’re based and where you’re thinking of going, I can suggest the lowest-impact route and options tailored to that trip.
