# Data Security Best Practices for Small Businesses

## Foundation Basics

- **Use strong, unique passwords** for all accounts
- **Enable multi-factor authentication (MFA)** everywhere possible
- **Keep software updated** - patches fix known vulnerabilities
- **Use reputable antivirus/anti-malware** software

## Access Control

- Give employees **only the access they need** (principle of least privilege)
- **Remove access immediately** when employees leave
- Maintain an inventory of who has access to what
- Use separate accounts rather than sharing credentials

## Data Management

- **Know what data you have** and where it's stored
- **Encrypt sensitive data** at rest and in transit
- Establish a **data retention policy** - delete what you don't need
- Classify data by sensitivity level

## Backups

- Follow the **3-2-1 rule**: 3 copies, 2 different media types, 1 offsite
- **Test your backups regularly** - untested backups often fail
- Keep at least one backup offline (protects against ransomware)

## Employee Training

- Train staff to **recognize phishing emails**
- Establish clear security policies in writing
- Create a culture where reporting suspicious activity is encouraged

## Network Security

- Use a **business-grade firewall**
- Separate guest WiFi from business networks
- Use a VPN for remote workers
- Disable unused ports and services

## Vendor/Third-Party Management

- Review security practices of vendors with data access
- Use written agreements covering data protection
- Limit third-party access to only what's necessary

## Incident Planning

- Have a **written incident response plan**
- Know your legal notification requirements
- Keep contact information for IT support readily available

## Practical Starting Points

If resources are limited, prioritize in this order:
1. MFA on email and financial accounts
2. Regular backups
3. Employee phishing awareness
4. Software updates
