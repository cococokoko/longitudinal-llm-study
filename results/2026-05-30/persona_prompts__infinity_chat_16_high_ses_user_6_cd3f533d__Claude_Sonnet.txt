## Substantive Critiques of Capitalism

### Market Failures (Economic)
- **Externalities**: Markets systematically misprice costs imposed on third parties - pollution, carbon emissions, noise
- **Public goods underproduction**: Markets won't adequately fund national defense, basic research, or infrastructure without intervention
- **Information asymmetries**: Sellers often know more than buyers, creating exploitation opportunities (Akerlof's lemons problem)
- **Natural monopolies**: Some industries trend toward concentration, eliminating competitive efficiency benefits

### Structural Critiques
- **Short-termism**: Quarterly earnings pressure systematically underinvests in long-term value
- **Rent-seeking**: Significant economic activity captures existing value rather than creating it
- **Financialization**: Capital allocation increasingly serves speculation over productive investment
- **Boom-bust cycles**: Inherent instability requiring repeated intervention

### Distributional Concerns
- Wealth concentration can become self-reinforcing through capital returns
- Bargaining power asymmetries between capital and labor
- Inheritance perpetuates inequality independent of merit

## Important Counterpoints Worth Considering
- Most alternatives have performed worse on poverty reduction
- Many "failures" reflect regulatory capture rather than markets themselves
- Mixed economies address many critiques without abandoning markets
