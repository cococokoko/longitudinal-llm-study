How can I learn to cook healthy meals quickly?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
You don’t need to become a “chef” or spend hours in the kitchen to eat well. The trick is building a small system you can repeat, not chasing new recipes every day.

Start with a simple formula you can mix and match:
- a protein (chicken, eggs, beans, tofu, fish)
- a carb (rice, pasta, potatoes, bread, tortillas)
- vegetables (fresh or frozen)
- a sauce or seasoning (soy sauce, olive oil + garlic, salsa, yogurt-based sauces)

If you can combine those four things, you can make dozens of quick meals without thinking too hard.

Speed comes from preparation, not skill. A few habits make a big difference:
- Cook in batches 2–3 times a week (rice, roasted veggies, grilled chicken)
- Keep “shortcut” foods around (pre-washed greens, frozen veggies, canned beans, rotisserie chicken)
- Use high-heat, fast methods: stir-frying, sheet-pan roasting, scrambling, wraps

Learn a handful of “default meals” you can do without a recipe. For example:
- Stir-fry: protein + frozen veg + soy sauce (10 minutes)
- Sheet pan: chop everything, oil + seasoning, bake (20–25 min, minimal effort)
- Bowl: rice + protein + veggies + sauce
- Omelet or eggs + toast + spinach
- Wraps: fill a tortilla with leftovers + sauce

For learning, don’t overwhelm yourself:
- Pick 5–7 meals and repeat them until they’re automatic
- Watch short cooking videos (YouTube, TikTok) for techniques, not just recipes
- Focus on basics: how to chop, how to cook chicken safely, how to season

Also, accept “good enough.” A simple meal like rice, beans, avocado, and salsa is healthy. It doesn’t need to look fancy.
