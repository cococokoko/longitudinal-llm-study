Nice—this is a good rabbit hole to go down, especially if you’re thinking about eventually moving out of warehouse work or just understanding the bigger system you’re part of.

I’ll skip the shallow “start a Shopify store in 24 hours” stuff and point you toward books that actually go deep into how ecommerce works—strategy, operations, psychology, and scale.

First, the closest thing to an “inside the machine” view:

- The Everything Store by Brad Stone  
This is about Amazon, but it’s really about logistics, fulfillment, pricing strategy, and customer obsession at scale. Since you work in a warehouse, a lot of this will hit differently—you’ll see how your kind of role fits into a massive system.

- Amazon Unbound by Brad Stone  
The sequel. More about AWS, marketplace dynamics, and how Amazon expanded. Helps you understand modern ecommerce infrastructure and power dynamics.

Now for actually building and running ecommerce businesses:

- The E-Myth Revisited by Michael Gerber  
Not ecommerce-specific, but crucial. It explains why most small businesses fail and how to build systems instead of just working harder. Very relevant if you ever want to run a store without burning out.

- Built to Sell by John Warrillow  
Short but powerful. Teaches how to structure a business so it isn’t dependent on you. Applies directly to ecommerce brands.

- The Lean Startup by Eric Ries  
More about testing ideas and not wasting time/money. Useful if you’re starting from scratch and don’t want to guess what will sell.

Now deeper into ecommerce mechanics and strategy:

- Ecommerce Evolved by Tanner Larsson  
One of the more detailed books on funnels, offer structure, upsells, and conversion strategy. It’s more advanced than most “beginner Shopify” content.

- DotCom Secrets by Russell Brunson  
A bit hyped in tone, but underneath that, it explains sales funnels, customer journeys, and how to structure offers online. Still very relevant.

- 100M Offers by Alex Hormozi  
Not strictly ecommerce, but extremely strong on crafting offers that actually convert. This is where a lot of stores fail.

Marketing, psychology, and conversion (arguably the most important layer):

- Influence by Robert Cialdini  
Classic. Explains why people buy—used everywhere in ecommerce.

- Building a StoryBrand by Donald Miller  
Teaches how to communicate your product clearly so people actually understand why they should care.

- Scientific Advertising by Claude Hopkins  
Old but foundational. Helps you think in terms of testing and measurable results.

Operations, supply chain, and fulfillment (this ties directly to your current job):

- The Goal by Eliyahu Goldratt  
Not ecommerce-specific, but it teaches bottlenecks, throughput, and efficiency in operations. You’ll start seeing warehouses completely differently.

- Principles of Supply Chain Management by Joel Wisner (more textbook-like)  
If you want a deeper, more technical understanding of how goods move from factory to customer.
