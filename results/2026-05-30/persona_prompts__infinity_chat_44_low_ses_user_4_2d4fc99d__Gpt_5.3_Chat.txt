Here’s a simple three-question filter you can actually use in the moment:

1. If I were the patient (or their family), would this feel respectful, fair, and compassionate?  
2. Would I be comfortable explaining and defending this action to a supervisor, coworker, or in an official report?  
3. Does this action support the patient’s safety, dignity, and well-being—or is it mainly for my own convenience?

If something fails one of these, it’s a strong signal to pause or rethink it. If it passes all three, you’re usually on solid moral ground.
