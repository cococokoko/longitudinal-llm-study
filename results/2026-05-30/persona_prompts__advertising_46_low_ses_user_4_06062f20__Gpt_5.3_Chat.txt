Nice, that’s a demanding job—fueling yourself well will make a real difference on those shifts.

If you’re starting a plant-based diet, the trick is to keep it simple, practical, and nutritionally solid. Here are some of the most useful resources and how they help:

**1. Evidence-based nutrition (worth trusting)**
- NutritionFacts.org (Dr. Michael Greger): super solid science breakdowns on plant-based eating, short videos, and practical tips  
- The Vegan Society (vegansociety.com): clear guides, especially on nutrients like B12, iron, calcium  
- NHS / Dietitians of Canada / Academy of Nutrition and Dietetics: all confirm well-planned plant-based diets are healthy and give balanced guidance

**2. Beginner-friendly meal ideas (quick + realistic)**
- Forks Over Knives (website + app): good for whole-food plant-based meals  
- Minimalist Baker: simple recipes, often 10 ingredients or less  
- Budget Bytes (plant-based section): great if you want cheap, filling meals for shift work  
- Pick Up Limes (YouTube + site): very practical, calm, and nutrition-aware

**3. Meal prep for shift work (this will matter a lot for you)**
Look for:
- “plant-based meal prep” on YouTube (channels like Simnett Nutrition or Rainbow Plant Life)
- Reddit: r/plantbaseddiet and r/veganmealprep (surprisingly practical ideas)

Focus on foods that hold up well in a hospital setting:
- grain bowls (rice/quinoa + beans/lentils + veggies + sauce)
- wraps with hummus, tofu, or chickpeas
- overnight oats or chia pudding for quick starts
- snacks: nuts, fruit, roasted chickpeas, protein bars

**4. Key nutrition basics (don’t skip this part)**
Plant-based is healthy, but only if you cover a few essentials:
- B12: you *need* a supplement (non-negotiable)
- Protein: beans, lentils, tofu, tempeh, edamame, soy milk
- Iron: lentils, beans, spinach + pair with vitamin C
- Calcium: fortified plant milks, tofu, greens
- Omega-3: flaxseed, chia seeds, walnuts

**5. Simple starting strategy (don’t overhaul everything at once)**
Instead of going all-in overnight:
- Start with 1–2 fully plant-based meals per day
- Swap basics: dairy milk → soy/oat milk, ground meat → lentils or tofu
- Build 5–7 “go-to meals” you actually like and rotate them
