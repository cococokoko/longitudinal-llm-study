Where can I find unique handmade gifts?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
If you want genuinely unique handmade gifts (not mass-produced stuff pretending to be “artisan”), these are solid places to look:

• Etsy – still one of the biggest hubs for handmade items. The key is to dig a bit: filter by small shops, read shop descriptions, and avoid listings with identical photos across multiple stores.

• Local craft markets & artisan fairs – honestly one of the best options. You get one-of-a-kind pieces and can talk directly to the maker. Check community boards, Facebook events, or sites like Eventbrite.

• Instagram & TikTok creators – a lot of independent artists sell directly through their profiles now. Search hashtags like #handmadejewelry, #ceramics, #fiberart, etc., and you’ll find small makers before they blow up.

• Specialty marketplaces:
  - Uncommon Goods (more curated, slightly pricier)
  - Novica (supports global artisans, often traditional crafts)
  - Folksy (UK-based Etsy alternative)
  - Made Trade (ethical + handmade focus)

• Local boutiques / museum gift shops – underrated. They often stock high-quality handmade goods from regional artists.

• Art schools or student showcases – students sell really creative stuff, often at lower prices.
