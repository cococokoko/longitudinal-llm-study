# Mastering the Art of Cooking Steak

Given your background, you'll appreciate a systematic, evidence-based approach.

## Understanding the Fundamentals

### The Science Behind a Good Steak
- **Maillard reaction** occurs at ~285°F (140°C) - creates that brown crust and complex flavors
- **Protein denaturation** determines texture at different temperatures
- **Resting** allows myosin to reabsorb moisture redistributed during cooking

---

## Key Variables to Control

### Cut Selection
| Cut | Best Method | Fat Content |
|-----|-------------|-------------|
| Ribeye | Pan sear/grill | High |
| NY Strip | Pan sear/grill | Medium |
| Filet | Pan sear | Low |
| Skirt/Flank | High heat, thin | Medium |

### Temperature Targets (Internal)
- Rare: **125°F**
- Medium-rare: **130-135°F** *(widely considered optimal)*
- Medium: **140-145°F**
- Well done: **160°F+**

---

## Reliable Techniques

### The Reverse Sear Method *(highly recommended)*
1. Season generously with salt **24 hours ahead**
2. Low oven (250°F) until ~115°F internal
3. Hard sear in cast iron with high-smoke-point oil
4. Rest 5-10 minutes

### Classic Pan Sear
- **Dry the surface completely**
- Cast iron screaming hot
- Butter baste with aromatics (thyme, garlic)
- Flip frequently for even cooking

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
- Skipping the dry brine (salt ahead of time)
- Cold steak straight from refrigerator
- Moving it constantly
- Skipping the rest period
- Overcrowding the pan

---

## Tools Worth Having
- **Instant-read thermometer** (Thermapen recommended)
- Cast iron skillet
- Wire rack for reverse sear
