How long doesn it take to get over rejection from a crush

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
There isn’t a fixed timeline, but for most people it’s anywhere from a few days to a few weeks for the sharp sting to fade, and maybe a month or two before it feels fully “neutral.” If the crush was intense or built up for a long time, it can take longer.

What you’re feeling right after rejection is a mix of disappointment, bruised ego, and lost “what ifs,” so it can feel bigger than the actual relationship would’ve been. That’s normal.

A few things that tend to speed it up:
- Limiting how much you see or check up on them (at least for a bit)
- Not replaying the interaction over and over
- Staying busy with people and things that make you feel good
- Letting yourself feel a bit bad without trying to force it away

If it’s still hitting hard after a couple months or getting worse instead of better, that’s when it’s worth taking a closer look at what’s keeping it stuck.
