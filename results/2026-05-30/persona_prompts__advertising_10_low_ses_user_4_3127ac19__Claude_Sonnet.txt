# Learning a New Language Effectively

Great goal! Here are some practical strategies that can work well, especially with a shift worker's schedule:

## Building a Foundation
- **Start with high-frequency words** - the top 1,000 words cover most everyday conversation
- **Learn phrases, not just vocabulary** - context helps retention
- **Focus on one language** - spreading across multiple slows progress

## Consistent Practice Methods
- **Duolingo/Babbel** - good for short breaks between shifts
- **Anki flashcards** - spaced repetition is very effective for retention
- **Podcasts while commuting** - passive listening builds ear recognition

## Active Learning
- **Speaking practice matters most** - apps like iTalki connect you with native speakers
- **Label objects** around your home
- **Think in the language** throughout your day

## Taking Advantage of Your Work Setting
- Many hospitals have **multilingual patients and coworkers**
- Learning **medical vocabulary** in your target language could be genuinely useful professionally
- Short shift breaks are **perfect for 5-10 minute practice sessions**

## Realistic Expectations
- **15-30 minutes daily** beats 3 hours once a week
- Consistency over intensity
- Expect a **plateau around 3-6 months** - push through it
