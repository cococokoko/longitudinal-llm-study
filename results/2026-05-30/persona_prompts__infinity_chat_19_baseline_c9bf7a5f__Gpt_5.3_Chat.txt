── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
If you want solid coverage of Design and Analysis of Algorithms (DAA), the “best” book really depends on how theoretical vs practical you want to go. Here are the strongest options (all still very relevant around 2023+):

**If you want a standard, all-in-one textbook (most popular):**
- *Introduction to Algorithms* — Cormen, Leiserson, Rivest, Stein (CLRS)  
  This is the classic. Very comprehensive: sorting, graphs, dynamic programming, greedy, NP-completeness. It’s a bit dense and mathematical, but it’s the gold standard for courses.

**If you want something more intuitive and easier to digest:**
- *Grokking Algorithms* — Aditya Bhargava  
  Very beginner-friendly, visual, and intuitive. Great if CLRS feels overwhelming. It won’t go very deep, but it builds strong intuition.

- *Algorithms* — Robert Sedgewick & Kevin Wayne  
  Clean explanations + practical focus. Pairs well with their online Princeton course. Good balance of theory and implementation.

**If you want strong conceptual + interview-style understanding:**
- *The Algorithm Design Manual* — Steven Skiena  
  Very readable, lots of real-world perspective, and a great catalog of problems. Less formal than CLRS, more practical.

**If you want a more theoretical/mathematical angle:**
- *Algorithm Design* — Jon Kleinberg & Éva Tardos  
  Excellent for understanding *why* algorithms work. Very clean explanations of paradigms like greedy, DP, and network flow.

- *Introduction to the Design and Analysis of Algorithms* — Anany Levitin  
  Easier than CLRS, structured around design techniques. Good for coursework.

**If you want a modern, free option:**
- *Algorithms* — Jeff Erickson (available free online)  
  Deep, rigorous, and surprisingly readable. Great exercises and explanations.

---

**Quick picks depending on your goal:**
- Beginner → *Grokking Algorithms*  
- University course → *CLRS* or *Kleinberg & Tardos*  
- Practical + coding → *Sedgewick & Wayne*  
- Balanced + readable → *Skiena*  
- Free + high quality → *Jeff Erickson’s book*
