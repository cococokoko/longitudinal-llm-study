I work shifts at a local hospital as a patient care aide.

What are the best tools for remote team collaboration?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Nice—healthcare shifts make coordination extra tricky, so the “best” tools are really the ones that reduce friction across handoffs and weird schedules.

Here are the strongest options, depending on what you need most:

**For day-to-day communication (fast, reliable)**
- **Microsoft Teams** – very common in healthcare settings, integrates with Outlook and scheduling, good for shift handoffs and quick updates.
- **Slack** – cleaner and more flexible than Teams, great for organizing conversations by channel (e.g., “night shift,” “ICU updates”), but less tied into hospital systems.

**For scheduling + shift coordination**
- **When I Work** – built specifically for shift workers; easy swaps, availability tracking, notifications.
- **Deputy** – similar, a bit more robust for compliance and staffing needs.
- **Shiftboard** – used in some healthcare environments; good for larger teams.

**For task tracking / handoffs**
- **Trello** – simple boards for tracking patient-related non-clinical tasks or team responsibilities.
- **Asana** – more structured if you’re coordinating across multiple roles or departments.
- **Notion** – flexible “all-in-one” workspace (notes, tasks, docs), but takes setup.

**For quick documentation & shared info**
- **Google Workspace (Docs/Drive)** – easy sharing, real-time editing.
- **OneNote** – great if your org already uses Microsoft; good for shift notes and logs.

**For secure healthcare communication (important)**
- **TigerConnect** – designed for healthcare; HIPAA-compliant messaging, often replaces pagers.
- **Vocera** – hands-free communication, more hardware-integrated in hospitals.

---

**If I had to narrow it down for your situation:**
- Teams or TigerConnect for communication  
- When I Work or Deputy for scheduling  
- OneNote or Google Docs for shared notes  

That combo usually covers 90% of shift-based collaboration without overcomplicating things.
