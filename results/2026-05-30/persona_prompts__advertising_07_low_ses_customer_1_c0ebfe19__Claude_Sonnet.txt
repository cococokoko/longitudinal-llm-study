Effective ways to reduce household energy use.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here are some practical ways to reduce household energy use:

## Quick & Easy Changes
- Switch to LED bulbs
- Unplug devices when not in use (they draw "phantom" power)
- Use power strips to cut power to multiple devices at once
- Wash clothes in cold water
- Run dishwashers and laundry only with full loads

## Heating & Cooling
- Set thermostat a few degrees lower in winter, higher in summer
- Use fans instead of AC when possible
- Seal drafts around doors and windows with weatherstripping
- Keep vents unblocked by furniture

## Appliance Habits
- Use microwave or toaster oven instead of full oven when possible
- Keep refrigerator coils clean
- Air dry dishes and clothes when practical
- Cook multiple things at once when using the oven

## Slightly More Effort
- Install a programmable thermostat
- Add insulation to water heater
- Check if your utility offers **free energy audits**

## Worth Knowing
- Many utilities offer **budget billing** to smooth out monthly costs
- Some areas have **assistance programs** for energy costs
- Check for rebates on energy-efficient appliances

Given a busy and unpredictable schedule, focusing on the **set-it-and-forget-it** options like programmable thermostats and power strips might give you the most benefit with the least ongoing effort.
