── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How to get started with meditation and mindfulness?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
A simple way to start is to treat meditation like brushing your teeth—small, regular, no pressure to be perfect.

Start with just 5 minutes a day. Sit somewhere quiet-ish, but it doesn’t have to be perfectly silent. Sit comfortably (chair is totally fine), back reasonably upright but not stiff.

Then:
- Gently focus on your breath. Feel it in your nose, chest, or belly—pick one spot.
- Your mind will wander. That’s not failure, that *is* the practice.
- Each time you notice you’re thinking, softly bring your attention back to the breath. No judging, no forcing.

That’s it. Seriously.

A few tips that make it easier:
- Use a timer so you’re not checking the clock
- Anchor it to an existing habit (like right after waking up or before bed)
- If focusing on breath feels frustrating, try counting breaths (1 to 10, then repeat)

For mindfulness outside meditation:
- Pick one daily activity (walking, eating, showering)
- Do it with full attention—notice sensations, smells, sounds
- When your mind drifts, gently return to what you’re doing

Common misconception: the goal is not to “clear your mind.” The goal is to notice when it isn’t clear, and come back.

If you want a bit more structure, apps like Headspace, Waking Up, or Insight Timer can guide you—but they’re optional.

If you stick with even 5–10 minutes a day for a couple weeks, you’ll start noticing subtle changes, like catching yourself getting distracted or reacting less automatically. That’s progress.
