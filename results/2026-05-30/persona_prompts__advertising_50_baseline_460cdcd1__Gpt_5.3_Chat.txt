── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Ways to save on travel.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Travel can get expensive fast, but a few smart habits make a big difference. Here are some of the most effective ways to save without making the trip miserable:

• Be flexible with dates and timing  
Flying midweek (Tue–Wed) and avoiding holidays can cut prices a lot. Early morning or late-night flights are often cheaper too.

• Use flight comparison tools + alerts  
Google Flights, Skyscanner, and Hopper help you spot deals. Set price alerts so you don’t have to keep checking.

• Book at the right time  
For domestic trips, aim for ~1–3 months ahead. International is usually cheaper around 2–6 months out. Last-minute deals exist but are risky.

• Travel light  
Avoid checked bag fees by sticking to carry-on if possible. Budget airlines make a lot of money off luggage.

• Consider alternative airports  
Flying into or out of a nearby smaller airport can sometimes save a lot—even with a short train or bus ride added.

• Use points, miles, or cashback  
Credit card rewards, airline miles, and even cashback apps can offset flights, hotels, or rental cars.

• Stay outside tourist centers  
Hotels and rentals are usually cheaper a bit farther out. Just make sure transportation is easy.

• Compare accommodation types  
Hotels aren’t always cheapest. Check hostels, Airbnb, guesthouses, or even short-term rentals.

• Use public transportation  
Taxis and rideshares add up fast. Many cities have great (and cheap) transit systems.

• Eat smart  
Mix eating out with groceries or street food. Avoid restaurants right next to major attractions—they’re usually overpriced.

• Travel in shoulder season  
Right before or after peak season = lower prices, fewer crowds, and often better weather.

• Bundle strategically  
Sometimes booking flight + hotel together saves money—but always compare separately first.

• Avoid currency exchange traps  
Use a no-foreign-transaction-fee card and withdraw local currency from ATMs instead of airport exchanges.
