# Improving Productivity While Working From Home

Here are some practical strategies to help you perform well, especially important during your probation period:

## Structure Your Day
- **Set consistent start/end times** to establish routine
- **Create a morning routine** to mentally "switch on" for work
- **Schedule breaks** - short breaks actually improve focus
- **Use time-blocking** to dedicate focused periods to specific tasks

## Create Your Workspace
- Designate a specific area for work if possible
- Minimize distractions (phone notifications, household noise)
- Ensure good lighting and comfortable setup
- Keep your workspace tidy

## Stay Visible to Your Employer
- **Communicate proactively** - update your manager regularly
- **Respond promptly** to messages and emails
- **Document your progress** and completed tasks
- Over-communicate slightly during probation to build trust

## Manage Your Energy
- Take proper lunch breaks away from your screen
- Stay hydrated and move regularly
- Avoid working excessive hours unsustainably

## Tools That Help
- Task management apps (Todoist, Trello)
- Focus timers (Pomodoro technique)
- Calendar blocking

## Key Tip for Probation
Since you're building your reputation, **consistency matters more than occasional bursts of effort**. Reliable, steady output demonstrates dependability to your new employer.
