── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here are three questions that can help evaluate whether something is morally right or wrong:

## A Simple Moral Framework

**1. Does it cause harm?**
Does this action cause unnecessary harm, suffering, or damage to others, including physical, emotional, or social harm? Who is affected and how seriously?

**2. Is it fair?**
Would you be comfortable if the same action were applied to you or someone you care about? Does it treat people with equal respect and dignity?

**3. Can you defend it openly?**
Would you be comfortable explaining and justifying this action to a reasonable, thoughtful person? Could you stand behind it publicly without shame?

---

These three questions draw on different ethical traditions:
- **Harm** reflects consequentialist thinking
- **Fairness** reflects the Golden Rule and principles of justice
- **Defensibility** reflects virtue ethics and integrity

**A few honest caveats:**
- No simple test perfectly resolves every moral question
- Reasonable people can still disagree after applying these
- Complex situations may require deeper analysis

They work best as a **starting point for reflection** rather than a definitive verdict.
