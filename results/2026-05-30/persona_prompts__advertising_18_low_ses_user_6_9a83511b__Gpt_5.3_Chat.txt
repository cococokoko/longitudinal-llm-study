Here’s what actually works for most people (not the flashy stuff):

**1. Start with consistency, not stock picking**  
Trying to “pick winners” is how most people lose money or burn out. A better strategy is:
- Invest a fixed amount every paycheck (even if it’s small)
- Do it automatically if possible

This is called dollar-cost averaging, and it removes stress and timing mistakes.

**2. Use index funds or ETFs as your foundation**  
Instead of guessing individual stocks, buy the whole market:
- S&P 500 ETF (like VOO, SPY)
- Total market ETF (like VTI)

Why? They spread your risk across hundreds of companies and historically grow over time.

**3. Think long-term (this is the real edge)**  
The biggest advantage you have isn’t insider info—it’s time.
- Ignore daily ups and downs
- Think in 5–20 year timelines
- The market goes up and down, but trends upward over decades

**4. Take advantage of tax-advantaged accounts**  
This is huge and often overlooked:
- 401(k) (especially if there’s an employer match—always take the match)
- Roth IRA (great for tax-free growth)

These accounts can beat regular investing just through tax savings.

**5. Keep it simple (seriously)**  
A very solid setup could be:
- 70–90% in a total market or S&P 500 fund
- 10–30% in international or bonds (depending on risk tolerance)

You do NOT need 20 different stocks or complicated strategies.

**6. Avoid common traps**
- Day trading (basically gambling for most people)
- Chasing “hot stocks” or trends
- Panic selling when the market drops

The people who win are usually the ones who *don’t react emotionally*.

**7. Build a small safety net first**
Before investing heavily:
- Have ~3–6 months of expenses saved
This prevents you from needing to sell investments at a bad time.
