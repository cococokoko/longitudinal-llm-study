**The Problem of Overpopulation**

Overpopulation occurs when the number of people in a specific area—or on the planet as a whole—exceeds the carrying capacity of the environment. It is not just about the sheer number of people, but the ratio of people to available resources such as food, water, and land. 

**Primary Causes**
The rapid surge in global population, particularly over the last century, is driven by several factors:
*   **Medical Advancements:** Improved healthcare, vaccines, and antibiotics have drastically reduced mortality rates and increased life expectancy.
*   **Agricultural Progress:** Innovations in farming have increased food production, allowing populations to grow.
*   **Lack of Education and Family Planning:** In many developing regions, a lack of access to contraceptives and reproductive education leads to higher birth rates.

**Major Consequences**
Overpopulation puts immense pressure on the planet and human society, leading to several critical issues:
*   **Resource Depletion:** The demand for fresh water, arable land, and fossil fuels is outpacing the Earth’s ability to regenerate them.
*   **Environmental Degradation:** To accommodate growing populations, forests are cleared for housing and agriculture, leading to habitat loss, a decline in biodiversity, and increased greenhouse gas
