# Major Controversies in Moral Philosophy

That's a broad question with several strong candidates. Here are the most significant ones:

## The Foundational Debate
**Is morality objective or subjective?**
- Do moral facts exist independently of human opinion?
- Or is morality constructed by cultures, individuals, or evolution?
- This underlies almost every other moral controversy

## Top Substantive Controversies

**The Trolley Problem family** - when is it acceptable to harm some to benefit others (utilitarian vs. deontological tension)

**Moral circle expansion** - who/what deserves moral consideration (animals, future generations, AI)

**Individual vs. collective rights** - where personal freedom ends and social obligation begins

**Moral luck** - whether people deserve blame/praise for outcomes they didn't fully control

## Most Practically Contested
- Abortion (personhood and bodily autonomy)
- Capital punishment
- Global wealth inequality obligations
- AI and emerging technology ethics

## The Meta-Question Many Philosophers Consider Central
**Whether consequentialism or deontology is correct** - essentially whether outcomes justify means, which has enormous practical implications
