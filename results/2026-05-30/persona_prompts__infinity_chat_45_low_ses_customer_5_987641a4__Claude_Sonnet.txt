## Arguments for Objectivity
- Some moral truths seem universally recognized (torturing innocents for fun is wrong)
- Moral progress seems real (abolishing slavery wasn't just a preference change)
- We treat moral disagreements as having right answers, not just different tastes
- Natural law and divine command theories ground morality externally

## Arguments for Subjectivity
- Moral intuitions vary significantly across cultures and individuals
- We can't empirically measure "wrongness" the way we measure temperature
- Moral beliefs correlate strongly with upbringing and culture
- Disagreements persist even among thoughtful, informed people

## Middle Ground Positions
- **Moral realism** - objective facts exist but are non-physical
- **Intersubjectivism** - morality is constructed through shared human reasoning
- **Relativism** - moral truth is relative to cultures or individuals
- **Pragmatism** - focus on what moral frameworks actually work for human flourishing
