Nice, that’s a fun (and very doable) goal 😄

Let’s get you started in a simple, not-overwhelming way. Japanese has a few big pieces, so I’ll guide you through them step by step.

First, the basics of the language:

Japanese uses:
- Hiragana (basic phonetic alphabet)
- Katakana (used for foreign words)
- Kanji (Chinese characters with meaning)

Don’t worry about all three at once. Start here:

Step 1: Learn Hiragana (top priority)
This is the foundation. You need it to read anything.

For example:
- あ = a
- い = i
- う = u
- え = e
- お = o

Once you learn all hiragana (about 1–2 weeks with practice), everything else gets much easier.

Step 2: Learn basic phrases
Here are a few useful ones:

- こんにちは (konnichiwa) = hello
- ありがとう (arigatou) = thank you
- はい (hai) = yes
- いいえ (iie) = no

Step 3: Understand sentence structure
Japanese is different from English.

English: I eat sushi  
Japanese: I sushi eat  
→ 私は寿司を食べます (watashi wa sushi o tabemasu)

Verb goes at the end. That’s a big rule.

Step 4: Start with super simple grammar
Example:

- です (desu) = “is / am / are”

私は学生です  
watashi wa gakusei desu  
= I am a student

Step 5: Daily practice (very important)
Even 15–20 minutes a day works if you’re consistent.

- Duolingo (easy start)
- LingoDeer (better for grammar)
- Anki (flashcards for memory)
- YouTube (search: “Japanese for beginners hiragana”)
