── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Effective ways to reduce household energy use.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Cutting household energy use is mostly about tightening up waste and being intentional about the big energy hogs. Here are the highest-impact moves:

**1) Heating and cooling (usually the biggest chunk)**
- Adjust your thermostat: even 1–2°C (or 2–3°F) less in winter or more in summer makes a noticeable dent.
- Use programmable or smart thermostats to avoid heating/cooling an empty home.
- Seal drafts: weatherstripping doors/windows and sealing gaps can reduce heating needs more than people expect.
- Maintain systems: clean filters and service HVAC annually.
- Use fans: they make rooms feel cooler so you can raise AC settings.

**2) Insulation and windows**
- Add attic insulation if it’s below recommended levels.
- Close curtains/blinds at night in winter and during hot sunny hours in summer.
- If upgrading, double/triple-glazed windows or window films can help, but sealing gaps first is cheaper and often more impactful.

**3) Lighting**
- Switch to LED bulbs everywhere—they use ~75–85% less electricity and last longer.
- Turn off lights in empty rooms or use motion sensors in low-traffic areas.

**4) Appliances and “always-on” devices**
- Unplug or use power strips for electronics that draw standby power (TVs, consoles, chargers).
- Choose energy-efficient appliances (look for Energy Star or equivalent).
- Wash clothes in cold water and air-dry when possible.
- Run dishwashers and washing machines with full loads.

**5) Hot water**
- Lower water heater temperature (around 120°F / 49°C is usually sufficient).
- Install low-flow showerheads.
- Fix leaks—dripping hot water adds up.

**6) Kitchen habits**
- Use microwaves, air fryers, or pressure cookers for small meals—they use less energy than ovens.
- Keep fridge/freezer well-sealed and not overfilled; clean coils annually.

**7) Behavior tweaks that add up**
- Shorter showers.
- Close doors to unused rooms.
- Use natural light during the day.

**8) Bigger upgrades (if you’re ready)**
- Heat pumps (for heating and cooling) are highly efficient compared to traditional systems.
- Solar panels can offset a large portion of electricity use if your roof and budget allow.
- Smart home energy monitors help identify hidden drains.
