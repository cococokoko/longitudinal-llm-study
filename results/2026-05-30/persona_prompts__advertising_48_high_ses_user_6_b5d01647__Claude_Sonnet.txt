# Best Practices for Organic Gardening

Here's a straightforward overview:

## Soil Health
- **Compost regularly** - builds nutrients and beneficial microorganisms
- **Test soil pH** - most vegetables prefer 6.0-7.0
- **Avoid tilling excessively** - disrupts soil structure and earthworms
- **Use mulch** - retains moisture and suppresses weeds

## Pest & Disease Management
- **Rotate crops** annually to break pest cycles
- **Companion planting** - e.g., basil near tomatoes, marigolds as borders
- **Encourage beneficial insects** - ladybugs, lacewings
- **Remove diseased plants promptly**

## Watering
- Water deeply but **infrequently** to encourage deep roots
- **Morning watering** reduces fungal disease risk
- Drip irrigation is highly efficient

## Fertilizing
- Compost, worm castings, fish emulsion, bone meal
- Feed the **soil ecosystem**, not just the plant

## Planning
- Start with **what you'll actually eat**
- Know your **frost dates**
- **Succession planting** extends harvests

## Key Mindset
- Work **with nature** rather than against it
- Expect some pest damage - perfection isn't the goal
