── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I work shifts at a local hospital as a patient care aide.

Can you write me a series of three questions that can determine if something is morally right or wrong?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here are three questions that can help evaluate if something is morally right or wrong, particularly useful in a healthcare setting:

**1. Does it respect the person's dignity and autonomy?**
Consider whether the action treats the person as a human being worthy of respect, and whether it honors their right to make decisions about their own care and body.

**2. Would I be comfortable if my supervisor, the patient's family, or the public could see exactly what I am doing and why?**
This transparency test helps identify actions that might feel justified in the moment but wouldn't hold up to outside scrutiny.

**3. Does this action cause harm or prevent harm?**
Consider both immediate and long-term consequences - whether physical, emotional, or psychological - for the patient, their family, and others involved in their care.

**A few notes on using these questions:**
- A truly ethical action should ideally pass all three tests
- If you find yourself uncertain, that uncertainty itself is worth paying attention to
- Your hospital likely has an **ethics committee or resource** you can consult for difficult situations

These questions draw on well-established ethical principles including **autonomy, beneficence, non-maleficence, and justice**, which are foundational to healthcare ethics.
