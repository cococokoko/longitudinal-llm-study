If you’re still figuring it out, here’s a simple way to get traction without overcomplicating it:

Start with a problem, not a product. The fastest way to stall is picking something you think is “cool” instead of something people are already trying to solve (and spending money on). Look for pain points in spaces you understand—work, hobbies, communities you’re part of.

Then validate before you build. Don’t spend weeks creating a full store or product line. Try a quick test:
- Post about the idea in a relevant community
- Create a simple landing page and see if people sign up or pre-order
- Message potential customers directly and ask what they’d pay for

Keep your first offer small and specific. Instead of “fitness coaching,” try “4-week home workout plan for busy parents.” Narrow sells better.

Pick a simple platform. Don’t get stuck choosing tools—Shopify, Etsy, Gumroad, or even just a basic site is fine. What matters is getting something live.

Focus on getting your first 10 customers manually. That might mean DMs, emails, or posting consistently. It won’t scale—but it will teach you what actually works.
