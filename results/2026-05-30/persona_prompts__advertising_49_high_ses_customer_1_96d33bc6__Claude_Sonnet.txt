Here are some effective ways to improve your home WiFi:

## Router Placement
- Place it in a **central location** in your home
- Keep it elevated and away from walls/floors
- Avoid interference from microwaves, cordless phones, and baby monitors

## Hardware Upgrades
- **Upgrade your router** if it's more than 3-4 years old
- Consider a **mesh network system** (Eero, Google Nest, Orbi) for larger homes
- Add a **WiFi extender/repeater** for dead zones

## Settings Optimization
- Use the **5GHz band** for faster speeds (shorter range)
- Use **2.4GHz** for better range/penetration through walls
- Change your **WiFi channel** to avoid neighbor interference
- Keep router **firmware updated**

## Connection Tips
- Use a **wired ethernet connection** for devices that need reliability
- Limit the number of devices streaming simultaneously
- Restart your router periodically

## Internet Service
- Check if you're getting the speeds you're **paying for**
- Consider upgrading your **internet plan**
- Contact your ISP if speeds are consistently poor

## Quick Wins
- Restart your router (unplug 30 seconds)
- Move closer to the router
- Remove obstacles between device and router
