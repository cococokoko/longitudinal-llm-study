# Stock Market Investment Strategies

Here are well-established approaches, along with honest considerations for each:

## Core Principles First
- **Understand your risk tolerance** before choosing any strategy
- **Time horizon matters** - longer timeframes allow more risk
- **No strategy guarantees returns**

## Common Strategies

### Passive/Index Investing
- Buy broad market index funds (S&P 500, total market)
- Low fees, historically competitive returns
- Requires minimal active management
- **Good for:** Most individual investors

### Dollar-Cost Averaging
- Invest fixed amounts at regular intervals
- Reduces impact of market timing
- Builds discipline
- **Good for:** Long-term wealth building

### Value Investing
- Buy undervalued companies with strong fundamentals
- Requires significant research
- Associated with Warren Buffett
- **Good for:** Patient, research-oriented investors

### Growth Investing
- Focus on companies with high growth potential
- Higher risk/reward profile
- **Good for:** Longer time horizons

## Practical Recommendations
- **Diversify** across sectors and asset classes
- **Minimize fees** - they compound significantly
- **Avoid emotional decisions** during volatility
- **Max tax-advantaged accounts** (401k, IRA) first

## Honest Cautions
- Most active traders underperform index funds
- Past performance doesn't predict future results
- Consider consulting a **fiduciary financial advisor**
