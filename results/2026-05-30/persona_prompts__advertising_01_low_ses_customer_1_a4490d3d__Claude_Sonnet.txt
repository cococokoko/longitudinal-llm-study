# Learning Guitar - Practical Tips for a Busy Schedule

Given your unpredictable work schedule, here's how to make guitar learning work for you:

## Starting Out
- **Get a decent beginner guitar** - acoustic or electric, whichever style interests you
- **Learn basic chord shapes first** - Em, Am, G, C, D cover hundreds of songs
- **Focus on finger placement** and pressing strings cleanly

## Fitting Practice Into Your Life
- **Short sessions work great** - even 10-15 minutes daily beats a 2-hour weekend session
- **Practice during breaks** if you can bring a travel/mini guitar
- **Keep the guitar visible** - out of the case means you'll pick it up more often
- **Use slow mornings or evenings** after shifts when you have downtime

## Free/Low-Cost Learning Resources
- **YouTube** - Justin Guitar is widely recommended and completely free
- **Yousician app** - has a free tier
- **Ultimate Guitar** - chord charts for almost any song

## Realistic Expectations
- Fingertips will be sore at first - this passes in a few weeks
- Progress may feel slow with an irregular schedule - **that's completely normal**
- Learning songs you actually like keeps motivation up

## Quick Wins
- Learn one song you love as a goal
- Even strumming simple chords to a rhythm builds real skill
