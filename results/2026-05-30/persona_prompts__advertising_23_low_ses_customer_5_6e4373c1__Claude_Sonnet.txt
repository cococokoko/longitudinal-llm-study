── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here are some practical strategies to help you cook healthy meals quickly:

## Build a Strong Foundation
- **Learn 5-10 versatile base recipes** you can adapt (stir-fries, sheet pan meals, soups)
- **Master basic techniques** like roasting, sautéing, and steaming
- **Start simple** - don't try complex recipes when you're learning

## Smart Planning
- **Meal prep on weekends** - chop vegetables, cook grains, prep proteins in batches
- **Plan a weekly menu** so you're not deciding what to cook when you're already tired
- **Keep a rotating list** of meals your family actually enjoys

## Kitchen Efficiency
- **Stock a healthy pantry** - canned beans, frozen vegetables, whole grains, olive oil
- **Use time-saving tools** - sheet pans, slow cookers, instant pots
- **Cook once, eat twice** - make larger portions for leftovers

## Practical Tips for Busy Households
- **Involve family members** in age-appropriate prep tasks
- **Keep healthy shortcuts** on hand - pre-washed salad greens, rotisserie chicken
- **Have 3-4 "emergency meals"** you can make in under 20 minutes

## Learning Resources
- YouTube cooking channels
- Budget-friendly cookbooks focused on quick meals
- Recipe apps with filters for prep time
