# How to Improve Home WiFi

Here are practical tips that work well even with a busy schedule:

## Quick Fixes (5 minutes or less)
- **Restart your router** regularly (unplug, wait 30 seconds, plug back in)
- **Move your router** to a central location in your home
- **Keep it elevated** and away from walls/floors
- **Avoid interference** - keep away from microwaves, cordless phones, baby monitors

## Router Placement Tips
- Place it **out in the open**, not in a cabinet
- Higher positions generally work better
- Central to where you use devices most

## Reduce Interference
- Switch to **5GHz band** for faster speeds nearby
- Use **2.4GHz band** for better range/penetration through walls
- Change your **WiFi channel** in router settings to avoid neighbor overlap

## Affordable Upgrades
- **WiFi extender/repeater** (~$20-50) - boosts signal to dead zones
- **Mesh WiFi system** - better whole-home coverage
- **Upgrade your router** if it's more than 5 years old

## Check Your Internet Plan
- Make sure you're getting the speeds you're paying for
- Test at **fast.com** or **speedtest.net**
- Contact your ISP if speeds are consistently low

## Security Tip
- Make sure your WiFi has a **strong password** so neighbors aren't slowing your connection
