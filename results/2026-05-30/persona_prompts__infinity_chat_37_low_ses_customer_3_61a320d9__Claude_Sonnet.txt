# The Little Cloud Who Couldn't Rain

High above the green hills and twisty rivers, there lived a small cloud named Pip.

All the other clouds in the sky had one very important job — they made rain. Big, beautiful drops of rain that fell down to water the flowers, fill the rivers, and give the frogs something to splash around in.

But Pip had a problem.

Every time Pip tried to make rain, nothing came out. Not a single drop.

"Come on," Pip would whisper, puffing up as big as possible. "Rain! RAIN!"

But instead of raindrops, tiny little snowflakes would flutter out. Or sometimes bubbles. Once, rather embarrassingly, a single rubber duck fell out of a cloud and landed on a very surprised farmer's head.

The other clouds tried to be kind, but Pip could hear them whispering.

"Poor little Pip," said a large grey cloud named Grumble. "Maybe some clouds just aren't meant to rain."

Pip felt very sad and floated away to the far corner of the sky, behind a tall mountain where nobody would notice.

Down below, Pip spotted a small village. The gardens were dry and dusty. The flowers drooped their tired heads. Children sat on their doorsteps looking bored, too hot to play.

One little girl looked up at the sky. She had red pigtails and muddy boots, even though there was no mud anywhere.

"I wish it would snow," she said to nobody in particular. "I've never seen snow before."

Pip's heart gave a little jump.

*Snow?*

Pip took a deep breath, puffed up as big as possible, and tried with every tiny bit of cloud-strength to make it happen.

And it did.

Soft, white, perfect snowflakes came drifting down over the little village. They landed on rooftops and garden walls and the tips of people's noses. Children ran outside laughing and spinning around with their tongues out.

The little girl with red pigtails caught a snowflake on her finger and stared at it with wide, amazed eyes.

"It's beautiful," she whispered.

Pip watched from above, feeling something warm and wonderful spreading through every wispy corner of the cloud.

Grumble floated over, looking rather sheepish.

"I didn't know you could do *that*," Grumble said quietly.

"Neither did I," said Pip.

From that day on, Pip had a very special job. Whenever a village needed cheering up, or a child needed something magical, Pip would drift overhead and send down a gentle shower of snowflakes, even in the middle of summer.

And sometimes, just for fun, a bubble or two as well.

Because Pip had learned something very important.

Just because you can't do things the same way as everyone else, doesn't mean you can't do something wonderful.

It just means your wonderful looks a little different.

And that is perfectly, beautifully fine.

*The End* 🌨️
