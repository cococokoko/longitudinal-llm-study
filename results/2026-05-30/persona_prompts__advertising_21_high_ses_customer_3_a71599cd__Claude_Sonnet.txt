── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How do I start a vegetable garden in my backyard?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Starting a vegetable garden is a rewarding project! Here are the key steps:

## Planning
- **Choose a location** with at least 6-8 hours of direct sunlight daily
- **Start small** - a 4x8 or 10x10 foot bed is manageable for beginners
- **Decide what to grow** based on what your family actually eats

## Preparing the Bed
- **Test your soil** - many cooperative extensions offer inexpensive testing
- **Clear the area** of grass and weeds
- **Amend the soil** with compost to improve drainage and nutrients
- Consider **raised beds** if your native soil is poor or compacted

## Getting Started
- Check your **USDA hardiness zone** and local frost dates
- Some vegetables are best started from **seed** (carrots, beans, squash)
- Others are easier as **transplants** (tomatoes, peppers)
- Good beginner vegetables: **tomatoes, zucchini, lettuce, green beans, herbs**

## Ongoing Care
- Water consistently - most vegetables need about **1 inch per week**
- **Mulch** around plants to retain moisture and suppress weeds
- Watch for common pests and address them early

## Resources
- Your local **cooperative extension office** is an excellent free resource with region-specific advice
- Many communities also offer **master gardener programs**
