## Learning Coding with a Busy Warehouse Schedule

Here's a practical approach that fits your situation:

### Start Simple - Pick One Language
- **Python** is widely recommended for beginners - readable and versatile
- **JavaScript** if you're interested in web development
- Don't jump between languages early on

### Realistic Learning Options for Long Work Days

**Short sessions work fine:**
- Even 20-30 minutes daily beats occasional long sessions
- Use breaks or commute time if possible

**Free resources to start:**
- freeCodeCamp.org
- The Odin Project
- CS50 (Harvard's free course on edX)
- Codecademy

**YouTube channels:**
- Traversy Media
- Programming with Mosh

### Honest Expectations
- Expect **6-12 months** before feeling genuinely comfortable
- The first few weeks feel confusing for almost everyone
- Consistency matters more than intensity

### Practical Tips
- **Write code, don't just watch tutorials** - this is the biggest mistake beginners make
- Build small projects as soon as possible
- Use your phone for reading/watching when you can't be at a computer

What's your goal - career change, side projects, or general knowledge? That might affect which direction makes most sense.
