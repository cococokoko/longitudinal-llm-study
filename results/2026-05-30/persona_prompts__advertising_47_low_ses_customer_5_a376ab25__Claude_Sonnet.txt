# Marathon Training Tips

Here are some practical tips to help you prepare and succeed:

## Training
- **Build mileage gradually** - increase weekly distance by no more than 10% per week
- **Follow a structured plan** - most plans are 16-20 weeks long
- **Include long runs** - build up to 18-22 miles before race day
- **Rest days matter** - recovery is when your body actually gets stronger

## Nutrition & Hydration
- **Practice fueling during training** - test gels, chews, or real food on long runs
- **Stay hydrated daily**, not just during runs
- **Carb load strategically** in the 2-3 days before the race
- **Don't try anything new on race day**

## Time Management Tips
*(Since you're balancing a busy household)*
- Schedule runs like appointments - early mornings often work well
- Communicate your training schedule with family members
- Use some runs for mental decompression time
- Batch meal prep to support your nutrition needs efficiently

## Race Day
- **Start slower than you feel like you should**
- Break the race into smaller segments mentally
- Know the course and aid station locations
- Have a backup pace goal

## Recovery
- Prioritize sleep whenever possible
- Address minor injuries early before they become major
