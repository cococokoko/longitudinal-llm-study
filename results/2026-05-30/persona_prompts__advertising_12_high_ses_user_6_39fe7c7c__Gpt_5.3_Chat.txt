Eco-friendly travel options usually fall into a few levers: mode shift, efficiency within a mode, and demand reduction.

Mode shift (highest impact)
- Rail over short-haul flights, especially in regions with electrified networks (Europe, parts of Asia). Emissions can be 70–90% lower.
- Coach/bus over single-occupancy car or short flights for regional travel.
- For urban movement: public transit, cycling, walking, or e-mobility (shared bikes/scooters).

Efficiency within a mode
- Aviation: choose nonstop flights (takeoff/landing drive emissions), fly economy (lower per-seat footprint), prefer newer aircraft and airlines with higher load factors.
- Use Sustainable Aviation Fuel (SAF) programs where available—corporate agreements can materially reduce lifecycle emissions (though supply is limited and costly).
- Electric vehicles for car travel, ideally charged on low-carbon grids; otherwise hybrid vehicles.
- Rail operators powered by renewables where possible.

Demand reduction (often overlooked but highest ROI)
- Replace trips with high-quality virtual collaboration for internal meetings or early-stage client work.
- Hub-and-spoke trip planning: cluster multiple meetings into a single trip.
- Longer stays, fewer trips (reduce frequency, increase productivity per trip).

Corporate program levers
- Internal carbon pricing to steer choices (e.g., make air travel “more expensive” than rail in expense systems).
- Default booking settings: rail-first for sub-4–5 hour routes, economy class default, approved airline list based on efficiency.
- SAF procurement or book-and-claim schemes tied to travel volume.
- Travel emissions tracking integrated into expense platforms with team-level accountability.
- Incentives aligned to reduced travel or lower-carbon choices (not just cost).

Trade-offs to be candid about
- Time vs emissions: rail can be slower depending on geography; productivity en route often offsets part of that.
- SAF is not a silver bullet yet—limited supply and premium pricing.
- Offsets can play a role but should be last-mile, high-quality (removals preferred), not a substitute for reduction.
