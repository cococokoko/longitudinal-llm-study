# Improving Indoor Air Quality

Great practical question! Here are the most effective approaches:

## Quick & Affordable Steps
- **Open windows regularly** - even 10-15 minutes daily helps significantly
- **Use exhaust fans** in kitchens and bathrooms during/after use
- **Remove shoes at the door** - reduces tracked-in pollutants considerably
- **Keep humidity between 30-50%** to prevent mold growth

## Cleaning Habits
- Vacuum with a HEPA filter vacuum weekly
- Wash bedding regularly in hot water
- Dust with damp cloths rather than dry ones
- Clean or replace HVAC filters every 1-3 months

## Plants & Natural Options
- Some houseplants can help modestly (snake plants, pothos)
- **Realistic note:** plants alone won't dramatically change air quality

## Bigger Investments Worth Considering
- **Air purifiers with HEPA filters** - genuinely effective, especially for bedrooms
- Whole-home air filtration upgrades

## Things to Avoid/Reduce
- Synthetic air fresheners and heavy candles
- Harsh chemical cleaners (especially in enclosed spaces)
- Smoking indoors

## As a Busy Parent - Priorities
The **highest impact/lowest effort** combination is probably:
1. Regular filter changes
2. Good ventilation habits
3. A bedroom air purifier for sleeping
