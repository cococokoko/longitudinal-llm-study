── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How can I improve my credit score quickly?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
You can boost a credit score faster than most people think, but “quickly” usually means a few weeks to a couple of months—not overnight. The fastest gains come from fixing utilization and errors.

Here’s what actually moves the needle soon:

**1. Get your credit utilization down (biggest short-term impact)**
- Aim to use under 30% of your limit, ideally under 10%.
- If your card limit is $1,000, keep the reported balance below $300 (or even $100).
- Pay down balances before the statement closing date, not just the due date—that’s what gets reported.

**2. Pay everything on time (no exceptions)**
- Even one late payment can hurt a lot.
- If you’ve missed one recently, get current ASAP—damage lessens over time, but recent lateness hits hard.

**3. Ask for a credit limit increase**
- Higher limit + same spending = lower utilization.
- Ask your issuer if it’s a “soft pull” first so it doesn’t ding your score.

**4. Become an authorized user**
- If someone with a long, clean credit history adds you to a low-balance card, it can help pretty quickly.
- Make sure their account is spotless—otherwise it backfires.

**5. Dispute errors on your credit report**
- Check all three bureaus (Experian, Equifax, TransUnion).
- If there are mistakes (wrong balances, accounts, late payments), dispute them—fixes can bump your score within a month.

**6. Pay down specific accounts strategically**
- Focus on cards that are maxed out first—even one maxed card hurts more than several low-balance ones.

**7. Don’t open a bunch of new accounts**
- New hard inquiries and lower average account age can drag your score down short-term.

**What won’t help quickly**
- Closing old accounts (usually hurts)
- Carrying a balance “for your score” (myth—pay in full when you can)
- Credit repair companies (you can do the same things yourself for free)

If you want, tell me your rough situation (score range, number of cards, balances, any late payments), and I can give you a more targeted plan that could move your score in the next 30–60 days.
