── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
what I should look for when I Compare Expat Insurance?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Good question—expat insurance can look similar on the surface but differ a lot where it matters. Here’s what to actually pay attention to:

- **Coverage scope (inpatient vs outpatient)**  
  Some plans only fully cover hospitalization and barely cover doctor visits, prescriptions, or diagnostics. If you want everyday healthcare included, check outpatient limits carefully.

- **Geographic coverage**  
  “Worldwide” often excludes the U.S. or prices it much higher. Make sure your main country of residence and any places you travel to are included.

- **Annual limits and caps**  
  Look at the total maximum (e.g., $1M vs unlimited) and also sub-limits (like caps on maternity, mental health, or physical therapy). Low sub-limits can quietly reduce value.

- **Pre-existing conditions**  
  Many plans exclude them or impose waiting periods. Some offer partial coverage after a set time—read this part closely.

- **Deductibles and co-pays**  
  A cheaper premium often means higher out-of-pocket costs. Check:
  - Deductible per year or per condition  
  - Co-pay per visit  
  - Coinsurance percentages  

- **Emergency and evacuation coverage**  
  This is critical in countries with limited healthcare. Look for:
  - Medical evacuation to another country  
  - Repatriation (bringing you home)

- **Network and direct billing**  
  Can you go to hospitals without paying upfront? A strong direct billing network saves a lot of hassle.

- **Claims process**  
  Check how easy it is to file claims, typical reimbursement time, and whether they have an app. This matters more than people expect.

- **Maternity and family coverage**  
  Usually comes with waiting periods (often 10–12 months). If this is relevant, timing matters.

- **Mental health and preventive care**  
  Not always included or may have low caps.

- **Policy flexibility**  
  Can you adjust coverage, countries, or deductibles later? Important if your situation changes.

- **Reputation and support**  
  Look for reviews specifically about claim payouts and customer service—not just price.
