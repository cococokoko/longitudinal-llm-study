── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How do I start a vegetable garden in my backyard?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Starting a vegetable garden is a great project! Here's a straightforward guide:

## Planning
- **Choose a location** with at least 6-8 hours of sunlight daily
- **Start small** - a 4x8 or 10x10 foot bed is manageable for beginners
- **Decide on a style** - raised beds, in-ground rows, or containers

## Preparing the Soil
- Remove grass and weeds from your chosen area
- Loosen soil 12 inches deep if planting in-ground
- Add compost to improve drainage and nutrients
- Aim for loose, dark, crumbly soil

## Choosing What to Grow
- Start with easy vegetables like:
  - Tomatoes, zucchini, lettuce, green beans, radishes
- Consider your climate and growing season
- Check seed packets for days to maturity

## Planting
- Follow spacing instructions on seed packets
- Some vegetables start from seed directly in soil
- Others (like tomatoes) are easier to buy as seedlings
- Plant after your last frost date

## Ongoing Care
- **Water** consistently, usually 1 inch per week
- **Weed** regularly so plants aren't competing
- **Fertilize** every few weeks if needed
- Watch for pests and disease

## Helpful Tips
- Keep a simple garden journal
- Talk to neighbors or a local garden center about your specific region
- Be patient - gardening involves some trial and error
