# Managing Stress and Anxiety as a Physician-Scientist

Running a research group carries a **distinctive stress profile** - combining clinical demands, grant pressures, personnel management, and publication expectations simultaneously.

## Common High-Impact Sources

- Grant deadlines and funding uncertainty
- Trainee performance and wellbeing responsibility
- Clinical duties competing with research time
- Imposter syndrome at leadership level
- Administrative burden

## Evidence-Based Strategies

### Cognitive/Behavioral
- **Compartmentalization** - designated worry time rather than constant background anxiety
- **Cognitive reframing** of rejection (grants, papers) as normal process
- Identifying what is actually within your control

### Structural/Practical
- **Protecting deep work blocks** - reduces cognitive fragmentation stress
- Delegation with clear expectations
- Realistic goal-setting with your team

### Physiological
- Sleep prioritization (often the first casualty)
- Exercise - even brief, consistent activity
- Monitoring caffeine/alcohol as coping mechanisms

### Social/Professional
- Peer groups with other PI-level colleagues
- Mentorship relationships in both directions
- Being honest with your team about uncertainty

## Worth Considering
- Whether stress is **situational vs. chronic**
- Whether anxiety is affecting clinical judgment
- Formal support (therapy, particularly CBT) without stigma
