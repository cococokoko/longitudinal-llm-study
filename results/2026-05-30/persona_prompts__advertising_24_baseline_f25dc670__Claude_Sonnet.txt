# Best Ways to Save for Retirement

## Tax-Advantaged Accounts (Start Here)

**Employer-Sponsored Plans**
- **401(k) or 403(b)** - contribute enough to get any employer match first (it's free money)
- Contribution limit: $23,000/year (2024), plus $7,500 catch-up if 50+

**Individual Retirement Accounts**
- **Traditional IRA** - tax deduction now, pay taxes in retirement
- **Roth IRA** - pay taxes now, tax-free growth and withdrawals later
- Limit: $7,000/year (2024)

## General Principles

- **Start early** - compound interest rewards time significantly
- **Be consistent** - automate contributions so you don't skip them
- **Increase contributions** when income rises
- **Minimize fees** - choose low-cost index funds when possible

## Beyond Basic Accounts

- HSA (Health Savings Account) - triple tax advantage, good for healthcare costs
- Taxable brokerage accounts after maxing tax-advantaged options
- Real estate investment
- Pay off high-interest debt (guaranteed "return")

## Common Rules of Thumb

- Save **10-15%** of income for retirement
- **Emergency fund first** (3-6 months expenses)
- Prioritize: employer match → high-interest debt → IRA → max 401(k)

## Worth Considering

- Consult a **fee-only financial advisor** for personalized guidance
- Your timeline, income, and goals matter significantly
