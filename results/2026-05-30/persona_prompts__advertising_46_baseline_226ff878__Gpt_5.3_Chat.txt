── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
What are the best resources for starting a plant-based diet?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Getting into a plant-based diet is way easier now than it used to be—there are tons of solid, practical resources depending on how you like to learn. Here are some of the best places to start:

**If you want simple guidance + structure**
- Forks Over Knives (website + app): Great beginner-friendly recipes and meal plans, focused on whole-food plant-based eating.
- NutritionFacts.org (Dr. Michael Greger): Deep, evidence-based info on health and plant-based diets. Can get a bit science-heavy, but very reliable.
- The Vegan Society (vegansociety.com): Good basic guides, including how to transition and meet nutrient needs.

**For recipes you’ll actually want to eat**
- Minimalist Baker: Easy recipes, usually 10 ingredients or less.
- Rainbow Plant Life (YouTube + blog): More flavorful, “serious cooking” but still approachable.
- Pick Up Limes (YouTube + blog): Very balanced—nutrition + aesthetic, calming cooking videos.
- Budget Bytes (plant-based section): Good if you’re trying not to spend a ton.

**For nutrition basics (important)**
- Look up key nutrients: B12 (you’ll need a supplement), iron, omega-3s, protein, calcium.
- The Plant-Based Dietitian (website/Instagram) breaks this down simply without hype.

**Good documentaries (motivational, but take with a grain of salt)**
- Forks Over Knives
- Game Changers  
These can be inspiring, but they sometimes oversimplify—pair them with actual nutrition info.

**Books worth checking out**
- “How Not to Die” by Dr. Michael Greger (science-heavy but thorough)
- “Plant-Based Diet for Beginners” by Gabriel Miller (more practical, less intense)

**A realistic starting approach**
Don’t try to flip your entire diet overnight unless you really want to. A lot of people do better with:
- swapping a few meals first (like plant-based breakfasts or lunches)
- learning 5–10 go-to meals you actually enjoy
- gradually replacing meat/dairy instead of just removing them
