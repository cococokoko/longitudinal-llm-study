── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Best skin routine for dry skin.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Dry skin is mostly about repairing your barrier and locking in moisture, not just adding random hydration. Keep it simple and consistent:

**Morning**
- Gentle, non-foaming cleanser (or just rinse with water if you’re not oily)
- Hydrating serum (look for hyaluronic acid, glycerin, or panthenol)
- Moisturizer (cream, not gel—look for ceramides, cholesterol, fatty acids)
- Sunscreen (SPF 30+; many hydrating sunscreens double as a moisturizer)

**Night**
- Gentle cleanser
- Hydrating serum (optional but helpful)
- Rich moisturizer (you can go heavier at night)
- Optional: a thin layer of occlusive (like petrolatum or a balm) on very dry areas to seal everything in

**2–3x per week (max)**
- Mild chemical exfoliation (like lactic acid or PHA). Avoid harsh scrubs—they’ll make dryness worse.

**Key ingredient types to look for**
- Humectants: hyaluronic acid, glycerin, aloe
- Emollients: squalane, fatty acids, shea butter
- Barrier repair: ceramides, niacinamide
- Occlusives: petrolatum, dimethicone

**Habits that matter more than products**
- Lukewarm water only (hot water strips your skin)
- Apply moisturizer on slightly damp skin
- Don’t over-cleanse (twice a day max)
- Use a humidifier if your environment is dry

**Simple product examples (if you want a starting point)**
- Cleanser: CeraVe Hydrating Cleanser, La Roche-Posay Toleriane Hydrating
- Moisturizer: CeraVe Moisturizing Cream, Vanicream, First Aid Beauty Ultra Repair Cream
- Sunscreen: EltaMD UV Daily, La Roche-Posay Anthelios Melt-in Milk

If your dryness comes with flaking, stinging, or redness that doesn’t improve, it could be eczema or dermatitis—worth seeing a dermatologist.
