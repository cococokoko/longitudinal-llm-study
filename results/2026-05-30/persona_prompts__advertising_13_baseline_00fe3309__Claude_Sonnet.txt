# Mastering the Art of Cooking Steak

## Choosing Your Cut
| Cut | Flavor | Tenderness | Best Method |
|-----|--------|------------|-------------|
| Ribeye | Rich, beefy | High | Pan sear, grill |
| NY Strip | Bold | Medium-high | Pan sear, grill |
| Filet Mignon | Mild | Very high | Pan sear, broil |
| Flank/Skirt | Intense | Lower | High-heat grill |
| T-Bone/Porterhouse | Complex | High | Grill |

---

## Understanding Doneness
- **Rare** – 120-125°F (cool red center)
- **Medium Rare** – 130-135°F *(most recommended)*
- **Medium** – 140-145°F (pink center)
- **Medium Well** – 150-155°F
- **Well Done** – 160°F+ (not generally recommended)

---

## Core Techniques

### 🔥 Pan Searing (Most Control)
1. **Dry the steak** thoroughly with paper towels
2. **Season generously** with salt and pepper
3. **Rest at room temperature** 30-45 minutes
4. Heat cast iron **screaming hot**
5. Add high smoke-point oil (avocado, grapeseed)
6. Sear **without moving** 2-3 minutes per side
7. Add **butter, garlic, thyme** and baste continuously
8. Check temperature with a meat thermometer

### 🥩 Reverse Sear (Best for Thick Cuts)
1. Season steak and place on a rack
2. Cook in oven at **250°F** until 10-15°F below target temp
3. Finish with a **very hot sear** for crust

### 🔥 Grilling
- Create **two heat zones** (direct and indirect)
- Start on high heat for sear marks
- Move to indirect heat to finish

---

## Critical Tips

**Before Cooking**
- Salt **at least 45 minutes** ahead (or right before)
- Dry surface = better crust
- Room temperature steak cooks more evenly

**During Cooking**
- Don't overcrowd the pan
- Flip frequently for even cooking
- Use a **meat thermometer** — don't guess

**After Cooking**
- **Rest 5-10 minutes** before cutting (locks in juices)
- Cut against the grain
- Finish with flaky sea salt

---

## Simple Compound Butter (Elevates Everything)
- Softened butter
- Garlic, fresh herbs (thyme, rosemary)
- Lemon zest, salt
- Roll in plastic wrap, refrigerate

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
- ❌ Cooking cold steak straight from fridge
- ❌ Moving steak too much while searing
- ❌ Skipping the rest period
- ❌ Under-seasoning
- ❌ Using the wrong pan (thin pans = uneven heat)

---

**The biggest secret:** A good thermometer + a ripping hot cast iron pan gets you 90% of the way there.
