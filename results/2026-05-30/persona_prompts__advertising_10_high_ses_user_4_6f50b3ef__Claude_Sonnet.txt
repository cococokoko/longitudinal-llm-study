# Learning a New Language Effectively

As a physician-scientist, you likely have strong analytical skills and a busy schedule — here's a structured, evidence-based approach:

## Core Principles

- **Comprehensible input** is the primary driver of acquisition (Krashen's hypothesis has reasonable support)
- **Spaced repetition** dramatically improves vocabulary retention
- **Consistency beats intensity** — 30 min/day outperforms 3.5 hrs on weekends

---

## Practical Framework

### Foundation (Months 1-3)
- Learn the **top 1,000-2,000 most frequent words** using Anki with spaced repetition
- Master basic grammar structure — don't aim for perfection
- Apps like **Duolingo** are fine for habit-building, limited for real acquisition

### Immersion (Ongoing)
- Podcasts, shows, and books at your level
- **Comprehensible input tools:** Language Reactor, Dreaming Spanish
- Change phone/device language settings

### Output
- iTalki or Preply for **structured conversation practice**
- Speaking early reduces anxiety later

---

## Realistic Expectations
| Language Distance | Hours to Proficiency |
|---|---|
| Spanish/French | ~600 hrs |
| Russian/Arabic | ~1,100 hrs |
| Mandarin/Japanese | ~2,200 hrs |

---

## Given Your Schedule
- **Protect 20-30 min daily** — commute, lunch, exercise time
- Prioritize **listening** when multitasking is possible
