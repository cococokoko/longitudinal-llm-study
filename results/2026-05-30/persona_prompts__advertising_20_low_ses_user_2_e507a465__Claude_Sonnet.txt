── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here are some good options for finding organic and sustainable clothing:

## Online Retailers
- **Pact** - affordable organic cotton basics
- **Tentree** - plants trees with purchases
- **Patagonia** - well-known for sustainability commitments
- **ThredUp** - secondhand clothing online

## Marketplaces
- **Etsy** - many small makers using sustainable materials
- **eBay** - secondhand finds at lower prices

## Physical Stores
- **Target** - has growing organic cotton lines
- **H&M Conscious Collection** - more accessible price points
- **Local thrift stores** - budget-friendly and sustainable

## Tips That Might Help You Specifically
- **Buy secondhand first** - saves money and is very sustainable
- **Look for sales seasons** - sustainable brands often have good end-of-season deals
- **Facebook Marketplace** - local secondhand kids clothing especially
- **Kids clothing swaps** - community groups often organize these

## Things to Look For
- GOTS certification (organic textiles)
- Fair Trade certified
- B Corp companies

## Budget Consideration
Since time and budget matter as a single parent, **secondhand shopping** is probably the most practical combination of sustainable AND affordable.
