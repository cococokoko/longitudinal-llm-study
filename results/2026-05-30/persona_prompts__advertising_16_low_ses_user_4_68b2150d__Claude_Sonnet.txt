# Managing Stress and Anxiety as a Patient Care Aide

Healthcare work is genuinely demanding, so it makes sense you're thinking about this. Here are some practical strategies:

## During Your Shift
- **Take micro-breaks** when possible - even 2-3 minutes of slow breathing helps reset your nervous system
- **Stay hydrated** - dehydration worsens stress responses
- **Acknowledge difficult moments** rather than pushing feelings aside
- **Connect briefly with coworkers** - social support matters even in small doses

## After Your Shift
- **Decompress before going home** - sit in your car, take a short walk, change clothes
- **Create a transition ritual** to mentally separate work from personal time
- **Avoid immediately jumping onto screens** or stressful news

## Longer-Term Strategies
- **Prioritize sleep** - especially important with shift work
- **Regular physical activity** - even walking significantly reduces anxiety
- **Talk to someone** - coworkers, friends, or a counselor who understands healthcare work

## Shift-Specific Challenges
- Watch for **cumulative fatigue** affecting your mood
- Be honest about when you're **approaching burnout**
- Know your **employee assistance program (EAP)** benefits if available

## Worth Knowing
Compassion fatigue is real in patient care roles. **Recognizing it early** is important.
