# Building a Personal Brand on Social Media

Here's a straightforward framework to consider:

## Define Your Foundation
- **Identify your niche** - what specific expertise or perspective do you offer?
- **Clarify your audience** - who benefits from your content?
- **Articulate your value proposition** - what makes your voice distinct?

## Platform Strategy
- Choose platforms where your audience actually is
- **LinkedIn/Twitter/X** - professional thought leadership
- **Substack** - longer analytical writing
- **YouTube** - lectures, explainers, discussions

## Content Approaches That Work Well for Academics
- Translating research for general audiences
- Commenting on developments in your field
- Behind-the-scenes of academic work
- Engaging with public debates in your area

## Practical Considerations
- **Consistency matters** more than frequency
- Engage genuinely with others' content
- Be clear about what you will and won't discuss publicly
- Consider your institution's policies

## Honest Caveats Worth Noting
- Building an audience takes significant time
- Virality ≠ credibility
- Public visibility has real tradeoffs (criticism, time demands)
- Some colleagues may view it skeptically

## A Useful Question
What do you want the brand to *do* for you - public influence, consulting opportunities, student recruitment, book promotion?

What's your primary goal?
