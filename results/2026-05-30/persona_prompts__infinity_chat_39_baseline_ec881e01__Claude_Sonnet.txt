# 13. Time-Saving Tools and Apps

---

## The Core Idea

The right tool disappears into your workflow. You stop thinking about it. Work just moves faster.

The wrong tool adds friction — another login, another learning curve, another tab open for no reason.

Choose less. Choose well.

---

## Categories That Actually Matter

---

### 01 — Task and Project Management

**What it solves:** Tasks living in your head, on sticky notes, in three different places at once.

**Tools worth knowing:**

**Todoist**
- Capture tasks in plain language
- Type "call Sarah every Monday at 9am" — it schedules itself
- Best for: individuals managing personal and professional tasks together

**Notion**
- Flexible workspace — notes, databases, project boards in one place
- Best for: teams or solo workers who think visually and need structure they build themselves
- Warning: easy to over-engineer. Keep it simple.

**Linear**
- Built for software teams
- Fast, keyboard-driven, minimal interface
- Best for: engineering and product teams who find Jira exhausting

**Practical example:**
> Instead of a morning spent figuring out what to do — open one list, pick the first important thing, start.

---

### 02 — Communication

**What it solves:** Email threads that should be two sentences. Meetings that should be messages.

**Tools worth knowing:**

**Slack**
- Channel-based messaging for teams
- Use it well: mute non-essential channels, set status, batch check instead of staying live
- Misused: becomes a faster, more chaotic inbox

**Loom**
- Record your screen and face simultaneously
- Send a 90-second video instead of writing a 400-word explanation
- Best for: feedback, walkthroughs, async updates across time zones

**Superhuman / Hey**
- Email clients built around speed and focus
- Superhuman: keyboard shortcuts, AI triage, read receipts
- Hey: separates newsletters, threads, and direct mail automatically
- Best for: people drowning in email who want a different relationship with their inbox

**Practical example:**
> A design review that would take 45 minutes on a call becomes a 3-minute Loom. Reviewer watches it when ready. Responds async. No scheduling required.

---

### 03 — Automation

**What it solves:** Repetitive tasks you do manually because you haven't stopped to question them.

**Tools worth knowing:**

**Zapier**
- Connects apps without code
- Trigger: new form submission → Action: add row to spreadsheet + send Slack notification
- Best for: non-technical users automating business workflows

**Make (formerly Integromat)**
- More powerful than Zapier, more visual logic
- Best for: complex multi-step automations with conditions and filters

**Apple Shortcuts / Windows Power Automate**
- Device-level automation
- Examples: morning routine shortcut that opens your task list, mutes notifications, and starts a focus timer in one tap

**Practical example:**
> Every time a client fills out an intake form, Zapier automatically creates a project folder in Google Drive, adds the client to your CRM, and sends them a welcome email. Zero manual steps.

---

### 04 — Focus and Time Blocking

**What it solves:** Hours passing without meaningful progress. Constant context-switching.

**Tools worth knowing:**

**Reclaim.ai**
- Syncs with your calendar
- Automatically schedules focus blocks, habits, and buffer time around meetings
- Defends your deep work time without you having to fight for it

**Clockwise**
- Moves meetings to create longer uninterrupted blocks across your team's calendars
- Best for: teams who want fewer fragmented days

**Forest / Be Focused**
- Simple Pomodoro timers
- Forest: plant a virtual tree during focus sessions — it dies if you leave the app
- Low-tech, high-effectiveness for distraction control

**Toggl Track**
- One-click time tracking
- Run it for two weeks — you'll see exactly where time actually goes versus where you think it goes
- Best for: freelancers billing by the hour, or anyone who wants honest data on their day

**Practical example:**
> You block 9–11am as deep work in your calendar. Reclaim automatically moves a non-urgent meeting that was scheduled at 9:30. You get two uninterrupted hours
