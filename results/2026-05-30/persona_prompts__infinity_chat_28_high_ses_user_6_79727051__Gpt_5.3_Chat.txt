Got it — you’re probably not looking for “how to start a Shopify store,” but the deeper mechanics, strategy, and systems behind serious ecommerce. Here are some of the most in-depth, high-signal books, grouped so you can zero in on what matters.

**Core ecommerce strategy & business models**
- *7 Powers* — Hamilton Helmer  
  Not ecommerce-specific, but one of the sharpest frameworks for understanding durable advantage (scale economies, switching costs, network effects). Very applicable to marketplaces and platforms.

- *The Everything Store* — Brad Stone  
  Deep case study of Amazon’s operating model, logistics thinking, and long-term strategy.

- *No Ordinary Disruption* — McKinsey Global Institute  
  Broader, but useful for macro forces shaping ecommerce (digitization, platforms, globalization).

---

**Conversion, UX, and digital psychology (critical for ecommerce performance)**
- *You Should Test That!* — Chris Goward  
  One of the best practical frameworks for experimentation and conversion optimization.

- *Evil by Design* — Chris Nodder  
  Behavioral economics applied to digital interfaces (dark patterns, persuasion mechanics).

- *Hooked* — Nir Eyal  
  Overused, but still useful for thinking about habit-forming product loops.

- *Don’t Make Me Think* — Steve Krug  
  Not deep academically, but foundational for usability thinking.

---

**Growth, experimentation, and scaling**
- *Lean Analytics* — Alistair Croll & Benjamin Yoskovitz  
  Strong on metrics, cohort thinking, and what actually matters at each stage.

- *Hacking Growth* — Sean Ellis & Morgan Brown  
  More tactical, but solid on growth loops and experimentation culture.

- *Product-Led Growth* — Wes Bush  
  Especially relevant for hybrid ecommerce/SaaS or subscription commerce models.

---

**Marketplaces, platforms, and network effects**
- *Matchmakers* — David S. Evans & Richard Schmalensee  
  Deep dive into multi-sided platforms (pricing, liquidity, chicken-and-egg problems).

- *The Business of Platforms* — Cusumano, Gawer, Yoffie  
  More academic, but excellent on platform strategy and governance.

- *Platform Revolution* — Parker, Van Alstyne, Choudary  
  Accessible but still substantive overview of platform economics.

---

**Operations, logistics, and supply chain (often under-covered, but decisive)**
- *The Box* — Marc Levinson  
  History of containerization; surprisingly relevant to understanding global commerce economics.

- *Competing Against Time* — Stalk & Hout  
  Classic on time-based competition — still very relevant to fulfillment and last-mile.

- *Amazon Unbound* — Brad Stone  
  Goes deeper into logistics, AWS cross-subsidization, and ecosystem strategy.

---

**Data, personalization, and recommendation systems**
- *Prediction Machines* — Ajay Agrawal et al.  
  Strong framework for thinking about AI’s role in decision-making (pricing, recommendations).

- *Algorithms to Live By* — Christian & Griffiths  
  Conceptual, but helpful for thinking about search, sorting, and exploration vs exploitation.

---

**Payments, pricing, and monetization**
- *The Art of Pricing* — Rafi Mohammed  
  Practical pricing strategies (bundling, dynamic pricing, segmentation).

- *Priceless* — William Poundstone  
  Behavioral pricing insights — highly relevant for ecommerce conversion.

---
