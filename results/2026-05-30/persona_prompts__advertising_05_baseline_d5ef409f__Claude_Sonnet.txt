# Healthy Meal Planning on a Budget

## Core Principles

- **Plan before you shop** — reduces impulse buying and waste
- **Cook at home** — restaurant meals cost 3-5x more
- **Embrace whole foods** — less processed = cheaper + healthier
- **Use everything** — minimize food waste

---

## Budget-Friendly Healthy Foods

### Proteins
- Eggs
- Canned tuna/salmon
- Dried or canned beans/lentils
- Chicken thighs (cheaper than breasts)
- Tofu

### Grains & Starches
- Oats
- Brown rice
- Whole wheat pasta
- Potatoes/sweet potatoes

### Produce
- Frozen vegetables (just as nutritious)
- Seasonal fresh produce
- Bananas, apples, cabbage, carrots

---

## Practical Strategies

**Batch cooking** — cook once, eat multiple times
**Meal prep Sunday** — prep ingredients for the week
**Shop sales** — plan meals around what's discounted
**Buy store brands** — often identical quality
**Freeze extras** — bread, meat, leftovers

---

## Sample Weekly Budget (~$50 for one person)

| Day | Meal Idea |
|-----|-----------|
| Monday | Lentil soup + bread |
| Tuesday | Rice & bean bowls |
| Wednesday | Egg stir-fry with frozen veggies |
| Thursday | Pasta with canned tomatoes |
| Friday | Baked chicken thighs + sweet potato |
| Weekend | Oatmeal, leftovers, simple salads |

---

## Quick Tips
- **Never shop hungry**
- Check unit prices, not just total price
- Spices make cheap food taste great
- Water > juice or soda
