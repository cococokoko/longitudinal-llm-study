Effective ways to reduce household energy use.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here are some effective strategies to reduce household energy use:

## Quick Wins
- Switch to LED lighting
- Unplug devices when not in use (phantom load)
- Use power strips with switches
- Adjust thermostat settings (programmable/smart thermostats)

## Heating & Cooling (typically largest expense)
- Seal air leaks around windows, doors, and outlets
- Add insulation to attic and walls
- Change HVAC filters regularly
- Use ceiling fans strategically
- Close vents in unused rooms

## Appliances & Habits
- Run dishwashers and washing machines with full loads
- Wash clothes in cold water
- Clean dryer lint traps and check exhaust vents
- Choose ENERGY STAR appliances when replacing
- Air dry dishes and clothes when possible

## Water Heating
- Lower water heater temperature to 120°F
- Fix leaky faucets
- Install low-flow fixtures
- Consider tankless water heaters

## Bigger Investments
- Solar panels
- Better windows (double/triple pane)
- Heat pump systems
- Home energy audit (often free through utilities)

## Monitoring
- Smart meters and energy monitors help identify consumption patterns
- Many utility companies offer free usage analysis

A home energy audit is often the best starting point since it identifies your specific highest-impact opportunities.
