# Tips for Running a Marathon

## Training
- **Follow a structured plan** - Most plans are 16-20 weeks long
- **Build mileage gradually** - Increase weekly mileage by no more than 10% per week
- **Include long runs** - Build up to at least one 20-mile run before race day
- **Rest and recovery** - Schedule rest days to prevent injury
- **Cross-train** - Swimming, cycling, or yoga can complement your running

## Nutrition & Hydration
- **Practice fueling during training** - Use gels, chews, or real food on long runs
- **Stay hydrated** - Drink water consistently, not just when thirsty
- **Carb load** - Increase carbohydrate intake 2-3 days before the race
- **Don't try anything new on race day**

## Gear
- **Get fitted for proper shoes** - Visit a running store for gait analysis
- **Wear moisture-wicking clothing** - Avoid cotton
- **Use anti-chafe products** - Body Glide or petroleum jelly on friction areas

## Race Day Strategy
- **Start slow** - Going out too fast is the most common mistake
- **Run your own race** - Ignore the crowd pace early on
- **Break it into segments** - Focus on smaller milestones
- **Expect the wall** - Around mile 18-20, push through mentally

## Mental Tips
- Practice **positive self-talk**
- Visualize crossing the finish line
- **Smile** - it genuinely helps!
