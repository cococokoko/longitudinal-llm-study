── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
what are some most in-depth books about ecommerce websites

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
If you’re looking for *deep, not fluffy* books on ecommerce websites—stuff that actually gets into strategy, systems, and how things work under the hood—these are some of the best:

- **“Ecommerce Evolved” – Tanner Larsson**  
  Very tactical and systems-focused. Goes deep into funnels, customer journeys, offer structures, and backend optimization. One of the more “operator-minded” books.

- **“Building a StoryBrand” – Donald Miller**  
  Not ecommerce-specific, but extremely useful for how your website communicates. Helps you structure product pages and landing pages that actually convert.

- **“Don’t Make Me Think” – Steve Krug**  
  Classic on usability. Not ecommerce-specific, but critical if you care about how people navigate and buy on your site.

- **“Hooked” – Nir Eyal**  
  Focuses on behavioral design and habit-forming products. More relevant if you're building retention loops (subscriptions, repeat purchases, apps tied to ecommerce).

- **“The Conversion Code” – Chris Smith**  
  Good mix of psychology, funnels, and digital sales processes. Practical for improving conversion rates.

- **“You Should Test That” – Chris Goward**  
  One of the better books on A/B testing and conversion rate optimization. Very relevant for ecommerce optimization.

- **“Lean Analytics” – Alistair Croll & Benjamin Yoskovitz**  
  Helps you understand what metrics actually matter at different stages of an ecommerce business.

- **“Platform Revolution” – Parker, Van Alstyne, Choudary**  
  More advanced and conceptual—useful if you're thinking beyond a store into marketplace or multi-sided platforms.

- **“The Everything Store” – Brad Stone**  
  Not a how-to, but a deep dive into Amazon’s evolution. Great for understanding ecommerce at scale.

- **“Retail Analytics” – Emmett Cox**  
  More technical. Covers data, forecasting, customer behavior—useful if you want to go deeper into analytics and decision-making.

If you want something *more technical (actual website building, architecture, performance)* vs *marketing/strategy*, say the word—I can narrow it down hard in that direction.
