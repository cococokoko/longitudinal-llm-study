# Healthy Meal Planning on a Budget

Here are practical strategies that work well for larger households with many responsibilities:

## Core Strategies

**Plan Before You Shop**
- Plan meals for the full week before buying anything
- Check what you already have first
- Build meals around what's on sale

**Buy Smart**
- Dried beans, lentils, and rice are incredibly affordable and nutritious
- Frozen vegetables are just as healthy as fresh and last longer
- Buy larger cuts of meat and portion them yourself
- Store brands are usually identical quality

## Budget-Friendly Staples
- Eggs (versatile and cheap protein)
- Oats
- Canned tomatoes
- Cabbage, carrots, potatoes
- Peanut butter

## Time-Saving Tips for Busy Households
- **Batch cook on weekends** - make large pots of soup, rice, or beans
- **One-pot meals** stretch ingredients further
- Cook proteins in bulk and use different ways throughout the week
- Prep vegetables all at once

## Stretching Meals Further
- Add beans or lentils to meat dishes to increase portions
- Soups and stews feed more people economically
- Breakfast foods work great for dinner

## Rough Weekly Budget Ideas
- Focus on **cost per serving** not package price
- Aim for meals around **$1-2 per person**
