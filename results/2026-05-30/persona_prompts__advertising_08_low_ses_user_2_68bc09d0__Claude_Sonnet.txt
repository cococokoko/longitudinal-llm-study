# Finding Hiking Trails as a Single Parent

Here are some practical ways to find great trails that work for your situation:

## Trail-Finding Resources
- **AllTrails app** - filter by difficulty, length, and kid-friendly ratings
- **hikingproject.com** - detailed trail information
- **Local parks & recreation websites** - often list family-friendly options
- **Facebook parenting groups** - real recommendations from local parents

## What to Look For
- **Paved or wide paths** for strollers if needed
- **Short loops** so you can turn back easily
- **Restroom facilities** at trailheads
- **Low elevation gain** for younger kids
- **Interesting features** like streams or wildlife to keep kids engaged

## Practical Tips for Solo Parenting on Trails
- **Start small** - build up distance gradually
- **Go early** - less crowded, cooler temperatures
- **Tell someone** your planned route
- **Pack extra snacks** - they solve many problems
- **Download offline maps** in case of no signal

## Making It Easier
- Look for **hiking groups for single parents** in your area
- **Nature centers** often have guided family walks
- Consider **baby carriers** for toddlers on longer trails

## Safety First
- Bring more water than you think you need
- Check weather before going
- Keep a basic first aid kit handy
