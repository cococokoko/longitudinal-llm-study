Here are some well-regarded approaches, with honest context:

## Common Strategies

**Long-term Investing (Buy & Hold)**
- Invest in diversified index funds (S&P 500, total market)
- Historically averages ~7-10% annually
- Low time commitment, lower fees
- **Often best for busy people managing multiple responsibilities**

**Dividend Investing**
- Focus on stocks paying regular dividends
- Creates passive income stream
- Generally lower volatility

**Value Investing**
- Buy undervalued companies (Warren Buffett approach)
- Requires significant research time

**Growth Investing**
- Focus on high-growth companies
- Higher risk/reward potential

**For someone with family financial responsibilities:**
- Prioritize **emergency fund first** (3-6 months expenses)
- Avoid strategies requiring constant monitoring
- Keep risk appropriate to your obligations
- Tax-advantaged accounts (401k, IRA) often beat active trading after taxes

## What Research Actually Shows
- Most active traders **underperform** simple index funds
- Time *in* the market typically beats timing the market
- Fees and taxes significantly impact returns
