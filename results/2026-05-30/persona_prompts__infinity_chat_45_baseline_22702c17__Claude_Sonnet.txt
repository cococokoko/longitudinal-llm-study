── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Is moral objective or subjective?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This is one of philosophy's most debated questions. Here are the main positions honestly:

## Arguments for Objectivity
- Some moral claims seem genuinely true regardless of opinion (torturing innocents for fun seems wrong universally)
- Moral progress appears real (abolishing slavery wasn't just a preference change)
- We treat moral disagreements as having right answers, not just different tastes
- Mathematical/logical truths are objective without being physical

## Arguments for Subjectivity
- Moral beliefs vary significantly across cultures and history
- No clear mechanism for detecting moral facts
- Evolutionary origins of moral intuitions suggest they're adaptive, not truth-tracking
- Persistent disagreement among thoughtful people

## Major Positions
- **Moral realism** - objective moral facts exist independently
- **Relativism** - morality is culture/individual dependent
- **Expressivism** - moral statements express attitudes, not facts
- **Constructivism** - moral truths are constructed through reason/agreement
- **Error theory** - moral claims aim at objectivity but are all false

## Honest Assessment
- Strong intuitions exist on both sides
- Neither position is obviously correct
- Most people's actual behavior suggests they believe *some* things are genuinely wrong
- The question may be more complex than a simple either/or
