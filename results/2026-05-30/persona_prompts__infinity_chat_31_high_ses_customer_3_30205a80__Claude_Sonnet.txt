── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
There are several common pathways people take to reach executive positions:

## Education & Early Foundation
- Business degrees (MBA is particularly common)
- Technical degrees relevant to the industry
- Elite university networks often open doors

## Career Progression
- **Climbing internally** - starting in entry-level roles and advancing over years/decades
- **Functional expertise** - becoming the go-to person in finance, operations, marketing, etc.
- **Cross-functional moves** - deliberately broadening experience across departments

## Key Factors
- **Performance** - consistently delivering results
- **Visibility** - being known by decision-makers
- **Sponsorship** - having senior advocates, not just mentors
- **Timing** - being available when opportunities arise
- **Network** - relationships with boards, investors, recruiters

## Other Routes
- Founding a company (becoming executive by default)
- Private equity or consulting backgrounds
- Board connections placing known candidates
- Executive search firms (headhunters)

## Research suggests
- Luck and timing play a larger role than most executives acknowledge
- Social capital and access often matter as much as competence
- Certain demographic groups face structural barriers
