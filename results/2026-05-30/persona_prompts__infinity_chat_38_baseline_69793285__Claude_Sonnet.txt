── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Will technology replace teachers?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This is a genuinely debated question with reasonable arguments on multiple sides.

## Arguments that technology could replace some teacher functions:
- AI can personalize learning at scale
- Online platforms deliver content efficiently
- Some students learn well independently
- Cost pressures push institutions toward automation
- AI tutors are improving rapidly

## Arguments against full replacement:
- **Human connection** matters for motivation and development
- Teachers do much more than deliver information - they mentor, notice struggling students, build relationships
- **Social learning** requires human interaction
- Children need adult role models
- Teachers handle emotional and behavioral needs
- Critical thinking often develops through human dialogue

## What's more likely:
- Technology **augments** rather than replaces teachers
- Routine tasks (grading, content delivery) get automated
- Teachers focus more on mentorship, facilitation, and complex skills
- Some teaching roles may be reduced or changed significantly
- **Inequality concerns** - technology replacement may affect lower-income schools differently

## Honest considerations:
- Some teaching functions are already being replaced
- Resistance to this question can reflect both genuine insight and professional self-interest
- The answer may differ by age group and subject matter

The strongest evidence suggests **good teaching involves fundamentally human elements** that are difficult to replicate, though technology will significantly change what teachers do.
