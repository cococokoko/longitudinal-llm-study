# Improving Indoor Air Quality

Here are the most effective approaches:

## Ventilation
- Open windows regularly when outdoor air quality permits
- Use exhaust fans in kitchens and bathrooms
- Ensure HVAC systems are properly maintained
- Consider energy recovery ventilators (ERVs)

## Filtration
- Use HEPA air purifiers in frequently occupied rooms
- Replace HVAC filters regularly (every 1-3 months)
- Choose filters with higher MERV ratings (8-13 for most homes)

## Source Control
- Use low-VOC paints, cleaning products, and furnishings
- Avoid smoking indoors
- Store chemicals properly and use them in ventilated areas
- Test for radon and mitigate if necessary

## Humidity Management
- Keep indoor humidity between **30-50%**
- Use dehumidifiers in damp areas to prevent mold
- Fix water leaks promptly

## Reducing Specific Pollutants
- Install carbon monoxide detectors
- Have gas appliances inspected regularly
- Use doormats and remove shoes to reduce tracked-in pollutants
- Vacuum with HEPA-equipped vacuums

## Plants
- Some houseplants may offer modest air quality benefits, though research is **mixed** on their practical impact
