## Overview

Overpopulation refers to the condition in which a region's human population exceeds the carrying capacity of its environment and available resources. The global population has surpassed **8 billion people**, raising significant concerns across multiple disciplines, including environmental law, public policy, and human rights.

## Key Concerns

- **Resource Depletion** – Increased demand for water, food, and energy strains finite natural resources
- **Environmental Degradation** – Expanded human activity accelerates deforestation, pollution, and climate change
- **Economic Inequality** – Rapid population growth in developing regions can outpace economic development
- **Infrastructure Strain** – Healthcare, housing, and educational systems face mounting pressure
- **Geopolitical Tension** – Competition for scarce resources can contribute to conflict

## Policy Considerations

Governments and international bodies have pursued various regulatory responses, including family planning initiatives, immigration policy reform, and sustainable development frameworks under instruments such as the **UN Sustainable Development Goals**.

## Conclusion

Overpopulation remains a complex, multifaceted challenge requiring carefully balanced legal, ethical, and policy solutions that respect individual rights while addressing collective sustainability.
