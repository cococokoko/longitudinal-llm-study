Dry skin is mostly about protecting your barrier and keeping water in your skin, not just adding moisture once and hoping it sticks. A simple, consistent routine usually works better than a complicated one.

Morning:
Use a gentle, non-foaming cleanser or just rinse with lukewarm water if you’re not oily. Look for something labeled “hydrating” or “cream cleanser.” While your skin is still slightly damp, apply a hydrating serum with ingredients like hyaluronic acid or glycerin. Then go in with a richer moisturizer—look for ceramides, cholesterol, and fatty acids (these help repair the skin barrier). Finish with sunscreen SPF 30 or higher; dry skin is often more sensitive, and sun exposure worsens dryness over time.

Evening:
Cleanse again gently—avoid anything that leaves your skin feeling tight. If you want an active, use something mild like lactic acid or a low-strength retinoid a few nights a week, but don’t overdo it; dry skin gets irritated easily. Then apply a hydrating serum again if you like, followed by a thicker moisturizer than in the morning. At night you can “seal” everything with an occlusive a few times a week—something like petrolatum (Vaseline) or a balm—to lock in moisture.

A few key habits that matter more than products:
Avoid hot showers; they strip oils quickly. Pat your skin dry instead of rubbing. Apply moisturizer within a minute or two of washing—this makes a noticeable difference. Use a humidifier if your environment is dry. And be cautious with exfoliation; once or twice a week max, and skip harsh scrubs.

If your dryness is persistent, flaky, or irritated, it’s worth checking whether there’s an underlying issue like eczema or dermatitis—then the routine needs to be more targeted.
