# Best Practices for Organic Gardening

## Soil Health
- **Compost regularly** - add finished compost to improve structure and nutrients
- **Test your soil** periodically to understand pH and nutrient levels
- **Avoid tilling excessively** - preserves soil structure and beneficial organisms
- **Use cover crops** like clover or rye to prevent erosion and add nitrogen

## Fertilizing Naturally
- Compost, worm castings, and aged manure
- Bone meal, blood meal, and kelp for specific nutrients
- **Feed the soil, not just the plants**

## Pest & Disease Management
- **Companion planting** - marigolds deter pests, basil repels aphids
- Encourage beneficial insects (ladybugs, lacewings)
- Use row covers for physical protection
- Neem oil and insecticidal soap for targeted treatment
- Remove diseased plants promptly

## Water Management
- Water deeply and less frequently to encourage deep roots
- **Drip irrigation** reduces disease and water waste
- Mulch heavily to retain moisture

## General Tips
- **Rotate crops** each season to prevent disease buildup
- Choose disease-resistant varieties
- Start with what grows well in your climate
- Keep a garden journal to track what works

## Key Principle
> Healthy soil → healthy plants → fewer problems
