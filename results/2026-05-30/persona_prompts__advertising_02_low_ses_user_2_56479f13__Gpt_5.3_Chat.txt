Got you — let’s keep this simple, effective, and realistic for a busy schedule.

Dry skin isn’t just about “adding moisture,” it’s about *keeping it in*. So your routine should focus on gentle cleansing + layering hydration + sealing it.

**Morning (2–3 minutes)**
- Cleanse lightly (or just rinse with water if not oily)
- Apply a hydrating layer (look for glycerin, hyaluronic acid, or aloe)
- Use a richer moisturizer (creams > lotions; look for ceramides or shea butter)
- Sunscreen (SPF 30+, non-negotiable even for dry skin)

**Night (3–5 minutes)**
- Gentle cleanser (no harsh foaming or “deep clean” types)
- Hydrating serum (optional but helpful if very dry)
- Thick moisturizer
- If skin is *very* dry: seal with a thin layer of petrolatum (like Vaseline) on top a few nights a week

**Quick upgrades that make a big difference**
- Apply products to slightly damp skin — locks in more hydration
- Avoid hot water on your face (it dries you out fast)
- Use a humidifier if your home is dry
- Keep a small moisturizer where you’ll actually use it (bathroom + bedside)

**What to avoid**
- Scrubs or frequent exfoliation (they worsen dryness)
- Alcohol-heavy toners
- “Foaming” cleansers that leave your skin tight

**If you only have 30 seconds:**
Splash face → moisturizer → sunscreen in the morning  
Cleanse → thick moisturizer at night
