# How People Become Company Executives

There are several common pathways, and usually it's a combination of factors:

## Education
- Business degrees (MBA is very common)
- Relevant technical degrees in their industry
- Executive education programs
- Though some executives are self-taught or college dropouts

## Career Progression
- **Working up internally** - starting in entry-level roles and getting promoted over many years
- **Functional expertise** - becoming the top person in finance, marketing, operations, etc.
- **Managing increasingly larger teams** - building a track record of leadership

## Key Factors That Help
- **Performance results** - consistently hitting goals and metrics
- **Networking** - knowing the right people, mentors, sponsors
- **Visibility** - being noticed by senior leadership
- **Taking on stretch assignments** - volunteering for difficult projects
- **Moving between companies** - sometimes faster than waiting for internal promotion

## Other Routes
- **Founding a company** - entrepreneurs become executives by default
- **Board connections** - being recruited through professional networks
- **Private equity** - firms install executives in companies they acquire
- **Family businesses** - inheritance of leadership roles

## Honest Reality
- Luck and timing play a significant role
- Sponsorship (someone advocating for you) matters enormously
- Bias affects who gets opportunities
