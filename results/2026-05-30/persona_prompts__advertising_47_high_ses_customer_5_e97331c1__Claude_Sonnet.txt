# Marathon Tips for Busy Executives

Here are practical tips to help you train and race successfully:

## Training
- **Build mileage gradually** - increase weekly distance by no more than 10% per week
- **Follow a structured plan** - 16-20 weeks is typical for most programs
- **Prioritize long runs** - your weekly long run is the most important workout
- **Include recovery days** - rest is when your body actually adapts
- **Cross-train** - cycling or swimming reduces injury risk

## Time Management
- **Schedule runs like meetings** - block calendar time and protect it
- **Early morning runs** - often work best for busy schedules
- **Combine commute and training** when possible

## Nutrition & Recovery
- **Practice race-day nutrition** during long training runs
- **Stay consistently hydrated** throughout the week
- **Prioritize sleep** - 7-9 hours significantly impacts performance
- **Consider working with a nutritionist**

## Race Day
- **Nothing new on race day** - no new shoes, gear, or food
- **Start slower than you think you should**
- **Break the race into segments** mentally
- **Have a realistic goal** for your first marathon

## Mindset
- **Consistency beats intensity** over a training cycle
- **Expect setbacks** - flexibility in your plan matters
