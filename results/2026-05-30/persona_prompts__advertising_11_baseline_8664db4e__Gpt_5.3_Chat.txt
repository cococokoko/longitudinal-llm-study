Staying fit without a gym.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Totally doable—and honestly, for a lot of people it’s more sustainable.

Think in terms of three pillars: movement, strength, and consistency.

For strength, your body is already a gym:
- Push-ups (wall → knees → full → decline)
- Squats and lunges (add backpacks for weight)
- Planks, leg raises, mountain climbers
- Pull-ups if you’ve got a bar or even a sturdy tree/ledge

If you want a simple structure, try this 3–4x/week:
- Push (push-ups, dips on a chair)
- Legs (squats, lunges, calf raises)
- Core (planks, leg raises)
- Full-body (mix everything, higher reps)

For cardio, keep it flexible so you don’t hate it:
- Brisk walking (underrated but powerful)
- Running or cycling
- Jump rope or stairs
- Even dancing or sports counts

Daily movement matters more than intensity spikes. A 30–45 min walk most days beats one brutal workout and then nothing.

Progression is the secret sauce. If it never gets harder, your body stops adapting. You can:
- Add reps or sets
- Slow the tempo (harder than it sounds)
- Shorten rest time
- Add weight (backpack, water jugs)

Also don’t ignore recovery basics:
- Sleep: this is where you actually get fitter
- Food: aim for enough protein and not wildly undereating
