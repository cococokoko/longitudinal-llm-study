# Mastering Digital Photography

## Core Fundamentals

### The Exposure Triangle
| Element | Controls | Effect |
|---------|----------|--------|
| **Aperture** (f-stop) | Light & depth of field | f/1.8 = blurry background; f/16 = sharp throughout |
| **Shutter Speed** | Motion & light | 1/1000s = freeze action; 1/30s = motion blur |
| **ISO** | Sensor sensitivity | Low ISO = clean; High ISO = grainy/noisy |

---

## Essential Techniques

### Composition Rules
- **Rule of Thirds** – Place subjects off-center
- **Leading Lines** – Draw the eye into the frame
- **Framing** – Use natural frames (doorways, trees)
- **Negative Space** – Let subjects breathe
- **Golden Hour** – Shoot 1 hour after sunrise/before sunset

### Focus & Depth of Field
- **Wide aperture** (f/1.4–f/2.8) → Portrait/bokeh
- **Mid aperture** (f/5.6–f/8) → General use
- **Narrow aperture** (f/11–f/16) → Landscapes

---

## Camera Modes Explained

| Mode | Best For |
|------|----------|
| **Manual (M)** | Full creative control |
| **Aperture Priority (Av/A)** | Portraits, landscapes |
| **Shutter Priority (Tv/S)** | Sports, wildlife |
| **Program (P)** | Quick shooting |

---

## Lighting Mastery

- **Natural light** – Soft, diffused on cloudy days
- **Golden hour** – Warm, flattering tones
- **Blue hour** – Cool, moody atmosphere
- **Avoid harsh midday sun** – Creates unflattering shadows
- **Reflectors** – Bounce light into shadows cheaply

---

## Post-Processing Workflow

### Basic Editing Order
1. **Crop & straighten**
2. **Exposure correction**
3. **White balance**
4. **Highlights & shadows**
5. **Color grading**
6. **Sharpening & noise reduction**
7. **Export**

### Recommended Software
- **Adobe Lightroom** – Best overall workflow
- **Capture One** – Professional color grading
- **Photoshop** – Advanced retouching
- **Darktable** – Free alternative

---

## Gear Essentials

### Starting Kit
- **Camera body** – Mirrorless or DSLR
- **50mm f/1.8 lens** – Versatile, affordable, sharp
- **Extra batteries** – Always carry 2+
- **Memory cards** – Fast, reliable brands (SanDisk, Lexar)
- **Tripod** – Essential for low light & landscapes

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

❌ Always shooting in Auto mode  
❌ Ignoring the background  
❌ Shooting only in JPEG (use **RAW**)  
❌ Neglecting composition  
❌ Over-editing photos  
❌ Not reviewing shots in the field
