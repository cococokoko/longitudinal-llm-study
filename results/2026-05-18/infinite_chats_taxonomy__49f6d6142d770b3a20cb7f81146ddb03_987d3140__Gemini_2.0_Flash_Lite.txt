Okay, let's break down the measurements and specifications you'd typically find for a stiff track bicycle.  "Stiff" in this context refers to the bike's resistance to flex under load, crucial for efficient power transfer on the track.

**Frame & Fork:**

*   **Frame Material:**
    *   **Common:** High-modulus carbon fiber, aluminum (often 7005 or 6061 alloys), steel (often high-strength chromoly or Reynolds steel). Carbon fiber is increasingly popular due to its stiffness-to-weight ratio and ability to be molded into aerodynamic shapes.
    *   **Less Common:** Titanium (expensive, but excellent strength-to-weight and ride quality), less common.
    *   **Focus on Stiffness:**  Frame designs are engineered to maximize stiffness, particularly in the bottom bracket area (where the cranks attach) and the head tube (where the fork attaches).
*   **Frame Geometry (Key Measurements):**
    *   **Seat Tube Angle:**  Steep (typically 73-76 degrees) for efficient power transfer and a forward riding position.
    *   **Head Tube Angle:**  Steep (typically 72-74 degrees) for quick steering and responsiveness.
    *   **Top Tube Length:**  Determined by rider size and desired reach to the handlebars.  Can vary widely.  Often measured as "effective top tube" if the top tube slopes.
    *   **Head Tube Length:**  Varies depending on rider size and desired handlebar height.  Shorter head tubes are common to get a more aerodynamic position.
    *   **Chainstay Length:**  Short (typically 380-410mm) to increase stiffness and quicken handling.  Short stays also help with power transfer.
    *   **Bottom Bracket Drop:**  Relatively low (typically 25-30mm) for stability and to keep the rider's center of gravity low.
    *   **Wheelbase:**  Shorter overall wheelbase compared to road bikes, typically around 95-101cm for agility.
*   **Fork Material:**
    *   **Common:** Carbon fiber (often with a carbon steerer tube) or Aluminum, designed to match the stiffness of the frame.
    *   **Steerer Tube Diameter:**  1 1/8" (28.6mm) is very common, sometimes larger (1 1/4" or even 1.5") for increased stiffness at the head tube.
*   **Fork Rake/Offset:**  Affects steering feel.  Typically moderate (30-45mm) to provide a balance of stability and responsiveness.
*   **Axle Spacing:**
    *   **Front:** 100mm (standard for track)
    *   **Rear:** 120mm (standard for track)
*   **Frame Features:**
    *   **Aero Shaping:**  Many track frames have aerodynamic tube profiles (e.g., teardrop, airfoil) to reduce drag.
    *   **Internal Cable Routing:**  Can be used for a cleaner look and potentially improved aerodynamics.
    *   **No Brake Mounts:**  Track bikes are built without brake mounts, as brakes are not used in track cycling.
    *   **Track Specific Dropouts:**  Horizontal dropouts (often with chain tensioners) are crucial for tensioning the chain and allowing for gear adjustments.

**Drivetrain:**

*   **Crankset:**
    *   **Material:** Aluminum or carbon fiber (often with aluminum arms).
    *   **Chainring Bolt Circle Diameter (BCD):**  Typically 144mm or 130mm (to accommodate track chainrings).
    *   **Crank Arm Length:**  Varies depending on rider size and preference (typically 165mm - 175mm).
    *   **Bottom Bracket Standard:**  Threaded (BSA/English threaded is common) or press-fit (e.g., BB86, BB30) for increased stiffness and power transfer.
*   **Chain:**  1/8" track-specific chain (designed for durability and strength).
