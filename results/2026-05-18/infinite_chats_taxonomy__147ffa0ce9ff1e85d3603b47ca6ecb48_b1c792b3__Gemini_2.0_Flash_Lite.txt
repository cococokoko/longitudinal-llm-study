── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
what kind of information can be pulled out of tax documents for a tax analysis page

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Tax documents are a treasure trove of financial information that can be used for a comprehensive tax analysis. Here's a breakdown of the types of information you can pull out, categorized for clarity:

**I. Income & Revenue Information:**

*   **Gross Income:**
    *   Wages, salaries, tips (Form W-2)
    *   Interest income (Form 1099-INT)
    *   Dividend income (Form 1099-DIV)
    *   Business income (Schedule C, Form 1065, Form 1120, Form 1120-S)
    *   Capital gains and losses (Schedule D, Form 1099-B)
    *   Rental income (Schedule E)
    *   Social Security benefits (Form SSA-1099)
    *   Unemployment compensation (Form 1099-G)
    *   Alimony received (if applicable)
    *   Other income (Form 1099-MISC/NEC, etc.)
*   **Adjustments to Income (Above-the-Line Deductions):**
    *   Educator expenses
    *   IRA contributions (traditional)
    *   Student loan interest
    *   Health savings account (HSA) deduction
    *   Moving expenses (if applicable)
    *   Self-employment tax
    *   Self-employed health insurance deduction
    *   Alimony paid (if applicable)
    *   Other adjustments (e.g., jury duty pay turned over to employer)

**II. Deductions & Credits:**

*   **Itemized Deductions (Schedule A):**
    *   Medical expenses (subject to AGI limitation)
    *   State and local taxes (SALT) (limited)
    *   Home mortgage interest
    *   Charitable contributions (cash and non-cash)
    *   Casualty and theft losses (subject to limitations)
    *   Other itemized deductions (e.g., gambling losses)
*   **Standard Deduction:** (Based on filing status)
*   **Tax Credits:**
    *   Child tax credit
    *   Child and dependent care credit
    *   Education credits (American Opportunity Tax Credit, Lifetime Learning Credit)
    *   Retirement savings contributions credit (Saver's Credit)
    *   Residential energy credits
    *   Earned Income Tax Credit (EITC)
    *   Premium Tax Credit (for health insurance)
    *   Foreign tax credit
    *   General Business Credits (for businesses)

**III. Business-Specific Information (if applicable):**

*   **Business Expenses:**
    *   Cost of goods sold (COGS)
    *   Advertising
    *   Car and truck expenses
    *   Commissions
    *   Depreciation
    *   Employee benefits
    *   Insurance
    *   Interest on business loans
    *   Legal and professional fees
    *   Office expenses
    *   Rent or lease expenses
    *   Repairs and maintenance
    *   Supplies
    *   Taxes and licenses
    *   Travel expenses
    *   Utilities
    *   Wages
    *   Other business expenses
*   **Business Income/Loss:** (From Schedule C, K-1s, etc.)
*   **Depreciation & Amortization Schedules:**
*   **Qualified Business Income (QBI) Deduction Information (Form 8995 or 8995-A):**
    *   QBI amount
    *   Qualified property
    *   Wages
    *   UBIA (Unadjusted Basis Immediately After Acquisition) of qualified property
*   **Entity Information:** (EIN, entity type, etc.)

**IV. Tax Liability & Payments:**

*   **Taxable Income:** (Calculated after all deductions are applied)
*   **Tax Liability:** (Calculated based on the tax brackets for the filing status)
*   **Tax Payments:**
    *   Federal income tax withheld (Form W-2, 1099-R)
    *   Estimated tax payments (Form 1040-ES)
    *   Prior year overpayment applied to current year
    *   Credits applied
