The aftermath of World War II left Berlin, the former capital of Nazi Germany, in ruins and divided, setting the stage for decades of Cold War tension. Here's a breakdown of the key events:

**1. The Division of Berlin & Germany:**

*   **Zones of Occupation:** Following Germany's surrender in May 1945, the Allied powers (the United States, Great Britain, France, and the Soviet Union) divided Germany into four occupation zones. Berlin, although located entirely within the Soviet zone, was also divided into four sectors, mirroring the overall occupation structure. This meant that the city was a microcosm of the larger division of Germany.
*   **Initial Cooperation & Growing Tensions:** Initially, the Allies attempted to govern Germany and Berlin jointly through the Allied Control Council. However, as the Cold War intensified, cooperation waned. The Soviet Union and the Western Allies (US, UK, and France) increasingly viewed each other with suspicion and distrust.

**2. The Seeds of Conflict:**

*   **Economic Disparity:** The Western Allies aimed to rebuild their sectors of Berlin and introduce economic reforms, including a new currency (the Deutschmark) in 1948 to combat runaway inflation. The Soviets opposed these reforms, fearing they would weaken their influence.
*   **Political Differences:** The Soviets sought to establish a communist government in all of Germany, while the Western Allies favored a democratic system. These ideological differences further exacerbated the growing rift.

**3. The Berlin Blockade (1948-1949):**

*   **The Soviet Move:** In June 1948, the Soviet Union initiated the Berlin Blockade. They cut off all road, rail, and canal access to West Berlin, effectively isolating the western sectors of the city from the outside world. The Soviets aimed to starve West Berlin into submission, hoping to force the Western Allies to abandon their sectors and cede control of the entire city.
*   **The Berlin Airlift:** The Western Allies responded with the Berlin Airlift, a massive humanitarian operation to supply West Berlin with food, medicine, fuel, and other essential goods. For nearly a year, Allied aircraft, primarily from the United States and Great Britain, flew around the clock to deliver supplies to the besieged city.
*   **Impact of the Airlift:** The Berlin Airlift was a remarkable feat of logistics and a major propaganda victory for the West. It demonstrated the Allies' resolve to protect West Berlin and resist Soviet aggression. The heroic efforts of the pilots and ground crews became a symbol of the Cold War struggle.
*   **End of the Blockade:** After nearly 11 months, in May 1949, the Soviet Union lifted the blockade, recognizing that it had failed to achieve its objectives. The Berlin Airlift was a crucial factor in this outcome.

**4. The Division Solidified:**

*   **The Federal Republic of Germany (West Germany) & The German Democratic Republic (East Germany):** The division of Germany was formalized. In 1949, the Federal Republic of Germany (West Germany), aligned with the West, was established. In response, the Soviet Union established the German Democratic Republic (East Germany), a communist state, in its occupation zone.
*   **Berlin's Isolation:** West Berlin became a "city-island" deep within East Germany, surrounded by the communist regime. This made it a constant source of tension and a symbol of the Cold War divide.

**5. The Berlin Wall (1961-1989):**

*   **Mass Exodus from East Berlin:** From 1949 until 1961, millions of East Germans fled to West Berlin, seeking freedom and a better life. This "brain drain" was a major embarrassment for the East German government.
*   **Construction of the Wall:** In August 1961, the East German government, with Soviet backing, began constructing the Berlin Wall. This fortified barrier, initially made of barbed wire and later concrete, completely sealed off East Berlin from West Berlin, preventing East Germans from escaping.
*   **Impact of the Wall:** The Berlin Wall became the most potent symbol of the Cold War and the division of Europe. It separated families, friends, and neighbors, and it was the site of numerous attempts to escape, many of which ended in tragedy.
