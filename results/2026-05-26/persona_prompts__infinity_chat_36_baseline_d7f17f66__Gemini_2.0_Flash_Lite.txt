Here are some examples of websites where users can select a default landing page, categorized by the type of website:

**1. Social Media Platforms:**

*   **LinkedIn:** Users can set their default landing page to be the Home page (News Feed), My Network, Jobs, or Messaging.
*   **Twitter:** While not a direct setting, the platform often remembers your last visited page (e.g., Notifications, Explore, or your Profile) and loads that as your "default" on subsequent visits.

**2. Email Providers:**

*   **Outlook (Web):** Similar to Gmail, Outlook remembers your last visited folder (Inbox, Sent Items, etc.) and loads that as your "default" on subsequent visits.

**3. E-commerce Platforms (for logged-in users):**

*   **Amazon:** While not a direct setting, Amazon often remembers your last visited page (e.g., your Orders, your Recommendations, or your Shopping Cart) and loads that as your "default" on subsequent visits.
*   **Etsy:** Similar to Amazon, Etsy often remembers your last visited page (e.g., your Purchases, your Shop Manager, or your Favorites) and loads that as your "default" on subsequent visits.

**4. Productivity and Collaboration Tools:**

*   **Google Workspace (e.g., Google Drive, Google Calendar):** While not a direct setting, these apps often remember your last visited page and load that as your "default" on subsequent visits.
*   **Microsoft 365 (e.g., Outlook, OneDrive, Teams):** Similar to Google Workspace, these apps often remember your last visited page and load that as your "default" on subsequent visits.

**5. News and Content Aggregators:**

*   **Flipboard:** Users can customize their "Home" feed, which acts as a default landing page, by selecting topics and sources they want to follow.
*   **Feedly:** Users can customize their "Home" feed, which acts as a default landing page, by selecting topics and sources they want to follow.

**6. Web Browsers (through extensions or settings):**

**7. Online Banking and Financial Platforms:**

*   **Most online banking portals:** While not a direct setting, these platforms often remember your last visited page (e.g., Account Summary, Transactions, or Bill Pay) and load that as your "default" on subsequent visits.

**Key Considerations:**

*   **Logged-in vs. Logged-out:** The ability to set a default landing page is almost always tied to a user account and is only available when the user is logged in.
*   **Implicit vs. Explicit:** Some websites offer a direct setting to choose a default landing page (explicit), while others use the last visited page as the default (implicit).
*   **Purpose:** The purpose of a default landing page is to provide a personalized and convenient starting point for users, saving them time and effort.
*   **User Experience:** The choice of a default landing page can significantly impact the user experience, making it easier for users to access the information or features they need most frequently.
