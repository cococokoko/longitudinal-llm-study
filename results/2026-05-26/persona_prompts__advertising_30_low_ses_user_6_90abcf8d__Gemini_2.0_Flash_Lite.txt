That's a great goal! Learning to code can be a fantastic way to improve your skills, potentially open up new career paths, and even automate some tasks in your warehouse job. Here's a breakdown of the best ways to start learning coding from scratch, tailored to your situation with long hours:

**1. Choose Your First Language (and Why):**

*   **Python:** Highly recommended for beginners.
    *   **Pros:** Easy to read and understand syntax (like plain English), versatile (web development, data science, automation, scripting), large and supportive community, lots of free resources.
    *   **Cons:** Can be slower than some other languages for certain tasks.
*   **JavaScript:** Essential for web development (front-end and back-end).
    *   **Pros:** Runs in web browsers (instant feedback), huge job market, can create interactive websites and applications.
    *   **Cons:** Can be a bit trickier to grasp initially due to its quirks.
*   **HTML/CSS:** Not technically programming languages, but *essential* for web development.
    *   **Pros:** Easy to learn the basics, provides immediate visual results.
    *   **Cons:** Limited in functionality on their own.

**For your situation, I recommend starting with Python.** It's beginner-friendly, and you can use it for a wide range of tasks, including automating warehouse-related tasks later on.
