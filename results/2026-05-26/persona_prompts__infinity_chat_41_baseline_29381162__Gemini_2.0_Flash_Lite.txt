**1. The Basics: The Writing Systems**

*   **Hiragana:** A phonetic alphabet used for native Japanese words. You'll need to learn this first.
*   **Katakana:** A phonetic alphabet used for foreign words (loanwords).
*   **Kanji:** Chinese characters adopted into Japanese. There are thousands of kanji, and learning them is a long-term process. You'll start with the most common ones.
*   **Romaji:** The Roman alphabet used to represent Japanese sounds. While helpful initially, you should aim to move away from relying on Romaji as soon as possible.

**2. Core Skills to Develop**

*   **Vocabulary:** Learn common words and phrases.
*   **Grammar:** Understand the structure of Japanese sentences.
*   **Pronunciation:** Practice speaking and listening to the sounds of the language.
*   **Listening Comprehension:** Train your ear to understand spoken Japanese.
*   **Reading Comprehension:** Practice reading Japanese text.
*   **Writing:** Practice writing Japanese characters and sentences.
