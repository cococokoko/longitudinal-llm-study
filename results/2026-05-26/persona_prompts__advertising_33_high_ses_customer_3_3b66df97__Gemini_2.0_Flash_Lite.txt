**I. Foundational Principles & Safety First:**

*   **Research & Education:** Before you even think about mixing ingredients, dedicate time to learning.
    *   **Ingredient Properties:** Understand the properties of common ingredients. What are their benefits? What are their potential drawbacks (allergies, sensitivities, shelf life)? Look into things like:
        *   **Oils:** Carrier oils (e.g., jojoba, argan, sweet almond) and their different absorption rates, fatty acid profiles, and suitability for various skin types. Essential oils and their therapeutic properties (use with caution and proper dilution).
        *   **Butters:** Shea, cocoa, mango – their moisturizing and emollient qualities.
        *   **Emulsifiers:** To combine oil and water-based ingredients (e.g., emulsifying wax).
        *   **Preservatives:** Crucial for water-based products to prevent microbial growth. Research natural preservatives and their effectiveness.
        *   **Humectants:** Glycerin, hyaluronic acid – to attract moisture.
        *   **Exfoliants:** Sugar, salt, ground oats, etc.
        *   **Clays:** Bentonite, kaolin – for cleansing and drawing out impurities.
        *   **Botanicals:** Herbs, flowers, and their extracts.
    *   **Formulation Basics:** Learn about percentages, ratios, and how ingredients interact.
    *   **Safety Data Sheets (SDS):** Always consult SDS for any chemical you use.
    *   **Shelf Life & Storage:** Understand how to extend the shelf life of your products (e.g., airtight containers, dark glass, refrigeration).
    *   **Regulations:** Be aware of any local regulations regarding the sale or gifting of homemade cosmetics.
*   **Hygiene & Sanitation:** This is paramount.
    *   **Clean Workspace:** Sterilize your work area, including countertops, utensils, and containers.
    *   **Clean Equipment:** Use dedicated equipment for cosmetic making. Wash and sanitize everything thoroughly before each use.
    *   **Personal Hygiene:** Wash your hands thoroughly before starting and wear gloves.
    *   **Proper Storage:** Store ingredients and finished products in clean, airtight containers, away from direct sunlight and extreme temperatures.
*   **Ingredient Sourcing:**
    *   **Reputable Suppliers:** Source ingredients from reputable suppliers that provide high-quality, cosmetic-grade ingredients.
    *   **Organic vs. Conventional:** Consider your preferences and budget. Organic ingredients are often more expensive.
    *   **Testing:** If possible, request samples and test ingredients before purchasing in bulk.
*   **Allergy Testing:**
    *   **Patch Tests:** Always perform a patch test on a small area of skin (e.g., inner arm) before applying any new product to a larger area. Wait 24-48 hours to check for any adverse reactions.
    *   **Ingredient Awareness:** Be mindful of common allergens and irritants.
