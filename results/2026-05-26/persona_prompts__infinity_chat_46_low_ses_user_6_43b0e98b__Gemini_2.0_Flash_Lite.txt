**1. The Source of Morality:**

*   **The Core of the Controversy:** Where does morality come from? Is it:
    *   **Objective and Universal:** Does a set of moral truths exist independently of human opinion, perhaps rooted in a divine being, natural law, or inherent human reason?

*   **Why it's Controversial:** This question touches on fundamental beliefs about the nature of reality, the existence of God, the role of reason, and the possibility of shared values. It fuels debates about:
    *   **Religious vs. Secular Ethics:** Can morality exist without religion?
    *   **Cultural Relativism:** Are all moral systems equally valid?
    *   **Moral Progress:** Can we improve our moral understanding over time?

**2. The Application of Moral Principles:**

*   **The Core of the Controversy:** Even if we agree on some basic moral principles (e.g., don't kill, don't steal), how do we apply them to complex situations? This leads to debates about:
    *   **Utilitarianism vs. Deontology:** Should we focus on maximizing overall happiness (utilitarianism) or follow moral rules regardless of consequences (deontology)?
    *   **Justice and Fairness:** How do we distribute resources and opportunities fairly?
    *   **Rights vs. Responsibilities:** What rights do individuals have, and what responsibilities do they owe to others and society?
*   **Why it's Controversial:** This is where abstract principles meet real-world dilemmas. It fuels debates about:
    *   **Healthcare:** Access to care, rationing, end-of-life decisions.
    *   **Economic Inequality:** Fair wages, wealth distribution, social safety nets.
    *   **Environmental Ethics:** Climate change, resource depletion, animal rights.
    *   **War and Peace:** Just war theory, pacifism, the use of force.

**3. The Nature of Good and Evil:**

*   **The Core of the Controversy:** What motivates human behavior? Are people inherently good, inherently evil, or a mix of both?
*   **Why it's Controversial:** This question influences our understanding of:
    *   **Punishment and Rehabilitation:** How should we treat criminals?
    *   **Human Nature:** Can we trust others?
    *   **Forgiveness and Redemption:** Can people change?

The "biggest" controversy is hard to definitively name, as it depends on your perspective.
