Okay, that's a great goal! Cooking a perfect steak is definitely something to be proud of. Given your fast-food job with an unpredictable schedule, we need to think about a few things:

*   **Time:** You might have limited time to cook, so we'll focus on techniques that are relatively quick.

Let's break down the process of mastering steak:

**1. Understanding Steak Cuts:**

*   **Budget-Friendly Options:**
    *   **Sirloin:** A lean and flavorful cut, good for quick cooking.
    *   **Flank Steak/Skirt Steak:** Great for marinating and grilling, often used for fajitas.
    *   **Chuck Eye Steak:** Sometimes called the "poor man's ribeye," it can be a good value.
*   **Mid-Range Options:**
    *   **Ribeye:** Known for its marbling and flavor.
    *   **New York Strip:** A classic, with good flavor and texture.
*   **Higher-End Options (for special occasions):**
    *   **Filet Mignon:** Very tender, but can lack flavor compared to other cuts.
    *   **Porterhouse/T-Bone:** Large cuts with both filet and strip.

**2. Key Cooking Methods:**

*   **Pan-Searing:** This is a great option for quick weeknight meals.
    *   **Equipment:** Heavy-bottomed skillet (cast iron is ideal, but stainless steel works too).
    *   **Process:**
        1.  **Pat the steak dry:** This is crucial for a good sear. Use paper towels.
        2.  **Season generously:** Salt and pepper are your best friends. Consider other seasonings like garlic powder, onion powder, or a steak seasoning blend.
        3.  **Heat the pan:** Get the pan *very* hot. You want it smoking slightly. Use a high-smoke-point oil like avocado oil, canola oil, or grapeseed oil.
        4.  **Sear the steak:** Place the steak in the hot pan and *don't move it* for 2-4 minutes per side (depending on thickness and desired doneness). This is where the crust develops.
        5.  **Add aromatics (optional):** During the last minute of cooking, you can add butter, garlic cloves, and herbs (like rosemary or thyme) to the pan and baste the steak with the melted butter.
        6.  **Rest the steak:** This is *essential*. Remove the steak from the pan and let it rest on a cutting board for 5-10 minutes before slicing. This allows the juices to redistribute, resulting in a more tender and flavorful steak.
*   **Grilling:** Great for flavor, especially in warmer weather.
    *   **Equipment:** Grill (charcoal or gas).
    *   **Process:**
        1.  **Prepare the grill:** Clean the grates and preheat the grill to medium-high heat.
        2.  **Season the steak:** Same as pan-searing.
        3.  **Grill the steak:** Cook for 3-5 minutes per side, depending on thickness and desired doneness.
        4.  **Rest the steak:** Same as pan-searing.
*   **Reverse Sear:** This method is great for thicker cuts and ensures even cooking.
    *   **Equipment:** Oven and skillet.
    *   **Process:**
        1.  **Season the steak:** Same as above.
        2.  **Low and slow in the oven:** Place the steak on a wire rack in a preheated oven at a low temperature (225-275°F / 107-135°C) until it reaches an internal temperature about 20°F (11°C) below your desired doneness.
        3.  **Sear in a hot skillet:** Heat a skillet with oil over high heat. Sear the steak for 1-2 minutes per side to develop a crust.
        4.  **Rest the steak:** Same as above.
