Okay, as a partner at a major consulting firm, you're likely time-constrained and need a learning path that's efficient, relevant, and allows you to quickly grasp the fundamentals while understanding the strategic implications. **1. Define Your "Why" and Set Realistic Goals:**

*   **Why are you learning to code?**  This is the most crucial question. Are you:
    *   **Understanding the technology landscape better?** (e.g., for client conversations, strategy development)
    *   **Being able to speak the language of your technical teams?** (e.g., improve communication, project oversight)

    *   "I want to understand the basics of Python and be able to read and interpret simple code within X months."
    *   "I want to build a simple script to automate Y task within Z weeks."
    *   "I want to be able to discuss the pros and cons of different coding languages with my team."

**2. Choose the Right Language(s) and Tools:**

*   **Python is generally the best starting point for your needs.**
    *   **Why Python?**
        *   **Readability:** Python's syntax is designed to be clear and easy to understand, making it ideal for beginners.
        *   **Versatility:** Python is used in a wide range of applications, including data science, machine learning, web development, and automation.
        *   **Large Community and Resources:**  There's a vast amount of online documentation, tutorials, and support available.
        *   **Relevance to Consulting:** Python is heavily used in data analysis, automation, and building prototypes, all of which are relevant to your work.

        *   **Codecademy:** Interactive, beginner-friendly courses.
        *   **DataCamp:** Focuses on data science and Python.
