That's fantastic! Learning guitar is a rewarding journey. To help you get started, let's break down the process and cover some key areas.  Here's a comprehensive guide:

**1. Getting Started: The Essentials**

*   **Choosing a Guitar:**
    *   **Acoustic vs. Electric:**
        *   **Acoustic:**  No need for an amp, good for beginners, portable.  Consider a steel-string acoustic (most common) or a nylon-string (classical) guitar. Nylon strings are easier on the fingers initially.
        *   **Electric:** Requires an amplifier, offers a wider range of sounds, can be more challenging initially due to the need for additional equipment.
    *   **Size:**
        *   **Full-size (Dreadnought):** Standard size, good for adults and older teens.
        *   **3/4 size (Parlor):** Smaller, good for younger players or those with smaller hands.
        *   **1/2 size:** Even smaller, for very young children.
    *   **Budget:**  Start with a beginner-friendly guitar.  You don't need to spend a fortune.  Look for reputable brands like Yamaha, Fender (Squier), Epiphone, Ibanez, or Seagull.  A good acoustic guitar for beginners can be found for $150-$300.
*   **Accessories:**
    *   **Picks (plectrums):**  Experiment with different thicknesses (thin, medium, heavy) to find what feels comfortable.
    *   **Tuner:** Essential for keeping your guitar in tune.  You can use a clip-on tuner, a phone app (like GuitarTuna or Fender Tune), or a digital tuner.
    *   **Guitar Strap (for electric or acoustic if you want to stand):**  Make sure it's adjustable and comfortable.
    *   **Guitar Case or Gig Bag:**  Protects your guitar.
    *   **Capo (optional, but useful):**  Clamps onto the fretboard to raise the pitch of all strings, allowing you to play in different keys.
    *   **Guitar Stand (optional):**  For storing your guitar safely.
    *   **String Winder (optional):** Makes changing strings much faster.
    *   **Extra Strings:**  You'll break strings eventually!
*   **Finding a Comfortable Playing Position:**
    *   **Sitting:** Sit upright with good posture.  Rest the guitar on your leg (right leg for right-handed players, left leg for left-handed players).  The guitar should be angled slightly towards you.
    *   **Standing:** Use a guitar strap adjusted to a comfortable height.  Keep your back straight.
*   **Holding the Guitar:**
    *   **Left Hand (for right-handed players):**  Fingers press down on the strings behind the frets (the metal bars).  Use the tips of your fingers, as close to the fret as possible without touching it.  Keep your thumb on the back of the neck, providing support.
    *   **Right Hand (for right-handed players):**  Use a pick to strum the strings.  Hold the pick between your thumb and index finger.  Alternatively, you can use your fingers to pluck the strings (fingerpicking).

**2. Basic Techniques**

*   **Tuning:**
    *   **Standard Tuning (E A D G B e):**  The most common tuning.  Learn the notes of each string.
    *   **Using a Tuner:**  Clip the tuner onto the headstock or use a phone app.  Pluck each string and adjust the tuning pegs until the tuner indicates the correct note.
*   **Strumming:**
    *   **Downstrokes:** Strum from the top (thickest string) to the bottom (thinnest string).
    *   **Upstrokes:** Strum from the bottom to the top.
    *   **Rhythm:**  Practice strumming in time with a metronome or a drum beat.  Start slowly and gradually increase the tempo.
*   **Basic Chords:**
    *   **Learn the essential chords:**
        *   **Major Chords:**  G, C, D, E, A
        *   **Minor Chords:**  Em, Am, Dm
    *   **Chord Diagrams:**  Use chord diagrams (charts that show where to place your fingers on the fretboard).  There are many free resources online (Ultimate-Guitar.com, Chordify.net, etc.).
    *   **Practice Chord Changes:**  Switch between chords smoothly and quickly.  Start slowly and gradually increase the speed.
