**Question:** 
True or False: Blue birds, such as Blue Jays and Eastern Bluebirds, get their bright color from blue pigments in their diet.

**Answer:** 
**False!** There is actually no such thing as blue pigment in the feathers of these birds. 

Instead, their color is an optical illusion caused by something called "structural coloration." The microscopic structure of their feathers acts like a tiny prism, absorbing all other colors of light and reflecting only the blue light back to our eyes. If you were to take a blue feather and crush it up, or look at it from underneath without light shining directly on it, it would actually look brown or gray!
