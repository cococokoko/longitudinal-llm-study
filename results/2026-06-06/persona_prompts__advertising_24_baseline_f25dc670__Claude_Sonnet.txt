# Best Ways to Save for Retirement

## Tax-Advantaged Accounts (Start Here)

**Employer-Sponsored Plans**
- **401(k) or 403(b)** - contribute enough to get any employer match (that's free money)
- 2024 contribution limit: $23,000 ($30,500 if 50+)

**Individual Retirement Accounts**
- **Traditional IRA** - tax deduction now, pay taxes in retirement
- **Roth IRA** - pay taxes now, tax-free withdrawals later
- 2024 limit: $7,000 ($8,000 if 50+)

## Key Strategies

- **Start early** - compound interest rewards time significantly
- **Automate contributions** so you don't skip them
- **Increase contributions** when you get raises
- **Diversify investments** across stocks, bonds, and other assets
- **Reduce fees** by choosing low-cost index funds

## General Guidelines

- Many advisors suggest saving **10-15% of income** for retirement
- Prioritize: employer match → high-interest debt → IRA → max 401(k)
- Adjust risk based on your timeline (more stocks when young, more bonds near retirement)

## Other Options
- HSA accounts (triple tax advantage)
- Taxable brokerage accounts
- Real estate

## Worth Considering
- Your specific situation matters - a **financial advisor** can give personalized guidance
