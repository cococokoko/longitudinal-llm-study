Keeping pets healthy and happy generally comes down to consistent care, appropriate nutrition, physical and mental stimulation, and preventive healthcare.

- Schedule regular veterinary checkups

- Provide a balanced diet
  - Feed a nutritionally complete diet appropriate for your pet's species, age, size, and health status.
  - Monitor weight and body condition to avoid obesity.
  - Limit treats and avoid foods that are toxic to pets.

- Ensure access to fresh water
  - Clean, fresh water should be available at all times unless your veterinarian advises otherwise.

- Support physical activity
  - Dogs typically benefit from regular walks, play, and exercise tailored to their age and health.
  - Cats often enjoy interactive toys, climbing structures, and opportunities to explore their environment.
  - Small mammals, birds, and reptiles have their own activity and habitat needs.

- Provide mental enrichment
  - Use puzzle feeders, training sessions, toys, scent games, and social interaction.
  - Rotate toys periodically to maintain interest.

- Maintain a safe environment
  - Keep medications, household chemicals, toxic plants, and dangerous foods out of reach.
  - Ensure fencing, leashes, carriers, or enclosures are secure.

- Focus on dental health
  - Regular tooth brushing, dental diets, chews, or professional cleanings can help prevent dental disease.

- Groom appropriately
  - Brush coats as needed.
  - Trim nails when necessary.
  - Monitor skin, ears, eyes, and coat for signs of problems.

- Provide social interaction
  - Most pets benefit from positive attention and predictable routines.
  - Use reward-based training methods rather than punishment.

- Consider age-related needs
  - Puppies, kittens, and young animals need training and socialization.
