Can you give me a powerful rhetorical question for an essay about the harms of social media on teens?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here are a few powerful rhetorical questions you could use:

**Provocative/Emotional:**
- "If we wouldn't hand a teenager a product known to cause anxiety and depression, why do we hand them a smartphone without a second thought?"

**Challenging the Reader:**
- "At what point do we stop calling it connection when it leaves so many young people feeling more alone than ever?"

**Sharp and Direct:**
- "How many more studies, how many more struggling teenagers, will it take before we treat social media addiction with the same urgency as any other public health crisis?"

**Thought-provoking:**
- "If the engineers who built these platforms restrict their own children's screen time, what does that tell us about what they actually created?"

---

**Tips for using it effectively:**
- Place it at the **opening** to hook readers or at the **end** to leave an impression
- Make sure it connects directly to your **specific argument**
- Avoid overusing them - **one strong question** is more powerful than several
