# Learning Guitar as a Busy Professional

Given your background, you likely appreciate **structured, efficient approaches** to skill acquisition. Here's a practical framework:

## Getting Started

**Choose your instrument**
- Acoustic - more versatile for beginners, no equipment needed
- Electric - easier on fingers, better for certain genres
- Classical - nylon strings, gentler on fingers

**Essential first investments**
- Decent beginner guitar ($150-400 range is the sweet spot)
- Tuner app (GuitarTuna is free and reliable)
- A few picks in different thicknesses

---

## Efficient Learning Path

1. **Master basic chord shapes first** (G, C, D, Em, Am)
2. **Learn chord transitions** before worrying about speed
3. **Practice 15-20 minutes daily** - consistency beats long sessions
4. **Add strumming patterns** once transitions feel natural

## Learning Resources
- **Structured:** JustinGuitar.com (free, highly regarded)
- **On-demand:** Fender Play or TakeLessons for flexibility
- **High-accountability:** Weekly lessons with a local instructor
