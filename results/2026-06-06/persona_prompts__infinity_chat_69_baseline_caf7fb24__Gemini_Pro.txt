── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
What is the difference between analysis and design? Can one begin to design without analysis? Why? Be concise.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
**The Difference:**
*   **Analysis** defines the problem. It focuses on *what* needs to be done and *why* by breaking down requirements, user needs, and constraints. 
*   **Design** defines the solution. It focuses on *how* to solve the problem by synthesizing those requirements into a blueprint or plan.

**Can you design without analysis?**
Technically yes, but it is highly discouraged and usually leads to failure. 

**Why?**
Designing without analysis is essentially guessing. If you do not fully understand the problem, you risk solving the *wrong* problem, ignoring critical constraints, and wasting time and money building a solution that fails to meet actual needs. It is like building a house without surveying the land or asking the owner how many rooms they need.
