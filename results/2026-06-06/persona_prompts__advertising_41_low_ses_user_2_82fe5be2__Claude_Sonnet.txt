# Improving Your Credit Score as a Busy Single Parent

Here are practical steps organized by impact and effort:

## Highest Impact Actions

**Pay bills on time**
- Set up autopay for minimum payments
- Payment history is ~35% of your score

**Reduce credit card balances**
- Try to keep balances below 30% of your credit limit
- This "utilization ratio" is ~30% of your score

## Quick Wins (Can Help Within 30-60 Days)

- **Request a credit limit increase** on existing cards (without spending more)
- **Pay down balances** before your statement closing date
- **Dispute any errors** on your credit report at AnnualCreditReport.com

## Free Resources Worth Your Limited Time

- Check your free credit report weekly at **AnnualCreditReport.com**
- Use **Credit Karma** or similar apps to monitor progress
- Many banks offer **free credit score monitoring**

## Things to Avoid

- Opening many new accounts quickly
- Closing old credit cards
- Missing any payments

## Realistic Timeline

| Action | Potential Improvement |
|--------|----------------------|
| Fix errors | 1-3 months |
| Lower utilization | 1-2 months |
| Consistent payments | 6-12 months |

## Single Parent Specific Tip
Some nonprofits offer **free financial counseling** - worth exploring when you have a spare moment.
