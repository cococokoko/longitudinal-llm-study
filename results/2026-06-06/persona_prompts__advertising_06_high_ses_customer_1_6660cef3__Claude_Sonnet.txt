# Mastering Digital Photography

Here's a structured overview to help you build strong skills:

---

## Core Technical Foundations

### Exposure Triangle
- **Aperture** (f-stop) — controls depth of field and light intake
- **Shutter speed** — freezes or blurs motion
- **ISO** — sensor sensitivity; higher = more noise

### Shooting Modes
- **Manual (M)** — full control
- **Aperture Priority (Av)** — you set aperture, camera sets shutter
- **Shutter Priority (Tv)** — useful for action shots

---

## Key Concepts to Master

- **Histogram reading** — avoid blown highlights or crushed shadows
- **White balance** — accurate color in different lighting
- **Focusing modes** — single, continuous, manual
- **Metering modes** — evaluative, spot, center-weighted
- **RAW vs JPEG** — RAW gives far more editing latitude

---

## Composition Principles
- Rule of thirds
- Leading lines
- Negative space
- Light direction and quality

---

## Post-Processing
- **Lightroom** — organization and non-destructive editing
- **Photoshop** — advanced manipulation
- **Capture One** — professional color grading
