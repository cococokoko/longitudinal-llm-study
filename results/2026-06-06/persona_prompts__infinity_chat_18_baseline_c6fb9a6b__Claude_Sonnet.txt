# Review: *The Last Frequency* (2024)

★★★★½

Director Mara Okonkwo's debut feature is a quiet devastation. Set in a near-future where human memory can be broadcast like radio signals, *The Last Frequency* follows a grieving sound engineer who discovers his late wife left him a transmission he was never meant to receive.

What could have been cold science fiction becomes something achingly intimate. The film resists spectacle at every turn, preferring long silences and the texture of ordinary rooms to flashy technology. The central performance from Idris Elba is career-best work — restrained in a way that makes every small crack in his composure feel enormous.

The third act stumbles slightly, introducing a corporate conspiracy subplot that the film doesn't quite have the appetite to follow through on. It feels borrowed from a different, lesser movie.

But the final ten minutes recover everything. Without spoiling anything, Okonkwo finds an ending that is simultaneously logical and completely unexpected — the kind that sits with you on the drive home and then ambushes you three days later while you're making coffee.

*The Last Frequency* is a film about whether love is something we carry or something we transmit. It doesn't fully answer the question. That's exactly right.
