── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Yes. If by “everything” you mean all facts, all knowledge, and all skills, it is effectively impossible for any human being to know everything.

There are a few reasons:

- Human time is limited. Even if you spent every waking hour learning, there is far more information than any one person could absorb.
- Knowledge keeps growing. New discoveries, inventions, events, books, and research are constantly being produced.
- Different fields are incredibly deep. People often spend decades becoming experts in just one area.
- Memory and attention are limited. Learning something is different from retaining and being able to use it effectively.

That doesn't mean learning broadly is pointless. In practice, people build:
- A foundation of general knowledge.
- Deeper expertise in a few areas that matter to them.
- The ability to find and evaluate information when they need it.
