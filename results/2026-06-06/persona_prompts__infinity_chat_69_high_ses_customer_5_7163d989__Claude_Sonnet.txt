── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
## Analysis vs. Design

**Analysis** answers *"What does the system need to do?"* — understanding requirements, problems, and constraints.

**Design** answers *"How will the system do it?"* — defining architecture, components, and implementation approach.

---

## Can You Design Without Analysis?

**Technically yes, practically no.**

Skipping analysis means you risk:
- **Building the wrong thing** — solving a misunderstood problem
- **Costly rework** — defects found late are exponentially more expensive to fix
- **Scope creep** — no baseline to evaluate change against

---

## The Executive Reality

In fast-moving tech environments, teams often compress or parallelize analysis and design — this is acceptable **if you acknowledge the risk**. What's *not* acceptable is confusing speed with skipping rigor entirely.

> **Design without analysis is just expensive guessing.**

The most successful technology leaders treat analysis as risk reduction, not bureaucracy — the investment in understanding the problem upfront consistently outperforms the cost of redesigning a well-built solution to the wrong problem.
