── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Name an economic value of an additional year of schooling.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The most direct and widely recognized economic value of an additional year of schooling is **increased individual earnings** (often referred to as the "wage premium"). 

On average, global economic studies estimate that each additional year of education increases a person's future earnings by approximately **9% to 10%**. 

This increase in economic value happens for a few reasons:
* **Human Capital:** Additional schooling equips individuals with advanced skills, knowledge, and problem-solving abilities, making them more productive and valuable to employers.
* **Signaling:** An extra year of schooling (especially if it results in a degree) signals to employers that a candidate possesses discipline, intelligence, and a strong work ethic.
* **Lower Unemployment Risk:** Statistically, individuals with more years of schooling face significantly lower rates of unemployment and experience shorter periods of joblessness during economic downturns. 

*(Note: On a macroeconomic level, an additional year of schooling across a population also leads to **higher GDP growth** and **increased tax revenues** for the government).*
