# A World With Double the Oxygen

Doubling atmospheric oxygen from 21% to roughly 42% would have dramatic consequences. Fires would ignite far more easily and burn with extraordinary intensity — even damp vegetation could combust spontaneously. Wildfires would become catastrophic and nearly uncontrollable.

Physiologically, humans and animals would initially experience hyperoxia, potentially causing oxygen toxicity, lung damage, and seizures. Insects, which breathe through passive diffusion, would grow significantly larger, as occurred during the Carboniferous period when oxygen levels were elevated.

Aircraft and combustion engines would require complete redesign. Ultimately, a single lightning strike could trigger continent-spanning infernos, making Earth largely uninhabitable.
