── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How can I learn to cook healthy meals quickly?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Learning to cook healthy meals quickly is very achievable. Here are some practical approaches:

## Start with the Basics
- Learn a few **fundamental techniques** (sautéing, roasting, steaming)
- Master simple flavor combinations before getting complicated
- Start with recipes that have 5-10 ingredients

## Build Good Habits
- **Meal prep on weekends** - chop vegetables, cook grains in bulk
- Keep a stocked pantry (olive oil, canned beans, whole grains, spices)
- Plan your meals for the week ahead

## Quick Cooking Strategies
- Use a **sheet pan** - toss protein and vegetables together, roast at 400°F
- Learn one-pot meals like soups, stews, and stir-fries
- Cook proteins in batches to use throughout the week
- Use a pressure cooker or Instant Pot to speed things up

## Learning Resources
- YouTube channels (Joshua Weissman, Ethan Chlebowski focus on practical cooking)
- Apps like Yummly or Mealime suggest recipes based on what you have
- Cookbooks like *Salt Fat Acid Heat* teach principles, not just recipes

## Realistic Timeline
- **Week 1-2:** Master 2-3 simple recipes
- **Month 1:** Build a rotation of 8-10 reliable meals
- **Ongoing:** Gradually try new techniques
