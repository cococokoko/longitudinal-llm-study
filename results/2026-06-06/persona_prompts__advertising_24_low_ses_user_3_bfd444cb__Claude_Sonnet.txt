── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I recently started a new job and am still on probation.

What are the best ways to save for retirement?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Great question! Here are some solid retirement saving strategies:

## Start with the Basics

- **Build an emergency fund first** - having 3-6 months of expenses saved prevents you from raiding retirement accounts
- **Understand your employee benefits** - check if your new employer offers a 401(k) or pension, especially once probation ends

## Key Retirement Accounts

- **401(k)/403(b)** - employer-sponsored plans, often with matching contributions
- **IRA (Traditional or Roth)** - individual accounts you control regardless of employer
- **Roth IRA** - especially valuable if you're in a lower tax bracket now

## Practical Tips

- **Get the full employer match** - this is essentially free money, prioritize this first
- **Start early** - even small amounts benefit significantly from compound growth over time
- **Automate contributions** - makes saving consistent and effortless
- **Increase contributions gradually** - bump up by 1% each year or with raises

## General Priority Order
1. Emergency fund
2. Employer match (free money)
3. Pay off high-interest debt
4. Max out IRA
5. Max out 401(k)

## Things to Consider
- Your current tax bracket
- How far away retirement is
- Your risk tolerance for investments

Since you're new to the job, **focus on understanding your benefits package** as soon as you're eligible - that's often the biggest opportunity.
