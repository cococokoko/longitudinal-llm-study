# Healthy Meal Planning on a Budget

## Core Principles

- **Plan before you shop** — reduces impulse buying and waste
- **Cook at home** — restaurant meals cost 3-5x more
- **Embrace whole foods** — less processed = cheaper + healthier
- **Use everything** — minimize food waste

---

## Budget-Friendly Healthy Foods

### Proteins
- Eggs
- Canned tuna/salmon
- Dried or canned beans/lentils
- Chicken thighs (cheaper than breasts)
- Tofu

### Grains & Starches
- Oats
- Brown rice
- Whole wheat pasta
- Sweet potatoes

### Vegetables & Fruits
- Frozen vegetables (just as nutritious)
- Cabbage, carrots, onions
- Bananas, apples, seasonal produce

---

## Practical Strategies

**Batch cooking** — cook once, eat multiple times
**Meal prep Sunday** — prep ingredients for the week
**Shop sales & use store brands**
**Buy in bulk** for staples
**Repurpose leftovers** creatively

---

| Day | Meal Idea |
|-----|-----------|
| Monday | Lentil soup + bread |
| Tuesday | Rice & bean bowls |
| Wednesday | Egg stir-fry with veggies |
| Thursday | Pasta with vegetables |
| Friday | Leftovers remix |

## Realistic Budget Targets
- **$50-75/week** for one person is very achievable
- **$150-200/week** for a family of four
