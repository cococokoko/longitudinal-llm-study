# Mastering Digital Photography

Here's a practical guide tailored to someone with a busy, unpredictable schedule:

---

## Starting Simple
- **Learn your smartphone camera first** - it's always with you
- Understand the **exposure triangle**: aperture, shutter speed, ISO
- Shoot in **RAW format** when possible for more editing flexibility

---

## Learning in Short Bursts
Since your schedule is unpredictable, try:
- **5-minute practice sessions** during breaks
- Photograph your **workplace environment** - lighting, textures, candid moments
- One concept per week rather than overwhelming yourself

---

## Key Skills to Build
1. **Composition rules** - rule of thirds, leading lines
2. **Lighting awareness** - natural vs artificial
3. **Focus control** - knowing what to make sharp
4. **Editing basics** - brightness, contrast, color

---

## Free Resources
- YouTube channels (Peter McKinnon, Tony Northrup)
- **Google Snapseed** - free mobile editing
- Reddit communities like r/photography

---

## Realistic Practice Ideas
- Document your commute
- Practice during slow moments at work
- Weekend dedicated shooting sessions when schedule allows
