Here are some effective ways to improve your home WiFi:

## Router Placement
- Position it **centrally** in your home
- Keep it **elevated** and in the open
- Avoid walls, appliances, and metal objects nearby

## Hardware Upgrades
- **Upgrade your router** if it's more than 3-4 years old
- Consider a **mesh network system** (Eero, Google Nest, Orbi) for larger homes
- Add a **WiFi extender/repeater** for dead zones

## Settings Optimization
- **Change the WiFi channel** to reduce interference from neighbors
- Use **5GHz band** for faster speeds nearby, **2.4GHz** for longer range
- Keep **firmware updated**
- Use a strong, unique password (security affects performance)

## Connection Tips
- Use **ethernet cables** for stationary devices (TVs, gaming consoles, computers)
- **Restart your router** periodically
- Limit bandwidth-heavy devices during peak use

## Service Considerations
- Check if your **internet plan** matches your actual needs
- Contact your **ISP** if speeds are consistently below what you're paying for
- Consider upgrading to a **fiber connection** if available

## Quick Wins
- Restart your router
- Move closer to the router
- Disconnect unused devices
