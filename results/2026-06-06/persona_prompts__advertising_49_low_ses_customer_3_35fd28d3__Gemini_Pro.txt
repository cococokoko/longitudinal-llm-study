Since you might also want to be mindful of your budget until you're officially past probation, here is a step-by-step guide to improving your WiFi, starting with completely free fixes and moving up to paid upgrades.

### 1. The "Free and Fast" Fixes
*   **Move your router:** Your router should be in a central location in your home, ideally elevated (like on a bookshelf) and out in the open. WiFi signals struggle to pass through thick walls, metal, and water. Keep it away from microwaves, cordless phones, and large fish tanks.
*   **Switch to the 5GHz band:** Most modern routers are dual-band (2.4GHz and 5GHz). The 2.4GHz band reaches further but is slower and easily congested by smart home devices. Connect your work computer to the 5GHz band for faster, more reliable speeds.
*   **Update and reboot:** It sounds cliché, but unplugging your router for 30 seconds and plugging it back in clears the cache and can fix underlying bugs. Also, log into your router's app or web interface to ensure the firmware is up to date.
*   **Kick off bandwidth hogs:** If you have roommates or family members streaming 4K video or downloading huge files while you are working, it will throttle your connection. See if your router has a "Quality of Service" (QoS) setting, which allows you to prioritize your work computer's traffic over everything else.

### 2. The "Cheap and Reliable" Fix (Highly Recommended for Work)
*   **Use an Ethernet cable:** If your workspace is anywhere near your router, bypass WiFi entirely for your work computer. A $10 Ethernet cable will give you the most stable, fastest connection possible. If you want to guarantee you won't drop out of an important meeting with your new boss, this is the way to do it.
