Managing stress and anxiety usually works best when you combine short-term techniques for immediate relief with longer-term habits that reduce your overall stress level.

For immediate stress or anxiety:
- Slow your breathing. For example, inhale for 4 seconds, exhale for 6 seconds, and repeat for a few minutes.
- Focus on your surroundings using your senses. Notice 5 things you can see, 4 you can touch, 3 you can hear, 2 you can smell, and 1 you can taste.
- Take a brief walk or do light physical activity to release tension.
- Reduce stimulation if possible (step away from stressful news, social media, or a chaotic environment for a while).

For ongoing stress management:
- Maintain a consistent sleep schedule.
- Exercise regularly, even if it's just 20–30 minutes of walking most days.
- Eat regular meals and stay hydrated.
- Limit excessive caffeine and alcohol, which can worsen anxiety for some people.
- Schedule time for activities you enjoy and for social connection.
- Break large tasks into smaller, manageable steps.

Helpful mental strategies:
- Identify specific worries and separate what you can control from what you cannot.
- Challenge catastrophic thinking by asking:
  - What evidence supports this fear?
  - What evidence does not?
  - What is the most likely outcome?
- Keep a journal to track stress triggers and patterns.

If stress or anxiety is interfering with work, school, relationships, sleep, or daily functioning, it may be helpful to speak with a mental health professional. Treatments such as cognitive behavioral therapy (CBT) and, in some cases, medication can be very effective.
