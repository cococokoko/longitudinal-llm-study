── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How to plan an environmentally friendly wedding?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Planning an environmentally friendly wedding involves reducing waste, lowering emissions, and making sustainable choices without sacrificing the experience. Here are some practical ways to do it:

1. Choose a sustainable venue
- Select a venue close to most guests to reduce travel emissions.
- Consider outdoor venues, botanical gardens, community halls, or eco-certified venues.
- Ask about recycling, composting, renewable energy use, and water conservation practices.

2. Keep the guest list intentional
- A smaller guest list generally means less food waste, travel, energy use, and material consumption.
- Focus on inviting the people most important to you.

3. Use digital invitations
- Send invitations and RSVPs electronically.
- If you prefer printed invitations, choose recycled paper, tree-free paper, or paper embedded with seeds that can be planted.

4. Choose sustainable attire
- Rent wedding attire or purchase secondhand.
- Select outfits that can be worn again.
- Consider designers that use ethical and sustainable materials.
- Encourage wedding party members to wear outfits they already own or can reuse.

5. Select eco-friendly rings and jewelry
- Look for recycled metals.
- Consider vintage or heirloom jewelry.
- Choose ethically sourced gemstones or lab-grown diamonds if desired.

6. Plan a low-waste menu
- Work with caterers who source local and seasonal ingredients.
- Estimate guest numbers carefully to reduce food waste.
- Offer more plant-based menu options, which often have a lower environmental impact.
- Arrange for safe donation of surplus food when possible.

7. Use sustainable decorations
- Rent decorations instead of buying new ones.
- Choose reusable items such as candles, fabric runners, and potted plants.
- Avoid single-use plastics, balloons, and disposable décor.
- Repurpose ceremony flowers for the reception.

8. Choose eco-conscious flowers
- Buy locally grown, seasonal flowers.
- Work with florists who avoid excessive packaging and floral foam.
- Consider potted plants that guests can take home and replant.

9. Reduce waste during the event
- Use reusable dishes, glassware, and linens.
- Provide clearly labeled recycling and compost bins.
- Avoid individually packaged favors and products.

10. Rethink wedding favors
- Skip favors altogether if they are unlikely to be used.
- Offer edible treats, local products, seed packets, or charitable donations in guests' honor.

11. Consider transportation
- Arrange group transportation such as shuttles.
- Encourage carpooling.
- Choose venues near public transit when possible.

12. Select sustainable vendors
- Ask vendors about their environmental practices.
- Support local businesses to reduce transportation impacts.
- Look for vendors who minimize packaging and waste.

13. Manage your wedding registry thoughtfully
- Request experiences, charitable donations, or items you genuinely need.
- Choose durable, high-quality products over disposable or trend-based items.

14. Donate or reuse after the wedding
- Donate flowers to hospitals, care homes, or community organizations where permitted.
- Sell, donate, or reuse décor and attire.
- Recycle materials whenever possible.

15. Measure and offset remaining impacts
- Some couples choose to calculate the event's carbon footprint and support verified carbon-reduction projects.
- Offsetting is generally most effective after efforts to reduce emissions directly.

A sustainable wedding does not have to be perfect. Focusing on a few high-impact areas—guest travel, food choices, venue selection, and reducing single-use items—can make a meaningful difference.
