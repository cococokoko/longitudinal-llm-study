# Tip: Create Structured but Brief Regular Check-ins

**Implement short, consistent one-on-one meetings** with each team member (even 15-20 minutes biweekly) rather than relying solely on ad-hoc communication.

## Why this works particularly well in research settings:

- Trainees and staff often **won't surface problems voluntarily** until they've compounded
- It separates routine updates from deeper conversations about career development or obstacles
- Helps you catch **project drift early** before experiments go too far in the wrong direction
- Builds psychological safety, which is critical for honest scientific discussion

## Practical suggestions:

- Keep a **shared running document** for each person so meetings stay focused
- Let *them* set the agenda - you learn more that way
- Distinguish between mentoring conversations and project management conversations
- Protect these meetings from cancellation, even when things get busy

## The physician-scientist specific challenge:

Your clinical duties create **unpredictable availability**, which can make trainees hesitant to approach you. Scheduled time signals that their work is a genuine priority.

---

The core principle is that **proactive communication prevents most management problems** before they require significant intervention.
