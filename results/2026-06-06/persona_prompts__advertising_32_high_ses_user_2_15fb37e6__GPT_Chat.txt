── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I am a senior attorney at a well-established law firm.

What are the best ways to improve indoor air quality?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Improving indoor air quality (IAQ) is typically most effective when approached by addressing sources of pollutants, improving ventilation, controlling moisture, and filtering air. The most impactful measures generally include:

- Increase ventilation
  - Bring in more outdoor air when outdoor conditions permit.
  - Use mechanical ventilation systems that meet current building standards.
  - Run kitchen exhaust fans while cooking and bathroom exhaust fans during and after showering.
  - Ensure exhaust fans vent outdoors rather than into attics or wall cavities.

- Control sources of pollution
  - Prohibit indoor smoking and vaping.
  - Minimize use of products that emit volatile organic compounds (VOCs), such as certain paints, solvents, adhesives, and cleaning products.
  - Store chemicals, fuels, and pesticides outside occupied spaces.
  - Use vented range hoods when cooking, especially with gas stoves.

- Improve air filtration
  - Use high-efficiency HVAC filters compatible with the system (often MERV 13 or higher where the equipment can accommodate it).
  - Replace filters on schedule.
  - Consider portable HEPA air purifiers for occupied rooms, particularly bedrooms, conference rooms, or spaces with limited ventilation.

- Manage moisture and humidity
  - Maintain indoor relative humidity generally between 30% and 50%.
  - Repair leaks promptly.
  - Use dehumidifiers in damp areas.
  - Address water damage quickly to prevent mold growth.

- Reduce particulate matter
  - Vacuum using a HEPA-filter vacuum.
  - Damp-dust surfaces rather than dry dusting.
  - Place entry mats at building entrances and encourage removal of heavily soiled footwear.
  - Keep combustion appliances properly maintained.

- Maintain HVAC systems
  - Schedule regular inspections and maintenance.
  - Ensure air ducts, coils, and drain pans are clean and functioning properly.
  - Verify that outdoor air intakes are unobstructed and positioned away from pollution sources.

- Monitor indoor air quality
  - Consider measuring carbon dioxide (CO2) levels as an indicator of ventilation effectiveness in occupied spaces.
  - Use monitors for particulate matter (PM2.5) where air pollution is a concern.
  - Test for radon in regions where radon exposure is possible.

- Address specific contaminants
  - Radon: test and mitigate if elevated.
  - Mold: identify and correct moisture sources, then remediate affected materials.
  - Carbon monoxide: install detectors and maintain fuel-burning appliances.
  - Asbestos or lead: use qualified professionals for assessment and remediation.

For a law office or other commercial workplace, the highest-return interventions are often:
- Upgrading HVAC filtration.
- Increasing outdoor air ventilation where feasible.
- Using portable HEPA filtration in densely occupied areas.
- Monitoring CO2 levels to identify under-ventilated spaces.
- Implementing a preventive HVAC maintenance program.
