What are the best apps for tracking fitness and nutrition?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The best app depends on your goals (weight loss, muscle gain, endurance training, general health, etc.), but these are among the strongest options in 2025:

For nutrition tracking

• MacroFactor
  - Excellent for calorie and macro tracking
  - Uses your weight trends and food intake to estimate your metabolism and adjust recommendations
  - Strong analytics and evidence-based approach
  - Paid subscription

• Cronometer
  - Best for detailed nutrient tracking
  - Tracks vitamins, minerals, and micronutrients more thoroughly than most competitors
  - Popular with athletes and people following specialized diets
  - Free version is quite capable

• MyFitnessPal
  - Largest food database
  - Easy barcode scanning and food logging
  - Strong community and integrations
  - Some features require a premium subscription

• Lose It!
  - User-friendly interface
  - Good for weight loss and calorie tracking
  - Easier to use than some more data-heavy apps

For fitness and workout tracking

• Strong
  - One of the best strength-training trackers
  - Simple workout logging
  - Tracks progress over time
  - Great for gym users

• Hevy
  - Similar to Strong but with stronger social features
  - Excellent exercise library
  - Growing rapidly among strength athletes

• Fitbod
  - Generates workouts automatically
  - Adapts recommendations based on your recovery and available equipment
  - Good for beginners and intermediates

• Garmin Connect
  - Excellent if you use Garmin devices
  - Detailed training, recovery, sleep, and performance metrics
  - Free with Garmin hardware

• Strava
  - Best for running and cycling
  - Strong social and competitive features
  - Good route planning and activity analysis

For all-in-one health tracking

• Apple Health
  - Central hub for iPhone users
  - Integrates with hundreds of fitness and nutrition apps
  - Tracks activity, sleep, heart rate, and more

• Google Health Connect
  - Similar role for Android
  - Syncs data between multiple fitness and health apps

• WHOOP
  - Focuses on recovery, strain, and sleep
  - Excellent for athletes who want readiness metrics
  - Requires a membership and wearable

Recommended combinations

• General health: Cronometer + Apple Health (or Health Connect)
• Weight loss: MacroFactor or Lose It! + daily step tracking
• Muscle gain: MacroFactor + Strong or Hevy
• Running: Strava + Garmin Connect
• Data enthusiasts: Cronometer + Garmin Connect + Apple Health
