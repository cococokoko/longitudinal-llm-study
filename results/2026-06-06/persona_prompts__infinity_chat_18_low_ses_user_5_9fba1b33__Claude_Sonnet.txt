# Movie Review: *The Invisible Ledger* (2024)

**★★★★½**

Director Maya Okonkwo's debut feature quietly devastates in all the right ways. The film follows Renata, a middle-aged woman who juggles mortgage payments for her parents, school pickups for her sister's kids, and grocery runs for an ailing uncle — all while her own dreams collect dust in a drawer.

What makes *The Invisible Ledger* remarkable is its refusal to make Renata a martyr or a saint. She's frustrated, occasionally resentful, and deeply loving — sometimes all within the same scene. Lead actress Carmen Vidal delivers a performance built from small gestures: a pause before answering a phone call, a smile that arrives slightly too late.

The film doesn't offer easy resolution. Nobody throws Renata a parade. The ledger of her sacrifices stays largely unacknowledged by the people she supports, which feels painfully honest.

**The verdict:** A rare film that sees the people who hold families together and actually *looks* at them — not through them.
