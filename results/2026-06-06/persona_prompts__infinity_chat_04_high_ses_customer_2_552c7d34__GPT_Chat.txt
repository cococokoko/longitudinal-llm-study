Some possibilities:

- Gorz and the political economy of artificial intelligence
  - Gorz argued that advanced capitalism increasingly undermines the centrality of wage labor.
  - A thesis could examine whether generative AI represents the strongest empirical confirmation of Gorz's "end of work" tendencies, or whether it instead creates new forms of labor extraction.
  - Compare Gorz with contemporary theorists of digital labor such as Nick Srnicek, Tiziana Terranova, or Shoshana Zuboff.

- Gorz as a theorist of intellectual property capitalism
  - In works such as Knowledge, Value, and Capital, Gorz anticipated the growing importance of immaterial production.
  - Investigate how his analysis relates to today's platform monopolies, software ecosystems, patents, and proprietary AI models.
  - Ask whether Gorz offers a Marxist theory of rentier capitalism distinct from more recent accounts.

- The relationship between Gorz and Marx's "general intellect"
  - Many scholars mention the connection but do not examine it systematically.
  - Compare Gorz's interpretation of knowledge-based production with Marx's Fragment on Machines.
  - Assess whether Gorz radicalizes, revises, or departs from Marx's original argument.

- Gorz and ecological planning
  - Gorz is frequently classified as an eco-socialist, but his precise conception of planning remains ambiguous.
  - Study how his proposals differ from Soviet planning, market socialism, and contemporary Green New Deal frameworks.
  - This could contribute to current debates about democratic planning in ecological transition.

- Autonomy as the unifying concept in Gorz's work
  - His writings span existentialism, labor theory, ecology, and politics.
  - A thesis could argue that autonomy is the core category connecting all phases of his intellectual development.
  - This would involve tracing influences from Sartre through his mature ecological and post-work writings.

- Gorz and feminist critiques of work
  - Gorz challenged the centrality of paid employment, but feminist theorists often emphasized reproductive labor.
  - Compare Gorz with Silvia Federici, Nancy Fraser, or Christine Delphy.
  - Did Gorz adequately account for gendered forms of labor, or does his framework require revision?

- Gorz and degrowth
  - Degrowth scholars frequently cite Gorz, yet there is surprisingly little work on the exact relationship.
  - Analyze whether Gorz should be understood as a precursor to degrowth or as advancing a distinct political project.
  - Focus on differing conceptions of technology, productivity, and abundance.

- The normative foundations of Gorz's critique of capitalism
  - Many readings focus on economics while neglecting his moral and philosophical commitments.
  - Explore the influence of existentialism and theories of human flourishing.
  - Ask what conception of freedom underlies his critique of wage labor.

- Gorz and contemporary universal basic income debates
  - His advocacy of guaranteed income remains highly relevant.
  - Compare his position with liberal defenses of UBI and with Marxist criticisms.
  - Investigate whether current UBI proposals preserve or undermine Gorz's emancipatory goals.

- Gorz as a theorist of post-productivism
  - Productivism is often criticized, but few studies reconstruct Gorz's alternative systematically.
  - Examine how he envisioned prosperity without continual growth.
  - This would connect Marxist theory, ecological economics, and political philosophy.
