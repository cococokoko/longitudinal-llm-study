# Tips for Running a Marathon

## Training
- **Follow a structured plan** (16-24 weeks for most runners)
- Build mileage gradually — no more than 10% increase per week
- Include long runs, tempo runs, and easy recovery days
- Don't skip rest days — they're when adaptation happens

## Nutrition & Hydration
- Practice your race-day nutrition during training runs
- Carbohydrate-load 2-3 days before the race
- Learn to take fluids while running
- Use gels/chews every 45-60 minutes during the race

## Race Day Strategy
- **Start slower than you feel you should** — the most common mistake
- Run the first half conservatively, negative split if possible
- Know the course elevation profile
- Don't try anything new on race day (shoes, food, gear)

## Recovery
- Plan for 2-4 weeks of easy running post-race
- Prioritize sleep throughout training
- Address minor injuries early before they become major

## Practical Advice
- Train in the shoes you'll race in
- Body Glide or anti-chafe balm on friction points
- Practice your pre-race morning routine

## Mindset
- Break the race into segments mentally
- Have a backup goal in case conditions are tough
- The last 10K is a separate race — prepare mentally for it
