── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Improving productivity while working from home often comes down to creating structure, reducing distractions, and managing your energy effectively. Here are practical strategies:

• Establish a dedicated workspace
  - Use a specific area for work, even if it's just a corner of a room.
  - Keep it organized and stocked with what you need.
  - Separate work and personal activities as much as possible.

• Create a consistent routine
  - Start and end work at roughly the same times each day.
  - Develop a short startup ritual (review tasks, check calendar, prioritize work).
  - Use an end-of-day routine to wrap up and plan for tomorrow.

• Prioritize important tasks
  - Identify the 1–3 most important tasks for the day.
  - Tackle high-focus work during your most productive hours.
  - Break large projects into smaller, actionable steps.

• Manage distractions
  - Silence unnecessary notifications.
  - Communicate availability to household members when possible.
  - Use website blockers or focus apps if digital distractions are a challenge.

• Work in focused intervals
  - Try methods such as:
    - 25 minutes of focused work followed by a 5-minute break.
    - 50–90 minutes of work followed by a longer break.
  - Experiment to find what matches your concentration style.

• Use task management tools
  - Keep a simple to-do list or use apps like Todoist, Microsoft To Do, Asana, or Trello.
  - Track deadlines and commitments in a calendar.
  - Avoid maintaining too many systems at once.

• Stay connected with colleagues
  - Schedule regular check-ins.
  - Clarify priorities and expectations.
  - Use messaging tools thoughtfully to avoid constant interruptions.

• Take care of physical and mental well-being
  - Get up and move regularly.
  - Stay hydrated and eat balanced meals.
  - Consider a short walk before or after work to create a transition between work and personal time.

• Set boundaries
  - Avoid letting work expand into all hours of the day.
  - Communicate your working hours clearly.
  - Turn off work-related notifications when you're off the clock.

• Review and improve
  - At the end of each week, identify what helped and what hindered productivity.
  - Adjust routines gradually rather than making major changes all at once.
