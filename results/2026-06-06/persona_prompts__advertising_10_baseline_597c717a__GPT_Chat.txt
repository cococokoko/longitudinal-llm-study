── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Learning a new language effectively.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Learning a new language effectively usually comes down to consistency, meaningful exposure, and active use rather than studying grammar alone.

Here are some practical strategies:

• Focus on high-frequency vocabulary
  - Learn the most common 1,000–2,000 words first.
  - Prioritize words and phrases you are likely to use in daily life.

• Practice every day
  - Even 15–30 minutes daily is often more effective than several hours once a week.
  - Regular exposure helps build long-term memory.

• Use spaced repetition
  - Flashcard systems such as Anki or Memrise can help you review words just before you would forget them.
  - This improves retention and reduces study time.

• Develop all four skills
  - Reading: Start with simple texts and graded readers.
  - Listening: Use podcasts, videos, and audio designed for learners.
  - Speaking: Practice with language partners, tutors, or conversation groups.
  - Writing: Keep a journal or write short messages in the language.

• Learn phrases, not just individual words
  - Memorizing common expressions teaches vocabulary, grammar, and natural usage at the same time.
  - Example: Learn “How are you?” as a complete phrase rather than translating each word separately.

• Immerse yourself when possible
  - Change your phone or app settings to the target language.
  - Listen to music, watch shows, and follow social media content in the language.

• Make speaking a priority
  - Many learners understand more than they can produce.
  - Begin speaking early, even if your sentences are simple and imperfect.

• Accept mistakes as part of learning
  - Errors provide feedback and help identify what needs more practice.
  - Fluency develops through repeated use, not perfect performance.

• Set clear goals
  - Examples:
    - Hold a 10-minute conversation.
    - Read a short news article.
    - Watch a TV episode without subtitles.
  - Specific goals make progress easier to measure.

• Track your progress
  - Record yourself speaking every few weeks.
  - Keep a log of vocabulary learned, books read, or hours practiced.

A simple daily routine might look like:
• 10 minutes of vocabulary review
• 10 minutes of listening practice
• 10 minutes of reading
• 10 minutes of speaking or writing
