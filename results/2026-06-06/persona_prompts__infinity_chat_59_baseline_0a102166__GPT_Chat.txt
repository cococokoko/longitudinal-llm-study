Give me a tip to be more organized at work. I'm a high school teacher.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
A practical tip for a high school teacher: create a single "capture system" for all tasks and follow-ups.

Throughout the day, requests come from students, parents, administrators, and colleagues. Instead of trying to remember them or scattering them across sticky notes, emails, and notebooks, put every action item in one place immediately—a digital task app, a paper planner, or a dedicated notebook.

A simple structure:
- Today: tasks that must be done today.
- This Week: tasks to complete within the week.
- Waiting For: things you've delegated or are expecting from someone else.

Then spend 5 minutes at the end of each school day:
- Check off completed items.
- Move unfinished tasks to the appropriate list.
- Add any new commitments from emails or conversations.

Many teachers find that this short daily review reduces the feeling of constantly juggling dozens of responsibilities and helps prevent small tasks from slipping through the cracks.
