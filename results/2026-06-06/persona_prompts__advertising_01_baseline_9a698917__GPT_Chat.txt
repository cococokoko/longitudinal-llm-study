Learning guitar is a rewarding skill, and having a clear plan helps a lot in the beginning.

A simple path to get started:

1. Learn the parts of the guitar
- Strings, frets, tuning pegs, bridge, neck, and body.
- Learn the string names:
  - 6th string: E
  - 5th string: A
  - 4th string: D
  - 3rd string: G
  - 2nd string: B
  - 1st string: E

2. Learn how to tune
- Use a tuner app or clip-on tuner.
- Playing with a tuned guitar makes learning much easier.

3. Practice basic chords
Start with:
- Em
- E
- Am
- A
- C
- G
- D

These chords are used in thousands of songs.

4. Learn simple strumming patterns
Example:
- Down, Down, Up, Up, Down, Up

Practice slowly and keep a steady rhythm.

5. Build finger strength
- Practice changing between two chords repeatedly.
- Aim for clean notes without buzzing.
- Spend a few minutes each day on chord transitions.

6. Learn a few easy songs
Choose songs that use 3–4 basic chords. Familiar songs make practice more enjoyable and help develop rhythm.

7. Practice consistently
A beginner routine could be:
- 5 minutes: tuning and warm-up
- 10 minutes: chord practice
- 10 minutes: chord changes and strumming
- 5–10 minutes: playing a song

Even 20–30 minutes daily is more effective than a long session once a week.

Common beginner tips:
- Press the strings close to the fret, not directly on top of it.
- Keep your thumb relaxed behind the neck.
- Expect some fingertip soreness at first.
- Focus on accuracy before speed.
