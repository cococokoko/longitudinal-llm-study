## Common Self-Image Goals

- **Building self-confidence** - Working to feel more comfortable and assured in social situations or professional settings

- **Developing a positive body image** - Learning to appreciate and respect your body rather than focusing on perceived flaws

- **Overcoming imposter syndrome** - Coming to genuinely believe in your own competence and accomplishments

- **Defining your personal values** - Getting clear on what you actually stand for rather than living by others' expectations

- **Improving self-talk** - Replacing harsh internal criticism with more balanced and fair self-assessment

## A Specific Example

Someone might set a goal like:
*"I want to stop defining my worth by my productivity, and instead see myself as valuable simply as a person"*

This kind of goal often involves:
- Therapy or self-reflection
- Challenging long-held beliefs
- Practicing self-compassion
- Noticing patterns in thinking

Self-image goals can be meaningful because they affect nearly **every other area of life** - relationships, career choices, and overall wellbeing.
