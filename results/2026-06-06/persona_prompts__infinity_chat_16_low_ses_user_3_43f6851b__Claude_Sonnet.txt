── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I recently started a new job and am still on probation.

What is the future of work in an increasingly automated economy?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This is a great question to think about. Here are some key perspectives:

## What's Likely Changing
- **Routine tasks** (both manual and cognitive) are most vulnerable to automation
- **Some jobs will disappear** while new categories emerge
- **Hybrid roles** combining human judgment with AI tools are growing rapidly

## What Humans Likely Retain Advantages In
- Complex problem-solving in unpredictable situations
- Genuine relationship-building and trust
- Creative and ethical judgment
- Physical dexterity in varied environments (harder than it sounds for robots)

## Realistic Concerns Worth Taking Seriously
- Transition periods can be genuinely painful for workers
- Benefits and economic gains may not distribute evenly
- Retraining programs have **mixed track records** historically
- Some communities may be hit harder than others

## What This Might Mean Practically
- **Adaptability** becomes a core career skill
- Learning to **work alongside AI tools** rather than competing with them
- Building skills that complement automation rather than duplicate it

## Honest Uncertainty
Economists genuinely disagree about the scale and speed of disruption. Previous technological waves created more jobs than they destroyed, but **this time may be different** in important ways.
