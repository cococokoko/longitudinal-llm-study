What is the difference between analysis and design? Can one begin to design without analysis? Why? Be concise.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
- Analysis is the process of understanding a problem, its requirements, constraints, stakeholders, and goals.
- Design is the process of creating a solution that meets those requirements and goals.

Can one begin to design without analysis?
- Technically, yes—people can start sketching ideas or prototypes immediately.
- However, effective design generally requires at least some analysis. Without understanding the problem, the design may address the wrong needs, violate constraints, or fail to achieve the intended objectives.
- In practice, analysis and design often overlap and iterate: initial analysis informs design, and design work can reveal the need for further analysis.
