Reducing household energy use can lower utility bills and, in many cases, reduce wear on appliances. If you're managing a larger household or supporting extended family members, focusing on the biggest energy users often delivers the best results.

- Adjust heating and cooling efficiently

  - Replace HVAC filters regularly.

- Improve insulation
  - Add or upgrade attic insulation if needed.

  - Use weatherstripping and door sweeps to reduce air leaks.

- Reduce water-heating costs
  - Lower the water heater temperature to a safe, efficient setting.
  - Install low-flow showerheads and faucet aerators.
  - Wash clothes with cold water when appropriate.

- Use lighting efficiently
  - Replace incandescent bulbs with LEDs.
  - Turn off lights in unoccupied rooms.
  - Take advantage of natural daylight when possible.

- Optimize appliance use
  - Run dishwashers and washing machines with full loads.
  - Clean refrigerator coils periodically.
  - Keep refrigerator and freezer temperatures at recommended levels.
  - Air-dry dishes or laundry when practical.

- Manage electronics and standby power
  - Unplug devices that draw power when not in use.
  - Use smart power strips for entertainment centers and home offices.
  - Enable energy-saving modes on computers and televisions.

- Cook more efficiently
  - Match pot sizes to burners.
  - Keep lids on pots to reduce cooking time.
  - Use microwaves, pressure cookers, or air fryers when suitable, as they often use less energy than a full-size oven.

- Monitor energy consumption
  - Review utility bills for seasonal trends.
  - Use home energy monitors or smart plugs to identify high-use devices.
  - Check whether your utility offers time-of-use rates and shift flexible activities accordingly.

  - Keep dryer vents clean.
