Give a numerical example to illustrate the concept of partial derivative.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
A partial derivative measures how a function changes with respect to one variable while holding the other variables constant.

Example:

Consider the function

f(x, y) = x²y + 3y

Suppose we want the partial derivative with respect to x.

Treat y as a constant:

∂f/∂x = 2xy

Now evaluate it at the point (x, y) = (2, 4):

∂f/∂x(2,4) = 2 × 2 × 4 = 16

Interpretation:
- Near the point (2,4), if x increases by a small amount while y remains fixed at 4, the function f increases by approximately 16 times that small amount.

For example:
- f(2,4) = 2²·4 + 3·4 = 28
- Increase x from 2 to 2.01 while keeping y = 4:
  - f(2.01,4) = (2.01)²·4 + 12 = 28.1604
  - Actual change = 28.1604 − 28 = 0.1604
- Using the partial derivative:
  - Estimated change ≈ 16 × 0.01 = 0.16

The estimate (0.16) is very close to the actual change (0.1604).

Similarly, the partial derivative with respect to y is

∂f/∂y = x² + 3

and at (2,4):

∂f/∂y(2,4) = 2² + 3 = 7

This means that near (2,4), increasing y by a small amount while keeping x fixed causes f to increase by approximately 7 times that small amount.
