If you're looking to reduce the environmental impact of travel, here are some of the most effective options:

- Walk or cycle for short distances.
  - Zero direct emissions.
  - Often the healthiest and most affordable choice.

- Use public transportation.
  - Trains, subways, trams, and buses generally produce far fewer emissions per passenger than private cars.
  - Electric rail systems are especially efficient.

- Choose trains over flights when practical.
  - Rail travel typically has a much lower carbon footprint than air travel, especially for short- and medium-distance journeys.

- Carpool or use ride-sharing.
  - Sharing a vehicle reduces emissions per person compared with driving alone.

- Drive a low-emission vehicle.
  - Electric vehicles (EVs) can significantly reduce emissions, especially when charged using renewable energy.
  - Hybrid vehicles can also be a more efficient option than conventional cars.

- Fly less frequently.
  - When flying is necessary, consider nonstop flights, which generally produce fewer emissions than routes with multiple connections.
  - Pack light, as aircraft weight affects fuel consumption.

- Stay in environmentally conscious accommodations.
  - Look for hotels or lodging with recognized sustainability certifications, energy-efficient operations, and waste-reduction programs.

- Support local businesses while traveling.
  - Buying local food and services can reduce transportation-related emissions and support local economies.

- Travel more slowly and stay longer.
  - Fewer trips with longer stays can reduce transportation emissions compared with frequent short trips.

- Consider carbon offset programs.
  - While not a substitute for reducing emissions, reputable offset programs can help fund projects such as reforestation or renewable energy.

For commuting, public transit, cycling, walking, and carpooling are usually the most environmentally friendly practical choices. For longer-distance travel, trains are often the best balance of convenience and sustainability.
