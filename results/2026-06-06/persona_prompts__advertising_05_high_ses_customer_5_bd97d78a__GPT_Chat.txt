Healthy meal planning on a budget is largely about choosing nutrient-dense staples, planning around sales, and minimizing food waste. A simple system can reduce both costs and decision fatigue.

Key principles
- Build meals around affordable staples:
  - Beans, lentils, chickpeas
  - Rice, oats, whole-wheat pasta
  - Potatoes and sweet potatoes
  - Eggs
  - Frozen vegetables and fruit
  - Canned fish (tuna, sardines, salmon)
  - Plain yogurt
  - Seasonal produce
- Prioritize protein and fiber at each meal to improve satiety.
- Buy store brands when possible.
- Use a weekly plan and shopping list to avoid impulse purchases.
- Cook once, eat multiple times through leftovers and batch cooking.
- Freeze extra portions before they spoil.
