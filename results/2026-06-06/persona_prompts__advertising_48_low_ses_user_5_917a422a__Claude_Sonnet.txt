# Best Practices for Organic Gardening

Here are practical, reliable approaches organized by category:

## Soil Health (Foundation of Everything)
- **Compost regularly** - kitchen scraps, yard waste, coffee grounds
- Test soil pH before planting (most vegetables prefer 6.0-7.0)
- Add organic matter annually to improve structure
- Avoid tilling excessively to protect soil microbes

## Pest & Weed Management
- **Companion planting** - basil near tomatoes, marigolds as borders
- Hand-pick pests when populations are small
- Use mulch (3-4 inches) to suppress weeds and retain moisture
- Encourage beneficial insects with diverse plantings

## Watering
- Water deeply but less frequently to encourage deep roots
- Morning watering reduces disease risk
- Drip irrigation is most efficient

## Practical Tips for Busy Households
- **Start small** - one raised bed is manageable
- Choose **low-maintenance crops** first (lettuce, herbs, zucchini)
- Involve family members in age-appropriate tasks
- Preserve harvests by freezing or canning to reduce grocery costs

## Cost-Saving Strategies
- Save seeds from heirloom varieties
- Trade plants/seeds with neighbors
- Make your own compost instead of buying amendments

## What to Avoid
- Synthetic pesticides and fertilizers
- Overwatering
- Planting the same crops in the same spot yearly (rotate!)
