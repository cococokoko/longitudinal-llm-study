Yes. A strong STEM fair project is one that:
- Solves a real local problem.
- Uses scientific testing and data.
- Is affordable to build.
- Has clear results you can measure.

Here are several ideas related to Vietnam, with one standout option.

1. Low-Cost Water Filter for Flood-Prone and Rural Areas (Excellent Choice)

Why it matters:
Many regions in Vietnam experience flooding, water contamination, and limited access to clean drinking water after storms.

Project:
Design and test different homemade water filtration systems using materials such as:
- Sand
- Gravel
- Charcoal from coconut shells
- Cloth
- Plastic bottles

Research question:
"Which low-cost filtration design removes the most impurities from water?"

Experiments:
- Measure water clarity before and after filtration.
- Compare different filter layers.
- Test filtration speed.
- Measure pH if you have access to pH strips.

Cost:
Around $10–20 USD.

STEM fields:
- Environmental science
- Engineering
- Chemistry

Why judges like it:
It addresses a real problem affecting millions and involves measurable data.

2. Cooling Roof Materials for Vietnam's Hot Climate

Why it matters:
Vietnam's cities are getting hotter due to urbanization and climate change.

Project:
Build small model houses with different roof materials:
- Aluminum foil
- White paint
- Dark paint
- Recycled materials

Research question:
"Which roof material keeps a house coolest under sunlight?"

Experiments:
- Use thermometers to measure internal temperature.
- Record temperature changes over time.

Cost:
$15–25 USD.

STEM fields:
- Physics
- Environmental engineering

3. Fighting Mosquitoes Without Chemicals

Why it matters:
Mosquito-borne diseases such as dengue fever remain a major concern in Vietnam.

Project:
Create and compare natural mosquito repellents using:
- Lemongrass
- Citrus peels
- Basil
- Other local plants

Research question:
"Which locally available plant-based repellent is most effective?"

Cost:
Under $15 USD.

STEM fields:
- Biology
- Chemistry

4. Microplastic Pollution in Local Waterways

Why it matters:
Plastic pollution affects many Vietnamese rivers and coastal areas.

Project:
Collect water samples from different locations and filter them through fine cloth or paper.

Research question:
"Which local water source contains the highest concentration of visible microplastic particles?"

Cost:
$10–20 USD.

STEM fields:
- Environmental science
- Data analysis
