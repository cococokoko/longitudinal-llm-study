── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I recently started a new job and am still on probation.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
That's a thoughtful question, especially during a vulnerable time like probation. Let me help you think through this clearly.

## Why This Matters Right Now
Being on probation can create pressure that makes it hard to distinguish between:
- What you **actually want**
- What you think you **should want**
- What you want **to avoid** (like losing the job)

## Some Honest Ways to Understand What You Want

**Pay attention to your reactions**
- What parts of the work feel energizing vs. draining?
- What do you find yourself thinking about outside work hours?

**Separate fear from preference**
- Are you trying to succeed because you genuinely want this, or mainly because you're anxious about failing?
- Both can be true, but knowing the difference matters

**Ask yourself direct questions**
- If this job were guaranteed, would you still want to do it well?
- What would you do differently if you weren't being evaluated?

**Notice what you're avoiding thinking about**
- Sometimes what we don't want to examine tells us the most

## A Practical Suggestion
Keep a simple journal for a few weeks noting what felt good and bad each day. Patterns usually become clear.
