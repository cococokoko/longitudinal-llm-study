# Tips for Running a Marathon

Here are some helpful tips to prepare for and complete a marathon:

## Training
- **Follow a structured plan** - Build mileage gradually (no more than 10% per week)
- **Include long runs** - Weekly long runs build endurance
- **Cross-train** - Cycling or swimming reduces injury risk
- **Rest days matter** - Recovery is part of training

## Nutrition & Hydration
- **Practice fueling during training** - Test gels, chews, or real food
- **Stay hydrated** - Drink consistently, not just when thirsty
- **Carb load** - Increase carbohydrates 2-3 days before race day
- **Nothing new on race day** - Stick to what you trained with

## Gear
- **Invest in good shoes** - Get fitted at a running store
- **Wear moisture-wicking clothing** - Prevents chafing
- **Break in gear before race day**

## Race Day Strategy
- **Start slow** - Many runners go out too fast
- **Run your own pace** - Ignore the crowd
- **Use aid stations** - Don't skip them
- **Break the race into segments** - Focus on smaller goals

## Recovery
- **Plan for rest after the race**
- **Refuel within 30-60 minutes of finishing**
- **Expect soreness for several days**

## Practical Note
Given your long hours on your feet at work, **pay extra attention to foot care and recovery** - your legs are already working hard daily, so quality sleep and rest days are especially important.
