── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Decorating your home on a budget is often about prioritizing high-impact changes and being selective about where you spend money.

Some practical strategies:

- Start with a plan
  - Choose a color palette and overall style before buying anything.
  - Measure rooms and make a list of what you actually need.

- Rearrange before you buy
  - Moving existing furniture can dramatically change how a room feels.
  - Remove unnecessary items to create a cleaner, more intentional look.

- Use paint strategically
  - Paint is one of the most cost-effective upgrades.
  - Consider an accent wall, repainting old furniture, or refreshing trim and doors.

- Focus on lighting
  - Add floor lamps or table lamps rather than relying solely on overhead lighting.
  - Use warm-toned bulbs to create a more inviting atmosphere.

- Shop secondhand
  - Thrift stores, estate sales, flea markets, and online marketplaces often have quality furniture and décor at a fraction of retail prices.
  - Solid wood pieces can often be refinished or painted.

- Upgrade textiles
  - New throw pillows, blankets, curtains, or rugs can significantly change a room's appearance.
  - Neutral large items with a few colorful accents tend to be versatile and affordable.

- Create wall décor affordably
  - Frame inexpensive prints, photographs, or downloadable artwork.
  - Group smaller pieces into a gallery wall for greater visual impact.

- Add plants
  - Houseplants can make spaces feel more vibrant and finished.
  - Many varieties are inexpensive and easy to maintain.

- DIY where it makes sense
  - Simple projects such as painting furniture, installing shelves, or creating artwork can save money.
  - Focus on projects within your skill level to avoid costly mistakes.

- Spend selectively
  - Invest in items that receive heavy use, such as sofas or mattresses.
  - Save on accessories, side tables, and decorative items that can be sourced secondhand.

A useful rule is to make one noticeable improvement at a time rather than trying to redecorate an entire home at once. This often produces better results while keeping costs manageable.
