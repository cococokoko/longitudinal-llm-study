# CUBICLE OF THE DAMNED
### A Play in Three Acts
*In the Style of Scott Adams' Dilbert*
*Set in a post-apocalyptic wasteland, circa sometime after everything went wrong*

---

## CAST OF CHARACTERS

**DILBERT** — An engineer. Somehow still wearing his tie, which has curled upward at the tip. The apocalypse has changed nothing about his fundamental outlook.

**THE POINTY-HAIRED BOSS (PHB)** — Survived the apocalypse through sheer managerial incompetence, which apparently confers immunity. Wears a hardhat over his signature pointy hair.

**WALLY** — Has been sitting in the same folding chair since the bombs fell. His coffee mug is somehow still full. No one questions this.

**ALICE** — Carries both a fist of death AND a crossbow. The crossbow is for the mutants. The fist is for everyone else.

**DOGBERT** — A small dog wearing a crown made of bottle caps. Has declared himself Supreme Overlord of the Wasteland. This is not meaningfully different from his previous goals.

**ASOK** — The intern. Still an intern. Will always be an intern. The apocalypse did not change his employment status.

**CATBERT** — Former Evil Director of Human Resources. Now Evil Director of Wasteland Resources. Essentially the same job.

**THE GARBAGE MAN** — Now called THE ORACLE OF REFUSE. He was always wise. People just listen now.

---

## ACT ONE
### "THE ALL-HANDS MEETING AT THE END OF THE WORLD"

*SETTING: A collapsed office building in a scorched wasteland. A whiteboard on wheels has survived completely intact. Someone has written "SYNERGY" on it in marker. A motivational poster reading "HANG IN THERE" shows a cat hanging from a cliff over a sea of fire. Folding chairs are arranged in a semicircle around a pile of rubble. WALLY is already seated, coffee in hand. DILBERT enters, picking his way through debris, tie curled upward.*

---

**DILBERT:** *(to audience)* The bombs fell on a Tuesday. Which was actually an improvement over our Tuesday status meetings. At least the bombs had a clear agenda.

*(PHB enters, climbing over a collapsed wall, clipboard in hand.)*

**PHB:** Good news, people. I've scheduled our quarterly all-hands meeting. I think it's important that we maintain our cadence even during the extinction-level event.

**DILBERT:** The building is on fire.

**PHB:** That's a great point, Dilbert. I'm going to put you in charge of the fire. Can you have a status report by end of day?

**DILBERT:** End of day for whom? Civilization has collapsed.

**PHB:** I need it in my inbox by five.

**DILBERT:** Your inbox is a pile of ash.

**PHB:** *(writing on clipboard)* I'm noting that you're being resistant to feedback.

*(ALICE enters, crossbow slung over shoulder, dragging a mutant by the ankle.)*

**ALICE:** I've secured the perimeter. There were fourteen mutants. There are now zero mutants. I also fixed the Henderson account, reorganized our supply cache by category AND alphabetically, and I built a water filtration system out of a coffee maker and moral outrage.

**PHB:** Alice, I'm going to need you to document all of that in a one-page summary.

**ALICE:** *(knuckles cracking)* Which hand would you like me to use to write it?

**PHB:** Whichever is more efficient.

**ALICE:** *(dangerously)* That's what I thought.

*(WALLY has not moved. His coffee steams mysteriously.)*

**DILBERT:** Wally, how is your coffee still hot? The power grid has been down for six months.

**WALLY:** I've learned to ask no questions about the things that bring me comfort.

**DILBERT:** That's either very zen or very disturbing.

**WALLY:** In my experience, those are the same thing.
