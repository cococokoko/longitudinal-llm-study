**Question:** What is scientifically unique about the feathers of almost all blue birds, such as Blue Jays and Eastern Bluebirds, regarding their color?

**Answer:** They don't actually contain any blue pigment! The color is created by an optical illusion called "structural coloration." Microscopic structures within the feathers scatter light, absorbing longer wavelengths (like red and yellow) and reflecting only the blue wavelengths back to our eyes. If you were to grind up a blue feather, the dust would actually look brown or gray.
