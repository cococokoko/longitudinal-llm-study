Hardwood flooring offers several advantages compared with carpet, vinyl, laminate, and some other flooring types:

- Durability: Solid hardwood can last for decades, and in many cases over 50 years, if properly maintained.
- Refinishable surface: Unlike many flooring materials, hardwood can often be sanded and refinished multiple times to remove scratches, stains, and wear.
- Appearance: Many people value the natural beauty, grain patterns, and warmth of real wood. Hardwood also tends to have a timeless look that works with many design styles.
- Increased home value: Hardwood floors are often viewed as a desirable feature by homebuyers and may contribute positively to resale appeal.
- Easy cleaning: Dust, pet hair, and debris can be swept, vacuumed, or mopped up more easily than from carpet.
- Better for allergy concerns: Hardwood does not trap dust, pollen, pet dander, and other allergens to the same extent as carpet.
- Variety: Hardwood comes in many species, colors, plank widths, and finishes, allowing for a wide range of design choices.
- Longevity of appearance: High-quality hardwood can maintain its appeal for many years and can often be restored rather than replaced.

Compared with specific alternatives:

- Versus carpet:
  - Easier to clean spills and dirt.
  - Less likely to retain odors.
  - Generally lasts longer.

- Versus laminate:
  - Real wood can usually be refinished.
  - Often considered more premium and may add more resale value.

- Versus vinyl:
  - Natural material rather than a manufactured surface.
  - Can often be repaired and refinished instead of fully replaced.

Potential drawbacks to consider:

- Higher upfront cost than many alternatives.
- Susceptible to moisture damage, making it less ideal for some bathrooms, laundry rooms, or areas prone to flooding.
- Can scratch or dent from heavy furniture, pets, or dropped objects.
- May require periodic refinishing over its lifespan.
