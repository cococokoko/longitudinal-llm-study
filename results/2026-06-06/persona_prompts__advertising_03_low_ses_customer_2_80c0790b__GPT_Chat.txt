- Create a dedicated work zone

  - Keep frequently used items within reach to reduce interruptions.

- Plan your day around priorities
  - Identify 1–3 high-impact tasks each day.
  - Schedule your most demanding work during your peak energy hours.

- Use time blocking
  - Reserve specific periods for focused work, meetings, email, and breaks.
  - If your schedule is influenced by caregiving responsibilities, build flexibility into your blocks rather than planning every minute.

- Reduce distractions
  - Silence nonessential notifications.
  - Close unnecessary browser tabs and apps.
  - Consider using website blockers during focus periods.

- Work in focused intervals
  - Techniques such as 25–50 minutes of focused work followed by a short break can help maintain concentration.
  - Adjust the length based on your workload and schedule.

- Set clear boundaries
  - Communicate availability to colleagues and household members when possible.
  - Use calendar blocks or status indicators to show when you're focusing.

- Batch similar tasks
  - Handle email, administrative work, and routine tasks in groups rather than switching between them throughout the day.

- Keep a simple task system
  - Use a notebook, task app, or digital planner.
  - Capture tasks immediately and review them daily.

- Take intentional breaks
  - Short walks, stretching, or stepping away from your screen can improve focus and reduce fatigue.

- Review and adjust weekly
  - Spend a few minutes each week identifying what worked and what caused delays.
  - Make small adjustments rather than overhauling your entire routine.
