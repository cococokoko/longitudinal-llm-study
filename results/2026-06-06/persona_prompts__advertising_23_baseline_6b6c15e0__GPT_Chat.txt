── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How can I learn to cook healthy meals quickly?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Learning to cook healthy meals quickly is mostly about building a small set of skills, recipes, and habits rather than mastering lots of complicated techniques.

Here’s a practical approach:

1. Learn a few core cooking methods
Focus on 3–4 techniques that work for many ingredients:
- Roasting: vegetables, chicken, fish
- Stir-frying: vegetables, tofu, lean meats
- Sautéing: quick meals in one pan
- Boiling/steaming: grains, eggs, vegetables

Once you know these methods, you can mix and match ingredients without following detailed recipes.

2. Build meals around a simple formula
A healthy meal can often be:
- Protein: chicken, fish, eggs, tofu, beans, lentils
- Vegetables: fresh or frozen
- Healthy carbohydrate: brown rice, potatoes, quinoa, whole-grain pasta
- Healthy fat: olive oil, avocado, nuts, seeds

For example:
- Grilled chicken + roasted broccoli + rice
- Bean stir-fry + vegetables + quinoa
- Salmon + sweet potato + salad

3. Start with 10–20 minute recipes
Look for meals such as:
- Vegetable omelets
- Stir-fries
- Sheet-pan chicken and vegetables
- Grain bowls
- Bean-based soups
- Tacos with lean protein and vegetables

Avoid recipes with long ingredient lists at first.

4. Keep healthy convenience foods on hand
These can save significant time:
- Frozen vegetables
- Pre-washed salad greens
- Canned beans
- Rotisserie chicken
- Frozen fish fillets
- Microwaveable brown rice or quinoa

Convenience foods can still be nutritious.

5. Learn a handful of go-to recipes
Choose 5–7 meals and make them repeatedly until they're automatic.
Examples:
- Chicken and vegetable stir-fry
- Turkey chili
- Baked salmon and vegetables
- Lentil soup
- Egg and vegetable scramble
- Burrito bowl
- Pasta with vegetables and protein

6. Practice meal prep
Once or twice a week:
- Cook a batch of protein
- Prepare grains
- Wash and chop vegetables
- Portion meals into containers

Even 30–60 minutes of prep can make weekday cooking much faster.

7. Improve one skill at a time
Focus on:
- Knife skills
- Seasoning with salt, herbs, spices, and acids (lemon, vinegar)
- Cooking proteins without overcooking them
- Timing multiple components together

Small improvements make meals both faster and more enjoyable.

8. Use reliable learning resources
Good options include:
- YouTube cooking channels focused on beginners
- Budget Bytes
- EatingWell
- America's Test Kitchen
- Cookbooks aimed at simple weeknight cooking

A beginner-friendly goal is to learn one new recipe each week while repeating recipes you already know. Within a couple of months, you'll usually have enough confidence and repetition to prepare healthy meals quickly without relying heavily on recipes.
