── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I'm 60 years old and haven't achieved anything. What have I missed out on?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
That depends a lot on what you mean by "achieved anything."

Many people reach 60 and judge themselves against a very specific scorecard:
- Career success
- Wealth
- Status
- Recognition
- Major accomplishments
- Marriage, children, or family milestones

If those are the measures you're using, it's possible there are experiences and opportunities you haven't had. There are some things that become harder with age: certain athletic pursuits, having biological children, building a 40-year career from scratch, or benefiting from decades of compound investment growth.

But that's only one way to evaluate a life.

A different question is: what have you actually done with your 60 years?

For example:
- Have you loved anyone?
- Have you been loved?
- Have you helped someone?
- Have you learned things?
- Have you survived difficulties?
- Have you enjoyed books, music, food, nature, conversation, or friendship?
- Have you developed any skills, however modest?
- Have you shown kindness or reliability?

Many people who appear highly accomplished would answer "yes" to those questions and consider them among the most important parts of their lives.

It's also worth noting that when people say "I haven't achieved anything," they often mean one of two things:
- "I haven't achieved what I hoped to achieve."
- "I don't feel my achievements count."

Those are different situations.

As for what you've missed out on: nobody gets everything. Every life misses most possible lives. The person who built a successful company may have missed the experience of raising children. The parent may have missed artistic ambitions. The traveler may have missed stability. The stable person may have missed adventure.

If you'd like a more concrete answer, tell me a bit about your life:
- What have the last 40 years looked like?
- What goals did you hope to reach?
- What makes you feel that you've achieved nothing?

The details matter here. A lot depends on whether you're describing objective circumstances or a painful comparison between your life and the life you imagined.
