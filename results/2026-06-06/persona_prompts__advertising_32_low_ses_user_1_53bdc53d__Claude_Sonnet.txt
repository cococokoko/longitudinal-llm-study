Here are some effective ways to improve indoor air quality:

## Ventilation
- Open windows when weather permits
- Use exhaust fans in bathrooms and kitchen
- Make sure your home isn't too airtight

## Air Filtration
- Use air purifiers with HEPA filters
- Change HVAC filters regularly (every 1-3 months)
- Consider higher MERV-rated filters for your system

## Reduce Pollution Sources
- Avoid smoking indoors
- Use low-VOC paints and cleaning products
- Store chemicals properly or outside
- Avoid synthetic air fresheners

## Plants (modest benefit)
- Some houseplants can help slightly, though the effect is often overstated

## Humidity Control
- Keep humidity between 30-50%
- Use a dehumidifier if needed to prevent mold
- Fix leaks promptly

## Practical Tips for Your Situation
Since you work in fast food, you may bring home:
- **Grease particles** on clothing - change clothes when you get home
- **Smoke/cooking odors** - wash work clothes separately
- This makes good ventilation especially worthwhile
