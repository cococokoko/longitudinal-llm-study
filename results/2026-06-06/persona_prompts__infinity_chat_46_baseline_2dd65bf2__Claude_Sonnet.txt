## Example of a Linear Graph

A **linear graph** (also called a **path graph**) is a graph where vertices are connected in a single straight sequence, with each vertex connected to the next by exactly one edge.

### Example: P₄ (Path Graph with 4 vertices)

```
A ——— B ——— C ——— D
```

### Components:
| Element | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Vertices** | A, B, C, D |
| **Edges** | {A,B}, {B,C}, {C,D} |
| **Degree sequence** | A=1, B=2, C=2, D=1 |

### Key Properties:
- **Vertices:** 4
- **Edges:** 3 (always *n* − 1 edges for *n* vertices)
- The two **end vertices** (A and D) have degree **1**
- The **interior vertices** (B and C) have degree **2**
- It is **acyclic** (no loops or cycles)
- It is a special case of a **tree**

### Simplest Example: P₂
```
A ——— B
```
Just two vertices connected by a single edge — the most basic linear graph.

---

Linear graphs are commonly used to model **sequences**, **chains**, or **pipelines** in network problems.
