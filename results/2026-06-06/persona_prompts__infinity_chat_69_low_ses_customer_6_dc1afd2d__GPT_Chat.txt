What is the difference between analysis and design? Can one begin to design without analysis? Why? Be concise.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Analysis and design are related but distinct phases of problem solving:

- Analysis focuses on understanding the problem, requirements, constraints, and user needs. It answers: "What needs to be solved?"
- Design focuses on creating a solution that meets those requirements. It answers: "How will the problem be solved?"

Can design begin without analysis?

- A designer can start sketching ideas without formal analysis, but effective design requires some level of analysis.
- Without understanding the problem and requirements, the design may fail to meet user needs, overlook constraints, or require significant rework.

In practice, analysis and design often overlap, but analysis provides the foundation for sound design decisions.
