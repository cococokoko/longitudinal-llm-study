Reducing household energy use is often most effective when you focus first on the largest energy consumers: heating and cooling, water heating, lighting, and major appliances.

Some of the highest-impact measures include:

- Improve insulation and air sealing
  - Seal gaps around doors, windows, ducts, and utility penetrations.
  - Add or upgrade attic, wall, and crawl-space insulation.
  - Weather-strip exterior doors and windows.

- Optimize heating and cooling
  - Install a programmable or smart thermostat.
  - Adjust thermostat settings a few degrees lower in winter and higher in summer.
  - Replace HVAC filters regularly.
  - Schedule periodic maintenance for heating and cooling systems.

- Upgrade lighting
  - Replace incandescent or halogen bulbs with LEDs.
  - Use occupancy sensors or timers in less-frequently used areas.
  - Turn off lights when rooms are unoccupied.

- Reduce water-heating energy
  - Lower the water heater temperature to an efficient setting recommended by the manufacturer.
  - Install low-flow showerheads and faucet aerators.
  - Insulate hot-water pipes and older water heaters where appropriate.
  - Wash clothes in cold water when suitable.

- Use energy-efficient appliances
  - Choose high-efficiency appliances when replacements are needed.
  - Run dishwashers and washing machines with full loads.
  - Air-dry dishes or clothes when practical.

- Address standby power consumption
  - Unplug infrequently used electronics.
  - Use advanced power strips to reduce "phantom" energy use.
  - Enable energy-saving settings on computers, televisions, and other devices.

- Improve window performance
  - Use blinds, shades, or curtains to manage heat gain and loss.
  - Consider energy-efficient windows if existing windows are old or poorly performing.

- Manage refrigerator and freezer efficiency
  - Keep door seals in good condition.
  - Set temperatures according to manufacturer guidance.
  - Clean condenser coils periodically if accessible.

- Use energy monitoring tools
  - Review utility bills for usage trends.
  - Consider a home energy monitor or smart-meter data to identify unusually high consumption.

3. Thermostat optimization.
4. HVAC maintenance and efficiency upgrades.
5. Water-heating improvements.
