What are the best resources for starting a plant-based diet?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
If you're starting a plant-based diet, the most helpful resources usually fall into four categories: nutrition guidance, meal planning, recipes, and practical support.

Nutrition and health resources
- Physicians Committee for Responsible Medicine (PCRM) — evidence-based guides, meal plans, and nutrition information.
  - pcrm.org
- NutritionFacts.org — science-focused videos and articles about plant-based nutrition.
  - nutritionfacts.org
- Harvard T.H. Chan School of Public Health Nutrition Source — balanced nutrition information, including healthy plant-based eating.
  - hsph.harvard.edu/nutritionsource
- Academy of Nutrition and Dietetics — articles on vegetarian and vegan nutrition.
  - eatright.org

Books for beginners
- "Plant-Based on a Budget" by Toni Okamoto — practical and affordable.
- "The Plant-Based Diet for Beginners" by Gabriel Miller — simple introduction and meal plans.
- "How Not to Die" by Michael Greger — discusses research on diet and health.
- "Fiber Fueled" by Will Bulsiewicz — focuses on plant diversity and gut health.

Recipe websites
- Forks Over Knives
  - forksoverknives.com
- Minimalist Baker
  - minimalistbaker.com
- Rainbow Plant Life
  - rainbowplantlife.com
- Nora Cooks
  - noracooks.com
- Love and Lemons
  - loveandlemons.com
- Budget Bytes (many affordable plant-based recipes)
  - budgetbytes.com

Meal planning and tracking
- Forks Meal Planner
- Mealime (includes vegetarian and vegan meal plans)
- Cronometer (helpful for tracking nutrients such as protein, iron, calcium, vitamin B12, and omega-3s)

Key nutrients to learn about
- Protein: beans, lentils, tofu, tempeh, edamame, seitan, soy milk
- Vitamin B12: typically requires fortified foods or a supplement
- Iron: legumes, tofu, seeds, leafy greens
- Calcium: fortified plant milks, tofu made with calcium sulfate, greens
- Omega-3s: flaxseed, chia seeds, walnuts; some people use algae-based supplements
- Vitamin D: may require supplementation depending on sun exposure and location

A simple way to start
- Replace one meal a day with a plant-based option.
- Build meals around:
  - A protein source (beans, tofu, lentils)
  - Whole grains (brown rice, oats, quinoa)
  - Vegetables and fruit
  - Healthy fats (nuts, seeds, avocado)
- Keep easy staples on hand:
  - Canned beans
  - Frozen vegetables
  - Oats
  - Tofu
  - Peanut butter
  - Whole-grain bread or rice
