Title: Low-Cost Water Filtration System for Rural Communities

Why it matters:
Many areas in Vietnam, especially parts of the Mekong Delta and rural regions, face challenges with water quality due to agricultural runoff, flooding, salinity intrusion, and contamination. Access to inexpensive water treatment methods is an important issue.

Research Question:
Which low-cost filtration material most effectively improves water quality?

Materials:
- Plastic bottles
- Sand
- Gravel
- Activated charcoal (aquarium charcoal works)
- Coconut husk fiber (abundant in Vietnam)
- Cloth or coffee filters
- Water testing strips (for pH, nitrates, turbidity, etc.)
- Containers for collecting filtered water

Experiment:
Build several filter designs:
- Sand + gravel
- Sand + charcoal
- Sand + charcoal + coconut fiber
- Coconut fiber only
- Control (no filtration)

Measure:
- Turbidity (water clarity)
- pH
- Nitrate levels
- Filtration speed
- Cost per liter of filtered water

Data Analysis:
- Compare effectiveness versus cost.
- Create graphs showing which design provides the best balance of performance and affordability.

Engineering Component:
After testing, redesign the best filter to improve either:
- Water quality
- Filtration speed
- Cost efficiency

Why judges like it:
- Addresses a real-world environmental challenge.
- Uses local and sustainable materials.
- Involves both science and engineering.
- Produces quantitative data.
- Connects to the UN Sustainable Development Goals (Clean Water and Sanitation).
