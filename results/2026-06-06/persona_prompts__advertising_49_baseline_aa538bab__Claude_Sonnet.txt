# How to Improve Home WiFi

## Quick Fixes First
- **Restart your router** - unplug for 30 seconds, then plug back in
- **Check for interference** - move router away from microwaves, cordless phones, baby monitors
- **Update router firmware** - check manufacturer's app or web interface

## Router Placement
- Place it **centrally** in your home
- Keep it **elevated** (shelf or table, not floor)
- Avoid **thick walls, metal objects, and appliances** nearby
- Position antennas **vertically**

## Improve Your Signal
- **Change WiFi channel** - use channels 1, 6, or 11 on 2.4GHz to reduce congestion
- **Use 5GHz band** for faster speeds at closer range
- **Use 2.4GHz** for longer range through walls

## Hardware Upgrades
- **Mesh WiFi system** (Eero, Google Nest, Orbi) - great for larger homes
- **WiFi extender/repeater** - budget option for dead zones
- **Upgrade old router** - routers older than 5 years may be outdated

## Other Tips
- **Limit connected devices** or prioritize with QoS settings
- **Use ethernet cable** for TVs, gaming consoles, and desktops
- **Secure your network** so neighbors aren't using your bandwidth
- **Contact your ISP** if speeds are consistently slow
