# Best Practices for Organic Gardening

## Soil Health
- **Compost regularly** - add finished compost to improve structure and nutrients
- **Test your soil** periodically to understand pH and nutrient levels
- **Avoid tilling** excessively, which disrupts beneficial soil organisms
- **Use cover crops** like clover or rye to prevent erosion and add nitrogen

## Pest & Disease Management
- **Rotate crops** each season to break pest and disease cycles
- **Encourage beneficial insects** - ladybugs, lacewings, and ground beetles
- **Plant companion plants** (e.g., marigolds deter aphids)
- **Remove diseased plants** promptly to prevent spread
- **Use physical barriers** like row covers when needed

## Watering
- Water deeply and **less frequently** to encourage deep roots
- **Water at the base** of plants, not leaves, to reduce disease
- Use **drip irrigation or soaker hoses** for efficiency
- **Mulch** to retain moisture and suppress weeds

## Fertilizing
- Prioritize **compost and aged manure**
- Use **fish emulsion or kelp meal** for quick nutrient boosts
- Avoid over-fertilizing, which can harm plants and soil

## General Tips
- **Start with healthy transplants or seeds**
- **Observe your garden regularly** to catch problems early
- **Keep records** of what works each season
- **Be patient** - organic systems improve over time
