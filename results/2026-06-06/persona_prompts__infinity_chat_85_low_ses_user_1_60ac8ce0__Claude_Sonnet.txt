Can you give me a powerful rhetorical question for an essay about the harms of social media on teens?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here's a strong rhetorical question for your essay:

**"If we wouldn't hand a teenager a product known to cause anxiety, depression, and self-harm, why do we scroll past the evidence that social media does exactly that?"**

This works well because it:
- **Creates a logical comparison** that's hard to dismiss
- **Challenges the reader's consistency** in their thinking
- **Implies urgency** without being preachy

A few alternatives depending on your essay's tone:

- *"When did we decide that a teenager's mental health was worth less than an algorithm's engagement metrics?"*
- *"How many more studies do we need before we treat social media addiction with the same seriousness as any other public health crisis?"*

**Quick tip:** Place it near your introduction or conclusion for maximum impact - rhetorical questions hit hardest when they frame your argument or leave the reader thinking.
