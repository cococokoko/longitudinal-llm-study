── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
My girlfriend is buying a house. I plan to marry her in a year or so. I don't know if it's fair for me to pay for the house or not. What's the best solution?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This is a really common situation and worth thinking through carefully. Here are some practical considerations:

## Key Questions to Ask Yourself

- Are you moving in together now?
- Will you contribute to mortgage payments?
- What happens if the relationship doesn't work out?

## Realistic Options

**Option 1: Don't contribute financially**
- She owns it, she pays for it
- Clean and simple until you're married
- You could pay rent instead

**Option 2: Contribute to expenses but not equity**
- Pay shared living costs, utilities, groceries
- Not the mortgage itself
- Protects you both

**Option 3: Formal co-ownership agreement**
- Get legally documented before contributing money
- A lawyer can draft a cohabitation agreement
- Protects both parties if things change

**Option 4: Wait until marriage**
- Address property ownership then
- Many couples do this deliberately

## Things Worth Considering

- Contributing to someone else's mortgage without ownership **builds them equity, not you**
- Marriage changes legal rights significantly
- A year is still uncertain - statistically many engagements don't result in marriage
- **A lawyer consultation is genuinely worth it** here

## My Honest Suggestion
Talk openly with your girlfriend about expectations, and **consult a family law attorney** before making financial commitments. This protects both of you.
