── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
What is the difference between analysis and design? Can one begin to design without analysis? Why? Be concise.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
## Analysis vs. Design

| | **Analysis** | **Design** |
|---|---|---|
| **Focus** | *What* the system must do | *How* the system will do it |
| **Question** | Problem understanding | Solution construction |
| **Output** | Requirements, models of current/desired state | Blueprints, architecture, specifications |

---

## Key Difference
- **Analysis** = understanding and defining the **problem**
- **Design** = creating a **solution**

---

## Can You Design Without Analysis?

**No** — not effectively.

**Why:**
- Design decisions must be grounded in requirements; without analysis, you don't know *what* you're solving
- You risk building the **wrong system** correctly
- Leads to costly rework, missed requirements, and misaligned solutions

> *"There is nothing so useless as doing efficiently that which should not be done at all."* — Peter Drucker

---

## In Practice
While the two phases **overlap** in iterative/agile methods, some level of analysis *always precedes* design — even informally. Skipping it entirely means designing based on **assumptions**, not understanding.
