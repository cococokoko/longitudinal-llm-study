This is a really common situation and there are a few reasonable approaches to think through:

## Key Considerations

**Your current situation matters:**
- You're not married yet, so legally you have no ownership claim if you just pay
- A lot can change in a year
- You want to protect both of you fairly

## Practical Options

**Option 1: Contribute to shared expenses, not the mortgage**
- Pay rent/utilities while she covers the mortgage
- Keeps finances cleaner before marriage
- You're still contributing meaningfully

**Option 2: Formal co-borrower arrangement**
- Go on the mortgage together now
- Gives you legal ownership stake
- More complicated if things don't work out

**Option 3: Written agreement**
- If you contribute significantly, document it
- A simple legal agreement can protect both parties
- Relatively inexpensive with an attorney

**Option 4: Wait and reassess after marriage**
- Marriage changes the financial picture significantly
- You could refinance together later

- What does she expect from you financially?
- What are your **combined** long-term financial goals?
- Have you discussed finances openly as a couple?
