Stress affects both the body and the mind in many ways. When a person experiences stress, the body releases hormones such as adrenaline and cortisol. These hormones prepare the body to respond to challenges by increasing heart rate, blood pressure, and energy levels. While this response can be helpful in short-term situations, long-term stress can have negative effects on physical health.

Chronic stress may weaken the immune system, making it easier to get sick. It can also contribute to headaches, muscle tension, digestive problems, sleep disturbances, and an increased risk of heart disease. People under constant stress may feel tired and have difficulty recovering from illness.

Stress also affects mental and emotional well-being. It can cause feelings of worry, irritability, frustration, and sadness. High levels of stress may make it harder to concentrate, remember information, and make decisions. Over time, ongoing stress can increase the risk of anxiety and depression.

Managing stress is important for maintaining overall health. Regular exercise, healthy eating, sufficient sleep, relaxation techniques, and social support can help reduce stress and improve well-being. By recognizing stress and taking steps to manage it, people can protect both their physical and mental health.
