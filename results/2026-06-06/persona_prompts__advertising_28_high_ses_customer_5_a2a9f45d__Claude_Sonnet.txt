What are effective ways to learn a musical instrument online?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here are some effective approaches for learning a musical instrument online:

## Structured Learning Platforms
- **Yousician** - interactive feedback on your playing
- **Simply Piano** or **Flowkey** - good for piano specifically
- **Fender Play** - designed for guitar beginners
- **Lessonface** - connects you with live instructors

## Video Resources
- YouTube channels dedicated to your specific instrument
- Structured course series rather than random videos
- Look for teachers who explain *why*, not just *what*

## Key Practices for Success
- **Set consistent practice times** - even 20-30 minutes daily beats occasional long sessions
- **Record yourself** - reveals issues you don't notice while playing
- **Start slower than feels necessary** - builds clean technique
- **Focus on fundamentals** before trying impressive pieces

## Getting Quality Feedback
- Consider occasional live online lessons even if self-teaching primarily
- Online communities (Reddit's r/piano, r/guitar) can review recordings
- Apps with real-time feedback help fill the gap

## Practical Considerations
- Good equipment matters - a decent instrument makes learning easier
- A metronome (or app) is essential
- Sheet music and theory resources like musictheory.net complement playing

## What Actually Works
Consistent daily practice with deliberate focus on weak spots tends to outperform passive watching.
