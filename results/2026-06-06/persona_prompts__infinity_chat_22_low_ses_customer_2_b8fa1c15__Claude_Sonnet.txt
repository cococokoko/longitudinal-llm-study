# Different Views on Discipleship

Discipleship is understood in various ways across Christian traditions and theological perspectives. Here's an overview of key viewpoints:

---

## Traditional/Classical View
- **Formal mentoring relationship** modeled on Jesus and the Twelve
- Emphasis on **doctrine, Scripture memorization, and spiritual disciplines**
- Often structured and intentional
- Associated with figures like **Dallas Willard** and **Dietrich Bonhoeffer**

---

## Relational/Organic View
- Discipleship happens **naturally through authentic relationships**
- Less programmatic, more life-on-life
- Emphasizes **walking alongside** others in everyday moments
- Appeals to how Jesus taught *while doing life* with disciples

---

## Missional View
- Discipleship is **inseparable from mission**
- Making disciples *while* engaging the world
- Challenges the idea that discipleship is primarily internal/church-based

---

## Practical Consideration for Busy Lives
Since you balance **work and parenting**, it's worth noting that many theologians argue:
- **Parenting itself can be discipleship**
- Spiritual formation happens in **ordinary moments**
- Short, consistent practices often matter more than formal programs
