── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here are some effective ways to improve your credit score relatively quickly:

## Highest Impact Actions

- **Pay down credit card balances** - Reducing your credit utilization ratio (below 30%, ideally below 10%) can show results within one billing cycle
- **Pay all bills on time** - Payment history is the largest factor in your score
- **Request a credit limit increase** - This improves utilization without paying down debt

## Quick Administrative Steps

- **Check your credit reports for errors** at AnnualCreditReport.com and dispute inaccuracies
- **Become an authorized user** on a family member's account with good history
- **Ask for goodwill deletions** of late payments from creditors

## Things to Avoid

- Don't close old accounts (reduces available credit)
- Avoid opening multiple new accounts quickly
- Minimize hard inquiries

## Realistic Timeline

| Utilization reduction | 30-45 days |
| Dispute resolution | 30-45 days |
| Payment history improvement | Several months |
