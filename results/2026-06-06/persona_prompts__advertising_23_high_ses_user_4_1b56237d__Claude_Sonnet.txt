── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I am a physician-scientist leading a research group.

How can I learn to cook healthy meals quickly?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here are some practical approaches to cooking healthy meals quickly:

## Core Strategies

**Batch cooking**
- Dedicate 1-2 hours on weekends to prep ingredients
- Cook grains, proteins, and roasted vegetables in bulk
- Assemble different combinations throughout the week

**Simple frameworks**
- Protein + vegetable + grain/starch covers most nutritional needs
- Sheet pan meals require minimal active time
- One-pot dishes reduce cleanup

## Skills Worth Developing

- Knife skills (speeds up prep significantly)
- Learning 5-6 reliable recipes well rather than many poorly
- Understanding flavor combinations so you can improvise

## Practical Tools

- Instant Pot or pressure cooker
- Sheet pans
- Good chef's knife
- Pre-cut or frozen vegetables (nutritionally comparable to fresh)

## Time-Saving Habits

- Keep a stocked pantry (canned beans, olive oil, spices, pasta)
- Mise en place - organize ingredients before cooking
- Clean as you go

## Resources

- **Books:** *Salt Fat Acid Heat* (Nosrat), *How to Cook Everything Fast* (Bittman)
- **Approach:** Mediterranean diet principles are well-supported and relatively simple

Given your schedule, **batch cooking + a few reliable recipes** is probably the highest-return investment.
