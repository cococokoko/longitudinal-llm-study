── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Is there life on other planets?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
That's one of the biggest open questions in science. Here's an honest summary:

## What We Know
- Life exists on Earth, showing it's *possible* under the right conditions
- The universe contains billions of galaxies, each with billions of stars and planets
- Many planets exist in "habitable zones" with potential for liquid water
- Basic organic chemistry appears widespread in space

## What We've Found So Far
- **No confirmed evidence** of life beyond Earth
- Mars shows signs of ancient water but no confirmed life
- Some scientists find the chemistry of Venus's atmosphere interesting but inconclusive
- Moons like Europa and Enceladus have subsurface oceans that could potentially support microbial life

## The Honest Assessment
- **Microbial life elsewhere** - many scientists consider this plausible given the scale of the universe
- **Intelligent life** - much more uncertain
- The **Fermi Paradox** raises a genuine puzzle: if life is common, why haven't we detected signals?

## What's Happening Now
- James Webb Telescope is analyzing exoplanet atmospheres
- NASA has active Mars missions
- SETI continues monitoring for signals

The scientific consensus is essentially **"we don't know yet"** - which is genuinely exciting rather than disappointing.
