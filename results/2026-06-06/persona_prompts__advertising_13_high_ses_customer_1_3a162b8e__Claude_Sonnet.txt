# Mastering the Art of Cooking Steak

## Understanding the Fundamentals

### Key Variables
- **Cut selection** - affects cooking method
- **Thickness** - determines timing
- **Temperature management** - the critical factor
- **Resting** - often overlooked but essential

---

## Cut Selection Guide

| Cut | Fat Content | Best Method | Ideal Doneness |
|-----|-------------|-------------|----------------|
| Ribeye | High | Pan sear/grill | Medium-rare |
| NY Strip | Medium | Pan sear/grill | Medium-rare |
| Filet | Low | Pan sear + butter baste | Medium-rare |
| Skirt/Flank | Lean | High heat, quick | Medium-rare |
| Chuck | High | Reverse sear | Medium |

---

## Core Techniques

### The Reverse Sear (Most Reliable)
1. Season generously, ideally **24 hours ahead**
2. Oven at **250°F** until internal temp reaches **115-120°F**
3. Rest briefly, then **screaming hot sear** 60-90 seconds per side

### Classic Pan Sear
1. **Dry the surface** completely
2. Cast iron ripping hot
3. High smoke-point oil
4. Sear, flip frequently, **butter baste** with aromatics

---

## Temperature Targets

- Rare: **120-125°F**
- Medium-rare: **130-135°F**
- Medium: **140-145°F**

---

## The Details That Separate Good from Great

- **Dry brine** overnight uncovered in refrigerator
- **Room temperature** myth - 30 min makes minimal difference
- **Rest** at least 5-10 minutes before cutting
- Cut **against the grain**
- Finish with **flaky salt**
