I work full-time in a fast food job with an unpredictable schedule.

What are the best ways to improve indoor air quality?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
If your schedule is unpredictable, the most effective air-quality improvements are usually the ones that work continuously without requiring much daily attention.

In order of impact:

1. Reduce pollution at the source
- Don't smoke or vape indoors.
- Use kitchen exhaust fans when cooking, especially when frying or using gas stoves.
- Use bathroom fans to reduce moisture and mold growth.
- Store chemicals, paints, solvents, and strong cleaners in sealed containers.
- Remove mold and fix water leaks promptly.

2. Improve filtration
- If you have central heating/air conditioning, use a high-quality air filter that your system can handle (often MERV 11–13).
- Replace filters on schedule.
- Run the HVAC fan according to manufacturer recommendations if energy costs are acceptable.

3. Use a portable HEPA air purifier
- A true HEPA purifier can significantly reduce dust, pollen, pet dander, and some smoke particles.
- Place it in the room where you spend the most time, often the bedroom.
- Choose one sized appropriately for the room.
- Run it continuously on a low or medium setting if possible.

4. Ventilate when outdoor air is good
- Open windows and doors periodically to exchange indoor air.
- If you live near heavy traffic, wildfire smoke, or industrial pollution, check outdoor air quality before opening windows.

5. Control humidity
- Aim for roughly 30–50% relative humidity.
- Use a dehumidifier in damp spaces if humidity is high.
- In very dry climates, a humidifier may improve comfort, but keep it clean to prevent microbial growth.

6. Clean dust effectively
- Vacuum with a HEPA-filter vacuum if possible.
- Damp-dust surfaces instead of dry dusting, which can stir particles into the air.
- Wash bedding regularly.

7. Manage pet-related particles
- Brush pets regularly.
- Wash pet bedding.
- Keep pets out of bedrooms if allergies are a concern.

8. Pay attention to combustion appliances
- Ensure furnaces, water heaters, fireplaces, and gas appliances are properly maintained.
- Install carbon monoxide detectors on every level of your home.

If you're looking for the best value for money with minimal effort, a good HEPA air purifier in your bedroom plus proper HVAC filtration usually provides the biggest improvement for most people.
