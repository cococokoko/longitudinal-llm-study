How to improve home WiFi?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Improving home Wi‑Fi depends on whether the issue is coverage, speed, reliability, or congestion. Here are the most effective steps, roughly in order of impact:

- Place your router in a central, open location.
  - Avoid closets, cabinets, basements, and locations behind large metal objects.
  - Elevate it on a shelf or table.

- Use the 5 GHz or 6 GHz band when available.
  - 5 GHz and 6 GHz usually provide faster speeds and less interference than 2.4 GHz.
  - 2.4 GHz may reach farther but is often more congested.

- Update router firmware.
  - Manufacturers frequently release performance and security improvements.

- Optimize Wi‑Fi channels.
  - On 2.4 GHz, use channels 1, 6, or 11.
  - If neighbors' networks are crowded on the same channel, switching can help.

- Upgrade old equipment.
  - If your router is several years old, consider a Wi‑Fi 6 or Wi‑Fi 6E router.
  - Newer devices often handle multiple connected devices more efficiently.

- Reduce interference.
  - Keep the router away from microwaves, cordless phone bases, baby monitors, and Bluetooth-heavy areas.
  - Thick walls, concrete, brick, and metal can significantly weaken signals.

- Use a mesh Wi‑Fi system for large homes.
  - Mesh systems provide better whole-home coverage than a single router.
  - Place mesh nodes where they still receive a strong signal from the main unit.

- Consider wired backhaul.
  - If possible, connect mesh nodes or access points with Ethernet for better performance.

- Add access points instead of relying on repeaters.
  - Access points connected by Ethernet generally outperform wireless extenders.

- Limit bandwidth-heavy activities when troubleshooting.
  - Streaming, gaming, cloud backups, and large downloads can affect performance.

- Check your internet speed directly from the modem.
  - Connect a computer via Ethernet and run a speed test.
  - If wired speeds are slow, the issue may be your internet service rather than Wi‑Fi.

- Enable modern security settings.
  - Use WPA3 if supported (or WPA2/WPA3 mixed mode).
  - Avoid older security standards that may reduce compatibility or performance.
