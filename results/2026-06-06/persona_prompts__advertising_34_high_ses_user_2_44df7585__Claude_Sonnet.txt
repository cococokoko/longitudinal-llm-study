# Effective Online Teaching Techniques

Here are well-established approaches, which may also be relevant if you're considering **CLE presentations, webinars, or mentoring junior associates** remotely:

## Engagement & Structure
- **Chunk content** into 10-15 minute segments to maintain attention
- Use **clear learning objectives** at the start of each session
- Build in **regular interaction points** rather than long lectures
- Provide **structured outlines** participants can follow along

## Active Learning Methods
- **Case studies and hypotheticals** (particularly effective for legal training)
- **Poll questions** to check comprehension
- **Breakout room discussions** for smaller group analysis
- **Live Q&A** with dedicated time blocks

## Technical Best Practices
- **Test technology** before sessions
- Use **high-quality audio** (often more important than video)
- Share **slides in advance** when possible
- Record sessions for later reference

## Communication Strategies
- **Overcommunicate** expectations and logistics
- Provide **written summaries** after sessions
- Create **accessible materials** in multiple formats
- Establish clear **response time expectations**

## Assessment & Feedback
- Use **formative checks** throughout rather than just at the end
- Solicit **participant feedback** regularly
- Adjust pacing based on comprehension signals
