Tips for running a marathon.

Running a marathon is as much about preparation and pacing as it is about fitness. Here are some key tips:

Before the marathon:
- Follow a structured training plan, ideally 12–20 weeks depending on your experience.
- Build mileage gradually, increasing weekly distance by no more than about 10% at a time.
- Include a weekly long run to prepare your body and mind for extended effort.
- Practice your race-day nutrition and hydration during training.
- Taper in the final 2–3 weeks by reducing training volume to allow recovery.
- Prioritize sleep, especially in the week leading up to the race.
- Avoid trying new shoes, clothing, or nutrition on race day.

Race-day strategy:
- Start slower than feels necessary. Many runners go out too fast and struggle later.
- Follow a pacing plan based on your training and fitness.
- Stay hydrated, but don't overdrink. Use aid stations according to your practiced strategy.
- Consume carbohydrates during the race if it lasts longer than about 90 minutes. Common options include gels, chews, or sports drinks.
- Break the race into smaller segments mentally rather than focusing on all 26.2 miles (42.2 km) at once.
- Expect some discomfort, but distinguish normal fatigue from signs of injury or medical issues.

Nutrition:
- Eat a carbohydrate-rich meal the evening before and a familiar pre-race breakfast.
- Avoid excessive fiber, fat, or unusual foods before the race.
- Aim for regular carbohydrate intake during the marathon, typically around 30–60 grams per hour for many runners, though needs vary.

Gear:
- Wear well-tested running shoes that are broken in but not excessively worn.
- Dress for the weather and remember that you'll warm up as you run.
- Use anti-chafing products on areas prone to irritation.

Mental approach:
- Have a plan for difficult moments, especially after mile 20 (around km 32), where many runners hit the "wall."
- Focus on maintaining effort and form rather than obsessing over pace if conditions become challenging.
- Stay positive and flexible if the race doesn't unfold exactly as planned.

Recovery:
- Keep moving for a short period after finishing.
- Rehydrate and eat a mix of carbohydrates and protein.
- Allow adequate recovery before returning to hard training.
