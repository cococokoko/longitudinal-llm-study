── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I am a partner at a major consulting firm.

How might society evolve if telepathy became possible?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This is a genuinely interesting question worth thinking through carefully rather than just enthusiastically.

## Likely Significant Disruptions

**Economic/Professional**
- Negotiation, sales, and persuasion industries would fundamentally transform
- Legal systems would face enormous pressure around consent and evidence
- Consulting itself would change - client relationship dynamics shift when intentions are transparent

**Political**
- Propaganda becomes harder but manipulation might find new forms
- Leadership selection criteria would change dramatically
- Democratic deliberation could improve OR deteriorate depending on implementation

## Honest Complications Worth Considering

**The optimistic narrative has problems:**
- People might develop stronger cognitive compartmentalization rather than becoming more honest
- Power asymmetries could worsen if telepathy is unevenly distributed or controllable
- Privacy elimination historically correlates with authoritarianism, not liberation

**The "transparency solves everything" assumption is questionable:**
- Many social conflicts stem from genuine value differences, not just miscommunication
- Knowing others' thoughts doesn't mean agreeing with them

## What Actually Matters Most

The critical variables would be:
- Is it voluntary or involuntary?
- Is it universal or selective?
- Who controls access to it?

These questions matter more than telepathy itself - **technology's social impact usually depends on governance structures**, not the technology alone.
