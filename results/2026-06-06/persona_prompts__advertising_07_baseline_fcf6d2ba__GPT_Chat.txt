── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Effective ways to reduce household energy use.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Reducing household energy use can lower utility bills and decrease environmental impact. Some of the most effective measures include:

- Improve insulation and air sealing
  - Seal gaps around doors, windows, pipes, and vents.
  - Add or upgrade attic, wall, and floor insulation.
  - Use weatherstripping and door sweeps to reduce drafts.

- Optimize heating and cooling
  - Install a programmable or smart thermostat.
  - Adjust thermostat settings a few degrees lower in winter and higher in summer.
  - Replace HVAC filters regularly.
  - Have heating and cooling systems serviced annually.

- Use energy-efficient lighting
  - Replace incandescent bulbs with LED bulbs.
  - Turn off lights when rooms are unoccupied.
  - Use natural daylight when possible.

- Upgrade appliances
  - Choose energy-efficient models when replacing old appliances.
  - Look for high-efficiency refrigerators, washers, dryers, and dishwashers.
  - Run full loads in washing machines and dishwashers.

- Reduce water-heating energy
  - Lower the water heater temperature (commonly around 120°F/49°C, if appropriate).
  - Install low-flow showerheads and faucet aerators.
  - Fix leaking hot-water faucets.
  - Wash clothes in cold water when suitable.

- Cut standby power use
  - Unplug devices not in use.
  - Use advanced power strips to reduce "phantom" energy consumption.
  - Enable energy-saving modes on electronics.

- Improve window efficiency
  - Use insulated curtains or blinds.
  - Close blinds during hot days to reduce heat gain.
  - Consider double- or triple-pane windows if replacing windows.

- Use appliances more efficiently
  - Air-dry clothes when practical.
  - Clean dryer lint filters after each use.
  - Keep refrigerator coils clean and maintain proper temperatures.

- Manage cooking energy
  - Use microwaves, pressure cookers, or air fryers for suitable meals.
  - Keep lids on pots to reduce cooking time.
  - Match pot size to burner size.

- Monitor energy use
  - Review utility bills to identify trends.
  - Use smart plugs or energy monitors to find high-consumption devices.
  - Take advantage of utility energy audits if available.

For many households, the biggest savings usually come from improving insulation and air sealing, optimizing heating and cooling systems, switching to LED lighting, and upgrading older appliances when replacement is needed.
