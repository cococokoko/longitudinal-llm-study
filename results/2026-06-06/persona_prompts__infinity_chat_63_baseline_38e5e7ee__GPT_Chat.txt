── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Give a numerical example to illustrate the concept of partial derivative.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
A partial derivative measures how a function changes with respect to one variable while keeping the other variables fixed.

Example:

Consider the function

f(x, y) = x²y + 3y

Suppose we want the partial derivatives at the point (2, 4).

1. Partial derivative with respect to x

Treat y as a constant:

∂f/∂x = 2xy

At (2, 4):

∂f/∂x(2,4) = 2 × 2 × 4 = 16

Interpretation:
When y is fixed at 4, a small increase in x changes f at a rate of about 16 units per unit increase in x.

2. Partial derivative with respect to y

Treat x as a constant:

∂f/∂y = x² + 3

At (2, 4):

∂f/∂y(2,4) = 2² + 3 = 7

Interpretation:
When x is fixed at 2, a small increase in y changes f at a rate of about 7 units per unit increase in y.

Numerical check:

The function value at (2,4) is

f(2,4) = 2² × 4 + 3 × 4 = 16 + 12 = 28

Increase x slightly from 2 to 2.1 while keeping y = 4:

f(2.1,4) = (2.1)² × 4 + 12 = 29.64

Change in f = 29.64 − 28 = 1.64

Approximation using the partial derivative:

Δf ≈ (∂f/∂x)Δx = 16 × 0.1 = 1.6

The actual change (1.64) is close to the approximation (1.6), illustrating the meaning of the partial derivative.
