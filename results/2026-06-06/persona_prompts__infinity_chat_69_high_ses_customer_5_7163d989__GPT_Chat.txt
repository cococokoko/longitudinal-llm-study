What is the difference between analysis and design? Can one begin to design without analysis? Why? Be concise.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Analysis and design are related but distinct activities:

- Analysis focuses on understanding the problem: goals, requirements, constraints, stakeholders, processes, and current conditions. It answers, “What needs to be solved?”
- Design focuses on creating a solution: architecture, workflows, interfaces, components, and implementation approaches. It answers, “How should we solve it?”

Can design begin without analysis?

- Yes, but only at a preliminary or exploratory level. Early design concepts, prototypes, or hypotheses can help clarify requirements and uncover unknowns.
- However, effective design ultimately depends on analysis. Without sufficient analysis, design decisions are more likely to address the wrong problem, miss key constraints, or require costly rework.

In practice, analysis and design are often iterative: initial analysis informs design, and design activities frequently reveal the need for deeper analysis.
