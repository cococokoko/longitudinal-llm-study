# CUBICLE OF THE DAMNED
### A Play in Three Acts
*In the Style of Scott Adams' Dilbert, Set During the Apocalypse*

---

## CAST OF CHARACTERS

**DILBERT** — An engineer trying to optimize survival in a post-apocalyptic wasteland, still wearing his signature white shirt with the curled tie, now slightly singed

**DOGBERT** — Dilbert's dog, morally flexible as ever, has somehow become Warlord of Sector 7

**THE POINTY-HAIRED BOSS (PHB)** — Survived the apocalypse through sheer managerial incompetence, which apparently confers immunity

**WALLY** — Has not noticed the apocalypse. Is still looking for coffee.

**ALICE** — Fist of Death now legally classified as a weapon of mass destruction by the remaining government, which is three people in a bunker

**ASOK** — The intern. Still an intern. The apocalypse did not come with a promotion.

**CATBERT** — Evil HR Director. Thriving. Suspiciously thriving.

---

## ACT ONE: THE MORNING STANDUP

*SCENE: The ruins of an open-plan office. Half the ceiling has collapsed. Mutant rats wear tiny business casual attire. A flickering fluorescent light buzzes overhead. DILBERT stands before a whiteboard that reads "Q4 SURVIVAL GOALS." Scattered around him are his colleagues, seated on overturned filing cabinets and chunks of concrete. Outside, the sky is a permanent shade of burnt orange. Distant explosions punctuate the dialogue like a percussion section.*

---

**DILBERT:**
Good morning, everyone. Before we begin, I want to acknowledge that the eastern wall has been breached by a roving band of cannibals, but I've already submitted a facilities ticket, so that should be resolved within six to eight business weeks.

**WALLY:**
*(staring into an empty mug)*
Has anyone seen the coffee machine?

**ALICE:**
The coffee machine was destroyed in the meteor strike fourteen months ago, Wally.

**WALLY:**
*(long pause)*
So there's no coffee?

**ALICE:**
There is NO CIVILIZATION, Wally. There are WARLORDS and PLAGUE and the SKY IS ON FIRE.

**WALLY:**
*(nodding slowly)*
So... decaf situation.
