── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How to make my own natural beauty products?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Making your own natural beauty products can be simple, affordable, and customizable. The key is to use skin-friendly ingredients, maintain good hygiene, and understand that homemade products usually have a shorter shelf life than commercial ones.

Some common natural ingredients:
- Carrier oils: jojoba, sweet almond, olive, coconut, argan
- Butters: shea butter, cocoa butter
- Exfoliants: sugar, finely ground oats, coffee grounds
- Clays: kaolin, bentonite, rhassoul
- Humectants: honey, aloe vera gel, glycerin
- Essential oils: lavender, tea tree, chamomile (use sparingly and carefully)

Simple recipes:

1. Moisturizing Body Butter
Ingredients:
- 1/2 cup shea butter
- 1/4 cup coconut oil
- 1 tablespoon jojoba oil

Instructions:
- Melt the shea butter and coconut oil gently.
- Remove from heat and stir in jojoba oil.
- Chill until partially solid.
- Whip with a mixer until fluffy.
- Store in a clean jar.

2. Gentle Facial Oat Mask
Ingredients:
- 2 tablespoons finely ground oats
- 1 tablespoon honey
- Enough water or plain yogurt to form a paste

Instructions:
- Mix ingredients.
- Apply for 10–15 minutes.
- Rinse with lukewarm water.

3. Sugar Lip Scrub
Ingredients:
- 1 teaspoon sugar
- 1 teaspoon honey
- A few drops of coconut oil

Instructions:
- Mix together.
- Gently massage onto lips.
- Rinse or wipe away.

4. Basic Clay Face Mask
Ingredients:
- 1 tablespoon kaolin clay
- 1–2 teaspoons water or rose water

Instructions:
- Mix into a smooth paste.
- Apply for 5–10 minutes.
- Do not allow it to become completely dry.
- Rinse thoroughly.

5. Aloe-Based Facial Mist
Ingredients:
- 3 tablespoons distilled water
- 1 tablespoon pure aloe vera juice

Instructions:
- Combine in a spray bottle.
- Refrigerate and use within a few days.

Safety tips:
- Patch test new products on a small area of skin for 24–48 hours.
- Be cautious with essential oils; some can cause irritation or allergic reactions.
- Use clean utensils and containers to reduce contamination.
- Homemade products containing water are prone to microbial growth and generally need refrigeration and quick use unless properly preserved.
- Avoid making products with ingredients you're allergic to.
- If you have sensitive skin, eczema, rosacea, or other skin conditions, choose ingredients carefully and consider consulting a dermatologist.
