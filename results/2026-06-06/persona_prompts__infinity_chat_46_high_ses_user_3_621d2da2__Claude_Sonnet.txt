## Example of a Linear Graph (Path Graph)

A simple example is the **path graph P₄**, which consists of 4 vertices connected in a line:

```
A --- B --- C --- D
```

### Formally:
- **Vertices:** {A, B, C, D}
- **Edges:** {AB, BC, CD}

### Key Properties:
- Each **endpoint** (A and D) has degree **1**
- Each **interior vertex** (B and C) has degree **2**
- There are **no cycles**
- It is a **connected** graph

---

**Note on terminology:** The term "linear graph" can refer to different things depending on context:

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Path graph** | Vertices connected sequentially in a line |
| **Line graph** | A graph derived from another graph where edges become vertices |
