That's a genuinely interesting philosophical question, and your work situation actually connects to it in a real way.

## The short answer: Yes, it's impossible to know everything

Here's why this is pretty well-established:

**Practical limits**
- There's simply too much information - it grows faster than any person could absorb
- Time and attention are finite
- Your unpredictable schedule is actually a good real-world example of how circumstances limit what you can even *plan* to learn

**Deeper logical limits**
- Kurt Gödel demonstrated mathematically that even formal systems can't prove everything true within themselves
- Some things are genuinely uncertain or unknowable in principle
- Knowing everything would require knowing future events that haven't happened yet

**What's worth thinking about though**
- "Knowing everything" and "knowing enough" are very different goals
- Expertise in specific areas is genuinely achievable
- Your direct experience working a demanding, unpredictable job gives you real knowledge many people lack

## The honest nuance
Some philosophers debate *exactly* what counts as "knowing" something, which affects how you frame the question.

But the core point - that complete, total knowledge is impossible for any person - is pretty hard to seriously argue against.
