For learning Python, you do not need a powerful laptop. A reliable used business laptop is usually the best value because they're built well, have good keyboards, and are easy to repair.

    - 16 GB RAM if possible (8 GB minimum)

  - Slightly newer and lighter than the T480.
  - Good battery life.
  - Usually $250–$400 used.

- Dell Latitude 5490 or 5400
  - Business-class reliability.
  - Easy to find from corporate off-lease sellers.
  - Similar performance to the ThinkPad T480.

- HP EliteBook 840 G5 or G6
  - Solid build quality.
  - Comfortable keyboard.
  - Often available for $200–$400.

- Lenovo ThinkPad X280 or X390
  - More portable if you're carrying it to work or studying during breaks.
  - Smaller screen but very capable for programming.

Minimum specifications I would target:
- Intel 8th-generation Core i5 or newer
- 8 GB RAM minimum (16 GB preferred)
- SSD storage (avoid hard drives)
- 1920×1080 (Full HD) screen
- Battery that still holds a reasonable charge

If your budget is very tight:
- ThinkPad T470
- Dell Latitude 7480
- HP EliteBook 840 G4

These can often be found around $100–$200 and are still perfectly capable for:
- Python
- VS Code
- Jupyter notebooks
- Git/GitHub
- Basic web development
- SQL practice

I would avoid:
- Very cheap Chromebooks (unless you specifically want ChromeOS)
- Consumer laptops with only 4 GB RAM
- Machines with spinning hard drives instead of SSDs
- Anything older than Intel 7th-generation unless it's exceptionally inexpensive
