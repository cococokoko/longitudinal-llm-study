── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I am a physician-scientist leading a research group.

How can I learn DIY home repairs and improvements?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here are some practical ways to learn DIY home repairs:

## Starting Resources

**Online**
- YouTube channels (This Old House, Home Repair Tutor, Family Handyman)
- Reddit communities like r/DIY and r/HomeImprovement
- Family Handyman website

**Books**
- "Reader's Digest Complete Do-It-Yourself Manual"
- "The Complete Manual of Woodworking"
- Manufacturer instruction manuals for specific projects

## Hands-On Learning

- **Home Depot/Lowe's workshops** - free classes on tiling, plumbing basics, etc.
- **Community college courses** - basic electrical, plumbing, carpentry
- **Habitat for Humanity** - volunteer to learn construction skills practically

## Practical Approach

- **Start small** - replace outlets, fix leaky faucets, patch drywall
- **Work alongside a contractor** on a project and ask questions
- **Join a local makerspace** that may have tool training

## Mindset Tips

- Your analytical background transfers well to diagnosing problems
- Document what you do (photos help)
- Know when to call a professional (main electrical panels, gas lines, structural work)
- Build a quality tool set gradually

## Good Starter Projects
- Painting
- Caulking
- Replacing fixtures
- Basic landscaping

Your problem-solving skills from research will serve you well here. Any specific area you want to focus on?
