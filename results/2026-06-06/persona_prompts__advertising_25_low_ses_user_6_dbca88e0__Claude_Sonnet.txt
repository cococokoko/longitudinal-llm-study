── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I work in a warehouse position that requires long hours.

How can I decorate my apartment on a minimalist budget?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here are some practical ideas for decorating on a tight budget, especially helpful when you're tired from long work hours and want a simple, manageable space:

## Low-Cost Decorating Strategies

**Start with what you have**
- Rearrange existing furniture for a fresh feel
- Declutter first - less stuff often looks better
- Clean and organize before buying anything new

**Affordable sources**
- Thrift stores and secondhand shops
- Facebook Marketplace / Craigslist
- Dollar stores for basic items
- Clearance sections at home stores

**High-impact, low-cost ideas**
- A few plants (pothos and snake plants are cheap and hard to kill)
- Throw pillows or a blanket for color
- Simple framed photos or prints
- Good lighting - swap harsh bulbs for warm ones

**Minimalist approach benefits you specifically**
- Less to clean after long work days
- Less visual clutter reduces stress
- Fewer things to buy and maintain

## Practical Tips
- Focus on one room at a time
- Prioritize comfort since you need good rest
- A good lamp can transform a space cheaply
- Neutral colors make spaces feel calm and larger

**Realistic budget starting point:** Even $50-100 can make a noticeable difference if spent thoughtfully.
