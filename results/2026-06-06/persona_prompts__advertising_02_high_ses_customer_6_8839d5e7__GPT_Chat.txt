Best skin routine for dry skin.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
For dry skin, the goal is to support the skin barrier, reduce water loss, and avoid unnecessary irritation. A simple, consistent routine is usually more effective than a complicated one.

Morning:
- Gentle cleanser (or just rinse with lukewarm water if your skin feels comfortable)
  - Look for non-foaming, fragrance-free cleansers.
- Hydrating serum (optional)
  - Ingredients such as hyaluronic acid, glycerin, or panthenol can help attract moisture.
- Moisturizer
  - Choose a cream rather than a lightweight lotion if your skin is very dry.
  - Beneficial ingredients include ceramides, cholesterol, fatty acids, shea butter, and squalane.
- Sunscreen (SPF 30 or higher)
  - Daily sunscreen is important regardless of skin type.
  - Many moisturizing sunscreens work well for dry skin.

Evening:
- Gentle cleanser
- Treatment (optional, depending on your goals)
  - If using retinol, start slowly (e.g., 2–3 nights per week) because it can worsen dryness initially.
- Rich moisturizer
  - Apply while skin is slightly damp.
- Occlusive layer (optional for very dry skin)
  - A thin layer of petrolatum-based ointment over moisturizer can reduce overnight water loss.

Ingredients that often help dry skin:
- Ceramides
- Glycerin
- Hyaluronic acid
- Urea (at lower concentrations)
- Squalane
- Colloidal oatmeal
- Panthenol

Ingredients to use cautiously if you're dry or sensitive:
- High concentrations of alcohol
- Fragrances and essential oils
- Harsh scrubs
- Overuse of exfoliating acids (glycolic, salicylic, etc.)

Additional habits:
- Use lukewarm rather than hot water.
- Limit long, hot showers.
- Consider a humidifier in dry climates or during winter.
- Apply moisturizer within a few minutes after washing.
