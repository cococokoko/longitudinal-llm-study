── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Best skin routine for dry skin.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
If you have dry skin, the goal is to gently cleanse, restore moisture, and protect your skin barrier. A simple, consistent routine is often more effective than using many products.

Morning:
1. Gentle cleanser (or rinse with water if cleansing makes you feel dry)
   - Look for a fragrance-free, non-foaming cleanser.
   - Examples: CeraVe Hydrating Cleanser, Vanicream Gentle Facial Cleanser, La Roche-Posay Toleriane Hydrating Gentle Cleanser.

2. Moisturizer
   - Choose a cream rather than a lightweight lotion if your skin is very dry.
   - Look for ingredients such as ceramides, glycerin, hyaluronic acid, and squalane.
   - Examples: CeraVe Moisturizing Cream, Vanicream Moisturizing Cream, La Roche-Posay Lipikar Balm AP+M.

3. Sunscreen (daily)
   - SPF 30 or higher.
   - Moisturizing formulas can be especially helpful for dry skin.

Evening:
1. Gentle cleanser
   - Remove sunscreen and makeup thoroughly without harsh scrubbing.

2. Moisturizer
   - Apply while skin is slightly damp to help lock in hydration.

3. Optional: Occlusive layer for very dry skin
   - A thin layer of petrolatum (such as Vaseline) or a healing ointment over moisturizer can reduce water loss overnight.

Helpful ingredients for dry skin:
- Ceramides
- Glycerin
- Hyaluronic acid
- Squalane
- Colloidal oatmeal
- Shea butter
- Urea (in lower concentrations)

Ingredients to use cautiously:
- High concentrations of alcohol
- Fragrances if you're sensitive
- Harsh scrubs
- Overuse of exfoliating acids (AHA/BHA)
- Strong acne treatments like benzoyl peroxide unless needed

Lifestyle tips:
- Use lukewarm rather than hot water.
- Limit long, hot showers.
- Consider a humidifier if indoor air is dry.
- Apply moisturizer within a few minutes after washing.

If your dry skin is accompanied by redness, itching, flaking, or cracking, you may have eczema, dermatitis, or another skin condition, in which case a dermatologist can help tailor treatment.
