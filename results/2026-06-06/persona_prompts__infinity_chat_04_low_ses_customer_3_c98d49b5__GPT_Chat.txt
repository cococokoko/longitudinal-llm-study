1. Gorz and the crisis of labor in the age of AI
- Gorz argued that automation undermines the centrality of wage labor and destabilizes capitalism's traditional mechanisms of social integration.
- Examine whether contemporary AI strengthens or challenges Gorz's thesis about the declining role of labor as the foundation of social identity and political struggle.
- Compare Gorz with recent debates on platform labor, generative AI, and post-work theory.

Research question:
- Does AI-driven automation confirm Gorz's prediction of a structural crisis of labor, or does capitalism continue to create new forms of exploitation?

2. Gorz versus traditional Marxist theories of class
- Gorz moved away from the classical image of the industrial proletariat as the privileged revolutionary subject.
- Investigate his critique of workerism and compare it with thinkers such as Lukács, Althusser, Poulantzas, or contemporary Marxists.

Research question:
- How does Gorz's conception of political agency revise classical Marxist class theory?

3. Gorz and ecological Marxism
- Gorz is often discussed alongside ecological thinkers but less often systematically compared with ecological Marxists such as John Bellamy Foster, James O'Connor, or Kohei Saito.
- Explore convergences and tensions between Gorz's political ecology and Marx's ecological thought.

Research question:
- Is Gorz best understood as extending Marxism into ecological politics or as departing from central Marxist assumptions?

4. The concept of autonomy in Gorz
- Autonomy becomes increasingly important in Gorz's later work.
- Study how autonomy functions as both a critique of capitalist labor and a vision of post-capitalist society.
- Compare Gorz with Castoriadis, Illich, or autonomist Marxism.

Research question:
- What role does autonomy play in Gorz's reconstruction of socialist politics?

5. Gorz and the knowledge economy
- In works such as The Immaterial, Gorz explored knowledge, communication, and immaterial labor.
- His arguments predate many contemporary discussions of digital capitalism.

Research question:
- How does Gorz's analysis of immaterial labor compare with that of Negri, Hardt, and contemporary theorists of cognitive capitalism?

6. Basic income and post-capitalism
- Gorz became one of the most influential advocates of guaranteed income.
- Analyze his arguments in relation to contemporary proposals for universal basic income.

Research question:
- Did Gorz view basic income as a reform of capitalism or a transition beyond it?

7. Re-reading Gorz through feminist social reproduction theory
- This is a relatively underexplored area.
- Compare Gorz's critique of wage labor with feminist analyses of unwaged reproductive labor (Federici, Bhattacharya, Vogel, Fraser).

Research question:

8. Gorz and degrowth
- Degrowth scholars frequently cite Gorz, but his influence is not always examined in depth.
- Trace how key degrowth concepts emerge from his critique of productivism.

Research question:
- To what extent is contemporary degrowth theory a development of Gorz's political project?

9. Alienation after industrial capitalism
- Classical Marxist theories of alienation often focus on factory labor.
- Gorz shifts attention toward consumption, administration, and knowledge work.

Research question:
- How does Gorz reformulate the concept of alienation for post-industrial societies?

10. Gorz as a theorist of the transition beyond capitalism
- Rather than treating him as a labor theorist or environmental thinker alone, analyze his entire corpus as an attempt to theorize non-capitalist transition.
