── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I work in a warehouse position that requires long hours.

How can I learn to cook healthy meals quickly?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Great goal! Here are some practical approaches for someone with long, tiring work days:

## Planning Strategies
- **Meal prep on days off** - cook large batches of grains, proteins, and vegetables
- **Plan a weekly menu** so you're not deciding when exhausted
- **Keep a simple rotation** of 5-7 reliable recipes

## Quick Cooking Methods
- **Sheet pan meals** - toss everything together, roast 25-30 minutes
- **Slow cooker/Instant Pot** - start it before work, ready when you get home
- **Stir fries** - fast, flexible, and nutritious
- **Eggs** - incredibly quick protein source for any meal

## Smart Shortcuts
- Buy pre-cut vegetables when budget allows
- Canned beans and fish are nutritious and require no prep
- Frozen vegetables are just as nutritious as fresh
- Rotisserie chicken can be used multiple ways

## Learning Resources
- YouTube channels like **Joshua Weissman** or **Ethan Chlebowski** focus on practical cooking
- Start with **5-ingredient recipes** to build confidence
- Apps like **Mealime** suggest recipes based on time available

## Realistic Tips
- **Don't aim for perfection** - simple healthy meals beat elaborate ones you won't make
- Prep ingredients the night before
- Keep healthy snacks ready for when you're too tired to cook
