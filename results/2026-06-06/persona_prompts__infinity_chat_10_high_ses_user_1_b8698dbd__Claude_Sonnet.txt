── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This is a genuinely interesting empirical question. Here's an honest assessment of what the research suggests:

## What Research Actually Shows

**Some positive correlations found:**
- Higher cognitive ability correlates with broader moral circle thinking
- Intelligence associates with reduced prejudice in some studies
- Cognitive empathy (perspective-taking) shows modest positive correlation with IQ

**Complicating factors:**
- Emotional/affective compassion and cognitive intelligence appear **largely independent**
- High intelligence can actually enable **more sophisticated rationalization** of selfish behavior
- Some research suggests intelligent people are better at *appearing* compassionate strategically

## Honest Limitations

- Most studies use narrow intelligence measures
- Compassion itself is difficult to operationalize consistently
- Publication bias affects this literature significantly
- Correlations found are generally **weak**

## What's Reasonably Well-Supported

- The relationship is **not strong** in either direction
- Intelligence and compassion are **largely separable traits**
- You likely observe this clinically - technically brilliant surgeons aren't systematically more compassionate toward patients

## Worth Noting

Your professional context is relevant - you probably have direct observational data suggesting these traits vary **independently** among highly intelligent colleagues, which aligns with the research.
