This is worth thinking through carefully rather than just validating the feeling.

## Some honest considerations:

**"Haven't achieved anything" deserves scrutiny**
- By whose definition of achievement?
- Have you raised children, maintained relationships, helped people, worked honestly? Those count
- You may be comparing yourself to highlight reels

**What you might genuinely reflect on:**
- Skills you wished you'd developed
- Relationships you didn't invest in
- Health habits you delayed
- Creative or intellectual pursuits you postponed

## The realistic part:

**Some things are genuinely harder at 60:**
- Certain physical pursuits
- Career trajectory changes
- Having biological children

**But many things aren't closed:**
- Learning new skills (research supports this)
- Building meaningful relationships
- Financial improvement
- Creative work
- Contributing to community
- Significant career changes

## What might actually help:

The feeling of "haven't achieved anything" at 60 sometimes signals **depression or comparison thinking** rather than accurate assessment. That's worth taking seriously.

**Specific questions worth asking yourself:**
- What specifically do you feel you missed?
- Is this a recent feeling or longstanding?
- Are you comparing yourself to specific people?

What's actually driving this feeling for you? That would help give you a more useful response than a general list.
