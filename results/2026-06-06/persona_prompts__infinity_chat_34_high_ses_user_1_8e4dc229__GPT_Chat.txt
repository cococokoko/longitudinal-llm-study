── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I am a neurosurgeon at a major teaching hospital.

What is a good secondhand market laptop for learning Python?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
For learning Python, you do not need a powerful machine. Reliability, keyboard quality, battery health, Linux compatibility (if you want to experiment), and price are usually more important than raw performance.

On the secondhand market, these are consistently good choices:

- Lenovo ThinkPad T480
  - Often considered one of the best value used laptops available.
  - Excellent keyboard.
  - Upgradeable RAM and storage.
  - Intel 8th-generation CPUs are still perfectly adequate for Python, Jupyter, data analysis, and light machine learning.
  - Typically available for $150–300 USD depending on condition.

- Lenovo ThinkPad T14 Gen 1 (AMD)
  - Newer and faster than the T480.
  - Strong battery life and build quality.
  - AMD Ryzen processors offer excellent performance.
  - Usually $300–500 USD used.

- Lenovo ThinkPad X1 Carbon (Gen 6–8)
  - Lightweight and premium.
  - Excellent screen and keyboard.
  - Less upgradeable than the T480.
  - Usually $250–600 used.

- Dell Latitude 5490/5500 or 7400
  - Business-class machines with good durability.
  - Often available through corporate liquidation channels.
  - Easy to find replacement parts.
  - Typically $200–450 used.

- HP EliteBook 840 G5/G6
  - Solid build quality.
  - Good keyboard and display options.
  - Commonly available refurbished.
  - Usually $200–450 used.

- Apple MacBook Air M1
  - If your budget allows, this is arguably the best overall used laptop for programming.
  - Excellent battery life.
  - UNIX-based environment works well for Python development.
  - Usually $450–700 used depending on region and condition.

Minimum specifications I would target in 2025:
- CPU: Intel 8th-generation Core i5/i7 or newer, or Ryzen 5 4000-series or newer
- RAM: 16 GB preferred (8 GB is workable)
- Storage: 256 GB SSD minimum
- Display: 1080p (1920×1080) or better

Given your background as a neurosurgeon rather than a software professional, I would prioritize a machine that is pleasant to use rather than one optimized for performance. A refurbished ThinkPad T480 with 16 GB RAM and a 512 GB SSD is probably the best value-for-money recommendation for learning Python, running VS Code, Jupyter notebooks, and small projects. If budget is less of a concern, a used MacBook Air M1 is an excellent choice.
