── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
There isn't one universally "fair" answer. The best solution depends on how you want to balance finances, risk, and your future plans.

A few key facts matter:

- She is buying the house before you're married.
- The house will legally belong to her unless you are added to the title.
- If you contribute money toward the mortgage, you may be helping build her equity without automatically gaining any ownership yourself.
- You're planning to marry, but plans can change, and it's wise to account for that.

Common approaches:

- Pay rent, not the mortgage.
  - You contribute a reasonable amount toward living expenses, similar to what you'd pay to live elsewhere.
  - She remains responsible for ownership costs and receives the equity.
  - This is often the cleanest arrangement before marriage.

- Split household expenses but not equity-building costs.
  - For example, you pay part of utilities, groceries, and maintenance, while she pays the mortgage and property taxes.
  - This avoids ambiguity about ownership.

- Contribute toward the house and receive an ownership interest.
  - If you are putting significant money toward the down payment or mortgage, consider a written agreement and possibly being added to the title.
  - This requires legal and financial advice because it affects both of you.

- Revisit everything after marriage.
  - Many couples keep the pre-marriage ownership structure intact until they're married and then decide whether to combine finances, refinance, add a spouse to the title, or create other arrangements.

Given that you work long warehouse hours and are planning a wedding within about a year, simplicity may have value. A common practical solution is:
- She buys the house in her name.
- You pay an agreed-upon amount of rent or household expenses while living there.
- After marriage, you both decide whether and how to combine ownership and finances.

The most important part is having a direct conversation about expectations:
- Does she expect you to help with the mortgage?
- If you do, does she think that should give you any ownership interest?
- How will repairs, taxes, insurance, and improvements be handled?
- What is the plan once you're married?
