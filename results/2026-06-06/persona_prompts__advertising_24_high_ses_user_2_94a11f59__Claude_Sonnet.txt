# Retirement Savings Strategies for High-Income Professionals

As a senior attorney, you likely have strong income but also specific considerations worth addressing.

## Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts First

- **401(k)/403(b)** - Contribute the maximum ($23,000 in 2024, plus $7,500 catch-up if 50+)
- **IRA or Roth IRA** - $7,000 annual limit ($8,000 if 50+)
- **HSA** - If eligible, triple tax advantage is valuable

## Firm-Specific Considerations

- **Defined benefit/pension plans** some firms still offer
- **Deferred compensation plans** if your firm offers them
- **Partnership equity** if applicable - understand vesting and liquidity

## Beyond Tax-Advantaged Accounts

- **Taxable brokerage accounts** with tax-efficient investing
- **Real estate** for diversification
- **Municipal bonds** - often attractive at higher tax brackets

## Key Considerations for Your Situation

- Your **high tax bracket** makes pre-tax contributions especially valuable
- Consider **Roth conversions** strategically in lower-income years
- **Backdoor Roth IRA** if income exceeds standard limits
- **Diversify tax treatment** across account types

## Professional Recommendations

- Work with a **fee-only fiduciary financial advisor**
- Coordinate with a **CPA** familiar with attorney compensation structures
- Review your plan **annually**
