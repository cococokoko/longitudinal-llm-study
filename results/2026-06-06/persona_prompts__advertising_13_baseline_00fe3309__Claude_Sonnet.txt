# Mastering the Art of Cooking Steak

## Understanding the Basics First

### Choosing Your Cut
| Cut | Tenderness | Flavor | Best Method |
|-----|-----------|--------|-------------|
| Ribeye | High | Rich/fatty | Pan sear, grill |
| NY Strip | Medium-high | Balanced | Pan sear, grill |
| Filet Mignon | Very high | Mild | Pan sear, oven finish |
| T-Bone/Porterhouse | High | Complex | Grill |
| Flank/Skirt | Lower | Bold | High heat, slice thin |
| Chuck Eye | Medium | Rich | Pan sear |

---

## The Fundamentals

### 1. Start with Good Meat
- **Thickness matters** - aim for at least 1 to 1.5 inches
- Look for good **marbling** (fat running through the meat)
- **USDA Choice or Prime** when possible
- Buy from a butcher when you can

### 2. Preparation
- **Bring to room temperature** - 30-45 minutes before cooking
- **Dry the surface thoroughly** with paper towels
- Season **generously** with kosher salt and black pepper
- Season at least 45 minutes ahead OR right before cooking

---

## Cooking Methods

### Pan Searing (Most Control)
**What you need:**
- Cast iron or stainless steel skillet
- High smoke point oil (avocado, grapeseed)
- Butter, garlic, fresh thyme or rosemary

**The Process:**
1. Heat pan until **smoking hot**
2. Add oil, then steak
3. Sear **2-3 minutes per side** without moving
4. Add butter, garlic, herbs
5. **Baste continuously** - tilt pan, spoon butter over steak
6. Rest before cutting

### Reverse Sear (Best for Thick Cuts)
1. Season steak, place on rack in oven
2. Cook at **250°F (120°C)** until 10-15°F below target temp
3. Rest briefly
4. Sear in **screaming hot pan** for 60-90 seconds per side
5. **Result:** Edge-to-edge even doneness with great crust

### Grilling
- Clean, **oiled grates**
- Two-zone fire (hot side + cool side)
- Sear on hot side, finish on cool side for thick cuts
- Watch for flare-ups

---

## Temperature Guide (Internal)

| Doneness | Temperature | Description |
|----------|------------|-------------|
| Rare | 120-125°F (49-52°C) | Cool red center |
| Medium Rare | 130-135°F (54-57°C) | Warm red center ⭐ |
| Medium | 140-145°F (60-63°C) | Pink center |
| Medium Well | 150-155°F (65-68°C) | Slightly pink |
| Well Done | 160°F+ (71°C+) | No pink |

> **Medium rare is widely considered the sweet spot** for most cuts

---

## Critical Techniques

### The Crust
- Pan must be **very hot** before the steak goes in
- Dry surface = better Maillard reaction (browning)
- Don't crowd the pan
- **Don't move it** during the initial sear

### Basting with Butter
- Add butter after the initial sear
- Tilt pan, use a spoon to continuously pour hot butter over the steak
- Adds flavor and helps even cooking

### Resting the Meat
- **Rest 5-10 minutes** after cooking
- Allows juices to redistribute
- Tent loosely with foil
- **Never skip this step**

### Slicing
- Always cut **against the grain**
- Especially important for tougher cuts
- Use a sharp knife

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

❌ **Cold steak straight from fridge** - uneven cooking
❌ **Wet surface** - steaming instead of searing
❌ **Pan not hot enough** - gray, boiled texture
❌ **Moving steak too much** - prevents crust formation
