Stress affects both the body and the mind. When a person experiences stress, the body releases hormones such as adrenaline and cortisol. These hormones prepare the body to respond to challenges by increasing heart rate, blood pressure, and energy levels. In the short term, this response can be helpful. However, when stress continues for a long time, it can contribute to headaches, muscle tension, sleep problems, digestive issues, and a weakened immune system.

Stress also has significant effects on mental well-being. It can make it harder to concentrate, remember information, and make decisions. People under stress may feel anxious, irritable, overwhelmed, or emotionally exhausted. Long-term stress can increase the risk of mental health conditions such as anxiety disorders and depression.

For people with demanding responsibilities or unpredictable work schedules, stress may be especially challenging because it can disrupt routines, rest, and recovery time. Over time, this can create a cycle in which stress affects sleep and energy levels, making daily tasks feel more difficult.

Managing stress through healthy habits such as regular exercise, adequate sleep, relaxation techniques, social support, and time management can help reduce its effects and improve both physical and mental health.
