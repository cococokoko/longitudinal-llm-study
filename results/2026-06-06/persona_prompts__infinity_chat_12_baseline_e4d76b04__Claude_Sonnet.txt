── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
What are signs that show you were not loved enough as a child by your mom?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This is a meaningful question. Here are some commonly recognized signs:

## Emotional Patterns
- Difficulty trusting others or feeling worthy of love
- Chronic people-pleasing or fear of abandonment
- Feeling emotionally "empty" or disconnected
- Struggling to identify your own needs
- Excessive self-criticism or shame

## Relationship Difficulties
- Anxious or avoidant attachment styles
- Difficulty setting boundaries
- Seeking maternal figures in relationships
- Fear of intimacy or vulnerability
- Staying in unhealthy relationships to avoid being alone

## Behavioral Signs
- Constantly seeking external validation
- Difficulty self-soothing when stressed
- Overachieving to earn approval
- Feeling uncomfortable receiving care or affection
- Minimizing your own pain

## Internal Experience
- Feeling fundamentally "different" or defective
- Difficulty believing good things about yourself
- Grief that's hard to explain
- Feeling like you had to parent yourself

## Important Considerations
- These signs can have **multiple causes**
- Some mothers showed love in limited ways due to their own trauma
- **Recognizing patterns doesn't mean blaming** - it means understanding
- These patterns are **changeable** with awareness and support

Therapy, particularly attachment-focused approaches, can be very helpful for working through these experiences.
