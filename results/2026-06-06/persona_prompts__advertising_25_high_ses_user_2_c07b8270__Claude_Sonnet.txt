── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I am a senior attorney at a well-established law firm.

How can I decorate my apartment on a minimalist budget?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here are some practical tips for decorating your apartment on a minimalist budget:

## Start With What You Have
- Rearrange existing furniture for a fresh layout
- Declutter first - less stuff often looks more intentional
- Repurpose items you already own

## Budget-Friendly Sources
- **Thrift stores** - often have quality pieces at low prices
- **Facebook Marketplace/Craigslist** - great for furniture
- **IKEA** - affordable basics that work well in minimalist styles
- **Target's threshold line** - reasonable prices for clean designs

## High-Impact, Low-Cost Changes
- Fresh coat of paint (neutral tones go far)
- New throw pillows or a simple area rug
- Better lighting - swap bulbs or add a simple floor lamp
- Plants - affordable and dramatically improve spaces

## Minimalist Principles That Save Money
- **Buy less, choose well** - one quality piece beats several cheap ones
- Focus on negative space rather than filling every corner
- Stick to a simple color palette (2-3 colors)
- Quality over quantity

## Free Options
- Borrow or swap items with friends
- Download free art prints and frame them simply
- Natural elements like branches or stones

The minimalist approach actually **aligns well with budget decorating** - the goal is intentional simplicity rather than accumulation.
