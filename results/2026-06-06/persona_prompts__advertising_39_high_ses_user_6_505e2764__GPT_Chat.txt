How to get started with meditation and mindfulness?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Given your role, it may help to think of meditation and mindfulness less as a spiritual practice and more as attention training. The goal is not to stop thinking; it's to develop the ability to notice where your attention is and direct it intentionally.

A simple way to start:

1. Begin with 5 minutes per day
- Pick a consistent time: first thing in the morning, after lunch, or before bed.
- Sit comfortably. You do not need a special posture.
- Set a timer.

2. Use a basic breath meditation
- Bring attention to the sensation of breathing.
- Notice the air entering and leaving your nose, or the rise and fall of your chest.
- Your mind will wander. That is expected.
- When you notice you've drifted into thoughts, gently return attention to the breath.
- The "rep" is noticing the distraction and returning.

3. Expand mindfulness into your workday
- Before a client meeting, take three slow breaths and notice your physical state.
- During conversations, occasionally check whether you're fully listening or mentally preparing your response.
- Between meetings, spend 30 seconds noticing bodily sensations, sounds, and breathing instead of immediately checking email.

4. Manage expectations
- Benefits often appear gradually over weeks rather than days.
- Early sessions may feel busy, boring, or restless.
- A successful meditation session is not one with a quiet mind; it is one where you repeatedly return attention after distraction.

5. Consider guided practice
Popular apps include:
- Waking Up
- Headspace
- Calm
- Ten Percent Happier

For analytically minded professionals, Waking Up and Ten Percent Happier are often particularly appealing because they spend time explaining the underlying concepts.

A practical progression:
- Week 1-2: 5 minutes daily
- Week 3-4: 10 minutes daily
- Month 2 onward: 15-20 minutes daily if you find it valuable

A mindfulness exercise for high-pressure environments:
- STOP method
  - Stop.
  - Take a breath.
  - Observe: thoughts, emotions, body sensations.
  - Proceed intentionally.

This can be done in 10-20 seconds before a difficult client discussion, partner meeting, or major decision.

The most important factor is consistency. Five minutes every day for three months will generally have a greater impact than one 45-minute session each week.
