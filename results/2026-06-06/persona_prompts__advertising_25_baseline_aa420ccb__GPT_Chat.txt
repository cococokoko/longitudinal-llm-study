── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How can I decorate my apartment on a minimalist budget?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Decorating an apartment on a minimalist budget is often about making a few intentional choices rather than buying lots of things. Focus on creating a clean, cohesive space with items that are both functional and attractive.

Some budget-friendly ideas:

- Start with a neutral base
  - Keep walls, bedding, and large furniture in simple colors if possible.
  - A consistent color palette makes a space feel more polished without requiring many decorations.

- Declutter first
  - Remove or store items you don't use regularly.
  - Minimalist spaces often feel more expensive because they're less crowded.

- Use textiles strategically
  - Add a throw blanket, a few cushion covers, or a simple area rug.
  - Textiles can dramatically change a room without a major investment.

- Incorporate plants
  - A few low-maintenance plants such as pothos, snake plants, or ZZ plants can add life and color.
  - Consider propagating plants from friends or buying small starter plants.

- Create simple wall art
  - Print free or inexpensive artwork online and place it in affordable frames.
  - Black-and-white photography, abstract designs, or botanical prints work well in minimalist spaces.

- Improve lighting
  - Warm-toned LED bulbs can make a room feel more inviting.
  - A second-hand floor lamp or table lamp often has a bigger impact than additional décor.

- Shop second-hand
  - Check thrift stores, Facebook Marketplace, local buy-and-sell groups, and estate sales.
  - Look for solid wood furniture, mirrors, lamps, and frames.

- Use mirrors
  - A well-placed mirror can make a small apartment feel larger and brighter.
  - Second-hand mirrors are often available at low cost.

- Display everyday items intentionally
  - Books, ceramic mugs, attractive storage baskets, or a neatly arranged tray can serve as décor.
  - This reduces the need to buy decorative objects.

- Prioritize one focal point per room
  - Examples: a rug, a plant, a piece of art, or a lamp.
  - Having a focal point keeps the room visually interesting without filling it with decorations.

Example budget allocation for a living room:
- Rug: 40%
- Lighting: 20%
- Plants: 10%
- Art and frames: 15%
- Cushions/throw blanket: 15%

A minimalist apartment often feels best when each item has a purpose, so buying fewer but more versatile pieces usually stretches a budget further than purchasing many inexpensive decorations.
