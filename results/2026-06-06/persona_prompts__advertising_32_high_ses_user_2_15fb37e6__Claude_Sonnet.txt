── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
# Improving Indoor Air Quality

Here are the most effective approaches:

## Ventilation
- Open windows regularly when outdoor air quality permits
- Use exhaust fans in kitchens and bathrooms
- Ensure HVAC systems are properly maintained

## Filtration
- Use HEPA air purifiers in frequently occupied rooms
- Replace HVAC filters regularly (typically every 1-3 months)
- Consider higher MERV-rated filters for your system

## Source Control
- **Reduce pollutants at the source** - often the most effective strategy
- Choose low-VOC paints, furniture, and cleaning products
- Avoid smoking indoors
- Store chemicals properly

## Humidity Management
- Maintain humidity between **30-50%**
- Use dehumidifiers to prevent mold growth
- Fix water leaks promptly

## Common Pollutant Reduction
- Test for radon, especially in lower levels
- Have gas appliances inspected regularly
- Use carbon monoxide detectors
- Minimize synthetic fragrances and aerosols

## Plants
- Some houseplants may offer modest air quality benefits, though research on their effectiveness is mixed

## Professional Assessment
- Consider an indoor air quality audit if you have specific concerns
- Particularly worthwhile for older buildings
