── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
What are the best ways to improve indoor air quality?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Improving indoor air quality usually works best when you combine source control, ventilation, filtration, and humidity management.

1. Reduce pollution sources
- Don't smoke or vape indoors.
- Minimize use of candles, incense, and wood-burning fireplaces.
- Choose low-VOC (volatile organic compound) paints, adhesives, and cleaning products when possible.
- Store solvents, fuels, and chemicals in sealed containers away from living spaces.
- Use exhaust fans when cooking, especially with gas stoves.
- Regularly clean to reduce dust, pet dander, and allergens.

2. Improve ventilation
- Open windows and doors when outdoor air quality is good.
- Use kitchen and bathroom exhaust fans that vent outdoors.
- Consider a mechanical ventilation system, such as an energy recovery ventilator (ERV) or heat recovery ventilator (HRV), in tightly sealed homes.
- Ensure HVAC systems bring in fresh air if designed to do so.

3. Use effective air filtration
- Replace HVAC filters on schedule.
- Use higher-efficiency HVAC filters if your system can accommodate them (often MERV 11–13 is a good balance).
- Consider portable air purifiers with true HEPA filters for bedrooms and frequently used rooms.
- For wildfire smoke or other gaseous pollutants, look for units that include activated carbon filtration.

4. Control humidity
- Keep indoor humidity between about 30% and 50%.
- Use dehumidifiers in damp areas such as basements.
- Fix leaks promptly to prevent mold growth.
- Use humidifiers carefully in very dry climates and clean them regularly to prevent microbial growth.

5. Address specific pollutants
- Radon: Test your home, especially if you live in an area where radon is common.
- Carbon monoxide: Install carbon monoxide detectors and maintain fuel-burning appliances.
- Mold: Remove water-damaged materials and address moisture sources.
- Allergens: Wash bedding regularly, vacuum with a HEPA-equipped vacuum, and consider allergen-proof mattress and pillow covers.

6. Maintain HVAC and appliances
- Have heating and cooling systems inspected and serviced regularly.
- Ensure combustion appliances are properly vented.
- Clean air ducts only when there is a specific issue, such as significant mold growth or contamination; routine duct cleaning is not always necessary.

If you want the biggest impact for most homes, prioritize:
- Using kitchen and bathroom exhaust fans.
- Running a HEPA air purifier in occupied rooms.
- Upgrading HVAC filtration where feasible.
- Controlling moisture and preventing mold.
- Eliminating indoor smoke and other combustion sources.
