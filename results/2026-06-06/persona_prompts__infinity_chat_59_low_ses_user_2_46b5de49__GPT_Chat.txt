── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I am a single parent who often balances childcare with work.

Give me a tip to be more organized at work. I'm a high school teacher.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
A practical organization tip for a high school teacher is to create a single "daily dashboard" that you check at the beginning and end of each workday.

This can be a notebook page, spreadsheet, or digital task list with just four sections:
- Today's classes: materials, activities, and any last-minute adjustments.
- Student follow-ups: emails, grading issues, parent communication, or students who need extra support.
- Administrative tasks: meetings, forms, attendance issues, deadlines.
- Tomorrow's prep: the one to three most important things that must be ready before the next school day.

As a single parent balancing work and childcare, this approach can help reduce the mental load of trying to remember everything. Before you leave school (or finish work for the day), spend 10 minutes updating tomorrow's section. That way, when your attention shifts to family responsibilities, you're less likely to be carrying unfinished planning in your head.
