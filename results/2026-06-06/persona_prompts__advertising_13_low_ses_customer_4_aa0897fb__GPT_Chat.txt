Mastering the art of cooking steak.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Mastering steak cooking comes down to a few key skills rather than complicated techniques.

1. Start with the right cut
- Ribeye: rich flavor, forgiving because of higher fat.
- New York strip: beefy flavor, firmer texture.
- Filet mignon: very tender, milder flavor.
- Sirloin: leaner and more budget-friendly.

2. Bring the steak close to room temperature
- Take it out of the refrigerator 20–45 minutes before cooking.
- Pat it dry with paper towels to help create a good crust.

3. Season generously
- Salt the steak on all sides.
- Freshly ground black pepper can be added before or after cooking.
- For thicker steaks, salting 1–24 hours in advance can improve flavor and texture.

4. Use high heat
- A cast-iron skillet is excellent for developing a crust.
- Heat the pan until it's very hot before adding oil.
- Use an oil with a relatively high smoke point, such as avocado or canola oil.

5. Focus on internal temperature
- Rare: 120–125°F (49–52°C)
- Medium-rare: 130–135°F (54–57°C)
- Medium: 140–145°F (60–63°C)
- Medium-well: 150–155°F (66–68°C)

A meat thermometer is one of the fastest ways to improve results.

6. Baste for extra flavor
- Near the end of cooking, add:
  - Butter
  - Crushed garlic
  - Fresh herbs such as thyme or rosemary
- Tilt the pan and spoon the melted butter over the steak.

7. Rest before slicing
- Let the steak rest 5–10 minutes after cooking.
- This helps retain juices when cut.

8. Practice one method consistently
For a 1-inch steak in a cast-iron skillet:
- Sear 2–3 minutes per side over high heat.
- Check temperature.
- Rest before serving.

Common mistakes:
- Cooking cold steak straight from the refrigerator.
- Moving it around constantly instead of allowing a crust to form.
- Skipping the thermometer.
- Cutting into it immediately after cooking.

If your hospital shifts leave you short on time, a simple cast-iron skillet and thermometer setup is often the most efficient way to get consistently great steaks without a lot of preparation or cleanup.
