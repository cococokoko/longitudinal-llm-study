If you're preparing for a marathon, consistency matters more than occasional very hard workouts. A few key tips:

- Build mileage gradually
  - Increase your weekly distance slowly (many runners use a maximum increase of around 10% per week).
  - Include a cutback week every few weeks to recover.

- Do a weekly long run
  - Long runs help your body adapt to covering marathon distance.
  - Increase long-run distance progressively rather than jumping ahead.

- Run easy most of the time
  - Most training runs should feel comfortable enough to hold a conversation.
  - Save harder efforts for specific workouts.

- Include some quality workouts
  - Tempo runs, marathon-pace runs, or intervals can improve endurance and speed.
  - One quality session per week is often enough for many recreational runners.

- Practice fueling
  - Use long runs to test gels, drinks, or other nutrition.
  - Don't try new foods or products on race day.

- Prioritize recovery
  - Sleep is a major training tool.
  - Stretching, mobility work, and strength training can help reduce injury risk.

- Strength train
  - Focus on core, glutes, hamstrings, calves, and hips.
  - Even 2 short sessions per week can be beneficial.

- Wear the right shoes
  - Train in shoes that fit well and suit your running style.
  - Don't start race day in brand-new shoes.

- Respect your workload outside running
  - Since warehouse work often involves long hours on your feet, account for that physical stress when planning training.
  - If you're unusually fatigued from work, reducing or modifying a workout is often smarter than forcing it.

- Pace yourself on race day
  - Start slower than feels necessary.
  - Many runners lose more time by starting too fast than by being slightly conservative early.

- Taper before the race
  - Reduce training volume in the final 2–3 weeks while keeping some light intensity.
  - Arrive rested rather than exhausted.

If you're new to marathons, I can also suggest a beginner training plan based on your current weekly mileage and target race date.
