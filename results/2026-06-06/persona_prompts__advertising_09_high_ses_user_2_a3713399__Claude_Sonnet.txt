# Building a Personal Brand on Social Media as a Senior Attorney

## Why It Matters
- Attract high-value clients directly
- Establish thought leadership in your practice area
- Differentiate yourself from competitors
- Build referral networks beyond traditional channels

---

## Platform Strategy

### **LinkedIn** *(Priority #1)*
- Most professional audience
- Ideal for B2B and corporate clients
- Long-form articles perform well
- Engage with other attorneys and business leaders

### **X/Twitter**
- Good for legal commentary on current events
- Builds media relationships
- Faster-moving, shorter content

### **YouTube/Podcast**
- Excellent for complex legal topics
- Demonstrates deep expertise
- Longer shelf life for content

---

## Content That Works Well

| Content Type | Example |
|---|---|
| Legal commentary | Breaking down new legislation |
| Case studies | Anonymized client wins |
| Industry insights | Trends affecting your clients |
| Behind the scenes | How legal strategy works |
| Myth-busting | Common legal misconceptions |

---

## Critical Considerations

**⚠️ Ethics & Compliance**
- Know your state bar advertising rules
- Avoid specific legal advice
- Include appropriate disclaimers
- Be careful with confidentiality

---

## Practical Starting Points
- Post **2-3 times per week** consistently
- Engage genuinely with comments
- Define **one specific niche** rather than being general
- Repurpose content across platforms
