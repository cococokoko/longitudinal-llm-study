As a senior attorney at an established law firm, a strong personal brand on social media can help you attract clients, strengthen referral relationships, increase speaking opportunities, support business development, and establish yourself as a thought leader in your practice area.

A few principles tend to work particularly well for experienced attorneys:

- Focus on expertise rather than self-promotion.
- Provide practical insights that demonstrate judgment and experience.
- Stay consistent with your firm's policies, ethics rules, and client confidentiality obligations.
- Build credibility through education, not marketing slogans.

A useful framework is to define:

- Your audience:
  - Potential clients?
  - General counsel?
  - Business owners?
  - Executives?
  - Other lawyers who may refer work?

- Your niche:
  - Employment law
  - Corporate transactions
  - Litigation
  - Intellectual property
  - Regulatory compliance
  - Estate planning
  - Another specialty

- Your content pillars:
  - Legal developments and analysis
  - Risk management tips
  - Industry trends
  - Case studies (appropriately anonymized)
  - Lessons from years of practice
  - Commentary on major news affecting clients

LinkedIn is usually the highest-value platform for senior attorneys because the audience is professional and decision-oriented.

Examples of effective posts:

- "Three issues executives should consider before signing a non-compete agreement."
- "What a recent appellate decision means for employers."
- "Five contract provisions that often create disputes years later."
- "A common misconception about fiduciary duties."

You can also build authority through:

- Short video commentary (1–3 minutes)
- Articles and newsletters
- Podcast appearances
- Speaking engagements shared on social media
- Commentary on legal and business news

A simple content schedule might be:

- Monday: Legal update
- Wednesday: Practical business risk insight
- Friday: Professional observation or industry trend

One common mistake senior professionals make is trying to sound like influencers. Your advantage is experience, judgment, and credibility. Content that demonstrates how you think through complex issues often performs better than content designed purely for engagement.
