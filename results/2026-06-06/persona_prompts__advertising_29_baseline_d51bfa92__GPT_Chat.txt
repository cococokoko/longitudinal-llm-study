- Provide proper nutrition

  - Follow feeding recommendations and avoid overfeeding.
  - Ensure fresh, clean water is always available.

- Schedule regular veterinary care
  - Arrange routine wellness exams.
  - Keep vaccinations and parasite prevention (such as flea, tick, and heartworm protection) up to date as recommended.
  - Discuss any changes in behavior, appetite, or activity with a veterinarian.

  - Dogs generally benefit from daily walks and play.
  - Cats often enjoy interactive toys, climbing structures, and opportunities to explore safely.
  - Small mammals, birds, and reptiles also need species-appropriate enrichment and movement opportunities.

- Provide mental stimulation
  - Use toys, puzzles, training exercises, and enrichment activities.
  - Rotate toys periodically to maintain interest.
  - Offer opportunities for natural behaviors, such as scratching for cats or foraging activities for some birds and small animals.

- Maintain a safe and comfortable environment
  - Give your pet a clean, secure place to rest.
  - Keep harmful substances, toxic plants, and dangerous objects out of reach.
  - Ensure appropriate temperature, lighting, and habitat conditions for the species.

- Focus on grooming and hygiene
  - Brush your pet as needed.
  - Trim nails when appropriate.
  - Maintain dental health through recommended dental care practices.
  - Keep living areas clean.

- Support social and emotional well-being
  - Spend quality time with your pet.
  - Use positive reinforcement for training.
  - Learn your pet's body language and preferences to reduce stress.

- Monitor health changes
  - Watch for changes in eating, drinking, weight, energy level, mobility, or behavior.
