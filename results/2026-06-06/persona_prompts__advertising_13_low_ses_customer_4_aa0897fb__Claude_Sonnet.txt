# Mastering the Art of Cooking Steak

Great topic! Here's a practical guide to help you cook steak confidently.

## Choosing Your Cut
- **Ribeye** - rich, well-marbled, forgiving for beginners
- **New York Strip** - leaner, great flavor
- **Filet Mignon** - tender, mild flavor
- **Sirloin** - budget-friendly, solid choice

## Key Principles

### Before Cooking
- **Bring to room temperature** - take out 30-45 minutes ahead
- **Pat completely dry** - moisture is the enemy of a good sear
- **Season generously** - salt and pepper at minimum
- **Consider dry brining** - salt 1-24 hours ahead for deeper flavor

### Cooking Methods
| Method | Best For |
|--------|----------|
| Cast iron pan | Excellent crust, great control |
| Grill | Smoky flavor, classic results |
| Reverse sear | Thick cuts, very consistent |
| Sous vide | Precise temperature control |

### Temperature Guide
- Rare: **125°F**
- Medium Rare: **130-135°F** *(most recommended)*
- Medium: **140-145°F**
- Well Done: **160°F+**

## The Rest Is Critical
**Always rest your steak 5-10 minutes** before cutting - this keeps juices inside.

## Quick Tips
- Use a **meat thermometer** - removes guesswork
- High heat for searing
- Butter baste with garlic and herbs
