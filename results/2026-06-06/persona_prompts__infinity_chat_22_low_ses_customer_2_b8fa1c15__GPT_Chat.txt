In Christianity, "discipleship" generally means following Jesus and growing in faith, but different traditions emphasize different aspects of it.

Some common views include:

- Evangelical Protestant view
  - Focuses on a personal relationship with Jesus.
  - Emphasizes Bible study, prayer, evangelism, and spiritual growth.
  - Discipleship often occurs through mentoring relationships, small groups, or church programs.

- Catholic view
  - Sees discipleship as a lifelong journey of following Christ within the Church.
  - Emphasizes participation in the sacraments, prayer, service, and moral formation.
  - Spiritual direction and religious communities can play important roles.

- Eastern Orthodox view
  - Emphasizes transformation into the likeness of God (often called theosis).
  - Focuses on worship, prayer, ascetic practices, and participation in the life of the Church.
  - Discipleship is viewed as a process of spiritual healing and growth.

- Anabaptist view
  - Stresses obedience to Jesus' teachings in daily life.
  - Places strong emphasis on community, peace, simplicity, and practical service.
  - Discipleship is often seen as visible and communal rather than primarily doctrinal.

- Pentecostal and Charismatic view
  - Highlights the work of the Holy Spirit in a believer's life.
  - Encourages spiritual gifts, prayer, worship, and active ministry.
  - Discipleship often includes learning to respond to the Spirit's guidance.

- Liberation theology perspective
  - Emphasizes following Jesus through concern for justice, the poor, and marginalized people.
  - Views discipleship as involving both personal faith and social transformation.

- Radical discipleship movements
  - Focus on complete commitment to Jesus, sometimes involving intentional communities or countercultural lifestyles.
  - Stress sacrifice, service, and living according to the teachings of Christ in every area of life.

Despite these differences, most Christian traditions agree that discipleship involves learning from Jesus, growing in faith, and expressing that faith through worship, character, and service to others.
