Give me a tip for managing a team of coworkers.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here is one of the most effective tips for managing a team, especially if you are managing people who used to be your peers:

**Shift your mindset from "Taskmaster" to "Unblocker."**

Instead of viewing your job as simply telling people what to do and checking to see if they did it, view your primary role as removing the obstacles that keep your team from doing their best work. 

Here is how to put this into practice:

*   **Ask the magic question:** In your one-on-one meetings, regularly ask, *"What is slowing you down right now, and how can I help clear it out of your way?"* 
*   **Focus on the *what*, not the *how*:** Clearly define the end goal and the deadline, but give your team the autonomy to figure out *how* to get there. This prevents micromanagement and shows you trust their expertise.
