# Finding the Perfect Hiking Trails

Here are some great ways to discover trails that match your needs:

## Online Resources
- **AllTrails** - Most popular app/website with reviews, maps, and difficulty ratings
- **Hiking Project** - Detailed trail data with photos
- **TrailLink** - Great for rail-trails and paved paths
- **Recreation.gov** - For national parks and federal lands

## Key Factors to Consider

### Difficulty
- **Easy** - Flat, short, well-marked
- **Moderate** - Some elevation gain, longer distance
- **Hard** - Steep, technical, longer duration

### Personal Needs
- Your fitness level
- Available time
- Who's coming (kids, dogs, beginners)
- Desired scenery (waterfall, summit, forest)

## Practical Tips
- **Check recent reviews** for current trail conditions
- **Match elevation gain** to your experience level
- **Consider season** - some trails are impassable in winter or spring
- **Look at trailhead access** - parking, permits required
- **Download offline maps** before you go

## Safety Considerations
- Tell someone your plans
- Check weather forecasts
- Know your turnaround time
- Bring the **10 essentials**

## Finding Local Trails
- Visit local REI stores for staff recommendations
- Contact ranger stations
- Join local hiking clubs or Facebook groups
