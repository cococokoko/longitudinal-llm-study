── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Give me a 3-day plan for Osaka with a flight leaving on the 3rd day at 7pm.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here is a highly optimized, 3-day itinerary for Osaka. Since your flight leaves at 7:00 PM on Day 3, you will need to head to the airport around 4:00 PM (for international flights) or 5:00 PM (for domestic). 

This itinerary is structured geographically to minimize travel time and maximize your experience of Osaka’s food, culture, and modern sights.

---

### **Day 1: Historic Osaka & Neon Nights (Central & South)**
*Focus: History, traditional culture, and the iconic Dotonbori street food scene.*

*   **Morning: Osaka Castle (9:00 AM – 12:00 PM)**
    *   Start your trip at **Osaka Castle**, the city's most famous landmark. 
    *   Explore the surrounding park and head inside the castle tower (which houses a museum and offers great views from the top). 
    *   *Tip:* Grab a matcha ice cream or a snack at the Jo-Terrace complex near the station.
*   **Lunch: Kuromon Ichiba Market (12:30 PM – 2:30 PM)**
    *   Take the subway to Nippombashi Station. Walk through this bustling covered market, known as "Osaka's Kitchen."
    *   **What to eat:** Freshly grilled seafood (scallops, crab legs), wagyu beef skewers, and fresh strawberries.
*   **Afternoon: Shinsaibashi & America-mura (2:30 PM – 5:30 PM)**
    *   Walk off lunch by exploring **Shinsaibashi-suji**, a massive shopping arcade.
    *   Cross over to **America-mura (Amemura)**, the epicenter of Osaka’s youth culture, vintage clothing shops, and quirky street art.
*   **Evening: Dotonbori & Namba (6:00 PM – Late)**
    *   As the sun sets, head to **Dotonbori** to see the neon lights turn on, including the famous **Glico Running Man** sign.
    *   Take a 20-minute **Tombori River Cruise** (highly recommended at night).
    *   **Dinner:** Go on a street-food crawl. Try **Takoyaki** (octopus balls) at *Kukuru* or *Acchichi Honpo*, and **Okonomiyaki** (savory cabbage pancakes) at *Ajinoya* or *Chibo*.

---

### **Day 2: Modern Heights & Retro Vibes (North & South)**
*Focus: Futuristic views, retro Osaka, and deep-fried delicacies.*

*   **Morning: Umeda Sky Building (9:30 AM – 11:30 AM)**
    *   Head to the Umeda district (North Osaka). Visit the **Umeda Sky Building** and ride the futuristic glass escalator to the **Floating Garden Observatory** for a 360-degree view of the city.
*   **Lunch: Osaka Station City / Lucua Osaka (12:00 PM – 1:30 PM)**
    *   The area around Osaka/Umeda station is a foodie paradise. Head to the basement floors of the **LUCUA** department store (Lucua Food Hall) for incredible ramen, sushi, or bento boxes.
*   **Afternoon: Shinsekai & Tsutenkaku Tower (2:00 PM – 5:00 PM)**
    *   Take the subway south to **Shinsekai**, a neighborhood frozen in the 1960s. 
    *   Take photos of the retro storefronts and the **Tsutenkaku Tower**. 
    *   If you want a quirky experience, visit **Spa World** nearby (a massive multi-story hot spring theme park) or just wander the nostalgic streets.
*   **Dinner: Kushikatsu in Shinsekai (5:30 PM – 7:30 PM)**
    *   Shinsekai is the birthplace of **Kushikatsu** (deep-fried meat and vegetables on skewers). 
    *   Eat at *Yaekatsu* or *Kushikatsu Daruma*. (Remember the golden rule: No double-dipping in the sauce!).
*   **Nightcap: Hozenji Yokocho (8:00 PM – 9:30 PM)**
    *   Finish your night at this quiet, stone-paved alleyway near Dotonbori. It feels like stepping back into the Edo period. Visit the moss-covered statue at Hozenji Temple.

---

### **Day 3: Bay Area & Departure (West Osaka)**
*Focus: Stunning bay views, giant aquarium, and a smooth departure.*

*Logistics Tip:* Check out of your hotel in the morning. Either leave your bags at the hotel front desk, or (highly recommended) store them in a coin locker at **Namba Station** or **Tennoji Station**, as these stations have direct train lines to the airport.

*   **Morning: Osaka Aquarium Kaiyukan (9:30 AM – 12:30 PM)**
    *   Take the Chuo Subway Line to Osakako Station. 
    *   Visit **Kaiyukan**, one of the largest and most spectacular public aquariums in the world. The centerpiece is a massive 9-meter-deep tank housing a whale shark.
*   **Lunch: Tempozan Harbor Village (12:30 PM – 2:00 PM)**
    *   Right next to the aquarium is the **Tempozan Marketplace**. 
    *   Eat at the **Naniwa Kuishinbo Yokocho**, a food theme park inside the mall designed to look like 1960s Osaka. It features stalls of all of Osaka's famous food brands in one place.
*   **Early Afternoon: Tempozan Ferris Wheel (2:00 PM – 2:45 PM)**
    *   Before you leave, ride the giant Ferris wheel next to the mall for beautiful views of Osaka Bay, the harbor, and Universal Studios Japan in the distance.
*   **Afternoon: Retrieve Bags & Head to Airport (3:00 PM – 4:00 PM)**
    *   Head back to your hotel/locker to grab your bags.
    *   **To Kansai International Airport (KIX):**
        *   *From Namba Station:* Take the **Nankai Rap:t Limited Express** (approx. 35-40 minutes).
        *   *From Osaka/Umeda Station:* Take the **JR Kansai Airport Rapid Service** (approx. 65 minutes) or the **Haruka Express** from Tennoji.
    *   **To Itami Airport (ITM - Domestic):** Take the airport limousine bus from Namba or Umeda (approx. 30 minutes).

*   **4:30 PM / 5:00 PM:** Arrive at the airport, check-in, and catch your **7:00 PM flight**.
