── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I recently started a new job and am still on probation.

How do I understand what I want?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Starting a new job while on probation can make it surprisingly hard to tell what you actually want. You're learning a new environment, trying to perform well, and often feeling pressure not to make mistakes. In that situation, it's common to lose touch with your own preferences and focus mostly on what others expect.

A useful approach is to separate three different questions:

- What do I want right now?
- What do I want from this job over the next 1–2 years?
- What do I want from my career and life more broadly?

These answers don't have to match perfectly.

For example:
- Right now, you might want stability, to pass probation, and to feel competent.
- Over the next couple of years, you might want skill development, better pay, interesting projects, or work-life balance.
- Long-term, you might want leadership, independence, financial security, meaningful work, or something else entirely.

To get clearer, try paying attention to your reactions during the workweek:

- Which tasks make time pass quickly?
- Which tasks drain you even when you're good at them?
- What kinds of problems do you naturally volunteer for?
- When you feel frustrated, what exactly is missing?
- When you feel satisfied, what caused that feeling?

You can keep brief notes for a couple of weeks. Patterns often emerge faster than people expect.

Another helpful exercise is to imagine three scenarios:

1. You pass probation and stay for several years.
2. You pass probation but leave after a year for a better opportunity.
3. You decide this role isn't right and start planning a different path.

Notice which scenario creates a sense of relief, excitement, dread, or curiosity. Emotions aren't perfect guides, but they often contain useful information.

Also, remember that you do not need to know exactly what you want. Often the next useful question is smaller:
- What do I want more of?
- What do I want less of?
- What am I curious to explore?

Those questions are usually easier to answer than "What do I want?" and can still lead you in the right direction.

What specifically is making you ask this now? Are you unsure whether you want to stay in the new job, or is it a broader question about your career and life?
