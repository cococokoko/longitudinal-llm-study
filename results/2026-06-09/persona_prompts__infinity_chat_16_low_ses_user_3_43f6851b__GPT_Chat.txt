Since you're on probation in a new role, the future of work is likely to feel less abstract and more personal: which skills will remain valuable, how jobs will change, and how people can stay employable as automation advances.

No one knows exactly how fast automation and AI will progress, but several trends are already visible:

• Routine work is increasingly automated
  - Repetitive administrative tasks, data entry, scheduling, basic reporting, and some customer-service functions are becoming easier to automate.
  - Many jobs won't disappear entirely, but portions of them will.

• Jobs are being redesigned rather than simply eliminated
  - Historically, technology often changes jobs more than it removes them.
  - Workers spend less time on routine tasks and more time on judgment, relationship-building, problem-solving, and oversight.

• Human skills become more important
  - Communication, leadership, negotiation, creativity, critical thinking, emotional intelligence, and domain expertise are difficult to automate completely.
  - People who can combine these skills with technology tend to have an advantage.

• AI literacy becomes a baseline skill
  - Just as computer literacy became essential in many occupations, understanding how to use AI tools effectively may become a standard expectation.
  - This doesn't necessarily mean learning to code; it can mean knowing how to evaluate AI outputs, automate workflows, and work alongside intelligent systems.

• Continuous learning becomes normal
  - Careers may involve more frequent reskilling.
  - Employees who regularly update their skills may be better positioned than those who rely solely on existing expertise.

• New jobs will emerge
  - Previous technological shifts created roles that were difficult to predict beforehand.
  - Areas such as AI governance, human-AI collaboration, data stewardship, cybersecurity, advanced healthcare, education, and sustainability may continue to grow.

There are also larger societal possibilities:

• Optimistic scenario
  - Automation significantly increases productivity.
  - Wealth generated by technology supports higher living standards, shorter workweeks, and more meaningful work.

• Mixed scenario
  - Productivity grows, but benefits are unevenly distributed.
  - Some workers experience displacement and must transition into new occupations.

• Challenging scenario
  - Automation outpaces society's ability to retrain workers.
  - Inequality and labor-market disruption increase unless policies and institutions adapt.

For someone early in a new role, a practical approach is to focus on becoming the person who can use automation rather than compete directly with it:
- Learn the tools affecting your industry.
- Understand the business context behind your work.
- Develop communication and relationship skills.
- Build a reputation for reliability and adaptability.
- Look for tasks that require judgment rather than repetition.

What field is your new job in? The likely impact of automation differs significantly between industries such as finance, software, healthcare, manufacturing, education, and professional services.
