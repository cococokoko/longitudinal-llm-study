Write 100-300 words on how stress affects the body and mind.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
When stress strikes, it triggers a "fight-or-flight" survival response, flooding the body with hormones like cortisol and adrenaline. While this is useful in short bursts, chronic stress takes a heavy toll on both the body and the mind, especially when your daily routine is unpredictable and leaves little time for recovery.

Physically, constant stress keeps your muscles constantly tensed, often leading to chronic headaches, shoulder tightness, and physical exhaustion. It elevates your heart rate and blood pressure, putting long-term strain on your cardiovascular system. Stress also disrupts your digestive tract—causing stomach aches or appetite changes—and weakens your immune system, making you much more vulnerable to catching whatever cold is going around. 

Mentally and emotionally, stress drains your cognitive battery. It clouds your focus, impairs decision-making, and makes even simple tasks feel overwhelming. When your brain is constantly on high alert, it becomes incredibly difficult to quiet your thoughts, leading to insomnia or fragmented, unrefreshing sleep. Over time, this mental fatigue erodes your patience, leaving you feeling irritable, anxious, and emotionally drained. 

Ultimately, stress acts like an engine running constantly in the red. Without adequate time to rest and reset, both the body and mind begin to wear down, making proactive self-care essential for long-term well-being.
