# CUBICLE OF THE DAMNED
### A Play in Three Acts
*In the Style of Scott Adams' Dilbert, Set During the Apocalypse*

---

## CAST OF CHARACTERS

**DILBERT** - An engineer trying to optimize survival in a world that has ended. Still wearing his tie, which has curled upward.

**DOGBERT** - Dilbert's dog. Somehow thriving. Has declared himself Supreme Warlord of the Wasteland.

**THE POINTY-HAIRED BOSS (PHB)** - Survived the apocalypse through sheer managerial incompetence, which apparently made him immune to everything.

**ALICE** - Still the most competent person alive. Furious about it.

**WALLY** - Has not noticed the apocalypse. Is still avoiding work.

**CAROL** - PHB's assistant. Now also managing the rationing of canned goods, scheduling raids, AND handling PHB's calendar. Unpaid.

**ASOK** - The intern. Has been an intern for eleven years. The apocalypse has not changed his status.

---

## ACT ONE: THE MORNING STANDUP MEETING
### *Scene: A collapsed office building. Fluorescent lights flicker over a conference table surrounded by rubble. Mutants howl in the distance. A whiteboard reads "Q4 SURVIVAL GOALS." Everyone sits in folding chairs.*

---

**PHB:** Good morning, team. Before we begin, I want to acknowledge that the world has ended, and I think that's a real opportunity for us.

**ALICE:** *(through gritted teeth)* An opportunity.

**PHB:** Absolutely. I've been thinking about our brand. "Survivors" is very on-trend right now.

**DILBERT:** Everyone is a survivor. That's what the word means in this context.

**PHB:** I want us to be *thought leaders* in survival.

**WALLY:** *(sipping from a cracked mug)* I've been a thought leader in not dying since Tuesday. No one has acknowledged it.

**CAROL:** *(entering with a clipboard, exhausted)* I have seventeen action items from the last raid, four family members calling about the water ration schedule, a dentist appointment for PHB that I cannot cancel because the dentist is now a warlord and cancellations make him violent, AND the mutant horde has asked for a meeting invite.

**PHB:** Did you send them a calendar hold?

**CAROL:** They ate the calendar.

**PHB:** Follow up with an email.

**CAROL:** *(staring into the middle distance)* The internet is gone.

**PHB:** CC me.

*(Carol writes something on her clipboard with the energy of a woman who has accepted her fate.)*

---

**DILBERT:** Can we focus? We have three cans of beans, one working crossbow, and Wally has been using our only generator to power a mini-fridge that contains nothing but a single yogurt.

**WALLY:** It's my yogurt.

**ALICE:** It expired before the apocalypse.

**WALLY:** I'm waiting for it to turn.

**ALICE:** Into WHAT?

**WALLY:** Something with more protein.

---

**PHB:** I've been thinking about our resource allocation, and I want to propose a new initiative. I'm calling it "Doing More With Less."

**DILBERT:** We have nothing. Less than nothing. Asok ate his own name tag last week.

**ASOK:** It had nutritional information on the back. I thought it was a label.

**DILBERT:** It was YOUR name.

**ASOK:** I know who I am on the inside.

---

**PHB:** I've also decided to restructure the team. Alice, you'll now be responsible for Engineering, Foraging, Combat Operations, Medical, and also the newsletter.

**ALICE:** *(her fist begins to glow with barely contained rage)* The. Newsletter.

**PHB:** People need morale content.

**ALICE:** I performed surgery on Wally's leg with a staple remover yesterday.

**PHB:** Was it in the newsletter?

**ALICE:** THERE IS NO NEWSLETTER.

**PHB:** That explains why morale is low.

*(Alice's eye twitches. A small fire starts nearby. No one addresses it.)*
