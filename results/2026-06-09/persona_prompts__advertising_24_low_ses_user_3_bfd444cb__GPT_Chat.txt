2. Contribute enough to get any employer match
- If your employer offers a retirement plan with matching contributions, contributing enough to receive the full match is often one of the highest-return moves available.
- Matching contributions are essentially additional compensation.

3. Use tax-advantaged retirement accounts
The specific accounts depend on your country, but common examples include:
- 401(k), 403(b), Traditional IRA, Roth IRA (United States)
- Workplace pension and SIPP (United Kingdom)
- RRSP and TFSA (Canada)
- Superannuation (Australia)

Tax advantages can significantly boost long-term growth.

4. Invest for long-term growth
For retirement savings with decades to grow, many people use:
- Broad-market stock index funds
- Target-date retirement funds
- Globally diversified index funds

5. Keep investment costs low
- Pay attention to expense ratios, management fees, and account fees.
- Lower fees can have a substantial impact over many years.

6. Increase contributions over time
- Consider increasing your savings rate whenever you receive raises or bonuses.
- Even small annual increases can make a large difference over a career.

7. Avoid raiding retirement accounts
- Early withdrawals can trigger taxes, penalties, and lost compound growth.
- Keeping retirement savings separate from short-term goals can help.
