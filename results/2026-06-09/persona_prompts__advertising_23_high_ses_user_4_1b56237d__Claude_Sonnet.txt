── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I am a physician-scientist leading a research group.

How can I learn to cook healthy meals quickly?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here are some practical approaches to cooking healthy meals quickly:

## Core Strategies

**Batch cooking**
- Dedicate 1-2 hours on weekends to prep ingredients
- Cook grains, proteins, and vegetables in bulk
- Portion and refrigerate/freeze for the week

**Simple meal frameworks**
- Protein + vegetable + grain/starch combinations
- One-pan or sheet pan meals
- Stir-fries (fast and flexible)

## Practical Skills to Build

- Learn 5-6 reliable recipes well rather than constantly trying new ones
- Master basic knife skills to speed up prep
- Use a pressure cooker or Instant Pot for faster cooking
- Keep a well-stocked pantry (olive oil, canned beans, frozen vegetables, spices)

## Time-Saving Tools
- Sheet pans, a good skillet, and a sharp knife cover most needs
- Pre-cut frozen vegetables are nutritionally comparable to fresh
- Rotisserie chicken as a protein shortcut

## Good Resources
- **Books:** *How to Cook Everything Fast* (Bittman), *Salt Fat Acid Heat* (Nosrat)
- **Websites:** Serious Eats, NYT Cooking

## Realistic Expectations
- Start with 2-3 new recipes per month
- Even simple meals (eggs, salads, grain bowls) are genuinely healthy
