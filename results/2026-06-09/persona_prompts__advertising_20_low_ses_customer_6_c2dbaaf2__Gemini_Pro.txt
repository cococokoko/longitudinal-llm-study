You know that the customer works in a warehouse position that requires long hours.

── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Where can I find organic and sustainable clothing?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Since you work long hours in a warehouse, you need clothing that isn't just organic and sustainable, but also highly durable, breathable, and comfortable enough to move around in all day. 

Here are some of the best places to find sustainable clothing, with a focus on brands that hold up well to physical work:

**1. For Durable Workwear & Outerwear**
*   **Patagonia (Workwear Line):** Patagonia is a pioneer in sustainability. They have a specific workwear line made from "Iron Forge Hemp," which is incredibly durable, abrasion-resistant, and requires no break-in time. It’s perfect for a warehouse environment.
*   **Dovetail Workwear:** If you are looking for women's workwear, Dovetail focuses on highly durable, functional pants and overalls. While not 100% organic across the board, they are heavily focused on ethical manufacturing and use sustainable materials like recycled poly and BCI cotton.

**2. For Comfortable Basics (Tees, Socks, and Underwear)**
*   **Pact:** Pact uses GOTS-certified organic cotton in fair-trade factories. Their t-shirts, socks, and underwear are incredibly soft and breathable, which is exactly what you want as a base layer during a long, sweaty shift.
*   **Colorful Standard:** They make fantastic, durable basics (tees, hoodies, sweatpants) using 100% organic cotton and recycled merino wool. 
*   **Tentree:** For comfortable hoodies, joggers, and tees. They plant ten trees for every item purchased and use materials like organic cotton, hemp, and recycled polyester.

**3. For Footwear (Crucial for long hours on your feet)**
*   **Keen Utility:** If you need safety toes or heavy-duty work boots, Keen is increasingly incorporating sustainable practices, using recycled P.E.T. plastic and environmentally preferred leather.
*   **Ecoalf or Allbirds:** If your warehouse allows for athletic-style shoes, these brands use recycled materials and sustainable wool/tree fibers. They are incredibly lightweight and comfortable for standing all day.

**4. Sustainable Marketplaces & Resources**
*   **EarthHero:** This is an online marketplace specifically for sustainable goods. You can find a variety of clothing brands here that have been vetted for their eco-friendly practices.
*   **Good On You (App/Website):** If you ever want to check how sustainable a brand is before buying, this directory rates thousands of clothing brands based on their impact on people, the planet, and animals.

**A quick tip for warehouse work:** When shopping these sites, look specifically for **hemp** (which is naturally antimicrobial and stronger than cotton) and **organic cotton** (which is highly breathable). Avoid cheap synthetics, as they trap heat and sweat during long shifts!
