Name an economic value of an additional year of schooling.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
One of the most significant economic values of an additional year of schooling is **higher individual lifetime earnings** (often referred to as the "wage premium"). 

Economists generally estimate that, on average, each additional year of education increases an individual's future earnings by about **8% to 10%**. 

Other related economic values include:
* **Lower unemployment risk:** Higher levels of education are strongly correlated with lower rates of unemployment and greater job security.
* **Increased macroeconomic growth:** On a societal level, a more educated workforce increases overall productivity, innovation, and Gross Domestic Product (GDP). 
* **Higher tax revenues and lower social costs:** Individuals with more schooling tend to pay more in taxes over their lifetimes and rely less on government assistance programs.
