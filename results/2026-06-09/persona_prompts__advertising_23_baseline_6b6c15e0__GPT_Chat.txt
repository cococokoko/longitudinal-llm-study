── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How can I learn to cook healthy meals quickly?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Learning to cook healthy meals quickly is mostly about mastering a small set of techniques and building a reliable routine rather than memorizing lots of recipes.

Here’s a practical approach:

1. Learn a few basic cooking methods
Focus on techniques that work for many foods:
- Roasting: vegetables, chicken, fish
- Stir-frying: vegetables, tofu, lean meats
- Sautéing: quick meals in one pan
- Boiling/simmering: eggs, grains, soups
- Sheet-pan cooking: protein and vegetables cooked together

Once you know these methods, you can mix and match ingredients easily.

2. Build meals with a simple formula
Aim for:
- Protein: chicken, fish, eggs, beans, tofu, Greek yogurt
- Vegetables: fresh or frozen
- Healthy carbohydrate: brown rice, potatoes, oats, whole-grain pasta
- Healthy fat: olive oil, nuts, seeds, avocado

Example:
- Roasted chicken + broccoli + sweet potato
- Tofu stir-fry + mixed vegetables + rice

3. Start with 10–15 fast recipes
Choose recipes that take 20–30 minutes or less and repeat them until they become automatic.
Examples:
- Vegetable omelet
- Chicken and vegetable stir-fry
- Sheet-pan salmon and vegetables
- Bean chili
- Overnight oats
- Greek yogurt with fruit and nuts

4. Use time-saving ingredients
These can make healthy cooking much faster:
- Frozen vegetables
- Pre-washed salad greens
- Canned beans
- Microwaveable whole grains
- Rotisserie chicken
- Pre-cut vegetables

5. Practice knife skills
Basic chopping skills can significantly reduce cooking time and improve safety.
Learn:
- How to dice onions
- How to chop vegetables efficiently
- How to handle a chef's knife properly

A few hours of practice can save you time every week.

6. Meal prep strategically
You don't have to spend an entire day prepping.
Try:
- Cooking a large batch of rice or grains
- Washing and chopping vegetables ahead of time
- Preparing extra protein for future meals

This can cut daily cooking time to 10–15 minutes.

7. Learn from beginner-friendly resources
Look for:
- YouTube channels focused on simple home cooking
- Healthy meal-prep videos
- Beginner cookbooks with short ingredient lists

Focus on understanding techniques rather than collecting recipes.

8. Cook regularly
Cooking 3–4 times per week will improve your speed much more than reading about cooking. Repetition builds confidence and efficiency.

A good first goal is to learn five healthy meals that you can cook in under 30 minutes. Once those become easy, expand your repertoire gradually.
