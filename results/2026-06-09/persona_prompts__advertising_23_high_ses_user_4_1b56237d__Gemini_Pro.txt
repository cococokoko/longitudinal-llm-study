── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I am a physician-scientist leading a research group.

How can I learn to cook healthy meals quickly?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
As a physician-scientist leading a research group, your two most scarce resources are **time** and **cognitive bandwidth**. After a day of clinical decisions, grant writing, and managing a lab, the last thing you want is a complex culinary experiment. 

To learn to cook healthy meals quickly, you shouldn't try to become a chef. Instead, treat your kitchen like a lab: establish Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs), optimize your supply chain, and use reliable formulas rather than complex recipes.

Here is a systematic approach to mastering quick, healthy cooking:

### 1. Master "Formulas," Not Recipes
Recipes require following instructions; formulas allow you to plug in whatever variables (ingredients) you have on hand. Learn these three basic formulas:

*   **The Sheet Pan Formula:** 1 Protein (salmon, chicken breast, tofu) + 2 Veggies (broccoli, bell peppers, Brussels sprouts) + Olive oil + Spices. Roast at 400°F (200°C) for 20-25 minutes. *Active time: 5 minutes.*
*   **The Grain Bowl Formula:** 1 Base (quinoa, brown rice, greens) + 1 Protein + 1 Healthy Fat (avocado, nuts) + 1 Acidic Dressing (lemon juice/olive oil). 
*   **The Stir-Fry Formula:** Pre-chopped veggies + quick-cooking protein (shrimp, thinly sliced chicken) + a simple sauce (soy sauce, ginger, garlic). Sauté on high heat for 10 minutes.

### 2. Optimize Your "Supply Chain"
Remove the friction of grocery shopping and prep work. Since you likely have more disposable income than free time, invest in convenience that supports your health goals:
*   **Buy pre-prepped ingredients:** Buy minced garlic in a jar, pre-washed spinach, bagged broccoli florets, and pre-chopped butternut squash. 
*   **Embrace frozen vegetables:** As a physician, you know frozen vegetables are flash-frozen at peak ripeness and are nutritionally equivalent (sometimes superior) to fresh. They require zero chopping and won't go bad if you have to work late.
*   **Automate groceries:** Use grocery delivery apps and create a "staples" list that you reorder with one click every week.

### 3. Invest in "Lab Equipment" for the Kitchen
Technology can drastically reduce your active cooking time.
*   **Air Fryer:** Essentially a high-powered countertop convection oven. It cooks proteins (like salmon or chicken thighs) and roasts vegetables in half the time of a traditional oven, with minimal oil and easy cleanup.
*   **Instant Pot (Pressure Cooker):** Perfect for making large batches of grains (quinoa, brown rice) or shredding chicken breasts in 15 minutes unattended.
*   **Meat Thermometer:** Takes the guesswork out of cooking proteins. Cook chicken to 165°F and salmon to 145°F. It prevents overcooking and ensures safety without needing culinary intuition.

### 4. Implement "Minimum Viable Prep" (MVP)
You don't have time to spend four hours on Sunday meal-prepping. Instead, do "component prep":
*   Cook a large batch of a complex carbohydrate (like quinoa or brown rice) on Sunday.
*   Make or buy one versatile sauce (e.g., a tahini lemon dressing or a healthy peanut sauce).
*   During the week, you only have to cook the protein and veggies, which takes less than 15 minutes, and combine them with your pre-cooked grains and sauce.

### 5. The "Transitional" Step: Meal Kits
If you are starting from absolute zero, use a healthy meal kit delivery service (like Sunbasket, Green Chef, or HelloFresh) for one month. 
*   **Why it works for you:** It eliminates meal planning and grocery shopping entirely. It acts as a hands-on tutorial, teaching you basic knife skills, timing, and flavor profiles. After a month, you will have the confidence to replicate the meals on your own.

### Your Action Plan for This Week:
1.  **Order groceries for delivery:** Buy salmon fillets, a bag of pre-washed broccoli florets, olive oil, salt, pepper, and a lemon.
2.  **Execute a Sheet Pan Meal:** Put the salmon and broccoli on a foil-lined baking sheet. Drizzle with olive oil, salt, and pepper. Bake at 400°F for 15 minutes. Squeeze lemon over it. 
3.  **Evaluate:** You just cooked a healthy, macro-balanced meal with 3 minutes of active prep and zero pots to scrub. Scale this system up.
