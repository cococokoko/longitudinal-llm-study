── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I am a senior attorney at a well-established law firm.

Building a personal brand on social media.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
As a senior attorney, building a personal brand on social media is less about becoming an influencer and more about establishing credibility, visibility, and trust among clients, referral sources, peers, and potential recruits.

A strong legal personal brand usually combines:
- Demonstrated expertise
- Professional judgment
- Consistent visibility
- A recognizable point of view

Key questions to define your brand:
- What type of law do you practice?
- Who do you want to attract? (clients, referral partners, in-house counsel, boards, media, recruits)
- What do you want to be known for?
- What makes your approach different from other attorneys in your field?

For example:
- Corporate attorney: "Helping founders and growth-stage companies navigate complex transactions."
- Employment attorney: "Practical guidance for employers managing workforce risk."
- Litigation attorney: "Strategic dispute resolution for businesses facing high-stakes conflicts."

Platforms to prioritize:
- LinkedIn: Usually the most valuable platform for senior attorneys.
- X (Twitter): Useful for legal commentary, policy discussions, and media visibility.
- YouTube: Powerful if you enjoy explaining legal developments.
- Podcasting: Effective for relationship-building and demonstrating expertise.

Content that performs well for attorneys:
- Analysis of new laws, regulations, and court decisions
- Practical business implications of legal developments
- Frequently asked client questions
- Lessons learned from industry trends
- Risk-management tips
- Commentary on emerging issues in your practice area
- Speaking engagements and published articles
- Behind-the-scenes insights into legal strategy (without discussing confidential matters)

A simple LinkedIn content framework:
- 40% educational content
- 30% commentary on industry developments
- 20% personal professional insights
- 10% firm achievements and business development

Examples:
- "Three things employers should know about the latest FTC rule."
- "What this recent appellate decision means for commercial landlords."
- "The most common contract mistake I see in M&A transactions."
- "A question I was asked repeatedly this quarter by general counsel."

Posting frequency:
- 2–4 quality LinkedIn posts per week
- Comment thoughtfully on others' posts daily
- Publish a longer article once or twice per month

Important ethical considerations:
- Follow your jurisdiction's attorney advertising rules.
- Avoid creating unintended attorney-client relationships.
- Include appropriate disclaimers where necessary.
- Never discuss client matters without explicit authorization.
- Avoid guaranteeing outcomes or making misleading claims.

A particularly effective strategy for senior attorneys is to become known for interpreting legal developments through a business lens. Clients often care less about the legal rule itself and more about:
- What changed?
- Why does it matter?
- What should I do next?
