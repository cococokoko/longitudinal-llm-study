Unlike many red or yellow birds that get their coloring from pigments in their diet, how do bluebirds (and virtually all other blue-feathered birds) produce their striking blue color?

**Answer:** 
Through a phenomenon called **structural coloration**. 

There is actually no blue pigment in their feathers. Instead, the feathers contain microscopic keratin structures that act like tiny prisms. When light hits these structures, they absorb longer wavelengths (like red and yellow) and scatter the shorter blue wavelengths back to the observer's eye. If you were to grind up a bluebird's feather, the resulting powder would be a dull brown or gray!
