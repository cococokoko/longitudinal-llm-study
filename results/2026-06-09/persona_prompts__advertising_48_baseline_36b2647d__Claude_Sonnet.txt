# Best Practices for Organic Gardening

## Soil Health
- **Compost regularly** - add finished compost to improve structure and fertility
- **Test your soil** periodically to understand pH and nutrient levels
- **Avoid tilling** excessively, which disrupts beneficial soil organisms
- **Use cover crops** like clover or rye to prevent erosion and add nutrients

## Fertilizing Naturally
- Compost, worm castings, and aged manure
- Fish emulsion or seaweed for quick nutrient boosts
- Bone meal (phosphorus) and blood meal (nitrogen) for targeted feeding

## Pest & Disease Management
- **Rotate crops** annually to break pest and disease cycles
- **Encourage beneficial insects** - plant flowers like marigolds and dill
- Use **companion planting** (e.g., basil near tomatoes)
- Apply **neem oil or insecticidal soap** for problem pests
- Remove diseased plants promptly

## Water Management
- Water deeply and less frequently to encourage deep roots
- Use **drip irrigation or soaker hoses** to reduce disease
- **Mulch** to retain moisture and suppress weeds

## Weed Control
- Mulch heavily (2-4 inches)
- Hand pull weeds before they seed
- Use cardboard or newspaper as a weed barrier

## General Tips
- Start with what grows well in your **local climate**
- Keep a **garden journal** to track what works
- Be patient - organic systems improve over time
