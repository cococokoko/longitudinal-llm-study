── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How can I decorate my apartment on a minimalist budget?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Decorating on a minimalist budget is mostly about choosing a few high-impact pieces and avoiding clutter. You can make an apartment feel intentional and comfortable without spending much.

Here are some practical ideas:

- Start with a neutral base
  - Use simple colors like white, beige, gray, or soft earth tones.
  - A consistent color palette makes inexpensive items look more cohesive.

- Focus on lighting
  - Good lighting can transform a room more than expensive furniture.
  - Add a floor lamp, table lamp, or warm LED bulbs instead of relying only on overhead lights.

- Use textiles strategically
  - A single area rug can define a space.
  - Add a few throw pillows or a blanket in complementary colors.
  - Choose quality-looking fabrics rather than lots of decorative items.

- Decorate with plants
  - A few low-maintenance plants can add life and color.
  - Consider pothos, snake plants, or ZZ plants.
  - Even one larger plant often has more impact than several small decorations.

- Create simple wall art
  - Print free or inexpensive artwork and place it in affordable frames.
  - Black-and-white photography and minimalist line drawings work especially well.

- Shop secondhand
  - Check thrift stores, Facebook Marketplace, garage sales, and local buy/sell groups.
  - Look for solid furniture that can be cleaned or lightly refinished.

- Prioritize function
  - Choose pieces that serve a purpose and look good.
  - Examples: a stylish storage basket, a bookshelf, or a bench with storage.

- Reduce visual clutter
  - Store items in matching bins or baskets.
  - Leave some surfaces empty to create a clean, minimalist feel.

- Use mirrors
  - A large mirror can make a room feel brighter and more spacious.
  - Thrift stores often have inexpensive mirrors.

- Follow the "one statement piece" rule
  - Pick one focal point per room: a rug, artwork, lamp, or chair.
  - Keep the rest simple so the room feels intentional rather than crowded.

Example budget for a living room:
- Secondhand rug: $40–$80
- Floor lamp: $20–$50
- Plant: $10–$30
- Framed prints: $20–$40
- Throw blanket and pillow: $20–$40

Total: roughly $110–$240, depending on what you can find secondhand.
