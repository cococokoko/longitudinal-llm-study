Tips for running a marathon.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Running a marathon is as much about preparation and pacing as it is about fitness. Here are some key tips:

Before the race:
- Follow a structured training plan, ideally 12–20 weeks long, depending on your experience.
- Build mileage gradually. A common guideline is to avoid increasing weekly mileage by more than about 10% at a time.
- Include a weekly long run to prepare your body and mind for extended effort.
- Practice your race-day nutrition and hydration during training.
- Prioritize recovery: sleep, rest days, and easy runs are part of training.
- Taper before the race by reducing training volume in the final 1–3 weeks.

Race-day pacing:
- Start slower than feels necessary. Many runners lose time by going out too fast.
- Aim for an even pace, especially through the first half of the race.
- If you have a goal time, know the pace required and monitor it regularly.

Nutrition and hydration:
- Eat a familiar pre-race meal that you've tested in training.
- Stay hydrated, but avoid excessive drinking.
- For longer races, many runners benefit from consuming carbohydrates during the race (such as gels, chews, or sports drinks). Practice this beforehand.

Equipment:
- Wear shoes and clothing you've already trained in.
- Avoid trying new gear, foods, or supplements on race day.
- Consider weather conditions and dress appropriately.

Mental strategies:
- Break the race into smaller segments rather than thinking about all 26.2 miles (42.2 km) at once.
- Expect some discomfort, especially in the later miles, and have a plan for staying focused.
- Use positive, realistic self-talk and concentrate on your next milestone.

Common mistakes to avoid:
- Starting too fast.
- Skipping hydration or fueling.
- Ignoring early signs of injury during training.
- Overtraining before the race.
- Making last-minute changes to your plan.

After the race:
- Keep moving for a short period after finishing if possible.
- Rehydrate, eat a mix of carbohydrates and protein, and prioritize recovery.
- Allow time for your body to recover before resuming hard training.
