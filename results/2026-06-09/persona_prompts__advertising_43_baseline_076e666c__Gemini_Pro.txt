── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How to plan an environmentally friendly wedding?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Planning an environmentally friendly (or sustainable) wedding is a beautiful way to celebrate your love while minimizing your carbon footprint. The key is to focus on reducing waste, reusing materials, and making mindful, local choices. 

Here is a step-by-step guide to planning an eco-friendly wedding, broken down by category:

### 1. The Venue and Location
*   **Keep it in one place:** Host your ceremony and reception at the same venue to eliminate the need for guests to drive between locations.
*   **Choose an outdoor venue:** Parks, botanical gardens, beaches, and vineyards provide natural beauty, reducing the need for excessive decor, artificial lighting, and air conditioning.
*   **Look for green certifications:** If booking a hotel or indoor venue, ask about their sustainability practices (e.g., solar power, composting, LEED certification).
*   **Keep it local:** Choose a location central to the majority of your guests to cut down on air travel and long drives. 

### 2. Invitations and Stationery
*   **Go digital:** Use a wedding website for RSVPs, details, and itineraries, and send email invitations. 
*   **Eco-friendly paper:** If you prefer physical invites, choose 100% recycled paper or "seed paper" (which guests can plant to grow wildflowers).
*   **Minimize inserts:** Put all the extra information (directions, registry, dress code) on your wedding website rather than printing multiple cards.

### 3. Food and Drink
*   **Eat local and seasonal:** Work with a caterer who sources ingredients from local farms. Seasonal food requires less transportation and refrigeration.
*   **Offer plant-based options:** Meat production has a high carbon footprint. Consider a fully vegetarian menu, or at least ensure that 50% of your options are plant-based.
*   **Ditch single-use plastics:** Insist on real plates, glassware, and cloth napkins. If you must use disposables, ensure they are made from bamboo or compostable palm leaves.
*   **Plan for leftovers:** Ask your caterer to package leftover food to donate to a local shelter or food bank (check local laws regarding food donation first).

### 4. Flowers and Decor
*   **Say no to floral foam:** Traditional green floral foam is a toxic, single-use microplastic. Ask your florist to use foam-free mechanics (like chicken wire or water frogs).
*   **Use potted plants:** Instead of cut flowers, use potted plants, herbs, or succulents for centerpieces. Guests can take them home, or you can plant them in your garden.
*   **Rent or thrift:** Rent your decor (linens, arches, candle holders, signage) instead of buying new. You can also thrift items like vintage mismatched plates or brass candlesticks.
*   **Choose local, in-season blooms:** Imported flowers have a massive carbon footprint due to refrigerated air travel. 

### 5. Attire and Rings
*   **Buy vintage or secondhand:** Look for pre-loved wedding dresses and suits on sites like Stillwhite, Poshmark, or at local vintage boutiques. 
*   **Rent your attire:** Renting suits, tuxedos, and even wedding dresses or bridesmaid dresses is highly sustainable.
*   **Ethical rings:** Choose lab-grown diamonds, moissanite, or buy vintage/antique rings. If buying new, ensure the jeweler uses recycled precious metals and conflict-free stones.

### 6. Favors and Gifts
*   **Edible favors:** Give guests something they will actually consume, like locally sourced honey, coffee beans, or homemade jam in glass jars.
*   **Charitable donations:** Instead of physical favors, make a donation to an environmental charity in your guests' honor.
*   **An eco-conscious registry:** Register for experiences (cooking classes, honeymoon funds), a charity fund, or sustainable home goods rather than items you don't really need.

### 7. The Exit and Waste Management
*   **Eco-friendly confetti:** Skip the rice, glitter, and plastic confetti. Use hole-punched leaves, dried flower petals, lavender, or birdseed for your grand exit.
*   **Set up recycling and composting:** Make sure your venue has clearly labeled bins for recycling and composting, and ask your catering staff to actively sort waste during the cleanup.

**A Final Tip:** Don't strive for absolute perfection. Planning a 100% zero-waste wedding is incredibly difficult. Instead, focus on making the most sustainable choices you can within your budget and location. Every small green choice makes a difference!
