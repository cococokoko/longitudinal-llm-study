── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Best skin routine for dry skin.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The best skincare routine for dry skin focuses on **hydration (adding water)** and **moisturization (locking in that water)** while avoiding anything that strips the skin’s natural lipid barrier. 

Here is a highly effective, dermatologist-approved daily routine, along with key ingredients to look for and habits to avoid.

---

### **The Golden Rules for Dry Skin**
*   **Apply products to damp skin:** Never let your face dry completely after washing. Apply your next steps immediately to lock in moisture.
*   **Avoid hot water:** Wash your face with lukewarm water. Hot water strips the skin of its natural oils.
*   **Look for these key ingredients:** 
    *   *Humectants (draw water in):* Hyaluronic Acid, Glycerin, Aloe Vera.
    *   *Emollients/Ceramides (repair the barrier):* Ceramides, Squalane, Shea Butter.
    *   *Occlusives (lock it all in):* Petrolatum, Jojoba Oil.

---

### **Morning Routine (AM): Protect & Hydrate**
The goal for the morning is to prep your skin for the day and protect it from environmental stressors.

1.  **Cleanse (or just rinse):** 
    *   If your skin is very dry, skip cleanser in the morning and just rinse with lukewarm water. 
    *   If you prefer to wash, use a **non-foaming, hydrating milk or cream cleanser**.
2.  **Hydrating Toner or Essence (Optional but highly recommended):**
    *   Apply a soothing, alcohol-free toner containing glycerin or hyaluronic acid. Pat it into the skin with your hands (skip the cotton pad to avoid friction).
3.  **Hydrating Serum:**
    *   Apply a **Hyaluronic Acid** or **Beta-Glucan** serum onto damp skin. 
4.  **Moisturizer:**
    *   Use a nourishing cream that contains **ceramides** (to rebuild your skin barrier) or **squalane**. 
5.  **Sunscreen (Crucial):**
    *   Dry skin loves chemical or mineral sunscreens with a dewy finish. Look for SPF 30 or higher that doubles as a hydrating lotion.

---

### **Evening Routine (PM): Repair & Nourish**
The night is when your skin repairs itself. This is the time for richer textures and barrier repair.

1.  **Double Cleanse (If wearing makeup/SPF):**
    *   **Step 1:** Use a **cleansing balm or oil**. This breaks down makeup and sunscreen without drying out your skin.
    *   **Step 2:** Use your gentle, hydrating cream cleanser to wash away any residue.
2.  **Hydrating Toner/Essence:**
    *   Pat onto damp skin.
3.  **Treatment/Serum:**
    *   **For anti-aging/texture:** Use a gentle Retinol (start 2x a week) formulated with moisturizing ingredients, or a Lactic Acid serum (a gentle AHA that also hydrates).
    *   **For barrier repair:** Use a serum with **Niacinamide** or **Panthenol (Vitamin B5)**.
4.  **Rich Night Cream:**
    *   Use a thick, lipid-rich moisturizer. Look for ingredients like shea butter, ceramides, and fatty acids.
5.  **Face Oil or "Slugging" (Optional - Great for winter):**
    *   Pat 2–3 drops of **Squalane, Rosehip, or Jojoba oil** over your moisturizer to seal everything in.
    *   *Alternatively:* Apply a very thin layer of Vaseline or Aquaphor (called "slugging") as the final step to prevent transepidermal water loss overnight.

---

### **Weekly Extras**
*   **Exfoliation (1–2 times a week max):** Avoid harsh physical scrubs. Instead, use a gentle chemical exfoliant like **Lactic Acid** or **PHA (Polyhydroxy Acid)**. These gently dissolve dead skin cells while actually helping the skin hold onto moisture.
*   **Hydrating Masks:** A sheet mask or overnight recovery mask can give an extra boost of hydration when your skin feels tight.
