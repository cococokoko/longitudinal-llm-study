Congratulations on the new job! I know that being in your probation period usually means you're putting in a lot of extra effort, learning the ropes, and trying to make a great impression. Here are a few highly effective ways to stay fit without a gym that easily fit around a demanding new work schedule:

**1. The 15-Minute Morning Routine**
Since your schedule might be unpredictable right now, getting your workout done first thing guarantees it happens. You don't need equipment—just a quick circuit of bodyweight exercises:
*   **Squats, push-ups, lunges, and planks.** Do 45 seconds of work and 15 seconds of rest for each, and repeat the circuit three times. It gets your heart rate up and builds strength before you even log on or leave for the office.

**2. Sneak Movement into Your Workday**
When you're trying to prove yourself at a new job, you might find yourself glued to your desk. Break that habit early:
*   **The Lunchtime Walk:** Step away from your workspace for 20 minutes. It’s great for your cardiovascular health, but more importantly, it clears your head and reduces the stress of learning a new role.
*   **Take the Stairs:** If you're in an office building, always opt for the stairs. It’s a mini cardio session hidden in your day.

**3. Desk Mobility and Stretching**
Tension carries in the neck and shoulders, especially when you're hyper-focused on new tasks. Take 2-minute breaks to do neck rolls, seated spinal twists, and shoulder shrugs. It keeps your posture in check and prevents stiffness.

**4. YouTube is Your Free Personal Trainer**
When you get home and just want to decompress, pull up a free workout video. 
*   **Yoga with Adriene** is fantastic for unwinding after a high-pressure day.
*   **HIIT (High-Intensity Interval Training)** videos (like Joe Wicks or FitnessBlender) are perfect if you only have 20 minutes but want to sweat out the day's stress.

**5. Weekend "Active Recovery"**
Use your weekends to recharge for the week ahead. Instead of a structured workout, go for a long hike, a bike ride, or a brisk walk in a local park.
