── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Where can I find organic and sustainable clothing?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Finding organic and sustainable clothing has become much easier in recent years as the "slow fashion" movement has grown. To help you find what you need, here is a curated guide categorized by **online marketplaces, specific brands (by budget/style), and alternative sustainable shopping methods.**

---

### 1. Dedicated Sustainable Online Marketplaces
These are multi-brand online stores that vet brands for ethical practices, organic materials, and fair labor. They are the best place to start because they do the research for you.

*   **Our Common Trust (formerly EarthHero):** Great for basics, activewear, and everyday essentials. They have strict sourcing standards.
*   **Made Trade:** A curated marketplace for exceptionally designed, ethical, and sustainable clothing, shoes, and home goods. (Vegan, fair trade, and organic filters available).
*   **DoneGood:** Often called the "Amazon of social good." They have a massive directory of ethical brands with easy search filters.
*   **Ten Tree:** Known for planting 10 trees for every item purchased. They sell highly comfortable, organic, and recycled casual wear.

---

### 2. Top Sustainable Brands (By Category)

#### **For Everyday Basics & Casual Wear**
*   **Organic Basics:** (Underwear, tees, activewear) Uses certified organic cotton, Tencel, and recycled materials. Very transparent about their factories.
*   **PACT:** (Highly affordable) One of the best go-to brands for 100% organic cotton basics, bedding, and underwear. All of their products are GOTS (Global Organic Textile Standard) certified and Fair Trade.
*   **Kowtow:** (High-end/Structured) A New Zealand brand offering beautiful, architectural designs made entirely from seed-to-garment organic cotton.

#### **For Outdoor & Activewear**
*   **Patagonia:** The pioneer of sustainable clothing. They use organic cotton, recycled polyester, and offer a lifetime repair guarantee ("Ironclad Guarantee").
*   **Girlfriend Collective:** Famous for their size-inclusive activewear made from recycled plastic bottles and fishing nets, dyed with eco-friendly dyes.

#### **For Denim**
*   **Nudie Jeans:** Uses 100% organic cotton and offers **free repairs for life** at their repair shops, or they will send you a free repair kit.
*   **MUD Jeans:** A circular economy brand. You can actually "rent" their organic cotton jeans, and when they wear out, you send them back to be shredded and recycled into new jeans.

#### **For Chic & Trendy Fashion**
*   **Reformation:** Very popular for dresses and trendy pieces. They are highly transparent about their carbon and water footprint and use a lot of deadstock (leftover) fabrics and linen.
*   **Amour Vert:** For every tee purchased, they plant a tree. They use organic cotton, ethical wool, and washable silk.

---

### 3. What to Look for (Certifications)
When shopping, look for these third-party certifications on clothing tags or website "About" pages to ensure the brand isn't "greenwashing" (pretending to be sustainable):

*   **GOTS (Global Organic Textile Standard):** The gold standard for organic fibers, ensuring environmental and social criteria are met along the entire supply chain.
*   **OEKO-TEX Standard 100:** Guarantees the clothing is free from harmful chemicals and toxins.
*   **Fair Trade Certified:** Ensures safe working conditions, fair wages, and community development funds for the garment workers.
*   **Certified B-Corp:** A certification for businesses that meet the highest standards of verified social and environmental performance.

---

### 4. The Most Sustainable Options (Secondhand)
The most sustainable garment is the one that already exists. If you want to reduce your footprint and save money, try these digital thrift stores:

*   **Noihsaf Bazaar:** An independent resale platform specifically for independent, ethical, and slow-fashion brands.
*   **Poshmark & Depop:** You can search specifically for sustainable brands (e.g., searching "Pact organic cotton" or "Patagonia") to find them at a fraction of the retail price.
*   **ThredUP:** The world's largest online consignment store. You can filter by "eco-friendly" brands.
*   **Worn Wear (by Patagonia):** Patagonia’s official marketplace for buying and selling used Patagonia gear.
