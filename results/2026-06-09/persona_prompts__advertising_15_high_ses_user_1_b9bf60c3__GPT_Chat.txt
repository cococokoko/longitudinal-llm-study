── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Decorating your home on a budget is often more about planning and creativity than spending a lot of money. A few approaches can make a significant difference:

- Start with a plan
  - Focus on one room at a time.
  - Choose a simple color palette (2–4 colors) to create a cohesive look.
  - Prioritize high-impact areas that guests and household members notice most.

- Rearrange before buying
  - Move existing furniture and accessories to different rooms.
  - Remove clutter to make spaces feel larger and more intentional.

- Use paint strategically
  - A fresh coat of paint is one of the most cost-effective upgrades.
  - Consider painting an accent wall, old furniture, or shelving.

- Shop secondhand
  - Look at thrift stores, estate sales, flea markets, and online marketplaces.
  - Solid wood furniture can often be refinished and may outlast inexpensive new pieces.

- Focus on lighting
  - Add floor lamps or table lamps to create warmth.
  - Replace outdated lampshades or light fixtures when feasible.

- Add textiles
  - Throw pillows, blankets, curtains, and rugs can quickly refresh a room.
  - Neutral larger pieces paired with a few colorful accents can be economical.

- Incorporate artwork affordably
  - Frame prints, photographs, or even pages from books and magazines.
  - Create a gallery wall using inexpensive matching frames.

- Bring in plants
  - Houseplants can add color and texture at relatively low cost.
  - Propagating plants from cuttings can be even more economical.

- Update hardware
  - New cabinet handles, drawer pulls, and doorknobs can modernize a space without major renovation.

- DIY selectively
  - Simple projects such as painting furniture, building shelves, or creating wall art can save money.
  - Focus on projects that match your skills and available time.

Sample budget allocation for refreshing a room:
- Paint: 25%
- Lighting: 20%
- Textiles (rug, pillows, curtains): 30%
- Decor and artwork: 15%
- Plants and accessories: 10%

The biggest budget mistake is buying many small decorative items before addressing fundamentals such as layout, lighting, color, and textiles. Those foundational elements usually have the greatest visual impact per dollar spent.
