### 1. The Ancient Near Eastern and Biblical Context
In the Hebrew Bible (particularly in Leviticus and Kings), Moloch is presented as a deity of the Ammonites associated with a specific, abhorrent ritual: "passing children through the fire." 

However, 20th-century biblical scholarship (notably the work of Otto Eissfeldt) introduced a significant debate regarding whether "Moloch" was originally the name of a specific god or a sacrificial term. 
* **The Deity Hypothesis:** Some scholars argue Moloch was a local manifestation of a Canaanite/Phoenician underworld deity (perhaps related to *Malik* or *Milcom*).

### 2. The Demonization in Rabbinic and Christian Demonology
In later rabbinic commentary (such as the Talmud) and medieval Christian grimoires, Moloch was fully anthropomorphized and demonized. 
* He was often depicted as a colossal bronze statue with the head of a bull and outstretched arms, heated from within by a furnace, upon which children were placed.
