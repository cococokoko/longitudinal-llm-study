── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Effective ways to reduce household energy use.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Reducing household energy use can lower utility bills, reduce wear on equipment, and decrease environmental impact. Some of the most effective strategies include:

• Improve heating and cooling efficiency
  - Use a programmable or smart thermostat.
  - Adjust temperatures a few degrees when sleeping or away from home.
  - Replace HVAC filters regularly.
  - Seal air leaks around doors, windows, and vents.
  - Add insulation in attics, walls, or crawl spaces if needed.

• Reduce water-heating energy
  - Lower the water heater temperature to a safe, efficient setting.
  - Install low-flow showerheads and faucet aerators.
  - Wash clothes in cold water when appropriate.
  - Fix leaking faucets promptly.

• Upgrade lighting
  - Replace incandescent bulbs with LED bulbs.
  - Turn off lights in unused rooms.
  - Use natural daylight whenever possible.

• Use appliances efficiently
  - Run dishwashers and washing machines with full loads.
  - Air-dry dishes or clothes when practical.
  - Clean refrigerator coils and maintain appliances according to manufacturer recommendations.
  - Choose ENERGY STAR-certified appliances when replacing old equipment.

• Reduce standby power consumption
  - Unplug devices that are rarely used.
  - Use smart power strips to cut power to electronics when not in use.
  - Enable energy-saving modes on computers, televisions, and other devices.

• Improve refrigerator and freezer efficiency
  - Keep refrigerator and freezer temperatures at recommended settings.
  - Check door seals and replace worn gaskets.
  - Avoid leaving doors open longer than necessary.

• Manage cooking energy
  - Use lids on pots and pans.
  - Match burner size to cookware size.
  - Use microwaves, toaster ovens, or pressure cookers for small meals when appropriate.

• Monitor usage
  - Review utility bills for trends.
  - Use a home energy monitor or utility-provided tracking tools to identify high-consumption devices.
  - In larger households supporting multiple family members, tracking usage patterns can help identify opportunities to save without reducing comfort.

• Consider longer-term investments
  - Install high-efficiency windows if existing windows are very inefficient.
  - Upgrade to a high-efficiency HVAC system when replacement is needed.
  - Explore rooftop solar or community solar programs if available in your area.

For many households, the biggest savings typically come from heating and cooling improvements, water-heating efficiency, and switching to LED lighting.
