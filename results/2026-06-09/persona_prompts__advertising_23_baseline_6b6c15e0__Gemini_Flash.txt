── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How can I learn to cook healthy meals quickly?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Learning to cook healthy meals quickly is all about **strategy, not just speed**. By mastering a few basic techniques, organizing your kitchen, and changing how you shop, you can easily whip up nutritious meals in 15 to 30 minutes. 

Here is a step-by-step guide to becoming a fast, healthy home cook.

---

### 1. Master the "Formula" (Stop relying on complex recipes)
Instead of following 20-step recipes, build your meals using a simple formula:
$$\text{Protein} + \text{Quick-Cooking Veggie} + \text{Healthy Carb} + \text{Flavor Booster}$$

*   **Protein:** Chicken breast, tofu, shrimp, canned beans, eggs, or salmon.
*   **Veggie:** Spinach, zucchini, bell peppers, broccoli florets, or cherry tomatoes.
*   **Carb:** Quinoa, brown rice (pre-cooked), sweet potato (microwaved), or whole-wheat couscous.
*   **Flavor:** Olive oil, garlic, lemon juice, soy sauce, sriracha, or dried herbs.

### 2. Invest in "Shortcuts" (Smart Shopping)
You don’t have to chop everything from scratch. Use the grocery store to save time:
*   **Pre-prepped veggies:** Buy pre-washed spinach, bagged broccoli florets, riced cauliflower, or spiralized zucchini.
*   **Frozen veggies:** Frozen vegetables are frozen at peak ripeness (highly nutritious) and require zero chopping. Just toss them into stir-fries, soups, or pastas.
*   **Canned goods:** Canned chickpeas, black beans, and diced tomatoes are instant bases for healthy meals.
*   **Pre-cooked grains:** Buy microwaveable pouches of quinoa, brown rice, or farro (ready in 90 seconds).

### 3. Learn the Fastest Cooking Methods
Some cooking methods are naturally faster than others:
*   **Stir-Frying:** High heat, thin cuts of meat, and veggies. Done in 10 minutes.
*   **Sheet Pan Meals:** Toss chopped veggies and a protein (like chicken breast or salmon) in olive oil and spices, lay them on a baking sheet, and bake at 400°F (200°C) for 20 minutes. Zero active cooking time and easy cleanup.
*   **Steaming in the Microwave:** You can steam broccoli, green beans, or fish in the microwave in 3–5 minutes using a damp paper towel or a covered, microwave-safe bowl.

### 4. Do "Component Prep" (Not full meal prep)
Spending 4 hours on Sunday cooking identical meals for the week can be exhausting and boring. Instead, prep **components** that you can mix and match in under 10 minutes:
*   **The Grain:** Cook a large batch of quinoa or brown rice on Sunday.
*   **The Protein:** Roast 3–4 chicken breasts or bake a block of tofu.
*   **The Veggies:** Wash and chop onions, peppers, and broccoli so they are ready to cook.
*   **The Sauce:** Make one versatile dressing or sauce (like a lemon-tahini or garlic-soy sauce).

### 5. Upgrade Your Kitchen Tools
The right tools will shave minutes off your prep time:
*   **A Sharp Chef’s Knife:** A dull knife is slow and dangerous. A sharp knife glides through veggies in seconds.
*   **An Air Fryer:** Cooks proteins and roasts veggies in half the time of a traditional oven, with barely any oil.
*   **A Garlic Press:** No need to finely mince garlic; just press it.
*   **A Microplane/Zester:** For grating garlic, ginger, or citrus zest directly into the pan for instant flavor.

---

### 3 Ultra-Fast, Healthy Meal Ideas to Start Tonight

#### 1. The 10-Minute Shrimp & Veggie Stir-Fry
*   **The Quick Prep:** Use frozen shrimp (thaws in 5 minutes in cold water) and a bag of pre-cut stir-fry veggies.
*   **The Cook:** Sauté shrimp and veggies in a hot pan with a splash of sesame oil, soy sauce, and minced garlic for 5–7 minutes.
*   **Serve over:** Microwaveable brown rice.

#### 2. The 20-Minute Sheet Pan Salmon & Asparagus
*   **The Quick Prep:** Place a salmon fillet and a handful of trimmed asparagus on a baking sheet.
*   **The Cook:** Drizzle with olive oil, salt, pepper, and lemon slices. Bake at 400°F (200°C) for 12–15 minutes.
*   **Serve with:** A side of pre-cooked quinoa.

#### 3. The 5-Minute Mediterranean Chickpea Salad (No-Cook)
*   **The Quick Prep:** Drain a can of chickpeas. 
*   **The Cook:** Toss chickpeas with halved cherry tomatoes, diced cucumber, crumbled feta cheese, olive oil, lemon juice, and dried oregano. 

### How to start this week:
Pick **one** of the meals above. Buy the ingredients (using shortcuts like pre-washed veggies), and make it. Once you see how easy it is, pick another. Speed comes with repetition!
