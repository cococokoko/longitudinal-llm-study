── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How to make my own natural beauty products?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Making your own natural beauty products is a fun, cost-effective way to avoid harsh chemicals and customize your skincare routine. 

Here is a beginner-friendly guide to getting started, including essential ingredients, basic equipment, and a few easy recipes.

### 1. Stock Your Natural Beauty Pantry
You don’t need to buy everything at once. Start with a few versatile ingredients:
*   **Carrier Oils:** Jojoba oil (great for all skin types), sweet almond oil, coconut oil, or olive oil.
*   **Butters:** Shea butter or cocoa butter (for deep moisture).
*   **Exfoliants:** Brown sugar, sea salt, coffee grounds, or ground oatmeal.
*   **Clays:** Bentonite clay or French green clay (for face masks).
*   **Liquids:** Rose water, alcohol-free witch hazel, aloe vera gel, and raw honey.
*   **Essential Oils:** Lavender (calming), Tea Tree (antibacterial/acne), Sweet Orange (brightening). *Note: Always dilute essential oils in a carrier oil; never apply them directly to the skin.*

### 2. Basic Equipment
*   Glass jars or tins for storage (Mason jars work great)
*   A double boiler (or a heat-safe glass bowl placed over a pot of simmering water)
*   Measuring cups and spoons
*   A small whisk or dedicated mixing spoons

---

### 3. Easy Beginner Recipes

#### A. Brown Sugar & Vanilla Body Scrub
*Great for exfoliating dry skin on your body (too harsh for the face).*
*   **Ingredients:** 1 cup brown sugar, ½ cup carrier oil (like coconut or sweet almond oil), 1 teaspoon vanilla extract (or 10 drops of lavender essential oil).
*   **Instructions:** Mix all ingredients in a bowl until it looks like wet sand. Store in an airtight glass jar. Use in the shower 1–2 times a week.

#### B. Soothing Honey & Oat Face Mask
*Great for calming irritated or acne-prone skin.*
*   **Ingredients:** 2 tablespoons finely ground oatmeal, 1 tablespoon raw honey, 1 teaspoon water or milk.
*   **Instructions:** Mix into a paste. Apply to a clean face and leave on for 15 minutes. Rinse off with warm water. (Make this fresh each time you use it).

#### C. Simple 3-Ingredient Lip Balm
*Deeply moisturizing and protects against chapped lips.*
*   **Ingredients:** 1 tablespoon beeswax pellets (use candelilla wax for a vegan option), 1 tablespoon shea butter, 1 tablespoon coconut oil.
*   **Instructions:** Place all ingredients in a double boiler over medium heat. Stir gently until completely melted. Carefully pour the liquid into lip balm tins or tubes. Let it sit undisturbed for 30 minutes to harden.

#### D. Rose & Witch Hazel Facial Toner
*Balances the skin's pH and tightens pores.*
*   **Ingredients:** ½ cup alcohol-free witch hazel, ½ cup rose water.
*   **Instructions:** Pour both ingredients into a spray bottle and shake well. Spritz on your face after cleansing and before moisturizing.

### 4. Crucial Safety and Storage Tips
*   **Beware of Water:** Products made *without* water (like lip balms, body butters, and oil serums) can last 6 months to a year. Products made *with* water (like lotions or aloe-based sprays) will grow mold and bacteria within a week unless you use a broad-spectrum preservative. Keep water-based DIYs in the fridge and throw them out after 7–10 days.
*   **Sanitize Everything:** Wash your bowls, spoons, and storage containers with hot, soapy water and wipe them down with rubbing alcohol before making your products.
*   **Always Patch Test:** Even natural ingredients can cause allergic reactions. Apply a small amount of your new product to your inner wrist and wait 24 hours to ensure you don't have a reaction.
*   **Keep out of Sunlight:** Store your creations in a cool, dark place to prevent the oils from going rancid.
