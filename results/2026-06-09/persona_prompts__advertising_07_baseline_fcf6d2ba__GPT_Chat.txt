── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Effective ways to reduce household energy use.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Reducing household energy use can lower utility bills and reduce environmental impact. Some of the most effective approaches include:

• Improve home insulation
  - Add insulation to attics, walls, and floors where needed.
  - Seal air leaks around doors, windows, vents, and utility penetrations.
  - Use weatherstripping and caulking to prevent drafts.

• Upgrade lighting
  - Replace incandescent and halogen bulbs with LED bulbs.
  - Turn off lights when rooms are unoccupied.
  - Use motion sensors or timers in appropriate areas.

• Optimize heating and cooling
  - Install a programmable or smart thermostat.
  - Adjust thermostat settings a few degrees to reduce energy use.
  - Replace HVAC filters regularly.
  - Schedule routine maintenance for heating and cooling systems.

• Use energy-efficient appliances
  - Choose appliances with high energy-efficiency ratings when replacing old units.
  - Run dishwashers and washing machines with full loads.
  - Use energy-saving settings whenever available.

• Reduce water-heating energy
  - Lower the water heater temperature to a safe, efficient setting.
  - Install low-flow showerheads and faucet aerators.
  - Insulate hot-water pipes and the water heater if appropriate.
  - Wash clothes in cold water when suitable.

• Manage electronics
  - Unplug devices that consume standby power or use smart power strips.
  - Enable sleep or power-saving modes on computers and other electronics.
  - Turn off equipment when not in use.

• Improve window efficiency
  - Use insulated curtains or blinds.
  - Close blinds during hot days to reduce cooling needs.
  - Open curtains during sunny winter days to gain passive solar heat.

• Adjust daily habits
  - Air-dry clothes when possible.
  - Use ceiling fans to improve comfort and reduce reliance on air conditioning.
  - Cook with microwave ovens, pressure cookers, or air fryers when appropriate, as they often use less energy than conventional ovens.

• Consider renewable energy
  - Install rooftop solar panels if feasible.
  - Explore community solar programs where available.

For most households, the biggest savings usually come from improving insulation and air sealing, optimizing heating and cooling systems, switching to LED lighting, and replacing older inefficient appliances.
