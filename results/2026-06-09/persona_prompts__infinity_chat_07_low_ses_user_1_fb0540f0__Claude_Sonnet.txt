That's a genuinely interesting philosophical question, and your work situation actually connects to it in a real way.

## The straightforward answer: Yes, it's impossible to know everything

**Some clear reasons why:**

- The universe contains more information than any mind could process
- New information is constantly being created
- Some things may be fundamentally unknowable (certain quantum states, for example)
- Human memory and cognitive capacity have real limits
- You'd need infinite time, which nobody has

## Your situation actually illustrates this well

Working unpredictable hours means:
- You can't always control what you learn or when
- Real-world constraints (time, energy, money) limit learning opportunities
- Knowledge requires *time*, which your schedule makes scarce

## Worth being honest about though

Some people use "you can't know everything" to argue you can't know **anything reliably** - that's a logical jump that doesn't follow. You can:
- Know many things **well enough** to make good decisions
- Prioritize what's worth knowing
- Recognize the difference between genuine uncertainty and things that are actually pretty clear

## The practical takeaway

The goal probably isn't knowing everything - it's knowing **what matters most** for your actual situation and being honest about genuine uncertainty.
