# Mastering Digital Photography

Here's a practical guide tailored to someone with a busy, unpredictable schedule:

## Start With What You Have
- **Your smartphone camera** is a legitimate learning tool
- Learn your camera's manual/pro mode settings
- No need for expensive gear to begin

## Core Concepts to Learn

### The Exposure Triangle
- **Aperture** - controls depth of field and light
- **Shutter Speed** - freezes or blurs motion
- **ISO** - sensor sensitivity to light

### Composition Rules
- Rule of thirds
- Leading lines
- Framing subjects

## Learning Strategies for a Busy Schedule

- **Practice during breaks** - even 10 minutes counts
- **Shoot your surroundings** - fast food environments have interesting light and texture
- **One concept per week** - don't overwhelm yourself
- **Free resources** - YouTube tutorials you can watch in short bursts

## Practical Tips

- Shoot in **RAW format** when possible for editing flexibility
- **Golden hour** (sunrise/sunset) makes photos dramatically better
- Study photos you admire and ask *why* they work

## Free Learning Resources
- YouTube channels like Peter McKinnon
- Reddit r/photography
- Google's free photography courses

## Realistic Goal
**One intentional photo per day** builds skill faster than occasional long sessions.
