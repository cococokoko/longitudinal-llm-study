  - Rotate toys and introduce new activities.
  - Use puzzle feeders or training games.
  - Provide opportunities for exploration and natural behaviors.

- Maintain a safe, comfortable environment
  - Give your pet a clean resting area.
  - Remove access to toxic plants, foods, medications, and hazardous objects.
  - Ensure proper temperature, lighting, and habitat conditions.

- Practice good grooming and hygiene
  - Brush as needed for your pet's coat type.
  - Trim nails when necessary.
  - Follow veterinary guidance on dental care, bathing, and ear cleaning.

- Offer social interaction and affection
  - Spend quality time together every day.
  - Learn your pet's preferences and body language.
  - Respect their need for rest and personal space.

  - Changes in appetite, energy, behavior, weight, drinking habits, or bathroom habits can indicate a problem.
