Here is a 3-day Osaka itinerary designed specifically with you in mind. As a single parent balancing work and childcare, you deserve a trip that is highly engaging for your child but logistically easy and low-stress for you. 

To make things as smooth as possible, **I highly recommend staying in the Namba area**. It has direct, easy train access to the airport, meaning you won't have to drag luggage and a child across the city on your final day.

### **Day 1: Ocean Wonders & Easy Entertainment (Bay Area)**
*This day is localized to one area so you don’t have to spend much time on trains.*

*   **Morning (9:30 AM): Osaka Aquarium Kaiyukan**
    *   Start your trip at one of the world’s largest aquariums. It’s incredibly stroller-friendly and mesmerizing for kids of all ages (the whale sharks are a massive hit). 
*   **Lunch (12:30 PM): Tempozan Marketplace**
    *   Located right next to the aquarium. It has a food court with plenty of kid-friendly options, so you don't have to stress about finding a quiet restaurant.
*   **Afternoon (2:00 PM): Legoland Discovery Center OR Tempozan Ferris Wheel**
    *   Both are inside/next to the Marketplace. Legoland is great if your child needs interactive playtime (and you need to sit on a bench with a coffee). The Ferris Wheel offers a relaxing, sit-down view of the city.
*   **Evening: Early Dinner & Rest**
    *   Head back to your hotel area. Grab an easy dinner at a family restaurant (like Gusto or Saizeriya, which are very welcoming to kids) or pick up some fun bentos from a convenience store if you're both exhausted.

### **Day 2: Castles, Play, & Neon Lights**
*A mix of culture and burning off energy.*

*   **Morning (10:00 AM): Osaka Castle & Park**
    *   The park is fantastic for letting kids run around safely. Instead of climbing all the stairs inside the castle, consider taking the **Gozabune Boat Ride** in the moat—it’s relaxing for you and an adventure for them.
*   **Lunch (12:00 PM): Jo-Terrace Osaka**
    *   A modern dining complex right outside the castle with plenty of spacious, family-friendly cafes.
*   **Afternoon (1:30 PM): Kids Plaza Osaka**
    *   *This is a lifesaver for parents.* It’s a massive, indoor interactive children’s museum. Your child can do science experiments, play in a mock grocery store, and climb through a giant indoor playground while you take a well-deserved breather.
*   **Evening (5:30 PM): Dotonbori**
    *   Head here before it gets too crowded. The giant moving crab signs and the neon Glico Running Man are visually thrilling for kids. Grab some casual street food like *takoyaki* (octopus balls) or melon pan for a fun, informal dinner.

### **Day 3: Giant Lions, Parks, & Departure**
*Your flight is at 7:00 PM, which means you need to be at Kansai International Airport (KIX) by 5:00 PM. You should aim to catch the airport train around 4:00 PM.*

*   **Morning (10:00 AM): Namba Yasaka Shrine**
    *   Start the day with a quick visit to this shrine. It features a massive, 12-meter-tall lion head building. It’s a fantastic, quick photo op that kids usually find really cool, and it's right in the Namba area.
*   **Lunch (11:30 AM): Namba Parks**
    *   This is a massive shopping complex with a beautiful, terraced rooftop garden. You can grab a nice lunch here and let your child explore the outdoor garden paths safely away from traffic.
*   **Afternoon (1:30 PM): Round1 Stadium or Souvenir Shopping**
    *   If you have older kids, Round1 in Sennichimae has arcade games and claw machines to kill a couple of hours. If you have a younger child, grab a treat at a cafe and do some light souvenir shopping in the Namba area.
*   **3:30 PM: Collect Luggage**
    *   Head back to your hotel to grab your bags.
*   **4:00 PM: Nankai Rapi:t Train to KIX**
    *   Board the Nankai Rapi:t train from Namba Station. It’s a reserved-seat, retro-futuristic blue train (kids love the look of it) that takes exactly 38 minutes to get to the airport. 
*   **4:45 PM: Arrive at KIX**
    *   Check in, grab a snack, and get ready for your 7:00 PM flight!

**A Quick Parent Survival Tip for Japan:**
Use *Takuhaibin* (luggage forwarding) if you are coming from another city to Osaka, or utilize the coin lockers at train stations. Not having to wrangle a suitcase *and* a child at the same time is the best gift you can give yourself on this trip!
