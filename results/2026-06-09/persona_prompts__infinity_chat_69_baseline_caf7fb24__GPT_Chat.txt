What is the difference between analysis and design? Can one begin to design without analysis? Why? Be concise.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
- Analysis focuses on understanding and defining the problem, requirements, constraints, and user needs.
- Design focuses on creating a solution that satisfies those requirements, including structure, components, and implementation approaches.

Can one begin to design without analysis?
- Yes, a preliminary or conceptual design can begin with limited analysis, especially in exploratory or iterative projects.
- However, meaningful design requires some level of analysis. Without understanding the problem and requirements, the design may fail to meet user needs, violate constraints, or solve the wrong problem. Analysis provides the foundation for effective design.
