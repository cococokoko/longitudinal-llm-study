── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Give me a tip to be more organized at work. I'm a high school teacher.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here is one highly effective, game-changing tip specifically for high school teachers:

**Implement a 15-Minute "Closing Shift" at the End of Your Day.**

Borrowing a concept from retail and food service, treat the last 15 minutes before you leave the building as your "closing shift." High school teaching is incredibly fast-paced, and mornings can be chaotic. By doing a closing shift, you are doing a massive favor for your "tomorrow morning" self. 

Here is what your 15-minute closing shift should include:

1. **Clear the physical space:** Erase the whiteboards, straighten the desks, and clear your teacher desk of the day's clutter. Put stray papers into a designated "to grade" or "to file" tray.
2. **Prep the digital space:** Close out of today's tabs. Open the slides, videos, or attendance tabs you will need for your *first period* class tomorrow. 
3. **Stage the physical materials:** Put the handouts, rubrics, or lab materials for tomorrow's first class right on your desk or by the door. 
4. **Write the "Top 3" sticky note:** Write down the 3 most important tasks you need to do during your planning period tomorrow (e.g., "Grade AP essays," "Email Sarah's parents," "Make copies for Period 4"). Stick it to your keyboard.

**Why this works:** 
When you walk in the next morning, you won't be met with the ghost of yesterday's chaos. If a student comes in early crying, or the copier breaks, or you get pulled into a surprise IEP meeting, your first period is already completely ready to go. It also creates a psychological boundary that tells your brain, "Work is done for the day," helping you leave the stress in the classroom.
