── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How to say a formal thank you for your time in the subject line of an email?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
When writing a formal email, the best subject lines are clear, concise, and provide context. Simply writing "Thank you for your time" can sometimes look like spam, so it is always best to include your name or the topic you discussed. 

Here are a few formal ways to say "thank you for your time" in a subject line, depending on the situation:

**After a Job Interview:**
*   Thank You: [Job Title] Interview – [Your Name]
*   Thank You – [Your Name] – [Job Title] Candidate
*   Appreciation for our interview on [Date] – [Your Name]

**After a Business Meeting or Consultation:**
*   Thank You: Our Meeting Regarding [Topic/Project]
*   Thank You for Your Time Today – [Your Name]
*   Following Up & Thank You – [Topic of Meeting]
*   Appreciation for our discussion on [Date]

**After Receiving Advice, Mentorship, or Help:**
*   Thank You for Your Guidance – [Your Name]
*   Sincere Thanks for Your Time and Insights
*   Thank You: [Topic Discussed] – [Your Name]

**General / All-Purpose:**
*   Thank You – [Your Name]
*   Thank You: [Topic]
*   Appreciation for your time and assistance

**Pro-Tip:** Use Title Case (capitalize the first letter of the major words) to make the subject line look polished and professional.
