You know that the customer recently started a new job and is still on probation.

── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How can I optimize my home office for productivity?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Congratulations on the new job! Since you are still in your probationary period, optimizing your home office right now is a brilliant move. Setting up a dedicated, efficient workspace will help you hit the ground running, learn the ropes quickly, and make a stellar impression on your new team. 

Here are the best ways to optimize your home office for maximum productivity during this crucial time:

**1. Perfect Your Video Call Setup**
Since you are meeting many of your colleagues and managers for the first time, your virtual presence matters. 
*   **Lighting:** Position your desk so you are facing a window for natural light. If that's not possible, invest in a ring light or a good desk lamp and place it behind your webcam. Avoid having a bright window directly behind you.
*   **Background:** Keep the area behind you clean and uncluttered. A tidy background signals professionalism and organization.
*   **Audio:** Use a good quality headset or an external microphone. Clear audio is actually more important than perfect video for effective communication.

**2. Prioritize Ergonomics**
You want to focus on your new tasks, not on a backache. 
*   **The Chair:** Invest in a supportive, adjustable ergonomic chair. 
*   **Monitor Height:** The top of your screen should be at or slightly below eye level to prevent neck strain. Use a laptop stand or a stack of sturdy books if necessary.
*   **Posture:** Your feet should be flat on the floor, and your elbows should be at a 90-degree angle when typing.

**3. Minimize Distractions**
During your probation, you'll be absorbing a lot of new information. 
*   **Declutter:** Keep only what you need for work on your desk. Clutter competes for your visual attention and drains mental energy.
*   **Digital Boundaries:** Put your personal phone in another room or on "Do Not Disturb" during your core working hours so you can fully focus on onboarding materials and new projects.

**4. Create "Work" and "Home" Boundaries**
When you're trying to prove yourself in a new role, it's easy to accidentally overwork and burn out.
*   **Dedicated Space:** If possible, set up your office in a room with a door. If you are in a shared space, use a rug, a bookshelf, or even a folding screen to visually separate your "office" from your living area.
*   **End-of-Day Routine:** When you finish work, close your laptop, tidy your desk, and physically walk away. This signals to your brain that the workday is over.

**5. Keep Essentials Within Reach**
Keep a notepad and pen handy. As a new hire, you will be taking a lot of notes during training sessions and meetings. Having water and healthy snacks nearby will also keep your energy up without requiring you to wander into the kitchen and lose your focus.

Taking the time to set this up now will give you the foundation you need to excel and confidently pass your probation. Best of luck in the new role!
