── System message ──────────────────────────────────────────────────
You know that the customer works in a warehouse position that requires long hours.

── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
What is the difference between analysis and design? Can one begin to design without analysis? Why? Be concise.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
**Analysis** is defining *what* needs to be done (understanding the problem). 
**Design** is planning *how* to do it (creating the solution).

**Can you begin to design without analysis?** No. 

**Why?** 
Think of it like working in the warehouse: Analysis is reading the manifest to know *what* items need to be shipped and to which dock. Design is planning *how* to stack the pallets and route the forklifts to get them there efficiently. 

If you start designing (stacking) without analysis (reading the manifest), you might build a perfectly stable pallet, but it will be the wrong items going to the wrong truck. You cannot build a successful solution if you don't first understand the problem.
