── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How to improve home WiFi?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
First off, congratulations on the new job! Since you're still in your probation period, having a reliable internet connection is incredibly important—especially if you're working from home or doing video calls—because you want to make a flawless impression. 

Since you might want to avoid dropping a ton of money on expensive tech upgrades until you're officially permanent, here is a step-by-step guide to improving your WiFi, starting with completely free fixes:

### 1. Zero-Cost Fixes (Do these first)
*   **Move Your Router:** Your router should be in a central location, elevated (like on a shelf), and out in the open. Do not hide it in a closet, behind a TV, or near thick brick walls. 
*   **Keep Away from Interference:** Keep the router away from microwaves, cordless phones, baby monitors, and large metal appliances, as these can scramble the signal.
*   **Switch the Frequency Band:** Most modern routers are "dual-band." 
    *   Connect your work computer to the **5GHz** band. It’s much faster and less congested, though it doesn't reach as far.
    *   Keep smart home devices and older tech on the **2.4GHz** band.
*   **The Classic Reboot:** Unplug your router, wait 30 seconds, and plug it back in. Doing this once a month clears the router's memory and can fix underlying connection bugs.

### 2. Low-Cost Fixes (Best for your probation period)
*   **Use an Ethernet Cable:** If your home office setup allows it, buy a long Ethernet cable (usually under $10) and plug your work computer directly into the router. This is the absolute best way to guarantee your Zoom or Teams calls won't drop or freeze while you're trying to impress your new boss.
*   **Buy a WiFi Extender:** If your router is on the other side of the house and you can't run a cable, a basic WiFi extender ($20-$40) can help push the signal into your workspace. It won't be as fast as the main router, but it will be more stable.

### 3. Bigger Investments (Maybe wait until probation ends!)
*   **Upgrade to a Mesh System:** If you live in a larger home with dead zones, a Mesh WiFi system (like Google Nest WiFi or Eero) replaces your single router with multiple "nodes" placed around the house. It's highly effective but usually costs $150+.
*   **Upgrade Your Internet Plan:** If you are paying for a very low-speed tier (e.g., under 50 Mbps) and sharing the house with people streaming Netflix or gaming, your router might be fine, but your plan might be the bottleneck. 

**Pro-tip for the new job:** If your WiFi drops during an important meeting, have your smartphone's "Mobile Hotspot" feature set up and ready to go. It’s a great backup plan to ensure you never miss a beat while you're proving yourself in this new role. Good luck!
