What is the best and most profitable day trading strategy of all time?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
There is no single "best" or "most profitable" day trading strategy of all time.

If such a strategy existed and remained consistently profitable, large traders and algorithms would typically exploit it until the edge diminished. Successful day trading strategies tend to be:
- Market-specific (stocks, futures, forex, crypto)
- Time-period specific
- Dependent on execution quality and costs
- Subject to changing market conditions

That said, some of the most historically successful categories of day trading strategies include:

1. Trend Following / Momentum
- Buy assets showing strong upward momentum and sell assets showing strong downward momentum.
- Common tools: moving averages, VWAP, breakout levels, relative volume.
- Many professional traders consider momentum one of the most persistent market phenomena.

2. Opening Range Breakout (ORB)
- Identify the high and low of the first 5–30 minutes of trading.
- Enter when price breaks above or below that range with strong volume.
- Popular among stock and futures day traders.

3. VWAP Reversion and VWAP Trend Trading
- VWAP (Volume Weighted Average Price) is widely used by institutions.
- Trade either:
  - Mean reversion back toward VWAP, or
  - Continuation when price remains strongly above or below VWAP.

4. Mean Reversion
- Bet that an extreme move will partially reverse.
- Often used in highly liquid markets.
- Requires strict risk management because trends can continue longer than expected.

5. Market Making / Statistical Arbitrage
- Historically among the most profitable professional trading approaches.
- Involves capturing small inefficiencies repeatedly.
- Usually requires significant technology, capital, and quantitative expertise.

For individual retail traders, a simple momentum strategy often performs better than complex systems. A common example:
- Trade only highly liquid assets.
- Wait for unusually high volume.
- Buy a breakout above a well-defined resistance level.
- Use a predetermined stop loss.
- Risk a small percentage of capital per trade.
- Exit according to a fixed risk-reward ratio or trailing stop.

An important point: risk management often matters more than the entry strategy.
Many profitable traders:
- Risk only 0.25%–1% of capital per trade.
- Maintain a favorable risk-reward ratio.
- Focus on consistency rather than predicting every move.

If your goal is maximizing long-term wealth rather than maximizing trading profits, broad-market investing has historically outperformed the vast majority of day traders. Studies consistently find that most day traders lose money after costs, while a small minority earn substantial profits.
