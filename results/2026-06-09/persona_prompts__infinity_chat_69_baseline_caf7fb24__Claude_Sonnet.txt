What is the difference between analysis and design? Can one begin to design without analysis? Why? Be concise.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
## Analysis vs. Design

| | Analysis | Design |
|---|---|---|
| **Focus** | **What** the system must do | **How** the system will do it |
| **Goal** | Understand the problem | Build the solution |
| **Output** | Requirements, models of the problem domain | Blueprints, architecture, specifications |

---

## Can You Design Without Analysis?

**Technically yes, practically no.**

**Why not:**
- Without analysis, you don't fully understand the **problem** you're solving
- You risk building the **wrong system** correctly
- Gaps in requirements lead to **costly rework** later
- Design decisions lack **justification**

**The core reason:** Design answers *how*, but without analysis establishing *what*, there's no valid target to design toward. You'd be solving an undefined problem — essentially guessing.
