── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How can I improve my credit score quickly?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here are some effective ways to improve your credit score relatively quickly:

## Highest Impact Actions

- **Pay down credit card balances** - Reducing your credit utilization below 30% (ideally below 10%) can show results within a billing cycle
- **Pay all bills on time** - Payment history is the biggest factor (~35% of your score)
- **Dispute errors on your credit report** - Check all three bureaus (Equifax, Experian, TransUnion) for mistakes

## Medium-Term Strategies

- **Become an authorized user** on someone else's account with good history
- **Ask for a credit limit increase** without spending more (lowers utilization ratio)
- **Keep old accounts open** to maintain credit history length
- **Avoid applying for multiple new accounts** at once

## Practical Steps to Start

1. Get your free credit reports at **AnnualCreditReport.com**
2. Set up automatic payments to avoid missed due dates
3. Track your utilization ratio monthly

## Realistic Expectations

- Small improvements: **1-2 months**
- Significant changes: **3-6 months**
- Major recovery (from serious issues): **1-2+ years**

## Things to Avoid

- Credit repair scams promising instant fixes
- Closing old credit cards
- Maxing out cards even temporarily
