── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How do I start a vegetable garden in my backyard?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Starting a vegetable garden is a rewarding project! Here are the key steps:

## Planning
- **Choose a location** with at least 6-8 hours of direct sunlight daily
- **Start small** - a 4x8 or 10x10 foot bed is manageable for beginners
- **Decide on a layout** - raised beds, in-ground rows, or containers

## Preparing the Soil
- Test your soil pH (most vegetables prefer 6.0-7.0)
- Amend with compost to improve drainage and nutrients
- Till or loosen the soil to about 12 inches deep

## Choosing What to Grow
- Start with easier vegetables like **tomatoes, zucchini, lettuce, beans, or radishes**
- Consider your climate and growing season
- Check seed packets for days to maturity

## Getting Started
- Decide between **seeds vs. transplants** (transplants are easier for beginners)
- Follow spacing recommendations to avoid overcrowding
- Plant after your last frost date

## Ongoing Care
- Water consistently - most vegetables need about 1 inch per week
- Mulch to retain moisture and suppress weeds
- Fertilize periodically
- Watch for pests and disease

## Helpful Resources
- Your local cooperative extension office often has region-specific advice
- The Old Farmer's Almanac has planting calendars by zip code
