Creating a successful YouTube channel.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
A tenured professor has a strong advantage on YouTube: credibility, deep expertise, and access to ideas that most creators cannot easily replicate. The challenge is translating expertise into content that a broad audience wants to watch.

Key principles:

- Teach one idea at a time.
  - Academic audiences tolerate complexity.
  - YouTube audiences respond to clarity and focus.
  - A video titled "Why Ancient Rome Ran Out of Money" will often outperform "Fiscal Structures in Late Republican Rome."

- Lead with curiosity.
  - Start videos with a question, mystery, controversy, or surprising fact.
  - Give viewers a reason to stay for the answer.

- Optimize for audience value, not academic completeness.
  - A 15-minute video that explains 80% of an idea clearly is often more effective than a 45-minute lecture that covers 100%.

- Develop a recognizable format.
  - Examples:
    - "One Big Idea in 10 Minutes"
    - "What Most People Get Wrong About..."
    - "The Research Behind..."
    - "Explaining Today's News Through History/Economics/Science"

- Use your research strategically.
  - Turn papers into:
    - Explainer videos
    - Myth-busting videos
    - Current-events analysis
    - Interviews with other experts

- Invest heavily in titles and thumbnails.
  - These often matter more for growth than small improvements in production quality.
  - Focus on clear benefits and curiosity.

- Publish consistently.
  - Weekly is often sufficient.
  - Consistency builds audience expectations and helps the recommendation system learn your audience.

- Study successful educational channels.
  - Depending on your field:
    - Veritasium
    - History Matters
    - Wendover Productions
    - Sabine Hossenfelder
    - Economics Explained
    - PBS-affiliated educational channels

A practical starting strategy:

1. Identify a narrow niche where you have exceptional expertise.
2. Create 20 video ideas before filming anything.
3. Publish one high-quality video per week for six months.
4. Review analytics:
   - Click-through rate
   - Audience retention
   - Watch time
5. Double down on topics that outperform.
