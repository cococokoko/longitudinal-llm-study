── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I work in a warehouse position that requires long hours.

How can I learn to cook healthy meals quickly?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Working a physically demanding warehouse job with long hours means you need high-quality fuel for energy, but you likely have zero desire to stand in a kitchen for an hour when you get home. 

The secret to quick, healthy cooking isn't learning fancy chef skills; it’s about **minimizing active cooking time and cleanup.** 

Here is a practical guide to making healthy meals quickly when you are exhausted:

### 1. Master the "Formula" (Stop Using Recipes)
Following recipes takes mental energy. Instead, memorize a basic meal formula: **Protein + Complex Carb + Vegetable + Sauce/Spice.**
*   **Protein:** Chicken breast, ground turkey, tofu, eggs, canned tuna, or beans.
*   **Carb:** Brown rice, quinoa, sweet potatoes, whole wheat pasta, or tortillas.
*   **Veggie:** Broccoli, spinach, bell peppers, green beans.
*   **Flavor:** Store-bought salsa, teriyaki sauce, hot sauce, or a simple spice blend (like taco seasoning).

### 2. Embrace "Healthy Shortcuts"
You do not need to chop everything from scratch. Use the grocery store to do the prep work for you:
*   **Frozen Vegetables:** They are just as healthy as fresh, already chopped, and won't go bad if you work overtime and forget about them. Microwave them in minutes.
*   **Pre-cooked Grains:** Buy the 90-second microwave pouches of brown rice or quinoa. 
*   **Rotisserie Chicken:** Buy one on your day off, shred the meat, and keep it in the fridge. Add it to wraps, salads, or rice bowls all week.
*   **Canned Beans:** Black beans, chickpeas, and lentils are incredibly healthy, cheap, and ready to eat after a quick rinse.

### 3. Invest in "Set It and Forget It" Appliances
If you can afford it, these appliances will change your life because they cook while you shower or relax:
*   **The Air Fryer:** Cooks chicken, fish, and roasted vegetables in 10–15 minutes with almost zero preheat time. 
*   **The Slow Cooker (Crockpot):** Throw ingredients (like chicken breasts, a jar of salsa, and black beans) into it before your shift. When you get home 10 hours later, dinner is hot and ready.
*   **A Rice Cooker:** Perfect rice every time, and it keeps it warm until you are ready to eat.

### 4. Rely on Sheet Pan Meals
If you are using an oven, use the "sheet pan" method. 
1. Line a baking sheet with foil or parchment paper (for zero cleanup).
2. Toss chopped protein (like sausage or chicken) and veggies (like broccoli and potatoes) in olive oil, salt, and pepper.
3. Spread them on the pan and bake at 400°F (200°C) for 20–25 minutes. 

### 5. Cook Once, Eat Thrice (Batching)
Never cook just one portion. If you are going to take 20 minutes to cook dinner, double or triple the ingredients. 
*   Eat one portion for dinner.
*   Put one in a container for tomorrow's warehouse lunch.
*   Put one in the fridge for tomorrow's dinner. 

### A Beginner’s 10-Minute Meal Plan to Try This Week:
*   **Meal 1 (Burrito Bowl):** 90-second microwave brown rice + rinsed canned black beans + shredded rotisserie chicken + a handful of spinach + salsa. (Time: 3 minutes).
*   **Meal 2 (Sheet Pan Sausage):** Sliced pre-cooked chicken sausage + a bag of frozen bell peppers and onions. Toss in olive oil, bake at 400°F for 20 mins. (Active time: 2 minutes).
*   **Meal 3 (Tuna Wrap):** Canned tuna mixed with a little mayo or Greek yogurt + spinach + whole wheat tortilla. Serve with a side of baby carrots. (Time: 5 minutes).

**Your First Step:** Next time you go to the store, buy a rotisserie chicken, two bags of frozen mixed vegetables, and three pouches of 90-second microwave rice. You'll instantly have the foundation for three days of effortless meals.
