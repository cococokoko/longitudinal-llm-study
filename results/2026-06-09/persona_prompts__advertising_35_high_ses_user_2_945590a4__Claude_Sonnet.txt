Here's a practical guide tailored to someone with professional expertise to share:

## Equipment Essentials
- **Microphone**: USB condenser mic (Blue Yeti, Audio-Technica AT2020) - $100-200 range
- **Headphones**: Closed-back for monitoring audio
- **Pop filter**: Reduces harsh sounds
- **Acoustic treatment**: Record in a quiet room with soft furnishings

## Planning Your Show
- **Define your niche** - legal topics, career advice, industry insights
- **Target audience** - clients, law students, fellow professionals
- **Episode format** - interviews, solo commentary, case discussions
- **Consistent schedule** - weekly or bi-weekly works well

## Recording & Editing
- **Software**: Audacity (free), Adobe Audition, or GarageBand
- **Remote interviews**: Riverside.fm or Zencastr for quality recordings
- **Keep episodes focused** - 20-45 minutes is typically ideal

## Distribution
- Upload to **Spotify, Apple Podcasts, Google Podcasts**
- Use hosting platforms like **Buzzsprout or Anchor**

- Leverage your **professional network**
- Cross-promote on **LinkedIn**
- Invite **notable guests**
- Repurpose content as articles
