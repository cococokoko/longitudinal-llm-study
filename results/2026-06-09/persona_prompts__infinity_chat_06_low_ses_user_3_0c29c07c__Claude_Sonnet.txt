── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I recently started a new job and am still on probation.

How do I understand what I want?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
That's a thoughtful question, especially during a transition period. Here are some ways to think about it:

## Understanding What You Want at Work

**Pay attention to your reactions**
- Notice what energizes you vs. drains you during the day
- What tasks make time pass quickly?
- What do you dread or avoid?

**Separate layers of "want"**
- What you want *right now* (comfort, approval)
- What you want *long-term* (growth, purpose, stability)
- These sometimes conflict

**Ask yourself honest questions**
- What would success look like in 1-2 years?
- What would make you proud of this job?
- What are you willing to tolerate vs. what's a dealbreaker?

**Be careful about probation pressure**
- New job anxiety can make you want to please everyone, which can blur your own preferences
- It's worth distinguishing *their* expectations from *your* actual goals

**Practical steps**
- Keep a simple journal of daily reactions
- Talk to people in roles you might want
- Give yourself permission to not have it figured out yet

## A Realistic Note
It often takes **3-6 months** to genuinely understand what you want from a new role, because you're still learning what the job actually is.

Is there a specific aspect you're trying to figure out - career direction, work-life balance, something else?
