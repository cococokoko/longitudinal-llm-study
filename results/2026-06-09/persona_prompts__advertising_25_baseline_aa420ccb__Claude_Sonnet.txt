── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How can I decorate my apartment on a minimalist budget?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here are some practical ways to decorate minimally and affordably:

## Start With What You Have
- Rearrange existing furniture for a fresh feel
- Declutter first - less stuff often looks better
- Repurpose items you already own

## Low-Cost Decorating Ideas
- **Thrift stores and Facebook Marketplace** for furniture and art
- **Plants** - even small ones add life and are relatively cheap
- **Removable wallpaper or wall decals** for renters
- **String lights** for warm, inexpensive ambiance
- **Throw pillows and blankets** to update a space cheaply

## Free or Nearly Free Options
- Frame interesting pages from old books or magazines
- Use natural elements like branches, stones, or pinecones
- Download and print free art from museum websites
- Swap items with friends

## Smart Buying Strategies
- Focus on a few quality pieces rather than many cheap ones
- Stick to a simple color palette so things coordinate easily
- Shop end-of-season sales
- Check IKEA for affordable basics

## Minimalist Principles That Save Money
- Empty space is actually part of the design
- Fewer, more intentional items look more polished
- Good lighting matters more than lots of decor

**The biggest tip:** Decide on a style or color scheme first so you avoid buying things that don't work together.
