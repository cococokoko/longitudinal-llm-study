Making your own natural beauty products can be simple and inexpensive, but it's important to prioritize safety and hygiene since homemade products don't contain the preservatives used in commercial formulations.

Some beginner-friendly options:

• Simple facial oil
  - 2 tablespoons jojoba oil or sweet almond oil
  - Optional: 1–2 drops vitamin E oil
  - Mix in a clean glass bottle.
  - Apply a few drops to damp skin.

• Basic sugar scrub
  - 1/2 cup sugar
  - 1/4 cup coconut oil or olive oil
  - Mix until combined.
  - Use gently on the body, not on sensitive facial skin.
  - Store in a clean container and avoid getting water into it.

• Oatmeal face mask
  - 2 tablespoons finely ground oats
  - Enough water or plain yogurt to form a paste
  - Apply for 10–15 minutes, then rinse.

• Lip balm
  - 1 tablespoon beeswax pellets
  - 1 tablespoon coconut oil
  - 1 tablespoon shea butter
  - Melt together, pour into a small container, and let cool.

• Hair conditioning treatment
  - 1 tablespoon coconut oil or argan oil
  - Apply lightly to hair ends for 20–30 minutes before shampooing.

Safety tips:
• Patch-test any new product on a small area of skin for 24–48 hours.
• Be cautious with essential oils; they can cause irritation and should be diluted properly.
• Use clean utensils and containers.
• Products containing water are more likely to grow bacteria and should be refrigerated and used quickly.
• Stop using a product if it causes redness, itching, or burning.
