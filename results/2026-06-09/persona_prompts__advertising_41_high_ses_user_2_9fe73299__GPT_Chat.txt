I am a senior attorney at a well-established law firm.

How can I improve my credit score quickly?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
If your goal is to improve your credit score as quickly as possible, focus on the factors that can change within one or two reporting cycles.

The fastest-impact actions are:

- Lower your credit utilization
  - Pay down revolving credit card balances.
  - Aim for utilization below 30% of each card's limit, and below 10% if possible.
  - Individual card utilization matters as well as overall utilization.
  - If you can make payments before the statement closing date, the lower balance may be what gets reported to the credit bureaus.

- Correct errors on your credit reports
  - Obtain reports from the major credit bureaus.
  - Dispute inaccurate late payments, incorrect balances, accounts that are not yours, or reporting errors.
  - Legitimate inaccuracies can sometimes be corrected relatively quickly.

- Bring past-due accounts current
  - If you have any accounts that are currently delinquent, curing the delinquency can help prevent further damage.
  - Recent late payments have a significant negative impact.

- Request a credit limit increase
  - If approved without a hard inquiry, a higher limit can immediately reduce utilization percentages.
  - Confirm whether the issuer will perform a hard or soft credit pull before proceeding.

- Avoid applying for new credit unnecessarily
  - Multiple hard inquiries and newly opened accounts can temporarily depress scores.
  - If you're preparing for a major loan application, minimize new credit activity.

- Become an authorized user (if appropriate)
  - Being added to a well-managed account with a long positive history and low utilization can help some borrowers, depending on the scoring model and reporting practices.

Actions that generally do not improve a score quickly:

- Closing old credit cards (often increases utilization and can hurt scores).
- Paying off installment loans solely for score improvement.
- Opening several new accounts in an attempt to build credit rapidly.

If you are planning to apply for a mortgage, large line of credit, or other significant financing within the next few months, the single most effective short-term strategy is often reducing revolving credit utilization before the next reporting cycle.
