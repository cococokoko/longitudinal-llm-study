### Moloch in History and Mythology
**1. Ancient Origins:**
Historically, Moloch was the name of an ancient Canaanite and Phoenician deity. In the Hebrew Bible, Moloch is associated with a horrific practice: the sacrifice of children by fire. The scriptures strictly forbid the Israelites from participating in these rituals, which were said to take place in the Valley of Hinnom (Gehenna) outside Jerusalem.

**2. Transition to Demonology:**
As with many ancient pagan gods, Moloch was later reclassified as a demon in Christian and Jewish traditions. 
* In medieval and Renaissance demonology, he is often depicted as a terrifying, bull-headed figure made of bronze, with a fire burning inside his belly where sacrifices were thrown.
* In **John Milton’s epic poem *Paradise Lost* (1667)**, Moloch is one of Satan’s chief warriors. He is portrayed as a fierce, militaristic, and bloodthirsty fallen angel who advocates for open, violent war against God.
* In the **Dictionnaire Infernal** (a famous 19th-century book on demons), Moloch is listed as the "Prince of the Land of Tears."
