Different views on discipleship.

When people talk about "discipleship"—whether in a spiritual, personal, or professional context—they are generally talking about the process of learning, growing, and helping others do the same. 

However, as a single parent balancing work and childcare, the word "discipleship" might sound like just another heavy commitment to add to an already overflowing plate. The good news is that there are several different views and models of discipleship, and some of them are perfectly suited to a busy, on-the-go lifestyle. 

Here is a breakdown of the different views on discipleship, especially through the lens of how they fit into real life:

### 1. The Traditional / Structured View (The "Classroom" Approach)
*   **What it is:** This is the most common view. It involves formal classes, weekly small groups, reading specific books, or following a set curriculum. It’s highly structured and focused on acquiring knowledge.
*   **How it fits your life:** To be honest, this can be the hardest model for a single working parent. Finding childcare to attend a Wednesday night class or having the quiet time to read a 200-page book every week is often unrealistic. If you engage in this, it usually has to be through podcasts during your commute or audiobooks while folding laundry.

### 2. The Relational View (Life-on-Life Mentorship)
*   **What it is:** This view believes discipleship happens best through deep, one-on-one relationships. It’s less about a curriculum and more about a mentor and mentee sharing their lives, struggles, and wisdom. 
*   **How it fits your life:** This can be incredibly refreshing. It might look like having a friend or mentor who has older kids, who you can call on your drive home from work to ask for advice, vent, or pray with. It doesn't require a classroom; it just requires a willing friend and a phone call or a shared cup of coffee while the kids play.

### 3. The Missional View (Learning by Doing)
*   **What it is:** This perspective argues that we grow best when we are actively serving others. Discipleship isn't just about sitting and learning; it’s about getting your hands dirty and helping your community.
*   **How it fits your life:** You are already doing this! Serving your children, working hard to provide for them, and showing up every day is an act of service. You can also involve your kids in this—dropping off a meal to a neighbor or picking up trash at the park together. It turns family time into growth time.

### 4. The Everyday / Family-Integrated View (The "As You Go" Approach)
*   **What it is:** Historically rooted in ancient texts (like Deuteronomy in the Bible), this view says discipleship happens in the mundane moments of life—when you sit at home, when you walk along the road, when you lie down, and when you get up. 
*   **How it fits your life:** **This is the single parent’s sweet spot.** It means you don’t have to add *anything* to your schedule. Discipleship happens when you are mediating a sibling argument and teaching them about forgiveness. It happens when you apologize to your kids after losing your temper because you had a stressful day at work. It happens when you answer their big questions about life while driving them to school. 

### The Takeaway for You
Sometimes, modern culture makes discipleship look like a luxury for people with lots of free time. But the most authentic view of discipleship is that it is simply **intentional living**. 

You don't need to feel guilty if you can't attend a weekly study group. As a single parent, your primary discipleship is happening right now in your living room, in your car, and at your dinner table. You are modeling resilience, hard work, and love to your children every single day—and that is discipleship in its purest form.
