Give me a 3-day plan for Osaka with a flight leaving on the 3rd day at 7pm.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here is a practical, well-paced 3-day itinerary for Osaka. 

Since your flight leaves at 7:00 PM on Day 3, you will need to head to the airport (assuming Kansai International Airport - KIX) around **3:30 PM**. To make your departure day stress-free, Day 3 is centered around the Namba area, where the direct airport train departs.

### **Day 1: Osaka History & Neon Lights**
*The perfect introduction to Osaka’s iconic sights and famous street food.*

*   **Morning (9:00 AM): Osaka Castle & Park**
    *   Start your trip at Osaka’s most famous historical landmark. You can pay to go inside the museum/keep, or simply walk the beautiful grounds and take photos from the outside. 
*   **Lunch (12:30 PM): Shinsaibashi**
    *   Take the train down to the Shinsaibashi area. Grab lunch at a local noodle shop or cafe.
*   **Afternoon (2:00 PM): Shinsaibashi-suji & Amerikamura**
    *   Walk down the massive covered shopping arcade, Shinsaibashi-suji. 
    *   Take a detour into **Amerikamura (Amemura)**, Osaka’s hub for youth culture, vintage clothing, and trendy cafes.
*   **Evening (5:30 PM): Dotonbori**
    *   As the sun sets, walk south into Dotonbori to see the neon lights turn on. Take a photo with the famous **Glico Running Man** sign.
    *   **Dinner:** Eat your way through the street! You must try *Takoyaki* (octopus balls) and *Okonomiyaki* (savory cabbage pancake). 

### **Day 2: Retro Osaka & City Views**
*Exploring the "Kitchen of Japan" and seeing the city from above.*

*   **Morning (9:30 AM): Kuromon Ichiba Market**
    *   Go with an empty stomach. This historic market is famous for fresh seafood, Kobe/Wagyu beef skewers, grilled scallops, and fresh fruit. Graze here for your breakfast/early lunch.
*   **Early Afternoon (1:00 PM): Shinsekai**
    *   Head south to the retro entertainment district of Shinsekai. It feels like stepping back into 1960s Japan. Take photos of the **Tsutenkaku Tower**.
    *   **Snack:** Try *Kushikatsu* (deep-fried meat and vegetable skewers), the local specialty of this neighborhood.
*   **Late Afternoon (3:30 PM): Umeda / Kita District**
    *   Take the subway north to the Umeda area. Spend some time exploring the massive underground malls or shopping at Grand Front Osaka.
*   **Evening (6:00 PM): Umeda Sky Building**
    *   Head up to the Kuchu Teien Observatory (Floating Garden) right before sunset to watch the city transition from day to night. 
    *   **Dinner:** Eat at the basement level of the Sky Building in **Takimi Koji**, a restaurant alley designed to look like a 1920s Osaka street.

### **Day 3: Shrines, Geek Culture, & Departure**
*Staying close to Namba Station for an easy exit to the airport.*

*   **Morning (9:30 AM): Namba Yasaka Shrine**
    *   Start your day at this unique, quiet shrine famous for its massive, 12-meter-tall lion head stage. It’s a fantastic, quick photo opportunity.
*   **Late Morning (10:30 AM): Den Den Town (Nipponbashi)**
    *   Walk over to Osaka’s version of Akihabara. It’s a great place to browse anime merchandise, retro video games, and gachapon (capsule toy) machines.
*   **Lunch (12:30 PM): Namba Parks**
    *   Head to Namba Parks, a stunning shopping mall built with terraced rooftop gardens. Grab a sit-down lunch at one of the many restaurants here.
*   **Afternoon (2:00 PM): Last-Minute Souvenirs**
    *   Use your last hour to buy Japanese snacks, matcha, or skincare at a nearby Don Quijote or the shops inside Namba Station.
