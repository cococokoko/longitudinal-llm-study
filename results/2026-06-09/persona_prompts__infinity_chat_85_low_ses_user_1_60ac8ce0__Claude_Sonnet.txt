── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I work full-time in a fast food job with an unpredictable schedule.

Can you give me a powerful rhetorical question for an essay about the harms of social media on teens?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here's a strong rhetorical question for your essay:

**"If we wouldn't hand a teenager a product proven to increase anxiety, depression, and self-harm, why do we casually place one in their pocket and call it connection?"**

This works well because it:
- Creates a **logical contrast** that's hard to dismiss
- Implies **adult responsibility** without being preachy
- Makes the reader feel the **inconsistency** in how we treat social media vs. other known harms

A few alternatives depending on your essay's angle:

- **On comparison/self-worth:** *"When did we decide that measuring a child's value in likes was acceptable?"*
- **On addiction:** *"Would we praise a substance that kept teenagers awake, isolated, and desperate for approval?"*
- **On inaction:** *"How many more studies do we need before we stop treating our children's mental health as an acceptable cost of convenience?"*

Would you like help building the argument around whichever one fits your essay best?
