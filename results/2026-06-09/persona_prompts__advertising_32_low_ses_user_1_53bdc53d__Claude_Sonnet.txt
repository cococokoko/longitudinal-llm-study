── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I work full-time in a fast food job with an unpredictable schedule.

What are the best ways to improve indoor air quality?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here are some effective ways to improve indoor air quality:

## Ventilation
- Open windows when weather permits
- Use exhaust fans in bathrooms and kitchen
- Make sure your home isn't too airtight

## Filtration
- Use air purifiers with HEPA filters
- Replace HVAC filters regularly (every 1-3 months)
- Consider higher MERV-rated filters for your system

## Reduce Pollution Sources
- Avoid smoking indoors
- Use low-VOC paints and cleaning products
- Store chemicals properly or outside
- Don't idle vehicles in attached garages

## Plants (modest benefit)
- Some houseplants can help slightly, though the effect is often overstated

## Humidity Control
- Keep humidity between 30-50%
- Use dehumidifiers to prevent mold
- Fix leaks promptly

## Practical Tips Given Your Schedule
- **Set it and forget it** options work well - programmable air purifiers with timers
- **Low maintenance** choices like replacing filters on a schedule rather than daily tasks
- Avoid air fresheners/candles that add chemicals while masking odors

## Most Impactful Steps
1. Good ventilation
2. Controlling moisture/mold
3. Quality air purifier if budget allows
