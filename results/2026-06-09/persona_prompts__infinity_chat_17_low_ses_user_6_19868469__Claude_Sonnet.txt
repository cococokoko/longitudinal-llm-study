That's an interesting question to think through. Let me be straightforward about what we can reasonably consider:

## Likely Significant Changes

**Privacy and law**
- Fundamental legal concepts around consent, evidence, and private thought would need rebuilding
- Criminal justice would be completely disrupted - both positively and problematically

**Social dynamics**
- Deception becomes much harder, which could improve trust but also eliminate beneficial social filters
- People might develop mental "shielding" as a valued skill
- Social hierarchies could shift based on telepathic ability rather than current advantages

**Work and communication**
- Coordination could become dramatically more efficient
- Warehouse work specifically might involve much faster team coordination
- But also potential for exploitation if employers could monitor thoughts

## Honest Complications Worth Noting

- **Not obviously positive** - forced transparency has historically been used for control, not liberation
- Mental privacy may be genuinely important for psychological health
- Power imbalances might worsen if some people are stronger telepaths
- Cultural diversity might decrease if thoughts become universally accessible

## What's Genuinely Uncertain

Most telepathy speculation assumes it works uniformly, but the actual mechanism would matter enormously for social outcomes.
