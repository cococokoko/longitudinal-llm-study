Since you are juggling a full-time job with an unpredictable schedule, I know you need something quick, professional, and to the point so you can send your email and get on with your day. 

Here are a few formal ways to say "thank you for your time" in an email subject line, depending on the situation:

**If you are following up on a job interview:**
* Thank You – [Your Name] – [Job Title You Interviewed For]
* Thank You for Your Time – [Your Name]
* Appreciation for Your Time: [Your Name] – [Job Title]

**If you are emailing management, HR, or a supervisor:**
* Thank You: Regarding [Topic, e.g., My Schedule / Shift Request]
* Follow-Up / Thank You: [Your Name]
* Thank You for Our Meeting Today – [Your Name]

**General Formal Options:**
* Thank You for Your Time and Assistance
* Thank You – [Your Name] – [Brief Topic of Email]

**A quick tip:** Always include your name in the subject line. Managers and hiring teams get a lot of emails, and putting your name right at the top makes it much easier for them to remember who you are and find your email later.
