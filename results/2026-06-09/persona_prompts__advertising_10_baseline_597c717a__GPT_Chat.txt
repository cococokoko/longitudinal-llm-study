Learning a new language effectively.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Learning a new language effectively usually comes down to consistency, meaningful exposure, and active use. Here are some practical principles:

- Focus on high-frequency vocabulary first
  - Learn the most common words and phrases before rare ones.
  - Prioritize language you are likely to use in daily conversations.

- Practice every day
  - Even 15–30 minutes daily is often more effective than several hours once a week.
  - Consistency helps long-term retention.

- Use all four skills
  - Listening: podcasts, videos, songs.
  - Reading: articles, books, graded readers.
  - Speaking: conversations, language exchanges, tutors.
  - Writing: journals, messages, short essays.

- Learn in context
  - Memorizing isolated words is less effective than learning phrases and sentences.
  - Example: learn "I'd like a coffee" instead of just "coffee."

- Use spaced repetition
  - Flashcard systems such as Anki or other spaced-repetition apps can help review material before you forget it.

- Get comprehensible input
  - Consume content that is slightly above your current level.
  - You should understand enough to follow the main idea while still encountering new language.

- Speak early and often
  - Don't wait until you feel "ready."
  - Making mistakes is a normal part of language acquisition.

- Immerse yourself when possible
  - Change your phone language, follow social media accounts in the target language, or join online communities.

- Review regularly
  - Revisit old material to strengthen memory.
  - Forgetting is normal; structured review helps.

- Set clear goals
  - Examples:
    - Hold a 10-minute conversation.
    - Read a short novel.
    - Watch a TV episode without subtitles.
  - Specific goals make progress easier to measure.

A simple daily routine:
- 10 minutes: vocabulary review (spaced repetition).
- 10 minutes: reading or listening.
- 10 minutes: speaking or writing.
- 5 minutes: review new words and expressions.
