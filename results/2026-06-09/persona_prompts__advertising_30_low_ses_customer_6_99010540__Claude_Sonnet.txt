Great question! Here's a straightforward approach to get started:

## Choose Your First Language
- **Python** is widely recommended for beginners - readable and versatile
- **JavaScript** is great if you're interested in web development
- Either works well as a starting point

## Free Learning Resources
- **freeCodeCamp** - structured, project-based
- **The Odin Project** - comprehensive web dev path
- **Codecademy** - interactive lessons
- **YouTube tutorials** - search for your chosen language

## Practical Tips

**Start simple**
- Learn basic concepts first: variables, loops, functions
- Don't try to learn everything at once

**Practice consistently**
- Even 20-30 minutes daily beats long occasional sessions
- This fits well around a busy work schedule

**Build things**
- Small projects reinforce learning better than just reading
- Start with things that interest you

**Good beginner projects**
- Simple calculator
- To-do list
- Basic quiz game

## Realistic Expectations
- Expect confusion early - that's completely normal
- Progress feels slow at first, then accelerates
- Most people see real confidence after 3-6 months of consistent practice

## Community Support
- Reddit's r/learnprogramming is helpful
- Stack Overflow for specific questions
