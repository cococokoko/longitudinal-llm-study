# Healthy Meal Planning on a Budget

## Core Principles

- **Plan before you shop** — reduces impulse buying and waste
- **Cook at home** — restaurant meals cost 3-5x more
- **Buy ingredients, not convenience foods** — pay for simplicity, not packaging
- **Eat less meat** — biggest single cost reducer

---

## Budget-Friendly Nutritious Foods

### Proteins
- Eggs (~$0.25 each)
- Canned beans/lentils
- Dried legumes (cheapest option)
- Canned tuna/sardines
- Chicken thighs (cheaper than breasts)
- Tofu

### Grains & Starches
- Oats
- Brown rice
- Whole wheat pasta
- Potatoes/sweet potatoes

### Produce
- Frozen vegetables (equal nutrition, lower cost)
- Seasonal fresh produce
- Cabbage, carrots, bananas, apples

---

## Practical Strategies

### Shopping
- ✅ Make a weekly meal plan first
- ✅ Shop with a list
- ✅ Buy store/generic brands
- ✅ Check unit prices, not package prices
- ✅ Use frozen and canned produce

### Cooking
- **Batch cook** on weekends
- **Repurpose leftovers** into new meals
- **Use the whole ingredient** (vegetable scraps for broth)

---

## Simple Cheap & Healthy Recipes

**Lentil Soup** (~$0.75/serving)
- Lentils, canned tomatoes, onion, garlic, spices

**Veggie Fried Rice** (~$1.00/serving)
- Leftover rice, eggs, frozen vegetables, soy sauce

**Bean Tacos** (~$1.25/serving)
- Canned black beans, tortillas, cabbage, salsa

---

## Reducing Food Waste
- Store food properly
- Use the **"first in, first out"** rule
- Freeze bread, meat, and leftovers before they go bad
- Plan meals around what needs to be used

---

## Quick Tips
> 💡 **Spices are worth the investment** — they make cheap ingredients taste great
> 💡 **Dried beans cost ~75% less** than canned
> 💡 **Farmers markets near closing time** often have discounts
