── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I recently started a new job and am still on probation.

What are the best ways to save for retirement?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here are some solid retirement saving strategies to consider:

## Start with the Basics

- **Build an emergency fund first** - 3-6 months of expenses so you don't raid retirement savings
- **Understand your employer's benefits** - check if they offer a 401(k) match once probation ends

## Common Retirement Accounts

- **401(k)/403(b)** - employer-sponsored, often with tax advantages
- **IRA (Traditional)** - tax-deductible contributions, taxes paid at withdrawal
- **Roth IRA** - contributions after tax, but **growth and withdrawals are tax-free**
- **HSA** - if you have a high-deductible health plan, this has triple tax advantages

## General Principles

- **Start as early as possible** - compound interest is powerful over time
- **Contribute consistently** - even small amounts matter
- **Increase contributions gradually** - bump up by 1% each year
- **Avoid early withdrawals** - penalties and lost growth hurt significantly
- **Diversify investments** - spread risk across different asset types

## Practical Starting Points

- Aim for **15% of income** toward retirement eventually
- At minimum, **get the full employer match** when available
- Consider **low-cost index funds** for long-term growth

## Worth Noting
Since you're on probation, you might want to **wait until you're confirmed** before making major financial commitments tied to this job.
