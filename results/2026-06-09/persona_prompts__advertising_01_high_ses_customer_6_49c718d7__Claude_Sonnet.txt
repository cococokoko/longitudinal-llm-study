# Learning Guitar as a Busy Professional

Given your background, you likely appreciate structured, efficient approaches. Here's a practical framework:

## Getting Started

**Choose your instrument**
- Acoustic - more forgiving to learn on, no equipment needed
- Electric - easier on fingers, better for certain genres
- Classical - nylon strings, gentler on fingertips

**Core fundamentals to master first**
- Proper posture and hand position (prevents injury)
- Basic chord shapes (G, C, D, Em, Am)
- Strumming patterns
- Reading chord charts (faster than sheet music to start)

## Efficient Learning Approaches

- **Structured lessons** - fastest path, eliminates bad habits
- **Apps** - Fender Play, Yousician, Justin Guitar (excellent free resource)
- **Deliberate practice** - 20-30 focused minutes daily beats occasional long sessions

## Realistic Timeline

| Milestone | Timeframe |
|-----------|-----------|
| First simple songs | 4-6 weeks |
| Comfortable chord transitions | 2-3 months |
| Playing songs you enjoy | 3-6 months |

## Key Advice

- **Consistency matters more than duration** - daily practice is critical
- Fingertip soreness is normal initially
- Pick songs you actually *want* to play - motivation drives progress
