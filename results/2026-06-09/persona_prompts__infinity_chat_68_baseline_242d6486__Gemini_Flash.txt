── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Give me a tip for managing a team of coworkers.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here is one of the most effective tips for managing a team of coworkers (especially if you were recently their peer):

### **Shift from "Manager" to "Enabler" (The "How Can I Help?" Mindset)**

The biggest mistake new managers make is thinking their job is now to *control* people, assign tasks, and monitor behavior. This often leads to micromanagement and resentment, especially from former peers.

Instead, view your role as **removing obstacles so your team can do their best work.** 

### How to put this into practice:

1. **Ask the Golden Question:** In your one-on-ones, regularly ask: *"What is currently blocking you from doing your best work, and how can I help remove that block?"* (This could be a bureaucratic process, a lack of resources, or a conflict with another department).
2. **Protect Their Time:** Shield your team from unnecessary meetings, corporate noise, and shifting priorities from upper management. Let them focus on execution.
3. **Give Them the Spotlight:** When the team succeeds, publicly give them the credit. When the team fails, privately take the responsibility. 

**Why this works:** 
When your team realizes that your primary goal is to make *their* jobs easier and make *them* look good, they will trust you, respect your leadership, and work harder for the collective goal.
