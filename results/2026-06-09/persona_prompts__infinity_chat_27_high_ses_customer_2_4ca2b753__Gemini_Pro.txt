**TITLE: THE POST-APOCALYPTIC PARADIGM SHIFT**

**CHARACTERS:**

*   **DILBERT:** A pragmatic survivor. Wears a tattered dress shirt, a frayed tie, and a makeshift hazmat suit. 
*   **THE BOSS:** Pointy hair, holding a chipped coffee mug. He has a slight green radioactive glow, which he ignores.
*   **WALLY:** Holding a cup of irradiated sludge. He has successfully avoided all survival duties since the world ended.
*   **DOGBERT:** A megalomaniacal canine who has somehow become the warlord of the local mutant faction.

**SETTING:**
The ruins of a corporate office building, now a fortified bunker. The fluorescent lights flicker ominously. A whiteboard reads: "Q3 SURVIVAL METRICS."

[SCENE START]

**THE BOSS**
(Takes a sip from his empty mug)
I’ve called this stand-up meeting to discuss our synergy in the wasteland. Our scavenging metrics are down twenty percent this week. 

**DILBERT**
That’s because the accounting department was eaten by radioactive scorpions on Tuesday. 

**THE BOSS**
(Frowns)
Did they submit their PTO requests before being consumed? Human Resources is going to be furious if we have unapproved absences. 

**DILBERT**
Human Resources mutated into a giant, gelatinous blob three months ago. They communicate entirely through toxic spores now. 

**THE BOSS**
Exactly. And they are sticklers for protocol. Dilbert, I need you to pivot your focus. Stop working on the water filtration system and start drafting a liability waiver for scorpion encounters. 

**DILBERT**
If I stop working on the water filter, we will all die of radiation poisoning by Friday. 

**THE BOSS**
Let’s not get bogged down in the technical details. I need proactive solutions, not complaints. Wally, what are your deliverables for the week?

**WALLY**
(Takes a slow sip of his glowing sludge)
I’m currently spearheading a feasibility study on whether standing perfectly still makes me invisible to the marauding cannibal tribes. 

**THE BOSS**
Excellent initiative. How is the data looking?

**WALLY**
It requires extensive field testing. I’ll need to remain in this chair, unbothered, for the next six to eight weeks to ensure a defensible sample size. 

**THE BOSS**
Make sure you document your findings. We can present them at the next all-hands meeting, assuming we still have hands. 

(DOGBERT enters. He is wearing a spiked leather collar and riding a small, heavily armored Roomba.)

**DOGBERT**
Greetings, peasants. I have returned from the irradiated zone. 

**DILBERT**
Dogbert? I thought you were captured by the Blood-Rider clan. 

**DOGBERT**
I was. But after a brief consultation, I convinced them that their pillaging strategy lacked a cohesive brand identity. I am now their Chief Executive Warlord. 

**THE BOSS**
(Nods approvingly)
Networking. That’s how you get ahead in the apocalypse. 

**DOGBERT**
I’m here to offer you a premium protection package. For the low cost of all your remaining canned goods and your absolute servitude, my mutants will only raid your bunker on alternating Thursdays. 

**DILBERT**
That sounds like extortion. 

**DOGBERT**
I prefer the term "mandatory retainer fee." It’s standard industry practice in the wasteland. Read the fine print. 

(Dogbert hands Dilbert a piece of scorched parchment.)

**DILBERT**
(Reading)
"Clause 4a: The Client agrees to be hunted for sport in the event of a breach of contract..." 

**THE BOSS**
(Interrupting)
Don't overthink it, Dilbert. It’s a vendor agreement. Just sign it so we can get back to our core competencies. 

**DILBERT**
Our core competency is currently starving in the dark. 

**THE BOSS**
And with Dogbert’s help, we can streamline that process. Meeting adjourned. Wally, keep up the good work. 

**WALLY**
(Staring blankly ahead)
I am a ghost in the corporate machine. 

(The Boss walks away, his green glow illuminating the dark hallway. Dilbert sighs, pulls a wrench from his pocket, and turns back to the broken water filter.)

**DILBERT**
(To himself)
I wonder if the scorpions are hiring. 

[SCENE END]
