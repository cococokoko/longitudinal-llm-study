Is it impossible to know everything?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
**Yes, it is absolutely impossible to know everything.** In fact, it’s impossible to know even a tiny fraction of everything. 

Given your situation, it makes complete sense why you might be asking this question. Working full-time in fast food is physically and mentally exhausting. When you add an unpredictable schedule to that, your brain is constantly in "survival mode." You are likely dealing with sleep disruption, stress, and a lack of routine, which drains your cognitive battery. 

When you are that exhausted, it is very easy to look at the world, the internet, or other people and feel a sudden, overwhelming sense of: *"There is so much I don't know. Am I falling behind? How does anyone keep up?"*

Here are a few things to keep in mind:

**1. The "Information Age" is an illusion of knowledge.**
We are bombarded with information every second of the day. It makes us feel like everyone else knows about politics, finance, history, science, and pop culture. They don't. Most people only know a little bit about a few things, and they just project confidence. 

**2. Your brain is currently occupied with adapting.**
An unpredictable schedule means you can't put your life on autopilot. You are constantly having to figure out when you'll sleep, when you'll eat, when you can do laundry, and when you can see friends. That takes up a massive amount of brainpower. You literally do not have the mental bandwidth right now to absorb a ton of outside information, and *that is completely okay.*

**3. You only need to know what matters to YOU.**
You don't need to know everything. You just need to know enough to navigate your own life. What are your goals? What brings you peace? What helps you survive your shift? Focus your limited energy on knowing those things. 

**4. Give yourself some grace.**
You are working a demanding, high-stress job that doesn't respect your time. If you feel like you don't know enough, or if you feel mentally foggy, it’s not because you lack intelligence or curiosity. It’s because you are tired. 

It is impossible to know everything. The smartest, most successful people in the world don't know everything. Right now, the best thing you can "know" is how to be kind to yourself while you navigate a really tough work schedule.
