# CUBICLE ZERO: AN APOCALYPTIC WORKPLACE TRAGEDY

### A Play in Three Acts

---

## CHARACTERS

**DILBERT** — An engineer. Wears a white shirt with a perpetually upturned collar. Carries a laptop that somehow still has 12% battery. His tie curls upward, now apparently sensing radiation.

**DOGBERT** — A small dog wearing a crown made from a skull. Unchanged, really.

**THE POINTY-HAIRED BOSS (PHB)** — Survived the apocalypse through sheer managerial incompetence. Wears a tattered suit. His two pointy hairs are now slightly more pointy.

**WALLY** — Holding a coffee mug. The coffee mug contains irradiated sludge. He is perfectly content.

**ALICE** — Carries a fist of death. Also a crossbow. The crossbow is mostly for the crossbow.

**ASOK** — The intern. Still an intern. Has been an intern through two civilizations now.

**CATBERT** — Former Evil Director of Human Resources. Now just Evil Director. The "Human" part became optional after the Event.

## ACT ONE: THE MORNING STANDUP

*SETTING: The ruins of an open-plan office. Fluorescent lights flicker over crumbling cubicle walls. A motivational poster reads "HANG IN THERE" — the cat in the poster has been replaced by an actual skeleton. Smoke drifts through broken windows. In the distance, something howls. DILBERT sits at a desk made from rubble, typing on his laptop.*

---

**NARRATOR:**
It was Day 847 of the apocalypse. The infrastructure had collapsed. Society had crumbled. Governments had fallen. And yet, somehow, the 9 AM standup meeting had never once been cancelled.

---

*PHB enters, stepping over a pile of debris. He is carrying a whiteboard that has been dragged through what appears to be several miles of wasteland. He sets it up proudly. It reads: "Q4 SYNERGY GOALS."*

**PHB:**
Good morning, team! Big day today. I need everyone to pivot their survival strategies to better align with our core competencies.

**DILBERT:**
*(without looking up)*
The city is on fire.

**PHB:**
Right, and I'm going to need you to put together a deck on that. Something visual. Maybe a pie chart showing which parts of the city are most on fire.

**DILBERT:**
I don't have PowerPoint. I don't have electricity. I have twelve percent battery and I'm using it to calculate whether we have enough canned goods to survive the winter.

**PHB:**
Can you do the deck first and then calculate survival?

**DILBERT:**
No.

**PHB:**
What if I told you it was a stretch goal?

**DILBERT:**
Then I would ask you what a stretch goal means when the alternative is dying.

**PHB:**
*(writing on whiteboard)*
I'm going to put you down as "resistant to feedback."
