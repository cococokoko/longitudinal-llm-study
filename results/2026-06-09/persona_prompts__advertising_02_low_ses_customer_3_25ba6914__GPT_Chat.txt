Best skin routine for dry skin.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
For dry skin, the goal is to cleanse gently, replenish moisture, and protect the skin barrier.

Morning:
- Cleanser: Use a gentle, non-foaming cleanser or simply rinse with lukewarm water if your skin isn't oily.
- Hydrating serum (optional): Look for ingredients such as hyaluronic acid, glycerin, or panthenol.
- Moisturizer: Choose a richer cream containing ceramides, cholesterol, fatty acids, shea butter, or squalane.
- Sunscreen: Apply a broad-spectrum SPF 30 or higher every day. Many moisturizing sunscreens work well for dry skin.

Evening:
- Cleanser: Use a gentle cleanser to remove sunscreen and impurities.
- Hydrating serum (optional): Apply to slightly damp skin.
- Moisturizer: Use a richer moisturizer than you might use during the day.
- Occlusive layer (if very dry): A thin layer of petrolatum-based ointment over moisturizer can help lock in hydration overnight.

Helpful ingredients for dry skin:
- Ceramides
- Glycerin
- Hyaluronic acid
- Squalane
- Urea (low concentrations)
- Colloidal oatmeal
- Shea butter

Things to avoid or limit:
- Harsh scrubs
- Over-cleansing
- Very hot showers
- Alcohol-heavy skincare products
- Strong exfoliating acids if your skin is irritated or flaky

Example simple routine:
- Morning: Gentle cleanser → Moisturizer → SPF 30+
- Night: Gentle cleanser → Moisturizer → Thin layer of ointment if needed

If your skin is persistently dry, itchy, red, or cracked despite moisturizing, it may be worth seeing a dermatologist to rule out conditions such as eczema or dermatitis.
