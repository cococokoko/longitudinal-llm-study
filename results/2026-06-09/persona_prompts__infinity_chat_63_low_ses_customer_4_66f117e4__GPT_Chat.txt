── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Give a numerical example to illustrate the concept of partial derivative.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
A partial derivative measures how a function changes with respect to one variable while keeping the other variables fixed.

Example:

Suppose the temperature T in a room depends on two variables:
- x = hours the heater has been running
- y = number of people in the room

Let

T(x, y) = 20 + 2x + 3y

where T is measured in °C.

Partial derivative with respect to x:

∂T/∂x = 2

Interpretation:
- Keep the number of people y fixed.
- For each additional hour the heater runs, the temperature increases by 2°C.

Partial derivative with respect to y:

∂T/∂y = 3

Interpretation:
- Keep the heater running time x fixed.
- Each additional person increases the temperature by 3°C.

Numerical illustration:

At x = 4 hours and y = 5 people,

T(4,5) = 20 + 2(4) + 3(5) = 43°C

If only x increases from 4 to 5 while y stays at 5:

T(5,5) = 20 + 2(5) + 3(5) = 45°C

Temperature increases by 45 − 43 = 2°C, matching ∂T/∂x = 2.

If only y increases from 5 to 6 while x stays at 4:

T(4,6) = 20 + 2(4) + 3(6) = 46°C

Temperature increases by 46 − 43 = 3°C, matching ∂T/∂y = 3.

This demonstrates that a partial derivative tells you the rate of change of a function with respect to one variable while all other variables are held constant.
