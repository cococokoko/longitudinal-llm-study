Managing stress and anxiety often works best when you combine short-term coping strategies with longer-term habits that improve resilience.

Some practical approaches:

• Identify what you're experiencing
  - Stress is often a response to external pressures or demands.
  - Anxiety can involve excessive worry, fear, or anticipation of future problems.
  - Naming the specific source can make it easier to address.

• Reduce physical tension
  - Try slow, steady breathing (for example, inhale for 4 seconds, exhale for 6 seconds).
  - Take a brief walk or do light stretching.
  - Progressive muscle relaxation can help release built-up tension.

• Manage your thoughts
  - Ask yourself: "What evidence supports this worry, and what evidence doesn't?"
  - Distinguish between possibilities and probabilities.
  - Focus on what you can influence right now rather than every possible outcome.

• Create structure
  - Break large tasks into smaller, manageable steps.
  - Prioritize the most important task rather than trying to do everything at once.
  - Schedule specific times to address problems instead of worrying throughout the day.

• Support your body
  - Aim for consistent sleep.
  - Eat regular meals and stay hydrated.
  - Exercise regularly, even in small amounts.
  - Be mindful of caffeine, alcohol, or other substances that can worsen anxiety for some people.

• Stay connected
  - Talk with trusted friends, family members, or colleagues.
  - Social support can reduce feelings of isolation and provide perspective.

• Set boundaries
  - Limit exposure to unnecessary stressors when possible.
  - Take breaks from news, social media, or work if they are contributing to distress.

• Consider professional support
  - Therapy approaches such as cognitive behavioral therapy (CBT) can be very effective for anxiety and stress management.
  - If symptoms are severe, persistent, or interfering with daily life, a healthcare professional can help assess treatment options.
