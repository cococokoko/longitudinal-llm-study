── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How to say a formal thank you for your time in the subject line of an email?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
To write a formal "thank you for your time" in an email subject line, you want to be **concise, professional, and clear** about the context of the email. 

Here are several options categorized by the situation:

### 1. After a Job Interview (Most Common)
These are polished, professional, and include your name so the hiring manager immediately knows who you are.
*   **Thank you for your time – [Your Name]**
*   **Thank you for the opportunity – [Your Name]**
*   **Appreciation for your time / Interview follow-up**
*   **Thank you: [Job Title] Interview – [Your Name]**
*   *Example:* Thank you: Marketing Manager Interview – Jane Doe

### 2. After a Business Meeting or Consultation
Use these after meeting with a client, partner, or vendor.
*   **Thank you for your time today**
*   **Sincere thanks for your time and insights**
*   **Appreciation for our meeting today**
*   **Thank you for your time / [Project/Topic Name] follow-up**
*   *Example:* Thank you for your time / Q4 Budget Review

### 3. After an Informational Interview or Networking Call
Use these when someone has done you a favor by giving you career advice or industry insights.
*   **Thank you for your time and guidance**
*   **With appreciation for your time and advice**
*   **Thank you for connecting today, [Name]**
*   **Great connecting with you / Thank you for your time**

### 4. Short & Ultra-Formal (Best for high-level executives)
If you are writing to a C-level executive or someone in a highly traditional industry (law, finance, government), keep it incredibly brief.
*   **Thank you for your time**
*   **With thanks: [Your Name]**
*   **In appreciation of your time**

### Key Tips for Formal Subject Lines:
*   **Capitalize properly:** Use Title Case (e.g., *Thank You for Your Time*) or Sentence Case (e.g., *Thank you for your time*). Avoid all lowercase.
*   **Keep it under 60 characters:** This ensures the entire subject line is visible on mobile devices.
*   **Send it within 24 hours:** A formal thank-you loses its impact if it is sent too late.
