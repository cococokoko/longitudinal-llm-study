# How to Improve Home WiFi

## Quick Fixes First
- **Restart your router** - unplug for 30 seconds, then plug back in
- **Check for interference** - move router away from microwaves, cordless phones, baby monitors
- **Update router firmware** - check manufacturer's app or admin panel

## Router Placement
- Place it **centrally** in your home
- Keep it **elevated** (shelf or table, not floor)
- Avoid **thick walls, metal objects, and appliances** nearby
- Point antennas **vertically** for better horizontal coverage

## Improve Your Signal
- **Change WiFi channel** - use channels 1, 6, or 11 on 2.4GHz to reduce congestion
- **Use 5GHz band** for faster speeds when close to router
- **Use 2.4GHz band** for better range through walls

## Hardware Solutions
- **WiFi extender/repeater** - boosts range cheaply (~$30-50)
- **Mesh WiFi system** - best for large homes (Google Nest, Eero, Orbi)
- **Powerline adapters** - use electrical wiring to extend network
- **Upgrade your router** - older routers may simply be outdated

## Other Tips
- **Limit connected devices** using bandwidth
- **Set a strong password** to prevent unauthorized users
- **Contact your ISP** - the problem may be outside your home
