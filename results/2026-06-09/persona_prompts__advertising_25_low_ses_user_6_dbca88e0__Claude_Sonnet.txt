── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I work in a warehouse position that requires long hours.

How can I decorate my apartment on a minimalist budget?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here are some practical ideas for decorating on a tight budget:

## Low-Cost Decorating Strategies

**Start with what you have**
- Rearrange existing furniture for a fresh feel
- Declutter first - less stuff often looks better
- Clean and repair items before replacing them

**Affordable sources**
- Thrift stores and secondhand shops
- Facebook Marketplace / Craigslist
- Dollar stores for basic items
- Discount stores like IKEA or Target clearance

**Simple high-impact changes**
- Paint is one of the cheapest ways to transform a space
- New throw pillows or a blanket can refresh a couch
- Plants (pothos and snake plants are cheap and hard to kill)
- Better lighting - swap harsh bulbs for warm ones

**Free options**
- Nature items like branches, pinecones, or rocks
- Printable art you frame yourself
- Swap items with friends or family

## Minimalist Approach Specifically
Since you mentioned minimalist, this actually works in your favor:
- **Fewer items = less spending**
- Focus on one or two quality pieces rather than lots of cheap things
- Neutral colors require less coordination
- Empty space is intentional, not unfinished

**Practical tip for your situation:** Since you work long hours, prioritize making your space feel **restful and calm** rather than decorative.
