Give an example of a linear graph in graph theory.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
In graph theory, the term **"linear graph"** is most commonly used as an alternative name for a **path graph** (denoted $P_n$), which is a tree with two nodes of degree 1, and all other nodes of degree 2. 

Here is a concrete example of a linear graph on four vertices, $P_4$:

*   **Vertex Set ($V$):** $\{v_1, v_2, v_3, v_4\}$
*   **Edge Set ($E$):** $\{\{v_1, v_2\}, \{v_2, v_3\}, \{v_3, v_4\}\}$

Geometrically, this graph can be drawn as a single straight line of vertices connected by edges in sequence.

*(Note: Occasionally, "linear graph" is used colloquially to refer to a **line graph**, $L(G)$, which represents the adjacencies between edges of a given graph $G$. If this is what you meant, a classic example is that the line graph of a star graph $K_{1,3}$ is the complete graph $K_3$, or a triangle.)*
