Working from home can be especially challenging when you're balancing work responsibilities with childcare. A few adjustments can often make a noticeable difference:

- Create a flexible daily structure
  - Identify your most important work tasks each day.
  - Schedule focused work during times when your child is occupied, at school, napping, or engaged in independent activities.
  - Accept that your schedule may need to be split into shorter work blocks rather than long uninterrupted stretches.

- Prioritize ruthlessly
  - Choose 1–3 high-impact tasks that must get done each day.
  - Tackle those before lower-priority emails, messages, or administrative work.

- Use time-blocking
  - Reserve specific periods for focused work, meetings, email, and family responsibilities.
  - Even 25–45 minute focus sessions can be highly productive.

- Reduce distractions
  - Silence nonessential notifications.
  - Keep only the apps and browser tabs you need open.
  - If possible, designate a specific workspace, even if it's just a corner of a room.

- Communicate boundaries and availability
  - Let coworkers know your typical working hours and any periods when you may be less available due to childcare responsibilities.
  - Use calendar blocks to signal focus time.

- Build in transition routines
  - Start work with a brief planning session.
  - End work by reviewing what was completed and identifying the next priority for tomorrow.

- Keep expectations realistic
  - Productivity at home doesn't always look like productivity in an office.
  - Measuring success by meaningful outcomes rather than hours of uninterrupted focus can be more effective.

- Use simple tools
  - A daily task list, calendar, and timer are often enough.
  - Avoid spending too much time setting up complex productivity systems.

A sample approach:
- Morning: Plan the day and complete the most important task.
- Midday: Meetings, email, and collaborative work.
- Afternoon: Second focus block or administrative tasks.
- End of day: Review progress and prepare tomorrow's priorities.
