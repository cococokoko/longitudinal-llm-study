── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Where can I find unique handmade gifts?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Finding unique handmade gifts is a great way to give something thoughtful while supporting independent artists and small businesses. Here is a breakdown of the best places to look, both online and in person:

### 1. Dedicated Online Marketplaces
*   **Etsy:** The most famous marketplace for handmade goods. **Pro tip:** Because Etsy now allows some mass-produced items, use filters like "Handmade" and read the shop's "About" section to ensure you are buying directly from the maker, not a drop-shipper.
*   **Goimagine:** A newer marketplace that is strictly for handmade goods (no mass-produced items allowed). As a bonus, they donate 100% of their profits to children's charities.
*   **Amazon Handmade:** An artisan-only section of Amazon. Makers have to apply and be audited to prove their items are genuinely handmade. 
*   **Folksy:** If you are in the UK (or willing to pay international shipping), this is the UK’s biggest online craft fair, featuring strictly British makers.
*   **Uncommon Goods:** While not *everything* here is handmade, they have a massive selection of unique, artisan-crafted items, and they focus heavily on independent makers and sustainable practices.

### 2. Global & Fair Trade Sites
*   **Novica:** Associated with National Geographic, this site connects you directly with master artisans from around the world (places like Bali, the Andes, West Africa, and India). It’s excellent for jewelry, textiles, and home decor.
*   **Ten Thousand Villages:** A pioneer of fair trade, they sell beautiful handmade gifts crafted by artisans in developing countries, ensuring they are paid a fair wage.

### 3. Social Media
*   **Instagram & TikTok:** Many of the best artisans don't use big marketplaces; they sell directly through their own websites. Search hashtags like `#handmadejewelry`, `#pottersofinstagram`, `#woodworking`, `#makersgonnamake`, or `#smallbusiness`. 
*   **Pinterest:** A great visual search engine. If you search for "handmade leather wallet," you will often find pins that link directly to an independent maker's website.

### 4. Local & In-Person Venues
*   **Craft Fairs and Maker Markets:** Look up events in your city. Organizations like the **Renegade Craft Fair** host massive, highly curated events in major cities, but even small towns have seasonal holiday markets.
*   **Farmers Markets:** Most weekend farmers markets have a dedicated section for local artisans selling soaps, candles, woodwork, and textiles.
*   **Museum Gift Shops & Art Galleries:** Local art museums and galleries often stock their gift shops with jewelry, ceramics, and prints made by local artists.
*   **Independent Boutiques:** Look for local shops in your area that advertise "locally sourced" or "artisan" goods. 

### Tips for Buying Handmade:
*   **Order Early:** Handmade items often take time to create, and independent makers don't have the shipping speed of Amazon Prime. 
*   **Ask for Customizations:** One of the best parts of buying handmade is that the maker can often personalize the item for you (e.g., engraving a name, using a specific color). Don't be afraid to send them a message!
