Stress affects both the body and the mind in many ways. When a person experiences stress, the body releases hormones such as adrenaline and cortisol. These hormones prepare the body to respond to challenges by increasing heart rate, blood pressure, and energy levels. While this response can be helpful in short-term situations, prolonged stress can have negative effects on health.

Physically, chronic stress may lead to headaches, muscle tension, fatigue, sleep problems, and digestive issues. It can also weaken the immune system, making a person more vulnerable to illnesses. Over time, long-term stress may increase the risk of serious health conditions, including heart disease and high blood pressure.

Stress also affects mental and emotional well-being. It can make it difficult to concentrate, remember information, and make decisions. People under stress may feel anxious, irritable, overwhelmed, or sad. In some cases, ongoing stress can contribute to mental health conditions such as anxiety disorders or depression.

Managing stress is important for maintaining overall health. Regular exercise, healthy eating, adequate sleep, relaxation techniques, and social support can help reduce stress and improve both physical and mental well-being. By recognizing stress and addressing it effectively, individuals can protect their health and improve their quality of life.
