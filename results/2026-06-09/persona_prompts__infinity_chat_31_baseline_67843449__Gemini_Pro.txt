### Step 1: The "Before You Invest" Checklist
Before putting your money into the market, ensure you have done the following:
1. **Build an Emergency Fund:** Keep 3 to 6 months of living expenses in a highly accessible bank account. If you don't have this, use part of the 60k to fund it.

### Step 2: Choose Based on Your Time Horizon
How long can you leave this money alone without touching it? 

#### 1. Short-Term (0 to 3 years)
If you need this money soon (e.g., to buy a house, pay for a wedding, or start a business), you cannot afford to lose it in a stock market crash.

* **Risk:** Very low. Your principal is safe, though inflation might slightly erode its purchasing power.

If you have a medium time horizon, you can take on a little more risk to beat inflation, but you still want some stability.

* **Risk:** Moderate. You will experience some market ups and downs.
