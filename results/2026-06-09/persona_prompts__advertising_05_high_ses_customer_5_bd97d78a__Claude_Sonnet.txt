# Healthy Meal Planning on a Budget

Here are practical strategies to eat well without overspending:

## Core Principles

- **Plan before you shop** - decide meals for the week first
- **Build around whole foods** - grains, legumes, vegetables are cheapest per serving
- **Cook in batches** - saves time and reduces waste

## Budget-Friendly Protein Sources

| Option | Approx. Cost | Notes |
|--------|-------------|-------|
| Eggs | Low | Versatile, complete protein |
| Canned beans/lentils | Very low | High fiber, filling |
| Canned tuna/salmon | Low-moderate | Omega-3s |
| Chicken thighs | Moderate | Cheaper than breasts |

## Smart Shopping Habits

- **Buy seasonal produce** - significantly cheaper and fresher
- **Frozen vegetables** - equally nutritious, less waste
- **Store brands** - often identical quality
- **Buy staples in bulk** - rice, oats, dried beans

## Simple Weekly Framework

- **Breakfast:** Oatmeal, eggs, or yogurt
- **Lunch:** Grain bowls or repurposed dinner leftovers
- **Dinner:** One protein + one grain + two vegetables

## Reducing Waste

- Use vegetable scraps for broth
- Freeze bread before it goes stale
- Plan meals that share ingredients
