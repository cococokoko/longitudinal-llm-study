What are the best resources for starting a plant-based diet?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
If you're starting a plant-based diet, it helps to combine practical nutrition guidance, recipes, and meal-planning resources. Here are some of the most useful options:

Nutrition and Evidence-Based Information
- NutritionFacts.org (Dr. Michael Greger)
  - Free videos and articles focused on plant-based nutrition and health research.
  - Good for understanding the science behind dietary recommendations.

- Physicians Committee for Responsible Medicine (PCRM)
  - Offers beginner guides, meal plans, recipes, and nutrition resources.
  - Particularly useful if you're interested in health-focused plant-based eating.

- Academy of Nutrition and Dietetics
  - Provides evidence-based nutrition information, including guidance on vegetarian and vegan diets.

Books
- "How Not to Die" by Michael Greger, MD
  - Explains the links between diet and chronic disease, with practical dietary advice.

- "Plant-Based on a Budget" by Toni Okamoto
  - Focuses on affordable, simple plant-based meals.

- "The Vegan Starter Kit" by Neal Barnard, MD
  - A beginner-friendly introduction to plant-based eating.

Recipes and Meal Planning
- Forks Over Knives
  - Large recipe database, meal-planning tools, and beginner resources.

- Minimalist Baker
  - Many simple plant-based recipes with relatively short ingredient lists.

- Nora Cooks
  - Vegan comfort food and everyday recipes that are easy for beginners.

- Rainbow Plant Life
  - Flavorful recipes and helpful cooking techniques for building satisfying plant-based meals.

YouTube Channels
- Pick Up Limes
  - Nutrition-informed content, meal prep ideas, and practical lifestyle tips.

- Rainbow Plant Life
  - Detailed cooking tutorials and beginner-friendly guidance.

- Simnett Nutrition
  - Fitness-oriented plant-based nutrition and recipes.

Key Nutrition Topics to Learn Early
- Protein sources: beans, lentils, tofu, tempeh, edamame, soy milk, seitan.
- Vitamin B12: essential supplement for people eating fully vegan diets.
- Iron: beans, lentils, tofu, fortified cereals, leafy greens; pair with vitamin C-rich foods.
- Omega-3 fats: flaxseeds, chia seeds, hemp seeds, walnuts, or algae-based supplements.
- Calcium: fortified plant milks, calcium-set tofu, certain leafy greens.
- Vitamin D: may require supplementation depending on sun exposure and location.

A Simple Beginner Plate
- 1/2 vegetables and fruit
- 1/4 whole grains (brown rice, oats, quinoa, whole-wheat pasta)
- 1/4 protein-rich foods (beans, lentils, tofu, tempeh)
- Include a source of healthy fat (nuts, seeds, avocado)
