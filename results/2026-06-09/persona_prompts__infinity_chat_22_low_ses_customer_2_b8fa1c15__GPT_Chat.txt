In Christianity, “discipleship” generally means following Jesus and growing in faith, but different traditions emphasize different aspects of what that looks like.

Some common views include:

- Discipleship as imitation of Jesus
  - Focuses on living as Jesus lived: loving others, serving, practicing humility, and obeying God's commands.
  - Common across many Christian traditions.

- Discipleship as spiritual formation
  - Emphasizes gradual transformation of character through prayer, Scripture, worship, and other spiritual disciplines.
  - Often highlighted in Catholic, Orthodox, and many Protestant traditions.

- Discipleship as learning and teaching
  - Sees discipleship as being taught the faith and then helping others grow in it.
  - Based on Jesus' command to “make disciples” (Matthew 28:19–20).

- Discipleship as evangelism and mission
  - Focuses on sharing the gospel and serving others.
  - Often emphasized in evangelical and missionary movements.

- Discipleship as participation in the church
  - Views growth as taking place within a community through worship, sacraments or ordinances, accountability, and service.
  - Strongly emphasized in Catholic, Orthodox, and many Protestant churches.

- Discipleship as radical commitment
  - Stresses sacrifice, simplicity, and complete devotion to Christ, even at personal cost.
  - Often associated with monastic traditions, Anabaptists, and some renewal movements.

- Discipleship as social action
  - Emphasizes justice, care for the poor, reconciliation, and transforming society according to Christian values.
  - Often associated with liberation theology and some mainline Protestant traditions.

Differences among major traditions:

- Catholic: Discipleship is nurtured through the sacraments, church life, prayer, and works of charity.
- Eastern Orthodox: Emphasizes spiritual transformation (theosis), participation in worship, prayer, and ascetic practices.
- Evangelical Protestant: Often focuses on personal conversion, Bible study, mentoring, and evangelism.
- Mainline Protestant: Frequently combines personal faith development with social responsibility and community engagement.
- Anabaptist: Strong emphasis on following Jesus' teachings in daily life, peace, community, and visible obedience.

Despite these differences, most Christians agree that discipleship involves following Jesus, growing in faith, and expressing that faith through action.
