# Healthy Meal Planning on a Budget

Great topic, especially for someone working shifts at a hospital! Here are practical strategies:

## Planning Basics
- **Plan meals weekly** before grocery shopping
- **Build around sales** at your local store
- **Cook once, eat multiple times** - batch cooking fits shift work perfectly

## Budget-Friendly Healthy Foods
| Food | Why It Works |
|------|-------------|
| Eggs | Cheap protein, quick to prepare |
| Dried beans/lentils | Very affordable, high protein/fiber |
| Frozen vegetables | Nutritious, long shelf life |
| Oats | Filling, inexpensive breakfast |
| Canned fish (tuna/salmon) | Easy protein on busy days |
| Brown rice/whole grains | Affordable complex carbs |

## Shift Work Specific Tips
- **Prep meals on days off** so you have grab-and-go options
- **Pack meals** rather than buying from the hospital cafeteria
- **Overnight oats** are perfect for early shifts
- **Slow cooker meals** can be ready when you get home

## Keeping Costs Down
- Buy store brands
- Use the whole vegetable (stems, etc.)
- Freeze bread before it goes stale
- Compare unit prices, not package prices

## Simple Weekly Budget Meals
- Stir fry with frozen veggies and rice
- Bean and vegetable soup
- Egg-based meals (frittata, scrambled eggs)
