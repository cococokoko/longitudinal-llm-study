── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Mastering the art of cooking steak.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
After a long, physically demanding shift on your feet at the hospital, you absolutely deserve a meal that feels like a high-end reward—and the best part about steak is that it is incredibly fast to cook. It’s the perfect high-protein meal to refuel you after taking care of patients all day or night.

Here is a foolproof guide to mastering the classic pan-seared steak, designed to be quick enough for a post-shift dinner but delicious enough to feel like a celebration.

### 1. Choose the Right Cut
*   **For a quick weeknight dinner:** **Sirloin** or **Flank steak**. They are leaner, cook fast, and are relatively inexpensive.
*   **For a "treat yourself" post-shift meal:** **Ribeye** or **New York Strip**. They have more marbling (fat), which means more flavor and a more forgiving cooking process.

### 2. The Prep (Do this right when you get home)
*   **Take the chill off:** Take the steak out of the fridge about 30 minutes before you cook it. (This is the perfect time to jump in the shower and change out of your scrubs). 
*   **Dry it off:** Pat the steak completely dry with paper towels. Moisture is the enemy of a good crust.
*   **Season aggressively:** Season generously with coarse Kosher salt and freshly cracked black pepper right before it goes in the pan. 

### 3. The Sear (Takes less than 10 minutes)
*   **Get a pan screaming hot:** A cast-iron skillet is best. Heat it over medium-high to high heat until it’s lightly smoking.
*   **Use the right oil:** Add a tablespoon of an oil with a high smoke point (like avocado, canola, or grapeseed oil—*not* extra virgin olive oil, which will burn).
*   **Lay it down:** Carefully lay the steak in the pan (lay it away from you so the oil doesn't splash). Let it sear undisturbed for about 2 to 3 minutes to build a beautiful brown crust. Flip it.

### 4. The "Chef" Touch: Butter Basting
*   Once you flip the steak, drop in a tablespoon or two of butter, a smashed clove of garlic, and a sprig of fresh thyme or rosemary if you have it.
*   As the butter melts and foams, tilt the pan slightly and use a spoon to continuously scoop the hot garlic-herb butter over the steak. Do this for about 1-2 minutes. This cooks the steak gently from the top and adds incredible flavor.

### 5. The Most Important Step: REST
*   Take the steak out of the pan when it's about 5 degrees *below* your target temperature (130°F for medium-rare). 
*   **Let it rest for 5 to 10 minutes** on a cutting board. If you cut into it immediately, all the juices will run out onto the board, leaving the meat dry. Resting lets the muscle fibers relax and redistribute the juices.

**A quick tip for your schedule:** If you work a stretch of 12-hour shifts, you can pre-salt your steaks and leave them uncovered on a wire rack in the fridge for up to 24 hours. This is called "dry brining." It dries out the surface for an amazing crust and seasons the meat all the way through, saving you a step when you get home exhausted!
