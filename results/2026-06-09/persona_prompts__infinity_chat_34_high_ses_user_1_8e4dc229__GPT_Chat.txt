For learning Python, you do not need a powerful machine. Reliability, keyboard quality, battery health, and Linux compatibility (if you want to experiment with Linux) matter more than raw performance.

On the secondhand market, these are usually the best values:

- Lenovo ThinkPad T480
  - One of the most commonly recommended used laptops.
  - Excellent keyboard.
  - Easy to repair and upgrade.
  - Supports Windows and Linux very well.
  - Look for 16 GB RAM if possible.
  - Typical used price: often $150–350 depending on condition and region.

- Lenovo ThinkPad T14 Gen 1 (AMD)
  - More modern and significantly faster than the T480.
  - Strong battery life.
  - Excellent for programming, data analysis, and light machine learning.
  - Typical used price: roughly $300–600.

- Dell Latitude 5400/5410/5420
  - Business-class machines designed for durability.
  - Good keyboards and serviceability.
  - Often available in large numbers from corporate lease returns.
  - Typical used price: $200–500.

- HP EliteBook 840 G6/G7
  - Premium business laptop.
  - Good display and build quality.
  - Usually lighter than older ThinkPads.
  - Typical used price: $250–550.

- Lenovo ThinkPad X1 Carbon (Gen 6–8)
  - Lightweight and pleasant to carry.
  - Excellent keyboard.
  - Less upgradeable than T-series ThinkPads.
  - Typical used price: $300–700.

Minimum specifications I would suggest today:
- CPU: Intel 8th-generation Core i5 or newer, or AMD Ryzen 5 4000-series or newer.
- RAM: 16 GB preferred (8 GB minimum).
- Storage: 256 GB SSD minimum.
- Screen: 1080p (1920×1080) or better.

If your goal is strictly:
- Learning Python, scripting, automation, web development, and data analysis: a ThinkPad T480 with 16 GB RAM is hard to beat.
- Python plus heavier data science, local AI models, or machine-learning experimentation: a ThinkPad T14 AMD Gen 1 or Gen 2 is a stronger long-term choice.
