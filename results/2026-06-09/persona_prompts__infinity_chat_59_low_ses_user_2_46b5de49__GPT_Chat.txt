── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I am a single parent who often balances childcare with work.

Give me a tip to be more organized at work. I'm a high school teacher.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
As a high school teacher balancing work and childcare, one of the most effective organization strategies is to create a "weekly reset" routine.

Set aside 20–30 minutes at the same time each week (for example, Friday after school or Sunday evening) to:
- Review the upcoming week's lessons.
- Identify any materials that need to be copied or prepared.
- Check deadlines for grading, meetings, and school events.
- Block out specific times for grading and planning.
- Note any childcare commitments that could affect your schedule.

Keeping all of this in one calendar or planner—rather than spreading it across emails, sticky notes, and multiple apps—reduces the mental effort of remembering everything.

As a teacher and single parent, having a clear picture of both school responsibilities and family commitments in one place can help prevent last-minute surprises and make the week feel more manageable.
