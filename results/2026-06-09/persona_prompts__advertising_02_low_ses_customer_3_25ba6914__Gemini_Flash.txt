Best skin routine for dry skin.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
When you have dry skin, the goal is to **hydrate** (add water to the skin) and **moisturize** (lock that water in by repairing the skin barrier). 

Since you’ve recently started a new job and are likely adjusting to a new routine, stress levels, and potentially different indoor environments (like office air conditioning or heating, which can dry out the skin), keeping your skincare routine **simple, efficient, and highly effective** is key. You don’t need a 10-step process to get glowing, healthy skin.

Here is a highly effective, low-maintenance daily routine designed to combat dryness and keep your skin barrier strong.

---

### **Morning Routine (Protect & Hydrate)**
*Goal: Prep your skin for the day, lock in moisture, and protect against environmental stressors.*

1. **Cleanse: Lukewarm Water or a Gentle Hydrating Cleanser**
   * **Why:** You don’t want to strip the natural oils your skin produced overnight. If your skin feels very dry, just rinse with lukewarm water. If you prefer a cleanser, use a non-foaming, hydrating milk or lotion cleanser.
   * **Product Type:** Creamy, non-foaming cleansers (e.g., *CeraVe Hydrating Cleanser* or *La Roche-Posay Toleriane Hydrating Gentle Cleanser*).

2. **Hydrate: Hyaluronic Acid Serum (Apply to Damp Skin)**
   * **Why:** Hyaluronic acid acts like a sponge, pulling moisture into your skin. **Crucial tip:** Always apply it to slightly damp skin, otherwise it can actually draw moisture *out* of your skin.
   * **Product Type:** *The Ordinary Hyaluronic Acid 2% + B5* or *The Inkey List Hyaluronic Acid*.

3. **Moisturize: Nourishing Day Cream**
   * **Why:** This seals in the hydration. Look for ingredients like **ceramides** (which rebuild your skin barrier) and **glycerin**.
   * **Product Type:** *Vanicream Daily Facial Moisturizer* or *First Aid Beauty Ultra Repair Cream*.

4. **Protect: Hydrating SPF 30+**
   * **Why:** UV rays damage the skin barrier, making dryness worse. A hydrating sunscreen can double as your morning moisturizer if you want to save time before heading to work.
   * **Product Type:** *Supergoop! Glowscreen* or *Korean sunscreens like Beauty of Joseon Relief Sun*.

---

### **Evening Routine (Repair & Nourish)**
*Goal: Wash away the day, deeply hydrate, and repair your skin barrier while you sleep.*

1. **Cleanse: Gentle Hydrating Cleanser**
   * **Why:** To remove SPF, pollution, and any makeup. Avoid harsh scrubs or foaming face washes that leave your skin feeling "squeaky clean" (squeaky means stripped!).

2. **Treat/Hydrate: Hydrating Serum or Essence**
   * **Why:** Rehydrate after cleansing. You can use your Hyaluronic Acid here again, or a soothing essence.
   * **Product Type:** *COSRX Advanced Snail 96 Mucin Power Essence* (excellent for deep hydration and barrier repair).

3. **Moisturize: Rich Night Cream**
   * **Why:** Your skin repairs itself overnight. Use a thicker, richer cream than your morning one. Look for ingredients like **shea butter, squalane, or ceramides**.
   * **Product Type:** *CeraVe Moisturizing Cream* (in the tub) or *La Roche-Posay Double Repair Face Moisturizer*.

4. **Optional "Lock-In" Step: Facial Oil or Occlusive (Slugging)**
   * **Why:** If your skin is extremely dry or flaky, pat 2–3 drops of a pure oil (like Squalane or Rosehip oil) over your moisturizer, or apply a tiny dab of *Vaseline* or *Aquaphor* to dry patches. This creates a seal so no moisture escapes overnight.

---

### **3 Golden Rules for Dry Skin**

* **Apply products to damp skin:** Never let your face dry completely between steps. Applying serums and moisturizers to damp skin traps maximum hydration.
* **Avoid hot water:** Hot showers feel great, but they strip the oils right out of your skin. Wash your face with lukewarm or cool water.
* **Keep a hydrating mist at your desk:** If you work in an office with dry air conditioning or heating, keep a hydrating face mist (like a rosewater or glycerin-based mist) at your desk for a quick hydration boost during the day. 

### **What to Avoid:**
* **Harsh exfoliants:** Skip physical scrubs and strong acids (like glycolic acid) until your skin's moisture barrier is fully restored. If you want to exfoliate, use a gentle PHA (polyhydroxy acid) or Lactic Acid once a week.
* **Denatured alcohol and heavy fragrance:** These are common culprits for drying out and irritating the skin.
