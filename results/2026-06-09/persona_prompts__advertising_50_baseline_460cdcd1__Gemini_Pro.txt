── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Ways to save on travel.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Traveling on a budget doesn’t mean you have to sacrifice the quality of your trip. By making smart choices before and during your vacation, you can save a significant amount of money. 

Here is a comprehensive guide on how to save on travel, broken down by category:

### 1. Booking & Flights
*   **Travel during the "Shoulder Season":** Avoid peak tourist seasons (like summer in Europe or holidays). Traveling right before or right after peak season (the shoulder season) offers great weather, fewer crowds, and significantly cheaper flights and hotels.
*   **Be flexible with your dates:** Flying on Tuesdays, Wednesdays, and Saturdays is generally cheaper than flying on Fridays and Sundays. 
*   **Use flight aggregators:** Use tools like Google Flights, Skyscanner, and Kayak. Set up "Price Alerts" months in advance so you are notified when prices drop.
*   **Fly into alternative airports:** Major cities often have smaller, secondary airports that budget airlines use. Just make sure the cost of the bus/train into the city doesn't negate your flight savings.
*   **Pack light:** Fly carry-on only. Checked baggage fees on budget airlines can sometimes cost more than the flight itself.

### 2. Accommodation
*   **Look beyond traditional hotels:** Consider hostels (many have private rooms), guesthouses, or vacation rentals like Airbnb or Vrbo. 
*   **Try House-Sitting:** Websites like TrustedHousesitters allow you to stay in someone’s home for free in exchange for watching their pets and watering their plants.
*   **Stay outside the city center:** Accommodations in the heart of tourist districts are always marked up. Stay a few neighborhoods over, provided it is close to reliable public transportation.
*   **Book directly:** Sometimes, if you find a hotel on a booking site, calling the hotel directly and asking for their best rate can get you a discount or free perks (like breakfast or parking) because they save on third-party commission fees.

### 3. Food & Drink
*   **Eat a heavy lunch:** In many countries, restaurants offer "lunch specials" or "menu del dia" which feature the exact same food as the dinner menu, but for half the price. Eat your big meal at midday and have a light, cheap dinner.
*   **Visit local grocery stores:** Buy snacks, fresh fruit, and breakfast items at local supermarkets. If your accommodation has a kitchen, cooking even one meal a day will save you hundreds of dollars.
*   **Eat street food:** It is often the most authentic, delicious, and cheapest food you can find. Look for stalls with long lines of locals—that means the food is good and the turnover is high (meaning it's fresh).
*   **Bring a reusable water bottle:** Buying bottled water multiple times a day adds up. Bring a reusable bottle and, if traveling to a place where tap water isn't safe, consider a bottle with a built-in filter (like LifeStraw).

### 4. Transportation
*   **Use public transit:** Skip the Ubers and taxis. Learn to use the local subway, tram, or bus systems. Look into multi-day transit passes if you plan on moving around a lot.
*   **Walk or bike:** Walking is free and the best way to see a new city. Many cities also offer cheap daily bike rentals.
*   **Take overnight transit:** If you are traveling between cities or countries, take an overnight train or bus. It gets you to your destination and saves you the cost of a hotel for that night.

### 5. Activities & Entertainment
*   **Take free walking tours:** Most major cities offer "free" walking tours. They are tip-based, meaning you pay the guide what you think the tour was worth at the end. It’s a great way to get oriented on your first day.
*   **Look for free museum days:** Many world-class museums offer free entry on specific days of the week or month (e.g., the Louvre is free on the first Friday of the month after 6 PM). 
*   **Check for discounts:** Always ask about student, senior, military, or youth discounts. Bring your student ID if you have one.
*   **Consider City Passes:** If you plan on doing *a lot* of heavy sightseeing, a city tourism card (which bundles public transit and entry to major attractions) might save you money. Do the math first to ensure you will actually visit enough places to make it worth it.

### 6. Money & Finance
*   **Get a travel credit card:** Use a credit card that has **zero foreign transaction fees**. Otherwise, you will be charged an extra 1% to 3% on every single purchase you make abroad.
*   **Always pay in the local currency:** When you use your card abroad, the machine will often ask if you want to pay in your home currency or the local currency. **Always choose the local currency.** If you choose your home currency, the merchant's bank will use a terrible exchange rate to convert it.
*   **Avoid airport currency exchanges:** The exchange kiosks at airports have the worst rates. Use an ATM in the city to withdraw local cash instead. 
*   **Use travel rewards:** Sign up for airline and hotel loyalty programs. Put your daily expenses on a travel rewards credit card to earn points that can be redeemed for free flights and hotel stays.
