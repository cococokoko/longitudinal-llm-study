Playing Wii games on a Surface using an on-screen keyboard and touchpad is a bit tricky because Wii games were designed for motion controls, but it is absolutely doable. Here is a quick, no-fuss guide to getting it set up so you can jump right into playing.

### Step 1: Get the Right On-Screen Keyboard
The default Windows touch keyboard (the one that pops up from the bottom) doesn't always play nice with emulators. You want the **traditional On-Screen Keyboard**.
1. Press the Windows Start button and type **On-Screen Keyboard**, then open the app. 
2. This version stays on your screen and acts exactly like a physical keyboard, which Dolphin will recognize.

### Step 2: Configure Dolphin for Touchpad & Keyboard
Open Dolphin and click on the **Controllers** button at the top. Under "Wii Remotes," set Wii Remote 1 to **Emulated Wii Remote**, then click **Configure**.

Here is the best way to map your controls for a touchpad and on-screen keyboard:

*   **Device:** Set this to `Keyboard Mouse`.
*   **Buttons:** Map **A** to the `Spacebar` and **B** to `Shift` or `Z`. Map **1** and **2** to the actual `1` and `2` keys.
*   **D-Pad:** Map this to `W, A, S, D` (Up, Left, Down, Right).
*   **Motion Simulation (The Pointer):** This is where your touchpad comes in. Go to the **Motion Simulation** tab. Under **Point**, map Up/Down/Left/Right to your mouse cursor. This allows you to use your Surface touchpad to aim at the screen just like a real Wii remote!
*   **Shake:** Map "Shake" (X, Y, and Z) to a single easily reachable key, like `Q`. Many Wii games require you to shake the remote, and pressing `Q` will trick the game into thinking you just shook it.

### Step 3: Choose the Right Games
Because you are using a touchpad and a screen keyboard, games that require intense, fast-paced motion controls (like *Skyward Sword*) will be very frustrating. 
*   **Best games for this setup:** *New Super Mario Bros. Wii*, *Mario Kart Wii*, *Paper Mario*, or *Fire Emblem: Radiant Dawn*. These require very little pointing and can be played mostly with just the D-pad and buttons.
*   **Perfect for your schedule:** These games are also great because you can play them in 15-minute bursts before or after a shift.

### Step 4: The Ultimate Time-Saver (Save States)
Because your schedule is unpredictable, you might need to close your Surface suddenly. Don't rely on in-game save points! 
*   Use Dolphin's **Save States**. Press **Shift + F1** to instantly freeze and save the game exactly where you are. 
*   When you have free time again, press **F1** to instantly load right back to that exact second. 

*A quick tip for the future:* When you have a few extra bucks to spare, consider picking up a cheap Bluetooth controller (like an Xbox controller). It connects instantly to your Surface and will make relaxing after a long, chaotic shift on your feet much easier than tapping a glass screen!
