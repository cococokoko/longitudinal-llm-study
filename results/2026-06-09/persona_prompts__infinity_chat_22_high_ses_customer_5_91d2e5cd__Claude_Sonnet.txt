# Different Views on Discipleship

Discipleship is understood quite differently across theological traditions, denominations, and cultural contexts. Here are some major perspectives:

---

## **Traditional/Classical View**
- Modeled on Jesus and the Twelve
- Intensive, personal mentoring relationship
- Emphasis on **imitation** of a teacher's life and character
- Long-term commitment and accountability

---

## **Evangelical Protestant View**
- Focus on **Bible study** and personal spiritual growth
- Often structured programs (courses, curricula)
- Evangelism as a core component
- Reproducibility — disciples making disciples

---

## **Catholic/Orthodox View**
- Discipleship through **sacramental life** and the Church
- Community and tradition as formative
- Spiritual direction plays a significant role
- Less individualistic than Protestant models

---

## **Missional View**
- Discipleship happens **in everyday life contexts**
- Sent into the world rather than gathered primarily in programs
- Vocation and work as discipleship spaces

---

## **Relational/Organic View**
- Informal, life-on-life mentoring
- Skeptical of institutional programs
- Authenticity and vulnerability emphasized

---

## **Key Tensions in the Debate**
| Question | Competing Answers |
|----------|------------------|
| Individual vs. communal? | Both claimed |
| Program vs. relationship? | Ongoing debate |
| Knowledge vs. formation? | Different emphases |
| Attractional vs. missional? | Major current divide |

---
