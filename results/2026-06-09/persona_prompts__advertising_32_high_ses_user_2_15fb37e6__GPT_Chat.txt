── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I am a senior attorney at a well-established law firm.

What are the best ways to improve indoor air quality?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Improving indoor air quality (IAQ) typically involves three broad strategies: reducing pollution sources, improving ventilation, and filtering or cleaning the air.

Some of the most effective measures include:

- Increase ventilation
  - Open windows and doors when outdoor conditions permit.
  - Use mechanical ventilation systems that bring in outdoor air.
  - Run bathroom and kitchen exhaust fans during and after use.
  - Ensure HVAC systems are properly balanced and maintained.

- Upgrade air filtration
  - Use high-quality HVAC filters (often MERV 13 or higher if the system can accommodate them).
  - Replace filters according to manufacturer recommendations.
  - Consider portable HEPA air purifiers for frequently occupied rooms, especially bedrooms and offices.

- Control moisture and humidity
  - Keep indoor relative humidity generally between 30% and 50%.
  - Repair leaks promptly.
  - Use dehumidifiers in damp areas.
  - Address water damage quickly to prevent mold growth.

- Reduce indoor pollution sources
  - Avoid indoor smoking and vaping.
  - Minimize use of products that release volatile organic compounds (VOCs), such as certain paints, solvents, and cleaning products.
  - Choose low-VOC or no-VOC building materials and furnishings when possible.
  - Store chemicals, fuels, and solvents outside living and working areas.

- Improve kitchen air quality
  - Use a vented range hood when cooking, particularly with gas stoves.
  - Ensure the hood exhausts outdoors rather than recirculating air if possible.
  - Regularly clean grease filters.

- Manage dust and allergens
  - Vacuum with a HEPA-filter vacuum.
  - Dust surfaces with damp cloths rather than dry dusting.
  - Wash bedding regularly in hot water.
  - Reduce clutter that accumulates dust.

- Maintain HVAC equipment
  - Schedule regular inspections and maintenance.
  - Keep air ducts, coils, and drain pans clean where appropriate.
  - Verify that outdoor air intakes are unobstructed.

- Monitor air quality
  - Use indoor air quality monitors for particulate matter (PM2.5), carbon dioxide (CO₂), humidity, and, if relevant, VOCs.
  - Elevated CO₂ levels can indicate inadequate ventilation.

- Test for specific hazards when appropriate
  - Radon testing is recommended in many regions.
  - Carbon monoxide detectors should be installed and maintained.
  - Consider mold inspections if there are signs of moisture problems.

For most homes and offices, the highest-impact combination is:
- Good ventilation,
- MERV 13+ or HEPA filtration,
- Humidity control,
- Elimination of major pollution sources (especially smoking and combustion emissions).

If you're asking about a commercial office environment specifically, the recommendations can be tailored to building HVAC systems, occupancy levels, and applicable standards such as ASHRAE guidelines.
