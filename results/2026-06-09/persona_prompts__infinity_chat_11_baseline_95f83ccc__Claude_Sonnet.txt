── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I'm 60 years old and haven't achieved anything. What have I missed out on?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This is worth thinking through carefully rather than just validating the feeling.

## Some honest considerations:

**"Haven't achieved anything" deserves scrutiny**
- By whose definition of achievement?
- Have you raised children, maintained relationships, helped people, worked honestly? Those count
- You may be comparing yourself to highlight reels

**What you might genuinely reflect on:**
- Skills you wished you'd developed
- Relationships you didn't invest in
- Health habits you delayed
- Creative or intellectual pursuits you postponed

## The realistic part:

**Some things are genuinely harder at 60:**
- Certain athletic peaks
- Some career trajectories
- Compound interest on savings started earlier

**But many things aren't closed:**
- Learning new skills (research supports this at any age)
- Meaningful relationships
- Creative work
- Contributing to others
- Physical health improvements
- Starting businesses (actually common in 60s)

## What might actually help:

The feeling of "haven't achieved anything" at 60 sometimes indicates:
- Depression worth addressing
- Specific regrets that need naming
- Comparison patterns that distort reality
- Genuine gaps you can still address

**What specifically feels missing?** That's probably more useful than a general achievement audit.
