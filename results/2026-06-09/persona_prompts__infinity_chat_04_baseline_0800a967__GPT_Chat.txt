1. Gorz and the Digital Economy

Core question:
- How would Gorz's critique of labor apply to platform capitalism, AI, and digital production?

- Gorz anticipated many features of contemporary digital capitalism: the declining centrality of labor-time, the growing importance of knowledge, and the difficulty of measuring value through traditional labor metrics.
- You could compare Gorz's "immaterial labor" insights with contemporary theorists such as Antonio Negri, Carlo Vercellone, Nick Srnicek, or Shoshana Zuboff.

Research questions:
- Does AI strengthen Gorz's argument that capitalism undermines its own value basis?
- Can digital labor still be understood through Marx's value theory, or does Gorz point toward a different framework?

2. Gorz and Ecological Marxism

Core question:
- Is Gorz better understood as a Marxist ecological thinker or as a critic of traditional Marxism?

Possible argument:
- Gorz may offer a bridge between Marxist theories of capital accumulation and ecological limits.
- Compare him with John Bellamy Foster, Kohei Saito, James O'Connor, and Jason Moore.

Research questions:
- Did Gorz develop an ecological critique that surpasses productivist tendencies in classical Marxism?
- What distinguishes Gorz's ecological politics from contemporary degrowth theory?

3. Gorz and Degrowth

Core question:

Possible argument:
- Many contemporary degrowth themes—reduced working time, autonomy, non-market activity, criticism of consumerism—appear in Gorz's work decades earlier.
- Yet Gorz may differ from modern degrowth theorists in important ways.

Research questions:
- What are the continuities and discontinuities between Gorz and degrowth?
- Can Gorz provide a stronger class analysis than contemporary degrowth literature often does?

4. Gorz's Critique of Work and Contemporary Anti-Work Politics

Core question:

Possible argument:

Research questions:
- Is Gorz's concept of autonomy richer than contemporary anti-work politics?
- How does Gorz compare with Kathi Weeks, David Graeber, or the post-work tradition?
