## Example of a Linear Graph (Path Graph)

A simple example is the **path graph P₄**, consisting of 4 vertices connected in a line:

```
A --- B --- C --- D
```

### Formally:
- **Vertices:** V = {A, B, C, D}
- **Edges:** E = {AB, BC, CD}

### Key Properties:
| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| Number of vertices | 4 |
| Number of edges | 3 |
| Degree of endpoints (A, D) | 1 |
| Degree of interior vertices (B, C) | 2 |

### Notes:
- Each vertex connects to at most **two neighbors**
- The graph is **acyclic** (no cycles)
- It has exactly **two vertices of degree 1** (the endpoints)
- Generally, a path graph **Pₙ** has *n* vertices and *n−1* edges

This is one of the most fundamental structures in graph theory and serves as a building block for trees and other more complex graphs.
