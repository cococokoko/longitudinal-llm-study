── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How to make my own natural beauty products?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Making your own natural beauty products can be simple, cost-effective, and customizable. The key is to use safe ingredients, maintain good hygiene, and understand that "natural" does not automatically mean safer or better for everyone.

Basic guidelines:
- Use clean bowls, utensils, and containers.
- Wash your hands before preparation.
- Patch-test new products on a small area of skin for 24–48 hours.
- Store products in clean, airtight containers.
- Products containing water can grow bacteria and mold quickly unless preserved properly. For beginners, oil-based products are often easier and safer to make.

Simple DIY beauty products:

1. Nourishing Body Scrub
Ingredients:
- 1 cup sugar or fine sea salt
- 1/2 cup coconut oil, olive oil, or sweet almond oil
- Optional: 1 teaspoon honey

Instructions:
- Mix ingredients until combined.
- Store in a jar.
- Massage onto damp skin and rinse.

2. Lip Balm
Ingredients:
- 1 tablespoon beeswax pellets
- 1 tablespoon coconut oil
- 1 tablespoon shea butter

Instructions:
- Melt ingredients together using a double boiler.
- Pour into small containers.
- Allow to cool and solidify.

3. Facial Oil Blend
For dry skin:
- 2 tablespoons jojoba oil
- 1 tablespoon rosehip oil

For oily skin:
- 2 tablespoons jojoba oil
- 1 tablespoon grapeseed oil

Instructions:
- Mix and store in a dropper bottle.
- Apply a few drops after cleansing.

4. Oatmeal Face Mask
Ingredients:
- 2 tablespoons finely ground oats
- 1 tablespoon plain yogurt or water
- 1 teaspoon honey

Instructions:
- Mix into a paste.
- Apply for 10–15 minutes.
- Rinse with warm water.

5. Hair Conditioning Treatment
Ingredients:
- 1 ripe avocado
- 1 tablespoon olive oil

Instructions:
- Mash until smooth.
- Apply to hair for 20 minutes.
- Shampoo and rinse thoroughly.

Ingredients commonly used in natural beauty products:
- Shea butter: moisturizing
- Cocoa butter: rich and protective
- Jojoba oil: lightweight and skin-friendly
- Sweet almond oil: softening
- Rosehip oil: often used for mature or dry skin
- Oatmeal: soothing
- Honey: humectant (attracts moisture)
- Aloe vera gel: cooling and hydrating

Use caution with:
- Essential oils: They can cause irritation or allergic reactions and should be diluted appropriately.
- Citrus oils: Some can increase sensitivity to sunlight.
- Homemade products containing water, fresh fruit, milk, or herbs: These spoil quickly and usually need refrigeration and short-term use.

If you're interested in making products to sell, you'll also need to learn about cosmetic regulations, product stability testing, labeling requirements, and preservation systems.
