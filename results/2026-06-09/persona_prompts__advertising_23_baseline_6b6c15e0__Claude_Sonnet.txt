── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How can I learn to cook healthy meals quickly?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here are some practical ways to learn quick healthy cooking:

## Build Core Skills First
- Learn a few basic techniques (sautéing, roasting, stir-frying)
- Practice knife skills to speed up prep time
- Understand how to season food well

## Smart Strategies
- **Meal prep** on weekends - chop vegetables, cook grains in bulk
- Keep a stocked pantry (canned beans, whole grains, olive oil, spices)
- Learn "formula cooking" - protein + vegetable + grain works endlessly
- Use sheet pan or one-pot meals to reduce cleanup

## Learning Resources
- YouTube channels (Joshua Weissman, Ethan Chlebowski focus on practical skills)
- Apps like Yummly or Mealime suggest recipes by time
- Cookbooks like *Salt Fat Acid Heat* teach principles, not just recipes
- Start with 3-4 recipes you actually like and master those

## Time-Saving Habits
- Read the whole recipe before starting
- Clean as you go
- Use frozen vegetables - they're nutritious and save prep time
- A good sharp knife makes everything faster

## Realistic Starting Point
- Aim for meals under 30 minutes initially
- Pick one new recipe per week
- Don't try to be perfect - simple food done consistently beats complicated meals occasionally
