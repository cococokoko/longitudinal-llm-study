Learning guitar is a great skill to build, and steady practice matters more than long practice sessions.

A simple beginner roadmap:

1. Learn the basics
- How to hold the guitar comfortably
- Names of the strings: E, A, D, G, B, E
- How to tune the guitar (a tuner app is fine)
- Proper fretting and picking technique

2. Start with a few essential chords
- Em (E minor)
- Am (A minor)
- C major
- G major
- D major
- E major

3. Practice chord changes
- Switch slowly between two chords at a time
- Focus on accuracy before speed
- Use a metronome at a comfortable tempo

4. Learn basic strumming patterns
- Downstrokes only at first
- Then try simple patterns like:
  - Down, Down, Down, Down
  - Down, Down-Up, Up-Down-Up

5. Play real songs early
- Choose songs that use 2–4 chords
- Playing music you enjoy helps motivation

6. Build a daily routine
- 5 minutes: tuning and warm-up
- 10 minutes: chord practice
- 10 minutes: strumming or rhythm
- 10 minutes: songs

Common beginner challenges:
- Sore fingertips: normal during the first few weeks
- Buzzing strings: usually caused by finger placement or pressure
- Slow chord changes: everyone experiences this initially
