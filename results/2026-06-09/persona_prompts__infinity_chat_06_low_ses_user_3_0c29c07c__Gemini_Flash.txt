── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I recently started a new job and am still on probation.

How do I understand what I want?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Starting a new job is a vulnerable and overwhelming time. When you are on probation, your brain is naturally in "survival and adaptation" mode—trying to learn names, systems, culture, and expectations. 

In this state, it is very difficult to know what you *actually* want because you are too busy trying to *fit in*. 

To understand what you want during this transition period, you need to shift from being a passive observer to an active researcher of your own experience. Here is a step-by-step framework to help you figure it out.

---

### 1. Separate "New Job Anxiety" from "I Don't Want This"
Before you can figure out what you want, you must filter out the noise. 
* **The Probation Fog:** In the first 30–90 days, almost everyone feels incompetent, overwhelmed, or out of place. This is normal.
* **The Test:** Ask yourself: *"Am I unhappy because the work/company is bad, or am I just uncomfortable because I am learning something new?"* 
* **Action:** Give yourself permission to not have it all figured out right now. Your only goal for the first month is to stabilize.

### 2. Run a "Daily Energy Audit"
Instead of asking yourself the massive question, "What do I want in my career?", look at the micro-moments of your day. Keep a small notebook or a digital document and track three things daily:

* **Energizers:** What tasks, conversations, or moments made you feel capable, interested, or fast-moving? (e.g., "I loved organizing that spreadsheet," or "I really enjoyed brainstorming with Sarah.")
* **Drainers:** What felt like pulling teeth? (e.g., "Writing that status report felt incredibly tedious," or "The 3-hour team meeting drained my soul.")
* **The Environment:** How do you feel about the *way* the company works? Do you like the level of supervision? Do you like the communication style?

*After 2–3 weeks, look for patterns. This will tell you what you actually value in a day-to-day job.*

### 3. Define Your "Three Pillars"
To understand what you want, you need to categorize your desires. We usually want things in three distinct buckets. Rate how your current job is fulfilling these:

1. **The Work (The "What"):** Do you actually like the tasks? Do you want to be a specialist, a manager, a creator, or an executor?
2. **The Lifestyle (The "How"):** Does this job allow you to live the life you want? (e.g., hybrid flexibility, strict 9-to-5 boundaries, high-income potential, low stress).
3. **The Culture (The "Who"):** Do you want a highly collaborative, social environment, or do you prefer to be left alone to do your work? Do you respect your manager?

*Which of these three is your non-negotiable priority right now?*

### 4. Use "Contrast" to Find Clarity
It is often much easier to identify what you *don't* want than what you *do* want. Use your dislikes as a compass.
* If you hate how disorganized your onboarding is, it means you value **structure and clear processes**.
* If you hate how micromanaged you feel, it means you value **autonomy and trust**.
* If you find the work boring, it means you value **intellectual challenge or creativity**.

*Turn every complaint you have about your new job into a statement of what you actually value.*

### 5. Leverage Your Probationary Status
Probation is a two-way street. The company is evaluating you, but **you are also evaluating them.** Use this time to ask questions and explore:
* **Shadow people:** Ask to sit in on meetings or shadow colleagues in different departments. Say, *"Since I'm new, I'd love to understand how your team interacts with ours."*
* **Observe the "veterans":** Look at the people who have been there for 2–5 years. Do you want their lives? Do you want their jobs? If the answer is a resounding "no," this is a major clue.

### 6. Give It a "Date of Evaluation"
Do not try to make a decision today. Set a specific date on your calendar—perhaps 2 weeks before your probation ends, or at the 90-day mark. 

Tell yourself: *"I am not allowed to quit or make any major decisions until [Date]. Until then, I am just gathering data."* This removes the daily pressure of "Do I like this? Is this what I want?" and allows you to just experience the job.

### Summary Questions to Ask Yourself Tonight:
1. *If I could wave a magic wand and change three things about my current workday, what would they be?*
2. *Am I running **toward** a specific goal, or am I just running **away** from discomfort?*
3. *What does a "good day" look like for me?*
