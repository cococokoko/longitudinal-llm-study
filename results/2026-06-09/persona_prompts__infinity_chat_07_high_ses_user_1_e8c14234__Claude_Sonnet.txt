This is a genuinely interesting epistemological question, and your background makes it worth engaging seriously.

## The straightforward answer: Yes, practically impossible

**Empirically:**
- The volume of published knowledge doubles roughly every few years
- Even within neurosurgery specifically, no one masters all subspecialties, techniques, and research
- You likely experience this directly - the field has expanded beyond any individual's complete grasp

## But the deeper question is more interesting

**There are different types of "impossible":**
- **Practically impossible** - clearly yes, given finite time and cognitive capacity
- **Theoretically impossible** - this is genuinely debatable

**Philosophical considerations worth thinking about:**
- Some argue knowledge has **fundamental limits** (Gödel's incompleteness theorems suggest this mathematically)
- **Unknown unknowns** - we can't even fully map what we don't know
- Some knowledge may be **physically inaccessible** (beyond observable universe, etc.)

## What's honest to acknowledge

- The question assumes "everything" is a coherent, bounded set - it may not be
- Your expertise likely gives you **calibrated awareness** of how much remains uncertain even in your specialty
- Recognizing limits is arguably a **marker of genuine expertise**
