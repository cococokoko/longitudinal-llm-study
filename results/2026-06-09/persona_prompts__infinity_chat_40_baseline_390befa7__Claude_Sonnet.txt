── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
My girlfriend is buying a house. I plan to marry her in a year or so. I don't know if it's fair for me to pay for the house or not. What's the best solution?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This is a really common situation and there are several reasonable approaches. Here are some things to consider:

## Key Questions to Think Through

**About your relationship:**
- Are you planning to live there together?
- How solid is the relationship really?
- Have you had serious financial conversations?

**About fairness:**
- What are your relative incomes/assets?
- Would you be building equity together or just paying her mortgage?

## Practical Options

**If you move in before marriage:**
- Pay rent at market rate (protects you both)
- Create a written cohabitation agreement
- Consider contributing to a down payment only if you're on the title

**If you want to contribute toward ownership:**
- Be added to the mortgage/deed
- Have a lawyer document any financial contributions
- Wait until marriage to combine finances

**The honest concern:**
- Money paid toward someone else's mortgage without being on the title is essentially a gift
- If the relationship doesn't work out, you likely won't get it back
- This is a legitimate financial risk worth taking seriously

## My Straightforward Suggestion
**Talk to her directly about it** - how you handle this conversation will tell you a lot about how you'll handle finances in marriage.

Consider consulting a **family law attorney** briefly, since property laws vary significantly by location.

What's your specific situation - are you planning to live there?
