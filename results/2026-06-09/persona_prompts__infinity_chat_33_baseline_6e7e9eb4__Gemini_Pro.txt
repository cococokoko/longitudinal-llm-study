── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Help me use my Microsoft Surface touchpad and on-screen keyboard to run Dolphin Emulator for the Wii.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Playing Wii games on Dolphin Emulator using only a Microsoft Surface touchpad and the Windows On-Screen Keyboard (OSK) is entirely possible, though it requires some specific setup. Because Wii games rely heavily on motion controls and pointing, you will need to map these functions carefully.

Here is a step-by-step guide to setting this up for the best possible experience.

### Step 1: Prepare Your Surface
1. **Enable the On-Screen Keyboard:** Press **Windows Key + Ctrl + O** to bring up the built-in Windows On-Screen Keyboard. 
2. **Keep it visible:** You will need to run Dolphin in **Windowed Mode** (not fullscreen). If you run it in fullscreen, the game will cover the keyboard.
3. **Touchpad settings:** Ensure "Tap to click" is enabled in your Windows Touchpad settings for easier clicking.

### Step 2: Open Dolphin Controller Settings
1. Open Dolphin Emulator.
2. Click on the **Controllers** button at the top.
3. Under "Wii Remotes," look at **Wii Remote 1**.
4. Change the dropdown menu to **Emulated Wii Remote**.
5. Click the **Configure** button next to it.
6. At the top left of the new window, ensure the **Device** is set to `Keyboard Mouse` (it might look something like `DInput/0/Keyboard Mouse`).

### Step 3: Map the Controls
Here is the most efficient way to map a Wiimote to a touchpad and OSK. Click the box next to each input in Dolphin, then press the corresponding key on your OSK or click your touchpad.

**Buttons:**
*   **A Button:** Map this to **Left Click** on your touchpad.
*   **B Button (Trigger):** Map this to **Right Click** on your touchpad.
*   **D-Pad (Up, Down, Left, Right):** Map these to **W, A, S, D** or the **Arrow Keys** on your OSK.
*   **- (Minus) / + (Plus):** Map to **Q** and **E**.
*   **1 and 2 Buttons:** Map to **1** and **2** on the OSK number row.
*   **Home:** Map to **Enter** or **Backspace**.

**Motion Simulation (Crucial for Wii Games):**
Many Wii games require you to "shake" the remote.
1. Go to the **Motion Simulation** tab.
2. Look for the **Shake** section (X, Y, and Z).
3. Map all three (X, Y, and Z) to the **Spacebar** on your OSK. Now, whenever a game asks you to shake the controller, just tap the Spacebar.

**IR Pointer (The Wii Screen Cursor):**
You want your touchpad to act as the Wii pointer.
1. Go to the **Motion Input** tab (or **Point** tab, depending on your Dolphin version).
2. Under **Point**, map them as follows:
    *   **Up:** `Cursor Y-` (Move your finger up on the touchpad)
    *   **Down:** `Cursor Y+` (Move your finger down)
    *   **Left:** `Cursor X-` (Move your finger left)
    *   **Right:** `Cursor X+` (Move your finger right)
3. *Note:* Check the "Relative Input" box if your cursor feels like it's flying off the screen too easily.

### Step 4: Nunchuk Setup (If your game requires it)
If you are playing a game like *Super Mario Galaxy* or *Zelda*, you need a Nunchuk.
1. On the main configuration screen, look at the **Extension** dropdown on the right side.
2. Select **Nunchuk**.
3. Click the **Configure** button next to it.
4. **Stick (Movement):** Map Up/Down/Left/Right to **I, J, K, L** (since WASD is your D-Pad).
5. **C and Z buttons:** Map to **U** and **O**.

### Important Tips for Playing This Way
*   **Multi-touch limitations:** The Windows On-Screen Keyboard does not handle pressing multiple keys at the exact same time very well. Games that require you to hold a button while pressing another might be frustrating.
*   **Game Selection:** This setup works best for games that don't require fast reflexes. Turn-based RPGs (like *Fire Emblem*), puzzle games, or point-and-click games will work great. Fast-paced platformers will be very difficult.
*   **Profile Saving:** Once you map all these keys, type a name (like "Surface Touchpad") into the **Profile** box at the top right of the controller config screen and click **Save**. This way, you don't have to remap it if you accidentally change a setting.

*Alternative Suggestion:* Because the Surface has built-in Bluetooth, if you find the OSK too frustrating, you can easily pair a standard Xbox, PlayStation, or Nintendo Switch Pro controller to your Surface and map the Dolphin controls to that instead!
