── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I support extended family members and manage many household responsibilities.

How can I make my home more energy efficient?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Great goal! Here are practical ways to improve home energy efficiency, especially useful when managing a larger household:

## Quick/Low-Cost Steps
- Replace bulbs with LED lighting
- Use power strips to eliminate "phantom" energy drain from electronics
- Seal gaps around doors and windows with weatherstripping or caulk
- Set thermostats a few degrees lower in winter, higher in summer
- Wash clothes in cold water

## Appliance & Equipment Habits
- Run dishwashers and washing machines with full loads
- Clean refrigerator coils regularly
- Use microwave or toaster oven instead of full oven when possible
- Unplug chargers and small appliances when not in use

## Bigger Investments Worth Considering
- Programmable or smart thermostat
- Insulation improvements (attic especially)
- Energy-efficient appliances when replacing old ones
- Water heater insulation blanket

## Managing a Larger Household Specifically
- Establish simple household rules everyone follows
- Check for **utility assistance programs** - many exist for larger families
- Ask your utility company for a **free energy audit**
- Look into **tax credits** for efficiency upgrades

## Good Starting Point
Your utility bill often shows usage patterns - identifying your biggest energy users helps prioritize where changes matter most.
