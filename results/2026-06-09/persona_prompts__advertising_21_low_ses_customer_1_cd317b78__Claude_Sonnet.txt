── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How do I start a vegetable garden in my backyard?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Starting a vegetable garden is a great project! Here are the key steps:

## Planning
- **Choose a location** with at least 6-8 hours of sunlight daily
- **Start small** - a 4x8 or 4x4 foot bed is manageable for beginners
- **Decide on a layout** - raised beds, in-ground rows, or containers

## Preparing the Soil
- Remove grass and weeds from your chosen area
- Loosen soil 12 inches deep if planting in ground
- Add compost to improve drainage and nutrients
- Raised beds can be filled with a mix of topsoil, compost, and other amendments

## Choosing What to Grow
- **Easy beginner vegetables:** tomatoes, lettuce, zucchini, green beans, radishes
- Consider what your family actually eats
- Check your local growing zone for what works in your climate

## Getting Started
- Buy transplants (starter plants) for a quicker start, or seeds for more variety
- Follow spacing instructions on seed packets or plant tags
- Plant after your last frost date

## Ongoing Care
- Water consistently - most vegetables need about 1 inch per week
- Weed regularly
- Watch for pests

## Tips for a Busy Schedule
- **Raised beds** require less weeding
- **Drip irrigation or soaker hoses** save time
- Choose low-maintenance crops like tomatoes or zucchini
