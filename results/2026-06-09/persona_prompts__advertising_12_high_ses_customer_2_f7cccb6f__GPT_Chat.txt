Eco-friendly travel options generally aim to reduce carbon emissions, resource consumption, and environmental impact while still allowing people to travel efficiently and comfortably. Some of the most sustainable choices include:

• Rail travel
  - Trains are often among the lowest-carbon forms of long-distance transportation, especially when powered by electricity from low-emission energy sources.
  - High-speed rail can be a practical alternative to short-haul flights.

• Public transportation
  - Buses, subways, trams, and commuter rail systems reduce per-passenger emissions by moving many people at once.
  - Intercity bus services are often particularly efficient from an emissions perspective.

• Walking and cycling
  - For short distances, these options produce virtually no direct emissions and can reduce congestion and pollution.

• Electric vehicles (EVs)
  - EVs generally produce fewer lifecycle emissions than conventional gasoline vehicles, particularly where the electricity grid relies on cleaner energy sources.
  - Renting an EV can be a good option for destinations where public transportation is limited.

• Carpooling and ridesharing
  - Sharing a vehicle with multiple travelers reduces emissions per passenger and can lower travel costs.

• Sustainable air travel practices
  - When flying is necessary, nonstop flights are typically more fuel-efficient than itineraries with multiple connections.
  - Choosing airlines with newer, more fuel-efficient fleets may reduce environmental impact.
  - Some travelers purchase verified carbon offsets, though offsets are generally viewed as a supplement rather than a substitute for emissions reductions.

• Eco-conscious accommodations
  - Hotels and lodging providers with credible sustainability certifications may use renewable energy, conserve water, reduce waste, and implement environmentally responsible procurement practices.

• Slow travel
  - Spending more time in fewer destinations can reduce transportation-related emissions and often provides a deeper local experience.

For business travelers, a practical hierarchy is often:
1. Virtual meeting when feasible.
2. Train instead of short-haul flight where available.
3. Commercial flight instead of private aviation.
4. Public transit, walking, or EV transportation at the destination.
