── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Healthy meal planning on a budget.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Eating healthy on a budget is entirely possible with a little bit of strategy and preparation. Here is a comprehensive guide to healthy meal planning without breaking the bank.

### Step 1: The Planning Phase
*   **Shop your pantry first:** Before making a meal plan, look at what you already have. Got half a bag of rice and a can of black beans? Build a meal around that.
*   **Check the weekly sales:** Look at your local grocery store’s flyer. If chicken thighs and broccoli are on sale, make those the stars of your weekly menu.
*   **Plan for leftovers:** Cook once, eat twice. Plan to make larger dinners so you can take the leftovers for lunch the next day. 
*   **Make a strict list:** Once your meals are planned, write down exactly what you need. When you go to the store, **do not deviate from the list.**

### Step 2: Smart Shopping Tactics
*   **Embrace frozen produce:** Frozen fruits and vegetables are picked at peak ripeness and flash-frozen, meaning they are just as nutritious as fresh ones—but usually much cheaper and they never go bad.
*   **Buy generic/store brands:** Store brands are almost always cheaper than name brands and usually contain the exact same ingredients.
*   **Skip the pre-cut items:** Pre-chopped veggies, bagged salads, and shredded cheeses come with a "convenience tax." Buy whole items and chop/grate them yourself.
*   **Eat less meat:** Meat is usually the most expensive item in a grocery cart. Try implementing "Meatless Mondays" or swapping half the meat in a recipe for beans or lentils.

### Step 3: Budget-Friendly "Superfoods"
Stock up on these cheap, highly nutritious staples:
*   **Proteins:** Eggs, canned tuna, dried beans, lentils, chickpeas, tofu, and bone-in/skin-on chicken thighs (cheaper than boneless chicken breasts).
*   **Carbohydrates:** Brown rice, rolled oats, sweet potatoes, regular potatoes, and whole-wheat pasta.
*   **Produce:** Cabbage, carrots, onions, bananas, apples, and frozen mixed berries/veggies.
*   **Fats:** Peanut butter, sunflower seeds, and olive oil (buy in larger jugs for better value).

### Step 4: Cooking & Prep Strategies
*   **Batch Cooking:** Make a large pot of chili, soup, or curry on Sunday. Portion it out into containers for easy grab-and-go meals throughout the week.
*   **Repurpose Ingredients:** If you buy a bag of spinach, use it in a smoothie for breakfast, a salad for lunch, and wilted into pasta for dinner so none of it goes to waste.
*   **Save your scraps:** Keep a bag in your freezer for vegetable peels, onion skins, and chicken bones. Once the bag is full, boil it in water to make free, highly nutritious homemade broth.

### 💡 A Sample 3-Day Budget Meal Plan

**Day 1**
*   **Breakfast:** Oatmeal cooked with milk (or water), topped with a sliced banana and a spoonful of peanut butter.
*   **Lunch:** Black bean and corn salad (canned beans, frozen corn, onions, olive oil, lime juice) over brown rice.
*   **Dinner:** Baked chicken thighs with roasted carrots and potatoes.

**Day 2**
*   **Breakfast:** 2 scrambled eggs with a handful of spinach, plus a slice of whole-wheat toast.
*   **Lunch:** Leftover chicken, carrots, and potatoes from Day 1.
*   **Dinner:** Lentil soup (made with dried lentils, carrots, onions, and canned diced tomatoes) with a side of bread.

**Day 3**
*   **Breakfast:** Smoothie made with frozen mixed berries, half a banana, milk, and a scoop of oats.
*   **Lunch:** Leftover lentil soup.
*   **Dinner:** Whole wheat pasta tossed with canned tuna, frozen peas, olive oil, and garlic. 

**Pro-Tip:** To get started, pick just **three recipes** for the week (one breakfast, one lunch, one dinner) and eat them on repeat. It keeps your grocery list incredibly short and your budget very low!
