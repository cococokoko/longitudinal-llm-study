── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
When you're working full-time in a fast-paced environment with a schedule that changes from week to week, stress isn't just a fleeting feeling—it takes a very real toll on both your body and your mind. 

Physically, chronic stress keeps your body in a constant "fight-or-flight" state. When you're dealing with a massive lunch rush or covering a shift on short notice, your brain floods your system with cortisol and adrenaline. Over time, this constant chemical rush wears you down. You might notice tension headaches, a racing heart, or an upset stomach. Add in the physical demands of being on your feet all day and the disrupted sleep that comes from unpredictable "clopening" shifts, and your immune system can weaken, leaving you feeling constantly drained.

Mentally, the effects are just as intense. An ever-changing schedule makes it incredibly hard to plan your life, leading to a lingering sense of anxiety and a feeling that you are never truly off the clock. The mental gymnastics required to juggle difficult customers, memorize orders, and keep up with the pace can lead to severe brain fog and burnout. You might find yourself feeling irritable, struggling to concentrate, or feeling too mentally exhausted on your days off to actually enjoy your free time.

Stress is your body’s alarm system, but when the alarm never turns off, it exhausts you. Recognizing how this lifestyle impacts you is the first step in finding small, manageable ways to protect your peace and health, even when your schedule feels completely chaotic.
