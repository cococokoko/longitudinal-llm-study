# Tip: Create Structured but Brief Regular Check-ins

**One-on-one meetings** with each team member (even 20-30 minutes biweekly) can be remarkably effective because they:

- Surface problems **before they escalate**
- Give trainees/staff dedicated time to raise concerns without competing for your attention
- Let you track progress on individual projects more accurately
- Build trust and psychological safety

## Practical suggestion:
Use a simple shared document where both you and the team member add agenda items beforehand. This keeps meetings focused and ensures neither side wastes the other's time.

---

**Why this matters specifically in a physician-scientist context:**

Your team likely includes people at very different career stages (students, postdocs, research coordinators, clinical staff) with **very different needs and pressures**. A structured check-in helps you avoid the common trap of only engaging with whoever is loudest or whose deadline is most immediate, while quieter or more independent members drift.
