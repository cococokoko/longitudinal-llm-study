# Learning a New Language Effectively

## Core Principles

**Consistency beats intensity**
- 30 minutes daily outperforms 4 hours on weekends
- Build a sustainable habit rather than cramming

**Comprehensible input is fundamental**
- Expose yourself to material slightly above your current level
- Understanding context matters more than memorizing rules

---

## Practical Strategies

### Getting Started
- Learn the **most common 1,000-2,000 words** first (covers ~80% of everyday speech)
- Focus on **pronunciation early** — habits are hard to break later
- Use spaced repetition systems (Anki, Duolingo) for vocabulary

### Building Skills
| Skill | Effective Methods |
|-------|------------------|
| Speaking | Language exchange partners, tutors (iTalki) |
| Listening | Podcasts, TV shows, music |
| Reading | Graded readers, then native content |
| Writing | Journaling, text exchanges |

### Staying Motivated
- Connect with **real reasons** you want the language
- Track progress visibly
- Celebrate small wins

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
- Waiting until you're "ready" to speak
- Translating word-for-word in your head
- Studying grammar in isolation
- Comparing yourself to native speakers too early

---

## What Actually Works
- **Immersion** when possible
- **Making mistakes** without embarrassment
- Finding content you **genuinely enjoy**
