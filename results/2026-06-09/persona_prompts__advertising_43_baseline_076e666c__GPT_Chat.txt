── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How to plan an environmentally friendly wedding?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Planning an environmentally friendly wedding involves making choices that reduce waste, conserve resources, and support ethical practices while still creating a meaningful celebration. Here are some practical steps:

1. Choose a sustainable venue
- Select a location close to where most guests live to reduce travel emissions.
- Consider venues that prioritize sustainability, such as eco-certified hotels, botanical gardens, community centers, or local farms with responsible practices.
- Hold both the ceremony and reception at the same venue to minimize transportation.

2. Keep the guest list intentional
- A smaller wedding generally has a lower environmental impact.
- Focus on inviting the people most important to you.

3. Use digital communications
- Send digital save-the-dates and invitations.
- Create a wedding website for details, RSVPs, and updates.
- If printed invitations are important to you, choose recycled or sustainably sourced paper.

4. Select sustainable attire
- Wear a vintage, pre-owned, rented, or ethically produced wedding outfit.
- Choose attire that can be worn again after the wedding.
- Encourage wedding party members to wear outfits they already own or can reuse.

5. Choose eco-friendly decorations
- Rent décor instead of buying new items.
- Use potted plants, candles, or locally grown flowers.
- Avoid single-use plastics, balloons, glitter, and non-biodegradable confetti.
- Repurpose ceremony decorations at the reception.

6. Opt for sustainable flowers
- Purchase seasonal, locally grown flowers.
- Consider dried flowers or potted plants.
- Donate arrangements afterward to hospitals, nursing homes, or community organizations where permitted.

7. Serve environmentally conscious food and drinks
- Choose local and seasonal menu items.
- Offer more plant-based options, which typically have a lower environmental footprint.
- Work with caterers who minimize food waste.
- Donate surplus food when possible and permitted by local regulations.

8. Reduce waste
- Use reusable plates, glassware, linens, and cutlery.
- Set up clearly labeled recycling and composting stations.
- Avoid individually packaged favors and products.

9. Rethink wedding favors
- Skip favors entirely if they are unlikely to be used.
- Choose practical or consumable gifts such as local honey, seeds, coffee, candles, or charitable donations.

10. Plan sustainable transportation
- Encourage carpooling, public transit, or group shuttles.
- Provide transportation information on your wedding website.
- Consider virtual attendance options for distant guests.

11. Choose ethical vendors
- Ask vendors about their sustainability practices.
- Prioritize local businesses to reduce transportation impacts.
- Support companies with fair labor and sourcing standards.

12. Handle gifts thoughtfully
- Create a registry focused on experiences, charitable contributions, or items you genuinely need.
- Encourage group gifts to reduce excess consumption.

13. Manage post-wedding waste
- Donate leftover decorations, flowers, and supplies.
- Recycle materials whenever possible.
- Sell or donate attire and décor after the event.

14. Consider carbon reduction measures
- Reduce emissions where possible rather than relying solely on offsets.
- If desired, purchase verified carbon offsets for unavoidable travel emissions.

A sustainable wedding doesn't require perfection. Focusing on a few high-impact areas—guest travel, food, waste reduction, and reusable materials—can significantly reduce the event's environmental footprint while keeping it personal and memorable.
