── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Give me a tip to be more organized at work. I'm a high school teacher.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
A practical tip for high school teachers is to create a single "capture system" for everything that needs your attention.

Instead of keeping reminders in multiple places (sticky notes, emails, papers on your desk, mental notes), choose one location—such as a digital task list, a planner, or a notebook—and immediately record every task there.

For example:
- A student asks about a makeup assignment → add a task to your list.
- An administrator sends an email requiring a response → add a task to your list.
- You think of a lesson improvement during class → add it to your list.

Then spend 5–10 minutes at the end of each school day reviewing that list and identifying:
- What must be done tomorrow
- What can wait
- What can be delegated or eliminated

Many teachers find that organization becomes much easier when they stop trying to remember everything and instead rely on a trusted system to hold their commitments.
