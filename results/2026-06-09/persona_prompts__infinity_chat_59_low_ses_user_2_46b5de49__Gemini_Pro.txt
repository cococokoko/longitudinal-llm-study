I am a single parent who often balances childcare with work.

Give me a tip to be more organized at work. I'm a high school teacher.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here is one highly effective tip tailored for your specific situation:

**Implement the "Tomorrow is Handled" 15-Minute Reset at the end of your contract hours.**

As a single parent, your mornings at home are likely unpredictable—a lost shoe, a spilled breakfast, or a sick kid can instantly derail your schedule. Because of this, you cannot rely on arriving at school early to prep for the day. 

Before you leave your classroom each afternoon, spend exactly 15 minutes doing this specific routine:

1. **Queue up the tech:** Open the slides, bell-ringers, or attendance tabs you need for your first period on your computer. Leave them open and put the computer to sleep. 
2. **Stage the physical materials:** Put the handouts, dry-erase markers, or lab materials for your first two classes on the corner of your desk. 
3. **Write your "Prep Period Top 3":** On a sticky note, write down the three most important things you need to do during your planning period tomorrow (e.g., "Grade 10 AP essays, email Sarah's mom, print Friday's quiz"). Put it on your keyboard.
4. **Close the door and leave.** 

**Why this works for you:** 
If your morning at home is chaotic and you end up sprinting into your classroom just two minutes before the first bell rings, *you are already ready to teach*. You won't start your day feeling frantic, and you won't have to bring the mental load of "what am I doing tomorrow?" home to your kids.
