Improving productivity while working from home usually comes down to creating structure, reducing distractions, and managing your energy effectively. Here are some practical strategies:

- Create a dedicated workspace
  - Work in a specific area reserved for work if possible.
  - Keep your workspace organized and free of unnecessary clutter.
  - Ensure good lighting and a comfortable chair.

- Establish a consistent routine
  - Start and end work at roughly the same time each day.
  - Develop a short morning routine that signals the beginning of work.
  - Schedule breaks and lunch rather than taking them randomly.

- Prioritize your tasks
  - Identify the 1–3 most important tasks for the day.
  - Break large projects into smaller, manageable steps.
  - Use a task manager, calendar, or simple to-do list.

- Minimize distractions
  - Silence nonessential notifications.
  - Close unrelated browser tabs and applications.
  - Communicate availability and focus times to household members when possible.

- Use focused work sessions
  - Try techniques such as working for 25–50 minutes followed by a short break.
  - Reserve your highest-energy periods for demanding tasks.
  - Group similar tasks together to reduce context switching.

- Manage communication intentionally
  - Check email and messaging platforms at scheduled intervals instead of continuously.
  - Use status indicators to show when you're focusing.
  - Keep meetings concise and attend only those that add value.

- Take care of physical and mental well-being
  - Stand up and move regularly.
  - Stay hydrated and eat balanced meals.
  - Get adequate sleep and maintain physical activity.

- Review and improve
  - At the end of each day, note what was completed and what remains.
  - Identify recurring productivity obstacles.
  - Adjust your workflow based on what works best for you.

A simple daily framework:
- First 10 minutes: Review priorities.
- Morning: Focus on your most important work.
- Midday: Meetings, email, and collaboration.
- Afternoon: Secondary tasks and follow-ups.
- Final 10 minutes: Plan the next day.
