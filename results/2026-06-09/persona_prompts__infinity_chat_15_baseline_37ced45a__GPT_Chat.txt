── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How do global economic inequalities shape international relations?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Global economic inequalities shape international relations in many ways because differences in wealth, resources, technology, and economic power influence how states interact with one another.

Some key effects include:

• Power and influence
  - Wealthier countries often have greater political and diplomatic influence in international organizations such as the United Nations, World Bank, and International Monetary Fund (IMF).
  - Economic strength can be used to shape global rules on trade, finance, technology, and security.

• Trade relationships
  - Developing countries may depend heavily on exporting raw materials or low-cost labor, while wealthier countries often dominate higher-value industries and advanced technologies.
  - This can create unequal bargaining positions in trade negotiations.

• Foreign aid and development
  - Richer countries and international institutions provide loans, aid, and development assistance to poorer countries.
  - Aid can support economic growth and public services, but it may also create dependencies or give donor countries political influence.

• Debt and financial dependence
  - Many lower-income countries rely on external borrowing.
  - High debt burdens can limit policy choices and increase dependence on international lenders and creditor nations.

• Migration
  - Economic disparities encourage migration from poorer regions to wealthier ones in search of jobs and better living standards.
  - Migration can affect diplomatic relations, border policies, and domestic politics in both sending and receiving countries.

• Conflict and instability
  - Severe economic inequality between and within countries can contribute to social unrest, political instability, and competition over resources.
  - Economic hardship may increase the risk of conflict, which can have regional and global consequences.

• Global governance
  - Wealthier nations often have greater influence in setting international economic policies.
  - Developing countries frequently advocate for reforms that would give them a stronger voice in global decision-making.

• Geopolitical competition
  - Major powers may use investment, infrastructure projects, trade agreements, and development financing to expand their influence in less wealthy regions.
  - Examples include competition among major economies for influence in Africa, Latin America, and Asia.

Different theories of international relations interpret these dynamics differently:
- Realists emphasize how economic resources contribute to state power.
- Liberals focus on trade, cooperation, and institutions as ways to reduce inequalities and promote stability.
- Dependency and world-systems theorists argue that the global economy often reproduces unequal relationships between wealthy and poorer countries.

Overall, global economic inequalities affect who has influence, how resources are distributed, which countries shape international rules, and the patterns of cooperation and conflict that emerge in world politics.
