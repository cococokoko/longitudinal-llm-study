# CUBICLE OF THE DAMNED
### A Play in the Style of Scott Adams, Set During the Apocalypse

---

**CHARACTERS:**

**DILBERT** — An engineer. Still wearing his tie. The tie still curls upward at the end. This is somehow the least strange thing happening.

**THE POINTY-HAIRED BOSS (PHB)** — Survived the apocalypse through sheer managerial incompetence. The apocalypse found him too confusing to kill.

**WALLY** — Has not noticed the apocalypse. Has been working on his coffee strategy since before civilization fell.

**ALICE** — Her fist of death has become significantly more relevant.

**DOGBERT** — Consulting the survivors. Charging double.

**RATBERT** — Enthusiastically eating the rubble.

---

**SETTING:** *A partially collapsed open-plan office. The ceiling is gone. A mushroom cloud is visible in the background. Several motivational posters hang crookedly on the remaining walls. One reads: "HANG IN THERE." A skeleton in business casual sits at a nearby desk. Its monitor still displays a screensaver.*

---

## ACT ONE: THE MORNING STANDUP

---

*Lights rise on DILBERT sitting at his desk, which is surrounded by smoldering wreckage. He types on his laptop. The laptop runs on a car battery connected by jumper cables. He stares at the screen with the hollow expression of a man who has accepted everything.*

**DILBERT:**
*(to audience)*
Day four hundred and twelve of the apocalypse. Management has scheduled a mandatory all-hands meeting to discuss our response strategy. The meeting was originally scheduled for day two. It was pushed back because the conference room was on fire.

*(beat)*

It is still on fire.

---

*PHB enters, stepping over a collapsed wall. He is wearing a hard hat over his usual hair. The hard hat has a small corporate logo on it. He carries a clipboard.*

**PHB:**
Good morning, Dilbert! Big day. Big day.

**DILBERT:**
The eastern seaboard is gone.

**PHB:**
Right, right. Which is actually an *opportunity* if you think about it.

**DILBERT:**
An opportunity.

**PHB:**
Real estate prices out there were *insane.*

*(He makes a chef's kiss gesture. A distant explosion rumbles.)*

**PHB:**
Now. I need you to put together a forty-slide deck on our post-apocalyptic digital transformation roadmap by end of day.

**DILBERT:**
End of *which* day? Days are now approximately nineteen hours because the Earth's rotation was affected by—

**PHB:**
Let's say five o'clock.

**DILBERT:**
Five o'clock *your* time or—

**PHB:**
Dilbert, I don't have time for technical details. That's why I have you.

*(PHB wanders off, stepping on a human skull without noticing.)*

**DILBERT:**
*(to audience)*
He has never once had time for technical details. In this way, the apocalypse has changed nothing.

---

## ACT TWO: THE COFFEE SITUATION

---

*WALLY sits at his desk. His desk is somehow completely intact. He holds a coffee mug. The mug reads: "WORLD'S OKAYEST SURVIVOR." He stares contentedly at nothing.*

**DILBERT:**
Wally, how are you still alive?

**WALLY:**
I've been asking myself that question since 1987.

**DILBERT:**
I mean specifically. The building is structurally compromised. There are armed factions fighting over the parking garage. HR sent an email saying benefits are suspended "pending review of the extinction event."

**WALLY:**
I got that email. I flagged it as low priority.

**DILBERT:**
How do you have coffee?

**WALLY:**
*(proudly)*
I have been hoarding coffee since the first signs of societal collapse.

**DILBERT:**
When was that?

**WALLY:**
The day they switched to the new expense report system. I knew then that civilization was unsustainable.

*(He sips his coffee with deep satisfaction.)*

**WALLY:**
I have enough coffee to last eleven years. I have enough food for four days. I have made my priorities clear.
