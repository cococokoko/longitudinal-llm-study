# Improving Your Credit Score as a Busy Single Parent

Here are practical steps organized by impact and effort:

## Highest Impact Actions

**Pay bills on time** (35% of your score)
- Set up autopay for minimum payments
- Use calendar reminders for due dates
- Even one missed payment hurts significantly

**Reduce credit card balances** (30% of your score)
- Try to keep balances below 30% of your credit limit
- Pay down highest utilization cards first

## Relatively Quick Wins

- **Check your credit report for errors** at AnnualCreditReport.com - dispute any mistakes
- **Ask for a credit limit increase** on existing cards (without spending more)
- **Become an authorized user** on a trusted person's account with good history

## Things to Avoid

- Don't close old accounts
- Avoid applying for multiple new credit cards at once
- Don't miss payments even during tight months

## Realistic Timeline

| Action | Potential Timeframe |
|--------|-------------------|
| Dispute errors | 30-45 days |
| Lower utilization | 1-2 billing cycles |
| Consistent payments | 3-6 months |

## Free Resources

- **Credit Karma** or **Credit Sesame** for free monitoring
- Many banks offer free credit score tracking
