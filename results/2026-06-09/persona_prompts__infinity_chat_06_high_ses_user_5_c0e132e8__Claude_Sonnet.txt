── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I am an executive at a large technology company.

How do I understand what I want?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This is a genuinely important question, and the fact that you're asking it suggests some self-awareness worth building on.

## Why This Is Hard for Executives Specifically

- You've spent years optimizing for **what others need from you**
- Success can mean your identity gets **merged with your role**
- You're surrounded by people who tell you what you want to hear
- Busyness becomes a way to **avoid the question entirely**

## Some Honest Approaches

**Create separation from your role**
- Ask yourself what you'd want if you couldn't tell anyone your title
- Notice what you think about when nothing is demanding your attention

**Distinguish between types of wanting**
- What you want *to have* vs. what you want *to do* vs. what you want *to be*
- What you want vs. what you think you *should* want
- Short-term relief vs. longer-term satisfaction

**Pay attention to actual experience**
- What moments recently felt genuinely meaningful vs. performed?
- What do you consistently avoid, and why?
- Where does your energy come from vs. drain?

**Test, don't just reflect**
- Reflection has limits - small experiments reveal more than thinking does

## A Direct Challenge

Sometimes people in powerful positions discover they've been **executing someone else's vision of success** for a long time.

That's worth sitting with honestly.
