Healthy meal planning on a budget is often easier when you optimize for a few principles: buy versatile staples, plan around sales, reduce food waste, and prepare meals that can be repurposed throughout the week.

Key budget-friendly foods:
- Proteins:
  - Eggs
  - Canned tuna or salmon

  - Chicken thighs
  - Tofu
  - Greek yogurt
- Carbohydrates:
  - Oats
  - Brown rice
  - Potatoes
  - Whole-grain pasta
  - Whole-wheat bread
- Vegetables:
  - Frozen vegetables (often cheaper and equally nutritious)
  - Carrots
  - Cabbage
  - Onions
  - Seasonal produce
- Fruits:
  - Bananas
  - Apples
  - Oranges
  - Frozen berries
- Healthy fats:
  - Peanut butter
  - Nuts purchased in bulk
  - Olive oil

A simple weekly planning framework:
1. Choose 2 proteins.
   - Example: chicken thighs and lentils
2. Choose 2–3 vegetables.
   - Example: broccoli, carrots, and spinach
3. Choose 1–2 carbohydrates.
   - Example: brown rice and potatoes
4. Build multiple meals from the same ingredients.

Example 3-day meal rotation:
- Breakfast:
  - Oatmeal with banana and peanut butter
  - Greek yogurt with fruit
  - Vegetable omelet
- Lunch:
  - Chicken, rice, and vegetables
  - Lentil soup with whole-grain bread
  - Tuna salad wrap
- Dinner:
  - Stir-fried vegetables with tofu and rice
  - Baked chicken with potatoes and carrots
  - Bean chili
- Snacks:
  - Fruit
  - Yogurt
  - Hard-boiled eggs
  - Nuts

Cost-saving tactics:
- Shop with a list and avoid impulse purchases.
- Buy store brands when possible.
- Use frozen fruits and vegetables to reduce waste.
- Cook larger batches and freeze portions.
- Compare unit prices rather than package prices.
- Plan meals around weekly promotions.
- Use leftovers intentionally—for example, roast chicken can become salads, wraps, soups, or grain bowls.
