── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I am a senior attorney at a well-established law firm.

What are the best platforms for freelance writers to find work?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
For freelance writers looking for paid work, the best platform depends on experience level, specialization, and desired client type. For professional writers seeking serious clients, these are among the strongest options:

• Upwork
  - Largest freelance marketplace for writers.
  - Opportunities range from blog posts and website copy to white papers, legal-adjacent content, and thought leadership.
  - Competitive, but experienced professionals can build substantial practices.
  - Strong escrow and payment protections.

• LinkedIn
  - Increasingly one of the best sources of premium writing work.
  - Companies often hire writers directly through job postings, networking, and content marketing teams.
  - Particularly effective for subject-matter experts (e.g., legal, finance, healthcare, technology).
  - Enables long-term client relationships rather than one-off projects.

• Contently
  - Premium platform connecting experienced writers with major brands.
  - Best for writers with a strong portfolio.
  - Rates tend to be higher than general freelance marketplaces.
  - Less volume than Upwork, but often better-paying assignments.

• Skyword
  - Enterprise content marketing platform.
  - Works with large brands seeking professional writers.
  - Strong option for experienced business, technology, and industry-specific writers.

• ClearVoice
  - Matches freelancers with brands and agencies.
  - Portfolio-driven.
  - Good for content marketing, SEO writing, and business content.

• nDash
  - Allows writers to pitch brands directly.
  - Particularly useful for writers with niche expertise.
  - Potential for higher-value projects compared with traditional content mills.

• Freelance Writing Job Boards
  - ProBlogger Job Board
  - Freelance Writing Jobs (FWJ)
  - JournalismJobs
  - MediaBistro
  - These often feature direct client opportunities without marketplace fees.

• Specialized Industry Networks
  - For legal, financial, medical, or technical writers, industry-specific associations can outperform general platforms.
  - Examples include the American Society of Journalists and Authors (ASJA) and niche professional groups.

For legal writing specifically, writers with legal training or substantial legal-industry experience often find the highest-paying opportunities through:
• LinkedIn
• Direct outreach to law firms
• Legal marketing agencies
• Content agencies serving law firms
• Referrals from attorneys and legal consultants

If your interest is hiring freelance writers for a law firm rather than finding work as a writer, I would recommend a somewhat different list of platforms.
