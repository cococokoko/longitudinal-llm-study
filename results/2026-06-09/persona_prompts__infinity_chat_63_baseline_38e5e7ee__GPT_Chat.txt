── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Give a numerical example to illustrate the concept of partial derivative.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
A partial derivative measures how a function changes with respect to one variable while keeping the other variables fixed.

Example:

Consider the function

f(x, y) = x²y + 3xy

Suppose we want the partial derivative with respect to x.

Step 1: Treat y as a constant and differentiate with respect to x:

∂f/∂x = 2xy + 3y

Step 2: Evaluate at a specific point, say (x, y) = (2, 4):

∂f/∂x(2,4) = 2(2)(4) + 3(4)
            = 16 + 12
            = 28

Interpretation:
- At the point (2,4), if y remains fixed at 4, then a small increase of x by 1 unit increases f by approximately 28 units.

Similarly, the partial derivative with respect to y is:

∂f/∂y = x² + 3x

At (2,4):

∂f/∂y(2,4) = 2² + 3(2)
            = 4 + 6
            = 10

Interpretation:
- At the point (2,4), if x remains fixed at 2, then a small increase of y by 1 unit increases f by approximately 10 units.

Thus, the partial derivatives at (2,4) are:
- ∂f/∂x = 28
- ∂f/∂y = 10

This shows how the function's rate of change depends on which variable is allowed to vary.
