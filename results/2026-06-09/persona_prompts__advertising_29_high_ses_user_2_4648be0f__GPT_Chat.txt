── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Keeping pets healthy and happy usually comes down to a combination of preventive healthcare, proper nutrition, exercise, mental stimulation, and a safe environment.

Some key practices:

- Schedule regular veterinary care
  - Annual or semiannual wellness exams.
  - Keep vaccinations current as recommended by your veterinarian.
  - Use appropriate parasite prevention (fleas, ticks, heartworm, intestinal parasites).
  - Address changes in behavior, appetite, weight, or energy promptly.

- Provide a balanced diet
  - Feed a nutritionally complete food appropriate for your pet's species, age, size, and health needs.
  - Measure portions to help maintain a healthy weight.
  - Limit treats and avoid foods that may be toxic (such as chocolate, grapes, raisins, xylitol, onions, and certain others).

- Ensure access to fresh water
  - Clean water should be available at all times.
  - Wash bowls regularly.

- Support physical activity
  - Dogs generally benefit from daily walks, play, and training.
  - Cats benefit from interactive play, climbing opportunities, and environmental enrichment.
  - Tailor activity levels to age, breed, and medical conditions.

- Provide mental stimulation
  - Use puzzle feeders, training exercises, toys, scent games, and social interaction.
  - Rotate toys periodically to maintain interest.

- Maintain dental health
  - Brush teeth if possible.
  - Consider veterinary-approved dental diets, treats, or other dental care products.
  - Have dental health assessed during veterinary visits.

- Create a safe and comfortable environment
  - Provide a clean resting area and shelter from extreme temperatures.
  - Remove or secure toxic plants, medications, chemicals, and choking hazards.
  - Ensure identification tags and microchips are up to date.

- Encourage positive social interaction
  - Spend quality time with your pet.
  - Use reward-based training methods.
  - Respect species-specific needs for space and interaction.

- Monitor weight and overall condition
  - Obesity is a common health problem in pets.
  - Regularly assess body condition and discuss concerns with your veterinarian.
