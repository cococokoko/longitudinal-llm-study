── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I am a neurosurgeon at a major teaching hospital.

What are the best tools for remote team collaboration?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The best remote collaboration tools depend on whether your priority is communication, project coordination, document collaboration, or clinical/security requirements. For a large professional team, a combination of tools usually works best.

Communication and Meetings
- Microsoft Teams
  - Strong integration with Microsoft 365
  - Chat, video meetings, file sharing, channels
  - Widely adopted in healthcare and academic institutions
- Slack
  - Excellent for fast communication and cross-functional collaboration
  - Large ecosystem of integrations
  - Often preferred by technology and research teams
- Zoom
  - Reliable video conferencing
  - Webinar and large-meeting capabilities
  - Often used alongside Teams or Slack

Project and Task Management
- Asana
  - User-friendly project tracking
  - Good for coordinating complex initiatives
- Monday.com
  - Flexible workflows and dashboards
  - Strong visibility across teams
- Jira
  - Best for technical, software, and process-improvement projects
- Trello
  - Simple Kanban-style task management
  - Easy to adopt for smaller teams

Document Collaboration
- Microsoft 365 (Word, Excel, PowerPoint, SharePoint, OneDrive)
  - Real-time co-authoring
  - Enterprise security and compliance features
- Google Workspace
  - Excellent collaborative editing
  - Simplicity and ease of use

Knowledge Management
- Confluence
  - Structured team documentation and project knowledge
- Notion
  - Combines notes, documentation, databases, and lightweight project management
- SharePoint
  - Strong enterprise document management and governance

Whiteboarding and Brainstorming
- Miro
  - Industry-leading virtual whiteboard
  - Excellent for workshops and planning
- Microsoft Whiteboard
  - Useful for Teams-centric organizations

For Healthcare and Academic Medical Centers
Key considerations often matter more than features:
- HIPAA and regulatory compliance requirements
- Institutional security policies
- Integration with Microsoft 365 and identity management
- Audit trails and data governance
- Ability to support multidisciplinary teams across departments

For a major teaching hospital, a common and effective stack is:
- Microsoft Teams for communication
- Microsoft 365/SharePoint for documents
- Asana or Monday.com for project management
- Miro for collaborative planning and education
