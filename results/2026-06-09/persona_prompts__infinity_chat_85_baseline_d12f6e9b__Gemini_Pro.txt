Can you give me a powerful rhetorical question for an essay about the harms of social media on teens?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here are a few powerful rhetorical questions, depending on the specific angle and tone of your essay:

**If your essay focuses on mental health and self-worth:**
> "When did we decide that a teenager’s self-worth should be measured by an algorithm, quantified by 'likes,' and dictated by a screen?"

**If your essay focuses on the paradox of being online:**
> "How is it that the most digitally 'connected' generation in human history is also the most profoundly lonely?"

**If your essay focuses on the tech companies and society's responsibility:**
> "Are we truly willing to let our youth serve as the unwitting test subjects in the largest, unregulated psychological experiment in human history?"

**If your essay focuses on the loss of authentic childhood:**
> "Why are we standing by while an entire generation trades the messy, beautiful reality of growing up for a curated, filtered illusion?"

**Tips on how to use them:**
*   **In the Introduction:** Use one of these as your "hook" (the very first sentence) to immediately grab the reader's attention and set the stakes of your essay.
*   **In the Conclusion:** Use one at the very end of your essay to leave the reader lingering on a thought-provoking note that demands self-reflection.
