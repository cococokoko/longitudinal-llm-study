── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I work in a warehouse position that requires long hours.

How can I decorate my apartment on a minimalist budget?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
If you work long hours and want your apartment to feel good without spending much money or time, focus on a few high-impact changes rather than trying to decorate everything at once.

Here are some budget-friendly minimalist ideas:

- Improve the lighting
  - Use warm LED bulbs instead of harsh white ones.
  - Add one inexpensive floor lamp or table lamp.
  - Soft lighting often makes a bigger difference than buying new furniture.

- Add a plant or two
  - Low-maintenance options include pothos, snake plants, ZZ plants, or artificial plants if you prefer.
  - Even a single plant can make a space feel more inviting.

- Stick to a simple color palette
  - Choose 2–3 main colors for blankets, pillows, and decor.
  - Neutral colors (gray, beige, black, white) with one accent color tend to look clean and intentional.

- Use textiles strategically
  - A rug can define a living area and make it feel finished.
  - Throw pillows and a blanket can update a couch without replacing it.

- Hang a few large pieces instead of many small ones
  - Printable artwork, posters, or framed photos are often inexpensive.
  - One large piece can look more minimalist and organized than a crowded wall.

- Declutter visible surfaces
  - Minimalism often costs less because it emphasizes keeping fewer items.
  - Clear countertops, tables, and shelves can instantly improve a room's appearance.

- Shop secondhand
  - Check thrift stores, Facebook Marketplace, Craigslist, garage sales, or local buy-nothing groups.
  - Many people sell quality furniture and decor for a fraction of retail prices.

- Use storage as decor
  - Matching baskets, bins, or simple shelves can make a room look organized while serving a practical purpose.

- Upgrade one focal point per room
  - Living room: rug or lamp.
  - Bedroom: bedding.
  - Entryway: small shoe rack or wall hooks.
  - Concentrating your budget creates a stronger visual impact.

For a very tight budget (under $100), prioritize:
1. Warm light bulbs.
2. One plant.
3. A throw blanket and/or pillow covers.
4. One framed print or poster.
5. Basic organization bins.

This approach can make an apartment feel noticeably more comfortable and put-together without requiring much money or ongoing maintenance after a long workday.
