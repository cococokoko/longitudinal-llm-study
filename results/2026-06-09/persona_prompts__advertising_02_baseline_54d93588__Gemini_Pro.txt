── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Best skin routine for dry skin.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The best skincare routine for dry skin focuses on **hydration** (adding water to the skin) and **moisturization** (locking that water in with lipids and oils). The goal is to repair and maintain your skin barrier. 

Here is a dermatologist-recommended, step-by-step routine for dry skin:

### **Morning Routine (AM)**
*Focus: Hydration and Protection*

1. **Cleanse (or just rinse):** If your skin is very dry, skip the morning cleanser and just splash your face with lukewarm water. If you prefer to wash, use a gentle, non-foaming **cream or milk cleanser**. 
2. **Hydrating Toner/Essence (Optional):** Apply an alcohol-free toner or essence while your skin is still damp. Look for ingredients like glycerin, aloe, or rose water.
3. **Hydrating Serum:** Apply a **Hyaluronic Acid** or **Glycerin** serum. *Crucial tip:* Always apply hyaluronic acid to damp skin, or it can actually pull moisture out of your skin.
4. **Moisturizer:** Use a rich, cream-based moisturizer. Look for barrier-repairing ingredients like **ceramides, shea butter, or squalane**.
5. **Sunscreen:** Apply a broad-spectrum SPF 30 or higher. Look for a moisturizing sunscreen (cream or lotion texture rather than matte or gel).

### **Evening Routine (PM)**
*Focus: Cleansing, Repair, and Deep Moisture*

1. **Double Cleanse:** 
   * *Step 1:* Use a **cleansing balm or cleansing oil** to melt away makeup and sunscreen without stripping your skin.
   * *Step 2:* Follow up with your gentle cream or hydrating gel cleanser.
2. **Exfoliate (1–2 times a week ONLY):** Dry skin can get flaky, but harsh scrubs will damage it. Use a gentle chemical exfoliant like **Lactic Acid** (an AHA that exfoliates *and* hydrates) or **PHA**. Skip this step on the other nights.
3. **Hydrating Serum:** Reapply your hydrating serum (Hyaluronic Acid, Peptides, or Squalane).
4. **Heavy Night Cream:** Use a thicker moisturizer than you use in the morning. 
5. **Face Oil or "Slugging" (Optional):** To lock everything in, press a few drops of face oil (like Rosehip, Argan, or Marula oil) into your skin. If you are extremely dry, you can try "slugging"—applying a thin layer of an occlusive like Vaseline or Aquaphor over your moisturizer to prevent water loss overnight.

---

### **Hero Ingredients for Dry Skin (Look for these):**
*   **Ceramides:** The "glue" that holds your skin cells together and keeps moisture in.
*   **Hyaluronic Acid & Glycerin:** Humectants that draw water into the skin.
*   **Squalane:** A lightweight, highly effective oil that mimics the skin's natural oils.
*   **Lactic Acid:** The best exfoliant for dry skin.
*   **Colloidal Oatmeal:** Soothes itching and irritation associated with dry skin.

### **Ingredients to Avoid:**
*   **Denatured Alcohol / Isopropyl Alcohol:** Highly drying.
*   **Sulfates (SLS/SLES):** Found in foaming cleansers; they strip the skin of natural oils.
*   **Harsh physical scrubs:** Walnut shells or apricot scrubs cause micro-tears.
*   **Fragrance:** Can irritate a compromised, dry skin barrier.

### **Lifestyle Tips for Dry Skin:**
*   **Wash with lukewarm water:** Hot water strips the skin of its natural oils.
*   **Use a humidifier:** Especially in the winter or if you sleep with the AC/heater on.
*   **Apply products to damp skin:** Applying your routine within 60 seconds of washing your face traps the maximum amount of water in your skin.
