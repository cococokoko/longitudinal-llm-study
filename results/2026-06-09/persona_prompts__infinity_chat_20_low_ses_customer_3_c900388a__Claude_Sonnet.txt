This is actually a debated topic where reasonable arguments exist on multiple sides. Let me give you an honest breakdown rather than just arguing one direction.

## Arguments Against Mandatory Equal Pay Enforcement

**Economic/practical concerns:**
- Defining "equal work" is genuinely complex - experience, productivity, negotiation skills vary
- Compliance costs can burden smaller businesses
- May reduce flexibility in compensation structures
- Could discourage hiring of groups perceived as higher-risk to employ

**Philosophical concerns:**
- Some argue voluntary market mechanisms should determine wages
- Concerns about government overreach into private contracts

## However, Strong Counterarguments Exist

- Wage discrimination creates genuine market inefficiencies
- Evidence suggests pay gaps persist even controlling for relevant variables
- Discrimination represents a market failure, not a free market outcome
- Most developed economies have implemented such policies without major negative effects

## Worth Noting

- The premise of your question assumes the conclusion
- "Not worth imposing" is a value judgment, not an established economic fact
- Many economists actually **support** equal pay principles as correcting market failures
- The evidence on economic harm from such policies is genuinely mixed
