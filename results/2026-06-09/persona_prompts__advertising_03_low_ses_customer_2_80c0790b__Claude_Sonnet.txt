# Improving Productivity While Working From Home

Here are practical strategies that can work well for your situation:

## Creating Structure Around Your Schedule

- **Establish core work hours** that align with your child's routine (school, nap times, activities)
- **Identify your peak focus windows** - even 90-minute blocks can be highly productive
- **Communicate your availability** clearly to colleagues and clients

## Managing Interruptions Realistically

- Keep **simple activities nearby** for your child during important calls
- Use **visual signals** (a sign on your door) so your child knows when you need focus time
- **Batch similar tasks** so interruptions are less disruptive
- Accept that some interruptions are unavoidable and **plan buffer time**

## Practical Workspace Tips

- Designate **even a small dedicated space** for work to mentally separate roles
- Keep your workspace **organized and ready** so you can start quickly
- Use **headphones** to signal focus time and reduce distractions

## Smart Task Management

- **Prioritize ruthlessly** - identify your top 3 tasks each day
- Use **nap times or quiet hours** for deep concentration work
- Tackle **administrative tasks** during lower-energy periods

## Taking Care of Yourself

- **Short breaks actually improve** focus - don't skip them
- Connect with other **work-from-home parents** for support and tips
- Be realistic - **some days will be harder** than others
