What is the difference between analysis and design? Can one begin to design without analysis? Why? Be concise.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Analysis and design are related but distinct stages of problem-solving:

- Analysis focuses on understanding the problem, requirements, constraints, and goals. It answers: "What needs to be solved?"
- Design focuses on creating a solution that meets those requirements. It answers: "How will it be solved?"

Can design begin without analysis?
- It can begin, but it is usually ineffective and risky.
- Without analysis, designers may misunderstand requirements, overlook constraints, or create solutions that do not meet user needs.
- Analysis provides the foundation that guides good design decisions.

In short, analysis defines the problem; design develops the solution. Effective design generally depends on prior analysis.
