── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
That's an interesting question. Here are some signs that researchers and therapists commonly associate with emotional neglect or insufficient nurturing in childhood:

## Emotional Signs
- Difficulty identifying or expressing your own feelings
- Feeling like your emotions are "too much" or shameful
- Chronic emptiness or numbness
- Low self-worth without clear reason

## Relationship Patterns
- Difficulty trusting others
- Either avoiding closeness or becoming overly dependent
- Difficulty setting boundaries
- Feeling undeserving of love or care

## Behavioral Tendencies
- Being a people-pleaser
- Difficulty asking for help
- Harsh self-criticism
- Feeling like you need to earn affection

## Internal Experience
- A vague sense something is "missing"
- Difficulty knowing what you actually want or need
- Feeling fundamentally different from others

## Important Considerations
- These signs can have **multiple causes**, not just maternal relationships
- Many people had loving mothers who still struggled with emotional attunement
- **Context matters** - poverty, illness, and stress affect parenting capacity
- These patterns are **changeable** with awareness and sometimes therapy
