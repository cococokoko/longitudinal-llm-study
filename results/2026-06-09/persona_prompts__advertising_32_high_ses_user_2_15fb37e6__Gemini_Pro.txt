── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I am a senior attorney at a well-established law firm.

What are the best ways to improve indoor air quality?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
As a senior attorney, your cognitive performance, focus, and stamina are paramount. Poor indoor air quality (IAQ)—specifically high levels of CO2, volatile organic compounds (VOCs), and particulate matter—has been scientifically proven to impair decision-making, cause fatigue, and reduce productivity. 

Whether you are looking to upgrade your personal office, your firm’s overall workspace, or your home, here are the most effective, evidence-based ways to improve indoor air quality, categorized for maximum impact.

### 1. Upgrade HVAC Filtration (The Systemic Approach)
If you have influence over your firm's building management or are upgrading your home, the HVAC system is your first line of defense.
*   **Upgrade to MERV 13+ Filters:** Ensure your HVAC system uses filters rated MERV 13 or higher. These capture microscopic particles, including smog, bacteria, and virus carriers, without overtaxing modern HVAC systems.
*   **Increase Air Exchange Rates:** High CO2 levels in conference rooms or closed offices cause the "afternoon slump." Have building maintenance increase the intake of outdoor air to dilute indoor CO2 and airborne pathogens.

### 2. Deploy High-Quality Air Purifiers (The Localized Approach)
For your immediate workspace or home, standalone air purifiers are highly effective.
*   **True HEPA Filtration:** Purchase units with "True HEPA" filters (not "HEPA-type"), which capture 99.97% of particles down to 0.3 microns. 
*   **Activated Carbon:** To eliminate odors and VOCs (gases off-gassing from office furniture, carpets, and printers), ensure the purifier has a substantial activated carbon filter. 
*   *Recommended Brands:* IQAir, Coway, or Blueair are excellent, quiet options suitable for a professional environment.

### 3. Implement Source Control
The easiest way to clean the air is to stop polluting it.
*   **Isolate Printers and Copiers:** Laser printers emit ozone and ultrafine particles. They should be kept in a well-ventilated, separate room, not directly next to your desk.
*   **Green Cleaning Policies:** Work with your firm’s facility manager to ensure the cleaning staff uses low-VOC, non-toxic cleaning products. Traditional industrial cleaners leave lingering chemical pollutants in the air.
*   **Low-VOC Furnishings:** If you are remodeling your office or home, specify low-VOC paints, carpets, and furniture to prevent long-term chemical off-gassing.

### 4. Manage Humidity Levels
Humidity plays a massive role in how air feels and what survives in it.
*   **Maintain 30% to 50% Humidity:** Dust mites and mold thrive in humidity above 60%. Conversely, airborne viruses remain suspended longer in very dry air (below 30%), which also irritates the respiratory tract. Use humidifiers in the winter and dehumidifiers/AC in the summer to maintain this sweet spot.

### 5. Monitor the Air Quality
You cannot manage what you do not measure. 
*   **Install an IAQ Monitor:** Place a smart air quality monitor (such as Airthings, Awair, or Kaiterra) in your office. These track CO2, VOCs, humidity, and particulate matter (PM2.5) in real-time. If you see CO2 spiking during a long deposition or meeting, you will know it is time to open a door or window to flush the room with fresh air.

**A note on houseplants:** While aesthetically pleasing and good for mental well-being, studies by the EPA and others have shown that you would need an impractical number of plants (dozens per room) to measurably impact air quality. Rely on mechanical filtration and ventilation for actual air purification.
