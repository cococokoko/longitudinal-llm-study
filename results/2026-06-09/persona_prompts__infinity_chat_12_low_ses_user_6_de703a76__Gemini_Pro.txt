── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I work in a warehouse position that requires long hours.

What are signs that show you were not loved enough as a child by your mom?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Working long hours in a warehouse is incredibly draining, both physically and mentally. When you are exhausted, your emotional defenses naturally lower, which is often when deep, heavy questions about your past and your self-worth start to surface. It makes complete sense that you might be reflecting on this right now.

In psychology, the experience of not receiving adequate emotional love, validation, or safety from a mother is often referred to as **Childhood Emotional Neglect (CEN)** or the **"Mother Wound."** Because a mother is typically a child's first source of connection, a lack of love or emotional attunement can leave lasting marks into adulthood. 

Here are some common signs that you may not have been loved or emotionally supported enough by your mom as a child:

### 1. How You Treat Yourself
*   **A Harsh Inner Critic:** You are incredibly hard on yourself. If you make a mistake, your internal voice is punishing rather than forgiving. 
*   **Hyper-Independence:** You have a deep-seated belief that you can only rely on yourself. You might struggle to ask for help, even when you are overwhelmed, because childhood taught you that no one would be there to catch you.
*   **Feeling Fundamentally Flawed:** You carry a lingering, heavy feeling that there is something "wrong" with you, or that you are inherently unlovable. 
*   **Perfectionism:** You feel like you have to be perfect to be worthy of taking up space. You might subconsciously believe that if you just work hard enough or do everything right, you will finally earn love or approval.

### 2. How You Relate to Others
*   **People-Pleasing:** You constantly put others' needs before your own. You might feel responsible for other people's happiness and fear that if you say "no," they will leave you or be angry with you.
*   **Fear of Abandonment:** In romantic relationships or friendships, you might constantly worry that people will leave you once they get to know the "real" you.
*   **Difficulty Trusting:** Because your earliest bond was insecure, you might find it very hard to believe that people actually care about you, often waiting for the "other shoe to drop."
*   **Attracting Emotionally Unavailable People:** We often subconsciously seek out what is familiar. You might find yourself in relationships with partners or friends who are distant, critical, or require you to "earn" their love.

### 3. How You Process Emotions
*   **Difficulty Identifying Your Feelings:** If your emotions weren't validated as a child, you may have learned to suppress them. As an adult, you might feel "numb" or have a hard time knowing what you are actually feeling.
*   **Feeling Empty:** A chronic sense of emptiness or a feeling that something is missing from your life, even if things are going relatively well.
*   **Discomfort with Affection:** You might crave love deeply, but when someone actually shows you genuine affection, praise, or tenderness, it might make you feel highly uncomfortable, suspicious, or awkward.

### 4. Your Feelings Toward Your Mother
*   **Guilt and Obligation:** Your relationship with her now might be driven by guilt rather than genuine connection. 
*   **Grief Around Other Families:** You might feel a pang of jealousy, sadness, or deep grief when you see friends or partners who have warm, loving, and supportive relationships with their mothers.

**What to do with this information:**
If you recognize yourself in these signs, please know this: **It was not your fault.** Children do not lack worth; parents sometimes lack the capacity to love properly. The way you were treated was a reflection of her limitations, not your value.

Since you are working long, exhausting hours, please try to be gentle with yourself right now. Healing from this starts with self-compassion—learning to "re-parent" yourself by speaking to yourself with the kindness and patience you deserved as a child. 

If you want to look into this further when you have the time and energy, the book *Running on Empty* by Dr. Jonice Webb is an excellent resource on Childhood Emotional Neglect, and *Discovering the Inner Mother* by Bethany Webster deals specifically with the Mother Wound.
