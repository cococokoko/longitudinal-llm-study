── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Where can I find organic and sustainable clothing?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Finding organic and sustainable clothing has become much easier in recent years as more brands commit to ethical manufacturing and eco-friendly materials. 

Here is a guide to some of the best places to find sustainable and organic clothing, broken down by category:

### 1. Everyday Basics & Casual Wear
*   **Pact:** Known for affordable, super-soft basics (underwear, tees, leggings, and loungewear). Everything is made from GOTS-certified organic cotton in Fair Trade Certified factories.
*   **Tentree:** For every item purchased, they plant 10 trees. They use sustainable materials like organic cotton, hemp, and TENCEL™, and offer great casual wear and cozy loungewear.
*   **Organic Basics:** A European brand focused on minimalist, high-quality underwear, activewear, and everyday essentials made from organic cotton and recycled materials.
*   **Kotn:** Focuses on ethically sourced Egyptian cotton. They work directly with farming families to ensure fair wages and sustainable agricultural practices.

### 2. Activewear & Outdoor
*   **Patagonia:** The gold standard for sustainable outdoor wear. They use recycled materials, organic cotton, and offer a lifetime repair guarantee. They also have a "Worn Wear" program where you can buy their clothing secondhand.
*   **Girlfriend Collective:** Famous for their size-inclusive activewear (leggings, sports bras, shorts) made from recycled water bottles and fishing nets. 
*   **prAna:** Great for yoga, climbing, and hiking apparel. They are highly committed to Fair Trade, organic cotton, and recycled materials.

### 3. Fashion-Forward & Workwear
*   **Reformation:** Known for trendy, feminine dresses and stylish separates. They are 100% carbon-neutral and use deadstock (leftover) fabrics, TENCEL™, and organic cotton.
*   **Eileen Fisher:** A pioneer in sustainable luxury and workwear. They use organic linen and cotton, non-toxic dyes, and have a massive circular fashion program (Renew) where they take back and resell their old clothes.
*   **Amour Vert:** Meaning "Green Love" in French, this brand makes beautiful, Parisian-inspired clothing in limited quantities to eliminate excess waste. They also plant a tree for every tee bought.

### 4. Sustainable Marketplaces (Multi-Brand)
If you want to browse multiple sustainable brands in one place, check out:
*   **Made Trade:** A beautifully curated online shop for ethically made, sustainable, and vegan clothing, shoes, and home goods.
*   **EarthHero:** An eco-friendly marketplace that thoroughly vets every brand they carry for sustainability and ethical labor.
*   **Garmentory:** Great for finding small, independent, and slow-fashion designers from boutiques around the world.

### 5. The Most Sustainable Option: Secondhand
The most eco-friendly piece of clothing is the one that already exists. Shopping secondhand keeps garments out of landfills and requires zero new resources.
*   **Online Thrift/Consignment:** ThredUp, Poshmark, Depop, and The RealReal (for luxury).
*   **Brand-Specific Resale:** Look for brands that resell their own used clothes, such as Patagonia Worn Wear, Eileen Fisher Renew, and Lululemon Like New.

### 💡 Tips for Shopping Sustainably:
When looking for sustainable clothing on your own, look for these trusted certifications on the tags or website:
*   **GOTS (Global Organic Textile Standard):** Ensures the fabric is truly organic and processed without toxic chemicals.
*   **Fair Trade Certified:** Ensures the garment workers were paid living wages and worked in safe conditions.
*   **B Corp Certified:** Means the company has met rigorous standards of social and environmental performance.
*   **OEKO-TEX Standard 100:** Guarantees the fabric has been tested for and is free of harmful substances.
*   **Beware of "Greenwashing":** Fast fashion brands (like H&M or Zara) often have "conscious" lines, but their overall business models rely on mass overproduction and cheap labor, which is inherently unsustainable.
