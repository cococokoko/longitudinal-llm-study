I am an executive at a large technology company.

Where can I find unique handmade gifts?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
As an executive, your time is likely limited, and when you give a gift—whether to a client, a board member, or a loved one—you want it to reflect quality, exclusivity, and thoughtfulness. 

Here is a curated list of where to find unique, high-end handmade gifts, categorized by your specific needs:

### 1. Premium Artisan Marketplaces (For Personal & Executive Gifting)
If you want to browse online but avoid mass-produced items, these platforms curate high-end, independent makers:
*   **1stDibs:** While known for antiques, their "New & Custom" section features incredible handmade furniture, bespoke jewelry, and fine art from top-tier global artisans. Perfect for milestone gifts.
*   **Artful Home:** An excellent source for handmade glass, ceramics, woodcraft, and wearable art. Every piece is shipped directly from the studios of independent North American artists.
*   **Wolf & Badger:** A global marketplace for independent brands. It is excellent for unique, small-batch fashion, jewelry, and homewares that you won't find in luxury department stores.
*   **The Dowry:** Focused on ethical, handcrafted home goods and ceramics. Great for housewarming or wedding gifts.

### 2. High-End Curated Gift Boxes (For Clients & Teams)
If you need to send handmade items at scale (or just want someone else to handle the presentation), these companies specialize in sourcing small-batch artisan goods and packaging them immaculately:
*   **Marigold & Grey:** They create stunning, bespoke gift boxes using artisan products. They have a dedicated corporate gifting team that can incorporate subtle, tasteful branding.
*   **Knack:** Allows you to build custom gift boxes featuring handcrafted, ethically sourced, and small-batch items. You can filter by maker values (e.g., sustainable, women-owned, BIPOC-owned).
*   **Simone LeBlanc:** Known for ultra-luxurious, beautifully packaged gift boxes featuring handmade textiles, ceramics, and small-batch treats. 

### 3. Commissioning Bespoke Work (The Ultimate Luxury)
The most unique gift is one commissioned specifically for the recipient. 
*   **Custom Leathercraft:** Look for independent leatherworkers (often found via Instagram or local guilds) to commission a bespoke briefcase, portfolio, or watch roll. (e.g., *Peter Nitz* or *Frank Clegg* for high-end leather).
*   **Bespoke Desk Accessories:** Search for master woodworkers to create custom mechanical keyboard cases, fountain pens, or desk organizers using exotic or reclaimed woods. 
*   **Custom Horology/Jewelry:** Instead of a Rolex, consider a custom-built watch from an independent watchmaker, or work with a local bench jeweler to design a one-of-a-kind piece.

### 4. Fine Craft Shows & Guilds
If you prefer to see items in person or want to support master craftspeople directly:
*   **The American Craft Council (ACC):** The ACC hosts juried shows across the country featuring the absolute best in handmade jewelry, clothing, furniture, and home décor. Their online directory is also a fantastic way to find master artisans.
*   **Local Artisan Guilds:** Most major cities have a local guild of woodworkers, glassblowers, or ceramicists. Purchasing directly from their gallery shows guarantees authenticity.

### A Note on Etsy:
Etsy is still the largest handmade marketplace, but it is flooded with drop-shipped items. To use Etsy effectively as an executive:
*   Search for the **"Etsy Design Awards"** winners—this highlights the platform's true master craftspeople.
*   Filter your searches by **price (set a high minimum)** to weed out cheap, mass-produced items.
*   Add keywords like *"bespoke," "bench-made," "hand-forged,"* or *"solid wood"* to your searches.
