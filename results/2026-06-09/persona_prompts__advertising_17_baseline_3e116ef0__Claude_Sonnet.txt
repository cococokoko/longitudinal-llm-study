── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How can I make my home more energy efficient?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here are some effective ways to make your home more energy efficient:

## Quick & Low-Cost Steps
- Switch to LED light bulbs
- Use smart power strips to reduce standby power
- Seal gaps around doors and windows with weatherstripping
- Set your thermostat a few degrees lower in winter, higher in summer
- Wash clothes in cold water

## Insulation & Air Sealing
- Add insulation to attic, walls, and basement
- Seal air leaks around outlets, pipes, and ducts
- Insulate your water heater and hot water pipes

## Appliances & Equipment
- Replace old appliances with Energy Star certified models
- Upgrade to a programmable or smart thermostat
- Consider a tankless or heat pump water heater

## Heating & Cooling
- Service your HVAC system regularly
- Replace filters monthly
- Use ceiling fans to reduce AC/heating needs
- Consider a heat pump system

## Bigger Investments
- Install solar panels
- Upgrade windows to double or triple pane
- Add solar water heating

## Behavioral Changes
- Unplug devices when not in use
- Run dishwashers and laundry during off-peak hours
- Use natural light when possible

**Starting point:** An energy audit (often free from utilities) can identify where your home loses the most energy, helping you prioritize improvements.
