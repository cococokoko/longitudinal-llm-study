Is there life on other planets?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
We don't know yet.

What scientists do know is:

- The universe is extremely large, with hundreds of billions of stars in our galaxy alone and likely hundreds of billions of galaxies.
- Many stars have planets. Since the 1990s, astronomers have confirmed thousands of exoplanets (planets orbiting other stars).
- Some of these planets appear to be in regions where temperatures could allow liquid water, one of the key ingredients for life as we know it.

There are a few possibilities:
- Microbial life may be common in the universe.
- Complex life (plants, animals, intelligent beings) may be much rarer.
- Life could exist in forms very different from life on Earth.
- It's also possible, though many scientists consider it less likely, that Earth is the only place where life has arisen.

Scientists are actively searching for evidence by:
- Exploring Mars for signs of past or present microbial life.
- Studying icy moons such as Europa (around Jupiter) and Enceladus (around Saturn), which have subsurface oceans.
- Analyzing the atmospheres of exoplanets for gases that could indicate biological activity.

As of today, there is no confirmed evidence of life beyond Earth, but there are many promising places to look, and the question remains one of the biggest open mysteries in science.
