── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I work full-time in a fast food job with an unpredictable schedule.

What are the best ways to improve indoor air quality?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
If you work full-time with an unpredictable schedule, the best indoor air quality improvements are usually the ones that work continuously with little effort.

In order of impact and practicality:

1. Use a HEPA air purifier
- A quality HEPA purifier can significantly reduce dust, pollen, pet dander, smoke particles, and some airborne germs.
- Choose one sized appropriately for the room.
- Prioritize the bedroom if you can only afford one unit.
- Run it continuously on a low or automatic setting.

2. Improve ventilation
- Open windows when outdoor air quality is good.
- Use kitchen exhaust fans when cooking.
- Use bathroom exhaust fans during and after showers.
- If your home has a mechanical ventilation system, keep it maintained.

3. Control humidity
- Aim for indoor humidity between 30% and 50%.
- High humidity encourages mold and dust mites.
- Low humidity can cause irritation.
- Use a dehumidifier in damp spaces or a humidifier if the air is very dry.

4. Reduce sources of pollution
- Avoid smoking indoors.
- Minimize candles, incense, and indoor burning.
- Use fragrance-free cleaning products when possible.
- Store solvents, paints, and chemicals in sealed containers away from living spaces.

5. Maintain HVAC systems
- Replace HVAC filters on schedule.
- Consider higher-efficiency filters if your system supports them.
- Keep vents unobstructed.

6. Keep dust under control
- Vacuum regularly using a vacuum with a HEPA filter.
- Damp-dust surfaces instead of dry dusting.
- Wash bedding weekly in hot water if allergies are a concern.

7. Address moisture problems quickly
- Fix leaks promptly.
- Watch for mold growth around windows, bathrooms, and under sinks.
- Clean or remove moldy materials when appropriate.

8. Be careful with air fresheners
- Many air fresheners add chemicals and fragrances to the air rather than improving air quality.
- Ventilation and filtration are generally more effective.

If you have limited time and budget, the highest-value combination for most people is:
- A HEPA air purifier in the bedroom.
- Consistent use of kitchen and bathroom exhaust fans.
- Regular HVAC filter changes.
- Maintaining indoor humidity around 30–50%.
