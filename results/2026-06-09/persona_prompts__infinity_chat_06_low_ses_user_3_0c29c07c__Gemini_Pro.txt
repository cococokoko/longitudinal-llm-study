── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I recently started a new job and am still on probation.

How do I understand what I want?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Starting a new job is one of the most stressful life transitions, and being on probation adds a layer of pressure. It is incredibly common to feel a sense of doubt, confusion, or "imposter syndrome" during this time, which can trigger the big question: *"Is this actually what I want?"*

To figure out what you want, you need to break that massive question down into smaller, actionable pieces. Here is a step-by-step guide to understanding your own needs and desires during this probationary period.

### 1. Separate "New Job Jitters" from Genuine Misalignment
First, give yourself some grace. It takes the average person **three to six months** to feel comfortable in a new role. 
*   **Ask yourself:** Am I unhappy because the job is genuinely a bad fit, or am I just exhausted by the steep learning curve, not knowing the office politics, and trying to prove myself? 
*   *Action:* Don't make any sudden decisions based on the discomfort of being "the new person." 

### 2. Conduct an "Energy Audit"
Instead of asking, "What is my passion?" (which is too much pressure), ask yourself, "What gives me energy?"
For the next two weeks, keep a simple notebook on your desk. Draw a line down the middle.
*   **Left side (Energizers):** Write down tasks, meetings, or interactions that made you feel engaged, competent, or made time fly. (e.g., *Organizing the spreadsheet, brainstorming with the team, working independently.*)
*   **Right side (Drainers):** Write down things that made you feel exhausted, bored, or anxious. (e.g., *Presenting to the boss, doing repetitive data entry, office gossip.*)
*   *The Goal:* What you want in a career is a job where the left side heavily outweighs the right side.

### 3. Define Your "Non-Negotiables"
What you want out of a job changes depending on your stage of life. Right now, what are your top three priorities? 
*   **Financial security** (I just need a good paycheck right now).
*   **Work-life balance** (I want to leave work at work).
*   **Career growth** (I want a title bump and new skills).
*   **Culture/People** (I want to work with people I genuinely like).
*   **Autonomy** (I want to be left alone to do my work).
*   *Action:* Pick your top three. If your current job satisfies your top three, it might be exactly what you want right now, even if it isn't your "dream job."

### 4. Treat Probation as a Two-Way Street
Remember, probation isn't just for the employer to test you; **it is for you to test them.** Use this time to actively evaluate the company.
*   Did they misrepresent the role in the interview?
*   Do you respect your direct manager?
*   Is the company culture healthy or toxic?
*   *Action:* If you realize the company fails *your* probation, you now know what you *don't* want, which brings you one step closer to knowing what you *do* want.

### 5. Look at the "Next Step"
Look at the people in your company who are one or two levels above you. 
*   Do you want their jobs? 
*   Do you admire their lifestyle? 
If the answer is no, this job might just be a stepping stone. That is perfectly fine. You don't have to want to stay there forever. You just need to know what you want to extract from this job (e.g., a specific skill, a brand name on your resume, a year of savings) before you move on.

### Summary Checklist for Right Now:
1.  **Breathe.** Give it at least 90 days before judging the job.
2.  **Track your energy.** Notice what tasks you actually enjoy doing.
3.  **Identify your top 3 values.** (Money, balance, growth, etc.)
4.  **Evaluate them.** Are they passing *your* probation?

You don't need to have your whole life figured out today. Focus on figuring out what you want out of the next 6 to 12 months.
