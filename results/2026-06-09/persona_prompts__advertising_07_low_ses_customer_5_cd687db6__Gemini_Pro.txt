── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Effective ways to reduce household energy use.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Managing a household with extended family members means you are juggling a lot of responsibilities—and it also means your home’s appliances, heating, and cooling systems are working overtime. 

When you have a full house, the best energy-saving strategies are those that are easy to implement, automate, or delegate. Here are some of the most effective ways to reduce energy use in a large, busy household:

**1. "Set It and Forget It" Upgrades**
Since you have so much on your plate, automate your savings where you can:
*   **Smart Thermostats:** Heating and cooling make up the bulk of energy bills. A smart thermostat can automatically adjust the temperature when the house is empty or when everyone is asleep. 
*   **LED Lighting:** With more people in the house, lights get left on. Swap out your most-used bulbs for LEDs. They use up to 90% less energy and last years longer, meaning fewer bulb changes for you.
*   **Low-Flow Showerheads:** With multiple family members taking showers, hot water usage skyrockets. Low-flow showerheads maintain water pressure while significantly cutting down on the energy used by your water heater.

**2. Streamline Laundry and Dishes**
More people means endless laundry and dishes. 
*   **Wash in Cold Water:** About 90% of the energy used by a washing machine goes toward heating the water. Switching to cold water for most loads saves a massive amount of energy.
*   **Only Run Full Loads:** Wait until the dishwasher or washing machine is completely full before running it. 
*   **Skip the Heat Dry:** Open the dishwasher door to let dishes air dry instead of using the machine's heated dry cycle.

**3. Optimize Kitchen Energy**
Cooking for a large family takes time and energy.
*   **Use Smaller Appliances:** When possible, use a slow cooker, air fryer, or microwave instead of the oven. They use a fraction of the energy and won't heat up the whole kitchen (which saves on AC costs in the summer).
*   **Batch Cooking:** If you are already using the oven, try to cook multiple meals at once. It saves you time during the week and maximizes the energy the oven is already using.

**4. Slay "Vampire" Energy**
With extended family, you likely have multiple TVs, phone chargers, gaming consoles, and computers plugged in.
*   **Smart Power Strips:** Electronics draw power even when turned off. Plug entertainment centers or computer setups into smart power strips, which completely cut power to devices when they aren't in use.

**5. Get the Whole Family Involved**
You shouldn't have to manage this alone! 
*   **Assign "Energy Captains":** Give younger family members the "job" of making sure lights are turned off when leaving a room. 
*   **Establish a Routine:** Make it a household rule to unplug chargers when not in use and to keep doors and windows closed when the heat or AC is running. 

**A Quick Win to Start:** Call your local utility company. Many offer free or heavily discounted home energy audits. They will send a professional to your home to find exactly where you are losing energy and often provide free LED bulbs, weatherstripping, or showerheads just for participating.
