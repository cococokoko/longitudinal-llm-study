── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How do I understand what I want?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Figuring out what you want is rarely a sudden "eureka" moment; it is usually a process of peeling back layers. Often, we struggle to know what we want because our own desires are buried under societal expectations, family pressures, or the fear of making the "wrong" choice.

Here is a practical framework to help you understand what you truly want:

### 1. Clear the "Shoulds"
Much of our confusion comes from confusing what we *want* with what we think we *should* do. 
*   **The Exercise:** Write down your current goals or desires. Next to each, ask yourself: *"Do I want this, or do I think I should want this?"* If it’s a "should," who put it there? Society? Your parents? Social media? 

### 2. Follow Your Jealousy
Jealousy gets a bad reputation, but it is actually a highly accurate compass. We don't get jealous of things we don't care about.
*   **The Exercise:** Think about the people you envy. What exactly do they have that makes you jealous? Is it their creative freedom? Their financial stability? Their close-knit family? Their adventurous lifestyle? Your envy is pointing directly at your hidden desires.

### 3. Create an "Anti-Vision"
Sometimes it is impossible to know what you want, but it is very easy to know what you *don't* want. 
*   **The Exercise:** Write down your absolute nightmare scenario for five years from now. What does your worst possible job look like? Your worst living situation? Your worst daily routine? Once you define exactly what you want to avoid, the opposite direction becomes your path forward.

### 4. Track Your Energy
Your brain can overthink, but your body rarely lies. Pay attention to how different activities, people, and environments make you feel physically and mentally.
*   **Energy Givers:** What are you doing when you lose track of time? What leaves you feeling energized, even if you're physically tired?
*   **Energy Takers:** What fills you with dread? What makes you feel heavy, bored, or exhausted? 
*   *What you want is usually found in the things that give you energy.*

### 5. Look Back at Your Childhood
Before the world told you what to care about, what did you naturally gravitate toward? 
*   Did you love building things? Organizing games? Drawing? Listening to people? Being in nature? The core essence of what you loved at age 8 or 10 often translates into what will fulfill you as an adult.

### 6. Stop Thinking and Start Experimenting
You cannot think your way into knowing what you want. **Action breeds clarity.** 
*   If you think you might want to be a writer, write a blog post. If you think you want to live in a new city, go visit it for a week. If you think you want to learn coding, take a free weekend class. 
*   Treat your life like a series of small, low-risk experiments. You will quickly figure out what you like and what you don't.

### 7. Ask Yourself the "Magic Wand" Questions
Grab a journal and answer these questions honestly, without worrying about how realistic they are:
*   If you had $10 million in the bank, but you still had to work 40 hours a week at *something*, what would you do?
*   If you knew you could not fail, what would you attempt?
*   If you had to teach one subject to a group of people for an hour with no preparation, what would you talk about?

**A Final Note:** 
Accept that "what you want" is not a permanent destination. What you want at 20 will be different from what you want at 30, 40, or 60. You don't need to figure out what you want for the rest of your life; you only need to figure out what you want for *right now*. Take the next logical step, and the path will reveal itself as you walk it.
