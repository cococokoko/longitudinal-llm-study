── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I have an apartment that costs about $100 a month. How do I survive?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Having an apartment that costs only $100 a month is an incredible advantage. In most parts of the world, housing is a person's largest expense. Because your rent is so low, your path to survival—and eventually thriving—is much easier than it is for most people. 

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to survive and build stability around a $100/month living situation:

### 1. The Golden Rule: Protect Your Housing
Because $100/month is exceptionally cheap (whether it is subsidized housing, a room rental, or in a low-cost-of-living country), **do whatever it takes to never lose this apartment.** 
*   Pay your rent before you pay for *anything* else. 
*   Follow all the rules of your lease or living arrangement.
*   Be a quiet, clean, and respectful tenant so your landlord never has a reason to evict you. If you lose this place, your rent could easily jump to $1,000+ elsewhere.

### 2. Calculate Your "Survival Number"
Figure out exactly how much money you need just to stay alive for one month. 
*   **Rent:** $100
*   **Utilities (Water/Electric):** ~$50-$100 (keep usage low)
*   **Food:** ~$100-$150 (if you cook cheap staples)
*   **Phone/Internet:** ~$30
*   **Transportation:** ~$50 (bus pass or bike maintenance)
*   **Total Survival Number:** **~$330 to $430 a month.**

This means you only need to earn about $10 to $15 a day to survive. 

### 3. Secure Your Food
You can eat very cheaply if you are willing to cook from scratch.
*   **Staples:** Buy rice, dried beans, lentils, oats, potatoes, and frozen vegetables. These are incredibly cheap, filling, and nutritious.
*   **Food Banks:** If you are struggling to get cash, locate local food pantries. There is no shame in using them; that is exactly what they are there for.
*   **Government Assistance:** If you are in the US, apply for SNAP (food stamps). With your low housing costs, SNAP could cover 100% of your food needs.

### 4. Generate Income
Because your expenses are so low, you do not need a high-paying, high-stress job to survive. 
*   **Part-time work:** Working just 10–15 hours a week at minimum wage in most places will cover your basic survival number.
*   **Gig work:** Day labor, dog walking, house cleaning, or delivering food can easily generate $400 a month.
*   **Plasma donation:** In many countries, donating blood plasma twice a week can yield $200–$400 a month, which almost covers your entire cost of living.

### 5. Keep Other Expenses Near Zero
*   **Transportation:** Do not buy a car if you can avoid it. Cars require gas, insurance, and repairs, which will destroy a low-income budget. Walk, get a cheap bicycle, or use public transit.
*   **Phone/Internet:** Look into government-subsidized phone plans (like the Lifeline program in the US, which provides free phone service to low-income individuals). 
*   **Healthcare:** Apply for Medicaid or your country's equivalent of low-income health insurance. 

### 6. Build an Emergency Fund
Once you have a job and your basic needs are met, you have a unique opportunity: **you can save money.** 
If you manage to make $1,000 a month, and your expenses are $400, you can save $600 a month. Put this money into a savings account and do not touch it. Build up an emergency fund of $2,000 to $3,000. This will ensure that if you get sick or lose your job, you can still pay your $100 rent for years without worrying.
