── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
What is the difference between analysis and design? Can one begin to design without analysis? Why? Be concise.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
**The Difference:**
*   **Analysis** focuses on the *problem*. It defines **"what"** needs to be done by gathering requirements, understanding user needs, and identifying constraints. 
*   **Design** focuses on the *solution*. It defines **"how"** the problem will be solved by creating blueprints, architecture, and interfaces based on the analysis.

**Can you design without analysis?**
Technically yes, but it is highly ill-advised and almost always leads to failure. 

**Why?**
Designing without analysis means you are building a solution without understanding the problem. This inevitably leads to solving the wrong problem, failing to meet actual user needs, and wasting time and resources. You cannot effectively build a solution if you don't know what you are trying to solve.
