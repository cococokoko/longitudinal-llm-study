── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I work in a warehouse position that requires long hours.

How can I learn to cook healthy meals quickly?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Working in a warehouse is physically demanding. After a long shift on your feet, the last thing you want to do is spend an hour chopping vegetables and washing five different pans. 

To eat healthy without burning out, you need to shift your mindset from **"cooking meals"** to **"assembling fuel."** 

Here is a practical, low-effort guide to eating healthy when you are physically exhausted.

---

### 1. The "No-Cook" Grocery List (The Cheat Codes)
You do not need to cook everything from scratch to eat healthy. Use the grocery store to do the prep work for you. 

*   **Proteins:** Rotisserie chickens (shred the meat for the week), canned tuna/salmon, hard-boiled eggs (bought pre-boiled), plain Greek yogurt, canned black beans or chickpeas (rinse and eat).
*   **Carbs:** Microwaveable brown rice or quinoa pouches (ready in 90 seconds), corn tortillas, whole-wheat pita pockets, rolled oats.
*   **Veggies:** Pre-washed bagged salads, frozen steam-in-bag vegetables (broccoli, green beans, mixed veggies), baby carrots, cherry tomatoes (no chopping required).
*   **Healthy Fats:** Avocados, nuts, seeds, olive oil.

### 2. The "One-Pot/One-Pan" Cooking Methods
If you are going to cook, minimize the dishes. These three methods require almost zero active supervision.

*   **The Sheet Pan Dinner:** 
    *   *How:* Toss a chopped protein (chicken breast, sausage, or tofu) and veggies (broccoli, bell peppers) in olive oil, salt, pepper, and garlic powder. Spread on a baking sheet lined with foil (for zero cleanup). 
    *   *Time:* Bake at 400°F (200°C) for 20–25 minutes. Go take a shower; dinner is ready when you get out.
*   **The Slow Cooker (Crockpot) / Instant Pot:**
    *   *How:* Dump a jar of salsa, a can of black beans, and 3 chicken breasts into a slow cooker. Cook on low for 6–8 hours while you are at work. 
    *   *Result:* Shredded salsa chicken for tacos, bowls, or salads for the next 4 days.
*   **The 90-Second Stir Fry:**
    *   *How:* Heat a pan, throw in pre-cut stir-fry veggies (from the produce aisle) and pre-cooked chicken or tofu. Add a splash of soy sauce and garlic. Serve over a microwave rice pouch.

### 3. Master "Component Cooking" (Not Meal Prep)
Traditional meal prep (eating the exact same chicken, broccoli, and rice for 5 days) gets boring fast. Instead, cook **components** on your day off.

Spend 45 minutes on Sunday prepping these three things:
1.  **Cook one big batch of protein:** Bake 4-5 chicken breasts or brown 2 lbs of ground turkey.
2.  **Cook one batch of grains:** Make a big pot of rice, quinoa, or pasta.
3.  **Wash/chop veggies:** Or just buy them pre-chopped.

**How to assemble during the week (under 5 minutes):**
*   **Night 1 (Mexican Bowl):** Rice + Ground Turkey + Black Beans + Salsa + Avocado.
*   **Night 2 (Greek Bowl):** Rice + Chicken + Cherry Tomatoes + Cucumbers + Tzatziki sauce.
*   **Night 3 (Quick Stir-Fry):** Toss the turkey and veggies in a pan with teriyaki sauce over rice.

### 4. High-Protein Warehouse Lunches (No Microwave Needed)
If your warehouse breakroom is far away or the microwave line is too long, pack lunches that taste good cold.

*   **The Adult Lunchable:** Turkey slices, cheese cubes, hard-boiled eggs, grapes, baby carrots, and hummus.
*   **The Loaded Wrap:** A whole-wheat tortilla stuffed with rotisserie chicken, bagged spinach, hummus, and feta cheese.
*   **The Mason Jar Salad:** Put the dressing at the very bottom, then hard veggies (carrots, cucumbers), then protein (chickpeas/chicken), then greens on top. It won't get soggy, and you just shake it up when ready to eat.

### 5. Energy & Hydration for the Shift
Working in a warehouse means you are an athlete. Treat your body like one.
*   **Keep a "Go-Bag" of healthy snacks:** Keep almonds, bananas, beef jerky, or protein bars in your locker or car. This prevents you from hitting the vending machine when your blood sugar drops.
*   **Hydrate with Electrolytes:** Dehydration mimics hunger and causes fatigue. Drink water throughout your shift, and consider adding an electrolyte tablet (like Nuun) if you sweat a lot.
