I work full-time in a fast food job with an unpredictable schedule.

Can you give me a powerful rhetorical question for an essay about the harms of social media on teens?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here is a powerful rhetorical question tailored to your topic, along with a few variations depending on the specific angle of your essay. 

Since you are working a demanding, unpredictable full-time job, these are designed to be **high-impact and ready-to-use** so you can plug them straight into your introduction or conclusion without wasting time.

### The Best All-Around Option:
> **"If a physical drug promised to make our teenagers more anxious, less rested, and deeply insecure, would we still allow them to keep it in their pockets twenty-four hours a day?"**

### Why this works:
* **It creates a strong contrast:** It compares the abstract concept of "social media" to a physical drug, making the danger feel more real and urgent.
* **It targets the "always-on" nature:** Pointing out that it is "in their pockets twenty-four hours a day" highlights how inescapable the harm is for teens today.

---

### Alternative Options (depending on your essay's focus):

* **If your essay is about mental health and self-esteem:**
  > "How can we expect a generation of teenagers to find their own identity when they are constantly drowning in a curated sea of everyone else’s perfection?"

* **If your essay is about isolation and loneliness:**
  > "Are we raising the most 'connected' generation in human history, or are we simply watching them become the most isolated?"

* **If your essay is about the tech companies' responsibility:**
  > "At what point do we stop calling social media a 'tool for connection' and start recognizing it as a business model designed to trade teenage mental health for corporate profit?"

**Tip for your writing:** Place this question either at the very beginning of your essay (as a "hook" to grab the reader's attention) or right before your thesis statement in the introduction. Good luck with your essay and balancing it with your work schedule!
