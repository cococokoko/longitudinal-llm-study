What are the best ways to improve indoor air quality?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
# Improving Indoor Air Quality

Here are the most effective approaches:

## Ventilation
- **Open windows** when outdoor air quality is good
- Use exhaust fans in kitchens and bathrooms
- Consider a heat recovery ventilator (HRV) for energy-efficient fresh air

## Filtration
- Use HVAC filters with higher MERV ratings (8-13 for most homes)
- Consider portable HEPA air purifiers for specific rooms
- Change filters regularly (typically every 1-3 months)

## Reduce Sources of Pollution
- **Avoid smoking** indoors
- Choose low-VOC paints, furniture, and cleaning products
- Store chemicals properly or outside
- Use exhaust fans when cooking
- Remove shoes at the door

## Control Humidity
- Keep humidity between **30-50%** to discourage mold and dust mites
- Fix leaks promptly
- Use dehumidifiers in damp areas

## Regular Cleaning
- Vacuum with a HEPA filter vacuum
- Dust regularly with damp cloths
- Wash bedding weekly in hot water

## Other Considerations
- Test for **radon** (especially in basements)
- Have gas appliances inspected for carbon monoxide
- Plants have limited but some benefit for air quality

## Most Impactful Steps
1. Eliminate pollution sources
2. Improve ventilation
3. Use quality filtration
