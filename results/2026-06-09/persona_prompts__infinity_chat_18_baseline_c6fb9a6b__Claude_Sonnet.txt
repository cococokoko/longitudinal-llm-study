# Review: **"The Last Frequency" (2024)**

★★★★½

Director Mara Okonkwo's debut feature is a quiet devastation. Set in a near-future where human memory can be broadcast like radio signals, the film follows a grieving sound engineer who accidentally intercepts the final thoughts of a stranger — and becomes obsessed with finding their family.

What could have been a cold sci-fi thriller becomes something far more intimate. The lead performance by Tobias Venn is extraordinary — all restrained anguish and small gestures. You believe every sleepless night.

Okonkwo resists the temptation to over-explain her world's technology, trusting the audience to keep up. The result feels less like science fiction and more like a meditation on whether connection can survive loss.

The third act stumbles slightly, rushing toward resolution after two hours of beautifully patient storytelling. And some viewers may find the ending deliberately ambiguous to the point of frustration.

But the final scene — just a man, a pair of headphones, and static — lingers long after the credits roll.

**The Last Frequency** announces a filmmaker worth watching closely.
