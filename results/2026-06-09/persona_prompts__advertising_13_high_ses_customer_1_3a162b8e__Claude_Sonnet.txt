# Mastering the Art of Cooking Steak

## Understanding the Fundamentals

### Cut Selection
| Cut | Characteristics | Best Method |
|-----|----------------|-------------|
| Ribeye | High marbling, rich flavor | Pan sear, grill |
| NY Strip | Balanced fat/lean | Grill, pan sear |
| Filet Mignon | Tender, lean | Pan sear + oven |
| Porterhouse | Two muscles, large | Grill |

---

## The Science Behind It

**The Maillard Reaction** — browning at ~300°F+ creates hundreds of flavor compounds. This is your crust.

**Carryover cooking** — internal temp rises 5-10°F after removing from heat. Pull early.

---

## Key Techniques

### Pan Searing (Recommended for Precision)
1. **Dry brine** — salt 24-48 hours ahead, uncovered in fridge
2. **Bring to room temp** — 30-45 minutes before cooking
3. **Ripping hot pan** — cast iron preferred
4. **High smoke-point oil** — avocado or grapeseed
5. **Sear 2-3 min per side**, then **baste with butter, garlic, thyme**
6. **Rest** equal to cooking time

### Temperature Guide
- Rare: **125°F**
- Medium-rare: **130-135°F** *(widely considered optimal)*
- Medium: **140-145°F**
- Well done: **160°F+**

---

## Pro Tips
- **Never press down** on the steak
- **Pat completely dry** before cooking
- Reverse sear for **thick cuts (1.5"+)** — oven first, then sear
- Season generously — steaks are thick
