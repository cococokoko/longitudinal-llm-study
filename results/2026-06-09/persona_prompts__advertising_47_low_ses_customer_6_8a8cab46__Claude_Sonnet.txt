# Tips for Running a Marathon

## Training
- **Follow a structured plan** - Most plans are 16-20 weeks long
- **Build mileage gradually** - Increase weekly mileage by no more than 10% per week
- **Include long runs** - Build up to at least one 20-mile training run
- **Rest days matter** - Allow your body to recover between runs

## Nutrition & Hydration
- **Practice fueling during training** - Don't try new foods on race day
- **Carbohydrate loading** - In the days before the race
- **Stay hydrated** - Drink water consistently, not just during runs
- **Use energy gels or chews** - Practice taking them during long runs

## Gear
- **Invest in good running shoes** - Get properly fitted at a running store
- **Moisture-wicking clothing** - Prevents chafing
- **Body Glide or anti-chafe products** - Apply to common friction areas

## Race Day
- **Start slow** - Many runners go out too fast
- **Stick to your pace plan**
- **Walk breaks are okay** - Many runners use a run/walk strategy
- **Know the course** - Identify water stations and elevation changes

## Recovery
- **Plan for post-race recovery** - Expect soreness for several days
- **Stretch and refuel** within 30-60 minutes after finishing
