Yes. A strong STEM fair project is one that:
- Solves a real local problem.
- Uses science and engineering, not just research.
- Is affordable and testable.
- Produces measurable results.

Here are several Vietnam-focused ideas, with one standout option.

1. Low-Cost Water Filtration System for Flood-Prone Areas (Excellent Choice)

Problem:
Many regions of Vietnam experience flooding, which can contaminate drinking water.

Project:
Design and test an inexpensive water filter using materials such as:
- Sand
- Gravel
- Charcoal/biochar from rice husks or coconut shells
- Plastic bottles

Research Question:
Which filter material combination removes the most impurities from contaminated water?

Experiments:
- Measure water clarity before and after filtration.
- Compare different filter layers.
- Test pH and total dissolved solids using inexpensive kits.

Why judges like it:
- Addresses a major issue in Vietnam.
- Uses environmental science and engineering.
- Low cost (often under $20–30).
- Generates clear data and graphs.

2. Rice Husk Biochar for Improving Plant Growth

Problem:
Vietnam produces millions of tons of rice husks annually, many of which become waste.

Project:
Convert rice husks into biochar and test whether adding biochar improves plant growth.

Experiments:
- Grow identical plants in:
  - Normal soil
  - Soil with 5% biochar
  - Soil with 10% biochar
- Measure height, leaf count, and biomass.

STEM Fields:
- Environmental science
- Agriculture
- Chemistry

Cost:
Very low if rice husks are available locally.

3. Smart Flood Warning Prototype

Problem:
Flooding affects many communities in central Vietnam.

Project:
Build a small flood-warning system using:
- Arduino
- Water-level sensor
- LED or buzzer

Research Question:
How accurately can a low-cost sensor detect dangerous water levels?

Cost:
Approximately $15–40 depending on components.

Advanced but still affordable.

4. Cooling Homes Naturally During Vietnam's Hot Seasons

Problem:
Rising temperatures increase electricity use from air conditioners.

Project:
Investigate low-cost cooling materials.

Experiments:
Compare:
- White paint
- Aluminum foil coating
- Plant-covered surfaces
- Traditional roofing materials

Measure:
- Surface temperature
- Indoor box temperature

This combines physics, engineering, and sustainability.

5. Mosquito Trap Optimization for Dengue Prevention

Problem:
Dengue fever remains a significant health concern in Vietnam.

Project:
Test different mosquito attractants:
- Sugar solution
- Fruit extracts
- Yeast and sugar mixture

Measure:
- Number of mosquitoes trapped
- Cost effectiveness

Safety Note:
Follow local health and safety guidelines and avoid breeding mosquitoes.
