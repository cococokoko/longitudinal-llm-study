── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I am an executive at a large technology company.

Where can I find unique handmade gifts?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
As an executive at a technology company, you likely value a few key elements when sourcing gifts: **impeccable quality, a compelling story (provenance), seamless logistics, and a high degree of curation** to save you time. 

Here is a curated guide on where to find unique, high-end handmade gifts, categorized by the type of gifting experience you want to deliver.

---

### 1. For Ultra-Premium & Bespoke Corporate Gifting (White-Glove Service)
If you are looking for high-end, handmade gifts for board members, key clients, or executive peers, these platforms offer curated, artisanal products with dedicated corporate concierge services.

*   **Artsy & 1stDibs:** While known for fine art, both platforms have extensive design and decorative arts sections. You can source one-of-a-kind, handmade ceramic vessels, hand-blown glass, and sculptural objects from independent global artisans. 
*   **The Citizenry:** They partner with master artisans globally (from Portuguese weavers to Mexican ceramicists) to create modern, minimalist home goods. They have a dedicated corporate gifting program that handles custom packaging and bulk shipping.
*   **Blue Pheasant / Made Goods:** Known for exquisite, handcrafted tableware and home accessories using unique materials like shagreen, hand-blown glass, and hand-carved wood. Excellent for high-end executive home gifts.

### 2. For Supporting Independent Artists & Makers (Highly Curated)
If you want to avoid the "mass-produced" feel of mainstream sites and find truly unique, studio-made pieces.

*   **Incollect & Wescover:** Wescover is particularly excellent for connecting you directly with the creators. You can find local and international artists who hand-make everything from custom wooden credenzas to hand-painted ceramic mugs. You can commission custom work directly through the platform.
*   **The New Craftsmen (UK-based, ships globally):** This platform represents the finest British craft makers. They sell exquisite, handmade items spanning textiles, silverware, glassware, and ceramics. Every piece has a deep story of heritage and craftsmanship.
*   **Savoir Faire / Odette (and similar boutique design registries):** Look to high-end design boutiques that curate small-batch, handmade goods from independent designers. 

### 3. For Tech-Adjacent & "Analog" Luxury
As a tech executive, gifting something beautifully "analog" can be a refreshing and highly appreciated gesture.

*   **Appointed or Shinola:** For handmade, heirloom-quality paper goods, leather journals, and desk accessories. Appointed offers beautiful, minimalist, American-made paper goods that can be custom-monogrammed.
*   **Grovemade:** Based in Portland, Oregon, they design and hand-craft premium wooden desk accessories (walnut and maple laptop stands, desk trays, and keyboard trays). It is the ultimate "high-end tech workspace" aesthetic.
*   **Local Artisan Guilds:** Look up the **Craft Council** (e.g., American Craft Council) or local glassblowing and woodworking guilds in your metropolitan area (such as the San Francisco Center for the Book or Brooklyn Glass). Commissioning a local artist supports the local economy and provides a great talking point.

### 4. For Curated Food, Wine, & Craft Spirits
Handmade doesn't have to mean objects; it can mean small-batch, artisanal culinary crafts.

*   **Goldbelly (Corporate):** Allows you to gift handmade, iconic regional foods from legendary artisans across the US (e.g., hand-stretched pizzas from NYC, hand-made pastries from San Francisco).
*   **Mouth.com:** Specializes in beautifully packaged, small-batch, indie craft food baskets (hand-batch pickles, artisanal chocolates, small-run bitters). They have a dedicated portal for corporate gifting.
*   **Sourced Craft Cocktails:** Delivers high-end, hand-batched cocktail kits with fresh ingredients and premium spirits, complete with a virtual mixologist if desired.

---

### Executive Tip: How to Source Efficiently
If you find a maker you love on Instagram or a design blog but don't have the time to manage the logistics:
1.  **Utilize an Executive Assistant (EA):** Provide them with this list and ask them to establish a corporate account with **The Citizenry** or **Wescover**.
2.  **Use a Corporate Gifting Platform:** Services like **Snappy**, **Loop & Tie**, or **Postal.io** allow you to send a curated collection of high-end, artisanal gifts where the recipient gets to choose their favorite item, handling all address collection and shipping logistics for you.
