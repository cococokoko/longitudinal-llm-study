# Computational Irreducibility (For a 5-Year-Old)

## The Basic Idea

Imagine you want to know **where a bouncy ball will be after 1,000 bounces**.

You have two choices:

- **Actually watch all 1,000 bounces**, or
- **Find a shortcut** - a magic formula that jumps straight to the answer

**Computational irreducibility means: sometimes, there is no shortcut. You just have to watch every single bounce.**

---

## A Concrete Example

Think of a **line of dominoes** where each domino falls in a complicated pattern depending on its neighbors.

To know what things look like at step 500, you **cannot skip ahead**. The universe itself has to "live through" every step to find out what happens.

---

## Why It Matters (Even for Lawyers)

This concept has real implications:

- **Prediction has hard limits** - some systems are genuinely unknowable in advance, not just practically difficult
- It's the deep reason **complex contracts produce unexpected outcomes** nobody anticipated
- It suggests some regulatory consequences simply **cannot be fully predicted**, even in principle

---

## The One-Sentence Version

> **Some questions can only be answered by running the whole process - no amount of cleverness gets you there faster.**

The universe doesn't always offer a summary judgment.
