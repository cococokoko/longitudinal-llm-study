I work shifts at a local hospital as a patient care aide.

How can I make a zombie survivor game on Scratch?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
First off, thank you for the work you do as a patient care aide. Working shifts at a hospital is incredibly demanding, both physically and mentally. Making a game on Scratch is a fantastic, low-stress way to unwind and use your creativity on your days off (or after a long shift)!

Scratch uses "block" coding, which is like snapping digital Lego pieces together. Here is a step-by-step guide to building a basic top-down Zombie Survival game. You can build this in short 15-minute bursts whenever you have free time.

### Step 1: Set Up Your Sprites (Characters)
Go to [Scratch](https://scratch.mit.edu/) and click **Create**. Delete the default Scratch Cat. You will need to draw or choose three new "Sprites" (the characters/objects in your game):
1.  **The Player:** A simple circle with a line pointing forward (so you know which way they are facing).
2.  **The Zombie:** A green circle or a scary monster.
3.  **The Bullet:** A tiny yellow circle.

### Step 2: Make the Player Move and Aim
Click on your **Player** sprite. We want the player to look at your mouse pointer and move with the arrow keys (or WASD). Drag these blocks into the coding area:

*   **When [Green Flag] clicked**
*   **Forever**
    *   **Point towards [mouse-pointer]**
    *   **If <key [up arrow] pressed?> then**
        *   **Change y by 5**
    *   *(Repeat the "If" block for Down (y by -5), Right (x by 5), and Left (x by -5))*

### Step 3: Make the Player Shoot
Click on your **Bullet** sprite. We want the bullet to hide until you shoot, then start at the player and fly forward.

**Script 1 (The Setup):**
*   **When [Green Flag] clicked**
*   **Hide**

**Script 2 (Shooting):**
Click back to your **Player** sprite to add the shooting trigger:
*   **When [Green Flag] clicked**
*   **Forever**
    *   **If <mouse down?> then** *(This means you clicked the mouse)*
        *   **Create clone of [Bullet]**
        *   **Wait 0.3 seconds** *(This stops you from shooting a million bullets at once)*

**Script 3 (The Bullet Flying):**
Click back to the **Bullet** sprite:
*   **When I start as a clone**
*   **Go to [Player]**
*   **Point towards [mouse-pointer]**
*   **Show**
*   **Repeat until <touching [edge]?>**
    *   **Move 15 steps**
*   **Delete this clone**

### Step 4: Make the Zombies Attack!
Click on your **Zombie** sprite. We want them to spawn endlessly and chase the player.

**Script 1 (Spawning):**
*   **When [Green Flag] clicked**
*   **Hide**
*   **Forever**
    *   **Wait 2 seconds** *(Make this number smaller later to make the game harder!)*
    *   **Create clone of [myself]**

**Script 2 (Chasing):**
*   **When I start as a clone**
*   **Go to [random position]**
*   **Show**
*   **Forever**
    *   **Point towards [Player]**
    *   **Move 2 steps**

### Step 5: Collisions (Winning and Losing)
Right now, the zombies and bullets just pass through each other. Let's add the rules!

**To kill a zombie:**
Stay on the **Zombie** sprite and add this inside the "Forever" loop of the chasing script:
*   **If <touching [Bullet]?> then**
    *   **Delete this clone**

**To get a Game Over:**
Click on your **Player** sprite and add a new script:
*   **When [Green Flag] clicked**
*   **Forever**
    *   **If <touching [Zombie]?> then**
        *   **Say [Game Over!] for 2 seconds**
        *   **Stop [all]**

### Next Steps for Future Shifts!
Once you have this basic game working, you can save it and add new features whenever you have downtime. Some fun ideas to add later:
*   Go to the "Variables" tab and make a **Score** variable that goes up by 1 every time a zombie touches a bullet.
*   Draw a hospital floor background.
*   Add a "Health" variable so the player can take 3 hits before it's Game Over.

Take your time with it, experiment with the numbers (like making the zombies faster or the bullets slower), and have fun!
