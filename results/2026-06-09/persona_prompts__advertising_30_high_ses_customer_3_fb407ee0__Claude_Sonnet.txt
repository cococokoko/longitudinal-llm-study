Here are some practical recommendations for starting to learn coding:

## Choose a First Language
- **Python** is widely recommended for beginners - readable syntax, versatile, strong job market
- **JavaScript** is good if you're interested in web development
- Both have massive learning communities

## Structured Starting Points
- **freeCodeCamp** - free, project-based curriculum
- **Codecademy** - interactive, beginner-friendly
- **CS50** (Harvard's free course) - rigorous, well-respected introduction
- **Python.org's official tutorial** - straightforward and reliable

## Key Principles for Learning Effectively
- **Write code daily**, even just 20-30 minutes
- **Build small projects** rather than just following tutorials
- **Read error messages carefully** - they're informative, not just obstacles
- **Don't memorize syntax** - focus on understanding concepts

## Common Beginner Mistakes to Avoid
- Jumping between languages too quickly
- Watching tutorials without actually coding along
- Waiting until you "understand enough" before building things

## Realistic Expectations
- Basic competency typically takes 3-6 months of consistent practice
- Frustration is normal and doesn't indicate lack of ability
