# Different Views on Discipleship

Discipleship — the process of following, learning from, and being formed by a teacher or tradition — is understood quite differently across theological, philosophical, and practical frameworks.

---

## Christian Perspectives

### Traditional/Catholic View
- Formal, structured formation within the Church
- Sacraments central to the discipleship process
- Emphasis on **obedience, tradition, and community**
- Discipleship as lifelong sanctification

### Protestant/Evangelical View
- Personal relationship with Jesus as foundation
- Bible study and accountability central
- Often emphasizes **intentional one-on-one mentoring**
- Goal: reproducing disciples (multiplication model)

### Missional View
- Discipleship happens **in everyday life contexts**
- Less program-driven, more organic
- Formation through mission and service together

---

## Key Theological Debates

| Question | Views |
|----------|-------|
| Who disciples? | Clergy vs. every believer |
| Where does it happen? | Church programs vs. daily life |
| What is the goal? | Conversion vs. transformation vs. multiplication |
| How long does it take? | Defined courses vs. lifelong process |

---

## Broader/Philosophical Views
- **Rabbinic tradition** — living with and imitating the rabbi
- **Secular mentorship** — skill and character development
- **Buddhist tradition** — following a teacher toward enlightenment
