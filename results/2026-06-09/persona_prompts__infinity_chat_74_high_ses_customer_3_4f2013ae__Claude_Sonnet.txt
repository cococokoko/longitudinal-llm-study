# A World With Double the Oxygen

Doubling atmospheric oxygen from 21% to roughly 42% would have dramatic consequences. Fires would ignite far more easily and burn with extraordinary intensity — even damp vegetation could combust spontaneously. Wildfires would become catastrophically widespread and difficult to extinguish.

Biologically, humans and animals might initially experience hyperoxia, causing seizures, lung damage, and cellular toxicity. However, insects, which breathe through passive diffusion, would likely grow significantly larger, as occurred during the Carboniferous period when oxygen levels were elevated.

Most combustible infrastructure — buildings, forests, clothing — would face unprecedented fire risk, fundamentally reshaping civilization's architecture and survival strategies.
