── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Help me use my Microsoft Surface touchpad and on-screen keyboard to run Dolphin Emulator for the Wii.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Running the Dolphin Emulator (for Wii games) on a Microsoft Surface using only the touchpad and the on-screen keyboard is entirely possible, but it requires a specific setup. 

Because Wii games rely heavily on motion controls and pointing, you will need to map these actions to your touchpad and keyboard.

Here is a step-by-step guide to setting up Dolphin, configuring your controls, and optimizing your on-screen keyboard for gameplay.

---

### Step 1: Optimize your Windows On-Screen Keyboard
The default Windows touch keyboard disappears when you click off it. You need the **legacy On-Screen Keyboard (OSK)**, which stays on top of your game.

1. Press the **Windows Search bar** and type **"On-Screen Keyboard"** (not "Touch Keyboard").
2. Open it. You will see a traditional keyboard window.
3. Click the **Options** key on this keyboard and check **"Turn on numeric keypad"** (this gives you more keys to map).
4. Drag this keyboard to the bottom or side of your screen. You will run Dolphin in "Windowed Mode" so you can see both the game and the keyboard.

---

### Step 2: Configure Dolphin's Controller Settings
Since you don't have a physical Wii Remote, you must emulate one using your keyboard and touchpad.

1. Open Dolphin.
2. Click on **Controllers** in the top toolbar.
3. Under **Wii Remotes**, set **Port 1** to **Emulated Wii Remote**.
4. Click **Configure** next to Port 1.

---

### Step 3: Map the Controls (The Key Step)
In the configuration window, you need to map the Wii Remote buttons to your keyboard and touchpad. Here is the best layout for a Surface setup:

#### **1. General Tab (Buttons)**
*   **A Button:** Map to **Left Click** on your touchpad (or the Spacebar).
*   **B Button:** Map to **Right Click** on your touchpad (or the Left Shift key).
*   **1 Button:** Map to **1** on your keyboard.
*   **2 Button:** Map to **2** on your keyboard.
*   **Minus (-):** Map to **Q**.
*   **Plus (+):** Map to **E**.
*   **Home:** Map to **H**.

#### **2. D-Pad Tab (The Wii Remote D-pad)**
*   Map Up, Down, Left, Right to your keyboard's **Arrow Keys** (or W, A, S, D).

#### **3. Motion Simulation Tab (Crucial for Wii)**
Wii games require pointing at the screen and shaking the controller.
*   **Point (IR):** This simulates pointing the Wii Remote at the TV.
    *   Set **Up, Down, Left, Right** to **Cursor X+, Cursor X-, Cursor Y+, Cursor Y-**. 
    *   *This allows you to aim by simply moving your finger on the Surface touchpad.*
*   **Shake:** Many games (like *Super Mario Galaxy* or *New Super Mario Bros.*) require shaking.
    *   Map **X, Y, and Z** shake to the **Middle Click** (three-finger tap on your touchpad) or a convenient key like **F**.

---

### Step 4: Adjust Dolphin Video Settings for Surface
Surface devices have very high-resolution screens, which can cause lag if Dolphin tries to match it.
1. Click **Graphics** in Dolphin.
2. Under the **General** tab, set **Backend** to **Direct3D 12** or **Vulkan** (these work best with Surface Intel/AMD graphics).
3. Under the **Enhancements** tab, set **Internal Resolution** to **1x Native (640x528)** or **2x Native (720p)**. Do not go higher, or the game will lag.
4. **Crucial:** Ensure **"Start in Fullscreen"** is **unchecked** so you can still see your On-Screen Keyboard.

---

### Step 5: How to Play
1. Launch your Wii game in Dolphin.
2. Position the game window on the top half of your Surface screen.
3. Position the Windows On-Screen Keyboard on the bottom half.
4. **To Play:**
    *   Use your **right hand** on the touchpad to move the Wii pointer and click (A/B buttons).
    *   Use your **left hand** to tap the On-Screen Keyboard for movement (Arrow keys/WASD) and other buttons.

### Pro-Tip for Surface Users: "Touchscreen as IR"
If you want to tap the screen directly to point (like a DS game), go to the **Motion Simulation** tab in Controller settings:
1. Right-click the **Up** button under **Point**.
2. Select your touchscreen device from the dropdown.
3. This is advanced and can be finicky, so using the **touchpad as a mouse pointer** is generally much easier and more accurate.
