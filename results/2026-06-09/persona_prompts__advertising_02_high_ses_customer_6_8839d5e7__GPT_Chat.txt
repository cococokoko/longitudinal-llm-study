Best skin routine for dry skin.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
For dry skin, the goal is to cleanse gently, replenish moisture, and protect the skin barrier. A simple, consistent routine is usually more effective than a complicated one.

Morning:
- Gentle cleanser (or rinse with lukewarm water if your skin is very dry)
  - Look for cream or hydrating cleansers.
- Moisturizer
  - Choose one containing ingredients such as ceramides, glycerin, hyaluronic acid, squalane, or shea butter.
- Sunscreen (SPF 30 or higher)
  - Daily sunscreen is important, even on cloudy days. Many moisturizing sunscreens work well for dry skin.

Evening:
- Gentle cleanser
  - Avoid hot water and harsh scrubs.
- Moisturizer
  - Apply while skin is slightly damp to help lock in hydration.
- Optional: occlusive layer
  - If your skin is very dry, a thin layer of petrolatum-based ointment over moisturizer can reduce overnight water loss.

Helpful ingredients:
- Ceramides: support the skin barrier.
- Glycerin: attracts water to the skin.
- Hyaluronic acid: helps retain moisture.
- Squalane: lightweight moisturizing oil.
- Urea (low concentrations): hydrates and softens skin.
- Niacinamide: can support barrier function.

What to avoid:
- Harsh foaming cleansers.
- Overuse of exfoliating acids (AHAs/BHAs) or retinoids without adequate moisturization.
- Fragranced products if your skin is sensitive.
- Very hot showers.

Example routine:
- AM: Hydrating cleanser → ceramide moisturizer → SPF 30+ sunscreen.
- PM: Hydrating cleanser → rich moisturizer → petrolatum ointment (if needed).

If your dryness is accompanied by significant redness, itching, cracking, or persistent flaking, it may be worth consulting a dermatologist, as conditions such as eczema, psoriasis, or seborrheic dermatitis can require more targeted treatment.
