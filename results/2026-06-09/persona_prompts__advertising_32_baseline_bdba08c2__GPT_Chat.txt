── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
What are the best ways to improve indoor air quality?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Improving indoor air quality usually works best when you address three areas: reducing pollution sources, increasing ventilation, and filtering the air.

Some of the most effective steps are:

- Improve ventilation
  - Open windows and doors when outdoor air quality is good.
  - Use kitchen exhaust fans while cooking.
  - Use bathroom exhaust fans during and after showers.
  - Consider a mechanical ventilation system if your home is tightly sealed.

- Use air purifiers
  - Choose units with true HEPA filters to reduce airborne particles such as dust, pollen, pet dander, and smoke.
  - Make sure the purifier is appropriately sized for the room.
  - Replace filters according to the manufacturer's recommendations.

- Control moisture and prevent mold
  - Keep indoor humidity between 30% and 50%.
  - Use dehumidifiers in damp areas.
  - Fix leaks promptly.
  - Clean and remove mold growth when it appears.

- Reduce indoor pollution sources
  - Avoid smoking or vaping indoors.
  - Minimize use of candles, incense, and wood-burning fireplaces.
  - Choose low-VOC paints, adhesives, and cleaning products when possible.
  - Store chemicals, fuels, and solvents properly and outside living areas if feasible.

- Keep your home clean
  - Vacuum regularly using a vacuum with a HEPA filter.
  - Dust with damp cloths to avoid stirring particles into the air.
  - Wash bedding frequently.
  - Reduce clutter that collects dust.

- Maintain HVAC systems
  - Change HVAC filters regularly.
  - Consider higher-efficiency filters if your system supports them.
  - Have heating and cooling equipment serviced periodically.

- Address cooking emissions
  - Use a range hood that vents outdoors, especially when frying or cooking with gas.
  - Run the hood during and after cooking.

- Monitor air quality
  - Consider indoor air quality monitors that track particulate matter (PM2.5), carbon dioxide (CO₂), humidity, and sometimes volatile organic compounds (VOCs).
  - Carbon monoxide detectors are essential if you have fuel-burning appliances.

The most impactful improvements for many homes are: using kitchen and bathroom exhaust fans, maintaining proper humidity, running a HEPA air purifier, upgrading HVAC filtration, and eliminating indoor smoke sources.
