## Analysis vs. Design

**Analysis** = Understanding **what** the problem is (requirements, constraints, current state)

**Design** = Determining **how** to solve it (structure, architecture, solution approach)

---

## Can You Design Without Analysis?

**Technically yes, practically no.**

- Without analysis, design is based on **assumptions** rather than actual requirements
- You risk building the **wrong solution** correctly
- Leads to costly rework, missed requirements, and scope creep

The only exception is **very simple, well-understood problems** where requirements are obvious — but even then, informal analysis still occurs mentally.

**Bottom line:** Skipping analysis is like building a warehouse layout without knowing what inventory needs to be stored or how workers will move through the space — you may finish the design, but it likely won't work efficiently in practice.
