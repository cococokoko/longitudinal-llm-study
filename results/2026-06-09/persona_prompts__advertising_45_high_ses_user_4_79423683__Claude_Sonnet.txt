── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I am a physician-scientist leading a research group.

How can I learn DIY home repairs and improvements?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here are some practical ways to learn DIY home repairs:

## Starting Resources

**Online**
- YouTube channels (This Old House, Home Repair Tutor, Fix It Home Improvement)
- Family Handyman website
- Reddit communities like r/DIY and r/HomeImprovement

**Books**
- "Reader's Digest Complete Do-It-Yourself Manual"
- "Black & Decker Complete Guide" series

## Hands-On Learning

- **Home Depot/Lowe's workshops** - free in-store classes on specific skills
- **Community college courses** - basic electrical, plumbing, carpentry
- **Habitat for Humanity** - volunteer builds teach real skills quickly

## Practical Approach

- **Start small** - replace outlets, fix leaky faucets, patch drywall
- **Work alongside experienced people** - neighbors, contractors willing to explain
- **Document your projects** - helps you remember techniques

## Mindset Tips

- Accept that first attempts take longer
- Know when to call professionals (main electrical panels, structural work, gas lines)
- Build a quality tool collection gradually

## Given Your Background

Your analytical and systematic thinking transfers well - you'll likely appreciate understanding *why* techniques work, not just the steps. Diagnostic thinking also helps troubleshoot problems.
