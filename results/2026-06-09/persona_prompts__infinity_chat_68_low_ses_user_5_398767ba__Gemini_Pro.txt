── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I support extended family members and manage many household responsibilities.

Give me a tip for managing a team of coworkers.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here is a management tip specifically tailored to your background: 

**Shift your mindset from "Caretaker" to "Coach."**

**Why this matters for you:** 
Because you manage a household and extended family, you are likely the "Chief Operating Officer" of your personal life. You are probably excellent at anticipating needs, catching falling balls, and just doing things yourself because "it’s faster." You are used to carrying the *mental load*. 

**The trap at work:** 
If you bring that caretaker energy to the office, you will quickly burn out, and your team will become overly dependent on you. If you always step in to fix their problems, they will never learn to fix things themselves.

**The tip in action:** 
Delegate the *thinking*, not just the *doing*. When a coworker comes to you with a problem, resist your natural urge to immediately solve it or take it off their plate. Instead, ask coaching questions:
* *"What do you think our best options are here?"*
* *"How would you approach solving this?"*
* *"What do you need from me to help you get this done?"*

By shifting from caretaker to coach, you empower your team to become self-sufficient, build their confidence, and—most importantly—protect your own energy, which you already need for your family at home.
