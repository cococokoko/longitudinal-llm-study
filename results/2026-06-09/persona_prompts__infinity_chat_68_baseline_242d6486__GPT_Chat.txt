── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Give me a tip for managing a team of coworkers.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
One effective tip is to create clear expectations and follow up consistently.

Make sure everyone understands:
- Their responsibilities
- What success looks like
- Deadlines and priorities
- How their work connects to the team's goals

Then, check in regularly—not to micromanage, but to identify obstacles early, answer questions, and keep everyone aligned. Many team problems stem from unclear expectations rather than lack of effort.

A simple practice is to end meetings with a quick recap:
- Who is doing what
- By when
- Any dependencies or risks

This reduces confusion and improves accountability.
