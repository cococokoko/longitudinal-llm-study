── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I am a neurosurgeon at a major teaching hospital.

What are the best tools for remote team collaboration?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
As a neurosurgeon at a major teaching hospital, your requirements for remote collaboration are uniquely demanding. You need tools that are not only efficient and mobile-friendly but, most importantly, **HIPAA-compliant** (capable of signing a Business Associate Agreement, or BAA) and capable of handling high-resolution imaging and sensitive Protected Health Information (PHI).

Here are the best remote collaboration tools categorized by your likely workflows—clinical communication, multidisciplinary meetings (like tumor boards), and academic research.

### 1. Secure Clinical Messaging & Paging
For rapid communication with residents, attendings, and nursing staff regarding patient care.
*   **TigerConnect:** The industry standard for clinical communication. It offers secure texting, voice, and video. It integrates seamlessly with most EHRs (like Epic and Cerner) and allows for role-based messaging (e.g., messaging "Neurosurgery On-Call" rather than a specific person).
*   **Epic Secure Chat / Haiku (if applicable):** If your hospital uses Epic, leveraging their native mobile app (Haiku) and Secure Chat is often the path of least resistance. It keeps all communication strictly within the patient’s chart.
*   **symplr Clinical Communications (formerly Halo Health):** Excellent for unified communication and managing complex on-call schedules for residents and attendings.

### 2. Video Conferencing (Tumor Boards & Remote Consults)
For multidisciplinary team meetings, remote patient consults, and teaching residents.
*   **Microsoft Teams (Healthcare Edition):** Often the best all-in-one choice if your hospital uses Office 365. It offers secure video, chat, and file sharing. It is highly effective for virtual tumor boards, allowing you to share screens to review MRIs/CTs securely.
*   **Zoom for Healthcare:** The healthcare-specific tier of Zoom includes a BAA and advanced encryption. It is highly reliable for telehealth consults and offers excellent screen-sharing resolution for reviewing PACS imaging with colleagues.
*   **Cisco Webex for Healthcare:** Known for enterprise-grade security, Webex integrates well with hospital hardware and EHR systems, making it a favorite for large teaching institutions.

### 3. File Sharing & Imaging Collaboration
For sharing research data, surgical videos, and anonymized (or secure) patient imaging.
*   **Box for Healthcare:** Box is highly secure, HIPAA-compliant, and uniquely features a **DICOM viewer integration**. This allows you and your team to view and collaborate on radiological imaging directly in the cloud without needing to download files.
*   **Microsoft OneDrive / SharePoint (Enterprise):** Best for collaborating on research papers, grant proposals, and administrative documents with your academic team. 

### 4. Project & Research Management
For managing clinical trials, retrospective studies, and resident administrative tasks.
*   **Smartsheet:** Offers a HIPAA-compliant enterprise tier. It is excellent for tracking clinical trial progress, managing research pipelines, and coordinating departmental schedules.
*   **Asana (Enterprise):** Also offers HIPAA compliance on its highest tier. It is highly visual and intuitive, making it great for keeping research assistants, residents, and co-authors on track with deadlines for publications.

### 3 Crucial Steps Before Adopting a Tool:
1.  **Consult Hospital IT/Compliance:** Even if a tool is HIPAA-compliant, your hospital *must* have a signed BAA with the vendor before you can share PHI. Your institution likely already has enterprise licenses for some of these tools (usually Microsoft Teams or Zoom).
2.  **Check EHR/PACS Integration:** The best tool is one that doesn't force you to duplicate work. Check if the tool integrates with your specific EHR (Epic, Cerner) and PACS (Visage, Sectra, etc.).
3.  **Prioritize Mobile Usability:** As a surgeon, you are rarely at a desk. Ensure whatever tool your team adopts has a robust, fast-loading mobile app. 

**Recommendation:** If you are looking for a single, unified platform to handle chat, video, and document collaboration for your research and clinical teams, **Microsoft Teams (Healthcare Edition)** is currently the most robust and widely supported option in major teaching hospitals.
