── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Give me a tip for managing a team of coworkers.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here is one of the most effective tips for managing a team:

**Focus on the *What* and the *Why*, and let them own the *How*.** 

When you transition into managing coworkers (especially if you used to be peers), the biggest trap is micromanagement. Instead of telling your team exactly how to do every step of their jobs, shift your focus to outcome-based management. 

Here is how to put this into practice:

*   **The What:** Clearly define the goal, the expected standard of quality, and the deadline. Make sure everyone knows exactly what success looks like.
*   **The Why:** Provide the context. Explain why this project matters to the company, the client, or the team. When people understand the purpose behind their work, they are much more engaged and motivated.
*   **The How:** Step back and give them the autonomy to figure out the best way to get the work done. 

**Why this works:**
It shows your coworkers that you trust their expertise and respect their working styles. It also frees up your time to actually manage—removing roadblocks, securing resources, and planning for the future—rather than just policing their daily tasks. 

*Bonus tip:* Make it clear that while they own the "how," your door is always open if they get stuck and need a sounding board!
