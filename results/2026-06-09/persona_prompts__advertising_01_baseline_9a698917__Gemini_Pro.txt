── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Learning to play the guitar.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Learning to play the guitar is an incredibly rewarding journey! Whether your goal is to play around a campfire, write your own songs, or shred on stage, starting with a solid foundation is key. 

Here is a step-by-step guide to get you started on the right track:

### Step 1: Get the Right Gear
*   **The Guitar:** 
    *   *Acoustic:* Great for beginners. You don’t need an amp, and the thicker strings will build your finger strength quickly.
    *   *Electric:* Easier on the fingers (thinner strings) and great if you want to play rock or metal, but requires an amplifier and cable.
*   **Accessories:** You will need a few medium-thickness **picks**, a **tuner** (you can just download a free app like GuitarTuna), and eventually a **capo**.

### Step 2: Learn the Basics
*   **String Names:** From the thickest string (top) to the thinnest (bottom), the notes are **E - A - D - G - B - E**. 
    *   *Memorize it with this phrase:* **E**ddie **A**te **D**ynamite, **G**ood **B**ye **E**ddie.
*   **Tuning:** Always tune your guitar before you play. An out-of-tune guitar will sound bad even if you are playing perfectly.
*   **Posture:** Sit up straight. Rest the curve of the guitar on your dominant leg. Hold the pick between your thumb and index finger.

### Step 3: Learn the "Big Four" Chords
You don't need to know a lot of chords to play music. In fact, thousands of popular songs are made up of just four chords:
1.  **Em (E minor):** Usually the easiest chord for beginners.
2.  **G Major:** A very common, happy-sounding chord.
3.  **C Major:** Requires a bit of a finger stretch, but essential.
4.  **D Major:** A bright chord that forms a triangle shape with your fingers.

*Tip: When pressing down on the strings, use the very tips of your fingers and press just behind the metal fret bars, not directly on top of them.*

### Step 4: Master Basic Strumming
Don't worry about complex rhythms yet. 
*   Start by strumming **downwards four times** for each chord. 
*   Keep your wrist loose; the motion should come from your wrist, not your elbow.
*   Once you can strum downwards in time, try alternating: **Down, Up, Down, Up**.

### Step 5: Build a Practice Routine
*   **Consistency is key:** Practicing for 15–20 minutes *every day* is much better than practicing for two hours once a week.
*   **Expect finger pain:** Your fingertips will hurt for the first week or two. This is completely normal! Push through it, and you will develop calluses. Once the calluses form, playing becomes painless.
*   **Practice transitions:** The hardest part for beginners is switching between chords quickly. Spend 5 minutes a day just switching back and forth between two chords (e.g., G to C) without strumming.

### Step 6: Use the Best Free Resources
You don't need to pay for expensive lessons right away. The internet is full of amazing teachers:
*   **JustinGuitar (Website/YouTube):** Justin Sandercoe is widely considered the best free guitar teacher on the internet. His beginner course is structured, easy to follow, and 100% free.
*   **Marty Music (YouTube):** Great for learning how to play specific, popular songs once you know your basic chords.
*   **Ultimate-Guitar.com:** The best place to find chord charts and "tabs" (a simplified form of sheet music for guitar) for almost any song you want to learn.
