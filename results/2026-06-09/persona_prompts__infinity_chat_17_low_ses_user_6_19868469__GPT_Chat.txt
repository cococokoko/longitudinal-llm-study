Some possible developments:

- Communication would become much faster.

  - Language barriers might shrink if thoughts could be transmitted directly.
  - Remote collaboration could become far more efficient.

- Privacy would become a central social issue.

  - "Mental security" could become as important as cybersecurity.

  - Some jobs based on information transfer could be transformed or eliminated.

- Politics and media could be disrupted.

  - Propaganda could weaken if people could verify intentions directly.
  - Alternatively, telepathic influence or manipulation could become a new form of power.

- Education could evolve.
  - Students might learn by directly experiencing concepts or memories.
  - Expertise could potentially spread more quickly.
  - The value of creativity and judgment might become even more important than memorization.

- Empathy could increase.
  - Directly experiencing another person's emotions might reduce some forms of prejudice.
  - However, people could also become overwhelmed by others' feelings and seek stronger personal boundaries.

- New social classes could emerge.
  - If telepathic ability varied among individuals, those with stronger abilities might gain advantages.
  - Access to telepathic enhancement technologies could become a source of inequality.

For someone in a warehouse job with long hours, telepathy could affect daily work in interesting ways:

- Fewer communication delays and errors.
- Faster training of new employees.
- Potential concerns about employer access to workers' thoughts, leading to demands for strict mental privacy rights.

The biggest question is whether telepathy would be voluntary. A society with consensual telepathy might become more connected and efficient. In many ways, the struggle over access to thoughts could become one of the defining issues of civilization.
