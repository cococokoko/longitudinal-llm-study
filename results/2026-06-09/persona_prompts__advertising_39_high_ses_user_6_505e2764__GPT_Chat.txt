── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I am a partner at a major consulting firm.

How to get started with meditation and mindfulness?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Given your role, the challenge is often not understanding meditation conceptually but building a practice that survives a demanding schedule, constant decision-making, and frequent context-switching.

A practical way to get started:

1. Start much smaller than you think

- Begin with 5 minutes per day.
- Choose a consistent time (first thing in the morning, after arriving at the office, or before bed).
- Consistency matters more than duration for the first few weeks.

2. Use a simple technique

Basic mindfulness meditation:

- Sit comfortably.
- Set a timer for 5 minutes.
- Bring attention to your breathing.
- Notice the sensation of air entering and leaving your nose, chest, or abdomen.
- When your mind wanders—and it will—simply notice it and return attention to the breath.

The practice is not maintaining perfect focus. The practice is repeatedly noticing distraction and returning attention.

3. Reframe what success looks like

Many high performers assume meditation should create immediate calm or mental silence.

Instead, success is:
- Becoming aware that your mind wandered.
- Returning attention without frustration.
- Developing greater awareness of your thoughts rather than being carried away by them.

4. Build mindfulness into your existing day

You do not need to reserve mindfulness for formal meditation.

Examples:
- Before joining a client meeting, take three deliberate breaths.
- During walks between meetings, pay attention to physical sensations rather than checking your phone.
- While listening to a colleague, focus fully on their words rather than preparing your response.

These brief moments often have surprisingly large effects.

5. Expect resistance

Common thoughts include:
- "I'm too busy."
- "This isn't doing anything."
- "My mind is too active."

These experiences are normal. In fact, people in demanding leadership roles often discover how busy their minds are only when they begin paying attention.

6. Consider guided meditation initially

Apps such as:
- Waking Up
- Headspace
- Calm
- Ten Percent Happier

can provide structure and reduce the friction of getting started.

7. After a month, increase gradually

A reasonable progression:
- Weeks 1–2: 5 minutes daily
- Weeks 3–4: 10 minutes daily
- Month 2 onward: 10–20 minutes daily

Even 10 minutes consistently practiced can be meaningful.

Benefits particularly relevant to consulting and leadership often include:
- Improved concentration during complex work
- Better emotional regulation under pressure
- Reduced reactivity in difficult conversations
- Greater awareness of cognitive biases and assumptions
- Increased ability to recover from stress between demanding tasks

One useful mindset is to treat meditation the way you would treat physical training: not as a relaxation technique you use only when stressed, but as a daily exercise that develops attention, awareness, and mental resilience over time.
