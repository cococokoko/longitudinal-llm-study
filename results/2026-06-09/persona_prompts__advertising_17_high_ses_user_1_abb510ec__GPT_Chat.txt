── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Improving home energy efficiency usually delivers the best results when you start with the largest sources of energy loss and consumption.

High-impact improvements:
- Get a professional home energy audit. This often identifies the most cost-effective upgrades for your specific house.
- Improve insulation:
  - Attic insulation is often one of the highest-return upgrades.
  - Insulate walls, crawl spaces, and basements where appropriate.
- Air sealing:
  - Seal gaps around doors, windows, electrical penetrations, plumbing penetrations, and attic hatches.
  - Weatherstrip exterior doors.
- Upgrade heating and cooling systems:
  - Replace older furnaces, boilers, or air conditioners with high-efficiency models.
  - Consider a heat pump if suitable for your climate.
  - Maintain HVAC equipment regularly and replace filters on schedule.
- Install a smart or programmable thermostat.

Windows and doors:
- If existing windows are in reasonable condition, air sealing and insulation may provide better value than full window replacement.
- For older, inefficient windows, consider double- or triple-pane low-emissivity (low-E) models.

Water heating:
- Lower water heater temperature if safe and practical.
- Insulate hot-water pipes.
- Upgrade to a heat-pump water heater when replacement is due.
- Install low-flow showerheads and fixtures.

Lighting and appliances:
- Replace remaining incandescent or halogen bulbs with LEDs.
- Choose ENERGY STAR–rated appliances when replacing older units.
- Unplug or manage devices that draw standby power, or use smart power strips.

Renewable energy:
- Consider rooftop solar if your roof, climate, utility rates, and local incentives make it financially attractive.
- Battery storage may be useful depending on electricity pricing and outage concerns.

Behavioral changes:
- Wash clothes with cold water when appropriate.
- Run full loads in dishwashers and washing machines.
- Use ceiling fans to improve comfort and reduce HVAC demand.
- Keep blinds or curtains closed during hot periods and open for passive solar heating in winter where beneficial.
