── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I am a partner at a major consulting firm.

How might society evolve if telepathy became possible?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This is a genuinely interesting question worth thinking through carefully rather than just enthusiastically.

## Likely Significant Disruptions

**Economic/Professional**
- Negotiation, sales, and legal advocacy would transform fundamentally - your industry included
- Information asymmetry, which drives enormous value creation, would largely collapse
- Authentication and verification industries become obsolete

**Political/Governance**
- Political deception becomes harder, but so does diplomatic discretion
- Privacy as a legal and social concept requires complete reconstruction
- Authoritarian control could actually *increase* if states control telepathic access

## Tensions Worth Taking Seriously

**The privacy paradox**: Most utopian telepathy narratives assume transparency creates trust. But humans currently *need* cognitive privacy to think through uncomfortable ideas before sharing them. Forced transparency might actually reduce honest thinking.

**Power asymmetry**: Who controls the technology matters enormously. Voluntary vs. involuntary access creates completely different societies.

**The authenticity problem**: Would relationships feel more genuine or would people develop new psychological defenses?

## Honest Uncertainties

- Whether humans could psychologically tolerate it
- Whether it would reduce conflict or simply make conflicts more visceral
- Whether consent frameworks could realistically function
