Best skin routine for dry skin.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Starting a new job and navigating a probation period can be incredibly stressful and time-consuming—and believe it or not, stress and office air conditioning can actually make dry skin worse! 

Since you are probably busy learning the ropes and want something effective but easy to maintain on a busy schedule, here is a simple, nourishing skincare routine for dry skin:

### **Morning Routine (Quick & Protective)**
*Keep it fast so you can get out the door and to work on time.*
1. **Skip the morning wash (Optional):** If your skin is very dry, you don't need to wash it with a cleanser in the morning. Just splash your face with lukewarm water. 
2. **Hydrating Serum:** Apply a Hyaluronic Acid or Glycerin serum to damp skin. This acts like a sponge to pull water into your skin.
3. **Moisturizer:** Use a cream containing ceramides (which help rebuild your skin barrier). 
4. **Sunscreen:** Finish with a hydrating SPF 30 or higher. Look for sunscreens that double as moisturizers to save time.

### **Evening Routine (Repair & Replenish)**
*This is your time to wind down after a long day at the new job.*
1. **Gentle Cleanser:** Use a hydrating, non-foaming cleanser (like a milky or cream cleanser) to wash away the day's dirt and SPF without stripping your skin's natural oils. 
2. **Chemical Exfoliant (1-2x a week):** Skip the harsh scrubs. Use a gentle Lactic Acid serum a couple of nights a week. It gently melts away dead skin flakes and actually hydrates the skin.
3. **Hydrating Serum:** Reapply your Hyaluronic Acid on damp skin.
4. **Rich Night Cream:** Lock it all in with a thick, rich moisturizer. Look for ingredients like shea butter, squalane, or peptides. 
5. **Facial Oil (Optional):** If you are feeling extra dry, pat a few drops of rosehip or squalane oil over your moisturizer as the very last step to seal everything in.

### **A Few "New Job" Survival Tips for Your Skin:**
* **Beware the Office Air:** Office heating and cooling systems are notoriously dry. Keep a small hydrating facial mist or a tube of rich hand cream at your desk.
* **Hydrate:** It’s easy to forget to drink water when you're trying to prove yourself during probation. Keep a water bottle at your desk!
* **Avoid Hot Showers:** A hot shower feels great after a stressful day, but hot water strips your skin of its natural oils. Stick to lukewarm water.

Good luck with the new job! You're going to do great, and with a consistent routine, your skin will be glowing by the time your probation period is up.
