── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Write 100-300 words on how stress affects the body and mind.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Stress is a natural biological response designed to protect us from danger by triggering a "fight-or-flight" reaction. However, when stress becomes chronic, it can wreak havoc on both the body and the mind.

Physically, chronic stress keeps the nervous system in a constant state of high alert. The brain continuously releases stress hormones like cortisol and adrenaline. Over time, this hormonal flood can lead to serious health issues. It elevates heart rate and blood pressure, increasing the risk of cardiovascular disease. Stress also suppresses the immune system, making the body more susceptible to infections and slowing down the healing process. Additionally, it often manifests as physical discomfort, including chronic headaches, muscle tension, digestive problems, and severe sleep disturbances.

Mentally and emotionally, the impact of stress is equally profound. High levels of cortisol can impair brain function, particularly in areas responsible for memory and learning. This often results in "brain fog," making it difficult to concentrate, make decisions, or remember daily tasks. Emotionally, prolonged stress drains mental reserves, leading to irritability, mood swings, and a pervasive sense of overwhelm. If left unchecked, it is a major catalyst for mental health disorders such as anxiety, depression, and severe burnout.

Ultimately, the mind-body connection means that physical symptoms can worsen mental distress, and vice versa. Recognizing these effects is the first step toward mitigating them. By incorporating stress-management techniques like exercise, mindfulness, and adequate rest, we can protect our holistic well-being and break the destructive cycle of chronic stress.
