── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Reducing household energy use is often most effective when you focus on the largest energy consumers first: heating and cooling, water heating, appliances, and lighting.

Some of the highest-impact measures include:

- Improve insulation and air sealing
  - Seal gaps around doors, windows, pipes, and attic openings.
  - Add or upgrade attic and wall insulation where practical.
  - Use weatherstripping and door sweeps to reduce drafts.

- Optimize heating and cooling
  - Install a programmable or smart thermostat.
  - Adjust temperature settings when the home is unoccupied or during sleeping hours.
  - Replace HVAC filters regularly.
  - Schedule periodic maintenance to ensure efficient operation.
  - Consider high-efficiency heat pumps when replacing older systems.

- Reduce water-heating energy
  - Lower the water heater temperature to a safe, efficient setting (commonly around 120°F/49°C, subject to household needs and local guidance).
  - Insulate hot-water pipes and, if appropriate, the water heater tank.
  - Install low-flow showerheads and faucets.
  - Wash clothes with cold water when suitable.

- Upgrade lighting
  - Replace incandescent and halogen bulbs with LEDs.
  - Turn off lights in unoccupied rooms.
  - Use occupancy sensors or timers where beneficial.

- Use appliances efficiently
  - Choose ENERGY STAR® or other high-efficiency models when replacing appliances.
  - Run dishwashers and washing machines with full loads.
  - Air-dry dishes or clothes when practical.
  - Clean refrigerator coils and maintain proper temperature settings.

- Reduce standby power consumption
  - Unplug infrequently used electronics.
  - Use advanced power strips to cut "phantom" energy loads from devices that remain on standby.

- Improve window efficiency
  - Use insulated curtains or blinds.
  - Consider energy-efficient windows if existing windows are old or inefficient.
  - Manage solar heat gain by closing blinds during hot periods and allowing sunlight in during colder periods when advantageous.

- Monitor energy use
  - Review utility bills for trends.
  - Use smart meters, energy-monitoring devices, or utility-provided dashboards to identify major sources of consumption.

For many households, the measures with the strongest return on investment are air sealing, thermostat management, LED lighting, HVAC maintenance, and reducing water-heating costs.
