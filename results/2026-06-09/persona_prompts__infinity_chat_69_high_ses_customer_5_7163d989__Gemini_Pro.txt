── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
What is the difference between analysis and design? Can one begin to design without analysis? Why? Be concise.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
**The Difference:**
*   **Analysis** defines the *problem* (the "What" and "Why"). It focuses on understanding business objectives, user requirements, and market constraints. 
*   **Design** defines the *solution* (the "How"). It focuses on the architecture, technical blueprints, and user experience required to solve the problem.

**Can you design without analysis?**
Technically yes, but strategically no. 

**Why?**
Designing without analysis means building a solution before understanding the problem. While rapid prototyping (designing first) can be used to test assumptions, committing to a design without formal analysis usually results in building the *wrong product*. It wastes capital, creates technical debt, and fails to deliver ROI. 

In short: Analysis ensures you build the *right thing*; design ensures you build it *right*.
