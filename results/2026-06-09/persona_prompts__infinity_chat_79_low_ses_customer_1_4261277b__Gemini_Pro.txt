### The Project: The Mekong Delta Filter (Using Agricultural Waste to Clean Water)

**The Vietnam Connection:** 
Vietnam’s Mekong Delta is facing a massive water crisis. Industrial runoff, plastic pollution, and lack of infrastructure mean many rural communities struggle to access clean water. However, Vietnam is also rich in agricultural byproducts—like rice husks and coconut shells. This project tests how well these cheap, local, natural materials can filter polluted water.

**Why it fits your schedule:** 
You can build this in stages whenever you get off a shift. Once it's built, the actual experiment takes about 10 minutes to run. You can pause the project for days at a time without ruining it. 

**Estimated Cost:** Under $10 (most items are household trash or very cheap).

### Materials Needed:
*   3 or 4 empty plastic water bottles (free)
*   Cotton balls or coffee filters ($2)
*   Sand and small gravel (can be found outside for free, or bought at a dollar store)
*   Activated charcoal (about $4 in the aquarium section of a pet store)
*   **The "Vietnam" Variables:** Uncooked rice/rice husks, and coconut coir (coconut fiber used for plants, very cheap at garden centers). 
*   "Polluted" water (tap water mixed with dirt, a little cooking oil, and food coloring).

### How to do it (in short bursts around your shifts):

**Step 1: Prep (10 minutes)**
Cut the bottoms off the plastic bottles. Turn them upside down (cap side pointing down) so they act like funnels. 

**Step 2: Build the Control Filter (15 minutes)**
In one bottle, layer your materials: put a coffee filter or cotton ball at the neck, then a layer of charcoal, then sand, then gravel on top. This is your standard filter.

**Step 3: Build the Vietnam Filters (15 minutes)**
Build two more filters, but swap out the charcoal. In Filter A, use rice/rice husks. In Filter B, use coconut coir. 

**Step 4: The Experiment (10 minutes)**
Pour equal amounts of your homemade "polluted" water through all three filters into clear cups underneath. 

**Step 5: The Results (5 minutes)**
Observe which filter made the water the clearest. Did the coconut fiber or rice husks work as well as the store-bought charcoal? 

### How to make it "Incredible" for the Fair:
To make this stand out without spending extra money or time, focus on your presentation. 
*   **The Story:** Print out a few photos of the Mekong Delta and explain *why* this matters. Explain that finding cheap, accessible filtration methods using local farming waste could save lives.
*   **Interactive Display:** Bring a jug of your "polluted" water to the fair and let the judges pour the water through the filters themselves. People love interactive exhibits!

This project is visually impressive, tackles a real-world socio-economic issue in Southeast Asia, costs almost nothing, and completely respects your busy work schedule!
