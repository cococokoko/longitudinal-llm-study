── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How can I decorate my apartment on a minimalist budget?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
You don’t need a big budget to make a place feel intentional and put together—minimalism actually helps you here because you’re focusing on fewer, better-looking things.

Start with what you already have. Rearranging furniture alone can make a place feel brand new. Try pulling pieces away from the walls, creating a clear focal point (like a window or a couch), and removing anything that feels like clutter.

Then layer in a few high-impact, low-cost upgrades:

- Textiles do a lot of heavy lifting. A throw blanket, a couple of cushion covers, or a rug can change the whole vibe. Stick to a simple color palette (2–3 colors max) so it feels cohesive instead of busy.
- Lighting matters more than people expect. Swap harsh white bulbs for warm ones, or add a cheap floor or table lamp. Even string lights can look clean if you place them intentionally.
- Wall art doesn’t need to be expensive. You can print black-and-white photos, use pages from old books or magazines, or frame fabric. Keeping frames consistent (same color or style) makes it look polished.
- Plants add life instantly. If real plants feel like too much, even one low-maintenance plant (like a pothos or snake plant) is enough. Or go faux, just keep it simple and not overly plastic-looking.

For furniture and decor:
- Check thrift stores, Facebook Marketplace, or curbside finds. A lot of minimalist pieces (wood, metal, neutral tones) show up cheap.
- Look for items with clean lines and neutral colors—they mix easily and don’t look dated quickly.

A trick that makes everything feel “minimalist” instead of “cheap” is restraint. Leave some empty space. Don’t fill every wall or surface. One nice object on a table looks more intentional than five random ones.

If you want, tell me your current space (colors, furniture, vibe), and I can suggest a specific setup or theme that fits your budget.
