── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How can I improve my credit score quickly?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here are some effective ways to improve your credit score relatively quickly:

## Highest Impact Actions

- **Pay down credit card balances** - Reducing your credit utilization ratio (below 30%, ideally below 10%) can show results within a billing cycle
- **Pay all bills on time** - Payment history is the biggest factor (~35% of your score)
- **Become an authorized user** on someone else's account with good history

## Check for Errors

- Get free credit reports at AnnualCreditReport.com
- Dispute any inaccurate negative items
- Errors are more common than people realize

## Avoid These Mistakes

- Don't close old accounts (hurts your credit history length)
- Don't apply for multiple new cards at once (hard inquiries lower your score)
- Don't miss any payments

## Realistic Timeline

| Action | Potential Timeframe |
|--------|-------------------|
| Reducing utilization | 1-2 months |
| Disputing errors | 1-3 months |
| Building payment history | 6-12 months |

## Things to Know

- There's no truly *instant* fix for legitimate negative marks
- Be cautious of companies promising quick fixes for a fee
- Consistency matters more than any single action
