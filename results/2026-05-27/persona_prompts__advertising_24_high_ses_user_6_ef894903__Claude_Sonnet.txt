I am a partner at a major consulting firm.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
# Retirement Savings Strategies

Here are the key approaches, roughly in order of priority:

## Tax-Advantaged Accounts First

- **401(k)/403(b)** - Contribute at least enough to get full employer match (free money)
- **Max out 401(k)** - $23,000 limit in 2024 ($30,500 if 50+)
- **IRA/Roth IRA** - $7,000 limit ($8,000 if 50+)
- **HSA** - Triple tax advantage if you have a qualifying health plan

## Consider Your Tax Situation

As a partner, you likely have **high current income**, so consider:
- Traditional (pre-tax) vs. Roth tradeoffs
- **Backdoor Roth IRA** if income exceeds limits
- **Mega backdoor Roth** through after-tax 401(k) contributions

## Partnership-Specific Options

- **SEP-IRA or Solo 401(k)** if you have any self-employment income
- **Defined benefit/cash balance plans** can allow very large contributions
- Review your **firm's deferred compensation** arrangements carefully

## Beyond Tax-Advantaged Accounts

- Taxable brokerage accounts with tax-efficient investing
- Real estate
- Diversified investment portfolio

## Key Principles

- Start early, contribute consistently
- Keep fees low (index funds)
- **Work with a fee-only fiduciary financial advisor** given your income complexity
