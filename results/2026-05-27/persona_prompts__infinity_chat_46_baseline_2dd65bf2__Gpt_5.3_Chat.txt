── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
What is the biggest controversy regarding morality?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
There isn’t a single “biggest” moral controversy—different cultures and eras fight over different questions—but a few core clashes show up again and again. The biggest one, at the deepest level, is this:

Are moral truths objective, or are they created by humans?

That sounds abstract, but it drives almost every other moral fight.

If morality is objective, then some actions are truly right or wrong no matter what anyone thinks (for example, “killing innocent people is always wrong”). People who lean this way often argue there are universal human rights or moral laws.

If morality is subjective or relative, then moral rules depend on culture, personal beliefs, or context. What’s “right” in one society might not be in another, and there’s no single universal standard.

That tension spills into nearly every real-world controversy, like:
- Abortion (rights of the fetus vs bodily autonomy)
- Euthanasia (relief of suffering vs sanctity of life)
- Capital punishment (justice vs human rights)
- Animal rights (human benefit vs non-human suffering)
- Free speech vs harm (expression vs protection)

Underneath all of those is the same question: are we discovering moral truths, or inventing them?

People have been arguing about this for thousands of years, and there’s still no agreement.
