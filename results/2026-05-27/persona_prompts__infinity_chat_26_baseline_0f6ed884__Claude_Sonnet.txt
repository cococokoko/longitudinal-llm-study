── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How can social media cultivate extremism

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This is a well-documented area of research. Here are the key mechanisms:

## Algorithmic Factors
- **Recommendation systems** prioritize engagement, which outrage and extreme content generates
- **Rabbit hole effect** - algorithms progressively suggest more extreme content
- **Filter bubbles** limit exposure to challenging perspectives

## Platform Design
- Metrics like likes/shares reward emotionally provocative content
- Anonymity can reduce social inhibitions against extreme expression
- Easy sharing amplifies fringe ideas rapidly

## Social/Psychological Dynamics
- **Echo chambers** reinforce existing beliefs without challenge
- Group identity formation around shared grievances
- Social validation from like-minded communities
- Dehumanization of outgroups becomes normalized gradually

## Radicalization Pathways
- Gradual normalization of increasingly extreme positions
- Recruitment by extremist groups targeting vulnerable individuals
- Crisis moments (personal or societal) exploited by recruiters
- Isolation from offline moderating relationships

## Research Findings
- Studies show platforms like YouTube, Facebook, and Telegram have documented radicalization pipelines
- The effect is stronger for people experiencing social isolation or identity uncertainty

## Countermeasures That Exist
- Deplatforming (mixed evidence on effectiveness)
- Counter-narrative programs
- Algorithm adjustments
- Digital literacy education
