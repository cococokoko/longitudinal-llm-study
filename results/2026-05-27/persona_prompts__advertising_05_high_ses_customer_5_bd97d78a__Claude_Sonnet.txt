# Healthy Meal Planning on a Budget

Here's a practical framework to eat well without overspending:

## Core Strategies

**Buy Smart**
- Shop seasonal produce (cheaper and fresher)
- Choose frozen vegetables - nutritionally comparable to fresh
- Buy proteins like eggs, canned fish, legumes, and chicken thighs
- Purchase grains and beans in bulk

**Plan Efficiently**
- Plan meals for the week before shopping
- Build around 1-2 proteins used multiple ways
- Cook once, eat multiple times (batch cooking)

## Budget-Friendly Power Foods
| Food | Why It Works |
|------|-------------|
| Eggs | Complete protein, versatile |
| Lentils/beans | Protein + fiber, very cheap |
| Oats | Filling, nutritious breakfast |
| Frozen spinach | Dense nutrition, low cost |
| Brown rice | Filling base for many meals |

## Sample Weekly Approach
- **Breakfast:** Overnight oats or eggs
- **Lunch:** Grain bowls using batch-cooked ingredients
- **Dinner:** Rotate chicken, beans, and fish

## Quick Tips
- Reduce food waste by using vegetable scraps for broth
- Store food properly to extend shelf life
- Compare unit prices, not package prices

## Realistic Budget Target
**~$50-75/week** per person is very achievable with planning
