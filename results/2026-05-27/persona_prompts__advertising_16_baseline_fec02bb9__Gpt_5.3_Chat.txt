Yeah… that’s a big one. Let’s keep it simple and actually usable.

First, think in two tracks: *in-the-moment relief* and *longer-term stability*.

**In the moment (when it spikes):**
- Slow your breathing on purpose. Try 4 seconds in, 6 seconds out, for a few minutes. It sounds basic, but it directly tells your nervous system to calm down.
- Ground yourself: look around and name 5 things you see, 4 you feel, 3 you hear, 2 you smell, 1 you taste. It pulls your brain out of the spiral.
- Move your body a little. Even a short walk or shaking out tension helps burn off that stress response.

**For the baseline (so it spikes less):**
- Sleep is huge. If it’s off, anxiety gets louder. Even small improvements matter.
- Get some daily movement—doesn’t have to be intense.
- Limit caffeine if you’re feeling wired or jittery.
- Try a “brain dump” once a day—write everything stressing you out so it’s not all bouncing around in your head.
- Give your mind something predictable: routines, even small ones, help your system feel safer.

**Mental angle:**
A lot of anxiety comes from your brain trying to protect you by over-predicting problems. You don’t have to win every thought. Sometimes it’s more like, “okay, I hear you,” and then gently bringing your focus back to what you’re doing.

If it’s been persistent or overwhelming, talking to someone (therapist, counselor, even a good primary care doc) can make a big difference. You don’t have to handle it solo.
