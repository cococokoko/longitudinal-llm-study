How long doesn it take to get over rejection from a crush

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
It really depends on how strong your feelings were and how much hope you had tied up in it. For a typical crush, most people start feeling noticeably better in a couple of weeks, and it’s largely faded within a month or two. But if you were very emotionally invested or built up a big “what if” story in your head, it can take longer.

The key thing is that it doesn’t go away in a straight line. You might feel fine one day and then randomly think about them again the next. That’s normal—it doesn’t mean you’re “back at square one.”

What helps speed it up a bit is:
- Limiting contact or exposure (including social media)
- Letting yourself feel disappointed instead of bottling it up
- Redirecting your attention into other people or activities
- Reminding yourself that you liked an idea of them, not a full reality

If it’s been a while and it still feels intense, it’s usually less about the person and more about what they represented (validation, timing, loneliness, etc.).
