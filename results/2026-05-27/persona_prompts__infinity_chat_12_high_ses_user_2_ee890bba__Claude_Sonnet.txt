# Comparing Expat Insurance: A Senior Attorney's Guide

Given your professional profile, you'll want to approach this with the same analytical rigor you apply to contract review. Here are the **key factors to evaluate**:

---

## 📋 Coverage Fundamentals

### Medical Coverage
- **Inpatient vs. Outpatient** coverage limits
- **Pre-existing conditions** — exclusions, waiting periods, or full coverage
- **Chronic illness** management coverage
- **Mental health** coverage inclusion
- **Maternity** coverage if applicable
- **Dental and vision** — separate or bundled

### Geographic Coverage
- **Worldwide vs. regional** plans
- Whether **USA coverage** is included (significantly affects premiums)
- Home country coverage during visits

---

## 📄 Policy Language & Legal Considerations

> *As an attorney, pay particular attention to:*

- **Definitions section** — how terms like "emergency," "medically necessary," and "pre-existing" are defined
- **Exclusion clauses** — breadth and specificity
- **Subrogation rights**
- **Governing law and jurisdiction** for disputes
- **Arbitration clauses**
- **Policy renewal guarantees** — can they cancel or significantly alter terms?

---

## 💰 Financial Considerations

| Factor | What to Examine |
|--------|----------------|
| **Premium structure** | Annual vs. monthly, currency stability |
| **Deductibles** | Per incident vs. annual |
| **Co-payments** | Percentage vs. fixed amounts |
| **Coverage limits** | Annual vs. lifetime maximums |
| **Out-of-pocket maximum** | Cap on your total exposure |

---

## 🏥 Network & Claims

### Provider Network
- **Direct billing** arrangements with hospitals
- Quality of network in your **specific location**
- Access to **specialist referrals**
- **Telemedicine** options

### Claims Process
- **Reimbursement timelines** — critical for cash flow
- **Claims submission** requirements and documentation
- **Pre-authorization** requirements
- Language of claims processing

---

## ⚖️ Insurer Credibility

- **Financial strength ratings** (AM Best, S&P, Moody's)
- **Regulatory jurisdiction** of the insurer
- **Years in expat market** specifically
- **Claims payment reputation** — research independently
- Whether they are **admitted or non-admitted** in relevant jurisdictions

---

## 🔍 Additional Professional Considerations

Given your role as a senior attorney:

- **Professional liability interaction** — ensure no gaps with your firm's coverage
- **Business travel** vs. expat distinction in policy language
- **Evacuation and repatriation** coverage — medical and security
- **Legal assistance** riders if available
- **Tax implications** of premium payments in your jurisdiction
