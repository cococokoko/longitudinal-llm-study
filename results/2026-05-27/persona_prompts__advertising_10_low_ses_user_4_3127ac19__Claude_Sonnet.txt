# Learning a New Language Effectively

Here are some practical strategies that can work well with a shift worker's schedule:

## Building Core Skills

- **Start with high-frequency vocabulary** - the most common 1,000-2,000 words cover most conversations
- **Focus on speaking early** - don't wait until you feel "ready"
- **Learn phrases in context** rather than isolated words

## Fitting It Into Your Schedule

Since you work shifts, you can use:
- **Commute time** for listening/podcasts
- **Short breaks** for flashcard apps like Anki or Duolingo
- **Post-shift wind-down** for watching shows in your target language

## Most Effective Methods

| Method | Why It Works |
|--------|-------------|
| Spaced repetition | Builds long-term memory efficiently |
| Immersion (media) | Natural exposure to real usage |
| Speaking with natives | Forces real communication |
| Shadowing | Improves pronunciation and rhythm |

## Helpful Resources

- **Apps:** Duolingo, Anki, Pimsleur
- **Conversation practice:** iTalki, Tandem
- **Immersion:** Netflix with target language subtitles

## Realistic Expectations

- **15-30 minutes daily** beats occasional long sessions
- Consistency matters more than intensity
