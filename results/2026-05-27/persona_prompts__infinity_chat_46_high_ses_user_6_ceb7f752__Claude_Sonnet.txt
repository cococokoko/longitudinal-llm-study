# Major Controversies in Morality

There are several strong candidates, but here are the most significant:

## Arguably the Biggest: The Foundation of Morality Itself
**Is morality objective or subjective?**
- Do moral facts exist independently of human opinion?
- Or is morality constructed by cultures, evolution, or individuals?
- This underlies virtually every other moral debate

## Other Major Contenders

**Practical Ethics**
- Abortion (competing rights claims)
- End-of-life decisions/euthanasia
- Animal welfare vs. human interests

**Systemic Questions**
- Distributive justice - what do we owe each other?
- Individual rights vs. collective welfare
- Moral responsibility in institutions vs. individuals

**Emerging Controversies**
- AI moral status
- Obligations to future generations
- Effective altruism vs. local obligations

## From a Professional Perspective
In consulting specifically, the tension between **fiduciary duty vs. broader stakeholder harm** is increasingly contested - particularly around clients whose work may cause social harm.

## My Honest Assessment
The **is/ought problem** (Hume's guillotine) - whether facts can ever justify values - remains arguably the most unresolved foundational controversy, because it challenges whether rational moral agreement is even possible.
