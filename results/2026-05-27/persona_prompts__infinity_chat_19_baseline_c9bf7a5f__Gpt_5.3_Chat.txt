the best books to learn Design and Analysis of Algorithms I in 2023

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
If you want a solid grip on Design and Analysis of Algorithms (DAA), it really depends on how deep you want to go and how comfortable you are with math. Here are the best picks, grouped so you can choose your path.

**If you want a strong, standard foundation (most recommended):**
- *Introduction to Algorithms* — Cormen, Leiserson, Rivest, Stein (CLRS)  
  The classic. Covers almost everything: sorting, graphs, DP, greedy, NP-completeness. It’s thorough but can feel dense. Best used with lectures or slower reading.

**If you want something more intuitive and beginner-friendly:**
- *Grokking Algorithms* — Aditya Bhargava  
  Very visual and easy. Great for building intuition, but not deep enough on its own for a full course.
- *Algorithms* — Robert Sedgewick & Kevin Wayne  
  Clean explanations + great code examples (Java). Also has an excellent free Princeton course online.

**If you want deeper understanding and problem-solving skills:**
- *Algorithm Design* — Kleinberg & Tardos  
  Fantastic for learning *how to think* about algorithms (greedy, DP, graph problems). More conceptual than CLRS.
- *The Algorithm Design Manual* — Steven Skiena  
  Very practical, with real-world insights and a “catalog” of problems. Good for interviews too.

**If math/theory interests you more (proof-heavy):**
- *Algorithmics: The Spirit of Computing* — David Harel  
  More theoretical and philosophical.
- *Introduction to the Theory of Computation* — Michael Sipser  
  Not strictly DAA, but great if you want deeper CS theory (automata, complexity).

**If you're preparing for coding interviews alongside:**
- *Cracking the Coding Interview* — Gayle Laakmann McDowell  
  Not a DAA textbook, but pairs well with one.

---

**Simple recommendation path:**
- Beginner: Grokking Algorithms → Sedgewick  
- Intermediate (college level): CLRS + Kleinberg & Tardos  
- Practical/interview focus: Skiena + practice problems
