# Best Practices for Organic Gardening

## Soil Health
- **Compost regularly** - add finished compost to improve structure and fertility
- **Test your soil** periodically to understand pH and nutrient levels
- **Avoid tilling** excessively, which disrupts beneficial soil organisms
- **Use cover crops** like clover or rye to prevent erosion and add nutrients
- **Mulch generously** to retain moisture and suppress weeds

## Fertilizing Naturally
- Compost, worm castings, and aged manure
- Fish emulsion or kelp meal for quick nutrient boosts
- Bone meal (phosphorus) and blood meal (nitrogen)
- Green manures turned into soil

## Pest & Disease Management
- **Rotate crops** each season to break pest cycles
- **Companion planting** (basil with tomatoes, marigolds as borders)
- Encourage beneficial insects like ladybugs and lacewings
- Use row covers for physical protection
- Neem oil or insecticidal soap for outbreaks

## Water Management
- Water deeply and less frequently to encourage deep roots
- Use drip irrigation to reduce disease and waste
- Water in the morning

## General Tips
- Choose disease-resistant varieties
- Start with healthy transplants or quality seeds
- Observe your garden regularly
- Keep records of what works
