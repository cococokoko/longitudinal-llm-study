How to make my own natural beauty products?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Making your own natural beauty products is actually pretty fun once you get the hang of a few basics. You don’t need a huge setup—just some simple ingredients, clean tools, and a bit of patience.

First, a few ground rules:
- Keep everything clean (jars, spoons, hands) to avoid bacteria.
- Start with small batches so you don’t waste ingredients if something doesn’t work.
- Use simple, gentle ingredients at first—your skin will thank you.
- Patch test anything new on your skin.

Here are a few easy beginner recipes:

Face oil (great for moisturizing)
- 2 tablespoons jojoba oil (balances skin)
- 1 tablespoon sweet almond oil (softens)
- 2–3 drops lavender essential oil (optional)

Mix and store in a small dropper bottle. Use a few drops on damp skin.

Simple face mask
- 1 tablespoon honey (antibacterial, soothing)
- 1 tablespoon plain yogurt (gentle exfoliation)

Mix, apply for 10–15 minutes, rinse off.

Sugar scrub (body or lips)
- 2 tablespoons sugar
- 1 tablespoon coconut oil
- Optional: a drop of vanilla or essential oil

Gently massage onto damp skin, then rinse.

Basic lip balm
- 1 tablespoon beeswax
- 1 tablespoon coconut oil
- 1 tablespoon shea butter

Melt gently (double boiler or microwave in short bursts), mix, pour into small containers, let it solidify.

A few tips as you go:
- Essential oils are potent—use very small amounts and skip them if you have sensitive skin.
- Natural doesn’t always mean non-irritating (like lemon juice or baking soda—be careful with those).
- Products without preservatives spoil faster. Store in a cool place and use within a few weeks.

If you want, I can help you build a routine or suggest recipes based on your skin type (oily, dry, acne-prone, etc.).
