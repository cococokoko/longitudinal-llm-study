── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
What is the biggest controversy regarding morality?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
There are several major controversies in moral philosophy. Here are some of the most significant:

## Foundational Debates

- **Moral realism vs. anti-realism** - whether objective moral facts actually exist
- **Where morality comes from** - reason, emotion, evolution, religion, social contract
- **Moral relativism** - whether morality is universal or culturally determined

## Practical Controversies

- **Abortion** - competing rights and personhood questions
- **Capital punishment** - justice vs. state killing
- **Animal rights** - moral status of non-human beings
- **Euthanasia** - autonomy vs. sanctity of life
- **War and violence** - when if ever they're justified

## Theoretical Tensions

- **Consequentialism vs. deontology** - do ends justify means, or are some acts inherently wrong?
- **Individual rights vs. collective welfare**
- **Moral luck** - whether people deserve blame for circumstances partly outside their control

## Perhaps the Deepest Controversy

Many philosophers consider the **is-ought problem** (Hume's guillotine) - whether you can derive moral obligations from factual statements - to be the most fundamental unresolved question.

What aspect interests you most? The debate can go in many directions depending on whether you're thinking about **practical ethics** or **moral philosophy** more abstractly.
