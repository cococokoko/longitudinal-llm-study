How do I start a vegetable garden in my backyard?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Starting a vegetable garden is a great project! Here are the key steps:

## Planning
- **Choose a location** with at least 6-8 hours of sunlight daily
- **Decide on size** - start small (4x4 or 4x8 feet) if you're a beginner
- **Pick your vegetables** - start with easy ones like tomatoes, lettuce, zucchini, or beans

## Preparing the Ground
- **Clear the area** of grass and weeds
- **Test your soil** - many garden centers offer simple test kits
- **Amend the soil** with compost to improve drainage and nutrients
- **Consider raised beds** if your soil quality is poor

## Getting Started
- **Check your last frost date** for your region before planting
- **Start seeds indoors** 6-8 weeks before transplanting, or buy starter plants
- **Follow spacing guidelines** on seed packets

## Ongoing Care
- **Water consistently** - most vegetables need about 1 inch per week
- **Mulch around plants** to retain moisture and reduce weeds
- **Fertilize** as needed based on your soil test
- **Watch for pests** and address them early

## Good Beginner Vegetables
- Tomatoes, lettuce, radishes, green beans, cucumbers, herbs
