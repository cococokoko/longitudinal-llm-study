# Safe Travel During a Pandemic for Single Parents

Here are practical, evidence-based options to consider:

## Lowest Risk Options
- **Driving your own vehicle** - minimizes contact with others
- **Staying local** - reduces exposure significantly
- **Virtual alternatives** - video calls instead of travel when possible

## If You Must Travel

### Before You Go
- Check current health guidelines for your destination
- Ensure everyone is up to date on relevant vaccinations
- Pack a health kit (masks, hand sanitizer, thermometer)
- Have a backup childcare plan if someone gets sick

### During Travel
- **Wear well-fitted masks** in crowded spaces
- Choose **less busy travel times**
- Maintain distance from others when possible
- Wash hands frequently
- Avoid touching your face

### Accommodation
- Consider options with kitchen facilities to reduce restaurant exposure
- Private rentals often safer than shared hotel common areas

## Practical Single Parent Considerations
- **Travel insurance** is especially important
- Know the nearest medical facilities at your destination
- Have emergency contacts readily available
- Keep children's health records accessible

## Key Questions to Ask Yourself
- Is this trip essential?
- What's my plan if a child gets sick away from home?
- Do I have support if I get sick?
