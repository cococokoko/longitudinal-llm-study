── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How can I improve my credit score quickly?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
If you want the fastest possible bump, focus on the factors that update frequently and carry the most weight. Here’s what actually moves the needle in the short term:

**1. Get your credit utilization down (biggest, fastest impact)**
This is the percentage of your credit limit you’re using.
- Aim for under 30%, ideally under 10%.
- Pay down credit card balances before the statement closing date (not just the due date).
- If you can’t pay everything, spread balances across cards instead of maxing one out.

**2. Pay everything on time (no exceptions)**
- Even one late payment can hurt a lot.
- If you already missed one recently, get current ASAP—damage lessens over time.
- Set autopay for at least the minimum so you never miss again.

**3. Ask for a credit limit increase**
- This lowers your utilization without needing to pay anything down.
- Do this only if it’s a soft pull (ask your issuer first).
- Don’t increase spending afterward.

**4. Don’t open or close accounts randomly**
- Opening new accounts can cause a small temporary drop.
- Closing old cards can hurt your utilization and average account age.

**5. Become an authorized user (fast shortcut if available)**
- If someone with a long, clean credit history adds you to a card, it can boost your score quickly.
- The account must have low utilization and perfect payment history.

**6. Dispute any errors on your report**
- Check all three bureaus (Experian, Equifax, TransUnion).
- If there’s a mistake, dispute it—removals can cause a quick jump.

**7. Pay off small collections (with strategy)**
- Some newer scoring models ignore paid collections.
- Try “pay for delete” if possible (not guaranteed, but worth asking).

**What won’t help quickly**
- Carrying a balance (that’s a myth—it doesn’t help)
- Paying loans off early (good financially, but not a quick score boost)
- Credit repair “hacks” that sound too good to be true
