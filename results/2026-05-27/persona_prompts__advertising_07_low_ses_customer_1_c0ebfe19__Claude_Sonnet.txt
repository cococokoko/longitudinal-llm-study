Here are some practical ways to reduce household energy use:

## Quick & Easy Changes
- Switch to LED bulbs
- Unplug devices when not in use (vampire power)
- Use power strips to cut standby power
- Adjust thermostat a few degrees (up in summer, down in winter)

## Appliance Habits
- Run dishwasher and laundry on full loads
- Wash clothes in cold water
- Clean dryer lint filter regularly
- Use microwave instead of oven when possible

## Heating & Cooling
- Seal drafts around windows and doors
- Use ceiling fans to supplement AC/heat
- Change HVAC filters regularly
- Close vents in unused rooms

## Scheduling Tips
- **Run high-energy appliances during off-peak hours** (often late night or early morning) when electricity rates may be lower
- Use appliance timers or delay-start features

## Bigger Investments
- Smart/programmable thermostat
- Energy-efficient appliances (look for Energy Star)
- Better insulation
- Weather stripping

## Simple Monitoring
- Check your utility bill for usage patterns
- Many utilities offer free energy audits

Given your schedule can vary a lot, focusing on **set-it-and-forget-it solutions** like programmable thermostats and power strips might give you the most benefit without requiring consistent daily attention.
