── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How can I reduce my carbon footprint with daily habits?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
You don’t need a perfect, all-or-nothing lifestyle to shrink your footprint—small, consistent habits add up fast. Here are practical, high-impact places to start:

**Food**
- Eat more plant-based meals; even swapping a few meat meals per week (especially beef/lamb) makes a big difference.
- Cut food waste: plan meals, store food well, freeze leftovers.
- Choose seasonal and minimally packaged foods when you can.

**Transport**
- Walk, bike, or use public transit for short trips.
- Combine errands into one trip; avoid frequent short car rides.
- If you drive, keep tires inflated and drive smoothly.
- Fly less; for unavoidable flights, choose nonstop and economy (lower emissions per passenger).

**Home energy**
- Turn off lights/electronics when not in use; use smart power strips.
- Switch to LED bulbs.
- Set your thermostat a bit lower in winter and higher in summer; use fans and layers.
- Wash clothes in cold water; air-dry when possible.
- If available, opt into a renewable electricity plan.

**Shopping & waste**
- Buy fewer, longer-lasting items; repair before replacing.
- Choose secondhand for clothes and furniture.
- Carry a reusable bottle, mug, and bags.
- Recycle correctly and compost if your area supports it.

**Water**
- Take shorter showers; fix leaks.
- Use low-flow fixtures if you can.

**Digital habits (small but easy wins)**
- Stream on Wi‑Fi instead of mobile data; download instead of re-streaming.
- Keep devices longer; skip unnecessary upgrades.

If you want, tell me your current routine (commute, diet, home setup), and I can suggest the biggest wins tailored to you.
