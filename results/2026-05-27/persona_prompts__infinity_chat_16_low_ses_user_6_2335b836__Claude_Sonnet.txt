── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I work in a warehouse position that requires long hours.

What's wrong with capitalism? State in-depth reasons why capitalism is inefficient and harmful.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here are some substantive criticisms that economists, sociologists and philosophers have raised:

## Economic Inefficiencies

- **Market failures** - public goods, externalities, and information asymmetries aren't handled well by markets alone
- **Boom-bust cycles** - inherent tendency toward periodic recessions and financial crises
- **Monopoly formation** - successful competition often *eliminates* competition over time
- **Short-termism** - quarterly profit pressure discourages long-term investment

## Distributional Problems

- Wealth concentration tends to increase over time (Piketty's research documents this)
- Bargaining power imbalances between employers and workers
- Returns to capital historically outpace wage growth

## Relevant to Your Situation Specifically

- Warehouse work productivity gains often don't translate to worker wages
- Scheduling and safety pressures when profit margins are prioritized
- Physical toll on workers while shareholders bear no physical risk

## Legitimate Counterarguments Worth Knowing

- Capitalism has dramatically reduced global poverty
- Alternative systems have their own serious documented failures
- Mixed economies address many criticisms while maintaining markets

## Honest Assessment

These are **real, documented problems** that serious economists across the political spectrum acknowledge, even when they disagree on solutions.

What specific aspect concerns you most? That might help focus the discussion.
