# Effective Online Teaching Techniques

## Engagement & Interaction
- **Break content into shorter segments** (10-15 minute chunks work better than long lectures)
- **Use polls and quizzes** during sessions to check understanding
- **Encourage chat participation** as an alternative to speaking
- **Cold calling with notice** - give students time to think before responding
- **Breakout rooms** for small group discussion

## Clear Communication
- Establish clear expectations upfront (response times, participation norms)
- Use multiple formats - video, text, audio - for different learning styles
- Provide written summaries of key points
- Be explicit about deadlines and navigation

## Building Community
- Create informal spaces for student interaction
- Use icebreakers and introductions
- Acknowledge students by name
- Show your own personality and presence

## Assessment & Feedback
- Frequent low-stakes assessments rather than few high-stakes ones
- Timely, specific feedback
- Peer review activities
- Self-reflection prompts

## Technical Practices
- Record sessions when possible
- Test technology before class
- Have a backup plan for technical failures
- Keep accessibility in mind (captions, readable fonts)

## What Research Suggests Works Best
- **Active learning** consistently outperforms passive watching
- **Regular presence** matters more than production quality
- **Responsiveness** strongly affects student satisfaction
