How to plan an environmentally friendly wedding?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Planning a genuinely low-impact wedding is mostly about cutting waste, minimizing travel, and being intentional about sourcing. You don’t have to make it austere—just efficient.

Start with the big levers:
- Guest count and travel: this is the dominant footprint. A smaller, more local guest list reduces emissions more than any other choice. If many guests must travel, choose a venue near major transit and provide shuttles.
- Venue: pick a place that already looks good. Botanical gardens, historic buildings, or restaurants need less décor and often have better energy practices. Ask about renewable energy, waste sorting, and water use.

Food and drink matter more than décor:
- Go plant-forward or fully vegetarian. This alone can cut catering emissions dramatically.
- Choose seasonal, local menus. Skip flown-in specialties.
- Avoid single-use anything—no disposable plates, cups, or mini plastic bottles.
- Work with caterers who donate leftovers or plan portions tightly.

Attire:
- Rent, buy secondhand, or choose something re-wearable. Tailoring an existing garment is often lower impact than buying new “eco” fashion.
- Encourage the wedding party to wear outfits they already own within a color palette.

Rings and jewelry:
- Vintage or recycled metals and stones are the simplest low-impact option.
- If new, look for certified recycled gold or lab-grown stones from reputable suppliers.

Invitations and communication:
- Digital invites are the lowest impact. If you want physical, use recycled paper and minimal inserts.
- Wedding websites handle RSVPs, schedules, and maps without paper clutter.

Flowers and décor:
- Seasonal, locally grown flowers or potted plants guests can take home.
- Rent décor items instead of buying. Avoid foam (it’s microplastic-heavy).
- Candles over electric lighting if the venue allows.

Waste planning:
- Ask the venue about composting and recycling streams.
- Set up clearly labeled bins; designate someone (or hire staff) to manage waste so it actually gets sorted.
- Avoid favors that become trash; if you want one, make it edible or useful.

Transportation:
- Provide group transport between hotel and venue.
- Consider offsets for unavoidable travel, but treat them as a last step after reducing emissions.

Vendors:
- Choose local vendors to reduce transport.
- Ask simple questions: how do you handle waste, sourcing, and energy? You’ll quickly see who’s thoughtful versus performative.

Registry:
- Experiences, cash funds, or contributions to a cause you care about keep physical goods to a minimum.
