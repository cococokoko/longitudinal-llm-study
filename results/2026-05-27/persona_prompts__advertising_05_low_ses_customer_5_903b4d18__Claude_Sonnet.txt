# Healthy Meal Planning on a Budget

Here are practical strategies that work well for larger households with multiple responsibilities:

## Core Strategies

**Plan Before You Shop**
- Plan meals for the full week before buying anything
- Check what you already have first
- Build meals around sales and seasonal produce

**Buy Smart**
- Dried beans, lentils, and rice are incredibly affordable and nutritious
- Frozen vegetables are just as healthy as fresh and last longer
- Buy larger cuts of meat and portion them yourself
- Store brands are usually identical quality to name brands

## Budget-Friendly Protein Sources
- Eggs
- Canned tuna/sardines
- Dried or canned beans
- Chicken thighs (cheaper than breasts)
- Peanut butter

## Time-Saving Tips for Busy Households
- **Batch cook on weekends** - make large pots of grains, beans, soups
- **One pot meals** stretch ingredients further
- **Repurpose leftovers** - roast chicken becomes soup, tacos, sandwiches
- Cook **double portions** and freeze half

## Realistic Weekly Budget Approach
- Start with 3-4 flexible base ingredients
- Build multiple meals from the same ingredients
- Keep a running list of what everyone will actually eat

## Quick Wins
- Oatmeal for breakfast saves significantly over cereals
- Soups and stews feed many people affordably
- Meatless meals 2-3 times weekly cuts costs dramatically
