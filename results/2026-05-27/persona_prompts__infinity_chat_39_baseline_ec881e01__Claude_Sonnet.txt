# 13. Time-Saving Tools and Apps

---

## The Core Idea

The right tool disappears into your workflow. The wrong one becomes another thing to manage. Choose less. Choose well.

---

## Categories That Actually Matter

---

### Task Management

**What it solves:** Thoughts living rent-free in your head.

**Tools worth knowing:**

- **Todoist** — Clean interface. Natural language input. Type "call dentist Friday 3pm" and it schedules itself. Works across every device without friction.
- **Things 3** (Apple only) — Beautifully minimal. No clutter. Captures tasks fast, organizes them quietly in the background.
- **Notion** — More powerful, more setup required. Worth it only if you need a second brain, not just a to-do list.

**Practical example:**
> You're in a meeting. Someone mentions a deliverable. You type it into Todoist in eight seconds. It's gone from your mind. You stay present.

**Rule:** One task manager. Not three.

---

### Calendar and Scheduling

**What it solves:** The back-and-forth of finding meeting times.

**Tools worth knowing:**

- **Calendly** — Share a link. People pick a time. No emails. No negotiation. Syncs with Google or Outlook automatically.
- **Google Calendar** — Still the standard. Color-code by life area. Block focus time like it's a meeting, because it is.
- **Fantastical** — Natural language scheduling. Type "lunch with Sarah next Tuesday noon" and it builds the event.

**Practical example:**
> A client asks to schedule a call. Instead of five emails, you send one Calendly link. They book. You both move on.

**Time saved per week:** Roughly 45 minutes of scheduling friction eliminated.

---

### Communication

**What it solves:** Email as a default for everything.

**Tools worth knowing:**

- **Slack** — Replaces internal email threads. Channels keep conversations organized by topic, not by person. Use it with discipline or it becomes noise.
- **Loom** — Record a short video instead of writing a long explanation. A two-minute Loom often replaces a thirty-minute meeting.
- **Superhuman / Hey** — Email clients that make inbox management faster. Keyboard shortcuts. Triage systems. Less time staring at a full inbox.

**Practical example:**
> Instead of scheduling a meeting to explain a complex process, you record a four-minute Loom walkthrough. Your colleague watches it when it suits them. Questions drop by half.

---

### Automation

**What it solves:** Repetitive tasks you keep doing manually.

**Tools worth knowing:**

- **Zapier** — Connects apps without code. When X happens in one app, Y happens in another automatically.
- **Make (formerly Integromat)** — More complex automations. Better for multi-step workflows.
- **Apple Shortcuts / Android Automation** — Device-level automation. No internet required.

**Practical examples:**

| Trigger | Automatic Action |
|---|---|
| New form submission | Creates task in Todoist + sends confirmation email |
| Invoice marked paid | Moves client to "active" folder + logs in spreadsheet |
| New podcast episode published | Posts to social media automatically |
| Email labeled "receipt" | Saves attachment to Google Drive folder |

**The mindset:** If you do something more than three times, ask whether a machine can do it instead.

---

### Focus and Deep Work

**What it solves:** Distraction disguised as productivity.

**Tools worth knowing:**

- **Freedom** — Blocks websites and apps across all devices simultaneously. Schedule blocks in advance so willpower isn't required in the moment.
- **Forest** — Plant a virtual tree. It grows while you work. It dies if you pick up your phone. Simple. Surprisingly effective.
- **Brain.fm / Focus@Will** — Music engineered for concentration. Not background music. Functional music designed to reduce mind-wandering.
- **Cold Turkey** — More aggressive than Freedom. Harder to override. Good for people who override Freedom.

**Practical example:**
> You have two hours to write a report. You set Freedom to block social media and news sites. You open Brain.fm. You start. The environment does the discipline work for you.
