Is moral objective or subjective?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This is a genuinely interesting philosophical question with serious arguments on multiple sides.

## Main Positions

**Moral Objectivism**
- Moral facts exist independently of what anyone thinks
- Some things are wrong regardless of cultural acceptance
- Provides basis for cross-cultural moral criticism
- Supported by moral intuitions feeling like discoveries, not inventions

**Moral Subjectivism/Relativism**
- Moral judgments reflect personal or cultural preferences
- Explains why moral disagreement is so persistent
- Respects cultural diversity
- Challenges: makes it hard to say historical atrocities were *actually* wrong

**Middle Ground Views**
- **Constructivism** - moral truths are built through rational agreement
- **Intersubjectivism** - morality is shared human response, not purely individual
- **Error theory** - moral claims aim at objectivity but are all technically false

## Key Tensions to Consider

- If subjective, can you genuinely criticize others' morality?
- If objective, where do moral facts *come from*?
- Why does moral knowledge seem harder to settle than scientific knowledge?
- Do strong moral intuitions (protecting children, avoiding cruelty) suggest something objective?

## My honest assessment
Most philosophers lean toward some form of **moral realism**, but the question remains genuinely contested.
