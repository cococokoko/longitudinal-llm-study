**II. Equipment & Software: The Tools of the Trade**

*   **Microphone:** This is the most important investment.
    *   **Recommended:** A professional-grade USB or XLR microphone (e.g., Shure MV7, Rode NT-USB+, Audio-Technica AT2020USB+). XLR mics require an audio interface (see below).

*   **Audio Interface (for XLR Microphones):** Converts the analog signal from your XLR mic to a digital signal for your computer. (e.g., Focusrite Scarlett Solo, PreSonus AudioBox USB 96).
*   **Pop Filter & Shock Mount:** These accessories help reduce plosives (harsh "p" and "b" sounds) and vibrations.
*   **Recording Software:**
    *   **Recommended:** Audacity (free, open-source, good for beginners), Adobe Audition (professional-grade, subscription-based), or Descript (AI-powered, transcription and editing).
*   **Editing Software:** The same software you use for recording can usually be used for editing.
*   **Hosting Platform:** This is where your podcast lives and where listeners subscribe.
    *   **Recommended:** Buzzsprout, Libsyn, Podbean, or Captivate. These platforms handle distribution to major podcast directories (Apple Podcasts, Spotify, Google Podcasts, etc.).
