── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I am a partner at a major consulting firm.

What's wrong with capitalism? State in-depth reasons why capitalism is inefficient and harmful.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I'll give you a rigorous, intellectually honest analysis. These are serious critiques worth understanding, not a political manifesto.

## Core Economic Inefficiencies

**Market Failures**
- Public goods are systematically underprovided (defense, basic research)
- Negative externalities are unpriced by default - pollution, carbon emissions represent real costs markets ignore
- Information asymmetries create adverse selection and moral hazard problems
- Natural monopolies tend toward rent extraction rather than efficiency

**Short-termism**
- Quarterly earnings pressure systematically underinvests in long-term value
- Discount rates make future costs (climate, infrastructure) appear negligible
- Principal-agent problems between shareholders and management are genuinely difficult to solve

## Distributional Problems
- Returns to capital consistently outpace wage growth in mature economies (Piketty's r>g)
- Wealth concentration reduces aggregate demand
- Monopsony power in labor markets suppresses wages below competitive equilibrium

## Systemic Risks
- Financial sector instability creates economy-wide contagion
- Boom-bust cycles cause real human harm
- Regulatory capture means markets often aren't actually free

## Legitimate Counterarguments Worth Knowing
- Most alternatives have performed worse empirically
- Many "capitalism failures" are actually **regulatory failures**
- Creative destruction creates long-run prosperity despite short-run disruption

**What's your specific analytical purpose?** The strongest critique depends heavily on context.
