── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
What are the best tools for remote team collaboration?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
It depends a bit on how your team works (async vs real-time, size, technical vs non-technical), but here’s a solid, modern stack that covers most needs without overcomplicating things:

**Communication**
- Slack: still the go-to for team chat, channels, integrations, and quick updates  
- Microsoft Teams: better if you’re already in the Microsoft ecosystem  
- Discord: surprisingly good for smaller or more casual teams  

**Video + Meetings**
- Zoom: most reliable for external calls and large meetings  
- Google Meet: simple and works well inside Google Workspace  

**Project & Task Management**
- Asana: great balance of structure and flexibility  
- ClickUp: very powerful, almost an “all-in-one” workspace (can feel heavy)  
- Trello: simple, visual kanban boards  
- Linear or Jira: better for engineering-focused teams  

**Docs & Knowledge Sharing**
- Notion: extremely flexible (docs, wikis, light project tracking)  
- Confluence: more structured, especially with Jira  
- Google Docs/Drive: still the easiest for real-time collaboration  

**File Sharing**
- Google Drive: easiest for most teams  
- Dropbox: strong syncing and reliability  
- OneDrive: best if using Microsoft 365  

**Whiteboarding / Brainstorming**
- Miro: top choice for collaborative whiteboards  
- FigJam: great if your team uses Figma  

**Async Video & Updates**
- Loom: quick video updates instead of meetings  
- Claap or Vimeo Record: similar async communication tools  

**All-in-one combos (if you want fewer tools)**
- Notion + Slack + Google Drive → popular lightweight stack  
- Microsoft Teams + OneDrive + Planner → all-in Microsoft stack  
- ClickUp (docs + tasks) + Slack → more consolidated approach  

If you tell me your team size and what kind of work you do (engineering, marketing, etc.), I can narrow this down to a much tighter setup so you’re not juggling too many tools.
