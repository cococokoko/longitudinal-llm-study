── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
what I should look for when I Compare Expat Insurance?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Good question—expat insurance can look similar on the surface but vary a lot where it actually matters. Here’s what to focus on when comparing plans:

**1. Coverage scope (what’s actually included)**
Make sure it covers inpatient, outpatient, emergency care, prescriptions, maternity (if relevant), and mental health. Some cheaper plans only cover hospitalization.

**2. Geographic coverage**
Check where you’re covered: worldwide, worldwide excluding the U.S., or only specific countries. This has a big impact on price and flexibility.

**3. Policy limits**
Look at both the annual maximum (total payout per year) and limits on specific categories (e.g., surgery, scans, maternity). A high overall limit doesn’t help if key items are capped low.

**4. Deductibles, co-pays, and cost sharing**
Lower premiums often mean higher out-of-pocket costs. Understand:
- Deductible (what you pay before coverage starts)
- Co-pay (fixed fee per visit)
- Co-insurance (percentage you pay)

**5. Pre-existing conditions**
This is a big one. Check:
- Are they excluded permanently?
- Covered after a waiting period?
- Covered with a loading (higher premium)?

**6. Network and direct billing**
Does the insurer have hospitals/doctors near you? Direct billing (they pay the provider directly) makes life much easier than paying upfront and claiming later.

**7. Claims process**
How easy is it to submit claims? App-based? Fast reimbursement? This matters more than people expect.

**8. Emergency evacuation and repatriation**
Critical if you’re in countries with limited healthcare infrastructure. Make sure it includes medical evacuation to a suitable facility.

**9. Maternity and family coverage**
If relevant, check waiting periods (often 10–12 months) and newborn coverage.

**10. Flexibility and renewability**
Can you keep the policy long-term? Are premiums likely to jump with age or claims? Is it guaranteed renewable?

**11. Customer support**
24/7 support, English-speaking assistance, and local help can be a lifesaver in emergencies.

**12. Price vs value**
The cheapest plan is often the most restrictive. Balance cost with what you’d realistically need in a bad situation.
