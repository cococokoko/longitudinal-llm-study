# Starting to Learn Coding from Scratch

Great question! Here's a practical roadmap:

## Choose Your First Language
Start with something beginner-friendly:
- **Python** - most recommended for beginners (readable, versatile)
- **JavaScript** - good if you're interested in web development
- **Scratch** - visual option if you're very new to logic/programming concepts

## Free Learning Resources
- **freeCodeCamp** - structured, project-based
- **The Odin Project** - comprehensive web dev curriculum
- **CS50** (Harvard, free on edX) - excellent fundamentals
- **Codecademy** - interactive, beginner-friendly
- **YouTube** - channels like Traversy Media, Corey Schafer

## Key Principles for Success

**Be consistent over intense**
- 30 minutes daily beats 5 hours on weekends

**Actually build things**
- Don't just follow tutorials - create small projects

**Get comfortable being stuck**
- Struggling is normal and part of learning

**Use Google freely**
- Even experienced developers search constantly

## Realistic Expectations
- Basic competency: **3-6 months** of consistent practice
- Job-ready skills: typically **1-2 years**
- It gets easier once fundamentals click

## Suggested First Steps
1. Pick Python or JavaScript
2. Complete one structured beginner course
3. Build a small project you actually care about

What are you hoping to build or do with coding? That might help narrow down the best starting point for you.
