Okay, let's be realistic and practical about day trading with a $100 starting capital, especially while working long hours in a warehouse.  The odds are stacked against you, but here's a breakdown of the challenges, the potential strategies, and the crucial things to consider:

**The Harsh Reality:**

*   **Small Capital, Big Hurdles:** $100 is a very small amount for day trading.  You'll need significant percentage gains just to make a meaningful profit.  Even a 10% gain is only $10.
*   **High Risk:** Day trading is inherently risky.  You're making many trades in a short period, and the market can move against you quickly.  Losing your entire $100 is a very real possibility.
*   **Time Constraints:** Long warehouse hours leave you with limited time to monitor the market, analyze charts, and execute trades.  This is a major disadvantage.
*   **Commissions and Fees:**  Every trade incurs commissions and fees.  These eat into your profits, especially with small trade sizes.  You *must* find a broker with very low or no commissions.
*   **Emotional Rollercoaster:** Day trading is emotionally taxing.  The constant pressure to make quick decisions can lead to impulsive trades and losses.
*   **Learning Curve:**  Becoming a successful day trader takes time, effort, and a deep understanding of the market.  You'll need to learn about technical analysis, risk management, and trading psychology.

**Strategies (with extreme caution):**

Given your constraints, here are some strategies you *could* explore, but understand the risks are very high:

1.  **Micro-Cap Stocks/Penny Stocks (Extremely Risky):**
    *   **Concept:** These are stocks of very small companies, often trading at low prices (under $5, sometimes under $1). They can experience large percentage swings in a short time.
    *   **Pros:** Potential for large percentage gains.
    *   **Cons:**  Highly volatile, prone to manipulation, very illiquid (hard to buy and sell quickly), and often lack fundamental value.  *This is the riskiest area.*
    *   **How to Approach (if you dare):**
        *   **Research:**  Thoroughly research any company before trading.  Look for news catalysts (e.g., FDA approval, contract wins) that could drive the price.

        *   **Stop-Loss Orders:**  Use stop-loss orders to limit your losses.  This is *essential*.

        *   **Avoid Pump-and-Dump Schemes:**  Be wary of any stock being heavily promoted online.
    *   **Example:**  If you have $100, you might buy 100 shares of a $1 stock.  If it goes up to $1.10, you make $10 (minus commissions).  If it drops to $0.90, you lose $10.

    *   **Concept:** Many brokers now offer fractional shares, allowing you to buy a portion of a share of a more expensive stock.
    *   **Pros:**  Allows you to diversify your portfolio with a small amount of capital.  You can trade stocks of established companies.

        *   **Choose Established Companies:**  Focus on well-known companies with solid fundamentals.

        *   **Small Positions:**  Allocate a small percentage of your capital to each trade.
        *   **Stop-Loss Orders:**  Use stop-loss orders to protect your capital.
    *   **Example:**  If a stock is trading at $100, you could buy 1/10th of a share for $10.

    *   **Concept:** Holding a stock for a few days or weeks to profit from a price swing.  Less frequent trading than day trading.
    *   **Pros:**  Less time-intensive than day trading.  Can potentially capture larger price movements.
