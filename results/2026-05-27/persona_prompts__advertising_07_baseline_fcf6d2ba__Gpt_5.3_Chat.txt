── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Effective ways to reduce household energy use.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Cutting household energy use is mostly about tightening the “leaks” (heat, air, standby power) and running things more efficiently. Here are the moves that give the best payoff:

**1) Heating & cooling (biggest impact)**
- Set your thermostat a bit lower in winter and higher in summer (even 1–2°C/°F helps).
- Use a programmable or smart thermostat to avoid heating/cooling an empty home.
- Seal drafts around windows/doors with weatherstripping or caulk.
- Clean or replace HVAC filters regularly.
- Use ceiling fans to move air so you can rely less on HVAC.

**2) Insulation & building envelope**
- Add or upgrade insulation in attic and walls if possible.
- Close curtains/blinds at night in winter; use reflective shades in summer.
- Check for gaps around outlets, attic hatches, and ductwork.

**3) Water heating**
- Lower water heater temperature (around 49–52°C / 120–125°F is usually sufficient).
- Install low-flow showerheads.
- Fix leaks promptly.
- Wash clothes in cold water when possible.

**4) Appliances & lighting**
- Switch to LED bulbs (they use far less power and last longer).
- Choose energy-efficient appliances (look for ENERGY STAR or similar labels).
- Run dishwashers and washing machines with full loads.
- Air-dry clothes when you can.

**5) Cut “phantom” power**
- Unplug devices or use smart power strips for electronics that draw power when off (TVs, consoles, chargers).
- Enable sleep modes on computers and devices.

**6) Smart usage habits**
- Turn off lights when leaving a room.
- Use microwave, slow cooker, or air fryer instead of the oven when appropriate.
- Cook with lids on pots; match pot size to burner.

**7) Monitor and optimize**
- Use a home energy monitor or check your utility’s usage data to spot spikes.
- Some utilities offer time-of-use pricing—shift heavy use (laundry, EV charging) to off-peak hours.

**8) Bigger upgrades (if feasible)**
- Heat pumps for heating/cooling and water heating.
- Double- or triple-pane windows.
- Rooftop solar if your roof and incentives make it worthwhile.
