── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Ways to save on travel.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Travel doesn’t have to be expensive—you just need to be a bit strategic. Here are some solid ways to cut costs without ruining the experience:

**Be flexible with timing**
Flights and hotels swing a lot in price. Traveling midweek, during shoulder seasons (just before or after peak), or at off-hours can save a surprising amount.

**Use flight comparison tools**
Google Flights, Skyscanner, and similar sites let you track prices and spot cheaper dates. Setting alerts helps you jump on drops.

**Pack light**
Budget airlines charge for bags. A carry-on can save you both money and time.

**Mix and match airlines**
Sometimes booking two one-way tickets on different airlines is cheaper than a round-trip.

**Consider alternative airports**
Flying into or out of a nearby city can be significantly cheaper, even after factoring in ground transport.

**Stay outside tourist centers**
Hotels in the middle of everything cost more. A short train or bus ride can cut nightly costs a lot.

**Try alternative accommodations**
Hostels, guesthouses, Airbnb, or even house-sitting can be much cheaper than hotels.

**Use public transport**
Skip taxis when possible. Trains, buses, and metro systems are usually far cheaper and often faster in cities.

**Eat like a local**
Avoid restaurants right next to major attractions. Street food and smaller local spots are usually better and cheaper.

**Look for free activities**
Many cities have free walking tours, museums with free days, parks, and cultural events.

**Bundle or use points**
Travel credit cards, airline miles, and hotel loyalty programs can shave off big chunks of cost if used wisely.

**Book smart—not always early**
Flights are often cheapest 1–3 months ahead for domestic trips and a bit longer for international. Booking too early or too late can cost more.
