── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I work in a warehouse position that requires long hours.

What are the best ways to save for retirement?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here are some practical retirement saving strategies that work well for warehouse workers:

## Take Advantage of Employer Benefits First
- **401(k) with employer match** - contribute at least enough to get the full match (it's free money)
- Check if your employer offers a **pension plan**
- Look into **union benefits** if you're in a union

## Individual Retirement Accounts (IRAs)
- **Traditional IRA** - contributions may be tax-deductible now
- **Roth IRA** - tax-free withdrawals in retirement
- 2024 contribution limit is **$7,000/year** ($8,000 if over 50)

## Practical Tips for Your Situation
- **Start early** - even small amounts compound significantly over time
- **Automate contributions** so you don't have to think about it
- Build an **emergency fund first** so you don't raid retirement savings
- Consider the **physical demands** of your job when planning retirement age

## Things Worth Considering
- Warehouse work can be hard on the body, so you may want to **retire earlier** than average
- Look into **disability insurance** as protection
- Social Security benefits increase if you **delay claiming** past 62

## Getting Help
- Many employers offer **free financial counseling**
- A **fee-only financial advisor** can give personalized advice
