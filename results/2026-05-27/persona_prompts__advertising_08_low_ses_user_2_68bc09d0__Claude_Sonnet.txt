# Finding Hiking Trails as a Single Parent

Here are some practical ways to find great trails that work for your situation:

## Trail-Finding Resources
- **AllTrails app** - filter by difficulty, length, and kid-friendly ratings
- **hikingproject.com** - detailed trail information
- **Local parks & recreation websites** - often list family-friendly options
- **Facebook parent groups** - real recommendations from local families

## What to Look For as a Single Parent
- **Clear, well-marked trails** so you can focus on your child
- **Loop trails** rather than out-and-back (easier to manage)
- **Restroom facilities** at trailheads
- **Cell service** for safety
- **Shorter distances** that match your child's age and energy

## Practical Tips
- **Go during off-peak hours** when trails are less crowded
- **Start small** and build up difficulty as you both gain confidence
- **Bring snacks** - they make everything better
- **Tell someone your plans** before heading out

## Making It Easier
- Consider **hiking groups for families** in your area
- **Baby carriers/backpacks** for younger children
- Pack a **small first aid kit**

## Safety First
- Download trail maps **offline** in case you lose signal
