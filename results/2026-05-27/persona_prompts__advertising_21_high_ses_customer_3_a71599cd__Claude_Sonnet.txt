── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How do I start a vegetable garden in my backyard?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Starting a vegetable garden is a rewarding project! Here are the key steps:

## Planning
- **Choose a location** with at least 6-8 hours of direct sunlight daily
- **Start small** - a 4x8 or 10x10 foot bed is manageable for beginners
- **Decide what to grow** based on what your family actually eats

## Preparing the Bed
- **Clear the area** of grass and weeds
- **Test your soil** - many cooperative extensions offer inexpensive testing
- **Amend the soil** with compost to improve drainage and nutrients
- Consider **raised beds** if your native soil is poor or compacted

## Getting Started
- **Check your growing zone** (USDA hardiness zone) to know timing
- **Start with easy crops** like tomatoes, zucchini, lettuce, beans, or radishes
- Decide whether to start from **seed or transplants** (transplants are easier for beginners)

## Ongoing Care
- Water consistently - most vegetables need about 1 inch per week
- Mulch to retain moisture and suppress weeds
- Watch for pests and address them early

## Helpful Resources
- Your local **cooperative extension service** offers region-specific advice
- **"Square Foot Gardening"** by Mel Bartholomew is a popular beginner guide
