── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How can I make my home more energy efficient?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Great question—there’s a lot you can do, and you don’t have to tackle everything at once. The biggest gains usually come from reducing heat loss, tightening up air leaks, and improving how efficiently your systems run.

Start with the “low effort, high impact” stuff:
- Switch to LED bulbs everywhere. They use a fraction of the energy and last much longer.
- Seal air leaks around windows, doors, and outlets with weatherstripping or caulk. Drafts quietly waste a lot of energy.
- Use a programmable or smart thermostat so you’re not heating or cooling an empty home.
- Wash clothes in cold water and air-dry when you can.

Then look at insulation and structure:
- Make sure your attic is well insulated—this is often the biggest heat loss area.
- If you can, upgrade wall insulation or add insulation to basements/crawl spaces.
- Double- or triple-pane windows help, but even simpler fixes like thermal curtains can make a noticeable difference.

Appliances and systems:
- Upgrade to energy-efficient appliances (look for ENERGY STAR ratings).
- Maintain your HVAC system—clean filters regularly and service it annually.
- If your water heater is old, consider a heat pump water heater—it’s much more efficient.

Bigger upgrades (more upfront cost, bigger payoff):
- Install a heat pump for heating and cooling if your climate and budget allow.
- Add solar panels if your roof and local incentives make it worthwhile.
- Consider a home energy audit—they’ll pinpoint exactly where your home is losing energy so you don’t guess.

Small habit shifts also help: turn things off when not in use, unplug “phantom load” electronics, and be mindful of heating/cooling settings.

If you want, tell me a bit about your home (size, climate, what kind of heating/cooling you use), and I can suggest the highest-impact upgrades for your specific situation.
