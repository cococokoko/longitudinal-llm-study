# Learning Guitar - Practical Tips for a Busy Schedule

Given your full-time fast food job with an unpredictable schedule, here's how to make guitar learning work for you:

## Starting Out
- **Get a decent beginner guitar** - acoustic or electric, around $100-200 is fine to start
- **Learn basic chords first** - G, C, D, Em, Am cover hundreds of songs
- **Use free resources** - YouTube (Justin Guitar is excellent), Ultimate Guitar app

## Fitting Practice Into Your Schedule
- **Short sessions work** - even 10-15 minutes daily beats a 2-hour weekend session
- **Practice during breaks** if you can bring a travel/mini guitar
- **No set schedule needed** - play whenever you have energy after shifts
- **Finger exercises** can be done almost anywhere

## Realistic Expectations
- Fingertips will be sore at first - this passes in a few weeks
- Progress may feel slow but **consistency matters more than duration**
- Don't stress if you miss days due to shift changes

## Budget-Friendly Approaches
- **Free apps**: Yousician, Justin Guitar, Fender Play (free trial)
- Buy used guitars on Facebook Marketplace or Craigslist
- YouTube has everything you need for free

## Quick Wins
- Learn 3-4 chords and you can play many popular songs almost immediately

**What kind of music do you want to play?** That can help focus your learning!
