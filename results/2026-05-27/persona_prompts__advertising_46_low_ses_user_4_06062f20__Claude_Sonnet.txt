── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here's a helpful overview of good resources for starting a plant-based diet:

## Books
- **"How Not to Die" by Dr. Michael Greger** - evidence-based nutrition info
- **"The China Study" by T. Colin Campbell** - research-focused
- **"Forks Over Knives"** - practical and beginner-friendly

## Websites & Apps
- **NutritionFacts.org** - free, science-based information
- **Forks Over Knives website** - recipes and meal plans
- **Cronometer app** - tracks nutrients to make sure you're meeting needs

## Practical Starting Tips
- Focus on **whole foods** like legumes, grains, vegetables, fruits, nuts
- Watch key nutrients: **B12, iron, calcium, omega-3s, vitamin D**
- Start gradually rather than switching all at once

## Helpful Communities
- Reddit communities like r/PlantBasedDiet
- Local community groups or cooking classes

## Professional Guidance
- Consider consulting a **registered dietitian** who specializes in plant-based nutrition
- This is especially worth considering given your healthcare work and physical demands

## Free Resources
- Many hospitals and health systems now have plant-based nutrition programs worth checking
