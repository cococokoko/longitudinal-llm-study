What are the best apps for tracking fitness and nutrition?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
It kind of depends on how deep you want to go and what keeps you consistent, but here are some of the strongest options right now, broken down by style:

If you want an all-in-one tracker (fitness + nutrition):
- MyFitnessPal — still the most popular. Huge food database, easy logging, decent workout tracking. Best if you just want something that works without thinking too hard.
- Cronometer — more precise than MyFitnessPal. Tracks micronutrients (vitamins, minerals), not just calories. Great if you care about accuracy or specific diets.
- Lifesum — more lifestyle-focused, with meal plans and a cleaner interface. Less “data-heavy,” more guided.

If your main focus is nutrition:
- MacroFactor — excellent if you like data but don’t want to guess calories. It adjusts your calorie targets based on your actual progress. Very smart, very effective.
- Lose It! — simpler and more user-friendly than MyFitnessPal. Good for weight loss without overwhelm.
- Yazio — nice design, solid meal planning features, especially popular in Europe.

If your main focus is workouts:
- Strong — great for weightlifting. Simple, fast logging, good progress tracking.
- Hevy — similar to Strong but more modern and social features.
- Nike Training Club — free guided workouts, good for variety and beginners/intermediate users.

If you want habit + health tracking beyond just fitness:
- Apple Fitness + Apple Health (if you’re on iPhone) — integrates everything: steps, workouts, sleep, calories.
- Google Fit (Android equivalent)
- WHOOP or Oura (with their apps) — more advanced recovery, sleep, and strain tracking if you’re into wearables.

If you want a “coach-like” experience:
- Noom — psychology-based weight loss coaching (more about behavior change than raw tracking).
- Future — real human coach connected to your workouts (expensive, but very hands-on).

Quick take:
- Best overall: MyFitnessPal or MacroFactor  
- Best for accuracy: Cronometer  
- Best for lifting: Strong or Hevy  
- Best for simplicity: Lose It!
