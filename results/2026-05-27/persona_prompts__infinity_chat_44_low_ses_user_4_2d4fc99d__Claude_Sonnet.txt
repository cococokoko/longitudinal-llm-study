Can you write me a series of three questions that can determine if something is morally right or wrong?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here are three questions that can help evaluate if something is morally right or wrong, particularly useful in a healthcare setting:

---

**1. Does it respect the person's dignity and autonomy?**
Consider whether the action treats the patient as a person worthy of respect, and whether it honors their right to make informed decisions about their own care.

**2. Would I be comfortable if others could see exactly what I am doing and why?**
This transparency test asks whether your action and your reasoning behind it could withstand open scrutiny from colleagues, supervisors, patients, and their families.

**3. Does it cause unnecessary harm or does it genuinely serve the patient's wellbeing?**
Consider whether the action minimizes harm and whether your primary motivation is the patient's best interest rather than convenience or other factors.

---

These three questions roughly align with core principles commonly used in healthcare ethics:

- **Autonomy** - respecting patient rights
- **Accountability** - acting with integrity
- **Beneficence/Non-maleficence** - doing good and avoiding harm
