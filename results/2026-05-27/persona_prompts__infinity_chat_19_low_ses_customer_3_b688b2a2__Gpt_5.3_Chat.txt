  Very well-structured, pairs nicely with their online course. Clear explanations and practical focus.

If you want a solid university-level core (Algorithms I standard):
- “Introduction to Algorithms” (CLRS) – Cormen, Leiserson, Rivest, Stein  
  The classic. Comprehensive and respected everywhere. It’s dense, so don’t try to read it cover-to-cover—use it as a reference and go topic by topic.

- “Algorithm Design” – Kleinberg & Tardos  
  A bit more readable than CLRS and very good at explaining *why* algorithms work. Great for understanding design techniques (greedy, divide & conquer, DP).

If you prefer a more practical / coding-oriented angle:
- “The Algorithm Design Manual” – Steven Skiena  
  Very approachable, full of real-world context and problem-solving guidance. Good balance between theory and practice.

- “Competitive Programming” – Halim & Halim  
  More problem-focused. Best if you want to get good at coding problems alongside learning concepts.
