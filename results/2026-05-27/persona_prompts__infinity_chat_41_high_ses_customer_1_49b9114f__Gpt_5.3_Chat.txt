Nice, that’s a great goal—Japanese is super rewarding but can feel like a maze at first. The key is not trying to do everything at once.

Here’s a simple way to get started without getting overwhelmed:

First, learn the two phonetic alphabets:
- Hiragana (used for native words and grammar)
- Katakana (used for foreign words)

You can realistically learn both in 2–3 weeks with daily practice. Apps like Kana drills or even simple flashcards work well.

At the same time, pick up a few basic phrases so it feels immediately useful:
- こんにちは (konnichiwa) – hello  
- ありがとうございます (arigatou gozaimasu) – thank you  
- すみません (sumimasen) – excuse me / sorry  
- はい / いいえ – yes / no  

Then start very light grammar:
Japanese sentence structure is usually Subject–Object–Verb:
“I eat sushi” → 私は寿司を食べます (watashi wa sushi o tabemasu)

After that, begin kanji gradually. Don’t try to brute-force thousands—start with the most common ~100 and build up. Spaced repetition (like Anki) is extremely effective here.

Since you’re in a high-demand field, keep it efficient:
- 15–20 min daily: kana or kanji (flashcards)
- 10–15 min: listening (short clips, beginner podcasts)
- Optional: 5–10 min speaking or shadowing
