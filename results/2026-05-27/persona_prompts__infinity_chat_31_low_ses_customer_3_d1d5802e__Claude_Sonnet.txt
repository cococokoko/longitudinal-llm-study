── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here are some common paths people take to become company executives:

## Education
- Business degrees (MBA is common)
- Specialized degrees relevant to their industry
- Continuous professional development

## Career Progression
- **Starting in entry-level roles** and working up through departments
- **Demonstrating consistent results** and taking on increasing responsibility
- Moving through management levels (team lead → manager → director → VP → C-suite)

## Key Factors
- **Performance** - consistently delivering strong results
- **Leadership skills** - managing teams effectively
- **Networking** - building relationships within and outside the company
- **Visibility** - getting noticed by senior leadership
- **Mentorship** - having sponsors who advocate for them

## Other Routes
- **Entrepreneurship** - founding their own company
- **Specialist expertise** - becoming the go-to person in a field
- **Lateral moves** - switching companies for senior roles
- **Acquisitions** - leadership roles following mergers

## Important Qualities Often Cited
- Strategic thinking
- Communication skills
- Adaptability
- Decision-making under pressure
- Emotional intelligence

## Worth Noting
- It typically takes **many years** of experience
- There's often an element of timing and opportunity
- Some industries promote faster than others
