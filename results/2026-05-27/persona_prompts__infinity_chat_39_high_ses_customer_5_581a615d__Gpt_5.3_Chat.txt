13. Time-Saving Tools and Apps

The goal isn’t more tools. It’s fewer, better ones that remove friction and reduce decisions.

Core principle  
Choose tools that automate routine work, centralize information, and reduce context switching. If a tool adds complexity, it fails.

Scheduling  
- Calendly or Motion: eliminate back-and-forth. Let others book within guardrails.  
- Example: set executive office hours; auto-route external requests to specific slots.

Communication  
- Slack with strict channel discipline or Teams with defined use cases.  
- Superhuman or Outlook rules: triage email into “now, later, never.”  
- Example: auto-label newsletters and batch-read once weekly.

Task and priority management  
- Todoist, Things, or Microsoft To Do for lightweight capture.  
- Asana, Linear, or Jira for team-level execution.  
- Example: one personal list, one team system. No duplication.

Notes and knowledge  
- Notion, Obsidian, or OneNote: single source of truth.  
- Use templates for recurring docs (1:1s, project briefs).  
- Example: every meeting gets a pre-filled note with agenda and decisions.

Automation  
- Zapier or Make: connect apps without engineering effort.  
- Native automations (Google Workspace, Microsoft Power Automate).  
- Example: form submission → auto-create task → notify owner → set deadline.

Meetings  
- Clockwise or Reclaim: protect focus time automatically.  
- Otter or Fireflies: transcribe and summarize.  
- Example: default 25/50-minute meetings; auto-record and extract action items.

Documents and signatures  
- Google Docs or Office 365 for real-time collaboration.  
- DocuSign or Dropbox Sign for approvals.  
- Example: standard contract templates with pre-approved clauses.

File management  
- Google Drive, OneDrive, or Dropbox with strict naming conventions.  
- Example: YYYY-MM-DD format; one folder per project; archive quarterly.

Password and security  
- 1Password or Bitwarden: eliminate reset cycles and shared passwords.  
- Example: shared vaults for teams; remove ad hoc credential sharing.

Focus and distraction control  
- Freedom or Cold Turkey: block time-wasting sites.  
- Built-in screen time limits on devices.  
- Example: block social apps during deep work windows.

AI assistance  
- ChatGPT, Copilot, or Gemini for drafting, summarizing, and analysis.  
- Example: turn a 10-page brief into a one-page decision memo in minutes.

Travel and expenses  
- TripIt for itineraries; Expensify or Ramp for automated expense capture.  
- Example: forward confirmations → auto-built itinerary; receipt scan → auto-categorized.

Principles for adoption  
- Default to one tool per function.  
- Automate first, delegate second, do manually last.  
- Review quarterly; remove what you don’t use.  
- Measure success by time reclaimed, not features added.
