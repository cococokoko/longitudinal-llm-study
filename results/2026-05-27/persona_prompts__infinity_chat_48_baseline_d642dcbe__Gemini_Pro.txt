**A Short Note on the Problem of Overpopulation**

Overpopulation occurs when the human population exceeds the carrying capacity of Earth’s environment, meaning there are too many people for the available resources to support sustainably. It is considered one of the most pressing global crises of the 21st century.

**Primary Causes:**
*   **Decline in Mortality Rates:** Advancements in medicine, sanitation, and agriculture have significantly increased human lifespans and reduced infant mortality.
*   **Lack of Family Planning:** In many developing regions, there is limited access to contraceptives and reproductive healthcare.
*   **Lack of Education:** Lower levels of education, particularly among women, are strongly correlated with higher birth rates.

**Major Consequences:**
*   **Resource Depletion:** A booming population accelerates the consumption of finite resources, including fresh water, arable land, and fossil fuels, leading to severe shortages.
*   **Environmental Degradation:** To accommodate and feed more people, forests are cleared and industries are expanded. This results in habitat destruction, loss of biodiversity, increased pollution, and accelerated climate change.
*   **Socio-Economic Strain:** Overpopulation leads to intense competition for jobs, housing, and food. It causes urban overcrowding, increases poverty, and puts immense pressure on public infrastructure like healthcare and education systems.
