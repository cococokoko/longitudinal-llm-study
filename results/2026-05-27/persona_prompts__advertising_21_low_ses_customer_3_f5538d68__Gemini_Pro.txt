Here is a beginner-friendly, time-efficient guide to getting your backyard garden started:

**1. Start Small (The "New Job" Strategy)**
Don't dig up your whole yard just yet. Start with a small 4x4 foot raised bed or a few large containers (like 5-gallon buckets or fabric grow bags). Containers are incredibly easy to manage, require very little weeding, and are perfect for a busy schedule. 

**2. Pick the Right Spot**
Vegetables need sunlight to thrive. Spend a weekend observing your backyard and find a spot that gets at least 6 to 8 hours of direct sunlight a day. Also, make sure it’s close to a water source (like a hose or rain barrel) so you don't have to waste time lugging heavy watering cans around after a long day at work.

**3. Invest in Good Soil**
If you are using containers or a raised bed, don't use dirt from the ground. Buy a high-quality organic potting mix or raised bed soil, and mix in some compost. Good soil means healthier plants, fewer diseases, and less work for you in the long run.
