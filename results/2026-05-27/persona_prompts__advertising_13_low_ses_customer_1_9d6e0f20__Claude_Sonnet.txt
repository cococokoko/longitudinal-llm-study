# Mastering the Art of Cooking Steak

Here's a practical guide that works around a busy, unpredictable schedule.

---

## Choosing Your Cut
| Cut | Best For | Price Range |
|-----|----------|-------------|
| Ribeye | Rich flavor, forgiving | $$$ |
| Strip (NY) | Balance of flavor/lean | $$ |
| Sirloin | Budget-friendly | $ |
| Chuck Eye | "Poor man's ribeye" | $ |

---

## The Core Method (Pan Sear)
**Works great even with limited time**

### What You Need
- Cast iron or heavy pan
- Salt, pepper, butter
- Tongs

### Steps
1. **Season generously** with salt and pepper
2. **Pat completely dry** - this is critical for a good crust
3. **Ripping hot pan** - heat until smoking
4. **Sear 2-3 minutes per side** without moving
5. **Add butter + garlic** at the end, baste constantly
6. **Rest 5 minutes** before cutting

---

## Temperature Guide
- Rare: 125°F
- Medium Rare: 130-135°F *(most recommended)*
- Medium: 140-145°F
- Well Done: 160°F+

---

## Quick Tips for Your Situation
- **Batch prep** on days off - season steaks ahead
- **Frozen steaks** cook surprisingly well from frozen
- Cheap cuts respond well to **marinades overnight**
