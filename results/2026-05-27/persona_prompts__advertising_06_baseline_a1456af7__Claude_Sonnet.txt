# Mastering Digital Photography

## Core Fundamentals

### The Exposure Triangle
| Element | Controls | Effect |
|---------|----------|--------|
| **Aperture** (f-stop) | Light & depth of field | f/1.8 = blurry background; f/16 = sharp throughout |
| **Shutter Speed** | Motion & light | 1/1000s = freeze action; 1/30s = motion blur |
| **ISO** | Sensor sensitivity | Low ISO = clean; High ISO = grainy/noisy |

---

## Key Concepts to Master

### 📷 Aperture
- **Wide aperture** (f/1.4–f/2.8) → portraits, low light
- **Narrow aperture** (f/8–f/16) → landscapes, architecture
- Controls **bokeh** (background blur)

### ⏱ Shutter Speed
- **Fast** (1/500s+) → sports, wildlife, children
- **Slow** (1/30s and below) → light trails, waterfalls, stars
- Use a **tripod** for anything below 1/60s

### 🔆 ISO
- Start at **ISO 100–400** outdoors
- Push to **ISO 1600–6400** indoors or at night
- Modern cameras handle high ISO surprisingly well

---

## Composition Rules

- **Rule of Thirds** – place subjects off-center
- **Leading Lines** – draw the eye into the frame
- **Framing** – use natural frames (doorways, trees)
- **Symmetry** – powerful for architecture and reflections
- **Negative Space** – let subjects breathe
- **Golden Hour** – shoot 1 hour after sunrise / before sunset

---

## Shooting Modes Explained

| Mode | Best For |
|------|----------|
| **Manual (M)** | Full creative control |
| **Aperture Priority (Av/A)** | Portraits, landscapes |
| **Shutter Priority (Tv/S)** | Action, sports |
| **Program (P)** | Quick shooting, learning |

> 💡 **Start with Aperture Priority** — it teaches exposure while keeping things manageable.

---

## Essential Settings

- **Shoot RAW** (not JPEG) for maximum editing flexibility
- **Auto White Balance** is fine to start; adjust in editing
- **Single-point autofocus** for precise control
- **Histogram** — avoid clipping highlights (blown-out whites)
- **Metering modes** — Evaluative/Matrix for most scenes

---

## Post-Processing Workflow

### Recommended Tools
- **Adobe Lightroom** – industry standard
- **Capture One** – professional color grading
- **Darktable** – free, powerful alternative

### Basic Editing Order
1. Exposure & contrast
2. Highlights & shadows
3. White balance
4. Color saturation/vibrance
5. Sharpening & noise reduction
6. Crop & straighten

---

## Gear Priorities

### What Actually Matters (in order)
1. **Lenses** > camera body
2. A **fast prime lens** (50mm f/1.8) is a game-changer
3. **Tripod** for long exposure & landscapes
4. **Extra batteries** and memory cards
5. Camera body upgrades last

### Recommended Starter Lenses
- **50mm f/1.8** – portraits, everyday (affordable)
- **35mm f/1.8** – street, environmental portraits
- **70-200mm f/2.8** – sports, wildlife, compression
- **16-35mm** – landscapes, architecture

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

❌ Always shooting in Auto mode
❌ Ignoring the background
❌ Centering every subject
❌ Over-editing (heavy filters, over-saturation)
❌ Buying gear instead of practicing
❌ Deleting photos in-camera before reviewing
