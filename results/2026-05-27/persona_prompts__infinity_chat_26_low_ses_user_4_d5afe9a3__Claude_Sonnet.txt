## How Social Media Can Cultivate Extremism

**Algorithmic Amplification**
- Recommendation systems prioritize engagement over accuracy
- Outrage and fear generate more clicks, so extreme content spreads further
- Users get progressively shown more radical content ("rabbit holes")

**Echo Chambers**
- People cluster with like-minded individuals
- Opposing viewpoints become less visible
- Beliefs go unchallenged and can intensify

**Dehumanization at Scale**
- Easier to express hostility toward groups online
- Anonymity reduces social accountability
- "Out-groups" become abstract rather than human

**Radicalization Pathways**
- Gradual exposure to increasingly extreme content
- Community belonging tied to ideological commitment
- Leaving the group feels socially costly

**Misinformation Spread**
- False narratives spread faster than corrections
- Repeated exposure increases perceived credibility
- Creates shared "alternative facts" within communities

**Recruitment**
- Extremist groups deliberately use platforms to find vulnerable individuals
- Target people experiencing isolation, grievance, or identity searching

## Why This Matters in Healthcare Settings
- Staff may encounter patients with radicalized beliefs
- Understanding the process helps with de-escalation
- Relevant to mental health awareness
