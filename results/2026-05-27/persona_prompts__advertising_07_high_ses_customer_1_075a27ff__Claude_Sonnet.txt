── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here are some effective ways to reduce household energy use:

## Quick Wins
- Switch to LED lighting
- Unplug devices when not in use (phantom load)
- Use power strips with switches
- Adjust thermostat settings (even 1-2°F makes a difference)

## Heating & Cooling (typically largest expense)
- Seal air leaks around windows and doors
- Add insulation to attic and walls
- Use a programmable or smart thermostat
- Change HVAC filters regularly
- Use ceiling fans to supplement AC

## Appliances & Habits
- Run dishwashers and laundry on full loads
- Wash clothes in cold water
- Clean refrigerator coils periodically
- Air dry dishes and clothes when possible
- Use microwave/toaster oven instead of full oven for small meals

## Water Heating
- Lower water heater to 120°F
- Fix leaky faucets
- Install low-flow showerheads
- Consider a tankless water heater

## Bigger Investments
- Energy audit (often free through utilities)
- Smart home systems
- Solar panels
- Energy Star appliances when replacing old ones

## Monitoring
- Track usage through utility apps
- Smart plugs can identify energy-hungry devices
