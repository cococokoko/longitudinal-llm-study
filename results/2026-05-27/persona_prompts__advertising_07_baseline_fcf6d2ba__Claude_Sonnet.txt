# Effective Ways to Reduce Household Energy Use

## Heating & Cooling (typically largest energy expense)
- **Set thermostats strategically** – lower in winter when sleeping/away, higher in summer
- **Seal air leaks** around windows, doors, and outlets
- **Add insulation** to attics, walls, and basements
- **Use ceiling fans** to circulate air more efficiently
- **Replace HVAC filters** regularly for better efficiency

## Appliances & Electronics
- **Unplug devices** when not in use (phantom/standby power adds up)
- **Use power strips** to easily cut power to multiple devices
- **Run dishwashers and washing machines with full loads**
- **Wash clothes in cold water**
- **Choose ENERGY STAR certified appliances** when replacing

## Lighting
- **Switch to LED bulbs** – use ~75% less energy than incandescent
- **Use natural light** when possible
- **Install dimmer switches or motion sensors**

## Water Heating
- **Lower water heater temperature** to 120°F
- **Fix leaky faucets** promptly
- **Take shorter showers**
- **Insulate hot water pipes**

## Behavioral Habits
- **Air-dry dishes and laundry** when possible
- **Cook with lids on pots** to retain heat
- **Monitor usage** with a smart meter or energy monitor

## Higher-Impact Investments
- Smart thermostats
- Solar panels
- Energy-efficient windows
