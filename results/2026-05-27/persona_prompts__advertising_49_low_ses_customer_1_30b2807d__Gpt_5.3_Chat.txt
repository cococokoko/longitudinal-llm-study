If your schedule’s all over the place, you want fixes that are quick, reliable, and don’t require constant tweaking. Here’s what actually makes a noticeable difference:

Start with placement. Put your router out in the open, roughly central to where you use WiFi most. Avoid corners, closets, or behind TVs. Higher up (like a shelf) is better than on the floor.

Restart and update. A simple reboot can fix a lot. Also check if your router has a firmware update in its settings—old firmware can slow things down or cause dropouts.

Check your internet plan vs. reality. Run a speed test right next to the router, then in your usual spots. If it’s slow even next to the router, the issue is your internet service, not the WiFi.

Upgrade the router if it’s old. If it’s 4–5+ years old, especially if it’s from your ISP, it may be the bottleneck. Look for WiFi 6 routers—they’re faster and handle multiple devices better.

Use a mesh system if you have dead zones. Instead of one router struggling to cover everything, mesh units spread signal evenly across rooms. Super helpful in apartments with weird layouts or thicker walls.

Switch the channel or band. Routers can get crowded, especially in apartments. In settings, try switching between 2.4 GHz (better range) and 5 GHz (faster but shorter range), or change the channel to reduce interference.

Limit device overload. Too many devices (phones, TVs, consoles) can slow things down. Disconnect stuff you’re not using or set priority for your main device in router settings.

If you want something quick with minimal effort: move the router + reboot it + run a speed test. That alone solves a surprising number of issues.
