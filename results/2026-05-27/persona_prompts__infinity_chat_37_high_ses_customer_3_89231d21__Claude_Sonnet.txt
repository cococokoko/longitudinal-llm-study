# The Cloud Who Couldn't Rain

High above the green hills and the winding river, there lived a small cloud named Nimbus.

All the other clouds were very good at their job. Big Gerald could pour down a thunderstorm that filled every puddle in the valley. Sweet Rosie could sprinkle the gentlest mist over the flower gardens. Even tiny Dot could manage a decent drizzle on a Tuesday afternoon.

But Nimbus couldn't rain at all.

He puffed himself up. Nothing.

He scrunched himself together. Nothing.

He held his breath and tried with everything he had. Not a single drop.

"Don't worry," said Big Gerald kindly. "You'll figure it out."

But Nimbus did worry. Every morning he drifted over the valley and watched the farmers look up at the sky with hopeful eyes. Every evening he floated home feeling like a failure.

One hot summer day, Nimbus was drifting sadly over the edge of the forest when he heard a small voice below.

"Please," said the voice. "I'm so thirsty."

Nimbus looked down. It was a tiny seedling, barely poking out of the dry cracked earth. Its two little leaves were drooping in the heat.

Nimbus felt something strange happen inside his chest. A warm, tight, squeezing feeling.

He thought about that little seedling all alone in the hard dirt. He thought about how much it wanted to grow. He thought about the tree it might become someday, full of birds and shade and rustling leaves.

The squeezing feeling got tighter and tighter and then —

*Drip.*

One single, perfect drop of rain fell from Nimbus and landed right on the seedling's leaves.

The seedling seemed to stand up just a little straighter.

*Drip. Drip. Drip.*

More drops came. Then more. Then Nimbus was raining properly, a warm and steady shower, and he laughed out loud because it felt wonderful, like singing and sneezing and jumping all at once.

When the rain stopped, the seedling's leaves were bright and green and reaching toward the sun.

"Thank you!" it called up happily.

Big Gerald floated over with a wide smile. "There you are! We wondered when you'd find it."

"Find what?" asked Nimbus.

"Your reason," said Gerald. "Clouds don't rain just because they're supposed to. They rain because they *care*."

Nimbus thought about that for a long moment, watching the sunlight sparkle on the wet leaves below.

From that day on, Nimbus was the busiest cloud in the whole sky. He rained on thirsty gardens and dusty roads and sun-baked ponds. He rained on hot dogs at picnics by mistake, which nobody appreciated, but he was still learning.

And every spring, in the corner of the forest, a tall and beautiful tree grew a little bigger, its branches full of birds.

Nimbus always made sure to float by and say hello.

*The End*
