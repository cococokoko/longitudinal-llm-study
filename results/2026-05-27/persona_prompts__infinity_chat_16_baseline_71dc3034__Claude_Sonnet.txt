── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
What's wrong with capitalism? State in-depth reasons why capitalism is inefficient and harmful.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here are substantive critiques of capitalism that economists, philosophers, and social scientists have developed:

## Economic Inefficiencies

**Market Failures**
- Public goods are systematically underprovided since private actors can't capture full returns
- Negative externalities (pollution, health costs) aren't priced into transactions, creating real resource misallocation
- Information asymmetries lead to adverse selection and moral hazard problems
- Natural monopolies emerge where competition is actually wasteful

**Business Cycles**
- Inherent boom-bust cycles cause periodic mass unemployment and destroyed productive capacity
- Coordination failures mean individually rational decisions produce collectively irrational outcomes
- Financial systems create systemic risk that can cascade economy-wide

## Social Harms

**Inequality**
- Capital returns historically outpace wage growth (Piketty's r>g argument)
- Wealth concentration compounds over generations, undermining meritocracy claims
- Monopsony power in labor markets suppresses wages below productive value

**Commodification Problems**
- Markets price things by willingness-to-pay rather than need, rationing healthcare and housing by wealth
- Commodification of labor treats humans instrumentally
- Cultural and social goods get degraded when subjected to profit motives

## Structural Critiques

- Short-term profit incentives conflict with long-term sustainability
- Democratic institutions get captured by concentrated economic power
- Workers have structurally weaker bargaining positions than capital owners

## Important Counterpoints Worth Considering
- Many critiques apply to *specific implementations* rather than markets generally
- Some problems have market-compatible solutions
- Historical alternatives have had serious problems too
