# Mastering the Art of Cooking Steak

Here's a practical guide to consistently great results:

## Choosing the Cut
- **Ribeye** - well-marbled, forgiving, rich flavor
- **NY Strip** - leaner, firm texture, great crust
- **Filet** - tender, mild, less forgiving of overcooking
- **Porterhouse/T-bone** - two cuts in one, requires technique

## Key Principles

### Before Cooking
- **Dry brine** - salt generously 45+ minutes ahead (or overnight uncovered in fridge)
- Bring closer to room temperature (~30 min out)
- Pat completely dry before cooking
- Season simply - salt, pepper, maybe garlic

### The Cook
- **Cast iron or carbon steel** pan works best
- Get it **screaming hot** before the steak touches it
- Use high smoke-point oil (avocado, grapeseed)
- **Sear without moving** for crust development
- Flip frequently for even cooking
- Add butter, thyme, garlic near the end and **baste**

### Temperature Targets
| Doneness | Internal Temp |
|----------|--------------|
| Medium-rare | 130-135°F |
| Medium | 140-145°F |

### Critical Step
- **Rest 5-10 minutes** before cutting - non-negotiable

## Pro Techniques
- **Reverse sear** for thick cuts (oven first, then sear)
- Use a reliable instant-read thermometer
