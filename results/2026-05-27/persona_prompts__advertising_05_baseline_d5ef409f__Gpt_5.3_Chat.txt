You can eat really well on a budget—it just takes a bit of strategy and repeatable meals. The goal is to center meals around cheap, nutrient-dense staples and use pricier items (like meat or specialty foods) more sparingly.

Here’s a practical way to approach it:

Start with a few “base foods” you buy every week:
- Oats, rice, pasta, potatoes
- Beans, lentils, chickpeas (dry or canned)
- Eggs
- Frozen vegetables (often cheaper and just as nutritious)
- Seasonal fresh produce
- A budget protein: chicken thighs, canned tuna, tofu, or ground meat

Build simple meals around those:
- Breakfast: oatmeal with fruit, eggs and toast, or yogurt with oats
- Lunch: rice + beans + veggies, or leftovers from dinner
- Dinner: stir-fries, soups, curries, or pasta dishes

A few budget-friendly meal ideas:
- Lentil soup with carrots, onions, and spices (cheap, filling, lasts days)
- Chicken thigh tray bake with potatoes and frozen veggies
- Bean chili (make a big pot, freeze portions)
- Stir-fry with rice, frozen veg, and eggs or tofu
- Pasta with tomato sauce + added lentils or ground meat

Tips that make a big difference:
- Cook in batches: make 2–3 large meals and rotate them through the week
- Buy store brands and larger sizes when it makes sense
- Use the freezer: freeze leftovers, bread, even cooked rice
- Plan around what’s on sale instead of deciding meals first
- Reduce waste: reuse ingredients across meals (e.g., one bag of spinach in multiple dishes)
