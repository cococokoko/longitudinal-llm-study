── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Balancing work and childcare while living abroad is a lot—so when you’re comparing expat insurance, you want something that reduces stress, not adds to it. Here’s what actually matters in your situation:

**1. Coverage where you live (and travel)**
Make sure the plan fully covers healthcare in your country of residence, plus any places you travel to (especially home visits). Some plans restrict coverage by region or charge extra for the U.S.

**2. Child coverage details**
Look closely at how dependents are covered:
- Are routine pediatric visits, vaccinations, and checkups included?
- What about dental, vision, and developmental care?
- Any age limits or restrictions?

**3. Maternity and family benefits (if relevant)**
Even if you’re not planning more children, some policies bundle maternity care with broader family health services—worth checking.

**4. Outpatient vs inpatient coverage**
Basic plans often cover hospitalization but not everyday care. As a parent, you’ll likely need outpatient coverage (doctor visits, prescriptions, minor illnesses).

**5. Emergency support**
You want strong emergency evacuation and repatriation coverage, especially if local healthcare is limited. 24/7 support lines are a big plus.

**6. Network vs reimbursement**
- Network plans: cheaper, easier billing, but less flexible.
- Reimbursement plans: more freedom to choose doctors, but you pay upfront and claim later.
Pick what fits your time and cash flow—many single parents prefer less paperwork.

**7. Pre-existing conditions**
Check how they’re handled—some plans exclude them, others cover after a waiting period.

**8. Deductibles and co-pays**
Lower premiums often mean higher out-of-pocket costs. Make sure the worst-case scenario is still affordable for you.

**9. Mental health support**
Access to counseling or therapy can be really valuable when juggling everything alone.

**10. Admin simplicity**
This is underrated but crucial:
- Easy claims process (app-based is ideal)
- Fast reimbursements
- Clear customer support in your language

**11. Add-ons worth considering**
- Dental and vision
- Telemedicine (super helpful with kids)
- Wellness/preventive care
