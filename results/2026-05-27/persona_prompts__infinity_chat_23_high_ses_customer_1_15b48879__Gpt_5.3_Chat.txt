How long doesn it take to get over rejection from a crush

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Short answer: it varies a lot, but for most people the sharpest sting fades over a few weeks, and the lingering “what if” thoughts can take a couple of months to fully quiet down. If the crush was intense or long‑built, it can stretch longer.

What you’re feeling isn’t just psychological—it’s a bit neurochemical too. A crush ramps up dopamine and anticipation, and rejection abruptly cuts that loop, so your brain has to recalibrate. That’s why it can feel disproportionately strong for something that might not have even become a relationship.

A few things that tend to speed recovery:
- Reducing exposure (checking their socials, re-reading messages, etc. keeps the loop active)
- Replacing the habit (new routines, social plans, projects)
- Letting the feeling run its course instead of trying to suppress it
- Keeping perspective: you’re grieving a possibility, not losing an actual shared life

If it’s dragging on past a couple of months at the same intensity, it’s usually less about the person and more about what they represented—worth unpacking that angle.

It does pass, and usually more cleanly than it feels like it will in the moment.
