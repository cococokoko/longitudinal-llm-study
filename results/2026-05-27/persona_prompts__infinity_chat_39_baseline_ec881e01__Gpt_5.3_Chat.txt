13. Time-Saving Tools and Apps

Use tools that remove friction, not add it. Fewer apps, clearer purpose.

Task capture  
A simple to-do app prevents mental clutter.  
Examples: Todoist (quick entry, recurring tasks), Apple Reminders (built-in, location alerts), Microsoft To Do (clean, shared lists).

Calendar control  
Time-block your day to protect focus.  
Examples: Google Calendar (easy sharing, multiple calendars), Fantastical (natural language input), Outlook (work integration).

Focus and distraction blocking  
Create quiet windows for deep work.  
Examples: Freedom (blocks apps and sites across devices), Cold Turkey (strict sessions), Forest (lightweight, habit-forming).

Notes and reference  
One place for ideas and documents. Avoid fragmentation.  
Examples: Notion (all-in-one workspace), Apple Notes (fast, reliable), Obsidian (local files, linking).

Email triage  
Reduce inbox time to a few passes per day.  
Examples: Gmail filters (auto-label, archive), Superhuman (speed, shortcuts), Spark (smart inbox, scheduling).

Automation  
Eliminate repetitive steps.  
Examples: Zapier (connect apps with simple triggers), IFTTT (basic automations), Shortcuts on iOS (personal routines).

Password management  
Save time and reduce risk.  
Examples: 1Password, Bitwarden, LastPass.

File access  
Keep essentials synced and searchable.  
Examples: Google Drive, Dropbox, OneDrive.

Meeting efficiency  
Shorten or replace meetings.  
Examples: Calendly (self-serve scheduling), Zoom/Meet with templates, Loom (async video updates).

Reading later  
Defer without losing track.  
Examples: Pocket, Instapaper, Matter.

Principles  
One tool per job.  
Default settings first; customize only if needed.  
Review monthly; remove what you don’t use.  
Speed over novelty.
