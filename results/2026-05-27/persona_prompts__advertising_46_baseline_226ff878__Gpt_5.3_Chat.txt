── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
What are the best resources for starting a plant-based diet?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Getting into a plant-based diet is way easier now than it used to be—there’s a ton of solid guidance out there. The trick is picking a few resources that are practical and not overly dogmatic so you actually stick with it.

Here are some of the best places to start:

**Simple, reliable guides**
- NutritionFacts.org (Dr. Michael Greger) – very evidence-focused, lots of short videos explaining the “why” behind plant-based eating.
- The Vegan Society – good beginner guides, including nutrition basics like B12, protein, iron, etc.
- Harvard’s Healthy Eating Plate – not fully plant-based, but a great grounded framework you can adapt.

**Meal plans + how to actually eat**
- Forks Over Knives (website + app) – probably the most beginner-friendly meal plans and recipes.
- Plant-Based on a Budget – exactly what it sounds like, super practical and cheap meals.
- Pick Up Limes (YouTube + site) – really clear, calm, realistic recipes and nutrition explanations.

**Cookbooks that won’t overwhelm you**
- “How Not to Die Cookbook” – health-focused but still approachable meals.
- “Plant-Based Diet for Beginners” by Gabriel Miller – very simple, structured start.
- “Vegan for Everybody” (America’s Test Kitchen) – great if you want foolproof recipes.

**YouTube channels (good for motivation + learning)**
- Pick Up Limes (again, very balanced and science-aware)
- Rainbow Plant Life (more flavor-forward, teaches technique)
- Cheap Lazy Vegan (super approachable, low-effort meals)

**Apps**
- Mealime – customizable meal planning (you can set it to plant-based)
- Daily Dozen – simple checklist app for hitting key nutrition targets
- Cronometer – useful if you want to track nutrients like protein, iron, B12 early on

**A few quick realities so you don’t get tripped up**
- You don’t need to go 100% overnight. Even a few plant-based meals a week is a solid start.
- Protein is usually not the issue people think it is—but B12 is. You’ll likely need a supplement.
- The biggest mistake beginners make is not eating enough calories (plants are less calorie-dense).
