# Improving Indoor Air Quality

Great practical question! Here are the most effective approaches, especially useful when you're home a lot with kids:

## High-Impact Steps

**Ventilation**
- Open windows when weather allows, even briefly
- Use exhaust fans in kitchen and bathrooms
- Run HVAC systems regularly

**Air Filtration**
- HEPA air purifiers work well, especially in bedrooms
- Change HVAC filters regularly (every 1-3 months)
- Look for MERV 11-13 rated filters

**Reduce Sources**
- Choose fragrance-free cleaning products
- Avoid air fresheners and scented candles (common irritants)
- Remove shoes at the door
- Vacuum with a HEPA vacuum regularly

## Lower Cost Options
- Keep a few houseplants (modest benefit but helpful)
- Baking soda absorbs some odors naturally
- Increase ventilation while cooking

## Things Worth Knowing
- **Cooking** is actually a major pollution source - always use range hood or open windows
- **New furniture/carpets** off-gas chemicals for weeks
- Kids are more vulnerable to air pollutants than adults

## Quick Wins as a Busy Parent
- No-shoe policy is easy and effective
- A good bedroom air purifier helps during sleep hours
- Fragrance-free products are simple swaps
