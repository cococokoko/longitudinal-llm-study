── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
# Improving Indoor Air Quality

Here are the most effective approaches:

## Ventilation
- Open windows regularly when outdoor air quality permits
- Use exhaust fans in kitchens and bathrooms
- Ensure HVAC systems are properly maintained
- Consider energy recovery ventilators (ERVs)

## Filtration
- Use HEPA air purifiers in frequently occupied rooms
- Replace HVAC filters regularly (every 1-3 months)
- Choose filters with higher MERV ratings (8-13 for most homes)

## Source Control
- **Reduce pollutants at the source** - most effective long-term strategy
- Use low-VOC paints, adhesives, and cleaning products
- Avoid smoking indoors
- Store chemicals properly or outside

## Humidity Control
- Maintain humidity between **30-50%**
- Use dehumidifiers in damp areas to prevent mold
- Fix leaks promptly

## Practical Steps
- Remove shoes at the door
- Vacuum with HEPA-filter vacuums regularly
- Choose hard flooring over carpet when possible
- Test for radon, especially in basements
- Have gas appliances inspected annually

## Plants
- Some plants may offer modest air-cleaning benefits, though research suggests the effect is **relatively minor** compared to mechanical ventilation
