Got it—so you need something effective but quick and low-effort. For dry skin, consistency and simplicity matter more than a complicated routine.

Here’s a practical routine you can stick to even on busy days:

**Morning (2–3 minutes)**
- Use a gentle, non-foaming cleanser (or just rinse with water if your skin isn’t oily in the morning)
- Apply a hydrating serum (look for hyaluronic acid or glycerin—optional but helpful)
- Use a rich moisturizer (look for ceramides, shea butter, or squalane)

**Night (3–5 minutes)**
- Gentle cleanser (to remove dirt and sunscreen)
- Moisturizer (same one as morning is fine)
- If very dry: add a thin layer of an occlusive on top (like petroleum jelly or a balm) a few nights a week to lock in moisture

**Extra (1–2 times a week, optional)**
- Gentle exfoliation (like lactic acid or a mild enzyme)—skip harsh scrubs
- Hydrating mask if you want, but not necessary

**What actually makes the biggest difference**
- Using lukewarm water (hot water dries you out fast)
- Applying moisturizer on slightly damp skin
- Not over-cleansing
- Keeping products simple and fragrance-free if your skin is sensitive

**Good no-fuss product types to look for**
- “Hydrating” or “cream cleanser”
- “Barrier repair” moisturizers with ceramides
- Thick creams over lotions (lotions are often too light for dry skin)
