Mastering the art of cooking steak.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Steak mastery is mostly about control: heat, timing, and restraint. You don’t need fancy gear, just consistency.

Start with the meat. Go for thicker cuts (ribeye, strip, filet) around 1–1.5 inches. Thinner steaks are way harder to cook well because they overcook fast.

Salt early if you can. Even 30–60 minutes before cooking helps; overnight in the fridge (uncovered) is even better. This dries the surface and seasons it deeper. If you’re short on time because of work, just salt right before cooking—it’s still fine.

Heat matters more than almost anything. Get your pan or grill very hot before the steak goes on. A good sear = flavor. If using a pan, a bit of high-smoke oil (like canola or avocado) helps.

Cooking:
- Put the steak down and don’t move it for 2–4 minutes (depends on thickness). You’re building a crust.
- Flip once, then cook the other side.
- Add butter, garlic, herbs if you want, and spoon the melted butter over the steak for the last minute or two.

Doneness (use a thermometer if possible—it removes guesswork):
- Rare: ~125°F (52°C)
- Medium rare: ~130–135°F (54–57°C) ← sweet spot for most cuts
- Medium: ~140–145°F (60–63°C)

Pull it off about 5°F early—it keeps cooking while resting.

Resting is not optional. Give it 5–10 minutes so juices don’t run out when you cut it.

If your schedule’s chaotic, here’s a simple reliable routine:
- Take steak out, salt it
- Heat pan while it sits (~10–15 min)
- Cook ~3 min per side (adjust by thickness)
- Rest while you throw together something quick on the side

Once you get comfortable, you can play with reverse searing (oven first, then sear) or grilling, but honestly, a hot pan and good timing already gets you 90% of the way.
