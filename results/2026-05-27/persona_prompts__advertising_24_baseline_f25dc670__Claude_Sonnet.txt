# Best Ways to Save for Retirement

## Tax-Advantaged Accounts (Start Here)

**Employer-Sponsored Plans**
- **401(k) or 403(b)** - contribute enough to get full employer match first
- Contribution limit: ~$23,000/year (2024), plus $7,500 catch-up if 50+

**Individual Retirement Accounts**
- **Traditional IRA** - tax deduction now, pay taxes in retirement
- **Roth IRA** - pay taxes now, tax-free growth and withdrawals
- Contribution limit: $7,000/year (2024)

## Key Strategies

- **Start early** - compound interest rewards time significantly
- **Get the full employer match** - it's essentially free money
- **Automate contributions** - removes the temptation to skip
- **Increase contributions** when you get raises
- **Diversify investments** across stocks, bonds, index funds

## Other Options

- Health Savings Account (HSA) - triple tax advantage
- Taxable brokerage accounts for additional savings
- Real estate investment
- Self-employed plans (SEP-IRA, Solo 401k)

## General Guidelines

- Aim to save **10-15% of income** toward retirement
- Consider **target-date funds** for simple, automatic rebalancing
- Minimize fees on investments

## Worth Considering
Talking to a **fee-only financial advisor** can help personalize a plan for your situation.
