── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How can I learn to cook healthy meals quickly?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
You don’t need to become a chef—you need a small system you can repeat.

Start by simplifying what “healthy + quick” means: a protein, a veggie, and something filling (like rice, pasta, or potatoes). If you can combine those in different ways, you’re basically set.

Here’s how to make it practical:

**1. Learn 5–7 “go-to” meals, not 50 recipes**  
Pick stuff that takes under 30 minutes and overlaps ingredients. For example:
- Stir-fry (chicken/tofu + frozen veggies + sauce)
- Sheet pan meal (protein + chopped veggies + oil, all roasted)
- Omelet or scrambled eggs with veggies
- Grain bowl (rice + protein + greens + dressing)
- Pasta with veggies + protein  
Once you know these, you can rotate endlessly without thinking.

**2. Use shortcuts without guilt**  
Healthy doesn’t mean everything from scratch. Use:
- Pre-cut or frozen vegetables  
- Rotisserie chicken or canned beans  
- Microwave rice or quick grains  
- Store-bought sauces (just check sugar/salt if you care)  
Speed matters more than purity if you want this to stick.

**3. Batch a few basics, not full meals**  
Instead of meal-prepping entire dishes, cook components:
- A pot of rice or quinoa  
- A batch of protein (chicken, tofu, beans)  
- Roasted veggies  
Mix and match during the week so you don’t get bored.

**4. Learn a few core techniques**  
You only really need:
- Sautéing (pan + oil + medium heat)
- Roasting (oven + high heat, ~400°F / 200°C)
- Boiling (for grains/pasta)
- Basic seasoning (salt, pepper, garlic, acid like lemon)  
That covers most meals.

**5. Keep it stupid simple on busy days**  
Have “fallback meals” that take 10 minutes:
- Eggs + toast + avocado  
- Greek yogurt + fruit + nuts  
- Canned beans + olive oil + salt + greens  
- Wraps with whatever’s in the fridge  
Consistency beats ambition.

**6. Build a tiny flavor toolkit**  
If food tastes good, you’ll keep cooking. Keep 5–6 things on hand:
- Garlic (or garlic powder)
- Soy sauce or tamari
- Olive oil
- Lemon or vinegar
- Chili flakes or hot sauce
- One spice blend you like
