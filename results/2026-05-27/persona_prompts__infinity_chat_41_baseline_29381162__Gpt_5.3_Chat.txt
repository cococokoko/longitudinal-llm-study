nice, that’s a great goal 😄 japanese is super fun once you get into it

before I dump a plan on you, here’s a simple way to think about it: you’re really learning 3 things at once
- the writing systems (hiragana, katakana, kanji)
- grammar (how sentences are built)
- vocabulary (words + meanings)

if you try to do all of it randomly, it gets overwhelming fast, so structure helps a lot.

here’s a solid beginner path you can start right away:

1) start with hiragana (first priority)
this is the basic alphabet used for most native words and grammar
- takes about 3–7 days to learn if you’re consistent
- use apps like “Kana Drill” or “Duolingo” or just charts + practice writing
- goal: read it without thinking

2) then katakana
used mostly for foreign words (like “coffee” → コーヒー)
- usually quicker to learn after hiragana (2–4 days)

3) basic grammar + simple sentences
don’t wait too long before starting this
learn stuff like:
- “X is Y” → これはペンです (this is a pen)
- particles: は (wa), を (o), に (ni), で (de)
- basic verbs like 食べる (to eat), 行く (to go)

good beginner resources:
- “Genki I” (textbook, very popular)
- “Tae Kim’s Guide to Japanese” (free online)
- Cure Dolly (YouTube, a bit weird style but very clear explanations)

4) start vocab early (but small chunks)
aim for like 5–15 words a day max
use:
- Anki (spaced repetition, super effective)
- or apps like Memrise

5) listen + repeat (even if you don’t understand everything)
this helps your brain get used to the sounds
- watch simple anime, kids shows, or beginner podcasts
- try shadowing (repeat what you hear)

quick reality check:
- you won’t “feel fluent” for a while, that’s normal
- consistency beats intensity (20–30 min daily > 3 hours once a week)
