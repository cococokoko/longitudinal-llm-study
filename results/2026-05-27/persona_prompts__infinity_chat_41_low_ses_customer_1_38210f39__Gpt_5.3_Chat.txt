Since you’ve got a full-time fast food job with a changing schedule, the key is *flexible, bite-sized learning* you can do anytime.

Here’s a simple way to start:

First: learn the basics of the writing system  
Japanese uses three scripts:
- Hiragana (main phonetic alphabet — start here)
- Katakana (for foreign words)
- Kanji (more complex characters, learned gradually)

Focus on hiragana first. You can realistically learn it in about 1–2 weeks with 10–15 minutes a day.

Good tools:
- Apps: Duolingo, LingoDeer, or Kana Drill
- Or just search “hiragana chart” and practice a few characters at a time

Second: pick up survival phrases early  
This keeps things fun and useful right away:
- こんにちは (konnichiwa) – hello  
- ありがとう (arigatou) – thank you  
- すみません (sumimasen) – excuse me / sorry  
- はい / いいえ (hai / iie) – yes / no  

Third: use “micro-sessions”  
Because your schedule is unpredictable, don’t aim for long study blocks. Instead:
- 5 minutes before work  
- a few minutes on break  
- a couple minutes before bed  

Even 10 minutes a day consistently beats 1 hour once a week.

Fourth: listen a lot (even passively)  
Play Japanese audio while:
- commuting  
- getting ready  
- doing chores  

You won’t understand everything, but your brain starts picking up rhythm and sounds.

Try:
- JapanesePod101 (beginner playlists)
- YouTube: “Japanese for beginners listening practice”

Fifth: don’t rush kanji  
A lot of beginners burn out here. You can start with a few basic ones later, but focus on hiragana + simple words first.
