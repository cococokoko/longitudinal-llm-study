# Stock Trading Strategies

Here are some well-regarded approaches, depending on your situation:

## Common Strategies

**Long-term Investing (Buy & Hold)**
- Low maintenance, fits busy schedules
- Index funds/ETFs reduce risk through diversification
- Historically reliable over 10+ year periods

**Dividend Investing**
- Regular income from dividend payments
- Relatively stable companies
- Good for supplementing household income

**Dollar-Cost Averaging**
- Invest fixed amounts regularly regardless of price
- Reduces timing risk
- Works well with limited time to monitor markets

## Important Considerations

**Given family/household responsibilities:**
- ✅ Prioritize **emergency fund first** (3-6 months expenses)
- ✅ Consider **time availability** - active trading requires significant attention
- ✅ **Risk tolerance** matters when others depend on you
- ✅ Tax-advantaged accounts (401k, IRA) often make sense first

## Honest Cautions
- Most active traders **underperform** simple index funds
- Day trading is high-risk and time-intensive
- No strategy guarantees returns

## Simple Starting Point
Many financial advisors suggest **low-cost index funds** (like S&P 500 ETFs) as a foundation before exploring other strategies.
