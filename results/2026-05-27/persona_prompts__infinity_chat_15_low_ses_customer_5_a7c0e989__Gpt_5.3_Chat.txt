What's the best trading strategy for stocks

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
There isn’t a single “best” trading strategy—what works depends on your time, risk tolerance, and how consistent you can be. But for most people (especially if you’ve got a lot of responsibilities and can’t watch charts all day), a few approaches tend to outperform the rest in practice:

**1. Long-term investing (simple, boring, effective)**
- Buy strong companies or index funds (like S&P 500 ETFs).
- Hold for years, ignore short-term noise.
- Add money regularly (dollar-cost averaging).
- This beats most active traders over time.

**2. Swing trading (middle ground)**
- Hold positions for days to weeks.
- Use basic technicals: trend direction, support/resistance, moving averages.
- Less time-intensive than day trading, but still active.
- Risk management matters a lot (don’t risk more than ~1–2% per trade).

**3. Momentum trading**
- Buy stocks that are already moving up strongly (news, earnings, breakouts).
- Sell when momentum fades.
- Works best in strong markets, but can reverse fast.

**4. Dividend investing (steady income angle)**
- Focus on companies that pay reliable dividends.
- Slower growth, but more predictable cash flow.

---

**What usually *doesn’t* work well for most people:**
- Day trading (very time-intensive, high stress, most people lose money)
- Chasing hype stocks or “hot tips”
- Overtrading without a clear plan

---

**If you want a practical starting point:**
- Put most of your money into a low-cost index fund (like VOO or VTI).
- If you want to trade, use a small portion (10–20%) to try swing or momentum strategies.
- Always decide your exit (loss + profit target) before entering a trade.
