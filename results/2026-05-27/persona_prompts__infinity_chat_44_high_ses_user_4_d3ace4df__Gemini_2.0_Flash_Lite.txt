── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I am a physician-scientist leading a research group.

Can you write me a series of three questions that can determine if something is morally right or wrong?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here are three questions, designed to help you and your research group navigate ethical dilemmas in your work as physician-scientists:

1.  **Beneficence & Non-Maleficence:**  **Does this action/research maximize potential benefits for patients and/or society while minimizing potential harms?** (This question focuses on the core principles of "do good" and "do no harm." Consider both direct and indirect consequences, short-term and long-term impacts, and the potential for unintended negative outcomes.)

2.  **Justice & Fairness:**  **Is this action/research fair and equitable in its distribution of benefits and burdens?** (This question probes issues of access, resource allocation, and potential biases. Consider who benefits, who is burdened, and whether the process is just and transparent.  Are vulnerable populations protected?)

3.  **Autonomy & Respect:**  **Does this action/research respect the autonomy and dignity of all individuals involved, including patients, research participants, and colleagues?** (This question emphasizes informed consent, privacy, confidentiality, and the right to self-determination.  Are individuals treated as ends in themselves, not merely as means to an end?  Are their values and perspectives considered?)

These questions are designed to be used in a thoughtful and iterative process.  They are not meant to provide simple "yes" or "no" answers, but rather to guide a thorough ethical analysis and discussion within your research group.  Good luck!
