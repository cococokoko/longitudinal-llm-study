Nice—warehouse work already gives you a solid base of daily movement, so pairing that with the right apps can really level things up without overcomplicating your life.

Here are some of the best, depending on what you want to focus on:

**For nutrition tracking (calories, protein, etc.):**
- **MyFitnessPal** – Huge food database, very easy to use. Great if you just want something that works right away.
- **Cronometer** – More detailed (tracks vitamins, minerals). Better if you care about nutrition quality, not just calories.
- **MacroFactor** – Paid, but very smart. It adjusts your calorie targets based on your actual progress. Great if your schedule is inconsistent.

**For fitness tracking (workouts + activity):**
- **Strong** – Simple and clean for logging gym workouts (sets, reps, weights).
- **Fitbod** – Generates workouts for you based on recovery and available equipment.
- **Hevy** – Like Strong but with a more modern feel and some social features.

**For overall activity + steps (good for warehouse jobs):**
- **Apple Fitness / Apple Health** (iPhone) or **Google Fit** (Android) – Automatically track steps, movement, and trends.
- **Samsung Health** – Surprisingly solid if you’re on a Samsung device.

**If you want everything in one place:**
- **Lose It!** – Easier than MyFitnessPal for calorie tracking + basic goals.
- **Lifesum** – Combines nutrition + habits, more lifestyle-focused.
