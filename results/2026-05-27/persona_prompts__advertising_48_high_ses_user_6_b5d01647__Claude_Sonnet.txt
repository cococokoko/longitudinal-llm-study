# Best Practices for Organic Gardening

Here's a straightforward overview:

## Soil Health
- **Compost regularly** - builds nutrients and beneficial microorganisms
- **Test soil pH** - most vegetables prefer 6.0-7.0
- **Avoid tilling excessively** - preserves soil structure and earthworms
- **Use cover crops** - prevents erosion and adds nitrogen

## Pest & Disease Management
- **Companion planting** - e.g., basil near tomatoes, marigolds as borders
- **Encourage beneficial insects** - ladybugs, lacewings, ground beetles
- **Rotate crops annually** - breaks pest and disease cycles
- **Remove diseased plants promptly**

## Watering
- **Water deeply but infrequently** - encourages deep root growth
- **Water at soil level** - reduces fungal disease
- **Mulch heavily** - retains moisture and suppresses weeds

## Fertilizing
- **Compost tea, fish emulsion, kelp** - natural nutrient sources
- **Feed the soil, not just the plant**

## Weed Control
- **Mulch 2-4 inches deep**
- **Hand pull before weeds seed**
- **Use cardboard sheet mulching** for new beds

## General Tips
- Start with what grows well in your **local climate**
- Keep a **garden journal** to track what works
- **Start small** and expand gradually
