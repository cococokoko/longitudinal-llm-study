Can you write me a series of three questions that can determine if something is morally right or wrong?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here’s a compact three-question framework you can use as a moral “stress test”:

1. **Harm and benefit:**  
   Does this action reduce or cause harm, and are the expected benefits real, proportionate, and fairly distributed?

2. **Respect for persons:**  
   Does it respect the autonomy, dignity, and rights of everyone affected—especially those with less power or voice?

3. **Universality and accountability:**  
   Would it be acceptable if everyone in a similar position acted this way, and would I stand by this decision if it were made public or applied to me?

If an action struggles under any one of these, it’s a signal to reconsider or refine it.
