Nice, that’s a great rabbit hole to fall into 🎸

At the start, don’t overcomplicate it. Focus on a few core things and you’ll progress way faster:

First, get comfortable holding the guitar and pressing strings cleanly. Your fingers will hurt a bit at first—that’s normal and goes away in a couple weeks.

Then learn a few basic open chords: G, C, D, E minor, A minor. With just those, you can already play a ton of songs.

Practice switching between chords slowly. That’s honestly where most beginners struggle, not the chords themselves.

For your right hand, start with simple strumming—downstrokes at first, then add upstrokes once it feels natural. Don’t worry about fancy patterns yet.

Also, try to play actual songs as early as possible. It keeps things fun and trains your timing way better than just drills.

If you want a simple daily routine:
- 5–10 min: finger warm-up / just pressing strings cleanly  
- 10 min: chord practice (switching between 2–3 chords)  
- 10–15 min: play along to a simple song
