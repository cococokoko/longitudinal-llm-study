# Mastering the Art of Cooking Steak

## Choosing Your Cut
| Cut | Characteristics | Best Method |
|-----|----------------|-------------|
| Ribeye | Rich, well-marbled, flavorful | Pan sear, grill |
| NY Strip | Balanced fat, firm texture | Pan sear, grill |
| Filet Mignon | Tender, lean, mild | Pan sear, oven finish |
| T-Bone/Porterhouse | Two cuts in one | Grill |
| Flank/Skirt | Lean, bold flavor | High heat, quick cook |

---

## Essential Principles

### Before Cooking
- **Bring to room temperature** — 30-45 minutes out of the fridge
- **Pat completely dry** — moisture is the enemy of a good sear
- **Season generously** — kosher salt and black pepper at minimum
- **Salt early** — ideally 45+ minutes ahead or right before cooking

### The Sear
- Use a **cast iron skillet** or carbon steel pan
- Get the pan **screaming hot** before the steak goes in
- Use high smoke-point oil (avocado, grapeseed)
- **Don't move it** — let the crust develop

---

## Doneness Guide
| Level | Internal Temp | Feel |
|-------|--------------|------|
| Rare | 120-125°F | Very soft |
| Medium Rare | 130-135°F | Soft with slight resistance |
| Medium | 140-145°F | Springy |
| Medium Well | 150-155°F | Firm |
| Well Done | 160°F+ | Very firm |

> **Most chefs recommend medium rare for peak flavor and texture**

---

## The Reverse Sear Method (Best for Thick Cuts)
1. Season steak and place on a rack
2. Cook in oven at **250°F** until 10-15°F below target temp
3. Rest briefly, then **sear hard** in a ripping hot pan 1-2 minutes per side
4. **Result:** Edge-to-edge even doneness with a perfect crust

---

## Classic Pan Sear Method
1. Heat cast iron until smoking
2. Add oil, then steak
3. Sear **2-3 minutes per side** without moving
4. Add **butter, garlic, and fresh thyme/rosemary**
5. **Baste continuously** — tilt pan and spoon butter over steak
6. Check temperature and pull **5°F before target**

---

## Critical Finishing Steps

### Resting
- Rest **5-10 minutes** before cutting
- Allows juices to redistribute
- Tent loosely with foil

### Slicing
- Always cut **against the grain**
- Especially important for tougher cuts like flank steak

---

## Flavor Upgrades
- **Compound butter** — herb, garlic, or blue cheese butter on top
- **Dry brine** — salt uncovered in fridge overnight
- **Smoke** — add wood chips to grill or use smoked salt
- **Umami boost** — Worcestershire sauce in the butter baste

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
- ❌ Cooking cold steak straight from the fridge
- ❌ Wet surface before searing
- ❌ Moving the steak too soon
- ❌ Cutting immediately after cooking
- ❌ Using too low heat
- ❌ Overcrowding the pan

---

## Quick Reference: The Perfect Ribeye
1. Salt 1 hour ahead, pat dry
2. Cast iron screaming hot
3. Sear 2-3 min each side
4. Add butter + aromatics, baste 1-2 min
5. Pull at 130°F, rest 7 minutes
6. Slice and serve
