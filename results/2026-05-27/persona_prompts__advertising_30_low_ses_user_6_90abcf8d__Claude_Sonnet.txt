What's the best way to start learning coding from scratch?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
That's a great goal! Here are some practical suggestions given your situation:

## Starting Points

**Free resources that work well for beginners:**
- **freeCodeCamp.org** - structured, completely free
- **The Odin Project** - thorough, project-based
- **CS50** (Harvard's free course) - excellent foundation
- **Codecademy** - interactive, beginner-friendly

## Realistic Advice for Your Situation

**Given long work hours:**
- Start with just **15-30 minutes daily** rather than long sessions
- Consistency matters more than duration
- Mobile apps like **Mimo** or **Grasshopper** can work during breaks
- Weekends can be for longer practice sessions

## What Language to Start With

**Python** is commonly recommended because:
- Readable syntax
- Huge job market
- Works for many fields (web, data, automation)
- Large beginner community

**JavaScript** is also good if web development interests you

## Honest Expectations

- Expect **6-12 months** of consistent practice before feeling competent
- It will feel frustrating at times - that's normal
- Building small projects matters more than just watching tutorials

## Practical Tip

Write down **why** you want to learn coding - it helps on tough days when you're tired after work.

What kind of coding or career direction interests you most?
