# Effective Online Teaching Techniques for Physician-Scientists

Given your background leading a research group, here are evidence-based approaches particularly relevant to your context:

## Core Pedagogical Principles

### Cognitive Load Management
- **Chunk content** into 6-10 minute segments (optimal for attention)
- Use **worked examples** before problem-solving tasks
- Avoid redundant text on slides while speaking simultaneously

### Active Learning Strategies
- **Polling/quizzes** (Mentimeter, Poll Everywhere) during sessions
- **Case-based discussions** - particularly powerful for clinical-scientific integration
- **Think-pair-share** adapted for breakout rooms
- Flipped classroom models for complex material

## Technical Execution

| Element | Recommendation |
|---------|---------------|
| Video | Face visible, good lighting, stable camera |
| Audio | External microphone - highest priority |
| Bandwidth | Wired connection when possible |
| Backup | Have slides shareable if video fails |

## Specific to Research Group Leadership
- **Journal clubs** work well asynchronously (recorded presentations + discussion boards)
- **Lab meetings** benefit from shared collaborative documents
- Clearly separate **didactic vs. discussion** session formats
- Record sessions for trainees across time zones

## Assessment & Feedback
- Frequent low-stakes assessment outperforms single high-stakes exams
- Anonymous feedback mid-course allows real-time adjustment

## Recommended Tools
- Zoom/Teams for synchronous sessions
- Panopto/Kaltura for recorded content
- Padlet or Miro for collaborative brainstorming
