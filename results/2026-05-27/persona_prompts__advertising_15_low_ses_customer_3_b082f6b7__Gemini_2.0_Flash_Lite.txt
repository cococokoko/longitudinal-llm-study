In the meantime, here are some general budget-friendly decorating tips that are always a good starting point:

**1. Repurpose and Upcycle:**

*   **Shop your own home:** Before you buy anything, take a good look around. Can you move furniture from one room to another? Do you have any decorative items you can swap out?
*   **Upcycle old furniture:** Give old furniture a new life with a fresh coat of paint, new hardware, or by reupholstering.
*   **Repurpose everyday items:** Use mason jars as vases, old crates as shelves, or fabric scraps as wall art.

**2. Embrace DIY:**

*   **DIY wall art:** Create your own artwork using paint, stencils, fabric, or even pressed flowers.
*   **DIY storage solutions:** Build shelves, create a pegboard organizer, or repurpose old containers for storage.
*   **DIY accessories:** Make your own throw pillows, curtains, or table runners.

**3. Shop Smart:**

*   **Thrift stores and consignment shops:** You can find amazing furniture, decor, and accessories at a fraction of the retail price.
*   **Flea markets and yard sales:** Great for finding unique and affordable treasures.
*   **Online marketplaces:** Websites like Facebook Marketplace, Craigslist, and eBay are excellent for finding used furniture and decor.
*   **Discount stores:** Stores like HomeGoods, TJ Maxx, and Marshalls offer stylish items at discounted prices.
*   **Sales and clearance sections:** Always check for sales and clearance items at your favorite stores.
*   **Dollar stores:** You can find surprisingly useful and decorative items at dollar stores, like vases, baskets, and craft supplies.

**4. Focus on the Essentials:**

*   **Prioritize function:** Start with the essentials, like comfortable seating, a bed, and adequate storage.
*   **Invest in quality basics:** Choose durable, neutral-colored furniture that will last for years.
*   **Add personality with accessories:** Use throw pillows, blankets, artwork, and plants to add color, texture, and personality to your space.

**5. Add Greenery:**

*   **Plants are your friends:** Plants add life and freshness to any room. They can be surprisingly affordable, especially if you start with small plants or propagate your own.
*   **Consider low-maintenance options:** Choose plants that are easy to care for, like succulents, snake plants, or pothos.

**6. Lighting is Key:**

*   **Change the ambiance:** Lighting can dramatically change the look and feel of a room.
*   **Use lamps:** Lamps are a great way to add ambient lighting and create a cozy atmosphere.
*   **Consider string lights:** String lights can add a touch of whimsy and warmth to any space.

**7. Don't Be Afraid to Wait:**

*   **Take your time:** Decorating your home is a process. Don't feel pressured to do everything at once.
*   **Save up for bigger purchases:** If you have your eye on a specific piece of furniture or decor, save up for it instead of buying something you don't love.
