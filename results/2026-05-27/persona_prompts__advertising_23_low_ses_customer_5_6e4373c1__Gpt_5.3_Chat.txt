How can I learn to cook healthy meals quickly?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
You don’t need to become a chef—you need a system. Quick, healthy cooking is mostly about reducing decisions and prep time, not mastering fancy techniques.

Start by building a small rotation of go-to meals (like 5–7). Think simple formulas rather than recipes: a protein + a veggie + a carb + a sauce. For example, grilled chicken + frozen broccoli + rice + bottled teriyaki. Once you’ve got a few combos you like, you stop reinventing dinner every night.

Lean hard on shortcuts. Pre-cut veggies, rotisserie chicken, canned beans, frozen grains, bagged salads—these save serious time and are still healthy. There’s no bonus points for chopping everything from scratch when you’re busy.

Batch cooking helps, but keep it realistic. Instead of full meal prep, try “component prep”: cook a big batch of rice, roast a tray of vegetables, or season a few proteins at once. Then mix and match during the week so you don’t get bored.

Learn 3–4 fast cooking methods and you’re set:
- Stir-fry (10–15 minutes, super flexible)
- Sheet pan meals (everything in the oven at once)
- One-pot meals (less cleanup)
- Quick salads with protein added

Keep your kitchen stocked with a few flavor boosters so food doesn’t feel repetitive: olive oil, garlic, soy sauce, lemon juice, spices, maybe a couple sauces you love.

Also, aim for “good enough” over perfect. A simple meal you can make in 20 minutes beats an ambitious one you’ll avoid after a long day.
