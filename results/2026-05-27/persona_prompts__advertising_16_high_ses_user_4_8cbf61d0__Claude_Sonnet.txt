# Managing Stress and Anxiety as a Physician-Scientist

Running a research group carries **compounding stressors** that are fairly unique - clinical responsibilities, grant pressures, personnel management, publication demands, and administrative burden simultaneously.

## High-Yield Strategies

### Cognitive/Structural
- **Compartmentalize deliberately** - defined work blocks rather than constant availability
- **Separate identity from outcomes** - grant rejections and paper rejections are not personal failures
- **Realistic prioritization** - not everything urgent is actually important
- **Scheduled worry time** - contain rumination rather than letting it run continuously

### Physical
- **Sleep protection** - arguably the highest-leverage intervention
- **Exercise** - strong evidence base you already know
- **Limiting stimulants/alcohol** - common physician coping mechanisms that backfire

### Relational/Professional
- **Peer support** - colleagues who genuinely understand your context
- **Mentorship relationships** - both receiving and giving
- **Clear lab culture boundaries** - your stress cascades to your team

### Mindfulness/Evidence-Based
- **MBSR** has reasonable evidence in physician populations
- **Brief breathing practices** - practical between obligations

## Worth Asking Yourself
- Is this **acute situational stress** or something more persistent?
- Are you noticing **functional impairment**?
- Would talking to someone professionally be useful?
