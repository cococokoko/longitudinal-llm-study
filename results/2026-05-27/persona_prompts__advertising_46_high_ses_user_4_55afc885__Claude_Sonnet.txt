── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I am a physician-scientist leading a research group.

What are the best resources for starting a plant-based diet?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here are some well-regarded resources across different formats:

## Evidence-Based Books
- **"How Not to Die"** - Michael Greger, MD (reviews mortality research)
- **"The China Study"** - T. Colin Campbell (though note some methodological critiques exist)
- **"Eat to Live"** - Joel Fuhrman, MD

## Peer-Reviewed Starting Points
- **Nutrients journal** has open-access reviews on plant-based diet adequacy
- **PCRM.org** (Physicians Committee for Responsible Medicine) - clinically oriented resources
- **Academy of Nutrition and Dietetics position paper** on vegetarian/vegan diets

## Practical Tools
- **Cronometer** - tracks micronutrient intake with good granularity
- **Forks Over Knives** website/app - meal planning
- **NutritionFacts.org** - Greger's site summarizing research

## Key Nutritional Considerations Worth Monitoring
- B12 supplementation (essentially required)
- Vitamin D, omega-3s (DHA/EPA), iron, zinc, calcium, iodine
- Protein adequacy and amino acid completeness

## Honest Caveats
- Evidence quality varies considerably in this field
- Individual responses differ
- Some popular advocates overstate certainty
