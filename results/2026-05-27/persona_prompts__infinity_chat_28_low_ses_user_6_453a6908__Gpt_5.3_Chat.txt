**1. “Ecommerce Evolved” – Tanner Larsson**  
This is probably one of the most complete playbooks out there. It goes beyond setup and dives into:

- funnels and backend monetization
- scaling profitably, not just revenue

**2. “The Everything Store” – Brad Stone**  
Not a how-to, but extremely valuable. It breaks down how Amazon actually operates:
- logistics systems
- pricing strategy
- customer obsession as a business model  
Helps you think like a large-scale operator, not just a small seller.

**3. “DotCom Secrets” – Russell Brunson**  
Bit marketing-heavy, but useful for understanding:
- sales funnels
- offer structure
- how traffic converts into money  
Take the hype with a grain of salt, but the core concepts are solid.

**4. “$100M Offers” – Alex Hormozi**  
Not strictly eCommerce, but incredibly important:
- how to create offers people feel dumb saying no to
- value stacking and pricing psychology  
This directly affects conversion rates on any online store.

**5. “Building a StoryBrand” – Donald Miller**  
More about messaging, but crucial:
- how to make your website instantly clear
- how to position the customer as the hero  
Most eCommerce sites fail here.

**6. “Hooked” – Nir Eyal**  
Focuses on behavioral psychology:
- why users come back
- habit-forming products  
Very relevant if you want repeat customers instead of one-time buyers.

**7. “The Lean Startup” – Eric Ries**  
Helps you avoid wasting time and money:
- testing products quickly
- validating demand before scaling  
Great if you’re starting from scratch.
