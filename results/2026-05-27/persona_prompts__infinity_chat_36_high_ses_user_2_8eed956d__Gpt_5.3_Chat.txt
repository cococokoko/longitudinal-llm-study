── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I am a senior attorney at a well-established law firm.

Give me some examples of websites where users can select a default landing page?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here are some familiar examples where users can choose or influence their default landing page:

- Google (google.com): You can set a preferred homepage in your browser, and within Google services you can customize what appears first (e.g., Google News or Google Discover feed preferences).

- Yahoo (yahoo.com): Historically strong at this—users can customize their Yahoo homepage with news, finance, weather, etc.

- MSN (msn.com): Microsoft allows personalization of the homepage with topics, layout, and preferred content.

- Reddit (reddit.com): Logged-in users effectively set their “landing page” via subscribed subreddits (home feed vs. r/all vs. custom feeds).

- Twitter/X (x.com): Users can choose between “For You” and “Following” as their default timeline experience.

- Facebook (facebook.com): While not called a “homepage setting,” users can prioritize feeds (Favorites, Groups, Pages), which affects the default landing experience.

- LinkedIn (linkedin.com): The main feed can be influenced by followed topics, connections, and newsletter subscriptions.

- YouTube (youtube.com): The homepage is highly personalized; users can also default to Subscriptions instead of Home via bookmarks or app behavior.

- Notion (notion.so): Users can set a default page to open on startup.

- Slack (slack.com / app): Users can configure which workspace/channel or view opens first.

- Salesforce (salesforce.com): Users can customize default landing tabs and dashboards upon login.

- Atlassian Jira/Confluence: Users can choose dashboards or spaces as their default landing view.

- SharePoint (Microsoft 365): Organizations and users can configure default start pages and sites.

- Banking apps/websites (various): Many allow users to set a preferred account summary or dashboard as the landing page after login.

If you’re thinking in a legal or product-design context, the key distinction is whether the platform offers an explicit “set as default” control versus an implicit personalization based on behavior or subscriptions.
