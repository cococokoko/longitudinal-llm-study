Milo and the Missing Morning

Milo the fox loved mornings more than anything. He loved the golden light that slipped through the trees, the way dew sparkled on the grass, and the cheerful songs of birds waking up the forest.

But one day, Milo woke up and something was very wrong.

It was still dark.

Milo blinked. He stretched. He yawned. He waited.

Nothing.

“No sunrise?” he muttered. “That’s odd.”

He poked his head out of his burrow. The forest was quiet. Too quiet. Even the birds were silent.

Milo decided this was a job for someone very brave and very clever. Luckily, that was him.

He trotted over to see Hazel the Hedgehog.

“Hazel! The morning is missing!” Milo said.

Hazel peeked out from her cozy pile of leaves. “Missing? Did you check under your pillow?”

“This is serious,” Milo said. “If morning doesn’t come, how will flowers bloom? How will we know when to wake up?”

Hazel frowned. “That does sound serious.”

Together, they went to find Otto the Owl, who knew many things.

Otto blinked slowly from his tree. “Hmm,” he said. “I did notice the night seems… longer than usual.”

“So where is the sun?” Milo asked.

Otto fluffed his feathers. “There is only one way to find out. We must go to the Hill of First Light.”

The three of them set off through the dark forest. Along the way, they met Bella the Bunny, who was hopping in circles.

“I can’t tell if I’m late or early!” she cried. “Everything is mixed up!”

“Come with us,” said Milo. “We’re going to fix it.”

At last, they reached the Hill of First Light. But instead of a glowing sunrise, they found a small, glowing ball stuck behind a pile of clouds.

It was the sun.

And it looked… stuck.

“Oh dear,” said Hazel.

“How did this happen?” asked Bella.

A tiny voice squeaked, “I didn’t mean to!”

Out from behind the clouds came Pip the Mouse, trembling.

“I was playing up here,” Pip said, “and I bumped into the clouds. They rolled over and—well—trapped the sun.”

Milo stepped forward. “It’s okay. We can fix this.”

“How?” asked Pip.

Milo looked at his friends. “We push.”

“All of us?” said Hazel.

“All of us,” Milo said.

They lined up—fox, hedgehog, owl, bunny, and mouse—and pushed the clouds with all their might.

“Push!” said Milo.

“Push!” said Bella.

“Push!” squeaked Pip.

At first, nothing happened.

Then… the clouds wiggled.

Then… they rolled.

And suddenly—

WHOOSH!

The sun popped free, rising into the sky and spilling golden light everywhere.

The forest woke up at once. Birds sang. Flowers opened. The world felt right again.

“We did it!” cheered Bella.

Pip smiled shyly. “I’m sorry I caused trouble.”

Milo grinned. “Everyone makes mistakes. What matters is fixing them.”

Otto nodded. “And helping each other.”

From that day on, Pip was much more careful—and also very good at cloud-moving.

And Milo?

He still loved mornings.

But now, he loved his friends even more.
