── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Best skin routine for dry skin.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Dry skin lacks oil (sebum) and often has a compromised skin barrier, leading to flakiness, tightness, dullness, and premature fine lines. The goal of a dry skin routine is to **hydrate** (add water), **moisturize** (add oil), and **protect the skin barrier**.

Here is the ultimate dermatologist-approved morning and night routine for dry skin, along with key ingredients to look for and avoid.

---

### **Morning (AM) Routine: Hydrate & Protect**
The focus of the morning routine is to prep your skin for the day and protect it from environmental damage without stripping its natural oils.

1. **Cleanse (or just rinse):** 
   * *What to do:* Wash your face with **lukewarm water only**, or use a very gentle, non-foaming, hydrating cream cleanser. Avoid foaming cleansers, which strip the skin.
   * *Product type:* Hydrating Cleanser (e.g., *CeraVe Hydrating Cleanser* or *La Roche-Posay Toleriane Hydrating Gentle Cleanser*).
2. **Hydrating Toner or Essence (Apply to damp skin):**
   * *What to do:* Pat a hydrating, alcohol-free toner onto damp skin. This acts like a sponge, helping subsequent products absorb better.
   * *Key ingredients:* Glycerin, Beta-Glucan, or Hyaluronic Acid.
3. **Hydrating Serum:**
   * *What to do:* Apply a serum to lock in water. 
   * *Key ingredients:* Hyaluronic Acid or Vitamin B5 (Panthenol).
4. **Moisturizer:**
   * *What to do:* Apply a medium-to-thick moisturizer to seal in the hydration.
   * *Key ingredients:* Ceramides (to repair the barrier) and Squalane.
5. **Sunscreen (Crucial):**
   * *What to do:* Apply a broad-spectrum SPF 30 or higher. Look for "hydrating" or "dewy" formulas.
   * *Product type:* Chemical sunscreens often feel more moisturizing, but physical sunscreens with zinc oxide are great if your dry skin is also sensitive.

---

### **Evening (PM) Routine: Nourish & Repair**
The night is when your skin repairs itself. This is the time for heavy-duty moisture and barrier repair.

1. **Double Cleanse (If wearing makeup/SPF):**
   * *Step 1:* Use a **cleansing balm or oil** to gently dissolve makeup and sunscreen without stripping the skin.
   * *Step 2:* Follow with your **hydrating cream cleanser** to clean the skin.
2. **Hydrating Toner or Essence:**
   * *What to do:* Apply to damp skin immediately after washing.
3. **Treatment/Barrier Serum:**
   * *What to do:* Use a serum that targets skin barrier repair or gentle anti-aging.
   * *Key ingredients:* Niacinamide (strengthens the barrier) or a gentle Retinol (buffered with moisturizer if your skin is sensitive).
4. **Rich Night Cream:**
   * *What to do:* Use a thick, emollient cream. 
   * *Key ingredients:* Ceramides, Shea Butter, and Fatty Acids. (e.g., *LRP Lipikar AP+M Triple Repair* or *Skinfix Barrier+ Triple Lipid-Peptide Cream*).
5. **Facial Oil or "Slugging" (Optional, but highly recommended for very dry skin):**
