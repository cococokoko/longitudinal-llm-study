── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I am a single parent who often balances childcare with work.

Give me a tip to be more organized at work. I'm a high school teacher.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
As a high school teacher balancing work and childcare, one of the highest-impact organization strategies is to create a "next-day launchpad" before you leave each afternoon.

Spend 10–15 minutes at the end of the school day to:
- Write down your top 3 priorities for tomorrow.
- Lay out or digitally organize any materials you'll need for your first class.
- Open and save the documents you'll use next, or place physical handouts in order.
- Note any time-sensitive parent communications or grading tasks.

This reduces the mental load of deciding where to start when you arrive the next morning, which is especially helpful when childcare responsibilities make mornings unpredictable. Even if the rest of the day changes, you'll begin with a clear plan and avoid losing time searching for materials or remembering what needs attention first.
