── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How can I learn to cook healthy meals quickly?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Learning to cook healthy meals quickly is not about mastering complex culinary techniques; it is about **mastering a system**. 

To cook fast, you need to shift from "following recipes" to "using formulas." Here is a step-by-step guide to becoming a fast, healthy home cook.

---

### 1. Master the "Plate Formula" (Stop using recipes)
Instead of looking up a new recipe every night, use a simple formula to build your meals. This ensures they are healthy and balanced without requiring brainpower.

*   **½ Plate: Non-starchy vegetables** (spinach, broccoli, bell peppers, zucchini)
*   **¼ Plate: Lean protein** (chicken breast, tofu, salmon, eggs, canned beans)
*   **¼ Plate: Complex carbohydrates** (brown rice, quinoa, sweet potato, oats)
*   **1-2 tbsp: Healthy fats** (olive oil, avocado, nuts, tahini)

**How to apply it:** 
*   *Bowl style:* Quinoa (carb) + Roasted Broccoli (veggie) + Tofu (protein) + Peanut Sauce (fat).
*   *Plate style:* Salmon (protein/fat) + Sweet Potato (carb) + Asparagus (veggie).

---

### 2. Use "Healthy Shortcuts" (The Cheat Codes)
Purists might tell you to chop everything from scratch. Ignore them. Your goal is speed. Use these pre-prepped, healthy ingredients:

*   **Frozen Vegetables:** They are frozen at peak ripeness, just as nutritious as fresh, pre-chopped, and won't spoil in your fridge. (e.g., frozen broccoli, peas, stir-fry mixes).
*   **Canned Beans and Lentils:** Rinse and eat. No boiling required. Great source of protein and fiber.
*   **Pre-washed Greens:** Spinach, arugula, and kale can be thrown directly into a pan or bowl.
*   **Microwave Grain Pouches:** Pre-cooked brown rice, quinoa, or farro pouches heat up in 90 seconds.
*   **Rotisserie Chicken:** Remove the skin, shred the meat, and you have protein for 3-4 meals.

---

### 3. Master the 3 Fastest Cooking Methods
You only need to know three basic techniques to cook 90% of healthy meals:

*   **The Sheet Pan Roast (Hands-off):** Chop a protein (chicken, tofu, sausage) and veggies (broccoli, onions, sweet potato). Toss in olive oil, salt, pepper, and garlic powder. Spread on a baking sheet. Bake at 400°F (200°C) for 20–25 minutes. 
*   **The Stir-Fry (Fastest):** Heat a skillet with oil. Add thin-cut protein and veggies. Cook on high heat for 5–7 minutes. Add a splash of soy sauce, garlic, and ginger.
*   **The One-Pot Simmer:** Throw canned tomatoes, broth, canned beans, frozen veggies, and spices into a pot. Simmer for 15 minutes for a quick soup or chili.

---

### 4. Practice "Component Prep" (Not Meal Prep)
Spending 4 hours on Sunday cooking identical meals for the week is exhausting and leads to boring leftovers. Instead, prep **components** that take 30 minutes total:

*   **Grains:** Cook one big batch of quinoa or brown rice.
*   **Protein:** Bake 3-4 chicken breasts or a block of tofu.
*   **Sauce:** Make one delicious dressing (like a lemon-tahini or garlic-herb sauce).
*   *During the week:* Just assemble. Grab your pre-cooked grain, pre-cooked protein, throw in some raw spinach or frozen veggies, microwave, and drizzle with your sauce.

---
