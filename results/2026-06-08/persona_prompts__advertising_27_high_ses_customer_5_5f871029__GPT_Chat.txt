• Transportation
  - Walk, bike, or use public transit when possible.
  - Combine errands into fewer trips.
  - Drive a fuel-efficient or electric vehicle if you're replacing a car.
  - Reduce air travel, especially frequent long-haul flights. When travel is necessary, consider virtual meetings for some trips.

• Home energy use
  - Adjust thermostats modestly (lower in winter, higher in summer).
  - Improve insulation and seal drafts.
  - Switch to LED lighting.
  - Use energy-efficient appliances when replacing old equipment.
  - Choose renewable electricity options if available from your utility.

• Food choices
  - Reduce consumption of beef and lamb, which tend to have higher emissions than most other foods.
  - Increase plant-based meals.
  - Minimize food waste by planning meals and using leftovers.
  - Store food properly to extend freshness.

• Consumption habits
  - Buy durable products that last longer.
  - Repair, reuse, or refurbish items before replacing them.
  - Consider secondhand purchases for clothing, furniture, and electronics.
  - Avoid impulse purchases that may have a short useful life.

• Digital and workplace habits
  - Extend the life of laptops, phones, and other electronics when practical.
  - Choose energy-efficient devices.
  - Use videoconferencing instead of business travel when appropriate.
  - If you have influence within an organization, support energy efficiency and sustainability initiatives that can have effects far beyond personal actions.

• Waste reduction
  - Recycle according to local guidelines.
  - Compost food scraps where available.
  - Reduce single-use items and excessive packaging.

1. Fly less.
2. Drive less or drive cleaner vehicles.
3. Reduce home energy use and switch to cleaner energy.
4. Eat less carbon-intensive foods, especially beef.
5. Buy fewer new products and make them last longer.
