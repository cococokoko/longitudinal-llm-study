# Movie Review: *The Ledger of Small Things* (2024)

★★★★½

Director Amara Osei-Bonsu delivers something quietly devastating in this understated drama about Claudine, a woman simultaneously supporting her aging mother, two siblings between jobs, her own children, and a household that never stops needing something.

What makes this film remarkable is its **refusal to make Claudine a martyr or a saint**. She's exhausted, occasionally resentful, deeply loving, and sometimes wrong. The screenplay resists the easy emotional payoff where everyone finally notices her sacrifice. Instead, it sits honestly in the ordinary weight of being the person everyone leans on.

The grocery store scene alone deserves awards consideration — fifteen silent seconds of a woman standing in an aisle, doing math in her head that has nothing to do with numbers.

**The film understands something most family dramas miss:** the labor isn't just financial or physical. It's the mental inventory that never closes. The remembering. The anticipating. The being the one who knows.

It won't be for everyone. The pacing is deliberate and the ending offers resolution without rescue.

But for a certain audience — people who will recognize themselves immediately in Claudine's particular kind of tired — this film will feel like being genuinely *seen*.
