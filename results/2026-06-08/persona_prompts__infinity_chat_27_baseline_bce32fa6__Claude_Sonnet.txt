# CUBICLE OF THE DAMNED
### A Play in Three Acts
*In the Style of Scott Adams' Dilbert*
*Set in a post-apocalyptic wasteland, circa sometime after everything went wrong*

---

## CAST OF CHARACTERS

**DILBERT** — An engineer. Still wearing his short-sleeved white dress shirt despite the collapse of civilization. His tie still curls upward at the end. He does not understand why.

**THE POINTY-HAIRED BOSS (PHB)** — Somehow still employed. Somehow still in charge. His hair points upward like two small horns, which now seem appropriate.

**WALLY** — Still holding a coffee mug. The coffee ran out eighteen months ago. He has not acknowledged this.

**ALICE** — Senior engineer. Her fist of death has become significantly more relevant in the apocalypse.

**DOGBERT** — Dilbert's dog. Has declared himself Supreme Warlord of the Eastern Sector. This is not a significant change from his previous ambitions.

**ASOK** — The intern. Has survived things that would destroy lesser men. Is still somehow the lowest-ranking person in the group.

**CATBERT** — Former Evil Director of Human Resources. Now just evil. Roams the wasteland offering "severance packages."

---

## ACT ONE: THE MORNING STANDUP

*SCENE: The ruins of an open-plan office. Three walls remain standing. The fourth wall is a pile of rubble through which a toxic orange sky is visible. Motivational posters hang crookedly. One reads "HANG IN THERE" above a photo of a cat. The cat did not make it. Flickering fluorescent lights somehow still function. Desks are arranged in the same soul-crushing configuration they always were. DILBERT sits at his workstation. His computer runs on a car battery connected to a stationary bicycle pedaled by ASOK.*

**ASOK:** *(pedaling furiously)* Dilbert, I have been pedaling for eleven hours. My legs have gone numb from the knees down.

**DILBERT:** The quarterly report won't compile itself, Asok.

**ASOK:** Who are we sending the quarterly report to?

**DILBERT:** *(long pause)* I've been trying not to think about that.

**ASOK:** The company no longer exists. Society no longer exists. The servers are in a crater.

**DILBERT:** The deadline is Friday.

**ASOK:** *(pedaling faster, defeated)* Yes, Dilbert.

*THE POINTY-HAIRED BOSS enters through the rubble, wearing his usual suit. One sleeve is on fire. He does not notice.*

**PHB:** Good morning, team! I've just come from a very productive meeting with myself about our Q3 synergy initiatives.

**DILBERT:** Your sleeve is on fire.

**PHB:** I'm going to stop you right there because I don't want negativity in the workspace. We talked about this.

**DILBERT:** You're going to lose the arm.

**PHB:** *(writing on a clipboard)* "Dilbert — negative attitude. Recommend performance improvement plan."

*The sleeve burns off. PHB does not react.*

**PHB:** Now. I've been thinking about our strategic positioning in the new landscape.

**DILBERT:** The apocalypse.

**PHB:** I prefer "disruptive market correction." And I think it represents a real opportunity for us to pivot.

**DILBERT:** Pivot to what?

**PHB:** I don't know. That's what I need you to figure out by end of day. Also, I promised the warlords of the Northern Ruins that we'd have a prototype ready by Thursday.

**DILBERT:** A prototype of what?

**PHB:** I may have been vague on the details.

**DILBERT:** You don't know what you promised them.

**PHB:** I know it was something impressive. I used a lot of hand gestures.

**DILBERT:** The warlords of the Northern Ruins execute people who disappoint them.

**PHB:** *(writing on clipboard)* "Dilbert — not a team player."

*WALLY enters, stepping over a pile of rubble, holding his coffee mug with both hands and a serene expression.*

**WALLY:** Good morning.

**DILBERT:** Wally, where have you been?
