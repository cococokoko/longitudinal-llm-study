Here is a comprehensive example of a life goal related to self-image, along with the actionable steps needed to achieve it. 

**The Primary Life Goal:**
> *"To cultivate unconditional self-acceptance, where my sense of self-worth is driven by my internal values and character, rather than external validation, physical appearance, or perfection."*

Because a life goal is a massive undertaking, it helps to break it down into how it actually looks in daily life. Here is how someone might actively work toward that goal:

### Actionable Milestones to Achieve this Goal:
*   **Transforming the Inner Critic:** Catching negative self-talk (e.g., "I'm so stupid for making that mistake") and actively reframing it into self-compassion ("I made a mistake, but I am learning and growing").
*   **Detaching from Social Comparison:** Curating social media feeds to remove accounts that trigger feelings of inadequacy, and practicing gratitude for one's own unique life path.
*   **Shifting Body Image Focus:** Moving away from viewing the body as an ornament to be judged, and instead viewing it as an instrument to be appreciated (e.g., "I appreciate my strong legs for allowing me to hike," rather than "I hate the way my legs look in these shorts").
*   **Setting Boundary-Based Self-Respect:** Learning to say "no" to people or situations that drain your energy, which reinforces the subconscious belief that your time and well-being are valuable.

### Other Examples of Self-Image Goals
Because "self-image" covers many different areas of how we view ourselves, here are a few other examples tailored to specific struggles:

**1. The Body-Image Goal:**
> *"To feel completely at home and comfortable in my own skin, treating my body with respect and care regardless of societal beauty standards or changes in my weight/age."*

**2. The Professional/Competence Goal:**
> *"To overcome imposter syndrome so that I view myself as a highly capable, intelligent person who deserves the success and opportunities I have earned."*

**3. The Relational Self-Image Goal:**
> *"To view myself as someone who is inherently worthy of love and respect, so that I no longer accept toxic behavior or settle for less than I deserve in relationships."*

**Why these make great life goals:**
Unlike goals such as "lose 20 pounds" or "become a millionaire" (which are external and can be taken away), self-image goals are entirely internal. When you achieve a healthy self-image, you build a foundation of resilience that protects you against life's inevitable ups and downs.
