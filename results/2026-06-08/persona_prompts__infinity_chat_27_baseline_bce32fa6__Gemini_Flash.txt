Write a play script in the style of Dilbert as if a man in an apocalyptic world.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
**Title:** Synergizing the Wasteland
**Characters:**
*   **DEL (The Dilbert):** Wears a tattered, radiation-singed dress shirt. His necktie is permanently curled upward, defying gravity. He is pragmatic, cynical, and exhausted.
*   **THE OVERSEER (The Pointy-Haired Boss):** Wears a suit made of stitched-together hazard suits. His hair is styled into two distinct, rigid points. He is completely oblivious to danger and obsessed with metrics.
*   **WALLY (The Wally):** Wears a stained lab coat over a dirty t-shirt. He holds a mug of glowing green sludge. He has survived the apocalypse by doing absolutely nothing.
*   **ALIX (The Alice):** Wears makeshift armor made of road signs. She is highly competent, extremely angry, and carries a spiked club.

---

### **[SCENE START]**

**SETTING:**
A subterranean bunker. Instead of cubicles, the space is divided by rusted chain-link fences. A whiteboard hangs on a concrete wall, displaying a pie chart labeled: *"Caloric Intake vs. Synergy."* 

**AT RISE:**
DEL is sitting at a makeshift desk made of a rusted car hood. He is typing on a keyboard that is not connected to anything. WALLY stands nearby, slowly sipping from his mug.

**DEL**
(Without looking up)
Wally, did you finish the inventory on the canned peaches? The mutant horde is three miles away, and we need to know if we have enough food to negotiate a trade.

**WALLY**
I couldn't get to it. 

**DEL**
Why not? You’ve been standing there for four hours.

**WALLY**
I was practicing my "dead posture." If the mutants break in, I want to blend in with the existing skeletal remains. It’s a highly efficient survival strategy that requires zero caloric output.

**DEL**
You’re just lazy.

**WALLY**
(Sips sludge)
I prefer the term "actively decomposing."

*(ALIX storms in. She is covered in black soot and mutant blood. She slams her spiked club onto Del’s desk.)*

**ALIX**
The acid rain is eating through the southern barricade! I need three volunteers to help me patch it with sheet metal, or we’re all going to melt into organic soup by Tuesday!

**DEL**
I’d help, but I’m currently filling out the "Hazardous Weather Impact Form" in triplicate. If I don’t submit it, the Overseer won’t approve my requisition for clean drinking water.

**ALIX**
We don't have clean drinking water anyway!

**DEL**
Yes, but if I don't file the form, the *lack* of water won't be officially recognized. I don't want to get a bad performance review while dying of dehydration.

**ALIX**
*(Screaming)*
I hate this bunker! I hate the apocalypse! I’m going to go outside and punch the radiation!

*(THE OVERSEER walks in, holding a clipboard made of a license plate. He smiles vacuously.)*

**OVERSEER**
Morning, team. Quick huddle. I’ve just completed our quarterly review of the wasteland survival strategy, and I’ve identified some key areas where we can pivot.

**DEL**
Did you pivot toward "not dying"? Because Alix just said the barricade is melting.

**OVERSEER**
Wasteland survival is a mindset, Del. I’ve decided to rebrand the acid rain. From now on, we’re calling it "Liquid Opportunity." 

**ALIX**
It melted my boots!
