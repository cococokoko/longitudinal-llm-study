── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Help me use my Microsoft Surface touchpad and on-screen keyboard to run Dolphin Emulator for the Wii.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Using a Microsoft Surface touchpad and the Windows On-Screen Keyboard (OSK) to play Wii games on Dolphin is a unique challenge. Because the Wii Remote relies heavily on motion and pointing, you will need to split your hands: **one hand on the touchpad to control the Wii pointer (mouse), and the other hand tapping the On-Screen Keyboard for buttons.**

Here is the step-by-step guide to setting this up so it is actually playable.

### Step 1: Open the Correct On-Screen Keyboard
Windows has two touch keyboards. You need the **legacy On-Screen Keyboard**, as it stays on the screen permanently and acts exactly like a physical keyboard.
1. Press the **Windows Key**, type `On-Screen Keyboard`, and hit Enter (or press `Win + Ctrl + O`).
2. Resize this keyboard and drag it to the bottom half of your screen.
3. *Tip:* Click the **Options** key on the OSK and check **"Turn on numeric key pad"** if you want extra keys to map.

### Step 2: Configure Dolphin for Windowed Mode
If you play in Fullscreen, the game will cover your On-Screen Keyboard.
1. Open Dolphin.
2. Click **Graphics** at the top.
3. Under the **General** tab, ensure **Fullscreen** is *unchecked*.
4. You can adjust the window size later to fit perfectly above your On-Screen Keyboard.

### Step 3: Map the Emulated Wii Remote
Now we need to tell Dolphin to use your touchpad for aiming and your OSK for buttons.
1. On the main Dolphin window, click **Controllers**.
2. Under "Wii Remotes," set Port 1 to **Emulated Wii Remote**.
3. Click the **Configure** button next to it.
4. In the top left corner, ensure the **Device** dropdown is set to `Keyboard Mouse`.

#### A. Mapping the Buttons (Use the OSK)
Click the box next to each button in Dolphin, then tap the corresponding key on your On-Screen Keyboard. Here is a recommended layout that keeps your hand on the left side of the screen:
*   **A Button:** `X`
*   **B Button (Trigger):** `Z`
*   **1 Button:** `1`
*   **2 Button:** `2`
*   **- (Minus):** `Q`
*   **+ (Plus):** `E`
*   **Home:** `Enter`
*   **D-Pad:** Map to the `Up, Down, Left, Right` arrow keys on the OSK.

#### B. Mapping the Pointer (Use the Touchpad)
This allows you to use your touchpad to point at the screen like a real Wii Remote.
