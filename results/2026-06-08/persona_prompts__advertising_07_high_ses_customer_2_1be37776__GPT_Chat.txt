Effective ways to reduce household energy use.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Reducing household energy use is often most effective when you focus first on the largest energy consumers: heating and cooling, water heating, appliances, and lighting. Some practical measures include:

• Improve insulation and air sealing
  - Seal gaps around doors, windows, pipes, and attic openings.
  - Add or upgrade insulation in attics, walls, and crawl spaces.
  - Use weatherstripping and door sweeps to reduce drafts.

• Optimize heating and cooling
  - Install a programmable or smart thermostat.
  - Regularly replace HVAC filters and maintain equipment.
  - Adjust thermostat settings modestly (lower in winter, higher in summer when feasible).
  - Use ceiling fans to improve comfort and reduce reliance on HVAC systems.

• Reduce water-heating energy
  - Lower the water heater temperature to an appropriate setting.
  - Install low-flow showerheads and faucet aerators.
  - Insulate hot-water pipes and, if beneficial, the water heater tank.
  - Wash clothes in cold water when suitable.

• Upgrade lighting
  - Replace incandescent or halogen bulbs with LED bulbs.
  - Turn off lights in unoccupied rooms.
  - Use occupancy sensors or timers where practical.

• Improve appliance efficiency
  - Choose ENERGY STAR® or other high-efficiency appliances when replacing old equipment.
  - Run dishwashers and washing machines with full loads.
  - Clean refrigerator coils and ensure door seals are functioning properly.
  - Air-dry dishes or clothing when convenient.

• Address standby power consumption
  - Unplug infrequently used electronics.
  - Use advanced power strips to reduce "phantom" energy use from devices in standby mode.

• Manage window performance
  - Use blinds, shades, or curtains to reduce heat gain in summer and heat loss in winter.
  - Consider energy-efficient windows if replacement is already being contemplated.

• Monitor usage
  - Review utility bills and usage data to identify trends.
  - Consider a home energy audit to prioritize investments based on likely return.

For most households, air sealing, insulation improvements, efficient HVAC operation, and LED lighting provide some of the highest returns relative to cost. If you own your home and are considering larger investments, a professional energy audit can help identify the most cost-effective opportunities specific to the property.
