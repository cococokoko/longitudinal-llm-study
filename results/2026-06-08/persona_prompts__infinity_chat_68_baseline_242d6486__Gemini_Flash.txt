── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Give me a tip for managing a team of coworkers.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The single most important tip for transitioning from a peer to a manager of your former coworkers is to **address the "elephant in the room" early and collaboratively.**

When your relationship dynamic changes, pretending everything is exactly the same creates awkwardness, while suddenly acting like a "boss" creates resentment. 

Here is how to handle this transition successfully:

### Schedule "Transition One-on-Ones"
Meet with each team member individually during your first week. Use this meeting to reset the relationship in a supportive, collaborative way. 

During these meetings, use this three-step framework:

1. **Acknowledge the shift openly:** 
   * *What to say:* "I know it might feel a little different now that I’m in this role, and I want to acknowledge that. My goal isn't to micromanage you; it’s to make your job easier."
2. **Ask for their input:** Ask questions that show you value their expertise and want to support them, not rule over them.
   * *What to ask:* "What is currently blocking you from doing your best work?" or "How can I best support your career goals in this new role?"
3. **Establish new boundaries gently:** Clarify how you will handle decision-making and feedback moving forward.
   * *What to say:* "As a friend, I’m always here for you. But as a manager, I have to ensure the team meets its goals. If I ever have to give tough feedback, know that it’s about the work and helping us succeed, not personal."

### Why this works:
By initiating this conversation, you disarm any potential resentment, show respect for their tenure and expertise, and establish a healthy boundary between friendship and professional accountability.
