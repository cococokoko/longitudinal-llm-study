── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Give me a tip for managing a team of coworkers.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The single most effective tip for managing a team of coworkers—especially if you were recently their peer—is to **shift your mindset from "boss" to "enabler."**

When you lead people who used to be your equals, pulling rank or acting like a traditional "boss" will almost always trigger resentment. Instead, position yourself as the person whose job it is to make *their* jobs easier. 

Here is how to put this into practice:

### 1. Ask the "Magic Question"
In your one-on-ones, regularly ask this question: 
> *"What is currently blocking you from doing your best work, and how can I help remove that obstacle?"*

This reframes your authority. You aren't policing them; you are using your new influence to clear roadblocks (like bureaucratic red tape, lack of resources, or conflicting priorities) so they can succeed.
