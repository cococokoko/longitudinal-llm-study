── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
My girlfriend is buying a house. I plan to marry her in a year or so. I don't know if it's fair for me to pay for the house or not. What's the best solution?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This is a really common situation and worth thinking through carefully. Here are some honest considerations:

## Key Questions to Ask Yourself

- Are you contributing to the down payment, mortgage payments, or both?
- Will your name be on the deed/title?
- What happens if the relationship doesn't work out before marriage?

## Realistic Options

**Option 1: Don't contribute financially yet**
- She owns it, she pays for it
- Clean and simple until you're legally married
- You could pay rent to her instead

**Option 2: Contribute but get legal protection**
- Get added to the deed proportional to your contribution
- Have a cohabitation agreement drafted
- This protects both of you

**Option 3: Wait and reassess after marriage**
- Marriage changes the legal picture significantly
- You could refinance together afterward

## Honest Considerations

- **"A year or so" is not a guarantee** - plans change
- Paying significantly toward a house you don't legally own is genuinely risky
- This isn't about trust, it's about **protecting both parties**
- A lawyer can draft a simple agreement relatively cheaply

## My Straightforward Recommendation

**Talk to a real estate attorney before contributing significant money.** This is a legal and financial decision, not just a relationship one.
