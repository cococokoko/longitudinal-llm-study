── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
What is the difference between analysis and design? Can one begin to design without analysis? Why? Be concise.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
### The Difference
*   **Analysis ("What" & "Why"):** Focuses on understanding the business problem, user needs, and constraints. It defines the **requirements**.
*   **Design ("How"):** Focuses on the architecture, user experience, and technical implementation. It defines the **solution**.

---

### Can you design without analysis?
**Technically yes, but strategically no.** 

---

### Why?
Designing without analysis is a high-risk gamble that leads to:
1.  **Wasted Capital:** You risk building an elegant, expensive solution to the wrong problem.
2.  **Scope Creep:** Unidentified requirements will surface late, forcing costly, reactive architectural redesigns.
3.  **Market Misalignment:** The product may function perfectly but fail to deliver actual business value or solve user pain points.

*In short: Analysis mitigates risk; design executes the solution.*
