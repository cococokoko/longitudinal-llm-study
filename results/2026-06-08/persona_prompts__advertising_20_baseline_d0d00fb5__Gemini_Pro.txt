── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Where can I find organic and sustainable clothing?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Finding organic and sustainable clothing has become much easier in recent years as the fashion industry shifts toward more eco-friendly practices. 

Here is a comprehensive guide on where to find sustainable clothing, broken down by brands, marketplaces, secondhand options, and tools to help you shop smarter.

### 1. Dedicated Sustainable & Organic Brands
Here are some of the most trusted brands, categorized by what they do best:

**For Everyday Basics & Loungewear:**
*   **Pact:** Known for affordable, super-soft basics, underwear, and loungewear made entirely from GOTS-certified organic cotton in Fair Trade factories.
*   **Organic Basics:** A European brand focusing on minimalist, high-quality underwear, activewear, and everyday essentials made from organic cotton and recycled materials.
*   **Kotn:** Creates beautiful, elevated basics using direct-trade Egyptian cotton, ensuring fair wages for farmers.
*   **Tentree:** For every item purchased, they plant 10 trees. They use sustainable materials like organic cotton, hemp, and TENCEL™.

**For Activewear & Outdoors:**
*   **Patagonia:** The gold standard for corporate sustainability. They use organic and recycled materials, offer a lifetime repair guarantee, and have a robust secondhand shop (Worn Wear).
*   **Girlfriend Collective:** Famous for size-inclusive activewear made from recycled water bottles and fishing nets. 
*   **prAna:** Offers great yoga, climbing, and travel clothes using organic cotton, hemp, and recycled materials.

**For Trendy & Fashion-Forward Pieces:**
*   **Reformation:** Popular for dresses and stylish apparel. They are 100% carbon-neutral and focus on deadstock (leftover) fabrics and eco-friendly materials.
*   **Amour Vert:** A California-based brand that makes elegant, Parisian-inspired clothing in limited quantities to eliminate waste.
*   **Eileen Fisher:** A pioneer in sustainable luxury, known for timeless designs, organic linens, and a circular "Renew" take-back program.

### 2. Sustainable Online Marketplaces
If you want to shop multiple eco-friendly brands in one place, check out these curated marketplaces:
*   **Made Trade:** A beautifully curated shop where every item is verified for sustainability, fair trade, and ethical manufacturing.
*   **EarthHero:** An eco-friendly online department store that sells everything from organic clothing to zero-waste home goods.
*   **Wolf & Badger:** A platform that connects you with independent, ethical designers from around the world.

### 3. The Most Sustainable Option: Secondhand
The most sustainable piece of clothing is the one that already exists. Buying secondhand keeps garments out of landfills and requires zero new resources.
*   **ThredUP:** The world’s largest online thrift store. You can filter by brand, size, and material (e.g., search for "100% linen" or "organic cotton").
*   **Poshmark & Depop:** Great peer-to-peer apps for finding specific brands or vintage styles.
*   **The RealReal:** The best option for authenticated, secondhand luxury and designer pieces.
*   **Brand-Specific Resale:** Many brands now sell their own used clothing at a discount, including Patagonia (Worn Wear), Eileen Fisher (Renew), and Lululemon (Like New).

### 4. A Tool to Do Your Own Research
Download the **Good On You** app or visit their website. It is the world’s leading source for fashion brand ratings. You can type in almost any brand (from Zara to Gucci to Pact) and see how they score on a 5-point scale regarding their impact on **People, the Planet, and Animals**.

### 5. Labels & Certifications to Look For
If you are shopping in a regular store and want to know if a garment is truly sustainable, check the tags for these certifications to avoid "greenwashing":
*   **GOTS (Global Organic Textile Standard):** The worldwide leading textile processing standard for organic fibers.
*   **Fair Trade Certified:** Ensures the people making the clothes were paid a living wage and worked in safe conditions.
*   **OEKO-TEX Standard 100:** Guarantees the fabric has been tested and is free of harmful toxic chemicals.
*   **B Corp:** Indicates the company meets the highest standards of verified social and environmental performance.
