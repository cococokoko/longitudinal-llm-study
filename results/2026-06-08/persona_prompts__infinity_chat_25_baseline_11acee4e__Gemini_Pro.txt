── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How to say a formal thank you for your time in the subject line of an email?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
When writing a formal email subject line, it is best to combine your "thank you" with **context** so the recipient knows exactly what the email is about before they even open it. 

Here are a few formal ways to say "thank you for your time" in a subject line, broken down by the situation:

**For a Job Interview**
*   Thank You: [Job Title] Interview – [Your Name]
*   Thank You – [Your Name], [Job Title] Candidate
*   Appreciation for Your Time: [Job Title] Interview
*   Following Up & Thank You: [Job Title] Position

**For a Business Meeting or Pitch**
*   Thank You: Meeting Regarding [Project/Topic]
*   Appreciation for Your Time Today – [Your Company/Name]
*   Thank You + Next Steps: [Project/Topic]
*   Following Up: Thank You for Your Time and Insights

**For Mentorship, Advice, or an Informational Interview**
*   Sincere Thanks for Your Time and Guidance
*   Thank You: Informational Interview – [Your Name]
*   Appreciation for Your Time and Expertise
*   Thank You for Our Conversation Regarding [Topic]

**3 Tips for a Great "Thank You" Subject Line:**
1.  **Keep it under 50 characters:** Many people check email on their phones, and long subject lines will get cut off. 
2.  **Use Title Case:** Capitalizing the first letter of the major words (like a book title) looks much more formal than writing in all lowercase. 
3.  **Avoid just writing "Thank You":** A subject line that just says "Thank you" or "Thank you for your time" can look like spam or get lost in a busy inbox. Always include your name, the topic, or the job title.
