Here are some effective ways to improve your home WiFi:

## Placement & Hardware
- **Move your router** to a central location, elevated, away from walls and obstructions
- **Upgrade your router** if it's more than 3-4 years old
- Consider a **mesh network system** (Eero, Google Nest, Orbi) for larger homes

## Settings & Configuration
- **Change your WiFi channel** to avoid interference from neighbors
- Use **5GHz band** for faster speeds near the router; **2.4GHz** for longer range
- Keep router **firmware updated**
- Set a strong password to prevent unauthorized users

## Reduce Interference
- Keep router away from microwaves, cordless phones, and baby monitors
- Distance from **thick walls, metal objects, and appliances**

## Extend Coverage
- Add a **WiFi extender/repeater** for dead zones
- Use **powerline adapters** to extend network through electrical wiring
- Run **ethernet cables** for stationary devices when possible

## Quick Wins
- **Restart your router** regularly
- Check if your **ISP plan** matches your actual needs
- Limit bandwidth-heavy background apps/devices

## Worth Considering
- Contact your ISP to check for line issues
- Run a speed test to establish your baseline
