Write 100-300 words on how stress affects the body and mind.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
When stress becomes a constant companion rather than a temporary reaction, it takes a heavy toll on both the body and the mind. 

Physically, chronic stress keeps the body in a perpetual "fight-or-flight" mode, flooding your system with cortisol and adrenaline. Over time, this constant state of alert manifests as physical exhaustion, persistent muscle tension (especially in your shoulders, back, and feet), and tension headaches. When your daily schedule is unpredictable, stress also wreaks havoc on your sleep cycles and digestion, making it incredibly difficult to truly recharge, even when you finally have a moment to sit down.

Mentally, the impact is just as draining. Stress hijacks the brain’s ability to focus, leading to "brain fog," forgetfulness, and difficulty making decisions. It wears down your emotional reserves, leaving you feeling irritable, anxious, or completely overwhelmed by minor setbacks. When you are constantly adapting to changing, fast-paced demands, your mind never feels fully "off the clock," leading to a state of mental burnout where you feel constantly on edge.

Recognizing these symptoms is crucial. When life demands constant flexibility and high energy, understanding that your fatigue and anxiety are real, physical responses to stress can help you practice self-compassion and prioritize small, quiet moments of recovery.
