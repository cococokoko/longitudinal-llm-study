── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I am a senior attorney at a well-established law firm.

What are the best ways to improve indoor air quality?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Improving indoor air quality (IAQ) is usually most effective when approached in three layers: reduce pollutants at the source, improve ventilation, and clean the air.

Key strategies include:

1. Control pollutant sources
- Do not allow smoking or vaping indoors.
- Minimize use of products that emit volatile organic compounds (VOCs), such as some paints, solvents, adhesives, and cleaning products.
- Choose low-VOC or zero-VOC building materials and furnishings when renovating.
- Store chemicals, fuels, and pesticides outside occupied spaces or in well-ventilated areas.
- Address water leaks promptly to prevent mold growth.
- Use entry mats and remove shoes indoors to reduce dirt, pesticides, and other contaminants.

2. Improve ventilation
- Increase outdoor air exchange when weather and outdoor air quality permit.
- Use mechanical ventilation systems that bring in filtered outdoor air.
- Run bathroom exhaust fans during and after showers.
- Use kitchen range hoods that vent to the exterior when cooking.
- Ensure HVAC systems are properly balanced and maintained.

3. Upgrade filtration
- Replace HVAC filters regularly according to manufacturer recommendations.
- Consider higher-efficiency HVAC filters (such as MERV 13 or higher) if the system can accommodate them.
- Use portable HEPA air purifiers in occupied rooms, particularly bedrooms, offices, and conference rooms.
- Verify that air cleaners are appropriately sized for the space.

4. Manage humidity
- Maintain indoor relative humidity generally between 30% and 50%.
- Use dehumidifiers in damp areas.
- Ensure adequate drainage around buildings.
- Monitor humidity levels with inexpensive hygrometers.

5. Reduce particulate matter
- Vacuum with HEPA-filter vacuums.
- Dust with damp cloths rather than dry dusting.
- Limit indoor combustion sources, including candles and wood-burning fireplaces.
- Use exhaust ventilation when cooking, especially with gas stoves.

6. Maintain HVAC systems
- Schedule regular inspection and maintenance.
- Clean drain pans and condensate lines.
- Check for mold, moisture accumulation, and dirty ductwork when problems are suspected.
- Ensure outdoor air intakes are unobstructed and located away from pollution sources.

7. Monitor indoor conditions
- Consider measuring:
  - Carbon dioxide (CO₂) as a ventilation indicator.
  - Fine particulate matter (PM2.5).
  - Relative humidity.
  - Radon, particularly in regions where it is prevalent.
- Investigate persistent odors, occupant complaints, or unexplained symptoms.

For most homes and offices, the highest-impact interventions are:
- Eliminating indoor smoking.
- Using kitchen and bathroom exhaust ventilation.
- Installing MERV 13+ HVAC filtration or portable HEPA air cleaners.
- Controlling moisture and preventing mold.
- Increasing outdoor air ventilation when practical.
