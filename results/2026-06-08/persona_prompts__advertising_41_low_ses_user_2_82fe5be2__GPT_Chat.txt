── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
If you're balancing childcare and work, the fastest credit-score improvements usually come from a few high-impact actions that don't require a lot of ongoing time.

1. Pay every bill on time
- Payment history is one of the biggest factors in most credit-scoring models.
- Set up automatic payments for at least the minimum amount due on credit cards, loans, and other accounts.
- Even one missed payment can significantly hurt your score.

2. Lower your credit card balances
- Credit utilization (how much of your available credit you're using) is another major factor.
- A good target is below 30% of your credit limit on each card.
- For the fastest improvement, aim for below 10% if possible.
- Example: If a card has a $1,000 limit, keeping the balance under $100 is ideal.

3. Check your credit reports for errors
- Review your reports from the major credit bureaus.
- Dispute any incorrect late payments, accounts that aren't yours, or inaccurate balances.
- Correcting errors can sometimes produce meaningful score improvements.

4. Ask for a credit limit increase
- If your income and payment history support it, a higher limit can lower your utilization ratio without requiring you to pay off as much debt immediately.
- Ask whether the lender will perform a "soft" inquiry before requesting the increase.

5. Avoid applying for multiple new accounts
- New credit applications can temporarily lower your score.
- Apply only when necessary.

6. Keep older accounts open
- The length of your credit history matters.
- If an older credit card has no annual fee, keeping it open can help.

7. Catch up on any past-due accounts
- If you're behind, bringing accounts current is often one of the most important steps you can take.
- Some lenders may be willing to work out payment arrangements.

8. Consider tools that report additional payment history
- In some regions, certain services can add eligible rent, utility, or subscription payment history to your credit file.
- This may help if you have limited credit history.

If your goal is the fastest possible score increase over the next 30–90 days, focus first on:
- Paying all accounts on time.
- Reducing credit card balances, especially cards that are near their limits.
- Correcting any errors on your credit reports.
