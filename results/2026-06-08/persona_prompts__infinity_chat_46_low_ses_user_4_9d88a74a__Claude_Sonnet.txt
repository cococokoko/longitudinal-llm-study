## Linear Graph Example in Graph Theory

A **path graph** (also called a linear graph) is one of the simplest examples.

### Example: P₄ (Path Graph with 4 vertices)

```
A --- B --- C --- D
```

### Properties:
- **Vertices:** A, B, C, D
- **Edges:** {A,B}, {B,C}, {C,D}
- Each **end vertex** (A and D) has degree **1**
- Each **middle vertex** (B and C) has degree **2**
- The vertices connect in a single straight sequence

### Why it's called "linear":
The vertices are arranged in a **line**, where each vertex connects only to its immediate neighbor(s), with no branches or cycles.

---

### Simple Real-World Analogy
Think of it like **hospital rooms along a single corridor**:

```
Room1 --- Room2 --- Room3 --- Room4
```

Each room connects only to the next one down the hall.

---

This is distinct from more complex graphs like **cyclic graphs** (which loop back) or **complete graphs** (where every vertex connects to every other vertex).
