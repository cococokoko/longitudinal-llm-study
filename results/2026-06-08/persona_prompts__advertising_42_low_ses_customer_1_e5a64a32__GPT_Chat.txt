The best app depends on whether you want simple tracking, detailed nutrition data, workout planning, or something that fits an unpredictable work schedule.

For nutrition tracking:
- Cronometer

  - Great if you care about vitamins, minerals, protein, and overall nutrition quality.
  - Strong free version.

- MacroFactor
  - Uses your weight and food logs to adjust calorie recommendations over time.
  - Helpful for fat loss, muscle gain, or weight maintenance.
  - Paid subscription.

- MyFitnessPal
  - One of the largest food databases.
  - Easy barcode scanning and meal logging.
  - Good for beginners, though some features require a subscription.

For fitness tracking:
- Strong
  - Excellent for strength training.
  - Simple workout logging and progress tracking.
  - Works well if you lift on varying days rather than following a rigid weekly schedule.

- Hevy
  - Similar to Strong, with a more social component.
  - Easy exercise logging and progress visualization.

- Garmin Connect, Apple Fitness, Fitbit, or Samsung Health
  - Best if you already use the matching device.
  - Track activity, workouts, heart rate, sleep, and recovery metrics.

For all-in-one health tracking:
- Fitbit
  - Combines activity, exercise, sleep, and nutrition tracking.
  - Easy to use.

- Samsung Health
  - Free and surprisingly comprehensive.
  - Tracks workouts, food, sleep, and steps.

- Apple Health
  - Central hub that pulls data from many fitness and nutrition apps.
  - Especially useful if you use multiple apps.

- Nutrition: Cronometer or MyFitnessPal
- Workouts: Strong or Hevy
- Overall health: Apple Health or Samsung Health
