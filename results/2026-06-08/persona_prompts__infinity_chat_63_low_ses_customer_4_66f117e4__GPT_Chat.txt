── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Give a numerical example to illustrate the concept of partial derivative.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
A partial derivative measures how a function changes with respect to one variable while keeping the other variables fixed.

Example:

Suppose the number of patients needing assistance during a shift is modeled by

f(x, y) = 2x² + 3xy + y²

where:
- x = number of hours into the shift
- y = number of patients currently assigned
- f(x, y) = workload score

Partial derivative with respect to x:

Treat y as a constant:

∂f/∂x = 4x + 3y

Evaluate at x = 2 and y = 5:

∂f/∂x(2,5) = 4(2) + 3(5) = 8 + 15 = 23

Interpretation:
- At the point (2,5), increasing x by a small amount increases the workload score at a rate of about 23 units per additional hour, assuming the number of assigned patients stays fixed at 5.

Partial derivative with respect to y:

Treat x as a constant:

∂f/∂y = 3x + 2y

Evaluate at x = 2 and y = 5:

∂f/∂y(2,5) = 3(2) + 2(5) = 6 + 10 = 16

Interpretation:
- At the point (2,5), increasing the number of assigned patients by a small amount increases the workload score at a rate of about 16 units per additional patient, assuming the shift time stays fixed at 2 hours.

This illustrates the key idea: each partial derivative measures the effect of changing one variable while holding the others constant.
