### 1. The Undisputed Champion: Quantitative Statistical Arbitrage
The most successful trading entity in history is **Renaissance Technologies**, specifically their **Medallion Fund**, run by mathematician Jim Simons. From 1988 to 2018, the fund generated average annual returns of **66% before fees** (about 39% net of fees). They have minted dozens of billionaires and have never had a losing year in decades.

#### How the Strategy Works:
*   **The Core Premise:** StatArb does not try to predict where a stock will go in the long term. Instead, it looks for historical, mathematical relationships between assets that have temporarily decoupled.
*   **Mean Reversion:** If Stock A and Stock B historically move together, and Stock A suddenly drops while Stock B rises with no fundamental news, the algorithm will buy Stock A and short Stock B, betting they will revert to their historical average.
*   **High Frequency:** These trades are held for seconds, minutes, or hours—the definition of day trading. They win only about 50.5% to 51% of their trades, but they execute millions of trades a day. Over millions of iterations, a 51% win rate is a statistical certainty of wealth generation (similar to the house edge in a casino).

#### The Catch for Individuals:
This strategy requires supercomputers, direct-market-access fiber-optic cables, and PhDs in astrophysics. It is virtually impossible for an individual retail trader to execute this at the institutional level.

The answer is **Event-Driven Catalyst Trading (specifically, News and Earnings Gap Trading)**. 

As an attorney, your core skill is analyzing complex information quickly and assessing probability. This strategy leverages that exact skill set.

#### How it Works:
1.  **The Catalyst:** You look for stocks that have a major catalyst overnight or pre-market (e.g., earnings beats/misses, FDA approvals, regulatory rulings, M&A announcements).
2.  **The Gap:** The stock "gaps" up or down significantly before the market opens.
3.  **The Execution (The "Opening Range Breakout" or "ORB"):**
