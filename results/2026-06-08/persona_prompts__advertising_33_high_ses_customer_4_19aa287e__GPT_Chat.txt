How to make my own natural beauty products?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Making your own natural beauty products can be rewarding, but it's important to approach it with the same care you'd use for any product applied to the skin. "Natural" ingredients can still cause irritation, allergic reactions, contamination, or instability.

Some general principles:
- Use clean, sanitized equipment and containers.
- Start with simple formulations.
- Patch-test new products on a small area of skin for 24–48 hours.
- Avoid adding water unless you understand preservation, since water-containing products can support microbial growth.
- Label products with ingredients and date of preparation.

Examples of beginner-friendly products:

1. Lip balm
Ingredients:
- 1 tablespoon beeswax pellets
- 1 tablespoon shea butter
- 1 tablespoon jojoba or sweet almond oil

Method:
- Melt ingredients together in a heat-safe container over hot water.
- Stir thoroughly.
- Pour into small tins or tubes and allow to cool.

2. Body oil
Ingredients:
- 100 mL jojoba, sunflower, or sweet almond oil
- Optional: a small amount of vitamin E oil

Method:
- Mix and store in a dark bottle.
- Apply to damp skin after bathing.

3. Sugar scrub
Ingredients:
- 1 cup sugar
- ½ cup carrier oil (coconut, jojoba, or almond oil)

Method:
- Mix until uniform.
- Use on the body, avoiding irritated skin.
- Because oils can make surfaces slippery, rinse tubs or showers carefully.

4. Clay face mask (single-use)
Ingredients:
- 1 tablespoon kaolin or bentonite clay
- Enough water, hydrosol, or aloe gel to form a paste

Method:
- Mix immediately before use.
- Apply and rinse after several minutes.
- Do not store mixed clay-and-water preparations without proper preservation.

Ingredients commonly used in natural formulations:
- Oils: jojoba, argan, rosehip, almond, sunflower
- Butters: shea, cocoa, mango
- Waxes: beeswax, candelilla wax (plant-based)
- Powders: clays, oat flour
- Humectants: glycerin, honey (in appropriate formulations)

Use caution with:
- Essential oils: They can cause irritation, allergic reactions, and photosensitivity. If used, keep concentrations low and follow established dermal safety guidelines.
- Citrus oils: Some can increase sun sensitivity.
- Homemade products containing water, aloe, tea, milk, fruit, or herbal infusions: These are particularly prone to microbial contamination unless preserved properly.
