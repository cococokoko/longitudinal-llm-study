### 1. The Challenges and Negative Consequences

Because most algorithms are designed to maximize **user engagement** (keeping people on the platform as long as possible), they tend to prioritize content that triggers strong emotional reactions. This design choice has several downstream effects:

*   **Echo Chambers and Filter Bubbles:** Algorithms learn what users like and agree with, continuously feeding them similar viewpoints. Over time, this insulates individuals from opposing perspectives, creating "filter bubbles." This lack of exposure to diverse viewpoints can lead to increased political and social polarization.
*   **The Amplification of Outrage and Sensationalism:** Content that provokes anger, fear, or moral outrage typically receives the highest engagement (clicks, shares, comments). Consequently, algorithms promote sensationalized or extreme viewpoints over nuanced, moderate, or complex discussions.
