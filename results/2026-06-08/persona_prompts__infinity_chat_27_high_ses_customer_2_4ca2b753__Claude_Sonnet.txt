# CUBICLE OF THE DAMNED
### A Play in the Style of Scott Adams, Set During the Apocalypse

---

**CHARACTERS:**

**DILBERT** — An engineer. Still wearing his tie. It curves upward despite everything.

**DOGBERT** — A small dog with delusions of godhood. Thriving, frankly.

**THE POINTY-HAIRED BOSS (PHB)** — Somehow still employed. By whom is unclear.

**WALLY** — Has not noticed the apocalypse. Has noticed the coffee is gone.

**ALICE** — Her fist of death is now literally a fist of death.

**THE INTERN** — Has been here since before the collapse. No one remembers hiring him.

---

## ACT ONE: THE MORNING STANDUP

*A scorched wasteland. Rubble everywhere. A single fluorescent light flickers overhead, suspended from nothing. Four office chairs sit in a circle around a whiteboard that reads "Q4 SURVIVAL GOALS." DILBERT enters, briefcase in hand, tie pointing skyward. He steps over a skeleton.*

**DILBERT:**
Good morning. I see the Henderson account is still unresolved.

*WALLY enters carrying an empty coffee mug. He stares into it with profound grief.*

**WALLY:**
The coffee machine was destroyed in the firestorm of civilization's collapse.

**DILBERT:**
When do they expect it back online?

**WALLY:**
They don't. Society has ended. There is no "they."

**DILBERT:**
So... end of week?

**WALLY:**
nnnnYes. End of week.

*THE POINTY-HAIRED BOSS enters wearing a hardhat over his pointy hair, which simply punctures through it.*

**PHB:**
Good news, people. I've been doing some big-picture thinking.

**DILBERT:**
*(to audience)*
He's been doing big-picture thinking since the meteor hit. The picture keeps getting bigger. The thinking does not.

**PHB:**
I've decided we need to pivot.

**ALICE:**
*(entering, knuckles glowing)*
Pivot from what? The office is a crater. Our clients are ash. The internet has been replaced by screaming.

**PHB:**
Right, so this is actually a huge opportunity.

**ALICE:**
I want to hit him so hard he becomes a different geological era.

**PHB:**
I'm thinking we rebrand. Instead of "software solutions," we become "post-apocalyptic software solutions." It's very on-trend.

**DILBERT:**
Who is the customer?

**PHB:**
Survivors.

**DILBERT:**
What is their budget?

**PHB:**
Canned goods and emotional trauma.

**DILBERT:**
What is our margin?

**PHB:**
*(long pause)*
I'll need you to build a spreadsheet.

**DILBERT:**
*(to audience)*
I have been asked to build a spreadsheet. Excel was destroyed in the firestorm. I will build the spreadsheet in the dirt with a stick. This is not the most pointless spreadsheet I have ever built.
