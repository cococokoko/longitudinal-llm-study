### The Shortcut vs. The Long Way

Imagine I ask you: *"If I give you 2 apples every day for 10 days, how many apples will you have?"* 
You don't need to sit there for 10 days to find out. You can just do a quick math trick in your head ($2 \times 10 = 20$) and give me the answer instantly. You took a **shortcut**. 

But now, imagine we are playing a game of **Pinball**. 
I pull the plunger back and shoot the silver ball into the machine. I ask you: *"Exactly where will the ball be in 45 seconds?"*

There is no math trick for this. You can't just multiply two numbers to find the answer. The ball is going to bounce off a bumper, hit a flipper, roll down a ramp, and hit another bumper. The *only* way to know where the ball will be in 45 seconds is to stand there and watch it bounce around for exactly 45 seconds. 

You cannot skip to the end. You have to let it play out, step by step. 

That is **computational irreducibility**. 
*   **Compute** = to figure something out.
*   **Irreducible** = cannot be made shorter. 

It is a fancy science term that simply means: **Some things in life have no shortcuts. You just have to go through every single step to see how it turns out.**

***

### How this applies to your new job

Right now, you are the pinball, and your probation period is the pinball machine. 

You might be feeling anxious, wishing there was a "shortcut" or a magic formula you could memorize to instantly prove to your boss that you are great at this job. You might wish you could just skip to month six.

But a new job is **computationally irreducible**. 
* You can't build trust with your boss using a shortcut; you have to show up and do good work day after day.
* You can't instantly know the company culture; you have to experience a bunch of meetings and water-cooler chats to understand it.
* You can't magically know how to solve every problem; you have to make a few small mistakes, ask questions, and learn from them.

Every single day builds on the day before it. You cannot skip the steps. 

### The Takeaway
If you feel like things are taking a long time to "click," or if you feel like you are just taking it one day at a time—**that means you are doing it exactly right.** 

There is no cheat code to skip the probation period. Just show up, take it one step at a time, and let the process play out. You've got this!
