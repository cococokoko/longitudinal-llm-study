**Question:** 
Unlike birds with red or yellow feathers, bluebirds (and almost all other blue-colored birds) do not actually possess any blue pigment in their feathers. What physical phenomenon causes them to appear blue to the human eye?

**Answer:** 
**Structural coloration** (specifically, light scattering or the Tyndall effect). 

The feathers of a bluebird contain microscopic air pockets and keratin structures that scatter incoming light, reflecting only the blue wavelengths back to our eyes while absorbing the other colors. If you were to crush a bluebird feather, it would lose its structure and look dull brown or gray!
