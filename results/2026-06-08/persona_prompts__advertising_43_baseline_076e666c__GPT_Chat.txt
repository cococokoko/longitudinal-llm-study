── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How to plan an environmentally friendly wedding?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Planning an environmentally friendly wedding involves making thoughtful choices that reduce waste, conserve resources, and support ethical businesses. You don't have to do everything perfectly—even a few changes can significantly lower the event's environmental impact.

Here are key areas to consider:

• Choose a sustainable venue
  - Select a location close to where most guests live to reduce travel emissions.
  - Consider venues that prioritize sustainability, such as those using renewable energy, recycling programs, or water-saving practices.
  - Hold both the ceremony and reception at the same venue to minimize transportation.

• Reduce guest travel impact
  - Encourage carpooling, public transportation, or shared shuttles.
  - Provide information about eco-friendly travel options.
  - If many guests would need to fly long distances, consider a smaller gathering or hybrid participation options.

• Use digital communications
  - Send electronic save-the-dates and invitations.
  - Create a wedding website for event details and RSVPs.
  - If using printed materials, choose recycled or sustainably sourced paper.

• Choose sustainable attire
  - Buy secondhand, vintage, or rental wedding attire.
  - Select outfits that can be worn again after the wedding.
  - Support designers who use ethical labor and sustainable materials.

• Select eco-conscious decorations
  - Rent décor instead of purchasing disposable items.
  - Use potted plants, locally grown flowers, or seasonal blooms.
  - Avoid single-use plastics, balloons, glitter, and non-biodegradable confetti.
  - Repurpose ceremony decorations for the reception.

• Serve sustainable food and drinks
  - Work with caterers that source local, seasonal ingredients.
  - Offer more plant-based menu options, which generally have a lower environmental footprint.
  - Estimate guest counts carefully to reduce food waste.
  - Arrange donation of safe, leftover food where permitted.

• Minimize waste
  - Use reusable dishes, glasses, and linens instead of disposable alternatives.
  - Provide clearly labeled recycling and compost bins.
  - Avoid wedding favors that are likely to be discarded.

• Choose meaningful, low-waste favors
  - Consider edible favors, seed packets, charitable donations, or locally made products.
  - You can also skip favors entirely.

• Source ethical wedding rings and gifts
  - Look for recycled metals or responsibly sourced gemstones.
  - Consider vintage or heirloom jewelry.
  - Create a registry focused on experiences, charitable contributions, or items you'll genuinely use.

• Manage flowers responsibly
  - Choose local, seasonal flowers whenever possible.
  - Donate arrangements afterward to hospitals, care homes, or community organizations if local regulations allow.
  - Compost floral materials after the event.

• Track and offset emissions if desired
  - Calculate the wedding's carbon footprint, especially travel-related emissions.
  - Support reputable carbon-reduction or environmental projects, while prioritizing emission reductions first.

Sample priorities if you're working with a limited budget:
1. Reduce guest travel.
2. Use digital invitations.
3. Choose reusable tableware.
4. Serve local, seasonal food with plant-based options.
5. Rent or reuse décor.

The most environmentally friendly wedding is often one that is smaller, uses fewer new resources, and focuses on meaningful experiences rather than excess consumption.
