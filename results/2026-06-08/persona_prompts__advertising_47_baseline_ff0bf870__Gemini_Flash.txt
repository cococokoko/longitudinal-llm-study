── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Tips for running a marathon.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Running a marathon (26.2 miles / 42.2 km) is a monumental achievement. Whether you are a first-timer or looking to set a personal record, success requires a mix of physical preparation, mental toughness, and smart strategy. 

Here is a comprehensive guide to running a marathon, broken down into phases.

---

### Phase 1: Training & Preparation (Months 1–4)
The training block is where the marathon is actually won. 

*   **Respect the 10% Rule:** Never increase your weekly mileage by more than 10% from the previous week. This is the golden rule for avoiding overuse injuries (like shin splints or IT band syndrome).
*   **Prioritize the "Long Run":** Your weekly long run is the most important workout. It teaches your body to burn fat for fuel, builds mental stamina, and strengthens your muscles and joints.
*   **Don't Skip Strength Training:** Run-specific strength training (squats, lunges, planks, and glute bridges) twice a week will keep your joints stable and prevent injuries.
*   **Incorporate Rest:** Rest days are when your muscles actually rebuild and get stronger. Treat rest days with the same respect as your hard run days.

---

### Phase 2: Nutrition & Hydration (The Fuel)
You cannot run a marathon on an empty tank, and you cannot digest food on race day that you haven't practiced eating.

*   **The Golden Rule: "Nothing New on Race Day."** Use your long training runs to test everything: your pre-run breakfast, your energy gels, your hydration packs, and even your socks.
*   **Practice "In-Run" Fueling:** Your body can only store about 90 minutes' worth of glycogen. For runs longer than 60 minutes, consume 30–60 grams of carbohydrates (gels, chews, or sports drinks) per hour.
*   **Hydrate Daily:** Don't just drink water the morning of a run. Stay hydrated throughout the week. If your urine is the color of pale lemonade, you are well-hydrated.

---

### Phase 3: Gear & Apparel
The wrong gear can turn a great run into a painful ordeal.

*   **Get Professionally Fitted for Shoes:** Go to a local running specialty store. Buy shoes that are a half-size to a full-size larger than your casual shoes, as your feet will swell over 26.2 miles.
*   **Say No to Cotton:** Wear synthetic, moisture-wicking fabrics (polyester, nylon, merino wool). Cotton holds sweat, which leads to heavy clothes and terrible chafing.
*   **Anti-Chafe is Your Best Friend:** Apply Body Glide or Vaseline to areas prone to friction: inner thighs, underarms, sports bra lines, and toes. (Men should consider nipple protection/band-aids).

---

### Phase 4: Race Week & Tapering
Tapering means cutting back your mileage in the 2–3 weeks before the race to let your body fully recover and build up energy stores.

*   **Trust the Taper:** You will feel restless, and you might feel "fake" aches and pains (often called "taper tantrums"). This is normal. Do not try to squeeze in one last hard workout.
*   **Prioritize Sleep Two Nights Before:** You might not sleep well the night before the race due to nerves. That’s okay. The sleep you get *two nights before* (Friday night for a Sunday race) is the most critical.
*   **Carb-Load Smartly:** Don't just eat a massive bowl of pasta the night before (which can lead to bloating). Instead, gradually increase your carbohydrate intake 2–3 days before the race, focusing on easy-to-digest carbs (rice, oatmeal, bananas, bagels).

---
