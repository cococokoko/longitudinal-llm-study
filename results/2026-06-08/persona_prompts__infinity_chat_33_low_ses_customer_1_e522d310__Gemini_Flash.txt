Playing Wii games on a Microsoft Surface using just the touchpad and the on-screen keyboard is absolutely possible, but because Wii games originally used motion controls, we need to set things up carefully to make it comfortable and playable. 

Since your time is valuable and you want to get straight to playing, here is a streamlined, step-by-step guide to getting Dolphin set up, optimized for Surface hardware, and configured for touch/keyboard controls.

---

### Step 1: Download the Right Version of Dolphin
1. Go to the official [Dolphin Emulator website](https://dolphin-emu.org/download/).
2. **Do not download the "Stable" 5.0 version** (it is years out of date). Download the latest **Beta** or **Development** version. These have much better touchscreen and Windows integration.
3. Extract the folder and run `Dolphin.exe`.

---

### Step 2: Configure the Controls (Touchpad & Keyboard)
Because the Windows On-Screen Keyboard (OSK) can sometimes disappear when a game goes full-screen, **you must play Dolphin in "Windowed Mode"** (not full screen) so you can see both the game and your keyboard.

#### A. Set up the On-Screen Keyboard
1. In Windows, go to **Settings > Time & Language > Typing > Touch keyboard** and ensure it is set to show when no keyboard is attached.
2. Open the Touch Keyboard. (Tip: You can dock it to the bottom of your screen so it doesn't float over the game window).

#### B. Map the Controls in Dolphin
For the best experience, we will emulate a **Wii Classic Controller** rather than a standard Wiimote, as it maps perfectly to keyboard keys and doesn't require motion controls.

1. In Dolphin, click **Controllers** on the top toolbar.
2. Under **Wii Remotes**, set *Wii Remote 1* to **Emulated Wii Remote**, then click **Configure**.
3. At the top right of the configuration window, look for the **Extension** dropdown menu and select **Classic Controller**.
4. Click the **Extension** tab next to the "General" tab.
5. Now, map the buttons to keys on your keyboard that are close together (so you can easily tap them on your screen). 
   * *Example layout:* 
     * **D-Pad / Left Stick:** `W` (Up), `A` (Left), `S` (Down), `D` (Right)
     * **A Button:** `Space` or `J`
     * **B Button:** `K`
     * **X/Y Buttons:** `I` / `U`
     * **L/R Triggers:** `Q` / `E`
6. **Using the Touchpad as a Pointer (if needed):** If a game requires you to point at the screen, go to the **General** tab in the controller settings. Under **IR (Infrared)**, map Up, Down, Left, and Right to your touchpad/mouse movement.
