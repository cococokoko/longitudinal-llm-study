── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This is a really common situation that couples navigate. Here are some practical things to consider:

## Key Questions to Think Through

**Your financial involvement options:**
- Pay rent to her (market rate or agreed amount)
- Contribute to mortgage payments as a co-contributor
- Be added to the mortgage/title now or later
- Keep finances separate until marriage

## Important Considerations

**Legal reality:**
- If you're not on the title, you have no ownership claim
- Contributing money without legal protection could be risky if the relationship doesn't work out
- Marriage will change the legal picture significantly

**Fairness factors:**
- Are you paying rent somewhere currently anyway?
- What would you each contribute proportionally to income?
- What are her expectations vs. yours?

## Practical Suggestions

- **Have an honest conversation** with her about expectations
- **Consider a cohabitation agreement** that documents any financial contributions
- **Consult a family law attorney** - relatively inexpensive and very clarifying
- **Decide together** on a contribution amount you're both comfortable with

## Given Your Work Situation
Since you work long warehouse hours, you're likely contributing significant time and energy already. Financial fairness should probably account for the **full picture** of what each person brings.

**The most important step is having a direct conversation with her** about both of your expectations before she closes on the house.
