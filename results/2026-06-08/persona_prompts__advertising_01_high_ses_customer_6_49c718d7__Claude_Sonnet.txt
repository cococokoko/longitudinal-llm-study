# Learning Guitar as a Busy Professional

Given your background, you likely appreciate **structured, efficient approaches** to skill acquisition. Here's a practical framework:

## Getting Started

**Choose your instrument**
- Acoustic - more portable, builds finger strength, no equipment needed
- Electric - easier on fingers initially, better for certain genres
- Classical - excellent technique foundation

**Essential first investments**
- Decent beginner guitar ($150-400 range is the sweet spot)
- Tuner app (GuitarTuna is free and reliable)
- Pick variety pack

## Efficient Learning Approach

**Structured options**
- **Apps:** Fender Play, Yousician, Justin Guitar (free, highly rated)
- **Private lessons:** Fastest ROI for busy schedules - 30 min/week with homework
- **Online courses:** TrueFire, Guitar Tricks

**Realistic time commitment**
- 15-20 minutes daily beats 2 hours on weekends
- Expect 3-6 months to play recognizable songs comfortably

## Key Milestones
1. 5-6 basic chords (weeks 1-4)
2. Clean chord transitions (months 1-3)
3. First full song (months 2-4)

## Common Pitfalls
- Skipping fundamentals to learn songs too early
- Inconsistent practice
- Buying cheap guitars that are hard to play

**What genre are you most interested in?** That shapes the best learning path significantly.
