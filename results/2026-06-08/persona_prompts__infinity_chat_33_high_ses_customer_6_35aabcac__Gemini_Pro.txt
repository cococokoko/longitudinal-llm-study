Here is an executive summary and a step-by-step implementation plan for running Dolphin Emulator on your Microsoft Surface using only the touchpad and on-screen keyboard (OSK). 

**Executive Summary:**
While it is technically feasible to map Wii controls to a touchpad and OSK, the lack of tactile feedback and the screen real estate taken up by the keyboard will create a highly constrained gaming experience. This setup is viable for slow-paced, turn-based games, but will struggle with action or motion-heavy titles. 

Below is the configuration guide, followed by a brief risk assessment and a recommended alternative to maximize the return on your leisure time.

---

### Phase 1: System Configuration

**1. Enable the Full On-Screen Keyboard**
The default Windows touch keyboard is optimized for typing, not gaming. You need the legacy On-Screen Keyboard to ensure key presses register correctly in Dolphin.
*   Press the **Windows Key**, type **On-Screen Keyboard**, and open the app. 
*   *Tip:* Pin this to your taskbar for easy access. You can resize this window and place it at the bottom of your screen.

**2. Configure Dolphin Emulator Inputs**
Open Dolphin and navigate to **Controllers** at the top of the window. Under "Wii Remotes," set Wii Remote 1 to **Emulated Wii Remote**, then click **Configure**.

*   **Device Selection:** In the top left dropdown, ensure your device is set to `Keyboard Mouse`.
*   **Basic Buttons:** 
    *   **A Button:** Map to `Left Click` (Touchpad tap) or a highly accessible key like `F`.
    *   **B Button (Trigger):** Map to `Right Click` (Touchpad two-finger tap) or `Spacebar`.
    *   **D-Pad:** Map to `W, A, S, D` or the Arrow Keys on your OSK.
    *   **1, 2, +, -:** Map to easily reachable keys on the OSK (e.g., `1, 2, Q, E`).
*   **Motion Simulation (The Pointer):** 
    *   Navigate to the **Motion Simulation** tab.
    *   Under the **Point** section, map Up, Down, Left, and Right to your mouse cursor (`Cursor Y-`, `Cursor Y+`, `Cursor X-`, `Cursor X+`). This allows your Surface touchpad to act as the Wii Remote's infrared pointer.
*   **Motion Simulation (Shake):**
    *   Many Wii games require you to shake the controller. In the **Motion Simulation** tab, under **Shake**, map X, Y, and Z to a single prominent key on your OSK (e.g., `Shift` or `Enter`).

### Phase 2: Strategic Game Selection

Because you are using an OSK (which limits simultaneous multi-key inputs) and a touchpad, you should select games that do not require rapid reflexes or complex motion controls.

*   **Highly Recommended:** Turn-based strategy or RPGs (*Fire Emblem: Radiant Dawn*, *Opoona*), or slow-paced simulation games (*Animal Crossing: City Folk*).
*   **Not Recommended:** Action/Platformers (*Super Mario Galaxy*, *Donkey Kong Country Returns*) or heavy motion games (*The Legend of Zelda: Skyward Sword*). 
*   **Strategic Pivot:** Consider playing **GameCube** games on Dolphin instead of Wii games. GameCube games require zero motion controls and map much more cleanly to a standard keyboard layout.
