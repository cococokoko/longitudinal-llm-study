── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How do I understand what I want?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Figuring out what you truly want is one of the most important—and difficult—journeys of life. Often, our true desires are buried under societal expectations, family pressures, fear of failure, or simply the noise of daily life. 

To understand what you want, you have to become a detective of your own mind. Here is a step-by-step guide to help you uncover your genuine desires.

---

### Phase 1: Clear the Noise (The "Should" Audit)
Before you can hear what you *want*, you have to quiet the voices telling you what you *should* do.
*   **Identify the "Shoulds":** Write down a list of your current goals. Next to each one, ask: *“Am I doing this because I want to, or because I feel I should?”* (e.g., "I should buy a house," "I should want a promotion").
*   **The "No One is Watching" Test:** Ask yourself: *“If I could never tell anyone about my achievements, and no one would ever see what I do, what would I still want to do?”* This strips away the desire for status and validation.

### Phase 2: Look for Clues in Your Daily Life
Your brain and body are constantly giving you clues about what you want. You just need to start noticing them.
*   **Pay attention to envy:** Envy is a powerful compass. When you feel a pang of jealousy toward someone, don’t suppress it. Ask: *“What specific part of their life do I wish I had?”* Is it their freedom? Their creative outlet? Their relationship?
*   **Track your energy, not just your time:** For one week, keep an "Energy Log." Note which activities leave you feeling drained and which leave you feeling energized and alive. Your true desires lie in the things that energize you, even if they are difficult.
*   **Notice your daydreams:** When your mind wanders during a boring task, where does it go? What tabs do you open on your browser when you are procrastinating? Your distractions often point to your latent desires.

### Phase 3: Practical Exercises
Here are three exercises to help bypass your logical, fearful brain and tap into your subconscious.

#### 1. The "Perfect Day" Exercise
Write a detailed narrative of your ideal day, five years from now. 
*   *Where do you wake up? Who is next to you?*
*   *What does the room look like?*
*   *What do you do in the morning? What do you eat?*
*   *What kind of work or activities do you do?*
*   *How do you spend your evening?*
Be as specific as possible. This exercise helps you identify the *feeling* of the life you want, which is often more important than the specific job title or location.

#### 2. The "Five Whys"
When you think you want something, drill down to the root cause. Ask "Why?" five times.
*   *“I want to make a lot of money.”* (Why?)
*   *“So I can buy a house in the country.”* (Why?)
*   *“So I can have peace and quiet.”* (Why?)
*   *“Because my current life feels chaotic and overwhelming.”* (Why?)
*   *“Because I am overcommitted and don't have boundaries.”*
*   **Discovery:** You don't necessarily want "a lot of money" right now; you want peace, quiet, and boundaries.

#### 3. The Process of Elimination
If you don't know what you want, start with what you *don't* want. It is often much easier to identify what makes you miserable. Write down a list of things you absolutely do not want in your life (e.g., a long commute, micromanagement, superficial friendships). The opposite of these things will point you toward your desires.

### Phase 4: Action Over Analysis
You cannot think your way into knowing what you want; you have to **act** your way there. Clarity comes from engagement, not thought.
*   **Run "Micro-Experiments":** If you think you might want to be a writer, don't quit your job to write a novel. Write for 15 minutes a day for two weeks. If you think you want to live in a different city, spend a week working remotely from there. 
*   **Treat life as data collection:** If you try something and hate it, that is not a failure. It is valuable data. You are now one step closer to knowing what you *do* want.
