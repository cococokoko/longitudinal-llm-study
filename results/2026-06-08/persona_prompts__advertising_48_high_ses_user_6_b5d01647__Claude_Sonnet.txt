# Best Practices for Organic Gardening

Here's a straightforward overview:

## Soil Health
- **Compost regularly** - builds nutrients and beneficial microorganisms
- **Test soil pH** - most vegetables prefer 6.0-7.0
- **Avoid tilling excessively** - disrupts soil structure and earthworms
- **Use cover crops** - clover, rye to prevent erosion and add nitrogen

## Pest & Weed Management
- **Companion planting** - basil near tomatoes, marigolds as pest deterrents
- **Crop rotation** - prevents disease buildup year over year
- **Hand-pick pests** early before populations grow
- **Mulch heavily** - suppresses weeds and retains moisture

## Watering
- **Water deeply and infrequently** rather than shallow daily watering
- **Morning watering** reduces fungal disease risk
- **Drip irrigation** is most efficient

## Fertilizing
- Use **compost, worm castings, or fish emulsion**
- Avoid synthetic fertilizers that disrupt soil biology

## Key Principles
- **Work with nature** rather than against it
- **Start small** and expand as you learn your specific conditions
- **Observe regularly** - early detection solves most problems
