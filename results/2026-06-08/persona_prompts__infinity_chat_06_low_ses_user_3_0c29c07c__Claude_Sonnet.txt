── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I recently started a new job and am still on probation.

How do I understand what I want?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
That's a really thoughtful question, especially during a transition period. Here are some ways to think about it:

## Understanding What You Want at Work

**Pay attention to your reactions**
- Notice what energizes you vs. drains you during the day
- What tasks make time pass quickly?
- What do you dread or avoid?

**Separate layers of "want"**
- What you want *right now* (stability, income, to pass probation)
- What you want *longer term* (career growth, certain skills, work-life balance)
- These can coexist and sometimes conflict

**Ask yourself honest questions**
- What would success look like in 6 months?
- What would make you proud of this job?
- What are you willing to tolerate vs. what's a dealbreaker?

## During Probation Specifically

- It's normal to feel uncertain - you don't have full information yet
- Focus on **observing** the environment before deciding how you feel about it
- Give yourself permission to **reserve judgment** for a few months

## A Practical Suggestion

Keep a simple journal noting daily highs and lows - patterns often become clear over weeks.
