How can I improve my credit score quickly?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Improving a credit score quickly depends on what's currently affecting it, but these are generally the fastest ways to see positive movement:

- Lower your credit utilization
  - If you have credit card balances, paying them down can have a significant impact.
  - Aim to keep utilization below 30% of your credit limits, and below 10% is often even better.
  - Example: If you have a $5,000 credit limit, try to keep the reported balance under $500.

- Make all payments on time
  - Payment history is one of the most important factors in credit scoring.
  - Set up automatic payments or reminders to avoid missing due dates.

- Ask for a credit limit increase
  - If approved without a hard credit inquiry, a higher limit can lower your utilization ratio.
  - Avoid increasing spending after receiving a higher limit.

- Correct credit report errors
  - Check your credit reports for inaccurate late payments, accounts that aren't yours, or incorrect balances.
  - Disputing and removing errors can sometimes improve scores relatively quickly.

- Pay down cards before the statement closing date
  - Credit card issuers typically report balances around the statement date.
  - Paying before that date can result in a lower reported balance.

- Become an authorized user
  - If a trusted family member has a long-standing account with perfect payment history and low utilization, being added as an authorized user may help your score.

Things that usually do not improve your score quickly:
- Opening several new accounts.
- Closing old credit cards (this can sometimes hurt utilization and account age).
- Applying for multiple loans or credit cards in a short period.
