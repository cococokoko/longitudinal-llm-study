For immediate relief:
- Slow, controlled breathing: Try inhaling for 4 counts, exhaling for 6 counts, and repeating for a few minutes.
- Grounding techniques: Notice 5 things you can see, 4 you can touch, 3 you can hear, 2 you can smell, and 1 you can taste.
- Physical movement: A short walk, stretching, or light exercise can help reduce physical tension.
- Reduce stimulation: Step away from stressful news, social media, or overwhelming environments when possible.

For ongoing stress management:
- Maintain a regular sleep schedule.
- Exercise regularly, even in small amounts.
- Eat balanced meals and stay hydrated.
- Limit excessive caffeine or alcohol if they seem to worsen anxiety.
- Schedule time for activities you enjoy and social connection.
- Break large tasks into smaller, manageable steps.

Mental strategies:
- Identify specific worries and separate what you can control from what you cannot.
- Challenge catastrophic thoughts by asking what evidence supports them and what alternative outcomes are possible.
- Keep a journal to track stressors, emotions, and patterns.
- Practice mindfulness or meditation regularly.

When to seek additional support:
- If anxiety or stress is interfering with work, school, relationships, sleep, or daily functioning.
- If symptoms feel overwhelming or persist for weeks.
- If you experience panic attacks or significant distress.
