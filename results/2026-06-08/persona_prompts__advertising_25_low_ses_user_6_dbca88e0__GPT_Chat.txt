── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I work in a warehouse position that requires long hours.

How can I decorate my apartment on a minimalist budget?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
If you work long hours and want your apartment to feel better without spending much money, focus on a few high-impact changes rather than buying lots of decor.

Some budget-friendly ideas:

- Improve lighting
  - Warm LED bulbs can make a room feel much more comfortable.
  - Use floor lamps or table lamps from thrift stores instead of relying only on overhead lighting.

- Add plants
  - Low-maintenance options like pothos, snake plants, or ZZ plants tolerate neglect well.
  - If even that feels like too much, high-quality artificial plants can still add visual warmth.

- Use textiles
  - A throw blanket on a couch or bed can add color and texture.
  - Affordable pillows, rugs, or curtains can dramatically change a room's appearance.

- Create wall art cheaply
  - Print free public-domain artwork and place it in inexpensive frames.
  - Frame personal photos, travel pictures, or even pages from old books and magazines.

- Declutter and organize
  - Minimalism often looks best when surfaces are mostly clear.
  - Matching storage bins or baskets can make a space feel intentional and tidy.

- Rearrange furniture
  - Sometimes changing the layout costs nothing and makes a room feel completely different.
  - Try creating a clear focal point, such as a couch facing a bookshelf, TV, or window.

- Add mirrors
  - Thrift stores often have inexpensive mirrors.
  - Mirrors can make small apartments feel larger and brighter.

- Use secondhand sources
  - Check Facebook Marketplace, Craigslist, OfferUp, thrift stores, and estate sales.
  - Many people give away lamps, shelves, artwork, and furniture for very little money.

- Pick a simple color palette
  - Two or three colors throughout the apartment can make everything look more cohesive.
  - Neutral tones with one accent color are often easiest to maintain.

For someone working long warehouse shifts, I would prioritize:
1. Better lighting.
2. A comfortable throw blanket and pillows.
3. One or two plants.
4. A small piece of wall art.
5. Decluttering visible surfaces.

Those five changes can often make an apartment feel significantly more welcoming while staying under $100 if you shop secondhand.
