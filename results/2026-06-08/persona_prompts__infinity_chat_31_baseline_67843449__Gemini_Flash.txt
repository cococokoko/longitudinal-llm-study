What is the best investment if I invest 60,000 euros?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
There is no single "best" investment for €60,000, because the right choice depends entirely on your **financial goals, time horizon, and risk tolerance**. 

Before deciding where to put your money, you must ask yourself: **When do you need this money back?**

Here is a breakdown of the best investment strategies for €60,000 based on different timeframes and risk levels, tailored for European investors.

---

### Scenario 1: Short-Term (0 to 3 years)
*If you need this money soon (e.g., for a house down payment, a wedding, or emergency fund).*
* **Goal:** Capital preservation (make sure you don't lose a single Euro).
* **The Strategy:** Low-risk, highly liquid assets.

1. **High-Yield Savings Accounts (HYSA):** Many European fintechs and banks (like Trade Republic, Bunq, or Santander) currently offer 3% to 4% interest on cash balances. 
   * *Return on €60k:* ~€1,800 to €2,400 per year (before tax).
2. **Term Deposits (Festgeld / Comptes à terme):** You lock your money away for 1 to 3 years in exchange for a guaranteed fixed interest rate. 
3. **Money Market Funds (MMFs):** These invest in short-term debt and currently yield close to the European Central Bank (ECB) deposit rate.

---

### Scenario 2: Medium-Term (3 to 7 years)
*If you want your money to grow, but might need it in a few years.*
* **Goal:** Beat inflation with moderate growth.
* **The Strategy:** A balanced portfolio of bonds and equities.

1. **The "60/40" Portfolio:** 
   * **60% (€36,000) in a Global Stock ETF** (like the MSCI World).
   * **40% (€24,000) in Government/Corporate Bonds** (or a Bond ETF).
2. **Robo-Advisors:** Platforms like Scalable Capital, Liqid, or Yomoni (depending on your country) will automatically build and rebalance a medium-risk portfolio for you based on your profile.

---

### Scenario 3: Long-Term (7+ years)
*If you are investing for retirement, your children’s future, or general wealth building, and you don't need to touch this money for a decade.*
* **Goal:** Maximum wealth accumulation.
* **The Strategy:** Broadly diversified equities (stocks).

1. **All-World Stock ETFs (The "Set and Forget" Method):**
   This is widely considered the best long-term investment for most people. By buying a single ETF, you own a tiny slice of thousands of companies worldwide (Apple, Microsoft, Nestlé, ASML, etc.).
   * **What to buy:** Look for **Vanguard FTSE All-World (VWCE)** or **iShares Core MSCI World (IWDA)**.
   * **Historical Return:** Historically, global stock markets return about 7% to 9% per year on average over long periods.
   * *Potential value of €60k in 15 years (at 7% compound interest):* **~€165,000**.
2. **Real Estate (REITs):** If you want real estate exposure but €60k isn't enough to buy a physical property, you can invest in Real Estate Investment Trusts (REITs) through the stock market, which pay regular dividends.
