### 2. The Best Strategies for *Retail* Traders (Individuals)
For individual traders, the most consistently profitable strategies rely on **Risk Management** rather than predicting the future. The two most common frameworks are:

* **Trend Following (Momentum):** Identifying a strong upward or downward trend and "riding the wave" using indicators like Moving Averages. You cut losses quickly if the trend reverses and let profits run.
* **Mean Reversion:** Operating on the assumption that prices eventually return to their average. If a stock is extremely overbought or oversold (measured by indicators like the RSI), you trade in the opposite direction.

#### The Real "Holy Grail" of Trading:
The only strategy that guarantees survival is **Risk Mitigation**. 
* **The 1% Rule:** Never risk more than 1% of your total account value on a single trade. If you have a $10,000 account, you should never lose more than $100 on a bad trade.
* **Risk-to-Reward Ratio:** Only taking trades where the potential profit is at least 2x or 3x the potential loss. (If you lose $100 when wrong, you must make $200–$300 when right). This allows you to be wrong 60% of the time and still make money.
