# Marathon Training & Racing Tips

## Training
- **Build mileage gradually** – increase weekly mileage no more than 10% per week
- **Run 4-5 days per week** with a mix of easy runs, tempo runs, and long runs
- **Peak long run** should reach 18-22 miles, 3-4 weeks before race day
- **Include rest days** – recovery is when your body actually adapts
- **Cross-train** (cycling, swimming) to build fitness with less impact

## Nutrition & Hydration
- Practice fueling during long runs (gels, chews, or real food every 45-60 min)
- **Don't try anything new on race day**
- Carb-load 2-3 days before the race
- Stay consistently hydrated throughout training, not just race week

## Race Day
- **Start slower than you feel you should** – the most common mistake
- Run your own pace, not the crowd's
- Break the race into segments mentally
- Know the course and where aid stations are

## Recovery
- Plan for 2-4 weeks of easy running post-race
- Sleep and nutrition are critical for recovery

## Mindset
- Respect the distance – it demands preparation
- Have a goal, but also a backup goal
- Miles 18-22 will be hard – **expect it and plan for it**
