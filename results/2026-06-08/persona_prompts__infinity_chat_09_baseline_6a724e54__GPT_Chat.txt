Is there life on other planets?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
We don't know yet.

There are a few important points:

- The universe is enormous. There are hundreds of billions of stars in our galaxy alone, and likely hundreds of billions of galaxies.
- Astronomers have discovered thousands of exoplanets (planets orbiting other stars), including some that may have conditions suitable for liquid water.
- On Earth, life appeared relatively early in the planet's history, which some scientists consider a hint that life might arise under the right conditions elsewhere.
- However, we currently have no confirmed evidence of life beyond Earth.

Scientists are actively searching for signs of life by:
- Studying Mars for evidence of past or present microbial life.
- Investigating icy moons such as Europa (around Jupiter) and Enceladus (around Saturn), which have subsurface oceans.
- Analyzing the atmospheres of exoplanets for potential biosignatures—gases that could be produced by living organisms.

As for intelligent life, there is even less evidence. The Search for Extraterrestrial Intelligence (SETI) looks for signals that might indicate technological civilizations, but no confirmed detections have been made.

So the current scientific answer is: life elsewhere is plausible and many scientists think it may exist, but there is no definitive proof yet.
