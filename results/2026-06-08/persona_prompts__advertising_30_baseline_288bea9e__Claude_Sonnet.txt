# Starting to Code from Scratch

Great question! Here's a practical roadmap:

## Choose Your First Language Wisely
- **Python** - Most recommended for beginners (readable, versatile)
- **JavaScript** - Good if you're interested in web development
- **Scratch** - Visual option if you want something very beginner-friendly

## Free Learning Resources
- **freeCodeCamp.org** - Structured, project-based
- **The Odin Project** - Comprehensive web dev curriculum
- **CS50** (Harvard, free on edX) - Excellent fundamentals
- **Codecademy** - Interactive, beginner-friendly

## Key Principles for Success

**Start small and build up**
- Learn basic concepts before jumping to complex projects
- Variables, loops, functions, then data structures

**Practice consistently**
- 30 minutes daily beats 5 hours once a week

**Build actual projects**
- Apply what you learn immediately
- Even simple projects reinforce concepts

**Embrace getting stuck**
- Struggling is normal and part of learning
- Google and Stack Overflow are tools professionals use daily

## Honest Expectations
- Expect frustration early on - that's completely normal
- Basic competency typically takes **3-6 months** of consistent practice
- It gets more rewarding as concepts connect

## My Suggestion
**Start with Python + freeCodeCamp or CS50**, focus on one resource rather than jumping around.

What kind of things are you hoping to build? That might help narrow down the best starting point for you.
