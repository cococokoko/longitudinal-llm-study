── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I recently started a new job and am still on probation.

What is the future of work in an increasingly automated economy?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This is a great question to think about. Here are some key perspectives:

## What's Likely Changing
- **Routine tasks** (both manual and cognitive) are most vulnerable to automation
- **Some jobs will disappear** while new categories emerge
- **Job content will shift** even when job titles remain the same

## Reasons for Concern
- Automation speed may outpace workers' ability to retrain
- Benefits and displacement costs often fall unevenly on lower-income workers
- Historical transitions caused real hardship even when they eventually worked out

## Reasons for Optimism
- Technology has historically created more jobs than it eliminated *over time*
- Productivity gains can raise living standards broadly
- Many human skills remain genuinely difficult to automate

## Skills That Tend to Hold Value
- Complex problem-solving and judgment
- Interpersonal and caregiving work
- Creativity and novel thinking
- Adaptability itself

## Honest Uncertainties
- AI may be **qualitatively different** from previous automation waves
- We don't know how quickly adaptation will happen
- Policy responses (retraining programs, safety nets) will matter enormously

## Practical Takeaway
Building **adaptable skills** and staying current in your field is probably more reliable than trying to predict which specific jobs survive.

Is there a particular industry or aspect of this you're most concerned about? That might help focus the discussion.
