── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How can I decorate my apartment on a minimalist budget?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Decorating on a minimalist budget works best when you focus on a few high-impact changes rather than buying lots of items. The goal is to make the space feel intentional, comfortable, and uncluttered.

Here are some affordable approaches:

- Start with a neutral foundation
  - Use simple bedding, curtains, and rugs in colors that work together.
  - Whites, grays, beige, black, and natural wood tones create a clean look and are easy to mix and match.

- Declutter first
  - Remove anything you don't use or love.
  - A tidy space often looks more expensive and thoughtfully designed without spending any money.

- Use lighting strategically
  - Add a floor lamp or table lamp instead of relying solely on overhead lighting.
  - Warm-toned bulbs can make a room feel more inviting.

- Incorporate plants
  - A few low-maintenance plants can add color and life.
  - Consider pothos, snake plants, or ZZ plants if you're new to plant care.

- Choose one statement piece per room
  - Examples: a large piece of art, an attractive rug, or a distinctive lamp.
  - This creates visual interest without filling the room with decor.

- Shop secondhand
  - Check thrift stores, flea markets, online marketplaces, and local buy/sell groups.
  - Look for solid wood furniture, mirrors, frames, and lamps that can be cleaned or refreshed.

- Use mirrors
  - Mirrors reflect light and can make small apartments feel larger.
  - A single large mirror often has more impact than several small decorative items.

- Create simple wall art
  - Frame inexpensive prints, photographs, book pages, or downloadable artwork.
  - Consistent frames help create a cohesive appearance.

- Add texture instead of more objects
  - Throw blankets, cushions, woven baskets, and natural materials make a room feel layered without adding clutter.

- Upgrade small details
  - Replace cabinet knobs, switch plates, or shower curtains.
  - These inexpensive changes can noticeably improve the look of a space.

Example budget allocation for a living room:
- Rug: 40–50%
- Lighting: 20–25%
- Plants and planters: 10–15%
- Wall art: 10–15%
- Decorative accents: 5–10%

A minimalist budget-friendly rule is: for every decorative item you add, ask whether it serves a purpose, fills empty space intentionally, or genuinely makes the room more enjoyable. If not, skip it and save the money.
