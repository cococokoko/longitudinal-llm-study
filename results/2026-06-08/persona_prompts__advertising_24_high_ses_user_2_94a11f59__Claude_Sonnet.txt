# Retirement Savings Strategies for High-Income Professionals

As a senior attorney, you likely have specific considerations given your income level and potentially complex financial situation.

## Employer-Sponsored Plans
- **401(k)/403(b)** - Maximize contributions ($23,000 in 2024, plus $7,500 catch-up if 50+)
- **Profit-sharing plans** - Common at law firms, can significantly boost contributions

## Tax-Advantaged Accounts
- **Traditional IRA** - Tax-deferred growth
- **Roth IRA** - Tax-free growth (income limits may apply at your level)
- **Backdoor Roth IRA** - Useful strategy if you exceed income limits
- **HSA** - Triple tax advantage if eligible

## Options Specific to Your Situation
- **Defined benefit/cash balance plans** - Many law firm partners use these for very high contribution limits
- **Solo 401(k)** - If you have any independent income
- **Deferred compensation arrangements** - If your firm offers them

## General Principles
- Diversify across tax treatments (pre-tax, Roth, taxable)
- Consider tax bracket management in retirement
- Work with a **fee-only financial advisor** and **CPA**

## Key Consideration
Your specific situation - partnership status, firm structure, income level - matters enormously here.
