# Mastering the Art of Cooking Steak

## Key Principles

### Selecting the Right Cut
- **Ribeye** - well-marbled, rich flavor, forgiving to cook
- **NY Strip** - balanced fat/lean ratio, firm texture
- **Filet Mignon** - tender, mild flavor, less fat
- **T-Bone/Porterhouse** - two cuts in one, impressive presentation

---

## The Process

### Before Cooking
- **Dry brine** - salt generously 45 min to 24 hours ahead
- Bring to **room temperature** (30-45 min out of fridge)
- Pat **completely dry** before cooking - moisture is the enemy of crust

### Temperature Targets
| Doneness | Internal Temp |
|----------|--------------|
| Rare | 120-125°F |
| Medium Rare | 130-135°F |
| Medium | 140-145°F |
| Well Done | 160°F+ |

### Cooking Methods
- **Cast iron sear** - highest heat, best crust
- **Reverse sear** - low oven first, then sear (great for thick cuts)
- **Sous vide** - precise temperature control

### The Finish
- **Butter baste** with thyme and garlic
- **Rest** 5-10 minutes before cutting
- Cut **against the grain**

---

## Pro Tips
- Use a **reliable instant-read thermometer**
- Season more than you think necessary
- High smoke-point oil for searing (avocado, grapeseed)
