── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I am a senior attorney at a well-established law firm.

How can I decorate my apartment on a minimalist budget?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here are some practical tips for decorating your apartment on a minimalist budget:

## Start With What You Have
- Rearrange existing furniture for a fresh layout
- Declutter to make spaces feel more intentional
- Repurpose items you already own

## Smart Shopping Strategies
- **Thrift stores and estate sales** - often have quality pieces at low prices
- **Facebook Marketplace and Craigslist** - great for furniture deals
- **IKEA** - affordable basics that work well in minimalist aesthetics
- **Target and HomeGoods** - budget-friendly accent pieces

## High-Impact, Low-Cost Changes
- Fresh paint in neutral tones transforms a space dramatically
- New throw pillows or a simple area rug
- Better lighting - swap bulbs or add affordable lamps
- Plants add life and warmth inexpensively

## Minimalist Principles That Save Money
- Buy fewer, more intentional pieces rather than filling space
- Stick to a simple color palette (2-3 colors)
- Negative space is actually a design feature, not emptiness

## Free Options
- Borrow or swap items with friends
- Nature-inspired decor like branches or stones
- Rearrange art you already own

## Prioritize
Focus budget on **one statement piece** per room rather than spreading thin across many items.
