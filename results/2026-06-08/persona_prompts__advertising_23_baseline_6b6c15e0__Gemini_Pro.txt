── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How can I learn to cook healthy meals quickly?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Learning to cook healthy meals quickly comes down to **simplifying your approach**. Instead of trying to learn complex recipes, you should focus on mastering basic techniques, stocking your kitchen smartly, and using "meal formulas."

Here is a step-by-step guide to get you cooking healthy, fast meals in a matter of days.

---

### Step 1: Master the "Meal Formula"
Forget recipes for now. Recipes require measuring and reading, which slows you down. Instead, memorize this basic healthy meal formula:
**Lean Protein (1/4 plate) + Complex Carb (1/4 plate) + Vegetables (1/2 plate) + Flavor (Sauce/Spices)**

*   **Proteins:** Chicken breast/thighs, salmon, tofu, eggs, canned beans, ground turkey.
*   **Carbs:** Brown rice, quinoa, sweet potatoes, whole wheat pasta, oats.
*   **Veggies:** Broccoli, spinach, bell peppers, zucchini, asparagus.
*   **Flavor:** Olive oil, garlic powder, soy sauce, salsa, pesto, hot sauce, lemon juice.

### Step 2: Learn Three Fast Cooking Methods
You only need to know three basic techniques to make hundreds of different meals.

1.  **The Sheet Pan Roast (Hands-off & Easy):**
    *   Chop a protein (e.g., chicken sausage), a carb (e.g., sweet potatoes), and a veggie (e.g., broccoli).
    *   Toss them all in olive oil, salt, pepper, and garlic powder.
    *   Spread on a baking sheet and roast at 400°F (200°C) for 20–25 minutes.
2.  **The Stir-Fry (Super Fast):**
    *   Heat oil in a large pan over medium-high heat.
    *   Cook your protein (e.g., sliced chicken or tofu) until done. Remove from pan.
    *   Add chopped veggies and cook for 5 minutes.
    *   Add the protein back in, pour in a simple sauce (like soy sauce + a little honey), and serve over quick-cooking rice.
3.  **The "Assemble-Only" Grain Bowl (No Cooking Required):**
    *   Start with a base of pre-cooked grains (like microwaveable quinoa).
    *   Add a pre-cooked protein (canned black beans, canned tuna, or rotisserie chicken).
    *   Add raw or microwave-steamed veggies (spinach, cherry tomatoes, avocado).
    *   Top with a healthy store-bought dressing or salsa.

### Step 3: Embrace Grocery Store Shortcuts
Cooking quickly means not doing everything from scratch. There is no shame in buying ingredients that do the prep work for you:
*   **Frozen vegetables:** They are just as healthy as fresh, already chopped, and won't go bad.
*   **Pre-washed greens:** Bags of spinach or arugula can be thrown into any meal.
*   **Microwaveable grains:** Packets of pre-cooked brown rice or quinoa take 90 seconds.
*   **Rotisserie chicken:** Buy one, shred the meat, and use it in salads, bowls, and wraps all week.
*   **Canned beans and lentils:** Rinse them and they are ready to eat.

### Step 4: Stock a Minimalist Kitchen
You don't need fancy gadgets. To start, you only need:
*   **1 Good Chef’s Knife:** (An 8-inch knife is perfect). Keep it sharp; dull knives slow you down.
*   **1 Large Cutting Board**
*   **1 Large Non-Stick Skillet**
*   **1 Baking Sheet**
*   **Basic Seasonings:** Salt, black pepper, garlic powder, olive oil, and one spice blend you love (like taco seasoning or Italian seasoning).

### Step 5: Batch Cook the Basics
You don't have to meal prep your entire week on Sunday, but doing a *little* bit saves hours later.
*   **Cook once, eat twice:** Whenever you make dinner, double the recipe. Put half in a container for lunch or dinner the next day.
*   **Prep your carbs:** Boil a large batch of rice, quinoa, or pasta on Sunday. Keep it in the fridge to scoop out for meals Monday through Wednesday.
