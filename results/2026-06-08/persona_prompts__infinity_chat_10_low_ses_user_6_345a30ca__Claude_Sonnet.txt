## What Research Suggests

**Some positive correlations found:**
- Higher cognitive ability is associated with more systematic moral reasoning in some studies
- Intelligence correlates with some prosocial behaviors in certain contexts

**Significant complications:**
- The correlation is **weak and inconsistent** across studies
- Intelligence can be used to **rationalize selfish behavior** just as easily as compassionate behavior
- Highly intelligent people show motivated reasoning too
- Emotional intelligence and cognitive intelligence are largely **separate dimensions**

## Important Honest Points

- Many researchers consider compassion more related to **personality traits** (agreeableness, empathy) than raw intelligence
- Intelligent people can be **quite cruel** - history provides many examples
- Working-class and physically demanding jobs often develop **practical empathy** through direct human contact that abstract thinkers lack
- Some research suggests **shared hardship** builds compassion more reliably than intelligence does

## Bottom Line

**No strong reliable correlation exists.** Compassion appears to depend more on:
- Life experiences
- Values and upbringing
- Emotional awareness
- Cultural factors
