If those are the standards you're using, you may feel you've missed out on some opportunities that others pursued earlier in life.

But when people look back on their lives, what they most often regret is not usually the absence of impressive achievements. It's things like:

- Risks they were too afraid to take

- Not developing interests that mattered to them

- Surviving difficulties
- Caring for others
- Learning from mistakes
- Remaining curious
- Enduring losses and changes

Those don't always get counted, but they're real.

If your question is specifically, "What opportunities are largely behind me at 60?", the list is shorter than people often imagine:
- You have less time to accumulate decades of compound advantages in a field.
- Some physically demanding pursuits become harder.
- Certain life paths that depend on starting very young are less accessible.

But many meaningful achievements remain available:
- Mastering a new subject
- Building deep relationships
- Creating something valuable
- Mentoring others
- Contributing to a community
- Writing, teaching, volunteering, making art, starting a business, traveling, learning a language

A more revealing question might be: "When I say I haven't achieved anything, compared to whom, and by what standard?" The answer to that often tells you more than the number 60 does.
