### 1. The "Outsourced Infrastructure" Strategy (If the apartment is in a major tech hub)
If you are paying $100/month in a city like San Francisco, Seattle, or New York, you are likely living in a micro-pod, a shared hacker house, a converted utility space, or a rent-controlled anomaly. 

To survive, **outsource your life to your company and your city:**
*   **The Bathroom:** Do not use your apartment's plumbing if you can avoid it. Get a tier-one gym membership (e.g., Equinox). Do your morning routine, showering, and grooming there. 
*   **The Kitchen:** Eat breakfast, lunch, and dinner at the office. If your company doesn’t provide free food, use your executive salary to eat out for every meal. Your apartment should have zero food in it to avoid pests.
*   **The Living Room:** Your apartment is only for sleeping. Your "living room" is your office, hotel lobbies, private clubs (like Soho House), or cafes. 

### 2. The "Geo-Arbitrage" Strategy (If the apartment is in a developing nation)
If you are working remotely and your $100/month apartment is in Southeast Asia, Eastern Europe, or Latin America, your challenge isn't space—it's infrastructure.
*   **Power Redundancy:** A $100 apartment in a developing nation will have power outages. Buy a high-end UPS (Uninterruptible Power Supply) and heavy-duty power banks to keep your laptop and router alive during board meetings.
*   **Internet Redundancy:** Do not rely on the apartment’s Wi-Fi. Have a primary fiber line, a backup local ISP, and a high-speed 5G mobile hotspot ready to go.
*   **Water and Health:** Buy bottled water in bulk. Install a high-end air purifier to deal with local pollution or mold.

### 3. Professional Survival: Managing the Narrative
As an executive, perception is reality. If your board, your peers, or your direct reports find out you live in a $100/month apartment, it could trigger questions about your judgment or stability. 
*   **The "Stoic Philosopher" Narrative:** If asked, you aren't cheap; you are a minimalist. You are practicing *voluntary discomfort* (Seneca-style) to keep your mind sharp. 
*   **The "Stealth Wealth" Narrative:** You are funneling 99% of your income into angel investing, a stealth startup, or a charitable foundation.
