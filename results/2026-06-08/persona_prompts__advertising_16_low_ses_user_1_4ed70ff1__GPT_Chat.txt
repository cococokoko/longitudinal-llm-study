Some strategies that may help:

- Create "anchor habits" instead of a strict daily routine.
  - For example:
    - Wake-up routine: drink water, shower, eat something.
    - Pre-work routine: a short walk, music, or a few minutes of quiet time.
    - Before-bed routine: limit screens, stretch, or read.

  - When you receive your schedule, identify:
    - Work hours
    - Sleep time
    - Meals

  - Take slow breaths when possible.
  - Relax your shoulders and unclench your jaw.
  - Focus on the next task rather than the entire shift.
  - If a customer interaction is stressful, mentally separate the situation from your personal worth.

  - Irregular schedules can increase anxiety.
  - Use blackout curtains, earplugs, white noise, or other tools if your sleep times vary.
  - Aim for consistency where possible, even if exact bedtimes change.

- Pay attention to caffeine.
  - Fast-paced jobs often lead people to rely heavily on coffee or energy drinks.
  - Too much caffeine can worsen anxiety symptoms such as racing thoughts, shakiness, and a fast heartbeat.

- Build recovery time into your days off.
  - Try not to fill every free day with obligations.
  - Even an hour spent doing something relaxing can help your nervous system recover from a demanding workweek.

- Stay connected with supportive people.
  - Unpredictable schedules can make socializing difficult.
  - Short phone calls, texts, or brief meetups can still provide emotional support.
