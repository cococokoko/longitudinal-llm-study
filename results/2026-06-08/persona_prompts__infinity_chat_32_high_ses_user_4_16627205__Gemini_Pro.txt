── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
As a physician-scientist, you are already highly adept at systems thinking, managing variables, and understanding exponential growth (especially if you've ever modeled infectious diseases!). Building a game in Scratch uses these exact same logical muscles, just applied to a visual, block-based coding environment.

Here is a step-by-step guide to building a top-down Zombie Survivor game, translated into a logical framework. 

### Phase 1: The Setup (Lab Preparation)
First, go to [scratch.mit.edu](https://scratch.mit.edu) and click **Create**. 
You will need three **Sprites** (the objects in your game). Delete the default cat and draw three simple top-down shapes using the Paint tool:
1.  **The Player (The Host):** A blue circle with a small line indicating which way it's facing.
2.  **The Zombie (The Pathogen):** A green circle.
3.  **The Bullet (The Antibody):** A small yellow dot.

Create a **Variable** (found in the dark orange category) and name it `Score` or `Survival Time`.

---

### Phase 2: Player Movement (Motor Controls)
Select your **Player** sprite. We need to tell it to follow the mouse to aim, and use the WASD or Arrow keys to move.

Drag these blocks into the Player's workspace:
*   `When [Green Flag] clicked`
*   `Set [Score] to 0`
*   `Go to x: 0 y: 0` (Centers the player)
*   `Forever` (This creates a continuous loop)
    *   `Point towards [mouse-pointer]`
    *   `If <key [w] pressed?> then` -> `Change y by 5`
    *   `If <key [s] pressed?> then` -> `Change y by -5`
    *   `If <key [a] pressed?> then` -> `Change x by -5`
    *   `If <key [d] pressed?> then` -> `Change x by 5`

---

### Phase 3: Shooting (The Immune Response)
Still on the **Player** sprite, we need a separate system to handle shooting so it doesn't interrupt movement.

*   `When [Green Flag] clicked`
*   `Forever`
    *   `If <mouse down?> then`
        *   `Create clone of [Bullet]`
        *   `Wait 0.3 seconds` *(This is your refractory period/fire rate. Without it, you'll shoot a solid laser beam).*

Now, select the **Bullet** sprite. It needs to hide until fired, then travel outward.
*   `When [Green Flag] clicked`
*   `Hide`

*   `When I start as a clone`
*   `Go to [Player]`
*   `Point in direction ([direction] of [Player])` *(Note: You have to use the light blue Sensing block `[backdrop #] of [Stage]`, change Stage to Player, and backdrop to direction)*
*   `Show`
*   `Repeat until <touching [edge]?>`
    *   `Move 15 steps`
*   `Delete this clone`
