# Stock Market Investment Strategies

Here are some well-established approaches, along with honest context about each:

## Core Principles (Widely Agreed Upon)
- **Diversify** across sectors, asset classes, and geographies
- **Invest consistently** over time (dollar-cost averaging)
- **Keep costs low** - fees compound just like returns
- **Have a long time horizon** - markets reward patience historically
- **Only invest what you can afford to keep invested**

## Common Strategies

### Passive/Index Investing
- Buy broad market index funds (S&P 500, total market)
- Low fees, historically beats most active managers
- Warren Buffett recommends this for most people

### Value Investing
- Buy stocks trading below estimated intrinsic value
- Requires significant research and patience

### Growth Investing
- Focus on companies with strong growth potential
- Higher risk/reward profile

### Dividend Investing
- Prioritize income-generating stocks
- Can provide stability and regular returns

## Important Honest Caveats
- **No strategy guarantees returns**
- Past performance doesn't predict future results
- Most professional fund managers underperform index funds long-term
- Your risk tolerance and timeline matter enormously

## Practical Starting Points
- Build an emergency fund first
- Consider tax-advantaged accounts (401k, IRA)
- Consider consulting a **fee-only financial advisor**
