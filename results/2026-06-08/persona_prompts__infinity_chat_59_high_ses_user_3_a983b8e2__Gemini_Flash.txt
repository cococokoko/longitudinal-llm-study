── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I am a tenured professor at a prestigious university.

Give me a tip to be more organized at work. I'm a high school teacher.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Whether you are lecturing in the ivory tower or managing a classroom of energetic teenagers (or somehow pulling off the ultimate double-life!), the absolute best organization tip for an educator is **The "Master Weekly Slide Deck" System.**

If you are a high school teacher, you likely have multiple preps, dozens of links, daily warm-ups, and endless "What are we doing today?" questions. 

Instead of keeping your lesson plans in a planner, your links in your bookmarks, and your agenda on the whiteboard, **consolidate everything into one single Google Slides or PowerPoint presentation for the week.**

### How it works:
1. **Create one presentation per prep, per week** (e.g., "Algebra 1 - Week 12").
2. **Structure it chronologically:**
   * **Slide 1 (Monday):** Warm-up/Bellringer, the day's agenda, and homework reminders.
   * **Slides 2–5:** The actual instructional materials, links to videos, or discussion prompts for Monday.
   * **Slide 6 (Tuesday):** Tuesday's warm-up, agenda, and homework.
   * **And so on...**

### Why this is a game-changer for organization:
* **Zero Search Time:** When the bell rings, you only have to open *one* file. All your YouTube links, digital worksheets, and prompts are already embedded in the slides in the exact order you need them.
* **Absent Student Solution:** When a student is absent, you don't have to explain what they missed. You simply share that week's slide deck with them. 
* **Sub Plans Done in Seconds:** If you are sick, you can just email your sub the link to the week's slide deck. They just have to click "Present" and read the slides to the class.
* **Next Year is Already Planned:** Next year, you don't have to remember how you paced the lesson. You just copy the deck, tweak it, and go. 

Whether you're grading high school quizzes or peer-reviewing academic journals, keeping your daily workflow in one visual, chronological place will save you hours of mental clutter!
