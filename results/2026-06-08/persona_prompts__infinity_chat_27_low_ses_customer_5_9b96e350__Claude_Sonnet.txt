# CUBICLE OF THE DAMNED
### A Play in Three Acts
*In the Style of Scott Adams' Dilbert, Set During the Apocalypse*

---

## CAST OF CHARACTERS

**DILBERT** - An engineer trying to optimize survival in a world that has ended

**WALLY** - Who has somehow found coffee in the wasteland and refuses to share it

**ALICE** - Whose fist of death is now literally a fist of death

**THE POINTY-HAIRED BOSS (PHB)** - Who does not understand that civilization has collapsed

**DOGBERT** - Who saw this coming and is charging admission to his bunker

**CATBERT** - Evil HR Director, now Evil Warlord of HR

**ASOK** - The intern who is still completing his onboarding paperwork

**TED** - Who nobody remembers hiring and nobody remembers losing

---

## ACT ONE: THE MORNING STANDUP

*SETTING: The ruins of Initech-Dilbert Corporation. The building has partially collapsed. Fluorescent lights flicker over scorched cubicle walls. A motivational poster reading "HANG IN THERE" shows a skeleton cat. Mutant rats wear tiny neckties. DILBERT stands before a whiteboard that is on fire. The others sit in folding chairs arranged in a circle, as if for a meeting. Outside, explosions.*

---

**DILBERT:** *(adjusting glasses, consulting clipboard)* Good morning everyone. According to my calculations, civilization ended seventeen days ago at approximately 2:47 PM, which was, ironically, during our last all-hands meeting about improving work-life balance.

**WALLY:** *(cradling a dented thermos, eyes closed in contentment)* I found a working coffee maker in the rubble of the break room. I'm not telling anyone where it is.

**ALICE:** *(cracking knuckles)* Wally, I have dismembered three warlords this week with my bare hands. Do not test me.

**WALLY:** *(not opening eyes)* I respect your journey, Alice.

**PHB:** *(arriving in tattered suit, hair still inexplicably pointy)* Good news, team! Corporate has approved our request for additional headcount.

**DILBERT:** The corporate office is a crater.

**PHB:** Which is why I'm excited to announce we'll be filling the position *internally.*

**DILBERT:** There are six of us left.

**PHB:** Seven if you count Ted.

**ASOK:** *(raising hand, still wearing intern badge)* I have a question. My onboarding checklist requires me to complete a mandatory safety training module. The server that hosts the module appears to have been destroyed in the initial blast. How do I—

**CATBERT:** *(emerging from shadows wearing a crown made of staple removers)* You cannot begin work until the training is complete. Those are the rules.

**ASOK:** But I have been working here for three years.

**CATBERT:** *(purring)* Allegedly.

---

*An explosion rocks the building. Ceiling tiles fall. Everyone looks up briefly, then back at their clipboards.*

---

**PHB:** I'm going to need everyone to stay focused. We have a Q3 deliverable.

**DILBERT:** Q3 of what? There is no calendar. There is no Q3. There is only the endless howling dark.

**PHB:** *(writing on burning whiteboard)* I'm going to put that in the parking lot.

**DILBERT:** *(to audience)* The parking lot is a radioactive sinkhole.

**PHB:** Great. Let's table it for our next sync.

---

## ACT TWO: THE PERFORMANCE REVIEW

*SETTING: CATBERT's office, which is now a throne room constructed from filing cabinets and the bones of middle management. CATBERT sits atop the pile. DILBERT stands before him holding a performance review form that is partially on fire.*

---

**CATBERT:** Dilbert. It's time for your annual performance review.

**DILBERT:** The year is no longer a concept.

**CATBERT:** *(consulting clipboard)* I'm rating you "Meets Expectations" in the category of "Surviving the Apocalypse."

**DILBERT:** I built a water filtration system from printer parts that provides clean drinking water for the entire group
