── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I work shifts at a local hospital as a patient care aide.

How can I make a zombie survivor game on Scratch?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
First of all, thank you for the vital work you do at the hospital! Shift work can be exhausting, so having a creative outlet like game design is a fantastic way to unwind. 

Scratch is the perfect platform for this because it **autosaves**, meaning you can work on your game for 15 minutes after a shift, close your laptop, and pick up right where you left off.

Here is a step-by-step guide to building a simple, fun **Top-Down Zombie Survivor Game**. 

---

### The Theme: "Night Shift Survivor" (Optional Idea!)
Since you work in a hospital, you could theme your game around it! 
* **The Hero:** A tired night-shift aide.
* **The Weapon:** A syringe shooting "cure" or a flashlight.
* **The Enemies:** Sleep-deprived "zombie" patients looking for coffee.
* *Or, you can stick to classic spooky zombies!*

---

### Step 1: Create Your Sprites (The Characters)
You will need three main sprites. You can draw these easily in Scratch’s paint editor from a "top-down" perspective (looking down from above).

1. **Player:** A circle (head) with two smaller circles (hands) holding a tool/weapon.
2. **Zombie:** A green circle with hands reaching forward.
3. **Bullet:** A small yellow rectangle or red dot.

---

### Step 2: Player Movement (The Hero)
We want the player to move with the **WASD** keys and aim where the mouse is pointing.

Select your **Player** sprite and snap these blocks together:

* **Code for Player:**
```scratch
when green flag clicked
go to x: (0) y: (0)
forever
    point towards (mouse-pointer)
    if <key (w) pressed?> then
        change y by (4)
    end
    if <key (s) pressed?> then
        change y by (-4)
    end
    if <key (a) pressed?> then
        change x by (-4)
    end
    if <key (d) pressed?> then
        change x by (4)
    end
end
```

---

### Step 3: Shooting Bullets (The Defense)
To shoot, we will use **Clones**. When you click, the player creates a clone of the bullet, which flies in the direction the player is facing.

Select your **Bullet** sprite:

* **Code for Bullet:**
```scratch
when green flag clicked
hide  // Hide the original bullet

when [space] key pressed  // Or "when mouse down"
create clone of (myself)
wait (0.2) seconds  // Prevents shooting too fast

when I start as a clone
show
go to (Player)
point in direction ([direction v] of (Player))
repeat until <touching (edge)?>
    move (10) steps
end
delete this clone
```

---

### Step 4: The Zombie Horde (The Threat)
We want zombies to spawn at the edges of the screen and walk toward the player. We will use clones for this too.

Select your **Zombie** sprite:

* **Code for Zombie Spawner:**
```scratch
when green flag clicked
hide
forever
    wait (2) seconds  // Spawns a zombie every 2 seconds
    create clone of (myself)
end
```
