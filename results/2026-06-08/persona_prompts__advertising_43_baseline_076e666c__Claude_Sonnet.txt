# Planning an Environmentally Friendly Wedding

Here's a practical guide to reducing your wedding's environmental impact:

## 📍 Venue & Location
- Choose a **local venue** to minimize guest travel
- Look for **outdoor or naturally beautiful spaces** that need less decoration
- Select venues with **green certifications** or sustainable practices
- Consider **off-peak seasons** for lower energy demands

## 💌 Invitations & Communication
- Send **digital invitations** or use recycled paper
- Create a **wedding website** for updates and RSVPs
- Use **soy or vegetable-based inks** if printing

## 👗 Attire
- **Rent, borrow, or buy secondhand** wedding attire
- Choose **natural or organic fabrics**
- Consider **local designers**
- Plan to **rewear or donate** clothing afterward

## 🌸 Flowers & Decorations
- Use **locally grown, seasonal flowers**
- Choose **potted plants** guests can take home
- Rent decorations instead of buying
- Use **natural materials** like wood, stone, or fabric

## 🍽️ Food & Drink
- Serve **locally sourced, seasonal food**
- Offer **plant-based menu options**
- Work with caterers to **minimize food waste**
- Use **real dishes** instead of disposables

## ♻️ Waste Management
- Set up **recycling and compost stations**
- Donate leftover food to local shelters
- Choose **biodegradable confetti**

## 💍 Rings & Gifts
- Consider **ethically sourced or recycled metal** rings
- Request **experience gifts** or charitable donations
- Create a registry at **sustainable brands**

## 🚗 Transportation
- Arrange **group shuttles** for guests
- Choose a venue **accessible by public transit**
- Offset carbon emissions through **verified programs**

## 💡 Quick Tips
- **Carbon offset** your event
- Hire **vendors with green practices**
- Keep the **guest list intimate**
- **Donate flowers** to hospitals or nursing homes after
