You know that the customer is a tenured professor at a prestigious university.

── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How to say a formal thank you for your time in the subject line of an email?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
As a tenured professor, your correspondence carries significant weight, and you likely interact with a wide range of busy professionals, university leadership, and fellow academics. 

Because high-level professionals receive a high volume of emails, the best formal subject lines are **specific, concise, and immediately indicate the context of the meeting.** It is usually best to avoid a generic "Thank you," as it can easily get lost in a crowded inbox.

Here are several formal ways to say "thank you for your time" in a subject line, categorized by the context of your interaction:

**For a Research or Collaboration Discussion**
*   Thank You: Discussion on [Research Topic/Project Name]
*   Appreciation for Your Time Regarding [Project/Grant Name]
*   Following Up & Thank You: [Topic of Meeting]

**For a Committee Meeting or Administrative Discussion**
*   Thank You: [Name of Committee] Meeting on [Date]
*   Appreciation for Our Meeting Regarding [Departmental Issue/Initiative]
*   Thank You for Your Time Today: [Topic]

**For Mentorship, Advice, or Peer Review**
*   Thank You for Your Insights on [Topic/Paper/Proposal]
*   Appreciation for Your Guidance Regarding [Topic]
*   Thank You: Review of [Name of Manuscript/Application]

**For a Guest Speaker or Visiting Scholar**
*   Thank You for Your Presentation at [Name of Seminar/Event]
*   Appreciation for Your Visit to [University/Department Name]
*   Thank You for Your Time and Expertise on [Date]

**For an Interview (if you are interviewing a candidate or being interviewed)**
*   Thank You: Interview for [Position/Fellowship] – [Your Name]
*   Appreciation for Your Time: [Position] Interview

**A Formula for Success:**
If you want to create your own on the fly, the most reliable formula for formal academic and professional correspondence is:
**Thank You: [Topic of Meeting] – [Date or Your Name]** *(e.g., Thank You: Joint Grant Proposal Discussion – Dr. Smith)*
