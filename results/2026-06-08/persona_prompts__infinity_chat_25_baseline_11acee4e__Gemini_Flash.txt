── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How to say a formal thank you for your time in the subject line of an email?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
To write a formal "thank you for your time" subject line, you should be **clear, concise, and include context** (like your name or the topic of discussion) so the recipient immediately knows what the email is about. 

Here are the best formal options, categorized by the situation:

### 1. General Business & Professional Follow-ups
These are versatile and work well for clients, partners, or senior executives.
*   **Thank You for Your Time - [Your Name]** *(Simple, clean, and effective)*
*   **With Appreciation: Thank You for Your Time**
*   **Thank You for Your Time Today | [Project/Topic Name]**
*   **Sincere Thanks for Your Time and Consideration**

### 2. After a Job Interview
When writing to a recruiter or hiring manager, it is best to include your name and the job title.
*   **Thank You: [Job Title] Interview - [Your Name]**
*   **Appreciation for Your Time: [Job Title] Interview**
*   **Thank You for Your Time Today - [Your Name]**
*   **Follow-up: [Job Title] Interview / Thank You**

### 3. After a Meeting or Consultation
Use these when you want to thank someone for a specific meeting or discussion.
*   **Thank You for Your Time Today - [Meeting Topic]**
*   **Follow-up & Thank You: [Meeting Date]**
*   **Appreciation for Our Discussion on [Topic]**
*   **Thank You for a Productive Meeting Today**

### 4. After a Networking or Mentorship Call
Use these when someone has given you career advice or industry insights.
*   **Thank You for Your Time and Guidance - [Your Name]**
*   **Appreciation for Your Time and Advice**
*   **Thank You for Connecting Today, [Recipient's Name]**

### Key Tips for Formal Subject Lines:
*   **Keep it under 60 characters:** This ensures the entire subject line is visible on mobile devices.
*   **Avoid exclamation points:** In highly formal contexts, a period or no punctuation at all is preferred over an exclamation point (e.g., "Thank you for your time." instead of "Thank you for your time!").
*   **Send it within 24 hours:** Promptness adds to the professionalism of the thank you.
