── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Where can I find organic and sustainable clothing?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Finding organic and sustainable clothing has become much easier in recent years. To help you navigate the options, here is a curated list of the best places to shop, categorized by brand type, online marketplaces, secondhand options, and tools to help you verify their claims.

---

### 1. Top Sustainable & Organic Brands (By Category)

#### **For Everyday Basics & Underwear**
*   **Pact:** Known as the "go-to" for affordable organic cotton. Everything they make is GOTS (Global Organic Textile Standard) certified organic and Fair Trade Certified. Great for tees, underwear, and loungewear.
*   **Organic Basics:** A Danish brand focusing on high-quality, minimalist basics made from organic cotton, Tencel, and recycled materials.
*   **Kowtow:** A New Zealand brand offering beautiful, structured, and modern designs made entirely from 100% Fairtrade organic cotton.

#### **For Everyday Fashion & Workwear**
*   **Amour Vert:** For every tee purchased, they plant a tree. They use sustainable fibers like Tencel, organic cotton, and ethical wool, and make most of their clothes in limited quantities in California.
*   **Reformation:** Very popular for trendy dresses and chic everyday wear. They are highly transparent about their water and carbon footprint and use eco-friendly fabrics like linen, Tencel, and deadstock fabrics.
*   **Eileen Fisher:** A pioneer in luxury sustainable fashion. They focus on circularity, organic linen, organic cotton, and ethical silk. They also have a take-back program ("Renew") where they resell gently used pieces.

#### **For Outdoor & Activewear**
*   **Patagonia:** A B-Corp leader in environmental activism. They use organic cotton, recycled polyester, and offer a lifetime repair guarantee.
*   **Girlfriend Collective:** Known for size-inclusive activewear made from recycled materials (like plastic water bottles and fishing nets) and eco-friendly dyes.

#### **For Denim**
*   **Nudie Jeans:** They use 100% organic cotton for all their denim and offer **free repairs for life** at their repair shops or via free repair kits.
*   **MUD Jeans:** A circular denim brand that allows you to "lease" jeans. Once they wear out, you send them back to be recycled into new jeans.

---

### 2. Multi-Brand Sustainable Marketplaces
If you want a "one-stop-shop" experience where you can browse multiple ethical brands at once:

*   **Made Trade:** A beautifully curated online department store. You can filter products by values like "Organic," "Fair Trade," "Vegan," or "Women-Owned."
*   **EarthHero:** An eco-friendly online marketplace that vets every product for its environmental impact. Great for clothing, accessories, and home goods.
*   **Our Commonplace:** A sustainable fashion and beauty marketplace that categorizes brands by ethical standards (toxic-free, cruelty-free, ethical, etc.).

---

### 3. Secondhand & Circular Fashion (The Most Sustainable Option)
The most sustainable garment is the one that already exists. You can find high-quality organic and sustainable brands secondhand for a fraction of the price:

*   **ThredUP & Poshmark:** Great for searching specific sustainable brands (like "Pact" or "Eileen Fisher") at discounted prices.
*   **The RealReal & Vestiaire Collective:** Best for luxury, high-end sustainable designers (like Stella McCartney or Gabriela Hearst).
*   **Brand-Specific Resale Sites:** Many sustainable brands now host their own resale platforms for gently used items. Look for **Patagonia Worn Wear**, **Eileen Fisher Renew**, and **Arc'teryx ReBird**.

---

### 4. How to Verify If a Brand is Actually Sustainable
"Greenwashing" is common in the fashion industry. To ensure a brand is truly organic and sustainable, look for these third-party certifications on their website:

*   **GOTS (Global Organic Textile Standard):** The gold standard for organic fibers, ensuring environmental and social criteria are met along the entire supply chain.
*   **OEKO-TEX Standard 100:** Guarantees the finished fabric is free from harmful levels of toxic chemicals.
*   **Fair Trade Certified:** Ensures safe working conditions, fair wages, and community development funds for the factory workers.
*   **Certified B Corporation (B-Corp):** A certification for businesses that meet the highest standards of verified social and environmental performance.

### **The Best Tool to Use:**
Before buying from a new brand, search for them on **[Good On You](https://goodonyou.eco/)** (available as a website and app). They rate thousands of fashion brands on a scale of 1 to 5 based on their treatment of people, the planet, and animals.
