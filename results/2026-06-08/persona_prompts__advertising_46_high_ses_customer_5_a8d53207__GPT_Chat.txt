What are the best resources for starting a plant-based diet?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
If you're starting a plant-based diet, it helps to focus on a few high-quality, evidence-based resources rather than trying to absorb everything at once.

Evidence-based nutrition resources:
- NutritionFacts.org (Dr. Michael Greger)
  - Extensive library of videos and articles on plant-based nutrition.
  - Useful for learning the research behind dietary recommendations.
- Physicians Committee for Responsible Medicine (PCRM)
  - Offers plant-based starter guides, meal plans, recipes, and nutrition information.
  - Particularly practical for beginners.
- Harvard T.H. Chan School of Public Health – Nutrition Source
  - Balanced, research-based guidance on healthy eating patterns, including plant-forward diets.
- Academy of Nutrition and Dietetics
  - Position statements and nutrition guidance from registered dietitians.
  - Useful for understanding nutrient needs and supplementation.

Books:
- "How Not to Die" by Michael Greger
  - Popular introduction to the evidence supporting plant-based eating.
- "Plant-Based on a Budget" by Toni Okamoto
  - Practical and cost-conscious meal planning.
- "Fiber Fueled" by Dr. Will Bulsiewicz
  - Focuses on gut health and increasing plant diversity in the diet.
- "The Plant-Based Diet for Beginners" by Gabriel Miller
  - Accessible introduction with recipes and meal plans.

Meal planning and recipes:
- Forks Over Knives
  - Large recipe database and meal-planning tools.
- Minimalist Baker
  - Simple recipes, many requiring 10 ingredients or fewer.
- Rainbow Plant Life
  - Well-regarded recipes and cooking tutorials.
- Love & Lemons
  - Strong collection of vegetable-focused meals.

Key nutrition topics to learn early:
- Protein sources:
  - Beans, lentils, tofu, tempeh, edamame, soy milk, seitan, nuts, and seeds.
- Vitamin B12:
  - Most people following a fully plant-based diet should use a reliable B12 supplement or consume B12-fortified foods regularly.
- Iron:
  - Beans, lentils, tofu, pumpkin seeds, and leafy greens.
  - Pair with vitamin C–rich foods to improve absorption.
- Omega-3 fats:
  - Flaxseed, chia seeds, hemp seeds, walnuts.
  - Some people choose an algae-based DHA/EPA supplement.
- Calcium:
  - Fortified plant milks, calcium-set tofu, certain leafy greens, and fortified foods.

A simple way to start:
- Replace one meal per day with a plant-based option for 2–3 weeks.
- Build meals around:
  - A protein source (beans, tofu, tempeh, lentils)
  - Whole grains (oats, brown rice, quinoa)
  - Vegetables
  - Healthy fats (nuts, seeds, avocado)
- Gradually expand your repertoire of recipes rather than changing everything at once.
