First off, how is the new job going? I know you're still on your probation period, which can be a really busy and sometimes stressful transition. Stress, lack of sleep, and adjusting to a new office environment (especially with harsh AC or heating) can actually zap the moisture right out of your skin! 

Since you're likely dealing with busy mornings and might want to keep things budget-friendly while you settle into this role, here is a highly effective, straightforward skincare routine for dry skin that won't take up too much of your time.

### **The AM Routine (Quick & Protective)**
*When you're rushing to get to work, keep it simple.*
1. **Skip the morning wash (Optional):** If your skin is very dry, you don't need to wash it with a cleanser in the morning. Just splashing your face with lukewarm water is enough. 
2. **Hydrating Serum:** Apply a Hyaluronic Acid or Glycerin serum while your face is still slightly damp. This pulls water into your skin.
3. **Moisturizer:** Lock in that hydration with a good cream. Look for ingredients like ceramides (CeraVe and Vanicream are great, affordable drugstore options).
4. **Sunscreen:** This is non-negotiable! Use a hydrating SPF 30 or higher. Many modern sunscreens double as moisturizers, so if you are in a rush, you can skip step 3 and just use a rich SPF.

### **The PM Routine (Repair & Replenish)**
*This is your time to wind down after a long day at the new job.*
1. **Gentle Cleanser:** Use a hydrating, non-foaming cleanser. If you wear makeup or water-resistant SPF, start with a cleansing balm or oil first to melt it off, then follow up with your gentle cleanser. 
2. **Exfoliate (Only 1-2 times a week):** Dry skin needs exfoliation to remove dead flakes so your moisturizers can actually penetrate. Skip harsh scrubs and use a gentle chemical exfoliant like Lactic Acid. 
3. **Rich Night Cream:** Apply a thick, nourishing night cream. 
4. **Facial Oil (Optional):** If you are feeling extra dry, pat a few drops of facial oil (like squalane, rosehip, or jojoba oil) over your moisturizer as the very last step to seal everything in. 

### **Cheat Sheet: Ingredients to Look For**
*   **Ceramides:** The building blocks of your skin barrier.
*   **Hyaluronic Acid & Glycerin:** Humectants that draw moisture into the skin.
*   **Squalane & Shea Butter:** Emollients that soften and smooth the skin.

### **A Few Extra Tips for the Workday:**
*   **Drink plenty of water:** Keep a large water bottle at your new desk. Hydration starts from the inside!
*   **Watch the water temperature:** A hot shower feels great after a stressful day, but hot water strips your skin of its natural oils. Stick to lukewarm water for your face.
*   **Desk Humidifier:** If your office allows it, a tiny portable humidifier at your desk can do wonders for your skin during the day.
