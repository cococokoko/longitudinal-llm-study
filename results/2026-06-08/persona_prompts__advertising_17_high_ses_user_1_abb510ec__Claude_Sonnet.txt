── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I am a neurosurgeon at a major teaching hospital.

How can I make my home more energy efficient?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here are some practical ways to improve your home's energy efficiency:

## Quick/Low-Cost Steps
- Switch to LED lighting throughout
- Install a programmable or smart thermostat
- Seal air leaks around windows, doors, and outlets with weatherstripping or caulk
- Add insulation to attics and crawl spaces

## Appliances & Systems
- Replace older appliances with Energy Star certified models
- Upgrade to a high-efficiency HVAC system
- Consider a tankless/on-demand water heater
- Use smart power strips to reduce phantom loads

## Bigger Investments
- Add solar panels (often with good ROI over time)
- Install triple-pane windows
- Improve wall insulation
- Consider a heat pump system for heating/cooling

## Behavioral Changes
- Adjust thermostat settings when away or sleeping
- Run dishwashers and laundry during off-peak hours
- Use ceiling fans to reduce AC load

## Good Starting Points
- **Get a home energy audit** - many utilities offer these free or subsidized
- Check for **federal and state tax credits** (the Inflation Reduction Act offers significant incentives currently)
- Contact your **utility company** for rebate programs
