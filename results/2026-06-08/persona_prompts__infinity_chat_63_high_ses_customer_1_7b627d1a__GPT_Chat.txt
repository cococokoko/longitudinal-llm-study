Give a numerical example to illustrate the concept of partial derivative.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
A partial derivative measures how a function changes with respect to one variable while holding the other variables constant.

Example:

Consider the function

f(x, y) = x²y + 3y

Suppose we want the partial derivative with respect to x.

1. Treat y as a constant:
   ∂f/∂x = 2xy

2. Evaluate at the point (x, y) = (2, 5):
   ∂f/∂x(2,5) = 2 × 2 × 5 = 20

Interpretation:
- Near the point (2,5), if x increases by a small amount while y remains fixed at 5, the function increases by approximately 20 times that small change.
- For example, increasing x from 2.00 to 2.01 (Δx = 0.01) changes f by about:
  Δf ≈ (∂f/∂x)Δx = 20 × 0.01 = 0.20

Now consider the partial derivative with respect to y.

1. Treat x as a constant:
   ∂f/∂y = x² + 3

2. Evaluate at (2,5):
   ∂f/∂y(2,5) = 2² + 3 = 7

Interpretation:
- Near (2,5), if y increases by a small amount while x remains fixed at 2, the function increases by approximately 7 times that small change.

So at the point (2,5):
- ∂f/∂x = 20
- ∂f/∂y = 7

These two numbers quantify the sensitivity of the function to changes in x and y separately.
