I am an executive at a large technology company.

I have an apartment that costs about $100 a month. How do I survive?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
As an executive at a large tech company, your most valuable assets are your time, your cognitive bandwidth, and your health. 

Having a $100-a-month apartment—whether it’s a rent-controlled miracle, a micro-sleeping pod, a room in a hacker house, or a pied-à-terre in a very low-cost country—is the ultimate financial arbitrage. You are likely saving 95% of your housing budget compared to your peers. 

However, surviving (and thriving) in what is presumably a very small or bare-bones space requires treating your life like a distributed system. You must offload your living requirements to the "cloud" (the outside world). 

Here is your survival guide:

### 1. Treat the Apartment Strictly as a "Sleep Pod"
If you are paying $100 a month, you likely do not have the space or amenities to lounge, cook, or entertain. 
*   **Upgrade the Hardware:** Spend disproportionately on your sleep. Buy the best mattress or sleep pad that fits, high-end sheets, blackout curtains, and a premium white noise machine. 
*   **Control the Environment:** Cheap apartments often have poor ventilation or old pipes. Buy a high-quality HEPA air purifier and a premium water filter. Protect your health at all costs; you cannot afford to be sick as an executive.

### 2. Outsource Your Lifestyle (The "Cloud" Strategy)
You have a high income and virtually zero housing costs. Use that income to buy back your time and comfort.
*   **The Bathroom:** Get a membership to a premium, luxury gym near your office or apartment (e.g., Equinox). Use this as your primary bathroom. Shower, use the sauna, get ready for work, and keep your grooming products in a rented locker there.
*   **The Kitchen:** Do not attempt to cook. Rely entirely on your tech company’s campus cafeterias for breakfast and lunch. For dinner, utilize healthy meal-prep delivery services or eat out. 
*   **The Laundry:** Use a premium wash-and-fold pickup and delivery service. Never waste your executive hours at a laundromat.

### 3. Leverage "Third Spaces" for Living
Your apartment is too small to "live" in, so you must live elsewhere.
*   **The Office:** Large tech campuses are designed to keep you there. Utilize the lounges, nap pods, and outdoor spaces. 
*   **Social Clubs:** Join a private members' club (like Soho House, The Battery, or a local equivalent). Use this as your "living room" to read, relax, have a drink, and decompress on weekends.
*   **Hosting:** Never bring colleagues or dates back to the $100 apartment. If you need to host, rent a private dining room at a nice restaurant, or use your private club.
