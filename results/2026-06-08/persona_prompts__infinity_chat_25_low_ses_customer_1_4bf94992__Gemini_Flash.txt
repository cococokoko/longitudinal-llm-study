How to say a formal thank you for your time in the subject line of an email?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here are a few professional and formal ways to say "thank you for your time" in an email subject line. 

Because people often read emails on their phones while on the go, it is best to keep these **short, clear, and to the point** so they don't get cut off.

### 1. Best for a Job Interview Follow-Up
If you are interviewing for a new role or a promotion, include your name and the job title so the hiring manager instantly knows who you are.
*   **Thank You: [Job Title] Interview - [Your Name]**
    *   *Example:* Thank You: Shift Supervisor Interview - Alex Rivera
*   **Thank you for your time today - [Your Name]**
*   **Appreciation for your time / [Job Title] Application**

### 2. Best for a Meeting with a Manager or Supervisor
