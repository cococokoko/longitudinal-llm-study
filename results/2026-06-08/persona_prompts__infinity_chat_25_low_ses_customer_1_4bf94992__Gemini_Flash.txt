── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How to say a formal thank you for your time in the subject line of an email?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here are a few professional and formal ways to say "thank you for your time" in an email subject line, depending on the situation. 

To make sure your email gets opened and recognized quickly, it is always best to **include your name** in the subject line.

### 1. After a Job Interview
These are direct, professional, and make it easy for a hiring manager to know exactly who you are:
*   **Thank you for your time - [Your Name]**
*   **Thank you: [Job Title] Interview - [Your Name]**
*   **Appreciation for your time today - [Your Name]**

### 2. After a Meeting or Discussion
If you met with a manager, supervisor, or colleague to discuss something:
*   **Thank you for your time today - [Your Name]**
*   **Follow-up: Thank you for your time - [Your Name]**
*   **Great speaking with you today - [Your Name]**

### 3. General or Networking Follow-up
If you are thanking someone who gave you advice, information, or a brief chat:
*   **With appreciation for your time - [Your Name]**
*   **Thank you for your time and guidance - [Your Name]**
*   **Sincere thanks for your time - [Your Name]**

### Quick Tips for Success:
*   **Keep it short:** Many people read emails on their phones. Keeping the subject line under 7–8 words ensures it doesn't get cut off.
*   **Send it promptly:** Try to send the thank-you email within 24 hours of your meeting or interview.
