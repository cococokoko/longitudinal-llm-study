  - Most training should feel comfortable enough to hold a conversation.

  - Test gels, sports drinks, and snacks during long runs.
  - Never try new foods or fueling strategies on race day.

- Stay hydrated.
  - Drink regularly throughout training.
  - Learn how much fluid you typically need during longer efforts.

- Strength train.
  - Two short sessions per week focusing on legs, core, and hips can reduce injury risk.

  - Sleep is one of the most important performance tools.
  - If you work long hours in a warehouse or spend a lot of time on your feet, pay extra attention to recovery and avoid stacking hard workouts on especially demanding workdays.

- Invest in proper shoes.
  - Get fitted at a running specialty store if possible.
  - Replace shoes when they become worn out.

- Listen to warning signs.
  - Persistent pain, altered running form, or worsening soreness may require rest or evaluation by a medical professional.

- Follow your practiced fueling plan.
- Break the race into smaller segments mentally.
