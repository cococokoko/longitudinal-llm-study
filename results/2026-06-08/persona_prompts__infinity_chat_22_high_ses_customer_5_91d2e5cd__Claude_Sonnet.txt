# Different Views on Discipleship

Discipleship is understood quite differently across theological traditions, denominations, and cultural contexts. Here are some major perspectives:

---

## Traditional/Classical View
- **Formal mentoring relationship** modeled on Jesus and the Twelve
- Emphasis on **doctrine, Scripture memorization, and spiritual disciplines**
- Often structured, intentional, and long-term
- Associated with figures like **Dallas Willard** and **Dietrich Bonhoeffer**

---

## Relational/Organic View
- Discipleship happens **naturally through authentic community**
- Less programmatic, more life-on-life
- Emphasis on **modeling and shared experience**
- Influenced by missional church movements

---

## Programmatic/Institutional View
- Church-based **curriculum and small groups**
- Measurable spiritual growth milestones
- Common in large evangelical churches
- Efficient but sometimes criticized as **transactional**

---

## Missional View
- Discipleship is **inseparable from mission**
- Making disciples *while* engaging the world
- Influenced by **Lesslie Newbigin** and others

---

## Catholic/Sacramental View
- Formation through **liturgy, sacraments, and tradition**
- Community and church authority are central
- Ongoing rather than event-based

---

| Question | Competing Answers |
|----------|------------------|
| Individual vs. communal? | Both emphasized differently |
| Content vs. character? | Knowledge vs. transformation |
| Structured vs. organic? | Programs vs. relationships |
| Local vs. global? | Church-centered vs. missional |
