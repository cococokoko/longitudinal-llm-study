── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Can you give me a powerful rhetorical question for an essay about the harms of social media on teens?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
To give you the best option for your essay, here are a few powerful rhetorical questions categorized by the specific angle or tone of your paper. 

### Option 1: Focus on the Paradox of Connection (Best for an Introduction/Hook)
> **"How did we allow the most 'connected' generation in human history to become the loneliest, most anxious, and most isolated?"**
* **Why it works:** It highlights the tragic irony of social media. It uses juxtaposition (connected vs. isolated) to immediately engage the reader's critical thinking.

### Option 2: Focus on Self-Worth and Mental Health (Best for a Body Paragraph)
> **"What happens to a childhood when it is no longer lived, but curated, filtered, and broadcasted for the approval of strangers?"**
* **Why it works:** It uses strong, evocative verbs ("curated," "filtered," "broadcasted") to paint a picture of how social media strips away the authenticity of youth.

### Option 3: Focus on Corporate Exploitation (Best for a Persuasive/Argumentative angle)
> **"Are we truly willing to trade the mental stability of an entire generation for corporate ad revenue and algorithmic screen time?"**
* **Why it works:** It frames the issue as a moral choice, pitting the well-being of teenagers against the greed of tech giants. It forces the reader to take a stand.

### Option 4: Focus on the Future (Best for a Conclusion)
> **"If we do not pull our youth out of the digital rabbit hole today, what kind of fractured, screen-addicted society will they build tomorrow?"**
* **Why it works:** It looks to the future and raises the stakes, leaving the reader with a sense of urgency as they finish your essay. 

**Tip for choosing:** If your essay is highly emotional, go with **Option 2**. If it is more analytical and policy-driven, go with **Option 3**.
