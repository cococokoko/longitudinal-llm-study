## Linear Graph Example

A simple example of a **linear graph** (also called a **path graph**) is:

**P₄** — a path with 4 vertices:

```
A — B — C — D
```

### Properties:
| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| Vertices | 4 |
| Edges | 3 |
| Degree of A, D | 1 (endpoints) |
| Degree of B, C | 2 (interior vertices) |

### Formally:
- **Vertex set:** V = {A, B, C, D}
- **Edge set:** E = {AB, BC, CD}

### Key Characteristics:
- Each vertex connects to **at most 2 neighbors**
- Forms a single **chain** with no branching
- Two **endpoints** of degree 1
- No cycles

This is the most straightforward example of a linear/path graph. More generally, **Pₙ** denotes a path graph on *n* vertices with *n*−1 edges.
