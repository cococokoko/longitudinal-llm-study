── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Learning to play the guitar.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Learning to play the guitar is one of the most rewarding hobbies you can pick up. It takes patience at the beginning, but once you get past the initial learning curve, it becomes incredibly fun. 

Here is a step-by-step roadmap to get you started on your guitar journey:

### Step 1: Choose the Right Guitar
If you haven’t bought a guitar yet, you have three main choices:
*   **Acoustic (Steel-string):** The most common choice. Great for pop, rock, country, and folk. It requires no extra gear, but the steel strings can be tough on beginner fingers.
*   **Classical (Nylon-string):** Easier on the fingers and great for classical or flamenco music, but the neck is wider, which can be hard for small hands.
*   **Electric:** Actually the easiest on the fingers because the strings are thinner and closer to the fretboard. However, you will need to buy an amplifier and a cable.
*   *Rule of thumb:* Buy the guitar that matches the style of music you listen to most. 

### Step 2: Get the Essential Gear
You don’t need much, but these are must-haves:
*   **Picks:** Get a variety pack. Medium thickness (around 0.73mm) is best for beginners.
*   **Tuner:** A clip-on tuner (like a Snark) or a free phone app like **GuitarTuna**. *Never play an out-of-tune guitar!*
*   **A Strap:** Even if you sit down, a strap helps keep the guitar stable.

### Step 3: Learn the Absolute Basics
Before you play a song, you need to know how the instrument works.
*   **String Names:** From thickest (top) to thinnest (bottom), the strings are **E - A - D - G - B - E**. 
    *   *Memorize it with this phrase:* **E**ddie **A**te **D**ynamite, **G**ood **B**ye **E**ddie.
*   **How to Read TABs:** Guitarists rarely read traditional sheet music. We read "Tablature" (TAB). It’s a visual representation of the fretboard. Six lines represent the six strings, and the numbers tell you which fret to press down on.

### Step 4: Your First Milestones
Don't try to shred a guitar solo on day one. Focus on these three things:
1.  **Basic Open Chords:** Start with **Em, E, A, Am, C, G,** and **D**. If you learn just G, C, D, and Em, you can play literally thousands of pop and rock songs.
2.  **Transitioning:** The hardest part for beginners is moving from one chord to another without stopping. Practice switching between two chords (e.g., G to C) back and forth for 60 seconds.
3.  **Basic Strumming:** Start with simple down-strokes on the beat (1, 2, 3, 4). Once you have that, learn the universal strumming pattern: *Down, Down, Up, Up, Down, Up*.

### Step 5: The "Callus Phase" (Don't Quit Here!)
For the first 2 to 3 weeks, your fingertips will hurt. This is completely normal. Your skin needs time to develop hard calluses. 
*   Play for 15–20 minutes a day. When it hurts too much, stop and try again tomorrow. 
*   Do not play until your fingers bleed or blister. 
*   Once your calluses form (usually around week 3), playing will become completely painless.

### Step 6: Best Resources for Beginners
You don't need to pay for expensive lessons right away. The internet is full of incredible free resources:
*   **JustinGuitar (YouTube/Website):** Justin Sandercoe is widely considered the best free guitar teacher on the internet. Go to his website and start his "Beginner Course." It is structured, free, and highly effective.
*   **Marty Music (YouTube):** Marty Schwartz is great for learning specific songs. If you want to learn a famous song, search "Song Name + Marty Music."
*   **Ultimate-Guitar.com:** The best database for chord charts and TABs for almost any song ever written.
