── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
What are the best ways to improve indoor air quality?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Improving indoor air quality (IAQ) is crucial for your health, as the average person spends about 90% of their time indoors. The Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) outlines three primary strategies for improving IAQ: **source control, improved ventilation, and air cleaners.** 

Here is a comprehensive guide to the best ways to improve the air quality in your home or office, broken down by category.

---

### 1. Source Control (Stop Pollution at the Source)
The most effective way to improve indoor air is to eliminate individual sources of pollution or reduce their emissions.
*   **Eliminate Synthetic Fragrances:** Plug-in air fresheners, aerosol sprays, and scented candles release Volatile Organic Compounds (VOCs) like formaldehyde and benzene. Opt for naturally scented products or simply rely on fresh air.
*   **Switch Cleaning Products:** Many conventional household cleaners contain harsh chemicals. Switch to non-toxic, fragrance-free, or natural alternatives (like white vinegar and baking soda).
*   **Ban Indoor Smoking:** Secondhand smoke contains thousands of chemicals. Never allow smoking or vaping indoors.
*   **Vent Gas Appliances:** Gas stoves release nitrogen dioxide and carbon monoxide. Always use an exhaust hood that vents to the *outside* when cooking, or open a nearby window.
*   **Choose Low-VOC Materials:** When renovating or buying new furniture, look for low-VOC paints, glues, and pressed-wood products. 

### 2. Improve Ventilation (Bring in Fresh Air)
Diluting indoor pollutants with outdoor air is the second most effective strategy.
*   **Open Windows Daily:** Even just 10–15 minutes of cross-ventilation (opening windows on opposite sides of the house) can flush out stale air and pollutants. *Note: Check your local Air Quality Index (AQI) and pollen counts first; keep windows closed on high-smog or high-pollen days.*
*   **Use Exhaust Fans:** Always run exhaust fans in the bathroom while showering and in the kitchen while cooking to remove moisture and fumes. Leave them on for 15 minutes after you finish.
*   **Trickle Vents:** If your windows have trickle vents, keep them open to allow a constant, small flow of fresh air.

### 3. Upgrade Air Filtration and Purification
If you cannot remove the source or ventilate adequately, you must filter the air.
*   **Upgrade Your HVAC Filter:** If you have central air, upgrade your filter to a **MERV 13** rating (if your system can handle it). This captures fine particles, including smoke, smog, and virus carriers. Change the filter every 1 to 3 months.
*   **Invest in a True HEPA Purifier:** Buy a standalone air purifier for the rooms you spend the most time in (like the bedroom). Ensure it uses a **True HEPA filter** (which captures 99.97% of particles) and an **Activated Carbon filter** (which absorbs odors and VOCs).
*   **Avoid Ozone Generators:** Never buy an air purifier that intentionally generates ozone. Ozone is a lung irritant and can worsen asthma.

### 4. Control Humidity
High humidity breeds mold and dust mites, while low humidity irritates your airways. The ideal indoor humidity level is between **30% and 50%**.
*   **Use a Dehumidifier:** If your home is damp (especially in basements), use a dehumidifier to keep moisture levels down.
*   **Fix Leaks Promptly:** Repair leaking pipes, roofs, or windows immediately to prevent black mold from taking root.
*   **Clean Humidifiers:** If you use a humidifier in the winter, clean it every few days and use distilled water. A dirty humidifier will blast bacteria and mold spores directly into the air.

### 5. Adopt Better Cleaning Habits
Physical dust contains dead skin, pet dander, pollen, and chemical residue. Removing it properly is key.
*   **Vacuum with a HEPA Filter:** Standard vacuums can spit fine dust back into the air. Use a vacuum equipped with a HEPA filter and vacuum high-traffic areas at least once a week.
*   **Damp Dusting:** Never use a dry feather duster, which just pushes dust into the air. Use a damp microfiber cloth to trap dust on surfaces.
*   **Wash Bedding Weekly:** Wash your sheets, pillowcases, and blankets in hot water (at least 130°F / 54°C) every week to kill dust mites.
