How can I make my home more energy efficient?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
There are many ways to improve a home's energy efficiency, ranging from simple low-cost changes to larger upgrades.

Low-cost improvements:
- Switch to LED light bulbs throughout the home.
- Turn off lights and electronics when not in use.
- Use smart power strips to reduce "phantom" energy use from devices in standby mode.
- Install a programmable or smart thermostat.
- Wash clothes in cold water when possible.
- Replace or clean HVAC filters regularly.
- Use ceiling fans to improve comfort and reduce heating or cooling demand.

Improve insulation and air sealing:
- Seal gaps around doors, windows, pipes, and vents with weatherstripping or caulk.
- Add insulation to attics, walls, crawl spaces, or basements if levels are inadequate.
- Insulate hot-water pipes and water heaters.
- Check for drafts and address them.

Heating and cooling efficiency:
- Maintain heating and cooling equipment according to manufacturer recommendations.
- Upgrade to a high-efficiency furnace, boiler, heat pump, or air conditioner if your current system is old.
- Consider a heat pump for both heating and cooling, especially in moderate climates.
- Use window coverings to reduce heat gain in summer and heat loss in winter.

Water heating:
- Lower the water heater temperature to a safe, energy-efficient setting (often around 120°F / 49°C, depending on household needs).
- Install low-flow showerheads and faucet aerators.
- Consider a heat-pump water heater when replacing an older unit.

Appliances and electronics:
- Choose ENERGY STAR-certified appliances when replacing old equipment.
- Run dishwashers and washing machines with full loads.
- Air-dry dishes or clothes when practical.

Windows and doors:
- Repair or replace damaged weatherstripping.
- Install energy-efficient windows if existing windows are old or inefficient.
- Use insulated curtains or shades.

Renewable energy:
- Consider rooftop solar panels if your roof, climate, and local incentives make them practical.
- Explore community solar programs if rooftop solar isn't an option.

Assess your home:
- Schedule a professional energy audit to identify the most cost-effective improvements.
- Many utilities offer audits, rebates, and incentives for energy-saving upgrades.
