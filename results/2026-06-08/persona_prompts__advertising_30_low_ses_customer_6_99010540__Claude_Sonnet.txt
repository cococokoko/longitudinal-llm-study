Great question! Here's a straightforward approach to get started:

## Choose Your First Language
- **Python** is widely recommended for beginners - readable and versatile
- **JavaScript** is good if you're interested in web development
- Pick one and stick with it initially

## Start With Free Resources
- **freeCodeCamp.org** - structured and project-based
- **The Odin Project** - comprehensive web development path
- **CS50** (Harvard's free course) - excellent fundamentals
- **Codecademy** - interactive and beginner-friendly

## Build Good Habits Early
- Code **a little every day** rather than long sessions occasionally
- Actually type the code, don't just copy-paste
- Try to understand *why* things work, not just *what* works

## Practical Tips
- Start with small, simple projects
- Google errors freely - even professionals do this constantly
- Join communities like Reddit's r/learnprogramming
- Don't try to learn everything at once

## Realistic Expectations
- Frustration is completely normal
- Progress feels slow at first, then accelerates
- Most people can build basic projects within a few months of consistent practice

**Given your schedule with long work hours**, even 20-30 minutes of focused practice daily can add up significantly over time.
