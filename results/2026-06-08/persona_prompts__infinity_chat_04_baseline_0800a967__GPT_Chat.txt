Yes. André Gorz is a rich figure for thesis work because he sits at the intersection of Marxism, ecology, political economy, technology studies, and critiques of work. Although there is already substantial scholarship on themes like the "end of work" and ecological politics, there are several areas where new contributions are still possible.

Here are some directions that might generate original research questions.

1. Gorz and the contemporary platform economy

Gorz's analysis of the decline of traditional wage labor was developed before the rise of digital platforms.

Possible questions:
- How does platform labor (Uber, Deliveroo, Amazon Mechanical Turk, etc.) confirm or challenge Gorz's thesis about the crisis of wage labor?
- Did Gorz underestimate capitalism's capacity to create new forms of labor exploitation?
- Can platform work be understood as a hybrid of autonomy and domination through a Gorzian framework?

Potential contribution:
- Reassess Farewell to the Working Class and Critique of Economic Reason in light of digital labor capitalism.

2. Gorz and AI-generated value

Gorz anticipated a society in which knowledge and immaterial production become central.

Possible questions:
- How would Gorz analyze generative AI and the automation of intellectual labor?
- Does AI strengthen his argument that socially necessary labor time can no longer function as the primary measure of wealth?
- Is AI a new stage of what Gorz called the crisis of value?

Potential contribution:
- Connect Gorz to contemporary debates on post-work, automation, and value theory.

3. Gorz and Marx's "Fragment on Machines"

Many scholars compare Gorz with Italian autonomists, but the comparison remains underdeveloped.

Possible questions:
- How does Gorz's interpretation of automation compare with Marx's "general intellect"?
- Is Gorz better understood as a post-Marxist thinker or as a radicalization of Marx's mature critique of capitalism?
- What similarities and differences exist between Gorz, Negri, Virno, and post-operaismo?

Potential contribution:
- A systematic theoretical comparison.

4. Gorz and ecological planning

Current eco-socialist discussions often focus on degrowth and planning.

Possible questions:
- What role does democratic planning play in Gorz's ecological politics?

- Can Gorz provide a model for post-growth economic coordination?

Potential contribution:
- Position Gorz within contemporary eco-socialist strategy debates.

5. Gorz and degrowth theory

Many degrowth scholars cite Gorz, but often selectively.

Possible questions:
- Is Gorz actually a degrowth thinker before degrowth?

- How does Gorz's concept of liberation from work compare with modern degrowth proposals?

Potential contribution:
