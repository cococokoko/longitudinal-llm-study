Healthy meal planning on a budget is largely about choosing nutrient-dense staples, minimizing food waste, and building meals around affordable ingredients that can be used in multiple ways throughout the week.

Key principles

- Plan before you shop.
  - Create a simple weekly menu.
  - Check what you already have at home.
  - Build a shopping list and stick to it.

- Prioritize affordable nutrient-rich foods.
  - Beans, lentils, and chickpeas
  - Eggs
  - Oats
  - Brown rice and whole-grain pasta
  - Frozen vegetables and fruit
  - Canned tuna, salmon, or sardines
  - Plain yogurt
  - Seasonal produce
  - Potatoes and sweet potatoes

- Buy versatile ingredients.
  - For example, a bag of spinach can be used in omelets, salads, soups, and pasta dishes.
  - Cooked chicken can become salads, wraps, grain bowls, and stir-fries.

- Reduce waste.
  - Use leftovers for lunches.
  - Freeze extra portions.
  - Repurpose ingredients across multiple meals.

Example budget-friendly weekly meal framework

Breakfasts
- Oatmeal with fruit and nuts
- Greek yogurt with berries
- Eggs with whole-grain toast

Lunches
- Lentil soup and fruit
- Chicken and vegetable rice bowl
- Tuna salad wrap with carrots

Dinners
- Bean chili with brown rice
- Baked chicken, roasted vegetables, and potatoes
- Vegetable stir-fry with tofu and rice
- Whole-grain pasta with vegetables and beans
- Salmon, sweet potatoes, and frozen broccoli

Healthy snack options
- Apples or bananas
- Carrots and hummus
- Hard-boiled eggs
- Plain popcorn
- Nuts in moderate portions
- Cottage cheese

Cost-saving tactics
- Buy store brands when quality is comparable.
- Compare price per unit rather than package price.
- Use frozen produce when fresh is expensive.
- Purchase larger quantities of staple foods that store well.
- Cook in batches once or twice per week.

For a busy executive schedule, a practical approach is to prepare two proteins, one grain, and several vegetables on the weekend. Mix and match them throughout the week to create different meals with minimal daily cooking. This typically provides a strong balance of nutrition, convenience, and cost control.
