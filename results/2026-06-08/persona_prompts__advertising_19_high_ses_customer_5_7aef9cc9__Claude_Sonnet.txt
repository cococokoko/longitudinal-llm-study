── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here are some evidence-based approaches to improving mental health:

## Daily Habits
- **Sleep consistently** - 7-9 hours is strongly linked to mental wellbeing
- **Regular exercise** - even 20-30 minutes has measurable mood benefits
- **Limit alcohol** - it's a depressant despite feeling like stress relief
- **Reduce caffeine** if you notice anxiety

## Stress Management
- **Set boundaries** between work and personal time
- **Practice mindfulness or meditation** - apps like Headspace can help
- **Take real breaks** during the day, not just scrolling
- **Delegate** where possible

## Social Connection
- Maintain relationships outside of work
- Be honest with trusted people about how you're doing
- Consider a peer group or executive coach

## Professional Support
- **Therapy** is genuinely effective - many executives find it valuable for both personal and professional clarity
- Your company's EAP (Employee Assistance Program) may offer confidential resources
- A psychiatrist if symptoms are significant

## Things Worth Examining
- Whether your workload is sustainable
- Whether your role aligns with your values
- How much control you feel you have

**One honest note:** As an executive, you may face pressure to appear fine. Seeking help is actually a strength, not a weakness.
