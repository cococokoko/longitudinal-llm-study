### **Project Title:** *From Waste to Water: Using Rice Husk Biochar to Clean Vietnam’s Polluted Waterways*

#### **The Vietnam Connection (The "Why"):**
Vietnam is one of the world’s largest exporters of rice, which generates millions of tons of **rice husks** (agricultural waste) every year. At the same time, Vietnam—especially the Mekong Delta—faces a severe water crisis due to industrial pollution, agricultural runoff, and arsenic contamination in groundwater. 

This project tests a "circular economy" solution: turning Vietnam's most abundant agricultural waste (rice husks) into a low-cost water filtration system.

---

### **The Science Concept:**
When rice husks are heated without oxygen, they turn into **biochar** (charred rice hulls). This biochar is highly porous and acts like a magnet, trapping heavy metals, organic pollutants, and dyes through a process called **adsorption**.
