## Analysis vs. Design

**Analysis** answers *"What does the system need to do?"* — understanding requirements, problems, and constraints.

**Design** answers *"How will the system do it?"* — defining architecture, components, and implementation approach.

---

## Can You Design Without Analysis?

**Technically yes, practically no.**

Skipping analysis means you risk:
- **Building the wrong thing** — solving a misunderstood problem
- **Costly rework** — defects found late are exponentially more expensive to fix
- **Scope creep** — no baseline to evaluate change against

In fast-moving environments (startups, prototyping), teams often compress or parallelize analysis and design — but they're still doing *some* analysis, even informally.

---

## The Executive Takeaway

Analysis and design are distinct disciplines that feed each other. Organizations that skip rigorous analysis in favor of moving quickly to design often **spend more time and money overall**, not less. The discipline to ask *"what problem are we actually solving?"* before *"how do we build it?"* is one of the highest-leverage investments in any technology initiative.
