### Project Title: **Project Lotus: Purifying Textile Wastewater Using Agricultural Waste (Bio-Adsorption)**

### 🇻🇳 The Vietnam Connection
Vietnam is one of the world’s largest exporters of textiles and garments. While this boosts the economy, textile factories often discharge wastewater containing synthetic dyes into rivers, harming aquatic life and local communities. At the same time, Vietnam is an agricultural powerhouse, producing millions of tons of agricultural waste (like rice husks, banana peels, and coffee grounds) every year. 

**The Idea:** What if we use Vietnam’s agricultural waste to clean up its industrial water pollution? 

### 🔬 The Science (STEM Breakdown)
*   **Science (Chemistry):** You will explore **Adsorption** (not absorption). Agricultural waste contains cellulose and lignin. When dried and crushed, these materials become highly porous. The molecules of synthetic dyes stick to the surface of these pores, removing the color and toxins from the water.
*   **Technology:** You will use a free smartphone app (a Colorimeter or Light Meter app) to measure the exact transparency of the water before and after filtration.
*   **Engineering:** You will design and build a multi-stage water filtration column using recycled plastic bottles.
*   **Math:** You will calculate the flow rate of the water and graph the percentage of dye removed over time.

### 🛒 Materials Needed (Extremely Affordable)
*   **Agricultural Waste:** Rice husks, banana peels, or used coffee grounds (Free).
*   **Polluted Water Substitute:** Water mixed with blue or red food coloring, or Methylene Blue (available cheaply at pharmacies/aquarium shops).
*   **Hardware:** 2-3 empty plastic water bottles (recycled), cotton balls or coffee filters, scissors.
*   **Tech:** A smartphone with a free "Colorimeter" or "Lux Meter" app downloaded.

### 🛠️ Step-by-Step Experiment
**Phase 1: Prepare the Bio-Adsorbents**
1. Collect banana peels and rice husks. 
2. Wash them thoroughly, then bake them in an oven at a low temperature (or leave them in the hot Vietnam sun for a few days) until completely dry and crispy.
3. Crush them into a coarse powder using a blender or a mortar and pestle. 

**Phase 2: Build the Engineering Column**
1. Cut the bottom off a plastic water bottle and turn it upside down (cap pointing down). Poke a small hole in the cap.
2. Place a layer of cotton balls at the bottom (near the cap) so your powder doesn't fall through.
3. Pack the bottle with your crushed banana peels or rice husks. 

**Phase 3: The Test**
1. Mix your "textile wastewater" (water + food dye). 
2. Pour the dyed water into the top of your filter.
3. Collect the water that drips out of the bottom into a clear glass. 
4. **The Magic:** The water dripping out should be significantly clearer than the water you poured in!
