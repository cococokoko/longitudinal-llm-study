Here are a few powerful rhetorical questions you can use, depending on the specific angle of your essay.

### Option 1: Focus on Mental Health & Isolation (Best for a general hook)
> **"How did a tool designed to connect the entire world leave an entire generation of teenagers feeling more isolated, anxious, and alone than ever before?"**
* **Why it works:** It highlights the tragic irony of social media—that "connection" has actually caused loneliness.

### Option 2: Focus on Self-Esteem & Body Image (Best for essays on comparison/filters)
> **"What happens to a teenager's self-worth when they spend their formative years comparing their messy, real-life struggles to everyone else's polished, filtered highlight reels?"**
* **Why it works:** It paints a vivid picture of the psychological damage caused by the "comparison trap."

### Option 3: Focus on Tech Addiction & Manipulation (Best for essays on algorithms/big tech)
> **"Are we truly comfortable living in a society where the mental health of our youth is the price we pay for a tech company's advertising revenue?"**
* **Why it works:** It is provocative and shifts the blame onto the powerful corporations designing these addictive platforms.

### Option 4: Focus on the Loss of Childhood (Best for a dramatic, emotional opening)
> **"When did we decide that a teenager's value should be measured in double-taps, views, and the approval of strangers, rather than the content of their character?"**
* **Why it works:** It appeals to the reader's sense of morality and nostalgia for a simpler time.
