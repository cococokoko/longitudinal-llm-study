# Mastering Digital Photography

Here's a structured overview to help you build strong skills:

---

## Core Technical Foundations

### Exposure Triangle
- **Aperture** (f-stop) — controls depth of field and light intake
- **Shutter Speed** — freezes or blurs motion
- **ISO** — sensor sensitivity; higher = more noise

### Shooting Modes
- **Manual (M)** — full control
- **Aperture Priority (Av)** — you set aperture, camera sets shutter
- **Shutter Priority (Tv)** — useful for action shots

---

## Composition Principles
- Rule of thirds
- Leading lines
- Framing within the frame
- Negative space
- Light direction and quality

---

## Key Skills to Develop
- **RAW vs JPEG** — RAW gives far more editing latitude
- **Histogram reading** — avoid blown highlights/crushed shadows
- **White balance** — critical for accurate color
- **Focus modes** — single vs continuous tracking

---

## Post-Processing
- Lightroom or Capture One for organization and editing
- Understanding curves, color grading, masking

---

## Practical Suggestions
- Shoot consistently in one genre to build intuition
- Study light — **golden hour** and **overcast** are forgiving
- Review your failures analytically
