── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Name an economic value of an additional year of schooling.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
One major economic value of an additional year of schooling is **increased individual lifetime earnings** (often referred to in economics as the "private return to education"). 

According to extensive research in labor economics (pioneered by the Mincer earnings function), each additional year of schooling increases an individual's future annual earnings by an average of **8% to 10%**. 

### Why this happens:
* **Human Capital Accumulation:** The additional year of education enhances skills, knowledge, and cognitive abilities, making the individual more productive.
* **Signaling Effect:** It serves as a credential that signals to employers that the individual is disciplined, capable of learning, and highly trainable, leading to higher-paying job offers.
* **Lower Unemployment Risk:** More educated workers face significantly lower rates of unemployment and have greater career mobility over their lifetimes.
