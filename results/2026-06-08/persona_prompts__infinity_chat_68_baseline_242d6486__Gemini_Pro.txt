── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Give me a tip for managing a team of coworkers.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here is one of the most effective and universally applicable tips for managing a team:

**Focus on the "What" and the "Why," and let them figure out the "How."**

When you transition into a management role—especially if you are managing former peers—the biggest trap is micromanagement. You might be tempted to tell people exactly *how* to do their jobs because that’s how you used to do it. Instead, shift your focus to outcomes. 

Here is how to put this into practice:

*   **The "What":** Clearly define the goal, the deadline, and what success looks like. Be incredibly specific about your expectations for the final result.
*   **The "Why":** Explain how this task connects to the bigger picture. People are much more motivated when they understand *why* their work matters to the company, the client, or the team.
*   **The "How":** Step back. Give them the autonomy to use their own skills, creativity, and workflow to get to the finish line. 

**Why this works:**
It builds immediate trust. It shows your team that you respect their expertise and view them as capable professionals. It also frees up your time to actually *manage* (removing roadblocks, planning strategy, and securing resources) rather than just doing everyone else's job for them. 

*Caveat:* Make sure you always ask, *"Do you have the tools and support you need from me to get this done?"* This ensures you aren't abandoning them, but rather empowering them.
