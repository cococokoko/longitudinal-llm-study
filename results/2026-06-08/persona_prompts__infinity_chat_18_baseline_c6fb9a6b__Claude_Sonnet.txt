# Review: *The Last Frequency* (2024)

★★★★½

Director Mara Okonkwo's debut feature is a quiet devastation. Set in a near-future where human memory can be broadcast like radio signals, *The Last Frequency* follows a grieving sound engineer who discovers his late daughter's memories are still transmitting from somewhere in the mountains of rural Iceland.

What could have been a cold sci-fi thriller becomes something far more intimate. The film resists explaining its own technology, trusting the audience to simply accept the premise and feel its weight. The result is less *Black Mirror* and more *Terrence Malick directing a ghost story*.

Lead actor Demián Bautista gives a career-best performance — mostly silent, mostly searching — while newcomer Yuki Tanaka haunts every frame she appears in, which is never quite enough.

The third act stumbles slightly, trading the film's earned ambiguity for one scene of unnecessary explanation. But Okonkwo recovers beautifully in the final ten minutes, ending on an image so simple and so crushing that the theater I saw it in stayed completely silent through the credits.

*The Last Frequency* is the kind of film that makes you drive home a little slower, thinking about everyone you'd still want to hear from.

**Not to be missed.**
