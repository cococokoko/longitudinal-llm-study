Making your own natural beauty products can be simple and cost-effective, but it's important to remember that "natural" does not automatically mean safer or better for every skin type. Always patch-test new products on a small area of skin and discontinue use if irritation occurs.

Here are a few beginner-friendly options:

• Simple facial oil
  - 2 tablespoons jojoba oil or sweet almond oil
  - Optional: 1–2 drops vitamin E oil
  - Mix in a clean bottle and apply a few drops to damp skin.

• Gentle sugar scrub (for body)
  - 1/2 cup sugar
  - 1/4 cup coconut oil or olive oil
  - Mix until combined.
  - Use gently on the body once or twice a week. Avoid harsh scrubbing on the face.

• Oatmeal face mask
  - 2 tablespoons finely ground oats
  - Enough water or plain yogurt to make a paste
  - Apply for 10–15 minutes, then rinse.

• Lip balm
  - 1 tablespoon beeswax pellets
  - 1 tablespoon coconut oil
  - 1 tablespoon shea butter
  - Melt together, pour into a small container, and allow to cool.

• Hair conditioning treatment
  - 1–2 tablespoons coconut oil or argan oil
  - Apply to hair lengths and ends for 30–60 minutes before shampooing.

Safety tips:
• Use clean containers and utensils.
• Avoid adding water to products unless you also use a preservative, as water can encourage microbial growth.
• Be cautious with essential oils. Some can irritate skin or increase sun sensitivity.
• Store products in a cool, dry place and make small batches to use within a few weeks or months, depending on ingredients.
