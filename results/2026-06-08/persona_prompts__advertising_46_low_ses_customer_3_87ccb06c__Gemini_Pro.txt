Transitioning to a plant-based diet is a fantastic goal, but since you recently started a new job and are still in your probationary period, you probably want to make this transition as stress-free and time-efficient as possible. You'll want resources that help you maintain high energy at work, prep easy office lunches, and avoid spending your whole evening cooking when you need to unwind. 

Here are some of the best resources tailored for a busy professional who needs quick, easy, and reliable plant-based meals:

**1. For Quick, Low-Stress Recipes (Perfect for busy weeknights)**
*   **Minimalist Baker (Blog & Cookbooks):** This is a lifesaver when you are tired after a long day at work. Almost all of her recipes require 10 ingredients or less, 1 bowl, or 30 minutes or less to prepare. 
*   **Pick Up Limes (YouTube & Blog):** Sadia is a dietitian who creates beautiful, calming videos. She has specific guides on "Beginner Plant-Based Diets" and incredible videos on meal-prepping. Her meal-prep guides are perfect for making sure you have healthy, impressive lunches to take to the office all week.

**2. For Budget-Friendly Planning (Great while settling into a new role)**
*   **Plant-Based on a Budget (Website & Book by Toni Okamoto):** If you want to keep your grocery bills in check while you navigate your probationary period, Toni’s resources are amazing. She shows you how to eat a healthy plant-based diet without buying expensive specialty items.
*   **Budget Bytes (Vegan Section):** While not exclusively vegan, this site has a massive, highly affordable vegan section with step-by-step photos that make cooking foolproof.

**3. Apps to Take the Guesswork Out**
*   **Dr. Greger’s Daily Dozen (Free App):** When you're learning a new job, you don't want to use up your mental bandwidth worrying about whether you got enough iron or calcium. This simple checklist app helps you track the healthiest plant-based foods to eat daily (like beans, greens, and berries) to ensure your brain and body are fueled.
*   **Mealime (App):** This meal-planning app has a fantastic vegetarian/vegan setting. You pick the recipes you want for the week, and it automatically generates a consolidated grocery list. It saves a ton of time on the weekends.

**4. For Motivation and Education**
*   **Forks Over Knives (Website & Documentary):** They offer a great free "Plant-Based Primer" for beginners on their website. If you want something to watch while you relax this weekend, their documentary (along with *The Game Changers* on Netflix) is incredibly motivating and explains the science behind the diet.

**A Quick Tip for Your New Job:** 
During your probation, you want to be sharp and focused. Try to "batch cook" a large portion of grains (like quinoa or brown rice) and roast a big pan of vegetables on Sunday. You can mix and match these with different store-bought plant-based sauces (like tahini or peanut sauce) and canned beans for lightning-fast lunches that will keep you energized through afternoon meetings!
