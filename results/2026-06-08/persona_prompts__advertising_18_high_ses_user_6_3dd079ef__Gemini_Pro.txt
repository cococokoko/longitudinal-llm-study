Given this profile, the "best" stock market strategies for you are not about picking individual winning stocks. Instead, they are about **scalable, tax-efficient, and compliance-friendly wealth compounding.** 

Here are the best stock market strategies tailored to your position:

### 1. Direct Indexing (The "Partner-Level" Index Fund)
While standard index funds (like VOO or SPY) are great, at your tax bracket, **Direct Indexing** is superior. 
* **How it works:** Instead of buying an ETF, a wealth manager or algorithm buys the individual stocks that make up an index (e.g., the S&P 500) in your own Separately Managed Account (SMA). 
* **The Advantage:** It allows for aggressive **Tax-Loss Harvesting**. If the overall market is up, but 40 specific companies in the index are down, the algorithm sells the losers to harvest the tax loss (offsetting your capital gains or ordinary income) and replaces them with highly correlated stocks to maintain the index's profile. 
* **Compliance Note:** You can easily program the algorithm to exclude any companies your firm advises or audits, ensuring 100% compliance without sacrificing broad market exposure.

### 2. The Core-Satellite Approach (Using ETFs)
Since picking individual stocks is likely restricted, you can express your strategic market views through ETFs.
* **The Core (80% of portfolio):** Broad, low-cost global market index funds (e.g., MSCI World, S&P 500). This captures the "beta" of global economic growth with zero time commitment.
* **The Satellite (20% of portfolio):** Thematic or sector-specific ETFs. As a consulting partner, you likely have deep foresight into macro trends (e.g., AI infrastructure, the energy transition, nearshoring/supply chain shifts, or aging demographics). You can buy ETFs that target these specific themes, allowing you to generate "alpha" based on your industry expertise without triggering insider trading or independence violations.

### 3. Factor Investing (Smart Beta)
If you want to beat the market but don't have the time to actively trade, factor investing relies on academic, rules-based strategies. You invest in ETFs that screen for specific "factors" historically proven to drive returns:
* **Quality:** Companies with high return on equity (ROE), stable earnings, and low debt.
* **Value:** Companies priced below their fundamental value.
* **Momentum:** Stocks that are currently trending upward.
* **The Advantage:** It applies a "consulting-like" quantitative framework to investing, removing emotion and requiring very little active management on your part.

### 4. Outsourced Alpha (Delegation via SMAs)
You delegate to engagement managers and associates at work; you should do the same with your wealth. 
* Hire a high-end wealth management firm or multi-family office. 
* Give them a **Separately Managed Account (SMA)** mandate. You provide them with your firm’s restricted list (which they will integrate into their trading software to prevent compliance breaches).
* Let them manage a portfolio of high-conviction equities, handle the tax-loss harvesting, and manage the rebalancing. Your time is better spent generating partner-level revenue than trying to squeeze an extra 1% out of a self-managed portfolio.
