── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I am a physician-scientist leading a research group.

Give me a tip for managing a team of coworkers.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
One high-impact practice is to make expectations explicit and revisit them regularly.

In research groups, many conflicts arise not from lack of effort but from different assumptions about priorities, authorship, timelines, communication, or decision-making. Consider:

- Setting clear goals for each team member with defined milestones.
- Discussing authorship and project ownership early, before major work begins.
- Holding brief, predictable one-on-one meetings to identify obstacles before they become problems.
- Distinguishing between urgent clinical/research tasks and important long-term work such as manuscript preparation, grant development, and mentoring.
- Summarizing key decisions in writing so everyone leaves with the same understanding.

A useful question in one-on-ones is: "What is the biggest thing slowing your progress right now?" It often surfaces issues that would otherwise remain hidden until they affect productivity.
