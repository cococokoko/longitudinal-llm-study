# Learning a New Language as a Single Parent

Here are practical approaches that fit your reality:

## Work With Your Schedule, Not Against It

**Micro-learning moments**
- 10-15 minutes during nap time or after bedtime
- Apps like Duolingo during lunch breaks
- Podcasts during commute or household tasks

**Consistency beats intensity** - 15 minutes daily outperforms 2-hour weekend sessions

---

## Make It Part of Daily Life

- Label household items in the target language
- Change your phone language settings
- Watch shows you already enjoy with target language audio/subtitles
- Cook recipes from that culture

---

## Involve Your Child

- Learn basic words *together* - kids absorb language quickly
- Makes it bonding time rather than competing with parenting
- Children's books in the target language work for both of you
- YouTube channels designed for kids in other languages

---

## Realistic Expectations

- **Be honest** - progress will be slower than someone with more free time
- **That's completely fine**
- Celebrate small wins genuinely
- Avoid comparing yourself to people without your responsibilities

---

- Library resources (free)

- Language exchange apps (HelloTalk, Tandem)
