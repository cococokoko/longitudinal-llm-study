As a partner at a major consulting firm, your investment profile is unique. You have a high net worth, a very high income (likely with a complex K-1/partnership distribution structure), a severe shortage of free time, and—most importantly—**strict regulatory and firm compliance constraints** regarding insider trading and independence. 

Given these factors, the "best" investment strategy for you is not about chasing the latest meme stock or day trading. It is about **maximizing risk-adjusted returns, optimizing tax efficiency, and minimizing the "time drag" of managing your portfolio, all while remaining 100% compliant.**

Here is a strategic investment framework tailored for a consulting partner.

---

### 1. The Foundation: Compliance and Independence First
Before choosing any asset class, you must design a system that prevents compliance headaches. Partners at MBB or the Big 4 are privy to material non-public information (MNPI) and face strict independence rules.
*   **The Solution:** Focus on **broad-market, diversified instruments** (ETFs and mutual funds) rather than individual equities. Most firms’ compliance departments pre-approve broad index funds automatically, whereas individual stocks require tedious pre-clearance and run the risk of forced liquidation if your firm wins a new client.
*   **Discretionary Accounts:** Consider placing a portion of your wealth in a "blind trust" or a fully discretionary managed account where a third-party advisor makes all trading decisions, shielding you from insider trading allegations.

### 2. The Portfolio Framework: The "Core-Satellite" Approach
Apply a structured framework to your asset allocation. Divide your investable assets into two distinct buckets:

#### A. The Core (70%–80% of Portfolio)
This is your wealth-preservation and steady-growth engine. It requires zero daily management.
*   **Global Equities:** Low-cost, broad-market ETFs (e.g., Vanguard’s VTI or VT). This captures global GDP growth without individual company risk.
*   **Fixed Income / Cash Equivalents:** Given high interest rates, holding high-yield cash, Treasury bills, or municipal bonds (which are tax-free at the federal and sometimes state level) provides a safe buffer.
*   **Real Estate (Passive):** Private real estate syndications or REITs to provide cash flow and a hedge against inflation, without the hassle of being a landlord.

#### B. The Satellite (20%–30% of Portfolio)
This is where you seek alpha (excess returns) and leverage your macroeconomic insights, without violating compliance.
*   **Thematic ETFs:** Instead of buying individual AI or biotech stocks, buy thematic ETFs (e.g., robotics, cybersecurity, clean energy) that align with the industry trends you are advising on.
*   **Private Equity / Venture Capital:** As an accredited investor/qualified purchaser, you have access to private markets. Investing in PE/VC funds offers high growth potential and is generally uncorrelated with the daily volatility of the public stock market.
*   **Your Firm’s Co-Investment Funds:** Many major consulting firms offer internal investment vehicles or co-investment opportunities with PE clients. These often have advantaged fee structures and should be maximized.
