── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I am a physician-scientist leading a research group.

How can I make a zombie survivor game on Scratch?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
As a physician-scientist, you are likely highly attuned to systems, feedback loops, and experimental design. Game development is remarkably similar: you are designing a closed system with inputs (player controls), processes (game logic, physics), and outputs (visuals, score, game over states).

Scratch is an excellent, low-friction tool for rapid prototyping. For a zombie survivor game, a **Top-Down Shooter** is the most intuitive and rewarding genre to build first. 

Here is a step-by-step guide to building a Minimum Viable Product (MVP) of your zombie survivor game, structured like a research protocol.

---

### Phase 1: The "Hypothesis" (Game Design)
*   **The Player:** Controlled with WASD keys, aims with the mouse pointer, shoots with the mouse click.
*   **The Enemies (Zombies):** Spawn at the edges of the screen and track the player's coordinates.
*   **The Win/Loss Condition:** Survive as long as possible. If health reaches 0, Game Over.

---

### Phase 2: Preparing the Reagents (Sprites & Variables)
You will need four Sprites:
1.  **Player** (A top-down view of a person, perhaps holding a tool/weapon).
2.  **Bullet** (A simple yellow dot or line).
3.  **Zombie** (A green version of the player).
4.  **Reticle/Crosshair** (Optional, but helps with aiming).

Create three Global **Variables** (accessible by all sprites):
*   `Player Health` (Start at 100)
*   `Score` (Start at 0)
*   `Game Active` (Set to "Yes" or "No")

---

### Phase 3: Coding the Mechanics (The Protocol)

#### Step 1: Player Movement and Aiming
Select your **Player** sprite and write this script. We want the player to move and constantly face the mouse pointer.

```scratch

set [Player Health v] to [100]
set [Score v] to [0]
set [Game Active v] to [Yes]
go to x: (0) y: (0)
forever
    point towards (mouse-pointer v)
    if <key [w v] pressed?> then
        change y by (4)
    end
    if <key [s v] pressed?> then
        change y by (-4)
    end
    if <key [d v] pressed?> then
        change x by (4)
    end
    if <key [a v] pressed?> then
        change x by (-4)
    end
    if <(Player Health) < [1]> then
        set [Game Active v] to [No]
        stop [all v]
    end
end
```

#### Step 2: Shooting Mechanics (The Bullet)

*First, hide the master bullet and listen for the mouse click:*

```scratch
end

```
