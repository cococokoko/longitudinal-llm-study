- Prioritize the most important tasks
  - Identify 1–3 high-impact tasks each day.
  - Tackle demanding work during the hours when your energy and focus are highest.

  - Even a small, consistent area can help signal "work mode."
  - Keep frequently used tools within reach to reduce interruptions.

  - Schedule specific blocks for focused work, meetings, email, and administrative tasks.
  - Protect focus blocks by limiting notifications and unnecessary multitasking.

- Manage distractions intentionally
  - Silence nonessential alerts.
  - Keep a notepad nearby to capture unrelated thoughts instead of acting on them immediately.

- Work in focused intervals
  - Try techniques such as 25–50 minutes of focused work followed by a short break.
  - Regular breaks can help maintain concentration throughout the day.

- Batch similar tasks
  - Group email, messaging, paperwork, or routine activities together.
  - Switching between different types of work frequently can reduce efficiency.

- Plan for interruptions
  - If you're balancing work with childcare responsibilities, build buffer time into your schedule rather than assuming uninterrupted focus all day.
  - When possible, reserve key tasks for quieter periods and use shorter windows for routine work.

- Review your day
  - Spend a few minutes at the end of the day noting what was completed and what needs attention tomorrow.
