── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How might society evolve if telepathy became possible?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This is a genuinely interesting question worth thinking through carefully rather than just imagining utopia or dystopia.

## Likely Complex Consequences

**Privacy and autonomy challenges**
- Consent frameworks would need complete rebuilding
- Involuntary thought exposure could be deeply traumatic
- Power imbalances if some people can block transmission and others can't
- Surveillance states would have unprecedented tools

**Social dynamics**
- Deception becomes harder, but people might develop new mental filtering skills
- Social performance and authentic thought might diverge in new ways
- Minority groups face greater vulnerability to majority prejudice
- Intimacy could deepen OR become overwhelming

**Institutional changes**
- Legal systems would struggle with thought evidence vs. action
- Democracy might improve with better information sharing, or worsen with mob mentality
- Mental health treatment transformed, but also potentially weaponized

## What History Suggests
- New communication technologies typically create both liberation and new control mechanisms
- People adapt by developing new social norms around the technology
- Benefits tend to distribute unequally initially
- Privacy concerns usually get addressed *after* significant harm occurs

## Honest Uncertainties
- Whether shared thoughts would increase empathy is genuinely debatable
- Human psychology around boundaries is complex enough that outcomes are hard to predict
