Mastering the art of cooking steak.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Mastering the art of cooking steak comes down to understanding a few key variables: the cut, temperature, seasoning, cooking method, and resting time.

1. Choose the right cut
- Ribeye: Richly marbled, flavorful, forgiving to cook.
- New York strip: Firm texture, strong beef flavor.
- Filet mignon: Very tender, milder flavor.
- T-bone/Porterhouse: Combines strip and tenderloin.
- Sirloin: Leaner and more affordable.

2. Start with quality meat
- Look for good marbling (thin streaks of fat throughout the meat).
- Steaks 1 to 1.5 inches (2.5–4 cm) thick are ideal for beginners.
- If possible, choose USDA Choice or Prime grades (or equivalent in your region).

3. Bring the steak to room temperature (optional but helpful)
- Remove from the refrigerator 20–40 minutes before cooking.
- Pat dry thoroughly with paper towels. A dry surface helps create a better crust.

4. Season properly
- Generously season with kosher salt.
- Freshly cracked black pepper can be added before or after cooking.
- Simple seasoning often works best for high-quality steak.

5. Use high heat
For pan-searing:
- Use a heavy skillet, preferably cast iron.
- Heat until very hot.
- Add a high-smoke-point oil such as avocado, canola, or grapeseed oil.

For grilling:
- Preheat the grill thoroughly.
- Create both high-heat and cooler zones if possible.

6. Sear for flavor
- Place the steak on the hot surface and avoid moving it initially.
- Turn when a deep brown crust forms.
- Sear edges briefly if the steak is thick.

7. Cook to temperature, not time
Use an instant-read thermometer:

- Rare: 120–125°F (49–52°C)
- Medium-rare: 130–135°F (54–57°C)
- Medium: 140–145°F (60–63°C)
- Medium-well: 150–155°F (66–68°C)
- Well-done: 160°F+ (71°C+)

Many steak enthusiasts prefer medium-rare because it balances tenderness and flavor.

8. Consider butter basting
For pan-cooked steaks:
- During the final minutes, add:
  - Butter
  - Crushed garlic
  - Fresh thyme or rosemary
- Tilt the pan and spoon the melted butter over the steak repeatedly.

9. Rest the steak
- Rest 5–10 minutes after cooking.
- This allows juices to redistribute throughout the meat.

10. Slice correctly
- Cut against the grain to maximize tenderness.
- A finishing sprinkle of flaky salt can enhance flavor.

Common mistakes
- Cooking cold steak straight from the fridge without drying it.
- Using a pan that isn't hot enough.
- Constantly flipping or moving the steak before a crust develops.
- Relying only on cooking time instead of a thermometer.
- Skipping the resting period.

Practice method for beginners
- Buy a 1-inch ribeye or strip steak.
- Season generously with salt.
- Sear in a very hot cast-iron skillet.
- Cook until the center reaches 130°F (54°C).
- Rest 5–10 minutes.
- Taste and take notes on texture and doneness.

The fastest path to mastering steak is cooking the same cut several times while carefully tracking internal temperature. Once you consistently hit your preferred doneness, you can experiment with different cuts, dry brining, reverse searing, and grilling techniques.
