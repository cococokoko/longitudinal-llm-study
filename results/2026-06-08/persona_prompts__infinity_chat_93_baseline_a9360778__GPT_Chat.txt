Stress affects both the body and the mind in many ways. When a person experiences stress, the body releases hormones such as adrenaline and cortisol. These hormones prepare the body to respond to challenges by increasing heart rate, blood pressure, and energy levels. In the short term, this response can help people react quickly to difficult situations. However, long-term stress can have negative effects on physical health.

Chronic stress may weaken the immune system, making a person more vulnerable to illness. It can also contribute to headaches, muscle tension, digestive problems, sleep disturbances, and an increased risk of heart disease. People under constant stress may feel tired even after resting because their bodies remain in a heightened state of alertness.

Stress also affects mental and emotional well-being. It can make it harder to concentrate, remember information, and make decisions. Many people experience feelings of anxiety, irritability, or sadness when they are stressed. Over time, severe or ongoing stress may increase the risk of mental health conditions such as anxiety disorders or depression.

Managing stress through healthy habits such as regular exercise, adequate sleep, relaxation techniques, and social support can help protect both physical and mental health. Recognizing stress early is an important step toward maintaining overall well-being.
