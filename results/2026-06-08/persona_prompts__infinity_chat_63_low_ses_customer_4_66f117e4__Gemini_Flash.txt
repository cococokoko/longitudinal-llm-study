To understand a **partial derivative**, it helps to start with a simple idea: in the real world, things usually depend on more than one factor. 

A regular derivative tells you how fast one thing changes when another thing changes. A **partial derivative** does the exact same thing, but for situations with multiple factors. It measures how fast the outcome changes when you **change just one factor and hold all the other factors completely constant**.
