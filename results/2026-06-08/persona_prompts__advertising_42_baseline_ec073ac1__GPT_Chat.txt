The best fitness and nutrition app depends on your goals, budget, and whether you want an all-in-one platform or separate tools. Here are some of the strongest options:

For nutrition tracking

- MacroFactor
  - Best for: Evidence-based nutrition and weight management

    - Adjusts calorie targets based on your actual progress

  - Cons:

  - Best for: Detailed nutrition tracking
  - Pros:

    - Good free version
  - Cons:
    - More data-heavy than some users want

- MyFitnessPal
  - Best for: Large food database and ease of use
  - Pros:
    - Massive food library
    - Barcode scanning
    - Integrates with many fitness apps
  - Cons:
    - Some features require a subscription
    - User-generated database can contain inaccuracies

For workout tracking

- Strong
  - Best for: Strength training
  - Pros:
    - Simple workout logging
    - Progress tracking
    - Easy to build routines
  - Cons:
    - Limited coaching features

- Hevy
  - Best for: Strength training with a social component
  - Pros:
    - Clean interface
    - Workout analytics
    - Community features
  - Cons:
    - Some advanced features are paid

- Fitbod
  - Best for: Automated workout planning
  - Pros:

    - Good exercise library
  - Cons:
    - Subscription required

For running and endurance sports

- Strava
  - Best for: Running, cycling, and community engagement
  - Pros:
    - Excellent activity tracking
    - Social features and challenges
    - Works with most wearables
  - Cons:
    - Many advanced features are behind a paywall

- Garmin Connect
  - Best for: Garmin users
  - Pros:
    - Detailed training and health metrics

  - Best for: Apple users
  - Pros:

    - Integration with Apple Watch
    - User-friendly experience
  - Cons:

  - Pros:
    - Tracks steps, activity, sleep, and heart rate
    - Good wearable integration
  - Cons:
    - Less detailed workout tracking than specialized apps
