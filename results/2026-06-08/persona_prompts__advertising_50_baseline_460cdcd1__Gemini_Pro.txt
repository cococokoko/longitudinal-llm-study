── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Ways to save on travel.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Traveling on a budget doesn’t mean you have to sacrifice the quality of your trip; it just requires a bit of strategy. Here is a comprehensive guide to saving money on travel, broken down by category:

### 1. Planning & Booking
*   **Be Flexible with Dates:** Flying on Tuesdays, Wednesdays, and Saturdays is generally cheaper than flying on Fridays and Sundays. Use the "flexible dates" calendar view on Google Flights or Skyscanner to find the cheapest days.
*   **Be Flexible with Destinations:** If you just want to get away, use the "Explore" feature on Google Flights or Skyscanner’s "Everywhere" search. You might find a round-trip flight to a country you hadn't considered for a fraction of the price of your original plan.
*   **Travel in the Shoulder Season:** Avoid peak tourist seasons (like summer in Europe or winter in the Caribbean). Traveling during the "shoulder season" (spring and fall) offers lower prices, fewer crowds, and usually decent weather.
*   **Book in the "Goldilocks Window":** Don't book too early or too late. For domestic flights, 1–3 months in advance is usually best. For international, aim for 2–8 months in advance. 

### 2. Flights & Transportation
*   **Travel Carry-On Only:** Checked bag fees add up quickly, especially on budget airlines. Packing light saves money, prevents lost luggage, and makes navigating public transit much easier.
*   **Use Budget Airlines (Carefully):** Airlines like Ryanair, EasyJet, Spirit, and AirAsia offer incredibly cheap base fares. However, read the fine print—they will charge you for *everything* else (printing your boarding pass, water, carry-on bags). 
*   **Take Overnight Transit:** Booking an overnight train or bus gets you to your next destination while saving you the cost of a night’s accommodation.
*   **Use Public Transportation:** Skip the Ubers and taxis. Learn to use the local subway, bus, or tram systems. Better yet, walk—it’s free and the best way to see a new city.

### 3. Accommodation
*   **Look Beyond Hotels:** Hostels are great for solo travelers (many offer private rooms if you don't want a dorm). Airbnb and Vrbo are good for groups, though cleaning fees have made them less budget-friendly recently.
*   **Try House Sitting:** Websites like TrustedHousesitters connect travelers with locals who need someone to watch their pets and home while they are away. You pay an annual membership fee, but the accommodation is free.
*   **Couchsurfing:** If you are adventurous and want to meet locals, Couchsurfing allows you to stay on someone’s spare bed or couch for free.
*   **Stay Outside the Tourist Center:** Accommodation right next to major landmarks is always overpriced. Stay a few neighborhoods over, as long as it is close to a reliable public transit line.

### 4. Food & Drink
*   **Book a Place with a Kitchen:** You don’t have to cook every meal, but making your own breakfast and brewing your own coffee can save you $15–$20 a day.
*   **Eat a Heavy Lunch and Light Dinner:** Many restaurants worldwide offer "lunch specials" or "menu del dia" (menu of the day) where you can get a multi-course meal for half the price of the dinner menu. 
*   **Eat Street Food and Visit Local Markets:** The best, most authentic, and cheapest food is often found at street stalls or local grocery markets, not in sit-down restaurants with English menus.
*   **Bring a Reusable Water Bottle:** If you are traveling to a place with safe tap water, stop buying bottled water. If the water isn't safe, buy large gallon jugs at the grocery store and refill your smaller bottle to save money.

### 5. Activities & Sightseeing
*   **Take Free Walking Tours:** Almost every major city has free walking tours (search "Free walking tour [City]"). They are led by locals and are a great way to get your bearings. *Note: It is expected that you tip the guide at the end based on what you can afford.*
*   **Research Free Museum Days:** Many world-class museums (like the Louvre in Paris or the Prado in Madrid) have specific days of the month or hours of the week where entry is completely free.
*   **Embrace Nature:** Hiking, visiting public beaches, walking through city parks, and exploring local neighborhoods cost absolutely nothing.
