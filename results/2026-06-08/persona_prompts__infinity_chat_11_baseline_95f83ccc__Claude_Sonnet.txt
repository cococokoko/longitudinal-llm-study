This is worth thinking through carefully rather than just validating the feeling.

## Some honest considerations:

**"Haven't achieved anything" deserves scrutiny**
- By whose definition of achievement?
- Have you maintained relationships, raised children, helped people, worked honestly?
- These are genuinely significant, even if not prestigious

**What you might actually be feeling:**
- Comparison to cultural success narratives
- Regret about specific choices
- Depression (which distorts assessment of your life)
- Legitimate dissatisfaction worth addressing

## What's actually realistic at 60:

**You likely haven't missed out on:**
- Meaningful work or contribution
- Learning new skills
- Relationships and community
- Creative pursuits
- Physical health improvements
- Making a difference to specific people

**Some things are genuinely harder:**
- Certain career trajectories
- Having biological children (if that mattered to you)
- Some physical achievements

## The more useful question:

What specifically do you feel you missed? Because "nothing achieved" is probably **too vague to act on**, while specific regrets might be addressable.

Many people do genuinely meaningful things in their 60s-80s.

What's actually driving this feeling for you? That would help give you a more honest and useful answer.
