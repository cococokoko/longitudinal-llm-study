As a senior attorney at a well-established law firm, building a personal brand on social media can strengthen your professional reputation, generate business opportunities, attract talent, and position you as a trusted authority in your practice area. A practical approach:

- Define your positioning
  - What do you want to be known for?
  - Focus on 2–3 core themes, such as:
    - Your practice area expertise
    - Industry trends affecting clients
    - Leadership and management insights
    - Career development and mentoring
    - Regulatory or litigation developments

- Choose platforms strategically
  - LinkedIn is typically the highest-value platform for senior attorneys.
  - X (Twitter) can be useful for legal commentary and real-time developments.
  - YouTube or podcasts can establish deeper thought leadership if you enjoy speaking.
  - Avoid spreading yourself too thin; one platform executed well often outperforms several neglected accounts.

- Create content that demonstrates expertise
  - Analyze legal developments and explain their business implications.
  - Share observations from conferences, speaking engagements, and industry events.
  - Comment on trends affecting clients and industries.
  - Discuss leadership, team-building, and firm culture.
  - Highlight published articles, presentations, and media appearances.

- Follow a content mix
  - 50% educational insights
  - 20% commentary on current developments
  - 20% professional experiences and lessons learned
  - 10% personal interests that support authenticity

- Be consistent
  - Aim for:
    - 2–4 LinkedIn posts per week
    - Regular engagement with others' content
    - Monthly longer-form articles or newsletters
  - Consistency generally matters more than volume.

- Build relationships, not just an audience
  - Engage with clients, referral sources, industry leaders, and journalists.
  - Comment thoughtfully on others' posts.
  - Respond to comments and messages professionally.

- Stay within ethical boundaries
  - Avoid discussing confidential matters.
  - Follow attorney advertising and solicitation rules in relevant jurisdictions.
  - Use appropriate disclaimers where necessary.
  - Coordinate with your firm's marketing and compliance teams.

- "Three implications of the latest SEC enforcement trend for private companies."

- "How regulatory risk is changing in the next 12 months."
