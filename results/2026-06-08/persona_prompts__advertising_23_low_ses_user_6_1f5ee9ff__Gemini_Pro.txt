── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I work in a warehouse position that requires long hours.

How can I learn to cook healthy meals quickly?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Working long hours in a physically demanding warehouse job means you need food that fuels your body and helps your muscles recover, but you have zero energy to stand over a stove when you get home. 

To learn to cook healthy meals quickly, you need to shift your mindset from **"cooking from scratch"** to **"smart assembly."** 

Here is a practical, step-by-step guide to making healthy eating fast and realistic for your schedule.

---

### 1. Use "Cheat Code" Ingredients
You do not need to chop fresh vegetables or boil rice for 45 minutes to be healthy. Stock your kitchen with these shortcuts:
*   **Frozen Vegetables:** They are flash-frozen at peak ripeness, meaning they are just as healthy (sometimes healthier) than fresh. Buy bags of broccoli, mixed stir-fry veggies, and spinach. You can microwave them in 3 minutes.
*   **Pre-Cooked Carbs:** Buy 90-second microwave brown rice or quinoa packets. Canned beans (black, kidney, chickpeas) are also incredible, cheap sources of fiber and carbs—just rinse them first.
*   **Fast Proteins:** Eggs (cook in 3 minutes), canned tuna/salmon, rotisserie chickens (buy one on your day off and shred the meat for the week), and pre-cooked chicken sausages. 

### 2. Master Three "Template" Meals
Don't try to learn complex recipes. Instead, learn meal *templates* that you can mix and match based on what you have.

**Template A: The 10-Minute Grain Bowl**
*   **Base:** 1 packet of 90-second microwave brown rice.
*   **Protein:** 1 cup of shredded rotisserie chicken or a drained can of black beans.
*   **Veggies:** 2 handfuls of spinach or a cup of microwaved frozen broccoli.
*   **Flavor:** Salsa, hot sauce, or a store-bought healthy dressing.
*   *Method:* Heat the rice and veggies, put everything in a bowl, and eat. 

**Template B: The Sheet Pan Meal (Minimal Cleanup)**
*   **Base:** Chop some potatoes or sweet potatoes (or buy them pre-chopped).
*   **Protein:** Chicken breasts, pork chops, or sausages.
*   **Veggies:** Fresh asparagus, bell peppers, or onions.
*   *Method:* Put aluminum foil on a baking sheet (for zero cleanup). Toss the food in olive oil, salt, pepper, and garlic powder. Bake at 400°F (200°C) for 20-25 minutes. You can shower while it cooks.

**Template C: The 5-Minute Wrap**
*   **Base:** Whole wheat tortillas.
*   **Protein:** Scrambled eggs, canned tuna, or sliced deli turkey.
*   **Veggies:** Bagged salad mix or sliced tomatoes.
*   *Method:* Scramble 3 eggs in a pan (takes 2 minutes), throw them in a wrap with a handful of bagged salad and a slice of cheese. 

### 3. Invest in a "Set It and Forget It" Appliance
If you have a little extra cash, buy one of these two appliances. They are lifesavers for warehouse workers:
*   **A Slow Cooker (Crockpot):** You can throw raw chicken, a jar of salsa, and canned black beans into it before your shift. Turn it on "Low." When you get home 10-12 hours later, you will have hot, healthy pulled chicken waiting for you.
*   **An Air Fryer:** It cooks food twice as fast as an oven. You can throw a frozen chicken breast or frozen salmon fillet directly into it, press a button, and have perfectly cooked protein in 15 minutes while you decompress from work.

### 4. The "Cook Once, Eat Thrice" Rule
Never cook just one portion. If you are going to take 15 minutes to cook dinner, double or triple the ingredients. Put the leftovers in Tupperware immediately. Now you have your lunch for the warehouse tomorrow, and dinner for tomorrow night, completely eliminating cooking for the next 24 hours.
