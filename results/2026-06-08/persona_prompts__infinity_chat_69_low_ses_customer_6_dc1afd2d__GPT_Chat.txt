What is the difference between analysis and design? Can one begin to design without analysis? Why? Be concise.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Analysis and design are related but distinct:

- Analysis focuses on understanding the problem, requirements, constraints, and goals. It answers: “What needs to be done?”
- Design focuses on creating a solution that meets those requirements. It answers: “How will it be done?”

Can design begin without analysis?

- In a limited sense, yes. A designer can start generating ideas or rough concepts based on assumptions.
- However, effective design generally requires analysis first. Without understanding the problem and requirements, the design may fail to meet user needs, violate constraints, or require significant rework.

In short, analysis provides the foundation for design, making the design process more accurate and efficient.
