Between these two options, the **used Adidas sneaker** is almost certainly the better choice for you. 

Here is a practical breakdown of why, especially if you are spending long hours on your feet or need a shoe that can handle a demanding daily routine:

### 1. Comfort and Support (The Biggest Factor)
*   **Adidas:** As an athletic brand, Adidas designs shoes with biomechanics in mind. Even a used pair will likely feature superior cushioning (like Cloudfoam or Boost technology), arch support, and shock absorption. If you are standing or walking for hours, this is the difference between feeling fine at the end of the day versus having sore feet, knees, and lower back.
*   **Zara:** Zara is a fast-fashion brand. Their shoes are designed for aesthetics, not ergonomics. They often have flat, hard insoles, stiff synthetic materials, and very little arch support. Wearing them for long periods can quickly become painful.

### 2. Durability and Build Quality
*   **Adidas:** These are built to withstand wear and tear. The stitching, glue, and rubber outsoles are designed for activity. A gently used pair of Adidas will likely outlast a brand-new pair of Zara sneakers.
*   **Zara:** Fast-fashion shoes tend to wear out quickly. The synthetic leather can crack, the soles can split, and they generally aren't built to survive heavy daily use. 

### 3. Safety and Grip
*   **Adidas:** Usually features high-quality rubber outsoles with good tread patterns, offering decent grip on various surfaces.
*   **Zara:** Often uses cheaper, smoother plastic-blend soles that can be incredibly slippery, especially on wet or smooth floors.

---

### How to make the "Used Adidas" choice work for you:
If you go with the Adidas, just do a quick quality check before buying:
1.  **Check the tread:** Make sure the bottom of the shoe isn't worn completely flat, as you'll need that grip.
2.  **Check the inside heel:** Ensure the fabric inside the heel isn't worn down to the plastic, which can cause blisters.
3.  **Sanitize them:** Give them a quick spray with a disinfectant (like Lysol) or toss them in the washing machine on a cold, gentle cycle (air dry only!) to make them feel brand new.

**Verdict:** Go with the **Adidas**. Your feet will thank you.
