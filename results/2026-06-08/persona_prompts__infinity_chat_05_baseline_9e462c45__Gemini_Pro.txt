Here is a suggestion for a highly tailored smartwatch feature designed specifically for senior citizens, particularly those experiencing mild cognitive decline or early-stage dementia.

### Feature Name: **"Gentle Guide" (Familiar-Voice Wayfinding & Safe Zones)**

**The Problem:** 
Many seniors value their independence and enjoy going for walks, but memory issues or early-stage dementia can cause them to suddenly become disoriented or lost in their own neighborhoods. Traditional GPS trackers alert the family but do nothing to help the senior in the moment, while loud alarms can cause severe panic and confusion.

**How "Gentle Guide" Works:**
This feature combines geofencing, simplified navigation, and personalized audio to safely guide a senior back home without causing anxiety.

1. **Custom Safe Zones:** Family members or caregivers use a companion smartphone app to draw a "Safe Zone" (e.g., a 1-mile radius around the senior's house). 
2. **Gentle Intervention:** If the senior wanders outside this zone, the watch does not beep loudly. Instead, it gives a gentle, continuous haptic pulse on the wrist to get their attention.
3. **Familiar Voice Audio:** When the senior raises their wrist, the watch plays a pre-recorded audio message from a loved one. For example: *"Hi Mom, it looks like you’ve walked a little far. Look at your watch, and follow the arrow to head back home."* Using a familiar voice prevents the panic that a robotic AI voice might cause.
4. **Simplified Visual UI:** The standard watch face disappears and is replaced by a massive, high-contrast, brightly colored arrow pointing in the direction of their home, along with the distance (e.g., "3 Blocks"). 
5. **Caregiver Loop:** Simultaneously, the caregiver receives a silent notification on their phone with the senior's live GPS location, battery level, and a one-touch button to call the watch directly.
6. **Auto-Answer Speakerphone:** If the caregiver calls the watch, it auto-answers on speakerphone after three rings, ensuring the caregiver can speak to the senior even if the senior forgets how to answer the call.

**Why this is perfect for seniors:**
* **Preserves Dignity and Independence:** It allows seniors to continue their daily walks without feeling like they are being "babysat."
* **Reduces Panic:** By using familiar voices and simple visual cues rather than loud alarms and complex maps, it de-escalates a potentially terrifying situation.
* **Accounts for Dexterity/Tech Illiteracy:** The senior does not need to press any buttons, navigate any menus, or know how to use a map app for the feature to activate and guide them. 
* **Peace of Mind for Family:** Caregivers know they will be alerted
