# Best Practices for Organic Gardening

## Soil Health
- **Compost regularly** - add finished compost to improve structure and fertility
- **Test your soil** periodically to understand pH and nutrient levels
- **Avoid tilling** excessively to preserve soil microbiome
- **Use cover crops** like clover or rye to prevent erosion and add nutrients
- **Mulch generously** to retain moisture and suppress weeds

## Pest & Disease Management
- **Rotate crops** each season to break pest and disease cycles
- **Encourage beneficial insects** like ladybugs, lacewings, and parasitic wasps
- **Plant companion plants** (e.g., marigolds deter aphids and nematodes)
- **Remove diseased plants promptly** to prevent spread
- **Use physical barriers** like row covers when needed

## Watering
- Water deeply and less frequently to encourage deep root growth
- **Water at the base** of plants to reduce fungal issues
- Use **drip irrigation** when possible for efficiency

## Fertilizing
- Prioritize **compost and worm castings**
- Use **organic amendments** like fish emulsion, bone meal, or kelp
- Feed the soil, not just the plant

## General Tips
- **Start with healthy transplants** or quality seeds
- **Observe your garden regularly** to catch problems early
- **Keep records** of what works each season
- **Be patient** - organic systems improve over time
