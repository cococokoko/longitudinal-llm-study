── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I am an executive at a large technology company.

How do I understand what I want?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This is a genuinely important question, and the fact that you're asking it suggests some self-awareness worth building on.

## Why This Is Hard for Executives Specifically

- You've spent years optimizing for **what others need from you**
- Success can mean your identity gets **merged with your role**
- You're surrounded by people who tell you what you want to hear
- Busyness becomes a way to **avoid the question entirely**

## Some Honest Approaches

**Create actual silence**
Not meditation as productivity hack - genuine unstructured time where the question can surface

**Notice resentment and energy**
What drains you despite "success"? What energizes you that you dismiss as unimportant?

**Separate layers**
- What do you want others to *think* you want?
- What do you want because you've always wanted it?
- What do you *actually* want right now?

**Talk to people who knew you before your title**
They often see things you've lost track of

**Take your dissatisfactions seriously**
Executives often rationalize away discomfort that contains real information
