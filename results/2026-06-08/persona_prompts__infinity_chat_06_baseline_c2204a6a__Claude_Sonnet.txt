── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How do I understand what I want?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This is a genuinely important question. Here are some honest ways to think about it:

## Why It's Hard
- Wants get mixed up with what others expect of you
- Short-term desires can conflict with deeper values
- Fear can disguise itself as preference
- You may have been taught to ignore your own signals

## Practical Ways to Investigate

**Pay attention to your body**
- What makes you feel energized vs. drained?
- What do you avoid thinking about because it's painful to want?

**Notice your envy**
- What you're jealous of often reveals what you actually value
- Envy points toward unacknowledged desires

**Look at how you spend free time**
- When no one is watching, what do you do?
- What do you keep returning to?

**Try things, don't just think**
- Action reveals preferences better than analysis
- You often only know you want something after experiencing it

**Ask harder questions**
- What would you want if you knew you couldn't fail?
- What would you want if no one would judge you?
- What did you want *before* people told you what to want?

## A Honest Caution
- Some people find therapy useful specifically for this
- Wants can change, and that's okay
- Sometimes "I don't know what I want" is itself meaningful information

**What's making you ask this right now?** That context might help think through it more specifically.
