How to improve home WiFi?

First off, congratulations on the new job! Since you're still in your probationary period, I know how important it is to make a flawless impression. If you are working from home, doing virtual onboarding, or just need to de-stress with some Netflix after a long day, a dropped connection is the last thing you need right now. 

Since you might also want to keep an eye on your budget until you are officially off probation, here is a guide to improving your home WiFi, starting with completely free fixes and moving up to bigger investments.

### 1. The "Free & Fast" Fixes (Start Here)
*   **Move your router:** Your router should be in a central location, elevated (like on a bookshelf), and out in the open. WiFi signals struggle to pass through thick walls, metal, and water. Keep it away from microwaves, cordless phones, and large fish tanks.
*   **Switch your frequency band:** Most modern routers are "dual-band." 
    *   Use the **5GHz band** for your work computer and TV. It’s much faster and less congested, though it doesn't reach as far.
    *   Use the **2.4GHz band** for smart home devices or devices in far-away rooms. It’s slower but punches through walls better.
*   **Reboot and update:** It sounds cliché, but unplugging your router for 30 seconds and plugging it back in clears its short-term memory and can fix routing issues. While you're at it, log into your router's app or web interface and check for firmware updates.
*   **Kick off bandwidth hogs:** Make sure your devices aren't downloading massive updates (like gaming consoles or cloud backups) during your working hours. 

### 2. The "Cheap & Reliable" Fixes
*   **Use an Ethernet cable for work:** If you are taking important video calls for your new job, don't rely on WiFi at all. Buying a long, $10 Ethernet cable to plug your work computer directly into the router is the single best way to guarantee you won't freeze during a meeting with your new boss.
*   **Change the WiFi channel:** If you live in an apartment building, your neighbors' WiFi might be interfering with yours. You can use a free app like "WiFi Analyzer" on your phone to see which channels are crowded, and then log into your router to switch to a less crowded channel (Channels 1, 6, and 11 are usually best for 2.4GHz).

### 3. The "Investment" Fixes (Maybe wait until probation is over!)
*   **Upgrade to a Mesh WiFi System:** If you have a larger home or dead zones that a single router just can't reach, a Mesh system (like Google Nest WiFi, Eero, or TP-Link Deco) uses multiple nodes placed around the house to blanket it in a strong, seamless signal. 
*   **Buy a new router:** If you are using the default router your Internet Service Provider (ISP) gave you 5 years ago, it's likely outdated. Buying your own modern router (look for "WiFi 6") can drastically improve speeds and handle more devices at once.
*   **Upgrade your internet plan:** Sometimes the WiFi is fine, but the actual pipe coming into your house is too small. Check your internet speed (using a site like Speedtest.net). If you are getting under 50-100 Mbps and have multiple people using the internet, it might be time to call your ISP and upgrade your plan.

**Pro-tip for the new job:** If your WiFi is acting up right before an important meeting, you can always use your smartphone as a mobile hotspot as an emergency backup. 

Good luck with the WiFi troubleshooting, and best of luck as you finish out your probation period! You've got this.
