── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
What are the best apps for tracking fitness and nutrition?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The "best" app for tracking fitness and nutrition depends heavily on your specific goals (e.g., losing weight, gaining muscle, tracking macros, or training for a marathon). 

Here is a breakdown of the best apps on the market, categorized by what they do best.

---

### 1. Best All-in-One & Nutrition Apps

#### **MacroFactor** (Best for Science-Based Dieting & Muscle Building)
Unlike other apps that shame you for going over your calories, MacroFactor uses a smart, adherence-neutral algorithm. It estimates your actual energy expenditure (TDEE) based on your weight trends and food intake, adjusting your targets weekly.
*   **Best for:** Bodybuilders, athletes, and data-driven individuals.
*   **Pros:** No ads, fastest food logger on the market, algorithm adapts to your metabolism, "no-guilt" philosophy.
*   **Cons:** No free version (paid subscription only).

#### **MyFitnessPal** (Best for Food Database Size)
The most famous calorie tracker in the world. It has a database of over 14 million foods, making it easy to find almost any restaurant meal or packaged food.
*   **Best for:** General weight loss and beginners.
*   **Pros:** Massive food database, integrates with almost every fitness wearable.
*   **Cons:** The free version is heavily ad-supported; the barcode scanner is locked behind the premium paywall in many regions.

#### **Cronometer** (Best for Health & Micronutrient Tracking)
If you care about vitamins, minerals, and overall health—not just calories and macros—Cronometer is the gold standard. Every food submission is verified for accuracy by staff.
*   **Best for:** Vegans, vegetarians, people with medical dietary needs, and health enthusiasts.
*   **Pros:** Tracks 80+ micronutrients, highly accurate database, free barcode scanner.
*   **Cons:** The interface is less modern and can feel overwhelming.

#### **Lose It!** (Best User-Friendly Weight Loss App)
A highly visual, gamified app designed specifically for weight loss. It offers fun challenges and a very intuitive interface.
*   **Best for:** Casual users looking to lose weight.
*   **Pros:** Cheap premium tier (often on sale), easy-to-use interface, snap-a-photo food logging.
*   **Cons:** Database has some duplicate/inaccurate user-submitted entries.

---

### 2. Best Strength & Gym Workout Trackers

#### **Hevy** (Best Overall Gym Tracker)
Hevy is a clean, modern workout tracker that allows you to log your weightlifting routines, track progressive overload, and see charts of your progress. It also has a social feed to follow friends.
*   **Best for:** Weightlifters, powerlifters, and general gym-goers.
*   **Pros:** Beautiful UI, great Apple Watch/Wear OS integration, easy to create routines.
*   **Cons:** Free version limits you to 4 custom routines.

#### **Strong** (Best Minimalist Gym Tracker)
Strong is the predecessor to Hevy. It is incredibly simple, fast, and gets out of your way while you are lifting.
*   **Best for:** People who want a digital notebook for the gym without social features.
*   **Pros:** Very clean, reliable, excellent plate calculator.
*   **Cons:** Rarely updated by developers anymore.

#### **Fitbod** (Best for AI-Generated Workouts)
If you don't know what exercises to do, Fitbod is perfect. It uses AI to design a workout for you based on your available gym equipment, target muscles, and how recovered you are from your last workout.
*   **Best for:** Beginners and people who want variety in their workouts.
*   **Pros:** Takes the thinking out of planning workouts, great video demonstrations.
*   **Cons:** Requires a subscription; sometimes suggests odd exercise transitions.

---

### 3. Best Cardio & Outdoor Fitness Apps

#### **Strava** (Best for Running, Cycling, & Swimming)
Strava is the ultimate social network for athletes. It uses GPS to track your runs, rides, and hikes, allowing you to compare your times on specific "segments" against others.
*   **Best for:** Runners, cyclists, and triathletes.
*   **Pros:** Unmatched community, great route planning, highly motivating.
*   **Cons:** Many advanced analysis features are locked behind the premium subscription.
