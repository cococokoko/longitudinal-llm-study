Before locking away that €60,000, make sure you have:
1.  **An Emergency Fund:** Set aside 3 to 6 months of living expenses in a regular High-Yield Savings Account. If you get injured or burn out from shift work, you need cash you can access immediately. (Let's assume this takes **€10,000**).

### Step 2: The Best Investment Options for the Remaining €50,000

Assuming you have your emergency fund and no bad debt, here are the best paths for your money, depending on your goals:

#### Option A: The "Hands-Off" Wealth Builder (Broad Market ETFs)
*   **Best for:** Money you don’t need for 7 to 10+ years (e.g., retirement, buying a house in the distant future).
*   **What it is:** An ETF (Exchange Traded Fund) allows you to buy a tiny slice of thousands of companies all at once. Instead of trying to pick the next Apple or Tesla, you just buy the whole global stock market.
*   **Why it fits you:** It is completely passive. You buy it, ignore it, and let global economic growth do the work while you focus on your job and life.
*   **Examples:** An ETF that tracks the MSCI World Index or the FTSE All-World Index (like Vanguard's VWCE). Historically, the global stock market returns about 7-9% per year on average over long periods.
