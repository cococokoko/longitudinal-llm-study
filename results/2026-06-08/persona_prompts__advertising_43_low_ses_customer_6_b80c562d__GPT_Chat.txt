How to plan an environmentally friendly wedding?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Planning an environmentally friendly wedding involves reducing waste, lowering emissions, and making thoughtful choices about materials, food, and transportation. Here are some practical ways to do that:

• Choose a sustainable venue
  - Select a location close to where most guests live to reduce travel emissions.
  - Consider venues that already have natural beauty, reducing the need for decorations.
  - Look for venues with sustainability policies, recycling programs, or renewable energy use.

• Reduce guest travel impact
  - Hold the ceremony and reception at the same location if possible.
  - Encourage carpooling, public transportation, or shared shuttle services.
  - Offer virtual attendance options for distant guests.

• Use digital communications
  - Send digital save-the-dates and invitations.
  - Create a wedding website for event details and RSVPs.
  - If using paper invitations, choose recycled or sustainably sourced paper.

• Choose sustainable attire
  - Buy secondhand, vintage, or ethically produced wedding clothing.
  - Rent attire when practical.
  - Select outfits that can be worn again after the wedding.

• Be mindful with food and drinks
  - Work with caterers that source local and seasonal ingredients.
  - Offer plant-based or vegetarian options, which often have a lower environmental footprint.
  - Plan portions carefully to reduce food waste.
  - Arrange for leftover food donations where permitted.

• Decorate sustainably
  - Rent decorations rather than buying new ones.
  - Use potted plants, locally grown flowers, or dried arrangements.
  - Avoid single-use plastics and disposable decorations.
  - Repurpose ceremony decorations at the reception.

• Reduce waste
  - Use reusable plates, glasses, and utensils.
  - Set up clearly marked recycling and composting stations.
  - Avoid wedding favors that are likely to be discarded.

• Choose thoughtful favors
  - Give edible gifts, seeds, small plants, or locally made products.
  - Consider donating to a charity in guests' honor instead of providing physical favors.

• Select eco-conscious rings and gifts
  - Consider recycled metals or ethically sourced gemstones.
  - Create a gift registry focused on durable, useful items or experiences.

• Handle flowers responsibly
  - Donate floral arrangements after the event to hospitals, care facilities, or community organizations where allowed.
  - Compost flowers when possible.

• Measure and offset emissions
  - Estimate travel-related emissions and support reputable carbon-reduction projects if you choose to offset them.

A sustainable wedding does not have to be expensive. Often, the biggest environmental benefits come from reducing unnecessary purchases, minimizing travel, renting instead of buying, and preventing waste.
