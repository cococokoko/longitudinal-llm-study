── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Ways to save on travel.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Saving money on travel doesn’t mean you have to sacrifice comfort or miss out on great experiences. It’s all about strategic planning, flexibility, and knowing where to look. 

Here is a comprehensive guide on how to save money before and during your next trip, broken down by category.

---

### 1. Flights & Transportation (The Biggest Expense)
*   **Be flexible with dates and destinations:** Use the "Explore" feature on Google Flights or Skyscanner. Enter your departure city and leave the destination as "Everywhere" to find the cheapest places to fly.
*   **Fly on off-peak days:** Tuesdays, Wednesdays, and Saturdays are generally the cheapest days to fly. Avoid Fridays and Sundays.
*   **Travel during the "Shoulder Season":** This is the period between peak season and off-season (e.g., visiting Europe in September/October or April/May). The weather is still good, but flights and hotels are significantly cheaper.
*   **Use flight alerts:** Set up alerts on **Google Flights**, **Skyscanner**, or **Hopper** for your desired route. Sign up for newsletters like **Going (formerly Scott’s Cheap Flights)** for mistake fares and flash sales.
*   **Fly budget airlines (with caution):** Airlines like Ryanair, Spirit, or EasyJet offer cheap base fares, but they charge for *everything* else. Travel with a personal item only (backpack) to avoid baggage fees.
*   **Embrace public transit:** Skip taxis and rideshares (Uber/Lyft). Take trains, buses, or subways from the airport to the city center. 

### 2. Accommodation
*   **Look beyond traditional hotels:** 
    *   **Hostels:** Modern hostels offer private rooms that are often cheaper than hotels, plus communal kitchens where you can cook.
    *   **Housesitting:** Websites like **TrustedHousesitters** allow you to stay in someone’s home for free in exchange for watching their pets.
    *   **Home Exchanges:** Swap homes with someone in your destination city using platforms like HomeExchange.
*   **Book directly with the hotel:** Use search engines (Booking.com, Expedia) to find hotels, but check the hotel’s official website before booking. Often, they will match the price or throw in free breakfast/room upgrades if you book directly.
*   **Stay slightly outside the tourist center:** Accommodations just a 15-minute subway ride outside the main tourist zone can be 30% to 50% cheaper.
*   **Join loyalty programs:** They are free to join and often offer immediate discounts (usually 5% to 10% off) and free Wi-Fi.

### 3. Food & Drink
*   **Eat your big meal at lunch:** Many high-end restaurants offer lunch specials or "menus of the day" (common in Europe and Latin America) that are half the price of their dinner menus.
*   **Shop at local grocery stores:** Buy pastries, fruit, cheese, and snacks at local supermarkets for breakfast or lunch picnics. 
*   **Eat street food and visit local markets:** This is often where you will find the most authentic—and cheapest—food in any city.
*   **Avoid restaurants near major tourist attractions:** If a restaurant has menus in five languages and a host outside coaxing you in, it is a tourist trap. Walk three blocks away to find cheaper, better food.
*   **Bring a reusable water bottle:** In countries with safe tap water, this saves $5–$10 a day. If the water isn't safe, buy a bottle with a built-in filter (like LifeStraw).

### 4. Activities & Sightseeing
*   **Take free walking tours:** Almost every major city has them (search "[City Name] free walking tour"). They are a great way to get oriented. (Note: You should tip the guide, but it’s still much cheaper than a paid tour).
*   **Look for free museum days:** Many world-class museums have free admission on certain days of the week or month (e.g., the Louvre is free on the first Friday night of the month, except in summer).
*   **Use city passes wisely:** Passes like the "Go City" pass can save you money, but *only* if you plan to visit multiple high-ticket attractions in a short time. Do the math before buying.
*   **Leverage student, youth, or senior discounts:** Always carry your ID. Many attractions offer steep discounts for students under 26 or seniors.
