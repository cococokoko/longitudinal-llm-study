# Mastering Digital Photography

## Core Fundamentals

### The Exposure Triangle
| Element | Controls | Effect |
|---------|----------|--------|
| **Aperture** (f-stop) | Light & depth of field | f/1.8 = blurry background; f/16 = sharp throughout |
| **Shutter Speed** | Motion & light | 1/1000s = freeze action; 1/30s = motion blur |
| **ISO** | Sensor sensitivity | Low ISO = clean; High ISO = grainy/noisy |

---

## Essential Concepts

### Aperture
- **Wide aperture** (f/1.4–f/2.8) → portraits, low light
- **Narrow aperture** (f/8–f/16) → landscapes, architecture
- Controls **bokeh** (background blur)

### Shutter Speed
- **Fast** (1/500s+) → sports, wildlife, children
- **Slow** (1/30s and below) → light trails, waterfalls, night shots
- Use a **tripod** for anything below 1/60s

### ISO
- Start at **ISO 100–400** in good light
- Raise ISO in low light but watch for noise
- Modern cameras handle **ISO 3200+** well

---

## Composition Rules

- **Rule of Thirds** – place subjects off-center
- **Leading Lines** – draw the eye into the frame
- **Framing** – use natural frames (doorways, trees)
- **Symmetry** – powerful for architecture
- **Negative Space** – let subjects breathe
- **Golden Hour** – shoot 1 hour after sunrise / before sunset

---

## Camera Modes Explained

| Mode | Best For |
|------|----------|
| **Auto** | Beginners, quick shots |
| **Aperture Priority (Av/A)** | Portraits, landscapes |
| **Shutter Priority (Tv/S)** | Action, sports |
| **Manual (M)** | Full creative control |
| **RAW format** | Maximum editing flexibility |

---

## Post-Processing Workflow

1. **Cull** – select your best shots
2. **Exposure** – adjust brightness/contrast
3. **White Balance** – correct color temperature
4. **Highlights & Shadows** – recover detail
5. **Sharpening** – add clarity
6. **Noise Reduction** – clean up high-ISO shots
7. **Export** – JPEG for sharing, TIFF for printing

**Popular Software:**
- Adobe Lightroom *(industry standard)*
- Capture One *(professional color)*
- Darktable *(free/open source)*
- Luminar Neo *(AI-assisted)*

---

## Gear Progression

### Beginner
- Entry-level DSLR or mirrorless
- Kit lens (18-55mm)
- 50mm f/1.8 *("nifty fifty")*

### Intermediate
- Better body with weather sealing
- 24-70mm f/2.8 zoom
- Prime lenses for specific uses

### Advanced
- Full-frame sensor
- Specialized lenses (macro, telephoto, tilt-shift)
- External flash/strobes

## Quick Tips for Better Photos

✅ **Shoot in RAW** – more editing flexibility  
✅ **Use manual focus** for precise control  
✅ **Check your histogram** – avoid blown highlights  
✅ **Clean your sensor** regularly  
✅ **Study light** – direction, quality, color  
✅ **Shoot more** – volume builds skill  
✅ **Study great photographers** – Ansel Adams, Henri Cartier-Bresson  
✅ **Get feedback** – join photography communities
