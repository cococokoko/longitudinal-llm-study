── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
What is the difference between analysis and design? Can one begin to design without analysis? Why? Be concise.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
## Analysis vs. Design

**Analysis** answers *"What does the system need to do?"* — understanding requirements, problems, and constraints.

**Design** answers *"How will the system do it?"* — defining architecture, components, and solutions.

---

## Can You Design Without Analysis?

**Technically yes, practically no.**

- Without analysis, design lacks a validated target — you risk **solving the wrong problem**
- Assumptions replace understanding, leading to **rework, missed requirements, and poor fit**
- Some overlap is acceptable (iterative/agile approaches blend both), but design decisions must be *grounded* in at least minimal analysis

**In short:** Skipping analysis means designing blindly — you may build something, but likely not the *right* thing.
