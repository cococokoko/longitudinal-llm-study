── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I support extended family members and manage many household responsibilities.

How can I make my home more energy efficient?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Great question! Here are some practical ways to improve energy efficiency, especially useful when managing a busy household:

## Quick/Low-Cost Steps
- **Switch to LED bulbs** throughout the home
- **Use power strips** to eliminate "phantom" energy drain from electronics
- **Adjust thermostat settings** - even 2-3 degrees makes a meaningful difference
- **Seal drafts** around doors and windows with weatherstripping or caulk

## Appliance & Usage Habits
- Run dishwashers and washing machines with **full loads**
- Wash clothes in **cold water** when possible
- **Unplug chargers** and small appliances when not in use
- Use **ceiling fans** to reduce heating/cooling needs

## Bigger Investments Worth Considering
- **Programmable/smart thermostat** - pays for itself relatively quickly
- **Insulation improvements** in attic or walls
- **Energy-efficient appliances** when replacing old ones
- **Water heater insulation** or upgrading to tankless

## Managing a Larger Household Specifically
- Set **clear household habits** everyone follows
- Check if your utility offers **free energy audits**
- Look into **assistance programs** - many utilities offer rebates for efficiency upgrades

## Good Starting Point
An **energy audit** (often free from utilities) identifies where you're losing the most energy so you can prioritize effectively.
