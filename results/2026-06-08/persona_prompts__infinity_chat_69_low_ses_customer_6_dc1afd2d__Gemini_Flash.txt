── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
What is the difference between analysis and design? Can one begin to design without analysis? Why? Be concise.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here is the difference, explained simply and practically:

### The Difference
*   **Analysis is the "What":** Understanding the problem, gathering requirements, and defining the needs. 
*   **Design is the "How":** Planning the solution, creating the blueprint, and deciding how to build it.

---

### Can you design without analysis? 
**Technically yes, but practically no.** 

### Why?
Designing without analysis is like setting up warehouse shelving before knowing what inventory is arriving. You might build a great system, but if the boxes don't fit the shelves, the entire effort is wasted. 

Without analysis, you risk:
1.  **Building the wrong thing** (solving a problem that doesn't exist).
2.  **Wasting time and energy** on constant redesigns.
3.  **Missing critical requirements** that are expensive to fix later.
