# Tips for Running a Marathon

## Training
- **Follow a structured plan** - Most plans are 16-20 weeks long
- **Build mileage gradually** - Increase weekly mileage by no more than 10% per week
- **Include long runs** - Build up to at least one 20-mile training run
- **Rest days matter** - Allow your body to recover between runs

## Nutrition & Hydration
- **Practice fueling during training** - Use gels, chews, or real food every 45-60 minutes
- **Stay hydrated** - Drink water and electrolytes regularly
- **Carb load** - Increase carbohydrate intake 2-3 days before the race
- **Nothing new on race day** - Only use foods/gels you've tested in training

## Race Day
- **Start slow** - Many runners go out too fast and struggle later
- **Stick to your pace plan** - Negative splits (faster second half) work well
- **Dress appropriately** - Wear moisture-wicking, tested clothing
- **Arrive early** - Allow time for warm-up and logistics

## Recovery
- **Walk after finishing** - Keep moving to prevent stiffness
- **Refuel within 30 minutes** - Protein and carbs help recovery
- **Rest adequately** - Plan for 2-4 weeks of easy activity post-race

## Mindset
- **Break the race into segments** - Focus on smaller goals
- **Expect tough patches** - Mental resilience is key
