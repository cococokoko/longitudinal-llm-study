# How to Improve Home WiFi

## Quick Fixes First
- **Restart your router** (unplug for 30 seconds)
- **Move closer** to the router temporarily to test
- **Check for interference** from microwaves, cordless phones, baby monitors

---

## Router Placement
- Place it **centrally** in your home
- Keep it **elevated** (shelf or table, not floor)
- Avoid walls, cabinets, and metal objects
- Keep away from other electronics

---

## Router Settings
- **Change WiFi channel** (use apps like WiFi Analyzer to find less crowded channels)
- **Use 5GHz band** for faster speeds nearby, **2.4GHz** for longer range
- **Update router firmware**
- Use **WPA3 or WPA2** security (not WEP)

---

## Hardware Upgrades
- **Upgrade old router** (replace if 5+ years old)
- **Add a WiFi extender/repeater** for dead zones
- **Mesh WiFi system** (Eero, Google Nest, Orbi) for whole-home coverage
- **Powerline adapters** to extend network through electrical wiring

---

## Reduce Congestion
- Limit devices streaming simultaneously
- Schedule large downloads for off-peak hours
- **Prioritize devices** using QoS settings in router

---

## Check Your Internet Plan
- Run a **speed test** (fast.com or speedtest.net)
- Compare results to your **paid plan speed**
- Contact your ISP if speeds are consistently low
