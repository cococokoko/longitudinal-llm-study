── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
What are the best resources for starting a plant-based diet?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
If you're starting a plant-based diet, the most useful resources are the ones that help with three things: nutrition, meal planning, and practical recipes.

Evidence-based nutrition resources:
- NutritionFacts.org (Dr. Michael Greger)
  - Free videos and articles focused on research related to plant-based nutrition.
  - Useful for learning about nutrients, disease prevention, and healthy eating patterns.
- Physicians Committee for Responsible Medicine (PCRM)
  - Offers beginner guides, meal plans, and nutrition information.
  - Particularly helpful for transitioning from an omnivorous diet.
- Harvard T.H. Chan School of Public Health Nutrition Source
  - Balanced, science-based information about healthy eating, including plant-forward diets.

Registered dietitian resources:
- Plant-Based Dietitian (Sharon Palmer, RD)
- The Vegan RD (Virginia Messina, RD)
- Pick Up Limes (created by a registered dietitian)
  - Provides recipes, meal plans, and practical nutrition guidance.

Recipe and meal-planning websites:
- Forks Over Knives
  - Extensive recipe database and meal-planning tools.
- Minimalist Baker
  - Many simple plant-based recipes with limited ingredients.
- Love & Lemons
  - Vegetable-focused recipes that appeal to both beginners and experienced cooks.
- Rainbow Plant Life
  - Flavorful recipes and cooking tutorials.
- Budget Bytes
  - Includes affordable vegetarian and vegan options.

Books for beginners:
- "How Not to Die" by Michael Greger, MD
- "Plant-Powered for Life" by Sharon Palmer, RD
- "Plant-Based on a Budget" by Toni Okamoto
- "The Vegan Starter Kit" by Neal Barnard, MD

Apps:
- Cronometer
  - Helps track nutrients such as protein, iron, calcium, vitamin B12, and omega-3 fats.
- Mealime
  - Useful for meal planning and grocery lists.
- Forks Meal Planner
  - Structured plant-based meal planning.

Key nutrients to learn about early:
- Protein
- Vitamin B12 (supplementation is generally recommended for people eating fully vegan diets)
- Iron
- Calcium
- Iodine
- Omega-3 fats
- Vitamin D (depending on sun exposure and location)

A simple beginner approach:
- Fill half your plate with vegetables and fruit.
- Include a protein source at most meals (beans, lentils, tofu, tempeh, edamame, soy milk).
- Choose whole grains when possible.
- Add nuts and seeds regularly.
- Consider a reliable vitamin B12 supplement if following a fully plant-based/vegan diet.
