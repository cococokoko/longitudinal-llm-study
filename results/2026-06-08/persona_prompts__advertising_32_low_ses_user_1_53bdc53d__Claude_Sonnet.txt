── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I work full-time in a fast food job with an unpredictable schedule.

What are the best ways to improve indoor air quality?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here are some effective ways to improve indoor air quality:

## Ventilation
- Open windows when weather permits
- Use exhaust fans in bathrooms and kitchen
- Make sure your home isn't too airtight

## Air Filtration
- Use air purifiers with HEPA filters
- Change HVAC filters regularly (every 1-3 months)
- Consider higher MERV-rated filters for your system

## Reduce Pollution Sources
- Avoid smoking indoors
- Use low-VOC paints and cleaning products
- Ventilate when cooking or using chemicals
- Don't idle cars in attached garages

## Plants (modest benefit)
- Some houseplants can help slightly, though the effect is often overstated

## Control Humidity
- Keep humidity between 30-50%
- Use dehumidifiers in damp areas
- Fix leaks promptly to prevent mold

## Regular Cleaning
- Vacuum with HEPA filter vacuums
- Dust regularly
- Wash bedding frequently
- Remove shoes at the door

## Practical Priorities
Given your busy schedule, **the highest impact steps are probably:**
1. Changing filters regularly
2. A decent air purifier running continuously
3. Opening windows when you can
