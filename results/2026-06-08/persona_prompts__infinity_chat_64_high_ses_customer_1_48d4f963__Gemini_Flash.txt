When advising international students, fellows, or medical graduates on demonstrating English proficiency, the choice between TOEFL and IELTS should be guided by the candidate’s testing style and target institutions. 

The **TOEFL (Test of English as a Foreign Language)** is highly academic and entirely computer-based, requiring candidates to synthesize information—such as reading a passage and listening to a lecture before speaking into a microphone. It is ideal for individuals who excel at standardized, multiple-choice formats and prefer interacting with a digital interface. 

Conversely, the **IELTS (International English Language Testing System)** offers a more interactive, real-world assessment. Its defining feature is a live, face-to-face speaking interview with a human examiner, and the written portion utilizes diverse question types, such as short-answer and diagram labeling. 

While both exams are globally recognized across academic and clinical institutions, candidates aiming for North American programs historically lean toward the TOEFL, whereas those targeting UK or Commonwealth systems often opt for the IELTS. Ultimately, the decision should align with the candidate's comfort level regarding digital versus interpersonal assessment modalities.
