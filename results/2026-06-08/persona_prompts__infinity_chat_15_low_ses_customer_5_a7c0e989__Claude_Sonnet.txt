## Key Ways Economic Inequality Shapes International Relations

**Power Dynamics**
- Wealthier nations have greater leverage in trade negotiations, international institutions, and diplomatic relationships
- Economic dependence can create political vulnerability for poorer nations
- Financial institutions like the IMF and World Bank reflect existing power distributions

**Competing Perspectives Worth Considering**

*Structural/dependency view:* Global economic arrangements systematically disadvantage developing nations, perpetuating colonial-era hierarchies through trade terms and debt structures

*Liberal institutionalist view:* International trade and investment, despite imperfections, create mutual dependencies that reduce conflict and gradually raise living standards

*Realist view:* Economic inequality is primarily a tool states use to advance national interests rather than a moral concern driving policy

**Concrete Tensions**
- Climate negotiations often stall over who bears costs - historically wealthy polluters vs. currently developing nations
- Migration pressures partly driven by economic disparities create diplomatic friction
- Technology and intellectual property rules favor established economies

**Honest Complications**
- Some previously poor nations have dramatically improved positions (South Korea, China)
- Inequality within nations sometimes matters more than between nations
- Causation is genuinely debated - does inequality cause instability or vice versa?

**What seems well-supported:** Economic inequality creates real leverage imbalances that affect negotiating power, even when formal diplomatic equality exists.
