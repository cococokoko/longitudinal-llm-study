# The Day's Farewell

The sky bleeds gold and rose and red,
As daylight lays itself to rest,
And all the hurried thoughts that fled
Through busy hours slow in my chest.

The horizon drinks the burning sun,
A quiet fire, a gentle close,
And something in me comes undone—
That tender ache that no one knows.

For beauty passing makes us feel
Both small and somehow more alive,
A moment too complete and real
To hold, yet too bright to survive.

So I stand still and let it go,
This painted ending, warm and wide,
And carry just its fading glow
Long after darkness fills the sky.
