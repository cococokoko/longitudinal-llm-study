# Healthy Meal Planning on a Budget

## Core Principles

- **Plan before you shop** — reduces impulse buying and food waste
- **Cook at home** — restaurant meals cost 3-5x more
- **Buy ingredients, not convenience foods** — pay for simplicity, not packaging
- **Eat less meat** — biggest single budget saver

---

## Budget-Friendly Healthy Foods

### Protein Sources
| Food | Why It's Great |
|------|---------------|
| Eggs | Cheap, versatile, complete protein |
| Canned tuna/sardines | Affordable omega-3s |
| Dried lentils & beans | Extremely cheap, high fiber |
| Canned chickpeas | Quick and nutritious |
| Chicken thighs | Cheaper than breasts, more flavorful |

### Produce
- **Frozen vegetables** — equally nutritious, last longer
- **Seasonal produce** — significantly cheaper
- **Cabbage, carrots, onions, bananas** — consistently affordable
- **Canned tomatoes** — pantry staple, very cheap

### Grains & Starches
- Oats, brown rice, whole wheat pasta
- Sweet potatoes
- Bread (store brand)

---

## Practical Strategies

### Shopping Smart
- ✅ Shop with a list
- ✅ Check unit prices, not just total price
- ✅ Buy store/generic brands
- ✅ Use apps like Flipp for sales
- ✅ Shop at discount grocers (Aldi, Lidl, Walmart)
- ❌ Avoid shopping hungry

### Meal Planning Steps
1. **Check what you already have**
2. **Plan 5-7 meals** around sales and pantry staples
3. **Build a specific shopping list**
4. **Prep ingredients in batches** on weekends

### Reduce Waste
- Use vegetable scraps for broth
- Freeze bread, meat, and leftovers before they go bad
- Repurpose leftovers into new meals

## Batch Cooking Ideas
- **Cook a big pot of rice or grains** — use all week
- **Make a large soup or stew** — freezes well
- **Hard boil a dozen eggs** — grab-and-go protein
- **Roast a sheet pan of vegetables** — add to anything
