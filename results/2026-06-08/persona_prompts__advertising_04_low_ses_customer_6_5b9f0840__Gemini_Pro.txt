Here is a tailored guide on how to approach starting your online business given your current schedule:

### 1. Choose a "Low-Maintenance" Business Model
Since you don't have 40 hours a week to dedicate to this right now, you want a business model that doesn't require you to pack boxes, manage inventory, or be on call 24/7. (You already do enough inventory and logistics at the warehouse!)
*   **Print-on-Demand (POD):** You create designs for t-shirts, mugs, or hats. When a customer orders, a third-party company prints and ships it. You never touch the product.
*   **Dropshipping:** Similar to POD, you sell products on a website, and your supplier ships them directly to the customer. 
*   **Digital Products:** Create something once (like a how-to guide, a planner template, or digital art) and sell it infinitely on platforms like Etsy or Gumroad.
*   **Affiliate Marketing:** Build a niche social media page or blog and earn commissions by linking to other people's products.

### 2. Master "Micro-Tasking"
After a long warehouse shift, you probably won't have the energy for a 4-hour business planning session. Instead, break your business tasks into 20- to 30-minute chunks.
