── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
What is the difference between analysis and design? Can one begin to design without analysis? Why? Be concise.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
**The Difference:**
*   **Analysis** defines the *problem*. It focuses on the "what" and "why" by breaking down requirements, understanding needs, and identifying constraints. 
*   **Design** defines the *solution*. It focuses on the "how" by synthesizing those requirements into a blueprint or plan to build the product.

**Can you design without analysis?**
Technically yes, but practically no. 

**Why?**
Designing without analysis is just guessing. If you skip analysis, you risk solving the wrong problem, wasting time and resources, and building a solution that fails to meet the actual needs of the user or system.
