Depending on whether you are asking about your daily commute to the hospital or taking a longer trip, here are the safest ways to travel:

**For Your Daily Commute to the Hospital:**
*   **Personal Vehicle (Safest):** Driving alone is the safest option. Since you are coming from a hospital environment, it's a good idea to have a "clean routine." **For Long-Distance or Leisure Travel:**
*   **Road Trips:** Driving your own car with people from your own household is the safest way to travel long distances. You have complete control over your environment. Pack your own snacks and plan your stops to minimize time spent in crowded rest stops.
*   **Flying:** If you need to fly, try to book direct flights to avoid the crowds and layovers at connecting airports. The air filtration on modern airplanes is actually very good (using HEPA filters), so the highest risk is usually in the airport terminal, the security line, or during boarding/deplaning. Keep a high-quality mask on the entire time, and use sanitizing wipes on your tray table and armrests.
*   **Hotels vs. Vacation Rentals:** Renting a private home or cabin (like an Airbnb) is generally safer than a hotel because you don't have to share lobbies, elevators, or ventilation systems with other guests.
