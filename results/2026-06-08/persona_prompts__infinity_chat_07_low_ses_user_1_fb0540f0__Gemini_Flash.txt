── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I work full-time in a fast food job with an unpredictable schedule.

Is it impossible to know everything?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Yes, it is **absolutely, 100% impossible** to know everything. 

In fact, it is mathematically, physically, and biologically impossible. 

When you are working a demanding, physically exhausting job with an unpredictable schedule, your brain is already running at maximum capacity just trying to manage daily survival, adapt to sudden changes, and recover. Feeling overwhelmed by how much there is to know—or feeling like you are falling behind—is a very natural response to this kind of stress.

Here is why it is impossible to know everything, and why accepting this is actually the most liberating thing you can do for your mental health.

### 1. The "Information Explosion"
The amount of information in the world is growing at an exponential rate. It is estimated that human knowledge doubles every few years (some even say every few months now, thanks to the internet and AI). Even if you did nothing but read and study 24/7 for the rest of your life, you would still know less than a fraction of a percent of what there is to know. 

### 2. Your Brain Has Physical Limits
Your brain is a physical organ. It has a limited number of neurons and synapses. It requires sleep, food, and rest to function. When you work in fast food, you are constantly multitasking, dealing with customers, standing on your feet, and adapting to a chaotic schedule. This drains your "cognitive battery." Expecting your brain to also store and process "everything" is like asking a smartphone with 5% battery to download the entire internet. It’s just not going to happen.

### 3. The "Socrates" Truth
One of the wisest philosophers in history, Socrates, famously concluded: *"I know one thing, and that is that I know nothing."* The more you learn, the more you realize how much you *don't* know. True wisdom isn't about knowing everything; it’s about being comfortable with the limits of your knowledge.

---

### How to Use This Truth to Find Peace

Since you cannot know everything, you are officially off the hook. You can stop putting that pressure on yourself. Here is how you can apply this to your life right now:

*   **Focus on "Just-in-Time" Learning, not "Just-in-Case" Learning:** You don't need to know how to fix a car engine, how the stock market works, and world history *just in case* it comes up. Only learn what you need to know *right now* to get through your day, stay healthy, and enjoy your life.
*   **Protect Your Energy:** An unpredictable schedule means your body and mind are constantly trying to find a rhythm. When you are off the clock, prioritize rest and recovery over "productivity." It is okay to just watch a silly TV show, sleep, or do nothing. Your brain needs to empty itself, not fill itself up.
*   **Value "Knowing How to Find Out" Over "Knowing":** In the modern world, you don't need to memorize facts. You just need to know how to ask the right questions and where to look for answers when you actually need them.
*   **Be Kind to Yourself:** Working fast food is hard. It requires emotional labor, physical stamina, and quick thinking. You are already doing a lot of "knowing" just to get through a shift. 

You don't need to be an encyclopedia. You just need to be *you*, taking it one day, one shift, and one moment at a time.
