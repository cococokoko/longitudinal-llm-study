# Best Practices for Organic Gardening

Here are practical, reliable approaches organized by category:

## Soil Health (Foundation of Everything)
- **Compost regularly** - kitchen scraps, yard waste, coffee grounds
- Test soil pH before planting (most vegetables prefer 6.0-7.0)
- Add organic matter annually to improve structure
- Avoid tilling excessively to protect soil microbes

## Pest & Disease Management
- **Companion planting** - basil near tomatoes, marigolds as borders
- Encourage beneficial insects (ladybugs, lacewings)
- Remove diseased plants promptly
- Neem oil and insecticidal soap for problem pests
- Rotate crops each season to break pest cycles

## Water Management
- Water deeply but less frequently (encourages deep roots)
- Morning watering reduces disease risk
- Mulch heavily to retain moisture and suppress weeds

## Practical Tips for Busy Households
- **Start small** - one raised bed is manageable
- Grow high-value crops first (herbs, tomatoes, greens)
- Involve family members in simple tasks
- Perennial plants (asparagus, herbs) reduce annual workload

## Cost-Saving Approaches
- Save seeds from open-pollinated varieties
- Make your own compost instead of buying
- Trade plants/seeds with neighbors

## Good Beginner Crops
Lettuce, zucchini, herbs, green beans, radishes
