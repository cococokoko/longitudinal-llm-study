── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How can I improve my credit score quickly?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Improving your credit score "quickly" usually means seeing results in **30 to 60 days**, as this is how long it takes for creditors to report new data to the credit bureaus. 

Because your credit score is essentially a mathematical formula, you can use specific strategies to manipulate the math in your favor. Here are the fastest ways to boost your score:

### 1. Lower Your Credit Utilization (Fastest Impact)
Credit utilization—how much of your available credit you are using—makes up 30% of your FICO score. 
* **The Strategy:** Pay down your credit card balances. If you have a card with a $10,000 limit and a $5,000 balance, your utilization is 50%. You want to get this **under 30%**, and ideally **under 10%**.
* **The "Insider" Trick:** Pay your balance *before the statement closing date*, not just the due date. Creditors usually report your balance to the bureaus on the statement closing date. If you pay it down before that day, a $0 (or very low) balance gets reported, instantly boosting your score.

### 2. Request a Credit Limit Increase
If you don't have the cash to pay down your balances, you can improve your utilization ratio by increasing your total credit limit.
* **The Strategy:** Call your credit card issuer or log into your app and request a limit increase. If your limit goes from $5,000 to $10,000, your utilization ratio instantly cuts in half.
* **Warning:** Ask the issuer if they will do a "soft pull" or a "hard pull" on your credit. You only want to do this if it is a **soft pull**, as a hard pull can temporarily ding your score.

### 3. Become an Authorized User ("Piggybacking")
This is one of the most effective ways to see a massive jump in your score in a single month.
* **The Strategy:** Ask a trusted family member or partner who has a credit card with a **long history of on-time payments and a very low balance** to add you as an "authorized user." 
* **How it works:** You don't even need them to give you the physical card. Once you are added, the entire positive history of that specific credit card is copied onto your credit report, usually within 30 days.

### 4. Dispute Credit Report Errors
A study by the FTC found that 1 in 5 people have an error on their credit report. Removing a negative error will cause an immediate score jump.
* **The Strategy:** Go to **AnnualCreditReport.com** (the only federally authorized free site) and pull your reports from Equifax, Experian, and TransUnion. 
* **What to look for:** Look for late payments you actually paid on time, accounts you don't recognize, or debts that are listed multiple times. Dispute these directly on the credit bureaus' websites. By law, they have 30 days to investigate and remove unverified negative marks.

### 5. Use "Experian Boost" or Rent Reporting
Traditionally, paying your utility bills or rent didn't help your credit score, but that has changed.
