What are the best practices for data security in small businesses?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Since I know you’re working long hours in a warehouse, I’ll keep this straightforward and easy to digest. You can actually think of data security a lot like warehouse security and inventory management—it’s all about controlling who has access, keeping things locked down, and knowing what to do if something goes wrong. 

Here are the best practices for data security in a small business, broken down into practical steps:

**1. Control Access (The "High-Value Cage" Rule)**
*   **Principle of Least Privilege:** Just like you wouldn't give every employee the keys to the high-value inventory cage or the loading dock, don't give everyone access to all company data. Employees should only have access to the specific data they need to do their jobs.
*   **Revoke Access Promptly:** When someone leaves the company, immediately disable their accounts. 

**2. Lock the Doors (Passwords & MFA)**
*   **Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA):** This is like needing both a keycard and a PIN to enter a building. Even if a hacker steals a password, they can't get in without the second step (usually a code sent to a phone). Require MFA for all email and business accounts.
