── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How can I improve my credit score quickly?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here are some effective ways to improve your credit score:

## Highest Impact Actions
- **Pay down credit card balances** - lowering your credit utilization ratio (below 30%, ideally below 10%) can show results quickly
- **Pay all bills on time** - payment history is the biggest factor (~35% of score)
- **Dispute errors on your credit report** - check all three bureaus (Equifax, Experian, TransUnion) for mistakes

## Moderate Impact
- **Become an authorized user** on someone else's account with good history
- **Ask for a credit limit increase** (without spending more) to improve utilization ratio
- **Avoid closing old accounts** - length of credit history matters

## Things to Avoid
- Don't apply for multiple new credit cards at once (hard inquiries lower your score)
- Don't miss any payments
- Don't max out cards

## Realistic Timeline
- Some changes (like paying down balances) can reflect within **30-60 days**
- Building a strong history takes **months to years**
- There's no legitimate overnight fix

## Free Resources
- **AnnualCreditReport.com** - free credit reports
- Many banks offer free credit score monitoring
