── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Give me a tip to be more organized at work. I'm a high school teacher.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
As a high school teacher, you are managing multiple preps, hundreds of students, and a constant influx of both physical and digital paperwork. 

The single most effective tip to streamline your day is to **implement a strict "One Color per Period" system.**

Assign a specific color to each class period (or each "prep" if you teach multiple sections of the same class) and apply it to **both** your physical and digital spaces.

Here is how to set it up:

### 1. The Physical Space
* **Turn-in Bins:** Have a stack of plastic trays where students submit paper assignments. Period 1 is Red, Period 2 is Blue, Period 3 is Green, etc. Students will automatically know exactly where their papers go.
* **Teacher Folders:** Keep your master copies, extra handouts, and graded-but-not-yet-returned papers in matching colored folders. 
* **Whiteboard/Agenda:** Write the daily agenda or homework on your board using the dry-erase marker that matches that class's color.

### 2. The Digital Space
* **Google Drive / Desktop Folders:** Color-code your digital folders to match your physical ones. (In Google Drive, you can right-click a folder and change its color).
* **Google Classroom / Canvas:** Change the banner or theme color of each digital classroom to match its designated color.
* **Digital Planner / Calendar:** Color-code your lesson planning calendar. At a glance, you’ll know exactly what you are teaching and when.

### Why this works so well for High School:
* **It eliminates decision fatigue:** When a student asks, "Where do I turn this in?" or "Do you have an extra copy of yesterday's worksheet?", you don't have to think. You just look for the color.
* **It prevents grading mix-ups:** It is incredibly easy to accidentally file Period 3's essays in Period 5's stack. Visual color cues act as a speed-bump for your brain, preventing these tedious mistakes.
* **It trains the students:** High schoolers are visual. They will quickly learn that "Blue = Period 2" and will start self-policing where they turn in work and where they look for absent work.
