I recently started a new job and am still on probation.

How do I understand what I want?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Starting a new job, especially while on probation, can make it surprisingly hard to know what you want. You're adapting to a new environment, learning expectations, and trying to make a good impression. In that situation, it's common for your own preferences to become less clear.

A useful question is: what exactly are you trying to understand what you want about?

For example:
- Whether you want to stay in this job long-term?
- What kind of career you want?
- What you want from work versus life outside work?
- Whether you're happy in the role?
- Something else entirely?

In general, people often discover what they want by paying attention to their reactions rather than trying to reason it out all at once. You might try keeping brief notes for a few weeks:
- What parts of the day give you energy?
- What parts drain you?
- What tasks make time pass quickly?
- What situations make you feel proud or satisfied?
- What frustrations keep recurring?

Patterns usually emerge before clear conclusions do.

Since you're still on probation, it may also help to separate two questions:
- "What do I want eventually?"
- "What do I need to do to succeed in this probation period?"

You don't necessarily need to answer the first question immediately. Sometimes the pressure to figure out your future can make it harder to notice your actual preferences in the present.
