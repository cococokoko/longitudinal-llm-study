To understand the US-China relationship, imagine a massive, 24/7 global fulfillment center. 

**The US and China are the two Lead Hands on the floor.** 

They absolutely dislike each other, but they are forced to work the same grueling 12-hour shift, and neither can be fired. Here is how their relationship plays out on the warehouse floor:

### 1. The Forced Partnership (Interdependence)
*   **The US** is the Lead Hand who handles the front office, the logistics software, and the high-paying shipping contracts. They have the fancy iPad and control the outbound dock.

*   **China** argues that the US is lazy, overpaid, and hoards all the best pallet jacks. China feels that because they do the heaviest lifting, they deserve to run the whole warehouse, not just the packing line.

### 3. Fighting Over the Forklifts (The Tech & Military Race)
