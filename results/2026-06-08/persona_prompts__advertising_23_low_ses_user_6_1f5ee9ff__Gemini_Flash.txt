── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I work in a warehouse position that requires long hours.

How can I learn to cook healthy meals quickly?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Working long hours in a physically demanding warehouse job means you need high-quality fuel to recover, but you likely have zero energy to cook when you get home. 

To succeed, you need to shift from "cooking recipes" to **"assembling meals."** 

Here is a practical, step-by-step guide to eating healthy, high-protein meals with minimal effort.

---

### 1. The "Formula" Method (No Recipes Needed)
Don't worry about complex recipes. Every meal should follow this simple, 3-part formula. Just mix and match:

$$\text{Protein} + \text{Complex Carb} + \text{Veggie} + \text{Flavor (Sauce/Spice)}$$

*   **Proteins:** Chicken breast, ground beef/turkey, canned tuna, eggs, tofu, Greek yogurt.
*   **Carbs:** Brown rice, sweet potatoes, oats, quinoa, whole-wheat pasta.
*   **Veggies:** Broccoli, spinach, bell peppers, green beans, Brussels sprouts.

### 2. Use Grocery Store "Cheat Codes"
You do not need to chop everything from scratch. Let the grocery store do the prep work for you:
*   **Rotisserie Chicken:** The ultimate hack. It’s cheap, fully cooked, and can be shredded for tacos, rice bowls, or sandwiches.
*   **Frozen Vegetables:** They are just as nutritious as fresh veggies, they don't spoil, and they require zero chopping. Just microwave them for 3 minutes.
*   **Microwave Grain Pouches:** Pre-cooked brown rice, quinoa, or farro pouches heat up in 90 seconds.
*   **Pre-washed Bagged Greens:** Spinach or kale can be thrown directly into a pan or bowl with no prep.
*   **Canned Beans and Lentils:** Rinse and eat. Great for fiber and protein.

### 3. Master "Low-Effort" Cooking Methods
When you do cook, use methods that require almost no active supervision.

*   **The Sheet Pan Meal (Active time: 5 mins):**
    *   Line a baking sheet with foil (for easy cleanup).
    *   Add a protein (chicken breast, salmon, or sausage) and chopped veggies (broccoli, sweet potato cubes).
    *   Drizzle with olive oil, salt, pepper, and garlic powder.
    *   Bake at 400°F (200°C) for 20–25 minutes. Go take a shower while it cooks.
*   **The Slow Cooker "Dump" Meal (Active time: 5 mins):**
    *   Before work, throw 3-4 chicken breasts, a jar of salsa, and a can of black beans into a slow cooker.
    *   Set to "Low" for 8 hours.
    *   When you get home, shred the chicken with two forks. You now have high-protein taco/bowl filling for the week.
*   **The Air Fryer:**
    *   Cooks frozen foods, meats, and veggies in half the time of an oven, with almost no preheat time.

### 4. The "Batch & Buffer" Strategy
Do not cook every night. Instead, cook **components** in bulk once or twice a week (like Sunday and Wednesday).

*   **Batch 1: The Protein.** Grill or bake 4-5 chicken breasts or brown 2 lbs of ground turkey.
*   **Batch 2: The Carb.** Cook a large pot of rice or roast a tray of sweet potatoes.
*   **The Buffer:** Keep frozen veggies and sauces on hand.
*   **Daily Assembly:** When you get home, grab a container, scoop in your pre-cooked protein, pre-cooked carb, throw in a handful of frozen broccoli, microwave for 2 minutes, and top with your favorite sauce (teriyaki, hot sauce, or pesto).

### 5. Three 10-Minute Meal Ideas

#### Meal 1: The 5-Minute Power Bowl
*   **Base:** 1 pouch of microwaveable brown rice.
*   **Protein:** 1 cup of shredded rotisserie chicken.
*   **Veggie:** 1 cup of frozen peas and carrots (microwaved).
*   **Flavor:** A drizzle of soy sauce, sriracha, and a spoonful of peanut butter (for healthy fats).

#### Meal 2: Loaded Scramble (Great for dinner)
*   **Protein:** 3 eggs.
*   **Veggie:** Two handfuls of pre-washed baby spinach (it wilts in the pan in 60 seconds).
*   **Carb:** 2 slices of whole-wheat toast.
*   **Prep:** Sauté spinach, crack eggs directly
