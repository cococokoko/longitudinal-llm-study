# Improving Productivity While Working From Home

Here are practical strategies tailored to your situation as a single parent:

## Setting Up Your Environment
- **Designate a workspace** - even a corner with a consistent setup signals "work mode"
- Keep essentials within reach to minimize interruptions
- Use noise-canceling headphones during focused work

## Managing Your Schedule Around Childcare
- **Work in blocks** aligned with nap times, school hours, or quiet activities
- Identify your **2-3 most critical tasks** each day rather than long to-do lists
- Front-load important work during your child's most predictable calm periods
- Be honest with employers/clients about your realistic availability windows

## Handling Interruptions Realistically
- Accept that **some interruptions are unavoidable** - build buffer time in
- Create a simple signal system with older children (red/green card on door)
- Keep age-appropriate independent activities ready for urgent work moments

## Protecting Your Energy
- **Batch similar tasks** together to reduce mental switching costs
- Use templates and shortcuts to speed up routine work
- Take genuine breaks - even 10 minutes recharges focus

## Practical Tools
- Time-blocking apps (Google Calendar, Todoist)
- Communication tools that allow async responses
- Meal prep to reduce decision fatigue during the day

## Key Mindset Shift
**Done is better than perfect.** A productive day as a single parent looks different than other situations - and that's completely valid.
