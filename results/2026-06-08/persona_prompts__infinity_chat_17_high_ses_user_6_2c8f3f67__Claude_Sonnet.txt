── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I am a partner at a major consulting firm.

How might society evolve if telepathy became possible?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This is a genuinely interesting question worth thinking through carefully rather than just enthusiastically.

## Likely Significant Disruptions

**Economic/Professional**
- Negotiation, sales, and consulting itself would transform fundamentally - information asymmetry largely disappears
- Legal systems built around intent and testimony would need rebuilding
- Advertising and persuasion industries collapse or radically change

**Political**
- Authoritarian systems become harder to maintain without thought control mechanisms
- But also: majority opinion becomes immediately visible, potentially crushing minority viewpoints
- Political manipulation becomes simultaneously harder and potentially more sophisticated

## Honest Complications Worth Considering

**The privacy problem is severe**
- Consent frameworks would need to be completely rebuilt
- Mental illness, trauma, and neurodiversity become publicly visible in ways that could increase rather than decrease stigma

**It likely wouldn't be equal**
- Who controls the technology/ability matters enormously
- Asymmetric telepathy (some can read, others can't) creates potentially the most dangerous power concentration imaginable

**Human psychology might not adapt well**
- Research on transparency suggests people actually become *more* defensive, not more honest, when observed
