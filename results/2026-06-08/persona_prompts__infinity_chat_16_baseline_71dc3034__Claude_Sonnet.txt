── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
What is the future of work in an increasingly automated economy?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
This is a genuinely important question worth thinking through carefully rather than just optimistically or pessimistically.

## What We Actually Know

**Historical pattern:** Previous automation waves displaced specific jobs but created new ones overall - though transitions were often painful for affected workers.

**Current situation differs in some ways:**
- AI can now affect cognitive work, not just physical/routine tasks
- Speed of change may exceed workers' ability to adapt
- Benefits have concentrated rather than distributed broadly

## Competing Honest Perspectives

**Reasons for optimism:**
- New job categories consistently emerge
- Productivity gains could fund broader prosperity
- Many tasks requiring judgment, relationships, and creativity remain difficult to automate

**Reasons for concern:**
- Transition costs fall heavily on specific communities
- New jobs may require skills displaced workers can't easily acquire
- Historical analogies may not hold at this scale

## What Seems Reasonably Clear

- **Some jobs will definitely disappear** - particularly routine cognitive tasks
- **Inequality risk is real** without deliberate policy responses
- **Education systems need significant reform** to prepare people for changing demands
- **The outcome isn't predetermined** - policy choices matter enormously

## Honest Uncertainty

Anyone claiming certainty about specific job numbers or timelines is overconfident. The actual outcome depends heavily on policy decisions, social choices, and technological developments we can't fully predict.
