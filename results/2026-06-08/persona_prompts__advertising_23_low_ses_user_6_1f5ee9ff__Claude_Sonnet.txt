── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I work in a warehouse position that requires long hours.

How can I learn to cook healthy meals quickly?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Great goal! Here are some practical approaches for someone with long hours and limited energy:

## Start Simple
- **Master 5-10 basic recipes** rather than trying lots of new things
- Focus on meals with **5 ingredients or fewer** initially
- Sheet pan meals and one-pot dishes minimize cleanup

## Prep Strategically
- **Batch cook on days off** - cook grains, proteins, and vegetables in bulk
- Pre-chop vegetables when you have energy
- Cook double portions so you have leftovers

## Quick Cooking Methods
- Stir-fries (10-15 minutes)
- Eggs in many forms (fast protein)
- Canned beans and legumes (already cooked)
- Frozen vegetables (nutritious and no prep)

## Practical Tools Worth Considering
- Rice cooker (set and forget)
- Slow cooker/crockpot (prep before work, ready when home)
- Instant Pot (fast cooking)

## Realistic Expectations
- **Healthy doesn't have to mean complex** - rotisserie chicken with frozen vegetables is genuinely good
- Keep healthy convenience items stocked
- Plan meals for the week so you're not deciding when tired

## Learning Resources
- YouTube channels focused on budget/quick cooking
- Budget Bytes website is highly practical
