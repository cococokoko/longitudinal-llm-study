  - Position your desk to reduce visual distractions.

  - Use a chair that supports a neutral posture for long periods.

  - Place keyboard and mouse so your elbows remain near a 90-degree angle.
  - Consider a sit-stand desk if you spend many hours at your workstation.

• Technology
  - Invest in a reliable internet connection and backup connectivity if your work is time-sensitive.
  - Use dual monitors if you regularly review documents, research materials, contracts, or correspondence simultaneously.
  - Improve audio quality with a dedicated microphone or headset for virtual meetings.
  - Ensure automatic cloud backups and strong cybersecurity practices.

• Focus and workflow
  - Schedule blocks for uninterrupted deep work and protect them on your calendar.
  - Disable nonessential notifications.
  - Keep a single task list or case/project management system rather than maintaining multiple competing lists.
  - Prepare your workspace at the end of each day so the next day's most important task is immediately visible.

• Lighting and environment
  - Maximize natural light without creating screen glare.
  - Add adjustable task lighting for reading and document review.
  - Maintain a comfortable temperature and good air circulation.
  - Consider acoustic treatments, white noise, or noise-canceling headphones if interruptions are common.
