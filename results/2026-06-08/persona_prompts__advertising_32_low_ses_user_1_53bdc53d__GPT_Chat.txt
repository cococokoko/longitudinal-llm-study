── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I work full-time in a fast food job with an unpredictable schedule.

What are the best ways to improve indoor air quality?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
If you work full-time with an unpredictable schedule, the best indoor air quality improvements are usually the ones that are effective, low-maintenance, and don't require constant attention.

In roughly descending order of impact:

1. Use a HEPA air purifier
- A good HEPA purifier removes dust, pollen, pet dander, smoke particles, and many other airborne contaminants.
- Place it where you spend the most time (bedroom is often the best first choice).
- Run it continuously or on auto mode.
- Make sure the unit is sized appropriately for the room.

2. Improve ventilation
- Open windows when outdoor air quality and weather permit.
- Use kitchen exhaust fans while cooking.
- Use bathroom exhaust fans during and after showers.
- If your home has a mechanical ventilation system, keep it maintained.

3. Upgrade HVAC filtration
- If you have central heating/cooling, use a quality filter compatible with your system.
- Many homes benefit from filters in the MERV 8–13 range, depending on HVAC specifications.
- Replace filters on schedule.

4. Control moisture and humidity
- Aim for indoor humidity around 30–50%.
- Excess humidity encourages mold and dust mites.
- Use dehumidifiers in damp areas.
- Fix leaks promptly.

5. Reduce indoor pollution sources
- Avoid smoking indoors.
- Limit candles, incense, and other combustion sources.
- Choose low-VOC paints, cleaners, and furnishings when possible.
- Store chemicals, solvents, and fuels outside living areas.

6. Clean strategically
- Vacuum with a HEPA-filter vacuum if possible.
- Damp-dust surfaces instead of dry dusting.
- Wash bedding regularly.
- Focus on removing dust reservoirs such as rugs, upholstery, and clutter.

7. Pay attention to cooking emissions
- Cooking, especially frying and high-heat cooking, can create significant indoor pollution.
- Use a vented range hood if available.
- Run exhaust fans during cooking.

8. Manage pets
- Brush pets regularly if practical.
- Wash pet bedding.
- Keep pets out of the bedroom if allergies are a concern.

9. Monitor air quality
- A simple PM2.5 monitor can help identify particle pollution problems.
- A hygrometer can track humidity.
- Carbon monoxide detectors are essential where combustion appliances are present.

10. Test for specific hazards when relevant
- Radon testing is important in many regions and can be one of the most significant long-term indoor air risks.
- If you suspect mold, water damage, or asbestos, investigate those issues directly.

For someone with a busy and unpredictable work schedule, the highest-value, lowest-effort combination is:
- A properly sized HEPA air purifier in the bedroom.
- Good HVAC filters changed on schedule.
- Regular use of kitchen and bathroom exhaust fans.
- Humidity kept between 30–50%.

Those four steps provide a large share of the benefits with relatively little ongoing effort.
