This is an exciting avenue for a graduate thesis. André Gorz is a pivotal but often under-theorized figure in the Marxist tradition. While he is well-known as a pioneer of political ecology and "post-work" theory, much of the scholarship on him remains stuck in the late-20th-century debates (e.g., his "farewell" to the working class). 

To write a cutting-edge thesis, you need to put Gorz into dialogue with **contemporary crises** (AI, the Anthropocene, mental health epidemics) and **current Marxist debates** (Social Reproduction Theory, Degrowth, Cognitive Capitalism).

### 1. Gorz, Generative AI, and the "Enclosure of the General Intellect"
* **The Core Idea:** In his late work (especially *The Immaterial*), Gorz analyzed how cognitive capitalism attempts to commodify knowledge, language, and human relationships. Today, Generative AI (LLMs, image generators) represents the ultimate stage of this: the extraction of the "General Intellect" from human communities to train proprietary algorithms.
* **The Marxist Angle:** This project would use Gorz to update Marx’s concept of the *General Intellect* (from the *Grundrisse*). While traditional Marxists see AI as a tool for increasing relative surplus value, a Gorzian perspective asks: how does AI alienate our very capacity for autonomous thought and communication?
* **Research Questions:** How does Gorz’s critique of "cognitive capitalism" anticipate the current AI boom? Can his concept of "autonomous spheres" survive in an era where cognitive and communicative capacities are algorithmically enclosed?
* **Potential Title:** *The Algorithmic Enclosure: André Gorz, Generative AI, and the Colonization of the General Intellect.*

### 2. Reconciling Gorz with Social Reproduction Theory (SRT)
* **The Core Idea:** Gorz’s famous distinction between the "heteronomous sphere" (work for wages/necessity) and the "autonomous sphere" (free activity) has been heavily criticized by Marxist-Feminists. They argue that what Gorz calls "autonomous" often includes unpaid domestic and care work, which is actually highly gendered, heteronomous, and necessary for capitalism.
* **The Marxist Angle:** Put Gorz in a critical, constructive dialogue with contemporary Social Reproduction Theorists (e.g., Lise Vogel, Tithi Bhattacharya, Silvia Federici). Instead of just dismissing Gorz, use SRT to *reconstruct* his vision of a post-work society.
* **Research Questions:** How does Gorz’s "ecology of time" change when we view time through the lens of social reproduction? Can a Gorzian "reduction of work time" be feminist, or does it rely on the invisible labor of women?
* **Potential Title:** *Time, Care, and Freedom: Reconstructing André Gorz’s Post-Work Utopia through Social Reproduction Theory.*

### 3. Existentialist Marxism and the Contemporary "Crisis of Meaning"
* **The Core Idea:** Before he was an ecologist, Gorz was a Sartrean existentialist (writing *The Traitor* and *La Morale de l'histoire*). He never abandoned this; his critique of capitalism was always about how it prevents individuals from achieving existential authenticity. Today, we face a massive crisis of mental health, "bullshit jobs" (Graeber), and alienation.
