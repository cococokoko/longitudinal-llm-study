── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here are some effective approaches for learning a musical instrument online:

## Structured Learning Platforms
- **Yousician** - interactive feedback as you play
- **Simply Piano** or **Flowkey** - good for piano specifically
- **Fender Play** - guitar focused with structured curriculum
- **Lessonface** - connects you with live teachers via video

## Video Resources
- YouTube channels from qualified instructors
- Look for channels that explain *why* techniques work, not just how
- TED-Ed and similar educational content for music theory

## Key Practices That Actually Work
- **Consistent short sessions** (20-30 minutes daily beats occasional long sessions)
- **Slow, deliberate practice** rather than running through pieces quickly
- **Record yourself** to identify issues you can't hear while playing
- **Use a metronome** from the beginning

## Getting Feedback
- Consider occasional live online lessons even if self-teaching primarily
- Online communities (Reddit's r/learnguitar, etc.) can offer feedback
- Some apps use microphone analysis to give real-time feedback

## Realistic Expectations
- Progress is often nonlinear - plateaus are normal
- Most people underestimate the value of music theory basics
- 6-12 months of consistent practice typically yields noticeable competence
