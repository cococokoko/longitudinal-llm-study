── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How to make my own natural beauty products?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Making your own natural beauty products can be simple, affordable, and customizable. The key is understanding that "natural" doesn't automatically mean safer or longer-lasting, so hygiene and proper storage are important.

Basic supplies:
- Clean glass jars or bottles
- Mixing bowls and spoons
- Distilled water (if using water-based recipes)
- Carrier oils (jojoba, sweet almond, coconut, olive, argan)
- Butters (shea butter, cocoa butter)
- Natural exfoliants (sugar, salt, oats, coffee grounds)
- Optional: essential oils (used sparingly and with proper safety precautions)

Easy DIY recipes:

1. Sugar Lip Scrub
Ingredients:
- 1 tablespoon sugar
- 1 teaspoon honey
- 1 teaspoon coconut oil

Instructions:
- Mix ingredients into a paste.
- Gently massage onto lips for 30–60 seconds.
- Rinse and apply lip balm.

Storage:
- Store in a clean container for up to 1–2 weeks.

2. Moisturizing Body Butter
Ingredients:
- 1/2 cup shea butter
- 2 tablespoons coconut oil
- 1 tablespoon jojoba oil

Instructions:
- Melt ingredients gently using a double boiler.
- Cool until partially solidified.
- Whip with a hand mixer until fluffy.
- Transfer to a jar.

Storage:
- Keep in a cool place for up to several months.

3. Oatmeal Face Mask
Ingredients:
- 2 tablespoons finely ground oats
- 1 tablespoon plain yogurt
- 1 teaspoon honey

Instructions:
- Mix into a smooth paste.
- Apply for 10–15 minutes.
- Rinse with lukewarm water.

Use immediately.

4. Simple Facial Oil
Ingredients:
- 2 tablespoons jojoba oil
- 1 tablespoon argan oil

Instructions:
- Mix and store in a dropper bottle.
- Apply 2–3 drops to damp skin.

5. Coffee Body Scrub
Ingredients:
- 1/2 cup coffee grounds
- 1/4 cup brown sugar
- 2 tablespoons coconut oil

Instructions:
- Mix thoroughly.
- Use on damp skin with gentle circular motions.
- Rinse well.

Important safety tips:
- Patch-test any new product on a small area of skin for 24–48 hours.
- Avoid putting water into products unless you use a preservative; water can encourage bacterial growth.
- Essential oils can cause irritation and should be diluted properly. Some are unsafe during pregnancy or for certain medical conditions.
- Discard products if they develop an unusual smell, color, or texture.
- Avoid using lemon juice or other acidic ingredients directly on skin before sun exposure.
