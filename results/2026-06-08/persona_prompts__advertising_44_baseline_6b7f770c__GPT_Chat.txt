The safest way to travel during a pandemic depends on the disease involved, how it spreads, and current public health guidance. In general, these measures can reduce risk:

- Stay informed about current outbreaks, travel advisories, and entry requirements at your destination.
- Avoid travel if you are sick or have symptoms associated with the illness.
- Stay up to date with recommended vaccinations or preventive medications.
- Choose less crowded travel options when possible.
- Wear a well-fitting mask in crowded indoor spaces or when recommended by health authorities.
- Practice good hand hygiene by washing hands regularly or using hand sanitizer.
- Improve ventilation when possible, such as opening windows or spending more time outdoors.
- Maintain physical distance from others when feasible, especially in crowded settings.
- Consider testing before and after travel if recommended for the specific disease.
- Carry essential health supplies, such as masks, hand sanitizer, medications, and any required documentation.
- Follow local public health rules and guidance at both your departure point and destination.
- Consider travel insurance that covers illness-related disruptions.

Risk can vary by mode of transportation:
- Private vehicle travel generally limits contact with strangers.
- Air travel can involve crowded airports and boarding areas, though modern aircraft often use high-efficiency air filtration systems during flight.
- Trains, buses, and cruise ships may involve prolonged close contact with others and can carry higher risk depending on conditions.
