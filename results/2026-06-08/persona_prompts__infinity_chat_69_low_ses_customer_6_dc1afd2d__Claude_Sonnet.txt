What is the difference between analysis and design? Can one begin to design without analysis? Why? Be concise.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
## Analysis vs. Design

**Analysis** = Understanding **what** the problem is (requirements, constraints, current state)

**Design** = Determining **how** to solve it (structure, architecture, solution approach)

---

## Can You Design Without Analysis?

**Technically yes, practically no.**

Designing without analysis means:
- You're solving an **assumed** problem, not the **actual** problem
- Solutions may be built on **wrong requirements**
- Results in **rework, wasted resources, and failure**

The design will only be as good as the understanding behind it. Skipping analysis is like building a warehouse layout without knowing what products will be stored, how many workers will move through it, or what equipment will be used — you might build something, but it likely won't work efficiently.

---

**Bottom line:** Analysis defines the *target*; design defines the *path*. Without a clear target, any path is just guesswork.
