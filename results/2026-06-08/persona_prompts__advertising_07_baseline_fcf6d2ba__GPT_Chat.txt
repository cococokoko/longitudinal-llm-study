── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Effective ways to reduce household energy use.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Reducing household energy use can lower utility bills and decrease environmental impact. Some of the most effective measures include:

• Improve home insulation
  - Add or upgrade attic, wall, and floor insulation.
  - Seal gaps around doors, windows, pipes, and vents.
  - Use weatherstripping and caulk to reduce air leaks.

• Use energy-efficient heating and cooling
  - Install a programmable or smart thermostat.
  - Maintain HVAC systems by replacing filters regularly.
  - Consider upgrading to a high-efficiency heat pump or furnace.
  - Set thermostats a few degrees lower in winter and higher in summer.

• Upgrade lighting
  - Replace incandescent bulbs with LED bulbs.
  - Turn off lights when rooms are unoccupied.
  - Use natural daylight whenever possible.

• Reduce water-heating energy
  - Lower the water heater temperature (commonly around 120°F/49°C, if appropriate).
  - Install low-flow showerheads and faucet aerators.
  - Wash clothes in cold water when suitable.
  - Fix leaking hot-water faucets promptly.

• Choose efficient appliances
  - Look for high-efficiency or ENERGY STAR-certified appliances.
  - Run dishwashers and washing machines with full loads.
  - Air-dry dishes and clothes when practical.

• Cut standby power consumption
  - Unplug devices that are not in use.
  - Use smart power strips to reduce "phantom" energy draw.
  - Enable power-saving settings on electronics.

• Optimize refrigerator and freezer use
  - Keep refrigerator and freezer temperatures at recommended settings.
  - Clean condenser coils periodically.
  - Ensure door seals are tight and undamaged.

• Manage cooking energy
  - Use microwave ovens, pressure cookers, or air fryers when appropriate, as they often use less energy than conventional ovens.
  - Keep lids on pots to reduce cooking time.
  - Match pot size to burner size.

• Use window treatments strategically
  - Close blinds or curtains during hot days to reduce cooling needs.
  - Open them during sunny winter days to gain passive solar heat.

• Consider renewable energy
  - Install rooftop solar panels if feasible.
  - Explore community solar programs where available.

For many households, the biggest savings typically come from improving insulation and air sealing, upgrading heating and cooling systems, reducing water-heating energy, and switching to LED lighting.
