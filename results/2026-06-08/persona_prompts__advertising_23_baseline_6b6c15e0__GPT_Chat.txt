── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How can I learn to cook healthy meals quickly?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Learning to cook healthy meals quickly is mostly about simplifying your approach and building a small set of reliable habits.

Here are some practical steps:

• Master 5–10 basic meals
  - Stir-fries with vegetables and protein
  - Sheet-pan chicken and vegetables
  - Grain bowls (rice, quinoa, or farro with vegetables and protein)
  - Omelets or frittatas
  - Soups and stews
  - Pasta with vegetables and lean protein
  Focus on repeating these until they become automatic.

• Learn a simple meal formula
  - Protein: chicken, fish, tofu, beans, eggs
  - Vegetables: fresh or frozen
  - Carbohydrate: rice, potatoes, whole-grain pasta, bread
  - Healthy fat: olive oil, avocado, nuts, seeds
  Mix and match ingredients rather than following complex recipes every day.

• Use time-saving ingredients
  - Frozen vegetables
  - Pre-washed greens
  - Canned beans
  - Rotisserie chicken
  - Microwaveable grains
  These can cut preparation time dramatically while still supporting a healthy diet.

• Develop a few key cooking skills
  - Chopping vegetables efficiently
  - Sautéing
  - Roasting
  - Cooking grains
  - Basic seasoning with salt, pepper, herbs, garlic, and citrus
  A handful of techniques can be applied to hundreds of meals.

• Cook in batches
  - Prepare extra protein, grains, or vegetables.
  - Store portions for future lunches and dinners.
  - Even one hour of batch cooking can save several hours during the week.

• Keep your kitchen stocked
  Useful staples include:
  - Olive oil
  - Garlic and onions
  - Canned tomatoes
  - Beans and lentils
  - Rice or quinoa
  - Frozen vegetables
  - Eggs
  Having ingredients available reduces reliance on takeout.

• Use beginner-friendly resources
  - YouTube channels focused on simple home cooking
  - Websites with 20–30 minute recipes
  - Meal-prep creators who emphasize healthy, practical meals
  Watch techniques as much as recipes.

• Aim for speed, not perfection
  A healthy 15-minute meal made consistently is usually more valuable than an elaborate recipe you rarely have time to cook.

Example 15-minute dinner:
- Sauté frozen mixed vegetables in olive oil.
- Add canned beans or pre-cooked chicken.
- Season with garlic, paprika, salt, and pepper.
- Serve over microwaveable brown rice.
- Add a squeeze of lemon.

If you're a complete beginner, I can also give you a 2-week learning plan that builds cooking skills step by step.
