I work full-time in a fast food job with an unpredictable schedule.

Is it impossible to know everything?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The short answer is: **Yes, it is absolutely impossible to know everything.** 

But given the context you just shared—working a demanding, full-time fast-food job with a chaotic schedule—your question feels a lot deeper than just a philosophical thought. 

When you work in an environment that is fast-paced, high-stress, and unpredictable, it is incredibly common to feel overwhelmed by the sheer amount of things you are expected to know, remember, and handle on the fly. 

Here is why it is impossible to know everything, and why you need to give yourself a break right now:

### 1. Your brain is dealing with "Cognitive Overload"
In fast food, you are constantly bombarded with information: changing menus, new promotions, complex POS systems, specific food safety rules, and the unpredictable moods of hundreds of customers and coworkers. Add an unpredictable schedule to that, and your brain is never allowed to establish a comfortable, resting routine. When you are tired and stressed, it feels like you *should* know more than you do, but your brain is simply too exhausted to process it all. 

### 2. The desire to "know everything" is often a desire for control
When your schedule is unpredictable, you lack control over your own time and life. Subconsciously, we sometimes think, *"If I just knew everything—if I knew exactly how to handle every customer, every machine, every situation—I wouldn't feel so stressed."* We want knowledge because we want to feel safe and prepared. But because the world (and the food service industry) is chaotic, you will always encounter things you don't know. 

### 3. Even the experts only know a tiny fraction of things
There is a famous quote attributed to the ancient philosopher Socrates: *"I know that I know nothing."* The smartest people in the world are the ones who accept that their knowledge is limited. The goal of life isn't to be a walking encyclopedia; it’s to be adaptable. 

### How to handle the feeling of not knowing enough:
* **Normalize saying "I don't know."** If a customer asks a question you don't know the answer to, or a manager asks you to do something you haven't been trained on, it is a sign of strength to say, "I'm not sure, let me find out for you." 
* **Focus on adaptability, not memorization.** You don't need to know everything; you just need to know how to figure things out or who to ask for help when a problem arises.
* **Give yourself grace.** You are working a physically and mentally draining job. An unpredictable schedule disrupts your sleep, your social life, and your mental recovery time. Do not beat yourself up for making mistakes or forgetting things. 

You are a human being, not a hard drive. It is impossible to know everything, and no one should expect you to. You only need to know enough to get through the day, and you are allowed to learn the rest as you go.
