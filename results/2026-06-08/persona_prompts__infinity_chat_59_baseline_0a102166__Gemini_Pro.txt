── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Give me a tip to be more organized at work. I'm a high school teacher.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
As a high school teacher, you are juggling multiple preps, 100+ students, grading, and endless administrative tasks. Because your day is dictated by the bell schedule, you don't have the luxury of "catching up" during a long uninterrupted block of time. 

Here is one of the most effective, game-changing tips for high school teachers:

### Implement the "Friday Afternoon Reset"

Do not leave the building on Friday until you have spent 15–20 minutes doing a complete reset for Monday. By the end of the week, you are exhausted and just want to go home, but investing these 15 minutes will completely eliminate the "Sunday Scaries" and stop the chaotic Monday morning scramble.

To make it easy when your brain is fried on a Friday, follow the **4 C’s**:

**1. Clear (Your Desk)**
Take 3 minutes to clear your physical workspace. Put away stray pens, file away the week's leftover handouts, and put ungraded work into a specific "To Grade" folder. Do not leave a pile of ambiguous papers on your desk. 

**2. Copies & Materials**
Make your copies for Monday (and ideally Tuesday) *now*. The copier is almost always available on Friday afternoons, whereas there is always a stressed-out line for it on Monday mornings. Put Monday's handouts in a designated tray for each class period. 

**3. Classroom Board**
Erase Friday’s work. Write Monday’s date, daily objectives, and the agenda on your whiteboard or smartboard. When you walk in on Monday, the room will already look like it's ready to teach.

**4. Computer**
Close out of the 40 browser tabs you opened throughout the week. Reply to any urgent emails, and then close your inbox. Finally, open the slide deck or lesson plan you need for Monday’s first period so it is the very first thing you see when you wake up your computer.

**Why this works specifically for high school:**
High school mornings are notoriously chaotic—students come in early for help, administrators need things, or you get pulled into a hallway duty. If your room, copies, and digital workspace are already prepped from Friday, you can handle Monday morning interruptions with zero stress because your actual teaching job is already set up and waiting for you.
