If your goal is mastering digital photography, a structured approach that fits around an unpredictable full-time work schedule can help.

Key areas to learn:
- Camera fundamentals
  - Exposure triangle: aperture, shutter speed, ISO
  - Focus modes and metering
  - Shooting in RAW
- Composition
  - Rule of thirds
  - Leading lines
  - Framing and perspective
  - Use of light and shadow
- Lighting
  - Natural light
  - Golden hour and blue hour
  - Basic flash techniques
- Post-processing
  - Lightroom, Capture One, or similar software
  - Color correction
  - Exposure adjustments
  - Cropping and sharpening
- Specialized genres
  - Portraits
  - Landscapes
  - Street photography
  - Wildlife
  - Sports and action

A flexible learning plan:
- 15–20 minutes on workdays:
  - Watch one tutorial or read one lesson.
  - Practice a single concept with your camera or phone.
- 1–2 hours on days off:
  - Complete a focused photo session.
  - Edit your best images.
  - Review what worked and what didn't.

Suggested progression:
1. Learn exposure triangle.
2. Shoot in manual or aperture-priority mode.
3. Practice composition daily.
4. Learn RAW editing.
5. Build a small portfolio around a subject you enjoy.

Good resources:
- YouTube: Simon d'Entremont, Tony & Chelsea Northrup, Nigel Danson
- Courses: CreativeLive, Udemy, Skillshare
- Books:
  - "Understanding Exposure" by Bryan Peterson
  - "The Photographer's Eye" by Michael Freeman

A useful milestone is to complete a 30-photo project where each image intentionally demonstrates a different technique. This builds skill quickly without requiring a fixed schedule.
