── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Best skin routine for dry skin.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The best skincare routine for dry skin focuses on two main goals: **adding hydration** (water) to the skin and **locking in moisture** (oil/lipids) to repair the skin barrier. 

Here is a dermatologist-recommended, step-by-step routine designed to take your skin from tight and flaky to plump and glowing.

---

### ☀️ Morning Routine (Focus: Hydration & Protection)

**1. Cleanse (Gently!)**
*   **What to do:** If your skin is extremely dry, skip the morning cleanser and just splash your face with lukewarm water. If you prefer to wash, use a **cream, lotion, or milky cleanser**. 
*   **Avoid:** Foaming cleansers or anything containing sulfates (SLS), which strip the skin of natural oils.

**2. Hydrating Toner or Essence (Optional but recommended)**
*   **What to do:** Apply a milky or hydrating toner while your skin is still slightly damp. 
*   **Look for:** Glycerin, aloe, or rose water. Avoid any toners with denatured alcohol or witch hazel.

**3. Hydrating Serum**
*   **What to do:** Apply a **Hyaluronic Acid** or **Polyglutamic Acid** serum. 
*   **Crucial Tip:** Hyaluronic acid *must* be applied to damp skin. If applied to dry skin, it can actually pull water out of your skin, making it drier.

**4. Moisturizer**
*   **What to do:** Apply a rich, nourishing cream to seal in the serum. 
*   **Look for:** Ceramides (which act like the "mortar" between your skin cells), shea butter, and squalane.

**5. Sunscreen**
*   **What to do:** Apply a broad-spectrum SPF 30 or higher. Look for sunscreens marketed as "hydrating" or "dewy." Cream-based sunscreens are much better for dry skin than matte or gel-based ones.

---

### 🌙 Evening Routine (Focus: Repair & Deep Moisture)

**1. Double Cleanse**
*   **Step 1:** Use a **cleansing balm or cleansing oil** to melt away makeup, SPF, and daily grime without tugging at your skin.
*   **Step 2:** Follow up with your gentle, non-foaming cream cleanser from the morning.

**2. Hydrating Serum**
*   **What to do:** Reapply your hydrating serum (again, on damp skin). 

**3. Heavy Night Cream**
*   **What to do:** Nighttime is when your skin repairs itself. Use a thicker, heavier cream than you use in the morning. 

**4. Face Oil (The Game Changer)**
*   **What to do:** Pat 2-3 drops of face oil over your moisturizer. Oil acts as an occlusive, meaning it creates a seal over your skin so no moisture can evaporate while you sleep.
*   **Look for:** Squalane, rosehip, argan, or jojoba oil. 

---

### 🗓️ Weekly Treatments

*   **Exfoliation (1-2 times a week):** Dry skin needs exfoliation to remove dead, flaky cells so moisturizers can actually penetrate. **Do not use physical scrubs.** Instead, use a gentle chemical exfoliant like **Lactic Acid** or **Mandelic Acid** (these are AHAs that exfoliate while also drawing moisture into the skin).
*   **Hydrating Masks (1-2 times a week):** Look for overnight sleep masks or sheet masks packed with ceramides and hyaluronic acid.

---

### 🛒 The Dry Skin Cheat Sheet

**Ingredients to LOOK FOR:**
*   **Humectants (draw water in):** Hyaluronic acid, glycerin, panthenol (Vitamin B5), aloe vera.
*   **Emollients (smooth the skin):** Ceramides, squalane, plant oils, shea butter.
*   **Occlusives (trap water in):** Petrolatum (Vaseline/Aquaphor), beeswax, heavy oils.

**Ingredients to AVOID:**
*   Denatured alcohol (Alcohol Denat)
*   Fragrance (can irritate a compromised dry skin barrier)
*   Salicylic Acid (BHA) and Benzoyl Peroxide (unless treating specific acne spots)
*   Harsh physical scrubs (walnut shells, apricot scrubs)
