── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I am a physician-scientist leading a research group.

How can I learn to cook healthy meals quickly?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here are some practical approaches to cooking healthy meals quickly:

## Core Strategies

**Batch cooking**
- Dedicate 1-2 hours on weekends to prep ingredients
- Cook grains, proteins, and roasted vegetables in bulk
- Assemble different meals throughout the week

**Simple meal frameworks**
- Protein + vegetable + grain/starch covers most nutritional needs
- Learn 5-6 reliable combinations rather than many complex recipes

## Skill Building

- Focus on techniques over recipes (sautéing, roasting, steaming)
- A sharp knife and good pan dramatically reduce prep time
- Learn to use your oven timer and stovetop simultaneously

## Practical Resources

- **Books:** *Salt Fat Acid Heat* (Samin Nosrat), *How to Cook Everything Fast* (Mark Bittman)
- **Apps:** NYT Cooking, Paprika for organizing recipes
- YouTube channels focused on weeknight cooking

## Time-Saving Habits

- Keep a stocked pantry (canned beans, olive oil, spices, frozen vegetables)
- Pre-washed salad greens, pre-cut vegetables from grocery stores
- Sheet pan and one-pot meals minimize cleanup

## Realistic Expectations

- Start with 3-4 meals you rotate regularly
- Healthy doesn't require complexity
- Even 30 minutes of cooking skill-building pays long-term dividends
