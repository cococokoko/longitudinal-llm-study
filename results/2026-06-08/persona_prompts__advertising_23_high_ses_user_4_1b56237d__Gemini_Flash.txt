── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
As a physician-scientist, you are trained to manage complex systems, optimize protocols, and rely on evidence-based efficiency. To learn to cook healthy meals quickly, you should approach the kitchen not as an artistic studio, but as a **highly optimized laboratory**. 

By treating cooking as a system of repeatable protocols rather than a series of distinct recipes, you can minimize cognitive load, reduce active time to under 15 minutes per meal, and maintain high nutritional standards.

Here is a framework to design your personal culinary operating system.

---

### 1. The Conceptual Framework: "Modular" Cooking
Forget traditional recipes. They require too much working memory after a long day in the clinic or lab. Instead, adopt a **Modular Matrix** approach. Every healthy meal is simply a combination of four macro-components:

$$\text{Meal} = \text{Protein} + \text{Fiber (Vegetable)} + \text{Complex Carbohydrate} + \text{Flavor Vehicle (Fat/Acid/Sauce)}$$

By prepping these components in bulk (or buying them pre-prepped), you can assemble a variety of meals in under 5 minutes.

*   **Proteins:** Chicken breast, salmon, tofu, eggs, canned tuna, tempeh.
*   **Fiber:** Spinach, broccoli, Brussels sprouts, bell peppers, asparagus.
*   **Carbs:** Quinoa, brown rice, sweet potatoes, lentils, black beans.
*   **Flavor:** Olive oil, lemon juice, soy sauce, sriracha, pesto, Greek yogurt-based dressings.

### 2. Optimize Your "Lab" Equipment (High-Yield Tools)
Invest in tools that automate cooking and reduce active monitoring time. 
*   **The Air Fryer:** The ultimate tool for the busy professional. It cooks proteins and vegetables in 10–15 minutes with zero active monitoring and minimal cleanup.
*   **The Instant Pot (Pressure Cooker):** Ideal for hands-off cooking of grains, legumes, and tough meats. You can throw ingredients in, press a button, and walk away to write a grant.
*   **A High-Quality Chef’s Knife & Large Cutting Board:** Dull knives increase prep time and cognitive friction. Keep one chef's knife professionally sharpened.
*   **Glass Meal Prep Containers:** For storage, reheating, and visual inventory of your fridge.

### 3. Outsource and Automate (Leverage "Pre-Prepped" Reagents)
As a lab leader, you know when to outsource. Apply this to your kitchen. Buying pre-processed ingredients is a highly efficient trade-off of money for time.
*   **Frozen Vegetables:** Nutritionally equivalent (often superior) to fresh, because they are flash-frozen at peak ripeness. No washing, no chopping, no waste.
*   **Pre-washed Greens:** Arugula, spinach, and kale can be thrown directly into a pan or bowl.
*   **Microwavable Grain Pouches:** Pre-cooked quinoa, brown rice, or farro pouches heat up in 90 seconds.
*   **Rotisserie Chicken / Canned Fish:** Instant, high-quality protein with zero cooking time.
