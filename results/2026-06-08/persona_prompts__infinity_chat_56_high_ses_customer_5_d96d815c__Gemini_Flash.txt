Name an economic value of an additional year of schooling.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
From a microeconomic perspective, the most well-established measure is the **Private Rate of Return (or the Earnings Premium)**. 

Decades of labor economics research (pioneered by the Mincer equation) show that **each additional year of schooling yields an average 8% to 10% increase in an individual’s lifetime earnings.**

### Why this matters to a technology executive:
While this represents a personal financial gain for the individual, for an organization, this premium is a direct proxy for **increased labor productivity and cognitive adaptability**. 

In high-skill sectors like technology, that additional year of education translates to:
* **Faster Technological Adoption:** A highly educated workforce reduces the "friction cost" of transitioning to new tech stacks, AI integration, or novel methodologies.
* **Higher Innovation Capacity:** Economists have found a strong correlation between years of schooling and patent generation, driving R&D efficiency and accelerating time-to-market for complex products. 

In short, that extra year of schooling is a foundational investment in **human capital ROI**, driving both individual earning power and corporate innovation capacity.
