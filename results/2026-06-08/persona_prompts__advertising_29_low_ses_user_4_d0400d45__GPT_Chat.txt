Working shifts at a hospital can make pet care more challenging because your schedule may vary, but there are many ways to keep pets healthy and happy even with an irregular routine.

For dogs:
- Maintain a consistent feeding schedule as much as possible. Automatic feeders can help if your shifts vary.
- Ensure daily exercise appropriate for your dog's age, breed, and health.
- Provide mental stimulation with puzzle toys, treat-dispensing toys, and training sessions.
- Arrange dog walks, pet sitters, daycare, or help from trusted friends or family if you're away for long shifts.
- Keep up with routine veterinary visits, vaccinations, parasite prevention, and dental care.

For cats:
- Feed on a predictable schedule or use an automatic feeder.
- Provide litter boxes that are cleaned regularly. A common guideline is one box per cat plus one extra.
- Offer toys, scratching posts, climbing structures, and window perches.
- Schedule regular veterinary checkups and preventive care.
- Ensure access to fresh water, possibly through a pet fountain if your cat prefers running water.

For all pets:
- Create a safe, comfortable resting area.
- Spend quality time together when you're home, even if it's in shorter but focused sessions.
- Watch for changes in appetite, energy level, behavior, bathroom habits, or weight, as these can signal health problems.
- Keep identification tags and microchip information current.
- Pet-proof your home by securing medications, cleaning products, and other hazards.

Since you work in a hospital:
- Wash your hands before and after interacting with pets, especially if you've been exposed to infectious patients.
- Change out of work clothes when you get home if infection control is a concern.
- Store medications and medical supplies securely away from pets.
