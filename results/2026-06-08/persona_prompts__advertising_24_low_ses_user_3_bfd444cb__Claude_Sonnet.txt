── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I recently started a new job and am still on probation.

What are the best ways to save for retirement?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Great question! Here are some solid retirement savings strategies:

## Start with the Basics

- **Build an emergency fund first** - 3-6 months of expenses so you don't raid retirement savings
- **Understand your employer's benefits** - check if they offer a 401(k) or pension once probation ends

## Common Retirement Accounts

- **401(k)/403(b)** - employer-sponsored, often with matching contributions
- **IRA (Traditional or Roth)** - individual accounts you open yourself
- **Roth IRA** - especially good if you're in a lower tax bracket now

## Key Principles

- **Get the full employer match** - it's essentially free money
- **Start as early as possible** - compound interest rewards time significantly
- **Aim for 10-15% of income** saved for retirement
- **Increase contributions gradually** as your salary grows

## Practical Tips

- Automate contributions so you don't think about it
- Keep investment fees low (index funds are popular for this)
- Diversify your investments
- Avoid early withdrawals which have penalties

## While Still on Probation

- You can **open a Roth or Traditional IRA now** without waiting for your employer
- Contribute even small amounts to build the habit
