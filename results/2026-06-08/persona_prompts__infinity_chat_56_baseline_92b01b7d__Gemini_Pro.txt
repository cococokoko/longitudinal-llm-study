── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Name an economic value of an additional year of schooling.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
One of the most direct and significant economic values of an additional year of schooling is **increased individual earnings** (often referred to as the "wage premium"). 

On average, labor economists estimate that a single additional year of education increases an individual's future wages by roughly **8% to 10%**. 

This increase occurs because the extra year of schooling builds "human capital"—equipping the student with better skills, knowledge, and problem-solving abilities, which makes them more productive and valuable to future employers. 

*(On a broader, societal level, another economic value is **increased macroeconomic growth**, as a more educated workforce drives innovation, productivity, and higher tax revenues for the government.)*
