- Avoiding travel when you are sick or have symptoms.
- Staying up to date with recommended vaccinations or preventive measures for the disease in question.
- Choosing outdoor activities and well-ventilated spaces when possible.
- Practicing good hand hygiene, especially after touching high-contact surfaces.
- Wearing a well-fitting mask in crowded indoor settings if the illness spreads through the air or respiratory droplets.

Comparing common travel options:

- Private vehicle:
  - Often among the lower-risk options because you can limit contact with people outside your household.
  - Plan stops to avoid crowded indoor areas when possible.

- Train or bus:

- Air travel:
  - Airports can involve close contact with many people.
  - Modern aircraft typically have good air filtration systems, but risk can still arise during boarding, deplaning, and time spent in terminals.
  - Consider masking in crowded indoor areas.

- Cruise ships:
  - Historically associated with outbreaks because many people share indoor spaces for extended periods.
  - Check current health advisories and operator policies.

Before traveling:
- Review guidance from local and national public health authorities.
- Check destination requirements and healthcare access.
- Consider travel insurance that covers illness-related disruptions.
