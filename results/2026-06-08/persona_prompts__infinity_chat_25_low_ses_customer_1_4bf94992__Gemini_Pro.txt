How to say a formal thank you for your time in the subject line of an email?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
When you're juggling a full-time job with an unpredictable schedule, keeping your emails clear and to the point is incredibly helpful for both you and the person you're emailing. 

For a formal email subject line, the best approach is to be brief, include your name, and mention what the meeting was about. Here are a few great options depending on the situation:

**If you are thanking a manager for discussing your schedule or shifts:**
*   *Thank You: Schedule Discussion – [Your Name]*
*   *Thank You for Your Time Today – [Your Name]*
*   *Appreciation for your time regarding my schedule – [Your Name]*

**If you are thanking someone after a job interview:**
*   *Thank You – [Job Title] Interview – [Your Name]*
*   *Thank You for Your Time – [Your Name]*
*   *Following Up & Thank You – [Your Name]*

**If you are thanking management or HR for a general meeting:**
*   *Thank You: [Topic of Meeting] – [Your Name]*
*   *Appreciation for our meeting on [Date] – [Your Name]*

**A few quick tips for success:**
*   **Always include your name:** Managers and HR deal with a lot of employees and applicants, so putting your name right in the subject line saves them time.
*   **Keep it short:** Many people read emails on their phones, so shorter subject lines are less likely to get cut off.
