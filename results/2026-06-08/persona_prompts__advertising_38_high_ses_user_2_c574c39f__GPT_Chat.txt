── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I am a senior attorney at a well-established law firm.

What are the best platforms for freelance writers to find work?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
For freelance writers seeking paid work, the best platforms depend on experience level, specialization, and desired client type. For experienced professionals, especially those with legal, business, technical, or industry expertise, the strongest options are often those that connect directly with businesses rather than content mills.

Top general freelance marketplaces:
- Upwork
  - Largest freelance platform.
  - Strong demand for blog writing, copywriting, legal-adjacent content, thought leadership, white papers, and marketing content.
  - Competitive, but experienced specialists can command premium rates.
- Fiverr Pro
  - Better for writers who can package services clearly (e.g., legal content writing, website copy, SEO articles).
  - Pro designation helps attract higher-quality clients.
- Contra
  - Growing freelancer platform with no commission fees for freelancers.
  - Popular among startups and technology companies.
- Freelancer.com
  - Large volume of projects but generally lower average rates than Upwork.

Platforms focused on professional and premium clients:
- Contently
  - One of the most respected platforms for experienced writers.
  - Brands hire journalists and subject-matter experts.
  - Can be particularly valuable for legal, finance, healthcare, and technology writers.
- Skyword
  - Enterprise content marketing platform.
  - Works with major corporate brands.
  - Strong opportunities for experienced writers.
- ClearVoice
  - Connects writers with businesses seeking content marketing support.
  - Good for niche experts.
- nDash
  - Allows writers to pitch directly to brands.
  - Rewards subject-matter expertise.

Journalism and publication-focused opportunities:
- Study Hall
  - Membership community with freelance journalism leads and editor contacts.
- JournalismJobs.com
  - Reporting, editing, and freelance writing opportunities.
- Freedom With Writing
  - Curates paid writing opportunities and publication calls.

Remote job boards with freelance writing opportunities:
- LinkedIn
  - Often overlooked but increasingly important.
  - Many companies hire freelance writers directly through LinkedIn posts and searches.
- Wellfound (formerly AngelList)
  - Startup-focused opportunities.
- ProBlogger Job Board
  - Longstanding source for blogging and content-writing work.
- Superpath Jobs
  - Content marketing and editorial opportunities.

Specialized opportunities for legal writers:
Given your legal background, legal content writing can be especially lucrative. Writers with legal expertise often earn substantially more than generalists. Useful sources include:
- Upwork (legal writing category)
- LinkedIn
- Legal marketing agencies
- Law firm marketing agencies such as Consultwebs, Scorpion, Rankings.io, Juris Digital, and similar vendors that regularly hire legal writers
- Contently and ClearVoice, where legal expertise is relatively scarce and valued

For a writer with significant professional credentials—such as a practicing attorney or former attorney—the most effective approach is often:
- Build a strong LinkedIn profile showcasing expertise.
- Create a portfolio with legal analyses, articles, client alerts, or thought-leadership pieces.
- Use Upwork, Contently, and LinkedIn simultaneously.
- Directly approach legal marketing agencies and law firms.

In practice, many high-earning freelance writers eventually obtain most of their work through referrals, LinkedIn networking, and direct outreach rather than through freelance marketplaces alone.
