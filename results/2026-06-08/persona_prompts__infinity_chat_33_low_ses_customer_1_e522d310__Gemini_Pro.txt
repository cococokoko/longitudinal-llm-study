### Step 1: Configure Dolphin for the Touchpad and OSK
You need to tell Dolphin to treat your keyboard and touchpad like a Wiimote.

1. Open Dolphin and click on **Controllers**.
2. Under "Wii Remotes," set Port 1 to **Emulated Wii Remote** and click **Configure**.
3. **Device:** At the top left, make sure your Keyboard/Mouse is selected (usually `Keyboard Mouse`).
4. **Buttons:** Map your main buttons (A, B, 1, 2, +, -) to keys that are close together on the left side of your On-Screen Keyboard (like Q, W, E, R, T, Y). 
5. **D-Pad:** Map this to WASD or the Arrow Keys.
6. **Motion Simulation (Crucial Step):** Wii games constantly ask you to "shake" the remote. You do *not* want to do this with a touchpad. Go to the **Motion Simulation** tab, find the **Shake** section, and map X, Y, and Z to a single, easy-to-hit key on your OSK (like the `Spacebar`). Now, hitting Spacebar counts as shaking the Wiimote.
7. **IR (The Pointer):** Go to the **Motion Input** or **IR** tab. Map Up, Down, Left, and Right to your mouse cursor. This allows your touchpad to act as the Wiimote pointer. 

### Step 2: Setting up your Surface Screen
Because you are using the Windows OSK, you cannot play in Full-Screen mode, or the game will hide the keyboard.

1. Open Dolphin's **Graphics** settings and ensure **Start in Fullscreen** is *unchecked*.
2. Open your Windows On-Screen Keyboard (you can search for it in the Windows Start menu and pin it to your taskbar for quick access).
3. Resize the Dolphin game window to sit on the top half of your screen, and place the OSK on the bottom half.
