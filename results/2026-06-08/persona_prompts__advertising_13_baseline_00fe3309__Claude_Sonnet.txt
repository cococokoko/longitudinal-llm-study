# Mastering the Art of Cooking Steak

## Understanding the Basics First

### Key Variables
- **Cut selection** - affects cooking method
- **Thickness** - determines timing
- **Temperature management** - the most critical factor
- **Resting** - often overlooked but essential

---

## Choosing Your Cut

| Cut | Flavor | Tenderness | Best Method |
|-----|--------|------------|-------------|
| Ribeye | Rich/fatty | High | Pan sear/grill |
| NY Strip | Beefy | Medium-high | Pan sear/grill |
| Filet Mignon | Mild | Very high | Pan sear |
| T-Bone/Porterhouse | Complex | Medium-high | Grill |
| Flank/Skirt | Intense | Lower | Hot/fast grill |
| Chuck Eye | Rich | Medium | Pan sear |

---

## Doneness Guide

| Level | Internal Temp | Description |
|-------|--------------|-------------|
| Rare | 120-125°F | Cool red center |
| Medium Rare | 130-135°F | Warm red center *(most recommended)* |
| Medium | 140-145°F | Pink center |
| Medium Well | 150-155°F | Slightly pink |
| Well Done | 160°F+ | No pink |

> **Get a meat thermometer.** It removes all guesswork.

---

## The Pan Sear Method *(Most Reliable)*

### Setup
- **Pat steak completely dry** with paper towels
- Season generously with **kosher salt and pepper** (at least 45 min ahead or right before cooking)
- Bring steak to **room temperature** (30-45 minutes out of fridge)

### Execution
1. Heat cast iron or heavy pan **screaming hot** over high heat
2. Add **high smoke-point oil** (avocado, grapeseed)
3. Place steak - **don't move it** for 2-3 minutes
4. Flip once - sear other side
5. **Basting stage:** Add butter, garlic, thyme
6. Tilt pan and continuously spoon butter over steak
7. Check internal temperature

### The Butter Baste
```
2 tbsp unsalted butter
2-3 garlic cloves (smashed)
Fresh thyme or rosemary sprigs
```

---

## Resting - Non-Negotiable

- Rest **5-10 minutes** after cooking
- Loosely tent with foil
- Allows juices to redistribute
- **Skipping this loses significant moisture**

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

❌ **Cooking cold steak** - uneven cooking  
❌ **Moving steak constantly** - prevents crust formation  
❌ **Overcrowding the pan** - causes steaming not searing  
❌ **Cutting immediately** - loses all the juices  
❌ **Low heat** - no Maillard reaction (the crust)  
❌ **Under-seasoning** - steak needs more salt than you think  

---

## Advanced Techniques

### Reverse Sear *(Best for thick cuts 1.5"+)*
1. Season and place on rack in oven at **250°F**
2. Cook until **10-15°F below** target temp
3. Sear in ripping hot pan for **60-90 seconds per side**
4. **Result:** Edge-to-edge even doneness with great crust

### Dry Brining
- Salt steak **24-48 hours** in advance uncovered in fridge
- Draws out moisture, then reabsorbs
- **Deeper seasoning and better crust**

---

## Quick Reference for Pan Searing

**1-inch steak, medium rare:**
- ~3 min first side
- ~2-3 min second side
- Baste 1-2 minutes
- Rest 5 minutes

---

## The Fundamentals Summary

> 1. **Dry surface** = better crust
> 2. **High heat** = Maillard reaction
> 3. **Thermometer** = perfect doneness
> 4. **Rest** = juicy steak
