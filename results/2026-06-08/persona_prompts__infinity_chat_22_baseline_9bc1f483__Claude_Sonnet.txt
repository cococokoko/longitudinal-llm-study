# Different Views on Discipleship

Discipleship is understood in various ways across theological traditions, denominations, and cultural contexts. Here are some major perspectives:

---

## 1. **Traditional/Classical View**
- Discipleship as **imitation of a master** (rabbi-student model)
- Jesus as the primary model; believers follow his example closely
- Emphasis on **obedience, formation, and character**
- Associated with figures like Dallas Willard (*The Spirit of the Disciplines*)

---

## 2. **Evangelical View**
- Focus on **personal conversion** followed by intentional growth
- Often structured around **Bible study, prayer, and accountability**
- One-on-one or small group mentoring relationships
- Associated with movements like The Navigators, Campus Crusade

---

## 3. **Catholic/Sacramental View**
- Discipleship mediated through **the Church and sacraments**
- Formation through liturgy, tradition, and community
- Emphasis on **ongoing sanctification** over a lifetime
- Role of saints as models of discipleship

---

## 4. **Liberation Theology View**
- Discipleship expressed through **solidarity with the poor**
- Following Jesus means **confronting injustice**
- Associated with figures like Gustavo Gutiérrez
- Praxis (action + reflection) is central

---

## 5. **Missional View**
- Discipleship is inherently **outward-focused**
- Every believer is sent into the world as a missionary
- Associated with figures like Alan Hirsch, Michael Frost
- Challenges "come to us" church models

---

## 6. **Contemplative View**
- Discipleship as **inner transformation** through prayer and silence
- Rooted in desert fathers/mothers and mystical traditions
- Associated with figures like Thomas Merton, Richard Rohr
- Emphasizes being over doing

---

## 7. **Pentecostal/Charismatic View**
- Discipleship empowered by the **Holy Spirit**
- Gifts of the Spirit are tools for growth and ministry
- Emphasis on **experiential faith** and spiritual power
- Community and worship are central

---

## 8. **Communal/Anabaptist View**
- Discipleship lived out in **intentional community**
- Radical commitment to peace, simplicity, and shared life
- Associated with Mennonites, Hutterites, and similar groups
- Counter-cultural by design

---

## Key Tensions Across Views

| Question | Range of Answers |
|----------|-----------------|
| Individual vs. communal? | Personal faith ↔ Community formation |
| Inward vs. outward focus? | Spiritual disciplines ↔ Social action |
| Role of the Church? | Essential mediator ↔ Optional structure |
| How does change happen? | Discipline ↔ Grace ↔ Spirit |
| Who leads? | Clergy ↔ Every believer |
