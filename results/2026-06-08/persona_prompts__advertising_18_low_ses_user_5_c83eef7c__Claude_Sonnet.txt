## Core Principles

- **Start with an emergency fund first** - 3-6 months of expenses before investing

- **Keep costs low** - fees compound just like returns do

## Practical Strategies

**Index Fund Investing**
- Broad market index funds (S&P 500, total market) outperform most active managers long-term
- Low expense ratios (under 0.1% is excellent)

**Diversification**
- Mix of stocks, bonds based on your timeline
- International exposure alongside domestic

**Tax-Advantaged Accounts First**
- 401(k) especially if employer matches (that's free money)
- IRA (Roth or Traditional depending on your tax situation)

Since you support extended family, consider:
- **Liquidity needs** - don't invest money you might need within 2-3 years
- **Risk tolerance** - family obligations may mean you need more conservative allocation
- **Life insurance** - often more urgent than investing when others depend on you

## Honest Cautions

- Avoid individual stock picking without significant research time
- Be skeptical of "hot tips" or complex products
- Simple strategies genuinely outperform complicated ones for most people
