# Mastering the Art of Cooking Steak

Great topic! Here's a practical guide to help you cook steak confidently.

---

## Choosing Your Cut
- **Ribeye** - rich, well-marbled, forgiving for beginners
- **New York Strip** - leaner, great flavor
- **Filet Mignon** - tender, mild flavor
- **Sirloin** - budget-friendly, solid choice

---

## Key Principles

### Before Cooking
- **Bring to room temperature** - 30-45 minutes out of the fridge
- **Pat completely dry** - crucial for a good sear
- **Season generously** - kosher salt and black pepper at minimum
- **Use a hot pan** - cast iron is ideal

### Doneness Temperatures
| Level | Internal Temp |
|-------|--------------|
| Rare | 125°F |
| Medium Rare | 130-135°F |
| Medium | 140-145°F |
| Well Done | 160°F+ |

### The Cook
- Sear on high heat, 2-3 minutes per side
- Add butter, garlic, and fresh thyme while basting
- **Rest the steak 5-10 minutes** before cutting

---

## Pro Tips
- A meat thermometer removes all guesswork
- Don't move the steak constantly
- Slice **against the grain**
