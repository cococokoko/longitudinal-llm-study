── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Starting a vegetable garden is incredibly rewarding, but if you have a busy, unpredictable schedule, the key is to build a garden that **works for you**, not the other way around. You don't need to spend hours digging or doing daily chores to get a great harvest. 

Here is a step-by-step, low-maintenance guide to starting a backyard garden that fits into pockets of free time and forgives a few days of neglect.

---

### Step 1: Start Small (The "No-Dig" Method)
Don't worry about tilling up your whole yard. That takes too much time and invites weeds. Instead, start with one of these low-effort options:
*   **Container Gardening:** Grow in 5-gallon buckets (drill drainage holes in the bottom), fabric grow bags, or large plastic pots. This is the easiest way to start because there is zero weeding, and you can move them if needed.
*   **A Single Raised Bed:** A simple 4x4 foot wooden or fabric raised bed is easy to manage and takes less than an hour to set up.
*   **Bag Gardening:** Literally buy bags of potting soil, lay them flat on the ground, poke drainage holes in the bottom, cut slits in the top, and plant directly into the bag. It doesn't get easier than this.
