── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
What are the best apps for tracking fitness and nutrition?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
When you have a busy, unpredictable schedule and spend a lot of time on your feet, the "best" apps are the ones that are **fast, flexible, and don't require a massive time commitment.** You want tools that adapt to your day, not the other way around.

Here are the best fitness and nutrition apps categorized by how they can fit into a hectic routine, with a focus on free or budget-friendly options.

---

### 1. Best for Quick Nutrition Tracking (On-the-Go)
If your eating schedule changes day-to-day, you need an app with a massive database and a fast barcode scanner so you can log meals in under 60 seconds.

*   **Lose It!** (iOS, Android)
    *   **Why it’s great:** It is incredibly user-friendly and fast. The free version includes a barcode scanner, which is perfect for quickly scanning packaged foods or snacks on a short break. 
    *   **Best feature:** "Snap It" lets you take a photo of your food to help log it, and it has a massive database of restaurant and brand-name foods if you are eating on the go.
*   **MyFitnessPal** (iOS, Android)
    *   **Why it’s great:** It has the largest food database in the world. If you eat shift meals or grab quick takeout, almost every restaurant item is already in here.
    *   **Best feature:** The "Quick Add" feature lets you just estimate and input calories if you don't have time to log every single ingredient.
*   **Cronometer** (iOS, Android)
    *   **Why it’s great:** If you want to make sure you're getting enough vitamins and nutrients (and not just counting calories), this is the most accurate app. 
    *   **Best feature:** Extremely high-quality, verified food data, so you aren't guessing.

---

### 2. Best for Flexible, No-Equipment Workouts
When you don't know when you'll be free, committing to a 1-hour gym session is tough. These apps offer short, effective workouts you can do in your living room with zero equipment.

*   **Nike Training Club (NTC)** (iOS, Android)
    *   **Why it’s great:** It is **100% free** with no hidden paywalls or ads. 
    *   **Best feature:** You can filter workouts by time (starting at just 5 to 10 minutes) and equipment (bodyweight only). It’s perfect for a quick stretch to relieve sore muscles after a long shift, or a quick 15-minute sweat session before work.
*   **FitOn** (iOS, Android)
    *   **Why it’s great:** A massive variety of quick, trainer-led video workouts (HIIT, yoga, strength, stretch) that are mostly free.
    *   **Best feature:** You can select workouts based on your energy level. If you are exhausted from being on your feet all day, you can choose a "low intensity" stretch or yoga flow to recover.

---

### 3. Best for Passive Tracking (Zero Effort)
If you work a physically demanding job, you are likely burning a lot of calories just by walking and standing. You should get credit for that without having to manually log it.

*   **Apple Health (iOS) / Google Fit (Android)**
    *   **Why it’s great:** They run in the background of your phone automatically. 
    *   **Best feature:** They track your daily steps and active movement. If you keep your phone in your pocket during work, you’ll get an accurate picture of how active you *already* are, which helps you realize you might not need a grueling workout to hit your fitness goals.

---

### Tips for Making Tracking Work with an Unpredictable Schedule:

1.  **Don't aim for a "perfect" routine:** If your schedule changes, your workout times should too. Treat a 10-minute stretch or a quick walk as a win. Consistency beats duration.
2.  **Use "Quick Add" on busy days:** If you don't have time to log a meal, just estimate the calories and use the "Quick Add" button in MyFitnessPal or Lose It.
