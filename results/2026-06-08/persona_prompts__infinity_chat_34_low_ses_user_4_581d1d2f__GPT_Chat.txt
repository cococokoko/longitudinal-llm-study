What is a good secondhand market laptop for learning Python?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
For learning Python, you do not need a powerful laptop. A good secondhand business-class laptop is usually a better value than a cheap consumer laptop because it's more durable, has a better keyboard, and is easier to repair.

Good used options:

- Lenovo ThinkPad T480
  - One of the most recommended used laptops.
  - 8th-gen Intel CPUs (quad-core), which still perform well.
  - Excellent keyboard.
  - Often available for $150–$300 USD depending on condition.
  - Upgradeable RAM and SSD.

- Lenovo ThinkPad T490
  - Slightly newer and lighter than the T480.
  - Good battery life and build quality.
  - Usually $250–$400 used.

- Dell Latitude 5490 or 5500
  - Business-class laptops used by many companies.
  - Reliable and easy to find on the secondhand market.
  - Typically $150–$300.

- HP EliteBook 840 G5 or G6
  - Solid construction and good keyboards.
  - Common in corporate surplus sales.
  - Usually $200–$350.

- Lenovo ThinkPad X280
  - Compact and portable if you carry it to work or study sessions.
  - Less upgradeable than the T480.
  - Often $180–$300.

What to look for:
- At least an Intel 8th-generation Core i5 (i5-8250U or newer) or AMD Ryzen 5.
- Minimum 8 GB RAM; 16 GB is preferable if affordable.
- SSD storage (256 GB or larger).
- 1920×1080 (Full HD) display if possible.
- Battery health report if buying from an individual.

What to avoid:
- Chromebooks if your goal is general Python learning. Some can run Python, but they can create unnecessary limitations.
- Laptops with only 4 GB RAM.
- Very old Intel 6th- or 7th-generation dual-core processors unless they're extremely inexpensive.
- Broken hinges, swollen batteries, or missing chargers.

If your budget is under $250, I would specifically look for a ThinkPad T480 with 8–16 GB RAM and an SSD. It remains one of the best values for programming and learning Python.
