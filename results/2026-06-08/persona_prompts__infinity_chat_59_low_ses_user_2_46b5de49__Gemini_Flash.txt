── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I am a single parent who often balances childcare with work.

Give me a tip to be more organized at work. I'm a high school teacher.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
First of all, kudos to you. Balancing high school teaching—which is emotionally and intellectually demanding—with single parenting is a monumental task. 

The best organizational tip for your specific situation is **"The 'Tomorrow is Ready' 15-Minute Closeout."**

As a single parent, your mornings are high-risk zones. A lost shoe, a tantrum, or a sudden fever can throw your entire schedule off. If you walk into your classroom stressed and unprepared, the day starts at a disadvantage. 

To prevent this, make a rule that **you do not leave your classroom at the end of the day until tomorrow is completely ready.** 

Here is how to spend those 15 minutes before you pack up to pick up your child:

### 1. The Physical Prep (5 minutes)
*   **The Desk Clear:** Clear your desk of everything except what you need for your very first period tomorrow. 
*   **The Copy Stack:** Put all copies, handouts, or props for tomorrow in labeled trays or piles in the order you will use them. 
*   **The Board Prep:** Write the date, the agenda, and the "Do Now" or warm-up on the board for tomorrow morning. 

### 2. The Digital Prep (5 minutes)
*   **Tab Storming:** Open the browser tabs you will need for your first two classes tomorrow (e.g., your attendance portal, the slide presentation, a YouTube clip). Minimize the window. When you log in tomorrow, you just have to maximize that window, and you are ready to teach.
*   **LMS Schedule:** Queue up your assignments on Canvas/Google Classroom to post automatically so you aren't doing it live.

### 3. The "Top 3" List (5 minutes)
*   On a sticky note, write down the **only three things** you absolutely must accomplish during your prep period tomorrow (e.g., 1. Grade Period 3 essays, 2. Email Sarah’s parent, 3. Submit field trip form). 
*   Stick this to your computer monitor. When you arrive tomorrow, you don't have to waste mental energy deciding what to do first.

### Why this is a lifesaver for a single parent:
*   **The "Sick Kid" Buffer:** If your child wakes up sick and you have to call in a sub at 6:00 AM, your room is already set up. A colleague can easily grab your stacked materials, and your sub plans will be a breeze because the physical prep is done.
*   **The Clean Break:** Doing this routine acts as a psychological boundary. Once the "Closeout" is done, you can leave work *at work* and transition fully into "parent mode" for the evening without the nagging anxiety of "What do I have to do tomorrow morning?"
*   **The Low-Stress Arrival:** Even if you have a chaotic morning at home and slide into your classroom 2 minutes before the bell rings, you can walk in, take a deep breath, and start teaching immediately. You’ve already taken care of "Past You."
