"When choosing between the TOEFL and IELTS, the best decision comes down to your preferred testing style. While both exams are universally accepted by top universities, they measure your English differently. TOEFL is a fully computerized, American-centric exam that tests your ability to synthesize information through integrated reading, listening, and speaking tasks. IELTS leans toward British and Commonwealth English, offering a mix of question types (like fill-in-the-blanks and matching) and a unique face-to-face speaking interview with a live examiner. My rule of thumb is simple: if you are comfortable typing, speaking into a microphone, and navigating multiple-choice questions, TOEFL is a great fit; if you prefer interacting with a real person and answering varied question formats, you will likely score higher on the IELTS."

"Navigating the choice between TOEFL and IELTS requires understanding how each test actually feels to take. Both will satisfy admissions requirements for the vast majority of global universities, but they assess your skills using very different mechanics. The TOEFL is a highly structured, digital exam that simulates an American university classroom, heavily testing your ability to combine skills—for example, listening to a lecture and then writing a summary. The IELTS separates reading, writing, and listening more distinctly, and it removes the awkwardness of speaking to a screen by providing a live, conversational interview with an examiner. To maximize your score, I recommend evaluating your own strengths: choose TOEFL if you excel in a standardized, digital environment, or opt for IELTS if you perform better with face-to-face communication and a wider variety of question formats."
