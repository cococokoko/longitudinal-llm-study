── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here are some effective ways to improve your credit score relatively quickly:

## Highest Impact Actions

- **Pay down credit card balances** - Reducing your credit utilization ratio (below 30%, ideally below 10%) can show results within a billing cycle
- **Pay all bills on time** - Payment history is the largest factor (~35% of your score)
- **Dispute any errors** on your credit reports at Equifax, Experian, and TransUnion

## Moderate Impact Steps

- **Avoid closing old accounts** - Keeps your credit history length intact
- **Avoid opening multiple new accounts** - Each hard inquiry temporarily lowers your score
- **Become an authorized user** on a responsible person's account

## Practical Timeline

| Action | Potential Timeframe |
|--------|-------------------|
| Dispute errors | 30-45 days |
| Reduce utilization | 1-2 billing cycles |
| Payment history improvement | 3-6 months |

## Quick Checks

- Request free reports at **AnnualCreditReport.com**
- Use free monitoring tools like Credit Karma
- Consider asking creditors for a **goodwill adjustment** on old late payments
