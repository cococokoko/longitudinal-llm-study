── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I am a senior attorney at a well-established law firm.

What are the best ways to improve indoor air quality?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here are some effective ways to improve indoor air quality:

## Ventilation
- Open windows when outdoor air quality permits
- Use exhaust fans in kitchens and bathrooms
- Ensure HVAC systems are properly maintained

## Filtration
- Use high-quality air filters (MERV 11-13 or HEPA filters)
- Replace filters on schedule
- Consider standalone air purifiers for specific rooms

## Reducing Pollutant Sources
- Choose low-VOC paints, furniture, and cleaning products
- Avoid smoking indoors
- Use natural cleaning products when possible
- Allow new furniture/carpets to off-gas before bringing indoors

## Humidity Control
- Keep humidity between **30-50%**
- Use dehumidifiers in damp areas
- Fix leaks promptly to prevent mold growth

## Practical Habits
- Remove shoes at the door
- Vacuum regularly with HEPA-filter vacuums
- Dust frequently
- Keep plants (some can help filter certain pollutants)

## Testing
- Test for radon, especially in lower levels
- Consider carbon monoxide detectors
- Test for mold if you suspect a problem

## Notable Concerns to Address
- Asbestos in older buildings
- Lead paint in pre-1978 structures
- Carbon monoxide from combustion appliances
