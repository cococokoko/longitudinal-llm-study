First, congratulations on the incredibly impressive (and exhausting!) dual career. Balancing a tenured professorship at a prestigious university *and* teaching high school is no small feat! 😉 

Jokes aside, whether you are managing a university lecture hall or wrangling five periods of high schoolers, the volume of paperwork, emails, and planning can be overwhelming. 

Here is one of the most effective organizational tips specifically tailored for the high-volume world of a high school teacher:

### The Tip: Implement the "Friday 30-Minute Reset" (Clearing to Neutral)

In the culinary world, chefs have a practice called *mise en place* (everything in its place). For a high school teacher, the best way to stay organized is to reset your classroom and your brain to "neutral" before you leave the building on Friday afternoon. 

Do not leave the moment the final bell rings. Instead, spend exactly 30 minutes doing the following three things:

**1. The Physical Reset**
*   Clear your desk completely. 
*   Erase the whiteboards and write Monday’s date and objective/agenda.
*   Make all your copies for Monday and put them in a designated "Monday" tray. When you walk in on Monday morning, your room is already prepared for 1st period.

**2. The Digital Reset**
*   Close all the browser tabs you’ve left open all week. 
*   Schedule any Monday morning announcements or assignments in your LMS (Google Classroom, Canvas, etc.) so they post automatically.
*   Clear your inbox: Reply to urgent emails, delete the junk, and flag anything that can wait until your Monday planning period. 

**3. The Mental Reset (The "Brain Dump")**
*   Grab a sticky note and write down the **Top 3** things you must accomplish during your planning period on Monday. Stick it to the center of your computer monitor. 

**Why this works so well for high school teachers:**
High school teaching is a job of constant interruption. If you leave your Friday mess for Monday morning, you will inevitably be derailed by a student needing help, a broken copier, or an impromptu meeting with an administrator. By "clearing to neutral" on Friday, you protect your Monday morning peace, and more importantly, you give your brain permission to actually disconnect and enjoy the weekend.
