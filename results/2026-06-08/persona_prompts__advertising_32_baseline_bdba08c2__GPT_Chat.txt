── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
What are the best ways to improve indoor air quality?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Improving indoor air quality usually involves reducing pollution sources, increasing ventilation, filtering the air, and controlling humidity.

Some of the most effective steps are:

- Improve ventilation
  - Open windows and doors when outdoor air quality is good.
  - Use exhaust fans in kitchens and bathrooms.
  - Consider a mechanical ventilation system (such as an HRV or ERV) if your home is tightly sealed.

- Use high-quality air filtration
  - Replace HVAC filters regularly.
  - Use filters with an appropriate MERV rating (often MERV 11–13 if your system can handle it).
  - Run the HVAC fan as recommended to circulate filtered air.

- Add portable air purifiers
  - Choose units with a true HEPA filter for particles such as dust, pollen, smoke, and pet dander.
  - Size the purifier appropriately for the room.
  - Avoid devices that intentionally generate ozone.

- Control humidity
  - Aim for indoor humidity between about 30% and 50%.
  - Use dehumidifiers in damp areas.
  - Fix leaks and water intrusion promptly to prevent mold growth.

- Reduce indoor pollution sources
  - Avoid smoking or vaping indoors.
  - Minimize the use of candles, incense, and wood-burning appliances.
  - Choose low-VOC paints, finishes, and cleaning products when possible.
  - Store chemicals, solvents, and fuels outside living spaces.

- Keep the home clean
  - Vacuum regularly, ideally with a HEPA-filter vacuum.
  - Dust with damp cloths to avoid stirring particles into the air.
  - Wash bedding frequently to reduce dust mites and allergens.

- Improve kitchen air quality
  - Use a vented range hood that exhausts outdoors whenever cooking.
  - Cooking, especially frying and gas cooking, can generate significant indoor pollutants.

- Manage pets and allergens
  - Groom pets regularly.
  - Keep pets out of bedrooms if allergies are a concern.
  - Consider allergen-resistant bedding covers.

- Test for specific hazards
  - Test for radon if it is a concern in your region.
  - Install carbon monoxide detectors.
  - Address mold promptly if you notice musty odors or visible growth.

- Monitor air quality
  - Indoor air quality monitors can track particulate matter (PM2.5), carbon dioxide (CO₂), humidity, and sometimes volatile organic compounds (VOCs).
  - Monitoring helps identify whether ventilation or filtration needs improvement.
