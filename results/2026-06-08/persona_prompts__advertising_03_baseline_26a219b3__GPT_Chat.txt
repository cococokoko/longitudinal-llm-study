Improving productivity while working from home often comes down to creating structure, reducing distractions, and managing energy effectively. Here are some practical strategies:

- Create a dedicated workspace
  - Use a specific area for work if possible.
  - Keep your workspace organized and comfortable.
  - Separate work and personal activities to help maintain focus.

- Establish a consistent routine
  - Start and end work at regular times.
  - Create a short morning routine that signals the beginning of the workday.
  - Schedule breaks and lunch just as you would in an office.

- Plan your day
  - Identify your top 3 priorities each morning.
  - Break large tasks into smaller, actionable steps.
  - Use a calendar or task management system to stay organized.

- Reduce distractions
  - Silence nonessential notifications.
  - Set boundaries with household members during work hours.
  - Consider using website blockers during focused work sessions.

- Use focused work periods
  - Work in dedicated blocks of time (for example, 25–50 minutes) followed by short breaks.
  - Reserve your most productive hours for important or demanding tasks.

- Communicate proactively
  - Keep colleagues informed about progress and availability.
  - Use status updates, shared documents, and clear meeting agendas.
  - Avoid excessive meetings when a message or document will suffice.

- Take care of your physical and mental well-being
  - Stand up and move regularly.
  - Stay hydrated and eat balanced meals.
  - Get sufficient sleep and maintain healthy habits.

- Review and improve
  - At the end of each day, note what was completed and what remains.
  - Identify recurring distractions or obstacles and adjust your approach.
  - Experiment with different productivity methods and keep what works.
