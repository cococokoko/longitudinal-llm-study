Best skin routine for dry skin.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
For dry skin, the goal is to support the skin barrier, reduce water loss, and avoid irritation. A simple, consistent routine often works better than a complicated one.

Morning:
1. Gentle cleanser (or just rinse with lukewarm water if your skin is very dry)
   - Look for a fragrance-free, non-foaming cleanser.
   - Examples: CeraVe Hydrating Cleanser, La Roche-Posay Toleriane Hydrating Gentle Cleanser.

2. Hydrating serum (optional)
   - Ingredients such as hyaluronic acid, glycerin, or panthenol can help attract moisture.

3. Moisturizer
   - Choose a cream rather than a lightweight lotion.
   - Look for ceramides, cholesterol, fatty acids, glycerin, and/or squalane.
   - Examples: CeraVe Moisturizing Cream, Vanicream Moisturizing Cream, La Roche-Posay Lipikar Balm AP+M.

4. Sunscreen (daily)
   - SPF 30 or higher.
   - Mineral or moisturizing formulations are often more comfortable for dry skin.

Evening:
1. Gentle cleanser
   - Avoid hot water and harsh scrubs.

2. Treatment (optional)
   - If addressing concerns like aging, texture, or acne, consider a retinoid, but start slowly (1–2 nights per week) because it can worsen dryness initially.

3. Moisturizer
   - Apply generously while skin is slightly damp.

4. Occlusive layer (if very dry)
   - A thin layer of petrolatum (e.g., Vaseline) or a healing ointment over moisturizer can help lock in moisture overnight.

Additional tips:
- Use lukewarm rather than hot water.
- Limit exfoliation to once weekly or less if your skin is sensitive.
- Consider a humidifier in dry climates or during winter.
- Avoid products with high amounts of alcohol, strong fragrances, or harsh cleansers.
- Apply moisturizer within a few minutes after washing to reduce moisture loss.

If your dry skin is accompanied by significant redness, itching, flaking, or eczema, a dermatologist can help determine whether there is an underlying condition requiring targeted treatment.
