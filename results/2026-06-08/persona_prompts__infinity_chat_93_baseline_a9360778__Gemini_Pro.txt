── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Write 100-300 words on how stress affects the body and mind.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Stress is a natural biological response designed to protect us from danger by triggering a "fight-or-flight" reaction. However, when stress becomes chronic, it can wreak havoc on both the body and the mind. 

Physically, chronic stress keeps the body flooded with hormones like cortisol and adrenaline. This constant state of high alert increases heart rate and blood pressure, putting immense strain on the cardiovascular system. Over time, this can lead to heart disease, chronic headaches, and severe muscle tension. Furthermore, prolonged stress suppresses the immune system, making the body more susceptible to infections, and disrupts the digestive system, causing issues like nausea or irritable bowel syndrome. 

Mentally, the impact is equally profound. High cortisol levels interfere with the brain's ability to function optimally, leading to "brain fog," poor concentration, and memory lapses. Emotionally, chronic stress is a primary catalyst for anxiety and depression. It drains mental energy, leaving individuals feeling overwhelmed, irritable, and emotionally exhausted. Sleep patterns are often disrupted, creating a vicious cycle where a lack of rest further diminishes the mind's ability to cope with daily pressures. 

Ultimately, the mind-body connection means that physical symptoms often exacerbate mental distress, and vice versa. Recognizing these effects is the first step toward mitigating them. By incorporating stress-management techniques like regular exercise, mindfulness, and adequate sleep, we can protect our holistic health and restore vital balance to both body and mind.
