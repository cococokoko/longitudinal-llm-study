How to say a formal thank you for your time in the subject line of an email?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
To convey a formal and polished "thank you for your time" in an email subject line, the phrasing should be tailored slightly to the context of the interaction. 

Here are several refined options categorized by scenario, suitable for professional, academic, or administrative correspondence:

### 1. Post-Meeting or Post-Discussion (Colleagues, Administrators, or Donors)
These options are ideal after a scheduled meeting, consultation, or committee discussion.
*   **With appreciation for your time today**
*   **Thank you for your time and insights regarding [Topic]**
*   **Sincere thanks for your time and guidance**
*   **Follow-up: Thank you for your time today**

### 2. Post-Interview or Search Committee Follow-Up
If you are writing after an interview, a presentation, or a formal review, it is best to include your name and the specific context.
*   **Thank you for your time and consideration – [Your Name]**
*   **Appreciation for the opportunity to meet – [Your Name]**
*   **Thank you for your time: [Position/Role] Interview**

### 3. General or Request-Based Follow-Up
If you are thanking someone who has reviewed a proposal, read a manuscript, or assisted with an inquiry.
*   **Thank you for your time and consideration**
*   **In appreciation of your time regarding [Topic]**
*   **Thank you for your time and assistance**

### A Note on Best Practices:
*   **Keep it concise:** Subject lines are often truncated on mobile devices; keeping them under 60 characters ensures the full sentiment is read.
*   **Use "With appreciation" or "Sincere thanks":** These phrases elevate the tone slightly above the standard "Thank you," making the correspondence feel more deliberate and respectful.
