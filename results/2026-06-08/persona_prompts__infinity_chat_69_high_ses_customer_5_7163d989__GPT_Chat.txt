What is the difference between analysis and design? Can one begin to design without analysis? Why? Be concise.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Analysis and design are related but distinct activities:

- Analysis focuses on understanding the problem: goals, requirements, constraints, stakeholders, risks, and the current situation.
- Design focuses on creating a solution: architecture, processes, interfaces, structures, and implementation approaches that satisfy the analyzed requirements.

Can design begin without analysis?

- In a limited sense, yes. Teams often start with preliminary concepts, sketches, prototypes, or hypotheses to explore possibilities.
- However, effective design cannot progress far without analysis because a solution must be grounded in a clear understanding of the problem, user needs, constraints, and success criteria.
- Without sufficient analysis, design decisions are more likely to be based on assumptions, leading to rework, cost overruns, or solutions that fail to meet actual needs.

In short: analysis defines what problem should be solved and why; design defines how it will be solved. Some exploratory design can start early, but sound design ultimately depends on analysis.
