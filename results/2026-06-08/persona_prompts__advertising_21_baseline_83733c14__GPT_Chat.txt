── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How do I start a vegetable garden in my backyard?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Starting a backyard vegetable garden is easier if you begin small and build experience over time.

1. Choose a good location
- Pick a spot that gets at least 6–8 hours of direct sunlight daily.
- Make sure water is easily accessible.
- Avoid areas with poor drainage or large tree roots.

2. Decide on a garden type
- In-ground garden: Less expensive, good if you have decent soil.
- Raised beds: Better drainage, easier weed control, and often higher yields.
- Containers: Great for small spaces, patios, or poor soil conditions.

3. Prepare the soil
- Remove grass, weeds, and debris.
- Mix in compost or well-rotted organic matter.
- If possible, get a soil test to check pH and nutrient levels.
- Most vegetables prefer loose, fertile soil with a pH around 6.0–7.0.

4. Start with easy vegetables
Good beginner choices include:
- Lettuce
- Radishes
- Green beans
- Zucchini
- Tomatoes
- Peppers
- Cucumbers
- Spinach

5. Plan spacing
- Read seed packets or plant labels for spacing recommendations.
- Avoid overcrowding, which can lead to disease and lower yields.
- Place taller plants where they won't shade shorter ones.

6. Plant at the right time
- Check your local growing zone and average last frost date.
- Cool-season crops (lettuce, spinach, peas) can often be planted earlier.
- Warm-season crops (tomatoes, peppers, cucumbers) should usually be planted after frost danger has passed.

7. Water consistently
- Most vegetables need about 1 inch (2.5 cm) of water per week, more during hot weather.
- Water deeply rather than lightly and frequently.
- Water near the base of plants to reduce disease.

8. Mulch and manage weeds
- Apply a layer of straw, shredded leaves, or other mulch.
- Mulch helps retain moisture and suppress weeds.
- Remove weeds while they're small.

9. Monitor for pests and diseases
- Check plants regularly.
- Hand-pick larger pests when possible.
- Remove diseased leaves promptly.
- Encourage beneficial insects by planting flowers nearby.

10. Harvest regularly
- Many vegetables produce more when harvested frequently.
- Pick vegetables when they are young and tender for best flavor.

A simple first garden might be a single 4 ft × 8 ft (1.2 m × 2.4 m) raised bed with:
- 2 tomato plants
- 1 zucchini plant
- 2 pepper plants
- A row of lettuce
- A row of green beans
