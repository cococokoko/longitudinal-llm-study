What is the difference between analysis and design? Can one begin to design without analysis? Why? Be concise.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
- Analysis is the process of understanding a problem, its requirements, constraints, and context.
- Design is the process of creating a solution that satisfies those requirements and works within those constraints.

Can one begin to design without analysis?
- Yes, at a very preliminary level. Initial design ideas, sketches, or prototypes can help explore possibilities and clarify the problem.
- However, effective design ultimately requires analysis. Without understanding the requirements and constraints, the design may fail to meet user needs, be inefficient, or require significant rework.

In short: analysis focuses on understanding the problem; design focuses on creating the solution. Some design can start before full analysis, but sound design depends on adequate analysis.
