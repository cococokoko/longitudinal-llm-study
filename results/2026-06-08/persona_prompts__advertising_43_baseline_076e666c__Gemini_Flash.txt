── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
How to plan an environmentally friendly wedding?

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Planning an environmentally friendly (or "green") wedding is a beautiful way to celebrate your love while honoring the planet. The wedding industry is notoriously high-waste, but with conscious choices, you can significantly reduce your carbon footprint without sacrificing style, elegance, or fun.

Here is a comprehensive guide to planning a sustainable, eco-friendly wedding, broken down by category.

---

### 1. Venue & Travel (The Biggest Carbon Footprint)
Transportation is usually the largest contributor to a wedding’s carbon footprint. 

*   **Keep it Local:** Choose a venue close to where the majority of your guests live to minimize flights and long drives.
*   **One Location:** Host your ceremony and reception at the same venue. This eliminates the need for guests to drive between locations.
*   **Choose a Naturally Beautiful Venue:** Botanical gardens, state parks, beaches, or vineyards require very little additional decor. 
*   **Look for Eco-Certifications:** Seek out venues with LEED certification, or those that use solar power, practice composting, and have strict recycling policies.
*   **Provide Group Transport:** Rent a shuttle bus or encourage carpooling to get guests from their hotels to the venue.

### 2. Invitations & Paper Goods
Traditional wedding paper suites generate a lot of waste.

*   **Go Digital:** Send digital save-the-dates and invitations using platforms like Greenvelope, WithJoy, or Paperless Post. 
*   **Use Recycled or Seed Paper:** If you prefer physical invites, choose 100% recycled paper or "seed paper" (which guests can plant in their gardens to grow wildflowers).
*   **Simplify the Suite:** Instead of including multiple cards (RSVP, details, directions), send a single card with a QR code directing guests to your wedding website for all details and RSVPs.
*   **Eco-Friendly Signage:** Use chalkboards, wooden signs, or mirrors that can be erased and reused, rather than printing large foam-board signs.

### 3. Attire & Rings
The fashion industry has a heavy environmental impact, but wedding attire is ripe for sustainable alternatives.

*   **Rent or Buy Secondhand:** 
    *   For the bride: Look at sites like Stillwhite, Nearly Newlywed, or local consignment shops for pre-owned designer gowns.
    *   For the groom/wedding party: Rent suits or tuxedos (e.g., Black Tux, Generation Tux) rather than buying.
*   **Choose Sustainable Fabrics:** If buying new, look for designers who use organic cotton, linen, silk, or hemp, and practice ethical manufacturing.
*   **Wear it Again:** Choose bridesmaid dresses and groomsmen attire that your wedding party can actually wear to other events.
*   **Ethical Jewelry:** Choose wedding bands made from recycled metals. For diamonds, opt for lab-grown diamonds, vintage/heirloom stones, or conflict-free certified diamonds (like those with the Kimberley Process certification).

### 4. Flowers & Decor
Floral foam is highly toxic and non-biodegradable, and imported flowers are often sprayed with heavy pesticides and flown thousands of miles.

*   **Go Local and Seasonal:** Work with a florist who sources flowers from local flower farms. Seasonal blooms look fresher and have a much lower carbon footprint.
*   **Say No to Floral Foam:** Ask your florist to use sustainable mechanics, like chicken wire or reusable glass vases, instead of green floral foam.
*   **Potted Plants & Herbs:** Use potted plants, ferns, or herbs as centerpieces. Guests can take them home, or you can plant them in your garden after the wedding.
*   **Repurpose Arrangements:** Move your ceremony altar flowers to the sweetheart table
