Write 100-300 words on how stress affects the body and mind.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Stress is a natural survival mechanism, but when it becomes chronic, it takes a severe toll on both the body and the mind. 

Physically, stress triggers the "fight-or-flight" response, releasing a flood of hormones like cortisol and adrenaline. This increases heart rate and blood pressure, preparing the body for immediate action. However, prolonged exposure to these hormones can lead to serious health issues, including cardiovascular disease, a weakened immune system, chronic muscle tension, and digestive problems. It also disrupts sleep patterns, leaving the body fatigued and unable to properly repair itself.

Mentally, chronic stress impairs cognitive function. It floods the brain with anxiety, making it difficult to concentrate, make decisions, or retain information. Over time, this mental strain can lead to emotional exhaustion, irritability, and mood swings. It significantly increases the risk of developing mental health disorders, such as clinical depression and generalized anxiety disorder. 

Ultimately, the mind and body are deeply interconnected; physical discomfort feeds mental anxiety, and mental strain manifests as physical pain. Recognizing the signs of stress is the first crucial step toward managing it and protecting overall well-being.
