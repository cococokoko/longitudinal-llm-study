── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Give me a tip to be more organized at work. I'm a high school teacher.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
A practical tip for high school teachers: create a single "daily dashboard" for yourself.

At the start of each day, keep one page (paper or digital) with only three sections:
- Must do today: 1–3 high-priority tasks (e.g., grade quiz, call parent, prepare tomorrow's lab).
- During class: notes about students, behavior, or follow-ups that come up throughout the day.
- Later: tasks that don't need immediate attention but shouldn't be forgotten.

The key is to avoid scattering information across sticky notes, emails, planners, and random documents. Whenever something new arises, put it on the dashboard first. Then, at the end of the day, spend 5 minutes transferring unfinished items to tomorrow's dashboard or your longer-term task list.

Many teachers find that having one trusted capture system reduces mental clutter and makes it easier to keep track of both instructional and administrative responsibilities.
