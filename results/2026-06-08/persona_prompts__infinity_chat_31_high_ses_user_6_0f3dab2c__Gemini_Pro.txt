As a partner at a major consulting firm, your financial profile is unique. You have high earning power, a high marginal tax rate, severe time constraints, and—most importantly—**strict compliance and independence restrictions** regarding what you can legally and ethically invest in. 

Because €60,000 is likely a bonus, a distribution, or a few months of surplus savings rather than your entire net worth, the "best" investment depends on what role this money plays in your broader portfolio. 

Here is a structured framework of the best ways to deploy €60,000, tailored to your specific profile.

---

### 1. The "Compliance-Safe" Core: Broad-Market ETFs
For partners at MBB or Big 4 firms, buying individual stocks is a compliance nightmare. You constantly have to check restricted entity lists, clear trades with your independence office, and worry about insider trading optics.
*   **The Play:** Put the €60,000 into a globally diversified, low-cost ETF (e.g., MSCI World, FTSE All-World, or S&P 500). 
*   **Why it fits you:** Broad-based index funds are almost universally exempt from firm independence restrictions. They require zero time to manage, compound efficiently over time, and keep you out of trouble with your firm's risk management team.
*   **Expected Return:** Historically 7–9% annualized over the long term.

### 2. The Insider Advantage: Firm Co-Investments or Capital Calls
Many major consulting firms offer their partners access to internal investment vehicles, co-investment funds alongside private equity clients, or require capital contributions to fund firm growth.
*   **The Play:** Use the €60,000 to fund your next capital call, or invest in your firm’s internal partner investment fund.
*   **Why it fits you:** You have an informational advantage here. You know the health of your firm and the quality of the deals your PE practice is advising on. Furthermore, internal firm returns often outpace public markets to incentivize and retain partners.
*   **Expected Return:** Often 12–20%+, depending on the firm's performance and leverage.

### 3. The Tax-Efficiency Play: Maximize Pension / Tax-Advantaged Accounts
Since you are investing in Euros, you are likely based in a European jurisdiction with a marginal income tax rate of 45% to 50%+. 
*   **The Play:** If you haven't already, use the €60,000 to max out any tax-deductible pension contributions, statutory retirement accounts, or tax-free wrappers (like an ISA in the UK, PEA in France, or equivalent in your country).
*   **Why it fits you:** If you invest €60,000 and get a 45% tax relief on the contribution, you are generating an immediate, risk-free return. For high earners, tax optimization is often more lucrative than chasing alpha in the markets.

### 4. The Time-Poor Real Estate Play: REITs or Syndications
You likely work 60 to 80 hours a week. You do not have the time to be a landlord, deal with broken boilers, or manage tenants. However, €60,000 is a solid entry point for passive real estate.
*   **The Play:** Invest in Real Estate Investment Trusts (REITs) or a private real estate syndication/crowdfunding platform (where you are a limited partner). Alternatively, €60,000 can serve as a down payment on a buy-to-let property *only if* you hire a full-service property management company.
*   **Why it fits you:** It provides diversification away from equities and your firm's performance, offers a hedge against inflation, and generates passive yield without requiring your time.
