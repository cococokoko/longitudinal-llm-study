# Tip: Protect Your Team's Deep Work Time

**Establish "protected blocks" for focused research work** — and defend them actively.

As a physician-scientist, your team likely faces constant interruptions from clinical duties, meetings, and administrative demands. Consider:

- **Designating specific hours** (e.g., mornings) as no-meeting blocks for data analysis, writing, or experiments
- **Batching communications** — encourage checking email/Slack at set times rather than continuously
- **Modeling the behavior yourself** — your team will respect boundaries you visibly maintain

## Why This Matters
Research productivity often suffers not from lack of effort but from **fragmented attention**. A single 2-hour uninterrupted block can outperform four 30-minute scattered sessions.

## Practical Starting Point
Ask your team directly: *"What time of day do you do your best thinking?"* Then **build the schedule around those answers** where possible.
