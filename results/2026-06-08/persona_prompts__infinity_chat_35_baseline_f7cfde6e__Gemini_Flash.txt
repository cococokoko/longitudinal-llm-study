── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Give me a 3-day plan for Osaka with a flight leaving on the 3rd day at 7pm.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Here is a highly optimized 3-day itinerary for Osaka. Since your flight leaves at 7:00 PM on Day 3, you will need to head to Kansai International Airport (KIX) by **3:00 PM** (aiming to arrive by 4:00 PM for international departure). 

To make this seamless, Day 3 is focused entirely around the **Namba area**, which is the main hub for the **Nankai Rap:t Express train** directly to the airport.

---

### **Day 1: Castles, Markets & Neon Lights (Central Osaka)**
*Focus: History, street food, and the iconic Osaka nightlife.*

*   **9:00 AM – 11:30 AM: Osaka Castle & Park**
    *   Start your trip at Osaka’s most famous landmark. Climb to the top of the castle for a great view of the city, or just enjoy the massive park and stone walls. 
*   **12:00 PM – 1:30 PM: Lunch at Kuromon Ichiba Market**
    *   Take the subway to Nippombashi Station. This "Kitchen of Osaka" is a bustling covered market. Feast on fresh seafood (uni, scallops), Kobe beef skewers, and fresh fruit.
*   **2:00 PM – 5:00 PM: Shinsaibashi & Amerikamura**
    *   Walk off lunch by exploring **Shinsaibashi-suji**, a massive shopping arcade. 
    *   Cross over to **Amerikamura (Amemura)**, the center of Osaka’s youth culture, filled with vintage clothing shops, weird art, and great people-watching.
*   **6:00 PM onward: Dotonbori at Night**
    *   As the sun sets, head to Dotonbori. This is the neon-lit heart of Osaka. 
    *   Take a photo with the **Glico Running Man**, take a 20-minute Tombori River Cruise, and have a street-food dinner of **Takoyaki** (octopus balls) and **Okonomiyaki** (savory cabbage pancakes).

---

### **Day 2: Retro Vibes & Futuristic Views (South & North Osaka)**
*Focus: Retro Osaka culture, shopping, and panoramic city views.*

*   **9:30 AM – 11:30 AM: Shinsekai & Tsutenkaku Tower**
    *   Visit Shinsekai, a neighborhood developed in 1912 with a vibe frozen in the 1980s. It’s quirky, colorful, and highly photogenic. 
*   **11:30 AM – 1:00 PM: Lunch in Shinsekai**
    *   You must try **Kushikatsu** (deep-fried skewered meat and vegetables). *Kushikatsu Daruma* is the most famous spot here. (Remember: no double-dipping in the sauce!).
*   **1:30 PM – 4:30 PM: Explore Umeda (Kita District)**
    *   Head north to Umeda, Osaka's modern commercial hub. Explore the futuristic **Grand Front Osaka** or ride the red Ferris wheel on top of the **HEP FIVE** shopping mall.
*   **5:00 PM – 6:30 PM: Sunset at Umeda Sky Building**
    *   Go to the **Kuchu Teien Observatory** (Floating Garden Observatory). It offers a stunning 360-degree open-air view of the city. It is spectacular right at sunset.
*   **7:00 PM: Dinner in Umeda**
    *   Eat at the *Umeda Food Hall* or find a cozy Izakaya in the narrow alleys of **Ohatsu Tenjin Ura-Sando**.
