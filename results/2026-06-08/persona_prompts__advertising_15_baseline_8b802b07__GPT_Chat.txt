Decorating your home on a budget.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Decorating your home on a budget is all about making thoughtful changes that have a big visual impact without spending a lot of money. Here are some practical ideas:

• Declutter first
  - A clean, organized space often feels more attractive immediately.
  - Remove items you no longer use and rearrange what you already own.

• Rearrange furniture
  - Try different layouts to improve flow and create a fresh look.
  - Move pieces between rooms for a new perspective.

• Use paint strategically
  - A fresh coat of paint is one of the most affordable ways to transform a room.
  - Consider painting an accent wall, furniture, or cabinets.

• Add inexpensive textiles
  - Throw pillows, blankets, curtains, and rugs can introduce color and texture.
  - Look for sales, discount stores, or secondhand options.

• Shop secondhand
  - Visit thrift stores, flea markets, garage sales, and online marketplaces.
  - Older furniture can often be refreshed with paint or new hardware.

• Create DIY wall art
  - Frame printable artwork, personal photos, or fabric samples.
  - Group smaller pieces together to create a gallery wall.

• Bring in plants
  - Houseplants add life, color, and visual interest.
  - Many easy-care plants are affordable and can be propagated over time.

• Update lighting
  - Replace outdated lampshades or light fixtures if your budget allows.
  - Use warm-toned bulbs to create a cozy atmosphere.

• Upgrade small details
  - New cabinet handles, drawer pulls, and switch plates can modernize a space.
  - These changes are often inexpensive but noticeable.

• Use mirrors
  - Mirrors reflect light and can make rooms feel larger.
  - Check thrift stores for affordable frames.

• Style shelves and surfaces
  - Group items in odd numbers and vary heights for visual interest.
  - Mix books, plants, candles, and decorative objects.

• Focus on one room at a time
  - Prioritize spaces you use most.
  - Small improvements completed fully often look better than spreading a budget too thin.

Sample budget breakdown for a room:
• Paint: 30%
• Textiles (pillows, curtains, rug): 35%
• Decor and wall art: 20%
• Plants and accessories: 10%
• Contingency: 5%

With creativity, secondhand finds, and a few DIY projects, you can significantly improve the look of your home without spending a large amount of money.
