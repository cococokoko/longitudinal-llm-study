── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
I am a single parent who often balances childcare with work.

Give me a tip to be more organized at work. I'm a high school teacher.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
As a single parent and a high school teacher, you are managing two of the most demanding, unpredictable jobs in the world. Because your mornings at home are likely rushed and vulnerable to sudden changes (a lost shoe, a sick kid, traffic), the best thing you can do for your work organization happens at the *end* of the day. 

Here is the most impactful tip for your specific situation:

### **The 15-Minute "Walk-In-Ready" Reset**

Do not leave your classroom until tomorrow’s first period is completely set up, so that if you have a chaotic morning and arrive exactly as the bell rings, you can still teach flawlessly. 

Take the last 15 minutes of your contract day (or right after the students leave) to do these four things:
1. **Clear your physical desk:** Put away today's clutter. Place *only* the copies/materials you need for tomorrow's first class dead center on your desk.
2. **Queue your digital desk:** Open the slides, videos, or attendance tabs you need for tomorrow morning. Put your computer to sleep rather than shutting it down so everything is ready the second you wake it up.
3. **Prep the board:** Write tomorrow’s date, agenda, and "Do Now" or bell-ringer on the whiteboard. 
4. **Pack your bag:** Put your keys, badge, and anything going home in your bag and set it by the door. 

**Why this works for a single parent:** 
Time poverty is your biggest enemy. If you try to prep in the morning, a single parenting hiccup at home will derail your entire workday. By making your classroom "walk-in-ready" the afternoon before, you buy yourself a massive buffer. You can leave work at work, go home, focus on your child, and sleep better knowing tomorrow morning is already handled.

**A quick bonus tip for high school teachers:** 
Leverage your students! High schoolers are highly capable. Do not spend your precious time organizing a messy classroom, sorting papers, or stapling packets. Assign these tasks to students who finish their work early, or make it a rotating classroom job. Protect your energy for teaching and parenting.
