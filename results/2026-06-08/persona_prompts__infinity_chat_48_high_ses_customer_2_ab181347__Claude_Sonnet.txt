Building upon the administrative foundations laid by the Qin Dynasty, Han rulers implemented a series of policies that stimulated agricultural productivity, including land redistribution, reduced taxation, and the promotion of advanced farming techniques such as iron-tipped plows and improved irrigation systems. The state played a central role in economic management, with Emperor Wu (r. 141–87 BCE) notably instituting government monopolies over the production and sale of iron, salt, and alcohol, generating substantial imperial revenue while simultaneously curtailing the power of wealthy merchants.
