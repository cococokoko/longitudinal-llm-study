── Prompt ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Mastering the art of cooking steak.

── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
After a long, physically demanding shift on your feet at the hospital, you deserve a meal that feels like a massive reward but doesn't take hours to make. The beauty of mastering steak is that it’s actually one of the fastest, most satisfying, high-protein dinners you can make. 

Here is a foolproof guide to the classic **Pan-Seared Butter-Basted Steak**. It takes about 15 minutes of active cooking—perfect for when you just want to eat something amazing and crash.

### 1. The Prep (Do this right when you get home)
*   **Take the chill off:** Take your steak (a Ribeye or New York Strip about 1 to 1.5 inches thick) out of the fridge right when you walk in the door. Let it sit on the counter for 30–45 minutes while you shower and change out of your scrubs. 
*   **Dry it off:** Moisture is the enemy of a good crust. Pat the steak completely dry with paper towels.
*   **Season aggressively:** Season both sides generously with coarse Kosher salt and fresh black pepper. Don't be shy; a thick piece of meat needs a lot of seasoning.

### 2. The Sear (High Heat is Key)
*   **The Pan:** A cast-iron skillet is your best friend here, but any heavy-bottomed stainless steel pan will work. 
*   **The Oil:** Heat the pan over medium-high heat. Add a tablespoon of oil with a high smoke point (like avocado, canola, or grapeseed oil—*not* extra virgin olive oil, which will burn). 
*   **The Sizzle:** Once the oil is shimmering and just starting to smoke, carefully lay the steak in the pan (lay it away from you so the oil doesn't splash). 
*   **Leave it alone:** Let it sear for about 3 to 4 minutes until it develops a deep, dark brown crust. Flip it over.

### 3. The "Chef's Touch" (The Butter Baste)
*   Once you flip the steak, turn the heat down to medium-low.
*   Toss in 2 tablespoons of butter, a couple of smashed garlic cloves, and a sprig of fresh thyme or rosemary if you have it.
*   As the butter melts and foams, tilt the pan slightly toward you. Use a spoon to continuously scoop the foaming, garlic-herb butter and pour it over the top of the steak. Do this for about 1 to 2 minutes. This cooks the steak gently from the top down and infuses it with incredible flavor.

### 4. The Most Important Step: Rest
*   Take the steak out of the pan and put it on a cutting board. Pour the leftover butter from the pan right over the top.
*   **Let it rest for 5 to 10 minutes.** Do not cut into it immediately! If you do, all the juices will run out onto the board. Resting allows the muscle fibers to relax and redistribute the juices. (This is the perfect time to pack your bag for your next shift or just sit down and take a breath).

### 5. Slice and Serve
*   Slice the steak against the grain (perpendicular to the lines of the muscle fibers) for the most tender bite. 

**A Quick PCA Pro-Tip:** 
If you have a stretch of shifts coming up, cook two steaks. A hot steak is a great dinner tonight, but cold, thinly sliced steak is the ultimate "jealousy-inducing" breakroom lunch. Throw it over some mixed greens with a little vinaigrette, or put it in a wrap. It’s a massive upgrade from hospital cafeteria food and will give you the energy you need to get through your patient rounds!
