── Response ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Decorating your home on a budget is often about prioritizing high-impact changes and being selective about where you spend money.

Some practical strategies:

- Start with a plan
  - Choose a color palette and style before buying anything.
  - Measure rooms and make a simple layout to avoid costly mistakes.

- Rearrange what you already own
  - Moving furniture, artwork, or accessories between rooms can make a space feel new at no cost.

- Focus on paint
  - Paint is one of the most cost-effective upgrades.
  - Neutral wall colors can brighten a room and make it feel larger.

- Use secondhand sources
  - Check thrift stores, estate sales, flea markets, online marketplaces, and local buy/sell groups.
  - Solid wood furniture can often be found for less than the cost of new particleboard pieces.

- Upgrade textiles
  - New throw pillows, curtains, rugs, and bedding can dramatically change a room without major expense.
  - Mixing inexpensive textiles with a few quality pieces often looks more expensive than it is.

- Improve lighting
  - Add floor lamps or table lamps instead of relying solely on overhead lights.
  - Use warm-toned bulbs for a more inviting atmosphere.

- Create a gallery wall
  - Frame personal photos, prints, or downloadable artwork.
  - Consistent frames can make inexpensive art look cohesive.

- Add plants
  - Houseplants bring color and texture.
  - Many varieties are inexpensive and easy to maintain.

- Update hardware
  - Replacing cabinet handles, drawer pulls, or light switch covers can refresh kitchens and bathrooms for relatively little money.

- DIY selectively
  - Simple projects such as painting furniture or building basic shelves can save money.
  - Avoid complex DIY projects that may end up costing more to fix later.

A useful budgeting guideline is to spend most of your decorating budget on pieces you use daily (such as seating, mattresses, and frequently used tables) and save money on decorative accessories that can be changed easily over time.
