### Step 1: Choosing Your Cut
The journey to a perfect steak starts at the butcher counter. Look for steaks that are at least **1.5 to 2 inches thick** (thin steaks overcook in the middle before you can get a good crust).

*   **Ribeye:** The king of flavor. It has heavy marbling (white flecks of intramuscular fat) which melts during cooking, keeping the meat incredibly juicy and forgiving.
*   **New York Strip:** A great balance of deep beefy flavor and tenderness, with a thick band of fat along the edge.
*   **Filet Mignon:** The most tender cut, though it has less fat and flavor. Best cooked with a rich butter baste.
*   **Flat Iron or Flank:** Budget-friendly, thin, and quick-cooking. Excellent for slicing thin against the grain.
