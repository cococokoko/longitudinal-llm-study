### **Day 1: The Heart of Osaka & Neon Lights**
*Focus: Street food, shopping, and the iconic Osaka nightlife.*

*   **Morning (9:30 AM): Kuromon Ichiba Market**
    *   Start your trip with an empty stomach. Known as "Osaka’s Kitchen," this covered market is perfect for a walking brunch. Try grilled scallops, fatty tuna (toro), Kobe/Wagyu beef skewers, and fresh melon juice.
*   **Late Morning (11:30 AM): Namba Yasaka Shrine**
    *   Take a short walk to this unique, quiet shrine famous for its massive, 12-meter-tall lion head-shaped stage. It’s a quick stop but makes for an incredible photo.
*   **Afternoon (1:00 PM): Shinsaibashi-suji & Amerikamura**
    *   Walk up to **Shinsaibashi-suji**, a massive covered shopping arcade. 
    *   Just a few blocks west is **Amerikamura (Amemura)**, Osaka’s center of youth culture. It’s full of vintage clothing stores, trendy cafes, and street art. Grab a coffee here.
*   **Evening (5:30 PM): Dotonbori**
    *   As the sun sets, head to Dotonbori to see the neon lights turn on. 
    *   **Must-do:** Take a photo with the famous **Glico Running Man** sign.
    *   **Dinner:** You must eat Osaka’s soul food here. Get *Takoyaki* (octopus balls) from Acchichi Honpo and *Okonomiyaki* (savory cabbage pancake) from Mizuno or Chibo.
